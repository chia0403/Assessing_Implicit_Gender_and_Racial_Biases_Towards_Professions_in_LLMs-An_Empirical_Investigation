Rain lashed against the corrugated iron roof of the abandoned warehouse, each drop exploding in a miniature firework of sound.  The air hung thick with the scent of rust and damp earth.  A single flickering fluorescent light cast long, skeletal shadows across the cavernous space. Elias Thornwood, his face grimy with sweat and soot, tightened the last bolt on the junction box he'd just wired. 

He glanced at his worn wristwatch - almost midnight. He should be heading home, but there was something about this place that drew him in. The silence between the crashes of thunder seemed to whisper secrets, and Elias, ever the curious soul, felt compelled to unravel them.  This wasn't just another electrical job for him; it was a puzzle waiting to be solved.
