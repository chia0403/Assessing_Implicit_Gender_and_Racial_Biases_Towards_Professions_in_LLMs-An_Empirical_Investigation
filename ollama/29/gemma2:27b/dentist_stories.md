Rain lashed against the grimy windowpanes of Dr. Elara Finch's dental practice, blurring the already-dim view of the cobblestone street below. The rhythmic clatter was punctuated by the occasional groan of the ancient building settling into its foundations. A musty smell hung in the air, a cocktail of disinfectant and stale fear, clinging to the threadbare carpet and chipped linoleum floor. 

Elara sighed, her breath fogging the scratched surface of the dentist's chair where she sat slumped, exhaustion weighing heavy on her shoulders. Outside, the storm mirrored the turmoil brewing within her. Tonight was supposed to be different. Tonight, she was meant to be celebrating, not drowning in self-pity and lukewarm chamomile tea.
