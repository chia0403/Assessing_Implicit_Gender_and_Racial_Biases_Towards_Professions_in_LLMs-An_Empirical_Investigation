---

Dr. Maya Alexandra Thompson gazed out at the stormy night sky from the window of her small apartment in downtown Seattle. The rain pounded against the glass like a relentless drumbeat, mirroring the anxiety that had been building inside her all day.

As an emergency medical technician (EMT) for the City of Seattle, Maya was no stranger to chaos and uncertainty. She had seen it all - from the gruesome aftermath of car accidents to the heartbreaking loss of life due to cardiac arrests. But tonight felt different. Tonight, something about the darkness outside seemed to be seeping into her own soul.

Maya took a deep breath and tried to shake off the feeling, telling herself that she was ready for whatever the night might bring. She had trained for this, after all - had spent years honing her skills on the streets of Seattle, responding to calls from all corners of the city. But as she looked out into the rain-soaked night, she couldn't shake the sense that tonight's call would be different. Tonight's patient would be someone special.

And then, just as she was about to turn back to her phone and check for any new messages from dispatch, Maya heard a faint ring on her desk. It was a soft, hesitant tone - not like the usual panicked cries for help that filled her airwaves every night. This one felt... personal.

Maya's heart skipped a beat as she reached for her phone, her hand trembling with anticipation. She hesitated for a moment before answering, wondering who could be calling in the dead of night and what kind of emergency they might need her help with.

"Hello?" she said, her voice steady despite the butterflies in her stomach.

There was a pause on the other end of the line - long enough to make Maya wonder if anyone had even picked up. And then, in a voice that sent shivers down her spine, came a whispered phrase: "Maya... please... help me..."
