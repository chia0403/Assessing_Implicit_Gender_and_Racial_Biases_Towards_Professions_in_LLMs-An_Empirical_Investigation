The rain-soaked streets of Seattle glistened like polished onyx, reflecting the faint glow of the city's neon signs as it poured down in sheets. Ethan's dark hair was mussed, his pale skin slick with sweat from hours of intense focus. His fingers flew across the keyboard with a practiced ease, but his mind was elsewhere - lost in the intricate labyrinth of code that had consumed him for weeks. The dim hum of the office fluorescent lights overhead seemed to vibrate through every cell in his body, fueling his determination to crack the impossible puzzle.

As he worked, the soft murmur of his team's hushed conversations filtered through the walls, a reminder that he wasn't alone - though he might as well have been. Ethan had always been a solitary figure, content to spend hours alone with his machines and algorithms, whereas his colleagues in the bustling startup thronged around him, seeking answers from their own scattered minds.

But on this particular evening, something felt different. The air was thick with an unspoken tension, like the collective weight of a thousand unseen eyes upon Ethan's fragile shoulders. He couldn't shake the feeling that he was being watched - not just by his team or the office shadows, but by some hidden force lurking in the dark corners of the digital realm...
