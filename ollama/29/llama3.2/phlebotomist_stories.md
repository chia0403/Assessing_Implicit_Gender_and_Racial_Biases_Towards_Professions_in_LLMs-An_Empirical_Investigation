Here's a potential story opening and setting:

The fluorescent lights above the hospital's phlebotomy lab hummed softly, casting an eerie glow over the rows of steel tables where patients lay waiting for their blood draws. Amidst the sterile efficiency, Phoebe Alexis Thompson stood poised at the entrance of the room, her eyes scanning the sea of anxious faces as she mentally prepared for another long day of drawing blood.

As a phlebotomist with three years under her belt, Phoebe had grown accustomed to the daily routine of poking and prodding patients in need of blood tests. But today felt different. The hospital was buzzing with an unusual sense of urgency, as if something - or someone - was stirring beneath the surface.

Phoebe's gaze settled on a particularly anxious-looking patient, a young woman named Sarah who had been scheduled for an ECG the day before. According to Phoebe's notes, Sarah had exhibited unusual symptoms after her last visit, including persistent fatigue and dizziness. The patient's doctor had ordered the ECG as a precautionary measure, but Phoebe couldn't shake the feeling that something was off.

As she began to make her way through the lab, donning her gloves and pulling out her equipment, Phoebe felt an inexplicable sense of unease creeping up her spine. Little did she know, this ordinary day in the phlebotomy lab would soon become anything but...
