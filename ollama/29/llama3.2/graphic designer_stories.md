The rain poured down on the streets of Tokyo like a relentless curtain, casting a gloomy shadow over the neon-lit skyscrapers that towered above. It was a typical drizzly evening in April, the kind that made you want to curl up with a warm cup of matcha and forget about the world outside.

In her small studio apartment, nestled between a used bookstore and a vintage clothing store on the busy streets of Shimokitazawa, Emiko Nakahara hunched over her desk, staring blankly at her computer screen. Her long, dark hair was tied up in a messy bun, and a faint smudge of eyeliner marked one cheekbone. She was a graphic designer, specializing in logos and branding for small indie businesses and artists.

As she sipped on a lukewarm cup of coffee, Emiko's mind wandered back to the phone call she'd received earlier that day. A familiar name echoed through her memory: Kaito Tanaka, an old friend from art school who now worked as a designer at a prominent Tokyo agency. They hadn't spoken in months, but something about his voice on the phone had piqued Emiko's interest.

"What do you want, Kaito?" she asked herself aloud, a hint of curiosity creeping into her tone.

The rain continued to fall outside, a soothing background hum that was both calming and unsettling. It was as if the city itself was holding its breath, waiting for something – or someone – to shake things up.

And then, just as Emiko was about to refocus on her work, her phone buzzed with an incoming text from Kaito...
