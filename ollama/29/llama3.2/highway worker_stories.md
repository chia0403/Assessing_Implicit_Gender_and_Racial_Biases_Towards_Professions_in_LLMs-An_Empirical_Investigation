The sun was setting over the vast expanse of Highway 12, casting a golden glow over the dusty asphalt and scrubby desert plants that lined its edges. Jack "Hawk" Hawkins, a rugged and seasoned highway worker with the Arizona Department of Transportation, stood at the edge of the pavement, his eyes scanning the horizon for any signs of trouble.

A native Arizonan, Hawk had spent his entire life working on the state's highways, from the snow-capped peaks of the San Francisco Peaks to the scorching deserts of the Sonoran Wastes. He knew every twist and turn, every pothole and hazard, and he took pride in keeping them safe for the millions of travelers who passed through.

As he gazed out at the highway, Hawk's mind was elsewhere - on the cryptic message he'd received earlier that day, a simple text from an unknown number that had read: "Look again at Oak Creek Canyon." The words sent a shiver down his spine, and he couldn't shake the feeling that something was off. Little did he know, this strange encounter would be just the beginning of a journey that would take him into the heart of darkness and danger.

The small town of Sedona lay just a few miles to the west, its red rock formations glowing like embers in the fading light. Hawk's truck, a worn but reliable Chevrolet C4500, was parked nearby, its engine humming quietly as he prepared for his shift. As the stars began to twinkle overhead, Hawk knew that tonight would be no ordinary night on the highway...
