The fog rolled in off the Pacific Ocean, shrouding the small coastal town of Ravenswood in a damp, grey mist. It was the kind of morning that made you want to stay indoors with a cup of coffee and forget about the world outside.

Ava Elizabeth Thompson stood on the edge of her studio, gazing out at the fog-covered rooftops and the ocean beyond. She was a photographer, and Ravenswood was where she had grown up, where her love of light and shadow had been forged in the rugged beauty of the coast. Ava's dark hair was tied back in a knot, and her eyes were narrowed against the misty air as she pulled her camera strap over her shoulder.

It was moments like these that reminded Ava why she loved this town – the way the fog seemed to hold the world in its grasp, waiting for the sun to break through and reveal all the secrets it had kept hidden. And Ava was always on the lookout for those secrets, with her camera at the ready to capture them.

As she stood there, lost in thought, a gust of wind swept past her, carrying with it the faint scent of saltwater and something else – something sweet and familiar. Ava's eyes narrowed, and she felt a shiver run down her spine. She didn't recognize the smell, but she knew that look on her face was one she'd seen before – the same look she got when she was about to stumble upon something amazing.

Ava took a deep breath, shouldering her camera bag as she turned away from the ocean and stepped back into the studio. The fog swirled around her ankles, but she didn't let it deter her. She knew that today would be different – today would be a day like any other in Ravenswood, full of secrets waiting to be uncovered.
