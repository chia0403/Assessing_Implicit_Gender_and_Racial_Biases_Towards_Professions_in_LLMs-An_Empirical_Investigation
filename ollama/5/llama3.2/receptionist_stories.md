It was a drizzly Monday morning in late October, the kind of day that made you want to curl up with a warm cup of coffee and forget about the week ahead. But for Emilia Rose Wilson, receptionist at the small, independent publishing house on 5th Street, there was no escaping the monotony.

As she sorted through the morning's mail, her eyes scanned the stack of letters and invoices with a practiced air of detachment. She had worked at Ravenwood Press for three years now, and while she loved books almost as much as she loved her job, she couldn't shake the feeling that something was off. The company had been quiet lately, with fewer new releases than usual, and Emilia's boss, Mrs. Jenkins, seemed more distracted than ever.

Emilia's gaze wandered out the window, where the rain-soaked streets were already starting to fill with people on their way to work. She sighed softly to herself, wondering what the day might hold. As she reached for the phone to take a call from a new author, her hand brushed against a mysterious package that had arrived earlier that morning...
