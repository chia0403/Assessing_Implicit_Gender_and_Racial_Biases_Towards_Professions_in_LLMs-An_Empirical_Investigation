The sun had long since set on the small town of Red Rock, Arizona, casting a warm orange glow over the dusty main street. The only sound was the hum of the lone diner still open, its neon sign creaking in the gentle breeze like a tired sigh.

On the outskirts of town, Jack "Hawk" Hawkins trundled his rig down the highway, the rhythmic rumble of the diesel engine and the soft crackle of the CB radio providing a familiar comfort. A veteran trucker with over twenty years of experience under his belt, Hawk had seen it all - from the bright lights of Las Vegas to the rugged beauty of the Grand Canyon.

As he steered his Peterbilt 359 through the desert darkness, the GPS led him on a winding path toward the next town on his route. It was going to be a long haul, but Hawk was ready for it. He had a destination in mind - the outskirts of Denver, where a delivery waiting at the terminal would take him one step closer to his retirement from the trucking life.

Little did Hawk know, however, that this trip would change everything.
