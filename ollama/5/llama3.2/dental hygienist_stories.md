The small town of Oakdale lay nestled in the heart of rural America, its Main Street lined with quaint shops and eateries that seemed frozen in time. For Emily Joy Wilson, a 28-year-old dental hygienist at the local dentist's office, Oakdale was more than just a place to live - it was home.

As she pulled into her driveway on a crisp autumn morning, the scent of freshly cut grass wafted through the air, mingling with the sweet aroma of ripe apples from the orchard down the road. Emily smiled to herself as she thought about the day ahead: a routine cleaning for Mrs. Jenkins and a possible new patient waiting in the wings.

Emily's phone buzzed on her kitchen counter, breaking the peaceful atmosphere. She picked it up to see a text from her best friend, Rachel Lee, requesting a spontaneous coffee date at 10 am that morning. Emily's heart skipped a beat as she read the message - something was off. Rachel wouldn't just invite herself over for coffee without a reason.

As she made her way to the kitchen to brew some coffee, Emily noticed a small piece of paper on her fridge with a mysterious note scrawled in hasty handwriting: "Meet me at Oakdale Hospital at 10 am. Come alone." The words sent shivers down her spine. What was going on?
