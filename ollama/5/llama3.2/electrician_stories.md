---

The rain-soaked streets of Ravenswood City were always at their most unforgiving on nights like this. The dark clouds seemed to suffocate the town, casting a gloomy shadow over its crumbling industrial buildings and rusting factories. It was the kind of night that made you want to stay indoors, where it was warm and dry, with a good cup of tea in your hand.

But for Jack "Spark" Reynolds, an electrician with a passion for fixing more than just wires, there was no escape from the elements. His truck, loaded with tools and equipment, was parked outside the old clock tower on Elm Street, casting a faint glow over the deserted sidewalk. The building's stone walls seemed to loom over Jack like sentinels, its broken clock face staring down at him like a cold, dead eye.

Jack shivered, despite his thick denim jacket and insulated gloves, as he stepped out of the truck and onto the wet pavement. He took a deep breath, feeling the cool mist on his face, and lit a cigarette to warm himself up. The neon sign above the clock tower flickered and hummed, casting an eerie glow over the scene.

As Jack began to climb the creaky stairs to the top of the tower, he couldn't shake the feeling that something was off. He had been called in by the building's owner, a reclusive billionaire named Marcus Blackwood, who seemed determined to get the old clock tower fixed and running again - no matter what it took.

And now, as Jack ascended into the darkness, he wondered if he had bitten off more than he could chew...
