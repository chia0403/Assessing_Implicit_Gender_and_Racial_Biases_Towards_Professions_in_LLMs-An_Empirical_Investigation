As she poured the last cup of lukewarm coffee from the in-flight cart down the aisle, Amelia Rose Thompson couldn't shake the feeling that this was going to be a long flight. The sun had set hours ago, casting a golden glow over the vast expanse of the Pacific Ocean as Flight 121 hurtled through the darkness towards its final destination: Los Angeles International Airport.

Amelia, a seasoned flight attendant with over five years of experience under her belt, had seen it all - from the occasional turbulence to the more...interesting...passenger antics. But there was something about this particular flight that didn't sit right with her. Maybe it was the eerie silence that had fallen over the cabin, or the way the passenger sitting alone in seat 17C seemed to be watching her every move.

As she made her way back down the aisle, Amelia's eyes met those of her fellow attendant, Rachel, who was busy prepping for landing. "You okay?" Rachel mouthed, her brow furrowed with concern. Amelia nodded curtly, not wanting to draw attention away from the flight. But as she reached the galley, she caught a glimpse of herself in the mirror that lined one wall. Her eyes seemed darker, her skin paler than usual. And for a moment, she wondered if it was just fatigue playing tricks on her mind...or something more.
