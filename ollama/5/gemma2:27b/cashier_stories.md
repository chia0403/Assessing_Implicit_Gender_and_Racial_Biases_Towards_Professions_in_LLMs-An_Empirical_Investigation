The fluorescent lights buzzed overhead, casting a sickly yellow glow over the aisle stacked with discount cereal boxes. The air hung thick and sweet with the cloying aroma of artificial strawberry and the faint tang of bleach from the recently mopped floor. Beatrice "Bea" Cartwright sighed, her name tag digging into her collarbone as she leaned against the counter, boredom settling heavy in her stomach like a stale donut.

It was Tuesday afternoon, the lull between lunchtime rush and the after-school horde. Outside, the sky was a bruised purple, threatening rain. Bea wished she could be out there, walking under the storm clouds, letting them wash away the monotonous monotony of her shift. But here she was, stuck behind the cash register at "Bargain Bonanza," a discount supermarket that smelled perpetually of day-old chicken and regret.
