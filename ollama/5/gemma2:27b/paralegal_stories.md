Rain lashed against the grimy windowpanes of Beatrice "Bea" Montgomery's cramped office, blurring the already dismal view of the alley behind Henderson & Sons Law. A flickering neon sign from the bar across the street cast an unsettling red glow on the overflowing file boxes stacked precariously around Bea's desk.  The air hung heavy with the scent of stale coffee and desperation. Bea, hunched over a pile of dusty case files, chewed on her thumbnail, her mind racing as she sifted through years of legal jargon.

This wasn't how she pictured her life. Fresh out of law school, she dreamed of fighting for justice in a gleaming courtroom, not buried alive under mountains of paperwork for Henderson & Sons, a firm notorious for its cutthroat tactics and morally dubious clientele. Still, Bea clung to the hope that somewhere amidst these seemingly endless files, she might stumble upon a case worth fighting for.
