Rain lashed against the windowpane, blurring the neon glow of "Oakhaven Pharmacy" into an impressionistic smear. Inside, Eleanor Rosalind Thorne meticulously counted out a prescription for lisinopril, her brow furrowed in concentration. The rhythmic tick-tock of the ancient grandfather clock behind the counter was the only sound breaking the silence, a stark contrast to the chaotic symphony raging outside. 

Eleanor had been working at Oakhaven Pharmacy for ten years, ever since inheriting it from her eccentric great aunt Matilda. Nestled in the heart of Willow Creek, a sleepy town with more retirees than youngsters, the pharmacy was Eleanor's lifeblood, a comforting constant in an increasingly unpredictable world. But tonight, even the familiar scent of camphor and aspirin couldn't soothe her unease. Something felt off, a prickling sensation at the back of her neck that whispered of trouble brewing.
