Rain lashed against the grimy windows of the drafting room, blurring the neon glow of the city skyline beyond. Elias Hawthorne hunched over his drafting table, the warm light of his desk lamp illuminating his face etched with concentration. The air was thick with the scent of graphite and old paper, punctuated by the rhythmic scratching of his mechanical pencil as it danced across the vellum sheet.

Elias was a man of meticulous detail, each line he drew perfectly measured and precise. He specialized in architectural plans for renovation projects, breathing new life into crumbling facades and forgotten spaces. Tonight, however, his focus wavered. The relentless storm seemed to mirror the turmoil brewing within him.
