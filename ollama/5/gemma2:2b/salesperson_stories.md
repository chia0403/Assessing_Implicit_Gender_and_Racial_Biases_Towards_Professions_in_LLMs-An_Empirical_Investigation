The fluorescent lights hummed overhead like a swarm of tired bees in the oppressive air-conditioning of Smith & Sons Department Store. Rain lashed against the shop windows, each drop like a miniature drumbeat on the glass panes. Inside, Amelia "May" Davenport huddled under her worn blue coat, shielding herself from both the elements and the biting chill emanating from the cavernous store.  Her boots splashed through a puddle of water forming at the bottom of the cracked concrete fountain in the atrium.  

Amelia was used to this kind of storm. This year had been particularly rough, with layoffs and dwindling sales leaving her sales team more anxious than usual, and customers more wary than ever. Today's target for her, however, felt insurmountable even for a seasoned salesperson like herself.
