The fluorescent lights hummed above Elara June Bellweather's head, casting a sterile glow over the aisles of "Bargain Buys," a discount store on the outskirts of Harmony Springs. The air was thick with the scent of stale coffee and dust bunnies – a familiar aroma to anyone who had ever worked in retail. 

Elara sighed, her gaze drifting from the dusty shelves to the clock ticking relentlessly towards closing time. Six o'clock couldn't come soon enough. She dreamt of escaping this purgatory of price tags and perpetually bored customers for the comfort of her tiny apartment, a mug of herbal tea, and the worn pages of a fantasy novel. 


She scanned another item – a suspiciously cheap electric toothbrush – into the register. It was moments like these that made Elara question her life choices.  Why hadn't she pursued her passion for writing instead?  The answer, as always, came back in a dull thud: rent. Writing didn't pay the bills, not yet anyway.
