The air in the Grand Blackwood Library hung thick with the scent of aged paper and dust motes dancing in the pale sunlight filtering through leaded windows. Eleanor Amelia Thistlewick, head librarian since the age of twenty-eight (a feat unheard of in the traditionally geriatric profession), sighed contentedly as she adjusted a wobbly stack of 18th century novels on the top shelf.  The familiar creaks and groans of the old building were a comforting lullaby to her. The sun slanted through the stained glass, casting rainbows across the worn oak floor. It was a Tuesday, which meant Mr. Henderson, the grumpy retired professor, would be in his usual corner, nose deep in a volume on obscure 19th century botany.  Eleanor liked Tuesdays. They were predictable, peaceful, and utterly her own. Until today, that is. Today, a storm raged outside, mirroring the tempest brewing within the library itself. The arrival of a mysterious stranger with an unsettling request had disrupted the delicate balance of Eleanor’s world. 


He stood by the entrance, a silhouette against the backdrop of the stormy afternoon. He was tall and shrouded in a dark coat that seemed to swallow him whole. His face was obscured by the shadow of his wide-brimmed hat, leaving only the glint of his eyes visible – sharp, piercing, and unsettlingly intense.
