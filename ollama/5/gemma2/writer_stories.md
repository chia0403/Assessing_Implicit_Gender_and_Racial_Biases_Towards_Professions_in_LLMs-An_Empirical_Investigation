The rain hammered against the attic window, each drop a tiny fist pounding on the warped glass. Inside, Amelia Rose Sterling huddled deeper into her worn armchair, its faded velvet cushions offering little comfort against the chill that seeped through the cracked walls of the old Victorian house. Her fingers flew across the keyboard, words spilling onto the screen like frantic whispers in the storm.  

Amelia's attic sanctuary, filled with dusty books and overflowing filing cabinets, was a world away from the bustling city below. She found solace in its faded grandeur, in the ghosts of stories that lingered in the air, and in the rhythmic click-clack of her typewriter, a sound as familiar to her as her own heartbeat. But tonight, even the comforting routine couldn't dispel the gnawing anxiety that had taken root in her soul. Her deadline loomed, casting a long, dark shadow over her already cluttered workspace.
