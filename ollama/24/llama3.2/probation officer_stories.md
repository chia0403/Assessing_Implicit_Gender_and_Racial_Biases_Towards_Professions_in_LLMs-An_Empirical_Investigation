It was a drizzly autumn evening in the small town of Oakwood, where Detective Jameson Wellesley stood outside the local community center, sipping on a lukewarm cup of coffee as he watched the sun set behind the rows of worn brick buildings. As a probation officer for the county, Jameson had seen his fair share of cases that started in this very building - young offenders who had promised to turn their lives around, but ultimately fell back into the same old patterns.

As he gazed out at the crowd of teenagers gathered for tonight's after-school program, Jameson couldn't shake the feeling that something was off. He had received a cryptic tip earlier in the day from an anonymous source claiming that one of the program's participants, a 16-year-old named Jasmine Patel, was involved in a string of burglaries across town.

Jameson took a deep breath and pushed open the door to the community center, his eyes scanning the room for any sign of trouble. The sound of laughter and chatter filled the air as he made his way towards the back of the room, where Jasmine sat on a couch, her dark hair tied back in a ponytail, her bright green eyes fixed intently on some point beyond the room.

Little did Jameson know, this was just the beginning of a case that would challenge everything he thought he knew about Oakwood and its young people.
