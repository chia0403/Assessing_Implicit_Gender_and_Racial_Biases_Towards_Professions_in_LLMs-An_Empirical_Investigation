---

The sun was setting over the small town of Red Rock, casting a warm orange glow over the dusty Main Street. The smell of grease and gasoline hung in the air, mingling with the scent of freshly brewed coffee wafting from the local diner. It was a smell that Ethan Ryder knew all too well, as it often accompanied him after a long day's work at his father's auto repair shop.

Ethan Ryder, or "Hank" as he was known to the locals, wiped the sweat from his forehead and gazed out at the faded sign creaking in the gentle breeze. The sign read "Ryder's Repairs" in bold letters, a name that had been passed down from Ethan's father, who had started the shop back in the 1950s. Now, with the sun dipping below the horizon and the stars beginning to twinkle overhead, Hank felt a sense of contentment wash over him. He'd fixed everything from rusty old pickup trucks to fancy sports cars, but tonight was different. Tonight, he was waiting for something – or someone.

As he leaned against the hood of his own battered old truck, a worn-out Ford F-150 with a patchwork of scars and scratches, Hank's mind wandered back to the mysterious phone call he'd received earlier that afternoon. The voice on the other end had been low and gravelly, speaking in hushed tones about a "problem" with one of his favorite restoration projects – a 1969 Mustang Fastback. The words had sent a shiver down Ethan's spine, and now, as the darkness gathered around him, he couldn't shake the feeling that something was off.
