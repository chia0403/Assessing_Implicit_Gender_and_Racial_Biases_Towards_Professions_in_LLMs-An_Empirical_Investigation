The air in the operating room hung thick with anticipation, the sterile scent of disinfectant mingling with the metallic tang of blood. Dr. Amelia Grace Hawthorne, her brow furrowed in concentration, meticulously sutured the gaping wound in the patient's abdomen.  Sweat beaded on her forehead, not from exertion, but from the sheer pressure of the situation. This was no routine surgery; this was a race against time.

The setting sun cast long shadows across the bustling city of New Alexandria, its gleaming skyscrapers casting an oppressive silhouette against the dying light. The hospital itself, known as St. Michael's Mercy, stood like a beacon of hope in the heart of this urban jungle. Its walls held countless stories of life and death, joy and despair, but tonight, all eyes were focused on Room 3B, where Dr. Hawthorne fought to save another life.
