The air hung thick with the scent of lavender shampoo and anticipation. Sunlight streamed through the large storefront windows of "Shear Delight," illuminating dust motes dancing in the morning haze. Eleanor Rosalind Finch, a woman whose auburn curls mirrored the fiery hues of her personality, adjusted her emerald green stylist smock with a practiced flick of the wrist. Her gaze swept over the salon - its familiar chaos a comforting melody to her ears: the clatter of shears, the whirring of hairdryers, and the chatter of clients swapping gossip like cherished secrets. Today, however, Eleanor felt a tremor of unease beneath the usual hum of activity. The air crackled with an unspoken tension, a feeling that something was about to change, drastically. 


Eleanor wasn't sure what it was, but a prickle on the back of her neck warned her to pay attention.  The bell above the door jingled, announcing a new arrival, and Eleanor turned towards the sound, ready to greet them with her trademark warmth... only to find herself face-to-face with someone she hadn't seen in over ten years.
