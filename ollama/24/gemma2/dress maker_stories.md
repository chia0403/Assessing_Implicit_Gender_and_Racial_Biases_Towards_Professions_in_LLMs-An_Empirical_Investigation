The air hung thick with the scent of lavender and freshly cut silk in Eloise Beatrice Clementine’s workshop. Sunlight streamed through the dusty attic window, illuminating motes of dust dancing in its golden beam.  Eloise hummed softly to herself as she expertly pinned a gossamer veil to a midnight blue gown, her nimble fingers moving with practiced ease. The fabric whispered against her fingertips like a lover's caress, and Eloise felt the familiar surge of satisfaction that came with bringing beauty into existence. But beneath this quiet contentment, a knot of worry tightened in her chest.  The annual Grand Ball was just two weeks away, and she had yet to receive a single commission from any of the city’s elite.


Her workshop, tucked away on the third floor of an old Georgian townhouse in London's bustling West End, was usually abuzz with activity at this time of year. But this season felt different. An unsettling silence hung in the air, broken only by the rhythmic whirring of her ancient sewing machine and the occasional caw of a crow outside the window.  Eloise’s heart ached with a growing sense of premonition. What if this year was the one she couldn't weather?
