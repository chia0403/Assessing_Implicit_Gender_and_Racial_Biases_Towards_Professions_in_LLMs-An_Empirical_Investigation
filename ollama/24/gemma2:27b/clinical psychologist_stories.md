The air hung thick with the scent of jasmine and rain-soaked earth. Emilia Rossi, PhD, sat on her balcony overlooking the bustling market square in Florence, Italy. The cobblestone streets below teemed with life – vendors hawking fresh produce, tourists snapping photos, children chasing pigeons. Yet, Emilia felt a million miles away.

The vibrant city held no solace for her today. Her mind was consumed by her latest patient: Isabella, a young woman haunted by vivid nightmares and crippling anxiety. Emilia had exhausted all traditional therapeutic approaches, and Isabella's condition seemed to be worsening.  A gnawing sense of inadequacy crept in – she felt like she was failing Isabella, and the weight of that responsibility pressed heavily on her shoulders.
