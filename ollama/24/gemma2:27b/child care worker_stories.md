The pungent smell of disinfectant hung heavy in the air, mingling with the sweet scent of apple juice and crayons. Beatrice "Bea" Alvarez sighed, pushing a stray curl behind her ear as she surveyed the chaotic scene before her. Toys were scattered across the brightly colored rug like confetti after a parade, giggling children chased each other in circles, and a rogue sippy cup lay abandoned on its side, slowly leaking its sugary contents.

It was nap time at Sunnyside Daycare, but as usual, getting ten toddlers to settle down for their afternoon slumber was proving to be an epic battle of wills. Bea, with her endless patience and mischievous grin, was the only one who seemed remotely capable of taming this tiny whirlwind.
