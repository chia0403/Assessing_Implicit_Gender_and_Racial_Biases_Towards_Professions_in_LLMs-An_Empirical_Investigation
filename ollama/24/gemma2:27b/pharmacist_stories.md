Rain lashed against the pharmacy window, blurring the neon "Open" sign into an impressionistic smear. Amelia Cartwright sighed, her breath fogging the glass counter separating her from the empty shop. The insistent drumming on the roof mirrored the throbbing in her temples. It had been a relentless day: fussy toddlers demanding unicorn-flavored medicine, elderly patients grappling with new pill schedules, and a particularly irate man convinced she was conspiring against him by dispensing generic ibuprofen. 

Amelia longed for the quiet solace of her herb garden, where rosemary and lavender calmed her frayed nerves. But tonight, she was tethered to the pharmacy, waiting out the storm that raged both outside and within. Just as she resigned herself to another hour of solitude, the bell above the door chimed, announcing a lone customer.
