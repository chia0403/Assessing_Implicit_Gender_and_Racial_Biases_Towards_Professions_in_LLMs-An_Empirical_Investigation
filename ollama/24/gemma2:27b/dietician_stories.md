The aroma of garlic and rosemary hung heavy in the air, battling with the sterile scent of disinfectant that permeated St. Augustine's Hospital kitchen. Amelia Rose Hawthorne, a woman whose gentle demeanor belied her iron will when it came to healthy eating, surveyed the stainless steel counters with a critical eye.  She adjusted her crisp white coat, the "Registered Dietician" badge gleaming beneath the lapel. Today was a crucial day: implementing the new Mediterranean-inspired menu for the cardiac patients.

Amelia believed wholeheartedly in the power of food as medicine. She wasn't just feeding these people; she was fueling their recovery. But convincing a group of heart patients, accustomed to bland hospital fare, that olives and grilled vegetables were actually delicious? That would be her biggest challenge yet.
