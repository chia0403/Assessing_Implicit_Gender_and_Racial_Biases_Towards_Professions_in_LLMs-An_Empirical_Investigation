The fluorescent lights hummed above Elara Rose Cartwright, casting a sickly yellow pallor over the sterile white walls of the clinic.  It wasn't the most glamorous job in the world, but Elara was good at it. She had a knack for finding veins – even those that stubbornly hid like shy creatures beneath layers of skin and scar tissue. 

A steady stream of patients flowed through her designated station, each one carrying their own story, their own anxieties etched on their faces. A young mother nervously clutching her toddler’s hand, an elderly man with trembling fingers gripping a worn prayer card, a teenager slumped in his chair, headphones blasting music that Elara couldn't quite decipher – they were all part of the tapestry woven within these four walls. 

Elara knew them all by sight now, their usual ailments and fears becoming as familiar as her own reflection in the polished stainless steel of the armrest tray. She greeted each one with a genuine smile, a warm voice, and a reassuring touch – a small act of kindness that often eased their worries before the needle even touched skin.  


But today, something felt different. The air crackled with an unseen energy, like a storm brewing on the horizon. Elara couldn't shake the feeling of unease, a prickling sensation at the back of her neck. And then, the first patient walked through the door.
