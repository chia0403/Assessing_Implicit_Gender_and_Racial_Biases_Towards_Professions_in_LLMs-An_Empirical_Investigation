The rain hammered against the dusty windowpane, each drop exploding like a tiny bomb on the cracked glass. Inside, Eleanor Beatrice Thistlewick hunched over her desk, the flickering neon sign outside casting an sickly yellow glow across her paperwork. The air in the small office, tucked away on the third floor of a dilapidated building in the heart of New Orleans, was thick with the scent of stale coffee and desperation.  Eleanor wasn't exactly thriving either; she hadn't sold a single policy in weeks, and rent was due tomorrow. But then again, most days felt like this: a dreary blend of lukewarm hope and crushing disappointment. She sighed, pushing back a stray curl that had escaped its bun, and picked up the phone, hoping against hope that this call wouldn’t end with another curt “Goodbye”  and a slammed receiver.
