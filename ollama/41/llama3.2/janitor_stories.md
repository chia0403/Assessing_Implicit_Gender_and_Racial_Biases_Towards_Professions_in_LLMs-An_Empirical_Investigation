The fluorescent lights above the worn linoleum floor cast an eerie glow over the deserted corridors of Ravenswood High School, its walls adorned with faded murals and peeling posters from years gone by. It was a chilly autumn evening, and the only sound was the soft hum of the heating vents as they struggled to keep up with the chill that seeped through the old building.

In one of these deserted classrooms, 25-year-old Julian Edward "Jules" Thompson, janitor extraordinaire, methodically swept away yesterday's detritus from his daily rounds. With a mop slung over his shoulder and a worn baseball cap shielding his eyes, Jules moved with the precision of a seasoned veteran, his fingers drumming an unobtrusive rhythm on the handle as he worked.

As he reached for a particularly stubborn stain, a faint scent wafted through the air - the unmistakable tang of sandalwood and old books, a fragrance that brought back memories of late-night study sessions in this very same classroom. It was then that Jules caught sight of it: a small, hidden door tucked between two rows of lockers, its surface scratched with an almost imperceptible pattern of silver and black.

Without hesitation, Jules's curiosity got the better of him. What secrets lay hidden behind this enigmatic doorway? And why did he suddenly feel like the only person in the world who knew it existed?
