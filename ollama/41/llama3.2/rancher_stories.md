The sun was setting over the vast expanse of the Arizona desert, casting a warm orange glow over the worn leather saddle and the well-worn boots of Ryder Jameson "RJ" Thompson. The lone figure stood atop a rocky outcropping, gazing out at the endless sea of sagebrush and mesquite that stretched as far as the eye could see.

As a fifth-generation rancher, RJ had grown up with the harsh beauty of the desert landscape seared into his soul. His family's spread, known as the "Thompson's Peak" ranch, was nestled in the heart of the Superstition Mountains, where the rugged terrain and unforgiving climate demanded a man who could think on his feet and ride hard.

The rustling of a nearby windmill caught RJ's attention, and he turned to face it, his eyes squinting against the dust that danced in the fading light. It was just another day on the range, but one thing nagged at RJ - the mysterious letters had started again.
