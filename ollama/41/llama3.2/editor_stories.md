---

The rain poured down on the small town of Ravenswood like a relentless curtain, shrouding its streets and buildings in a misty gloom that seemed to cling to everything it touched. It was a chill autumn evening, and the kind of night that made you want to stay indoors with a good book and a warm cup of tea.

At her desk, surrounded by stacks of manuscript pages and scribbled notes, Emilia Rose Wellesley hunched over a particularly tricky edit. As an editor for the local publishing house, she had spent countless hours pouring over words, searching for the perfect sentence, or reworking entire paragraphs to get them just right.

Emilia's eyes scanned the page in front of her, her mind working overtime as she tried to coax out the best possible version of a novel that had been submitted to her by a relatively unknown author. She was determined to make it shine, but so far, nothing seemed quite right. The words felt flat, the characters two-dimensional, and the plot... well, the plot just didn't seem to be grabbing her.

As she read on, Emilia's thoughts began to wander, as they often did when she was deep in editing mode. She thought about her own writing projects - a memoir that had stalled for months, and a novel that she'd been struggling to bring into focus. She thought about the stress of meeting deadlines, and the pressure to produce something worth reading.

And then, just as she was starting to feel like she was getting nowhere, Emilia's phone buzzed on her desk. A text from her best friend, Rachel, flashed on the screen: "Hey, need a break? Want to grab coffee and talk about anything except writing?"

Emilia smiled wryly, knowing that she could always count on Rachel to take her mind off things. But as she reached for her phone to respond, something caught her eye - a message from an unknown number, with just two words: "Meet me."
