The air hung thick with the scent of antiseptic and fear. Dr. Elias Thorne adjusted his spectacles, peering down at the pale face crumpled on the gurney before him. Moonlight streamed through the grimy window of the makeshift infirmary, casting long, skeletal shadows across the worn wooden floorboards. Outside, the desolate landscape of New Eden groaned under a biting wind that whispered tales of forgotten plagues and abandoned hope. 

Elias' heart pounded against his ribs like a trapped bird. This was unlike anything he'd encountered in his years as a physician. The man on the gurney, a weathered farmer named Caleb, wasn't suffering from a common ailment. His skin shimmered with an unnatural iridescence, his veins pulsed with an eerie green light, and a low, guttural growl emanated from deep within his throat.
