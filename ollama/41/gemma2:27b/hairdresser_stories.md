The air hung thick with the scent of coconut oil and burnt hairspray, a familiar cocktail that always calmed Esmeralda "Esme" Delacroix. Outside, the neon lights of Bourbon Street buzzed with drunken energy, spilling onto the narrow cobblestone alley where Esme's salon, "Cherished Tresses," nestled between a voodoo shop and a dimly lit jazz bar. Inside, though, was a sanctuary of calm, punctuated by the rhythmic snip-snip of her shears and the soft murmur of Billie Holiday crooning from the vintage record player. Tonight, however, the usual tranquil hum was disrupted. A figure cloaked in shadow stood hesitantly at the doorway, his face obscured by the brim of a fedora. Esme set down her comb, her brow furrowed with curiosity. 


This was no ordinary client.
