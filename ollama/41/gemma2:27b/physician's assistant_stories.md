The insistent chirping of cicadas blended with the distant wail of a siren as Evelyn Rose Carmichael hurried through the humid Charleston night. Sweat clung to her brow despite the late hour, her scrubs sticking uncomfortably against her skin. She clutched a battered leather medical bag tighter, its contents a lifeline for the patient waiting anxiously at the end of this gritty alleyway. 

The air hung thick with the scent of jasmine and something else, acrid and unfamiliar. Evelyn navigated around overflowing trashcans and puddles reflecting the pale glow of streetlights, her heart pounding a frantic rhythm against her ribs. This wasn't the sterile environment of Mercy Hospital where she usually worked.  This was raw, real, and undeniably dangerous.
