The scent of crayons and disinfectant hung heavy in the air, a peculiar but familiar aroma that Amelia Rose Cartwright had come to associate with both comfort and chaos. Sunlight streamed through the dusty windows of her third-grade classroom at Oakwood Elementary, illuminating rows of mismatched desks and a brightly colored alphabet chart hanging crookedly above the chalkboard.  Amelia smoothed down her floral print dress and took a deep breath, bracing herself for the inevitable torrent of energy that would soon erupt as her students shuffled in. 

It was the first day back after summer break, and Amelia, despite years of experience, still felt the familiar flutter of nerves in her stomach. This year felt different, though. A sense of unease lingered in the air, a whisper of change that she couldn't quite place.
