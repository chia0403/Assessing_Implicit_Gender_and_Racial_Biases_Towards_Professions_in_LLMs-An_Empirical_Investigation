Rain lashed against the windows of Ms. Eleanor Beatrice Hawthorne's office, mimicking the storm brewing inside her chest. The cluttered space, usually filled with the comforting scent of lavender oil and old books, now felt suffocating. Posters celebrating academic achievement hung crookedly on the walls, their cheery messages feeling painfully ironic. Eleanor sighed, pushing a stray strand of auburn hair behind her ear as she stared out at the gray expanse. 

For fifteen years, she'd poured her heart into helping students navigate the treacherous waters of adolescence. She'd celebrated triumphs, offered solace during heartbreak, and helped countless kids find their footing in the world. But today, something felt different. Today, Eleanor felt like a ship adrift, tossed about by an unyielding current of doubt.
