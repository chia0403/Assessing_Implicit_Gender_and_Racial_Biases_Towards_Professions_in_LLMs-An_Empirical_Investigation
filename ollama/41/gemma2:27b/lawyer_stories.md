Rain lashed against the grimy windows of Eleanor Winthrop's office, blurring the already indistinct cityscape beyond. The steady drumming matched the rhythm of unease thudding in her chest. Her mahogany desk, usually impeccably organized, was cluttered with case files and half-empty coffee mugs. Stacks of legal textbooks threatened to topple over like unsteady dominoes.

Eleanor, a woman whose sharp intelligence shone through her tired eyes and the silver streaks threading her dark hair, stared at the photograph on her desk. A young boy with mischievous grin and bright blue eyes beamed back at her. This was why she did what she did, why she fought tooth and nail for those who couldn't fight for themselves. This time, it was a mother, desperate to gain custody of her son from a powerful, ruthless businessman. The stakes were higher than ever before, and Eleanor knew the outcome could shatter a family forever.

She took a deep breath, trying to quell the storm brewing within her. Time was running out. She needed to find a way to expose her opponent's lies, to uncover the truth hidden beneath layers of deceit. But how?
