The fluorescent lights hummed above Beatrice "Bea" Alcott's head, casting a pallid glow over the cluttered desks in her English classroom. Outside, the autumn wind whipped fallen leaves against the windows, their frantic dance mirroring the storm brewing inside Bea.  She clutched a crumpled email in her hand, its words burning into her like acid: "Due to budget cuts, your position as English teacher will be eliminated effective June 30th."

Bea's classroom, usually her haven of dusty novels and student essays scrawled with youthful angst, felt suffocating.  The worn Shakespeare posters on the wall mocked her with their timeless pronouncements about fate and ambition. She’d poured her heart into this school, into these students – what was she supposed to do now?
