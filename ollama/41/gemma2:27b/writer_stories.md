Rain lashed against the attic windowpane, mimicking the frantic drumming of Eleanor Ainsworth's fingers on her antique typewriter. The room smelled faintly of mildew and dust, a testament to its neglect over the past decade. Sunlight struggled weakly through the grime-coated glass, casting long, skeletal shadows across the jumbled stacks of manuscripts and overflowing bookshelves. Eleanor, her brow furrowed in concentration, ignored the dreary weather outside, her world shrunk down to the worn keys beneath her fingertips. 

She was on the precipice of a breakthrough, she could feel it. The words were finally flowing, weaving themselves into a narrative as captivating as the storm raging around her. But just as she reached for the crucial next phrase, a deafening crack split the air, plunging the attic into darkness. Eleanor gasped, her heart leaping into her throat.  The power was out.
