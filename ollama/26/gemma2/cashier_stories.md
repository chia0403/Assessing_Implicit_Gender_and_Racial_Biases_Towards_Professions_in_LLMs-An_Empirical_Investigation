The fluorescent lights hummed above Amelia Beatrice Finch's head, casting a sickly yellow pallor over the aisles of "Bargain Bonanza."  She stared at the conveyor belt, its stainless steel gleaming under the harsh glare. Another Tuesday night, another symphony of beeps and rustling plastic bags. The air hung heavy with the scent of discount perfume and stale popcorn from the snack machine in the corner. Outside, rain lashed against the windows, blurring the already dim streetlights into hazy halos. Amelia sighed, wishing for a moment that she could disappear into one of the dusty cardboard boxes stacked precariously in the backroom, just out of sight and sound. 

Her gaze drifted to the clock: 8:57 pm.  Five more minutes until her shift ended. Five more minutes until she could trade the monotony of scanning barcodes for the quiet solace of her tiny apartment, where a steaming mug of chamomile tea awaited.  She just needed to get through this last customer...
