The fluorescent lights hummed an irritating symphony above Amelia Beatrice Hawthorne's head as she wrestled with a tangled mess of electrical cords behind her desk. It was Friday afternoon, the last bell had rung ten minutes ago, and Amelia should have been eagerly anticipating the weekend. Instead, she found herself wrestling with the archaic projector in her tenth-grade English classroom, its dusty casing mocking her efforts.

Outside, the late summer sun cast long shadows across the cracked asphalt of Willow Creek High's parking lot. The air hung heavy with the scent of cut grass and impending thunderstorms, a combination Amelia usually found comforting. Today, however, it only added to her growing sense of frustration.
