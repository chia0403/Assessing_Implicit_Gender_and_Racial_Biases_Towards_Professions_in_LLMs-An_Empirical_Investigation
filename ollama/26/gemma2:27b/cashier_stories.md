The fluorescent lights hummed above Amelia Beatrice Cartwright's head, casting a sickly yellow glow over the conveyor belt and the endless stream of groceries that flowed across it. The air hung thick with the cloying sweetness of artificial strawberry from the nearby display of discount pastries. It was Tuesday afternoon at 3:17 pm, peak checkout time at the SaveRite Superstore, and Amelia felt her patience thinning like the plastic grocery bags she constantly refilled.

She scanned a jar of pickles with practiced ease, her mind drifting to the stacks of overdue library books sitting on her kitchen table. Rent was due next week, and the thought of it sent a familiar pang of anxiety through her chest.
