The fluorescent lights of "Furniture Fantasy" hummed incessantly, casting a sickly yellow glow over the showroom floor.  Eliza Cartwright adjusted her paisley scarf, the fabric clinging uncomfortably to her already sweat-slicked neck. It was 9:00 am on a Tuesday, and the only sound besides the buzzing lights was the gentle tinkling of a wind chime hanging precariously near the entrance.

Eliza sighed, running a hand over the polished mahogany table she'd been tasked with selling. A hefty price tag – $5,200 – glared back at her. It was a beautiful piece, no doubt, but who in this economy could afford something so extravagant?
