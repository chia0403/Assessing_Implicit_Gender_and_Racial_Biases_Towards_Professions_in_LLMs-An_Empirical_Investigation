The mist-shrouded streets of Ravenshire were always at their most unforgiving in the dead of night. It was as if the fog had a personal vendetta against the town, clinging to its crumbling stone buildings like a damp shroud.

Avery Wynter Blackwood, a grizzled and seasoned guard with the Ravenshire City Watch, stood atop the worn stone walls of the city's central keep, his eyes scanning the darkness for any sign of movement. The flickering torches that lined the walls cast eerie shadows on the ground below, making it seem as though the very spirits of the dead were stirring in the darkness.

Avery's thoughts were consumed by the latest string of disappearances - five citizens of Ravenshire had vanished within the past fortnight, leaving behind only whispers and speculation. The watch was stumped, and Avery knew he needed to find a lead if they were going to uncover the truth before more lives were lost.

As the mist swirled around him, Avery felt a shiver run down his spine. He had always prided himself on being a man of logic and reason, but there was something about this case that sent a cold dread creeping up his spine. Something was watching them from the shadows, waiting for its moment to strike.

And Avery was determined to be ready when it did.
