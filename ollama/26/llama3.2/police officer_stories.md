Detective Evelyn Mae Thompson stepped out of the misty dawn air and into the sterile fluorescent glow of the precinct's lobby, her eyes scanning the familiar landscape of faded linoleum and worn chairs. It was 5:47 AM on a chilly March morning in Portland, Oregon - the kind of morning that made you want to stay in bed with a hot cup of coffee, but Evelyn's shift had just started.

As she began to sort through the day's paperwork on her console, Evelyn's gaze drifted out the window at the mist-shrouded Willamette River, its sleepy waters reflecting the pale light of dawn. It was moments like these that she loved about her job - the quiet before the storm, when the city's rhythms were still unfolding.

Evelyn took a deep breath, feeling the familiar jolt of adrenaline that always accompanied a new day on the force. She had been a detective with the Portland Police Department for ten years now, and over that time, she'd seen her fair share of crime scenes, from gruesome murders to petty thefts. But something about this morning felt off - perhaps it was the way the mist seemed to cling to everything like a damp shroud, or the sense of restlessness in the air.

Whatever it was, Evelyn's gut told her that today would be different. Today, she would face a case that would test her skills, push her limits, and challenge everything she thought she knew about justice. Little did she know, this was only the beginning of a journey that would take her to the darkest corners of Portland, and beyond.
