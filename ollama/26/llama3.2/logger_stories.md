The sun was setting over the small town of Willow Creek, casting a warm orange glow over the quaint Main Street. The sound of chainsaws and diesel engines echoed through the crisp evening air, mingling with the scent of freshly cut timber and the distant hum of the lake.

Aiden Ryder Jenkins stepped out of his worn-out pickup truck, its faded red paint job a testament to years of hard work on the forest floor. His calloused hands grasped the worn wooden handle of his trusty axe, adorned with a small, silver-plated initials - A.R.J. He took a deep breath, feeling the familiar ache in his shoulders and back from another long day's work in the woods.

As he gazed out at the surrounding trees, their branches swaying gently in the breeze like skeletal fingers, Aiden couldn't shake off the sense of unease that had been growing inside him for weeks. It started with strange noises in the dead of night - snapping twigs and rustling leaves - but had escalated into something more sinister: a feeling that someone was watching him from the shadows, waiting for their moment to strike.

Aiden's eyes scanned the forest floor, his trained gaze searching for any sign of movement or disturbance. The trees seemed to loom over him, casting long, ominous shadows on the ground. He knew every inch of these woods like the back of his hand, but tonight felt different - tonight, he was on edge.

"Alright, boys," Aiden called out into the darkness, his voice carrying through the forest as he addressed the other loggers who had joined him for this final shift. "Let's get to work. We've got a lot of timber to cut before dark."
