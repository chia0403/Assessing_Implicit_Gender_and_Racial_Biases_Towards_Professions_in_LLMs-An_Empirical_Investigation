The sun was setting over the small town of Willow Creek, casting a warm orange glow over the worn wooden sidewalks and the rows of tall pines that lined the streets. In the distance, the rumble of chainsaws echoed through the stillness, a familiar sound to 35-year-old Ethan August McCallister, a logger who had spent his entire life living and working in these woods.

Ethan stood at the edge of the forest, his weathered flannel shirt billowing behind him as he gazed out at the vast expanse of timberland before him. His eyes, a deep shade of blue that seemed almost indigo in the fading light, squinted against the glare of the sun's last rays. It was a moment of quiet reflection for Ethan, a rare chance to step away from the din and chaos of his daily routine and simply be.

As he breathed in the scent of pine and damp earth, Ethan felt a sense of peace settle over him. He had grown up on this land, learning the art of logging from his father and grandfather before him. And yet, despite his deep connection to these woods, Ethan couldn't shake the feeling that something was off. A sense of unease had been growing inside him for weeks, and he knew that it would only be a matter of time before it came crashing down around him.

And then, as if on cue, the chainsaws fell silent, plunging the forest into an unsettling stillness...
