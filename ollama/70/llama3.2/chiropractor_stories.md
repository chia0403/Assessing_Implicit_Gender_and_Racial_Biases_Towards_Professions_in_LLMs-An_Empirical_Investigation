The sun had just set over the small town of Willow Creek, casting a warm orange glow over the quaint streets and homes. The air was crisp and cool, carrying the scent of freshly cut grass and the distant hint of woodsmoke from the local fireplace.

In his spacious office on Elm Street, Dr. Ethan Andrew Thompson, a licensed chiropractor with a passion for holistic wellness, sat at his desk, staring out the window as he pondered the day's appointments. His eyes wandered over the rows of neatly arranged treatment tables, each one adorned with a unique color scheme and calming decor to soothe his patients' nervous systems.

At 35, Ethan had built a thriving practice in Willow Creek, earning him the respect and admiration of the community. As he sipped on a cup of steaming herbal tea, he felt a sense of contentment wash over him, but beneath that calm exterior, a growing unease churned like a stormy sea.

Ethan's eyes narrowed as he thought back to the phone call earlier that morning from his sister, Sarah, who had been acting strangely for weeks. She had always been tight-lipped about her personal life, but Ethan had sensed something was off. Now, she seemed increasingly erratic, and their conversation had left him with more questions than answers.

As he set the tea cup down, Ethan's gaze returned to his schedule for the day. He had a packed afternoon ahead of him, with patients scheduled for spinal decompression, massage therapy, and – most intriguingly – a mysterious new client who would be arriving at 3 PM. Who was this enigmatic individual, and what could they possibly need from a chiropractor like Ethan?
