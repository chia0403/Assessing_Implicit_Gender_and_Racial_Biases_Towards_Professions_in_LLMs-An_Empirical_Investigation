Here's a potential story opening:

As she wiped down the last of the gel polish from her clients' nails, Emma Mae Thompson gazed out the window of her small nail salon in the heart of downtown Los Angeles. The sun had just begun to set, casting a warm orange glow over the bustling streets of Chinatown, where Emma's shop, "Polished Perfection," was nestled between a vintage clothing store and a Korean restaurant. It was a place where time seemed to stand still, despite the constant hum of activity outside.

Emma, a petite woman with curly brown hair and a kind smile, had been a manicurist for over a decade, and she took pride in her work. Her clients adored her, not just for her attention to detail, but also for her ability to listen and offer words of encouragement when they needed it most. From young professionals getting ready for big dates to elderly women celebrating milestone birthdays, Emma had seen it all.

As she counted down the till, a sense of unease began to creep over her. It was as if she could feel eyes on her, watching her from across the room. She glanced around, but saw nothing out of the ordinary - just the familiar faces of her regular clients and the occasional passerby. Still, the feeling persisted, and Emma couldn't shake the sense that something was off.

Just then, a knock at the door broke the silence, and Emma's heart skipped a beat as she wondered who could be interrupting her quiet evening shift...
