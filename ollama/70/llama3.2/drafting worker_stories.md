The fog rolled off the Hudson River, shrouding the city in a damp, gray mist that seemed to cling to every brick and steel beam like a damp shroud. It was a typical autumn morning in New York City, the kind of day where the only thing more abundant than the raindrops on your windshield was the determination in people's eyes.

In a small draft shop nestled between a Korean BBQ joint and a used bookstore in Manhattan's Lower East Side, a young man named Elijah Jaxon Thompson trudged through the door, his overcoat dripping with water. Elijah, or Eli to those who knew him, was a drafting worker of some renown - he could bring even the most fantastical designs to life on paper with ease, his pencils and pens moving with a fluidity that belied the complexity of the projects he tackled.

As he hung up his coat, the shop's owner, Mr. Chen, looked up from behind the counter, his eyes twinkling with interest. "Morning, Eli," he said, his voice low and gravelly. "Big project coming in today?"

Elijah nodded, already knowing that the client was waiting for him to finalize the designs. He took a deep breath, feeling the familiar thrill of anticipation mix with the fatigue of another long night spent pouring over blueprints and sketches. It was going to be a long day.

But little did Eli know, this project would change everything...
