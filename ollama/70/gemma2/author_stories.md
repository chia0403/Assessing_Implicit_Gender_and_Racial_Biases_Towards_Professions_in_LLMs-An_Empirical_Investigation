The flickering gaslight cast long, dancing shadows across Eleanor Beaumont's cluttered writing desk. She hunched over her latest manuscript, the worn leather of her journal creaking with every turn of the page. Rain lashed against the tall windows of her Edinburgh flat, a symphony of wind and water that mirrored the tempest brewing within her own mind.  Eleanor had spent weeks wrestling with this story, an intricate web of intrigue and romance set in Victorian London, but the words felt hollow, lacking the spark of inspiration she craved. She chewed on her pencil, staring at the blank page before her, a gnawing sense of inadequacy creeping into her heart. 


"Damn it all," she muttered, pushing back her fiery red hair that always seemed to escape its confines. "Where's the magic?"
