Rain lashed against the windows of The Harbor House, blurring the already muted colours of the autumnal sunset. Inside, Eleanor Ainsworth meticulously rearranged a set of brightly coloured blocks on the floor, her brow furrowed in concentration. The aroma of cinnamon and apples wafted from the communal kitchen, a comforting scent that did little to soothe Eleanor's growing unease. 

Eleanor was an occupational therapist, dedicated to helping her patients regain independence and purpose after debilitating injuries or illnesses.  But today, she faced a challenge unlike any she’d encountered before – Mrs. Evelyn Cartwright, a sprightly octogenarian who had inexplicably lost the ability to speak. 


The Harbor House was a charming, if somewhat outdated, seaside retirement home nestled on the cliffs overlooking the stormy Atlantic. Eleanor loved working there; the residents were like family, their stories woven into the very fabric of the building.  But Mrs. Cartwright’s sudden silence had thrown a wrench into Eleanor's usual routine, leaving her with more questions than answers.
