Rain hammered against the windows of Ms. Eleanor Ainsworth's third-grade classroom, mimicking the relentless drumbeat in her chest. Outside, the wind howled like a wounded beast, whipping branches against the brick walls of Willow Creek Elementary. The storm had been raging since dawn, trapping everyone indoors and transforming the normally cheerful school into a cavernous echo chamber of nervous coughs and shuffling feet.

Eleanor, with her auburn hair escaping its braid and her favorite sunflower-patterned dress slightly askew, chewed on her lip. She'd hoped to take advantage of the sunny spring afternoon for a nature walk, but this storm had other plans. Now, she faced thirty restless eight-year-olds with an energy surplus that threatened to erupt like Mount Vesuvius.
