The August air hung thick and heavy, saturated with humidity and the smell of freshly cut grass. Cicadas thrummed their incessant summer song from the towering oak trees that lined the field, their drone a constant backdrop to Eliza “Eli” Montgomery's pre-practice pep talk.

Eli stood on the 50-yard line, her whistle dangling from her neck like a conductor's baton, ready to orchestrate another season of triumph and heartache. Her weathered face, etched with years of sun and stress, held a fierce determination as she addressed the gathered team.  These weren't just players to Eli; they were extensions of herself, each one carrying the weight of her dreams and disappointments. This year, she vowed, would be different.
