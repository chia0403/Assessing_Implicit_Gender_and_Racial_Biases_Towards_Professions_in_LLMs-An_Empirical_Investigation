Rain hammered against the panoramic windows of Alistair Hawthorne’s office, blurring the glittering cityscape into an impressionist painting. The relentless downpour mirrored the storm brewing inside him. On his Bloomberg terminal, the Dow Jones Industrial Average was hemorrhaging points, and Alistair's stomach churned in sync with its descent.

Alistair wasn’t known for panicking. He prided himself on a cool head and a razor-sharp intuition honed over years navigating the treacherous currents of Wall Street. But today felt different. The market's plunge was sudden, inexplicable, and fueled by an unsettling whisper campaign circulating among his high-net-worth clients – whispers of impending disaster.

He needed answers. Fast.
