Rain lashed against the grimy windowpane, blurring the already hazy cityscape beyond.  The air inside the cramped drafting office was thick with the scent of stale coffee and ozone from the humming fluorescent lights overhead. Elias Finchley hunched over his drawing board, the glow of the desk lamp casting long shadows across his tired face.

Elias wasn't an architect or engineer; he was a simple draftsman, churning out technical drawings for industrial equipment that he barely understood. Each day was a monotonous cycle of lines and angles, precise measurements and repetitive symbols. Yet, today felt different. A gnawing unease twisted in his gut, fueled by the storm raging outside and the strange message scrawled on a crumpled piece of paper tucked under his keyboard: "Meet me at the clocktower midnight." 


Who would leave such a note? And why? Elias stared at the cryptic words, a shiver crawling up his spine. He knew he should ignore it, chalk it up to some cruel prank by his colleagues. But something about the message, its urgency and anonymity, drew him in like a moth to a flame.
