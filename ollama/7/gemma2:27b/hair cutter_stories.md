The incessant whirring of clippers was Amelia "Amy" Beatrice Thorne's lullaby. For twenty years, she'd wielded those silver serpents, transforming unruly manes into sculpted masterpieces. Her salon, tucked away on a cobblestone street in Edinburgh's Old Town, smelled perpetually of lavender shampoo and anticipation. Sunlight streamed through the arched window, illuminating motes of dust dancing above the worn leather chairs. 

Today, however, a peculiar tension hung in the air, thicker than the hairspray Amy liberally applied.  A stranger sat in her chair, his back ramrod straight, his shadowed face obscured by a wide-brimmed hat. He hadn't spoken a word since entering, simply sliding into the seat and fixing Amy with an unnervingly intense gaze. Amy, usually adept at striking up conversation, found herself uncharacteristically mute, her fingers hovering hesitantly above his dark, tangled locks.
