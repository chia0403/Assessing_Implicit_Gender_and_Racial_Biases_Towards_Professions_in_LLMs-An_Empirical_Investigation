The sun had barely risen over the bustling streets of downtown Los Angeles, casting a warm glow over the crowded sidewalks of Sunset Boulevard. In a small, unassuming salon nestled between a vintage clothing store and a ramen noodle joint, Kaitlyn Rae Thompson was already prepping for another day of styling hair and making people feel like their best selves.

As she expertly sectioned her client's long, curly locks into tiny parts, Kaitlyn couldn't help but feel a sense of satisfaction wash over her. She loved being a cosmetologist - there was something therapeutic about transforming someone's look from bland to grand, about making them feel confident and beautiful. And in a city as eclectic and fast-paced as LA, where people came from all walks of life and had all sorts of stories to tell, Kaitlyn felt like she got to be a part of that transformation every day.

With a flourish, she swept the curling iron across her client's hair, coaxing out a perfect wave. "Ta-da!" she exclaimed, stepping back to admire her handiwork. But as she turned to face her client, who was now giggling and playing with her phone, Kaitlyn couldn't shake the feeling that something was off. Maybe it was just the pre-dawn fog in her brain, or maybe it was a lingering sense of unease that had been bothering her all week - but whatever it was, Kaitlyn knew she needed to shake it off and focus on giving her clients the best day ever.

Little did she know, today was going to be a day like no other.
