The sun was setting over the vast expanse of the Red Rock Ranch, casting a warm orange glow over the rolling hills and vast open spaces that had been in Elijah "Eli" Jackson's family for generations. Eli, a rugged and weathered rancher in his late 30s, stood atop the porch of his family's old homestead, gazing out at the landscape he loved with a mix of pride and concern.

Below him, the cattle grazed peacefully in the fading light, their ears twitching as they sensed the approaching storm. Eli knew that the changing seasons were always a challenge for the ranch, but this year felt different. The drought had been particularly harsh, and he was worried about how his family's farm would hold up when the winter rains finally came.

As he stood there, Eli's mind wandered back to the long day of work he'd just put in, from fixing fences to tending to the herd. His stomach growled with hunger, reminding him that he still needed to get some supper started before sitting down to enjoy the peaceful evening by himself. The sound of a pickup truck rumbled up the driveway, breaking the silence and signaling the arrival of his neighbor, Jedediah "Jed" Thompson.

Eli's expression changed from contemplation to curiosity as he watched Jed pull into the yard, the old man's weathered face creased with worry lines that matched Eli's own. What was Jed doing here so early? And what news did he bring?
