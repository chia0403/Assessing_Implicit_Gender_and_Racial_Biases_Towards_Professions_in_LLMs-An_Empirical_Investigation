The rain poured down on the small town of Willow Creek like a relentless curse, casting a misty veil over the quaint streets and homes that lined its streets. Inside one of those homes, on Elm Street, plumber Jack Ethan McCormick was huddled in his creaky living room, sipping on a lukewarm cup of coffee and staring at the stack of unpaid bills on his worn-out couch.

Jack, a ruggedly handsome man in his late thirties with a thick beard and a mop of curly brown hair, had been struggling to make ends meet as a plumber. The economic downturn had taken its toll on the town's infrastructure, leaving Jack's small business with more work than clients. His wife, Sarah, had lost her job at the local bakery six months ago, and they were living paycheck to paycheck.

As he gazed out the window, watching the raindrops slide down the panes like tears, Jack couldn't help but feel a sense of restlessness. He needed a break, something to shake things up and bring some excitement back into his life. Little did he know, that excitement was just around the corner...
