The air hung thick and heavy with the scent of ripening tomatoes, their crimson globes plumping on the vine like miniature suns. Dust motes danced in the shafts of sunlight that pierced through the gaps in the barn's weathered roof. Emilia Rose Butterfield wiped her brow with a calloused hand, leaving a streak of dirt across her forehead.  She squinted at the horizon, where the sun was beginning its descent, casting long shadows across her small farm nestled in the heart of the Colorado Rockies. The wind whispered through the tall stalks of corn, rustling like secrets carried on the breeze. 


It had been a tough year. A late frost had decimated half her crop, and the drought lingered, sucking the moisture from the earth. But Emilia was stubborn, her spirit as resilient as the wildflowers that stubbornly bloomed even in the parched soil. She wouldn't let this season break her. Not when she had poured her heart and soul into this land, not when it was all she had left.
