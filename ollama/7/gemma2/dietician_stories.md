The aroma of burnt toast hung heavy in the air, mingling with the tang of antiseptic from the nearby hand sanitiser dispenser. Evelyn Beatrice Hawthorne, or "Bev" as she preferred, grimaced. This wasn't how her ideal Tuesday morning was supposed to unfold. She’d envisioned a serene start, perhaps meditating by the window while sipping green tea and planning the week's menu for her clients. Instead, her kitchen resembled a battlefield after breakfast had gone awry. 

Bev sighed, pushing the blackened toast aside with her spatula. Her apartment, tucked away in a quaint corner of Chicago’s Wicker Park neighborhood, was usually a sanctuary of calm. Today, it felt like a microcosm of her own internal struggle.  The city outside buzzed with relentless energy, mirroring the chaos swirling within Bev as she juggled the demands of her busy practice and the unspoken anxieties of her own life.
