Rain lashed against the windowpane, mimicking the frantic rhythm of Elara Thorne's heart. The cursor blinked accusingly on her screen, highlighting the unfinished logo design. Three days past deadline, her apartment felt more like a pressure cooker than a creative sanctuary. Scattered coffee cups and crumpled sketches littered the desk, testaments to her sleepless nights. Outside, the neon glow of the city reflected ominously in her wide, hazel eyes. This wasn't just any client; it was Zephyr Innovations, a tech start-up on the cusp of revolutionizing renewable energy, and Elara had promised them something groundbreaking. 

But inspiration, like the elusive sun behind the storm clouds, remained stubbornly out of reach.
