Rain lashed against the grimy windowpane of Detective Alistair Finch's office, mimicking the relentless pounding in his head. The Thames, usually a bustling artery of the city, lay sluggish and dark, reflecting the somber mood that had settled over Scotland Yard.

Finch, a veteran detective with salt-and-pepper hair and eyes that held the weariness of too many sleepless nights, stared at the evidence board pinned to the wall. The case file lay open on his desk: "The Vanishing Violinist," a title as haunting as the melody the victim, renowned virtuoso Anya Petrova, had been practicing before her disappearance.
