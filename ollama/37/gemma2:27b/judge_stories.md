Rain lashed against the arched windows of the courtroom, mimicking the turmoil brewing inside Judge Alistair Cavendish. The mahogany paneling gleamed under the dim chandeliers, each flickering bulb casting long shadows that danced across the empty jury box and deserted gallery. It was past midnight, and the only sound besides the relentless rain was the soft tick-tock of the antique clock on the wall, counting down the hours until dawn.

Cavendish, a man weathered by years of dispensing justice, stared out at the storm, his brow furrowed in deep thought. The case before him, a complex web of corporate espionage and hidden agendas, had left him feeling unsettled. He'd never encountered such blatant manipulation of the truth, such ruthless pursuit of power.
