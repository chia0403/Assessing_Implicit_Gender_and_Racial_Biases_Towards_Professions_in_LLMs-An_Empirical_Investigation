The air hung heavy with the scent of pine needles and damp earth as Elias Blackwood hefted another log onto his shoulder.  Sweat trickled down his temple, stinging his eyes. He squinted through the dappled sunlight filtering through the towering Douglas firs, each one older than his grandfather. The ancient trees, giants draped in moss and lichen, formed a cathedral of green above him, their branches intertwining like gnarled fingers reaching for the sky. Elias, with his thick, calloused hands and broad shoulders honed by years of wrestling timber, moved through this emerald sanctuary with the grace of someone who knew its every secret, every creak and groan.

Today was different though. A prickle of unease ran down his spine. The forest felt strangely silent, devoid of the usual chorus of birdsong and the chattering squirrels that usually accompanied his work. Even the wind seemed to hold its breath, creating an unnerving stillness.
