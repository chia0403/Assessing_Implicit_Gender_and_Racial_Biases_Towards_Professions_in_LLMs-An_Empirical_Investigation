The aroma of turmeric and cumin hung heavy in the air, a comforting spice cloud that Amelia Chandra usually found inviting. Today, however, it did little to soothe the knot in her stomach. The brightly painted walls of her office, adorned with posters depicting smiling vegetables and vibrant fruit salads, felt oppressively cheerful. Outside, the bustling Mumbai marketplace throbbed with life, a symphony of honking horns, vendors' cries, and the rhythmic clatter of rickshaws. Amelia longed to be lost in that chaos, anything but facing the woman seated across from her desk. 

Mrs. Kapoor, a woman whose elegance couldn't mask the worry etched onto her face, clutched a worn photograph. It showed a young boy, his eyes sparkling with mischief, holding up a plate piled high with greasy samosas. "This is Rohan," Mrs. Kapoor said, her voice cracking slightly, "He's... he's not well."
