The air hung thick with the scent of acetone and lavender polish as Esmeralda "Esme" Rodriguez meticulously filed down the jagged edge of a client's acrylic nail. Sunlight streamed through the dusty windowpane of "Essie's Nail Nook," casting long shadows across the worn, floral-patterned carpet. Outside, the honking symphony of rush hour traffic battled with the rhythmic chirping of sparrows nesting in the overgrown oak tree beside her shop. Esme, however, remained oblivious to the chaos unfolding beyond her window. Her focus was laser sharp, her practiced hands working with deft precision as she transformed chipped and forgotten nails into shimmering works of art.

But today, a knot of anxiety tightened in Esme's stomach. It wasn't the usual pre-rent jitters that came with running a small business. Today, something felt different. A man she'd never seen before had walked in earlier, his eyes shadowed and his gaze lingering on her like a hawk circling its prey. He'd booked an appointment for "a full set," but there was a stillness about him, a chilling intensity that sent shivers down Esme's spine.
