Rain lashed against the grimy windows of Dr. Eleanor Vance's office, blurring the already dim cityscape beyond. The neon sign of the diner across the street flickered erratically, casting an unsettling strobe effect on the overflowing ashtray perched precariously on her desk. Eleanor sighed, rubbing tired eyes as she stared at the latest case file open before her - "Jacob Thorne, 32, insomnia, recurring nightmares."  

Eleanor's office was a chaotic symphony of mismatched furniture and overflowing bookshelves, a testament to years spent battling the demons of the human mind. A worn leather armchair sat opposite her desk, perpetually occupied by a ghost of anxieties past. Today, however, it remained empty, waiting for Jacob Thorne and the secrets he carried within him.
