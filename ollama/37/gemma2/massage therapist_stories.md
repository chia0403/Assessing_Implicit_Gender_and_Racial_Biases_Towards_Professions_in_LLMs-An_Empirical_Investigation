The rain hammered against the windowpanes of Willow Creek Wellness, mimicking the insistent throbbing in Amelia Juniper's temples. The scent of lavender and eucalyptus hung heavy in the air, usually calming but today, it felt cloying, almost suffocating. 

Amelia massaged her eyes, trying to ease the tension that had been building all week. Between a string of demanding clients, an overflowing schedule, and the incessant nagging from her landlord about overdue rent, she was on the verge of unraveling. Her small haven, tucked away in a quiet corner of Willow Creek, felt more like a pressure cooker than a sanctuary.  


This wasn't how she envisioned things when she'd bought the place three years ago. Back then, Amelia Juniper had dreamed of creating a space where people could come to unwind, find solace, and leave feeling renewed. Now, her dream felt more like a burden.
