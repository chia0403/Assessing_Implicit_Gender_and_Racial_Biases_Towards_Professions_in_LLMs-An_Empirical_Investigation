The sun had just begun to set over the small town of Oakdale, casting a warm orange glow over the crumbling brick buildings and worn sidewalks. Dr. Sofia Elena Vasquez stepped out of her car and took a deep breath, feeling the cool evening air fill her lungs as she gazed out at the familiar landscape.

As a school psychologist for the Oakdale Public Schools district, Sofia had spent countless hours walking these same streets, watching children laugh and cry on their way to and from school. She had seen generations of families come and go, each with their own unique struggles and triumphs. But no matter how many students she worked with, Sofia couldn't shake the feeling that there was something more going on in Oakdale - something just beneath the surface, waiting to be uncovered.

Sofia's thoughts were interrupted by the sound of her phone buzzing in her pocket. She pulled it out to find a text from her colleague, Dr. Mark Reynolds: "Meet me at the old oak tree in Riverside Park at 5 pm. I need to talk to you about something."

Sofia's heart began to beat just a little faster as she wondered what could be so urgent that Mark would interrupt their usual Friday afternoon routine. She glanced around the empty parking lot, feeling a shiver run down her spine. Something was off in Oakdale tonight...
