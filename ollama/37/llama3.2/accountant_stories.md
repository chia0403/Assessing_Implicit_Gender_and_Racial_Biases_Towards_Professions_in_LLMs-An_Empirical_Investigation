Here's a story opening and setting:

The rain-soaked streets of downtown Chicago glistened like polished onyx, reflecting the towering skyscrapers that seemed to stretch up to the gray sky above. Inside the offices of Thompson, Lewis & Co., accounting firm, the fluorescent lights hummed overhead, casting an unforgiving glare over the rows of cubicles where the city's most meticulous number-crunchers worked.

On the 23rd floor, accountant Elliot Thompson sat at his desk, staring blankly at a spreadsheet on his computer screen. His eyes were bloodshot from lack of sleep and his mind was numb from the day's events - a particularly complicated tax return had taken longer than expected to finish, and he was already running behind schedule.

As he rubbed the fatigue from his eyes, Elliot couldn't help but think that this wasn't the life he had signed up for. He had always dreamed of making a real difference in people's lives, not just crunching numbers for corporations. But life had taken a different turn after college - his parents' business had gone bankrupt, and he had been forced to take on debt to put himself through law school.

Now, at 32 years old, Elliot felt like he was stuck in a rut, working long hours for a salary that barely covered his bills. He knew there had to be more to life than this - but what?
