The rain lashed against the windows of Dr. Alexandra "Lexi" Thompson's office, casting a gloomy shadow over the sterile white walls and the rows of neatly stacked medical texts that lined the shelves. As a renowned cardiothoracic surgeon at St. Michael's Hospital in downtown Seattle, Lexi was no stranger to high-stakes decision-making and life-or-death situations. But on this particular drizzly Tuesday morning, she couldn't shake the feeling of unease that had been building in her chest all week.

It started with a phone call from an old colleague, a fellow surgeon who had retired to a small coastal town just north of Seattle. He'd mentioned something about a patient - a young woman with a rare genetic disorder that made her heart nearly indestructible - and a cryptic warning: "Lexi, you need to be careful. You're playing with fire this time."

As Lexi sipped her lukewarm coffee and stared out the window at the rain-soaked streets below, she couldn't help but wonder what her colleague had meant. What new case was he talking about? And what exactly did he mean by "playing with fire"?
