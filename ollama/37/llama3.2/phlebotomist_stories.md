Here's a potential story opening and setting:

As the first light of dawn crept over the small town of Willow Creek, Phoebe Alexandra Thompson prepared for another day of drawing blood in the local medical center. She had been a phlebotomist at Willow Creek Memorial Hospital for three years, ever since graduating from the community college with an associate's degree in medical assisting.

Phoebe took pride in her work, meticulously cleaning and preparing each patient before inserting the needle, all while chatting with them about their favorite sports teams or weekend plans. It was a role that allowed her to connect with people on a personal level, even as she made them uncomfortable with the prick of the needle.

The hospital itself was an old building, its beige walls and tile floors bearing witness to generations of care. The scent of disinfectant and worn fabric filled the air, mingling with the soft hum of equipment and the occasional beep of an alarm. Phoebe's station near the lab was quiet, away from the chaos of the emergency department, where she worked alongside her friend and colleague, Rachel Patel.

As Phoebe reviewed her schedule for the day, a text message from her estranged father flashed on her phone. "Hey kiddo, I need to talk to you about something," it read. Her heart skipped a beat; their relationship had been strained since she was a teenager, but she still hadn't heard from him in years.

Phoebe hesitated, wondering what could have prompted his sudden message. With a mix of trepidation and curiosity, she set aside her patient chart and headed towards the hospital's break room to grab a cup of coffee before her first appointment...
