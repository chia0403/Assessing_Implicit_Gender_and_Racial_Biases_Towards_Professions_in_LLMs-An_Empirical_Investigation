The sun had just begun to set over the Manhattan skyline, casting a golden glow over the towering skyscrapers of Wall Street. Inside the sleek, modern offices of Winston & Co., a top-tier investment firm, the suits were already packing up their things for the day. But one man remained, hunched over his desk and staring intently at the screens in front of him.

Alexander "Alex" Winston III was a stock broker with a reputation for being ruthless and brilliant in equal measure. With his razor-sharp mind and uncanny ability to read market trends, he had built a lucrative career on Wall Street, raking in millions from his sharp deals and shrewd investments.

But as he gazed out the window at the glittering lights of the city below, Alex couldn't shake the feeling that something was off. A nagging sense of unease had been growing inside him all day, like a low-grade fever that refused to break. It started with a phone call from an old friend, claiming that his own company was on the verge of collapse - and now, as he scanned the financial news wires, Alex saw the same headline staring back at him: "Winston & Co. in Major Trouble?"

What was going on? And more importantly, what did it have to do with Alex himself?
