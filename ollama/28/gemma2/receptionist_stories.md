The fluorescent lights hummed over Amelia Josephine Bellweather's head, casting a sickly yellow pallor over the reception desk. Papers piled precariously on either side of her, threatening to engulf her like some bureaucratic avalanche. A half-eaten granola bar lay forgotten on a coaster next to a lukewarm cup of coffee, its scent battling with the faint aroma of disinfectant that permeated the entire waiting room.  

Amelia sighed, pinching the bridge of her nose. Another Tuesday at Carmichael & Associates, another day of answering phones, directing calls, and suppressing the urge to scream into the empty hallway. 


Outside, the city bustled with life: cars honked, construction crews hammered, and street vendors hawked their wares. But inside the sterile confines of the law firm, time seemed to crawl by in molasses-thick increments.
