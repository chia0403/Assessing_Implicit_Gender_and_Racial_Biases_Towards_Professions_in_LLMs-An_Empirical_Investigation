The air in The Dusty Microphone hung thick with cigarette smoke and stale beer. Neon signs flickered erratically, casting long, distorted shadows across the worn stage floor.  Rain lashed against the grimy windows, blurring the cityscape into a watercolor mess. 

This wasn't exactly what Eleanor "Ellie" Violet Rose had envisioned for her big break. She hadn't dreamt of singing to a handful of regulars nursing their sorrows and sipping watered-down whiskey in a dive bar on the edge of town. But with only a suitcase full of dreams and a voice that could melt steel, Ellie was willing to take whatever gigs she could get. 


Tonight, though, felt different. A nervous energy buzzed beneath her skin, making it difficult to focus on her guitar strings. There was something in the air, a palpable shift, as if the familiar gloom had given way to an unspoken anticipation.  Ellie adjusted the strap of her guitar and took a deep breath, ready to face whatever the night threw at her.
