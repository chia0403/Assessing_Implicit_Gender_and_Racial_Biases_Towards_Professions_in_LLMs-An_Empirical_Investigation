Dr. Emilia "Mia" Thompson stepped out of her small cottage and onto the misty dock that jutted into the harbor, the salty air filling her lungs as she gazed out at the grey waters of Ravenswood Bay. The fog swirled around her ankles, carrying the scent of seaweed and decay. It was a chill that went beyond the autumnal temperatures, a sense of foreboding that seemed to seep into every pore.

As a doctor, Mia had seen her fair share of crises, but there was something about this place that unsettled her. Perhaps it was the isolation, or the feeling that Ravenswood Bay was a town frozen in time, where secrets and stories were kept hidden beneath the surface. Whatever it was, Mia felt it deep in her bones.

She took a deep breath, the sound of seagulls crying overhead echoing off the wooden pilings. The town's small-town charm was still intact, but there was an undercurrent of tension that seemed to simmer just below the surface. And then, as if on cue, the phone in Mia's pocket buzzed with a call from the hospital.

"Dr. Thompson," she answered, her voice steady despite the unease growing in her chest.

"Dr. Thompson, it's Nurse Jenkins," the voice on the other end said, its tone tight with concern. "We've got another patient coming in. And this one...this one doesn't look like a typical patient."

Mia's heart skipped a beat as she wondered what kind of emergency could be brewing in Ravenswood Bay, and what lay ahead for her in the days to come.
