Dr. Alexandra "Alex" Thompson stepped out of her small office in the basement of the community center, stretching her arms over her head as she gazed up at the bright morning sunlight streaming through the grimy windows. As an occupational therapist specializing in pediatric rehabilitation, Alex had spent countless hours working with children who had been injured or born with developmental delays, helping them to regain their motor skills and independence.

The small town of Oakdale was quiet on this Monday morning, the only sound being the hum of the coffee shop's espresso machine and the soft chatter of the early risers sipping their lattes. Alex felt a sense of comfort in the familiar rhythms of her daily routine, but she couldn't shake off the feeling that something was about to disrupt her carefully crafted schedule.

As she walked towards the elevator, her eyes scanned the parking lot, taking in the usual array of rusty old cars and worn-out trucks that seemed to define Oakdale's character. But today, Alex noticed a sleek, black SUV parked by the entrance, its tinted windows reflecting the morning light like a mirror. She felt a shiver run down her spine as she wondered who could be visiting her on such an early morning.

With a sense of trepidation, Alex stepped into the elevator and pressed the button for the second floor, wondering what the day had in store for her.
