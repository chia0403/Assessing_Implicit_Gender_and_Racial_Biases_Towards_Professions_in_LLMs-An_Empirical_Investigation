Here's a potential story opening and setting:

The small town of Willow Creek, nestled in the heart of rural Ohio, was bathed in the warm golden light of sunset on a crisp autumn evening. The air was alive with the sweet scent of ripe apples from the local orchard, and the sound of children's laughter carried on the breeze as they played tag in the fading daylight.

On the outskirts of town, a rugged old house stood perched on the edge of a hill, its wooden beams weathered to a silvery gray that seemed to blend seamlessly into the surrounding landscape. This was the home of 32-year-old Jaxon "Jax" Ryder, a hardworking roofer with calloused hands and a quick smile.

Jax had grown up in Willow Creek, learning the ins and outs of his family's roofing business from a young age. He took pride in his work, whether it was patching leaky shingles or installing new roofs for the town's residents. But as the seasons changed and the days grew shorter, Jax couldn't shake the feeling that something was off - a sense of restlessness that had been building inside him for months.

As he stepped out of his truck and onto the gravel driveway, the crunch of gravel beneath his boots seemed to echo with an unspoken question: what's next?
