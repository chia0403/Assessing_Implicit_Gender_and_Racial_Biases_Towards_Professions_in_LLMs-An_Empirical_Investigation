It was a chilly autumn evening in the small town of Willow Creek, where the sun had just set over the quaint Main Street, casting a warm orange glow over the bustling diner. The smell of sizzling burgers and freshly brewed coffee wafted through the air, enticing passersby to come and grab a bite.

Amidst the chaos of the dinner rush, 25-year-old Avery Elizabeth Thompson expertly juggled multiple tables at once, her bright blue eyes scanning the room for any signs of trouble. As a server at McGillicuddy's Diner, she had seen it all - from rowdy college students to sentimental couples on dates. But tonight was different. Tonight, Avery felt like something was off.

A sense of unease had been building in her since the diner's owner, Mr. McGillicuddy, had received a mysterious phone call earlier that day. The gruff old man had stormed out of his office, muttering something about "uninvited guests" and "something that can't be ignored." Avery had tried to brush it off as mere small-town gossip, but her instincts told her otherwise.

As she delivered plates of steaming hot fries to a table of hungry high school students, Avery's mind wandered back to the phone call. She wondered what could have prompted such an ominous warning, and whether McGillicuddy was truly in over his head this time. Little did she know, tonight would prove to be just the beginning of a night that would change everything she thought she knew about her small town forever.
