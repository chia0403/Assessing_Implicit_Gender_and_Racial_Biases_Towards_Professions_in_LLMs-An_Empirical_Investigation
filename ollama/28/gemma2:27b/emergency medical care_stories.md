Rain lashed against the ambulance windows, blurring the neon glow of the city into an impressionistic mess of reds and yellows. Inside, Eleanor "Ellie" Vance gripped the steering wheel, her knuckles white. The siren's wail cut through the downpour, echoing Ellie's own frantic heartbeat. A code blue call had just come in from St. Jude's Hospital - a young boy, barely ten years old, was crashing.

Ellie glanced at her partner, Marco, who was double-checking their equipment with the practiced efficiency of a man who'd seen too many emergencies. His face, usually etched with a wry smile, was grim. They both knew this call could be rough.
