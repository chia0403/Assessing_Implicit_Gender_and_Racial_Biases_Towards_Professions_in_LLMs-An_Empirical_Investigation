Rain lashed against the grimy windows of Alistair Finnigan's cramped office cubicle. The fluorescent lights hummed overhead, casting a sickly yellow glow on the labyrinthine network of code scrolling across his monitor. Alistair, a systems analyst with permanently ink-stained fingers and a perpetual five o’clock shadow, chewed nervously on his thumbnail. Tonight was crunch time. 

He was staring down the barrel of the biggest project of his career – migrating the entire database system for the city's antiquated power grid to a cloud-based platform. The deadline loomed like a thundercloud on the horizon, and every keystroke felt weighted with the potential to plunge half the city into darkness.
