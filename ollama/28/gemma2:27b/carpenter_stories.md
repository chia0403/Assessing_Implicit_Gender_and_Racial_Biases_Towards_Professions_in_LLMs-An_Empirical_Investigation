The air hung thick with sawdust and the sweet scent of pine as Elias Hawthorne meticulously sanded the curve of a rocking chair leg.  Sunlight streamed through the dusty windows of his workshop, catching the fine layer of perspiration on his brow. Outside, the clamor of the bustling market square in Port Haven faded to a dull hum, replaced by the rhythmic rasp of sandpaper and the gentle clinking of tools. Elias's calloused hands moved with practiced ease, shaping the wood with an instinctive understanding honed over years of dedication to his craft.  Today, however, his usual focus was disrupted. A knot of anxiety tightened in his chest as he glanced at the half-finished mahogany table standing sentinel by the workbench. It was a commission from Lord Beaumont himself, and Elias couldn't shake the feeling that every stroke of the chisel, every precise measurement, carried the weight of expectation.
