The air in "The Rusty Mug" hung thick with the scent of stale beer, fried onions, and desperation. Rain lashed against the warped windowpanes, blurring the neon "Open" sign into an anemic smear. Emilia "Em" Reyes sighed, polishing a chipped glass for the tenth time that hour. 

It was Tuesday night, which meant the regulars: gruff Mr. Henderson nursing his whiskey sour in the corner booth, giggling college students huddled over pitchers of cheap beer, and occasionally, a lone traveler seeking refuge from the storm. Tonight, however, even the storm seemed to be holding its breath, mirroring the stagnant energy within the diner's walls. 

Em, with her bright pink streaks woven into her black braid and a silver nose ring glinting under the harsh fluorescent lights, scanned the empty tables, wishing for something – anything – to break the monotony. Her worn sneakers tapped against the linoleum floor, a rhythmic counterpoint to the mournful howl of the wind outside.
