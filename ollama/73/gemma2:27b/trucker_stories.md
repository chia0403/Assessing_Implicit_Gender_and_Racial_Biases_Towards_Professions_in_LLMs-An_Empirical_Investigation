The air hung thick with diesel fumes and the scent of pine needles as Silas "Hawk" Hawthorne wrestled his eighteen-wheeler around the hairpin turn on Highway 20.  Sunlight glinted off the snow-capped peaks of the Cascade Range, casting long shadows that danced across the asphalt like restless spirits. Hawk squinted, his weathered face creased with the effort of navigating the treacherous mountain road. Below him, the Columbia River snaked through the valley, a ribbon of silver reflecting the sapphire sky. 

He hadn't seen civilization for nearly two days, just endless stretches of pine forests and towering granite cliffs. It was solitude he craved, the silence broken only by the hum of his engine and the crackle of his CB radio. He preferred it that way; the open road offered a refuge from the world's noise and demands.
