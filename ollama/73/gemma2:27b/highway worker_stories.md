The sun hammered down on the asphalt, turning it into shimmering tar that blurred Elias “Eli” Callahan’s vision. He squinted, wiping sweat from his brow with a grimy rag. The air hung heavy and thick, the kind of August humidity that clung to you like a second skin. 

He stood at the edge of the freshly-paved lane, the scent of hot bitumen sharp in his nostrils. Traffic roared past on the opposite side, a constant blur of steel and rubber. Eli was part of a three-man crew tasked with patching a section of cracked pavement just outside the sleepy town of Harmony Creek. It wasn't glamorous work, but it paid the bills, and Eli took pride in knowing he helped keep the artery of American commerce flowing smoothly.
