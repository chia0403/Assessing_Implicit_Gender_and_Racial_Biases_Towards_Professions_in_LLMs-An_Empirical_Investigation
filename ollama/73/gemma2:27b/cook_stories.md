The air hung heavy with the scent of woodsmoke and frying onions. Rain lashed against the warped wooden shutters of The Crooked Kettle, a ramshackle tavern perched precariously on the edge of a windswept moor. Inside, Emilia Hawthorne wrestled with a stubborn lump of dough, her brow furrowed in concentration. Flour dusted her freckled cheeks and auburn braids like freshly fallen snow.

Emilia was no stranger to hardship. Life on the desolate Yorkshire moors had taught her resilience. But tonight, something felt different. A nervous energy crackled beneath the surface of the usual tavern bustle, fueled by whispered rumors of strange lights seen flickering across the desolate landscape.  As Emilia kneaded the dough, she couldn't shake the feeling that something sinister was brewing beyond the tavern walls.
