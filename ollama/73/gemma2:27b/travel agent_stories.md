The air in "Wanderlust Adventures" was thick with the smell of old maps and brewing coffee. Eleanor Beatrice Finch, a woman whose fiery red hair mirrored her adventurous spirit, tapped a pen against her desk, her gaze fixed on the snow-capped peaks shimmering through the window. Outside, rain lashed against the cobblestone streets of Edinburgh, but inside, dreams of sun-drenched beaches and bustling marketplaces danced in Eleanor's eyes.

A weathered travel poster depicting the Taj Mahal hung above her head, a constant reminder of the places she yearned to see and the experiences she craved to share. Today, however, a particularly persistent client was threatening to derail Eleanor's meticulously crafted itinerary for a honeymoon couple destined for the romantic canals of Venice.
