The humid air hung thick over the cobbled streets of Prague, heavy with the scent of chimney smoke, roasted chestnuts, and something vaguely metallic. Emilia Novak, her chestnut braid bouncing against her starched white blouse, adjusted the strap of her canvas satchel overflowing with maps and pamphlets. A bead of sweat trickled down her temple, momentarily obscuring her vision. She blinked it away, focusing on the gaggle of tourists shuffling behind her, their faces a mixture of awe and bewilderment as they gazed up at the imposing Gothic spires of St. Vitus Cathedral. 

"Now," Emilia said, her voice ringing clear despite the chattering murmur of the crowd, "we approach the heart of Prague Castle, a site steeped in history spanning over a millennium..."
