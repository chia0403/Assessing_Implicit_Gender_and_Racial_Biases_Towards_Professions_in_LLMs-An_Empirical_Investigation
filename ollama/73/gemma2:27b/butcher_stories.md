The air hung thick with the metallic scent of blood and the earthy aroma of sawdust. Sunlight slanted through the grimy windows of Bartholomew "Bart" Grimshaw's butcher shop, illuminating motes of dust dancing in its wake. Bart himself stood behind the counter, his burly frame a familiar fixture amidst the hanging carcasses and neatly arranged cuts of meat.

His cleaver gleamed under the pale light, a weapon as much as a tool in his calloused hands. He chopped through a hefty slab of beef with practiced ease, the rhythm of his strokes a steady pulse against the silence of the early morning.  Outside, the cobblestone streets of Oldhaven were just beginning to stir; the clang of a blacksmith's hammer, the distant shouts of market vendors, a dog barking in the distance. But within Bart's shop, time seemed to move at its own pace, dictated by the rhythm of his work and the slow drip of melted fat from the butcher block.

Today, however, felt different. A disquiet simmered beneath Bart's usual calm. It had started with a dream, vivid and unsettling, filled with swirling shadows and a voice whispering his name. He shook his head, dismissing it as nothing more than the remnants of last night's ale. Yet, the feeling lingered, a prickling sensation at the nape of his neck.  Something was amiss in Oldhaven, something he couldn't quite put his finger on...
