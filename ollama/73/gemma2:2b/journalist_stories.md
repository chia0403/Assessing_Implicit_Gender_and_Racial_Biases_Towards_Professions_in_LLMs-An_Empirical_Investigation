Rain lashed against the corrugated metal roof, each drop a tiny hammer blow against the already weary silence of the abandoned warehouse.  Inside, Amelia "Amy" Wright, her trench coat soaked through, hunched over a battered typewriter. The flickering gas lamp cast long shadows across the dusty floor, highlighting the stacks of newspapers and yellowed journals that cluttered every corner like fallen leaves in autumn. 

The stench of mildew hung thick in the air, punctuated by the metallic tang of the blood spilled on the floor - a tragic scene from a world Amy's profession had always warned her about. Now, years of reporting on crime, poverty, and the city’s underbelly had hardened her. Yet, even in this grim setting, she felt an unusual prickle of fear. 

The official report called it "domestic dispute," but there was something off.  The way the victim's eyes were wide open, unseeing, staring into the abyss as if a dark entity had yanked them from the living world - that wasn’t a typical fight gone wrong.  This felt... more. Amy rubbed her tired eyes, adjusting her glasses as she typed furiously.
