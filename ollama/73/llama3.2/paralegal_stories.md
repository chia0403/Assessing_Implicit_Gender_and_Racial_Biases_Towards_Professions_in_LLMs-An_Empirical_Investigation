The rain poured down on the city like a dirty blanket, casting a gloomy mist over the concrete jungle of downtown Chicago. Inside the office of Smith & Associates, P.C., Emily J. Thompson hunched over her desk, her eyes fixed on the stack of files in front of her. As a paralegal for the firm's litigation department, she had spent countless hours pouring over contracts, witness statements, and court transcripts - all in preparation for the high-stakes case that was about to go to trial.

At 32 years old, Emily was one of the top assistants at Smith & Associates, known for her razor-sharp mind, keen eye for detail, and unwavering dedication to the firm's clients. Her sharp jawline and piercing green eyes seemed to be set in a permanent scowl, a testament to the countless hours she had spent buried under a mountain of paperwork.

As she sipped her lukewarm coffee, Emily's phone buzzed with an incoming call from their client, James Parker - a wealthy businessman whose empire was on the line in this very trial. She picked up the receiver, her voice smooth and professional as she answered, "Smith & Associates, how can I help you?"

But before she could even utter a greeting, Emily's heart sank as she heard the sound of a single digit - 4 - ringing out into the phone. A number that sent shivers down her spine...
