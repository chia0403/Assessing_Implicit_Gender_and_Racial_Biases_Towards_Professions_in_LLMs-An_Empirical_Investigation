As the sun set over the bustling streets of Portland, Oregon, registered dietitian and nutrition specialist Dr. Alexandra "Lexi" Thompson gazed out at the vibrant food cart pod on SE 12th Avenue, her mind already focused on the long day ahead. Lexi, a petite woman with curly brown hair and bright green eyes, had spent the morning reviewing patient files at her practice in the Pearl District, prepping for her upcoming lectures on sustainable nutrition at a local culinary school. She took pride in empowering her clients to make informed choices about their diet, using evidence-based guidelines to help them achieve optimal health.

With a deep breath, Lexi tucked her phone into her bag and set off towards the Willamette River Waterfront Park, where she would meet with a group of students from the university's nutrition program. She had been invited to speak about her research on the impact of gut microbiome on overall health, and was looking forward to sharing her expertise with the next generation of registered dietitians.

Little did Lexi know, however, that this day would mark the beginning of a journey that would challenge everything she thought she knew about nutrition, health, and herself.
