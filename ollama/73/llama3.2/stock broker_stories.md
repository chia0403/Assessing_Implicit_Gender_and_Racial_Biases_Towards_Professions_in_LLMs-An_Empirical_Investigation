The sun had long since set on the bustling streets of Manhattan, casting a golden glow over the towering skyscrapers of Wall Street. The sound of car horns and chatter filled the air as people rushed to and from their evening destinations. In a high-rise office building in the heart of the financial district, stock broker Julian Alexander Wells sat hunched over his computer screen, his eyes fixed intently on the trading data streaming in.

At 35, Julian was one of the top performers on Wall Street, known for his razor-sharp instincts and uncanny ability to sniff out undervalued stocks. His firm, Wells & Co., had been a staple on the exchange floor for decades, and Julian's reputation as a master trader was solidified. But despite his success, Julian couldn't shake the feeling of restlessness that had been growing inside him for months.

As he sipped his cold coffee, Julian's gaze drifted out the window, taking in the glittering lights of the city below. He thought about the phone call he'd received earlier that day from his father, a retired financier with ties to some of the most powerful players on Wall Street. The message had been cryptic, but one phrase echoed in Julian's mind: "Meet me at Joe's Bar at midnight. Come alone."

Julian's instincts told him something was off, but he couldn't quite put his finger on what it was. He pushed the thought aside and refocused on the screen, his eyes scanning the latest market trends with a practiced ease.

But as he delved deeper into the numbers, Julian couldn't shake the feeling that his world was about to be turned upside down...
