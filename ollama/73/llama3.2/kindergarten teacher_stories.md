The sun was setting over the small town of Willow Creek, casting a warm orange glow over the quaint streets and homes. In a cozy little house on Elm Street, kindergarten teacher Emily Mae Wilson was wrapping up her day. She had spent the afternoon playing with blocks, singing songs, and reading stories to her students, watching as their little minds grew and learned.

Emily's own mind was filled with thoughts of the upcoming school dance, which she had promised her students would get to attend this year. She had been working tirelessly to save up enough money to rent a small hall and order some party favors, and she couldn't wait to see the excitement on their little faces when it all came together.

As she put away the last of her classroom supplies, Emily took a deep breath and let out a contented sigh. It had been a good day, despite the usual chaos that came with teaching five-year-olds. She felt grateful for her job, and for the opportunity to make a difference in the lives of these young children.

But as she turned to head upstairs to change into some comfortable clothes, Emily's phone buzzed on the counter. It was a text from an unknown number, and all it said was: "You're not who you think you are."
