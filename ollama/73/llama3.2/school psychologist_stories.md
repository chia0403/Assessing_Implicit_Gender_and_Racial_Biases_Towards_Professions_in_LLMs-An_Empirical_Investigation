Dr. Evelyn Alexandra Thompson stepped out of her car and onto the cracked asphalt of Oakwood High School's parking lot, the scent of freshly cut grass and the distant hum of the morning announcements carrying on the breeze. As the school psychologist, Dr. Thompson had spent countless mornings walking these halls, watching students navigate the complexities of adolescence. Today was no different, except that the familiar rhythm of the school day felt slightly off, like a note stuck in an otherwise harmonious melody.

The sun was just starting to rise over the hills surrounding the small town of Oakwood, casting a warm golden light over the deserted parking lot and the worn brick facade of the school. Dr. Thompson's eyes scanned the lot, taking in the rows of empty cars and the scattered trash cans, each one bearing a fresh layer of frost from last night's freeze.

It was going to be a long day.

As she made her way towards the entrance, Dr. Thompson couldn't shake the feeling that something was amiss. A sense of unease had been building in the town for weeks, ever since the sudden resignation of their beloved principal, Mr. Johnson. The departure had left a void that no one seemed to be able to fill, and whispers of uncertainty had begun to circulate among the faculty and staff.

Dr. Thompson's own thoughts were preoccupied with the students she would see today - the ones who might need her expertise to navigate the challenges of their daily lives. She took a deep breath, mentally preparing herself for the conversations that lay ahead, each one potentially more difficult than the last. Little did she know, today was going to be unlike any other day at Oakwood High School.
