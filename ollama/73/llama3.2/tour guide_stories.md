The sun had just begun to set over the ancient city of Petra, casting a warm orange glow over the intricate stone facades and bustling streets. The air was alive with the sounds of vendors calling out their wares and tourists haggling over prices for souvenirs.

Among the throngs of visitors, one person stood out - tour guide Amal Abdullahi, known to locals as "Amal the Guide" for her extensive knowledge of the city's history and hidden secrets. A Jordanian native with a warm smile and infectious enthusiasm, Amal had spent years studying the ancient civilizations that once flourished within Petra's walls.

As she led her latest group of tourists through the narrow canyon known as the Siq, Amal couldn't help but feel a sense of pride and ownership over this sacred place. She had grown up in the shadow of the Monastery, had climbed its steep stairs as a child, and had spent countless hours exploring its hidden corners.

Tonight was no different from any other night - the stars were out in full force, casting a celestial glow over the city's majestic landscape. But Amal couldn't shake the feeling that something was off. A sense of unease had been building within her all day, like a whisper in the wind.

She pushed the feeling aside as she led her group to the Treasury, its facade looming above them like a ghostly presence. "Welcome to the famous Al-Khazneh," she announced, her voice ringing out across the canyon. "One of the Seven Wonders of the World, and arguably the most breathtaking sight in Petra."

As the tourists began to snap photos and marvel at the ancient structure, Amal's eyes scanned the crowd, searching for something - or someone - that didn't quite fit. But what she saw made her heart skip a beat...
