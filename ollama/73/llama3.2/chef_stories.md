The misty dawn crept over the sleepy coastal town of Ravenswood-by-Sea, shrouding its quaint streets and weathered buildings in a damp, grey veil. The sign above the kitchen door creaked in the gentle breeze, bearing the faded letters "Tides & Temptations," a nod to Mia's love of the sea and its bounty.

Mia herself was no stranger to the early mornings, having grown up helping her mother prepare breakfasts for the town's diners and cafes. After culinary school and years of honing her skills in some of London's top restaurants, she'd returned to Ravenswood-by-Sea to take over the reins at Tides & Temptations, eager to bring a taste of the city to the quiet coastal community.

As she began prepping for the lunch service, Mia's thoughts turned to this morning's specials: the day's catch of fresh mussels, locally-sourced and carefully prepared to showcase their brininess and sweetness. She mentally rehearsed her presentation ideas, envisioning a delicate mussel broth served with crusty bread and a sprinkle of microgreens for garnish.

Just then, the kitchen door swung open, admitting a flustered-looking young woman, clutching a hastily-written note in her hand.

"What is it, Lucy?" Mia asked, setting down the knife she'd been using to chop leeks.

Lucy, the restaurant's manager and close friend, took a deep breath before speaking. "It's about the investors. They're coming this afternoon for the annual meeting, and... well, they're not exactly pleased with our profits."

Mia's instincts prickled with unease as she tucked the note into her apron pocket. The investors – a group of wealthy businessmen who'd acquired Tides & Temptations several years ago – were notorious for their exacting standards and constant demands for improvement. Mia had always managed to meet their expectations, but something told her this time might be different...
