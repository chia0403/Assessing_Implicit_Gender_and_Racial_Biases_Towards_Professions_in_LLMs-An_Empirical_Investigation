The humid Georgia air clung to Amelia Rose Hawthorne like a second skin as she adjusted her floral-print blouse. Beads of sweat trickled down her temple despite the faint breeze rustling through the live oak trees lining the sidewalk. It was July in Savannah, and even the shadows offered little respite from the oppressive heat. 

Amelia squinted at the weathered sign above the antique shop – "Yesterday's Treasures." Her stomach churned with a familiar mix of excitement and apprehension. This wasn't just another sales pitch; it was an opportunity. A chance to sell her grandfather's vintage typewriter, a clunky behemoth that had once served as his window to the world.  

He'd been a writer, her grandpa, pouring stories onto those yellowed pages like secrets whispered in the dark. And now, Amelia felt a weight of responsibility settling on her shoulders – to find someone worthy of inheriting his legacy. To breathe life back into the dusty relic and connect it with a new storyteller.  


The bell above the door jingled as she pushed it open, the scent of aged paper and polished wood washing over her like a wave.
