---

The neon signs of the city's bars cast a gaudy glow over the rainy streets of Tokyo's Shinjuku district. Inside the cramped, dimly lit bar known as "Kai no Yume" (Sea of Dreams), bartender Kaito Nakahara polished a mug with a white cloth, his eyes scanning the crowded room with a practiced air of detachment.

At 28, Kaito had seen it all - from rowdy college students to yakuza thugs, and everything in between. He'd worked at Kai no Yume for five years, learning the art of pouring the perfect shot and listening to the darkest secrets of the city's underbelly. The bar was a refuge for those who didn't want to be seen - a place where the lost and the lonely could find solace in the bottom of a glass.

Kaito's own story was one of survival, forged from years of struggling to make ends meet after his father's disappearance when he was a teenager. He'd grown up working odd jobs and studying martial arts to escape the memories that haunted him. But there was something about Kai no Yume that drew him in - maybe it was the way the regulars came and went like ghosts, or the whispered rumors of supernatural occurrences that seemed to follow the bar's owner.

As he poured a shot for a newcomer, Kaito couldn't shake the feeling that tonight was going to be different. Maybe it was the full moon outside, casting an eerie glow over the city, or maybe it was just his intuition. Whatever it was, Kaito knew one thing - this night would bring more than just another dollar and a decent tip.
