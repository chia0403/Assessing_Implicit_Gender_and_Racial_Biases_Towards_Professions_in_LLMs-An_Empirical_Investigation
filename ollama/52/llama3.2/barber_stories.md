The sun was setting over the bustling streets of Little Italy, casting a warm orange glow over the crowded sidewalks and storefronts. Inside "Bella Vita" barbershop, the sounds of sizzling espresso and lively chatter filled the air as patrons waited for their turn in the chair. Behind the counter, Amadeo "Mace" Bianchi expertly crafted a stylish fade on a young businessman's head, his scissors snipping away with precision.

At 32, Mace had spent over a decade honing his craft as one of Little Italy's top barbers, earning a reputation for his attention to detail and warm, welcoming demeanor. His shop was more than just a place to get a haircut – it was a gathering spot for the community, where friends and neighbors came to catch up and share stories.

As Mace finished up the businessman's haircut and handed him a steaming cup of espresso, he couldn't help but notice the stranger standing in the doorway, eyeing the shop with interest. The man was dressed in a long coat and fedora, his features shrouded in shadow – but there was something about him that caught Mace's attention, something that made him want to know more.

"Can I get you anything?" Mace asked, wiping his hands on a towel as he locked eyes with the mysterious stranger.
