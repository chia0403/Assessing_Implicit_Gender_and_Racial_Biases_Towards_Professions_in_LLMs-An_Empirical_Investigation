The sun had just set over the bustling streets of Tokyo's Shinjuku district, casting a warm orange glow over the crowded alleys and neon-lit billboards. Amidst the throngs of pedestrians rushing to grab dinner or meet with friends, a small nail salon stood tucked away on a quiet side street. The sign above the door read "Kokoro no Oto" (Heart's Song), and inside, 25-year-old Emiko Nakahara worked her magic on customers' nails.

As a skilled manicurist with a passion for art and design, Emiko took pride in transforming ordinary hands into works of beauty. With her delicate touch and creative eye, she had built a loyal client base among the Tokyo's fashionistas and entrepreneurs. But despite her success, Emiko felt a sense of restlessness, as if there was more to life than just perfecting her craft.

Tonight was no exception. As she worked on a client's intricate gel design, Emiko's mind began to wander to the mysterious letter she had received earlier that day...
