The rain-soaked streets of Tokyo reflected the melancholy mood that had settled over Emiko Nakahara, editor-in-chief of the prestigious literary magazine, "Kokoro". The warm glow of the city's evening lights seemed to mock her, like a cruel reminder of all that she had lost.

Emiko's eyes, once bright and full of life, now felt dull and tired, weighed down by the constant pressure of meeting deadlines and making impossible decisions about the stories that would be published in "Kokoro". Her hands, once deft and precise as they worked their magic on manuscripts, now felt clumsy and unsteady, as if the very words themselves had become her enemy.

It was a typical Wednesday evening, just like any other. But for Emiko, this day marked the two-year anniversary of her sister's death. The pain still lingered, a constant ache in her heart that she struggled to fill with the world around her. And as she stood there, lost in thought, the rain outside seemed to grow louder, a reminder that some wounds would never fully heal.
