As the last wisps of sunset faded from the sky, airline stewardess Emily Annabelle Thompson gazed out the window of Flight 121, her eyes tracing the silhouette of the rugged coastline below. She had been looking forward to this layover all week, and now that it was finally here, she couldn't shake the feeling that something was off.

The darkness of the Pacific Northwest evening seemed to press in around her as she made her way down the aisle, checking the cabin's interior one last time before shutting down for the night. The fluorescent lights overhead cast an eerie glow over the rows of seats, making the passengers' faces look like they were carved from stone. Emily's own face felt pale and drawn, a reflection of the fatigue that had been building up inside her for months.

It was just another layover in a long string of them, but this one felt different. Maybe it was the way the rain seemed to seep into every pore of the airport, making everything feel damp and gray. Or maybe it was the sense of unease that had settled over Emily like a shroud since she'd boarded the plane at Los Angeles earlier that day.

Whatever it was, Emily knew one thing for sure: this layover wouldn't be like any other.
