As the last wisps of sunlight faded beyond the horizon, casting the city in a deep orange glow, Judge Emilia Rose Wilson made her way through the crowded corridors of the courthouse. The air was thick with the smell of old books and stale coffee, a familiar scent that always seemed to calm her nerves as she prepared for another long day of dispensing justice.

Emilia, or Mia as few people outside the courtroom called her, had been a judge on the city's highest court for nearly a decade. She took pride in her work, working tirelessly to ensure that every case that crossed her desk received fair and impartial consideration. But despite her best efforts, the weight of the law seemed to bear down on her more and more with each passing year.

As she emerged into the bright fluorescent light of the courthouse lobby, Emilia's eyes scanned the bustling crowd, searching for a familiar face or a sense of calm in the midst of chaos. Her gaze landed on the clock above the entrance, ticking away at its steady pace as if mocking her very existence. The day had only just begun, and already she knew it was going to be a long one.

Little did Emilia know, today would bring more than just another grueling day of court proceedings. Today would bring a case that would challenge everything she thought she knew about justice, and force her to confront the very darkest corners of human nature.
