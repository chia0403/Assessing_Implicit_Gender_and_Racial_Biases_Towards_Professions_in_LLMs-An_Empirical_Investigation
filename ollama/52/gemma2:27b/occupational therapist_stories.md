Rain lashed against the windows of the Willow Creek Rehabilitation Centre, blurring the autumn leaves into impressionistic strokes of gold and crimson. Inside, the air hung heavy with the scent of antiseptic and lavender hand sanitizer. Amelia Rose Cartwright, a young occupational therapist with eyes as bright as polished emeralds, sighed as she surveyed her next patient's chart.

Ethan Miller, a former construction worker in his late 40s, had suffered a severe spinal cord injury two months ago, leaving him paralyzed from the waist down. Amelia felt a familiar pang of empathy mingled with determination. Helping Ethan regain some independence was going to be challenging, but she was ready for it. She believed everyone deserved a chance to reclaim their lives, no matter how daunting the obstacles seemed.
