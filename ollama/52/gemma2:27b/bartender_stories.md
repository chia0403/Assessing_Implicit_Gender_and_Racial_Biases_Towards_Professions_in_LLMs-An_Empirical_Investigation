Rain lashed against the grimy windows of The Drowned Mermaid, blurring the neon "Open" sign into a sickly green smear. Inside, the air hung thick with cigarette smoke and the scent of stale beer. Bartholomew "Bart" Quill, his salt-and-pepper hair slicked back under his faded bandana, wiped down the mahogany bar with a practiced hand, his gaze flickering between the rain-soaked street outside and the handful of regulars huddled in booths.

Bart had seen it all in his twenty years behind this bar: broken hearts mended with whiskey, dreams whispered over late-night beers, and enough brawls to make even the toughest sailor blanch. But tonight, something felt different. A prickling unease danced at the back of his neck, a feeling he couldn't quite shake as the storm raged outside, mirroring the turmoil brewing within him.
