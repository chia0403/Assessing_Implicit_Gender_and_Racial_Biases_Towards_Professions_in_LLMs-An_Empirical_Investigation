Rain lashed against the dusty windowpane, blurring the already hazy view of Elm Street. Inside the cramped office above Mrs. Higgins' bakery, Theodore Ainsworth hunched over a ledger, the faint aroma of yeast and sugar battling with the musty scent of aging paper. Theodore, a man who wore his spectacles perched precariously on the bridge of his nose and whose greying hair always seemed in danger of escaping its tidy comb-over, frowned at the figures before him. Something wasn't adding up. A discrepancy gnawed at him, a tiny anomaly that refused to be reconciled. The rhythmic tap-tap-tap of rain against glass mirrored the insistent beat of anxiety in his chest. This wasn't just any ledger; it belonged to the esteemed Blackwood Estate, and Theodore knew that even the slightest error could have dire consequences.
