The fluorescent lights buzzed overhead, casting a sickly yellow glow over the cluttered desk in Amelia Winthrop’s office. Stacks of assessment reports threatened to topple over, vying for space with brightly colored stress balls and worn-out copies of "Mindfulness for Teens." Outside her window, the autumn wind whipped through the barren branches of the ancient oak tree, scattering crimson leaves across the playground like confetti.

Amelia sighed, rubbing her tired eyes. The weight of another difficult case pressed down on her: thirteen-year-old Ethan Miller, who had been exhibiting signs of severe social anxiety and withdrawal. He’d refused to speak during their initial meeting, his gaze fixed firmly on the floor, his small frame trembling slightly.  

Today was their third session, and Amelia felt a growing sense of urgency. She needed to break through Ethan's wall, to uncover the root cause of his distress before it spiraled further out of control.
