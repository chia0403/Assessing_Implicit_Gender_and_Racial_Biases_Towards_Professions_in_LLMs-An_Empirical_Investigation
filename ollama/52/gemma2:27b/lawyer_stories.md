Rain lashed against the grimy windows of Beatrice "Bea" Albright's office, mirroring the storm brewing inside her. The mahogany desk, usually gleaming with pride under the warm glow of the antique lamp, was now obscured by stacks of legal files threatening to spill over onto the worn Persian rug. Bea ran a hand through her unruly curls, frustration twisting in her gut. 

The case file lay open before her, its contents as murky and unsettling as the November sky outside. The disappearance of Amelia Hawthorne, a young socialite from one of Boston's oldest families, had captivated the city. But for Bea, it was more than just another headline-grabbing mystery; it was a personal battle against injustice, a chance to expose the secrets lurking beneath the gilded veneer of Beacon Hill society.
