Rain lashed against the cockpit canopy, blurring the already-dim lights of the airstrip below. Evelyn "Eve" Sinclair gripped the yoke tighter, her knuckles turning white against the worn leather. The old Cessna sputtered and coughed, protesting the wind's assault as she fought to keep it steady in the turbulent air.

The island of Isla Perdida lay shrouded in a curtain of grey, its volcanic peak obscured by swirling mist. It wasn't the ideal landing conditions, but Eve had no choice. Her cargo – a critically ill child needing immediate medical attention on the mainland – wouldn’t wait for the storm to pass.
