The scent of rain clung to the cobblestone streets of Prague, mingling with the yeasty aroma wafting from a nearby bakery. Eleanor Vance, her fingers stained with ink and frustration, stared out the window of her attic apartment. Below, the Vltava River shimmered under a bruised sky, reflecting the city's melancholy beauty. 

Eleanor, a writer grappling with a crippling case of writer's block, longed for inspiration to strike like a bolt from the blue. Her latest novel, a gothic thriller set in the shadowy alleys of Prague itself, had stalled at chapter three. The characters refused to cooperate, their motivations murky and unclear. She yearned to unlock their secrets, to give them voices that resonated with truth.

But today, even the city's ethereal charm seemed unable to penetrate her mental fog.
