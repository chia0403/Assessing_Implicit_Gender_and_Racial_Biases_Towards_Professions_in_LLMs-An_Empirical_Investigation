The Arizona sun beat down on Elias "Eli" Ramirez's neck like a branding iron. Sweat stung his eyes, blurring the dusty horizon into a watercolor smear of ochre and burnt sienna. Eli wiped his brow with the back of his calloused hand, leaving a streak of grime across his forehead. The clang of steel against steel echoed through the construction site as cranes lifted massive beams into place, their groans blending with the insistent buzz of cicadas. He tightened the grip on his hammer, its worn handle familiar and comforting in his palm.

Eli was tasked with reinforcing the concrete foundation for a new luxury resort rising from the desert floor. It was ironic, he thought, that amidst the stark beauty of the Sonoran Desert, they were building an oasis of artificial indulgence. But work was work, and Eli needed to put food on the table for his abuela and little sister back in Phoenix.
