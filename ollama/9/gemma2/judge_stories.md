The air in Judge Eleanor Beaumont's chambers was thick with anticipation. The mahogany desk, polished to a mirror sheen, reflected her worried frown. Outside, the Miami sun beat down on the courthouse windows, turning them into shimmering furnaces. Inside, the room felt cooler, but only because of the chill that had settled over Eleanor since the morning’s news. 

Her clerk, Martha, tapped nervously on the doorframe, a stack of files threatening to spill from her grasp. “Your Honor,” she said, voice trembling slightly, “Mr. Hernandez is here.”


Eleanor closed her eyes for a moment, taking a deep breath. This wasn't just another case. This was personal.  This was Daniel Hernandez, the son of her childhood friend, standing accused of arson, facing decades in prison if convicted. And Eleanor knew, with a chilling certainty, that this was going to be the most difficult trial of her career.
