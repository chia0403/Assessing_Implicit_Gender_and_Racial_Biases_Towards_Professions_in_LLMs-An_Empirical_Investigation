The crisp autumn air carried the scent of fallen leaves and distant bonfires as Eleanor Blythe adjusted her stethoscope. Her reflection stared back from the polished window of her office, framed by the vibrant reds and oranges of the changing maple trees outside.  Eleanor was an occupational therapist at St. Jude’s Rehabilitation Center, a sprawling brick building nestled on the edge of a sleepy New England town. The morning sun cast long shadows across the grounds, illuminating the carefully tended gardens where patients practiced their balance exercises and the laughter of children playing in the playground echoed faintly through the open windows. 

Eleanor took a deep breath, steeling herself for the day ahead. St. Jude’s was more than just a place of healing; it was a tapestry woven with stories of resilience, loss, and hope. Today, she knew, those stories would unfold once again, each patient's journey intricately connected to her own.
