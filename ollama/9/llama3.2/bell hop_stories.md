It was a drizzly autumn evening in New Haven, Connecticut, the kind of night that made you want to curl up with a good book and a warm cup of coffee. But for Emilia "Mia" Thompson, bellhop at the historic Downtown Inn, the rain was just another excuse to stay on her feet.

As she trudged through the soggy lobby, her bright pink hair clips and worn-out sneakers making her stand out among the suits and ties that usually filled the room, Mia checked her watch for what felt like the hundredth time. She had a feeling this would be one of those nights - the kind where the guests were rowdy, the tips were scarce, and the only thing she got to take home was a damp towel.

Mia had been working at the Downtown Inn for five years, ever since college, when her grandmother told her that the hotel's legendary owner, Henry Wellington, needed someone with "heart" to help him keep the place running. Mia took it as a challenge, and the gruff old man had taken a liking to her straight-shooting attitude and perpetual smile.

But on nights like this, when the rain pounded against the windows and the wind howled through the corridors, Mia couldn't shake the feeling that she was more than just a bellhop - she was stuck in a life she never asked for.
