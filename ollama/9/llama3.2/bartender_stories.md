The neon sign creaked in the gentle breeze, casting a gaudy glow over the deserted street. Behind the counter, bartender Declan Patrick McGillicuddy polished a glass with a white cloth, his eyes scanning the dimly lit room as if searching for something - or someone.

Declan was a fixture in this small town's social scene, with a quick wit and a sharp tongue that could charm even the grumpiest of patrons. But tonight, he felt restless, like there was something just beyond his grasp. It had been six months since his sister, Aoife, had left town to pursue her dreams in the city, leaving Declan with a sense of emptiness that still lingered.

As the clock struck nine, the door swung open and a gust of cool air swept in, carrying with it the scent of rain and rebellion. A young woman, her long black hair plastered to her forehead, slipped into the pub, her eyes scanning the room as if searching for someone - or something. And Declan, sensing a kindred spirit, felt his heart stir with interest...
