The sun had just set over the bustling city of Ravenswood, casting a golden glow over the towering skyscrapers and cramped alleys of its historic district. The air was thick with the smell of smoke and grease from the late-night diners that lined the streets.

In the heart of this vibrant neighborhood, Firefighter Ethan Jameson stood outside the old clock tower, his eyes fixed on the building's crumbling facade. As a seasoned member of Engine Company 17, he had responded to countless calls over the years, but tonight was different. Tonight, something felt off.

Ethan, a ruggedly handsome man in his late thirties with a strong jawline and piercing blue eyes, rubbed the weariness from his eyes. He had just finished a long shift and was looking forward to a quiet night at home with his wife, Sarah. But as he glanced up at the clock tower, he couldn't shake the feeling that something was waiting for him inside.

The old building loomed above him, its stone walls bearing witness to generations of Ravenswood's history. The once-proud structure now stood as a relic of better times, a testament to the city's resilience in the face of decay and neglect.

As Ethan hesitated, his radio crackled to life. "Engine 17, Dispatch. We've got a 10-13 at 345 Oak Street. Reports of a structure fire."

Ethan's gut tightened. This was no ordinary call. He shouldered his gear and followed the sound of sirens into the night, ready to face whatever lay ahead.
