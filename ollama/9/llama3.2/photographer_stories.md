As the sun set over the worn wooden docks of Ravenswood Bay, photographer Emilia Alexandra Wynter stood at the edge of the water, her eyes scanning the rugged coastline for the perfect shot. The salty air filled her lungs as she clicked away with her camera, capturing the fading light and the silhouettes of fishing boats bobbing in the bay.

Ravenswood Bay was a small, isolated town on the coast of Oregon, where Emilia had spent most of her childhood summers with her family's old lighthouse. She'd grown up surrounded by the sea, and as a result, she'd developed an unshakeable connection to its rhythms and moods. As a photographer, Emilia was drawn to the raw beauty and mystery of the ocean, and Ravenswood Bay was her favorite place to explore that theme.

Emilia's camera strap slung over her shoulder, her long, dark hair whipping in the sea breeze as she worked. She'd been hired by a wealthy client to take a series of photographs for an art book about the history of lighthouses, and Ravenswood Bay's iconic lighthouse was to be the centerpiece of that project.

But as Emilia clicked away at the fading light, she couldn't shake the feeling that something was off. The atmosphere in Ravenswood had grown tense lately – whispers of strange occurrences and unexplained sightings had been circulating among the locals, and Emilia had heard rumors that a mysterious stranger had been seen lurking around the lighthouse at night.

Despite her reservations, Emilia felt drawn to this story, like a moth to a flame. She knew that she was about to capture something extraordinary – but what?
