The fog swirled through the narrow alleys of Ravenshire like a living entity, shrouding the old town in a damp, grey mist. It was a chill autumn morning, and the streets were already starting to fill with the smell of smoke from woodstoves and baking bread.

In a small, dimly lit shop on Baker's Row, Emilia Elara Blackwood was already hard at work. As the local butcher, she had spent her entire life surrounded by the sights and smells of meat: the rush of blood as she made a cut, the sizzle of fat in the pan, the sweet scent of freshly cured sausages.

Emilia's fingers moved deftly over the flesh, her hands stained with the juices of yesterday's lamb. She was a woman accustomed to working with raw meat, but there was something about this particular batch that made her skin prickle - a strange, unshakeable feeling that something was off. She pushed the thought aside, focusing instead on the task at hand: preparing the day's offerings for the townspeople.

The door above her workshop creaked open, and a blast of cold air swept in, carrying with it the faint sound of horse hooves and the murmur of hushed voices from outside. Emilia's eyes narrowed as she watched the figure step into the shop, their face obscured by a hood that cast a shadow over their features...
