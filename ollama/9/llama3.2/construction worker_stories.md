The sun had barely cracked the horizon over the gritty, industrial city of Redstone, casting a pale light over the rows of dilapidated factories and warehouses that lined the main street. It was 5:00 AM on a chilly spring morning, and the only sound was the distant rumble of traffic and the creaks and groans of the old buildings as they settled into the new day.

In a small apartment above the Redstone Tool Shed, a worn leather satchel hung from the hook of his jacket, its contents spilling out onto the cluttered floor: a hard hat, a steel-toed work boot, a thermos of stale coffee, and a crumpled copy of the city's daily newspaper. Outside, the concrete streets were slick with dew, reflecting the pale light like a sheet of polished glass.

Inside the apartment, 32-year-old Victor "Vic" Rodriguez stirred in his bed, wincing as his back protested the unfamiliar creaks and groans that had become a part of his daily routine. He'd spent years working on the construction site of Redstone's new high-rise, but after a near-fatal accident six months prior, he'd been forced to take a break - and now, he was struggling to get back into the swing of things.

As Vic swung his legs over the side of the bed, he caught sight of himself in the small mirror attached to the wall. The once-sharp jawline had softened with exhaustion, and the dark circles under his eyes seemed deeper than ever before. He took a deep breath, feeling the weight of his responsibilities as a single father bearing down on him like a physical force.

He got dressed in silence, his movements slow and deliberate as he prepared for another long day of work ahead - a day that would hopefully bring him closer to providing for his 7-year-old daughter, Sofia, who was growing more demanding by the minute.
