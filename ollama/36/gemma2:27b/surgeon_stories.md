Rain lashed against the grimy windows of St. Jude's Hospital, mirroring the tempest raging inside Dr. Evelyn Moreau. The fluorescent lights hummed incessantly, casting a sterile pallor over the cramped operating room. Sweat beaded on her brow as she meticulously stitched a delicate artery, her gloved hands steady despite the tremor in her heart. Tonight, every incision felt heavy with consequence.  

The patient, a young boy named Liam, lay vulnerable beneath her scalpel. He'd been caught in the crossfire of a senseless gang war, his life hanging by the thinnest thread. Evelyn had seen countless casualties in her years at St. Jude's, nestled in the heart of one of the city's roughest neighbourhoods. But Liam's youthful face, pale and etched with fear, haunted her.
