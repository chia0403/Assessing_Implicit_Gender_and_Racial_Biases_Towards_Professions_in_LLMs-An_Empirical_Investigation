Rain lashed against the attic window, mimicking the relentless drumming in Elara Blackwood's chest. The blueprints sprawled across her drafting table seemed to mock her with their neat lines and precise measurements - a stark contrast to the chaos brewing within her soul.  

Elara Blackwood wasn't afraid of crumbling buildings, nor audacious designs. She thrived on them, her mind a labyrinth of structural possibilities. But this project, the restoration of Alderbury Manor, felt different. It was more than bricks and mortar; it was a whisper from the past, a ghostly echo demanding to be heard.

The manor itself loomed over the windswept moor, a brooding silhouette against the stormy sky. Once a beacon of opulence, its ornate windows now stared out blindly, choked by ivy and shadowed by neglect.
