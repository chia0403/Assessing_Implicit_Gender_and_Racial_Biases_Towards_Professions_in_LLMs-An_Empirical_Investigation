The midday sun beat down on Elias Thorne's neck as he wrestled with a stubborn bundle of shingles. Sweat dripped from his brow, stinging his eyes. He wiped it away with the back of his hand, squinting up at the steep pitch of the Victorian roof he was reshingling. Below him, the manicured lawns and flowerbeds of Oakhaven seemed impossibly distant, like a miniature world contained within a glass globe.

Elias wasn't afraid of heights, not anymore. Years on rooftops had cured him of that fear.  But today, something felt off. A prickle of unease danced along his skin, heightened by the unnatural stillness of the air. It was as if Oakhaven itself was holding its breath.
