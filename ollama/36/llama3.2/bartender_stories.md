The sun had just set over the bustling streets of New Orleans' French Quarter, casting a warm orange glow over the crumbling architecture and lively nightlife. Inside the dimly lit bar, "Madame Luna's Curious Concoctions," bartender Étienne Baptiste LaFleur was prepping for another busy evening. With his rugged good looks and charming smile, Étienne had built a loyal following among the Quarter's regulars - from young artists to retired jazz musicians.

As he polished a glass with a white linen cloth, the sounds of the city spilled in through the open door: laughter, music, and the distant wail of a saxophone. The air was thick with the scent of cigar smoke, cheap perfume, and the faint tang of absinthe. Étienne took a deep breath, feeling at home among the eccentric characters that called Madame Luna's their own.

It had been five years since Étienne left his family's restaurant in rural Louisiana to chase his dreams in the city. He'd worked his way up from pouring drinks for tipsy tourists to becoming one of New Orleans' top bartenders - and, rumor had it, its most talented mixologist. But as the night wore on and the crowds grew thick, Étienne couldn't shake the feeling that something was off. A nagging sense of unease lingered beneath his usual calm exterior, like a whispered secret he couldn't quite remember.

And then, just as the first customers began to filter in, a mysterious woman walked through the door - her eyes locked on Étienne with an unnerving intensity...
