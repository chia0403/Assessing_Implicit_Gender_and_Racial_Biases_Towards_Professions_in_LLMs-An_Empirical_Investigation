Dr. Alexandra "Lexi" Thompson stepped out of her small apartment and onto the bustling streets of Tokyo's Shibuya ward, the neon lights of the city casting a kaleidoscope of colors across her face. As she sipped her coffee, Lexi gazed out at the throngs of pedestrians rushing to and fro, their faces buried in their phones or lost in thought. She smiled wryly to herself - it was a familiar scene, one that played out every day on this busy street.

Lexi had been living in Tokyo for three years now, ever since she'd finished her residency at the prestigious University of California, San Francisco Medical Center. She'd always dreamed of working abroad, and the Japanese government's offer to sponsor her had made it possible. As a pediatrician specializing in infectious diseases, Lexi was uniquely qualified to work on the front lines of Tokyo's healthcare system.

But as she began her walk to the hospital, Lexi couldn't shake the feeling that something was off. A sense of unease had been growing inside her for weeks, ever since she'd received a cryptic email from an unknown sender. The message had contained only two words: "Watch yourself."
