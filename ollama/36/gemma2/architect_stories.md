The steel beams groaned under Amelia Beatrice Hawthorne’s gaze. She traced the curve of the arch with her gloved hand, feeling the chill of the unfinished concrete beneath. The air hung heavy with the scent of dust and welding sparks, a symphony of construction that usually calmed her. But today, Amelia felt a tremor of unease.  

The setting sun cast long shadows across the cavernous space of the new concert hall, its skeletal frame reaching for the twilight sky like a desperate plea. It was Amelia's magnum opus, a project years in the making, designed to be a testament to human ingenuity and artistic expression. But as she stood there, amidst the symphony of construction noises, she couldn’t shake the feeling that something wasn't right. 


It started with whispers - rumors about an old foundation buried beneath the site, a history shrouded in shadows and secrecy.  Amelia had dismissed them at first, focusing on blueprints and structural integrity. But now, as dusk fell and the air grew colder, those whispers seemed to echo around her, whispering of forgotten secrets and unsettling truths.
