The fog rolled in off the ocean, shrouding the small coastal town of Ravenswood in a damp, grey mist. It was a typical winter morning at the offices of Hawkins & Co., a mid-sized law firm that catered to the local business community. In a cramped, windowless office on the second floor, secretary Emily Elizabeth Wellington sipped her lukewarm coffee and stared blankly at the computer screen in front of her.

At 25, Emily had been working as a secretary for five years, and she had seen it all - or so she thought. The firm was stable, if unexciting, and the work was routine: answering phones, scheduling appointments, and typing up reports all day long. But despite the monotony, Emily found herself strangely content in her role, perhaps because it allowed her to keep her head down and avoid the stresses of adult life.

As she checked her emails for what felt like the hundredth time that morning, Emily's gaze drifted out the window to the grey, rain-soaked streets below. She lived alone in a small apartment just off Main Street, within walking distance of the office. It was a quiet, predictable existence - one that Emily had grown accustomed to over the years.

But as she sat there, sipping her coffee and watching the fog swirl around the buildings outside, Emily couldn't shake the feeling that something was about to change. She just didn't know what.
