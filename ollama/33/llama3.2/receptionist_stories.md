The sun was setting over the small town of Willow Creek, casting a warm orange glow over the quaint streets and shops. Inside the worn brick building that housed "Willow Creek News," a lone figure sat hunched over her desk, her eyes fixed on the clock ticking away above the front counter. Alexis Mae Thompson, receptionist for the local newspaper, let out a deep sigh as she gazed at the stack of unpaid bills and dusty files accumulating in the corner of her office.

It was 5:45 PM on a typical Wednesday evening, but Alexis felt anything but typical. The past few months had seen a steady stream of layoffs and budget cuts in Willow Creek, and with it, a sense of unease that hung over the town like a dark cloud. The newsroom, once bustling with activity, now sat eerily silent, its only sound the soft hum of the fluorescent lights overhead.

Alexis, with her curly brown hair tied back in a ponytail and a faded copy of "The Great Gatsby" tucked into the pages of her worn notebook, felt like she was drowning in a sea of uncertainty. She had always been content to keep to herself, happy to spend her days answering phones and scheduling appointments for the town's most prominent residents. But lately, she couldn't shake the feeling that something was about to change.

And then, just as she was starting to lose hope, the phone on her desk rang, shrill and insistent. Alexis picked it up, a spark of excitement igniting within her chest. "Willow Creek News," she said, trying to sound more confident than she felt. But before she could say another word, a voice on the other end spoke out, its tone low and urgent.

"Alexis Thompson?" The voice was unfamiliar, but there was something about it that sent shivers down her spine. "I need to speak with you in person."
