The humid Georgia air hung thick with the scent of honeysuckle and impending rain as Amelia “Amy” Rose Thornton surveyed her caseload for the day.  She sat at her cramped desk in the Bibb County Probation Office, squinting through the glare reflecting off her computer screen. The fluorescent lights buzzed overhead, a constant soundtrack to the quiet hum of desperation that permeated the room. Files spilled from filing cabinets, threatening to cascade onto the threadbare carpet like miniature tombstones marking the lives of those who’d crossed paths with the law. Amy sighed, pushing back her auburn hair pulled into a tight bun. Another day, another mountain of paperwork and parole violations waiting for her sharp eyes and even sharper wit.  But today felt different. A prickling sensation danced along her spine, a feeling that something – or someone – was about to shake things up in her predictable world.
