The fluorescent lights hummed an incessant, almost hypnotic tune as Bertram “Bert” Sterling pushed his mop through the swirling dust motes in the echoing Grand Central Terminal lobby.  He'd spent the better part of his life here – fifteen years cleaning this space where hurried feet and weary souls converged like migratory birds, each carrying their own stories in their worn-out bags.  The smell of stale coffee and cheap cologne lingered like a familiar ghost from past lives. 

Today, the air was thick with anticipation. The city’s annual Christmas gala was about to kick off, an event that usually brought out hordes of tourists and glitter-clad socialites, but today, something felt different.  A palpable tension crackled in the air, as if the very building itself was holding its breath.
