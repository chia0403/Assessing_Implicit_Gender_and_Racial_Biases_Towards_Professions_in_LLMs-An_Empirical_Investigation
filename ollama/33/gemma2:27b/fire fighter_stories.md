The acrid smell of smoke stung Elias “Eli” Blackwood’s nostrils even before the alarm in the firehouse blared. It was a scent he knew intimately, one that clung to his gear like a second skin and often haunted his dreams. He scrambled out of his bunk, adrenaline already coursing through his veins. The dispatcher’s voice crackled over the loudspeaker, “Structure fire, three-story residential building on Elm Street. Possible occupants trapped.” Eli didn't need to hear more.

He raced down the polished metal pole, landing with a practiced thud and grabbing his helmet. Outside, the sirens wailed, their mournful cries echoing through the pre-dawn gloom. The sky was barely tinged with orange as they sped towards Elm Street, red lights painting the rain-slicked asphalt in flickering streaks of crimson.

Eli's heart hammered against his ribs. He knew that every second counted when lives were on the line. This wasn't just another routine call; this was a fight against the relentless devouring flames that threatened to consume everything in their path.
