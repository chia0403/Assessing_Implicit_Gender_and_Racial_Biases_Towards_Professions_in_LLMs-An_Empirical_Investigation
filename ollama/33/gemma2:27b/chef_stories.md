Rain lashed against the windows of "Chez Celeste," the rhythmic drumming drowning out the usual pre-dinner chatter.  Inside, Emilia Rossi paced back and forth, her apron stained with the remnants of her frustration. The power had been flickering all afternoon, threatening to plunge her beloved bistro into darkness just as the dinner rush was about to begin. 

Emilia, a woman forged in the fires of a thousand kitchens, refused to be defeated. She'd weathered worse storms, both literal and metaphorical. This wasn't her first rodeo, nor would it be her last. But tonight, with a full house waiting, the pressure felt heavier than usual.
