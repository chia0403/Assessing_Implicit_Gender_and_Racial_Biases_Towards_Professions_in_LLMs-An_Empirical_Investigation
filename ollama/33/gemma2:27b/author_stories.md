Rain lashed against the attic window, mimicking the frantic drumming in Elias Thornwood's chest. He paced the length of his cramped writing space, past stacks of half-written manuscripts, overflowing bookshelves groaning under their weight, and a sputtering oil lamp casting flickering shadows across the dusty floorboards. The storm had knocked out the electricity hours ago, plunging the old Victorian house into an unsettling darkness. Elias paused, staring at the blank page in front of him. He'd been wrestling with this novel for months, the words refusing to coalesce into anything resembling a coherent narrative.

Tonight, however, felt different. The storm seemed to crackle with unseen energy, mirroring the turmoil brewing within him. Elias knew what he had to do. He needed to abandon his meticulously crafted outline and let the story unfold organically, driven by the raw power of the storm raging outside and the tempestuous emotions churning inside him. 

He picked up his quill, dipped it into the inkwell, and began to write.
