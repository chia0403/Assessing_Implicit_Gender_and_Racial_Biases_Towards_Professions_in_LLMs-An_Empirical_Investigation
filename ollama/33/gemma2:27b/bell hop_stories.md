The Grand Blackwood Hotel loomed above the cobblestone street like a slumbering giant, its ornate facade illuminated by gas lamps that cast long, dancing shadows. The air hung thick with the scent of woodsmoke and roasted chestnuts from the vendor on the corner, a symphony of smells familiar to Archibald "Archie" Finch, who stood rigidly at his post near the revolving doors. 

Archie wasn't just any bellhop; he was a connoisseur of arrivals. He could tell by the way a guest gripped their luggage, the tilt of their hat, even the rhythm of their footsteps, whether they were seasoned travelers or nervous first-timers. Tonight, however, something felt different. A shiver ran down his spine as a sleek black car pulled up to the curb, its headlights piercing the swirling fog like accusing eyes.
