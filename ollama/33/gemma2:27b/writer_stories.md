Rain lashed against the attic windowpane, a relentless rhythm that mirrored Eloise Hawthorne's hammering heart.  The cramped space, crammed with stacks of dusty books and forgotten furniture, smelled faintly of mildew and old paper.  A single bare bulb cast harsh shadows across the room, illuminating Eloise hunched over her typewriter. The keys clicked insistently, each strike a desperate plea against the rising tide of writer's block that threatened to drown her latest novel.

Outside, the storm raged on, mirroring the tempestuous emotions swirling within Eloise. She was supposed to be finishing the final chapter, but the words refused to come.  Her muse had deserted her, leaving behind only a gnawing anxiety and a growing sense of inadequacy.
