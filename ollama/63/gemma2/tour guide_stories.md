The crisp morning air hung heavy with the scent of salt and brine as Elara Rosewater adjusted her straw boater hat, its faded pink ribbon mirroring the blush creeping across the sky above the choppy Atlantic.  She surveyed the group gathered before her: twelve tourists, wide-eyed and eager, their faces a mix of sunburn hues and nervous excitement. This was it - another day on the "Haunted History Tour" of Islehaven, a small, windswept island off the coast of Maine, known as much for its brooding cliffs and eerie fog as its quaint charm. 

Elara, with her unruly auburn curls escaping their braid and eyes that held the glint of both mischief and knowledge, was the only local who dared to guide these curious souls through Islehaven's shadowed past. She loved sharing the island's stories, especially the whispered legends about ghostly shipwrecks and solitary lighthouses haunted by melancholic spirits. But today, she felt a prickle of unease. The air seemed thicker than usual, charged with an unseen energy that made the hairs on the back of her neck stand on end.  Perhaps it was just the pre-dawn chill, or maybe... something else. 


Elara took a deep breath and launched into her introduction, hoping to quell the rising tension in her gut. "Welcome, everyone," she began, her voice carrying with a familiar warmth. "To Islehaven, where history whispers through the wind..."
