The air in Ward 4B hummed with the low thrum of machines and hushed whispers. It was always this way – a symphony of suffering punctuated by fleeting moments of relief. Elara Vance, her name tag pinned neatly to her crisp white uniform, navigated the maze of beds with practiced ease. The scent of antiseptic hung heavy in the air, mingling with the cloying sweetness of sugary IV drips and the faint, metallic tang of fear.  

Elara, a seasoned registered nurse with a heart as big as the ward itself, had seen it all – joy, despair, resilience, fragility. Tonight, however, felt different. A prickling unease, like static electricity in her fingertips, hinted at something beyond the usual routine.  A shadow of premonition stretched across her brow as she approached Room 312, its door slightly ajar, emitting a chillingly silent void.
