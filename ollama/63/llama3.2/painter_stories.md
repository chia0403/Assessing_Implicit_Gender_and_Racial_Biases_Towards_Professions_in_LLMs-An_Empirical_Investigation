---

The sun had long since set over the sleepy town of Ashwood, casting a warm orange glow over the narrow streets and half-timbered houses that seemed to lean in as if sharing a secret. In a small studio nestled between a vintage bookstore and a bakery with a perpetually tantalizing display of freshly baked bread, Emilia Faye Wellesley stood before her latest canvas, her brushes poised like tentative fingers on the verge of creating something truly remarkable.

As a painter, Emilia had always been drawn to the soft, golden light that seemed to seep from the very pores of Ashwood's weathered buildings. She felt it in her bones, this quiet beauty that spoke to her on a deep, primal level – and she was determined to capture it on canvas, no matter what the cost.

The studio itself was a reflection of Emilia's artistic soul: cluttered with half-finished canvases, paint-splattered easels, and the faint scent of turpentine that clung to everything like a warm hug. As she stood there, lost in the swirling colors of her imagination, Emilia felt the familiar thrill of anticipation building within her – for it was on nights just like this that she felt most alive.

Little did she know, however, that the stillness of Ashwood's evening air would soon give way to a storm of secrets and revelations that would shake the very foundations of her life...
