As the last wisps of sunlight faded from the sky, casting a warm orange glow over the small town of Willow Creek, Cosette Adèle Laurent adjusted her bright pink glasses and took a deep breath. She was preparing for another night like any other at "Haute Coiffure," the trendy salon where she worked as a senior cosmetologist.

The scent of lavender and honey wafted through the air, mingling with the hum of conversation from the evening's clients as they sipped champagne and indulged in laughter and gossip. Cosette's gaze swept over the room, her eyes locking onto the familiar faces of regulars like Rachel, who was celebrating her 30th birthday, and Jack, whose receding hairline seemed to be growing wider by the month.

Despite the comfortable familiarity of it all, Cosette felt a restlessness simmering beneath her polished exterior. It had been six months since she'd last seen her estranged father, a renowned hairstylist who'd disappeared under mysterious circumstances when she was just a teenager. The wound still lingered, and tonight's quiet evening at the salon seemed like an odd time to start digging into old memories and secrets.

Just as Cosette began to focus on the task at hand – carefully shaping Rachel's bob with her trusty scissors – a knock at the door interrupted her thoughts. It was her best friend and colleague, Lily, who flashed a mischievous grin and whispered, "I have news that'll blow your mind."
