The fluorescent lights above the counter cast an unforgiving glare over the small convenience store, casting long shadows across the worn linoleum floor. It was 2:47 AM on a Tuesday morning, and the only sound was the soft hum of the refrigeration units in the back room, their gentle buzz a stark contrast to the stillness that had fallen over the deserted streets.

Ava Mae Thompson stood behind the counter, her eyes bloodshot from a late night spent studying for her early morning exam. She had always found solace in the quiet rhythm of the store's operations, but tonight she felt like she was just going through the motions. As she mechanically scanned each item, her mind wandered back to the argument with her boyfriend, Alex, the night before.

Ava had been working as a cashier at this small convenience store for three years, and while it wasn't her dream job, it provided enough money for her to pursue her true passion - photography. She spent most of her free time taking pictures of the city's abandoned buildings and overgrown lots, capturing the beauty in decay.

As she rang up the final customer of the night, a weary truck driver who had stopped to grab a coffee and a pack of gum, Ava felt a sense of relief wash over her. The long hours were starting to take their toll, but for now, at least, she could close out her shift and head home. Little did she know, however, that tonight was about to change everything.
