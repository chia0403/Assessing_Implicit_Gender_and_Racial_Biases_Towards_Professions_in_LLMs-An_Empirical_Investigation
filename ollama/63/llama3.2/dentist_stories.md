The streets of Ashwood were always at their most unforgiving at dawn. The darkness of night clung to the crumbling brick buildings like a damp shroud, and the only sound was the distant hum of the city's coffee machines and the occasional wail of a siren in the distance. Dr. Emily Anne Windsor stood outside her office, fumbling for her keys as she gazed out at the bleak morning scene. As a dentist, she was no stranger to early mornings, but it seemed like every day was starting on its own peculiar note.

Emily took a deep breath, letting the cool air fill her lungs, and began to make her way up the steps of her office building. The sign above the door read "Windsor Dental Care" in bold letters, and Emily felt a pang of nostalgia wash over her as she pushed open the door and stepped inside. She had grown up in this very neighborhood, and had returned after years away to take over her father's practice.

As she changed out of her street clothes and into the crisp whites of her dental uniform, Emily couldn't shake the feeling that something was off. It wasn't just the lingering sense of unease that lingered from the strange phone call she'd received the night before - the one with the muffled voice and the cryptic warning to "be careful". No, it was something more fundamental. Something about this place, and her own place in it.

And as she made her way down to the basement to start her first patient of the day, Emily couldn't help but wonder: what secrets lay hidden beneath the surface of Windsor Dental Care?
