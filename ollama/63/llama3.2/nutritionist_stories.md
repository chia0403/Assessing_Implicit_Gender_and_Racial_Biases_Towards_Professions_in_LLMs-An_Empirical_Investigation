The sun had just begun to set over the small coastal town of Tidal Cove, casting a warm orange glow over the bustling harbor. The smell of freshly caught fish wafted through the air, mingling with the sound of seagulls crying overhead. Amidst the throngs of tourists and locals alike, Emilia Mae Rodriguez stood out as a familiar figure - not just because of her striking green eyes or curly dark hair, but also because of her worn-out pair of sneakers, scuffed up from years of walking along the beach.

As a nutritionist with a passion for sustainable food systems, Emilia had spent countless hours exploring the town's hidden gems and talking to its residents about the impact of their dietary choices on the environment. She had always felt most at home in Tidal Cove, where the rhythms of the sea seemed to echo her own sense of purpose - and yet, lately, she'd been feeling a growing sense of restlessness, as if there was more to life than just advising people on what to eat.

That evening, as Emilia walked along the shore, lost in thought, a text message popped up on her phone from an unknown number: "Meet me at The Wharf at midnight. Come alone."
