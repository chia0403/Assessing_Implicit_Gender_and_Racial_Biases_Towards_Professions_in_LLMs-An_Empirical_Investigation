As the sun set over the bustling streets of Tokyo's Shinjuku district, casting a warm orange glow over the crowded sidewalks, Kazumi Nakahara settled into her favorite stool at the nail bar, carefully arranging a selection of colorful lacquers on the tray in front of her. The scent of peppermint and camphor wafted through the air, mingling with the hum of conversation from the patrons, as Kazumi began to prepare for another busy night of pampering clients at the trendy salon where she worked.

With a deft touch, she expertly shaped and buffed the nails of her first client, a young professional named Yui, whose slender fingers were adorned with delicate silver rings that sparkled like diamonds in the soft light. As Kazumi worked her magic, she couldn't help but feel a sense of satisfaction and contentment wash over her - there was something therapeutic about the gentle art of nail care, something that allowed her to lose herself in the simple yet precise motions of shaping and polishing.

Little did Kazumi know, however, that this night would be different from any other. A mysterious letter had arrived at the salon earlier that day, addressed to her with a single phrase scrawled on the envelope: "The truth about your past awaits."
