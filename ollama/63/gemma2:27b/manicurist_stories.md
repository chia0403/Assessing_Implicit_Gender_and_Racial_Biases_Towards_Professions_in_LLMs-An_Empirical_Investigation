The air hung heavy with the scent of acetone and lavender, a familiar comfort to Elara Delacroix. Sunlight streamed through the large window of "Polished," her tiny nail salon tucked away on a side street in New Orleans' bustling French Quarter. Jazz melodies drifted in from a nearby bar, mingling with the rhythmic click-clack of Elara's file against a client's nails. 

Elara was known for her meticulous work and her uncanny ability to decipher a person's story simply by studying their hands. Today, though, her mind wasn't on her client's chipped French manicure. It was preoccupied with the unsettling dream she'd had the night before - a vision of shadowy figures lurking in the dimly lit alley behind her salon.
