Rain lashed against the windowpanes of "Bright Smiles Dental," blurring the neon sign into an impressionistic smear of pink and yellow. Inside, Elara Kensington expertly maneuvered her instruments around Mr. Henderson's molars, the rhythmic whir of the polisher filling the small room. The scent of spearmint toothpaste mingled with a faint metallic tang, a familiar olfactory cocktail that Elara had come to associate with comfort and routine. Yet, today, something felt different. A tremor of unease ran through her as she noticed Mr. Henderson's eyes, usually twinkling behind his thick glasses, were clouded with an unfamiliar anxiety.

“Everything alright, Mr. Henderson?” Elara asked gently, pausing the polisher.
