Rain lashed against the ambulance windows, blurring the neon glow of the "Open 24 Hours" sign above the diner across the street. Amelia Rosalind Davies, her dark curls escaping their usual braid, gripped the steering wheel tighter, knuckles white against the worn leather. The siren wailed a mournful tune, mirroring the dread knotting in her stomach. A multi-vehicle pileup on Highway 10, multiple victims reported. Her partner, Marco, sat beside her, his face grim under the flickering blue and red lights.

"ETA two minutes," he said, his voice calm despite the storm raging around them. Amelia nodded, forcing herself to breathe deeply. Years of experience had taught her to compartmentalize, to shut off the fear and focus on the task at hand. But tonight, something felt different. A prickle of unease crawled up her spine, a premonition she couldn't shake. This wasn't just another accident. This was something else entirely.
