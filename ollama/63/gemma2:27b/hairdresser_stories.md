The scent of lavender and bergamot hung heavy in the air, mingling with the faint sizzle of straightening irons and the rhythmic snip-snip of scissors. Sunlight streamed through the dusty windows of "Curl Up & Dye," casting dancing squares on the worn linoleum floor. Beatrice "Bea" Clementine Dubois, her auburn hair piled high in a loose bun, expertly maneuvered a pair of clippers around the ear of Mr. Henderson, whose salt-and-pepper beard was rapidly disappearing under her deft touch. 

Outside, the rhythmic clang of the trolley echoed down Sycamore Street, punctuated by the excited yelps of children playing hopscotch on the sidewalk. But inside "Curl Up & Dye," time seemed to slow down, a sanctuary where worries were untangled as skillfully as Bea untangled knots in her clients' hair.
