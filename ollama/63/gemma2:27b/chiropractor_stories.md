The scent of lavender oil hung heavy in the air, battling with the acrid tang of disinfectant for supremacy. Dr. Eleanor Vance sighed, idly tracing a fingertip along the smooth curve of her rosewood table. Rain lashed against the windowpane, blurring the already muted colours of her garden into an impressionistic smear. A gust rattled the loose pane, sending shivers down her spine. It was days like this Eleanor wished she'd stayed in bustling London instead of retreating to the quiet solitude of the Welsh countryside. 

Her practice, nestled amidst rolling green hills and sheep-dotted pastures, seemed a world away from the noise and urgency of her former life. Yet, despite the tranquil surroundings, Eleanor couldn't shake the feeling of being adrift. The steady stream of patients she'd anticipated hadn't materialised, leaving her with an abundance of free time and a gnawing sense of unease.
