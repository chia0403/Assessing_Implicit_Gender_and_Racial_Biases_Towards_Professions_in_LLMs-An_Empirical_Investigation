The dimly lit rehearsal space was quiet, except for the soft hum of the sound system and the occasional strum of an instrument in the distance. In the center of the room, Aurora Wilder Jenkins stood alone on stage, her eyes fixed intently on the mirror hanging against one wall. Her long, dark hair cascaded down her back like a waterfall, and her slender fingers drummed a staccato beat on the armrest of her chair.

Aurora's voice was her everything – the passion, the fire, the raw emotion that poured out of every note she sang. She had spent years honing her craft, working tirelessly to perfect her sound and connect with audiences around the world. And yet, as she gazed into the mirror, she couldn't shake the feeling that something was missing.

The neon lights of the city outside cast a vibrant glow over the space, but it was nothing compared to the stage lights she had grown accustomed to in her sold-out shows. The spotlight's warm glow seemed distant now, and Aurora felt like an actress playing a role she no longer recognized.

She took a deep breath, letting the sound of the city outside wash over her, and began to sing – the first notes trembling on her lips, tentative at first, then growing stronger as the melody flowed from her heart. It was a song about longing and loss, about chasing dreams that seemed forever out of reach.

As she sang, the shadows in the room seemed to move with her, swirling around her like dark wings, drawing her closer to the edge. And when the final note faded away, Aurora felt something stir within her – a spark that threatened to ignite into a flame, and change everything.
