The sun had just begun to set over the small town of Willow Creek, casting a warm orange glow over the quaint streets and homes. The air was crisp and cool, carrying the scent of freshly cut grass and blooming flowers from the nearby park.

Dr. Alexandra "Lexi" Thompson, a school psychologist with a kind smile and a sharp mind, sat at her desk in the counseling office of Willow Creek Elementary School, sipping a cup of lukewarm coffee that had long since lost its flavor. It was 3:45 PM on a typical Friday afternoon, and Lexi was preparing for the final day of school.

As she sorted through the stack of files on her desk, her eyes scanned the notes from the week's counseling sessions, searching for any red flags or patterns that might indicate trouble ahead. The students at Willow Creek Elementary were a diverse bunch, but they all seemed to be struggling with something - whether it was bullying, social anxiety, or just plain old-fashioned homesickness.

Lexi had always loved her job as a school psychologist, but lately, she'd been feeling overwhelmed by the sheer number of cases and the weight of responsibility that came with being a trusted adult. She knew every student's story, from the bright-eyed six-year-old who struggled to make friends to the quiet, reserved seventh-grader who hid behind a mask of confidence.

As she finished sorting through her files, Lexi heard the soft murmur of voices coming from outside her office door - the sound of students gathering in the hallway before the final bell. She smiled wryly to herself, knowing that the weekend was almost here, and with it, a much-needed respite from the constant chaos of school life.

But as she turned back to her desk, her eyes landed on a small piece of paper tucked between two folders - a note scrawled in hasty handwriting, with only two words: "Don't trust him."
