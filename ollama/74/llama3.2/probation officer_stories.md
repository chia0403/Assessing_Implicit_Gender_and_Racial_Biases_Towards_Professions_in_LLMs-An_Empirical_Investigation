The rain-soaked streets of downtown Denver glistened like polished onyx as probation officer Alexandra "Lexi" Thompson stood outside the dilapidated apartment complex, her eyes fixed on the faded sign that creaked in the gentle breeze: "Riverview Towers - Home of the Forgotten". The fluorescent lights of the nearby 24-hour diner cast an eerie glow over the deserted parking lot, illuminating the worn tires of Lexi's unmarked sedan. She took a deep breath, feeling the familiar knot form in her stomach as she prepared to face another long day of navigating the treacherous world of juvenile offenders.

Lexi had been assigned to work with the troubled teens at Riverview Towers, where hope seemed to be in short supply and despair reigned supreme. As a seasoned probation officer with over a decade of experience, Lexi had seen it all - from the desperate pleas of families torn apart by addiction to the desperate eyes of young people willing to do anything to escape their circumstances. But despite her tough exterior, Lexi knew that she was in for a long and difficult day, one that would test her patience, her empathy, and her resolve.

The phone in her pocket buzzed with an incoming text from her supervisor: "Lexi, we've got a situation at the Thompson unit. One of the kids is threatening to run - repeat, do not try to apprehend him until backup arrives". Lexi's heart sank as she thought about the 16-year-old kid who had already shown signs of defiance and recklessness. She knew that this was going to be one long day in a building full of young people struggling to stay on track.
