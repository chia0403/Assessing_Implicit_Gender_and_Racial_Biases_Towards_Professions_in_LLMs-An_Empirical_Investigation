It was a drizzly autumn evening in the small coastal town of Ravenswood-by-the-Sea, where the mist rolled in off the ocean like a damp blanket, shrouding the narrow streets and weathered buildings in a damp, grey veil. Inside the local tavern, "The Drunken Mermaid," the patrons huddled around the bar, nursing their drinks as they listened to the tales of the salty old sailor who sat on his stool, spinning yarns of sea monsters and sunken treasure.

Behind the bar, Elijah J. Winters polished a mug with a white apron wrapped tightly around his waist, his eyes scanning the room as he waited for the next round of orders to come in. At 32 years old, Elijah had spent more time behind the bar than out of it, and he knew every trick, every tip, and every story that went on in this town like the back of his hand. He was the keeper of secrets, the listener of confessions, and the purveyor of tales as tall as the sea itself.

As the door swung open to admit a new patron, Elijah's gaze locked onto the stranger, a young woman with piercing green eyes and hair as black as the night sky. She scanned the room, her eyes lingering on Elijah for just a moment before moving on to the other patrons. There was something about her that didn't quite fit in, something that made Elijah's instincts twitch like a fish on the hook.

Without thinking, he poured himself a shot of whiskey and took a sip, his eyes never leaving the mysterious woman as she moved closer to the bar...
