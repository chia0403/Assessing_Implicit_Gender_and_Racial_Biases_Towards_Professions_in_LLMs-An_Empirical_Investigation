It was a drizzly Friday morning in late April, the kind of day that made you want to stay inside with a warm cup of coffee and a good book. But for Emily Joy Thompson, the receptionist at Smith & Co., it was just another day of pretending to be awake.

Emily sat behind her desk, staring blankly at the clock on the wall as she waited for the office to come to life. The fluorescent lights overhead hummed and flickered, casting an eerie glow over the rows of cubicles and dusty files that lined the walls. Outside, the rain pounded against the windows like a relentless drumbeat, making the air feel thick with moisture.

As she yawned, Emily's gaze wandered around the office, taking in the familiar sights and sounds. The coffee machine on her desk was already half-empty from last night's late-night gaming session. Her coworkers were starting to trickle in, their faces a blur of morning breath and yawning as they shuffled towards their desks.

Emily's own life felt like a perpetual state of suspended animation. She had graduated from college with a degree in communications, but after months of job searching, she'd landed the receptionist gig at Smith & Co. It was a steady paycheck, but it didn't exactly fulfill her dreams of becoming a writer or a journalist.

As she began to sort through the day's mail and schedule appointments for her boss, Emily couldn't shake the feeling that something was missing. She felt like there was a part of her waiting to be unleashed, like a wild bird trapped in a gilded cage.
