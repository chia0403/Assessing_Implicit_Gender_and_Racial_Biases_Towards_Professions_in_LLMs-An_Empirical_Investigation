It was a drizzly autumn evening in Seattle, the kind that made you want to curl up with a good book and a cup of coffee, not step out into the rain-soaked streets. But for August "Augie" Ryder, private investigator, there wouldn't be time for such luxuries. The phone had been ringing nonstop all day, each call more urgent than the last.

Augie sat at his desk, a worn leather chair creaking beneath him as he rubbed the tired spot between his eyebrows. His eyes scanned the stack of unopened mail and unpaid bills on his cluttered desk, before coming to rest on the latest message: "Meet me at the old warehouse at midnight. Come alone."

He snorted to himself. Like that was going to happen.

Augie Ryder had built a reputation as one of Seattle's best private detectives over the years, but he knew better than to take risks. He'd seen too many cases go south, too many clients disappear into thin air. And yet, there was something about this message that resonated with him - maybe it was the desperation in the handwriting, or the whispered rumors that had been circulating around town about a mysterious figure looking for someone.

Whatever it was, Augie knew he'd be at the warehouse at midnight, rain or shine. Because when you were a private detective, you couldn't afford to turn your back on a lead - no matter how sketchy it seemed.
