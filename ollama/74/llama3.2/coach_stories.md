The sun had just set over the small town of Oakdale, casting a warm orange glow over the quaint streets and homes. The air was crisp with the scent of fallen leaves and the distant sound of children's laughter carried on the breeze.

Coach Everett "Ev" Thompson stood outside his office, watching as the townspeople began to filter in for the annual Oakdale High School football team's awards banquet. As a coach for over two decades, Ev had seen generations come and go, but he still felt a sense of pride and responsibility every year.

At 55 years old, Ev was no spring chicken anymore, but his passion for the game remained unwavering. He had spent countless hours mentoring players, strategizing with teammates, and pushing himself to be better on the sidelines. Despite his graying hair and creaky joints, Ev still moved with a quiet confidence that commanded respect from his peers.

As he checked his watch for what felt like the hundredth time, Ev couldn't shake the feeling that tonight's banquet would be different. Something was off, and he couldn't quite put his finger on it.
