Rain hammered against the warped glass pane above Eloise Winthrop's desk, each drop exploding in a miniature firework of water droplets. The flickering gaslight cast long, skeletal shadows across the cluttered ledgers spread before her. Eloise sighed, pushing a stray lock of auburn hair behind her ear. The musty scent of aged paper and mildew hung heavy in the air of Winthrop & Sons Bookkeeping, a cramped storefront squeezed between a butcher shop and a dubious pawnbroker on Drury Lane.

Outside, London roared with its usual nocturnal chaos; carriage wheels splashing through muddy streets, drunken laughter spilling from tavern doors, the distant wail of a police whistle piercing the gloom. Yet, within the sanctuary of her ledgers, Eloise found solace in the crisp lines of figures and the comforting rhythm of addition.  Tonight, however, even the familiar comfort couldn't banish the gnawing unease that twisted in her gut.
