Rain lashed against the grimy windowpane of Eloise Beaumont's office, blurring the already-faint neon glow of the "Open 24 Hours" sign across the street. The air hung thick with the scent of stale coffee and desperation, a familiar aroma in this part of the city. Eloise, hunched over her desk like a gargoyle guarding its secrets, chewed on the end of a chipped pencil, her brow furrowed as she scrutinized the manuscript before her. 

It was 3:17 am, and the only other sound besides the relentless drumming of rain was the rhythmic click-clack of Eloise's keyboard.  She ran a hand through her tangled auburn hair, momentarily wishing she hadn't skipped her evening shower for the third day in a row. The story on her screen, a sprawling fantasy epic titled "The Obsidian Throne," had arrived just hours ago, thrust upon her by a frantic messenger who barely spoke English.  Eloise, known in the publishing world as the 'midnight miracle worker,' was renowned for her ability to salvage even the most mangled manuscripts. But something about this one felt different, like it held a darkness she couldn't quite grasp.
