The midday sun beat down on Savannah, Georgia's cobblestone streets, turning them into shimmering ribbons of heat. Inside "Savannah Smiles", Dr. Amelia Blackwood adjusted her loupes, squinting at the molar stubbornly refusing to surrender its cavity. A bead of sweat trickled down her temple, threatening to land on the delicate instruments laid out beside her.  Amelia sighed.

Being a dentist in Savannah's historic district was charming, no doubt. Tourists often gawked at the ornate Victorian facade of her office, mistaking it for a museum. But charm didn't fill cavities. Today, Amelia felt as if she were wrestling an alligator rather than extracting a tooth.
