The flickering fluorescent lights cast long, skeletal shadows across the empty gymnasium floor.  Dust motes danced in the stale air, illuminated by the anemic glow.  Edgar Bartholomew "Bart" Higgins sighed, the sound swallowed by the vast emptiness of the space. He hefted his mop bucket, its sloshing contents a discordant counterpoint to the rhythmic squeak of his sneakers against the polished hardwood.

The gymnasium at St. Sybil's Academy for Gifted Youngsters was Bart's domain after hours.  He knew every creak in the bleachers, every scuff mark on the court, every cobweb lurking in the rafters. He cleaned it all: the spilled Gatorade stains from basketball practice, the glitter bombs left behind by cheerleading routines, the crumpled sheet music abandoned by the school's fledgling orchestra. 

Tonight, though, something felt different. A strange energy thrummed beneath the surface of the silence, sending goosebumps prickling up Bart's arms. He paused mid-sweep, his mop resting against the center line like a sentinel. The air crackled with unseen electricity, and for a moment, he thought he heard a faint whisper carried on the breeze whistling through the open windows.

Bart shivered, attributing it to the cold draft.  But as he turned back to his work, a glint of gold caught his eye from beneath the stage. Curiosity piqued, Bart set down his bucket and cautiously approached the darkened space...
