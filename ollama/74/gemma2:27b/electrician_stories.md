The midday sun beat down on Silas Montgomery's neck as he wrestled with a tangle of frayed wiring in the attic of Mrs. Henderson's Victorian home. Dust motes danced in the single beam of light filtering through a grimy skylight, illuminating the cobwebs that clung to every corner like ghostly drapes. The air hung heavy with the scent of mildew and forgotten dreams, punctuated by the occasional squeak of settling wood. 

Silas wiped a bead of sweat from his brow, his calloused fingers carefully separating the brittle wires. He was known in town for his meticulous work and uncanny ability to coax life back into even the most dilapidated electrical systems. This house, however, seemed determined to test his patience. Every creak and groan sounded like a warning, every flickering shadow like a lurking presence.
