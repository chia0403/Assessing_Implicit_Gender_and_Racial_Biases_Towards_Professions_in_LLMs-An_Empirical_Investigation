The sun had just set over the small town of Willow Creek, casting a warm orange glow over the quaint streets and homes. Inside the worn brick walls of Oakwood High School, guidance counselor Emily Mae Reynolds was grading papers in her cluttered office, the soft hum of the fluorescent lights overhead providing a soothing background noise. The smell of stale coffee and worn books filled the air as she pored over the latest progress reports for her students.

As she scribbled notes on a particularly tricky transcript, Emily's eyes wandered to the framed photo on her desk - a faded picture of herself as a bright-eyed college student, beaming with pride alongside her high school sweetheart. It had been twenty years since graduation, and yet the ache in her heart still lingered whenever she looked at that photo.

Emily sighed, pushing the memories aside for now. She had more pressing concerns on her mind - like the upcoming school board meeting, where a contentious debate was brewing over the proposed budget cuts. As guidance counselor, Emily had always prided herself on being an advocate for her students' needs, but she couldn't shake the feeling that this latest development might put them in harm's way.

Just then, her phone rang, shrill and insistent. Emily picked up, expecting it to be one of her colleagues with a pressing question or concern. But instead, a low, gravelly voice spoke on the other end: "Emily Reynolds? I need to talk to you about something."
