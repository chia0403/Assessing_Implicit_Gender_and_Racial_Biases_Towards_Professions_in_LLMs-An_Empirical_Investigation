The neon signs of the city's bustling bars and clubs cast a gaudy glow over the rain-soaked streets of downtown New Haven, casting long shadows across the pavement. Inside the dimly lit bar, "McGillicuddy's Tavern," 32-year-old bartender Declan Patrick O'Connor polishes a mug with a dirty rag, his eyes scanning the room for the next round of thirsty patrons.

Declan had worked at McGillicuddy's for five years, and knew its rhythms better than he knew his own. He'd seen it all - from rowdy college parties to quiet nights spent sipping whiskey with the regulars. But on a night like this, with the storm raging outside and the city's nightlife huddled indoors, Declan couldn't shake the feeling that something was off.

As he wipes down the bar for what felt like the hundredth time, his gaze drifts towards the door, half-expecting to see a familiar face walk in. The door had swung open earlier that night, admitting a chill wind and a young woman with piercing green eyes and long, curly brown hair. She'd ordered a whiskey on the rocks and sat alone at the end of the bar, her gaze lost in some far-off thought.

Declan's curiosity got the better of him, and he found himself wondering what had brought this stranger to McGillicuddy's, and whether their paths would cross again soon.
