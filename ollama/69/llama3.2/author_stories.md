The mist-shrouded streets of Edinburgh were always at their most unforgiving in the dead of winter, but Emilia Grey was no stranger to the city's chill. As she stepped out of her small flat above the bookstore on George Street, the cold air enveloped her like a damp shroud, carrying the scent of salt and seaweed from the Firth of Forth.

Emilia wrapped her scarf tighter around her neck and gazed out at the grey buildings, their stone facades seeming to absorb what little light there was. She took a deep breath, feeling the familiar ache in her chest - a reminder that this city, which had once been her sanctuary, now felt like a prison.

As an author of dark fantasy novels, Emilia was no stranger to the darker corners of human nature, but she couldn't shake the feeling that she'd stumbled into something sinister. Her latest manuscript, a tale of shadowy creatures and ancient curses, had left her with more questions than answers, and she knew she needed time and solitude to untangle the threads.

Little did she know, that solitude was about to be disrupted in ways she never could have imagined.
