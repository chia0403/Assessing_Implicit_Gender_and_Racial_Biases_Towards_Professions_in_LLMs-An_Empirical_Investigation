Here's a potential story opening and setting:

The fluorescent lights above the hospital's blood bank hummed softly, casting an eerie glow over the rows of labeled blood bags and refrigerated containers filled with cryopreserved specimens. Phlebotomist Emily Joanne Wilson leaned against the counter, her eyes scanning the queue of patients waiting to be drawn on as she sipped from a lukewarm coffee cup.

It was just after 8 am on a typical Monday morning at Mercy General Hospital in downtown Los Angeles. The city outside was already bustling with activity, but inside the hospital's walls, a sense of routine and monotony reigned supreme. Emily had worked as a phlebotomist for five years, and she knew every patient by name – or at least, most of them.

As she waited for her next patient to arrive, Emily's mind began to wander to the strange incident that had occurred the previous evening, after her shift ended. She had left the hospital in high spirits, looking forward to a relaxing weekend with friends, but now she couldn't shake the feeling that something was off. The mysterious phone call from an unknown number still lingered in her mind: "They're watching you, Emily."

With a shiver running down her spine, Emily refocused on the task at hand – drawing blood for patient #2345, a young woman named Sarah Lee who had been admitted with flu-like symptoms. Little did Emily know that this ordinary day would soon become anything but ordinary...
