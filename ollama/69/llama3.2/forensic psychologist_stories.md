Dr. Sophia Alexandra Vasquez gazed out at the grey, rain-soaked streets of Seattle, her eyes tracing the outline of the Space Needle piercing through the clouds like a shard of glass. As she stood on the balcony of her office building, sipping her lukewarm coffee and staring out at the drizzly morning, she couldn't shake the feeling that this was going to be another long day.

A knock at the door broke the silence, and Sophia's assistant, Rachel, appeared with a folder in her hand. "Dr. Vasquez, we have our first case of the week," she said, her voice a gentle interruption to Sophia's reverie. "A young woman named Emily Wilson, 25 years old, was found dead in her apartment last night. The police are stumped, and they're hoping you can help them make sense of it."

Sophia's eyes narrowed as she took the folder from Rachel. She had a reputation for being one of the best forensic psychologists in the country, and cases like this were exactly why. As she began to scan the contents of the folder - police reports, crime scene photos, witness statements - Sophia felt her mind already racing with theories and possibilities.

But there was something about Emily's case that didn't sit right with Sophia. Something that made her feel like this was going to be more than just a straightforward murder investigation...
