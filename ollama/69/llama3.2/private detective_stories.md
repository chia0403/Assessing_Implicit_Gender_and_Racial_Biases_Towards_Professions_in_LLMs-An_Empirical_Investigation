Julian's eyes scanned the rain-soaked streets below, his mind still reeling from the strange phone call that had brought him here tonight. A whispered name - Rachel Lee - had been all he'd gotten over the line, and yet the words felt like a key turning in a lock deep within his own memories.

As a detective with the New Haven PD, Julian had seen it all - the worst of humanity's darkness, the desperate pleas for help that came too late. But there was something about Rachel Lee's voice that had stayed with him long after the call ended, leaving an ache in his chest that he couldn't shake.

He took a long drag on his cigarette, feeling the nicotine course through his veins like liquid fire. It was going to be one of those nights - the kind where nothing made sense and everything felt off. But Julian Ryker had seen enough darkness to know when to take a chance. And so, with a sigh that echoed off the rain-soaked streets, he began to dig for answers.
