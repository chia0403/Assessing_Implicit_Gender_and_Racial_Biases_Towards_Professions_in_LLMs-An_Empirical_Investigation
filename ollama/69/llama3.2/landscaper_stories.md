**The Small Town of Willow Creek**

The sun was just starting to peek over the rolling hills of Willow Creek, casting a warm orange glow over the quaint streets and homes. The air was crisp and carried the sweet scent of blooming lilacs and fresh-cut grass. It was going to be one of those days in this small town where everything felt just right.

In a small yard on Elm Street, landscaper extraordinaire, Jasper Wyatt Thompson III, was already up and about, sipping coffee and surveying his domain. His neatly trimmed beard and worn leather gloves gave him a rugged, outdoorsy look that belied the precision and care with which he coaxed beauty from even the most barren of landscapes.

At 32 years old, Jasper had spent his whole life working in the hardscrabble world of landscaping, taking pride in transforming lawns into oases. He had built a reputation as one of Willow Creek's go-to guys for everything from garden design to hedge trimming, and he took his work very seriously. Despite the occasional cranky homeowner or difficult client, Jasper loved nothing more than spending his days outside, surrounded by the sights and sounds of nature.

Little did Jasper know that today was going to be a day like no other...
