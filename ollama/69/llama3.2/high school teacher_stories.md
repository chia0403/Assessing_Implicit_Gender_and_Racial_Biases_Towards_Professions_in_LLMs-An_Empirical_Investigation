The sun had just set over the small town of Oakdale, casting a warm orange glow over the quaint streets and homes. The air was filled with the sweet scent of blooming lilacs and the distant hum of crickets, a sound that seemed to lull the world into a peaceful slumber.

Inside the worn wooden frame of Oakdale High School's staff room, Ms. Alexandra Rose Jenkins poured herself a steaming cup of coffee, her eyes scanning the room for any signs of life. The staff had dwindled over the summer, with most of the teachers leaving to take vacations or new jobs elsewhere. But Alex, like many of her colleagues, couldn't seem to shake off the feeling that this year was going to be different.

As she sipped her coffee, her gaze wandered out the window to the empty parking lot beyond. It was a sight that had become all too familiar over the past few years: rows of deserted cars, a handful of students lingering around the entrance, and the occasional stray cat strolling through the grass. The once-thriving hallways of Oakdale High now felt like a ghost town, a reminder that even in a small community like Oakdale, change was always just around the corner.

Alex rubbed her eyes, feeling a twinge of fatigue wash over her. She had spent countless hours preparing lesson plans and grading papers over the summer, but she couldn't shake off the sense that something was off. And as she gazed out at the empty parking lot, she couldn't help but wonder what this new school year would bring.
