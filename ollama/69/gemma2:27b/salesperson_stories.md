The flickering neon sign outside "Bernie's Bargain Basement" cast long, sickly shadows across the cracked asphalt parking lot. Rain lashed down in sheets, blurring the already faded paint on the rusty shopping carts lining the entrance. It was hardly ideal weather for selling discount toasters and chipped porcelain dolls, but Amelia Beatrice Cartwright, a woman who possessed the unwavering optimism of a sunflower seeking the sun, wasn't deterred.

Tucking a stray strand of auburn hair behind her ear, Amelia adjusted the brim of her faded red beret, determined to make the best of this dreary Tuesday afternoon. Her name tag, slightly askew and bearing the cheerful slogan "Deals so good, they'll make you sing!" bounced gently against her starched white blouse as she braced herself for another day of haggling and hustling.
