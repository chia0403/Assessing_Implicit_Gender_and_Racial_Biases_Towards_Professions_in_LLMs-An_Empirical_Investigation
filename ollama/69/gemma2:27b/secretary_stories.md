The incessant click-clack of Penelope Hawthorne's typewriter keys was the only sound breaking the silence of Mr. Harrington’s cavernous office. Outside, rain lashed against the leaded glass windows, blurring the cityscape into an impressionistic watercolour. The air hung heavy with the scent of old paper and pipe tobacco, a combination peculiar to the man she served. Penelope glanced at the ornate grandfather clock in the corner – 5:47 pm.  Mr. Harrington was late.

Penelope sighed, adjusting her spectacles and smoothing the crisp collar of her starched blouse. Five years she'd been his secretary, five years navigating his eccentricities and deciphering his scrawled memos. He was a brilliant inventor, a man ahead of his time, but also notoriously absent-minded. Tonight, Penelope had a feeling something was amiss. The air itself felt charged, crackling with unspoken tension.
