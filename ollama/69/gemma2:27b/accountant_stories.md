Rain hammered against the grimy windowpanes of Theodore Alistair Finch's cramped office, mimicking the relentless drumming in his chest. Stacks of invoices towered precariously on his desk, threatening to spill over into the already overflowing wastebasket. The air hung thick with the scent of stale coffee and desperation, a pungent reminder of the mountain of debt that loomed over him like a vengeful storm cloud.

Theodore, a man whose tidy demeanor belied the chaos brewing within, adjusted his spectacles and stared at the latest spreadsheet. Numbers danced before his weary eyes, mocking his futile attempts to reconcile them. He'd been an accountant for twenty years, a meticulous keeper of ledgers and master of balance sheets. Yet, somehow, he couldn't seem to balance his own life.
