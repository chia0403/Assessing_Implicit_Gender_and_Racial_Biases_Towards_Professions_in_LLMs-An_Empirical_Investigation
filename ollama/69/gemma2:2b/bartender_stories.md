Rain hammered against the grimy windows of "The Rusty Tap," blurring the neon glow of the city's skyline into an abstract mess of streaks and splashes. Inside, the air was thick with the scent of stale beer, forgotten dreams, and lingering regret. It wasn't exactly glamorous.  But to **Bartholomew Thorne**, owner and primary bartender at The Rusty Tap, it was home. Home with its worn leather booths, chipped copper bar, and a cast of regulars whose lives were as tangled and faded as the peeling paint on the walls.

The storm outside mirrored the turmoil brewing within Bartholomew's soul. His eyes, usually sharp and witty behind his thick-rimmed glasses, were dull and heavy. A familiar ache throbbed in his chest as he watched a woman sit alone at the bar, her shoulders slumped like wilted flowers.  His usual jovial self had taken a backseat to the somber atmosphere that seemed to cling to him like a second skin.
