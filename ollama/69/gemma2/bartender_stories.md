The neon sign outside flickered erratically, casting lurid stripes across the rain-slicked asphalt. Inside, Amelia Rose "Amy" Thornton wiped down the already spotless mahogany bar, the scent of stale beer and whiskey lingering in the air like a ghost. The Dive Bar was quieter than usual, even for a Tuesday night. A few regulars sat hunched over their drinks, lost in thought or perhaps just trying to ignore the storm raging outside. Amy poured another glass of amber liquid for Old Man Henderson, his arthritic fingers clutching it with surprising strength as he grumbled about the state of the world. 

Amy sighed, leaning against the bar. The Dive Bar wasn't much, but it was her haven. A place where she could lose herself in the clinking glasses and hushed conversations, a temporary escape from the life that felt increasingly like an unsolved puzzle.


The door creaked open, letting in a gust of wind and rain, and Amy turned to greet the newcomer.
