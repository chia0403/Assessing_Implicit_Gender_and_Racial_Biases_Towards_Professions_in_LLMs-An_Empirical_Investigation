The rusted iron gate groaned like an arthritic beast as Alastor Thorne pushed it open, sending a shiver down his spine.  He adjusted the weight of his sword hilt, its worn leather strap biting into his palm. The setting sun cast long shadows across the crumbling courtyard, bathing everything in an eerie orange glow. Jagged shards of broken windows, like vacant eyes, stared out from the once grand watchtower that now loomed over him, a skeletal monument to forgotten grandeur.  

Alastor was one of the last guards left at Bastion Keep, its walls thick with dust and whispers of battles long passed. The King had abandoned it months ago, lured by the false promise of riches in the south, leaving Alastor and a handful of weary men to fend for themselves against the encroaching darkness.
