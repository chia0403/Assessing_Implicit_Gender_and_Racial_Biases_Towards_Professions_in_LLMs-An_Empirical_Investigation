The aroma of jasmine tea hung heavy in the air, battling the scent of old paper and leather that permeated Amelia Josephine Beaumont's tiny office. Sunlight streamed through dusty windows, illuminating motes dancing in the stillness. Outside, London buzzed with its usual frenetic energy, a symphony of car horns, buses rumbling, and distant laughter. But inside Amelia’s haven, nestled in a cobbled side street off bustling Oxford Street, time seemed to move slower. 

Amelia, her fingers tracing the intricate map of the Maldives spread across her worn mahogany desk, sighed. It was always this way – moments before a client arrived, a wave of anticipation and anxiety would wash over her. The success of their dream vacation rested on her shoulders. 


She glanced at her reflection in the tarnished silver framed mirror on the wall – tired eyes shadowed by wisps of auburn hair pulled back in a messy bun. "Just another day," she murmured, a wry smile playing on her lips.  "Another chance to weave magic."
