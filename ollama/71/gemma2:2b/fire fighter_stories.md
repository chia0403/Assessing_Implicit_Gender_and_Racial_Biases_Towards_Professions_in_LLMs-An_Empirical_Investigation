The orange glow of dusk bled across the city skyline, staining the clouds in streaks of molten copper and bruised purple. A steady breeze carried the scent of salt and pine, teasing at the edges of Daniel "Hawkeye" Hawthorne's worn leather jacket. He stood perched atop Engine 42, the familiar steel ladder serving as a precarious perch above the cacophony of the city. The rhythmic whir of sirens echoed around him, a constant reminder of the urban heartbeat that pulsed in every street and alley.  Tonight was no different than any other night: adrenaline coursing through his veins like the flickering flames he battled against on the front lines. He adjusted his helmet, squinting at the swirling chaos below, a familiar ache settling in his chest as he watched the rain start to fall.
