Rain lashed against the greasy diner window, blurring the neon "Open 24 Hours" sign into a shimmering smear. Inside, Elias Bartholomew Finchley III, or Eli as everyone called him, meticulously polished his silver flask with a napkin pilfered from a nearby booth. The clinking of silverware and the sizzle of bacon on the griddle provided a meager soundtrack to the symphony of despair playing in his head.

Eli wasn't cut out for this. Selling industrial-grade cleaning solutions in rural Nebraska was about as exciting as watching paint dry, and frankly, it paid about the same. He longed for the fast-paced world he'd glimpsed during his brief stint selling luxury cars in Miami. But that life had been a mirage, evaporating under the harsh desert sun of reality.

Now, stuck in this rain-soaked purgatory with a trunk full of bleach and degreaser, Eli contemplated his dwindling options. The diner door swung open, letting in a gust of wind and a figure silhouetted against the grey sky. A woman, her face partially obscured by a wide-brimmed hat, walked purposefully towards him, her boots leaving puddles on the worn linoleum floor.  Something about her stance, her determined gait, sparked a flicker of hope in Eli's weary soul.
