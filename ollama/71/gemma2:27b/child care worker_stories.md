Rain lashed against the windows of the Busy Bees Daycare Center, blurring the cheerful pastel murals painted on the walls into streaks of color. The air inside smelled faintly of disinfectant and overcooked macaroni, a familiar scent that usually comforted Amelia Rose Cartwright. Today, however, it did little to soothe the knot of anxiety tightening in her stomach.

Amelia perched on a miniature chair, carefully weaving a paper crown for three-year-old Leo, who was engrossed in building a precarious tower out of wooden blocks. Despite the storm raging outside, Leo seemed oblivious, humming happily as he placed each block with meticulous care. Amelia envied his uncomplicated joy. Her own mind was a whirlwind of worry, replaying the phone call she'd received earlier that morning.
