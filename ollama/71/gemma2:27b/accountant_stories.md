Rain lashed against the grimy windows of Alistair Finchley’s cramped office, mirroring the storm brewing inside him. Papers, meticulously sorted by date and invoice number just moments ago, now lay scattered across his desk like fallen leaves in a gale. The air hung heavy with the scent of stale coffee and desperation.

Alistair, a man whose neatly combed grey hair and perpetually pressed suit belied an inner turmoil, stared at the glowing screen of his ancient computer. Numbers swam before his eyes – figures that refused to reconcile, deadlines looming like guillotines. He was drowning in a sea of red ink, his normally meticulous accounting unraveling thread by thread. The small accounting firm he'd poured his life into, Finchley & Sons, was teetering on the brink of collapse.
