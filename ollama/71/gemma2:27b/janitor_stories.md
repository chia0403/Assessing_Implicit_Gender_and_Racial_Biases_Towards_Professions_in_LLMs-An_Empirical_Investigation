The flickering fluorescent lights buzzed overhead, casting a sickly yellow pallor over the deserted high school hallways. Theodore "Teddy" Billingsworth pushed his ancient janitorial cart down the corridor, its squeaky wheels a lonely counterpoint to the silence. The air was thick with the scent of stale sweat and disinfectant, a familiar perfume in Teddy's world.  He stopped at the end of the hall, leaning against his mop handle, gazing out the grime-coated window at the skeletal silhouette of the football field bathed in the dying light of dusk.

Tonight felt different. A prickle of unease crawled up Teddy's spine. It wasn't just the unsettling quiet that hung heavy in the air; it was a sense of something...watching him. He shook his head, dismissing the notion as the ramblings of an old man. Still, he couldn't shake the feeling that he wasn't alone.  

Suddenly, a muffled thud echoed from the depths of the building, followed by a whispered, "Help me."
