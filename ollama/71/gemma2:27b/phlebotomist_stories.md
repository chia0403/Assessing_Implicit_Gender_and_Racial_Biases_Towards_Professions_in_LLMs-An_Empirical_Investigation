Rain lashed against the windows of Mercy General, blurring the city lights into an impressionistic smear. Inside, the fluorescent hum of the lab was a stark contrast to the storm raging outside. Emilia Flores, her dark hair pulled back in a practical ponytail, expertly navigated the maze of centrifuges and vials, a familiar calm settling over her despite the frantic energy buzzing around her. Tonight was busier than usual; a car accident had brought in multiple victims, each needing bloodwork. 

Emilia thrived on the controlled chaos, finding solace in the precision of drawing blood, the delicate dance of needle and vein. Each successful draw felt like a small victory, a contribution to the larger tapestry of healing that unfolded within the hospital walls.  But tonight, something felt different. As she approached a gurney draped with a sheet, a strange premonition prickled her skin.
