Here's a potential story opening and setting:

The sun was setting over the vast expanse of Route 66, casting a golden glow over the deserted stretch of asphalt. Jack "Hawk" Hawkins, a ruggedly handsome highway worker in his mid-30s with a strong jawline and piercing blue eyes, trudged through the dusty underbrush, his boots kicking up small clouds of dirt as he made his way to the abandoned roadside diner that had become his temporary home for the night.

As a member of the Arizona Department of Transportation's maintenance crew, Hawk was no stranger to the harsh conditions of the desert landscape. But there was something about this particular stretch of highway - where the pavement gave way to scrubby mesquite trees and the only sounds were the wind rustling through the leaves and the occasional passing truck - that made him feel small and insignificant.

Hawk had been working on Route 66 for three years, ever since he'd lost his job at a construction site in Phoenix due to budget cuts. It was a hard life, but it was one he knew well, and he took pride in keeping the highway safe for travelers from all over the world. Tonight, though, Hawk felt restless - he'd received a mysterious package earlier that day with no return address, containing only a single word: "Run."

And now, as the sun dipped below the horizon, Hawk was starting to wonder if he should heed its message...
