The sun was setting over the ancient city of Petra, casting a warm orange glow over the narrow canyon known as the Siq. The air was thick with the scent of frankincense and myrrh, carried on the whispers of the wind through the narrow passageways.

In a small workshop nestled between two towering sandstone cliffs, Aziz ibn al-Hassan carefully chiseled away at a block of gleaming white limestone. His hands moved with precision and care, the blade gliding effortlessly across the stone as he worked to shape it into its final form: a magnificent column, adorned with intricate patterns of Arabesque script and fleur-de-lis.

As the last rays of sunlight faded from view, Aziz stepped back to survey his work, a sense of pride and satisfaction welling up within him. It was a small but beautiful creation, one that would soon find its way into the heart of the city's great temple, where it would stand as a testament to the skill and craftsmanship of the stone cutters who had shaped it.

But as Aziz gazed out at the breathtaking view before him, he couldn't shake the feeling that something was off. The usual sounds of the city - the chatter of merchants, the clanging of hammers, the calls of the vendors - were muted tonight, and a sense of unease hung in the air like a palpable mist.

Aziz's instincts told him to stay inside, to lock his door and wait for morning. But something about that silence seemed... wrong.
