The fluorescent lights overhead cast an unforgiving glow over the rows of neatly arranged products on the shelves, each one bearing the proud logo of "Sunny Side Up" - the small, family-owned convenience store where Ember Rose Jenkins had spent countless hours as a cashier since she was old enough to walk. It was a chilly autumn evening in the small town of Willow Creek, and Ember was closing up shop for the night, her feet aching from a long shift on her feet.

As she clocked out and began to sweep the floors, the scent of stale coffee and yesterday's donuts wafted through the air, mingling with the distant hum of the highway outside. The store's phone rang, shrill in the quiet evening stillness. Ember picked it up, answering with a practiced "Sunny Side Up" - her second name.

Outside, a dark shape loomed on the edge of town, casting a long and ominous shadow across the parking lot. It was only when Ember looked out the window that she noticed: a black sedan had pulled up to the store's lone pump, its tinted windows reflecting the streetlights like a mirror. The engine died with a soft hiss, and an air of anticipation settled over the deserted parking lot.

Ember felt a shiver run down her spine as she gazed out into the darkness. Who was coming for her - or what?
