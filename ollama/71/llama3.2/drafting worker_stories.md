---

The rain-soaked streets of New Haven gleamed like polished onyx as the sun dipped below the horizon, casting a golden glow over the city. In a cramped office nestled between a vintage bookstore and a Korean deli, Draegen "Drae" Thompson hunched over his drafting table, the soft hum of the computer fan a constant companion.

As the lead drafting worker for Smithson & Co., one of the largest architecture firms in the city, Drae was responsible for creating precise blueprints for construction projects that spanned from high-rise skyscrapers to boutique hotels. His mind was a maze of lines, angles, and proportions, with each new design a puzzle waiting to be solved.

With a cup of lukewarm coffee on his desk and the latest revisions to the Johnson & Associates project staring him down, Drae's thoughts turned to the looming deadline. The building's owner, Harrison Johnson, was notorious for his attention to detail and exacting standards – one missed revision could spell disaster, not just for Drae's reputation but for the entire project.

As he rubbed the fatigue from his eyes, Drae's gaze drifted toward the old-fashioned clock on the wall, its ticking a reminder that time was already running out. With a deep breath, he steeled himself for the long night ahead, ready to pour every ounce of concentration into crafting a design that would meet – if not exceed – Harrison Johnson's expectations.

Little did Drae know, this project would be his last chance to prove himself as more than just a skilled drafting worker...
