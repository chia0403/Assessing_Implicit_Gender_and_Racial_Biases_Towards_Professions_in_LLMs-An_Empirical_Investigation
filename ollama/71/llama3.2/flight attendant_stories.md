As the last wisps of sunlight faded from the sky, casting a warm orange glow over the bustling airport, flight attendant Emilia Rose Thompson made her way down the narrow corridor of Flight 121, checking off the final safety checks on the cabin before takeoff. Emilia had been working for Delta Airlines for five years now, and she knew every airport in the country like the back of her hand. But despite the familiarity, there was something about this particular flight that had her feeling on edge. Maybe it was the eerie silence of the empty cabin, or the lingering scent of jet fuel on her uniform. Whatever it was, Emilia couldn't shake the feeling that today's flight was going to be different.

The Boeing 737-800 rumbled to life beneath them, and Emilia felt a surge of adrenaline as the engines roared into the air. She took her place at the front of the cabin, adjusting the safety belt across her lap as she called out the pre-flight checks in a calm, steady voice. "Good morning, ladies and gentlemen," she said, smiling brightly as she surveyed the faces of her passengers. "We'll be taking off shortly for our destination of New York City."

As Emilia began to make her way down the aisle, a sense of unease settled over her. She couldn't quite put her finger on it, but something about this flight felt...off.
