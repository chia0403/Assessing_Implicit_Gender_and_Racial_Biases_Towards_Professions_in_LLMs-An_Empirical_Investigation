Here's a potential story opening:

The rain-soaked streets of New Haven gleamed like polished onyx as Elmer Fletcher III, affectionately known to his colleagues as "Fletch" among the hotel staff, trudged through the lobby of the historic Bellwether Hotel. His bell hop uniform was soaked and stained from a long night of collecting luggage for the evening's arrivals, but Fletch didn't let it faze him. He had been on his feet since 4 am, and he knew every nook and cranny of the hotel like the back of his hand.

As he expertly juggled a pair of suitcases and a dripping umbrella, Fletch's gaze drifted up to the ornate chandelier above the reception desk, its crystals refracting the dim light into tiny rainbows that seemed to dance in the air. It was moments like these that reminded him why he loved his job - not just for the tips or the thrill of the night shift, but for the stories the hotel held within its walls.

Fletch's thoughts were interrupted by the soft voice of Mrs. Patel, the hotel manager, who hovered beside him with a discreet smile and a hand on her hip. "Elmer, dear, I think it's time we got you cleaned up before your next shift," she said, her eyes sparkling with warmth behind her wire-rimmed glasses.

But Fletch knew that wasn't going to happen anytime soon. He had other plans - plans that involved sneaking into the hotel's storage room after hours and uncovering secrets hidden in plain sight...
