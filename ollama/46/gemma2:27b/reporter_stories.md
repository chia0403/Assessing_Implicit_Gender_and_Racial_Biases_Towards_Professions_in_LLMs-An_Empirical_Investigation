The neon glow of the "Golden Dragon" sign flickered ominously, casting long shadows across the rain-slicked alleyway. Esmeralda "Esme" Reyes tugged her threadbare trench coat tighter around her, cursing the sudden downpour that had turned the already grimy streets into a slick nightmare. Tonight was supposed to be a simple follow-up on the string of petty thefts plaguing Chinatown, not a foray into what felt like a scene from a noir film.  

The air hung heavy with the scent of fried dumplings and something else, acrid and unfamiliar, that pricked at Esme's nostrils. She hesitated before pushing open the door to the dimly lit noodle shop, her heart pounding against her ribs like a trapped bird. The clatter of chopsticks and murmured conversations washed over her, momentarily drowning out the unease gnawing at her gut.
