The midday sun beat down on Elias "Eli" Thorne's neck like a blacksmith's hammer, turning his skin a familiar shade of lobster red. Dust devils danced across the parched earth of the construction site, swirling rusty grit and scraps of lumber into miniature tornadoes. Eli wiped the sweat from his brow with the back of his calloused hand, squinting at the skeletal framework of the skyscraper rising before him. Steel girders groaned under the weight of concrete slabs yet to be poured, and the rhythmic clang of hammers echoed like a metallic heartbeat across the deserted site. It was Sunday, a day usually reserved for catching up on sleep and fixing leaky faucets with his old man, but Eli had agreed to overtime. Money was tight these days, especially with his little sister heading off to college in the fall. 


Eli hefted another heavy steel beam onto his shoulder, his muscles protesting with a dull ache. He glanced towards the horizon, where the sun began its slow descent, painting the sky in hues of orange and purple. It wouldn't be long before they called it a day. Just one more lift, Eli thought to himself, gritting his teeth and bracing his back. As he hoisted the beam into position, a strange glint from the dusty ground caught his eye. Something metallic, half-buried beneath a pile of discarded bricks...
