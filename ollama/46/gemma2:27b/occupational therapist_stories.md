Rain lashed against the windows of Willow Creek Rehabilitation Center, blurring the autumn leaves into an impressionist painting of rust and gold. Inside, the air smelled faintly of antiseptic and something sweeter, like overripe oranges - Mrs. Henderson's favorite hand lotion. Eloise Amelia Cartwright, OT, surveyed her workstation with a sigh. 

Eloise preferred sunshine and crisp mornings, but she knew rain meant patients were more likely to stay indoors, giving her ample time for one-on-one sessions. Today, she was particularly looking forward to working with Mr. Ramirez, a stroke survivor struggling to regain fine motor skills in his right hand.
