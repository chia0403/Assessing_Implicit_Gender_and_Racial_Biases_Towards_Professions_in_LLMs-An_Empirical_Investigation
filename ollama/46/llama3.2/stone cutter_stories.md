Here's a possible story opening and setting:

The sun was setting over the worn cobblestone streets of Raggedhaven, casting a warm orange glow over the small village nestled between two great hills. The air was alive with the smell of freshly cut stone, carried on the breeze from the quarries that dotted the hillsides like scars.

In a cramped workshop tucked away in a corner of the village square, Aurélien Dumont carefully measured out a piece of limestone for his latest commission. A skilled stone cutter, known throughout Raggedhaven for his exquisite craftsmanship and attention to detail, he worked with precision and patience, coaxing the raw material into shape by hand.

Aurélien's hands moved deftly, his fingers nimble from years of work on the stone. His eyes, a deep shade of brown, watched intently as the marble dust swirled around him, collecting in the small pile at his feet. He was lost in the rhythm of his work, his mind focused solely on creating something beautiful and lasting, like the stones he worked with every day.

As the light outside began to fade, casting long shadows across the workshop floor, Aurélien's thoughts turned to the mysterious commission that had brought him to this moment: a grand monument for the village elder's funeral. It was said to be a once-in-a-lifetime opportunity, but Aurélien couldn't shake the feeling that something wasn't quite right about it...
