Here's a potential story opening and setting:

The sun had just begun to set over the vast expanse of Route 66, casting a warm orange glow over the worn asphalt as Jack "Hawk" Hawkins steered his trusty Peterbilt 359 semi-truck down the highway. Hawk had been driving for hours, the rhythmic hum of the engine and the soft crackle of the CB radio lulling him into a state of relaxed focus.

Born on a crisp autumn morning in 1982, Jack Alexander "Hawk" Hawkins was a fourth-generation trucker who had grown up with the open road in his blood. His father had been a truck driver too, as had his grandfather before him, and Hawk had always felt a sense of restlessness when confined to a single location for too long.

As he navigated the curves of the highway, Hawk's mind began to wander to the upcoming stop at "Mae's Diner" in rural Oklahoma - a favorite haunt of his that served up some of the best chicken fried steak and country music in the region. The CB crackled with static as Hawk's dispatcher's voice chimed in on the frequency.

"Hawk, we've got a priority shipment headed to Dallas, Texas - pickup at 500 North Main and drop off at the FedEx terminal by midnight. You're on for this one."

Hawk nodded to himself, even though he knew his dispatcher couldn't see him. He took a deep breath of the highway's mix of diesel fumes and exhaust, feeling the familiar thrum of adrenaline as he began to plot out the best route to take. Little did he know, this trip was about to become a lot more complicated than just delivering packages...
