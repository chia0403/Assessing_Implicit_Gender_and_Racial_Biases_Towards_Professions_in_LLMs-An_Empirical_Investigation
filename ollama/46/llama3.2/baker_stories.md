The sun had barely risen over the sleepy town of Willow Creek, casting a warm orange glow over the quaint streets and homes. In a small bakery on Main Street, Ember Rose Greenwood was already elbow-deep in flour, her hands moving with practiced ease as she mixed together the day's first batch of bread.

Ember, or Em to those who knew her best, had been baking since she was old enough to hold a wooden spoon. Her family's bakery, "The Cozy Crumb," had been a staple of Willow Creek for generations, and Ember was determined to carry on the tradition. She took pride in every loaf that left her oven, from the soft, fluffy white bread to the rich, dark whole wheat.

As she worked, the scent of sweet pastries and freshly ground coffee wafted through the air, enticing the sleepy townspeople to come and sample Em's latest creations. The bakery was a hub of activity on any given morning, with locals gathering at the counter to chat with Em and her mother, who owned the business alongside Ember.

But today was different. Today, Em could feel it in her bones - something was off. A sense of restlessness had been building inside her for weeks, and she couldn't shake the feeling that this was a day when everything would change forever.
