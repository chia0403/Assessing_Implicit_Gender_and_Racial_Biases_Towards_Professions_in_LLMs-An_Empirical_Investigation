Rain hammered against the windows of Dr. Eleanor Vance’s office, mimicking the relentless drumming in her chest.  She tapped her pen against the worn surface of her desk, watching the swirling patterns in her tea as it cooled. Outside, the last vestiges of daylight were swallowed by the encroaching storm, casting long shadows across the manicured lawns of Willow Creek High School. 

The school building itself loomed behind her, a hulking brick structure that seemed to sigh with the weight of its secrets. It was a place where anxieties ran rampant – teenage angst simmered alongside academic pressures and unspoken fears. Eleanor knew this intimately, having spent the last five years navigating the labyrinthine corridors of young minds within its walls. Tonight, however, a new kind of unease clung to the air. A case file sat open on her desk, its contents whispering promises of darkness she wasn't sure she was prepared to face.  


The name at the top of the file – Jeremy Thorne – sent a shiver down her spine.
