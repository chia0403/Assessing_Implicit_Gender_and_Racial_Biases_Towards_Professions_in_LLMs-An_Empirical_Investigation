The air hung thick with the scent of burnt oil and gasoline, a perfume only an old garage like Tony "Grease Monkey" Galati's could cultivate.  Rain lashed against the corrugated tin roof, mimicking the staccato rhythm of hammering metal inside.  Tony, a mountain of a man with hands that looked more like shovels than tools, wrestled with a rusty engine block, his brow furrowed in concentration. Grease smeared his face like camouflage, highlighting the faint scars crisscrossing his forearms - souvenirs from years spent wrestling with temperamental machines. He was deep in the belly of the beast, trying to coax life back into a battered Ford pickup truck that had coughed its last breath outside just hours ago. The storm raged on, mirroring Tony's own internal battle: would he be able to fix this thing before his only customer for the day abandoned him?
