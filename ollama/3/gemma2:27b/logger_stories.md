The chainsaw's shriek tore through the dawn mist, its serrated teeth biting deep into the ancient redwood's flesh. Elias Blackwood, his face grimy with sawdust and sweat, wrestled the behemoth down inch by agonizing inch. The air hung thick with the scent of pine needles and damp earth, a perfume Elias had inhaled since he was a boy tagging along with his father in these very woods.

The towering trees of Northern California's Emerald Forest cast long shadows as the sun struggled to pierce the dense canopy. Elias paused, wiping his brow with the back of his calloused hand. He squinted up at the massive redwood, its crown lost in the swirling mist above. This one was special, older than any other he'd felled. It felt like a living monument, whispering secrets from centuries past.
