Rain lashed against the windows of St. Jude's Emergency Room, mimicking the frantic drumming in Amelia Rose Callahan's chest. The air thrummed with a chaotic energy – the sharp beep of monitors, the pained moans of patients, the clipped commands barked by doctors. 

Amelia, her auburn braid escaping its confines and falling over one shoulder, weaved through the controlled pandemonium. Her scrubs were already spattered with crimson, a grim reminder of the relentless tide of trauma that flowed through these doors every night. Tonight felt different though, heavier somehow.  A prickling unease danced along her spine, urging her to brace herself for something…unusual.
