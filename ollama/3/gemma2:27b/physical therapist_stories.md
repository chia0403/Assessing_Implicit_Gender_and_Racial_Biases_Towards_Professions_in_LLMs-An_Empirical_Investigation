The salty air whipped Amelia Rose Hawthorne's auburn braid across her face as she surveyed the crumbling Victorian mansion perched precariously on the cliff edge. Seagulls shrieked overhead, their cries echoing the unease churning in Amelia's stomach. This wasn't exactly the quaint seaside rehabilitation center she'd envisioned when accepting the position.  

The dilapidated building, with its peeling paint and boarded-up windows, looked more like a haunted house than a place to heal. Yet, there was a certain charm to its faded grandeur, a whisper of forgotten stories clinging to its weathered walls. Amelia took a deep breath, steeling herself for the unknown. She'd come here to help people regain their mobility, their independence.  Even if that meant facing her own fears in the process.
