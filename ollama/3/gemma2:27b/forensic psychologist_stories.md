Rain lashed against the grimy windows of the interrogation room, blurring the city lights into an impressionistic smear. Dr. Eleanor Vance adjusted her spectacles, the reflection of the flickering fluorescent light momentarily obscuring her sharp hazel eyes. Across the table sat David Thorne, accused of arson and a string of unsettling petty thefts in his quiet suburban neighbourhood. His thin frame seemed to shrink further into the unforgiving metal chair, his gaze fixed on a spot somewhere beyond Eleanor's shoulder.

The air hung heavy with tension, the sterile scent of disinfectant battling with the faint metallic tang that always lingered after a crime scene. Eleanor took a measured breath, years of experience grounding her as she prepared to navigate the labyrinthine mind of a man suspected of both calculated destruction and baffling randomness.
