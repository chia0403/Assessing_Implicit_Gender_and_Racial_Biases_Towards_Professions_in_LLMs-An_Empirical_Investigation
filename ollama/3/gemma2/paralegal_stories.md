The flickering fluorescent lights above hummed an unsettling tune as Emilia Beatrice Thorne meticulously sorted through the mountainous stack of case files on her desk. Her tiny office, wedged between a dusty bookshop and a bustling laundromat in downtown Phoenix, felt more like a purgatory for paper than a haven for justice. Sweat trickled down her temples, mingling with the faint scent of stale coffee and desperation that clung to everything within this four-walled cubicle. Outside, the Arizona sun blazed mercilessly, turning the asphalt into a shimmering mirage.  Inside, Emilia battled a different kind of heat - the stifling tension of another looming deadline, fueled by a demanding attorney and a case with more twists and turns than a desert rattlesnake.
