The sun had long since set over the small, seaside town of Ravenswood-by-Sea, casting a warm orange glow over the narrow streets and weathered buildings that lined its harbor. The air was crisp and cool, carrying the scent of saltwater and seaweed as it whispered through the alleyways.

In a small studio above a used bookstore on Main Street, Emilia Faye Winters stirred in her canvas-covered easel, stretching her arms and arching her back in a languid yawn. She rubbed the sleep from her eyes, revealing a tangle of dark brown hair that fell in loose waves down her back. As she swung her legs over the side of the stool, her gaze landed on the small, unfinished canvas before her – a blank slate waiting to be splashed with color and life.

Emilia Faye was a painter, known throughout Ravenswood-by-Sea for her hauntingly beautiful landscapes that seemed to capture the very essence of the sea itself. She spent most of her days holed up in this studio, surrounded by the echoes of her own dreams and the whispers of the town's secrets. And tonight, as she gazed out at the darkening sea, she felt a shiver run down her spine – for she knew that something was about to stir, something that would change everything.
