The mist-shrouded streets of Ashwood City were always at their most unforgiving in the dead of night. It was as if the fog had a life of its own, seeping into every crevice and alleyway to shroud everything in a damp, grey veil.

In this eerie environment, Constable Emilia Faye Wellington stood watch atop the stone walls that guarded the city's central keep. Her piercing blue eyes scanned the darkness below, her hand resting lightly on the hilt of her service revolver. The lanterns that lined the battlements cast flickering shadows on the ground, making it seem as though the very spirits of Ashwood City were stirring in the darkness.

As a seasoned guard, Emilia had seen it all - from rowdy tavern brawls to midnight visits from local thieves and smugglers. But tonight felt different. Tonight, she sensed a restlessness in the air that went beyond mere mischief or mayhem.

It was as though something was waiting just beyond the edge of perception, watching and waiting for its moment to strike. And Emilia Faye Wellington was about to become the focal point of that moment - whether she liked it or not.
