The fog rolled in off the pier, shrouding the small coastal town of Ravenswood in a damp, grey mist. The streets were empty, save for the sound of the wind whipping through the narrow alleys and the occasional creak of old wooden signs swaying in its grasp. In a small butcher's shop on Main Street, Emilia "Mia" Blackwood was preparing for another long day of working behind the counter.

As she expertly trimmed a piece of brisket, her eyes scanned the rows of cured meats, their aromatic scents mingling with the salty sea air outside. Mia had worked at her family's butcher shop since she was a teenager, learning the intricacies of traditional butchery from her father and grandfather before him. She took pride in her work, carefully selecting each cut to suit the needs of her customers.

The phone on the counter jolted Mia back to reality, shrill and insistent. She picked it up, expecting another order for a wedding or holiday feast. Instead, she heard a hesitant voice on the other end, asking about a particular cut of meat. The caller's uncertainty was piqued Mia's interest - who would be calling at this hour, asking for something so specific?
