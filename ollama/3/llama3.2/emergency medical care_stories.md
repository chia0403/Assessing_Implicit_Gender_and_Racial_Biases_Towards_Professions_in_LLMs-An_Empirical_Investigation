The sirens wailed outside, piercing the cool evening air as Dr. Alexandra "Lexi" Thompson stepped out of her small apartment and into the crisp night. She breathed in deeply, feeling the familiar mix of adrenaline and anticipation that came with every call from the emergency medical services (EMS) dispatch center.

As an ER doctor at Mercy Hospital, Lexi had seen it all - the gruesome accidents, the life-or-death emergencies, the countless moments when she'd had to make split-second decisions to save a patient's life. But nothing could have prepared her for the chaos that was about to unfold.

Lexi lived in the tight-knit community of Oakdale, where everyone knew each other's names and stories. The hospital was just a short walk from her apartment, and she often found herself running errands or grabbing coffee with colleagues between shifts. But tonight, something felt off. The dispatcher's voice on the radio was tense, and the tone of the calls was eerily similar - "multiple injuries" and "trauma alert".

As Lexi joined the throngs of people gathering outside the hospital, she couldn't shake the feeling that this night would be different. She checked her watch for what felt like the hundredth time, wondering if she should be heading to a regular shift or grabbing her bag for a more intense emergency response.

Whatever was happening tonight, Lexi knew one thing - she was ready to face it head-on.
