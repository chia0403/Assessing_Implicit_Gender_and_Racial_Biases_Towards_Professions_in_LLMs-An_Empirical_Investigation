Here's a potential story opening and setting:

The old mansion on Willow Creek had stood for over a century, its stone facade bearing witness to generations of laughter, tears, and whispered secrets. Amidst its dusty halls and cobweb-shrouded chandeliers, the housekeeper, Emilia Rose Thompson, went about her daily routine with quiet efficiency.

Emilia's eyes scanned the room, taking in the faint scent of yesterday's dinner lingering on the furniture, the faint scratch of the old wooden floorboards beneath her worn shoes. She hummed a soft tune as she worked, her hands moving deftly as she tidied up after the night's party: discarded champagne glasses, crumpled cocktail napkins, and the occasional misplaced guest's earring.

The mansion had been Emilia's home for five years now, ever since she'd left her small town in the Midwest to work for the wealthy and reclusive Mr. Edward Blackstone. It was a job that paid the bills and allowed her to keep her distance from the world beyond the manor's walls – but it also meant she knew every inch of its labyrinthine passages and hidden corners.

Tonight, however, something felt off. The staff had been unusually quiet during dinner, their conversations hushed and nervous. And as Emilia made her way up to the attic for the evening's linen change, she caught a glimpse of Mr. Blackstone himself watching her from the shadows – his piercing blue eyes glinting like ice in the dim light.

"Goodnight, Emilia," he called out, his voice low and husky. "I'll see you in the morning."

Emilia felt a shiver run down her spine as she smiled and replied, "Yes, sir." Little did she know, this was only the beginning of a night that would shake the very foundations of Willow Creek mansion...
