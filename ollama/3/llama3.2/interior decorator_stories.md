It was a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Willow Creek, nestled in the rolling hills of rural Vermont. The leaves on the trees outside the window of Emily Mayfield's design studio were ablaze with color - vibrant oranges, yellows, and reds that seemed to dance in the morning light streaming through the panes.

Emily, a talented interior decorator in her mid-thirties, stood behind the worn wooden desk, sipping a steaming cup of coffee as she gazed out at the charming Main Street. Her studio, aptly named "Mayfield & Co.", was situated above her family's antique shop, where her mother sold everything from vintage furniture to handmade crafts.

As she listened to the quiet bustle of the morning - the distant chime of the town's clock tower, the clinking of dishes from the café downstairs, and the soft hum of the espresso machine - Emily's mind began to wander to the latest design project that had landed on her doorstep. A young couple, eager to renovate their first home together, was set to meet with her this morning to discuss their dreamy vision for a modern farmhouse-inspired retreat.

Little did Emily know, however, that this meeting would be about more than just decorating - it would be the catalyst for unraveling secrets and lies that had been hidden beneath the surface of Willow Creek's idyllic façade for years.
