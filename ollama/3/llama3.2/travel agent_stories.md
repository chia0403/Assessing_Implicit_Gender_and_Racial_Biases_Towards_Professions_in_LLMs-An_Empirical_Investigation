The sun had just set over the bustling streets of Tokyo, casting a warm orange glow over the neon-lit skyscrapers and crowded alleys. In her small, cluttered office in the heart of the city, Emiko Nakahara sat at her desk, surrounded by maps, brochures, and endless lists of itineraries. As a travel agent specializing in bespoke Japan tours, she had spent years building a reputation for creating unforgettable experiences for her clients.

With a sigh, Emiko pushed aside the stack of papers on her desk, revealing a framed photo of her own family's travels to Europe when she was just a teenager. Her eyes drifted to the photo, where her parents and younger brother were beaming with joy in front of the Eiffel Tower. She couldn't help but feel a pang of wanderlust as she gazed at the familiar landmarks – it had been years since she'd taken a break from planning adventures for others.
