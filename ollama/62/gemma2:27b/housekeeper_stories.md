Rain lashed against the ornate stained glass windows of Blackwood Manor, each gust threatening to shatter the fragile mosaic of saints and sinners. Eleanor Cartwright shivered, pulling her threadbare cardigan tighter around her thin frame. The grand hall echoed with emptiness, its polished marble floor reflecting the flickering gaslight in a distorted dance. Dust motes swirled in the dimness, illuminated by the occasional flash of lightning.

Eleanor had been housekeeper at Blackwood Manor for nearly two decades. She knew every creak of the ancient floorboards, every whispered secret held within the tapestry-lined walls. Yet tonight, even she felt uneasy. There was a palpable weight to the silence, an unseen presence lurking in the shadows cast by the stormy night.
