Rain lashed against the grimy windows of the Legal Aid office, mimicking the torrent of frustration brewing inside Eleanor Rose Cartwright. Stacks of files threatened to engulf her desk, each one representing another soul drowning in legal quicksand. The air hung thick with stale coffee and desperation, punctuated by the rhythmic clatter of the ancient typewriter in the corner. 

Eleanor sighed, pushing a stray strand of auburn hair behind her ear.  She loved her job, truly she did. Fighting for those who couldn't fight for themselves was a calling, not just a career. But lately, it felt like an uphill battle against an avalanche of paperwork and indifferent bureaucracy. Today, the mountain seemed insurmountable.
