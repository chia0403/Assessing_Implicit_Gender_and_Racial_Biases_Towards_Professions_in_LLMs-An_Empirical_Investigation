Rain lashed against the stained glass windows of the Grand Archive, each icy drop a tiny hammer against the ancient lead. Inside, the air hung heavy with the scent of parchment and dust, illuminated only by flickering gas lamps that cast long, dancing shadows across towering shelves crammed with leather-bound tomes.  Bartholomew "Bart" Quill, his weathered face stoic beneath a greying beard, patrolled the labyrinthine aisles, his boots echoing softly on the polished stone floor. He was the sole guardian of this repository of forgotten knowledge, a duty he'd carried for twenty years with unwavering devotion. Tonight, however, something felt different. A prickling unease danced along his spine, a subtle shift in the archive's usual hushed stillness that whispered of an approaching storm.
