Rain lashed against the corrugated iron roof of the clinic, each drop sounding like a tiny hammer blow. The air hung thick with the scent of mildew and antiseptic, punctuated by the occasional sharp tang of iodine. Amelia "Amy" Chen shivered, pulling her thin cardigan tighter around her. Power flickered, momentarily plunging the small waiting room into darkness before sputtering back to life, casting long, distorted shadows on the cracked linoleum floor. Outside, thunder grumbled like a disgruntled giant.

Amy sighed, rubbing her tired eyes. It had been a long day, even by rural clinic standards. A farmer with a nasty gash from a barbed wire fence, a mother fretting over her wheezing toddler, and an elderly woman complaining of persistent aches - the usual parade of ailments that marched through her door every day. But tonight felt different. A restless energy thrummed beneath the surface, making the hairs on the back of her neck stand on end.
