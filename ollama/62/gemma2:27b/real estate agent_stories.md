The humid Charleston air clung to Eloise Beaumont like a second skin as she hurried down Rainbow Row, her sensible heels clicking against the uneven cobblestones. The pastel houses, usually so charming, seemed to mock her with their cheerful colors. Today was supposed to be a triumph: closing day on a historic mansion overlooking the Battery. Instead, it felt like Eloise was navigating a minefield of unexpected delays and escalating tension. 

The phone vibrated in her purse, and she knew it would be Mrs. Davenport, the seller, breathing down her neck about the missing chandelier fixture. Eloise sighed, already mentally composing a soothing response while trying to remember if the antique shop on Meeting Street still carried replacements. This wasn't how she envisioned her Friday afternoon unfolding.
