The midday sun beat down on Elias "Eli" Blackwood's neck as he wrestled with a stubborn length of rebar. Sweat stung his eyes, blurring the already hazy cityscape stretching out before him.  He was perched precariously on scaffolding high above the bustling streets of Chicago, the rhythmic clang of hammers and the whine of power tools composing the soundtrack to his day. Eli wasn't one for heights, but he'd learned to tolerate them, accepting the dizzying view as the price for a steady paycheck. Today, though, something felt different. A tremor ran through the steel structure beneath him, sending shivers down his spine. It wasn't the usual vibration of heavy machinery. This was deeper, more primal. Eli paused, rebar forgotten in his hand, and peered over the edge.

The street below seemed to shimmer, as if heat waves were distorting the asphalt. People shuffled along their paths oblivious, absorbed in their own world. But Eli knew something was wrong. He could feel it in the marrow of his bones, a tingling sense of foreboding that tightened like a vise around his chest.
