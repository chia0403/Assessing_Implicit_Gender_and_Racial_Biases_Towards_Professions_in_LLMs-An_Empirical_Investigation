Here's a potential story opening:

The sun had just begun to set over the small town of Willow Creek, casting a warm orange glow over the bustling streets. The smell of freshly cut meat wafted from the local butcher shop, where Elijah Edwin Bottomsworth was busy prepping for the evening's customers. Elijah, or "Eli" as he liked to be called, had been cutting and wrapping steaks, chops, and roasts for the good people of Willow Creek for nearly two decades. His family-owned shop, "Bottomsworth's Fine Cuts," was a staple of the community, where neighbors would gather to share stories and gossip while Eli worked his magic behind the counter.

As Eli expertly sliced through a particularly tender ribeye, he couldn't help but feel a sense of contentment wash over him. This was his happy place - surrounded by the sights and smells of meat, surrounded by people who knew and appreciated him. Little did he know, however, that tonight would bring more than just the usual chatter and orders for dinner.

Just as Eli was about to put away a tray of freshly baked sausages, the doorbell above the entrance jingled, signaling the arrival of a customer. And it wasn't one of his regulars...
