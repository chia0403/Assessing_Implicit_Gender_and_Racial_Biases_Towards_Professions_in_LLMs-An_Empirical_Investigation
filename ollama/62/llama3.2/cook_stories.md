The sun had long since set on the small coastal town of Ravenswood-by-the-Sea, casting a warm orange glow over the bustling harbor. The smell of saltwater and freshly caught fish wafted through the air, mingling with the sound of seagulls crying overhead.

In a cozy little kitchen above the local bakery, Emily Josephine Wellesley stirred a pot of simmering mussels broth, her hands moving with practiced ease as she watched the pots and pans clatter in the adjacent dining area. It was 4:45 PM on a typical Wednesday afternoon, and the bakery's lunch rush had long since subsided.

Emily, or "Em" to those who knew her best, had spent the last decade perfecting her craft as a chef and cook for the bakery, where she had learned the art of creating delicious treats from her grandmother, Agnes Wellesley. Now in her mid-thirties, Em was renowned for her ability to coax the most flavor out of even the humblest ingredients, and her baked goods were the talk of the town.

As she stirred the broth, a sense of contentment settled over her. She loved this life - the smell of flour and sugar, the warmth of the kitchen, and the satisfaction of watching people enjoy the fruits of her labor. But amidst the familiarity and routine, Em couldn't shake the feeling that something was about to change.
