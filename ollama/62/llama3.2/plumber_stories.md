The rain poured down on the small town of Willow Creek like a relentless curtain, casting a gloomy shroud over the crumbling Victorian homes and narrow streets. Inside his cramped plumbing shop on Elm Street, Jack "Hammers" Harrison scrubbed the remnants of last night's dinner from his hands, a habitual gesture that had become as soothing to him as the hiss of steam through his pipes.

At 42, Jack was no spring chicken, but he took pride in being one of the most reliable and skilled plumbers in town. He'd spent over two decades fixing clogged drains, unclogging toilets, and coaxing water from stubborn faucets. The locals knew him as a master of the hidden world beneath their feet, a wizard who could conjure hot water from the depths of a cold and dark pipe.

As he scrubbed his hands, Jack's mind wandered to the peculiar phone call he'd received just after dawn that morning. A woman on the other end had sounded frantic, asking him to come to the old Miller place out on the outskirts of town - said there was a problem with her sink. Something about "blood" and "water". Jack had raised an eyebrow when she'd hung up, but the thought of a mysterious client lingered in his mind like a damp draft from the cellar.

With a sigh, he hung his head towel over the back of his chair and began to prep for another long day beneath the town's rusty pipes. Little did he know, this was only the beginning...
