As the sun rose over the horizon, casting a golden glow over the desolate stretch of highway, Highway Maintenance Worker #3456, Ethan Ryder Jenkins, trudged towards his truck, the worn soles of his boots making barely a sound on the gravel. The air was crisp and cool, carrying the faint scent of diesel fuel and ozone, a familiar comfort to Ethan after years of working on the open road.

Ethan pulled off his reflective vest and slung it over the back of his seat, revealing a faded "World's Okayest Maintenance Worker" t-shirt beneath. He let out a deep sigh as he climbed into the cab, the worn leather creaking in protest. It was going to be one of those days.

The highway stretched out before him like a ribbon of blacktop, snaking its way through the rolling hills and rural towns that dotted the landscape. Ethan had been working on this stretch for three years now, and he knew every pothole, every loose gravel patch, and every section of road that needed extra attention. But even after all that time, there was always something new to find, some hidden hazard or unexpected challenge waiting just around the corner.

As he started the engine and pulled out onto the highway, Ethan's radio crackled to life, a voice on the other end of the line delivering a message that would change everything: "Highway Maintenance Worker #3456, we have a Code Red situation at mile marker 234. Repeat, Code Red. Please report immediately."
