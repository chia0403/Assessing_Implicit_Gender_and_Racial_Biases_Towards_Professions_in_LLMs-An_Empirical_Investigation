The sun was just starting to rise over the rolling hills of Willow Creek Farm, casting a warm orange glow over the fields and barns. Ethan Ryder Jenkins stirred in his worn leather armchair, rubbing the fatigue from his eyes. It had been a long night of tending to the farm's animals, but he knew it was necessary if they were going to make it through another year.

As he stood up, stretching his lanky frame, the sound of birds chirping and the distant lowing of cattle filled the air. Ethan took a deep breath, feeling the scent of damp earth and fresh hay fill his lungs. This was his life, and he wouldn't trade it for anything.

Ethan Jenkins had inherited the farm from his father, who had inherited it from his own father before him. It was a legacy that went back generations, and one that Ethan took very seriously. He spent every waking moment tending to the land, coaxing forth the best crops and raising the healthiest animals. It wasn't always easy, but it was what he loved.

As he made his way out into the chill morning air, Ethan's gaze fell upon the old stone farmhouse, its weathered facade a testament to the countless seasons that had passed through its doors. He knew every inch of this land, every curve and bend of the river that ran through it, and every hidden corner where the wildflowers bloomed in secret.

But on this particular morning, something felt off. A sense of unease stirred in Ethan's chest as he gazed out at the fields, his eyes scanning for any sign of trouble. And then, just as he was about to turn back towards the farmhouse, he saw it - a small piece of paper caught in the barbed wire fence that surrounded the north pasture.

Ethan's heart skipped a beat as he walked over to investigate. Who could have left this here, and why?
