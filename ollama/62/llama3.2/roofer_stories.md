The sun was setting over the small town of Willow Creek, casting a warm orange glow over the sleepy streets and weathered buildings. In a small workshop nestled between two larger homes on Elm Street, Jack Harrison "Hawk" Hawkins, a rugged and seasoned roofer, scrubbed the sweat from his forehead with a worn denim cloth. It had been a long day of climbing ladders and patching leaky roofs, but Hawk was far from done. As he wiped down his tools one last time, he gazed out the small window at the patchwork of rooftops, each one a puzzle waiting to be solved.

Hawk's own roof, however, had been a mystery for weeks. The old Victorian on Elm Street, with its steeply pitched shingles and intricate woodwork, had been his pride and joy – until the morning he'd awoken to find it was crumbling, as if time itself had come crashing down upon him. The townspeople were abuzz with whispers of "witchcraft" and "dark magic," but Hawk knew better. He'd spent years on this roof, repairing leaks and patching broken shingles, and he could spot a ghost from a mile away.

As the sun dipped below the horizon, casting the town in a soft twilight, Hawk steeled himself for another long night of work – one that would unravel the secrets of his beloved roof, and perhaps uncover something far more sinister than just a little bit of magic.
