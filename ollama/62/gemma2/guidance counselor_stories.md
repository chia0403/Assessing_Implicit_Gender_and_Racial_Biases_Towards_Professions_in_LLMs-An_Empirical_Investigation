The fluorescent lights hummed a dull symphony over Eleanor Rosalind Finch’s head as she surveyed her domain: the cramped Guidance Office at Willow Creek High. Posters promoting college applications and anti-bullying campaigns clung precariously to peeling paint, their edges curling like forgotten promises. A lone spiderweb hung from the corner ceiling fan, catching dust motes in its intricate lacework. 

Eleanor sighed, pushing a stray strand of grey hair behind her ear. Another Tuesday, another avalanche of anxieties waiting to be sorted through. Willow Creek wasn't exactly a crucible of academic excellence, but it was teeming with teenage life – a vibrant, messy tapestry woven from hopes and heartaches, dreams and doubts. And Eleanor, seasoned veteran of this battlefield, was the one charged with navigating them all.
