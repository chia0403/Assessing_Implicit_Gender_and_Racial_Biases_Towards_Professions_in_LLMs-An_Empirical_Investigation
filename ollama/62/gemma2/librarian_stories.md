The musty scent of aged paper hung heavy in the air, mingling with the faint aroma of Earl Grey tea that Ms. Eleanor Persephone Plumridge always brewed at precisely 3:15 pm.  Her fingers, slender and surprisingly strong for their age, traced the spines of forgotten classics lining the shelves of the Blackwood Manor Library, a hushed sanctuary tucked away in the sprawling estate on the Cornish coast. Rain lashed against the leaded glass windows, casting fractured rainbows across the polished oak floor. Eleanor sighed, adjusting her spectacles perched precariously on her nose. Another blustery afternoon, another day spent surrounded by stories whispering from countless volumes, waiting for someone to listen. 


But today felt different. There was a subtle shift in the air, a prickling sensation at the back of her neck that told Eleanor something unusual was about to occur. The silence, usually so comforting, now felt thick with anticipation.  She glanced towards the grand oak doors, the source of the unsettling feeling. Were those footsteps she heard? Or just the wind playing tricks in the old manor?
