The fog rolled in off the canal, shrouding the streets of Manchester in a damp, grey mist that seemed to cling to every brick and stone. It was a chill autumn evening, the kind that made you want to wrap your scarf tighter around your neck and hurry home from work.

Inside his small flat above the bakery on Deansgate, 32-year-old Alfie Bottomley scrubbed the sweat from his forehead with a dirty rag. He'd spent the day drafting designs for the city's newest skyscraper, hunched over his desk and sipping lukewarm tea as the rain lashed against the windows.

Alfie was a skilled draftsman, one of the best in the business, but he couldn't shake the feeling that something was off. The project had been a big one for the architecture firm, with plans to break ground within months. But now, as he stared out at the foggy streets below, he wondered if it was all just a massive mistake.

The phone on his desk jolted him back to reality, shrill and insistent. Alfie groaned, feeling the familiar weight of responsibility settle onto his shoulders. He picked up the receiver, hoping against hope that it wasn't another call from the firm's demanding boss...
