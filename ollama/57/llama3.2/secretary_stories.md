It was a drizzly Wednesday afternoon in late November, and Emma Rose Wellington sat hunched over her desk, staring blankly at the stack of papers on her to-do list. The fluorescent lights overhead cast an unforgiving glare on the worn leather chair, making it seem like she was sitting under the cold eye of a stern taskmaster. As the secretary to the reclusive millionaire, Harrison Grant, Emma had grown accustomed to the silence and isolation that came with working in his sprawling, modern office complex.

The rain pattered against the windows, casting a melancholy rhythm that seemed to match the dull ache in Emma's head. She rubbed her temples, feeling the familiar fatigue of another long day ahead. At 25, she had been working as Grant's secretary for nearly three years, and while it came with its perks - a comfortable salary and a well-stocked fridge - it was a job that left little room for excitement or adventure.

As she gazed out the window, Emma's mind began to wander to her own plans for the future. She had always dreamed of leaving this place behind, of making a name for herself in the world beyond the sterile walls of Grant's office. But for now, she was stuck, waiting and watching as the world outside moved on without her.

And then, just as she was starting to feel like she couldn't take it anymore, Emma's phone rang, shrill and insistent. She hesitated for a moment before picking up, her heart sinking as she heard the familiar voice on the other end of the line...
