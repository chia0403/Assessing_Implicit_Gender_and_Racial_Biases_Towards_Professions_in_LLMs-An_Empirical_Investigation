The dew-kissed grass of Ravenswood Manor stretched out before Emilia Rose Windsor like a canvas of emerald green, inviting her to begin another day of tending to the estate's ancient gardens. As the groundskeeper, Emilia took pride in her work, coaxing life from the earth and nurturing the beauty that had been cultivated for generations.

The early morning sun cast a warm glow over the manicured lawns and vibrant flowerbeds, illuminating the intricate stone pathways that wound through the estate like a serpent. The air was crisp with the scent of blooming roses and fresh-cut grass, a symphony of scents that Emilia knew would fill the lungs of her brother, James, when he arrived to join her for their daily work.

Ravenswood Manor had been in the Windsor family for centuries, its stone walls bearing witness to generations of laughter, tears, and whispers. The estate's grandeur was a testament to its rich history, but Emilia knew that behind every stately facade lay secrets and stories waiting to be uncovered.

As she made her way to the garden shed, Emilia couldn't shake the feeling that something was amiss. A faint unease had settled in the pit of her stomach since their father's passing, leaving behind a void that seemed impossible to fill. And now, with James's return from his travels, Emilia wondered if the whispers and rumors that had circulated about the manor's mysterious past were finally going to be answered.
