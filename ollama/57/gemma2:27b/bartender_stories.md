Rain lashed against the grimy windows of The Gilded Cage, blurring the neon glow of the "Open" sign into a feverish shimmer. Inside, Elias Thorne meticulously polished a chipped glass, the rhythmic swoosh of the rag the only sound competing with the low rumble of thunder. The air hung thick with the scent of stale beer and regret, punctuated by the occasional acrid whiff of cheap tobacco from the huddled figures at the bar. Elias didn't mind the smell; it was the olfactory signature of his domain, a place where dreams went to drown and secrets whispered themselves into existence.  Tonight, however, the usual hum of discontent felt different, charged with an unspoken tension that prickled at the back of Elias' neck. 


He glanced at the clock above the liquor shelf - 11:47 pm.  Almost closing time.

Then the door swung open with a bang, letting in a gust of wind and a figure cloaked in shadow.
