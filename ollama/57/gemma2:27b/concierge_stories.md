The opulent lobby of the Grand Astoria Hotel shimmered under the watchful gaze of a thousand crystal chandeliers. Eleanor Beatrice Cartwright adjusted her pristine white gloves, the starched fabric whispering against her skin as she straightened a stack of visitor badges on her mahogany desk. Outside, the Parisian afternoon buzzed with the honking of Citroën Deux Chevaux and the melodic strains of accordion music drifting from a nearby cafe.  But inside, Eleanor’s world was one of hushed whispers, meticulously orchestrated schedules, and the delicate dance of fulfilling every whim imaginable. Today, however, something felt different. A tremor of anticipation ran through her as she anticipated the arrival of Mr. Alistair Finch, a notoriously elusive art collector with a reputation for demanding the impossible.
