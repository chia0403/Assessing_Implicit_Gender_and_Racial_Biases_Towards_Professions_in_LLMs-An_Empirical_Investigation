Rain lashed against the windows of Dr. Evelyn Albright's office, mimicking the turmoil brewing within her chest.  The fluorescent lights hummed overhead, casting a sterile glow over stacks of student files piled high on her desk.  Beyond the glass, the playground at St. Albans Elementary sat deserted, swings swaying listlessly in the wind like forgotten mourners.

Evelyn sighed, pushing back a stray strand of auburn hair that had escaped its bun. Her heart ached with a familiar mixture of empathy and frustration.  She'd spent the last hour meeting with nine-year-old Liam, whose drawings were filled with disturbing images of shadows and monsters lurking in corners. His anxieties ran deep, fueled by something Evelyn couldn't quite grasp. This wasn't just childhood fear; it was something darker, more unsettling.

The bell for recess had long since rung, but Evelyn remained rooted to her chair, Liam's haunted eyes echoing in her mind. She knew she needed answers, and they likely lay hidden within the tangled web of Liam's home life.  But how to unravel it without alienating his already fragile parents?
