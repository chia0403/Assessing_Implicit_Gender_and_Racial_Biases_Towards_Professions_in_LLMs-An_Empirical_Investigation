The air hung thick with the scent of disinfectant and hay, punctuated by the occasional panicked squawk from the avian ward.  Dr. Eleanor Vance adjusted her stethoscope, its cold metal grounding her amidst the flurry of activity in the small animal clinic. Sunlight streamed through the windows, casting long shadows across the worn linoleum floor, illuminating dust motes dancing in the air. 

Eleanor loved this chaos. The constant stream of furry, feathered, and scaled patients, each with their unique story and ailment, kept her on her toes.  While some days were filled with routine checkups and vaccinations, others brought more complex cases that challenged her skills and knowledge. Today, it seemed, promised to be one of the latter. A frantic woman had called earlier about her elderly Labrador Retriever, Winston, who hadn't moved in hours, his usually playful eyes dull and unseeing.  


Eleanor took a deep breath, steeling herself for what she might find. Winston's fate rested in her hands, and Eleanor wouldn't let him down.
