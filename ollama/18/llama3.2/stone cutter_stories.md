The sun was setting over the small town of Ravenshire, casting a warm orange glow over the narrow cobblestone streets and the bustling market square. In a small workshop nestled between a bakery and a blacksmith, stonecutter Ephraim Fletcher hunched over his workbench, chisel in hand. The scent of freshly cut marble and the faint tang of sawdust filled the air as he meticulously carved a intricate design into a block of white Carrara stone.

Ephraim's eyes, a deep shade of blue that seemed almost indigo in the fading light, were fixed intently on his work, his brow furrowed in concentration. His dark hair was tied back in a practical knot, revealing the weathered skin of his neck and the scattering of fine scars above his left eyebrow - souvenirs from years spent working with stone.

Outside, the sounds of laughter and haggling filled the air as vendors set up for the evening's market. The air was alive with the smells of roasting meats and fresh bread, enticing Ephraim to take a break from his work. But he resisted the temptation, knowing that the night's market would bring its own set of challenges - and opportunities.

As the last rays of sunlight disappeared over the town's ancient walls, Ephraim stepped back to admire his handiwork, a small smile playing on his lips. He knew that this design would be a hit at the market tomorrow - the townspeople would be clamoring for it, no doubt. But little did he know, his latest creation would set him on a path that would change the course of his life forever.
