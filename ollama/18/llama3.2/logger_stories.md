The misty dawn crept over the small town of Ravenswood, shrouding its wooden buildings in a damp, grey veil. The air was heavy with the scent of pine and damp earth, carrying on it the distant thrum of chainsaws and the muted rustle of leaves.

In a clearing just outside of town, where the ancient forest gave way to rolling hills and scrubby underbrush, Logan Thompson made his way through the mist-shrouded woods. His boots sank into the damp soil as he walked, the worn leather creaking softly with each step. The chainsaw slung over his shoulder felt heavy, a reminder of the day's work ahead: harvesting timber from the oldest, most resilient trees in the forest.

Logan Thompson had spent his entire life working on these lands, his family's logging business a mainstay of the local economy for generations. He knew every tree, every branch, and every hidden clearing like the back of his hand. But today, something felt off. A nagging sense of unease had taken up residence in the pit of his stomach, and he couldn't shake the feeling that this day was going to be different from all the others.

As he walked, the silence of the forest was broken by the distant rumble of a pickup truck, its tires kicking up clouds of dust on the narrow dirt road. Logan's eyes narrowed, wondering who – or what – was out here on this autumn morning, and what they wanted.
