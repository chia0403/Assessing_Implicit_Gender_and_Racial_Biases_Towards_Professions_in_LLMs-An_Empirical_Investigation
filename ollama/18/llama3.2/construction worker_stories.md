The sun had just begun to set over the small town of Willow Creek, casting a golden glow over the dusty construction site where Jack Harris worked as a foreman for Thompson Construction. The air was thick with the smell of freshly poured concrete and the sound of heavy machinery humming in the background.

Jack Harris, a rugged 35-year-old man with a thick beard and a scruffy mess of brown hair, stood at the edge of the site, surveying the work that had been done so far on the day's project: a new multi-family complex for the local developers. He took a long drag on his cigarette, feeling the weight of the unfinished work ahead of him.

It was supposed to be an easy job - just a few more days of construction, and the whole thing would be wrapped up. But Jack had a bad feeling about it. The weather forecast said rain for the next three days, and he knew that would put their schedule back by at least a week. And then there were the rumors going around town... whispers of sabotage and accidents on other job sites.

Jack's gut told him something was off, but he pushed the doubts aside and focused on the task at hand: getting this project finished on time, no matter what it took.
