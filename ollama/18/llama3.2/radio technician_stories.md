She had received a cryptic message earlier that day from an anonymous source, hinting at a long-buried secret in the station's archives that could shake the very foundations of the community.

The WYRB building itself was a relic of a bygone era - a converted warehouse with peeling plaster walls and creaky wooden floors. The air inside was thick with dust and the scent of old electronics, but Mia knew every nook and cranny of this place like the back of her hand. She had grown up in Albany, working alongside her father at WYRB, who had taught her everything he knew about radio repair and broadcasting.

As she worked, Mia's thoughts turned to the message she had received that morning. It was a simple text - "Look again at old Channel 11" - but something about it felt off, like there was more to it than met the eye. Mia rubbed her tired eyes, trying to shake off the feeling of unease that had settled in the pit of her stomach.
