The fluorescent lights above the hospital's hematology lab flickered in unison as Dr. Sofia Elena Vasquez donned her gloves for what promised to be another long day of drawing blood from patients. It was 7:00 AM on a chilly February morning, and the small town of Willow Creek was already stirring to life outside the sterile walls of Mercy General Hospital. As she carefully made her way down the row of lab benches, Sofia's gaze swept over the familiar landscape of beakers, test tubes, and patients' charts.

Sofia had been a phlebotomist at Mercy General for five years, ever since she'd graduated from the University of Texas with a degree in biomedical science. She took pride in her work, knowing that every blood sample told a story - sometimes a sad one, but always an important one. As she made her way to the first patient's room, Sofia couldn't help but wonder what today would bring: another high-risk chemo session, perhaps, or a routine check-up for a young child with leukemia?

Little did she know, this day would turn out to be anything but ordinary...
