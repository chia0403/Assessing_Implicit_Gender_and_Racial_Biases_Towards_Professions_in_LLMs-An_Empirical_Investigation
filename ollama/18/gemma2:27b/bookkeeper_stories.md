Rain lashed against the grimy windows of Bartholomew Quill's tiny office, each drop exploding in a miniature firework display on the warped glass. The smell of damp wool and old paper hung heavy in the air, mingling with the acrid tang of Bartholomew's lukewarm tea. He hunched over his ledger, the flickering gaslight casting long shadows across the cramped space.  Bartholomew Quill, bookkeeper extraordinaire to the denizens of Grimsborough's shadier side, was knee-deep in a particularly perplexing set of accounts for "Madame Evangeline" - a name that whispered of velvet curtains and illicit pleasures.

The numbers refused to balance, each entry a cryptic riddle leading him further down a labyrinth of contradictions. A shiver ran down his spine as the wind howled like a banshee outside, mirroring the unease gnawing at his gut. Something wasn't right about these figures.
