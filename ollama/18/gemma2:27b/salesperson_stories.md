Rain lashed against the grimy windows of "Second Chance Autos," blurring the neon sign's promise of "Wheels for Every Wallet." Inside, the air hung thick with the scent of stale coffee and desperation. Bartholomew "Bart" Quincey III straightened his slightly wrinkled tie, eyeing the lone customer hunched over a dented Ford Fiesta.  

Bart wasn't your typical car salesman; he possessed an almost painful earnestness that clashed hilariously with the greasy world of used car lots. He dreamed of selling handcrafted bicycles one day, machines that whispered freedom and joy instead of chugging exhaust fumes. But for now, he was stuck peddling dreams on wheels, hoping to make enough commission to finally escape this asphalt purgatory.
