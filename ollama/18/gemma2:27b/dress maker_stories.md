The scent of lavender and chamomile hung thick in the air, mingling with the sharper tang of beeswax. Outside, the cobblestone streets of Montmartre buzzed with the energy of a Parisian morning, but inside "Mademoiselle Elodie's Atelier," time seemed to move at a gentler pace. Sunlight streamed through the dusty windowpanes, illuminating swirls of chiffon and silk draped over mannequins in various stages of undress. Elodie Beaumont, her auburn hair pulled back in a loose braid, traced the delicate curve of a bodice with a needle, her brow furrowed in concentration. A symphony of clinking pins, rustling fabric, and the rhythmic click of her sewing machine filled the small space, a comforting melody she'd known since childhood. Today, however, a disquiet hummed beneath the familiar rhythm. Elodie had received an unexpected commission, one that threatened to disrupt the delicate balance of her life.
