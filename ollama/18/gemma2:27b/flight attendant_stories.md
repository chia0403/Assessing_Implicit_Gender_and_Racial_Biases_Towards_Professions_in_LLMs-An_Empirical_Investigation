The stale air conditioner whirred above Amelia Rosalind Hawthorne's head as she adjusted her perfectly coiffed bun for the tenth time. Her smile, however, remained fixed, practiced perfection for the weary travelers shuffling aboard Flight 473 to Seattle.  The gate at JFK was a symphony of chaotic beeps and bored announcements. Outside, the relentless July sun beat down on the tarmac, mirroring the simmering frustration Amelia felt beneath her impeccably pressed uniform.

This wasn't how she pictured her life.  Five years ago, Amelia had dreamt of soaring through the skies as a pilot, not serving lukewarm coffee to disgruntled passengers. But dreams, like flights, could be easily rerouted by unforeseen turbulence.
