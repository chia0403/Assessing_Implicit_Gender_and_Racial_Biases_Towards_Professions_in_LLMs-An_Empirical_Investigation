The aroma of caramelized sugar hung thick in the air, mingling with the yeasty scent of rising dough. It was 6:00 am, and Eliza Beatrice Cartwright was already elbow-deep in flour, her brow furrowed in concentration as she kneaded a mountain of brioche dough. Sunlight streamed through the bakery window, illuminating the dust motes dancing above the worn wooden counter. Outside, the cobblestone streets of Edinburgh were still sleepy, but Eliza knew soon enough they'd be bustling with the morning rush. Tourists and locals alike would flock to "Eliza's Edibles," drawn by the promise of her legendary sourdough loaves and melt-in-your-mouth pastries.

Today was different, though. Today, a peculiar letter had arrived tucked amongst the usual bills and recipe requests. It promised an adventure, a chance to bake for royalty, far away from the familiar scent of home. Eliza's heart hammered in her chest. Should she heed the call?
