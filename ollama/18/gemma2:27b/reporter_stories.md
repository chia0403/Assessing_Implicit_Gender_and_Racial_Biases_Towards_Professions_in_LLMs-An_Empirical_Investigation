Rain lashed against the grimy windows of The Daily Grind, blurring the neon glow of the liquor store across the street. Amelia "Amy" Chen tapped her pen impatiently against the chipped Formica table, the sound swallowed by the clattering dishes and murmured conversations around her. The scent of burnt coffee and stale cigarettes hung heavy in the air, a familiar aroma that usually comforted Amy. Tonight, though, it did little to soothe her mounting anxiety.

She was supposed to meet her source, a man who called himself "The Raven," at 8 pm sharp.  He promised information on a story so explosive it could shake the city's foundations – something about corruption reaching the highest levels of power. But The Raven had been notoriously unreliable, and Amy couldn't help but wonder if she was being played.

The diner clock ticked past 8:15.  Amy glanced nervously at the door, her heart pounding a frantic rhythm against her ribs. Should she leave? Was this another dead end, like the last dozen leads she’d chased down in this relentless pursuit of truth?
