Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of Dr. Amelia Sharma's lab, blurring the twinkling cityscape beyond.  The incessant drumming mirrored the frantic beat of her heart.  Amelia gripped the metal countertop, knuckles white as bone.  Across from her, nestled on a bed of bioluminescent algae, pulsed the culmination of her life's work: Project Chimera.

The genetically modified organism, a shimmering fusion of reptile scales and avian feathering, lay dormant, its chest rising and falling in shallow breaths. Amelia had poured years into decoding ancient DNA, weaving together extinct species into this singular, breathtaking creation. Tonight was the night she'd awaken it.
