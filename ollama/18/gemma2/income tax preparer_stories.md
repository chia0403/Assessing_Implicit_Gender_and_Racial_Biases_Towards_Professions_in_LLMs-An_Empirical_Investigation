The fluorescent lights hummed above Beatrice "Bea" Pennyworth's head, casting a sterile glow over the stacks of W-2 forms, 1099s, and crumpled receipts that littered her desk. Her office, nestled in a modest strip mall on the outskirts of Des Moines, Iowa, smelled faintly of stale coffee and desperation. Outside, the March wind whipped around the building, rattling the windows and carrying with it the scent of thawing earth and the promise of spring. But inside Bea's world, it was still the bleak, soul-crushing season of tax preparation. 

Every year, the same frenzy unfolded. A wave of anxiety and financial uncertainty would wash over her clients, each bringing their own unique brand of chaos and paperwork to her tiny sanctuary of order.  This year, as Bea sharpened her pencils and brewed another pot of coffee, she felt a familiar twinge of both weariness and anticipation. She wasn't just crunching numbers; she was unraveling the tangled lives of people who had come to rely on her expertise, her empathy, and maybe even a little bit of magic.
