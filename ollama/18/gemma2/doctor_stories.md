Rain lashed against the windows of Dr. Eleanor Vance's office, each drop exploding in a miniature burst on the worn glass panes.  The wind howled like a banshee outside, rattling the old Victorian house perched precariously on the cliff overlooking the tumultuous sea. Inside, Eleanor, her auburn hair escaping its braid, paced anxiously by the fireplace, its flickering flames offering little comfort against the gathering storm both inside and out.

Her gaze was fixed on the antique clock ticking ominously on the mantelpiece, each tick a hammer blow to her already frayed nerves. It was almost midnight.  He was late. Uncharacteristically late.


Eleanor Vance, with her stethoscope dangling from her neck like a talisman and her brow furrowed in concern, knew something was wrong.
