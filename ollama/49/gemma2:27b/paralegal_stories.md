Rain lashed against the grimy windowpane of Amelia Blackwood’s cramped office cubicle. The fluorescent lights hummed overhead, casting an anemic glow on the stacks of legal documents threatening to engulf her desk. Amelia sighed, pushing a stray strand of fiery red hair back from her freckled face.  She was knee-deep in discovery for the Peterson case - a messy divorce where accusations flew like poisoned arrows – and her caffeine levels were dangerously low. 

The air hung heavy with the scent of stale coffee and desperation. It was a typical Wednesday afternoon at Adler & Greene, a mid-sized firm tucked away on a quiet side street in downtown Chicago. Amelia, despite her youthful appearance - barely twenty-five –  had already earned a reputation for being meticulous and fiercely dedicated. She knew every legal precedent, obscure statute, and loophole like the back of her hand. But even she was starting to feel the strain of this particular case.
