The fluorescent lights hummed above Esmeralda "Esme" Diaz's head, casting a sterile glow over the stainless steel instruments laid out on the tray. The smell of mint toothpaste and antiseptic hung heavy in the air, a scent Esme had long ago ceased to notice. Outside, the Arizona sun blazed mercilessly, turning the dusty parking lot into a shimmering mirage. Inside Oasis Dental Care, however, it was perpetually autumn. The waiting room was adorned with faux leaves and miniature pumpkins, an attempt by Dr. Singh to create a sense of coziness that always felt a tad out of place in the desert climate. Esme sighed. Another Tuesday, another endless parade of plaque and gingivitis. She longed for something more, a life beyond scraping tartar and polishing molars. 


Today, however, something felt different. A new patient was scheduled - Mr. Alistair Finch, an archaeologist with tales of ancient tombs and forgotten civilizations. Esme hoped his smile wouldn't be as decayed as the ruins he explored.
