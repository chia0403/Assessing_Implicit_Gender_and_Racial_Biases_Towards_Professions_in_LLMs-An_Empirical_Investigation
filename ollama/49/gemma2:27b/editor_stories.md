Rain lashed against the grimy windowpanes of Amelia Blackwood's cramped office, mirroring the storm brewing inside her. Stacks of manuscripts teetered precariously on every surface – overflowing from her desk onto the threadbare armchair, spilling onto the floor in a haphazard archipelago of paperbacks and hardcovers. The air hung heavy with the scent of stale coffee and desperation. 

Amelia, editor-in-chief at the struggling independent publishing house "Inkwell & Quill," ran a hand through her already disheveled auburn hair. The deadline for their flagship autumn release was looming, and the manuscript they'd pinned all their hopes on – a sprawling fantasy epic titled "The Dragon's Lament" – was proving to be more of a dragon's snarl than a mournful ballad.
