Rain lashed against the windows of Sunflower Kindergarten, blurring the vibrant crayon drawings taped to the glass. Inside, Eleanor Mae Cartwright adjusted her floral apron, a sigh escaping her lips. It was one of those dreary days that made even the most enthusiastic five-year-old yearn for a nap.  

The usual boisterous chatter had been replaced by a subdued hum as the children huddled around tables, noses pressed against their rain-streaked windows. Eleanor knew they were all secretly hoping for an early dismissal. But today was "Show and Tell" day, and Eleanor was determined to make it a memorable one, even if the weather outside wanted to dampen their spirits.
