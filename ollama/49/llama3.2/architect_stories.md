The sun had long since set on the city, casting a warm orange glow over the steel and concrete canyons of New Haven. The air was crisp with the scent of smoke from the street vendors who lined the sidewalks, their wooden carts laden with everything from skewered meat to hand-blown glass trinkets.

In her small studio apartment above the bustling Market Street, architect Sophia Isabella Reynolds sat at her cluttered desk, staring out the grimy window at the towering skyscrapers that seemed to stretch on forever. The soft hum of the city outside was a constant companion, a reminder of the world that lay just beyond the cramped, cluttered walls of her own tiny domain.

Sophia's eyes were fixed on the sketchbook in front of her, its pages filled with notes and doodles for a new project she'd been working on - a sustainable community center designed to meet the needs of New Haven's growing population. As she scribbled down ideas, her mind began to wander back to the strange letter she'd received that morning.

The words on the envelope had been simple: "Meet me at the old clock tower at midnight. Come alone."
