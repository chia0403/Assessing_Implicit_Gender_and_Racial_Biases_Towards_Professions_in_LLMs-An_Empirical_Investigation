The neon lights of "Eddie's Eats," a small diner nestled between a vintage clothing store and a used bookshop on Main Street, cast a warm glow over the rain-soaked sidewalk. Inside, the sounds of sizzling burgers and muted chatter filled the air as server extraordinaire, Alexandra "Lexi" Thompson, carefully balanced a tray laden with steaming plates of breakfast goodness.

At 22 years old, Lexi had been working at Eddie's Eats for three years, learning the ins and outs of the diner from its owner and mentor, the lovable but gruff Eddie himself. She knew every customer by name, every regular's preference, and every quirk that made them unique. From the early riser who always ordered the same eggs Benedict to the late-night revelers who devoured plate after plate of late-night fries, Lexi had seen it all.

As she made her way down the narrow counter, a bell above the door jingled, signaling the arrival of a new customer. Lexi's eyes flicked up to greet the stranger, a young woman with a look of determination etched on her face and a worn leather backpack slung over one shoulder. Something about the girl's demeanor seemed off, and Lexi felt a shiver run down her spine as she asked, "What can I get for you?"
