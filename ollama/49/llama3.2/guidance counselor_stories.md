The rain poured down on the small town of Oakdale, casting a gloomy mist over the streets and homes. In a nondescript building on Elm Street, Guidance Counselor Emily Alexandra Thompson sat at her desk, sipping lukewarm coffee and staring out the window at the stormy afternoon. She rubbed her tired eyes, feeling the weight of another long day ahead.

Emily had always loved working with teenagers, helping them navigate the ups and downs of high school and beyond. But lately, she'd been struggling to connect with her students, who seemed more interested in their phones and social media than in facing their own challenges. As the school's guidance counselor, Emily felt a sense of responsibility to support these young people as they navigated the complexities of adolescence.

Just then, her phone rang, breaking the silence. "Hello?" she answered, expecting it to be one of her students or perhaps a colleague with a question about a meeting. But instead, she heard the voice of Principal Jenkins on the other end of the line.
