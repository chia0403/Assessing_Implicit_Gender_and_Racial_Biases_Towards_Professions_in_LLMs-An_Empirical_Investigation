The sun had barely cracked the horizon over the small town of Willow Creek, casting a warm orange glow over the sleepy streets and homes. The only sound was the gentle hum of morning traffic on Main Street and the soft crackle of the local fire station's radios as they began their daily routine.

Inside the fire station, 32-year-old firefighter Ethan James Ryder sipped his coffee and checked the day's schedule on his phone. A veteran of six years with the department, Ethan was known for his calm demeanor and quick thinking in high-pressure situations. He had a reputation for being one of the best up-and-coming firefighters in the area, but he knew that reputation came with its own set of expectations.

As he scanned the day's schedule, Ethan noticed that Engine 12 would be responding to a reported fire at the old Miller place on the outskirts of town - the same property that had been the site of a devastating blaze just last year. The memory of that tragedy still lingered in his mind, and Ethan couldn't shake the feeling that something was off about today's call.

Despite his reservations, Ethan felt a surge of adrenaline as he grabbed his gear and headed out to meet Engine 12 and the rest of the crew at the fire station. Little did he know, this day would be one he would never forget.
