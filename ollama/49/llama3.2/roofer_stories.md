The sun was setting over the small town of Willow Creek, casting a warm orange glow over the quaint Main Street. On the outskirts of town, where the old Victorian houses gave way to more modest dwellings, a lone figure stood atop a rickety ladder, hammering away at a loose shingle on his own roof. His name was Harrison Fletcher Winters, but his friends and family called him Harry for short. As a roofer with over ten years of experience, Harry took pride in his work, ensuring every house he touched had a solid, watertight roof to protect the people inside from the elements.

The air was filled with the sweet scent of blooming lilacs and the distant hum of lawnmowers, as the residents of Willow Creek began their evening routines. The smell of freshly cut grass wafted through Harry's nostrils, mingling with the sweat on his brow, a familiar comfort after another long day on the job.

Just then, a sudden gust of wind shook the ladder beneath him, sending the hammer flying from his gloved hand. As Harry lost his footing, he felt himself tumbling through the air...
