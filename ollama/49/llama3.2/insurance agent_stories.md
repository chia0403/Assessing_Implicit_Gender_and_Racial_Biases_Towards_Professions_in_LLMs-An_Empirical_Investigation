The rain-soaked streets of downtown Seattle glistened like polished onyx as Emilia Victoria Winters stepped out of her office building, shaking the mist from her hair. As an independent insurance agent specializing in high-risk clients, Emilia had seen it all - the business owners who couldn't afford to close their doors, the families struggling to make ends meet, and the individuals who felt like they were one wrong turn away from financial ruin.

Emilia's own life wasn't any more straightforward. She'd grown up on the streets of Seattle's Capitol Hill neighborhood, the daughter of a single mother working multiple jobs to put food on the table. Emilia had learned early on how to navigate the system, how to talk her way out of trouble, and how to find opportunities in the unlikeliest of places.

Now, at 32, Emilia was making a name for herself as one of Seattle's top insurance agents. Her office was a cozy corner of the building, lined with vintage posters and stacks of dusty paperwork. She spent her days negotiating policies and mediating disputes between clients who couldn't agree on anything - except that their premiums were too high.

As she walked down the street, Emilia's phone buzzed in her pocket. It was a text from an unknown number: "Meet me at 5th and Pike. Come alone."

A shiver ran down Emilia's spine. She didn't recognize the number, but there was something about the message that felt... familiar.

With a deep breath, Emilia tucked her phone into her purse and continued on her way, feeling like she was being pulled into something much bigger than herself - and with no idea what lay ahead.
