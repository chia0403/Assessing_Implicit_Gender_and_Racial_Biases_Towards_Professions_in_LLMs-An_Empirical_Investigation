The sun was just starting to rise over the small town of Willow Creek, casting a warm orange glow over the dusty streets and fields that seemed to stretch on forever. In a small farmhouse nestled in the heart of the countryside, Elijah Jasper Blackwood stirred in his bed, rubbing the sleep from his eyes as he listened to the sound of birds chirping outside his window.

Elijah was a rugged man in his mid-40s, with calloused hands and a strong jawline that spoke of years spent working hard on the land. He had grown up on this farm, learning the ins and outs of farming from his father and grandfather before him. As one of the few remaining farmers in the area, Elijah took great pride in his work, coaxing life from the dry earth and nurturing the crops that sustained him.

Today was a day like any other, with the chores waiting to be done and the market fair just around the corner. But as Elijah swung his legs over the side of the bed, he couldn't shake the feeling that something was off. The air seemed heavy with an unspoken tension, and he found himself glancing out at the fields with a sense of unease.

As he made his way downstairs to start the day's work, Elijah noticed that the gate to the farm was slightly ajar, as it usually wouldn't be on a morning like this. A small flutter in his chest told him that something was amiss, and he couldn't help but wonder what might have caused it.
