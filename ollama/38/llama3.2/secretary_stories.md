It was a drizzly Monday morning in late April, the kind of day that made you want to stay indoors with a warm cup of coffee and forget about the world outside. But for Emily Elizabeth Wilson, secretary to the esteemed and slightly eccentric Dr. Sophia Patel, there was no such luxury. As she trudged through the misty streets to the office building on 5th Street, Emily couldn't shake the feeling that something was off.

The office itself was a labyrinth of cramped cubicles and dusty file cabinets, with only a handful of private offices scattered throughout like islands in a sea of beige. Dr. Patel's corner office, where Emily spent most of her days, was one of those islands – a cozy space filled with the scent of old books and the sound of soft jazz drifting from the speakers.

As she unlocked her own cubicle and settled into her chair, Emily gazed out the window at the rain-soaked streets below. It was a typical day in the city, with people rushing to and fro like ants on a wet pavement. But for Emily, it felt like just another day in the quiet routine of her job – until she noticed something that made her heart skip a beat.

On the front page of Dr. Patel's calendar, scrawled in hasty handwriting, was a single sentence: "Meet with Alistair Wellington at 3 PM – DO NOT MISS."
