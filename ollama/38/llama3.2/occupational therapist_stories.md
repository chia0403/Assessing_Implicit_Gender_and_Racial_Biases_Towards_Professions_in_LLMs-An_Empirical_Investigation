The sun had just begun to set over the bustling city of New York, casting a warm orange glow over the towering skyscrapers of Manhattan. In a small office nestled between a yoga studio and a vintage bookstore in the West Village, Dr. Astrid Helena Svensson, an occupational therapist with a passion for helping individuals with neurological disorders, sat at her desk, staring out the window.

Astrid took a deep breath, letting the scent of fresh coffee waft from the cafe downstairs fill her senses as she began to prepare for another day of working with her patients. She had spent years honing her skills in occupational therapy, and yet, every day brought new challenges and opportunities to make a difference in the lives of those struggling with the aftermath of traumatic brain injuries.

As she sorted through the stack of files on her desk, Astrid's mind wandered to the new patient who was set to start treatment that morning. A young woman named Sarah, who had been involved in a car accident just a week ago and was now facing a long and uncertain road to recovery.

Astrid's phone buzzed, breaking the silence, and she smiled as she picked up the receiver to greet her first call of the day...
