The rain poured down on the small town of Ravenswood like a never-ending curtain of grey, casting a gloomy shadow over the streets and homes that lined the quaint Main Street. Inside the cozy workshop of Electrician Edwin Victor Reed, the smell of ozone and burned wire filled the air as he tinkered with the wiring of an old lamp post outside the local diner.

Edwin, a man in his late 30s with a rugged jawline and piercing blue eyes that seemed to bore into those he met, was hunched over the workbench, his fingers nimble as he coaxed life back into the dormant electrical circuit. A small town boy at heart, Edwin had always been drawn to the hum of power and the thrill of fixing things that were broken.

As he worked, the sound of raindrops pattering on the roof above him provided a soothing background noise, but Edwin's mind was elsewhere. He couldn't shake the feeling that something was off in Ravenswood, that there was more to the town's tranquility than met the eye. And with a nagging sense that his latest repair job might be connected to it all, Edwin couldn't help but wonder what secrets lay hidden beneath the surface of this seemingly idyllic community.

Little did he know, tonight would bring more than just a typical rain-soaked evening in Ravenswood...
