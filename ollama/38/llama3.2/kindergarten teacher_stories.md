The sun was just starting to rise over the small town of Willow Creek, casting a warm golden light over the sleepy streets and homes. In a cozy little house on Elm Street, kindergarten teacher Emily Joanne Thompson was stirring in her bedroom, rubbing the sleep from her eyes. She stretched her arms above her head, feeling the familiar ache in her back from years of bending down to read with wobbly-bottomed students.

As she swung her legs over the side of the bed, Emily's gaze fell upon a faded photograph on her nightstand: a picture of herself as a young teacher, standing proudly beside a class of beaming five-year-olds. She smiled to herself, remembering the countless afternoons spent singing nursery rhymes and reading stories by the light of a candle.

The phone on her nightstand jolted Emily back to reality, shrill and insistent. She picked it up, expecting perhaps a message from her mother or sister - or maybe even a call from one of her students' parents with an emergency request for a homework extension. But instead, the voice on the other end was unfamiliar.

"Miss Thompson? This is Detective James. I'm calling about your student, Timmy Jenkins. There's been some...concerns raised about his recent behavior in class."
