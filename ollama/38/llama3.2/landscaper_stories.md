The sun had barely cracked the horizon over the small town of Oakdale, casting a warm glow over the quaint streets and homes. In his worn denim shirt and faded jeans, 32-year-old landscape specialist, August "Augie" Thompson, was already knee-deep in the dirt, pruning shears at the ready.

As he worked to tidy up the gardens of Oakwood Estates, Augie's mind wandered to the strange letter that had arrived at his small shop just that morning. The mysterious envelope, adorned with no return address and a single sentence scrawled on the inside: "Meet me at the old oak at midnight if you want to know the truth about your father."

Augie's stomach churned with a mix of curiosity and trepidation. His father, a man he had never met, was a topic of conversation that Augie had always avoided. The local nursery where he worked had been his family for as long as he could remember, but the stories about his father were sparse - just whispers of a mysterious past and an untimely departure.

The old oak in question stood at the edge of town, its gnarled branches stretching towards the sky like skeletal fingers. It was a spot that Augie had often taken his clients for sunset views, but tonight it held a different kind of significance.

As he finished up the last of his pruning and put away his tools, Augie couldn't shake the feeling that his life was about to take a drastic turn - one that would lead him down a path from which there was no return.
