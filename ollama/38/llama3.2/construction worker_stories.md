The sun was setting over the small town of Red Rock, casting a warm orange glow over the crumbling facades of Main Street's old buildings. It had been 20 years since the once-thriving downtown area had seen any real investment, and now it was a shadow of its former self. Amidst the neglect and decay, one building stood out - the old Red Rock Hardware store, where 35-year-old construction worker, Jason "Jax" Thompson, had spent countless hours as a kid helping his father with odd jobs.

Jax himself stood outside the hardware store, wiping the sweat from his brow as he surveyed the construction site in front of him. He was the foreman for the crew tasked with renovating the old high school that had been converted into apartments and office space. The project had been a labor of love for Jax, who grew up hearing stories about the school's glory days from his father, a retired teacher. But as he gazed out at the tangled mess of pipes and half-built rooms, he couldn't help but wonder if this was the real challenge ahead.

The night air carried the scent of freshly cut wood and grease, mingling with the distant hum of crickets and the faint rumble of thunder on the horizon. It was a moment of stillness before the storm, a brief respite from the chaos that Jax had grown accustomed to in his line of work. Little did he know, this was only the beginning of a summer that would shake the foundations of Red Rock to its core.
