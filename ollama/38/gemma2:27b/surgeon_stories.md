Rain lashed against the hospital windows, blurring the city lights into an impressionist smear. The wind howled like a banshee, rattling the old building's bones and sending shivers down Eleanor Vance's spine. She adjusted her surgical mask, the cool plastic momentarily calming the heat rising in her cheeks. This surgery was already pushing eleven hours, and fatigue gnawed at the edges of her focus.

Eleanor peered through the magnified lenses of her spectacles, tracing the delicate network of blood vessels feeding into the damaged organ. Every tremor of her hand could mean the difference between life and death for her patient. Outside, the storm raged on, a relentless symphony mirroring the battle being waged within her patient's chest.
