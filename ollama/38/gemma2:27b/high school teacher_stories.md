Rain lashed against the classroom windows, blurring the already gloomy autumn afternoon into a watercolour wash of grey and brown.  Eleanor Vance sighed, rubbing her tired eyes. The clock ticked relentlessly towards 3:15 pm, the time when she could finally escape the suffocating atmosphere of Northgate High's English department and retreat to the sanctuary of her book-lined apartment.

Today had been particularly trying. A pop quiz on Shakespeare's "Macbeth" had gone awry, with more blank stares than insightful answers. The usual chorus of teenage groans had accompanied the announcement of an upcoming essay on the play's themes of ambition and fate, adding a soundtrack of misery to her already dampened mood. Eleanor longed for a hot bath, a good book, and perhaps a glass (or two) of wine.
