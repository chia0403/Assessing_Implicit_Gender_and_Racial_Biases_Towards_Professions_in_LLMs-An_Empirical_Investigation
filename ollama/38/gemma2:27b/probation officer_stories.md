Rain lashed against the grimy windows of Evelyn Cartwright's cramped office, mirroring the storm brewing inside her.  Stacks of files threatened to topple from her desk, each one representing another life teetering on the edge of redemption or ruin. Outside, sirens wailed in the distance, a soundtrack to the gritty reality of Detroit she navigated daily.  Evelyn, with her salt-and-pepper curls escaping their bun and weary eyes magnified by thick-rimmed glasses, sighed deeply.  She was supposed to be making a difference, guiding these individuals back onto the straight and narrow. But today, hope felt as elusive as sunshine in this perpetually overcast city. 


Her pager vibrated insistently, jolting her from her thoughts. "Miller," it read, followed by a string of numbers she instantly recognized - Anthony Miller's case file. He was one of her most recent probationers, a young man with a history of petty theft struggling to stay clean.  Evelyn knew this call wouldn't be good.
