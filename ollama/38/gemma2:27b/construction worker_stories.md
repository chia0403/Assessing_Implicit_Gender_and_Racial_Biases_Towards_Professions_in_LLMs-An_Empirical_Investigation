The air hung thick with dust motes dancing in the harsh glare of the midday sun. The skeletal frame of the half-built skyscraper groaned against the wind, its metal bones straining towards the unforgiving blue sky. Below, Jasper "Jazz" Callahan wiped the sweat from his brow with a calloused hand, his gaze tracing the lines of the concrete foundation he was laying. 

The noise was relentless - the clang of hammers, the whine of drills, the rhythmic screech of cranes lifting tons of steel into place. It was a symphony of chaos that Jazz had grown accustomed to over his ten years in construction. Yet today, something felt different. A prickle of unease ran down his spine, an unwelcome guest amidst the familiar cacophony. 

He glanced towards the horizon, where the distant city skyline shimmered like a mirage, and wondered if it was just the heat playing tricks on him. Or maybe it was the cryptic message scrawled on the back of a blueprint he'd found tucked away in a dusty corner of the construction site office earlier that morning...
