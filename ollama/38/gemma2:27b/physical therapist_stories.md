Rain lashed against the windows of "Restorative Motion," blurring the neon OPEN sign into an impressionistic smear of red and green. Inside, Amelia Cartwright adjusted the heating pad on her patient's knee, the steady hiss a counterpoint to the insistent drumming of the storm.  The small PT clinic nestled on Elm Street was usually bathed in sunlight filtering through its large storefront windows, but today, even the cheerful sunflowers painted on the walls seemed subdued by the gloom. Amelia sighed, glancing at the clock. Her last patient for the day, a sprightly octogenarian named Mrs. Henderson, was running late. 

She hoped nothing serious had happened. Mrs. Henderson, with her infectious laugh and penchant for colorful scarves, was a ray of sunshine even on the gloomiest days. Amelia worried about her navigating the slick streets. Just then, the bell above the door jingled, announcing the arrival of a drenched figure huddled beneath a tattered umbrella.
