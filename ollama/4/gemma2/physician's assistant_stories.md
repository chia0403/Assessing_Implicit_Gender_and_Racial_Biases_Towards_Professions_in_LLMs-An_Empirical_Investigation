The smell of disinfectant hung heavy in the air, a familiar aroma that usually settled Eleanor "Ellie" Vance like a comforting blanket. But today, it felt cloying, suffocating even. The fluorescent lights hummed overhead, casting a sterile glow on the linoleum floor, reflecting back Ellie's own worried expression. She adjusted her stethoscope, its weight familiar and grounding in her hand. 

The waiting room of Dr. Ramirez's clinic was unusually quiet – just a young boy gnawing nervously on his thumbnail and an elderly woman clutching a worn photo album. It was unusual for the late afternoon shift to be this deserted. Ellie scanned the empty chairs, then glanced at the clock. Five minutes past four o’clock. Where was Dr. Ramirez? And more importantly, where were the patients? 


Ellie Vance, Physician's Assistant extraordinaire, wasn't used to idle waiting.
