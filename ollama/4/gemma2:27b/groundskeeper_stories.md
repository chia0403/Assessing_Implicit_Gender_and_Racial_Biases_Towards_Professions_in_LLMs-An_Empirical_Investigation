The rusted hinges whined like a mournful cello as Silas Blackwood wrestled open the shed door. Dust motes danced in the sliver of sunlight that sliced through the grimy windowpane, illuminating rows of forgotten tools: dented watering cans, a rusty scythe with a blade dulled by time, and coiled lengths of hose stiff as old bones. The air hung heavy with the scent of damp earth and decaying leaves – Silas’s familiar perfume.

He ran a calloused hand over the worn handle of his trusty rake, the wood smooth from years of use. A sigh escaped his lips, carrying with it the weight of another long summer day ahead. Blackwood Manor, once a grand estate, now stood in faded glory, its manicured lawns overgrown, its rose bushes choked by weeds. Silas was its sole caretaker, a silent guardian bound to its secrets and the memories that whispered through its crumbling stone walls.

Today, though, the whispers seemed louder, tinged with an unsettling urgency. A storm had raged through the night, leaving behind a trail of destruction – fallen branches, uprooted shrubs, a shattered birdbath lying in pieces on the cobblestone path. And amidst it all, a sense that something was amiss, something beyond the usual chaos of nature's fury.
