Rain lashed against the grimy windowpane of Beatrice "Bea" Hawthorne's tiny office cubicle. The fluorescent lights hummed overhead, casting a sickly yellow glow on her desk, cluttered with crumpled rejection letters and half-eaten granola bars. Bea, a petite woman with fiery red hair perpetually escaping its bun, sighed, her shoulders slumping further under the weight of another fruitless sales call. 

Outside, the city of Veridian throbbed with life - sleek hovercars weaved through neon-lit skyscrapers, holographic advertisements flickered in the twilight air. But inside this drab office building on the outskirts of the glittering metropolis, Bea felt like a relic from a bygone era.
