The rain-soaked streets of Seattle glistened like polished onyx as Dr. Elliot Thompson stepped out of his apartment building, clutching a steaming cup of coffee to ward off the chill. The neon lights of the Space Needle cast an ethereal glow over the city, as if trying to pierce the grey veil that seemed to suffocate the metropolis. Elliot took a sip of his coffee and checked his phone, wincing at the stack of unread emails from clients and colleagues.

As he hailed a taxi on the corner of Pike Street and 4th Avenue, Elliot couldn't shake off the feeling that something was off. He had been working non-stop for weeks, trying to meet an impossible deadline for his latest project: a cutting-edge AI system for a major tech firm. His mind reeled with lines of code, algorithms, and permutations, threatening to consume him whole.

The taxi lurched forward as Elliot slid into the backseat, tossing his bag onto the seat beside him. The driver, a gruff but kind-eyed man named Jack, shot him a nod as he peeled away from the curb. "Where can I take you tonight, Dr. Thompson?" he asked, his voice like a low rumble of thunder.

Elliot hesitated, unsure if he was ready to face whatever lay ahead. But the prospect of escaping the confines of his apartment and losing himself in the digital realm seemed like just what he needed. "The lab," he replied finally, his eyes drifting toward the towering skyscrapers of downtown as the city whizzed by outside the window.
