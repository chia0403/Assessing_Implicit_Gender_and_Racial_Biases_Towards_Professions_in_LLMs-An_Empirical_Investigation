The scent of aged paper and leather always held a strange allure for Elspeth Thorne, but tonight it felt even more potent, clinging to her like a familiar comfort in this bustling metropolis.  She stood amidst the towering shelves of the Metropolitan Library’s rare book wing, its dimly lit interior shrouded in silence broken only by the occasional creak of a floorboard or the soft rustle of pages turning. Tonight was special; she had just received word that an invaluable first edition of Poe's works had been donated anonymously and was now nestled amongst the dusty tomes, waiting to be catalogued. 

Elspeth’s fingers traced the spine of the leather-bound volume as a gust of wind blew through the open window, stirring the faint scent of pine from a nearby evergreen tree planted on the library's rooftop. The air held the promise of change and whispered secrets in a language Elspeth understood better than she realized.
