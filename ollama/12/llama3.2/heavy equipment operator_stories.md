The sun was setting over the vast expanse of the Arizona desert, casting a golden glow over the rows of sand dunes that stretched out as far as the eye could see. Jack "Hawk" Hawkins, a rugged and seasoned heavy equipment operator with a gruff demeanor to match his weathered face, sat atop his trusty bulldozer, affectionately dubbed "Bertha," in the middle of the dusty construction site.

It was late October, and the desert summer heat had finally begun to dissipate, replaced by a crisp autumn chill that hinted at the approaching winter months. The air was cool and clear, with only the occasional rustle of leaves or distant rumble of a windmill breaking the silence.

Hawk's eyes scanned the horizon, his gaze lingering on the distant outline of the abandoned mine shafts that dotted the landscape like skeletal fingers. He had worked on countless projects in this unforgiving terrain over the years, but something about this particular site had always felt off, even to him - a nagging sense of unease that lingered just beneath the surface.

Bertha's engine roared back to life as Hawk throttled up, sending a plume of dust and sand swirling around them. He knew every inch of this land, every hidden hazard and every shortcut through the desert, but something told him that today was going to be different...
