Here's a potential story opening and setting:

The old wooden sign creaked in the gentle breeze, bearing the faded letters "Moonlit Cove" in elegant script. It was a place where time stood still, where the sound of the waves lapped against the shore created a soothing melody that seemed to wash away all worries and cares.

Dr. Sofia Elara Vasquez stepped out of her car and onto the cracked asphalt of the parking lot, taking a deep breath of the salty air that filled her lungs. As an author of paranormal romance novels, she had always been drawn to this small coastal town in Oregon, where the veil between the worlds was said to be at its thinnest.

Sofia gazed out at the beach, watching as the last wisps of sunlight danced across the water, casting a golden glow over the waves. She felt a shiver run down her spine as she noticed something strange - a figure standing just beyond the edge of the tide pool, its back to her. The figure didn't seem to be moving, nor did it appear to be breathing. Yet, Sofia was certain that it was aware of her presence.

She pulled out her notebook and pen, and began to scribble down the first words of a new story idea that had been plaguing her for weeks. "The moon was full," she wrote, "and the sea was calm. But in the darkness, something stirred..."
