It was a drizzly autumn evening in the city of Ashwood, where the rain-soaked streets seemed to stretch on forever like a gray, endless highway. The neon signs of the bars and clubs along 5th Street reflected off the wet pavement, casting a gaudy glow over the crowded sidewalks.

The phone on her desk jangled loudly, breaking the silence of the office.

"Mia Wynter Investigations," she said wearily, answering the call with a voice that was as rough from disuse as the worn leather armchair behind her.

But it wasn't just any ordinary client on the other end of the line. It was someone who had been calling Mia for weeks, leaving cryptic messages and whispered pleas for help. Someone who knew Mia's business better than she wanted to think about...
