As the sun set over the small town of Willow Creek, casting a warm orange glow over the quaint streets and homes, 25-year-old Alexis Renee Thompson settled into her routine for another evening shift at Oakdale Child Care Center. The smell of freshly baked cookies wafted from the kitchen, mingling with the sound of children's laughter and chatter as they played in the playroom.

Alexis had worked at Oakdale for three years, serving as a lead caregiver for the three- to five-year-old preschool class. She took pride in creating engaging lesson plans, fostering a sense of community among her students, and providing individualized attention to each child's unique needs. Despite the long hours and demanding schedule, Alexis felt a deep satisfaction in her work, knowing that she was shaping young minds and laying the foundation for a lifelong love of learning.

As she changed into her uniform and began preparing for the evening's activities, Alexis's mind wandered to the strange occurrence from the previous day. One of the children, little Emma, had arrived home with a mysterious bruise on her elbow. When asked about it during snack time, Emma had simply shrugged it off, saying it was an accident. But Alexis couldn't shake the feeling that something wasn't quite right...
