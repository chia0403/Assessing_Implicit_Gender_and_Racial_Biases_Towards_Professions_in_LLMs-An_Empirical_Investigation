It was a crisp autumn evening in the small town of Willow Creek, where the sun dipped into the horizon behind the worn brick buildings of Main Street. The air was alive with the smell of wood smoke and damp earth, carried on the gentle breeze that rustled the leaves of the ancient oak trees lining the streets.

In a small apartment above the local diner, Firefighter Ryder James "RJ" Thompson sat at his desk, staring out the window as he sipped his cold coffee. It had been two weeks since the devastating fire at the old mill on the outskirts of town, and RJ was still trying to shake off the feeling of guilt that lingered in the back of his mind.

He thought about the family who had lost their home, the business owners who had struggled to keep their livelihoods afloat, and the countless hours he and his team had spent battling the flames. As a seasoned firefighter with over a decade of service under his belt, RJ had seen his fair share of tragedy and heartbreak. But this one still weighed on him.

As he gazed out at the quiet streets below, RJ's phone buzzed with an incoming text from his dispatcher: "RJ, we've got another call coming in. Possible structure fire at 345 Oak Street."

RJ's instincts kicked in, and he quickly grabbed his keys and headed for the door. This was going to be a long night.
