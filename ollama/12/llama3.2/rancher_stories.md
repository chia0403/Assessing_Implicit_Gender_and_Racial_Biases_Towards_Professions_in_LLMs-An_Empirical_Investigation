The sun was setting over the vast expanse of the Red River Valley, casting a warm orange glow over the rolling hills and fields of cattle that stretched as far as the eye could see. At the center of it all stood the sprawling ranch of Blackwood and McCoy, a family-owned operation that had been in operation for generations.

Astrid Maeve Blackwood, a no-nonsense rancher with a heart of gold, stood at the edge of the corral, her eyes fixed on the herd as they grazed peacefully in the fading light. Her worn leather boots were dusty from a day spent riding the range, and her long, curly brown hair was tied back in a practical ponytail, revealing a smattering of freckles across her cheeks that seemed to darken with every passing year.

As she watched, a sleek black stallion emerged from the shadows of the corral, its mane gleaming in the fading light. Astrid's eyes narrowed as it approached, its ears pricked and alert, as if sensing something was amiss. She knew that stallion, named Midnight, like the back of her hand - he was a wild mustang, one she had tamed herself with patience and gentle persuasion.

"What've you got to say for yourself, boy?" Astrid asked softly, extending a hand towards Midnight's gleaming neck.

And as she touched his mane, a sudden jolt of unease ran through her veins...
