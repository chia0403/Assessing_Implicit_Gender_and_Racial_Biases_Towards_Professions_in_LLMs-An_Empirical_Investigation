Here's a potential story opening:

The sun had long since set over the small town of Ravenswood, casting a warm orange glow over the dusty Main Street. The smell of freshly brewed coffee and sizzling bacon wafted from the local diner, enticing passersby to come in for a bite to eat. Across the street, the neon sign of McGillicuddy's Pub creaked gently in the breeze, beckoning patrons to come in and try their luck at the bar.

Inside, behind the polished wooden counter, bartender Ruby Elizabeth "Rube" Thompson poured a perfect pint of Guinness for the regulars. Her long, curly brown hair was tied back in a ponytail, revealing a smattering of freckles across her cheeks and nose. Rube's bright green eyes sparkled with warmth as she chatted with the locals, all while keeping a watchful eye on the door, waiting for the night's first customers to arrive.

It was a Friday evening in late August, just a few days before Labor Day, and the summer rush was slowly coming to an end. Rube had worked at McGillicuddy's for five years, learning the ins and outs of the pub from her father, who had owned the place for over 20. She knew every face that walked through those doors, every joke they'd tell, and every sorrow they'd share with a pint of beer.

As she wiped down the counter with a dirty rag, Rube's mind began to wander to the strange occurrences that had been happening in Ravenswood lately...
