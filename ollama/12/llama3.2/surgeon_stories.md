The dim glow of the hospital's emergency room overhead lights cast an eerie pallor over the chaos of beeping machines and murmured conversations. Dr. Evelyn Alexandra "Evie" Thompson stood at her operating station, her eyes fixed intently on the sterile surface in front of her as she scrubbed in for yet another long night of surgeries.

It was 11 PM on a chilly winter evening, and the ER was bustling with cases ranging from acute trauma injuries to routine elective procedures. The smell of antiseptic and disinfectant hung heavy in the air, mingling with the faint scent of worn leather and fresh surgical scrubs that Evie had carefully laid out for herself.

Evie's gaze drifted out the window, where the city lights cast a kaleidoscope of colors against the dark sky. She breathed in deeply, feeling the familiar rush of adrenaline and focus that came with preparing to face whatever challenges lay ahead. As a leading cardiothoracic surgeon at the prestigious New Haven Hospital, Evie had spent years honing her skills and developing a reputation as one of the best in the country.

But tonight felt different. Tonight, Evie's mind kept wandering back to the cryptic message she'd received earlier that evening - a brief text from an unknown number that read simply: "They're watching you, Evie."
