The flickering fluorescent lights buzzed in a dissonant symphony as Archibald "Archie" Finnigan mopped the linoleum floor of the Grand Blackwood Library's reading room. Dust motes danced in the pale yellow light, swirling around him like spectral butterflies.

Outside, a winter storm raged, whipping snow against the tall arched windows and sending icy gusts whistling down the chimney. Inside, though, all was hushed save for the rhythmic swish of Archie's mop and the soft rustle of turning pages from the lone occupant nestled in a velvet armchair by the fireplace. A plume of smoke curled lazily from the pipe clenched between his teeth, momentarily obscuring the man's face.

Archie knew better than to interrupt. This was Professor Alistair Finchley, Blackwood's resident scholar and Archie's occasional confidante. A man shrouded in mystery, with a thirst for arcane knowledge that rivaled any ancient grimoire he perused.
