Rain lashed against the windows of Room 213, blurring the already dull autumn afternoon into an indistinct watercolour smear. Amelia Hawthorne sighed, pushing a stray strand of auburn hair behind her ear as she surveyed the scene before her.  Fifteen pairs of eyes, glazed with boredom and apathy, stared back at her from beneath slumped shoulders and hooded sweatshirts.

The overhead fluorescent lights buzzed incessantly, adding to the sense of claustrophobia in the cramped classroom. Amelia adjusted her spectacles, the faded floral print on the frame a relic from a more optimistic time.  She was determined to make Shakespeare's "Macbeth" come alive for these teenagers, but the task felt Herculean today.


The air hung heavy with the scent of stale coffee and teenage angst, a pungent cocktail Amelia had grown accustomed to over her ten years teaching at Westbridge High. She glanced at the clock ticking away on the wall - 3:15 pm - only fifteen minutes left until the bell rang and she could escape this dreary tableau.

But Amelia Hawthorne wasn't one to give up easily.  She cleared her throat, a determined glint appearing in her hazel eyes. "Alright," she said, her voice ringing with unexpected force, "Let's talk about ambition. What drives us to want more?"
