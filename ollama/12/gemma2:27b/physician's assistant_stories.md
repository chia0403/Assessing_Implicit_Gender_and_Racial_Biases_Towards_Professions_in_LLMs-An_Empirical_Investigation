Rain lashed against the windows of the cramped clinic, mimicking the frantic rhythm in Amelia Rose Cartwright's chest. The flickering fluorescent lights cast harsh shadows on the worn linoleum floor, highlighting every speck of dust and scuff mark. It was past midnight, long after the last patient had shuffled out, their coughs and wheezes echoing faintly in her memory. Amelia, perched precariously on a stool behind the reception desk, chewed nervously on her thumbnail.

The storm had knocked out power lines hours ago, plunging the tiny Appalachian town of Havenwood into darkness. The only light came from a sputtering kerosene lamp on the counter, casting an eerie glow that danced across Amelia's tired face. Her pager, usually buzzing incessantly with calls for urgent care, lay silent, the weight of the unknown pressing down on her.
