The pre-dawn air hung heavy with the scent of yeast and caramelizing sugar. Amelia Rose Cartwright hummed along to the radio, her hands moving in a practiced ballet across the dough she was kneading. Outside, the cobblestone streets of Willow Creek were still shrouded in mist, the only sound the distant bleating of sheep from Farmer Giles' meadow beyond the bakery.

But inside "Amelia's Sweet Surrender," warmth emanated from the brick oven, promising golden crusts and pillowy insides. Amelia, her flour-dusted apron tied tight, was a maestro of her domain, creating edible symphonies for the sleepy town to wake up to. Today's masterpiece: blackberry scones with a lemon glaze, a recipe passed down through generations in her family.
