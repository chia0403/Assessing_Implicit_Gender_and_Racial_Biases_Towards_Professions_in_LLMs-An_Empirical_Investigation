It was a drizzly autumn evening in the small town of Ravenswood, nestled in the heart of rural England. The streets were quiet, save for the faint hum of a solitary streetlamp casting an eerie glow over the quaint high street. Inside the historic courthouse, Judge Emilia Isabella Windsor sat behind her worn leather desk, her piercing green eyes scanning the stacks of files and court documents before her.

As she poured herself a lukewarm cup of tea from the thermos that had been left on the edge of her desk by her secretary, the phone on her desk jolted her out of her reverie. The shrill ring pierced the air, breaking the silence of the evening. Emilia's eyes narrowed as she picked up the receiver, the nameplate on her desk a reminder of her esteemed position: "The Honourable Judge Emilia Isabella Windsor, Presiding Justice of the Peace Court for Ravenswood."

"Judge Windsor," a low, gravelly voice spoke on the other end of the line.
