The sun had just begun to set over the bustling city of New Haven, casting a warm orange glow over the worn streets and weathered buildings of the industrial district. Amidst the hum of cranes and the rumble of heavy machinery, Jack "Hawk" Hawkins trudged wearily towards the entrance of his latest construction site - a dilapidated warehouse on 5th Street that had been vacant for years.

As he swung his hard hat onto the back of his head, Hawk couldn't help but feel a sense of unease wash over him. The city was changing fast, and not always for the better. The gentrification was taking its toll, pushing out long-time residents and small businesses in favor of upscale condos and trendy restaurants. But Hawk wasn't about to lose his job just yet - he'd been working on this site for weeks, trying to scrounge up enough money to get his own place.

He yawned, feeling the exhaustion seep into his bones like a cold draft. It had been a long day, and he was looking forward to a quiet pint down at the local pub. But as he pushed open the creaky door and stepped inside, Hawk's eyes landed on something that made his heart skip a beat.

A piece of paper tacked to the bulletin board outside the entrance caught his eye - a cryptic message scrawled in red ink: "They're watching you, Hawk. Meet me at midnight if you want to know the truth."

Hawk's gut tightened as he gazed up at the words, his mind racing with possibilities. Who could be behind this? And what did they have to say about him?

As he hesitated for a moment, weighing his options, the sound of the construction site came crashing back into focus - the rumble of the bulldozer, the chatter of his coworkers, and the familiar din of the city itself. But Hawk's ears were no longer tuned in to that world. They were attuned to something else now - a mysterious message on a bulletin board, and a question that echoed deep within his own troubled heart: "Who is watching me?"
