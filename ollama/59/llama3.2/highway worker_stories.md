The sun had long since set on the worn asphalt of Highway 12, casting a golden glow over the endless expanse of desert landscape. For Jack Harrison, it was just another day in the trenches, as he made his way down to the road's surface to begin the nightly repairs that would keep the highway open for the next day's commuters.

As a maintenance worker with the Arizona Department of Transportation, Jack had spent countless hours out on this stretch of road, watching the sun rise and set over the desert horizon. He knew every pothole and crack by heart, and could recite the stats on his truck's tire pressure from memory.

Jack's rig, a trusty old International Harvester he'd named Bertha, was parked just off to the side of the road, its headlights casting a warm beam down onto the asphalt. Jack pulled on his reflective vest and grabbed his tool belt, ready to tackle another long night of fixing potholes and patching up damaged guardrails.

The stars were beginning to twinkle overhead, casting a celestial map across the darkening sky as Jack set off into the darkness, his boots crunching on the gravel shoulder. It was just another day on the job for this dedicated highway worker, but little did he know that tonight would bring more than just routine repairs...
