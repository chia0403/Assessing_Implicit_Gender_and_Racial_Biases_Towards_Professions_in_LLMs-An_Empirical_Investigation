The sun was setting over the small town of Willow Creek, casting a warm orange glow over the quaint streets and homes. The smell of freshly cut grass and baking bread wafted through the air, mingling with the sound of children's laughter and the occasional rumble of the local diner's jukebox.

On the outskirts of town, on a small plot of land surrounded by tall oak trees and a rusty old fence, stood the modest home of Elijah "Hawk" Hawkins. Hawk was a 35-year-old roofer with a rugged build, a bushy beard, and a penchant for telling tales that could put a room full of loggers to sleep.

As he climbed down from his porch, where he'd spent the day working on a leaky roof for the Watsons next door, Hawk gazed out over the sleepy town, feeling a sense of contentment wash over him. This was his place – Willow Creek, where everyone knew each other's names and the only thing that ever shook the peace was a storm rolling in from the east.

But as he made his way to the porch steps, Hawk noticed something odd: a small package waiting for him on the welcome mat. No note or explanation – just a plain brown box with no return address, and a single word scrawled across the top: "Wait".
