The sun beat down on Silas "Si" Blackwood's neck like a blacksmith's hammer, turning his collar red and damp with sweat. He squinted through the glare reflecting off the endless ribbon of asphalt stretching before him, a shimmering mirage in the desert heat. The air vibrated with the thrumming drone of cicadas, punctuated by the occasional screech of hawks circling overhead. Si wiped his brow with a calloused hand, leaving a streak of grime across his forehead.  

He was perched precariously on a ladder, leaning against the rusty frame of a "Road Work Ahead" sign. His task: to replace the faded and peeling orange lettering with fresh, vibrant warnings for the steady stream of vehicles hurtling past at breakneck speed. The work was tedious, thankless, and potentially deadly, but Si had learned to find a strange rhythm in it, a meditative flow amidst the cacophony of engines and horns.  

Today, however, something felt different. A prickle of unease danced along his spine, a feeling he couldn't quite place. The desert wind carried a faint scent of ozone, unusual for this time of year, and the shadows cast by the sparse scrub seemed to stretch longer than they should. Si shook off the unsettling sensation and focused on the task at hand, but the feeling lingered, like a ghost whispering in his ear...
