The air in Silas Hawthorne's shop hung heavy with the scent of beeswax and mothballs, a familiar comfort that had permeated his life for nearly forty years. Outside, the cobblestone streets of Prague echoed with the clatter of horse-drawn carriages and the shouts of vendors hawking their wares. But within the sanctuary of his cramped atelier, Silas was cocooned in a world of cloth, thread, and the rhythmic click of his shears.  Sunlight slanted through the grime-streaked windowpane, illuminating dust motes dancing in its path and casting long shadows from the spools of silk and velvet lining the walls. A half-finished doublet lay draped across his workbench, the intricate embroidery depicting a snarling griffin waiting patiently for Silas's skilled needle to bring it to life. 


Silas paused, his rheumy blue eyes squinting at the fine detail of the griffon's wing. He sighed, a deep rumble in his chest that spoke of weariness and unfulfilled dreams. His fingers, gnarled with age but still nimble, traced the pattern with a bittersweet longing. This doublet was destined for Lord Stefan, a rising noble known for his extravagance. It was exquisite work, yes, but it wouldn't bring Silas any closer to his own ambitions...
