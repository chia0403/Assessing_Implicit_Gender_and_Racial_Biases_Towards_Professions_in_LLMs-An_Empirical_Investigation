The fluorescent lights buzzed overhead, casting a sickly yellow glow over Elias Hawthorne's cluttered desk. The air hung heavy with the scent of stale coffee and despair. Outside, rain lashed against the grimy windows of Hawthorne & Sons Accounting, blurring the already dismal cityscape into an impressionist watercolor.

Elias, his tie askew and his face haggard from weeks of working late, stared at the spreadsheet before him, numbers swimming like agitated minnows in a murky pond. He was supposed to be reconciling the books for their biggest client, Blackwood Industries, but his mind was elsewhere - consumed by a nagging sense of unease he couldn't shake off.
