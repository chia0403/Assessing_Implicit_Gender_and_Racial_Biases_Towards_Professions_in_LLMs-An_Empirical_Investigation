The scent of lavender and sandalwood hung heavy in the air, battling with the sharper tang of bleach and hairspray for dominance.  Priscilla Clementine "Cleo" Jones hummed along to the old Motown tune playing on the radio as she expertly weaved highlights into Mrs. Henderson's already silver mane. Sunlight streamed through the large bay window, illuminating the dust motes dancing in its path and casting a golden glow over Cleo's cramped salon. It wasn't much - just two chairs, a beat-up sink, and enough styling products to rival Sephora - but it was hers.  Every snip, every curl, every expertly applied shade of color was a testament to her hard work and dedication. Today, however, something felt different. Cleo couldn't shake the feeling that she was on the precipice of something big, something that would change everything.

Outside, the usual bustle of Market Street buzzed with life, but Cleo barely registered it. A shiver ran down her spine, prickling the skin beneath her floral-print apron. She paused, scissors hovering mid-air above Mrs. Henderson's head. The radio crackled and then fell silent, plunging the salon into an unnerving quiet.
