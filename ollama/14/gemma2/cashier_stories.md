The fluorescent lights buzzed above Elara Beatrice Thistlewood's head like a swarm of agitated bees, their relentless hum a counterpoint to the tinny pop music drifting from the store speakers. It was always the same: stale air, the scent of cheap cleaning products and overripe bananas, and the endless stream of customers shuffling past her till at SuperMart 27. Elara scanned groceries with practiced ease, her fingers flying across the keypad as she mumbled receipts and change. The routine was comforting in its monotony, a shield against the world outside these brightly lit aisles. But today, something felt different. A prickle of anticipation danced on her skin, fueled by an unexplainable feeling that normalcy wouldn't prevail.
