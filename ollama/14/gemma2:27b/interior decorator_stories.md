The scent of lavender polish hung heavy in the air, battling with the faint whiff of ozone from Amelia Rose Hawthorne's latest project – rewiring an antique chandelier in her client’s Parisian apartment. Sunlight streamed through the arched window, illuminating dust motes dancing above a sprawling Aubusson rug. Outside, the rhythmic clatter of the boulanger delivering fresh croissants punctuated the quiet morning. 

Amelia surveyed the room, her hazel eyes critical yet appreciative. This was no ordinary Haussmannian flat. Ornate moldings framed walls adorned with faded Chinoiserie wallpaper, and a marble fireplace, chipped but grand, stood sentinel against one wall. It whispered stories of bygone elegance, of salons filled with witty conversation and the clinking of champagne glasses. Amelia’s mission: to breathe life back into this forgotten masterpiece while honoring its history.
