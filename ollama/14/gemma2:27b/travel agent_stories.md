Rain lashed against the windowpanes, blurring the Eiffel Tower postcard tacked precariously above Elara Finch's desk. It was Paris in Spring - at least according to the stock photo plastered across her computer screen. In reality, it was February in Manchester, and the only thing blooming outside were puddles the size of small cars.

Elara sighed, pushing a stray curl behind her ear. The phone hadn't rung all morning, which meant one thing: no exotic escapes, no sun-drenched beaches, no intrepid explorers seeking her guidance. Just the dreary symphony of rain and the faint smell of burnt coffee from the neglected pot in the corner.

She was Elara Finch, owner and sole proprietor of "Wanderlust Travel," a tiny agency tucked away on a cobblestone side street that saw more pigeons than potential clients these days.
