Rain lashed against the dusty storefront window, blurring the neon "Rx" sign into an indistinct smear. Inside, Amelia Hawthorne meticulously counted out twenty-five blue capsules, her brow furrowed in concentration. The bell above the door chimed, momentarily breaking the symphony of dripping rain and the low hum of the pharmacy refrigerator. Amelia glanced up, expecting a weary soul seeking refuge from the storm, but instead found herself facing a man who seemed untouched by the dreary weather outside.

He was tall and lean, with an air of quiet intensity about him. His dark eyes scanned the shelves behind her, pausing briefly on a bottle of lavender essential oil before settling back on Amelia's face. A faint smile touched his lips, and he spoke in a voice that was low and melodious, like the murmur of a distant stream.

"Good evening," he said. "I believe I'm looking for something a little... unusual."
