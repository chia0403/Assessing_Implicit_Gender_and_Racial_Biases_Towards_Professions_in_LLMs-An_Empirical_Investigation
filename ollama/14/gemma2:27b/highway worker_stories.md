The sun beat down on Thaddeus "Tad" McKinley's neck like a branding iron. Sweat trickled down his temples, stinging his eyes as he wrestled with the rusted bolt on the guardrail. The endless expanse of Nevada desert shimmered around him, baking the asphalt until it rippled like disturbed water. 

Tad squinted at the horizon, where a distant line of shimmering heat promised another day of relentless sun and dust devils dancing across the lonely highway. He loved the solitude of his work, the rhythmic clang of tools against metal, and the sense of accomplishment that came with mending the veins of this vast country. But today, something felt different. A prickling unease settled in his gut, a feeling that this day wouldn't be like the others.
