Rain lashed against the grimy windows of Eleanor Blackwood's office, mirroring the tempest raging within her. Papers littered her mahogany desk, each one a testament to the impossible case she was facing: defending a young woman accused of murdering her estranged husband, a man everyone believed was a saint. Eleanor ran a hand through her already disheveled auburn hair, her emerald eyes reflecting the flickering neon sign of the pawn shop across the street. 

The air in the office hung heavy with the scent of stale coffee and desperation. It was late, past midnight even, but Eleanor couldn't bring herself to leave. The weight of Amelia Cartwright's fate pressed down on her, a constant reminder that this wasn't just another case; it was a chance to right a wrong, to expose the truth hidden beneath layers of societal expectations and carefully crafted lies.
