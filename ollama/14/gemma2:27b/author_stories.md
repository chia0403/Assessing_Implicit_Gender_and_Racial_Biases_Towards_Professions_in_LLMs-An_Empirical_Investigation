Rain lashed against the attic windowpane, mimicking the frantic rhythm of Eloise Moreau's heart. The ancient house groaned around her, settling into its bones like an arthritic giant. Dust motes danced in the sliver of moonlight that pierced the gloom, illuminating stacks of yellowed manuscripts and overflowing notebooks.  Eloise, a woman with eyes as deep and restless as the stormy sea outside, chewed on the end of her pen, staring at the blank page before her. 

She'd come to this isolated cottage in Cornwall seeking inspiration, hoping the wild landscape and mournful cries of gulls would stir the slumbering muse within. Instead, she found herself paralyzed by a crippling writer's block, her mind a tangled web of half-formed ideas and discarded plots.  Tonight, the silence was deafening, broken only by the relentless drumming of rain and the unsettling creak of floorboards beneath her feet. Eloise felt a prickle of unease. Was it just the storm, or was something else stirring in the shadows of this ancient house?
