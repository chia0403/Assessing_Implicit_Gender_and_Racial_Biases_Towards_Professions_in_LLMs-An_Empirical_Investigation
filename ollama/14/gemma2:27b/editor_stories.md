Rain lashed against the grimy windowpanes of Eleanor Cavendish's office, blurring the already indistinct cityscape beyond.  The air inside smelled faintly of burnt coffee and desperation. Papers overflowed from her desk, forming precarious towers that threatened to topple at the slightest tremor. A single lamp cast a wan glow on Eleanor’s face, highlighting the worry lines etched deep around her eyes. 

Eleanor Cavendish wasn't just any editor; she was the Editor-in-Chief of "The Inkwell," a once prestigious literary journal now teetering on the brink of oblivion.  Circulation had plummeted, advertisers were fleeing, and the board was breathing down Eleanor’s neck, demanding drastic measures. Tonight, with another rejection letter crumpled in her hand, she knew something had to give.
