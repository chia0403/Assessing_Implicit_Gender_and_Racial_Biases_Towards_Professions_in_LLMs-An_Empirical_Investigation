Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of Alistair Finch's office, mirroring the storm brewing within him. The sleek glass and steel skyscrapers of Manhattan seemed to mock him from afar, shimmering testaments to wealth he could no longer touch. A once-bustling trading floor lay eerily silent, a graveyard of flickering screens displaying plummeting stock prices. Alistair Finch, renowned for his Midas touch and icy composure, now stared blankly at the carnage unfolding before him. His phone buzzed incessantly with frantic calls from panicked clients, each notification driving another nail into the coffin of his once-stellar career. He had always prided himself on his unwavering confidence, but today, a gnawing fear tightened its grip around his chest.  

The air in the office was thick with the metallic scent of impending doom. Alistair ran a hand through his thinning hair, the weight of responsibility crushing him. How could he have been so blind? The whispers of economic turmoil had been there all along, but he had dismissed them as mere background noise. Now, they screamed in his ears, each syllable a testament to his catastrophic misjudgment.
