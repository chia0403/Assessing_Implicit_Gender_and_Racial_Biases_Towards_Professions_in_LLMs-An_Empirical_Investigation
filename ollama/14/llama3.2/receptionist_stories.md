The sun had barely cracked the horizon over the sleepy town of Willow Creek, casting a pale glow over the quaint streets and shops that lined its Main Street. In the heart of this charming community stood the Oakwood Hotel, where 22-year-old Emily Joy Thompson sat hunched over her desk, sipping lukewarm coffee from a chipped mug.

As the receptionist at Oakwood, Emily was responsible for greeting guests, answering phone calls, and keeping track of the hotel's many schedules and reservations. She had worked here since she was 19, when her mother had brought her to an interview in college. Emily had grown up working at Oakwood, learning its ins and outs like the back of her hand.

On this particular morning, the hotel was quiet, with only a handful of guests checking in and out. The air was thick with the scent of freshly brewed coffee and stale air from the old hotel's pipes. As Emily typed away on her computer, she couldn't help but feel a sense of restlessness, like something was off. It wasn't just the dull routine of her job; it was the feeling that something was waiting for her around the corner, something that would change everything.

Just as Emily was starting to get lost in thought, the phone on her desk jolted her back to reality with a shrill ringtone. She picked up the receiver, prepared to greet another morning caller... but instead, she heard only silence.
