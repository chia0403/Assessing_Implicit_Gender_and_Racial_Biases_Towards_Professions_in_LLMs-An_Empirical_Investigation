The sun had long since set over the bustling streets of downtown Seattle, casting a warm orange glow over the crowded bars and restaurants on Pike Place. Inside, the sounds of laughter and clinking glasses filled the air as the lunch rush came to a close.

Ava Morales, a petite but fiery 22-year-old server with a quick wit and an even quicker tongue, expertly juggled multiple tables at once, her bright pink apron a stark contrast to the drab gray walls of the restaurant. With a name that reflected her Mexican-American heritage, Ava had grown up in the midst of Seattle's vibrant Latinx community, where food, music, and family were always at the forefront.

As she poured steaming cups of coffee for the sleepy afternoon crowd, Ava couldn't help but feel a sense of restlessness beneath her polished exterior. She was good at her job, but it was just that - a job. The real passion of her life lay elsewhere, in the words she loved to write and the stories she longed to tell. Little did Ava know, this ordinary day would soon become anything but...
