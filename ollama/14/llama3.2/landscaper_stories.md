---

The sun was setting over the small town of Oakdale, casting a warm orange glow over the quaint streets and homes. The air was filled with the sweet scent of blooming lilacs and the distant chirping of crickets. It was late May, and the summer solstice was just around the corner.

In a small shed behind his family's landscaping business, "Green Thumb Gardens," 32-year-old Julian Everett Brooks scrubbed the dirt from beneath his calloused hands, surveying the day's work with a practiced eye. As a landscaper, Julian took pride in transforming neglected yards into vibrant oases, bringing joy to the residents of Oakdale one garden at a time.

Julian's own backyard was a testament to his passion and skill. A lush tapestry of perennials, shrubs, and trees surrounded his modest bungalow, with a small patio area perfect for sipping lemonade on warm afternoons like this. As he finished up the day's cleaning, Julian couldn't help but feel grateful for another beautiful day in Oakdale, where the rhythms of nature seemed to match the rhythm of his own heart.

Little did Julian know, however, that tomorrow would bring more than just a typical Tuesday for Green Thumb Gardens...
