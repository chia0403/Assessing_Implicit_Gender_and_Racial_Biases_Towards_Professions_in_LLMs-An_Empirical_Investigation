The sun had just begun to set over the dusty terrain of the Arizona desert, casting a warm orange glow over the vast expanse of sand and rock that stretched out before Ryder Jackson Thompson. A seasoned heavy equipment operator with over a decade of experience under his belt, Ryder had spent countless hours operating everything from massive bulldozers to towering cranes. He was known for his rugged, no-nonsense approach to work, earned from years of navigating the unforgiving landscape of construction sites and oil rigs across the American Southwest.

As he climbed out of the cab of his trusty Caterpillar 966B excavator, Ryder took a moment to survey the lay of the land before him. The project was a massive new pipeline expansion that would soon be pumping millions of gallons of crude oil through the heart of the desert, bringing both promise and peril to this unforgiving landscape.

And Ryder knew he'd be at the center of it all.
