The misty dawn broke over the sleepy streets of Paris, casting a veil of mystery over the City of Light. In a small, family-owned hotel on the Rue de Rivoli, Concierge Élise Adèle Dupont stirred the embers of last night's bonfire, coaxing the dying flames back to life. The warm glow of the fire danced across her face, illuminating the sharp lines of her features and the piercing green of her eyes.

Élise had worked as a concierge at the Hôtel des Rêves for over five years, learning the intricacies of guest satisfaction and the secrets of the city from its most discerning residents. She took pride in anticipating every whim, from the perfect croissant to the best table at a Michelin-starred restaurant. But as she gazed out into the mist-shrouded morning, Élise couldn't shake the feeling that something was about to disrupt the delicate balance of her world.

The hotel's owner, Monsieur Lebrun, stood in the doorway, his eyes fixed on the fire with an intensity that sent a shiver down Élise's spine. "Tonight," he said, his voice low and gravelly, "we have a guest who will change everything."
