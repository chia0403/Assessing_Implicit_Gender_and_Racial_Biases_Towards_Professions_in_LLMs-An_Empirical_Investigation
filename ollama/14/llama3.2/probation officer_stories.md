The rain-soaked streets of downtown Atlanta glistened like polished onyx, reflecting the dim glow of streetlights that cast long shadows across the sidewalks. Inside the worn walls of the City County Probation Office, probation officer Alexandra "Lexi" Thompson stood at her desk, sipping lukewarm coffee from a chipped mug. Her eyes, red-rimmed from lack of sleep and the stress of her job, scanned the morning's stack of files with a mix of frustration and determination.

As she sorted through the reports, Lexi's mind wandered to the latest case file on her desk - 27-year-old Tyrone Jackson, a former gang member convicted of armed robbery. He was already serving three years in state prison when he'd received a surprise parole grant due to good behavior. Now, Lexi was responsible for monitoring his progress and ensuring he stayed on track. The catch: Tyrone had a history of reoffending, and the Department of Corrections had deemed him "high-risk" - a label that made her job even more challenging.

The phone on her desk jolted her out of her reverie, shrill and insistent. Lexi picked up, her voice firm as she greeted the caller: Tyrone himself, calling from his halfway house in Fulton County.

"Tyrone, what can I do for you?" she asked, already knowing that he'd be asking about something - or someone.

And with that simple greeting, Lexi's day had just become a lot more complicated.
