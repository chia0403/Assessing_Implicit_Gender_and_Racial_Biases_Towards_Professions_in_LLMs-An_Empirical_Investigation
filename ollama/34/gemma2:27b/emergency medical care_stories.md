Rain lashed against the ambulance windows, blurring the neon glow of the city into an Impressionist painting of red and green. Inside, Amelia "Amy" Chen gripped the steering wheel, knuckles white. Her partner, Mark, was hunched over the stretcher in the back, his face grim as he worked to stabilize a young woman with a chest wound. The wail of the siren echoed through the narrow streets, a desperate plea cutting through the downpour. 

They were en route to Mercy General, a behemoth of a hospital known for its trauma unit. Amy knew they wouldn't make it in time without a miracle. Every bump in the road sent a jolt of pain through her patient, and she could see Mark struggling to keep her breathing steady. This wasn't just another call; this was life hanging by a thread.
