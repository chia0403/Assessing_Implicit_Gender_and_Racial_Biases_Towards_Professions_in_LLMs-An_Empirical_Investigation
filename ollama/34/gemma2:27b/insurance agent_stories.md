Rain lashed against the grimy windows of Archibald "Archie" Finch's office, mimicking the relentless drumming anxiety in his chest.  His tie, normally meticulously knotted, hung askew, reflecting the disarray threatening to engulf him. The flickering neon sign outside cast an unsettling green hue across the worn carpet and stacks of dusty files piled precariously on his desk. This wasn't how Archie envisioned his mid-life career: selling insurance policies out of a cramped office in the heart of a decaying industrial district, his only companions the ghosts of past clients and the incessant buzz of the fluorescent lights overhead.  

But today was different. Today, a case had landed on his desk that smelled less of mothballs and more of danger - a claim so bizarre, so outlandish, it threatened to unravel the very fabric of Archie's carefully constructed reality.
