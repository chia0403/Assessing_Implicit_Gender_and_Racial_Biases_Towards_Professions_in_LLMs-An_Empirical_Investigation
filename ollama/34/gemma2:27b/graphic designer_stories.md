Rain lashed against the grimy window of Elara Beaumont's tiny studio apartment, blurring the neon glow of the ramen shop across the street. The air inside was thick with the scent of instant coffee and desperation. Elara, her auburn hair pulled back in a messy bun, stared at the blinking cursor on her computer screen, willing inspiration to strike. Her latest project – designing a logo for a sustainable vegan pet food company – felt like an insurmountable hurdle.

She'd been hired on a shoestring budget, promising a design that was both "eye-catching" and "eco-conscious," but all she could conjure were images of wilted lettuce and forlorn puppies. The deadline loomed large, a menacing shadow over her already chaotic life.
