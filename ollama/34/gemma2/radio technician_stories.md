The acrid tang of ozone hung heavy in the air, mingling with the greasy scent of motor oil that permeated everything within the cramped confines of "Sparks" - a ramshackle repair shop tucked away on a desolate stretch of highway 37 just outside Jericho, Arizona.  Rain lashed against the corrugated metal roof, the rhythmic drumming a counterpoint to the crackling static emanating from Elias Thorne's workbench. His brow furrowed in concentration as he scrutinized the disassembled radio receiver with his calloused fingers, searching for the source of its erratic transmission. Elias, a man weathered by years spent wrestling with tangled wires and capricious electronics, wasn’t one to be easily defeated. 


This particular radio, however, was proving to be a stubborn adversary. Its distorted whispers spoke of a mystery he couldn't quite decipher – a story unfolding beyond the dusty town limits, somewhere in the desolate expanse of the Arizona desert.  Elias felt an instinctive pull towards it, a yearning to unravel the enigma hidden within the static.
