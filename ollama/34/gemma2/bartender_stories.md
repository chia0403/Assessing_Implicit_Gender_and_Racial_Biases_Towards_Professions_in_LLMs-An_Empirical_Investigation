The amber glow of the neon "Open" sign cast long, distorted shadows across the rain-slicked pavement outside The Rusty Mug. Inside, the air was thick with the scent of stale beer, spilled whiskey, and desperation.  Elias Thorne, his weathered face illuminated by the flickering light behind the bar, meticulously polished a glass, his movements precise and practiced after years of slinging drinks for every kind of soul that wandered through those doors. The music - a bluesy lament from an old record player in the corner - mingled with the low murmur of conversation and the clinking of ice against glass. Elias glanced at the clock: 1:17 am. Another slow night in another forgotten corner of the city.
