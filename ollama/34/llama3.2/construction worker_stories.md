The sun was setting over the worn concrete of Pembleton Street, casting a golden glow over the bustling streets of downtown Portland. Jack "Hawk" Hawkins, a seasoned construction worker with calloused hands and a weathered face, trudged through the fading light, his tool belt slung over his shoulder. His boots scuffed against the pavement as he navigated the crowded sidewalks, dodging tourists and vendors selling handmade trinkets to passersby.

Hawk had worked on this site for months, building a new high-rise office complex that would soon dominate the city's skyline. He was one of the few workers who knew the building's layout inside and out, having spent countless hours inspecting the steel beams and reinforced concrete foundations from which it rose.

As he turned a corner onto 3rd Avenue, Hawk caught sight of his coworker, Maria Rodriguez, huddled over her phone in a doorway. She was a young engineer, fresh out of school, and Hawk had taken her under his wing as she learned the ins and outs of the trade. But something about Maria's expression worried him - she looked pale and shaken, her eyes darting nervously around the crowded street.

Hawk quickened his pace, his curiosity piqued. What was going on?
