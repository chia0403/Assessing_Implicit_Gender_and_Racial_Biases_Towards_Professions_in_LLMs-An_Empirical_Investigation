Here's a potential story opening and setting:

The sun had barely cracked the horizon over the small village of Ashwood, casting a warm orange glow over the thatched roofs and bustling town square. Amidst the chaos of market stalls and vendors calling out their wares, one figure stood out: Emrys ap Rhys, the village's finest stone cutter.

Emrys, with his wild shock of dark hair and weathered skin, moved with a quiet confidence as he made his way through the crowd. His eyes, a piercing blue that seemed to bore into those he met, scanned the stalls with a practiced intensity, searching for the perfect piece of material to work with. It was a habit formed over years of crafting beautiful and functional stone objects: furniture, statues, and even architectural features.

As the village's only stone cutter, Emrys had learned to be self-sufficient, able to shape and polish stone into works of art that were sought after by collectors and artisans from all over the region. His small workshop, tucked away in a quiet alleyway near the town square, was a testament to his skill: rows of beautifully polished slabs, each one telling a story of its own.

But Emrys's latest commission had been delayed, and with it, the village's most important celebration – the summer solstice feast – approached. The villagers were growing restless, and Emrys knew that if he didn't deliver soon, the entire town would be talking about his lack of focus...
