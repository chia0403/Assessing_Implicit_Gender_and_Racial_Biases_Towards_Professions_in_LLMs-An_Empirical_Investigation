The sun was setting over the small town of Willow Creek, casting a warm orange glow over the quaint streets and homes that lined the sidewalks. In a small yard off Elm Street, Jack Harrison, a ruggedly handsome landscaper with calloused hands and a weathered face, kneeled among the carefully tended gardens, his eyes scanning the meticulously pruned hedges and vibrant flowers.

As he worked, the sound of birds chirping and the gentle hum of a lawnmower drifted through the air, mingling with the sweet scent of blooming roses. It was moments like these that Jack loved most - the sense of satisfaction he got from bringing life and beauty to people's outdoor spaces, and the quiet contentment that came from knowing his work brought joy to those who appreciated it.

Just as Jack finished tidying up a particularly stubborn patch of deadheading petunias, his phone buzzed in his pocket. He hesitated for a moment before answering, wondering what could be so urgent on a peaceful evening like this...
