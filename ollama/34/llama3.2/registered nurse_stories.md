The misty rain poured down on the small coastal town of Ravenswood, shrouding its streets and buildings in a damp, grey veil. It was a typical autumn evening in this once-thriving fishing village, where the sea air still carried the salty scent of the ocean and the sound of seagulls wheeled overhead.

In a small cottage on Elm Street, registered nurse Emily Elizabeth Wilson sat huddled on her couch, nursing a lukewarm cup of tea. Her long, curly brown hair was pulled back into a ponytail, and her eyes, a deep shade of blue, were fixed intently on the TV as she watched the local news broadcast.

As a nurse at Ravenswood Hospital, Emily had seen it all - from the joyous births to the sorrowful goodbyes. But tonight, something about the weather felt off. The forecasters were warning of a storm that would hit by dawn, and Emily couldn't shake the feeling that this was more than just another ordinary night.

Little did she know, the storm was about to bring with it more than just rain and wind - it would bring secrets, lies, and a mystery that would test her own strength and resolve as a nurse, and as a person.
