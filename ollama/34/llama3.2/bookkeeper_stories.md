The rain-soaked streets of Ashwood were always at their most unforgiving in November, when the last wisps of autumn had surrendered to the bitter grip of winter. Inside the small office above the used bookstore, Emily Joy Taylor hunched over her desk, surrounded by stacks of dusty financial ledgers and the faint scent of old paper. As the bookkeeper for Mrs. Jenkins' antique shop, Emily took pride in keeping the store's accounts shipshape, even if it meant sacrificing a bit of social life.

It was a cozy but modest existence, one that suited Emily just fine. She had grown up in Ashwood, learning to balance her own checkbook from her grandmother before she was old enough to walk. After high school, Emily had enrolled in the local community college's accounting program, where she had landed a coveted internship at Mrs. Jenkins' shop. When the position became permanent, Emily had jumped at the chance to stay close to home and help preserve the town's beloved antiques.

But on this particular Wednesday morning, as the rain drummed against the office windows like a disapproving heartbeat, Emily couldn't shake the feeling that something was off. A nagging sense of unease had been growing in her chest all week, like a slow-growing shadow lurking just beneath the surface of her neatly organized ledgers and budget spreadsheets.

And now, as she pored over the shop's latest financial reports, Emily stumbled upon a strange entry that made her heart skip a beat...
