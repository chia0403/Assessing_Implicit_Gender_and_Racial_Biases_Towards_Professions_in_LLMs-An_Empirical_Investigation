The snowflakes danced in the dimly lit alleyway, their delicate edges barely visible in the flickering gaslight that lined the cobblestone street. The year was 1888, and the fog-shrouded city of Ashwood was a place where magic and mystery lurked around every corner.

In a small, cluttered shop nestled between a apothecary and a pawnbroker, Emilia Sophia Wychwood hunched over her workbench, her slender fingers moving with precision as she stitched together a new coat for the local lord's daughter. The scent of wool and thread filled the air, mingling with the faint hint of smoke from the pipe that Emilia was constantly lighting to soothe her nerves.

As a tailor, Emilia had spent years honing her craft in Ashwood's bustling markets, earning a reputation for creating garments that were both beautiful and functional. But despite her success, Emilia couldn't shake the feeling that she was being watched - that there was something lurking just beyond the edges of perception, waiting to pounce.

As she worked, the clock on the wall ticked away with a steady rhythm, its face worn smooth by years of use. The sound seemed to grow louder, more insistent, and Emilia felt a shiver run down her spine as she wondered what secrets the night might hold...
