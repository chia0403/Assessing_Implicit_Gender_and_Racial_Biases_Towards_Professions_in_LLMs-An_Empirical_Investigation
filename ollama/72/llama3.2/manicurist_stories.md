The sun had long since set over the bustling streets of Tokyo's Shinjuku district, casting a warm orange glow over the neon-lit skyscrapers and cramped alleyways. In a small nail salon tucked away on a side street, Emiko Nakahara was hard at work, her skilled fingers moving with precision as she shaped and polished the nails of her latest client.

Emiko, or "Miko" to those who knew her, had always been drawn to the art of making people feel beautiful. As a child, she would spend hours watching old movies and listening to music, imagining herself as a famous actress or pop star - but it was her talent for creating intricate nail designs that ultimately led her to pursue a career in the beauty industry.

Now, at 25 years old, Miko had established herself as one of Shinjuku's top manicurists, known for her attention to detail and creative flair. Her small salon, "Hana's Hands," was a cozy haven where clients could come to relax and unwind, surrounded by the soothing scents of lotus flower and cherry blossom.

As Miko worked on perfecting her latest design - a delicate floral pattern in shades of pink and purple - the door to her salon swung open, admitting a tall, dark-haired stranger who seemed out of place among the bustling streets. His eyes scanned the room, his gaze lingering on Miko as if searching for something... or someone.
