Here's a potential story opening and setting:

---

The rain-soaked streets of Edinburgh were always at their most unforgiving in March, but for Emilia Fothergill, it was just another gloomy day to be spent huddled over her latest manuscript. A bestselling author of historical fiction, Emilia had grown accustomed to the isolation that came with creating worlds and characters in the comfort of her own flat.

As she sipped a lukewarm cup of tea from a chipped mug, Emilia gazed out the steamy window of her cozy apartment, watching as the rain lashed against the grey stone buildings below. The city was coming alive, or rather, coming to life, with the sound of jazz drifting from the nearby pub and the murmur of conversation from the café across the street.

Emilia's own writing was quiet for now, but she knew that was about to change. Her publisher had just sent her a cryptic message – a single sentence, scribbled in haste on a torn piece of paper: "The past is not what it seems." – and Emilia's mind was already racing with possibilities.

As the hours ticked by, the rain showed no signs of letting up, but Emilia felt a thrill of excitement building within her. She had always known that her stories were rooted in the real world, but this latest development seemed to suggest that there was more to the truth than she had ever imagined...
