The sun had barely cracked the horizon over the small town of Willow Creek, casting a warm orange glow over the quiet streets and perfectly manicured lawns. In his small, weathered truck, landscaper Everett Wyatt "Wyatt" Thompson sipped on a lukewarm cup of coffee, gazing out at the sleepy neighborhood he had been serving for years. The soft hum of the engine and the occasional chirp of birds in the nearby trees were the only sounds that broke the stillness of the morning.

Wyatt rubbed the sleep from his eyes, his worn denim overalls a testament to his hard work and dedication to his craft. He had spent the previous evening prepping for this route, carefully planning out which lawns needed mowing, pruning, or fertilization, and checking his equipment one last time before setting off into the fray.

As he pulled up to the first house on the list, a sense of routine washed over him. This was what Wyatt loved about being a landscaper - the freedom to work outdoors, the satisfaction of watching a garden bloom under his care, and the quiet satisfaction of a job well done. Little did he know, this morning would be different from all the others...
