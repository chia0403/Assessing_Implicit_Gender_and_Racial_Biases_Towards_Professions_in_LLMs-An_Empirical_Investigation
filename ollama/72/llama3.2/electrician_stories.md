The rain-soaked streets of downtown Seattle gleamed like polished stone, reflecting the bright lights of the city's skyscrapers like a mirror. It was a chilly autumn evening, and the mist that rose from the Puget Sound hung heavy in the air, casting a mystical veil over the bustling streets.

In a small, cluttered shop nestled between a vintage clothing store and a used bookshop, Electrician Emilia Rodriguez wiped the sweat from her forehead as she struggled to keep up with the demanding pace of her work. The smell of ozone and burnt wire filled her nostrils as she expertly rewired a faulty ceiling fixture, her hands moving with precision and speed.

At 32, Emilia had built a reputation for herself as one of Seattle's top electricians, known for her attention to detail and her ability to get the job done no matter what. But tonight, something felt off. A sense of unease settled in the pit of her stomach, like a nagging whisper that refused to be silenced.

As she finished up the last wire and stepped back to admire her handiwork, Emilia's gaze drifted out the window, into the darkness beyond. The city seemed to stretch on forever, its towering skyscrapers piercing the sky like shards of glass. And in the distance, a faint flicker of light danced across the water, like a ghostly signal beckoning her towards something unknown.

It was then that Emilia felt it - a shiver that ran down her spine and made her skin prickle with gooseflesh. Whatever was happening out there in the city, she knew she was about to get drawn into its midst, whether she wanted it or not.
