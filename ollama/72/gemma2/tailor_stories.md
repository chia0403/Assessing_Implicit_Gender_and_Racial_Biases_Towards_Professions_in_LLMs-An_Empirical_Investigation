The air in Elias Thorne’s workshop hung thick with the scent of lavender and old leather.  Dust motes danced in the shaft of sunlight that pierced through the grime-coated window, illuminating swirling threads of crimson silk and charcoal tweed scattered across his workbench. Outside, London thundered with the clamor of carriages rattling on cobblestone streets and hawkers bellowing their wares. But inside, Elias Thorne, master tailor to the aristocracy, found solace in the quiet hum of his needle and the rustle of fabric. His nimble fingers danced over a bolt of midnight blue velvet, its plush surface smooth against his calloused fingertips. He was crafting a coat for Lord Cavendish, a man known for his opulent tastes and discerning eye –  a challenge Elias relished.

But today, a different kind of challenge awaited him. A ragged envelope bearing no return address lay on the workbench beside the velvet. Curiosity tugged at him, and with a sigh, he set aside his work to open it.
