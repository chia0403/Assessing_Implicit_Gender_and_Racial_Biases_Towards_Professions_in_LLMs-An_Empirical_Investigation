The scent of lavender and dust hung heavy in Amelia Thistlewick's atelier, clinging to the faded velvet drapes and antique Singer machine humming softly in the corner. Sunlight streamed through the tall windows, illuminating motes dancing in the air like miniature constellations. Outside, the cobblestone streets of Edinburgh were alive with the clatter of horse-drawn carriages and the cries of street vendors, a bustling symphony Amelia rarely allowed herself to hear.  

Her world existed within these four walls - bolts of silk whispering secrets, threads shimmering like captured rainbows, and patterns etched in time on her worn drafting table. 

Today, however, was different. A commission had arrived that threatened to disrupt Amelia's carefully ordered world. It wasn't the intricate embroidery or the delicate lacework she prided herself on; it was the sheer audacity of the request. The Lady Charlotte Montgomery, notorious for her extravagance and penchant for the scandalous, demanded a gown unlike any other - a dress woven from moonlight itself.  


Amelia Thistlewick, renowned seamstress to Edinburgh's elite, stared at the letter clutched in her gloved hand, a tremor of both excitement and trepidation rippling through her. Could she, would she, dare to attempt such an impossible feat?
