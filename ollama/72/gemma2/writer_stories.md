Rain lashed against the attic window, mimicking the frantic drumbeat in Amelia Beatrice Hawthorne’s chest. Outside, the old Victorian house groaned under the onslaught of the storm, its ancient timbers protesting with creaks and sighs. Inside, the air hung thick with the scent of dust and decaying paperbacks. Amelia hunched over her battered typewriter, the keys clicking out a staccato rhythm against the relentless drum of the rain. 

The manuscript sat before her, a jumbled mess of crumpled pages and abandoned ideas.  She’d been staring at it for hours, the cursor blinking accusingly at her from the computer screen. The deadline loomed like a hungry wolf, and Amelia felt trapped in its shadow. This novel, "The Shadow of Blackwood Manor," was supposed to be her breakout story, but the words just wouldn't come. 


Her gaze drifted towards the lone window, the storm-tossed branches of an ancient oak tree clawing at the glass like skeletal fingers. A shiver ran down her spine, a prickling sensation that had nothing to do with the chill in the air.  Maybe she needed inspiration. Maybe a walk through the storm would shake loose the creativity that felt so stubbornly out of reach. 


Amelia stood, her joints protesting after hours spent hunched over her desk. She glanced at the clock - midnight was fast approaching. Perhaps just a quick stroll around the overgrown garden...
