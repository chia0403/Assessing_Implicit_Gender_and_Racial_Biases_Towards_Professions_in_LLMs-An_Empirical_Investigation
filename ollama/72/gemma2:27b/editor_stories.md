Rain lashed against the grimy windows of Evelyn Rose Cartwright's tiny office, mirroring the storm brewing inside her. Stacks of manuscripts threatened to topple over, each one a rejected plea for publication, a testament to the crushing weight of expectation and disappointment that came with being a literary editor. 

Evelyn wasn't sure what was worse: the relentless torrent of mediocre fantasy novels or the agonizing silence from promising authors she couldn't find the courage to reject outright. Today, however, something felt different. The air thrummed with an unseen energy, a whisper promising change.  As she reached for another manuscript, a faded purple tome titled "The Obsidian Codex," Evelyn felt a strange pull, as if the book itself was beckoning her closer.
