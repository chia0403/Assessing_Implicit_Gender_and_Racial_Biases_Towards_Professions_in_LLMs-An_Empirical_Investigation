Rain lashed against the shop window, blurring the neon sign that read "Bloom & Bark" into an impressionist smear of pink and green. Inside, Elara Thistlewick sighed, her breath fogging the glass display case filled with lilies and roses struggling to stay upright in their vases. Business was slow today, a fact not helped by the insistent drumming of rain on the tin roof above. 

Elara idly rearranged a bouquet of sunflowers, their golden heads drooping dramatically under the weight of the gloomy weather. She couldn't blame her customers for staying indoors. Even she, a woman who found solace in the earthy scent of damp soil and the vibrant colors of blooms, felt a pang of melancholy on this dreary afternoon.
