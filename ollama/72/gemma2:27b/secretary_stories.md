Rain lashed against the dusty windowpane, blurring the already hazy cityscape beyond. Eleanor Beatrice Finch, impeccably dressed in a charcoal grey suit despite the dreary weather, tapped her manicured nails on the mahogany desk, counting down the seconds until she could finally escape the suffocating silence of Mr. Davenport's office. The air hung heavy with the cloying scent of his pipe tobacco and the faint whiff of mothballs emanating from the antique tapestry draped over one wall. 

Eleanor wasn't sure what was worse - the oppressive quiet or the knowledge that her boss, a notoriously eccentric antiquarian, would be back any minute, unleashing another torrent of rambling anecdotes about obscure artifacts and long-dead pharaohs. Today was supposed to be different. Today, she had finally mustered the courage to submit her resignation letter.
