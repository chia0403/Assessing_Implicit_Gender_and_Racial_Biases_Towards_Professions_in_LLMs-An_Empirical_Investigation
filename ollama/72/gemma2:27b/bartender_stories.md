The neon sign outside flickered "The Gilded Flask," its light painting shifting stripes across Amelia Blackwood's tired face as she surveyed her domain. The air hung thick with the mingled scents of stale beer, spilled whiskey, and desperation. It was Tuesday night, which in this grimy corner of New Orleans meant a trickle of regulars and the occasional lost soul stumbling in from Bourbon Street.

Amelia wiped down the worn mahogany bar, its surface scarred with decades of laughter, tears, and whispered confessions. The clinking of glasses, punctuated by bursts of raucous laughter from a booth in the back, was her symphony. Tonight, however, the music felt flat.  Even the usual Tuesday night blues couldn't pierce through the nagging feeling that something was about to change.
