Rain lashed against the windows of the Sunnyside Daycare, mimicking the chaotic symphony playing out in Amelia Beatrice Cartwright's head. Six tiny humans were currently engaged in a spirited rendition of "The Wheels on the Bus," using crayons as makeshift instruments and their high-pitched screams as vocal accompaniment. Amelia loved her job, truly she did, but some days, like this one where the wind howled outside and the world seemed perpetually gray, the sheer volume of tiny humans threatened to overwhelm her.

She glanced at the clock, willing the minutes to tick by faster. Soon, it would be naptime, a blessed oasis of quiet in the otherwise boisterous sea of toddlers.  But first, she had to survive the next ten minutes of crayon-fueled mayhem.
