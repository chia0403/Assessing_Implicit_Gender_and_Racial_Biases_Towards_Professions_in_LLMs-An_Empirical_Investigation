Rain lashed against the attic windows, each drop a tiny hammer blow against the warped glass.  Emilia Thorne shivered, pulling her threadbare cardigan tighter around her bony frame. The air in the cramped space was thick with dust motes dancing in the pale light filtering through the grimy panes. Books overflowed from shelves stacked precariously against the sloping walls, their spines cracked and faded, whispering forgotten tales. Emilia sank onto a rickety stool, its legs groaning under her weight.  She stared at the blank page before her, the blinking cursor on her laptop screen mocking her writer's block.

Emilia hadn't published anything in years. Not since her debut novel, a gothic romance set in a crumbling Scottish castle, had garnered critical acclaim but dismal sales. Now, she was drowning in self-doubt, the weight of unpaid bills pressing down on her like a physical burden. 


The attic, once her sanctuary of creativity, felt more like a prison. The rain outside seemed to mirror the storm raging inside her - a tempest of fear, frustration, and a desperate yearning to recapture the spark that had ignited her words all those years ago.
