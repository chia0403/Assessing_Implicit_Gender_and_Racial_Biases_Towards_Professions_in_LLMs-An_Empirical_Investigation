The city lights reflected off the wet pavement like a million tiny mirrors, casting a kaleidoscope of colors across the darkening streets of downtown Seattle. It was a chilly autumn evening in 2015, and the air was alive with the hum of rain-soaked traffic and the distant thrum of music from the bars on Pike Street.

In a small office nestled between a used bookstore and a vintage clothing store, systems analyst Emilia Victoria Reyes hunched over her computer, her eyes scanning lines of code with a practiced intensity. A 28-year-old native of Seattle, Emilia had grown up surrounded by the city's vibrant tech scene, but she'd always felt like an outsider among the startups and entrepreneurs who seemed to be making their fortunes by age 25.

As a systems analyst for a mid-sized financial firm, Emilia was content with her work, poring over spreadsheets and debugging code to keep the company's databases running smoothly. But on this particular evening, as she pored over lines of code in preparation for a meeting with her boss the next day, Emilia couldn't shake the feeling that something was off - a nagging sense of unease that had been building inside her all week.

Little did she know, tonight would be the night when everything changed.
