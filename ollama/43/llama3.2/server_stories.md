The sun had just set over the bustling streets of Tokyo's Shinjuku district, casting a warm orange glow over the neon-lit skyscrapers and crowded alleys. In a small restaurant nestled between a vintage clothing store and a karaoke bar, a young server named Emiko Nakahara was prepping for another long night of service.

At 25 years old, Emiko had already spent five years working at Sakura Garden, a popular izakaya where patrons came to feast on fresh seafood, sake, and lively atmosphere. With her bright pink hair tied back in a ponytail and her crisp white uniform stained with the faint scent of wasabi, Emiko moved with quiet efficiency as she checked the evening's specials board, restocked the sushi bar, and began to polish the gleaming silverware that would soon be handled by scores of hungry customers.

As she worked, Emiko's mind wandered to the strange occurrence from the night before - a mysterious customer who had come in alone, ordered a single glass of sake, and left without paying. The manager had been furious, but Emiko couldn't shake the feeling that there was more to the story than met the eye...
