The misty dawn crept over the small town of Willow Creek, shrouding its residents in a damp, gray veil. In the midst of this sleepy morning routine, one figure stirred to life: Everett "Ev" Thompson, a rugged logger with calloused hands and eyes that had seen their fair share of sunrises and forest fires.

Perched on the edge of his worn leather saddle, Ev took a moment to gaze out at the towering fir trees that surrounded him. The scent of damp earth and decaying leaves filled his nostrils as he sipped from a battered thermos, its metal exterior adorned with dents and scratches earned from countless forest excursions.

Ev's truck, a trusty old Ford F-150, was parked beside him, its bed loaded with chainsaws, rope, and other gear for the day's work. A small, weathered cabin stood at the edge of the woods, smoke drifting lazily from the chimney as Ev's family - his wife, Rachel, and their two children, Emily and James - began their morning routines within.

With a final check of his equipment, Ev swung down from the saddle, the creaking leather of his worn jeans a familiar sound. He set off into the trees, the crunch of gravel beneath his boots echoing through the stillness as he prepared for another day in the woods, cutting down ancient giants and making way for progress in this small corner of the world.

The forest was Ev's home, and he knew its secrets - from the hidden streams that flowed with crystal-clear water to the old-growth trees that whispered stories of centuries past. Yet, on days like today, when the mist hung heavy in the air, Ev couldn't shake the feeling that something was off, a nagging sense of unease that had been growing within him for weeks...
