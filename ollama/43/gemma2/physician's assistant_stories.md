The smell of antiseptic hung heavy in the air, thick as fog rolling across the dew-laden grass of the early morning meadow.  It was a scent Amelia Rose Blackwood knew better than her own heartbeat. As PA to Dr. Alistair Finch, she’d spent more hours in this sterile, white room at the edge of town than anywhere else. Outside, the wind whispered through the pines lining the road, but inside, only the hum of the fluorescent lights and the rhythmic click-clack of Amelia’s pen against her notepad broke the silence. 


The small clinic, perched precariously on a hill overlooking the sprawling wheat fields of rural Idaho, was their lifeline in this forgotten corner of the world.  A place where gossip travelled faster than the mailman, and everyone knew everyone else's business, from Mrs. Henderson's new hip to little Timmy's scraped knee. And Amelia, with her calm demeanor and practiced bedside manner, had become a pillar of comfort amidst the chaos. 


But today, something felt different. A tremor of unease danced beneath the surface of her usual composure. The morning air was charged with an unspoken tension, like the stillness before a storm. This wasn't just another day in the clinic; this was the day Amelia would face the unknown.
