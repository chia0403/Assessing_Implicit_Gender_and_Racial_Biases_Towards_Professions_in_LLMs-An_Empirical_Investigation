The fluorescent lights hummed above Ms. Eleanor Amelia Brighton's head, casting a sterile glow over the rows of empty desks in her Advanced Placement Literature classroom. The last bell had rung ten minutes ago, leaving behind an echoing silence that felt thicker than usual. It was Friday afternoon, and a palpable tension hung in the air – the kind that settled like dust after a long week.  

Eleanor sighed, pushing a stray strand of auburn hair out of her face.  Her classroom was usually a vibrant space, buzzing with discussions about Shakespearean sonnets and the intricacies of Hemingway's prose. Today, however, it felt like a tomb, devoid of the energy she craved. 


She glanced at the crumpled note on her desk – another anonymous threat, scrawled in menacing handwriting.  "You're next, Brighton." This wasn't the first one; they had started appearing a week ago, each one more unsettling than the last.
