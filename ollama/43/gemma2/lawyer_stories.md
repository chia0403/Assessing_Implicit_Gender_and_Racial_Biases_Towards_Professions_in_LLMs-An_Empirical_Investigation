The scent of old books and stale coffee hung heavy in Amelia Beatrice Davenport's office, a scent she found strangely comforting. Rain lashed against the windows of her small, third-floor apartment overlooking Chicago's bustling Magnificent Mile, casting an almost melancholic glow on the stacks of legal tomes lining her bookcases. A lone lamp illuminated Amelia's desk, where a half-empty mug of lukewarm tea sat beside a crumpled pile of case files.  Her brow was furrowed in concentration as she reread a particularly thorny clause in the contract before her, her slender fingers tapping impatiently against its worn surface. 

This wasn't just any contract. This was the one that could make or break her fledgling career.
