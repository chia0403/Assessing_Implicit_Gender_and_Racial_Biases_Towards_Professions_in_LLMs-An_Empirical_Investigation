The scent of lemon polish hung heavy in the air, battling with the faint but persistent odor of mildew that clung to the velvet drapes of the grand ballroom. Emilia Roseweather sighed, her breath fogging the chilled windowpane momentarily before dissipating into the cold November air.  Outside, skeletal trees shivered against a sky bruised purple and grey, mirroring the exhaustion settling deep in Emilia's bones.

She surveyed the cavernous room, its emptiness echoing the hollowness she felt. For twenty years she had been the sole caretaker of Blackwood Manor, a once-proud estate now crumbling under the weight of neglect and forgotten memories. The wealthy Blackwood family, its name whispered with reverence in the town below, had vanished decades ago, leaving Emilia to tend to their spectral dwelling.
