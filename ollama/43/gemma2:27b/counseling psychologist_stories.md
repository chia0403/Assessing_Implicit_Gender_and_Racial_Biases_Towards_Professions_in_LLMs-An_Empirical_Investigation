The flickering neon sign outside cast an unsettling green glow across Dr. Eleanor Vance's face as she stared out the rain-streaked window of her office. It was 8:47 pm, nearly two hours past closing time, and the usually bustling streets of downtown San Francisco were eerily deserted. A heavy downpour hammered against the glass, mirroring the storm raging within Eleanor herself. Tonight's session had been unlike any other – a young woman, barely out of her teens, confessed to witnessing something horrific, something she couldn't quite articulate but that left her trembling and haunted. 

Eleanor ran a hand through her already tousled auburn hair, pushing back the fatigue threatening to engulf her. This case was different. It felt…dangerous. The young woman's fear was palpable, woven into the very fabric of her being. Eleanor knew she couldn't simply dismiss it as youthful paranoia or stress. Something was wrong, deeply wrong. 


And Eleanor had a sinking feeling that this was just the beginning.
