Rain lashed against the grimy windowpane of Amelia Rose Cartwright's tiny studio apartment, blurring the neon glow of the "Open 24 Hours" sign across the street.  The air inside was thick with the scent of burnt coffee and despair.  Amelia stared at the glowing screen, her reflection superimposed over a design that looked less like an eye-catching poster for the local art festival and more like a toddler's finger painting gone wrong.

She sighed, running a hand through her already disheveled auburn curls.  Deadlines loomed, inspiration had evaporated, and even the relentless downpour seemed to mock her creative block. Amelia was a graphic designer, or at least that's what her business card proclaimed. Lately, it felt more like she was a "pixel pusher" drowning in mediocrity. 


The phone buzzed on her desk, startling her. It was probably another client, demanding revisions. Or worse, a new project that would push her already overflowing plate to the point of collapse. Amelia hesitated for a moment before answering, bracing herself for the inevitable onslaught.
