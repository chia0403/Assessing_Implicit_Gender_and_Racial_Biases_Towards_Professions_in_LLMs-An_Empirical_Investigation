Rain lashed against the cracked asphalt, each drop exploding in a miniature geyser of grime. A rusted fire escape groaned under the weight of Bartholomew "Barty" Quibble, his tool belt clinking rhythmically against the damp bricks as he scaled the side of Mrs. Goldstein's tenement building. The air was thick with the scent of wet garbage and desperation, a familiar bouquet in this forgotten corner of Brooklyn. Barty tightened his grip on the rusted railing, wincing as another bolt of lightning illuminated the grimy alleyway below. 

He sighed. Another leaky faucet, probably. It seemed every apartment in this building was perpetually weeping. But Mrs. Goldstein was kind, always offering him a warm slice of challah and a steaming cup of tea after he'd wrestled her pipes into submission. Besides, Barty liked the challenge. The labyrinthine plumbing systems of these old buildings were puzzles to be solved, each groan and gurgle revealing a secret about their history.
