Rain lashed against the grimy windows of the Blackwood & Sons office building, blurring the already dismal view of the cobblestone street below. Agatha Thistlewick adjusted her spectacles and sighed, a plume of white fog escaping her lips in the chilly air.  The clack of her typewriter seemed impossibly loud in the near-empty office, punctuated only by the occasional rumble of thunder.

Agatha was alone, as usual. Mr. Blackwood, a man whose temper was as volatile as the London weather, had stormed out hours ago, leaving Agatha to manage the overflowing inbox and a pile of invoices threatening to topple her desk.
