The air hung heavy with the scent of pine needles and damp earth as Elias Blackwood wrestled the behemoth Douglas fir to the ground. Sweat stung his eyes, but he didn't dare wipe it away. Not with his axe still swinging, not with the mountain looming above him like a watchful god.  Elias had been logging these ancient forests since he was a boy, following in his father's footsteps and his grandfather's before him.

The towering pines whispered secrets in the breeze, their needles shimmering emerald green against the bruised purple sky of dusk. Elias knew every creak and groan of this forest, every hidden path and treacherous slope. But today, something felt different. An unsettling stillness hung in the air, broken only by the rhythmic thud of his axe and the distant cry of a raven.
