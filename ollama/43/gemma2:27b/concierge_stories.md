Rain lashed against the arched windows of The Grand Blackwood Hotel, blurring the lights of the Parisian cityscape below into a shimmering watercolor. Inside, the air was thick with the scent of lilies and old money, a heady perfume that Amelia Dubois, Chief Concierge of The Grand Blackwood, inhaled deeply as she straightened a crystal vase on a side table.

Amelia, her name whispered reverently by guests and staff alike, possessed an uncanny ability to anticipate every need, to soothe ruffled feathers with a gentle smile, and to orchestrate the impossible with a flick of her perfectly manicured wrist. Tonight, however, even Amelia’s composure was threatened. A single red rose lay on her polished mahogany desk, its stem stripped bare, a note tucked beneath its velvety petals.

"The Nightingale sings," it read, in elegant cursive that sent a shiver down Amelia's spine. 


This was no ordinary request. This was the beginning of something dangerous. Something that threatened to unravel the meticulously woven tapestry of The Grand Blackwood’s opulent facade.
