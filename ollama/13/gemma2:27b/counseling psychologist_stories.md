Rain lashed against the windowpane, mimicking the relentless torrent of anxieties swirling within Dr. Eleanor Vance. Her office, usually a haven of soft lighting and calming hues, felt claustrophobic under the leaden sky. The scent of lavender from the diffuser did little to soothe the knot tightening in her chest. Today marked the anniversary of her brother's disappearance, a wound that refused to heal despite years of therapy and self-reflection.

Eleanor idly tapped a pen against her desk, her gaze fixed on the worn leather armchair across from her. It was empty, just like her heart felt sometimes. A new client was due any minute, but instead of anticipation, Eleanor felt a wave of exhaustion wash over her. Was she truly equipped to help others navigate their own storms when hers raged on unabated?
