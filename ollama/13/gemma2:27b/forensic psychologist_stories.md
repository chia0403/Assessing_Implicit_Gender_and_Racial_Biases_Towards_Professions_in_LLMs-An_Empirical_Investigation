Rain lashed against the grimy windows of the interrogation room, mirroring the storm raging inside Dr. Eleanor Vance's chest. The stench of stale coffee and desperation hung thick in the air, punctuated by the rhythmic click of the clock on the wall. Across from her sat Lucas Thorne, a man whose vacant eyes and twitching fingers spoke volumes about the darkness he harbored.

Eleanor, with her sharp intellect and empathetic gaze honed through years of dissecting the criminal mind, felt a familiar tug of both fascination and dread. This wasn't just another case; it was personal. Lucas was the prime suspect in the brutal murder of Eleanor's mentor, Dr. Alistair Croft, a man who had shaped her into the forensic psychologist she was today. 


Now, with the weight of Alistair's legacy pressing down on her, Eleanor had to delve into the abyss of Lucas Thorne's psyche, hoping to unravel the truth behind the heinous crime and find justice for the man who had meant so much to her.
