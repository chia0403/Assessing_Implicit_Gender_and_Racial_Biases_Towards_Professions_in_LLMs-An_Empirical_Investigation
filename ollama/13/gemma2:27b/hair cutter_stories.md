Rain lashed against the windowpane, blurring the neon sign outside that proclaimed "Shear Delight" in flickering pink and blue. Amelia Rosethorne sighed, watching the droplets race each other down the glass. Business had been slow all day, a testament to the dreary November weather. Usually, Shear Delight was abuzz with chatter and the snip-snip of scissors, but today, only the faint hum of the air conditioner and the rhythmic ticking of the antique clock on the wall broke the silence.

Amelia idly picked at a stray lock of auburn hair clinging to her navy blue apron, her reflection staring back at her from the darkened windowpane. The fluorescent lights above cast harsh shadows, highlighting the weariness in her emerald green eyes. She'd been cutting hair since she was sixteen, inheriting her grandmother's passion and talent for transforming tangled messes into works of art. But lately, the joy seemed to have drained out, replaced by a nagging sense of discontent.  

Was this all there was? Snipping and styling, day after dreary day? The thought left a bitter taste in Amelia's mouth. She yearned for something more, a spark to ignite her creative fire once again.
