Rain lashed against the windowpanes of Dr. Elias Thorne's dental practice, mirroring the storm brewing inside him. The quaint Victorian building, nestled amidst cobblestone streets and flickering gas lamps in the heart of Edinburgh's old town, usually exuded a comforting warmth. Today, however, it felt as cold and oppressive as the November sky above.

Elias, normally a beacon of cheer with his twinkling blue eyes and infectious grin, sat slumped behind his desk, paperwork piling up like neglected molars. The news from the bank earlier that day had been a gut punch – his practice was in danger of closing due to dwindling clientele. 


The rhythmic tick-tock of the grandfather clock seemed to mock his predicament, each second another nail hammered into the coffin of his dreams. He'd poured his heart and soul into this practice, painstakingly restoring it to its former glory after inheriting it from his beloved grandfather. Now, the very foundation he’d built was crumbling beneath him.
