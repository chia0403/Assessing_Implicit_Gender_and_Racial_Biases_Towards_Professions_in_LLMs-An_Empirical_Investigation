The air in the mahogany-paneled office hummed with anxious energy.  Outside, the relentless August sun beat down on Wall Street, but inside, shadows danced across the faces glued to glowing screens.  Theodore "Teddy" Blackwood, his crisp white shirt already damp with nervous perspiration, tapped a manicured finger against the glass desk. He'd made a bold prediction, one that could catapult him to the top of Davenport & Sons or send his career spiraling into oblivion. 

The fate of millions rested on Teddy's shoulders. Not just his own fortune, but the investments entrusted to him by clients who saw him as their financial savior. Today, he would prove them right, or tragically wrong.
