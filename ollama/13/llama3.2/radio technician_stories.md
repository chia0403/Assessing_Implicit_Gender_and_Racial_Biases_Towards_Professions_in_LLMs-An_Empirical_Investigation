The sun had just set over the small town of Ravenswood, casting a warm orange glow over the dusty streets and worn buildings. Augie, with his messy brown hair and wire-rimmed glasses, was a familiar figure in Ravenswood, known for his expertise in fixing even the most stubborn radios. His workshop, which he'd inherited from his grandfather, was a treasure trove of vintage electronics, dusty manuals, and forgotten memories.

As Augie worked, the sound of crickets and the distant hum of the diner's neon sign provided a soothing background noise. The air was thick with the scent of old metal, worn leather, and the faint hint of his own coffee-fueled sweat. It was a familiar smell that brought him comfort, like a warm blanket on a cold winter night.

Just as Augie was about to give up on the old radio, he heard a knock at the door. He looked up from his workbench, wiping his hands on a greasy rag, and called out, "Come on in!" Little did he know that this unexpected visitor would set off a chain of events that would change his life forever.
