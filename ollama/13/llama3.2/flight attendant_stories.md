The dim fluorescent lights above the galley hummed like a tired bee as Flight Attendant Alexandra Victoria Thompson poured herself another cup of lukewarm coffee from the thermos on the counter. The 3:47 PM departure time was still an hour away, but she had already spent the last twenty minutes reorganizing the overhead bins and restocking the snacks in the galley, her eyes scanning the cramped space with a practiced air.

As she sipped her coffee, Alexandra's gaze wandered out the window of the Boeing 737-800, watching as the sun began its slow descent towards the horizon. The sky was ablaze with hues of pink and orange, a breathtaking sight that usually put her in a good mood, but today it only made her feel restless.

She had been feeling on edge all week, ever since she'd received an unsettling message from someone claiming to be a former passenger who had died in the crash of Flight 121 six months prior. The mysterious note, with its cryptic words and disturbing implications, still haunted her dreams and made it hard for her to sleep at night.

Alexandra shivered, despite the warm cabin air, and took another sip of her coffee as she began to tidy up the galley once more. It was going to be a long flight, and she had a feeling that tonight was going to be one she would never forget.
