The fluorescent lights overhead cast an unforgiving glare over the worn Formica counter, where 22-year-old Alexis "Lexi" Thompson had spent countless hours scanning groceries for customers like her mom, Aunt Karen, and even herself. The smell of stale coffee wafted through the air, mingling with the hum of the refrigerated cases and the occasional clang of a tray being loaded into the dishwasher.

It was 4:30 AM on a drizzly Saturday morning at Sunshine Supermarket, Lexi's part-time job that paid barely enough for her to cover her rent on her small studio apartment. As she mechanically scanned the last item on Mrs. Patel's shopping list – a pack of imported basmati rice – Lexi couldn't shake the feeling that today was going to be different. Maybe it was the strange, lingering smell of gasoline outside or the peculiar look from the store manager, Mr. Lee, as he made his rounds before opening time. Whatever it was, Lexi felt like she was standing at a crossroads, her life – and her future – hanging precariously in the balance.

And then, just as the clock struck 5:00 AM, a loud crash echoed from outside, followed by an eerie silence that seemed to vibrate through every molecule of air...
