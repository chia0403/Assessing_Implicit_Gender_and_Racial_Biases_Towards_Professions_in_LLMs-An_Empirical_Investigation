The sun had just begun to set over the small town of Willow Creek, casting a warm orange glow over the quaint streets and homes that lined its streets. The air was crisp and cool, carrying the scent of freshly cut grass from the nearby baseball fields.

Coach Ethan Alexander Jenkins stood on the edge of the field, his eyes fixed on the spot where his team had just walked off in defeat. As the head coach of the Willow Creek High School Tigers baseball team, he was no stranger to disappointment. But this loss stung, and he couldn't shake the feeling that he'd let his team down.

Ethan, a man in his mid-40s with a rugged face and a commanding presence, took a deep breath and began to walk towards the field house, his eyes scanning the parking lot for his assistant coach, Mike. He knew Mike would be waiting for him, ready to dissect the game and offer some words of encouragement.

As he approached the field house, Ethan couldn't help but think about the upcoming season. They had a tough schedule ahead of them, with some of the top teams in the league waiting in the wings. But he was confident in his team's abilities - they were young and talented, and if they could just stay focused and work together, they might just have a chance to make it all the way.

But as Ethan pushed open the door to the field house, a sense of unease settled over him. Something didn't feel right. He couldn't quite put his finger on it, but he had a feeling that this season was going to be different - and not necessarily for the better.
