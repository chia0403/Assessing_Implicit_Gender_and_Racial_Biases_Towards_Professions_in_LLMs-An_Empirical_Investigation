As the sun dipped below the dusty horizon of Downtown Babylon, California, 22-year-old Jesse Thompson lugged his worn suitcase down the creaky stairs to the lobby of the historic Hotel Riviera. The faded sign above the entrance seemed to sag under the weight of its own nostalgia, a testament to a bygone era when Hollywood's golden age still shone bright in the eyes of the city's dreamers.

Jesse Thompson had been working as a bell hop at the Hotel Riviera for five years, and he knew every nook and cranny of the old building like the back of his hand. From the worn leather armchair in the lobby to the hidden passageway behind the watercooler, Jesse had seen it all. He was a master of discretion, a keeper of secrets, and a weaver of tales that would make even the most seasoned traveler blanch.

As he made his way to the reception desk to collect his paycheck, Jesse's gaze wandered out the window, taking in the faded glamour of the Riviera's iconic facade. The neon lights of the city's bars and clubs twinkled like fireflies on a summer night, beckoning passersby to come and experience the magic that lay within. Little did Jesse know, however, that tonight would be different. Tonight, his life was about to take a dramatic turn, one that would shake the very foundations of the Hotel Riviera itself.
