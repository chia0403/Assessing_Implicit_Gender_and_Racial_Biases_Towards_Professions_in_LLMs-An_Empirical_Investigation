The fog rolled in off the coast of Crescent Bay, shrouding the small logging town of Raven's Peak in a damp, gray mist. It was the kind of morning that made you want to stay indoors with a cup of coffee and a good book, but for Asher Wyatt Jenkins, it was just another day on the job.

Asher, a ruggedly handsome man in his mid-thirties with a strong jawline and piercing blue eyes, stood at the edge of the forest, eyeing the towering spruce trees that loomed before him. His calloused hands rested on the worn grip of his chainsaw, his eyes scanning the terrain for any sign of trouble. As one of the most experienced loggers in Raven's Peak, Asher knew every inch of this land like the back of his hand.

It was a chilly autumn morning, and the air was crisp with the scent of damp earth and decaying leaves. The trees creaked and groaned in the gentle breeze, their branches etched against the pale blue sky like skeletal fingers. It was going to be one of those days - the kind that made you wonder if the forest itself was trying to tell you something, but Asher wasn't one to listen.

Not today, at least.
