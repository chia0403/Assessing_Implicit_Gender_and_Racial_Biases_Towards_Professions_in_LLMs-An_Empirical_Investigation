The humid air hung heavy over Charleston's cobblestone streets, thick with the scent of honeysuckle and salt from the nearby harbor. Amelia "Amy" Cartwright adjusted her straw boater hat, its faded blue ribbon fluttering in the gentle breeze. Tourists shuffled around her feet, eager faces pointed towards the ornate iron gates leading into Rainbow Row, the iconic pastel-colored houses lining East Bay Street.

"Now," Amy announced, her voice clear and melodious despite the heat, "we're about to step back in time..."
