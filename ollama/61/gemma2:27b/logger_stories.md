The air hung heavy with the scent of pine needles and damp earth, a familiar comfort to Jedediah "Jed" Blackwood. Dawn was just breaking, painting the towering Douglas firs in hues of orange and purple. Jed, his face weathered like an old stump, leaned against his trusty Stihl chainsaw, surveying the clearing ahead. Sunlight slanted through the canopy, illuminating a tangle of fallen giants – victims of the winter storms that had ravaged the Cascade Mountains.  He inhaled deeply, the crisp air filling his lungs with a sense of purpose.

Today's job wasn't for the faint of heart. The logging company needed a veteran like Jed to tackle this particular section, where trees lay in precarious positions, tangled and threatening to shift with the slightest disturbance. A shiver ran down his spine – not from fear, but from the thrill of the challenge.
