Rain lashed against the warped windowpanes of Mrs. Gwendolyn Hawthorne’s office, mimicking the tumult in her own chest. The air hung thick with the scent of stale coffee and teenage angst, an aroma familiar to any high school guidance counselor. Outside, the manicured lawns of Willow Creek High were slicked with rainwater, reflecting the gloomy November sky.

Gwen sighed, pushing a stray curl back behind her ear. The fluorescent lights buzzed overhead, casting a sterile glow on the cluttered desk in front of her. Papers overflowed from folders, overflowing onto the floor beside stacks of outdated college brochures. Her gaze drifted to the poster on the wall, a hopeful montage of smiling graduates in caps and gowns, their futures shimmering with promise. Today, though, Gwen felt anything but hopeful. The phone on her desk buzzed incessantly, each ring a reminder of the heavy burden she carried – the weight of young lives teetering on the precipice of adulthood.
