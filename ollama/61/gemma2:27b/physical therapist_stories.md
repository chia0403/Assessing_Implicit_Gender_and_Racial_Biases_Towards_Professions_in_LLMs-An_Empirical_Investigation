Rain lashed against the windows of the small brick building, blurring the neon "Open" sign hanging crookedly above the door. Inside, Eleanor Vance sighed, her breath fogging the already steamed-up glass pane separating her tiny reception area from the treatment room. It was barely past 5pm, and she'd already seen six patients, each with their own symphony of aches and pains.

Eleanor loved helping people regain their mobility, but today, even the usual satisfaction felt muted. The relentless grey sky mirrored the melancholy that had settled in her chest. She missed working in a bustling hospital setting, surrounded by other therapists and nurses, sharing stories and laughter during breaks. Here, running her own practice in this sleepy coastal town, the silence was often deafening.
