Rain lashed against the attic windowpane, mimicking the frantic drumming of Amelia Blackwood's fingers on her laptop keyboard. Her cramped writing nook, tucked away in the eaves of her grandmother's rambling Victorian home, was bathed in the flickering glow of an antique desk lamp, casting long shadows that danced with the wind. Amelia, a woman whose spirit mirrored the stormy skies outside, wrestled with a particularly stubborn plot point in her latest gothic thriller.

The scent of woodsmoke and lavender wafted from downstairs, where her grandmother, Eleanor Blackwood, was undoubtedly stirring a pot of her famous chamomile tea.  Amelia sighed, running a hand through her tangle of auburn hair. Her deadline loomed like a specter, but the words refused to flow. Perhaps a change of scenery was in order.
