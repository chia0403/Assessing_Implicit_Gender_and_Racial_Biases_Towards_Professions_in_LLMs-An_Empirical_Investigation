Rain lashed against the grimy windows of the Blackwood & Sons Import-Export office, blurring the already hazy cityscape beyond.  The rhythmic clatter was punctuated by the insistent chirping of the ancient phone on Penelope Ainsworth’s desk – a sound as familiar and unwelcome as the damp chill that permeated the room. Penelope sighed, pushing a stray curl behind her ear and reaching for the receiver with a practiced hand. 

"Blackwood & Sons, Penelope speaking," she intoned, her voice crisp despite the weariness settling in her bones.

The office, a dimly lit warren of overflowing filing cabinets and dust-laden stacks of paper, was Penelope's domain. For ten years she had meticulously kept this chaotic ship afloat, fielding calls, managing appointments, and deciphering the often indecipherable scrawl of her employers - the enigmatic Blackwood brothers: Arthur, shrewd and calculating, and Theodore, flamboyant and unpredictable. 

But today, something felt different. The air hung heavy with unspoken tension, a prickle beneath Penelope’s skin. It was as if the storm raging outside mirrored a brewing tempest within the very walls of Blackwood & Sons.
