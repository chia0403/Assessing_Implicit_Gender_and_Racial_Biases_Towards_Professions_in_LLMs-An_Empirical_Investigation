Rain lashed against the windowpanes of the rehabilitation center, blurring the vibrant autumn foliage outside into an impressionist painting of reds and oranges. Amelia Rose Cartwright sighed, her breath fogging the glass momentarily. The dreary weather mirrored the mood she'd been wrestling with all week. 

Three months ago, Amelia had transferred from a bustling private practice to this underfunded community center, driven by a desire to make a tangible difference in people’s lives. But lately, the reality of limited resources and overwhelming patient needs felt like an insurmountable wall.


This morning's caseload was already proving challenging. She had a stroke survivor struggling with basic hand movements, a young man grappling with the aftermath of a debilitating accident, and Mrs. Henderson, whose worsening arthritis threatened her independence. Amelia knew she couldn’t save everyone, but the weight of their expectations pressed heavily on her shoulders.
