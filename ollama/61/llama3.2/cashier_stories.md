The fluorescent lights above the checkout counter hummed in unison with the beeps of the scanner as Emilia Rose Thompson rang up the evening's final customer. It was 9:45 PM on a Friday, and the small convenience store where she worked was closing its doors for the night. The air was thick with the scent of stale cigarettes and yesterday's coffee, and the sounds of the city outside - car horns and chatter - seemed to recede into the background as Emilia processed the transaction.

As she handed the customer their change and wished them a good night, Emilia's gaze wandered out the window at the crowded streets of downtown. She had always loved this time of day, when the city felt like it was humming along with her own thoughts. But tonight, something about the evening seemed off. The sky was darker than usual, and the shadows cast by the streetlights seemed to twist and writhe on the pavement.

As she locked up the register and began to count down the till, Emilia couldn't shake the feeling that something was waiting for her out there, just beyond the edge of town's bright lights.
