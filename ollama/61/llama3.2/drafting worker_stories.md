---

The fog rolled in off the canals, shrouding the city of Venice in a damp, grey mist that seemed to cling to every stone and brick like a bad omen. It was a chill autumn evening, and the year was 1888.

Amidst the winding streets and ornate bridges, one man navigated the fog-shrouded alleys with ease. His name was Alessandro Bianchi, a skilled drafting worker with a keen eye for precision and a talent for bringing even the most intricate designs to life on paper. As he walked, his long coat billowing behind him like a dark cloud, he carried a worn leather satchel slung over one shoulder, containing all the tools of his trade: pencils, pens, and papers.

Alessandro was on his way to meet his client, a wealthy merchant named Signor Rossi, who had commissioned him to create a detailed drawing of the new palace being built for his daughter's wedding. The job was a lucrative one, but Alessandro knew it would be a challenge – the palace's architect, a brilliant but reclusive man named Signor Moretti, was notorious for his demands and his ability to turn even the most promising young draftsman into a quivering mess.

As Alessandro turned a corner onto Campo Santa Marina, the sound of laughter and music drifted from the nearby piazza, where a group of revelers were celebrating the harvest season with torches, masks, and merriment. But Alessandro's thoughts were not on the festivities; he was too busy lost in thought, his mind already turning over the problems that lay ahead – and wondering what secrets Signor Moretti might be hiding behind those piercing eyes.
