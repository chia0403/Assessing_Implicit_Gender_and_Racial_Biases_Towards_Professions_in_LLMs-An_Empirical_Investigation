Here's a story opening and setting for you:

The snow fell gently over the small town of Willow Creek, casting a serene silence over its residents as they went about their day. It was a crisp winter morning in late January, with the sun just starting to peek above the horizon. The streets were empty, save for the occasional passerby hurrying to and fro.

In a small, wooden building at the edge of town, Guard Everett Wynter stood watch outside the Willow Creek Police Department's makeshift jail cell. He was a tall, imposing figure with piercing blue eyes and a strong jawline, his dark hair flecked with threads of grey that only appeared when he grew particularly concerned or tired.

Everett had been a guard for nigh on ten years, ever since he'd returned to Willow Creek after serving in the military. He took pride in keeping the peace and protecting its residents from harm, even if it meant putting up with the occasional grumpy deputy or cuffed prisoner. As he scanned the surrounding streets, his hand rested on the butt of his holstered pistol, a reassuring presence that told him all was well.

The small town's tranquility was about to be shattered, however. A faint commotion had started brewing just over by the local diner, and Everett could sense something amiss. The sound of raised voices and scraping chairs drifted out into the morning air, accompanied by an undercurrent of menace that made Everett's instincts sit up and take notice...
