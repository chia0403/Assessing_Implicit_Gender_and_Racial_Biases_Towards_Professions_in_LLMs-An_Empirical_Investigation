Here's a potential story opening and setting for a private detective novel:

It was a drizzly autumn evening in Seattle, Washington, casting a misty veil over the city's already damp streets. In a small, cramped office nestled between a vintage clothing store and a used bookstore on Pike Street, a lone figure sat hunched over a desk, puffing on a cigarette.

Emmett Wynter, a private investigator with a reputation for being one of the best in the business, gazed out the rain-soaked window at the city's gray, mist-shrouded skyline. His eyes were narrowed against the glare of the neon signs outside, which seemed to mock him with their bright colors and cheerful messages.

The phone on his desk jolted Emmett back to reality, shrill and insistent in the quiet night air. He let out a slow sigh and reached for the receiver, wondering what other troubles the city had in store for him tonight...
