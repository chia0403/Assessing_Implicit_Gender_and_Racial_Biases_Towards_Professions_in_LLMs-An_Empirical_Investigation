As the last wisps of sunlight faded from the windows of Oakwood High School, Dr. Kathryn "Katie" Thompson made her way down the deserted hallway, the soft hum of the fluorescent lights above her casting an eerie glow over the empty classrooms. She had spent the day mediating disputes and doling out advice to her students, but tonight she was alone with her thoughts.

The 3:45 PM bus had long since departed, leaving behind a sea of quiet desks and chairs, each one a testament to the countless hours that Katie had spent guiding Oakwood's teenagers through the trials and tribulations of adolescence. As a guidance counselor at one of the city's most struggling high schools, Katie had seen it all – from cafeteria fights over social media drama to students struggling with mental health issues in the wake of family tragedies.

Despite the challenges, Katie loved her job. She took pride in knowing each student by name and being able to offer them a listening ear, a supportive word, or a much-needed nudge in the right direction. But as she made her way to her office, tucked away in a corner of the school's administrative wing, Katie couldn't shake the feeling that something was off. A nagging sense of unease had been building inside her all day, and now, with the sun dipping below the horizon, it felt like it was reaching a fever pitch.

She pushed open the door to her office, revealing a space that was both sanctuary and battleground – a place where she could retreat from the chaos outside, but also confront the very real challenges that faced her students every day. Katie took a deep breath, letting the familiar scent of old books and stale coffee fill her lungs, and began to sort through the stack of papers on her desk...
