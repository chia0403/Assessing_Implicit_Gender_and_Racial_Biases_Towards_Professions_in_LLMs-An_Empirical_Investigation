The rain-soaked streets of New Haven gleamed like polished stone as Dr. Evelyn Anne Thompson stepped out of her small apartment building, her bright blue eyes scanning the darkening sky. She pulled her hood over her short, curly brown hair and began the familiar walk to the coffee shop where she met with her client for the night.

As a systems analyst for one of the city's largest tech firms, Evelyn had spent countless nights like this poring over lines of code and debugging complex software issues. But tonight was different. Tonight, she was on the hunt for something more sinister.

Evelyn had been hired by the city's mayor to investigate reports of a mysterious vulnerability in the city's central database system. The mayor claimed it was nothing to worry about, just a minor glitch that could be fixed with some routine maintenance. But Evelyn knew better. She had seen what this kind of thing could do - give governments and corporations an unbridled amount of power over their citizens.

She pulled open the door to "The Daily Grind" coffee shop and was enveloped in the warm glow of fluorescent lights. The aroma of freshly brewed coffee filled her nostrils, and she smiled wryly as she spotted her client waiting for her at a small table by the window. It was only going to be a long night.
