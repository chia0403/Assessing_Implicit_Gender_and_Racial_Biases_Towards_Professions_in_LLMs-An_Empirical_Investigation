Here's a potential story opening:

The rain poured down on the streets of downtown Portland like a dirty blanket, casting a gloomy mist over the already drab office buildings. Inside the accounting firm of Smith & Co., Emily Jane Wilson sat hunched over her desk, staring blankly at the rows of numbers and financial reports that seemed to stretch on forever.

As an accountant with over ten years of experience, Emily had seen it all - the triumphs and the tragedies, the successes and the failures. But tonight was different. Tonight, she was facing a problem that made her stomach twist into knots every time she thought about it: the audit notice from the IRS.

Emily took a deep breath and tried to focus on the task at hand, but her mind kept wandering back to the phone call she had received earlier that day from her boss, Mr. Johnson. He had sounded worried and nervous, and his words still echoed in Emily's ears like a warning: "The numbers don't add up, Emily. We need you to dig deep and find out what's going on."

With a sigh, Emily pushed her chair back and stood up, the creak of the wooden legs echoing through the empty office. She made her way to the window and gazed out at the rain-soaked city, feeling a sense of unease wash over her. What was she hiding? And from whom?

The clock on her computer screen read 8:45 PM. It was too late for the audit notice to be just a routine mistake, but it was also too early to believe that anyone would actually show up at the office in this kind of weather...
