The sun had barely cracked the horizon over the dense forest of Ashwood, casting a warm orange glow over the towering trees that seemed to stretch up to the sky like giants. In a small clearing surrounded by a babbling brook and a tangle of underbrush, Jedediah "Jed" Thompson stood atop his worn boots, surveying the landscape with a practiced eye.

As a logger for the local timber company, Jed had spent countless days working in these woods, learning the rhythms and secrets of the forest from his father and grandfather before him. He knew every twist and turn, every hidden stream and ancient tree that held some secret or other. And yet, on this particular morning, he couldn't shake the feeling that something was off.

It started with a strange dream the night before - a vivid, disturbing vision of flames licking at the trunks of the very trees he'd spent his life harvesting. He'd woken up in a cold sweat, his heart pounding in his chest, and now he couldn't get the image out of his mind.

Jed took a deep breath, feeling the familiar scent of pine and damp earth fill his lungs. It was going to be a long day ahead, but he was ready for it. He shouldered his pack and began making his way down from the clearing, the worn leather creaking beneath him as he moved. The forest was waiting, and Jed was eager to face whatever challenges lay within.
