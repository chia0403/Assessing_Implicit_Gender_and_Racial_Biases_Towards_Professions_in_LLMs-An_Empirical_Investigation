The neon sign outside flickered erratically, casting strobing shadows across the rain-slicked pavement. A steady drizzle fell on The Rusty Mug, blurring the world into a hazy watercolour painting. Inside, the air was thick with the scent of stale beer, regret, and cheap perfume. 

Bartholomew "Bart" Hawthorne wiped down the counter with a practiced hand, his movements fluid and economical despite the damp chill clinging to him. The rhythmic clinking of glasses against the wood punctuated the quiet murmur of conversation from the few patrons huddled around their drinks. Bart knew them all by name, by story, by the way they held their liquor. He was the keeper of secrets whispered over dimly lit tables, the confidante to lonely souls seeking solace in a warm shot and a sympathetic ear. 


Tonight, though, felt different. A prickle of anticipation danced on the edge of Bart’s awareness, a feeling that something unusual was about to happen.  He glanced at the clock – just past midnight. It was always around this time things got interesting.
