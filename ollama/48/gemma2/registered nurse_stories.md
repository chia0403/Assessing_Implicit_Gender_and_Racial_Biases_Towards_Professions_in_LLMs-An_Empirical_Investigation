The fluorescent lights hummed above Eleanor Blackwood's head, casting a sickly yellow pallor over the sterile white walls of the emergency room. A symphony of beeps, groans, and muffled sobs filled the air - a familiar lullaby to Eleanor after fifteen years on this floor. Her name tag, pinned neatly to her scrubs, read "Eleanor B., RN." But to the patients, she was simply 'Nurse Ellie,' a beacon of calm amidst the chaos, a steady hand offering comfort in moments of raw fear and pain. 

Tonight, however, felt different. A palpable tension hung in the air, thicker than the antiseptic scent clinging to everything. The usual flurry of activity had intensified, nurses rushing between beds like frantic bees, doctors barking orders into phones, their faces etched with concern.  A sense of foreboding settled over Eleanor, a prickle on the back of her neck that whispered of something out of the ordinary about to unfold.
