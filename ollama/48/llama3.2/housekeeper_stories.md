The rain-soaked streets of New Haven gleamed like polished stone as Emilia Rose Thompson stepped out of the misty dawn, her worn shoes making soft squelching sounds on the pavement. She adjusted the faded apron wrapped around her waist and took a deep breath, letting the damp air fill her lungs. It was going to be one of those days.

Emilia Rose had been the housekeeper at Ravenswood Manor for five years, ever since she'd taken the job with promises of steady pay and a roof over her head. She knew every creak in the old wooden floorboards, every hidden nook behind the dusty portraits, and every secret whispered by the wind through the glass panes. But despite her familiarity with the manor's intricate web of corridors and chambers, Emilia Rose couldn't shake the feeling that she was just a pawn in someone else's game.

As she made her way to the kitchen, the soft clinking of dishes and the hiss of the kettle accompanied her footsteps, punctuating the silence like a steady heartbeat. The morning light filtering through the grimy windows cast long shadows across the room, where the smell of old coffee beans mingled with the scent of stale air.

The phone on the wall jolted Emilia Rose from her reverie, shrill and insistent. She hesitated for a moment before answering, her fingers hovering over the receiver like she was afraid of what might come next.

"Ravenswood Manor," she said softly, her voice hesitant in the silence that followed.

The words "Mrs. Jenkins' birthday" spilled out of the phone, leaving Emilia Rose feeling as though she'd been punched in the gut.
