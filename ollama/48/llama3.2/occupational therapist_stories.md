Lexi pulled her waterproof jacket tighter around her shoulders as she walked towards the hospital's main entrance. Yet today, as she gazed out at the drizzly cityscape, Lexi felt anything but calm.

It had been three months since her own world had come crashing down around her – and it still hadn't fully stopped spinning. The accident that had left her with a broken collarbone and a lingering sense of disorientation was still fresh in her mind, and the hospital where she worked was no longer the sanctuary it once had been.

Lexi took a deep breath, mentally preparing herself for the long day ahead. She would help patients regain their motor skills, learn to navigate everyday challenges, and rebuild their confidence. But as she pushed through the revolving doors, she couldn't shake the feeling that something was waiting just beneath the surface – waiting to disrupt the carefully constructed world of rehabilitation, and force her to confront the unknown all over again.
