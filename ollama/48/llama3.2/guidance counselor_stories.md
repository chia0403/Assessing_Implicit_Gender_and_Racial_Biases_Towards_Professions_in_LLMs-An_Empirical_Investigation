The dim fluorescent lights above the rows of worn lockers cast a faint glow over the crowded hallway of Lincoln High School. It was just another typical Monday morning at the school, with students scrambling to get to their first-period classes on time. But amidst the chaos, one person stood out - not for her stylish outfit or trendy haircut, but for her calm and collected demeanor.

Emilia Victoria Reynolds, a guidance counselor in her mid-thirties, made her way through the throngs of students, offering words of encouragement and advice to those who sought it. Her warm smile and empathetic ear had earned her a reputation as one of the most trusted and respected figures on campus.

As she walked down the hallway, Emilia's eyes scanned the sea of faces, searching for any sign of trouble or distress. She knew that beneath the surface, many of these students were struggling with issues that seemed insurmountable - from bullying and social pressure to academic stress and family drama. But Emilia was determined to be a source of comfort and support, using her years of experience and training to help them navigate the complexities of high school life.

But on this particular morning, something felt off. A nagging sense of unease settled in the pit of Emilia's stomach as she approached her office, a small room tucked away near the library. There was a note on her desk, scribbled in hasty handwriting: "Meet me at my desk at 2 PM. Come alone."
