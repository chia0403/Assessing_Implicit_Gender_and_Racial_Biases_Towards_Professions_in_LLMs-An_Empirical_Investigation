The dimly lit streets of Ravenswood were always at their most treacherous after midnight. The fog that rolled in off the nearby moor clung to the buildings like a damp shroud, obscuring the faces of passersby and casting long shadows across the cobblestones. It was a night like any other for Guard Edwin Thistlewaite, a man who had spent the better part of a decade patrolling these very streets, ever vigilant for the whispers of trouble that seemed to emanate from every corner.

Edwin stood at the edge of the market square, his eyes scanning the crowded stalls and torch-lit facades with a practiced air. His uniform was immaculate, his greatcoat worn but serviceable, and his lantern held high as he made his way through the throngs of revelers. He had seen it all before - the rowdy gangs of drunken sailors, the desperate beggars huddled in doorways, the whispered secrets shared between shadowy figures. But tonight felt different. Tonight felt like something was waiting just beyond the edge of perception.

And then, out of the corner of his eye, Edwin caught a glimpse of her.
