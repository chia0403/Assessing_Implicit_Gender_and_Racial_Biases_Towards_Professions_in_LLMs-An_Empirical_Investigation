The sun was setting over the small town of Oakdale, casting a warm orange glow over the worn-out streets and faded neon signs. In a dusty garage on Elm Street, 32-year-old Jaxon "Jax" Ryder wiped the sweat from his forehead as he surveyed the latest project before him - a '68 Chevy Camaro with a mysterious sputtering problem.

As the owner's son, Jax had grown up working in his father's garage, learning the ins and outs of cars from a young age. Now, after years of honing his skills and building a reputation as one of Oakdale's best auto mechanics, he was determined to put down roots and make a name for himself.

Outside, the sounds of crickets and distant laughter drifted in through the open garage door, mingling with the smell of gasoline, oil, and worn leather. Jax smiled wryly to himself - he knew this wasn't going to be an easy night. The Camaro's engine was being finicky, and Jax had a feeling it was going to take more than just a spark plug to fix.

Just as he was about to get started, the garage door creaked open again, and his best friend, 25-year-old Lila "Lil" Sanchez, walked in with a look of determination on her face. "Jax, I need your help," she said, her voice low and urgent. "My dad's old buddy is in trouble."
