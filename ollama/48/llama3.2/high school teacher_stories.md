The sun was setting over the small town of Willow Creek, casting a warm orange glow over the quaint Main Street. The smell of fresh-cut grass and sizzling burgers wafted from the local diner, where the sound of laughter and clinking dishes filled the air. It was Friday evening, and the town was buzzing with excitement for the weekend.

In a small classroom on the second floor of Willow Creek High School, Ms. Emilia Sophia Patel was preparing for her last class of the week - Advanced Placement English Literature and Composition. A 35-year-old teacher with a Ph.D. from a prestigious university, Emilia had spent years honing her craft and building a reputation as one of the most dedicated and passionate educators in the school.

As she poured herself a cup of lukewarm coffee from the thermos on her desk, Emilia gazed out the window at the quiet streets below. She loved this time of day - the stillness before the chaos of the weekend set in, when the only sounds were the gentle hum of the fluorescent lights overhead and the occasional chirp of a bird outside.

Just as she was about to begin her lesson plan for the night, Emilia's phone buzzed with an incoming text from an unknown number. Her heart skipped a beat as she hesitated, wondering who could be reaching out to her so late on a Friday evening.
