The sun was setting over the small town of Ravenswood, casting a warm orange glow over the quaint streets and homes. In a small workshop nestled between a vintage clothing store and a bustling diner, Carpenter's son, Julian Atticus Flynn, was finishing up his latest project - a beautiful wooden rocking chair for the town's elderly librarian.

As he carefully applied the final coat of finish, the sound of sizzling burgers and lively chatter from the diner wafted through the open window, mingling with the scent of sawdust and wood oil that filled the workshop. Julian, a ruggedly handsome man in his mid-thirties with a strong jawline and piercing blue eyes, wiped the sweat from his forehead as he stepped back to admire his handiwork.

The rocking chair was a masterpiece - intricately carved with flowers and leaves, its wooden slats worn smooth by years of gentle use. Julian took pride in every project that left his workshop, knowing that they would become treasured possessions for the people who received them. He had always loved woodworking, learning from his father as a young boy and eventually opening his own shop in Ravenswood.

As he locked up the workshop for the day, Julian couldn't help but feel a sense of contentment wash over him. Life was simple here - just him, his tools, and the quiet rhythms of the town. But tonight felt different. Tonight, he had a feeling that something was about to disrupt the tranquility of Ravenswood's peaceful existence...
