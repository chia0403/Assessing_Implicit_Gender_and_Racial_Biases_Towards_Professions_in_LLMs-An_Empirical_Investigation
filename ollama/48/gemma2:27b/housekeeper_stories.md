Rain lashed against the arched windows of Blackwood Manor, each gust like a skeletal hand clawing at the ancient stone. Inside, Eleanor Finchley meticulously polished a silver candelabra, her movements as precise and rhythmic as a clockwork mechanism.  The flickering gaslight cast long shadows across the cavernous hall, illuminating dust motes dancing in the air and the faded grandeur of the once-opulent space. A heavy silence pressed down on Eleanor, broken only by the insistent drumming of the rain and the occasional creak of the manor settling around her.

Eleanor knew every nook and cranny of Blackwood Manor. For twenty years she had been its silent guardian, keeping its secrets safe behind polished surfaces and perfectly starched linens. 


What mysteries lurked within the manor's walls? What was Eleanor's connection to this place?
