The air in Eloise Amelia Beaumont's workshop hung thick with the scent of lavender and fresh linen. Sunlight streamed through the dusty windows, illuminating motes dancing in the air and casting long shadows across the chaos of fabric bolts, spools of thread, and half-finished garments draped over dress forms. Outside, London buzzed with the energy of a city on the brink of change; gas lamps flickered to life as twilight deepened, heralding a new era of industry and progress.  But within these four walls, time seemed to move differently. Eloise, her nimble fingers deftly weaving a tapestry of silk and velvet, existed in a world governed by the rhythmic click of her shears and the whispered rustle of fabric.
