It was a drizzly autumn evening in Portland, Oregon, and graphic designer Emilia "Mia" Thompson sat hunched over her desk, staring at the computer screen in front of her. The soft glow of the monitor cast an eerie light on her pale skin as she sipped on a lukewarm cup of coffee that had long since lost its flavor.

Outside, the rain drummed against the skylights above, casting a rhythmic melody over the small studio apartment that served as Mia's workspace. Her cluttered desk was a testament to her creative chaos - sketches and design samples littered the surface, alongside scraps of paper with half-baked ideas scrawled in hasty handwriting.

As she scrolled through her social media feed, Mia's eyes landed on an old photo of herself taken during her college days. She was beaming with pride, clutching a certificate from the prestigious art school where she had honed her skills and developed a passion for graphic design that only grew stronger with time.

But life hadn't quite turned out as planned. After graduating, Mia had struggled to make ends meet, taking on freelance projects and working multiple part-time jobs just to keep her head above water. Her portfolio was full of promising work, but her bank account was always thin.

As the rain continued to fall outside, casting a melancholy mood over the city, Mia couldn't shake off the feeling that she was stuck in limbo - her creative spark still burning bright, but her financial future looking increasingly uncertain...
