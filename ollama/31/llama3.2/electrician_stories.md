The stormy night air clung to the streets of Ravenswood like a damp shroud, casting long shadows across the deserted sidewalks. Inside his small workshop, Electrician Elianore "Eli" Thompson hunched over a tangled mess of wires and circuit boards, his eyes narrowed in concentration as he worked to diagnose the faulty wiring on a vintage espresso machine.

The scent of burnt insulation and ozone hung heavy in the air, mingling with the sweet tang of freshly brewed coffee wafting from the machine's now-silent brewer. Eli's fingers moved deftly over the components, his hands stained with grease and his hair slick with sweat as he coaxed the recalcitrant electronics back into working order.

As a self-taught electrician, Eli had learned to trust his instincts and rely on his own two eyes – or in this case, one eye, since the other was still recovering from the last repair job that had left him with a nasty paper cut. He worked in the dimly lit workshop, surrounded by tools and parts he'd scavenged from junkyards and salvage yards over the years, each piece telling a story of its own.

Outside, the storm raged on, its fury a reminder that even in the quietest corners of town, there was always something brewing – whether it was a tangled mess of wires or a mysterious hum in the darkness. Eli's thoughts turned to the phone call he'd received earlier that day, from an unknown number with a single phrase that had left him feeling more curious than concerned: "The lights are about to go out."
