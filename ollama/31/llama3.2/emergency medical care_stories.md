The storm had been brewing for hours, its dark clouds rolling in off the Gulf like an army of grey giants. The wind howled through the streets of New Orleans, whipping palm fronds into a frenzy and sending debris flying through the air. In the midst of this chaos, Emily Elizabeth "Evie" LaFleur stood at the edge of the hospital's helipad, her eyes fixed on the emergency helicopter as it touched down with a thud.

As an emergency medical technician (EMT) for Orleans Parish Hospital, Evie had seen her fair share of chaotic scenes. But there was something about this storm that seemed different. Maybe it was the way the wind seemed to carry a malevolent energy, or the way the rain pounded against the pavement like a thousand tiny drums. Whatever it was, Evie felt a shiver run down her spine as she watched the helicopter's doors swing open and a young woman stumble out into the storm.

The woman was soaked to the bone, her eyes wild with fear and her face pale as alabaster. She clutched a small child tightly in her arms, the little one whimpering and clinging to Evie like a lifeline. As Evie rushed forward to help, she felt a surge of adrenaline coursing through her veins. This was what she was trained for - the thrill of the unknown, the rush of adrenaline that came with saving lives in the midst of chaos.

But as she took the woman and child from them, Evie couldn't shake the feeling that something was off. The woman's eyes seemed to hold a deep-seated fear, a sense of desperation that went far beyond just trying to get out of the storm. And then there were the strange symbols etched into her palms...
