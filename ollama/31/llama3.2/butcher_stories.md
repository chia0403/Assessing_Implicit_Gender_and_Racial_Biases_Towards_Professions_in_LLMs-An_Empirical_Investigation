Here's a potential story opening:

The snowflakes danced in the flickering candlelight of the old butcher shop, casting a warm orange glow over the rows of cured meats and freshly butchered steaks. The sign above the door creaked in the gentle breeze, reading "Emmett Flynn & Sons" in bold, golden letters. Emmett Flynn, son of the late Gus Flynn, stood behind the counter, his eyes scanning the crowded streets of Ashwood as he waited for the morning's first customers.

It was a chilly winter evening in late December, and the small town of Ashwood was bustling with holiday shoppers and diners seeking to fill their freezers with hearty stews and roasts. Emmett's shop, nestled between a vintage bookstore and a bakery, had been a staple of the community for generations. His family had been butchers since before he was born, and Emmett took pride in carrying on the tradition.

As he wiped down the counter with a dirty rag, his mind wandered to the special order he had placed earlier that day - a massive roasted boar for the town's annual Christmas feast. It was a big job, one that required careful planning and attention to detail, but Emmett was up for the challenge. After all, he was the only butcher in Ashwood who still used traditional methods, passed down from his father and grandfather before him.

Just as Emmett was starting to relax, the doorbell above the entrance jangled, signaling the arrival of the first customers of the evening...
