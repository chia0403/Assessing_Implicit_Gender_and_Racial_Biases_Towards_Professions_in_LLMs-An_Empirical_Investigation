The flickering fluorescent lights of Northwood High's guidance office cast long shadows across the cluttered desk of Penelope Hawthorne, a woman whose kind eyes held an ocean of teenage anxieties. The air hummed with the quiet desperation of adolescence - forgotten homework assignments scrawled on sticky notes, crumpled college applications tossed haphazardly into overflowing inboxes, and the faint scent of stale pizza lingering from last week's student council meeting.  Penelope sighed, pushing a stray lock of auburn hair behind her ear. Today was "College Decision Day," a day that promised equal parts elation and heartbreak for Northwood's seniors.

As she glanced at the clock ticking relentlessly on the wall, Penelope braced herself for the emotional rollercoaster ahead. The first student was already waiting outside, his nervous tapping audible through the thin door.  Penelope took a deep breath, reminding herself of her mantra: "Every student has a path."  Today, she would guide them, one decision at a time.
