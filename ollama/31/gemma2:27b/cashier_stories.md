The fluorescent lights hummed overhead, casting a sickly yellow glow over the aisles of Bargain Basket.  The air hung thick with the scent of discount cleaning supplies and day-old pastries from the bakery section. Behind her register, Eloise Beaumont tapped a rhythm on the counter, her gaze drifting listlessly over the queue of impatient shoppers.

It was Tuesday, which meant double coupons and an influx of elderly bargain hunters armed with wrinkled flyers and dollar bills held together by rubber bands. Eloise sighed, wishing she was anywhere but here. Stuck in this retail purgatory, counting pennies and scanning expired yogurt for minimum wage, she yearned for something more.  Anything more.
