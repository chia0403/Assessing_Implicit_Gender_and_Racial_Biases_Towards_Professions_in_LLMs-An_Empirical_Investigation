Rain lashed against the windows of the small clinic, blurring the already fading light of dusk into an impressionist smear. Inside, Eloise Amelia Davenport meticulously cleaned her instruments, the rhythmic clinking a counterpoint to the storm raging outside.  The air smelled of antiseptic and something else, a faint metallic tang that she couldn't quite place. It was probably just the lingering scent of blood from Mrs. Henderson’s stubborn sprained ankle earlier that afternoon.

Still, Eloise felt a prickle of unease crawl up her spine. This rural Alaskan town of Sitka Creek wasn’t exactly known for its excitement, but tonight felt different. The wind howled with an unnatural intensity, and the power flickered ominously.
