The air in the Grand Blackwood Library hung heavy with the scent of aged paper and leather. Sunlight, filtered through the stained-glass windows depicting scenes from forgotten myths, painted intricate patterns on the worn oak floors. Eleanor Ainsworth, her silver braid catching the light as she straightened a stack of Tolkien novels, sighed contentedly. This was her sanctuary, a labyrinthine haven where stories whispered from every shelf and each dusty tome held the promise of adventure.

For Eleanor, being Head Librarian wasn't just a job; it was a calling. She understood the power of words, how they could transport readers to distant lands, introduce them to fantastical creatures, and unlock hidden truths within themselves. 


Today, however, something felt amiss. A shiver ran down her spine as she glanced at the grandfather clock ticking away in the corner. Its hands pointed towards 3:00 pm, the exact time when a peculiar series of events had begun unfolding exactly a year ago.
