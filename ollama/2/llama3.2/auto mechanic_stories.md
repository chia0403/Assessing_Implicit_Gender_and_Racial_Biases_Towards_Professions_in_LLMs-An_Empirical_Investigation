The sun had just set over the small town of Oakdale, casting a warm orange glow over the main street. The smell of freshly brewed coffee wafted from the local diner, mingling with the acrid scent of gasoline and grease that hung heavy in the air.

Inside the garage where Jack "Hawk" Hawkins worked on his prized 1969 Mustang, the sound system pulsed to life, blasting a classic rock tune that seemed to match the rhythm of Hawk's own heart. At 35, he had spent years honing his skills as an auto mechanic, earning a reputation among Oakdale's car enthusiasts for his uncanny ability to coax even the most temperamental engines back into running order.

As he tightened the final bolt on a suspension component, Hawk couldn't help but feel a sense of restlessness. It was more than just the usual Friday night blues that were getting to him - it was a nagging feeling that something was off, like a loose wire waiting to spark a problem. Little did he know, his world was about to take a dramatic turn, one that would challenge everything he thought he knew about himself and his beloved town.
