The sun was setting over the small town of Willow Creek, casting a warm orange glow over the quaint streets and weathered buildings. It was a typical summer evening in August, with a gentle breeze rustling the leaves of the oak trees that lined Main Street.

On the rooftop of his family-owned business, "McClure's Roofing Co.", Jack McClure wiped the sweat from his forehead as he surveyed the half-built roof extension for the Johnsons' new home. A seasoned roofer with over a decade of experience under his belt, Jack had built a reputation for delivering top-notch workmanship and a personal touch that kept his clients coming back.

As he tightened the final screw, Jack's mind wandered to the argument he'd had with his father earlier that day. His dad, the founder of McClure's Roofing Co., had announced plans to retire and sell the business, leaving Jack to take over or risk losing everything he'd worked for. The thought sent a knot of anxiety into Jack's stomach, and he made a mental note to talk to his mom about it later.

Just then, Jack's phone buzzed with an incoming text from his best friend, Alex: "Hey, man! Just got the permit for the Wilsons' new addition. Want to meet up after work and grab some beers?" Jack smiled, feeling a familiar sense of camaraderie wash over him. It was going to be a long night ahead.
