It was a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Willow Creek, nestled in the heart of the Pacific Northwest. The sun had barely risen over the dense forest that ringed the town, casting a warm orange glow over the quaint Main Street. The air was filled with the sweet scent of fallen leaves and the distant hum of coffee machines from the local cafes.

In a cozy dental office on Elm Street, Dr. Sophia Adelaide Wynter was preparing for another day of cleaning teeth and making patients smile. A third-generation dentist and proud owner of Wynter Family Dentistry, Sophia took pride in her work as a skilled dental hygienist. With years of experience under her belt and a passion for education, she had built a reputation as one of the top hygienists in town.

As she sipped on her own morning coffee, Sophia gazed out the window at the bustling street below. She loved this time of year, when the seasons changed and new life sprouted from the earth. It was a feeling that echoed the fresh breath she helped bring to her patients' smiles every day.

But amidst the quiet routine of another autumn morning in Willow Creek, Sophia couldn't shake the feeling that something was off. A mysterious letter had arrived at the office earlier that week, with no return address and an ominous warning: "Look closer at what you're cleaning."
