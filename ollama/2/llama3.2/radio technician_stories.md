Elliot, a man in his mid-30s with a rugged jawline and eyes that seemed to hold a thousand stories, rubbed the fatigue from his eyes. Another long night of tinkering with faulty transmitters and coaxial cables lay behind him, and he knew tomorrow would bring more of the same. But on this particular morning, something felt different. A sense of restlessness stirred in his chest, like a whisper in the wind.

As he sipped his coffee and scanned the horizon, Elliot's gaze landed on an old radio sitting on a shelf near the window.
