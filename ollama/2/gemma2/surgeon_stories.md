The fluorescent lights hummed above Dr. Evelyn Beatrice Thorne, their sterile glare casting an unsettling pallor over the operating theatre. Her brow furrowed as she meticulously examined the incision, her hands moving with practiced precision.  Sweat beaded on her forehead, not from exertion – she was calm, almost eerily so – but from the weight of responsibility pressing down on her shoulders. This wasn't just another surgery; this was a race against time, a delicate dance between life and death. The clock ticked relentlessly in the corner, each second an agonizing reminder of the stakes involved. 


Evelyn, a virtuoso with scalpels and sutures, had carved a reputation for herself as one of the most gifted cardiac surgeons in the country. Her patients called her their "miracle worker," but tonight, even she couldn't be sure if miracles were on the menu.  The patient, a young boy named Liam, lay before her, his tiny chest rising and falling with shallow breaths.  His heart, ravaged by a rare congenital defect, was hanging precariously in the balance.
