Rain lashed against the warped wooden windows of Serenity Spa, blurring the neon "Open" sign into an anemic smear of pink. Inside, Amelia Rose Hawthorne kneaded the knot in Mr. Henderson's left shoulder blade with practiced ease, her hands moving like hummingbirds over his tense muscles. The aroma of lavender and bergamot oil hung heavy in the air, momentarily masking the scent of mildew that clung stubbornly to the aging walls.

Mr. Henderson sighed contentedly, his eyes squeezed shut. "Amelia," he mumbled, voice thick with relaxation, "you're a miracle worker." 

Amelia smiled faintly. She heard that compliment at least twice a day. It never got old. But today, something felt different. The air crackled with an unease she couldn't quite place, a shiver down her spine despite the warmth of the heated massage table.
