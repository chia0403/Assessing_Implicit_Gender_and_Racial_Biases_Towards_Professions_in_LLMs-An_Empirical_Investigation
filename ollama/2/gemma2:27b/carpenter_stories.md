The scent of sawdust and pine hung heavy in the air, clinging to Elias Blackwood's clothes like a second skin. Sunlight slanted through the dusty windows of his workshop, illuminating motes dancing in the air and casting long shadows from the tools hanging on the wall. Elias himself was hunched over a workbench, his weathered hands moving with practiced ease as he planed down a piece of oak.

His workshop was tucked away on a quiet side street in the heart of Oakhaven, a small town nestled amongst rolling hills and whispering forests. The rhythmic thunk of Elias' hammer against wood mingled with the distant clang of the blacksmith's forge and the cheerful chirping of sparrows nesting in the eaves. It was a symphony of simple sounds, each one imbued with the quiet pulse of life that thrummed through Oakhaven.
