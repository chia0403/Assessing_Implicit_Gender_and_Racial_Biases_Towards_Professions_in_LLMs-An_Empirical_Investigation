Rain lashed against the mullioned windows of Hawthorne Manor, each gust rattling the aged glass in its frame. Eleanor Cavendish, with her perpetually damp hair escaping from its bun and her sensible shoes squelching on the flagstones, hurried down the dimly lit corridor towards the master suite. The heavy scent of lavender polish mingled uneasily with the sharper tang of mildew that seemed to permeate the ancient walls.

Eleanor had been housekeeper at Hawthorne Manor for nearly a decade, her life a steady rhythm of dusting antique furniture, polishing silver tarnished by generations, and navigating the capricious moods of the manor's owner, the reclusive Lord Ashford.
