Rain lashed against the dusty windows of Elias Thorne’s tailoring shop, blurring the gaslights into halos of orange. The air hung thick with the scent of damp wool and beeswax, punctuated by the sharp tang of iron from his recently sharpened shears. Elias himself, perched precariously on a three-legged stool, was wrestling with a particularly obstinate sleeve. A symphony of curses whispered under his breath as he coaxed the fine silk into submission, his nimble fingers working with practiced ease despite the frustration.

Outside, the cobblestone streets of London's Whitechapel district were awash in shadows and the rhythmic clatter of carriage wheels struggling through the downpour. Elias knew tonight wouldn’t bring much business; who would venture out in this weather unless they absolutely had to? Yet, he continued his work, the rhythmic snip-snip of his shears a lonely counterpoint to the storm's fury.
