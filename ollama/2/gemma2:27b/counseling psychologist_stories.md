Rain lashed against the windowpanes of Dr. Eleanor Vance's office, mirroring the turmoil brewing inside her own chest. Her cluttered desk was piled high with case files, each one representing a life teetering on the edge, a soul seeking solace in the dimly lit sanctuary she offered. The scent of lavender oil from her diffuser battled valiantly against the musty smell of old books and forgotten anxieties. Outside, the city of Seattle hummed with its usual frenetic energy, oblivious to the quiet desperation unfolding within these four walls. Eleanor stared at the phone, its insistent ringing a stark counterpoint to the rhythmic drumbeat of the rain. It was another late night, another desperate plea for help echoing through the line. But tonight felt different. A knot of apprehension tightened in her stomach, whispering of something amiss.
