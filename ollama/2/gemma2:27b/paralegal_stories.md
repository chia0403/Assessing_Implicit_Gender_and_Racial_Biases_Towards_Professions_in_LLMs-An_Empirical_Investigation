Rain lashed against the grimy windows of the law firm, blurring the neon glow of the "Justice For All" sign across the street. Eliza Blackwood, her dark hair pulled back in a tight bun, tapped impatiently on her keyboard, the rhythmic click-clack echoing through the near-empty office. The late hour had driven everyone else home, leaving Eliza alone with stacks of case files and a growing sense of unease.

Eliza wasn't just any paralegal; she possessed an uncanny intuition for spotting inconsistencies and uncovering hidden truths buried beneath layers of legal jargon. Tonight, however, her usually sharp mind felt clouded by a gnawing premonition. A new case file lay open on her desk, the details chillingly familiar. The victim: a young woman with piercing blue eyes, just like Eliza's own reflection staring back from her computer screen.
