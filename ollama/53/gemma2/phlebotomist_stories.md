The fluorescent lights hummed overhead, casting a sterile glow on the rows of pale chairs in the waiting area. The scent of disinfectant hung heavy in the air, mingling with the faint aroma of coffee from the vending machine tucked in the corner. It was always this way at Mercy General Hospital: hushed whispers punctuated by coughs and the occasional rustle of paperwork. 

This wasn't Eleanor Beatrice Lavender's favorite environment – she much preferred the quiet bustle of her own garden back home, where the only sounds were bees buzzing and leaves rustling. But here, amidst the pale walls and medical jargon, she found purpose. Eleanor was a phlebotomist, and despite the anxieties she sometimes witnessed in her patients, there was a strange satisfaction in drawing their blood, seeing the vials fill with crimson life force, knowing it would be used to diagnose, treat, and ultimately save lives.

Today, however, felt different. A knot of unease sat heavy in Eleanor's chest as she glanced at the clock – 8:57 am. She was waiting for her patient, Mr. Silas Blackwood, a man who had requested her specifically, citing "a particular skill set."  Eleanor had no idea what that meant, but his cryptic request had piqued her curiosity and, honestly, a little bit of fear.


What secrets did Mr. Blackwood hold? And what strange skill did he believe Eleanor possessed?
