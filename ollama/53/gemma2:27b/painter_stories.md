Rain lashed against the attic windowpane, mimicking the frantic rhythm of Elara Blackwood's heart.  She hunched over her easel, brush poised above a canvas splashed with turbulent shades of grey and crimson. Her studio, tucked away in the eaves of a centuries-old Victorian house overlooking the churning North Sea, felt more like a ship tossed upon stormy waves than a sanctuary for art.

Outside, the wind howled like a banshee, rattling the loose panes and threatening to tear the roof from its moorings. But Elara barely noticed. Her world had shrunk to the canvas before her, a chaotic landscape mirroring the storm raging both within and without.
