The air hung thick with the scent of pine needles and damp earth, a familiar perfume to Silas Jeremiah Blackwood. He ran a calloused hand over the rough bark of a towering Douglas fir, its needles whispering secrets in the morning breeze. Sunlight, filtered through the dense canopy, dappled the forest floor in emerald and gold.

Silas, with his broad shoulders and arms corded like ancient oak branches, was a man forged by this wilderness. Years spent wrestling giants to the ground had etched lines on his weathered face, each one a testament to his tenacity. Today, however, a shadow lingered over Silas's usually stoic features. The timber company he worked for was in trouble, and whispers of layoffs hung heavy in the air like smoke from a dying fire.


The fate of his livelihood, and the small cabin nestled deep within these ancient woods that he called home, depended on finding a solution, and fast.
