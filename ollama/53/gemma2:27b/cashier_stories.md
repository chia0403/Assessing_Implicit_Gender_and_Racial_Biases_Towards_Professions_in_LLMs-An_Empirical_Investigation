The fluorescent lights hummed overhead, casting a sickly yellow glow over the aisles of "Bargain Buys." Amelia Cartwright sighed, her gaze drifting from the endless stream of discounted cereal boxes to the clock ticking relentlessly above the register. It was 3:47 pm, the witching hour between after-school rush and dinner crowds. Usually, this meant a lull in customers, a chance for Amelia to catch her breath and maybe even sneak a peek at the dog-eared paperback tucked away beneath the counter.

But today, a peculiar tension hung in the air, thick as the dust motes dancing in the artificial light. A shiver ran down Amelia's spine, despite the stifling heat radiating from the overflowing freezer aisle. Something wasn't right.
