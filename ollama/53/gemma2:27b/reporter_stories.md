Rain lashed against the grimy windowpane of Beatrice "Bea" Augustine's cramped apartment, mirroring the storm brewing inside her. She chewed on the end of her pen, the cheap plastic tasting faintly of stale coffee and desperation. The headline glared at her from the crumpled newspaper clipping: "Local Artist Found Dead in Studio, Cause Unknown." Bea had been chasing this story for weeks, a lone wolf reporter clawing her way through the city's underbelly, determined to expose the truth behind Elias Thorne's untimely demise.

Thorne wasn't just any artist; he was a rising star, his surrealist paintings captivating the art world with their haunting beauty and unsettling symbolism. His death, shrouded in mystery and whispered rumours of foul play, had sent shockwaves through the city's creative community.  Bea's gut told her there was more to it than a simple accident.


This gritty, rain-soaked city, known as Obsidian Falls for its perpetual twilight and brooding architecture, held secrets close.  And Bea, fueled by a burning need to uncover them, was determined to pry those secrets loose.
