Rain lashed against the grimy windowpane, blurring the already indistinct view of the city below. Eleanor Cartwright sighed, her breath fogging the glass momentarily before dissipating into the stale office air. The rhythmic clatter of the ancient typewriter punctuated the silence, each keystroke echoing her own weary frustration.

Eleanor wasn't meant for this life, a cog in the machine of Mr. Blackwood's import-export business. She dreamed of dusty libraries and forgotten texts, of uncovering hidden histories and weaving them into vibrant tapestries of words. Yet here she was, surrounded by invoices and shipping manifests, her aspirations gathering dust alongside the antique globe in the corner.

But tonight felt different. A strange energy pulsed beneath the floorboards, a tremor that resonated with something deep within Eleanor. It started as a faint hum, barely perceptible, but it grew steadily stronger, until it felt like a heartbeat against her own chest.
