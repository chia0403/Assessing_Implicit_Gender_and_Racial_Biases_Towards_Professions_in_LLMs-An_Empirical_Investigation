---

The fluorescent lights above the checkout counter hummed in unison with the gentle beeps of the cash register as Emily Mae Wilson scanned the groceries for Mrs. Jenkins' weekly trip to the store. It was a typical Wednesday afternoon at Sunnydale Market, the kind of day where the aisles were bustling with families on their way home from school and the smell of fresh bread wafted through the air.

Emily, or Em to those who knew her, had worked as a cashier for three years, watching generations of Sunnydale residents grow up and move away. She loved her job, not just because she enjoyed chatting with regular customers like Mrs. Jenkins, but also because it gave her a sense of stability and normalcy in her own life.

As Em rang up the last item – a jar of homemade jam made by Mrs. Jenkins herself – she couldn't shake the feeling that something was off. The sun-drenched streets outside seemed quieter than usual, and the usually cheerful chatter of the shoppers had taken on a slightly nervous tone. It was as if everyone was waiting for something to happen, but no one knew what.

Just then, the store's manager, Mr. Patel, appeared at her side, a look of concern etched on his face. "Em, can I talk to you for a minute?" he asked, his voice low and serious.

Emily's heart skipped a beat as she wondered what was wrong. Was it something with the store? Her job? Or was it something more...personal?
