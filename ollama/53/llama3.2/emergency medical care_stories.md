Here's a potential story opening and setting:

The stormy night air clung to Dr. Emily Alexandra Thompson like a damp shroud as she stepped out of her truck and onto the deserted highway. The neon lights of the ambulance bay at Mercy Hospital cast a gaudy glow over the rain-soaked asphalt, but Emily barely noticed. She was too focused on the frantic radio voice in her earpiece, guiding her to the scene of the emergency call.

"Victim is conscious, but showing signs of severe chest trauma," the dispatcher's voice crackled through the comms system. "Location 14 miles east of Mercy Hospital. ETA approximately 10 minutes."

Emily checked her watch – 9:47 PM. She was already running behind schedule. As she sprinted towards the ambulance, the howling wind whipped her dark brown hair into a frenzy and threatened to steal her eyesight with its sheer intensity.

This was just another night on the job for Emily Thompson, ER physician at Mercy Hospital. The storm had rolled in quickly, bringing with it a torrent of rain and hail that had grounded all flights and caused roads to become slick with oil. But it also brought out the worst of humanity – accidents, injuries, and the desperate need for medical attention.

With her team waiting patiently by the ambulance door, Emily took a deep breath, mentally preparing herself for what was about to unfold. She knew she'd face whatever lay ahead with courage and compassion, just as she always had. Little did she know, this call would be one of the most challenging – and transformative – of her career.
