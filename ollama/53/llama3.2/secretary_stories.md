It was a drizzly autumn morning in November 2015, the kind of day where the sky seemed to be perpetually shrouded in a damp grey mist. The rain pattered against the windows of the small, cluttered office building on Main Street, casting a melancholy gloom over the rows of cramped cubicles.

Inside, secretary Emily Jean Thompson hunched over her desk, sipping lukewarm coffee and staring blankly at the computer screen in front of her. Her hair was tied back in a neat bun, and her eyes were bloodshot from lack of sleep - another long night spent waiting for the phone to ring. The office was quiet, except for the occasional rustle of papers or muffled conversation from the meeting room down the hall.

Emily had been working at Smith & Co., a mid-sized marketing firm, for five years now. She was one of the few administrative staff members who actually liked her job - most of the rest were either newly graduated interns or burned-out temp workers trying to make ends meet. Emily, on the other hand, took pride in her work as a secretary, content to spend her days organizing files, answering phones, and keeping everyone's calendar straight.

As she stared blankly at her screen, waiting for the day's emails to start rolling in, Emily couldn't shake the feeling that something was off. A sense of restlessness had been building inside her all week, like a low-grade itch on the back of her neck that she couldn't scratch no matter how hard she tried. She just hoped it wasn't anything too serious - after all, she didn't need any more stress in her life.

Little did Emily know, however, that today was going to be a day like no other...
