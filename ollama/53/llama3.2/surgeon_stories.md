The city skyline was ablaze with the golden glow of sunset as Dr. Ethan Alexander Thompson made his way back to the hospital, his feet aching in his worn-out surgical shoes. The long day had been filled with high-stakes operations and grueling discussions with colleagues, but it was nothing compared to what lay ahead.

As he walked through the emergency department's sliding glass doors, the beeping of machines and murmurs of worried voices enveloped him like a shroud. Ethan's eyes scanned the crowded room, his gaze settling on the chaos that was unfolding before him. A young patient, no more than twenty-five years old, lay on the gurney, her fragile body wracked with convulsions as the medical team worked to stabilize her.

Ethan's mind flashed back to the phone call he'd received earlier that day - a panicked message from one of his colleagues at St. Michael's Hospital, where Ethan was a renowned cardiothoracic surgeon. A young patient had arrived, critically injured in a car accident on the outskirts of town, and the ER team was stumped. The lab results were inconclusive, and the patient's condition was rapidly deteriorating.

As Ethan pushed his way through the crowd, he felt a familiar sense of unease creeping up his spine. Something about this case didn't add up. He couldn't quite put his finger on it, but his instincts were screaming at him to take a closer look. Little did he know, this case would test his skills as a surgeon and push him to the limits of human endurance.

And that was just the beginning.
