Rain lashed against the dusty windowpane of Blackwood Pharmacy, blurring the neon "Open" sign into an indecipherable smear. Eleanor Blythe sighed, pushing a stray auburn curl behind her ear as she surveyed the empty shop. It was barely past 3pm, and even the usual trickle of afternoon customers had dried up. Blackwood, with its population of just over 500 souls nestled in the Appalachian foothills, wasn't exactly bustling with life.

Eleanor ran a hand over the worn wooden counter, polished smooth by generations of pharmacists before her. Her grandfather had opened the shop in 1923, and she’d inherited it ten years ago after his passing. The air still carried the faint scent of lavender and chamomile, remnants of her grandmother's famous herbal remedies, a legacy Eleanor treasured. But lately, the aroma was tinged with something else – a sense of stagnation, mirroring the dwindling foot traffic. 


Eleanor needed to do something, anything, to breathe life back into Blackwood Pharmacy. But what?
