The humid air hung thick in the tiny salon, smelling faintly of acetone and lavender polish. Outside, cicadas thrummed their ceaseless summer song, but inside, the world was all sharp clicks of nail clippers and the gentle whir of the electric file. Eloise Delacroix, her dark curls pulled back in a messy bun, expertly sculpted an almond shape into a client's nails. Her brow furrowed in concentration as she smoothed the edges with a buffer, her tongue peeking out from the corner of her mouth – a habit she knew annoyed some clients but couldn't help.

Eloise loved her job. The quiet intimacy of holding someone's hand, transforming chipped and ragged nails into miniature works of art, listening to their joys and sorrows – it was a privilege, she thought. This afternoon, though, her usual calm was disrupted. A storm seemed to be brewing inside her chest, mirroring the dark clouds gathering outside.
