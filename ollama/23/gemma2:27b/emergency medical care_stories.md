Rain lashed against the ambulance windows, blurring the neon glow of the city into an impressionistic smear.  Inside, Amelia "Amy" Castillo adjusted the IV drip for her patient, a young man groaning in pain from a motorcycle accident. The sirens wailed their mournful song, cutting through the relentless drumming of the rain as Amy navigated the slick streets.

This was just another night shift for Amy. Five years on the front lines of the Chicago Emergency Medical Service had hardened her to the grit and chaos of urban life. Yet, tonight felt different. A knot of unease tightened in her chest, a feeling amplified by the incessant rain that seemed to mirror the turmoil brewing within her.
