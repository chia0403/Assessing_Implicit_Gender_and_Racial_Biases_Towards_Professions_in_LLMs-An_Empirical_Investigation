Here's a potential story opening:

The rain-soaked streets of Ashwood City gleamed like polished onyx as the night wind whipped through the narrow alleys, carrying the faint scent of ozone and decay. It was a smell that 32-year-old Elijah Jaxon Thompson knew all too well, for it often preceded trouble.

Elijah, an electrician with a keen eye for wiring and a quick mind for problem-solving, had just arrived at his latest gig: a dilapidated mansion on the outskirts of town that had been abandoned for decades. The once-grand estate now stood as a testament to neglect, its windows boarded up, its roof sagging under the weight of years.

As Elijah climbed out of his truck and surveyed the property, he couldn't shake the feeling that something was off. His client, the reclusive millionaire Henry Blackwood, had promised him a straightforward job: replacing the mansion's antiquated electrical system with modern conveniences. But now, as Elijah gazed up at the crumbling facade, he wondered if he'd been misled.

The phone in his pocket buzzed, shrill and insistent, breaking the spell of unease that had settled over him. He pulled it out to see a text from Henry's personal assistant: "Meet me inside, ASAP."

Elijah's instincts prickled with unease as he slipped on his tool belt and headed towards the entrance, ready to face whatever lay within...
