As the dim fluorescent lights of the airport's emergency exit sign flickered to life above her head, Aurora "Rory" Thompson gazed out at the sea of strangers rushing to catch their flights, feeling like she was stuck in neutral once again.

Aurora Thompson was a 28-year-old flight attendant for Sunline Airlines, and she had been working non-stop since taking off from Denver bound for Los Angeles that morning. The early morning alarm had barely set in when the copilot's voice crackled over the intercom, informing her of an unexpected turbulence warning over the Rocky Mountains. After a frantic dash to secure loose items and prepare passengers for impact, they had managed to ride out the storm without incident – but now, as she stood at the exit sign in Terminal 3, Rory couldn't shake off the feeling that this was just the beginning of a long, grueling day.

The airport's bustling atmosphere seemed to hum with an undercurrent of restlessness, as if everyone was waiting for something – or someone. Rory took a deep breath and mentally checked her list: check-in procedures complete, safety drills passed with flying colors, crew manifest confirmed, and all passengers accounted for... It was time to get moving.

But just as she turned to head back into the crowded terminal, Rory spotted him – a tall, dark-haired stranger standing by the departure board, his eyes fixed intently on her. For an instant, their gazes locked in a way that sent shivers down Rory's spine...
