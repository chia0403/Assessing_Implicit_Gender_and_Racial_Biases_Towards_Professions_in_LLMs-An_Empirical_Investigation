As the sun rose over the bustling streets of downtown Portland, Oregon, Emily Mae Taylor woke up to the sound of her alarm blaring in her small apartment above the salon where she worked as a hairstylist. She stretched her arms overhead, yawned, and swung her legs over the side of the bed, feeling the familiar ache in her back from another long day of standing on her feet.

Emily Mae, or Em to her clients, had always been passionate about beauty and makeup, ever since she was a teenager working at her mother's small salon in small-town Indiana. She had studied cosmetology in high school, worked hard to build a reputation for herself as a talented stylist, and eventually saved up enough money to move to Portland to pursue her dream of opening her own salon.

But for now, Em was content with running her own booth at "Luxe Locks," a popular salon on Southeast Hawthorne Boulevard. She spent her days chatting with regular clients, creating elaborate hairstyles for brides, and keeping the latest beauty trends in mind as she worked her magic on everyone from punk rockers to stay-at-home moms.

As Em began to get ready for another busy day of clients, she gazed out the window at the vibrant streets below. The morning sunlight highlighted the colorful murals that covered the walls of the adjacent shops and cafes, and the sound of music drifted up from the corner cafe, where a young singer was belting out a soulful tune on her guitar.

Em smiled to herself as she finished getting dressed in her crisp white apron and ready for another day of making people feel like their best selves.
