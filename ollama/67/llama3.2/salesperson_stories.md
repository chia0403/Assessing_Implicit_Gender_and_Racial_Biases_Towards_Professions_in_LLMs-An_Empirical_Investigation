The sun had long since set on the bustling city of New Haven, casting a golden glow over the crowded streets of downtown. Amidst the sea of neon lights and towering skyscrapers, 32-year-old Alexandra "Lexi" Thompson stood out as a tiny speck in her sleek black business suit, clutching her leather briefcase tightly to her chest.

As one of the top salespeople at Smith & Co., a prestigious marketing firm, Lexi had spent the better part of the day navigating the cutthroat world of corporate deals and high-stakes negotiations. Her clients were the biggest names in tech, finance, and entertainment, and she had built a reputation for being ruthless, charming, and always closing.

But as she stepped out of the elevator on the 23rd floor of the Smith & Co. headquarters, Lexi couldn't shake off the feeling that something was off. The air was thick with tension, and her usually keen intuition was screaming at her to be on high alert.

"Lexi, we need to talk," a low voice whispered in her ear, making her turn just in time to see her colleague and close friend, Rachel Lee, slipping away into the crowded hallway with an urgent look on her face.
