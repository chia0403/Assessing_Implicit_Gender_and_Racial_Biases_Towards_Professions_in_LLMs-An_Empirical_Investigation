The streets of Ravenshire were always at their most unforgiving at night. The flickering torches that lined the narrow alleys cast eerie shadows on the walls, making it seem as though the very spirits of the city's dark past were stirring once more. It was in this bleak and foreboding atmosphere that Emilia Rose Blackwood patrolled her assigned beat, a stern-faced guard tasked with maintaining order in the shadowy underbelly of Ravenshire.

As she walked, her eyes scanned the crowded taverns and seedy warehouses that seemed to spring up overnight like poisonous mushrooms. Her hand rested on the hilt of her sword, a comforting weight that reassured her of her own mortality, but not necessarily of the safety of those around her. For in this city, danger lurked in every alleyway and shadow, waiting to pounce.

Emilia Blackwood was a guard by necessity as much as by inclination. Her father, a former member of Ravenshire's Nightwatch, had been killed on duty when she was just a child, leaving her mother alone to raise her in the harsh reality of their city's unforgiving world. From that day on, Emilia had vowed to follow in his footsteps, to make him proud and protect the people of Ravenshire from those who would seek to harm them.

But as she turned a corner onto Market Street, Emilia couldn't shake the feeling that tonight was going to be different. A sense of unease settled over her like a shroud, making the torches seem to flicker in time with her own racing heart. She knew she shouldn't have taken the extra shift, but something about the night felt... off.

And then, just as she rounded the corner onto Blackstone Alley, Emilia saw it: a small, unassuming door hidden behind a stack of crates and barrels. The door was slightly ajar, a faint sliver of moonlight seeping out to illuminate the dust motes dancing in the air.
