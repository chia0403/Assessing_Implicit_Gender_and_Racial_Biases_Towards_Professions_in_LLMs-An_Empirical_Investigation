---

The fog rolled in off the coast, shrouding the small coastal town of Ravenswood Cove in a damp, gray mist. It was the kind of morning that made a person want to stay inside with a hot cup of coffee and forget about the world outside. But for Ember Stonebrooke, a ruggedly handsome carpenter with a heart as worn as the wooden beams he spent his days crafting, there was no such luxury.

Ember lived in a small, weathered cottage on the outskirts of town, overlooking the rocky shoreline where the sea spray crashed against the shore. The house had been in his family for generations, and Ember took pride in preserving its rustic charm while making it cozy enough for himself after a long day's work.

As he stepped out into the chill morning air, the fog swirled around him like a living entity, obscuring the familiar contours of the village. It was a strange kind of quiet, one that made Ember feel restless and ready to get to work. He breathed in deeply, feeling the dampness fill his lungs, and set off towards the nearby docks, where his truck was parked, loaded with tools and materials for the day's job.

The sound of seagulls filled the air as Ember walked down the winding road, the wooden boards beneath his boots creaking in protest. He knew every inch of this town, every hidden nook and cranny, every trick and charm that made Ravenswood Cove tick. And he was proud to be a part of it all.

Little did Ember know, however, that today would bring more than just another day's work. Today would bring secrets uncovered, old rivalries reignited, and the promise of a new beginning, one that would shake the very foundations of his life...
