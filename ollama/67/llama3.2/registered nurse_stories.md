The rain-soaked streets of Seattle were always at their most unforgiving on nights like this. The gray skies seemed to swallow all light and hope, leaving only a damp chill that seeped into the bones. It was exactly this kind of night when nurse Emma Rose Thompson decided she'd had enough.

As she stepped out of her small apartment in Capitol Hill, the cold air slapped her face like a wake-up call. She took a deep breath, feeling the familiar ache in her shoulders from a long day at Mercy West Hospital. The hospital's fluorescent lights and endless beeps had left her weary, but it was nothing compared to the exhaustion that came with trying to make sense of the city's latest tragedy.

A young woman, only 25 years old, lay on one of the hospital beds in room 304. She'd been rushed in a few hours ago, her chest pierced by a shard of glass from a broken windshield on the highway. The ER had done what they could, but Emma knew that sometimes it wasn't enough.

Emma pulled up the collar of her rain jacket and set off towards the hospital, her feet carrying her along the familiar path without a care in the world. Little did she know that this night would be different from any other - one that would test everything she thought she knew about medicine, compassion, and herself.
