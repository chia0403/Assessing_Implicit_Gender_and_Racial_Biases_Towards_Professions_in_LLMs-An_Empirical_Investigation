The fog rolled in off the ocean, shrouding the small coastal town of Ravenswood in a damp, gray mist. It was a chill that seeped into the bones, and not just because of the temperature. For Emily Anne Thompson, a chef at the local seafood restaurant, The Wharf, it was a reminder of the storm that had ravaged her family's fishing business three years ago, leaving them with little more than debt and heartache.

Emily Anne stood at the counter of The Wharf, surveying the kitchen she had built from scratch. It was a place where she could lose herself in the rhythm of cooking, where the sizzle of bacon and the simmer of sauces were the only sounds that mattered. She had always been driven by her passion for food, but after the loss of her family's business, she found herself questioning whether it was enough.

As she began to prep for the dinner service, the phone rang, shrill in the quiet kitchen. Emily Anne picked up, expecting it to be another restaurant or food critic with a complaint about the prices. But instead, it was a voice on the other end that sent a shiver down her spine...
