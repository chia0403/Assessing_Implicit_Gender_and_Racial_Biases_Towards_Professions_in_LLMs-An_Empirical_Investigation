The scent of bay rum and sandalwood wafted through the small shop on Main Street, mingling with the hum of conversation and the occasional snip of scissors. It was a chilly autumn morning in the small town of Willow Creek, where the sun had barely risen over the sleepy streets. Inside "A Cut Above" barbershop, owner and head barber, Ethan Michael Wellesley, stood behind his chair, surveying his domain with a practiced eye.

Ethan, a man in his mid-30s with a strong jawline and piercing blue eyes, had spent years perfecting the art of haircutting. He was renowned for his attention to detail and ability to coax even the most recalcitrant locks into submission. His shop was the go-to destination for men from all walks of life, seeking not only a stylish cut but also a listening ear and words of wisdom.

As he sipped his morning coffee, Ethan watched as his apprentice, young Jake Thompson, nervously fidgeted with scissors, waiting for the first customer to arrive. The phone rang, shrill in the quiet morning air, and Ethan picked it up, answering with a warm smile: "A Cut Above, this is Ethan. What can I do for you today?"

Little did he know, this would be the day that would change everything.
