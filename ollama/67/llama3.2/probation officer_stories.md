---

The rain-soaked streets of New Haven glistened like polished onyx, reflecting the dim glow of the streetlights that lined the sidewalks. Inside the worn, beige walls of the local community center, Probation Officer Alexandra "Lexi" Thompson stood at her desk, sipping lukewarm coffee and staring out the grimy windowpane. The fluorescent lights above hummed in a steady drone, casting an unforgiving glare over the tired faces of the afternoon's clients.

At thirty-five, Lexi had seen it all – from battered young mothers to hardened felons, each one with their own unique story of pain and redemption. As she waited for her next appointment, her mind wandered back to the latest case file on her desk: the troubled teenager who'd been arrested for shoplifting at the local mall, his parents struggling to keep up with the mounting court costs.

Just then, a knock at the door broke the silence, and Lexi's eyes turned toward the door, a small spark of interest igniting within them. She rose from her seat, smoothing out her rumpled blouse as she made her way to answer it.

"Can I help you?" she asked, her voice firm but not unfriendly.

The face that peered through the crack in the door was one Lexi knew all too well – that of Jake Reynolds, a familiar name and face that sent shivers down her spine. He was the kind of kid who'd grown up on the wrong side of town, with a rap sheet longer than his list of friends.

"What brings you to my office today, Jake?" she asked, her tone neutral but wary.

The look on his face told Lexi exactly what she needed to know – that something was off, and it wasn't just his tired eyes or the scuffed leather jacket slung over his shoulders. Something was very wrong indeed.
