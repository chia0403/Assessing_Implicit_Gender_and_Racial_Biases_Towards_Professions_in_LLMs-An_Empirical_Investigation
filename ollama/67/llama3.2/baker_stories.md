The sun had barely cracked the horizon over the sleepy town of Willow Creek, casting a warm orange glow over the quaint streets and homes. In a small bakery on Main Street, Agatha Rose Thompson was already up and at it, her hands moving with precision as she mixed and measured ingredients for the day's first batch of bread.

Agatha, or "Gaga" to those who knew her, had owned the bakery for over five years, and it had become a staple in the community. Her pies, cakes, and pastries were legendary, and people came from all over to taste her famous sourdough and buy a piece of homemade goodness. The bakery was cozy, with cream-colored walls and wooden floors, and the scent of freshly baked bread wafted through the air, making everyone's mouth water.

As she slid the last loaf into the oven, Agatha took a moment to gaze out the window at the quiet town waking up around her. She loved this time of day, when the world felt fresh and full of possibility. Little did she know, today would bring more than just a typical day in the life of a baker.
