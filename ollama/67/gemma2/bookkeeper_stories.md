The scent of old paper and stale coffee hung heavy in the air of Miller & Sons Accounting, clinging to Amelia Beatrice Hawthorne like a second skin.  She sat hunched over her desk, a mountain of invoices stacked beside her like fallen dominoes. Outside, the San Francisco fog rolled in, blanketing the city in a damp, ethereal veil.

Amelia's world existed within the neat lines and meticulous figures of ledgers. She found solace in the predictable rhythm of debits and credits, the satisfying click of keys on the vintage Olivetti typewriter she stubbornly refused to replace. But lately, even her sanctuary had begun to feel suffocating. A persistent sense of unease gnawed at her, a feeling that something was amiss, like a misplaced comma in the grand equation of her life.
