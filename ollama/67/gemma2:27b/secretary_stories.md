Rain lashed against the grimy windows of Henderson & Sons, blurring the already indistinct cityscape beyond. The rhythmic drumming was punctuated by the insistent clanging of the ancient radiator, a sound Amelia Cartwright had come to associate with impending doom.  The air in the office was thick with the scent of damp wool and stale cigarette smoke, both emanating from Mr. Henderson himself, who sat hunched over his desk like a gargoyle guarding its treasure.

Amelia stared at the pile of unanswered letters on her desk, each one a reminder of the ever-growing mountain of work threatening to engulf her. Henderson & Sons, once a bustling import-export firm, was now clinging desperately to life, its fortunes dwindling with every passing year.
