Rain lashed against the attic windowpane, mimicking the frantic rhythm drumming in Elara Nightingale's chest. The old house groaned under the onslaught, its timbers creaking like weary bones. She sat hunched over her battered typewriter, the keys clicking out a story that refused to coalesce into anything coherent. Her protagonist, a stoic lighthouse keeper named Silas, was supposed to be wrestling with his inner demons, but all Elara could conjure was a bland, forgettable outline.

Frustration gnawed at her. She'd come to this crumbling seaside manor seeking inspiration, hoping the salty air and mournful cries of gulls would stir something within her. But so far, all she'd found were cobwebs, dust bunnies, and a crippling writer's block.
