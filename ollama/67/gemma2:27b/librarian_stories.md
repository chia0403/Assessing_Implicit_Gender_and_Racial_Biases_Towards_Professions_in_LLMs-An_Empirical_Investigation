The scent of aging paper and leather was as familiar to Rosalind Eloise Finch as her own heartbeat.  It clung to the high-vaulted ceilings of the Blackwood Library, whispering tales of forgotten centuries in its musky embrace. Dust motes danced in the shafts of sunlight that pierced through the arched windows, illuminating rows upon rows of towering bookshelves.

Rosalind, with her spectacles perched precariously on the bridge of her nose and a perpetually surprised expression etched onto her gentle face, was navigating the labyrinthine stacks.  She hummed softly to herself, a melody from an obscure 18th-century opera she'd discovered tucked away in a forgotten tome, as she searched for a first edition copy of "Wuthering Heights" requested by a particularly eager patron.  

Today, however, a strange disquiet hung in the air. The usual hushed serenity was punctuated by an unsettling tremor that reverberated through the floorboards, sending shivers up Rosalind's spine. It felt ancient, primal, like the awakening of something long dormant beneath the library's very foundations...
