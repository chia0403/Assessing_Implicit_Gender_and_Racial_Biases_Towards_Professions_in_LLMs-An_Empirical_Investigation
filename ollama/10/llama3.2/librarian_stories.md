Here's a potential story opening:

The sun had long since set on the small town of Willow Creek, casting a warm orange glow over the quaint streets and homes. Inside the local library, Emily Anne Wilson sat hunched over her desk, surrounded by stacks of dusty tomes and flickering fluorescent lights. As the head librarian at the Willow Creek Public Library, Emily took pride in her work, carefully curating the shelves with a keen eye for discovery.

The smell of old books and stale air clung to her as she worked, but Emily didn't mind. She had always been a bookworm, finding solace in the musty pages of novels and non-fiction texts alike. As she delved into the stacks, her fingers trailed over the spines of the books, feeling the raised letters of titles like "The Great Gatsby" and "Jane Eyre". It was moments like these that Emily felt most at home, surrounded by the written word.

But tonight felt different. Tonight, the library's usual quiet was punctuated by an unsettling silence. The phone had stopped ringing hours ago, and the few patrons who had wandered in during the day had all left early, leaving Emily to her thoughts. As she worked, a creeping sense of unease began to settle over her, like a shiver down her spine.

And then, just as she was about to take a break for dinner, the lights flickered and went out, plunging the library into darkness...
