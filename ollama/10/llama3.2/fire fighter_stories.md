The sun had just set over the small town of Willow Creek, casting a golden glow over the sleepy streets and homes that lined its quaint Main Street. The smell of fresh-cut grass and BBQ wafted through the air as families gathered to enjoy the evening together.

Inside the Willow Creek Fire Department's headquarters, Firefighter Ryder Jackson was sipping on a lukewarm cup of coffee, his eyes fixed on the clock ticking away on the wall. It had been 12 long hours since he'd started his shift, and the adrenaline rush that usually came with responding to emergency calls had begun to wear off.

Ryder, a ruggedly handsome man in his mid-30s with a strong jawline and piercing blue eyes, sighed inwardly as he rubbed the fatigue from his eyes. He loved being a firefighter, but it was days like today when he couldn't help but feel like he was stuck in a rut.

Just then, the dispatcher's voice crackled over the radio, breaking the silence: "Engine 12, respond to 345 Oak Street. Reports of a possible structure fire."
