The sun was setting over the small town of Willow Creek, casting a warm orange glow over the quaint streets and homes. The air was thick with the scent of freshly cut grass and blooming flowers, a smell that 35-year-old landscaper, Jasper Wyatt Thompson, knew all too well. He had spent countless hours working in the sweltering summer heat, shaping gardens into havens for his clients to enjoy.

Jasper's truck, a trusty old Ford F-150 with a faded paint job and a name plate that read "Green Thumb," was parked outside the Thompson's family home, a cozy little bungalow on Elm Street. The house had been in Jasper's family for generations, and he took great pride in taking care of it, just as his parents and grandparents had before him.

As he stepped out of the truck, Jasper gazed up at the sprawling oak tree that towered over the front yard, its branches heavy with the weight of summer. He nodded to himself, a plan already forming in his mind for how he would tackle the pruning and cleanup needed to keep this beauty healthy and thriving. Little did he know, this was just the beginning of a season that would bring new challenges and opportunities to Jasper's life, ones that would test his skills as a landscaper and push him to confront secrets from his past.
