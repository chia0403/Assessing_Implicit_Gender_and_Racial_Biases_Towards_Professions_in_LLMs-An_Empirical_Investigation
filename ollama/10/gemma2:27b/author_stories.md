Rain lashed against the attic window, mimicking the frantic tattooing in Eleanor Cavendish's chest. The room smelled of dust and forgotten dreams, the air thick with the weight of decades past. Stacks of yellowed manuscripts threatened to topple over, each one a testament to her unfulfilled potential. Eleanor, once a fiery young writer brimming with ambition, now found herself grappling with a crippling case of writer's block.  

The old Underwood typewriter sat accusingly on her cluttered desk, its keys glinting under the single bare bulb that illuminated the room. It had been months since she'd managed to string together a coherent sentence, let alone weave a captivating tale.  

Eleanor stared out at the storm-ravaged garden below, its overgrown roses bowed under the relentless onslaught of rain. A sense of despair threatened to engulf her, but then a flicker of defiance ignited within her. She wouldn't succumb. Not today.
