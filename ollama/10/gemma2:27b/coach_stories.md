The humid air hung heavy over the cracked asphalt court, thick with the scent of sweat and sunbaked rubber. Cicadas buzzed their relentless symphony from the overgrown trees bordering the playground, punctuated by the occasional shriek of delighted children on the swings. Amelia "Amy" Rodriguez adjusted her baseball cap, pushing back a stray curl that had escaped its ponytail. Her eyes, sharp as a hawk's, scanned the uneven lines painted on the court, mentally calculating angles and trajectories.

This wasn't the gleaming hardwood floor or manicured grass she'd grown accustomed to coaching on, but it was all they had. And for these kids, this dilapidated court in the heart of El Paso was their sanctuary.
