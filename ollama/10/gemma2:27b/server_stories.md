The humid air clung to Eleanor Beatrice Hawthorne like a second skin as she navigated the crowded tables of "The Salty Siren," her apron flapping precariously against her legs. Saltwater taffy-colored light streamed through the grimy windows, casting long shadows across the worn wooden floorboards. Laughter and boisterous conversation collided with the rhythmic clang of dishes and the sizzle of frying fish, creating a symphony unique to the seaside diner. 

Eleanor sighed inwardly. Another Friday night, another barrage of demanding regulars, sticky-fingered children, and tourists who couldn't decide between the clam chowder or the lobster roll. Her smile, however, remained fixed in place, a carefully crafted mask hiding the weariness etched onto her twenty-three year old face.
