Rain lashed against the windowpanes of Bartholomew "Bart" Thistlewick's office, mimicking the relentless drumming in his chest. His desk, usually a haven of meticulously organized paperwork, was now a battlefield strewn with rejected claim forms and terse emails from irate customers. The flickering fluorescent lights cast long shadows that danced ominously across the worn carpet, amplifying the oppressive atmosphere. Bart, a man who prided himself on his calm demeanor and unwavering logic, felt a bead of sweat trickle down his temple. This wasn't just another Tuesday at Thistlewick & Sons Insurance; this was an existential crisis disguised as a particularly nasty hailstorm. 

The problem wasn't the weather, although it certainly didn't help. It was Mrs. Eloise Cartwright and her claim for a porcelain teacup shattered by a rogue squirrel. A teacup, mind you, worth more than Bart's monthly rent. And according to Mrs. Cartwright, an heirloom passed down through generations, imbued with the spirit of her great-great grandmother.  Bart pinched the bridge of his nose, trying to quell the burgeoning headache. This was going to be a long night.
