Rain hammered against Dr. Amelia Thorne's windowpane, each drop like a tiny drumbeat on her already frayed nerves.  The storm, mirroring the one brewing inside her own soul, was relentless. The soft murmur of therapy sessions she held with patients struggling through heartbreak and loss felt like a distant echo in her increasingly silent apartment. The last three months had been a blur: her husband's sudden passing, followed by a wave of work commitments that left her feeling like a lone ship adrift on a stormy sea.  

Amelia stared out at the city lights blurring into a kaleidoscope of gray and green, her mind racing through a labyrinth of memories. His laughter would have filled this emptiness with an almost tangible warmth; now, only the biting wind seemed to offer any solace.
