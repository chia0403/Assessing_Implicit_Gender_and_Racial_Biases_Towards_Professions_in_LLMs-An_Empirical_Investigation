The rusty bell shrieked, its sound echoing off the faded linoleum tiles of Northwood High's history hallway.  Amelia Winthrop sighed, pushing her glasses further up her nose as the last stragglers shuffled out of her classroom. Another day down, another hundred teenagers slightly less clueless about the Peloponnesian War than they were before. 

Outside, a late autumn wind whipped fallen leaves into frenzied dances. Amelia watched them twirl through the cracked pavement, their vibrant hues a stark contrast to the drab concrete and brick of the schoolyard. She longed for her own escape - a hot cup of tea by the crackling fireplace in her book-lined apartment, the scent of cinnamon candles filling the air. 

But first, she had to grade fifty essays on ancient Athenian democracy.
