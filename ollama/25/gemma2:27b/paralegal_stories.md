Rain lashed against the grimy windows of the law firm, blurring the already indistinct cityscape beyond. Inside, Amelia Rose Cartwright hunched over a stack of files, her brow furrowed in concentration. The air hung heavy with the scent of stale coffee and desperation. It was late, well past closing time, but Amelia rarely left before finishing what she started.  

She'd been working at Davies & Thorne for three years now, diligently climbing the ladder from intern to paralegal. Her colleagues often teased her about being a workaholic, but for Amelia, it wasn’t just about ambition. It was about justice – the elusive flicker of hope she sought to ignite in every case she touched. 


Tonight, though, that flicker felt dim. The case files before her detailed the plight of a single mother facing eviction, her meager savings devoured by medical bills. Amelia's heart ached for the woman, but the legal precedents seemed stacked against them.

A sudden gust of wind rattled the windowpane, startling Amelia from her thoughts. She glanced at the clock – nearly midnight. Time to call it a night. As she began gathering her things, a faint scratching sound emanated from behind her desk.
