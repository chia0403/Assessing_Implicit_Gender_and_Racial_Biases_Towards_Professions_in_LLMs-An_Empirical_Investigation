Rain lashed against the grimy windows of Detective Elias Blackwood's office, blurring the neon glow of the city beyond into an impressionist painting. The incessant drumming echoed the restless beat of his heart.  Elias stared at the file on his desk, the photograph of the victim – a young woman named Anya Petrova – staring back with vacant eyes. He traced her delicate jawline with a calloused finger, feeling a familiar pang of empathy mingled with frustration.

This wasn't Blackwood's first dance with death in the labyrinthine alleyways of New Haven. But Anya's murder, cold and brutal, felt different. It whispered of something darker, more insidious than the usual turf wars and drug deals that plagued his city.
