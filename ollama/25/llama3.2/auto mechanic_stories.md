The sun had long since set over the small town of Red Rock, casting a warm orange glow over the dusty streets and faded storefronts. In a small garage on Main Street, Jack "Hawk" Hawkins was tinkering with a 1969 Chevrolet Camaro, his hands covered in grease as he pored over a worn-out repair manual.

It had been a long day, filled with broken engines and leaky transmissions, but Hawk was used to it. He'd always loved working on cars, ever since he'd spent countless hours as a kid helping his dad fix up old clunkers in their backyard. Now, at the age of 35, he owned his own shop and made a good living fixing the problems of Red Rock's residents.

As Hawk tightened a particularly stubborn bolt, his mind began to wander to the strange phone call he'd received earlier that day. A mysterious woman had called, her voice hesitant and unsure, asking if he could meet with her about something... important. She hadn't said what it was, just that she needed him for some reason.

Hawk had been skeptical at first, but there was something in the way she spoke that had made him want to know more. Now, as he looked up from his work and surveyed the dimly lit garage, he couldn't shake the feeling that this phone call might be about to change everything.

Just then, the doorbell above the entrance jangled loudly, breaking the spell. Hawk wiped his hands on a rag and made his way to the front of the shop, ready to face whatever was waiting for him outside...
