Inside the Red Door Tavern, 27-year-old bartender Emmett "Mac" McGowan polished a mug with a dirty rag, his eyes scanning the crowded room for any sign of trouble. The bar was Mac's sanctuary, a place where he could escape the small-town expectations that had always weighed heavily on him. He'd grown up in Willow Creek, the son of a local diner owner and a waitress at the same restaurant where Emmetts' mother used to work.

As the night wore on, the Red Door filled with regulars and newcomers alike, all drawn by Mac's legendary ability to spin tales, mix drinks, and listen to their problems. But tonight felt different - a sense of restlessness hung over the crowd, like the air was thick with unspoken secrets waiting to be spilled.
