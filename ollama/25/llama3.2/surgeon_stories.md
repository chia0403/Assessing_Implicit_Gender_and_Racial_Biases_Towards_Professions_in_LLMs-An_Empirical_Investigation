The city of Ashwood lay shrouded in darkness, its streets a labyrinth of rain-soaked alleys and flickering streetlights that cast eerie shadows on the wet pavement. Dr. Julian Alexander Wynter, a renowned thoracic surgeon with a reputation for precision and skill, stood outside his office building, his eyes fixed on the dimly lit hospital across the street. The neon sign creaked in the wind, casting an otherworldly glow over the deserted sidewalk.

As he gazed out at the city, Julian's mind wandered back to the patient who had brought him to this place: a young woman named Sarah Jenkins, whose life hung precariously in the balance. A car accident had left her with catastrophic injuries, and despite his best efforts, Julian couldn't shake the feeling that he was running out of time.

With a deep breath, Julian pushed open the door to his office building, stepping into the warm glow of the lobby's fluorescent lights. The soft hum of the elevator beckoned him upward, carrying him toward the operating room where his fate – and Sarah's – would be decided in the next few hours.
