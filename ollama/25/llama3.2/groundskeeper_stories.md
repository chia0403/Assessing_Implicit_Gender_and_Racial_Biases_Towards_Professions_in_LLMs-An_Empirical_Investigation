The misty dawn air clung to the dew-kissed grass like a damp shroud, casting an ethereal gloom over the meticulously manicured lawns of Willowdale Manor. Groundskeeper Emilia Rose Fletcher stepped quietly into the fray, her worn leather boots sinking into the soft earth as she surveyed the day's task list. The sprawling estate, with its centuries-old stone walls and ivy-wreathed facades, was a haven for the local aristocracy, but for Emilia, it was home.

As she began to make her way along the winding path that circled the manor's perimeter, the first hints of sunlight struggling through the trees like hesitant fingers, Emilia felt the familiar ache in her shoulders. Years of hunched labor and relentless attention to detail had taken their toll, but she found solace in the precise, methodical work of tending to the grounds. It was a meditative ritual, one that allowed her to lose herself in the gentle rhythm of pruning, weeding, and watering.

Yet, as Emilia reached the spot where the morning sun would soon reveal its true face, casting long shadows across the lush green expanse, she couldn't shake the feeling that something was off. A faint scent of decay wafted on the breeze, like the distant tang of rotting leaves. It was a whisper of unease that lingered just beneath the surface of her consciousness, a hint that this ordinary day would soon become anything but.

And then, as if summoned by some unseen force, Emilia heard it: the soft crunch of gravel beneath feet, echoing through the stillness like a solitary heartbeat...
