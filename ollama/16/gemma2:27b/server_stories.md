The air hung thick with the scent of garlic and desperation. Eliza Marie Davenport adjusted her apron, the faded fabric scratchy against her calloused hands. Outside, the neon glow of "Luigi's Pizzeria" battled with the encroaching dusk, casting long shadows that danced across the cracked asphalt parking lot. Inside, a cacophony of clattering dishes, boisterous laughter, and Luigi's operatic bellow competed for dominance. 

Eliza navigated the crowded tables with practiced ease, her tray laden with steaming pizzas and bowls overflowing with pasta. It was Friday night, the busiest time of the week, and the restaurant throbbed with a frenetic energy that both exhausted and exhilarated Eliza.  Tonight, she needed the tips more than ever.
