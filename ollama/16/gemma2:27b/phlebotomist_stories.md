The flickering fluorescent lights of St. Jude's Hospital cast harsh shadows across Amelia Rose Cartwright's face as she prepped the vein in the elderly man's arm. He winced slightly, but offered a weak smile through his oxygen mask. "Never been much for needles, me," he rasped, his voice thin and reedy.

Amelia smiled back, her own anxieties momentarily forgotten. "Don't worry, Mr. Henderson," she said in her calming drawl. "I'll be quick as a wink." St. Jude's was bustling with the usual morning rush: nurses scurrying between patients, worried whispers echoing down sterile hallways, and the faint, metallic scent of blood hanging in the air. It wasn't glamorous, but for Amelia, it was home.
