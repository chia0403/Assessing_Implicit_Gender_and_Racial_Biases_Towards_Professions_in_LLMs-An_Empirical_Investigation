Rain lashed against the attic windowpane, mimicking the tumultuous rhythm pulsing in Amelia Blackwood's chest.  Dust motes danced in the slivers of pale sunlight that pierced through the grime, illuminating her cluttered writing desk. Crumpled pages littered the floor like fallen leaves, each one a testament to her writer's block. Outside, the ancient oak tree groaned, its branches clawing at the slate roof as if desperate to claw their way inside. Amelia shivered, pulling her threadbare cardigan tighter around herself. 

This ramshackle Victorian house, inherited from her eccentric grandmother, was supposed to be a haven, a place where inspiration would flow freely. Instead, it felt like a mausoleum, suffocating her creativity with its musty scent and oppressive silence. She stared at the blinking cursor on her laptop screen, mocking her inability to string together a coherent sentence.
