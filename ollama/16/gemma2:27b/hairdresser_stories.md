The insistent trill of the antique doorbell echoed through Beatrice "Bea" Cartwright's salon, bouncing off the mahogany shelves crammed with vintage hair products and framed photographs of glamorous coiffures from bygone eras. Bea paused mid-snip, strands of fiery red hair cascading onto the worn linoleum floor around her client, Mrs. Periwinkle.

"Duty calls," Bea chirped, placing a delicate hand on Mrs. Periwinkle's shoulder before bustling towards the front of the salon. Sunlight streamed through the arched window above the door, illuminating dust motes dancing in the air and casting a warm glow over the eclectic decor: velvet armchairs upholstered in paisley prints, framed botanical illustrations, and a porcelain doll collection perched precariously on a high shelf.

Bea took a deep breath, smoothing down her apron embroidered with "Bea's Beauty Boutique" before swinging open the door with a flourish. A gust of wind whipped through the salon, carrying with it the salty tang of the sea and the faint melody of street musicians playing a lively jig.
