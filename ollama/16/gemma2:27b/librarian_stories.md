Rain lashed against the arched windows of the Blackwood Library, each drop a frantic drummer on the ancient glass panes.  The scent of aging paper and leather mingled with the earthy aroma of petrichor drifting in from the storm-lashed streets of Edinburgh. Eleanor Ainsworth, her silver hair escaping its bun as she peered over a stack of overdue notices, sighed contentedly. 

There was nowhere Eleanor felt more at peace than amidst the whispering shelves and towering stacks of the Blackwood. For fifty years, she had been its guardian, its silent sentinel against the tide of time that threatened to erode the stories held within its walls. Tonight, however, a different kind of storm brewed. A sense of unease prickled her skin as the clock on the mantelpiece chimed ten.  Something was amiss.
