The neon sign outside flickered "The Rusty Sprocket," casting long shadows across the cracked asphalt parking lot. Inside, the air hung thick with cigarette smoke, cheap beer, and the murmur of weary conversations. Bartholomew "Bart" Higgins, his salt-and-pepper hair slicked back, expertly juggled three cocktail shakers, the rhythm a familiar lullaby in this dimly lit haven. He'd been behind the bar at The Rusty Sprocket for longer than he cared to remember, a silent observer to the unfolding dramas of the city's underbelly. Tonight, like every other night, Bart poured drinks and listened to stories, his keen eyes missing nothing and his lips sealed tight. But tonight, something felt different. A tension thrummed beneath the surface, a sense that the ordinary was about to shatter into something extraordinary.
