The morning sun sliced through the thin curtains, illuminating motes of dust dancing in the air like miniature galaxies. Amelia Beatrice Hawthorne yawned, stretching her arms high above her head, the faint scent of crayons and Play-Doh clinging to her pajamas. 

She glanced at the clock: 6:15 am. Another day at Little Sprouts Kindergarten was about to begin. Her classroom, a riot of color and whimsy with hand-painted murals on the walls and shelves overflowing with building blocks and dress-up clothes, awaited its daily influx of tiny humans. Amelia, despite the exhaustion that threatened to pull her back into slumber, couldn't help but smile. She loved seeing the world through their eyes, witnessing their boundless curiosity and unbridled enthusiasm. 


This year, however, felt different. A strange energy hummed beneath the familiar rhythm of school mornings, a silent anticipation that Amelia couldn't quite place.
