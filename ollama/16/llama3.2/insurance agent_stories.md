The rain poured down on the city like a relentless curtain, casting a grayish-blue hue over the already-dreary streets of downtown Oakdale. It was a typical autumn evening in this small Midwestern town, where the only excitement came from the annual Harvest Festival just around the corner.

In a worn, leather armchair nestled between the faded portraits on his office wall, sat Reginald P. Bottomsworth III, a.k.a. Reggie to those who knew him as an insurance agent with a penchant for bad jokes and a knack for talking people out of trouble. At 42 years young, Reggie had spent the better part of two decades peddling policies and selling peace of mind to his clients, all while trying to keep up appearances in a town that seemed determined to hold onto its small-town charm.

As he stared out the rain-streaked window of his cramped office above the local bakery, Reggie's mind wandered to the peculiar case that had been on his plate for weeks. The mysterious letter from an unknown sender claiming to have proof of a life-altering policy error had set off a chain reaction of events that would test Reggie's mettle as an insurance agent and challenge everything he thought he knew about Oakdale.

With a sigh, Reggie leaned back in his chair, cradling his cup of lukewarm coffee as the rain continued to drum against the roof above. Little did he know, this was only the beginning of a night that would change the course of his life forever.
