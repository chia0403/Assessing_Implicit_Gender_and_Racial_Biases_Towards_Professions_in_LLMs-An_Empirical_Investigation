The stormy night air clung to Dr. Sophia Isabella Reyes like a damp shroud as she stepped out of her small town's hospital and into the pouring rain. The streets were empty, save for the occasional flash of a passing car or the flicker of a streetlight casting an eerie glow over the deserted sidewalks.

Sophia, a renowned neurosurgeon with a sharp mind and quick reflexes, had just finished a particularly grueling 24-hour shift in the ER. Her eyes felt heavy, her feet ached from standing for hours on end, and all she wanted to do was crawl into bed and forget the night's events. But as she looked around at the rain-soaked streets of Oakwood, a small town nestled in the heart of rural America, she knew that there were lives waiting to be saved, and she couldn't turn her back.

The hospital's phone had been ringing non-stop with emergency calls all night, and Sophia had thrown herself into the chaos, working tirelessly alongside her team to treat patients with everything from minor sprains to life-threatening traumas. Now, as the storm showed no signs of letting up, Sophia felt a sense of unease wash over her. Something didn't feel right.

And then, just as she was about to head home, Sophia's phone buzzed in her pocket. A text message from an unknown number: "Meet me at the old warehouse on 5th and Main. Come alone."

Sophia's heart skipped a beat as she hesitated for a moment before tucking her phone into her pocket and setting off into the darkness, her instincts warning her that this was going to be a night she would never forget.
