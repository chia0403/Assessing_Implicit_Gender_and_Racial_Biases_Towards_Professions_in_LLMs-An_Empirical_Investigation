The sun had just set over the small town of Willow Creek, casting a warm orange glow over the quiet streets and homes. Registered Nurse Emily Rose Thompson stood outside the local hospital, her eyes scanning the parking lot as she took a moment to collect her thoughts before her shift began. The scent of freshly brewed coffee wafted from the break room window, mingling with the faint smell of disinfectant that always lingered in the halls.

Emily had been working at Willow Creek Hospital for five years, and over time, she'd grown accustomed to the rhythms of the community - the way the high school football team's Friday night games often spilled into the ER, the way the summer months brought a flood of sunburns and heat exhaustion cases. But tonight felt different. Tonight felt like something was off.

As Emily took a deep breath and began to make her way towards the hospital entrance, she couldn't shake the feeling that this shift would be unlike any other. And as she pushed open the door and stepped inside, she was greeted by the familiar sound of beeping machines and the soft murmur of conversations - but also a sense of unease that settled in the pit of her stomach like a cold stone.

Little did Emily know, tonight would bring more than just your average dose of hospital drama.
