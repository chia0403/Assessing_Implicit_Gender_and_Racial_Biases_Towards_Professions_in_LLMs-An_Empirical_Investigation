Here's a potential story opening and setting:

The sun was setting over the vast expanse of I-40, casting a golden glow over the rows of dilapidated truck stops and worn-down diners that lined the highway. Jack Harrison "Hawk" Hawkins, a grizzled trucker with a heart of gold and a penchant for whiskey, pulled his Peterbilt 359 into the parking lot of the rundown Travelodge, its neon sign creaking in the gentle breeze.

Hawk had been driving for weeks, hauling cargo from the Midwest to the East Coast, and he was starting to feel the weight of it all. The long hours, the endless miles, the constant din of the engine and the hum of the CB radio - it was taking its toll on his body and his mind. He just wanted to get back to Nashville, where his ex-wife, Sarah, and their young daughter, Emily, waited for him.

As he shut off the engine and stepped out of the cab, Hawk felt a sense of relief wash over him. The warm breeze carried the scent of BBQ and fried chicken from the truck stop's kitchen, and he could almost taste the familiar flavors of home. He took a deep breath, feeling the tension in his shoulders begin to ease, and began to make his way towards the motel office to check-in for the night.

Little did Hawk know, his peaceful evening was about to take a dramatic turn. As he pulled into his parking spot, he noticed a strange figure lurking in the shadows, watching him with an unnerving intensity...
