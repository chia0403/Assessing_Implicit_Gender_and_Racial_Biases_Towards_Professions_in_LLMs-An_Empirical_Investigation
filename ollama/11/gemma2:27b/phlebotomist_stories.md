Rain lashed against the windows of Mercy General, blurring the neon glow of the "Open 24 Hours" sign. Inside, Emilia Blackwood meticulously wiped down a blood pressure cuff, her movements as precise and rhythmic as a conductor leading an orchestra. The fluorescent lights hummed overhead, casting a sterile sheen on the worn linoleum floor. Emilia, with her auburn hair pulled back in a tight braid and eyes that held the steady gaze of someone who dealt with anxiety and pain daily, was accustomed to the hushed tension that permeated the waiting room at this hour.

Tonight, however, something felt different. A prickle of unease ran down her spine as she glanced towards the double doors leading to the emergency bay. The gurney that had been wheeled in earlier now sat empty, its sterile sheets rumpled and stained with a dark crimson. The air itself seemed to hold its breath, thick with unspoken dread.
