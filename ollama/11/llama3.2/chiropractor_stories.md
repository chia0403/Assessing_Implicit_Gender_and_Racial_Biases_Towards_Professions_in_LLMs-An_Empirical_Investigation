Here's a potential story opening and setting for a novel featuring a chiropractor as the main character:

Dr. Alexandra "Lexi" Thompson adjusted her white coat over her shoulders, the crisp fabric a stark contrast to the worn leather of her office's armchair. She sat back down in the chair, running a hand through her dark brown hair, which was tied up in a messy bun. Lexi took a deep breath, letting the scent of essential oils and fresh coffee fill her lungs as she gazed out at the quiet morning hours.

Her chiropractic clinic, "Thompson Spine & Wellness," was nestled in the heart of downtown Portland's Pearl District. The streets were bustling with activity on this crisp autumn morning – people sipping lattes on sidewalks, cyclists weaving through traffic, and street performers setting up their acts for the day's crowds. But inside Lexi's office, it was a peaceful oasis.

Lexi had always loved helping people find relief from pain and discomfort. She'd spent years studying the intricacies of human anatomy and honing her skills as a chiropractor, earning her a reputation as one of the most compassionate and knowledgeable practitioners in the city. As she settled into her chair, Lexi felt a sense of calm wash over her – this was where she belonged.
