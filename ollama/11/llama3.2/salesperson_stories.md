The sun had barely risen over the city, casting a golden glow over the bustling streets of Tokyo as Kenji Takahashi stepped out of his small apartment in the Shimokitazawa district. Kenji, a 30-year-old salesperson at a mid-sized electronics company, took a deep breath of the crisp morning air, feeling the familiar mix of excitement and trepidation that came with each new day.

As he made his way to the train station, Kenji gazed out at the towering skyscrapers of Shibuya and Harajuku, their neon lights still dim in the pre-dawn hours. He passed by small shops and cafes, already bustling with activity as the city's residents began their daily routines. The sounds of chatter, honking cars, and wailing sirens created a symphony that Kenji had grown to love over the years.

Kenji's thoughts were already on his upcoming sales meeting at the company's headquarters in Roppongi, where he would be pitching a new line of high-end smartphones to a group of skeptical executives. He had spent countless hours researching and preparing for this presentation, but he couldn't shake off the feeling that something was missing.

As he boarded the train and began his commute to work, Kenji pulled out his phone to check his schedule for the day. His eyes scanned the list of appointments and meetings, his mind racing with possibilities. Little did he know, today would be a day like no other...
