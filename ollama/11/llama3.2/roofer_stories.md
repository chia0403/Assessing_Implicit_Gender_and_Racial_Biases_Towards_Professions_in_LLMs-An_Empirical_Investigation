The smell of freshly cut grass and baked goods wafted through the air, mingling with the sound of children's laughter and the occasional rumble of a lawnmower.

In a small workshop above his family's old stone house, 32-year-old Ryder Jackson Harris adjusted his worn denim jeans and slipped on his favorite faded band t-shirt. As he prepared for another long day of work as a roofer, he gazed out the window at the rooftops of Willow Creek, each one a unique puzzle waiting to be solved.

After high school, Ryder had taken over the reins, determined to make a name for himself as one of the best roofers in town. With his trusty ladder and reliable tools by his side, he was always ready to tackle whatever challenges came his way.
