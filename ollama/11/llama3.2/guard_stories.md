The mist-shrouded streets of Ravenshire lay silent under the pale moonlight, its fog rolling in off the dark waters of the town's namesake river like a living entity. Within the old stone walls of the Watchtower, guard Edgar Fletcher stood watch on the battlements, his eyes scanning the darkness for any sign of movement. It had been three nights since the first incident – a stranger found wandering the streets, muttering to himself in a language no one could understand.

Now, as the wind whispered secrets through the broken panes of the tower's windows, Edgar felt an uneasy sense that something was off. The townsfolk were spooked by the mysterious apparition, and some even went so far as to say they'd seen shadowy figures lurking just beyond the edge of town.

Edgar wasn't one for superstitions – he was a practical man, with years of experience serving the Watchtower's duties. Still, a shiver ran down his spine as he gazed out into the darkness, wondering if this strange occurrence might be connected to something far more sinister lurking in the depths of Ravenshire.

As he stood there, lost in thought, a faint jingling sound echoed through the night air – the call of a lone horse's bell, echoing from the direction of the town gate. Edgar's hand instinctively went to his own sword, half-hidden beneath his cloak...
