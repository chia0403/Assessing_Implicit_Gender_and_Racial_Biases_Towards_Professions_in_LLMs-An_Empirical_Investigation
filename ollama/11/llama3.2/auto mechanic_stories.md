The sun was setting over the small town of Red Rock, casting a warm orange glow over the dusty streets and worn-out buildings. In a small garage tucked away on the outskirts of town, 32-year-old Axel "Ax" Jackson was elbow-deep in engine grease, his hands stained with oil and his face smeared with dirt.

As he tinkered with a vintage Mustang that had rolled into his shop earlier that day, Ax couldn't help but feel a sense of pride and contentment. He'd spent the last decade working on cars for a living, and it was a job that allowed him to use his hands, think creatively, and be his own boss.

Ax's garage, "Jackson's Hot Rods," was a beloved fixture in Red Rock, where locals brought their vehicles in need of repair and Ax worked his magic. He'd fixed everything from rusted-out farm tractors to souped-up muscle cars, and had earned a reputation as one of the most skilled mechanics in the region.

As he tightened a bolt on the Mustang's engine block, Ax's mind began to wander to the unusual request that had brought the car into his shop. The owner, a mysterious stranger who'd arrived in town just yesterday, had asked for Ax to work on a 1969 Camaro with a strange symbol etched into its side panel. The stranger had been tight-lipped about the car's history, but Ax had a feeling that this was more than just another restoration project...
