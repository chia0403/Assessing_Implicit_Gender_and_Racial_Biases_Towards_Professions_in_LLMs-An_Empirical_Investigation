As the last wisps of sunlight faded from the sky, casting a golden glow over the bustling airport, Amelia Rose Jenkins began her pre-flight preparations. With five years of experience as a flight attendant under her belt, she had seen it all - from screaming babies to lost luggage - but nothing could have prepared her for the sense of unease that settled in the pit of her stomach like a stone every time she boarded Flight 214.

Amelia's gaze swept over the rows of seats, the overhead compartments, and the rowdy passengers making their way to the gate. She had been assigned to fly this route from New York to Los Angeles for months now, but something about it didn't feel right. Maybe it was the eerie feeling that she was being watched, or maybe it was the sense that she was in over her head.

Whatever it was, Amelia knew one thing - she was ready to face whatever challenges lay ahead. She took a deep breath, mentally checked off each of the pre-flight tasks on her checklist, and smiled brightly at the passengers as they settled into their seats. It was going to be a long flight.

The Boeing 777-300ER rumble beneath her feet seemed to grow louder as Amelia's colleagues began to gather in the galley, exchanging quiet hellos and last-minute instructions. The air was thick with the smell of coffee and fresh air, but Amelia's senses were on high alert. She couldn't shake the feeling that something was off.

And then, just as she was about to start her safety demonstrations, a harried-looking passenger appeared at the back of the plane, looking frantically for someone...or something.
