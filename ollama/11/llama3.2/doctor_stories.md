Dr. Astrid Elara Vasquez stood at the edge of the hospital room, her eyes fixed on the frail figure lying in the bed. She had spent countless hours tending to the sick and injured over the years, but there was something about this patient that resonated with her on a deep level.

The patient, a young woman named Sophia Jenkins, had been admitted to the hospital just hours before, with symptoms that hinted at something far more sinister than mere mortal illness.

The setting was a small, coastal town in Oregon, where Dr. Vasquez had moved a year ago seeking a fresh start after a painful divorce and a grueling residency. The town's quaint charm and stunning natural beauty were still new to her, but the small hospital where she worked felt like home. As she looked around the room, Dr. Vasquez noticed the worn linoleum floor, the flickering fluorescent lights above, and the faint scent of disinfectant wafting through the air.

Outside, the stormy night sky seemed to be mirroring the turmoil brewing inside Dr. Vasquez's own mind. The wind howled like a restless spirit, causing the hospital's windows to rattle and shake, but she stood firm, transfixed by Sophia Jenkins' fragile form and the mystery that surrounded her...
