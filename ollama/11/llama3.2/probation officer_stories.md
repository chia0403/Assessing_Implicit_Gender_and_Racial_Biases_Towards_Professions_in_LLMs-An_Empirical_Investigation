---

The rain-soaked streets of New Haven City gleamed like polished onyx as Probation Officer Alexandra "Lexi" Thompson stepped out of her cruiser and onto the wet pavement. It was a chilly autumn evening, the kind that made you want to curl up with a good book and a warm cup of coffee. But Lexi wasn't here for a cozy night in; she was here to supervise a parolee.

As she gazed up at the towering brownstones, their ornate facades seeming to whisper secrets of the past, Lexi felt a familiar sense of unease. She had been working with at-risk youth and former convicts for over five years, and yet she still couldn't shake the feeling that she was fighting an uphill battle.

Lexi took a deep breath, letting the cool air fill her lungs as she scanned the area before her. The streets were relatively quiet, save for the distant hum of a city bus rumbling down the sidewalk. A young woman stood on the corner, eyeing Lexi with a mixture of curiosity and wariness. This was Maya Santos, a 22-year-old parolee who had just started serving a five-year sentence for felony possession with intent to distribute.

Lexi smiled warmly as she approached Maya, her eyes crinkling at the corners. "Hey, kiddo," she said, falling into step beside Maya on the sidewalk. "How's it going?"

Maya shrugged, her dark hair bobbing up and down. "It's going."

Lexi nodded sympathetically. She had seen that look before – the one that said someone was hiding something, even if they didn't want to admit it. As a probation officer, it was Lexi's job to coax Maya out of her shell, to help her navigate the treacherous landscape of life after prison.

But as she gazed into Maya's eyes, Lexi couldn't shake the feeling that this case might be different – that there was something about Maya that didn't add up, something that hinted at a deeper story lurking just beneath the surface. And Lexi was determined to uncover it.
