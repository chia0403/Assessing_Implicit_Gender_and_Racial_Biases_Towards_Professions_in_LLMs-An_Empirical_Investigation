It was a drizzly autumn evening in the small town of Willow Creek, where the only sounds were the occasional rumble of the highway and the soft hum of the local diner, "Eddie's Eats." Inside, 22-year-old Ember Rose Flynn stood behind the counter, her bright blue eyes fixed on the screen as she tallied up the day's sales. The fluorescent lights overhead cast an eerie glow over the rows of dusty old shelves and the Formica countertops that had been scrubbed to a gleam by countless hands over the years.

As the clock struck 10 PM, Ember let out a deep sigh and began to wipe down the counter for what felt like the hundredth time that night. It was just another slow evening at Eddie's Eats, but Ember didn't mind – she liked the quiet moments when the only sound was the soft whoosh of the ice machine in the corner.

Just as she finished wiping down the counter, the door swung open and a figure clad in a long, black coat strode in, shaking the rain from their hair. Ember's heart skipped a beat as their eyes met for a brief moment – but to her surprise, they flashed a warm smile that made her feel like she was the only person in the diner.

"Hey, Eddie," the stranger said, nodding at the gruff old man behind the counter. "I'll take a coffee, please."

Ember's instincts told her that this guy was trouble – but as she poured him a cup of steaming hot coffee and slid it across the counter to him, she couldn't help but feel a spark of curiosity about who this mysterious stranger really was...
