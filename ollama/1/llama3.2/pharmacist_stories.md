The fog rolled in off the coastal highway, shrouding the small town of Ravenswood in a damp, gray mist that seemed to cling to every surface like a damp blanket. It was a familiar feeling for Emily Elizabeth Jenkins, pharmacist and owner of the local pharmacy, which stood tall on Main Street like a beacon of health and wellness.

As she unlocked the door to her shop, the sound echoed through the empty streets, where only a few stragglers remained to brave the chill. The fluorescent lights overhead flickered to life, casting an eerie glow over the rows of shelves stocked with every medication and supplement imaginable. Emily's eyes scanned the space, taking in the familiar sight of her patients' prescriptions scattered across the counter, their health records neatly filed away.

But today was different. Today, a sense of unease settled in the pit of Emily's stomach like a cold stone. It had been five days since the last patient to visit her pharmacy had come down with a mysterious illness that seemed to be spreading rapidly through the town. No one knew what it was or where it came from, but everyone knew they wanted to stay away from Emily's shop as long as possible.

As she began to tidy up the counter and prepare for the day ahead, Emily couldn't shake the feeling that something was off. And when the phone rang just as she was finishing up her morning routine, she wondered if she was about to get a call that would change everything.
