The sun was setting over the bustling streets of Marrakech, casting a warm orange glow over the vibrant souks and alleys. In the heart of this ancient city, where the sounds of merchants hawking their wares mingled with the scent of spices and incense, Aziz ben Hassan prepared for another day as a stone cutter.

Aziz, whose full name was Aziz bin Hassan ibn Ali al-Mu'azzam, stood at his workbench, his calloused hands moving deftly as he shaped a block of dark grey granite into the perfect crescent moon. The morning light streaming through the narrow window highlighted the dust motes dancing in the air, and for a moment, Aziz felt at peace.

As a master stone cutter, Aziz had spent years honing his craft under the guidance of his father, who had learned from his own father before him. His workshop, tucked away in a quiet corner of the medina, was a treasure trove of ancient tools and techniques, passed down through generations of his family.

But as he worked, Aziz's mind wandered to the commission that had brought him to this point - a grand monument for the Sultan's palace, one that would be remembered for centuries to come. The pressure was mounting, and Aziz knew that every cut, every chisel stroke, had to be perfect if he wanted to live up to his reputation as one of the finest stone cutters in Marrakech.

And yet, as the evening sun dipped below the rooftops, casting the city in a warm, golden light, Aziz felt a sense of excitement building within him. Tonight, under the stars, he would work on the most ambitious project of his life...
