Here's a potential story opening:

The rain-soaked streets of Paris glistened like polished onyx as Emilia Adelaide Dupont stepped out of her small apartment building, onto the narrow stairway that led to the bustling Rue de Rivoli. The morning light, filtered through the misty veil of the City of Light, cast a pale glow over the rooftops and awnings, casting long shadows across the cobblestone pavement.

Emilia adjusted the worn leather strap of her bag, a familiar weight that seemed to anchor her to reality as she surveyed the chaotic scene before her. The café on the corner was already thronged with patrons, their faces pressed against the steam-kissed windows as they debated the merits of the latest literary releases. She felt an instinctual pang in her chest, a twinge of envy mixed with a dash of longing. Why couldn't she be one of them – sipping coffee and discussing the latest trends in French literature?

As she descended into the morning crowd, Emilia's mind began to wander back to her own manuscript, that stubbornly incomplete novel that had been occupying her thoughts for what felt like an eternity. It was a tale of love, loss, and redemption set against the backdrop of 19th-century France, with herself as the protagonist – but not in life, exactly, though she couldn't quite shake the feeling that she was somehow echoing those characters from her own past.

The sound of the café's door swinging open sent Emilia into a sudden flurry of thoughts. It was going to be one of those days...
