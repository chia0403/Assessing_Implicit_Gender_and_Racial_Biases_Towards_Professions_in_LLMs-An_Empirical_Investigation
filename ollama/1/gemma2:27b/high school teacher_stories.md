Rain lashed against the windows of Mrs. Eleanor Cavendish's classroom, mimicking the tempest brewing inside her. The worn, green chalkboard was still smeared with yesterday's incomplete geometry proofs, ghostly reminders of a lesson gone awry. Outside, the late October wind whipped fallen leaves into miniature tornadoes across the deserted schoolyard.

Eleanor sighed, rubbing her temples. Teaching at Hawthorne High had always been a challenge, but lately it felt like an uphill battle against an uncaring tide. Budgets were slashed, class sizes ballooned, and student apathy seemed to be reaching epidemic proportions.  Today's final straw had been Michael Thompson, a sullen sophomore who'd openly scoffed at her attempts to explain the Pythagorean Theorem.
