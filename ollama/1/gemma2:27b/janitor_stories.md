The flickering fluorescent lights buzzed in time with the low hum of the ventilation system, casting long, skeletal shadows across the deserted corridors of the Grand Blackwood Library. Elias Thorne, his stooped figure almost swallowed by the oversized janitorial uniform, pushed his mop bucket down the hallway, the squeak of its wheels a lonely counterpoint to the silence. The air hung heavy with the scent of dust and aging paper, a comforting aroma that Elias had grown accustomed to over his twenty years as the library's night janitor. 

Tonight, however, something felt different. A prickle of unease danced along Elias' spine, an instinctual warning he couldn't quite place. He glanced up at the towering shelves laden with ancient tomes, their spines whispering forgotten secrets in languages both familiar and strange. The shadows seemed deeper tonight, the silence more profound.
