Rain lashed against the grimy windows of Dr. Elias Thorne's cramped office, mirroring the storm brewing inside him. The fluorescent lights hummed overhead, casting an anemic glow on the overflowing filing cabinets and stacks of student assessments that threatened to engulf his desk. He sighed, running a hand through his thinning hair. Another Tuesday afternoon at Willow Creek High, another day wrestling with the anxieties, frustrations, and hidden traumas of teenagers.

Elias had always been drawn to helping others, especially the vulnerable. That's what led him to become a school psychologist, believing he could make a difference in the lives of young people navigating the treacherous waters of adolescence. But lately, the weight of their burdens felt heavier than usual.  He glanced at the clock - 3:15 pm. He was due to meet with Sarah Jenkins, a shy sophomore who had been exhibiting signs of severe social anxiety. Elias hoped he could help her find some solace amidst the chaos of high school life.
