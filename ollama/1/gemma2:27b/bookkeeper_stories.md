Rain lashed against the grimy windowpanes of The Counting House, blurring the already indistinct view of Baker Street. Eleanor Finchley, her spectacles perched precariously on the bridge of her nose, squinted at the ledger spread before her. The numbers swam, refusing to settle into the neat columns she craved. It wasn't the figures themselves that troubled her - Eleanor prided herself on her meticulous nature and innate grasp of arithmetic. No, it was the discrepancy, a stubborn anomaly tucked away in the monthly accounts for Mr. Thaddeus Grimshaw, purveyor of fine antique clocks. 

The rhythmic tick-tock of the grandfather clock in the corner, usually a comforting sound, now seemed to mock Eleanor's unease. Each measured click amplified the question swirling in her mind: where had the missing shillings gone?
