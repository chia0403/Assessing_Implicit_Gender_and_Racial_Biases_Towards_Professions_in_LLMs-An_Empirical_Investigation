The air hung thick with humidity and the scent of jasmine as Emilia Rose Thorncroft adjusted her silk blouse, smoothing out the wrinkles that had appeared during her frantic dash across the cobblestone streets of New Orleans. Beads of sweat trickled down her temples, mingling with the shimmer of gold dust that clung to her skin from a misplaced celebratory toast earlier that evening. 

Outside the courtroom doors, the cacophony of the French Quarter – musicians serenading tourists, vendors hawking their wares, and the clinking of glass as patrons enjoyed beignets at Cafe du Monde – was abruptly silenced by the heavy wooden thud. Emilia took a deep breath, steeling herself for the battle ahead. The Louisiana Supreme Court wasn't known for its leniency, and her client, a young jazz musician accused of arson, needed all the legal firepower she could muster.


This was it: a case that could make or break her career.  And Emilia Rose Thorncroft, fiery, determined, and armed with a wit sharper than a voodoo doll's needle, was ready to fight.
