The sun had just begun to set over the small town of Willow Creek, casting a warm orange glow over the quaint Main Street. The smell of fresh-cut grass wafted through the air, mingling with the hum of engines from the nearby airport. Captain Ethan Ryder Blackwood stood on the tarmac, gazing out at his plane, a sleek black Cessna 172, as he went through the pre-flight checks.

As the pilot and owner of Blackwood Aviation, Ethan had spent countless hours soaring through the skies above Willow Creek, delivering goods and people to their destinations with precision and care. He loved the freedom of flight, the feeling of being on top of the world, and the sense of adventure that came with every new trip.

But tonight was different. Tonight, Ethan was bound for a destination he'd never been before - the small island town of Ravenswood, located off the coast of Maine. The job was to fly a group of tourists on a scenic tour of the area's rugged coastline and picturesque lighthouses. It should have been an easy flight, but as Ethan began his pre-flight checks, he couldn't shake the feeling that something was off.

As he finished up the final checks and prepared for departure, Ethan's phone buzzed with an incoming text from his co-pilot, Rachel. "Ethan, we've got a problem," it read. "The weather forecast just came in and it looks like we're going to have to make some adjustments."

Ethan's heart rate quickened as he wondered what the issue could be. He looked up at the sky, watching as the sun dipped lower and lower, casting long shadows across the tarmac. This was not the kind of night he'd planned for.
