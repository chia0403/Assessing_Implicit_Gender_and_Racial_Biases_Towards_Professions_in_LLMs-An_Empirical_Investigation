The rain-soaked streets of New Haven City gleamed like polished steel under the faint moonlight that struggled to penetrate the dense cloud cover. It was a night much like any other in this sprawling metropolis, where the city's elite resided in opulent towers and the working class huddled in cramped tenements. In one of those tenements, on the third floor of a decrepit building on Elm Street, Kaidon Everett Harris stood watch.

As a security guard at the local university, Kaidon had seen his fair share of late-night escapades and whispered conversations. But tonight felt different. The air was thick with an almost palpable sense of unease, as if the city itself was holding its breath in anticipation of something unknown.

Kaidon's eyes scanned the dimly lit hallways, his gaze lingering on the flickering fluorescent lights that cast eerie shadows on the walls. He checked his watch for what felt like the hundredth time - 11:47 PM. The night shift had been quiet so far, with only a handful of students hurrying to their next class or slipping out for a late-night smoke. But Kaidon's instincts told him that tonight was going to be different.

As he stood at attention, his hand resting on the worn grip of his nightstick, Kaidon couldn't shake the feeling that something - or someone - was watching him from the shadows.
