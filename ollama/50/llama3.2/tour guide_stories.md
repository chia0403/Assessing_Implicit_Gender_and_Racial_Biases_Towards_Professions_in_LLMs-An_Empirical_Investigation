The sun had barely cracked the horizon over the ancient city of Petra, casting a warm orange glow over the narrow canyon known as the Siq. Tourist groups were already beginning to stir, their footsteps echoing off the sandstone walls as they made their way towards the iconic Treasury building.

Among them was Amal Hassan Al-Khateeb, a seasoned tour guide with a deep knowledge of Petra's history and secrets. Born and raised in Jordan, Amal had spent her entire life exploring the ancient ruins and learning from her family's generations-long tradition of guiding visitors through its winding streets and temples.

As she led her latest group of tourists - a lively bunch of Americans and Germans - down into the Siq, Amal couldn't help but feel a sense of pride and ownership over this ancient city. She had grown up playing among its ruins, climbing up the narrow paths and hiding in the shadows of the Treasury's imposing facade.

As they stopped to admire the intricate carvings on the canyon walls, Amal noticed a strange figure lingering at the edge of the group. A young woman with piercing green eyes and jet-black hair, she seemed out of place among the boisterous tourists and snapped cameras.

Amal felt a shiver run down her spine as their eyes met for a brief moment. Who was this mysterious stranger, and what brought her to Petra?
