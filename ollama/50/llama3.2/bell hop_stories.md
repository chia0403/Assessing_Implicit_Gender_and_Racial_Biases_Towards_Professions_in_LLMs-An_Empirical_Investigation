---

The neon signs of the city's iconic hotel flickered like fireflies on a summer evening, casting a gaudy glow over the bustling streets of New Haven. The year was 1955, and the world outside these doors seemed to be changing fast - but inside, the grandeur of the Golden Oak Hotel remained frozen in time.

As the last of the day's guests departed, or so it seemed, Jasper Wystan Thorne, a bell hop with a penchant for dusty stories and whiskey-soaked dreams, began his nightly rounds. With a tired grin and a flick of his wrist, he swept aside the day's dirt and dust, ready to face another night of secrets kept and whispered promises made in the shadows.

Jasper had worked at the Golden Oak for nigh on five years now, and in that time, he'd seen it all - from high-society weddings to hushed conversations between shady characters. But tonight felt different. Tonight, something was off, like a faint scent of smoke on the wind.

As he polished the gleaming silver doorknob with a worn cloth, Jasper's eyes wandered up to the peacock-blue sky outside. The stars were beginning to twinkle, like diamonds scattered across the velvet expanse. It was moments like these that reminded him why he loved his job - not just for the tips or the tales of adventure, but for the promise of tomorrow, when anything could happen.

Little did Jasper know, tonight would be no ordinary night at the Golden Oak...
