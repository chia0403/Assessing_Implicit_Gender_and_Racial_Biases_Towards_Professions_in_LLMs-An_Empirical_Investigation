The sun was setting over the small town of Red Oak, casting a warm orange glow over the worn streets and faded storefronts. Inside Joe "Mac" McCarthy's Auto Repair Shop, the smell of gasoline and burnt rubber wafted through the air as Mac himself worked on a '68 Chevy Impala, its chrome grille gleaming in the fading light.

Mac, a gruff but lovable man with a thick beard and a bushy brow, wiped the sweat from his forehead as he tightened a loose bolt. He'd spent years working on cars in this very shop, fixing everything from flat tires to blown engines. It was a job that required patience, attention to detail, and a healthy dose of skepticism - after all, you could fool an engine with some fancy tricks, but it was only a matter of time before something broke.

As the evening wore on, Mac's mind began to wander from the task at hand. He'd had a strange feeling all day, like someone was watching him from just out of sight. Shrugging it off as mere paranoia, he turned his attention back to the Impala, but the sensation lingered, leaving him with an uneasy sense of being on edge.

Just then, the shop's phone rang, shrill and insistent in the quiet evening air. Mac hesitated for a moment before answering, his voice gruff as he asked, "McCarthy's Auto Repair. How can I help you?"
