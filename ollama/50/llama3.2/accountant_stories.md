Here's a story opening and setting for you:

The rain poured down on the city like a relentless curtain, casting a gloomy shroud over the towering skyscrapers of downtown New Haven. Inside the office of Harrison Everett Wembly III, CPA, the fluorescent lights hummed in sync with the rhythmic beat of the storm outside, illuminating rows of neatly stacked files and dusty ledgers.

Harrison, affectionately known as Harry to his colleagues, sat hunched over his desk, poring over a stack of tax returns for a particularly troublesome client. His thick-framed glasses perched on the end of his nose, and his sandy-blond hair was slicked back in an attempt to keep it from falling across his forehead.

As he worked, Harry's mind wandered to the upcoming annual gala at the New Haven Country Club, where he would have to face the city's most influential business leaders, all while trying to keep a straight face about their dodgy financial dealings. It was a necessary evil in his line of work, but it never failed to make him roll his eyes.

The phone on Harry's desk jolted him back to reality, shrill and insistent. "Wembly & Associates," he growled, answering the call with a hint of skepticism. Little did he know that this call would set off a chain reaction of events that would shake the very foundations of his quiet life as an accountant...
