The fluorescent lights hummed overhead, casting an eerie glow over the dimly lit corridors of Ravenswood High School. It was a chilly autumn evening, the kind that made you want to curl up with a hot cup of coffee and forget about the day's troubles. But for 22-year-old Julian "Jules" Thompson, there were no comforts to be found.

As the school's janitor, Jules had grown accustomed to the drudgery of his job. He took pride in keeping the hallways clean, sweeping up crumbs and spills that other students might have left behind. It was a thankless task, but it paid the bills and allowed him to stay on campus during the late-night hours when most everyone else was gone.

Jules sighed as he pushed his cart through the deserted hallway, the wheels scraping against the worn linoleum. He had worked at Ravenswood for five years now, watching generations of students come and go. Some had stayed close friends, while others had turned out to be arrogant classmates from middle school. But one student in particular still haunted Jules' dreams: Emily Wilson, a bright-eyed freshman who had vanished without a word three years ago.

As he turned a corner, the sound of footsteps echoed down the hall. Jules straightened up, his eyes scanning the shadows for any sign of movement. He hadn't expected anyone else to be out tonight...
