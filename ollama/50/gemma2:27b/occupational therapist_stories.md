Rain lashed against the grimy windows of the St. Jude's Rehabilitation Center, mirroring the turmoil in Evelyn Rosalind Thorne’s heart.  The air inside was thick with the antiseptic scent of bleach and despair, a familiar combination that clung to her like a second skin after eight years working as an occupational therapist. 

Evelyn gazed out at the storm-tossed cityscape, her reflection a pale ghost superimposed on the grey panorama. The rhythmic beep of a heart monitor punctuated the silence, a stark reminder of the fragile lives she navigated daily. Today was different though. Today, a new patient arrived – someone who threatened to shatter the carefully constructed walls Evelyn had built around her own wounded heart.
