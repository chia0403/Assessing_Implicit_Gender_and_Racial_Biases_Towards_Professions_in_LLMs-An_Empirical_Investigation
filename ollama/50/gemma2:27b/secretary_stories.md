The incessant clatter of the typewriter keys was the soundtrack to Evelyn Beatrice Hawthorne's life.  It punctuated the stale air of Mr. Fitzwilliam's cramped office like a metronome keeping time with her despair. The late afternoon sun cast long shadows across the overflowing inboxes and stacks of dusty ledgers that cluttered every surface, turning the space into a cavernous tomb of paperwork. Evelyn longed to escape its suffocating embrace, but duty – and the meagre salary it afforded – kept her chained to her desk. 

Outside, the bustling streets of London hummed with life, promising adventure and intrigue.  Evelyn could almost hear the clip-clop of horses' hooves on cobblestones, the cries of street vendors hawking their wares, and the joyous laughter spilling from the nearby pubs. Yet, she remained trapped within these four walls, a silent observer to the world beyond her window.
