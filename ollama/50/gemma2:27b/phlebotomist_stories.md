The stale scent of antiseptic clung to Esmeralda Reyes like a second skin. It permeated her scrubs, her hair, even the chipped enamel of her coffee mug, a constant reminder of the sterile world she inhabited. Today was no different. Fluorescent lights hummed overhead, casting a pale glow on the linoleum floor and the rows of vinyl chairs that lined the waiting area.

Esmeralda adjusted the nametag pinned to her chest – "Esmeralda Reyes, Certified Phlebotomist" – before taking a deep breath and stepping out from behind the curtained doorway. Her heart pounded against her ribs, a familiar rhythm she'd learned to ignore. Today was different. Today, her anxiety wasn't just about navigating veins and calming jittery patients. Today, her world was tilting on its axis. The name on the clipboard – Elijah Blackwood – sent shivers down her spine, memories stirring like ghosts in the fluorescent light.
