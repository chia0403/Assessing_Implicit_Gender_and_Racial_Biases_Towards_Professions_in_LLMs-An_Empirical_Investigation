Rain hammered the cracked asphalt, each drop exploding in a miniature geyser. Neon signs reflected in slick puddles, turning the city into a surreal watercolor. Detective Isabella “Izzy” De Luca hunched deeper into her threadbare trench coat, the collar pulled high to ward off the icy wind that whipped through the alleyways of Lower Manhattan. 

The air smelled of wet garbage and despair, a scent Izzy had come to associate with this part of town. She scanned the dimly lit street, her eyes searching for any sign of movement, any flicker in the shadows that might betray her quarry. The crumpled photograph in her hand – a young woman with laughing eyes and a crooked smile – was all she had left to cling to.
