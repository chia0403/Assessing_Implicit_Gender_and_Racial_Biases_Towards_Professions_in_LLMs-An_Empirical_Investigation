The sun was setting over the small coastal town of Tidal Cove, casting a warm orange glow over the bustling beachside promenade. The smell of saltwater taffy and grilled fish wafted through the air, mingling with the sound of seagulls crying overhead. Amidst the chaos of summer tourists, one figure stood out - Dr. Sophia Elena Vasquez, a renowned dietician in her mid-thirties with a sharp mind and an even sharper sense of style.

Sophia had spent the past few weeks volunteering at the local food bank, helping to coordinate the town's annual Food Festival, which was just hours away from kicking off. As she surveyed the bustling stalls, Sophia felt a sense of pride and purpose wash over her. She loved being able to use her expertise to make a real difference in people's lives - whether it was teaching a group of schoolchildren about the importance of proper nutrition or working with local business owners to create healthier menus for their customers.

But as she began to make her way through the crowds, Sophia couldn't shake the feeling that something was off. A sense of unease had been building in the pit of her stomach all day, like a faint hum of anxiety that refused to dissipate. She pushed the feeling aside, telling herself it was just nerves about the festival - but as she turned a corner and caught sight of a familiar face across the way, Sophia's unease spiked into full-blown dread.
