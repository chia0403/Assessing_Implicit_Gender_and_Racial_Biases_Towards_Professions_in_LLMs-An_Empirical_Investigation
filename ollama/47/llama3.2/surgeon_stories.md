Here's a possible story opening and setting:

Dr. Ethan Julian Wells, a renowned cardiothoracic surgeon with a thriving practice in Boston, Massachusetts, stood at the edge of his office window, gazing out over the city skyline as the sun set behind the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT). The neon lights of the nearby Kendall Square district reflected off the wet pavement, casting a kaleidoscope of colors across the streets. Ethan's eyes, however, were fixed on the horizon, where the Charles River flowed into Boston Harbor.

It was moments like these that reminded him of why he had become a surgeon in the first place: to save lives and leave a mark on the world that would outlast his own mortality. As he turned away from the window, his gaze fell upon the framed photograph on his desk, taken during his residency at Harvard Medical School. It was a picture of his late grandmother, Elara, who had inspired him to pursue medicine in the first place.

Just then, his phone buzzed with an incoming call from the hospital's ICU coordinator, Dr. Patel. Ethan's heart skipped a beat as he answered the line, his mind already racing with possibilities...
