Here's a potential story opening and setting:

The sun had just begun to set over the ancient city of Petra, casting a warm orange glow over the narrow canyon known as the Siq. Tourist guides like Amira Hassan el-Sayed were used to this time of day, when the crowds of visitors thinned out and the shadows grew long and cool.

Amira, a native Jordanian with a sharp mind and a quick wit, led her group of tourists through the winding passageways of Al-Khazneh, or the Treasury, its intricate carvings and frescoes seeming to come alive in the fading light. She had spent years honing her knowledge of the site, from the history of the Nabataean Empire that once called Petra home, to the myths and legends that surrounded the ancient monuments.

As she pointed out the various features of the Treasury, Amira couldn't shake the feeling that something was off. It wasn't just the tourists, who seemed nervous and on edge today; it was the way the wind rustled through the canyon, whispering secrets in a language only she could hear.

And then, as she turned to gesture towards a particularly impressive carving, Amira caught sight of a figure standing at the far end of the passageway. It was a woman, dressed in worn jeans and a faded t-shirt, her eyes fixed intently on something that seemed almost invisible to the naked eye.

Amira's instincts prickled with unease as she felt the hairs on the back of her neck stand up. Who was this woman, and what did she want? And why did Amira feel like she knew exactly who she was, without needing to be told...
