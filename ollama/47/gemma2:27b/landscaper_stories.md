The late afternoon sun cast long shadows across Mrs. Hawthorne's sprawling lawn, turning the emerald green into a tapestry of deep jade and shimmering gold. Amelia "Amy" Blackwood knelt, delicately plucking a rogue dandelion from between the meticulously placed lavender sprigs. Sweat beaded on her forehead despite the breeze whispering through the towering elm trees, carrying with it the scent of freshly cut grass and honeysuckle.

This was Amy's favorite time of day - the quiet lull between the midday rush and the evening stillness. It was when the gardens truly came alive, humming with hidden activity. Bees buzzed lazily amongst the roses, butterflies flitted from bloom to bloom, and a lone robin sang its melodious song from the branches overhead.

But today, a sense of unease prickled at Amy's usually tranquil demeanor. Mrs. Hawthorne had been uncharacteristically distant during their usual morning tea, her eyes clouded with a worry that she refused to explain.
