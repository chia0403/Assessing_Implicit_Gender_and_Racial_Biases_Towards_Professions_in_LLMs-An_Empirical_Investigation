Rain lashed against the windows of Dr. Elara Finch's practice, blurring the lights of the street below into an impressionistic smear. The rhythmic drumming was strangely comforting, masking the anxious thrumming in her chest.  She stared at the reflection of her own worried face in the chrome instrument tray. Today was supposed to be different. Today was the day she finally felt brave enough to walk away from the sterile white walls, the whirring drills and the ever-present scent of antiseptic.

Today, Dr. Finch intended to follow her heart, not her meticulously planned career path.
