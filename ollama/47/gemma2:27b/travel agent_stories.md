Rain lashed against the grimy windowpane of Penelope "Penny" Worthington's office, mirroring the storm brewing within her. The Eiffel Tower, usually a beacon of Parisian romance, glowered from its place on Penny's computer screen, mocking her with its unattainable beauty. 

Penny Worthington Travel & Tours wasn't exactly flourishing. Dust motes danced in the stale air, punctuated by the rhythmic tick-tock of a grandfather clock inherited from her grandmother - a constant reminder of time slipping away. Her phone remained stubbornly silent, a cruel indictment of her dwindling clientele. The age of online booking sites and budget airlines had left traditional travel agents like Penny struggling to stay afloat.


But today was different. A manila envelope lay on her desk, addressed in elegant calligraphy: "Miss Penelope Worthington, Travel Connoisseur Extraordinaire."  Hope flickered within her chest, a fragile flame against the encroaching darkness. Who could possibly be sending her such an intriguing invitation?
