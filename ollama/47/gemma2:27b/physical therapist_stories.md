The air hung thick with the scent of antiseptic and desperation. Amelia Rose Carmichael, her brow furrowed in concentration, maneuvered the paralyzed leg of Mr. Henderson, an elderly gentleman whose stroke had stolen his mobility.  Sunlight slanted through the window of the small, cluttered therapy room at St. Jude's Rehabilitation Center, illuminating dust motes dancing in the air. Outside, the cacophony of city traffic droned on, a stark contrast to the hushed struggle unfolding within the room. Amelia's hands, calloused yet gentle, coaxed Mr. Henderson's leg through a series of exercises, her voice a soothing balm as she encouraged him with each tiny movement. 

"That's it, Mr. Henderson," she said, her blue eyes twinkling with genuine encouragement. "Just a little further..."
