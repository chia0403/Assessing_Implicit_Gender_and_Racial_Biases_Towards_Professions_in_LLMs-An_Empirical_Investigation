Rain lashed against the corrugated iron roof of the old warehouse, each drop a hammer blow echoing through the cavernous space.  The air hung heavy with the scent of damp earth and ozone, a metallic tang that always seemed to precede a storm. Elias Finch adjusted his headlamp, its beam cutting through the gloom, illuminating cobwebs strung like macabre decorations across rusted machinery.

Elias wasn't afraid of the dark or the storms. He was an electrician, after all, and darkness was just another challenge, another puzzle to be solved with the right wire and a good connection. Tonight's puzzle, however, was proving particularly stubborn. The old warehouse, slated for demolition, had been bought by a mysterious tech company rumored to be working on some groundbreaking project. Elias' job was simple: reroute the power from the outdated grid to the new substations they were installing. Simple in theory, but the building's wiring was a tangled mess of ancient cables and forgotten circuits, like the arteries of a long-dead beast.
