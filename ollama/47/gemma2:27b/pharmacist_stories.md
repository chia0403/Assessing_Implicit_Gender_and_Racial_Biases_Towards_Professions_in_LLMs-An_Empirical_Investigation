Rain lashed against the warped glass of the apothecary window, blurring the neon "Open" sign into an indistinguishable smear. The scent of lavender and chamomile, usually so comforting, seemed to waver in the humid air, struggling against the encroaching smell of damp earth. Eleanor Beaumont adjusted her spectacles, peering through the downpour at the solitary figure huddled beneath the awning. He looked young, barely out of his teens, his shoulders hunched against the wind like a frightened bird.

Eleanor sighed. It was nearly closing time, and she craved nothing more than to curl up with a good book and a steaming cup of tea. But there was something in the boy's posture, a desperation that tugged at her heartstrings. She unlocked the heavy oak door and beckoned him inside, the bell above jingling mournfully.
