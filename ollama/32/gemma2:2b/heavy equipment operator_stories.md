The air hung thick with diesel fumes and the scent of freshly turned earth.  Dust motes danced in the weak morning sun as it sliced through the haze hanging over the construction site. The rumble of bulldozers and the constant thrum of engines formed an orchestra, each instrument a necessary part of the symphony playing out before them - the construction of a sprawling corporate complex on the edge of town.  At the heart of this metallic cacophony stood Elara "Ellie" Beaumont, her broad shoulders hunched over the controls of a massive excavator. 

Ellie was known around the site as "The Iron Lady," not for her sharp wit or imposing stature - but for her raw power when it came to machinery.  She moved with the confidence of a bullfighter who'd spent a lifetime facing down angry beasts, each muscle taut and coiled beneath the earth-stained overalls that clung to her like a second skin.
