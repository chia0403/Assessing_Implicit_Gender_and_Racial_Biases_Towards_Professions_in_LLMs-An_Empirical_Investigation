Rain lashed against the grimy windowpane, blurring the neon glow of "Bob's Tax Haven" into an abstract smear of red and green. Inside, Eleanor Pendleton adjusted her spectacles, pushing them further up the bridge of her nose as she squinted at the tangled mess of receipts before her. April 15th loomed, casting a long shadow over the cramped office. The air hung thick with the scent of stale coffee and desperation, a potent cocktail that Eleanor had come to associate with tax season.

She tapped a stubby pencil against the desk, muttering numbers under her breath. Her client, a wide-eyed young man named Timothy, fidgeted in his seat, nervously adjusting his baseball cap. This was his first time filing taxes, and Eleanor could sense the fear churning beneath his bravado.
