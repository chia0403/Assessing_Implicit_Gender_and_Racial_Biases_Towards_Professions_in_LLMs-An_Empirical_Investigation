The fluorescent lights above the checkout counter cast an unforgiving glare over the rows of products on the shelves, making everything look like it was stored in a dimly lit prison cell. The air was thick with the scent of stale coffee and yesterday's pizza, the remnants of a long day at Joe's Diner where Emilia Rose Jenkins had been working as a cashier for what felt like an eternity.

As she mechanically scanned item after item on the conveyor belt, her mind began to wander back to the peculiar phone call she'd received from her estranged father earlier that morning. The voice on the other end had sounded just as rough and worn out as Emilia herself, but there was something in his tone that made her heart skip a beat.

"Meet me at the old oak tree in town square at midnight," he'd said, his words dripping with an unspoken urgency. "I've got something to tell you, kiddo."

Emilia's eyes drifted out the window, watching as the sun dipped below the horizon, casting the small town of Willow Creek in a warm orange glow. She felt like she was trapped between two worlds: the one she knew, where her life was routine and predictable, and the other, full of secrets and uncertainty, which her father seemed to be calling her towards.

As the clock ticked closer to closing time, Emilia couldn't shake the feeling that this mysterious meeting would change everything.
