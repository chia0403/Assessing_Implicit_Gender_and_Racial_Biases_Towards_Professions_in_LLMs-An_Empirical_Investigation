The sun had just begun to set over the small town of Ravenswood, casting a warm orange glow over the quiet streets and homes. The air was filled with the sweet scent of blooming lilacs and the distant hum of insects as Firefighter Ethan Ryder stood outside his station, gazing out at the tranquil scene.

Ethan, a ruggedly handsome man in his mid-thirties with a strong jawline and piercing blue eyes, had been a firefighter for over a decade. He had grown up in Ravenswood, working alongside his father and grandfather before him to protect their community from the dangers of fire and flames. Now, as the newest captain of Engine Company 17, Ethan was determined to put his own mark on the department and make his family proud.

As he sipped his coffee and surveyed the peaceful evening scene, a sudden buzz broke the calm atmosphere. The dispatcher's voice crackled over the radio, sending a shiver down Ethan's spine: "Engine 17, we have a report of a warehouse fire at the old Jenkins mill on the outskirts of town. Please respond with caution."
