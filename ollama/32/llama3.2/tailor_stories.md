In the sleepy town of Ashwood, where the misty dawn air clung to the cobblestone streets like a damp shroud, Emilia Fothergill stood hunched over her workbench, surrounded by the familiar scent of wool and thread. As the town's sole tailor, Emilia took pride in creating bespoke garments that were both functional and beautiful, much like the intricate embroidery patterns she had learned from her mother. The soft glow of candles cast a warm light on the rows of neatly stacked bolts of fabric, each one carefully curated to meet the unique needs of Ashwood's residents.

As she worked, Emilia's mind wandered to the latest commission from the town's rector, Mrs. Harcourt, who had requested a bespoke wedding gown for her daughter's nuptials in three weeks' time. The pressure to deliver was on, but Emilia was confident in her skills and the quality of her creations. Little did she know, however, that this upcoming wedding would be the catalyst for a chain of events that would shake the very foundations of Ashwood.
