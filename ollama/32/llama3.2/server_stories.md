The neon sign above the diner's door flickered like a tired heartbeat as it read "Eddie's Eats" in crooked letters. Inside, the air was thick with the smell of stale coffee and yesterday's grease. It was 3:47 AM on a drizzly Tuesday morning, and the only patrons were the regulars huddled at the counter, sipping their last cups of coffee before closing time.

Eleanor Mae "Ellie" Thompson wiped down the Formica table with a dirty rag, her eyes scanning the diner for any signs of trouble. She had worked at Eddie's Eats for five years, serving breakfast to truckers and late-night revelers alike. It was a job that required patience, a strong stomach, and an ear for gossip.

As she scrubbed down the table with a mixture of soap and hot water, Ellie's mind wandered to the strange phone call she had received just before her shift started. The voice on the other end had been distorted, but the words had sent a shiver down her spine: "They're watching you, Ellie. Be careful who you trust."

Ellie shook her head, chiding herself for being paranoid. It was probably just some crank caller looking for a laugh or trying to scam someone out of their lunch money. But as she poured herself a cup of lukewarm coffee and took a sip, she couldn't shake the feeling that something was off.

The diner's door swung open, and a soggy raincoat-clad figure stumbled in, shaking the water off their jacket like a wet dog. Ellie's eyes narrowed as she watched the stranger take a seat at the counter, ordering a bacon cheeseburger and a side of hash browns.

This new customer looked different from the usual crowd. There was something about the way they moved, the way their eyes scanned the diner like they were searching for something or someone. And those eyes... they seemed to be looking right through Ellie, straight into her soul.

Ellie's instincts told her to keep a close eye on this stranger, to watch and wait for any sign of trouble. Little did she know, her life was about to get a lot more complicated.
