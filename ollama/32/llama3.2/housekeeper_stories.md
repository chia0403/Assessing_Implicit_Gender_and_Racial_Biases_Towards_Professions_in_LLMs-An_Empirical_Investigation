The first light of dawn crept through the grimy windows of Ravenswood Manor, casting an eerie glow over the sprawling estate. In the kitchen, Emilia Adelaide Winters stirred the embers of last night's fire, her worn hands moving with practiced ease as she prepared breakfast for the family who called this place home.

As a housekeeper to the wealthy and reclusive Bellvue family, Emilia had grown accustomed to the rhythms of Ravenswood's daily routine. She knew the cadences of Mr. Bellvue's morning rituals, the way Mrs. Bellvue's voice rose in annoyance at the slightest infraction, and the soft-spoken demands of young Master Thomas.

But today was different. Today, Emilia sensed a restlessness in the air, a tension that vibrated through every stone floor and dusty paneling of Ravenswood's ancient walls. It was as if the very fabric of their lives was about to unravel, leaving nothing but threads of uncertainty in its wake.

And Emilia, with her sharp eyes and quick wit, was the first to feel it.
