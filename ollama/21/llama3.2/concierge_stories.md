Here's a potential story opening and setting:

---

The rain pounded against the pavement like a relentless drumbeat, casting a misty veil over the sleek skyscrapers of downtown Manhattan. Inside the luxurious Bellvue Hotel, where the city elite came to indulge in pampered luxury, a lone figure moved with quiet efficiency.

Avery Elara Wellesley, the concierge, stood behind the polished mahogany desk, her eyes scanning the floor plan on her tablet as she listened to the requests of a frazzled guest. With a nod and a smile, Avery expertly juggled multiple reservations, from a table for two at Le Bernardin to a last-minute room upgrade for a young businessman. Her fingers flew across the keyboard, sending emails and making calls with the precision of a surgeon.

As she worked, the soft glow of the hotel's crystal chandeliers cast a warm light on Avery's dark hair, which was pulled back into a sleek bun. Her eyes, a piercing shade of green, sparkled with a hint of mischief as she listened to a guest complain about the lack of parking options downtown. Avery had seen it all before – the tantrums, the demands, the occasional threat of violence when things didn't go one's way.

But amidst the chaos, Avery felt at home. She had always been a people person, with a natural talent for calming frazzled nerves and making guests feel like royalty. And as she expertly navigated the whirlwind of reservations and requests, Avery couldn't help but wonder what other secrets the Bellvue held – secrets that only she knew about.

As the rain continued to pour down outside, Avery's phone rang, shrill in the quiet moment before the next storm hit. She answered with a cheerful smile, ready to take on whatever the night might bring...
