Dr. Amarantha Rose "Mara" Thompson stood at the edge of the old, weathered dock, her worn leather boots scuffed against the wooden planks as she gazed out across the mist-shrouded waters of Ravenswood Lake. The moon was full overhead, casting an eerie silver glow over the lake's surface, where a single small boat bobbed listlessly in the gentle current.

As a professor of folklore and mythology at the nearby University of Ashwood, Mara had spent countless hours studying the legends and stories that wove their way through this very region. But despite her extensive knowledge, she couldn't shake the feeling that there was something out there, lurking just beneath the surface, waiting to be uncovered.

The air was crisp and cool, carrying the scent of damp earth and decaying leaves, as Mara's eyes scanned the darkness beyond the lake's edge. She felt a shiver run down her spine, despite the thickness of her woolen coat, as she sensed that something was watching her from just out of sight.

It was moments like these that made Mara wonder if she'd ever truly understood the power of stories - and the secrets they kept hidden in plain sight.
