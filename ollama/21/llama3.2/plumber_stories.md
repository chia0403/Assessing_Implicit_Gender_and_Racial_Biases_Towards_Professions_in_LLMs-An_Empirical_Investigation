The rain poured down on the small town of Ravenswood like a relentless curtain, casting a misty veil over the crumbling Victorian houses and rusty pipes that lined its streets. Inside the cramped confines of his plumbing shop, Jack "JD" Donovan huddled over a clogged drain, his bright blue eyes fixed intently on the tangled mess of hair and soap scum as he wrestled with the stubborn pipe.

The air was thick with the acrid smell of wet earth and mildew, and JD's face was slick with sweat as he worked. It was a chilly autumn evening, and the storm that had rolled in just a few hours ago showed no signs of letting up. As he worked, the sound of rain lashing against the shop's metal roof created a soothing background hum, but JD's mind was elsewhere – on the mysterious letter that had arrived at his doorstep just hours before, its cryptic message sending a shiver down his spine.

The letter, typed on plain white paper and bearing no return address, read simply: "Meet me at the old water tower at midnight. Come alone." JD's gut told him to ignore it – after all, he had more pressing concerns than some mysterious stranger's request – but as he sat there in the dimly lit shop, surrounded by his tools and his beloved pipes, he couldn't shake the feeling that something was off.

And as he glanced up at the clock on the wall, JD saw that midnight was fast approaching...
