The flickering neon sign outside cast an anemic glow on the rain-slicked asphalt, illuminating each raindrop as it exploded against the cracked pavement. Inside "The Drunken Sparrow," the air hung thick with the scent of stale beer and forgotten dreams. 

Elara Blackwood, her emerald eyes narrowed against the smoky haze, polished a glass with practiced ease. The chipped porcelain held a faint memory of tequila sunrise and regret, just like most things in this dive bar. Elara knew every creak of the floorboards, every drip from the leaky faucet, and every story etched into the weathered faces that crowded around her mahogany bar. 


She'd been tending to these souls for five years now, a silent confidante dispensing whiskey shots and weary smiles. Tonight, however, felt different. There was a tension in the air, a prickling sensation on the back of her neck that made her instinctively reach for the worn switchblade tucked into her bootlace. This wasn't just another Tuesday night at The Drunken Sparrow; this was something else entirely.
