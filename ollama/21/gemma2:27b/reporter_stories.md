Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of the ramshackle diner like a thousand tiny fists. Inside, the air was thick with the smell of frying bacon and stale coffee, punctuated by the mournful wail of a blues tune crackling from a dusty jukebox in the corner. Eloise "Ellie" Beaumont, her raincoat dripping puddles onto the worn linoleum floor, hunched over a chipped mug, her notepad clutched tightly in her hand.  She was in Harmony Creek, Alabama – population 842 and shrinking - to investigate rumors of strange lights in the sky and whispered tales of vanished livestock. The locals called it "The Glow," and Ellie, a reporter for the online news site “Strange & Unusual”, knew that this sleepy backwater town held a story waiting to be unearthed.

But first, she needed another cup of coffee. Strong enough to chase away the creeping unease that had settled in her gut.
