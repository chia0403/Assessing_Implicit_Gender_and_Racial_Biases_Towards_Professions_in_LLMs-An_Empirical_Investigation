The air hung thick with the scent of burnt coffee and desperation. Outside, rain lashed against the grimy windows of "Bargain Bonanza," blurring the neon sign into a swirling, indecipherable mess. Inside, Alistair Montgomery Finch III straightened his paisley tie for the tenth time that morning, the polyester scratching uncomfortably against his skin. 

He wasn't meant for this life. Alistair, with his meticulously coiffed hair and Oxford brogues polished to a blinding shine, belonged in mahogany-paneled offices, negotiating multi-million dollar deals. Yet here he was, hawking discount toaster ovens and dented washing machines to a clientele that seemed perpetually suspicious and eternally broke.
