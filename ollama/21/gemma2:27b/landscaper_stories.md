The humid Georgia air clung to Silas Blackwood like a damp shirt as he wrestled with the obstinate tangle of honeysuckle vines. Sweat beaded on his brow, stinging his eyes. He wiped it away with the back of his calloused hand, leaving a smear of dirt across his forehead. 

Silas's domain wasn't the manicured lawns and perfectly pruned hedges favored by some landscapers. He specialized in reclaiming the wild, coaxing beauty from overgrown lots and forgotten gardens. This particular project, an abandoned Victorian manor on the outskirts of Savannah, was a challenge even for him. Years of neglect had left the once-grand grounds choked with weeds, ivy threatening to swallow the crumbling brick facade whole.

But Silas saw potential beneath the tangled mess. He envisioned vibrant blooms spilling from ancient flowerbeds, pathways winding through dappled shade, and the sweet scent of jasmine carried on the breeze. It would take time and patience, but he had both in abundance.
