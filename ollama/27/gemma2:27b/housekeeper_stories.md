The scent of lemon polish hung heavy in the air, a thin veil over the musky aroma of old books and forgotten pipe tobacco. Amelia Thistlewick knelt on the worn Persian rug, her arthritic knees protesting with each careful scrub. Sunlight streamed through the arched windows of the grand library, illuminating dust motes dancing like miniature constellations.

The house itself was a Victorian behemoth, perched precariously atop a windswept cliff overlooking the churning grey sea. Amelia had been tending to its every need for over twenty years, her heartstrings as intricately woven into the tapestry of the place as the faded floral patterns on its threadbare armchairs.  Today, however, felt different. A prickle of unease danced at the nape of her neck, a discordant note in the usual symphony of creaking floorboards and rustling curtains.
