The midday sun beat down on Bartholomew "Bart" Higgins' back as he wrestled a sheet of asphalt shingles into place. Beads of sweat trickled down his temple, stinging his eyes, but Bart barely noticed. He was focused, a Zen master in the art of roofing. His hammer sang a rhythmic song against the nails, punctuating the occasional screech of gulls overhead.

The old Victorian house beneath him stretched out like a wrinkled giant, its ornate gables and turrets casting long shadows across the overgrown lawn. Bart knew every creak and groan of this building – he'd been patching its roof for over a decade, ever since Mrs. O’Malley, the eccentric widow who owned it, had hired him fresh out of trade school.

Today, however, something felt different. A prickling sensation danced at the back of his neck, a feeling of being watched. He glanced around, but there was no one in sight except for the occasional fluttering squirrel and the distant rumble of traffic. Still, the unease lingered, like a forgotten tool left out in the rain. Bart shrugged it off, blaming the heat, and continued his work. But as he reached for another shingle, a glint of gold caught his eye...
