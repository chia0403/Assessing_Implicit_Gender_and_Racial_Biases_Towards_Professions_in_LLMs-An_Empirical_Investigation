The air hung thick with the scent of frying onions and desperation. Outside, the midday sun beat down on the cracked asphalt of the parking lot, turning it into a shimmering mirage. Inside "Deals on Wheels," Reginald Bartholomew Fitzwilliam III, or Reggie as he preferred (though nobody ever did), adjusted his tie for the tenth time that morning. A faded promotional poster featuring a grinning cartoon car with impossibly large wheels leered down from behind him. 

Reggie wasn't sure what was more disheartening – the oppressive heat or the fact that not a single soul had stepped foot in the lot since he arrived at 8:00 sharp. Today was supposed to be his big comeback. After three months of being benched for “poor salesmanship,” (a euphemism for forgetting to mention the car's engine problems), Reggie was determined to prove himself to Mr. Henderson, the gruff owner of "Deals on Wheels" with a penchant for suspenders and suspicion.
