The fluorescent lights buzzed overhead, casting a sterile glare across the worn linoleum floor of Mercy Hospital's outpatient lab.  Esmeralda "Esme" Vargas expertly maneuvered a needle into Mrs. Henderson's arm, her movements practiced and precise. The older woman winced slightly, but Esme's soothing voice and gentle touch quickly calmed her nerves. 

Esme loved the quiet rhythm of drawing blood. It was a delicate dance of finding the vein, calming anxieties, and filling the vials with life's crimson essence. But today, a prickling unease hung in the air, heavier than the antiseptic smell. A string of recent unexplained illnesses had swept through town, leaving doctors baffled and whispers of contagion swirling through the community.
