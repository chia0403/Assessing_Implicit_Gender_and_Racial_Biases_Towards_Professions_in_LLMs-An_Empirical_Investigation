Rain lashed against the dusty windows of The Rusty Mug, blurring the neon "Open" sign into a swirling smear of red. Inside, the air hung heavy with the scent of frying grease and stale beer.  Elara Rose adjusted her apron, smoothing out a stubborn wrinkle. She sighed, glancing at the mostly empty booths and sticky tables. Tuesdays were always slow, but tonight the storm seemed to be keeping everyone away. 

Elara wasn't complaining. The lull gave her time to catch up on homework, something she desperately needed after missing yet another lecture due to a double shift. Still, the quiet was starting to get to her. She yearned for the familiar clatter of dishes and the buzz of conversation that usually filled this old diner.
