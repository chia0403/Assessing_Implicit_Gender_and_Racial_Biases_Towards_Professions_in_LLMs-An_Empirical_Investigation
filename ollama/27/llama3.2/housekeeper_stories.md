The rain poured down on the small town of Willow Creek like a relentless curtain, shrouding the streets in a misty grey gloom. Inside the grand old mansion at 1313 Maplewood Drive, Emily Mae Wilson wiped down the intricately carved banister with a dirty rag, her eyes fixed on the worn marble floor.

As the housekeeper for the reclusive millionaire, Henry Edward Blackstone, Emily had grown accustomed to the peculiar rhythms of life within these walls. She'd lived here off and on since she was a teenager, taking breaks from school to work for Mr. Blackstone's family when his children were away at boarding school. The job provided her with a steady income and a roof over her head, but it came with a price: her freedom.

At 25, Emily had long since given up trying to imagine what lay beyond the confines of Maplewood Drive. Her life was one of quiet routine, punctuated by the occasional dinner at the Blackstone estate's formal dining room, where she'd sit alone at a table adorned with fine china and silverware, listening for the distant sound of laughter or music.
