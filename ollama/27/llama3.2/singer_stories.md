The dimly lit recording studio on Sunset Boulevard was the perfect place for Astrid Ruby Winters to escape the chaos of her life. As she stepped off the bus that had carried her from the small town of Willow Creek, Montana, she felt the familiar weight of expectation bearing down on her shoulders. She had left home to chase her dreams as a singer, determined to make a name for herself in the cutthroat world of Los Angeles.

Astrid Ruby Winters, or "Ruby" to those who knew her, was 22 years old and had already gained a small but devoted following in the folk scene back East. But she knew that if she wanted to truly succeed, she needed to take a leap of faith and try her luck in the city of angels.

As she made her way through the crowded studio, Astrid's eyes scanned the room for familiar faces - the engineer, Mark, was already setting up his equipment; her manager, Rachel, was pacing nervously outside; and her best friend, Lily, was sipping on a coffee at the bar, looking worried. But one face was noticeably absent: that of her estranged father, Victor Winters, who had abandoned their family when Astrid was just a teenager.

Astrid's thoughts were interrupted by the sound of Mark calling out to her, "Ruby, we're good to go! You ready to lay down some tracks?" She took a deep breath, mentally preparing herself for the long night ahead. This could be the break she needed - but at what cost?
