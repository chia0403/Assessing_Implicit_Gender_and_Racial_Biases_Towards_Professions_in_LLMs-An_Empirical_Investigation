Mrs. Wellesley, with her curly brown hair tied back in a loose bun and a warm smile, stood at the front of her classroom, surveying the rows of neatly arranged desks and chairs. She had been teaching at Oakdale Elementary for over a decade, and she loved watching her students grow from nervous first-graders into confident, curious learners.

As she began to unpack her lesson plans and prepare her materials, Mrs. Wellesley's mind wandered to the strange letter she had received the day before. It was addressed to her, but there was no return address and no indication of who might have sent it. The note simply read: "Meet me at the old oak tree in the town square at 3 pm today. Come alone."

Mrs. Wellesley's instincts told her that this was some kind of prank or mistaken identity, but a small part of her couldn't shake the feeling that something more was going on. She tried to push the thought aside as she began to teach her first lesson of the day - "The Magic of Measurement" - but she couldn't help glancing at the clock every few minutes, wondering if this mysterious meeting would change everything.
