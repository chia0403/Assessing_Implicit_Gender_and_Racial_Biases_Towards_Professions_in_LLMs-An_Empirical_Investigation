---

The sun had just begun to set over the city, casting a golden glow over the dusty streets of Oakdale, where Jack "Hawk" Hawkins, a rugged 35-year-old construction worker, wiped the sweat from his brow as he climbed down from the scaffolding of the old warehouse he was renovating. The air was thick with the smell of fresh concrete and the distant hum of traffic, a cacophony that Hawk had grown to love over the years.

Hawk took a deep breath, feeling the fatigue of the long day slowly seep out of his muscles as he gazed out at the warehouse's facade. He'd been working on this project for months, tearing down old walls and installing new ones, but there was something about this place that felt different - like it held secrets and stories that only revealed themselves to those who dared to get close.

As he walked away from the scaffold, Hawk noticed a small piece of paper caught in the rusty hinges of the warehouse door. He plucked it out and examined it - a receipt from a local hardware store, dated yesterday, with a scribbled note on the back that read: "Meet me at the old oak at midnight. Come alone."

Hawk's instincts told him to ignore the note, but something about it had piqued his curiosity. He tucked the receipt into his pocket and began the long walk home, wondering what could have led someone to leave this cryptic message in a place where they knew he'd find it.

---

Setting: Oakdale, a small industrial town on the outskirts of a larger city, with a mix of old factories, warehouses, and residential neighborhoods. The story is set in a working-class community, where construction work was common and people were proud of their manual labor.
