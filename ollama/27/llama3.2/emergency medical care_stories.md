---

The sirens pierced the night air, echoing off the hospital's towering skyscraper as Dr. Alexandra "Lexi" Thompson rushed towards the ER doors. As an emergency medical technician (EMT) and paramedic, Lexi had seen her fair share of traumatic injuries and heart-stopping emergencies. But nothing could have prepared her for what lay ahead.

It was a chilly autumn evening in New Haven, Connecticut, and the hospital's ER was already buzzing with activity. The fluorescent lights overhead cast an unforgiving glow over the chaotic scene unfolding before Lexi. She had just finished a long shift at the city's dispatch center, where she and her team had responded to a string of calls involving car accidents, domestic disputes, and medical emergencies.

As she burst through the ER doors, Lexi was greeted by the familiar sight of her colleagues scrambling to triage patients and prep for the influx of wounded. The hospital's emergency room was a war zone, with sheets of paper flapping like flags in the wind as docs and nurses shouted orders and assigned tasks. Amidst the chaos, Lexi's trained eyes scanned the room, searching for the most critical cases.

Lexi had always been drawn to this work, despite the emotional toll it took on her. As an EMT, she was the first point of contact between a patient in crisis and the medical team that would ultimately save their life. It was a thrill unlike any other, and one that kept her coming back for more.

Tonight, however, Lexi sensed something was off. The dispatcher's words still echoed in her mind: "Possible multi-victim car accident on I-95... multiple critical injuries reported..." Whatever the specifics, Lexi knew she had to be ready to face whatever challenges lay ahead.
