The fluorescent lights buzzed overhead, their hum weaving a familiar lullaby through Amelia Blackwood's quiet concentration. Blueprints lay spread across her worn drafting table, each line and curve a testament to years spent mastering the language of architecture. Dust motes danced in the pale light filtering through the grimy window overlooking the alley behind Blackwood Drafting. The air hung heavy with the scent of graphite and stale coffee, a comforting aroma that Amelia associated with home more than her cramped apartment ever could.  

Outside, the city was just beginning to stir, its rumble barely audible through the thick brick walls. But here, in this small sanctuary of lines and angles, time seemed to stand still. At least until Mr. Blackwood, Amelia's gruff but surprisingly kind grandfather and owner of the firm, shuffled in with a stack of new plans and a grunt that meant "get to work."
