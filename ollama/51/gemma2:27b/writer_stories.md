Rain lashed against the attic window, the sound echoing through the cramped space like frantic applause. Amelia Blackwood shivered, pulling her threadbare shawl tighter around her shoulders. The single oil lamp cast flickering shadows across stacks of dusty manuscripts and overflowing inkwells, making the room feel more like a haunted library than a writer's haven.

Amelia stared at the blank page before her, the nib of her quill hovering uselessly above the parchment. Her latest novel, a gothic romance set in the misty moors of Yorkshire, had stalled weeks ago. The muse, it seemed, had flown south for the winter, leaving Amelia with nothing but writer's block and a gnawing sense of inadequacy.
