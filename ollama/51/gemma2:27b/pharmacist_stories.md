Rain lashed against the dusty windowpane, blurring the neon glow of "Oakhaven Pharmacy" into a hazy smear. Inside, Eleanor Beatrice Hawthorne meticulously counted out lavender capsules, her brow furrowed in concentration. The rhythmic clicking of the pill counter was the only sound breaking the hushed silence of the late evening. 

Eleanor, with her steel-grey bun perpetually escaping its pins and tortoiseshell glasses perched precariously on the bridge of her nose, was a fixture in Oakhaven. For thirty years, she'd dispensed remedies for everything from sniffles to sprains, becoming a confidante to generations of townsfolk. Tonight, however, something felt different. A prickling unease ran down her spine, a feeling that mirrored the ominous growl of thunder outside.
