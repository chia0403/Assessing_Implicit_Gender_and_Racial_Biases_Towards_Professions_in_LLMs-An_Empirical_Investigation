Rain lashed against the windows of Sunnyside Daycare, blurring the cheerful murals painted on the outside walls into streaks of color.  Inside, six-year-old Maisie wailed over a spilled cup of apple juice, her face crumpling like crumpled paper. Eloise Cartwright, with a sigh that belied her usual cheery disposition, knelt beside the sobbing child, offering a damp cloth and soothing words.

Eloise loved her job at Sunnyside. She relished the chaos and laughter, the sticky fingers and boundless energy of her charges. But today, even the playful chatter and clattering toys couldn't pierce the grey gloom that seemed to have settled over everything.
