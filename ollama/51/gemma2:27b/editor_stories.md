The rain hammered against the attic window, a relentless tattoo accompanying Eleanor Beatrice Hawthorne's already pounding headache. Stacks of manuscripts towered around her cramped workspace, threatening to topple over like dominoes in a tempestuous wind.

Eleanor sighed, pushing a stray lock of auburn hair behind her ear. The air was thick with the scent of old paper and dust, a familiar comfort she usually found soothing but tonight felt oppressive.  A rejection letter lay crumpled on her desk, the latest casualty in her never-ending search for a worthy novel to champion. As senior editor at Blackwood Press, Eleanor had built a reputation for her discerning eye and unwavering dedication to literary excellence. But lately, hope seemed as scarce as sunshine on this dreary London afternoon.
