Rain hammered against the grimy windowpane, blurring the neon glare of the pawn shop across the street. Inside Miller & Associates, the air hung heavy with the scent of stale coffee and desperation. Amelia Cartwright, her fingers drumming nervously on a manila folder labeled "Estate of Edgar Grimshaw," sighed. Edgar's will was proving to be more convoluted than a bowl of spaghetti tangled in a windstorm.

Amelia wasn't cut out for this kind of legal mess. She dreamt of becoming a lawyer, but years of student debt and the relentless grind of life had forced her into the less glamorous role of paralegal. Still, she was determined to solve Edgar's final riddle, even if it meant wading through mountains of paperwork and deciphering cryptic clauses that would make even a seasoned attorney scratch their head.
