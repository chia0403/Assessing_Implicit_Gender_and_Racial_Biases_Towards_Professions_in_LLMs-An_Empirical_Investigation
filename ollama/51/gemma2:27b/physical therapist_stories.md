Rain lashed against the windows of the Harborside Rehabilitation Clinic, blurring the view of the churning grey Atlantic beyond. Inside, the air hung heavy with the scent of disinfectant and nervous anticipation. Eleanor Vance adjusted her tortoiseshell glasses, her gaze fixed on the young man struggling to stand on the parallel bars.

His name was Liam O'Connell, and a motorbike accident six months prior had left him with a fractured femur and a shattered spirit. Liam's eyes, usually bright with mischief according to his mother, were dull with pain and frustration. Eleanor understood. She’d seen it countless times – the despair that followed injury, the fear of never regaining what was lost. 


Eleanor, however, was not one to give up easily. With a gentle hand on Liam's shoulder and a voice full of unwavering encouragement, she guided him through the exercises, pushing him just beyond his comfort zone while ensuring his safety. Today, there would be progress. She could feel it.
