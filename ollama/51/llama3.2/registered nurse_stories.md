The sun had just set over the bustling city of New Orleans, casting a warm orange glow over the French Quarter. The sounds of jazz music and laughter from the bars on Bourbon Street mingled with the scent of beignets and coffee wafting from Cafe du Monde. Amidst this vibrant backdrop, registered nurse Alexandra "Alex" Danielle Roussel stood outside her small apartment, nursing a cold cup of coffee and staring out at the Mississippi River.

As she gazed out at the water, Alex's mind was elsewhere - on the ER shifts that had just ended for the night, and the mysterious patient who still lingered in room 3, despite the hospital's assurance that he was stable. The cryptic message scrawled on his chart, "Eclipse is near," still lingered in her mind, refusing to be shaken.

Alex took a sip of her coffee, feeling the bitter taste burn her tongue. She knew she needed to focus on the present moment - but her instincts told her that something was off, and she couldn't shake the feeling that this case would unravel into something much bigger than herself.

As she finished her coffee, Alex's phone buzzed with a text from her colleague, Dr. Patel: "Hey, we need to talk about room 3. The patient's condition just took a turn for the worse." Alex felt a shiver run down her spine - what did this mean? And what was this Eclipse she had heard whispered in hushed tones around the hospital?

With a sense of trepidation, Alex set off towards the hospital, ready to face whatever lay ahead.
