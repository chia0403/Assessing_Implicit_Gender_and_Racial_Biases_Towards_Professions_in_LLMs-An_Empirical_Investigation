The misty dawn crept over the sleepy town of Ravenswood, casting an eerie gloom over the crumbling Victorian houses that lined its streets. In a small, cluttered cottage on Elm Street, author Emilia Jane Wellesley stirred in her bed, rubbing the sleep from her eyes as she gazed out the window at the fog-shrouded landscape.

Emilia was a reclusive writer, known for her Gothic novels and short stories that explored the darker corners of human nature. Her latest book, "The Shadow Weaver," had been met with critical acclaim, but it had also drawn unwanted attention to her personal life. She had grown accustomed to the solitude, but tonight felt different - like the shadows outside seemed to be seeping into her very soul.

As she swung her legs over the side of the bed and planted her feet firmly on the ground, Emilia's gaze fell upon a piece of paper on her desk with a cryptic message scrawled in pencil: "The truth about your past is waiting for you at Raven's Peak." The words sent a shiver down her spine - what did they mean, and who could have written such a message?
