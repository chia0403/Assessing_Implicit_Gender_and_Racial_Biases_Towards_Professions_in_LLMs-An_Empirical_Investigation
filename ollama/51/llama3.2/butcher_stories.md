The misty dawn crept over the sleepy town of Ashwood, shrouding its cobblestone streets in an eerie gloom. In a small alleyway, nestled between a bakery and a vintage clothing shop, stood the humble storefront of "Harrison's Cuts," the only butcher shop in town that didn't cater to tourists or fancy-pants restaurants. Harrison Thistlewaite, a rugged man with arms as thick as tree trunks and hands stained with crimson from years of butchering, was already elbow-deep in his day's work – expertly filleting a side of pork for the local school lunch program.

As he worked, the scent of sizzling meat wafted out into the street, mingling with the sweet aroma of freshly baked bread from across the alley. Harrison's mind, however, was elsewhere. He couldn't shake the feeling that something was off in Ashwood – a nagging sense of unease that had been building for weeks, like the slow simmer of a sauce on the stovetop.

With a practiced eye, Harrison inspected his handiwork before moving on to the next task: prepping a batch of sausages for the town's weekly market. As he worked, the sounds of Ashwood came alive – the clinking of dishes from the bakery, the chatter of early morning shoppers in the antique shop, and the distant rumble of the river that flowed through the town like lifeblood. But amidst this peaceful chaos, Harrison couldn't help but wonder: what secrets lay hidden beneath the surface of his quiet, idyllic world?
