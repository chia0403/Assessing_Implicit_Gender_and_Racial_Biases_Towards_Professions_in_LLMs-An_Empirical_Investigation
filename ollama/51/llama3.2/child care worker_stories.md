---

The sun was setting over the small town of Willow Creek, casting a warm orange glow over the quiet streets and homes. In a cozy little house on Elm Street, 25-year-old Emily Jeanette Thompson was finishing up her shift at the local community center's child care program. She had spent the afternoon playing with the three- to five-year-olds, singing songs and reading stories in her bright pink hair clips.

As she locked the door behind the last of the children's parents, Emily felt a sense of satisfaction wash over her. It was days like this that reminded her why she became a child care worker in the first place: the laughter, the hugs, and the feeling of being part of something bigger than herself. She took a deep breath, letting the scent of freshly cut grass and fresh paint waft through the open window.

Emily lived in a tiny apartment above her parents' house just across the street from the community center. It was a simple but comfortable life, filled with family dinners and late-night talks about everything and nothing. As she made her way up the stairs to join her family for dinner, Emily couldn't shake the feeling that something was off. She had received a mysterious phone call earlier in the day, asking her not to work tomorrow morning. The caller had hung up without saying anything, leaving Emily more curious than concerned.

Little did she know, this was just the beginning of a night that would change everything.
