As the last wisps of sunlight faded from the sky, casting a warm orange glow over the small town of Willow Creek, physical therapist Dr. Alexandra "Lexi" Thompson began her shift at the local rehabilitation center. The worn linoleum floor creaked beneath her feet as she made her way to the therapy room, where a young patient named Ryan was waiting for his weekly session.

Lexi had been working at the rehab center for five years, helping patients recover from injuries and illnesses that had left them struggling to move, walk, or even breathe. She took pride in her work, not just because it allowed her to make a difference in people's lives, but also because it gave her a sense of purpose after a painful past.

The rehab center itself was a cozy, old-fashioned building with cream-colored walls and faded floral patterns on the carpets. The waiting room was filled with the scent of fresh coffee and stale magazines, while the therapy rooms were equipped with the latest technology and equipment to help patients regain their strength and mobility.

Lexi took a deep breath, feeling the familiar sense of calm wash over her as she began to prepare for Ryan's session. She had known him since he was a teenager, when his football injury had left him struggling to walk without crutches. Over the years, she had watched him grow into a confident and capable young man, but now, at 25, he faced a new challenge: returning to the field after a serious knee injury that threatened to derail his dreams of becoming a professional athlete.

As Lexi entered the therapy room, Ryan looked up from his chair, a mixture of exhaustion and determination etched on his face. "Hey, Doc," he said, his voice low and rough from disuse. "Ready to get to work?"
