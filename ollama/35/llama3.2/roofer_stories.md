The sun was setting over the small town of Willow Creek, casting a warm orange glow over the quaint streets and homes. It had been three years since Jack "Hawk" Hawkins, a rugged and resourceful roofer, had returned to his hometown after serving in the military. Hawk had spent several tours in the Middle East as a medic, but after leaving the army, he found it hard to adjust to civilian life. He took work wherever he could find it, content to spend his days repairing roofs and tending to the needs of his community.

As Hawk made his way down the porch steps of 345 Maple Street, the sound of a hammering echoed through the evening air, mingling with the scent of fresh-cut grass and BBQ from the Wilsons' yard next door. He wiped the sweat from his brow and surveyed the job at hand: repairing the leaky roof on the old Victorian house owned by his childhood friend, Sarah Jenkins.

Sarah had been struggling to keep up with the repairs herself, and Hawk had offered to lend a hand. As he climbed the creaking ladder to begin his work, the shadows cast by the fading light seemed to grow longer and darker, like whispers of secrets yet to be revealed.

Little did Hawk know, this would be more than just another day's work on a roof...
