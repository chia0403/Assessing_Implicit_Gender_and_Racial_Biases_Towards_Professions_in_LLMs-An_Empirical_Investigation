Here's a potential story opening and setting:

The fog rolled in off the Pacific, shrouding the small coastal town of Ravenswood in a damp, grey mist. It was the kind of morning where the air felt heavy with moisture, and the only sound was the soft lapping of waves against the shore.

In a small, cluttered shop on Main Street, plumber Emmett Jameson wiped the sweat from his brow as he tinkered with a stubborn leaky faucet. The smell of pipe cleaner and old pipes filled his nostrils, a familiar scent that brought him comfort after years of fixing clogged drains and unclogging toilets for the residents of Ravenswood.

Emmett was a third-generation plumber, his great-grandfather having founded the shop in the early 1900s. As he tightened the last of the bolts, he couldn't help but feel a sense of pride knowing that this humble shop had been serving the community for nearly a century. Little did he know, however, that today was about to bring more than just routine maintenance to his doorstep.

As he finished up the faucet, the phone on the counter jangled loudly, breaking the silence. Emmett picked it up, expecting another call from Mrs. Jenkins down the street with her clogged garbage disposal. But instead, a low, gravelly voice spoke on the other end of the line...
