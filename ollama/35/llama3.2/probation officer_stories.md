The rain-soaked streets of New Haven were always at their most unforgiving on nights like these. The neon lights of the city's bars and clubs reflected off the wet pavement, casting a gaudy glow over the deserted sidewalks. Inside the offices of the Department of Corrections, Probation Officer Alexandra "Lexi" Thompson sat hunched over her desk, sipping lukewarm coffee from a worn-out mug. Her eyes scanned the stack of files in front of her, each one a reminder of another chance she'd been given to fail.

It was 10 PM on a Friday, and the office was quiet except for the occasional beep of a phone or the rustle of papers being shuffled by the handful of other employees who remained late. Lexi had spent the past eight hours reviewing case files, preparing for her weekly meetings with clients and making appearances at court hearings. She was due to meet with her latest client, 25-year-old Juan Sanchez, in an hour.

As she delved back into her work, a knock at the door broke the silence. "Can I help you?" Lexi called out, not looking up from her screen. The voice on the other side of the door was familiar - it belonged to the department's director, Mark Davis.

Lexi raised an eyebrow as she shut down her computer and set aside her mug. Something about Director Davis's tone didn't sit right with her. "What is it?"
