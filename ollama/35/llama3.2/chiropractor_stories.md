Dr. Alexandra "Lexi" Thompson adjusted her glasses for what felt like the hundredth time that morning, surveying the bustling streets of downtown Portland. As she stepped out of her cozy apartment above the clinic and into the crisp autumn air, the scent of freshly brewed coffee wafted from the café on the corner, enticing her to start her day with a warm cup.

Lexi Thompson, D.C., was a chiropractor with a passion for helping people find balance in their lives. Her clinic, nestled between a vintage bookstore and a yoga studio, was a haven for those seeking relief from the stresses of modern life. With years of experience under her belt and a kind bedside manner, Lexi had built a loyal patient base and earned a reputation as one of Portland's most compassionate chiropractors.

As she locked up her clinic and made her way to the café, Lexi couldn't shake the feeling that today was going to be different. A mysterious letter had arrived at her office earlier that morning, its contents cryptic but unsettling. The words "The Spine is Key" were scrawled on a plain white sheet of paper, accompanied by an enigmatic smiley face emoji. Lexi's curiosity was piqued – what did this message mean for her practice, and for herself?

With the letter still fresh in her mind, Lexi settled into her favorite corner table at the café, ordering a rich, bold coffee to start her day off right. As she sipped her drink, she couldn't help but wonder: what secrets lay hidden beneath the surface of Portland's seemingly tranquil streets?
