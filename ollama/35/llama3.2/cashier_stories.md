The sun had just set over the small town of Oakdale, casting a warm orange glow over the worn sidewalks and faded storefronts. Inside the local convenience store, where Ember Rose Jenkins stood scanning groceries for the evening's customers, the air was thick with the smell of stale coffee and yesterday's Chinese takeout.

Ember, or "Emmie" as her coworkers called her, had been working at Pete's Market for three years now, watching as the town's population ebbed and flowed like the seasons. She knew most of the regulars by name - there was Mr. Thompson, who always bought a pack of gum; Mrs. Patel, who would settle for nothing but organic milk; and young Jimmy, who would grab a Slurpee on his way home from school.

As she rang up a customer's purchase, Ember's gaze wandered out the window, taking in the quiet streets and the darkening sky. It was a peaceful evening, the kind that made her feel grateful to be living in Oakdale. But as she looked around at the rows of dusty snacks and wilted flowers on display, she couldn't shake the feeling that something was off - like there was a storm brewing just beneath the surface, waiting to erupt.

Just then, the bell above the door jangled, signaling the arrival of another customer. Ember smiled and turned back to the screen, ready to face whatever the night might bring.
