In the sweltering summer of 1995, Aurora "Rory" Elara Wynter stepped out onto the scorching hot pavement of Sunset Boulevard, her eyes scanning the crowded streets of Los Angeles like a hawk searching for its next meal. The neon lights of the city's iconic music venues and record stores reflected off the wet pavement, casting a gaudy glow over the throngs of people rushing to and fro.

As the lead singer of the fledgling band "Midnight Whiskey," Rory had just stepped off the stage at the iconic Viper Room after a blistering set that left the crowd begging for more. The rush of adrenaline still coursed through her veins like liquid fire, fueling her desire to create music that would shake the very foundations of the industry.

But as she gazed out into the sea of faces, Rory's eyes landed on one person in particular - a mysterious figure who had been watching her from the shadows for what felt like an eternity. His piercing blue eyes locked onto hers, sending a shiver down her spine that had nothing to do with the sweltering heat and everything to do with the whispered rumors of a rival band trying to sabotage Midnight Whiskey's chances at stardom.

With a sense of foreboding growing in the pit of her stomach, Rory turned and disappeared into the crowd, leaving behind the spotlight and the cheers of adoring fans. But little did she know, this was just the beginning of a journey that would take her to the very limits of fame, fortune, and heartbreak.
