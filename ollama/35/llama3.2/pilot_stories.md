As the last wisps of sunlight faded from the horizon, Captain Julian Alexander "Jules" Thompson banked the small Cessna 206 Twin Bonanza to the left, the aircraft's engines roaring as it descended into the sleepy airport of Ravenswood, a tiny airstrip nestled deep in the heart of rural Maine. The trees, ablaze with color in the late autumn evening, blurred together outside the cockpit window as Jules expertly navigated the plane through the twilight.

With the engines sputtering to a stop, Jules let out a sigh of relief and turned off the ignition, feeling the weight of the day's long flight lift from his shoulders. He had flown his passengers – a group of rowdy college students on their way home for Thanksgiving break – safely from Boston, but he couldn't shake the sense that something was off.

As he stepped out of the cockpit and stretched his arms over his head, Jules gazed out at the small crowd gathering around the aircraft. The airport's lone terminal building stood like a tiny sentinel behind them, its fluorescent lights flickering in the darkness like fireflies. Ravenswood was a place where time seemed to stand still – a world away from the hustle and bustle of modern life.

And yet, Jules couldn't help but feel that something was stirring here, just beneath the surface. A sense of unease had been growing inside him since he took off earlier that morning, and he couldn't quite put his finger on what it was. He shook his head, telling himself he was just tired – flying a long flight can be mentally exhausting – but as he gazed out at the sea of faces before him, Jules felt an inexplicable sense of foreboding.

"Hey, Captain," one of the students called out, jogging over to where Jules stood. "We're gonna need some help getting our gear off. It's been a long ride!"

Jules smiled and nodded, pushing aside his doubts for the moment. But as he began to unload the plane, he couldn't shake the feeling that something was about to go terribly wrong in this small, seemingly idyllic town.
