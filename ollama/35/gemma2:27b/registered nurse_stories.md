The stale scent of antiseptic hung heavy in the air, mingling with the faint metallic tang of blood. Eleanor Ainsworth tightened her grip on the IV pole, her knuckles white against the cold steel.  Her sensible shoes squeaked softly against the linoleum floor as she navigated the crowded hallway of St. Jude's Hospital, a symphony of beeps and whirs accompanying her every step. The overhead fluorescent lights cast a sterile glow over everything, highlighting the exhaustion etched on the faces of her fellow nurses and doctors. 

Eleanor, despite the weariness tugging at her own eyelids, felt a surge of adrenaline as she approached Room 214.  Her patient, Mrs. Josephine Hawthorne, was struggling. The heart monitor displayed a frantic rhythm, and Eleanor's gut clenched with a familiar dread. This wasn't just another night shift; this was a race against time.
