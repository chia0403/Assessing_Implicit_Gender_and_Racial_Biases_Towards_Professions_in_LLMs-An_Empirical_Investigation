The scent of sawdust and linseed oil hung heavy in the air, mingling with the sweet, earthy aroma of freshly cut cedar.  Elias Thorne ran a calloused thumb along the edge of the table he was carving, his brow furrowed in concentration. Outside, the relentless August sun beat down on the small workshop nestled amongst towering pines in the foothills of the Appalachians. Elias squinted through the dusty windowpane, watching the heat shimmer off the worn dirt path leading to his shop.

He'd inherited this place from his grandfather, a man whose hands had crafted furniture so exquisite they whispered tales of forgotten forests and ancient craftsmanship. Elias, though he loved the work, never felt quite worthy of carrying on the legacy. Today, however, with the rhythmic rasping of his plane and the smooth grain of cedar yielding beneath his touch, a flicker of confidence ignited in his chest.  

Maybe, just maybe, he was finally finding his own rhythm in the symphony of wood.
