Rain lashed against the frosted glass of "Willow Creek Apothecary," blurring the neon sign's glow into a hazy watercolour. Inside, Eleanor Beatrice Ainsworth meticulously counted out lavender capsules, her brow furrowed in concentration. The scent of chamomile and peppermint hung heavy in the air, a familiar comfort amidst the relentless symphony of the storm outside. Willow Creek, a sleepy town nestled amongst rolling hills and whispering pines, felt particularly isolated tonight. The power flickered ominously, casting long shadows across the shelves laden with jars of brightly coloured powders and dried herbs. Eleanor sighed, her fingers nimbly working despite the mounting tension. Tonight was different. Tonight, she wasn't just filling prescriptions; she was waiting.
