Rain lashed against the windows of Dr. Evelyn Albright's office, mimicking the storm brewing within her patient. The scent of lavender oil from the diffuser did little to soothe the tense atmosphere.  Evelyn, with her calm grey eyes and perpetually tousled auburn hair, leaned forward, "Tell me more about this recurring dream, David."

The cramped office, nestled on the third floor of a converted Victorian house in the heart of Portland, Oregon, felt oppressive despite its attempts at coziness. Mismatched armchairs cluttered the space, overflowing with well-worn cushions. A bookshelf groaned under the weight of psychology texts and self-help manuals, their spines faded by years of sunlight streaming through the rain-streaked window. 

David, a young man whose face was etched with anxiety, shifted uncomfortably in his seat. Outside, the wind howled, mirroring the unspoken fear that clung to him like a shroud.
