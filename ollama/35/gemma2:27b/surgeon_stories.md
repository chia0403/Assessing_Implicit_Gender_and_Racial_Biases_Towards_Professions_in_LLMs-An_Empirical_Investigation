Rain lashed against the arched windows of St. Sybil's Hospital, mimicking the frantic rhythm of Dr. Eleanor Vance's heart. The OR lights glared down, reflecting off the slick tiles and stainless steel instruments scattered around her. Sweat beaded on Eleanor's brow as she surveyed the scene before her - a young boy, no older than twelve, his chest rising and falling with shallow breaths, his life teetering precariously on the edge of a scalpel.

Eleanor Vance, a surgeon renowned for her steady hand and unwavering focus, felt a tremor run through her. This wasn't just another routine procedure. This was Liam, the son of her childhood friend, who lay open on the operating table, his heart struggling against a rare congenital defect.
