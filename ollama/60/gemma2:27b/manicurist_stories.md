The whirring of the electric file was a familiar lullaby to Esmeralda "Esme" Rodriguez, blending seamlessly with the chatter and laughter that filled her tiny nail salon, "Polished." Nestled between a bustling bakery and a dimly lit pawn shop in the heart of Brooklyn's Sunset Park neighborhood, Esme's haven buzzed with life.

The scent of lavender polish and acetone hung heavy in the air, mingling with the sweet aroma wafting from the bakery next door. Sunlight streamed through the storefront window, illuminating dust motes dancing over Esme's meticulously organized collection of polishes - hundreds of tiny bottles, each holding a promise of transformation. Today, however, Esme felt anything but transformed. A gnawing anxiety coiled in her stomach, mirroring the swirling glitter she was carefully applying to Mrs. Garcia's nails.
