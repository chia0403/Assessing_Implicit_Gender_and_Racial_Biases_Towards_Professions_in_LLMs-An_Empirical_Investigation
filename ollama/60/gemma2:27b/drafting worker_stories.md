The smell of graphite dust and stale coffee hung heavy in the air of the cramped drafting room. Amelia "Amy" Thornton hunched over her desk, the pale light of the overhead lamp casting harsh shadows across her meticulously detailed blueprints. Outside, the relentless summer sun beat down on the sprawling shipyard, turning the metal hulls into shimmering mirages. But Amy barely noticed. Her world was confined to the lines and curves she coaxed from her pen, each stroke bringing the leviathan vessel closer to life. 

A rhythmic clang echoed from the nearby forge, followed by the rasping hiss of welding torches. These sounds were a constant backdrop to her work, a symphony of industry that both energized and exhausted her. Today, though, a different sound pierced through the familiar cacophony - a sharp, insistent rapping at her door.
