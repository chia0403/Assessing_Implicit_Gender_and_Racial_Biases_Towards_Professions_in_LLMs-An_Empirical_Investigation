The scent of sawdust and cedar hung heavy in the air, a comforting aroma Elias Thorne had known since childhood.  Sunlight slanted through the grimy windowpanes of his workshop, illuminating motes of dust dancing in its beam. Outside, the clatter and shouts of the port city reverberated against the weathered brick walls of his building, but here, within the embrace of wood shavings and half-finished projects, a quietude reigned.

Elias ran a calloused hand over the smooth curve of a mahogany rocking chair he was crafting for the Governor's daughter.  The commission had been unexpected, lucrative, and terrifying all at once. He prided himself on his craftsmanship, but could he truly meet the expectations of someone so high-born?
