Rain lashed against the ambulance windows, blurring the neon glow of the city into an impressionistic smear.  Inside, Amelia Reyes, her face drawn and tense, fought to keep her breakfast down as they sped towards the scene. The dispatcher's voice crackled through the radio: "Possible GSW, victim unresponsive, location - alley behind The Rusty Wrench."

Amelia gripped the handrail, her knuckles white. Gunshot wounds were always a gamble, a terrifying dance between life and death. She'd seen too many young men, victims of senseless violence, their futures snuffed out before they even had a chance to bloom. 

Tonight, though, something felt different. A prickling unease crawled up her spine, the kind that came with experience, with seeing the darkness that lurked beneath the city's glittering facade. This wasn't just another call; it was a siren song of danger calling out to her.
