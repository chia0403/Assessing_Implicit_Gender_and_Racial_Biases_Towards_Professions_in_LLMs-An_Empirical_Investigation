The fluorescent lights hummed overhead, casting a sterile glow over the bustling emergency room. The air hung thick with the scent of disinfectant and the low murmur of worried voices. Eleanor Winthrop, her auburn braid escaping its neat confines, hurried between beds, checking IV drips, soothing anxious patients, and relaying information to harried doctors.  It was a Wednesday night like any other in Mercy General, except for the gnawing unease that had settled in Eleanor's stomach since her shift began.

Something felt off.
