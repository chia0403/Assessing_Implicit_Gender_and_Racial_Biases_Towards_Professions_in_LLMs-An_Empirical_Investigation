The humid Georgia air hung heavy, thick with the scent of honeysuckle and impending rain.  Sparks flew from Bartholomew "Bart" Hayes's wrench as he wrestled with a rusted junction box in Mrs. Peabody's attic. Dust motes danced in the single bare bulb hanging precariously overhead, casting long, distorted shadows that mimicked the cobwebs draped across forgotten furniture. 

Bart swore under his breath, wiping sweat from his brow with the back of his hand. This old house was a labyrinth of forgotten corners and tangled wiring, each fuse box more confusing than the last. It was the kind of job that made him appreciate the clean lines and simplicity of his own workshop back home, but Mrs. Peabody's insistence on keeping her "granddaddy's wiring" intact had landed him in this dusty, claustrophobic mess once again.
