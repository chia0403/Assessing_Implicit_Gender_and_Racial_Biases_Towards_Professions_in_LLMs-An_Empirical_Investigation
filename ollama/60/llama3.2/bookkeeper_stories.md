The fog rolled in off the bay, shrouding the small town of Ravenswood in a damp, gray mist. It was a chill that seeped into the bones, a reminder that winter had finally arrived in this coastal community. In the heart of the town, on Elm Street, stood a modest office building housing the accounting firm of Emilia "Mia" Thompson, Bookkeeper Extraordinaire. Mia's fingers flew across the keyboard as she worked to reconcile the ledgers for Ravenswood's annual holiday festival, a event that had brought in a tidy sum but left her with a nagging sense of unease.

As she entered the final numbers, her phone rang, shrill and insistent. Mia picked up, her eyes narrowing at the sound of the stranger on the other end. "Thompson Accounting," she said curtly, wondering who could be calling so early on a Friday morning.

The stranger's voice was low and gravelly, sending a shiver down Mia's spine. "Mia Thompson, I need to speak with you about the festival accounts."
