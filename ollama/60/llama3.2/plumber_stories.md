The rain poured down on the streets of Ashwood, casting a gloomy mist over the small town's crumbling Victorian homes and rusty industrial buildings. Inside his cluttered workshop, Jack Harrison Thompson wiped the sweat from his forehead with the back of his arm, surveying the tangled mess of pipes and wires that filled his cramped space.

As a plumber, Jack had seen it all - from burst pipes to clogged drains, he'd fixed them all in his years working for the town's aging water board. But today, he was waiting for a call that might change everything. The phone on his workbench jolted him out of his reverie, shrill and insistent. He picked it up, and the voice on the other end spoke in hushed tones.

"Hey, Jack? It's Mrs. Jenkins from 345 Elm Street. I've got a problem with my toilet...and I think it might be connected to something bigger."

Jack's ears perked up at the word "connected". He'd dealt with his fair share of tricky toilets, but there was something about this one that didn't sit right. He grabbed his toolbox and headed out into the rain-soaked night, ready to face whatever lay ahead.

Little did he know, the storm outside was just a prelude to the chaos that was about to unfold in Ashwood...
