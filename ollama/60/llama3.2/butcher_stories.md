The snow fell gently over the small town of Willow Creek, casting a serene silence over its quaint streets and homes. In a small shop on Main Street, Emilia Anastasia "Mia" Thompson carefully trimmed the last of the morning's butchering batch, her hands moving with practiced ease as she worked to create perfect, evenly sized roasts for the day's customers.

As a third-generation butcher at Thompson's Butcher Shop, Mia had grown up surrounded by the sights and smells of meat – from the pungent aroma of fresh blood to the rich scent of slow-cooked sausages. She took pride in her work, carefully selecting each cut of meat with precision and care, knowing that it would end up on someone's plate tonight.

The shop itself was a cozy, welcoming space, with wooden shelves stacked high with an assortment of cured meats, fresh produce, and an impressive array of cheeses. A large glass case held the day's specials – a beautiful rack of lamb, perfectly marbled ribeye steaks, and an assortment of homemade sausages in various flavors. As Mia finished up her work, she gazed out the window, watching as the snowflakes continued to fall gently over Willow Creek, casting a magical spell over the town.

Outside, the sky was a deep shade of indigo, with stars beginning to twinkle like diamonds scattered across the fabric of the universe. It was moments like these that Mia felt most at peace – surrounded by the beauty and wonder of the world, with her hands covered in the gentle touch of raw meat and her heart full of contentment.

But as she turned back to the shop, a chill ran down her spine. A piece of paper on the counter caught her eye, with a single message scrawled across it in hasty handwriting: "Meet me at the old oak at midnight. Come alone."
