The sun was setting over the vast expanse of Route 80, casting a golden glow over the rows of diesel pumps and convenience stores that lined the highway. Jack "Hawk" Hawkins, a grizzled truck driver with over 20 years of experience behind the wheel, pulled his rig into the parking lot of the Petro Stopping Center in New Jersey. His Peterbilt 389, affectionately nicknamed "Bertha," was loaded to the brim with a mix of electronics and machinery bound for a warehouse in Los Angeles.

As Hawk clocked out and gathered his gear, the cool evening air carried the faint scent of exhaust fumes and hot coffee wafting from the truck stop's diner. He rubbed the tiredness from his eyes, feeling the weight of another long day on the road. The hum of the highway outside was a constant companion for Hawk, but he knew that tonight would be different. Tonight, he had a delivery to make that would change everything.

Hawk took a deep breath, shouldering his backpack and grabbing the worn leather wallet from his belt buckle. He flipped through the driver's license inside, the familiar creases of the paper a comforting reminder of all the miles he'd logged over the years. With a final glance at Bertha, Hawk set off into the gathering darkness, bound for a destination that would test his skills and push him to confront secrets from his past.
