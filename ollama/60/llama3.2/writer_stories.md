---

The rain poured down on the streets of Edinburgh like a relentless veil, shrouding the city in a misty grey gloom. Inside the small, cluttered flat that served as her writer's lair, Emilia "Mia" Sophia Wynter huddled over her laptop, trying to make sense of the latest draft of her novel. The words blurred together on the screen, taunting her with their lack of coherence.

As she sipped lukewarm tea from a chipped mug, Mia gazed out the window at the grey stones that seemed to stretch on forever. She had always loved this city, with its winding alleys and ancient stories hidden in every brick and stone. But now, as she struggled to bring her latest story to life, it all felt like a distant memory.

The flat itself was a testament to Mia's love of nostalgia - old books stacked haphazardly on shelves, yellowed paperbacks spilling from the worn armchair, and a vintage typewriter that had once belonged to her grandfather, a Scottish author in his own right. But even the comforting familiarity of it all couldn't chase away the doubts that plagued her.

Mia's phone buzzed on the coffee table, shrill in the silence. She hesitated for a moment before answering, wondering who could be calling at such an early hour.

"Hello?" she said, trying to sound more awake than she felt.

The voice on the other end was low and smooth, sending a shiver down Mia's spine. "Mia Wynter? I hope I'm not interrupting anything important."

Mia's heart skipped a beat as she recognized the voice - it belonged to her old friend and literary rival, Finnley Blackwood.
