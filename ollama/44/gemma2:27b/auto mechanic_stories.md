The grease under Amelia "Amy" Rodriguez's fingernails was practically a permanent fixture. It wasn't dirt, not really – it was a badge of honor, a testament to her skill in coaxing life back into temperamental engines.  

The air hung heavy with the scent of motor oil and gasoline in Amy's cluttered garage, nestled behind her grandmother's rambling Victorian house on the edge of Willow Creek. Sunlight streamed through dusty windows, illuminating the jumble of tools, spare parts, and half-dismantled vehicles. A battered Ford pickup truck, its hood propped open, occupied the center stage, groaning softly as Amy tightened a bolt with practiced ease. 


This was her sanctuary – a world where metal sang to her soul and problems had tangible solutions. But today, a different kind of problem rattled her gears: a cryptic message scrawled on a crumpled napkin left on her workbench. It simply read, "Midnight. Old Sawmill Road. Come alone."
