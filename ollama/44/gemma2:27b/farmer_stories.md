Elias himself, a man weathered by years of toil under the Irish sun, squinted at the sky. The clouds were gathering, promising a storm. He tugged his woolen cap lower over his brow and adjusted the weight of the empty burlap sack slung across his shoulder. Today was market day in Navan, and Elias needed to sell his surplus potatoes before the rains came.
