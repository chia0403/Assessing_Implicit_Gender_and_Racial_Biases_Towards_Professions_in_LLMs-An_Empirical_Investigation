Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of Agnes Thistlewick's workshop, each drop exploding in a miniature burst of spray. The air inside was thick with the scent of solder and damp earth, punctuated by the occasional hiss of steam from Agnes' latest project: a copper contraption designed to unclog drains with sonic waves. 

Agnes, her greying hair pulled back in a tight bun, wiped a smudge of grease from her cheek with the back of her hand. "Blasted thing," she muttered, peering at the tangled wires protruding from the device. It wasn't supposed to spark like that.  She sighed, knowing this late-night tinkering session was likely to end in another frustrating dawn.

Suddenly, a sharp rap on the workshop door startled Agnes. She straightened up, wiping her hands on her oil-stained overalls. "Coming," she called out, already bracing herself for the inevitable deluge of leaky faucets and overflowing toilets that always seemed to coincide with torrential downpours.
