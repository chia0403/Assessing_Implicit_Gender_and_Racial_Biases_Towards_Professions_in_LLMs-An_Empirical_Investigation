Rain lashed against the dusty windowpanes of "Apothecary's Rest," blurring the neon glow of the "Open" sign into an anemic smear. Inside, Evangeline Beatrice Thorne meticulously counted out crimson pills, her silver spectacles perched precariously on the bridge of her nose. The air was thick with the scent of dried herbs and old paperbacks – a comforting aroma that usually soothed her. Tonight, however, it did little to quell the gnawing unease in her stomach.

The bell above the door jingled, startling Evangeline from her counting. A tall figure shrouded in a dark coat entered, their face obscured by the shadows of a wide-brimmed hat. The stranger's presence sent a shiver down her spine – a feeling she hadn't experienced since those unsettling nights during pharmacy school when she studied obscure, medieval remedies whispered to have otherworldly origins.

"Good evening," Evangeline said, forcing a smile. "What can I do for you?"
