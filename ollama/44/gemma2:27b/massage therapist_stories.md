Rain lashed against the windows of "Serenity Now," blurring the neon OPEN sign hanging precariously above the doorway. Inside, the air hummed with the gentle drone of a Himalayan singing bowl and the faint scent of lavender oil. Emilia Rose Hawthorne adjusted the warm towels on the massage table, her brow furrowed in concentration. It was her busiest night yet, the deluge outside apparently driving people to seek solace in her capable hands. 

Emilia loved the quiet intensity of her work. The way she could melt away tension with a practiced knead and coax weary muscles back to life. Yet tonight, something felt different. A prickling unease danced along her spine as she awaited her next client.
