Rain lashed against the grimy windows of St. Jude's Hospital, mirroring the tempest raging inside Dr. Eleanor Vance. The fluorescent lights hummed above, casting a sterile glow on the stainless steel instruments laid out before her in perfect order. Each scalpel gleamed with an unsettling sharpness, each clamp lay poised for action.

Eleanor hadn't slept in over 36 hours. Her scrubs clung to her like a second skin, damp with sweat and exhaustion. The acrid scent of disinfectant mingled with the metallic tang of blood, a familiar symphony in this dimly lit operating theatre. 


Tonight, she wasn't just fighting against a ruptured aorta; she was wrestling with a ghost from her past, a patient whose life hung precariously on the edge of a blade.
