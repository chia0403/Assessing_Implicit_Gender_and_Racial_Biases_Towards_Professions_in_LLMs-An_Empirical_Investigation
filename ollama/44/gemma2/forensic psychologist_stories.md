The air in Dr. Eleanor Vance's office hung heavy with the scent of stale coffee and regret. Rain lashed against the windows, mimicking the tempest brewing inside her.  Eleanor, a woman whose sharp gaze could dissect a suspect's facade quicker than a surgeon wielded a scalpel, felt a knot tightening in her chest. Across from her sat Mark Thompson, a man whose carefully constructed composure was fraying at the edges like an old tapestry. 

The setting: Chicago, a sprawling metropolis cloaked in a perpetual drizzle that seemed to seep into your bones. Eleanor's office, tucked away on the tenth floor of a nondescript building overlooking Lake Michigan, offered little comfort. The gray walls were plastered with motivational posters, ironically taunting her with promises of serenity and self-belief. Outside, the city hummed with a relentless energy, oblivious to the internal struggle playing out in this small, cluttered room.


Mark Thompson was no ordinary client. He had been referred to Eleanor by his lawyer, accused of a crime so heinous that it sent shivers down even her seasoned spine. The whispers surrounding the case were enough to make her blood run cold: a calculated and brutal act, fueled by unimaginable darkness. 

Eleanor needed answers. But could she find them within the labyrinthine corridors of Mark Thompson's mind before he slipped further into the abyss?
