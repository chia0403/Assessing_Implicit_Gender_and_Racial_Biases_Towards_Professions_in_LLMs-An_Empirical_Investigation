The stale scent of chalk dust and disinfectant hung heavy in Mrs. Evelyn Blackwood's classroom, clinging to her cardigan like a forgotten memory. Outside, the late April sun blazed through the dusty windows, painting stripes across the worn linoleum floor.  She surveyed the sea of faces before her, their expressions ranging from bored apathy to outright hostility. It was the last week of school, and the energy in Room 214 mirrored the restlessness of spring itself. 

Evelyn sighed, adjusting the glasses perched precariously on the bridge of her nose. Another year had come and gone, another crop of teenagers she'd tried to mold into literate, thinking individuals.  It was a noble pursuit, but sometimes, just sometimes, it felt like fighting a losing battle against the tide.
