The sun was setting over the vast expanse of the Montana wilderness, casting a golden glow over the rugged terrain as Ryder Cole McClure climbed into the cab of his trusty Caterpillar 924K bulldozer. The air was crisp and cool, carrying the scent of pine trees and damp earth, a familiar smell that filled Ryder's senses with a sense of comfort and control.

As he started the engine and began to warm up the machine, Ryder gazed out at the landscape before him - a sea of rolling hills, rocky outcroppings, and scrubby vegetation stretching as far as the eye could see. He had spent countless hours operating heavy equipment on these very lands, working for his family's construction company, McClure & Sons, which had been built by his grandfather.

Ryder's thoughts turned to the day's task: clearing a path through the dense forest for a new development project that would bring jobs and growth to this small town. It was work he loved, but also work that came with its own set of challenges - from navigating treacherous terrain to dealing with the occasional malfunctioning machine.

As he prepared to begin, Ryder's phone rang, shrill in the stillness. He picked it up, expecting a call from his foreman or maybe even his brother, Jake, who worked as a mechanic for the company. But instead, a voice on the other end spoke words that made Ryder's heart skip a beat: "Ryder, we need to talk."
