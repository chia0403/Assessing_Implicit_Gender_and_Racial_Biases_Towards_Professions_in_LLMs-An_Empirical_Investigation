The sun was setting over the vast expanse of the Red Rock Ranch, casting a warm orange glow over the rolling hills and scrubby mesquite trees that dotted the landscape. It was a sight that 35-year-old Ryder Jackson had grown up with and loved, having spent his entire life working on this land alongside his family.

Ryder stood at the edge of the ranch's main corral, gazing out at the gathering dust devils as the wind began to pick up. He wore a well-worn leather cowboy hat, cinched low over one eye, and his rugged denim jacket flapped open to reveal a white T-shirt beneath. His boots, scuffed and worn from years of hard work, were planted firmly on the ground.

As he watched, a lone figure emerged from the fading light, walking towards him with a purposeful stride. Ryder's heart quickened as he saw it was his sister, Jesse, who had returned to the ranch after several months away, her long brown hair tied back in a ponytail and her eyes fixed on some point beyond the horizon.

Jesse's face was etched with a mixture of fatigue and determination, a look that Ryder knew all too well. What was she running from, or what had brought her back to this place?
