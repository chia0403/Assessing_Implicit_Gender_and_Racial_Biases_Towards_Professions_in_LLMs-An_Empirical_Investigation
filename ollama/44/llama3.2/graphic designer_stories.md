The rain poured down on the city like a dirty blanket, casting a gloomy mist over the streets of Ashwood. It was a chill that seeped into the bones, a reminder that winter had finally arrived in the small town where graphic designer, Alexandra "Lexi" Thompson, called home.

Lexi sat hunched over her desk, surrounded by papers, pens, and empty coffee cups, trying to shake off the lingering fatigue of a long day at work. Her eyes, a deep shade of brown that seemed to hold a world of emotion, scanned the pages of her latest design project, a branding campaign for a local brewery. The font was supposed to be edgy, but it looked more like a rejected idea from a freshman art student.

As she sipped on her lukewarm coffee, Lexi's mind wandered back to the phone call that had shaken her the day before. Her best friend and partner in crime, Rachel Lee, had announced that she was leaving Ashwood for a big city job on the other side of the country. The thought of losing her BFF to the bright lights and endless possibilities of the city sent Lexi's heart skipping a beat.

Just then, the studio door creaked open, admitting a blast of cold air that carried with it the scent of wet earth and decaying leaves. Lexi looked up to see her long-time crush, Jake Harris, standing in the doorway, his eyes crinkling at the corners as he smiled.

"Hey, L-Train," he said, using the nickname only he knew how to pronounce. "What's cooking?"

Lexi felt a flutter in her chest as she met Jake's gaze. She had secretly harbored feelings for him since high school, but he'd never seemed to notice her beyond their friendship. Or so she thought...
