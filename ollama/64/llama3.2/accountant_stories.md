Here's a potential story opening:

The rain-soaked streets of New Haven reflected the grey mood that had settled over Arthur Everett Fanshawe, CPA. As he gazed out the office window of his small accounting firm, the droplets on the pane created a mesmerizing dance of light and shadow, a fleeting distraction from the mounting financial troubles plaguing his clients.

Arthur, or Arty as few knew him, was a man of numbers and order, with a keen mind for finance and a passion for precision. His firm, Everett & Co., had once been a respected name in New Haven's business community, but the past year had seen a steady decline in client base and revenue.

As he rubbed the weariness from his eyes, Arty couldn't shake the feeling that something was amiss. The city's annual gala for small businesses was just days away, and despite his efforts to drum up new leads, he was still short of clients to participate. The usually lucrative event would now be a costly burden, unless someone – or several someones – stepped in with an unexpected donation.

Little did Arty know that this year's gala held more than just financial implications for him...
