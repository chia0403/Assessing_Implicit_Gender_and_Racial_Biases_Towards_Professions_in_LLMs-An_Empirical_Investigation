Here's a potential story opening:

The rain-soaked streets of New Haven gleamed like polished stone under the faint glow of streetlights as Dr. Alexandra "Lexi" Thompson, a renowned cardiothoracic surgeon, stepped out of her sleek black sedan and onto the damp pavement. Her crisp white scrubs were stained with the remnants of last night's dinner, a souvenir from a late-night shift at Mercy Hospital, where she had spent the better part of eight hours in the OR, scrubbing away at hearts and lungs.

The cool misty air enveloped her as she gazed up at the imposing facade of her family's ancestral home, a stately Georgian mansion that loomed over the city like a sentinel. The once-grand estate, nestled among the leafy streets of New Haven's historic district, had been in Lexi's family for generations. Her great-great-grandfather, a pioneering surgeon himself, had founded the hospital where she now worked.

As Lexi approached the mansion, the front door swung open with a well-oiled creak, revealing her loyal butler, Mr. Jenkins, standing watchfully behind the polished mahogany. "Good evening, Doctor," he said, his voice low and soothing as always. "Your mother is waiting for you in the study."

Lexi smiled wryly to herself, wondering what had prompted her mother's summons at this ungodly hour. It was already 11 PM, and she had a long day ahead of her tomorrow in the operating room...
