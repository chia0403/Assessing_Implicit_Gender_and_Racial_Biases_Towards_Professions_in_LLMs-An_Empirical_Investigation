The sun was setting over the small town of Willow Creek, casting a warm orange glow over the dusty Main Street. The smell of fresh-cut hay and baking bread wafted from the local café, where Emma Mae Jenkins was busy preparing for the evening's customers.

Emma Mae, or Mae to those who knew her, had grown up on this land. Her family had farmed it for generations, and she was proud to carry on that tradition. She spent most of her days tending to the crops, fixing broken equipment, and coaxing life from the stubborn soil.

As a young girl, Mae had always dreamed of leaving Willow Creek behind and seeing the world beyond its narrow streets. But after her father's passing, she'd stayed put, determined to care for her aging mother and keep the family farm running smoothly. Now, at thirty-five years old, Mae was starting to feel the weight of responsibility settle in around her shoulders.

Tonight, as she wiped down the counter and prepared for the evening rush, Mae couldn't shake the feeling that something was off. The crops had been struggling all season, and the weather forecast looked worse than usual. She pushed the thought aside, focusing on the familiar rhythms of the café: the clinking of dishes, the chatter of patrons, and the soft hum of the old jukebox playing in the corner.

Just as she was starting to relax, a stranger walked into the café, his boots scuffling against the worn floorboards. Mae's eyes narrowed as she watched him take a seat at the bar, his piercing blue eyes scanning the room with an air of quiet curiosity...
