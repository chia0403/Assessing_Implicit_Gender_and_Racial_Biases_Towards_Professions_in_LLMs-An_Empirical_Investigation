The lights of the old coffee shop on Main Street cast a warm glow through the steam-streaked windows, drawing in weary locals and travelers alike with promises of comfort and solace.

Inside, Aurora "Rory" Wynter hunched over her computer, sipping lukewarm coffee and staring at the latest design concept for her new client: a small, struggling bakery on the outskirts of town. Rory's fingers danced across the keyboard, tweaking typography and color schemes in a quest to create a logo that would make the bakery stand out from the crowd.

As she worked, the sounds of the rain and the hum of the coffee shop blended into a soothing background noise, but Rory's mind was elsewhere. She had just received an unusual commission – one that required her to use her graphic design skills in ways she'd never attempted before. The client wanted a logo that would somehow capture the essence of... something else entirely.

Rory's eyes narrowed as she pondered the project's peculiarities. What could this mysterious client possibly want, and why did they need Rory's expertise? She shook off the feeling of unease, chalking it up to the strange energy of the rainy evening. After all, she'd handled stranger projects before – like that infamous branding campaign for "Mystery Meat Inc." or the time she'd designed a logo for a reclusive novelist's alter ego.

Still, there was something about this new project that didn't feel right...
