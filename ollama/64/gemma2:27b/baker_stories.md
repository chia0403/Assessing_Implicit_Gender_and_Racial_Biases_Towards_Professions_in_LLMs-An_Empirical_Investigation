The aroma of caramelized sugar and yeast hung thick in the air, a comforting shroud against the chill November wind that rattled the windows of "Butter & Bloom." Amelia Roseweather, her flour-dusted apron mirroring the dusting on her freckled nose, expertly maneuvered a tray laden with cinnamon buns out of the oven. The heat washed over her face, momentarily banishing the pre-dawn chill that seeped through the bakery's aging brick walls. 

Amelia hummed along to the radio playing softly in the corner, its cheerful melody incongruous with the melancholic grey sky looming outside. Her small town of Oakhaven was nestled amidst rolling hills, a tapestry of autumnal hues now fading into winter's muted tones. Today, the village square would be abuzz with activity for the annual Harvest Festival, and Amelia had baked enough treats to satisfy even the hungriest festival-goers.
