The air hung heavy with the smell of damp earth and woodsmoke as Silas Blackwood surveyed his fields. A veil of mist clung to the rolling hills surrounding his homestead, obscuring the distant peaks that usually crowned the horizon. Dawn was just breaking, casting an ethereal glow over the dew-kissed crops, but Silas felt no sense of peace. The crows cawed incessantly from the gnarled branches of a giant oak near the barn, their cries echoing his own unease. Something wasn't right.

Silas Blackwood had been tending this land for forty years, his calloused hands knowing its every curve and contour. He knew when the soil was thirsty, when the wind carried the scent of rain, and when something unnatural disturbed the balance of nature. This morning, that sense of unease was a heavy weight on his chest. It wasn't just the crows; it was a silence deeper than usual, a stillness in the air that felt like holding your breath.
