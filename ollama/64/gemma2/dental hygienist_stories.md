The fluorescent lights hummed above Amelia Beatrice Thistlewick's head, casting a sterile glow on the rows of gleaming stainless steel instruments. The air hung thick with the antiseptic scent of mouthwash and the faint metallic tang of blood – remnants from Mrs. Henderson's particularly stubborn calculus buildup.  Amelia sighed, leaning back against her metal stool in the dental chair beside her. 

Her tiny office, nestled between a dusty antique shop and a boisterous butcher's, was hardly glamorous. But she liked it that way. The familiar hum of the suction cleaner, the clink of instruments against trays, the soft murmur of Dr. Caldwell's instructions – these were the comforting sounds of her world. 

It wasn't exactly thrilling, but Amelia took pride in her work. She believed a healthy smile was a gateway to confidence, and she relished the small victories: a child's hesitant grin after a successful cleaning, a grateful nod from an elderly patient who could finally bite into their favorite apple. Today, however, Amelia felt a prickle of unease. 


Her gaze fell on a crumpled flyer taped to the wall near her sink – "Psychic Readings Available," it proclaimed in bold, dripping letters. The thought of a psychic wandering through her peaceful office felt unsettling. But perhaps, she mused, just maybe, they could offer some insight into the strange dreams that had been plaguing her lately.
