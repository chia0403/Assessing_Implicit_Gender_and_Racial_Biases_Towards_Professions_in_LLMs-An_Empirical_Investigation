The sterile scent of disinfectant hung heavy in the air, mingling with the faint, metallic tang of blood. It was the smell of Dr. Eleanor Blythe's comfort zone - a symphony of routine and predictability amidst the chaos of life outside her dental practice. Her tiny office, nestled on a quiet street corner in Savannah, Georgia, felt like a sanctuary from the humid summer air that clung to the city like a wet blanket. 

Eleanor, with her perpetually messy bun and eyes that sparkled behind thick-rimmed glasses, was a creature of habit. Every morning, she arrived precisely at 7:30 am, brewed a pot of strong black coffee, and meticulously reviewed patient charts, mentally preparing for the day's procedures. But today felt different. A prickling unease settled in her stomach, an unwelcome guest disrupting the usual calm. It started with a phone call, one that promised to upend Eleanor's world and lead her down a path she never imagined walking.
