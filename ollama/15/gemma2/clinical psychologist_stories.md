The late afternoon sun cast long shadows across Dr. Eleanor Vance’s neatly arranged desk, turning the brass paperweights into shimmering pools of gold. Dust motes danced in the golden light filtering through the blinds, illuminating the worn spines of self-help books lining the shelves behind her. A gentle breeze ruffled the curtains, carrying with it the scent of rain and freshly cut grass from the small courtyard outside. 

Eleanor leaned back in her chair, steepling her fingers, and surveyed the scene before her: the plush armchair across from her remained empty, awaiting its usual occupant – a troubled soul seeking solace, clarity, or perhaps just someone to listen. She sighed, the sound barely audible above the distant hum of traffic on the bustling city street outside. This wasn't what she had imagined when she'd chosen this career path.  


This was her sanctuary, her private space where she attempted to mend broken minds and soothe aching hearts. But today, a weight seemed to press down on her own chest, a familiar ache that whispered of exhaustion and disillusionment. Was it possible for someone dedicated to healing others to forget to heal themselves?
