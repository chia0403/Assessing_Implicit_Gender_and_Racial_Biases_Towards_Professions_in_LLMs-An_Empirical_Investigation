The scent of aged paper and leather hung heavy in the air, a familiar comfort to Amelia Beatrice Hawthorne as she dusted the spines of first edition novels lining the towering shelves. Sunlight slanted through the arched windows of the Blackwood Library, illuminating motes of dust dancing in the stillness. The silence was broken only by the rhythmic tick-tock of the grandfather clock in the corner, its pendulum swinging like a metronome keeping time with Amelia's methodical work. 

It had been a quiet Tuesday afternoon, the kind where even the usual bustle of patrons seemed to have taken a holiday.  But Amelia knew that wouldn't last. Blackwood Library was more than just a repository for books; it was a portal to other worlds, a refuge from reality, and sometimes, it held secrets waiting to be discovered. 


Today, she felt a prickling sensation at the nape of her neck, a subtle shift in the air that hinted at something unusual brewing. Amelia looked up from her task, her grey eyes scanning the deserted reading room, her senses on high alert.
