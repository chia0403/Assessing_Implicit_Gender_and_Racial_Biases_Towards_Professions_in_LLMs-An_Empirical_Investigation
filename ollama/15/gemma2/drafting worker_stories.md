The air in Drafting Studio 5 hung thick with the scent of burnt coffee and graphite. Amelia Harper, her forehead creased in concentration, hunched over her drafting table, meticulously drawing lines on the translucent vellum sheet before her. The fluorescent lights overhead hummed, casting a sickly yellow glow across the cluttered space. Walls were lined with shelves crammed full of architectural blueprints, technical manuals, and faded renderings, each one whispering tales of past projects completed and dreams deferred. Outside, the roar of construction echoed through the open windows, a constant reminder of the city's ceaseless growth, a growth Amelia felt increasingly disconnected from.  She longed to create structures that soared above the mundane, not just blueprints for yet another sterile office block.
