Rain lashed against the windows of the Sunnyside Rehabilitation Centre, mirroring the tempest brewing inside Amelia Rose Hawthorne.  She stared at the untouched stack of patient charts on her desk, each file representing a life yearning for normalcy, a chance to reclaim what they'd lost. But today, even the familiar comfort of helping others felt distant, eclipsed by a gnawing sense of uncertainty that had settled in her chest like a lead weight. 

Sunnyside wasn't just any rehab centre; it was nestled on the windswept cliffs overlooking the churning Atlantic Ocean, a sanctuary for those recovering from traumatic brain injuries. The very air seemed to hum with resilience and hope. Yet Amelia couldn't shake the feeling that something was amiss.
