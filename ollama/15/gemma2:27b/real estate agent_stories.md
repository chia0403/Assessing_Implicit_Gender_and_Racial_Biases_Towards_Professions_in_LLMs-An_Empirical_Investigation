Rain lashed against the windshield, blurring the neon lights of downtown Los Angeles into abstract streaks of red and blue. Evangeline "Evie" Thorne sighed, her breath fogging up the glass momentarily. She hated showing properties in this weather, but Mr. Hayashi had flown in from Tokyo specifically to see this Art Deco masterpiece perched on a hillside overlooking the city. 

Evie gripped the steering wheel tighter, navigating the slick asphalt. The house, known as "The Sapphire," was infamous for its eccentric history and breathtaking views. It had sat vacant for years, whispered to be haunted by the ghost of its original owner, a Hollywood screenwriter who'd met an untimely end within its walls. 


Evie wasn't superstitious, but even she felt a shiver as she pulled into the long, overgrown driveway. The house loomed above her, a silhouette against the stormy sky. Tonight, it didn't just feel like an empty mansion; it felt like something else entirely, something watchful and waiting.
