The stale air hung thick with anticipation, tinged with the faint scent of overripe bananas from someone's carry-on. Seraphina "Sera" Thorne smoothed down her navy blue uniform skirt for the hundredth time, her eyes darting between the blinking boarding sign and the restless queue snaking towards Gate 37. It was a Wednesday, just past noon, and Sera was itching to escape the fluorescent hum of Dallas-Fort Worth International Airport.

Today's flight: DFW to Reykjavik, Iceland. A route Sera secretly cherished for its promise of otherworldly landscapes and whispered tales of elves hidden in mossy hills.
