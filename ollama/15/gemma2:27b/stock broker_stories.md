Rain lashed against the panoramic window of Alistair Cavendish's office, mirroring the storm brewing within him. The sleek lines of his mahogany desk were cluttered with spreadsheets forecasting a market downturn so drastic it threatened to swallow everything he'd built.  Alistair, a man whose impeccably tailored suits and silvered hair belied a ruthless ambition, stared at the figures, each one a nail hammered into the coffin of his meticulously crafted empire. The city lights outside, normally twinkling beacons of opportunity, now seemed dim and distant, reflecting the uncertainty clouding his future. 


He needed to act. And fast.
