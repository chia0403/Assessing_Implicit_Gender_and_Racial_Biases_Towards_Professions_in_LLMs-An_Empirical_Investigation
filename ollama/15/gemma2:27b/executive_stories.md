Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of Amelia Cartwright's corner office, blurring the cityscape into an impressionistic watercolour of grey and yellow. The incessant drumming was a fitting soundtrack to the tension thrumming beneath her impeccably manicured nails. Twenty-five floors above the teeming streets of London, Amelia, Chief Operating Officer of Cartwright Enterprises, stared at the spreadsheet on her screen, its stark red figures mocking her efforts. 

The launch of Project Phoenix, a revolutionary green energy initiative, had been meticulously planned for months. Every detail, from securing government subsidies to hiring top-tier engineers, had been executed with Amelia's characteristic precision. Yet, here she was, staring at a projected loss of £20 million, threatening not only the future of Project Phoenix but also the stability of her family’s legacy.
