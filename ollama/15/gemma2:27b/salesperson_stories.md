The fluorescent lights hummed overhead, casting an anemic glow on the rows of gleaming chrome and stainless steel kitchen appliances lining the showroom floor. Amelia "Amy" Cartwright adjusted her blazer, the itchy polyester scratching at her collarbone. It was Wednesday afternoon, prime time for walk-ins, but so far, only a gaggle of teenagers had wandered through, giggling and snapping selfies with toasters they couldn't afford.

Amy sighed, the familiar scent of lemon cleaner and desperation clinging to the air. Selling appliances in this cutthroat market wasn't glamorous, but it paid the bills, allowing her to chase her dream of opening a bakery one day. She just needed to make quota this month, and then maybe, finally, she could afford to take that pastry-making class in Paris...
