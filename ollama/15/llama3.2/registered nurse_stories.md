The rain-soaked streets of Seattle gleamed like polished marble as Dr. Sophia Elizabeth Thompson stepped out of her small apartment and into the crisp evening air. She took a deep breath, feeling the familiar scent of the city's coffee shops and seafood restaurants mingling with the damp earthy smell of the Puget Sound. As she walked to the hospital, the sounds of Sisyphean traffic and construction echoed through the mist-shrouded streets, but Sophia felt a sense of calm wash over her.

A registered nurse at the prestigious University of Washington Medical Center, Sophia had seen it all – from joyous new births to devastating losses. Her years of experience had tempered her passion for helping others, but she still got caught up in the high-stakes drama of the hospital's fast-paced world. Tonight was no exception, as a mysterious patient had been admitted under the radar, sparking whispers among the staff.

Sophia quickened her pace, her eyes scanning the crowds of bundled-up commuters on their way home from work. She was already running late for her shift, and she knew that if she didn't get to the hospital soon, her supervisor would be breathing down her neck. But as she turned onto the hospital's main entrance, Sophia felt a shiver run down her spine – something about this patient didn't feel right.

And then, just as she was about to push open the automatic doors, a figure caught her eye. A woman with piercing green eyes and jet-black hair stood alone on the sidewalk, watching Sophia with an unnerving intensity.
