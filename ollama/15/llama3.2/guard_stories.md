Here's a potential story opening:

The dimly lit watchtower of Newhaven Prison stood sentinel over the sleepy coastal town, its stone walls bearing witness to centuries of secrets and scandals. Inside, Sergeant Elara Vexar stood at her post, surveying the rows of iron bars that separated the worst of society from the world beyond.

It was a chilly autumn evening in 1888, and the air was heavy with the scent of saltwater and decay. The town's residents were all tucked away in their beds, unaware of the storm brewing within the prison walls. Elara, a seasoned guard with over a decade of service, had seen it all - from riots to attempted escapes, from murders to miscarriages of justice.

As she checked her watch for what felt like the hundredth time that night, a faint scratching sound echoed through the corridors outside her quarters. Elara's eyes narrowed, her hand instinctively drifting to the grip of her pistol, hidden beneath her jacket. She had been on high alert since the arrival of the new prisoner, a young woman named Arabella Wychwood - accused of murdering her own husband in a fit of jealous rage.

Elara knew better than to underestimate anyone who walked through those prison gates, but there was something about Arabella that didn't sit right with her. Something that made her wonder if she had been too quick to judge...
