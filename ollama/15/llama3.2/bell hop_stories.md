It was a drizzly autumn evening in 1922, and the fog rolled off the Pacific Ocean like a damp shroud, shrouding the sleepy coastal town of Ravenswood in a misty veil. Inside the grand Hotel Waverley, where the wealthy elite came to play, the sound of jazz music floated through the lobby, mingling with the clinking of glasses and the soft murmur of hushed conversations.

In the midst of this luxurious chaos, a young man named Edwin Fletcher Wellington stood poised behind his bell hop cart, surveying the room with an air of quiet confidence. With his neatly pressed uniform and slicked-back black hair, Ed, as he was known to the regulars, had a way of moving unseen, yet always present, like a ghostly presence hovering just out of sight.

As he expertly juggled a tray laden with drinks and snacks for the guests, Ed's eyes roamed over the crowd, taking in the familiar faces of his friends among the wealthy patrons. He knew each one by name, each one had their own story to tell, and Ed was content to listen and collect tips, all while keeping the hotel running smoothly.

Little did Ed know, however, that this evening would bring more than just a typical night's work at the Hotel Waverley...
