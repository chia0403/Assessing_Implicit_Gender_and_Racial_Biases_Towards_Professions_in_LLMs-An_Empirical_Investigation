The sun had just set over the small town of Oakdale, casting a warm orange glow over the streets and homes that lined Main Street. The air was alive with the smell of freshly cut grass and the distant hum of crickets, a soothing soundtrack to the evening routine.

Inside the Oakdale Fire Department's station, Captain Ryder Jackson sat at his desk, sipping on a cold coffee and staring out the window at the quiet streets below. A veteran firefighter with over 10 years of service under his belt, Ryder had seen it all - from residential fires to high-rise emergencies. But tonight felt different. Tonight, the tension in the air was palpable.

As he scanned the horizon, Ryder's mind wandered back to the call that had come in just an hour before. A report of a possible structure fire at the old Miller place on Elm Street. The dispatcher had been tight-lipped about the details, but Ryder could tell by the urgency in her voice that this was no ordinary emergency.

Ryder's eyes narrowed as he thought back to the history of the abandoned mill building. Rumors had long circulated about the strange occurrences and unexplained noises that came from within its crumbling walls. Some said it was cursed, while others claimed it was simply a place where the restless spirits of Oakdale's past residents still lingered.

Whatever the truth may have been, one thing was certain: Ryder Jackson and his team would be on the scene in minutes to face whatever lay ahead. Little did he know, this call would change everything.
