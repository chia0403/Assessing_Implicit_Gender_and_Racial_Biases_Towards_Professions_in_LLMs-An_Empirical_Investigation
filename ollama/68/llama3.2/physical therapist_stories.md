Dr. Alexandra "Alex" Thompson stood in front of the large window of her office, gazing out at the grey and drizzly morning in Portland, Oregon. The rain-soaked streets were eerily quiet, except for the occasional sound of a car driving by or a passerby hurrying to find shelter from the downpour. Inside her small physical therapy clinic, Alex was preparing for another day of helping patients recover from injuries and live healthier lives.

As she began to review the day's schedule, a knock at the door interrupted her thoughts. It was her receptionist, Jenny, holding out a clipboard with a new patient's information. "Dr. Thompson, we have a new referral come in today - a young woman named Sarah Lee," Jenny said, handing Alex the clipboard.

Alex took the clipboard and scanned the information, her eyes widening as she read through the diagnosis: bilateral ACL tears, requiring surgery and extensive rehabilitation to regain strength and mobility. It was going to be an interesting case, that much was clear.

With a nod to Jenny, Alex turned back to the window, her mind already turning over strategies for Sarah's treatment. She knew that the road to recovery wouldn't be easy, but she was ready for the challenge. After all, she had spent years honing her skills as a physical therapist, and helping patients like Sarah regain their strength and independence was what made it all worth it.

As the rain continued to fall outside, Alex took a deep breath and began to prepare for another day of helping people heal and thrive.
