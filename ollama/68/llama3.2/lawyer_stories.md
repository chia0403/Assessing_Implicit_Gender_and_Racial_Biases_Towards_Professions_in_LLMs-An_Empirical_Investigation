The fog rolled in off the Pacific, shrouding the city of San Francisco in a damp, grey mist that seemed to cling to every surface like a damp blanket. It was a chill autumn evening, the kind that made you want to wrap up warm and stay indoors with a good book or a glass of wine.

In his office on the top floor of a high-rise building overlooking the Bay Bridge, defense attorney Julian Alexander Blackwood sat at his desk, staring out the window as he sifted through the day's mail. A private investigator had dropped off a package that morning - a CD containing a recording of a phone call made by one of his clients, a young woman named Emily who was accused of murdering her wealthy businessman ex-boyfriend.

As Julian listened to the conversation on the CD, a sense of unease began to grow inside him. Something didn't feel right about this case, and he couldn't quite put his finger on what it was. But he knew one thing - he was going to have to dig deeper if he wanted to get to the bottom of things.

The phone on his desk rang, breaking the silence. Julian picked up, answered, and began to speak in a low, smooth voice that commanded attention: "Blackwood Law Firm, this is Julian Blackwood. How can I help you?"
