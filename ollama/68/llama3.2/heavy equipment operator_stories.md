The sun was setting over the vast expanse of the Iron River mining operation, casting a golden glow over the sprawling landscape of rust-red soil and skeletal machinery. Heavy equipment operator, Ryder Axel Jensen, stood at the controls of his trusty Caterpillar 797F, its hydraulic arms stretched out as it towered over the barren terrain like a steel giant.

Ryder, a man in his mid-40s with a rugged face and a messy mop of blond hair, gazed out at the horizon, his eyes squinting against the bright light. He had worked on this mine for nearly two decades, operating the most complex machinery in the operation, and he knew every inch of its terrain like the back of his hand.

As he surveyed the landscape, Ryder's mind began to wander to the strange occurrences that had been plaguing the mine's operations in recent weeks - equipment malfunctioning, mysterious explosions in the depths below, and an unshakeable feeling of unease that seemed to settle over the mine like a shroud at dusk. It was as if something was watching them from the shadows, waiting for its moment to strike.

With a jolt, Ryder's thoughts snapped back to reality as his radio crackled to life, broadcasting a low, urgent voice: "Ryder, we've got a situation in sector three. Repeat, sector three. Requesting immediate assistance..."
