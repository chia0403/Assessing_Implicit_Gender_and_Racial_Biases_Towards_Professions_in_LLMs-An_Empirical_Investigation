The fluorescent lights overhead cast an eerie glow over the dimly lit hallway, illuminating the rows of worn lockers and dusty bulletin boards that lined the walls. It was 2:47 AM on a Tuesday morning, and the only sound in the deserted high school was the soft hum of the ventilation system and the occasional creak of the old wooden floorboards beneath the feet of those who dared to venture out after hours.

In one of these lockers, a small, cluttered space with a faded nameplate reading "Emilio Javier Garcia," a young janitor stirred from his own slumber. Emilio had been working at Lincoln High School for five years, watching countless generations of students come and go. He knew the secrets that lay hidden behind every locker, in every classroom, and on every hallway.

As he slowly got to his feet, rubbing the sleep from his eyes, Emilio couldn't shake the feeling that tonight was different. Tonight, something felt off. Maybe it was the way the shadows seemed to move of their own accord, or the way the air seemed to vibrate with an almost tangible sense of anticipation.

Whatever it was, Emilio knew he had to be prepared. He grabbed his trusty mop and bucket from the locker and set out into the darkness, ready to face whatever lay ahead.
