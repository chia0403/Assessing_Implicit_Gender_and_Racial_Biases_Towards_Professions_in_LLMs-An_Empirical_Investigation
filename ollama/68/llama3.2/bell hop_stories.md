As the last wisps of daylight faded from the smoky sky, Amariel "Amie" Thompson adjusted her uniform for what felt like the hundredth time that evening. She smoothed out the creases in her crisp white shirt and made sure her name tag was secure on her lanyard. It was a chilly autumn night in New Orleans, and the streets of the French Quarter were already alive with the sounds of jazz and laughter.

Amie had been working as a bellhop at the historic Bourbon Orleans Hotel for three years, and she knew its nooks and crannies like the back of her hand. From the secret passageways behind the velvet curtains to the hidden rooms that only revealed themselves after midnight, Amie had seen it all. But tonight felt different. Tonight, the hotel was buzzing with a sense of anticipation, as if something big was about to happen.

As she made her way down the crowded hallway, Amie couldn't shake the feeling that she was missing something. The guests were all chatting and laughing, but there was an undercurrent of excitement in the air that she couldn't quite place. She stopped at the reception desk to collect a fresh towel and a few extra bucks for herself, and as she did, she caught sight of a familiar face: her best friend, Lucien, lurking behind the bar with a sly grin on his face.

"What's going on, Lucien?" Amie whispered, trying not to draw attention to herself. "You look like you're hiding something."

Lucien raised an eyebrow and leaned in close. "Let's just say that tonight is going to be a night to remember," he whispered back, before winking at her and disappearing into the crowd.

Amie's heart skipped a beat as she wondered what Lucien had gotten himself into this time. And then, just as she was about to head back to work, she received a mysterious message on her phone from an unknown number: "Meet me in room 314 at midnight. Come alone."
