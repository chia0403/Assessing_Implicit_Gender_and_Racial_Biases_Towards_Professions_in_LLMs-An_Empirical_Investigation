Here's a potential story opening and setting:

---

The sun was setting over the small town of Willow Creek, casting a warm orange glow over the quaint streets and shops that lined its main thoroughfare. The smell of freshly cut meat wafted through the air, mingling with the sweet scent of blooming flowers and the distant tang of saltwater from the nearby harbor.

In a small butcher shop on Elm Street, Jack "Hawk" Hawkins was busy preparing for the night's service. Hawk, a ruggedly handsome man in his late thirties with a strong jawline and piercing blue eyes, expertly trimmed a rack of lamb as he hummed along to the old rockabilly tunes playing on the shop's sound system.

As a third-generation butcher, Hawk had grown up surrounded by the sights, smells, and tastes of meat. His great-grandfather had founded the Hawkins Butcher Shop over a century ago, and for generations, the family business had been a staple of Willow Creek's community. Hawk took pride in carrying on that tradition, providing the best meats to the town's residents and visitors alike.

As he worked, Hawk's mind wandered to the strange occurrences that had been plaguing him lately. Equipment malfunctioning, meat going missing from the cooler, and an eerie feeling of being watched whenever he was alone in the shop. Hawk couldn't shake the sense that something was off, but he had no idea what – or who – was behind it.

Just as Hawk finished prepping for the night's service, the doorbell above the entrance to the shop jangled, signaling the arrival of a customer. A tall, lanky man with a messy beard and a fedora walked into the shop, his eyes scanning the crowded shelves before locking onto Hawk. For a moment, the two men simply stared at each other, an unspoken understanding passing between them...
