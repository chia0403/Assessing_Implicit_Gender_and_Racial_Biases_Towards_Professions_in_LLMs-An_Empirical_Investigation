The sun had just begun to set over the small town of Willow Creek, casting a warm orange glow over the dusty streets and worn buildings. In a small workshop nestled between a vintage clothing store and a used bookstore, 27-year-old Everett "Ev" Thompson sat hunched over his drafting table, surrounded by scattered papers, pencils, and the faint scent of machine oil.

As a skilled drafting worker at the local architectural firm, Ev had spent countless hours pouring over blueprints, sketching out designs for new buildings and renovations. But tonight was different. Tonight, he was working on a special project - a restoration of the old Willow Creek Town Hall, a historic building that had been abandoned for decades.

Ev's fingers moved deftly over the drafting software on his computer screen, his eyes scanning the intricate details of the original 1920s design. He had always loved the old building, with its grand staircase and soaring ceilings, and he was determined to bring it back to its former glory. Little did he know, however, that his work would soon become a lot more complicated than just drafting blueprints...
