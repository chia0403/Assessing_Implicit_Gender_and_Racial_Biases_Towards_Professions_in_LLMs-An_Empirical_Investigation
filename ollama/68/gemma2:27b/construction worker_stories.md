The sun beat down on Elias "Eli" Thorne's neck, making the sweat beading there feel like molten lead. The air hung thick and heavy with the smell of dust, diesel, and ozone from the distant power lines. Eli wiped his brow with a calloused hand, leaving a streak of grime across his forehead.  He squinted at the towering steel frame rising above him, a skeletal monument to progress in this forgotten corner of the city. The new stadium, they called it. A beacon of hope for a struggling neighborhood. 

Eli wasn't so sure. He'd seen enough "beacons" come and go, leaving behind nothing but empty promises and shattered dreams. Still, there was something about the sheer scale of this project that held him captive. It was like building a giant metal birdcage, one bolt, one beam at a time. He wondered what creatures they planned to put inside.
