The rusty propeller coughed and sputtered, protesting against another spin into the unforgiving sky. Amelia "Ace" Lawson tightened her grip on the yoke, her knuckles white against the worn leather. Below her, the Alaskan wilderness stretched out in a tapestry of emerald spruce and sapphire lakes. The air was crisp, carrying the scent of pine needles and the distant whisper of glaciers calving. 

She'd been flying these remote routes for years, delivering supplies to isolated lodges and research stations nestled deep within the Wrangell-St. Elias National Park. This particular day, however, felt different. A gnawing unease coiled in her stomach, a sense that something was amiss. The radio crackled with static, offering no comfort. Ace scanned the horizon, searching for any sign of trouble, but all she saw were towering peaks and the swirling mists clinging to their slopes.
