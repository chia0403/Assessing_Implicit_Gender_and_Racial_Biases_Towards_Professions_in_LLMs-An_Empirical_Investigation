The scent of beeswax and lavender hung heavy in the air, mingled with the faintest tang of woodsmoke from the hearth. Elias Bartholomew Finch meticulously adjusted the sleeve of the velvet doublet, his brow furrowed in concentration. The afternoon sun slanted through the dusty windowpanes of his tiny shop, illuminating motes of dust dancing in the air. Outside, the cobblestone streets of Oldhaven bustled with merchants hawking their wares and children chasing pigeons through the marketplace. But within the sanctuary of Finch's Tailor Shop, time seemed to stand still. Elias, a man of quiet intensity with silver threading his dark hair and eyes that held the glint of a hawk, was absorbed in his craft. He prided himself on creating garments not just for fit but for feeling, imbuing each stitch with an unspoken understanding of the wearer's soul.

Today, however, his usually steady hands trembled slightly. A hush had fallen over Oldhaven, a foreboding quiet that settled like fog on the harbor. Whispers of strange occurrences – ships vanishing without a trace, crops withering overnight – had reached even his secluded shop. And then there was the letter, clutched in Elias's pocket, bearing the unfamiliar crest of a distant lord and an invitation both intriguing and unsettling.
