The city lights outside blurred into an impressionist smear of yellow and red, mirroring the chaotic jumble of half-finished projects scattered across her desk. Empty takeout containers teetered precariously beside a lukewarm mug of coffee, bearing silent witness to another late night fueled by caffeine and creative desperation.

Elsie, with her unruly auburn curls escaping from their messy bun and paint splattered jeans, was staring intently at the screen, her brow furrowed in concentration. The deadline for the new artisanal pickle company logo loomed ominously, and inspiration, like a fickle muse, had deserted her.
