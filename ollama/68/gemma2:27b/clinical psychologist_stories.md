Rain lashed against the grimy windowpane, blurring the neon glow of the Chinese restaurant across the street. Dr. Eleanor Vance, her forehead creased in concentration, tapped a pen rhythmically against the mahogany desk in her dimly lit office.  

The scent of old books and stale coffee hung heavy in the air, a comforting familiarity that usually soothed Eleanor's nerves. Tonight, however, even the familiar aroma couldn't penetrate the knot of anxiety twisting in her gut. The file on her desk – the one marked "Subject X" in bold, black letters – seemed to throb with an unspoken menace. 

Eleanor, a seasoned clinical psychologist known for her unflappable demeanor and sharp intellect, was facing a case unlike any she'd encountered before. Subject X, her latest referral, claimed to possess uncanny precognitive abilities. Claims that were backed by chillingly accurate predictions documented in meticulously detailed journals.
