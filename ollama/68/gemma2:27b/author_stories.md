Rain lashed against the attic window, mimicking the staccato rhythm of Eleanor Vance's fingers tapping on her ancient typewriter. Dust motes danced in the single beam of light emanating from the bare bulb hanging precariously from the ceiling. Outside, the wind howled like a banshee, tearing at the ivy clinging to the crumbling stone facade of the Victorian manor. Inside, Eleanor was wrestling with the final chapter of her latest gothic thriller, "Shadows Over Blackwood Manor."

The air in the attic was thick with the scent of damp wood and old paperbacks, each one whispering forgotten stories.  Eleanor leaned back in her creaky chair, rubbing her tired eyes. The deadline loomed large, a suffocating presence in the otherwise silent room.
