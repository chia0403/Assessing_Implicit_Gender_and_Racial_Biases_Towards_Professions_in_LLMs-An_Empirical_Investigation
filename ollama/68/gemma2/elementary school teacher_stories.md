The scent of stale crayons and impending naptime hung heavy in Ms. Eleanor Beatrice Finch's second-grade classroom. Sunlight slanted through the dusty windows, illuminating motes of dust dancing over rows of mismatched desks. A crumpled drawing of a purple unicorn with five legs lay abandoned on her desk, next to a half-eaten granola bar that seemed to be attracting an unwelcome swarm of ants. Eleanor sighed, pushing the unruly auburn curls away from her face.  

It was just another Tuesday at Sunnyside Elementary, a school nestled in the sprawling suburbs of Maplewood, where the grass was always greener and the problems were often more predictable than a storybook plot. But today, something felt different. A prickling sensation crawled up Eleanor's spine, whispering that this wouldn't be another ordinary day.
