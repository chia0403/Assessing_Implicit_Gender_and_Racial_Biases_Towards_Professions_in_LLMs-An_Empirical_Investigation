The fluorescent lights hummed above Dr. Amelia Beaumont's head like agitated wasps, their sickly yellow glow casting harsh shadows across her cramped office at Northwood High. Outside, the November air was biting, sending shivers down her spine even through the thick wool of her cardigan. A thin layer of frost clung to the windows, blurring the view of the parking lot where teenagers shuffled about like disaffected flocks of birds, their faces a tapestry of boredom and angst.  

Amelia sighed, pushing a stray strand of auburn hair behind her ear. Another day, another deluge of anxieties, insecurities, and teenage woes. This time, it was Jason Miller, a sophomore notorious for his temper tantrums and defiant glares, who sat opposite her, fiddling with the loose thread on his hoodie. 


"Jason," Amelia began, her voice gentle but firm, "you said you wanted to talk about your nightmares." She reached for a notepad, its surface already speckled with scribbled notes from previous sessions.
