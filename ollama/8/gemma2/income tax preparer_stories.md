The scent of stale coffee and desperation hung thick in the air of Mildred Penelope Butterfield's tiny office. Papers overflowed from file cabinets, threatening to spill onto the worn linoleum floor. Outside, the February wind howled like a banshee, rattling the single windowpane above Mildred's desk.  She sighed, pushing a stray curl behind her ear as she surveyed the battlefield of receipts and W-2s before her. 

It was tax season, and for Mildred, it meant weeks spent wrestling with numbers, deciphering cryptic forms, and soothing the anxieties of clients who seemed to find even the simplest tax question terrifying. This year, however, felt different.  A tremor of something akin to dread pulsed through her. It wasn't just the usual pre-deadline pressure; there was a weight in the air, an unsettling feeling that this season held more than its fair share of surprises.


And Mildred, with her trusty calculator and encyclopedic knowledge of the tax code, was determined to face them head-on.
