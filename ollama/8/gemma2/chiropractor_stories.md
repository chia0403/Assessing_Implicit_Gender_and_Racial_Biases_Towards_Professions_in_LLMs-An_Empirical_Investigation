The air hung thick with the scent of eucalyptus and peppermint as Dr. Amelia Rose Hawthorne adjusted the final lever on her adjusting table. Sunlight streamed through the large window in her quaint office, illuminating dust motes dancing in the warm air. Outside, the rhythmic hum of cicadas filled the summer afternoon in Charleston, South Carolina. 

Amelia loved her work. There was a tangible satisfaction in helping people find relief from their pain, a shared sense of accomplishment as they walked out of her office with newfound mobility and a brighter smile. But lately, Amelia had been feeling a tightness in her own shoulders, a dull ache that mirrored the anxieties creeping into her life. 


She glanced at the appointment schedule on her desk: Mr. Henderson, the retired librarian complaining of chronic neck pain; Mrs. Evans, a young musician battling lower back spasms from over-practicing; and then... an anonymous client requesting an initial consultation. Amelia frowned, wondering who this mysterious individual could be.
