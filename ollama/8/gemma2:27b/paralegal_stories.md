Rain lashed against the grimy windows of the law office, blurring the neon glow of the "Sullivan & Sons" sign hanging precariously above the doorway.  Inside, Amelia Beatrice Finch hunched over a mountain of legal documents, her brow furrowed in concentration. The scent of stale coffee and dusty files permeated the air, a familiar aroma that clung to her like a second skin after years working as a paralegal for the firm.

Tonight was different. Tonight, the usual humdrum of contract reviews and discovery requests was punctuated by an undercurrent of anxiety. Amelia had stumbled upon something unsettling while preparing for Mr. Sullivan's upcoming case: a discrepancy in financial records that hinted at something far more sinister than simple negligence. 


What should Amelia do? Tell her boss, risking her job and possibly his reputation? Or stay silent, burying the truth beneath another pile of paperwork? The decision weighed heavily on her conscience as the storm raged outside, mirroring the tempest brewing within.
