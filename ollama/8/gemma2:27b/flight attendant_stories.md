Rain lashed against the windows of the Boeing 747, blurring the neon cityscape beneath them into a watercolour smudge.  Eleanor Vance smoothed down her crisp blue uniform, trying to ignore the gnawing anxiety twisting in her stomach. This was her first transatlantic flight as lead attendant, and the turbulence wasn't helping matters.

The cabin lights flickered ominously, mimicking the storm raging outside. Passengers shifted nervously in their seats, their hushed whispers adding to Eleanor's unease.  She gripped the trolley tighter, willing herself to project an air of calm confidence.  

"This is just a bit of chop," she reminded herself, forcing a smile as a young girl approached, her eyes wide with fear. "Nothing to worry about."
