Rain lashed against the plate glass windows of the Blackwood Institute, obscuring the city lights beyond in a shimmering haze. The rhythmic drumming was the only sound that dared pierce the sterile quiet of the reception area. Eleanor Vance sat ramrod straight behind her polished mahogany desk, fingers poised over the keyboard but not typing.

The fluorescent lights hummed overhead, casting an unforgiving glare on the gleaming surfaces and stark white walls. A lone potted palm in the corner struggled valiantly against the oppressive atmosphere, its fronds drooping slightly under the weight of unseen burdens. Eleanor knew exactly how it felt.
