Rain hammered against the arched windows of Blackwood & Sons Solicitors, each drop exploding in a miniature firework display on the cobblestones below. Eleanor Beatrice Finch, hunched over her Olivetti typewriter, barely noticed the symphony of the storm raging outside. Her focus was laser-sharp on the document before her – a dense contract riddled with legal jargon that made her head spin. 

Eleanor wasn't born for this world of dusty tomes and starched collars. She yearned for adventure, for stories whispered in smoky taverns and journeys across uncharted lands. Yet here she was, trapped in a mahogany-panelled cage, surrounded by the scent of old paper and Mr. Blackwood's ever-present pipe tobacco.

But today, something felt different. A shiver ran down her spine as a particularly loud clap of thunder reverberated through the office. It wasn't just fear; it was anticipation, a tingling sense that something momentous was about to unfold. And then, the door creaked open, revealing a figure shrouded in rain and shadow…
