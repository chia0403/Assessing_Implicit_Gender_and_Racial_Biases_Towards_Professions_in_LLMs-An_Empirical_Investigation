The sky was a deep shade of indigo, with only a few wispy clouds scattered across it like cotton candy tufts. Captain Elianore "Eli" Victoria Winston stood at the helm of her trusty Cessna 172, scanning the horizon for any signs of life or danger. She had been flying solo for hours, the silence of the night broken only by the occasional chirp of a cricket or the soft hum of the engine.

As she banked and turned, the tiny town of Red Rock sprawled out before her like a patchwork quilt, its streets and buildings twinkling with lights that seemed to wink in time with the stars above. Eli felt a sense of peace wash over her, the thrill of flight coursing through her veins like adrenaline.

She had been flying for years, ever since she was a teenager, but this particular mission felt different. She had received a cryptic message from an old friend, a fellow pilot who had gone missing on a solo flight several months ago. The message had read: "Meet me at Red Rock. Come alone."

Eli's gut told her that something was off, but she couldn't shake the feeling that this might be the break she needed to finally solve the mystery of her friend's disappearance.

As she swooped down towards the runway, Eli's mind began to spin with possibilities and worst-case scenarios. She knew one thing for sure: she would get answers tonight, no matter what it took.
