Detective Emilia Rose "Mia" Thompson stepped out of the rain-soaked streets of downtown Seattle and into the warm, comforting glow of the precinct's break room. The neon lights of the city reflected off the wet pavement outside, casting a kaleidoscope of colors across the drab concrete. She shook the water from her coat, sending droplets flying in all directions as she made her way to the coffee machine.

It had been a long night - 12 hours on patrol, with only one decent cup of coffee to show for it. The rain had started around midnight, and now it was past dawn. Mia could feel the weight of the city's problems bearing down on her, the endless stream of calls and complaints that seemed to never end.

She poured herself a steaming cup of coffee and took a sip, feeling the bitter taste wash over her. As she stood there, sipping her coffee and watching the rain continue to fall outside, she couldn't shake the feeling that something was off. The city had a way of making you feel like everyone had a secret, like there was always something lurking just beneath the surface.

And then, just as she was starting to settle in for another long day on the job, her phone buzzed with an incoming call from dispatch. "Detective Thompson, we've got a 10-13 at the old warehouse on 5th and Main," the dispatcher's voice crackled over the line. "Reports of a body found inside."
