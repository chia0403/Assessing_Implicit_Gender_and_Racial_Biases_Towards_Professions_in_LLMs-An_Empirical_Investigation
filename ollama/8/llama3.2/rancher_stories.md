The sun was setting over the rolling hills of Red Rock Ranch, casting a warm orange glow over the vast expanse of open range. August "Gus" Ryder, a rugged and weathered rancher in his late 40s, stood at the edge of the corral, watching as the cattle began to stir for the night. The air was thick with the scent of sagebrush and the distant rumble of thunder echoed through the canyon walls.

Gus had lived on Red Rock Ranch all his life, taking over the family business from his father after high school. He knew every inch of this land, every rocky outcropping, every hidden spring, and every stretch of grass that would make a cow happy. But as he gazed out at the ranch, he couldn't shake the feeling that something was off. The weather had been strange all summer, with droughts and heatwaves that seemed to go on forever. And then there were the rumors.

Rumors that had started circulating among the other ranchers in town about a mysterious stranger who had been spotted lurking around the outskirts of Red Rock Ranch at night. Some said they'd seen a figure in black, others claimed it was a woman with long, dark hair. But one thing was for sure: Gus didn't like strangers on his land.

As he turned to head back to the house, Gus's boots crunched on the gravel path, sending up a cloud of dust that obscured his view for a moment. When the fog cleared, he saw something that made his heart skip a beat: a pair of horse shoes, half-buried in the dirt, with a piece of paper attached to one of the straps.

Gus's mind was racing as he picked up the paper and examined it. It was a note, scribbled in hasty handwriting:

"Meet me at the old windmill at midnight. Come alone."

Gus's gut told him this was trouble, but his curiosity was piqued. Who was behind this mysterious message, and what did they want?
