The fluorescent lights above the hospital ward flickered, casting an eerie glow over the rows of narrow beds and wheeled chairs. It was just another typical Monday morning at Mercy General Hospital, where Emily Jeanette Wilson had spent countless hours as a phlebotomist.

Emily stood outside Room 314, adjusting her scrubs for what felt like the hundredth time that day. Her eyes scanned the door, searching for any sign of life within. The patient's vitals were stable, but Emily knew better than to let her guard down when it came to her job. One wrong move could make all the difference between a successful draw and a potentially catastrophic mistake.

As she took a deep breath and pushed open the door, a faint scent of disinfectant wafted out, mingling with the sterile hum of the hospital's machinery. Emily smiled wryly to herself – it was going to be one of those days. She slipped on her gloves and got to work, her skilled hands moving with precision as she prepared for the day's first blood draw.

With over five years of experience under her belt, Emily had seen it all – from anxious new patients to the occasional difficult needle stick. But there was something about today that felt off, a nagging sense of unease that lingered just beneath the surface of her usually calm and collected demeanor. Little did she know, this particular day would be anything but ordinary...
