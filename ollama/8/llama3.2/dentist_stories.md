Dr. Elara Victoria Wellesley stood at the edge of the old wooden pier, her eyes fixed on the vast expanse of Lake Superior that stretched out before her. A gentle breeze carried the scent of pine and damp earth through the air, mingling with the faint smell of fish from the nearby lake.

As she gazed out at the lake, Elara's mind wandered back to the small town that had been her home for over a decade. She had left behind the hustle and bustle of city life as a dentist in Chicago to return to her roots in Willow Creek, Minnesota, where her family had lived for generations. The tranquil atmosphere and friendly faces of the townspeople had welcomed her back with open arms, but Elara couldn't shake the feeling that something was off.

She took a deep breath, the cool evening air filling her lungs, and began her walk along the pier. The wooden planks creaked beneath her feet as she made her way to the end of the pier, where a small fishing boat bobbed gently in the water. A faded sign above the boat read "Moonlit Serenade," and Elara's thoughts were suddenly interrupted by the sound of the boat's engine roaring to life.

A figure emerged from the shadows, a tall, dark-haired man with piercing blue eyes that seemed to see right through her. He smiled as he approached her, and Elara felt a spark of recognition that she couldn't quite explain.

"Dr. Wellesley," he said, his voice low and smooth. "I've been expecting you."
