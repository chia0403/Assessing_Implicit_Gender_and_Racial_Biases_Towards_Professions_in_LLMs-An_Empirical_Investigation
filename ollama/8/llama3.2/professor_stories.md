The sun had long since set on the bustling university town of Ashwood, casting a warm orange glow over the quaint streets and buildings. Professor Elara Asteria Wynter, a renowned expert in ancient languages and mythology, sat at her cluttered desk, surrounded by stacks of dusty tomes and scattered notes. The soft hum of the fluorescent lights overhead cast an eerie glow on the walls, illuminating the faint scent of old paper and forgotten knowledge.

As she poured over the latest translation of the Epic of Gilgamesh, a sense of unease settled in the pit of her stomach. It had been three days since she'd received the cryptic message from her colleague, Dr. Henry Lee, warning her about a possible discovery that threatened to upend everything she thought she knew about the ancient world.

Elara's eyes narrowed as she read over the passage again, her mind racing with possibilities. She was one of the few experts in the field who had dedicated their life to studying the forgotten languages and texts of ancient civilizations. And now, it seemed that her entire career might be on the verge of being upended by some unknown revelation.

As she sat at her desk, lost in thought, Elara couldn't shake the feeling that she was being watched. The silence of the late-night office was oppressive, and she knew she wasn't alone. Suddenly, a faint creaking sound echoed through the room, making her jump in her seat. It sounded like someone - or something - was moving around outside her door...
