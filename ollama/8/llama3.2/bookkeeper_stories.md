The rain poured down on the narrow streets of Ashwood, casting a misty veil over the town's quaint shops and homes. Inside the small office above the used bookstore, Emily Josephine Wellesley hunched over her desk, surrounded by stacks of financial ledgers and scattered papers. As the town's resident bookkeeper, she was no stranger to late nights and endless cups of coffee.

At 32, Emily had traded in her dreams of becoming a writer for the security of a steady income and the satisfaction of keeping Ashwood's finances running smoothly. Her clients were mostly small business owners and individuals who trusted her with their financial secrets. She took pride in her work, meticulously balancing books and identifying areas where her clients could cut costs.

As she worked, Emily's mind began to wander to the upcoming town council meeting, where a contentious proposal to raise taxes on local businesses was set to be debated. The fate of Ashwood's small shops hung in the balance, and Emily couldn't shake the feeling that something wasn't quite right. Little did she know, her next few days would be about to bring her a whole lot more than just financial ledgers.
