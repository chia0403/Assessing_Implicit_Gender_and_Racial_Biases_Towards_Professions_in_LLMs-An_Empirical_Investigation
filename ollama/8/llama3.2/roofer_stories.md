The sun was setting over the small town of Willow Creek, casting a warm orange glow over the quaint streets and homes. The scent of fresh-cut grass and wood shavings wafted through the air as he expertly stitched together the torn shingles with practiced ease.

As a roofer for over a decade, Jack had seen it all - from torrential downpours to scorching heatwaves. But there was something about this particular house that caught his eye. Maybe it was the faded Victorian charm or the hint of secrets hidden behind its weathered facade. Whatever the reason, Jack felt an inexplicable pull towards 345 Elm Street.

Just as he finished securing the last shingle, a faint creaking sound echoed from the attic above. Hawk's eyes narrowed, and his grip on the hammer tightened. Something was amiss. Little did he know, this small-town fixer-upper was about to become a whole lot bigger than just a simple roof repair...
