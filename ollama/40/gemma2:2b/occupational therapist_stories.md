The wind whipped around Amelia's face, carrying the scent of salty sea air and the distant cries of gulls. She stood on the craggy cliff edge overlooking the stormy Atlantic, her worn leather satchel slung over her shoulder. The setting sun cast long shadows across the turbulent waves below. Around her, the rugged Cornish coastline stretched before her, a tapestry woven from windswept cliffs and ancient fishing villages.  

Amelia, or 'Amy' as everyone called her, was an occupational therapist with a heart full of wanderlust and a talent for helping people find their way back to life after injury or loss. This was a corner of the world where she could truly be at home - amongst the craggy beauty and salty air, where the rhythm of nature seemed somehow intertwined with human resilience. Yet today, something felt different.  A knot of unease tightened in her stomach as she scanned the horizon for any sign of trouble. The storm wasn't just an angry wind; it was a palpable force threatening to unleash its wrath upon the coast.
