Rain lashed against the corrugated iron roof of the abandoned factory, each drop a tiny hammer blow against the silence. The air hung thick with the scent of rust and damp earth.  Elias Finch, his wiry frame hunched under a threadbare oilskin jacket, squinted through the gloom at the tangle of exposed wires before him. A single, flickering bulb cast long, skeletal shadows that danced on the concrete floor. 

This wasn't Elias' usual gig - rewiring quaint cottages and fixing faulty ceiling fans in suburban homes. This was something different, something whispered about in hushed tones in backroom dealings. He was here to restore power to a forgotten piece of machinery, a machine rumored to hold secrets older than the city itself.
