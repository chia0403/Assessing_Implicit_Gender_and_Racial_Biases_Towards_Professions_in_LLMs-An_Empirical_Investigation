The air in the attic studio hung thick with the scent of linseed oil and turpentine. Sunlight, fractured by the dusty skylight above, painted shimmering squares across Amelia Blackwood’s cluttered workspace. Brushes lay scattered like fallen soldiers, their bristles stiff with dried paint. Tubes of color, squeezed almost to oblivion, huddled together on a battered palette, their vibrant hues dulled by the years.

Amelia, her greying braid escaping its bun, stood before a canvas propped against an easel that groaned under the weight of her latest project. A cityscape unfolded beneath her brushstrokes – towering buildings etched in charcoal grey against a sky awash with the fiery hues of sunset. But something wasn't right. The energy, the pulse of the city, seemed to be missing. Amelia sighed, stepping back to survey her work with a critical eye.
