The neon sign above the "Lucky Anchor" flickered erratically, casting a sickly green glow across the rain-slicked cobblestones. Inside, Bartholomew "Bart" O'Connell polished a whiskey glass with practiced efficiency, his reflection momentarily distorted by the warped surface. The air hung thick with the scent of stale beer, spilled liquor, and desperation. It was a Tuesday night, which meant the usual crowd: weary dockworkers drowning their sorrows, lovelorn poets seeking inspiration (or oblivion), and the occasional shady character lurking in the shadows.

Bart had seen it all in his fifteen years behind the mahogany bar of the Lucky Anchor. He knew every creak of the floorboards, every crack in the mirror, every whispered secret that lingered in the smoke-filled air. Tonight, however, something felt different. A chill snaked down his spine, a prickle of unease he couldn't quite place.
