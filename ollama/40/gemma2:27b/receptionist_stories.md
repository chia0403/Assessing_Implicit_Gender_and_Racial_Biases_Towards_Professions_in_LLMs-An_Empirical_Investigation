The fluorescent lights hummed above Esmeralda Cartwright's head, their sickly yellow glow casting long shadows across the faux-marble countertop. The air hung thick with the scent of stale coffee and desperation, a pungent perfume unique to the waiting room of "Solutions Unlimited," a debt collection agency tucked away on the third floor of a crumbling office building downtown. 

Esmeralda tapped her perfectly manicured nails against the keyboard, the rhythmic click-clack providing a meagre counterpoint to the symphony of anxiety emanating from the half-dozen clients crammed into the uncomfortable plastic chairs. Their faces were a tapestry of worry and shame, each crease and furrowed brow telling a story of financial hardship Esmeralda had heard countless times before.
