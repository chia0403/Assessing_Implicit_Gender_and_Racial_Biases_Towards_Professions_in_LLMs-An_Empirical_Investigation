It was a drizzly autumn evening in the city of Ashwood, where the rain-soaked streets seemed to whisper secrets to each other as they curled around the old buildings like damp shrouds. Inside his cramped office above the pawnshop on 5th and Main, private detective August "Augie" Thompson hunched over a stack of dusty files and stale coffee, trying to make sense of the latest case that had landed on his doorstep.

The knock at the door was soft, almost hesitant, but Augie's instincts told him it wasn't going to be good news. He wiped the fatigue from his eyes with the back of his hand, revealing a faint smudge of bruise beneath them - a souvenir from the last case that had gone south. As he rose to answer the knock, the sound of rain drumming on the pavement outside seemed to grow louder, like the city itself was holding its breath in anticipation of whatever lay ahead.

Augie's eyes locked onto the figure standing in the doorway, a tall, slender woman with piercing green eyes and raven-black hair that fell in tangled locks down her back. She looked like she'd just stepped out of a nightmare - or a dream, depending on who you asked. Her name was Eve Wilder, and Augie had a feeling he was about to find out why she was standing at his doorstep, looking for help with a case that would test even the most seasoned detective's mettle.
