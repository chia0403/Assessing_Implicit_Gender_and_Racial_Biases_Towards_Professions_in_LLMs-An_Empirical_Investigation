The fog rolled in off the coast, shrouding the small town of Ravenswood in a damp, gray mist that clung to the wooden signs and weathered facades like a damp shroud. In the midst of this eerie atmosphere, Emilia "Mia" Wychwood moved with purpose, her worn leather gloves protecting her hands from the splinters and rough-hewn wood chips that seemed to be everywhere on the job site.

As a skilled carpenter, Mia had spent countless hours in Ravenswood's cramped workshops and lumber yards, building everything from cozy cabins for the townspeople to sturdy furniture for the local inn. But today was different. Today, she had been hired by the town's wealthy benefactor, Mr. Harrington, to work on a mysterious project that he refused to discuss.

As Mia lugged her toolbox up the creaky stairs of the old warehouse where she would be meeting with Mr. Harrington, the fog seemed to grow thicker, until it felt like she was wading through a damp, cold sea. The air inside the warehouse was stale and musty, but Mia's trained nose picked up on something else - a scent that reminded her of old books and forgotten memories.

She pushed open the heavy wooden door and stepped inside, her eyes adjusting to the dim light within. Mr. Harrington sat behind a large oak desk, his eyes fixed intently on her as he gestured for her to come closer...
