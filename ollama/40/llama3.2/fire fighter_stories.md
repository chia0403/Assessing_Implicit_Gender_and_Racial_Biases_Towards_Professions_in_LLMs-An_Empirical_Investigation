---

The rain lashed down on the city like a thousand tiny drummers, casting a rhythmic gloom over the streets of Ashwood. Inside Fire Station 17, Captain Ryder "Rye" Thompson sat at his desk, sipping cold coffee and staring out the window at the stormy night. A rookie firefighter with a passion for puzzles and codes, Rye had always felt like there was something missing in his life - like he was meant for more than just fighting fires.

As he gazed out into the darkness, Rye's mind began to wander back to the cryptic message he'd received earlier that day. It was an anonymous tip, delivered by a nervous voice on the phone: "Meet me at the old clock tower at midnight. Come alone." The words had sent a shiver down Rye's spine - who could be behind such a mysterious summons? And what did they want with him?

Just as Rye was starting to shake off the feeling, his radio crackled to life. "All units, we've got a 10-13 at 345 Elm Street. Reports of a possible warehouse fire."

Rye's eyes snapped back to attention as he grabbed his gear and headed for the door. Time to put his skills to the test - but little did he know, this night was about to get a whole lot more complicated.

---

In this opening, we meet Captain Ryder "Rye" Thompson, a firefighter with a passion for puzzles and codes who's starting to feel like there's something missing in his life. The mysterious message has piqued his interest, but he's not sure what it means or who sent it. As the night wears on and a fire breaks out at a warehouse, Rye is thrust back into action - but he can't shake the feeling that this night is going to be more than just another routine call.
