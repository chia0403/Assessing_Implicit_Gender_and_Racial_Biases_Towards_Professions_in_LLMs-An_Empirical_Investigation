The sun was setting over the small town of Willow Creek, casting a warm orange glow over the dusty streets and rundown buildings. The smell of freshly cut grass wafted through the air, mingling with the acrid tang of sawdust and gasoline from the construction site on Main Street.

On this particular evening, Jack "Hawk" Hawkins was walking home from his shift at the site, his boots scuffling against the pavement as he laced up his baseball cap. A rugged man in his mid-30s, with a strong jawline and a mop of dark brown hair that always seemed to be tousled, Hawk had been working on the town's infrastructure for over a decade. He knew every corner, every crevice, and every quirk of Willow Creek like the back of his hand.

As he strolled down Main Street, Hawk couldn't shake the feeling that something was off. The construction site, which had been going full throttle for months, seemed quieter than usual. The workers were all gone, leaving behind a sea of half-finished buildings and idle equipment. And the paychecks from the town council hadn't arrived in weeks.

Hawk's thoughts were interrupted by the sound of his phone buzzing in his pocket. He pulled it out to find a text message from his best friend, Vinnie: "Meet me at McGillicuddy's bar at 6. We need to talk."
