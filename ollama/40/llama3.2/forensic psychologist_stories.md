Dr. Alexandra "Lexi" Thompson stepped out of her car and onto the cracked asphalt of the abandoned parking lot, her eyes scanning the desolate landscape as if searching for clues. The fluorescent lights of the nearby highway cast an eerie glow over the scene before her, illuminating the discarded fast food wrappers, shattered glass, and torn pages of a newspaper that littered the ground.

It was late Friday afternoon, and the sun had already begun its slow descent into the horizon, casting long shadows across the deserted streets. The city's nightlife was just starting to stir, but out here, Lexi felt like she was one of the few souls who knew the real story. As a forensic psychologist with the local police department, she'd seen her fair share of crimes and tragedies, but this case had something about it that didn't add up.

Lexi took a deep breath, shouldering her backpack, which contained all her essentials for the long hours ahead: notes, photographs, and whatever other tools might help her unravel the tangled threads of human psychology. She'd been summoned to the scene by Detective James "Jim" Rodriguez, who'd called in the favor, having worked with Lexi on several cases before.

The sound of a car engine rumbled to life behind her, and Lexi turned to see Jim's patrol car pulling into the parking lot, kicking up clouds of dust that danced in the fading light. As she watched, he leapt out, his eyes scanning the scene before him with an intensity that belied his rugged exterior.

"Lexi!" he called, jogging towards her with a frown etched across his face. "I'm glad you're here. We got a real mess on our hands."
