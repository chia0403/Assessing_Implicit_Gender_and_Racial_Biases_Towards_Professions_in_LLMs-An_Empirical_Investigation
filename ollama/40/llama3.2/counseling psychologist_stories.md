Dr. Sofia Elena Vasquez stood at the edge of the Grand Canyon, the wind whipping her dark hair into a frenzy as she gazed out at the vast expanse of rust-red rock stretching out before her. The sun was setting, casting a golden glow over the landscape and illuminating the tiny figures of hikers and tourists making their way along the rim trail.

Sofia, a counseling psychologist with a specialization in trauma and PTSD, had come to this sacred place to find solace and clarity after a particularly difficult case. She had spent years working with veterans struggling to cope with the horrors they'd witnessed on the battlefield, but lately, she'd been feeling burned out and unsure if she was making a difference.

As she breathed in the cool desert air, Sofia felt a sense of restlessness wash over her. She needed to get back to work, to start seeing clients again and helping them heal from their own wounds. But as she turned to leave, she noticed something strange - a figure standing just off the trail, watching her with an intensity that made her skin prickle.

The figure was tall and lean, dressed in worn jeans and a faded leather jacket. Sofia couldn't make out their face, but there was something about them that seemed...familiar. She felt a shiver run down her spine as she wondered who this stranger might be, and what they wanted from her.
