It was a drizzly autumn evening in late October, and the streets of downtown Oakdale were shrouded in a misty veil. The leaves that had once painted the sidewalks with vibrant hues of orange, red, and yellow had long since fallen, leaving behind a dull gray carpet that seemed to suck the color out of everything.

In his small office above the antique shop on Main Street, insurance agent Everett Fletcher sat hunched over his desk, staring at the stack of files in front of him. The fluorescent lights overhead cast an unforgiving glare on the papers, making it seem like the words themselves were burning into his retina. Everett's eyes, a piercing blue that had once seemed so bright and full of life, now looked tired and worn down, as if the weight of the world was bearing down on him.

At 32, Everett had already seen more heartache and loss than most people saw in a lifetime. He had started his career as an insurance agent just out of college, thinking it would be a steady and secure path to success. But over the years, he had watched countless friends and colleagues struggle with claims that seemed impossible to process, or worse, get lost in the labyrinthine world of corporate red tape.

Everett's own life was no exception. Just last week, his mother had passed away, leaving him her small antique shop on Main Street, along with a host of unresolved debts and a deep sense of loss. As he stared at the stack of files in front of him, Everett couldn't help but wonder if this new chapter of his life would be any different than the last.

Little did he know, it wouldn't take long for the door to his office to swing open, and for the universe to throw another curveball in his direction.
