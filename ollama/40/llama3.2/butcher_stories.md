The sun had just set over the quaint town of Ashwood, casting a warm orange glow over the cobblestone streets and ancient buildings. The air was filled with the savory smell of roasting meats and fresh bread wafting from the kitchens of the local taverns. In a small alleyway off Main Street, the sign creaked in the gentle breeze: "Mortimer's Meat Market" - a family-owned butcher shop that had been serving Ashwood for generations.

Inside the shop, Mortimer Finchley was busily preparing for the evening's customer rush. His hands moved with precision and care as he expertly trimmed and wrapped the day's catch: succulent prime cuts of beef, tender lamb shanks, and plump sausages made from his own secret blend of spices. With over 20 years of experience under his belt, Mortimer took pride in providing the finest meats to the people of Ashwood. As he worked, the soft ticking of the grandfather clock in the corner of the shop provided a soothing background rhythm, and the warm light of the setting sun danced across the rows of gleaming cuts of meat.

Just as Mortimer was finishing up the final preparations for the day's customers, the door swung open and a chill ran down his spine. A mysterious figure, shrouded in shadows, stood hesitantly in the entrance, their eyes scanning the shop with an air of curiosity and suspicion. Mortimer's instincts told him that this person was not from around Ashwood - and they were certainly not here to purchase a simple cut of meat...
