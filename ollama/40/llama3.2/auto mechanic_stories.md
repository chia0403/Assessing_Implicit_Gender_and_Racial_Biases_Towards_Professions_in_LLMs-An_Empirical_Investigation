The sun was setting over the small town of Willow Creek, casting a warm orange glow over the crumbling asphalt and rusting hulks that lined Main Street. In a corner lot, nestled between a dilapidated gas station and a faded diner, stood "Mac's Garage," the humble auto repair shop owned by 35-year-old Maxwell "Mac" Thompson.

Mac, a gruff but lovable man with a thick beard and calloused hands, had spent the better part of his life under the hood of a car. Born and raised in Willow Creek, he'd grown up surrounded by the hum of engines and the smell of grease. After high school, Mac had apprenticed at the local garage, working his way up from a lowly apprentice to the shop's lead mechanic.

As the evening drew to a close, Mac wiped the sweat from his brow with a dirty rag and surveyed the empty lot. The neon sign above the diner flickered like a dying heartbeat, casting eerie shadows on the pavement. Suddenly, a rusty old pickup truck rattled into the lot, spewing dust and dirt everywhere. Mac raised an eyebrow as the driver, a scruffy young woman with a faded jacket and a look of determination etched on her face, leapt out of the cab.

"Well, well, well," Mac said, ambivalence dripping from his voice. "What brings you to Willow Creek, sweetheart?"
