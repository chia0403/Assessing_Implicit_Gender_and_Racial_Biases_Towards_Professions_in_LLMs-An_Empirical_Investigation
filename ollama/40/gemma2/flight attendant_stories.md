The humid Singaporean air clung to Amelia Josephine Davies like a second skin as she wheeled her cart down aisle 7B.  She could smell the faint whiff of durian from Economy – never a good sign – mingled with the sterile scent of disinfectant and overpriced perfume that seemed to permeate every inch of the aircraft. It was just after take-off, the initial jolt of leaving the tarmac having settled into a humdrum rhythm of service calls and restless passengers. Amelia tried to focus on her duties: distributing lukewarm water and pretzels, offering earplugs to those already snoring softly, and smoothing down crumpled pillows with practiced ease. But her mind kept wandering back to the email she’d received earlier that day – a cryptic message from an anonymous sender hinting at a secret hidden aboard this very flight.  


This wasn't just another long-haul journey to London; there was something different in the air, a palpable tension beneath the usual hum of travel anxieties. Amelia tightened her grip on the cart handle, her pulse quickening with a mixture of dread and exhilaration. This flight could be ordinary, or it could be anything but. And she was about to find out.
