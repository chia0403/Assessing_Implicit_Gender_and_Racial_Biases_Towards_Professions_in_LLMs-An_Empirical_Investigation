The rain hammered against the grimy windows of Ms. Elara Thorne's office, mimicking the relentless drumbeat in her chest. Outside, Chicago's South Side was a canvas painted in shades of grey, reflecting the gloom that had settled over her like a shroud. Another day, another slew of parolees to track, another mountain of paperwork to climb. The city felt as suffocating as the air inside her cramped cubicle, thick with the scent of stale coffee and despair. 

Elara wasn't naive; she knew the weight of her job. She was tasked with guiding lost souls back into the fold, a shepherd tending to a flock that constantly threatened to stray. But today, even the familiar routine felt heavier than usual. A gnawing unease had taken root in her gut, a premonition whispering of trouble brewing just beyond her window. 


Elara sighed, pushing a strand of unruly auburn hair from her eyes and grabbing the first file on her desk. "Anthony 'Tony' Jones," she muttered, tracing the bold letters with her fingertip. This was going to be an interesting case.
