The air in Amelia Beatrice "Amy" Finch's office was thick with the scent of stale coffee and desperation. Rain lashed against the windows of her small, cluttered Manhattan practice, blurring the already fading light of dusk. A worn legal dictionary lay open on her desk, its spine cracked from years of overuse, a testament to Amy’s relentless pursuit of justice – or at least, an attempt at it. 

Her current client, a jittery young man named Danny, fidgeted nervously in the chair opposite her. He clutched a crumpled handkerchief in his sweaty hand, his eyes darting around the room like trapped birds.  

"So," Amy began, her voice calm and measured, "you say you saw Mr. Henderson leaving the scene?"


Amy pushed a stray strand of auburn hair behind her ear, taking in Danny's pale face and trembling lips. She had dealt with her fair share of nervous clients over the years, but there was something about this young man that set her on edge.  This wasn’t just another petty theft case. This felt different, heavier somehow.
