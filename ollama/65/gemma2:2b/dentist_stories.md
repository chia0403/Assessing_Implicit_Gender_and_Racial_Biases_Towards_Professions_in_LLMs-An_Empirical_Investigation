## The Setting: 

The air hung heavy with the scent of formaldehyde and citrus-fresh hand sanitizer in Dr. Amelia Thorne's office. Sunlight, filtered through stained glass depicting smiling teeth, dappled the white tiles and chipped wood counter where she polished her instruments. It was a Wednesday afternoon, a time when most patients would be leaving after their anxieties had been alleviated by a thorough extraction or filling. But not today. 

Amelia surveyed the waiting room with an unsettling calm. Three chairs sat empty. A lone paperback of 'Pride and Prejudice' lay abandoned on a table next to a half-eaten donut, forgotten in its sugary solitude. Something felt off. 


## The Opening:

"Dr. Thorne?" a nervous voice called from behind the desk, breaking Amelia's contemplation.  A young woman with tear-filled eyes stood hesitantly before her. "I… I think there might be something wrong."

Amelia raised an eyebrow, adjusting her spectacles. "Is that so? Tell me about it, Ms. Hayes," she said, her voice calm and measured, though a flicker of unease crossed her own features. 

Ms. Hayes swallowed hard, unable to meet Amelia's gaze. Her hands trembled as she clutched the crumpled paper in her grip.  “I think… I think my fiancé, he's gone missing."
