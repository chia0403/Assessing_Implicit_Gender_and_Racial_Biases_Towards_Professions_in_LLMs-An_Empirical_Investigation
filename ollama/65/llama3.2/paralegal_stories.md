It was a drizzly autumn evening in late October when Alexandra "Lexi" Thompson stepped out of the worn leather armchair in her small apartment, stretching her long frame and arching her back to work out the kinks from a day spent hunched over her computer. The dim glow of the lamp on her coffee table cast a warm light on the rows of dog-eared law textbooks lining the bookshelves, and the faint scent of yesterday's Chinese takeout still lingered in the air.

Lexi yawned, feeling the fatigue of a long day at work begin to creep in. As a paralegal at the prestigious law firm of Mitchell & Associates, she was no stranger to late nights and endless cups of coffee. But tonight felt different - tonight was the night before the big case against wealthy tech mogul, Marcus Blackwood.

Rumors had been circulating for months about Blackwood's involvement in a string of high-profile data breaches, and Lexi had spent countless hours poring over court documents and witness statements to build her client's case. She had always prided herself on being detail-oriented, but the stakes were higher than ever now, and she couldn't shake the feeling that one tiny mistake would be enough to send everything crashing down.

As she made her way into the kitchen to pour herself a glass of wine, Lexi caught a glimpse of herself in the mirror that hung above the sink. Her dark hair was tied back in a tight ponytail, and her eyes looked tired but determined. She took a deep breath, feeling a surge of adrenaline course through her veins.

It was going to be a long night.
