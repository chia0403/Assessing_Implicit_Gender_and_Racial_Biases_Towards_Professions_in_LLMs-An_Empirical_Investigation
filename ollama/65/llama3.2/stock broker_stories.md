Here's a potential story opening:

The sun had just set over the Manhattan skyline, casting a golden glow over the bustling streets of Lower Manhattan. The sound of car horns and chatter filled the air as people rushed to and from their evening destinations. Amidst the chaos, Alexander Ryder Thompson stood tall, surveying his domain with a practiced eye.

At 32, Alexander was one of the top stock brokers at Smithson & Co., a prestigious investment firm in the heart of Wall Street. His sharp mind and quick wit had earned him a reputation as a master of the markets, and his clients trusted him implicitly to make their money grow.

Alexander's own wealth, however, was not solely based on his professional prowess. He was a member of one of New York City's oldest and most influential families, the Thompsons, whose name was synonymous with power and privilege in the city that never slept.

As he finished a phone call with a potential client, Alexander gazed out at the glittering lights of the financial district, his mind already racing ahead to tomorrow's challenges. Little did he know, however, that the next day would bring an unexpected storm to shake his carefully constructed world...
