Professor Emilia Helena Vasquez stepped out of the misty morning air and into the warm glow of her office, located in the heart of the University of Seattle's English department. As she poured herself a steaming cup of coffee from the thermos on her desk, the sounds of the bustling campus filtered through the windows - students chattering over breakfast at the cafeteria, the clang of pots and pans from the kitchen of the staff room, and the soft hum of printers in the background.

It was going to be a typical day at the university: meetings with departmental colleagues, grading papers, and no doubt endless emails from administrators begging for more research funding. But Emilia's mind was elsewhere. She had been working on her latest book project - a collection of essays on 19th-century American literature - for months now, and she was starting to feel the weight of deadlines looming over her.

As she settled into her chair, running her fingers over the worn leather armrests, Emilia couldn't shake the feeling that something was off. It wasn't just the lingering anxiety from the night before's failed attempt at a breakthrough - though that was certainly a factor. No, it was something more...a nagging sense of unease that had been growing inside her for weeks, like a low-grade fever that refused to break.

She took a sip of coffee, letting the bitter taste fill her mouth as she gazed out the window at the drizzly Seattle sky. The rain-soaked streets were always so gray and melancholy in the morning, but today they seemed particularly somber - as if even the city itself was holding its breath, waiting for something to happen.

And then, just as Emilia was starting to feel like she'd finally settled into her usual routine, a knock at the door shattered the silence.
