The sun was setting over the small town of Willow Creek, casting a warm orange glow over the quaint streets and homes. The smell of freshly cut grass and baking bread wafted through the air, mingling with the sound of children's laughter carried on the breeze.

In a small workshop nestled at the end of Elm Street, Jack "The Roofer" Reynolds was taking a well-deserved break from his latest project. He wiped the sweat from his forehead with a rag and leaned back in his creaky old chair, his eyes scanning the rows of tools and half-finished projects that lined the walls.

Jack had been a roofer for over twenty years, and he knew every inch of this town's rooftops like the back of his hand. He took pride in his work, and the people of Willow Creek knew him as the go-to guy for any roofing job, no matter how big or small.

As he sat there, sipping on a cold beer and watching the stars begin to twinkle overhead, Jack's mind began to wander to the strange letter he had received that morning. It was from an old client, one who had been out of town for years but seemed to have returned to Willow Creek with a proposition that would change everything.

The letter read: "Meet me at the old oak tree in the park at midnight. Come alone."
