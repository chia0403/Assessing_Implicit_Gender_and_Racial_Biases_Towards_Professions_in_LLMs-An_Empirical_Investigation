The acrid smell of burnt wiring hung thick in the air, clinging to Amelia "Amy" Fitzwilliam's overalls like a second skin.  Fluorescent lights flickered erratically above her head, casting grotesque shadows that danced across the grimy walls of the abandoned warehouse. Rain lashed against the corrugated metal roof, creating a steady drumbeat that amplified the silence between each ominous crackle of electricity. 

Amy, a woman built like a brick house with hands calloused from years of wrestling with wires and conduits, squinted through her safety glasses at the tangled mess before her. A network of copper cables snaked across the dusty floor like a metallic serpent, half-buried in plaster dust and debris. This wasn't just a simple power outage; this was a chaotic graveyard of forgotten electrical systems, whispering secrets of a past that seemed both fascinating and terrifying.  

She sighed, pushing her dark braid back over her shoulder. "Alright then," she muttered to herself, the echo bouncing off the empty space. "Let's see what kind of beast we're dealing with."
