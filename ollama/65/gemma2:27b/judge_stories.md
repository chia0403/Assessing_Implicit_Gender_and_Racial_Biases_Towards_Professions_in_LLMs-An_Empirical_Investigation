The mahogany bench felt cool against Amelia Hawthorne’s palms, a stark contrast to the fire in her gut. Sunlight streamed through the arched windows of Courtroom Three, illuminating motes of dust dancing in the stagnant air. Outside, the cicadas pulsed their relentless summer song, a jarring counterpoint to the tense silence that had fallen over the room. Every eye was on Amelia, the weight of expectation pressing down like a physical force.

Amelia adjusted her spectacles and took a deep breath, willing herself calm. This wasn’t just another routine case. This was different. The fate of a young man, Daniel Finch, hung precariously in the balance, accused of a crime that tore at the very fabric of their small coastal community – the theft of the town's beloved lighthouse lantern.
