The aroma of overcooked spaghetti sauce hung thick in the air, competing with the scent of old crayons and dust bunnies that clung to every corner of Mrs. Eleanor Thistlewick's third-grade classroom.  Sunlight slanted through the dusty windows, illuminating motes dancing in its path and casting long shadows from the mismatched furniture. Eleanor sighed, pushing a stray strand of auburn hair back under her headband. Grading papers was turning into a marathon session, fuelled by lukewarm coffee and sheer willpower. Her students' cursive loomed before her, a chaotic jumble of loops and angles that defied decipherment. 

Tonight, she knew, wouldn't be a night for grading. Tonight, Eleanor Thistlewick had a date.
