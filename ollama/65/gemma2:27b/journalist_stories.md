Rain hammered against the grimy windowpanes of Beatrice "Bea" Holloway's cramped apartment, mimicking the relentless pounding in her chest.  The air hung thick with the scent of cheap instant coffee and stale cigarette smoke, a familiar perfume in her world of chasing down leads and wrestling deadlines. Outside, the neon glow of the Hong Kong skyline bled through the incessant rain, painting the city in hues of electric blue and sickly yellow. Bea hunched over her battered laptop, its screen casting an eerie green light across her face, highlighting the dark circles under her tired eyes. The article on her screen – a scathing exposé on the city's underground organ trafficking ring – was almost finished, but one crucial piece remained missing: concrete proof. 


Bea knew she was walking a tightrope, balancing journalistic integrity against the very real threat of powerful figures who wouldn't hesitate to silence her. But the faces of the victims, gaunt and desperate, haunted her dreams. She had to see this through.
