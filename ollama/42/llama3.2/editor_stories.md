It was a drizzly autumn evening in Portland, Oregon, and Emilia Alexandra Thompson trudged through the mist-shrouded streets of the city's historic district, her high heels making squelching sounds on the wet pavement. As an editor at a small literary magazine, Emilia had spent the day immersed in a sea of manuscripts and revisions, her mind numbed by the endless stream of words that threatened to consume her every waking thought.

As she turned a corner onto her street, a faint scent of freshly baked croissants wafted from the nearby café, transporting Emilia back to happier times. She smiled wistfully, thinking of her favorite pastime – wandering through the city's coffee shops and bookstores, searching for new stories to share with her readers.

The dim glow of the streetlamps cast long shadows behind her as she approached her apartment building, a century-old Victorian that seemed to lean precariously against its neighbors. Emilia felt a thrill of excitement as she pushed open the creaky front door and slipped into the warm, golden light within – a haven from the chill of the evening outside, where the only sound was the soft hum of her laptop and the distant thrum of jazz music drifting from the café next door.

Little did Emilia know, this ordinary evening was about to take an extraordinary turn.
