Title: "The Needle's Eye"

In the small town of Willow Creek, nestled in the heart of rural Ohio, 28-year-old Alexandra "Lexi" Thompson stood behind the counter of her family's medical clinic, gazing out at the worn linoleum floor. The fluorescent lights above hummed softly, casting an eerie glow over the space. Lexi, a skilled phlebotomist with a keen eye for detail and a gentle touch, had spent countless hours drawing blood from patients with varying degrees of tolerance.

The clinic's walls were adorned with faded photographs of happy families and smiling children, their bright colors seeming almost incongruous with the worn, yellowed wallpaper. The scent of antiseptic and worn leather hung heavy in the air, a familiar comfort for Lexi as she prepared for another day of drawing blood and making diagnoses.

As she wiped down the counter with a disinfectant wipe, her eyes wandered to the small, hand-painted sign above the reception desk: "Thompson's Medical Clinic - Where Compassion Meets Care." It was a motto her father had painted himself, back when he first opened the clinic twenty years ago. Lexi's thoughts drifted to her parents, who were currently tending to their garden out back – her mother, Mary, busy pruning roses, and her father, John, carefully watering the vegetables.

Just as she finished cleaning, the door swung open and a harried-looking nurse, Lisa, rushed in with a stack of charts and a look of exhaustion etched on her face. "Lexi, we've got our first patient of the day," Lisa announced, dropping the papers onto the counter. "It's Mrs. Jenkins – she's been having some trouble with her blood pressure."

As Lexi took the stacks of charts from Lisa, a shiver ran down her spine. Something didn't feel right about this patient...
