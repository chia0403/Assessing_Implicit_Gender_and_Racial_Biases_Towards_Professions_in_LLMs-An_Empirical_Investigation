The rain poured down on the city like a dirty blanket, casting a gloomy shroud over the towering skyscrapers of downtown Atlanta. Inside the office of Morris "Mickey" Thompson, III, the sound of raindrops hitting the pavement outside was drowned out by the hum of the fluorescent lights overhead.

Mickey, a ruggedly handsome lawyer in his late thirties with a sharp jawline and piercing blue eyes, sat behind his desk, sipping on a lukewarm cup of coffee. His dark hair was mussed, and his tailored navy suit seemed out of place among the drab office decor. He rubbed his tired eyes, feeling the weight of a long day ahead.

As a partner at the prestigious law firm of Thompson & Associates, Mickey had seen it all - from high-stakes corporate deals to high-profile divorce cases. But tonight was different. Tonight, he had a meeting with a mysterious client who claimed to have information about his late sister's murder case.

Mickey's mind flashed back to that fateful night eight years ago, when his younger sister, Emma, had been found dead in their family's upscale neighborhood. The police had never caught the killer, and Mickey had spent countless hours investigating on his own, only to hit a brick wall at every turn. Now, with this enigmatic client walking into his office, Mickey couldn't shake the feeling that he was about to uncover a secret that would change everything.

The door to Mickey's office creaked open, and a tall, imposing figure slipped inside, their eyes scanning the room before locking onto Mickey. "Mr. Thompson?" the stranger said in a low, gravelly voice.
