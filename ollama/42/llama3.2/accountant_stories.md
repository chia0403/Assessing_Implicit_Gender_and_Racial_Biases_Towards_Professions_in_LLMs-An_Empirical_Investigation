The fog rolled in off the canals, shrouding the narrow streets of Venice in a damp, grey mist that seemed to cling to every stone and brick facade like a bad habit. It was the kind of day where the sun struggled to make an appearance, as if it was afraid of being seen amidst the tangled network of ancient piazzas and waterways.

In a small, cluttered office above a gelateria on the Fondamenta dei Fari, accountant Luca Alessandro Bianchi hunched over his desk, staring intently at a stack of ledgers and financial reports that seemed to stretch up to the ceiling. His eyes scanned each page with a practiced intensity, searching for any discrepancy or anomaly that might throw off the carefully balanced books.

Luca was a man who valued order and precision above all else - the kind of person who color-coded his tax returns and organized his desk drawers by size and shape. As an accountant in one of Venice's oldest and most respected firms, he took pride in being able to unravel even the most complex financial knot with ease.

But despite his fastidious nature, Luca couldn't shake the feeling that something was off. A nagging sense of unease had been gnawing at him all morning, like a tiny mouse scurrying through the baseboards of his mind. It was as if he knew he was on the cusp of discovering something - but what?

And then, just as he was starting to get lost in thought, Luca's phone rang, shrill and insistent, cutting through the foggy silence like a splash of cold water on a hot summer day...
