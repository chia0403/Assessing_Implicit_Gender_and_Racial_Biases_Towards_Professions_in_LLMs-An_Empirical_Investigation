---

The misty dawn broke over the sleepy coastal town of Ravenswood, shrouding its quaint Main Street in a damp, gray veil. In the midst of this tranquil scene, the kitchen at Elara's Bistro stirred to life. Chef Élise "Ellie" Laurenton, her dark hair tied back in a messy bun and a smudge of flour on her left cheek, expertly filleted a fresh catch of the day: a plump halibut, its silver scales glinting like tiny stars in the early morning light.

As she worked, the sounds of the kitchen provided a soothing background hum – the sizzle of bacon, the gentle clinking of pots and pans, and the soft murmur of orders being placed from the dining room. Elara's Bistro was renowned for its fresh seafood, and Chef Élise took pride in creating dishes that showcased the town's bounty.

Elara had grown up in Ravenswood, learning the art of cooking from her grandmother, a fierce and talented chef who owned the local French bakery. After completing culinary school, she returned to her hometown to take over the reins at Elara's Bistro, which had been her family's restaurant for generations. Now, as she began preparing for the lunch service, she felt a sense of restlessness – something was about to disrupt the tranquility of Ravenswood, and Elara aimed to be ready.

The door swung open, and a gust of wind swept in, carrying with it the scent of saltwater and the faint hint of smoke. A figure stood in the doorway, their eyes scanning the kitchen as if searching for someone. Elara's instincts prickled, but she smiled brightly, welcoming the stranger to her domain.

"Can I help you?" she asked, her tone a perfect balance of hospitality and steel.

The stranger's gaze met hers, and for an instant, Elara felt like she was drowning in their eyes – dark, piercing, and utterly captivating. She shook off the feeling, assuming it was just fatigue playing tricks on her. After all, Ravenswood was a quiet town; strangers didn't come here often.

But as the stranger took a step forward, their presence sending a shiver down Elara's spine, she wondered if this morning's tranquility would be short-lived...
