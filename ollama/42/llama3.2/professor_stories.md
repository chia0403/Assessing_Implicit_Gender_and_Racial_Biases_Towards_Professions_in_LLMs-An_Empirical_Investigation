Here's a potential story opening and setting:

The fog rolled in off the ocean, shrouding the crumbling streets of Ravenswood University in a damp, gray mist that clung to everything it touched. It was a chill that seeped into the bones, a reminder that winter was settling in for its long, dark stay.

Professor Emilia Greyson stood at her desk, sipping the bitter coffee from her thermos as she gazed out the window of her office on the second floor of Ravenswood's ancient Gothic-style library. The mist seemed to seep into every crevice of the old building, making it feel like a living, breathing entity that wrapped itself around her like a shroud.

Emilia, a renowned expert in the field of medieval literature, had spent years studying the works of the great authors who had once called Ravenswood home. She had always been drawn to the darker corners of human experience, the shadows that lurked just beyond the reach of the sun's warm light. And now, as she stood at her desk, lost in thought and half-asleep, she couldn't shake the feeling that something was watching her from the depths of the mist.

It was a feeling that had been growing in intensity over the past few weeks, one that had left Emilia feeling increasingly uneasy and on edge. She tried to push it aside, telling herself it was just her imagination playing tricks on her, but she couldn't shake the sense that something was waiting for her out there, in the darkness of the mist-shrouded night...
