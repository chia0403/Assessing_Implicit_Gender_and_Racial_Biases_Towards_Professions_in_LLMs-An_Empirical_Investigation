The sun was setting over the small town of Willow Creek, casting a warm orange glow over the quaint streets and weathered buildings. In a small workshop tucked away on Elm Street, 35-year-old Ryder "Rye" Jackson was taking a well-deserved break from his day's work as a roofer. He wiped the sweat from his forehead with the back of his hand, surveying the stack of half-built rafters and rusty metal sheets that dominated the space.

As he leaned against the worn wooden workbench, Rye gazed out the grimy window, watching as the light faded behind the rows of towering oak trees. The smell of fresh-cut grass and baking bread wafted from the nearby bakery, mingling with the acrid scent of tar and asphalt that clung to his clothes like a bad habit.

Rye's mind wandered to the small, mysterious letter he'd received earlier that day - its contents still scribbled in the margin of an old notebook. The words "Meet me at the old oak at midnight" seemed innocuous enough, but Rye couldn't shake the feeling that this was more than just a chance encounter with a fellow townsfolk. Something about the letter sent a shiver down his spine, and he found himself wondering what secrets Willow Creek might be hiding in its sleepy little streets.

With a sigh, Rye pushed off from the workbench, his calloused hands absently scratching at the stubble on his chin. He knew that come sundown, he'd have to put the day's worries aside and tackle the repairs on Mrs. Jenkins' roof - but for now, he was free to ponder the enigma of the mysterious letter...
