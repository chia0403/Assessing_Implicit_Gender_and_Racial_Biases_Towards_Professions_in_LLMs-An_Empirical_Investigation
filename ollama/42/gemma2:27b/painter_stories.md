The late afternoon sun bled crimson across the canvas Amelia Thorne was struggling with. Her studio, tucked away on the third floor of a dilapidated Victorian townhouse overlooking the Thames, was awash in the warm glow, dust motes dancing in its golden rays. The air hung thick with the scent of linseed oil and turpentine, a comforting olfactory tapestry Amelia had come to associate with creation. Yet, today, inspiration felt as elusive as a wisp of smoke.

Amelia sighed, stepping back from the canvas. Her latest attempt at capturing the ephemeral beauty of a sunset over the river was turning into a muddy mess. The fiery hues refused to blend harmoniously, instead clashing like discordant notes in a symphony. Frustration gnawed at her. It had been weeks since she'd felt the effortless flow of creativity that defined her best work.
