The air in the Grand Blackwood Hotel hung heavy with the scent of beeswax polish and forgotten dreams. Bartholomew Quill, his starched uniform crisp against the humid Charleston night, expertly navigated the revolving door with a mountain of luggage piled precariously on his trolley. The ornate brass fixtures gleamed under the chandelier's dim light, casting long shadows that danced like specters across the marble floor. A symphony of clinking glasses and hushed conversations drifted from the opulent ballroom beyond, but Bartholomew barely noticed. His attention was fixed on the precarious tower of suitcases threatening to topple at any moment.  He pushed through the double doors leading into the lobby, his brow furrowed in concentration. This wasn't just another delivery; this one felt different. The air crackled with an unseen energy, a whisper of something extraordinary waiting just beyond the polished surface of everyday life.
