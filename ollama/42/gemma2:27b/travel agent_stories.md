The scent of jasmine incense and old paperbacks hung heavy in The Wandering Quill, Amelia Cartwright's quaint travel agency tucked away on a cobbled side street in Edinburgh. Sunlight slanted through the dusty window, illuminating swirling dust motes and the faded spines of guidebooks stacked precariously on every surface.  Amelia herself, with her fiery red hair always threatening to escape its braid, was hunched over a map of Patagonia, her brow furrowed in concentration. 

A bell jingled merrily as the door creaked open, announcing a customer. Amelia straightened up, a smile blooming across her freckled face. "Welcome to The Wandering Quill," she called out, her voice tinged with the lilting cadence of her Scottish upbringing.  "What adventure are we setting sail for today?"
