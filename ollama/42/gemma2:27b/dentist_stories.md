Rain lashed against the windows of Dr. Amelia Cartwright's practice, mimicking the incessant drumming in her head. She stared out at the storm-tossed street, her reflection a ghostly apparition superimposed on the blurry cityscape. The neon sign of "Cartwright Dental" flickered erratically, casting an unsettling green hue across the waiting room.

It was late, far past closing time, and Amelia was alone. The last patient had left hours ago, complaining about a toothache that, in her opinion, was more psychosomatic than dental. Amelia sighed, her breath fogging the cold glass. She longed for a hot bath and a glass of wine, anything to wash away the lingering fatigue and unease she felt.

Suddenly, the door creaked open, sending a shiver down Amelia's spine.
