Here's a potential story opening and setting:

Professor Emilia Astrid Thompson stood at the edge of the cliff, gazing out at the stormy sea that crashed against the rugged coastline of Cornwall, England. The wind whipped her long, dark hair into a frenzy as she clutched the worn wooden railing, her eyes fixed on the waves below. It was a moment of solitude, one she had been craving all day.

The university's annual winter conference was winding down, and Emilia had managed to sneak away from the chaos to clear her head before returning to her department chair duties at Exeter University. She had spent the previous few days listening to lectures, attending panels, and networking with colleagues – but today, she had wanted to escape it all.

As a renowned expert in medieval studies, Emilia had grown increasingly disillusioned with the academic politics and petty squabbles that seemed to plague her field. The pressure to publish, the endless requests for funding, the constant need to produce – it was taking its toll on her passion for research.

She took a deep breath of the salty air, feeling the tension in her shoulders begin to ease. Maybe this break would be just what she needed to recharge and refocus. As she looked out at the sea, Emilia felt a sense of freedom wash over her – but little did she know, her peace was about to be shattered by an event that would change everything.

The sound of footsteps behind her broke the silence, and Emilia turned to see a figure approaching her with an air of urgency. But it wasn't until she saw the look on their face that she realized this interruption might be more than just a casual meeting...
