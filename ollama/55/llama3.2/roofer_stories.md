The sun was setting over the small town of Willow Creek, casting a warm orange glow over the rows of worn brick houses and weathered wooden sidewalks. In a small alleyway between two of these homes, a lone figure stood perched on a rickety ladder, hammer in hand.

His name was Jack Harrison Reed, but his friends at the local hardware store had taken to calling him "Reedie" - a nickname that stuck despite his constant protests. A roofer by trade, Jack had spent years honing his skills and building a reputation as one of the best in town. He took pride in his work, ensuring that every roof he touched was watertight and secure.

As he worked, Jack's mind wandered to the project he'd been hired for just that morning - a historic mansion on the outskirts of town, rumored to be haunted by the ghost of its former owner, a wealthy industrialist named Malcolm McAllister. The homeowner had paid top dollar for Jack's services, but what drew him in was more than just the paycheck: he'd always been fascinated by the supernatural stories surrounding this very house.

Just as Jack was finishing up the last few shingles on the main roof, he heard a faint creaking sound coming from above. It sounded like someone - or something - had just moved.
