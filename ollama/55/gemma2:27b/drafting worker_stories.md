The air in the drafting room hung heavy with the scent of graphite and stale coffee.  Fluorescent lights buzzed overhead, casting a sterile glow on the rows of desks cluttered with T-squares, compasses, and rolled blueprints. Elias Finch, his brow furrowed in concentration, meticulously traced the outline of a gear onto tracing paper, the soft scratching of his pencil the only sound breaking the silence. Outside, the rain hammered against the windows of the dilapidated factory building, mirroring the relentless rhythm pounding in Elias's chest. Today was the day he had to submit his designs for the new steam engine piston. A promotion, and a chance to finally escape the suffocating monotony of his drafting job, hinged on this one crucial design.  

Elias paused, his hand hovering over the paper. Doubt gnawed at him. His sketch felt...off. The proportions seemed wrong, the angles too sharp. He needed inspiration, a spark to ignite his creativity.  He glanced at the clock ticking away on the far wall. Time was running out.
