Rain lashed against the grimy windowpanes of the watchtower, blurring the already indistinct outline of the Iron Citadel beyond.  Wind howled like a banshee, rattling the rusted bars that held the flimsy wooden shutters in place.

Inside, Bartholomew "Bart" Grimshaw shifted restlessly on his stool. His thick leather jerkin offered little warmth against the chill seeping through the stone walls. He idly traced a finger along the chipped edge of the worn wooden table before him, his gaze flicking between the flickering lantern casting long shadows across the room and the heavy iron-bound door leading out onto the rain-swept battlements.

Bart wasn't cut out for guard duty. He yearned for adventure, for the thrill of the open road and the glint of gold in a dusty tavern. But fate, it seemed, had other plans.  His family owed a debt to Lord Alaric, the Citadel's stern ruler, and Bart had been offered up as payment.

Tonight, however, something felt different. A prickling sensation crawled up his spine, an unsettling feeling of being watched. The wind seemed to whisper secrets he couldn't decipher, and even the lantern flame danced more violently than usual.
