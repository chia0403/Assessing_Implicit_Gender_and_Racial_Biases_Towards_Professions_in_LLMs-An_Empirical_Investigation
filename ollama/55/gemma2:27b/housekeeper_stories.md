Rain lashed against the stained-glass windows of Blackwood Manor, each drop an icy fingernail scratching at the ancient panes. Eloise Hawthorne tightened her grip on the feather duster, its frayed plumes doing little to quell the rising damp in the cavernous hallway. The scent of mildew and decaying wood hung heavy in the air, mingling with the faint perfume of lavender from the forgotten potpourri in a dusty corner.  Blackwood Manor loomed around Eloise like a brooding giant, its shadows stretching long and menacing in the flickering gaslight.

This wasn't Eloise's first encounter with a crumbling estate. She'd made a career out of cleaning up after neglect, but something about Blackwood felt different. The silence was unnerving, punctuated only by the howling wind and the occasional groan from the aging timbers above. It was as if the house itself held its breath, waiting for something to happen.
