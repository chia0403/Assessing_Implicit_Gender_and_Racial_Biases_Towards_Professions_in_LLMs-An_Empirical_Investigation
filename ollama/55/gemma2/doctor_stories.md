The fluorescent lights hummed overhead, casting a sterile glow over Dr. Amelia "Amy" Harper's cramped office. Papers overflowed from overflowing trays, threatening to engulf her like a paper tsunami. Outside the window, the Chicago skyline was a hazy canvas of concrete and steel, swallowed by the smog of rush hour traffic. 

The phone rang, shattering the silence. Amy snatched it up, her voice tight with fatigue. "Harper's Clinic," she said, forcing a cheerful lilt into her tone.  She longed for the quiet solitude of her lab, where equations and chemicals held sway over the unpredictable chaos of human emotions. But lately, her clinic had become an unrelenting vortex of broken bones, sniffles, and whispered anxieties, leaving little room for her own pursuits.
