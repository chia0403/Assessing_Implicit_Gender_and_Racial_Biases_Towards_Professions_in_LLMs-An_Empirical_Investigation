The dim fluorescent lights overhead cast an eerie glow over the small, cramped kitchen of McGillicuddy's Pub, where 25-year-old Jamie Elizabeth Thompson had been working as a server for the past three years. It was a typical Wednesday evening, with the usual assortment of rowdy college students and locals nursing pints of Guinness in the corner booths. The air was thick with the smell of stale beer, sweat-drenched t-shirts, and the faint hint of last night's leftover pizza.

Jamie, a petite blonde with a scattering of freckles across her cheeks and a mop of curly brown hair, expertly juggled multiple tables at once, her eyes darting back and forth as she took orders and delivered plates of soggy fries. It was a chaotic dance, one that she had mastered over countless nights just like this.

Despite the chaos, Jamie felt a sense of comfort in the rhythms of the pub. She knew every regular's name, every favorite drink, and every story that came with them. This was her domain, and she ruled it with a quiet confidence that belied her 25 years.

As she expertly balanced a tray of drinks on her fingers, Jamie's gaze wandered to the back door, where a gust of wind blew in from the alleyway outside, carrying with it the faint scent of rain. It was a signal that things were about to get interesting...
