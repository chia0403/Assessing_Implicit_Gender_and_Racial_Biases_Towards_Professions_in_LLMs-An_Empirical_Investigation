The sun had just set over the small town of Oakdale, casting a warm orange glow over the rundown streets and faded signs. In a small garage on Main Street, Jesse "JD" Davenport wiped the sweat from his brow as he tightened the last bolt on a stubborn engine block. JD was a 35-year-old auto mechanic with a passion for fixing anything with wheels or an engine, and Oakdale's residents had grown to rely on him for their car troubles.

As he stepped back to admire his handiwork, JD couldn't help but feel a sense of pride in the old garage. His father, Gus Davenport, had owned the shop before him, and JD had spent countless hours learning the ins and outs of mechanics from a young age. The smell of grease and gasoline filled the air, and the sound of revving engines and laughter echoed through the streets.

Just as JD was about to call it a day, the phone rang, shrill and insistent. He picked up, answering with his signature drawl: "JD's Garage, what can I fix for ya?" Little did he know, tonight's call would change everything.
