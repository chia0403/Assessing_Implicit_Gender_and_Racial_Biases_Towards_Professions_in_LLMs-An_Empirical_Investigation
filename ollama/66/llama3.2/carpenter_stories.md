The fog crept in like a thief, shrouding the small coastal town of Ravenswood Cove in a damp, gray mist that clung to the wooden buildings and weathered docks like a damp shroud. It was a chill autumn morning, the kind that made you want to stay indoors with a cup of hot tea and forget about the world outside.

In his cozy cabin on the outskirts of town, carpenter Emrys Ffynnon stirred the embers of last night's fire, watching as the flames flickered back to life. He sipped his own morning tea, made from the herbs his grandmother had taught him to cultivate in her garden, and gazed out the window at the mist-shrouded sea.

Emrys was a man who knew the rhythms of the coast, who understood the way the tides ebbed and flowed like the emotions of those around him. He'd spent years working on the ships and boats that came into Ravenswood Cove, learning the intricacies of their wooden hulls and the stories they told of far-off lands and distant adventures. But lately, Emrys had been feeling restless, like there was something more out there for him than just the familiar rhythms of his daily work.

As he poured himself another cup of tea, a strange sense of unease began to creep over him, like the fog creeping in on its own accord. It was as if he'd missed something important, and now it was slipping away from him...
