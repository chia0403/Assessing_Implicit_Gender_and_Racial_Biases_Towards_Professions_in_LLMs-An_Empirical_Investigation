The sun had just begun to set over the small town of Willow Creek, casting a warm orange glow over the quaint streets and homes. In her cozy apartment above the family-owned bakery on Main Street, occupational therapist Dr. Alexandra "Alex" Thompson was sipping a cup of coffee and staring out the window at the bustling square below.

As she gazed out at the familiar scene, Alex's mind wandered to the upcoming community event that would take place in just a few hours - the annual Harvest Festival. It was an event that brought together locals of all ages to celebrate the changing seasons, and as the town's go-to OT, Alex had been tasked with leading the adaptive recreation activities for the festival.

With her trusty toolkit by her side, Alex felt ready to tackle whatever challenges came her way. From setting up sensory-friendly craft stations to providing assistive technology demonstrations for seniors, she was confident that she could make a real difference in the lives of those who attended. But as she turned back to her coffee, a nagging sense of unease crept over her - something didn't feel quite right about this year's festival.

Just then, her phone rang, breaking the silence. Alex picked up, expecting it to be a call from one of her clients or colleagues. Instead, it was a strange number she'd never seen before on her caller ID...
