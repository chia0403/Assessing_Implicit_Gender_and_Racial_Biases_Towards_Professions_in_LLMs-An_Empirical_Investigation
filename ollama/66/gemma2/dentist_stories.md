Dr. Eleanor Vance adjusted her surgical mask, the cool plastic pressing against her cheekbones.  The waiting room buzzed with the low hum of fluorescent lights and anxious whispers. Outside, rain lashed against the windows of her quaint dental practice, "Smile Haven," nestled on a tree-lined street in the heart of Willow Creek.  Eleanor surveyed the pastel walls adorned with whimsical tooth fairy murals and sighed. It wasn't quite the bustling metropolis she had envisioned when she moved here three years ago, but Willow Creek had a certain charm - a quiet desperation that tugged at her soul. Today, however, all Eleanor wanted was to retreat into the familiar comfort of the sterilization room, far away from the gnawing tension in the air.


Today was different.
