The fluorescent lights buzzed overhead, casting an unsettling pallor over the chipped linoleum floor of the QuickStop.  Elara Jones sighed, her breath fogging the air for just a fleeting moment before being sucked away by the whirring ceiling fan. A lone fly buzzed incessantly against the windowpane, mirroring Elara's own restless energy. Her nametag, slightly askew, gleamed under the unforgiving light, a constant reminder of her monotonous existence behind the register.

The bell above the door tinkled, momentarily breaking the silence. Elara forced a smile and braced herself for the next customer, praying it wouldn't be Mrs. Henderson with her endless coupons and complaints about expired milk.
