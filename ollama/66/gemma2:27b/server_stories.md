The air hung thick with the aroma of garlic bread and simmering marinara sauce.  Eleanor "Ellie" Beaumont navigated the crowded tables of Vincenzo's Trattoria with practiced ease, her tray laden with steaming plates of spaghetti and meatballs. The low hum of conversation mingled with the clinking of forks and the occasional burst of laughter, creating a symphony unique to Friday night dinners in Little Italy. Ellie smiled, her heart warming at the familiar scene. This was her haven, her stage, where she could weave through tables like a seasoned performer, anticipating needs before they were even voiced, leaving smiles and satisfied sighs in her wake. But tonight, something felt different. A nervous flutter danced in her stomach, a subtle shift in the energy of the restaurant that made the hairs on the back of her neck prickle.
