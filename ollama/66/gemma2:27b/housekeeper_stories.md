Rain lashed against the grimy windows of the Blackwood Manor, each drop exploding in a miniature starburst on the ancient glass. Eleanor Croft, her silver braid whipping around her face like a live thing, wrestled with the stubborn knot in the heavy velvet curtains. Dust motes danced in the weak shaft of sunlight that pierced through the gloom, illuminating the faded grandeur of the drawing-room.  

Eleanor had been housekeeper at Blackwood Manor for twenty years. She knew every creak of the floorboards, every whisper of wind through the crumbling stone walls. The manor, once a vibrant tapestry of life and laughter, now stood silent, a mausoleum to forgotten memories.
