The rain pattered against the windows of Rachel Elizabeth Thompson's small office, casting a gloomy veil over the rows of dusty ledgers and scattered financial reports that filled her cramped workspace. As an accountant with a practice that was slowly dwindling, Rachel found herself struggling to make ends meet in this quaint but economically stagnant town.

The year was 1985, and the world outside seemed to be moving at a breakneck pace, leaving small-town America like Ashwood, Vermont, feeling increasingly left behind. The once-thriving textile mill had closed its doors two years ago, taking dozens of jobs with it. Now, the main street was lined with empty storefronts and faded signs, a testament to the town's slow decline.

Rachel sipped her lukewarm coffee, her eyes scanning the stacks of papers on her desk as she prepared for another long day of crunching numbers and chasing down unpaid bills. She had always been driven by a sense of responsibility, ever since her parents had passed away when she was just a teenager, leaving her to care for her younger brother and manage their small business. But now, as she approached her thirtieth birthday, Rachel couldn't shake the feeling that something was missing – that there must be more to life than the monotony of tax returns and financial statements.

As she reached for the phone to book a meeting with a potential new client, a knock at the door broke the silence. It was only a matter of time before someone came calling, but Rachel steeled herself for whatever might come next – the possibility of a new lead, or perhaps something far more unexpected...
