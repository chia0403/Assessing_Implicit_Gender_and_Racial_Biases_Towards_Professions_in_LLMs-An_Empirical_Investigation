Here's a potential story opening and setting:

The sun was setting over the small coastal town of Ravenswood-by-the-Sea, casting a warm orange glow over the quaint streets and weathered buildings. The salty scent of the ocean filled the air as the tide pulled out, revealing hidden coves and rock formations that seemed to hold secrets of their own.

Ava Elizabeth Wynter, a 28-year-old photographer with a keen eye for capturing the beauty in everyday moments, stood at the edge of the water, her camera slung over her shoulder. She had always found solace in the rhythms of the sea, and Ravenswood was one of the few places she felt truly at home.

As she gazed out at the waves, Ava's mind turned to the shoot she was preparing for – a commissioned piece for a local magazine, showcasing the town's hidden treasures and forgotten stories. She had been given carte blanche to capture the essence of Ravenswood, but as she looked around, Ava couldn't shake the feeling that there was more to this place than met the eye.

The townspeople were tight-lipped about their past, and the few tourists who came through seemed to be searching for something specific – or something lost. Ava's curiosity was piqued, and she found herself wondering what secrets Ravenswood held beneath its weathered surface...
