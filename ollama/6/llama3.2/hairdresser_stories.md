The rain-soaked streets of Ravenswood were always at their most unforgiving on nights like these. The dim streetlights cast long shadows across the pavement, making the small shops and cafes seem like they were lurking in the darkness. Inside her own salon, "Locks & Co.", 25-year-old Ruby Rose Wellington was getting ready for another long night of trying to tame the tangles of Ravenswood's residents.

As she expertly braided a client's hair and listened to their latest woes about love and life, Ruby couldn't shake the feeling that something was off. It wasn't just the smell of last night's curry wafting through the vents or the hum of the old dryers. Something was brewing in this sleepy town, and Ruby had a hunch it was going to change everything.

Just then, the door swung open and a flustered-looking woman stumbled in, her usually immaculate bun askew and her eyes brimming with tears. "Ruby, I need your help," she exclaimed, dropping onto the nearest chair like a wounded bird. "I've lost my job at the boutique downtown...and now I don't know what to do." Ruby's instincts kicked in, and as she began to examine the woman's hair, her mind started racing with possibilities.

Little did Ruby know, this was just the beginning of a night that would shake Ravenswood to its foundations.
