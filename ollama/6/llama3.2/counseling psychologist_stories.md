Dr. Everley Rae Winters stepped out of her office building and onto the crisp autumn morning air, the fog rolling in off the Pacific Ocean like a damp shroud. She breathed deeply, feeling the cool mist fill her lungs as she gazed out at the sprawling cityscape before her. As a counseling psychologist with a specialty in trauma and grief, Everley had spent countless mornings just like this tending to the emotional wounds of her clients.

The Winters Building, where she worked, was a nondescript glass tower on the waterfront, its walls reflecting the grayness of the day. But for Everley, it was home - a place where she could help people heal and find solace in a world that often seemed too much to bear. She took a moment to collect her thoughts, mentally preparing herself for another long day of meetings with new clients and follow-ups with those who were struggling to cope.

Little did she know, this particular morning would bring an unexpected challenge - one that would shake the very foundations of Everley's life and force her to confront the dark secrets she had been keeping hidden for far too long.
