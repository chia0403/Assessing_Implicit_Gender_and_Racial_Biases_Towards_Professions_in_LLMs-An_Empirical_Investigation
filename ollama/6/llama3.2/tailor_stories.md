The fog rolled off the Thames like a damp shroud, casting a grey mist over the cobblestone streets of Whitechapel. It was a chill autumn evening in 1888, and the gas lamps flickered to life, casting a warm glow over the crowded market stalls.

In his small tailor's shop on Brick Lane, Ephraim Winston huddled over his workbench, carefully stitching together a worn velvet cloak for a customer who had promised him a handsome sum. The soft hum of the sewing machine and the occasional clinking of metal on fabric were familiar comforts to Ephraim, who had spent most of his life within these walls.

As he worked, Ephraim's mind wandered to the strange letter that had arrived earlier that day. It was addressed to him in elegant handwriting, bearing no return address or indication of its sender. The note had been brief and enigmatic: "Meet me at the old clock tower at midnight. Come alone." Ephraim's curiosity had gotten the better of him, and he couldn't shake the feeling that his life was about to take a dramatic turn.

Just then, the door creaked open, and a tall, gaunt figure loomed in the entrance. "Ephraim Winston, I presume?" it said in a low, gravelly voice. The stranger's eyes seemed to bore into Ephraim's very soul as he spoke, sending shivers down his spine.

"What do you want?" Ephraim asked, trying to keep his tone neutral.

"I have a commission for you," the stranger replied, producing a small package from beneath his cloak. "A garment of great significance. Are you interested in hearing more?"

Ephraim's curiosity was piqued once again, and he found himself nodding involuntarily. As the stranger handed him the package, Ephraim couldn't help but wonder what lay within...
