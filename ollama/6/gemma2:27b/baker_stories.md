The yeasty aroma of rising dough mingled with the sweet scent of cinnamon and cloves, a familiar symphony to Amelia Rose Hawthorne. Her bakery, "Amelia's Ambrosia," sat nestled on a cobblestone street in the heart of Willow Creek, a quaint town where time seemed to move at a slower, sweeter pace. Sunlight streamed through the dusty windowpanes, illuminating flour dust motes dancing in the air like tiny fairies. Amelia, her rosy cheeks flushed from the heat of the ovens and her hands dusted white, hummed along to an old jazz tune playing on the radio as she kneaded a batch of sourdough. Today was market day, and she had a mountain of pastries to bake: flaky croissants, melt-in-your-mouth scones, and her signature blueberry lavender muffins, all waiting to tantalize the taste buds of Willow Creek's residents. 


But today felt different. A strange energy thrummed beneath the surface of Amelia's usual calm. It started with a dream, vivid and unsettling, of a woman cloaked in shadow, whispering warnings about a "crumbling crust" and a "souring heart." Amelia had dismissed it as a figment of her overworked imagination, but the uneasiness lingered. As she shaped the dough into perfect loaves, her eyes darted nervously towards the door, expecting...something. But what?
