The scent of lavender and woodsmoke hung heavy in the air, clinging to Amelia Rosalind Hawthorne's threadbare cardigan like an unwelcome guest. Rain lashed against the attic windowpane, each drop a tiny hammer blow against the glass. Outside, the wind howled through the ancient oaks that encircled her crumbling cottage, their gnarled branches clawing at the slate roof.

Amelia sighed, pushing back a stray curl from her forehead. The words wouldn't come. They had deserted her, abandoning the half-written manuscript lying open on her rickety desk like a shipwrecked sailor. For weeks she had been wrestling with this story, a gothic mystery set in the very woods that now roared their discontent. But inspiration, once a constant companion, had fled, leaving behind a hollow ache in its place.
