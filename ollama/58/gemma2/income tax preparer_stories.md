The fluorescent lights hummed above Amelia Beatrice Caldwell’s head, casting a sterile glow over the sea of meticulously organized filing cabinets that lined the walls of her small office. The air hung thick with the scent of stale coffee and desperation, a familiar aroma for this time of year. Outside, February rain lashed against the windowpanes, mirroring the relentless torrent of tax forms cascading onto Amelia's desk.

She sighed, pushing back a stray strand of auburn hair that had escaped her braid. Another day, another mountain of paperwork. Being an income tax preparer wasn't glamorous, but it was honest work, and in this sleepy town nestled in the foothills of Appalachia, honesty went a long way. 


Amelia tapped the latest form with a tired finger. "Mr. Stanley Henderson," she muttered under her breath, scanning his name. "Let's see what financial shenanigans you’ve cooked up this year."
