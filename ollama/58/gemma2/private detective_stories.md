The flickering neon sign outside the "Blue Moon Diner" cast a sickly yellow light across Elias Vance's trench coat as he sat hunched over a lukewarm cup of coffee. Rain lashed against the grimy windows, blurring the already indistinct outline of the city beyond. The smell of stale grease and desperation hung heavy in the air, a familiar aroma for anyone who'd spent too much time wading through the underbelly of New Orleans. Elias, with his weary eyes and perpetually furrowed brow, knew that scent well.  

He was three days into his latest case, chasing a ghost through the labyrinthine back alleys of the French Quarter, a trail colder than the January air biting at his exposed skin. His client, a woman named Camille Dubois, had vanished without a trace, leaving behind only a cryptic note and a lingering scent of jasmine perfume. Now, Elias sat alone, staring into the murky depths of his coffee, trying to decipher the riddle before him before time ran out.
