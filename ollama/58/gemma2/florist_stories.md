The scent of lilies hung heavy in the air, a cloying sweetness that usually brought Amelia Willowbrook solace.  Today, however, it felt oppressive, mirroring the weight she carried within her own chest. 

Amelia’s shop, "Blooming Hues," was nestled on a quiet cobblestone street in the heart of Oakhaven, a town steeped in history and charm.  The building itself was an old Victorian, its paint chipped but still boasting a cheerful lavender hue that matched the geraniums cascading from overflowing window boxes. Inside, sunlight streamed through stained glass windows, illuminating rows upon rows of vibrant blooms: blushing roses, velvety peonies, and sunflowers reaching for the warmth like golden fingers. 

But the usual symphony of buzzing bees and chirping birds was muted today. Amelia's usually nimble fingers moved listlessly as she arranged a bouquet of forget-me-nots – a common order after a passing storm had wreaked havoc on Oakhaven’s historic cemetery, leaving families to mourn their lost loved ones. The weight of the town’s grief pressed down on her, amplifying the emptiness that had been growing inside her ever since...
