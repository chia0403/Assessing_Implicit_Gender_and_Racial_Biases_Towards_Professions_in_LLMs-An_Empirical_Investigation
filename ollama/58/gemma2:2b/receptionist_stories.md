The fluorescent lights hummed their monotonous song above Evelyn Thorne's head. It was always buzzing with an undercurrent of anxiety that she could feel crawling up her spine like a lone spider on an exposed wire. Every day, it seemed like the same frantic symphony: ringing phones, endless paperwork, and the never-ending tide of dissatisfied customers.  Evelyn's job was to be the face of Thorne & Co., a prestigious but intimidating law firm nestled in the heart of Chicago's bustling Loop. 

The clock ticked relentlessly past noon, its metallic click echoing through the sterile reception area. Evelyn, her tired eyes scanning a mountain of incoming legal documents, felt like she was drowning in paperwork and despair. The phone rang, shattering the silence.  This was it, another chance to salvage the day.
