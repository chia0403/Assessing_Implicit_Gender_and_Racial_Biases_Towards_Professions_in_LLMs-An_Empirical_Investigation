The rain-soaked streets of New Orleans glowed like polished onyx in the dim light of the setting sun. The smell of wet earth and decay wafted through the air, mingling with the sweet scent of magnolias. Amidst this eerie beauty, Emile Thibodeaux stood huddled beneath the awning of his family's electrical shop, a place that had been the heartbeat of the French Quarter for generations.

As an electrician with a mind for problem-solving and a knack for getting his hands dirty, Emile had spent countless nights like this one, tinkering with circuits and rewiring buildings. But tonight was different. Tonight, he'd received a mysterious phone call from a stranger, requesting his presence at the long-abandoned LaFleur Manor on the outskirts of town.

The man's words still echoed in Emile's mind: "I need you to fix something that's been broken for too long. Something that could change everything." With a sense of trepidation and curiosity, Emile had agreed to take on the job, unaware of what lay ahead. As he watched the last rays of sunlight disappear behind the rows of ancient oak trees, he couldn't shake the feeling that his life was about to take a drastic turn.
