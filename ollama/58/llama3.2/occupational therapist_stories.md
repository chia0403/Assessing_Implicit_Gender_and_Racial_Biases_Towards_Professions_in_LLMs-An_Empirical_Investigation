The rain-soaked streets of Seattle were always at their most unforgiving on days like this, when the grey skies seemed to press down on the city like a physical force. Dr. Sophia Elara Jensen stepped out of her apartment building, her bright yellow raincoat and matching umbrella a stark contrast to the drizzle that clung to everything around her.

As she made her way down the sidewalk, Sophia's eyes scanned the crowded street, her trained gaze taking in the telltale signs of someone struggling - the hesitant gait, the fidgety fingers grasping for balance on a cane. She had seen this before, countless times, but it never got any easier to watch.

Sophia was an occupational therapist at Harborview Medical Center, one of the top hospitals in the country, and she had spent years honing her skills in helping patients recover from even the most devastating injuries. But despite her expertise, she couldn't help feeling a twinge of frustration with the system that seemed designed to fail its most vulnerable citizens.

As she walked towards the hospital, Sophia's phone buzzed in her pocket - it was the morning's first report of an emergency patient, one who would soon change everything for this busy occupational therapist.
