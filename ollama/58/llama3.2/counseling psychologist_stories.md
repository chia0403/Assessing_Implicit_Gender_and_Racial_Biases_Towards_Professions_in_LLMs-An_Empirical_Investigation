Here's a potential story opening:

Dr. Ava Maria Rodriguez stepped out of her office building, gazing up at the towering skyscrapers that seemed to stretch on forever in the heart of downtown Manhattan. As she breathed in the crisp spring air, she couldn't help but feel a sense of unease wash over her. It was the same feeling she'd grown accustomed to since moving back to New York City after a five-year stint in suburban Chicago.

Ava, a licensed counseling psychologist with a specialty in trauma and anxiety disorders, had returned to her hometown to start anew after the sudden loss of her mother to breast cancer. The memories of her mother's illness still lingered, but Ava was determined to rebuild her life and make a name for herself in the competitive world of NYC mental health.

As she checked her watch for what felt like the hundredth time that morning, Ava's thoughts turned to her first client of the day: 17-year-old high school student, Emily Wilson. Emily had been struggling with PTSD symptoms after witnessing a violent incident at school, and Ava was determined to help her work through her trauma.

With a deep breath, Ava tucked her phone into her bag and set off towards the subway station, ready to face whatever challenges the day might bring. Little did she know, this would be just the beginning of a difficult case that would push her skills as a counselor to their limits...
