The snowflakes fell gently from the grey sky, casting a serene silence over the cobblestone streets of 19th century London. Amidst the bustling market stalls and horse-drawn carriages, one small shop stood out - "Winston Fletcher, Tailor & Seamstress" - its sign creaking in the gentle breeze. Inside, Winston Fletcher, a slender man with unruly brown hair and spectacles perched on the end of his nose, hunched over his worktable, his needle flying as he stitched together a bespoke coat for a wealthy client.

The sound of hammering from the nearby clock tower echoed through the shop, punctuating the stillness. Winston's fingers moved deftly, his hands accustomed to the feel of fine fabrics and threads, as he worked on bringing his latest creation to life. The scent of wool, silk, and leather wafted through the air, mingling with the faint tang of sweat and fabric dye.

It was a chilly winter evening in December 1885, and Winston's shop was a warm refuge for those seeking quality garments and good company. But as he worked, Winston's thoughts began to wander, his mind distracted by rumors of strange happenings on the outskirts of town...
