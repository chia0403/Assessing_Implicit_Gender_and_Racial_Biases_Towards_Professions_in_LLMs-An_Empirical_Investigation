The sun had long since dipped below the horizon, casting a warm orange glow over the small coastal town of Ravenswood-by-Sea. The air was filled with the sweet scent of saltwater and the distant sound of seagulls wheeling overhead.

In her small, cluttered studio apartment above the local art supply store, Emilia Faye Waters sat hunched over her camera, her fingers poised over the shutter release as she waited for the perfect shot. A seasoned photographer, Emilia had spent years honing her craft in Ravenswood-by-Sea, capturing the town's quirky charm and its resilient residents on film.

As she reviewed her shots from the day's shoot – a series of portraits of local fishermen – Emilia felt a sense of satisfaction wash over her. She loved this time of year, when the summer crowds had dissipated and the town was quiet, save for the sound of the waves crashing against the shore. It was then that she felt most alive, most connected to the world around her.

But as she looked out the window, Emilia's eyes landed on something that made her heart skip a beat: a figure standing alone on the beach, watching the stars begin to twinkle in the night sky.
