---

The sun was just starting to rise over the sleepy suburban town of Oakdale, casting a warm orange glow over the quiet streets and homes. In her cozy little apartment above the family-owned bakery on Main Street, kindergarten teacher Emilia Rose Thompson stirred in bed, rubbing the sleep from her eyes. She stretched her arms overhead, feeling the softness of her worn-out quilt and the comfort of her own four-poster bed.

Emilia had always loved teaching, but it was only after she became a kindergarten teacher that she truly felt like she'd found her calling. There was something about watching those little ones learn to tie their shoes for the first time or read their first book on their own that made her heart swell with joy. And Oakdale's tight-knit community made it feel like a second family to her.

As she swung her legs over the side of the bed and planted her feet firmly on the floor, Emilia heard the familiar sound of the bakery's mixers whirring below - her mom, Dorothy, was already up and prepping for the day's batch of fresh-baked bread. She smiled to herself as she thought about the warm, comforting smell that would waft out into the street later that morning.

But today wasn't going to be like any other day in Oakdale. Today, Emilia had a feeling that something was off...
