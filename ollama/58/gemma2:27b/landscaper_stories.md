The relentless August sun beat down on Elias Thorne's neck as he wrestled with a stubborn rose bush. Sweat beaded on his forehead, mingling with the grime ingrained from years of coaxing life from the unforgiving earth. Elias ran a hand through his unruly brown hair, pushing it away from his hazel eyes that squinted against the glare. He was knee-deep in Mrs. Hawthorne's overgrown garden, a riot of tangled vines and forgotten blooms begging for order.

Elias wasn't just a landscaper; he was an artist, sculpting nature into harmonious visions. But today, inspiration felt as elusive as shade under this scorching sky.  The air hung heavy with the scent of jasmine and impending thunderstorms, a potent cocktail that did little to soothe his restless spirit. A nagging feeling tugged at him, a whisper in the wind that something was amiss. He couldn't shake the sense of being watched, eyes peering through the rustling leaves of the ancient oak tree shading the patio.
