Rain lashed against the windows of Dr. Evelyn Moreau's office, mimicking the storm brewing within her. Outside, the playground at Maplewood Elementary lay deserted, the swings swaying listlessly in the wind. Inside, the scent of stale coffee and lavender hand sanitizer hung heavy in the air, a constant reminder of the emotional weight Evelyn carried every day.

She stared at the file on her desk, the name "Ethan Carter" scrawled across it in bold, black ink. Ten years old, withdrawn, exhibiting signs of severe anxiety. His case was particularly troubling; his silence spoke volumes, hinting at a darkness Evelyn couldn't quite decipher. The clock ticked relentlessly, each second amplifying the urgency she felt. Ethan needed her help, but how could she reach a child who had built walls so high they seemed impossible to climb?
