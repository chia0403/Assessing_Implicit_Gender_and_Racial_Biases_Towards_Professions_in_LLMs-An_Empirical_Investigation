The air in Elodie Beaumont’s atelier was thick with the scent of lavender sachets and beeswax. Sunlight streamed through the arched window, illuminating dust motes dancing above bolts of silk, velvet, and brocade. A half-finished gown shimmered on the mannequin, its emerald satin bodice adorned with delicate silver embroidery that Elodie had spent days meticulously stitching. Outside, the cobbled streets of Paris buzzed with life – the clatter of carriages, vendors hawking their wares, and the melodic strains of a street musician’s accordion.

But inside Elodie's world, all was hushed. Her brow furrowed in concentration as she examined the fit of a sleeve on a velvet bodice destined for Madame Dubois, a notoriously discerning socialite.
