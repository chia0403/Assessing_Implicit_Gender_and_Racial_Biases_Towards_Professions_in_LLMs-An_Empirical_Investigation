The sun had just set over the small coastal town of Ravenswood-by-Sea, casting a warm orange glow over the quaint streets and homes. The sound of seagulls filled the air as Emily Rose Windsor, a dedicated and compassionate registered nurse, made her way home from her shift at Ravenswood General Hospital. She lived in a cozy little bungalow on Elm Street, just a short walk from the hospital where she spent most of her waking hours caring for the townspeople.

As she walked, the smell of saltwater and seaweed filled Emily's lungs, transporting her back to her childhood days exploring the rocky shores and tide pools with her grandfather. She felt a sense of peace wash over her, a sense of normalcy that came from being in a place where everyone knew each other's names and stories.

But as she approached her front door, Emily couldn't shake the feeling that something was off. A faint sense of unease had been growing inside her all day, ever since she'd received an unusual phone call during her shift. The voice on the other end had been muffled and distorted, but the words had sent a shiver down Emily's spine: "They're watching you, Emily."
