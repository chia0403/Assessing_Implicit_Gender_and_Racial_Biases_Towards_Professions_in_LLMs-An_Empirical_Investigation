The sun had just begun to rise over the sleepy town of Willow Creek, casting a warm golden light over the quaint streets and homes. In a small bakery on Main Street, the scent of freshly baked bread wafted through the air, enticing passersby with its sweet aroma.

Inside the bakery, Emilia Rose Thompson was already hard at work, her flour-dusted hands moving deftly as she mixed and kneaded dough for the day's batch of croissants. The soft hum of the mixer and the gentle clinking of utensils against ceramic pans filled the air, creating a soothing melody that seemed to lull the world outside into a peaceful slumber.

As Emilia worked, her eyes drifted out the window, watching as the town began to stir. She loved this time of day, when the world was still and quiet, and she could lose herself in the simple pleasures of baking. It was moments like these that reminded her why she had become a baker in the first place - for the joy it brought her, and for the chance to share that joy with others.

But as the morning wore on, Emilia couldn't shake the feeling that something was off. A sense of unease settled in the pit of her stomach, like a small stone weighing her down. She tried to push it aside, focusing instead on the day's tasks ahead, but the feeling only grew stronger, until she could feel it pressing against her like a physical presence.

And then, just as she was finishing up the last of the croissants, Emilia's phone rang, shattering the peaceful atmosphere and sending a jolt of unease coursing through her veins.
