The sun had long since dipped below the horizon, casting a deep orange glow over the desolate stretch of highway that stretched out before Jack "Hawk" Hawkins like a twisted ribbon of asphalt. The only sound was the soft hum of his Peterbilt 389's engine and the occasional distant rumble of another truck passing by on the other side of the median.

Jack, a grizzled veteran of the open road with over twenty years of experience behind the wheel, had been driving for hours. He'd started out early that morning in Chicago, bound for Los Angeles with a load of electronics headed southbound to meet its destination at the port. It was a long haul, but one he knew like the back of his hand. Jack had spent countless nights like this one, sleeping in cramped diners or roughing it under the stars, as he made his way across the country.

As he cruised down the highway, the GPS on his dashboard began to beep and flash, warning him that he was approaching a stretch of road work ahead. Jack groaned inwardly, knowing that meant a potential delay - and with the storm brewing on the horizon, he didn't want to be stuck in traffic for too long.

He slowed down, scanning the horizon for any signs of the work crew, his eyes squinting against the fading light...
