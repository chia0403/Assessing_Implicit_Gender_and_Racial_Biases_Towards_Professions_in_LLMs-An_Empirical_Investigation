Here's a potential story opening:

The sun was setting over the small town of Willow Creek, casting a warm orange glow over the narrow streets and weathered homes that lined them. In one of those homes, on Elm Street, 32-year-old Ryder Fletcher gazed out at the fading light, his eyes tracing the worn shingles of the roof he'd spent countless hours repairing and replacing.

Ryder was a seasoned roofer with years of experience under his belt, but after a particularly brutal storm had ravaged his own home's roof just six months prior, he'd found himself taking on more jobs than he could shake a hammer at. It wasn't the most glamorous work, but it paid the bills and allowed him to live in the one place that mattered most: Willow Creek.

As Ryder watched, a faint mist began to rise from the rooftops, like a veil of dampness that shrouded the town's secrets. He knew that mist was just an excuse for more bad weather on the horizon, but he also knew that it usually brought with it one thing: trouble.

And as he stood there, lost in thought, Ryder couldn't shake the feeling that this latest storm would be different. This one felt like it had a name to it, like some ancient entity was stirring beneath his feet...
