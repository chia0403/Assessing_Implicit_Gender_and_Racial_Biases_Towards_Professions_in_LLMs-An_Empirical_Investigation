The rain poured down on the city like a dirty blanket, casting a gloomy shroud over the already downtrodden streets of Ashwood. It was a typical Wednesday morning in late November, the kind of day where the only thing that seemed to thrive was the mist that clung to every surface like a damp, cold breath.

In a small, cluttered office above a used bookstore on Elm Street, Alexandra "Lexi" Thompson sat at her desk, nursing a lukewarm cup of coffee and staring out the grimy window. A seasoned reporter for the Ashwood Daily News, Lexi had seen it all - from city council scandals to high-profile murders. But as she gazed out at the rain-soaked streets below, she couldn't shake the feeling that something was off.

A sudden jolt of adrenaline ran through her veins as her phone buzzed on her desk, shrill and insistent in the quiet morning air. Lexi's eyes snapped to the caller ID, displaying a number she hadn't seen in years - a number that belonged to her former mentor and friend, the late journalist Rachel Jenkins.

"Hello?" Lexi replied, her voice firm but wary.

The line was dead silent for a moment before a hesitant whisper echoed back: "Lexi...I need your help..."
