**Opening Scene**

The rain pattered against the windowpane like a relentless drumbeat, casting a gloomy shadow over the small insurance agency where 32-year-old Ethan Ryder spent most of his days. He sat at his desk, sipping on a lukewarm cup of coffee that had gone cold hours ago, and staring blankly at the stack of unpaid claims files in front of him. The fluorescent lights above hummed a monotone melody, making Ethan feel like he was trapped in some kind of existential purgatory.

As an insurance agent, Ethan had seen it all - from catastrophic accidents to unexpected deaths, and everything in between. He had built a reputation for being thorough, reliable, and always willing to listen. But the job was taking its toll on him. The stress of dealing with people's worst fears, the constant juggling act between paperwork and policy details, and the soul-sucking drudgery of it all were slowly draining the life out of him.

Just as Ethan was about to give up for the day, his phone rang, shrill and insistent. He hesitated for a moment before picking up, wondering who on earth would be calling at this ungodly hour...
