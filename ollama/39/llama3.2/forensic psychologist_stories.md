Dr. Emilia Rose Thompson stepped out of her office building and onto the crisp morning air, gazing out over the rain-soaked streets of Seattle. The gray sky matched her mood perfectly, a reflection of the darkness she'd been carrying with her since the latest case had gone cold. As a forensic psychologist, Emilia had spent years studying the minds of perpetrators and victims alike, but this particular investigation had left her feeling like she was staring into the abyss.

The sound of raindrops hitting the pavement seemed to match the steady beat of her heart as she pulled out her phone and checked for any new messages from the detective on the case. The Smithson murder had been a particularly gruesome one, with the body found in an alleyway just hours before Emilia was scheduled to testify in court.

As she waited for the coffee shop down the street to open, Emilia couldn't shake the feeling that she'd missed something crucial. Something that could have cracked the case wide open. She took a deep breath and pushed open the door, stepping into the warm glow of the coffee shop as the rain pounded against the windows behind her.

The aroma of freshly brewed coffee enveloped her, momentarily lifting her spirits. But as she waited in line to order, Emilia's mind began to wander back to the case files, her eyes scanning the notes and evidence for any tiny detail that might have slipped her attention. It was a habit she'd developed over years of working with crime scenes, but one that often left her feeling like she was chasing shadows.

"Hi, I'll have a large coffee, black," Emilia said to the barista, trying to shake off the sense of unease that had settled in her stomach. Little did she know, this morning was about to get a lot more complicated.
