As the last wisps of sunlight faded from the skyscraper windows, casting a golden glow over the steel and glass towers of downtown Manhattan, Alexandra "Lexi" Thompson stood at the edge of her office floor, gazing out the window of her 32nd-floor executive suite. The hum of the city never truly slept, but for Lexi, it felt like the world outside had grown quiet while she remained stuck in a sea of paperwork and boardroom battles.

Lexi, CEO of NovaTech Inc., was no stranger to the cutthroat world of tech finance. She had spent years clawing her way up the corporate ladder, earning the respect of her peers and the envy of her competitors. But tonight, as she stared out at the glittering lights of Times Square, Lexi couldn't shake the feeling that something was off.

It wasn't just the nagging sense of unease that had been growing in the back of her mind for weeks - a nagging feeling that NovaTech's latest acquisition deal might not be as airtight as it seemed. It was the way the shadows cast by the skyscrapers seemed to grow longer and darker, like fingers reaching out to snatch at the last threads of her sanity.

And then, just as she was starting to lose herself in the maze of her own thoughts, Lexi's phone buzzed on her desk, shrill and insistent. She turned away from the window, her eyes scanning the line-up of glowing notifications on her phone's screen. One name stood out - a text from an unknown number that read simply: "Meet me at the rooftop bar at 10pm. Come alone."
