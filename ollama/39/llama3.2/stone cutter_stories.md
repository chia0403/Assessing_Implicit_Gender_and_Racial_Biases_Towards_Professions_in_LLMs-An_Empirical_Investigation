The sun was setting over the small town of Ravenshire, casting a warm orange glow over the cobblestone streets and half-timbered buildings. In the midst of this tranquil scene, a lone figure emerged from the shadows of the old stone quarry on the outskirts of town. His name was Ember Wyatt Stonebrook, a skilled stone cutter with hands that had spent years honing their craft.

Ember, a man in his late 30s with a rugged face and a wild tangle of brown hair, moved with a quiet confidence as he made his way through the quarry's narrow passageways. He wore a worn leather apron over his clothes, and his hands were stained with dust and mortar from a day spent cutting and shaping stone. The air was thick with the smell of damp earth and fresh-cut limestone, and Ember breathed it in deeply, feeling at home among the ancient rocks that had been his livelihood for nigh on 20 years.

As he worked, the sound of hammering echoed through the quarry, punctuated by the occasional shout or whistle from one of the other workers. The sun's rays slanted down into the passageway, illuminating the rough-hewn walls and casting eerie shadows across the floor. It was a place where time seemed to stand still, where the rhythms of the past blended with the present in ways that Ember felt deeply connected to.

But today, something felt different. A sense of restlessness stirred within him, like the whispered promise of changes to come...
