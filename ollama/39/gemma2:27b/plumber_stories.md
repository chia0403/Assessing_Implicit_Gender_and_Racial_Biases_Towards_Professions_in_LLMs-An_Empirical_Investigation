The rhythmic clang of Stanley Fitzwilliam's wrench echoed through the cavernous basement, punctuated by the steady drip-drip-drip from a stubborn pipe joint above.  Stanley, a man whose physique resembled a well-worn toolbox – sturdy and crammed with years of experience – grunted in frustration. He'd been wrestling with this leaky beast for an hour now, sweat stinging his eyes and grime coating his hands. The air hung heavy with the smell of mildew and something distinctly metallic, adding to the unsettling atmosphere of the abandoned Victorian mansion.

Stanley had taken on the job at a steep discount, lured by the promise of restoring plumbing in a historical landmark. Now, staring up into the gloom where shadows danced menacingly, he regretted his bargain. The house, perched precariously on a cliff overlooking the stormy Atlantic, felt alive with unseen whispers and creaks that sent shivers down his spine.
