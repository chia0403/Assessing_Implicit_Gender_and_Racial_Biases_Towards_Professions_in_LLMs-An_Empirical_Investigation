The fluorescent lights hummed a monotonous tune above Eleanor Beatrice Hemlock's head, casting a sickly yellow pallor over the stacks of invoices that threatened to engulf her desk. Numbers swam before her eyes, blurring together into an incomprehensible jumble. It was Tuesday afternoon, which meant three more hours of wading through receipts, reconciling accounts, and battling the ever-present urge to shove her calculator down the gaping maw of the office shredder. Eleanor sighed, massaging her temples. 

The setting: A cramped, dimly lit cubicle in a middling sized accounting firm nestled on the outskirts of bustling Manchester. The air hung thick with the scent of stale coffee and desperation. Outside, the dreary November sky mirrored the mood within - grey, overcast, and devoid of any promise of sunshine.
