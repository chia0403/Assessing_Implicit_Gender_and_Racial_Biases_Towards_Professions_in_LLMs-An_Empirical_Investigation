The air hung thick with the scent of acetone and vanilla, a familiar perfume in Lily Beatrice Harper's world. Her tiny nail salon, nestled between a ramen shop and a laundromat on Bleecker Street, hummed with the gentle buzz of drills and the murmur of gossip exchanged over chipped polish and cuticle oil. The afternoon sun slanted through the grimy windowpanes, casting long shadows across the rows of brightly coloured polishes lining the shelves, each bottle reflecting a miniature rainbow. Lily Beatrice, her name as long and intricate as the French manicures she crafted, sat perched on a stool behind the counter, smoothing down her lilac apron.  Her gaze swept over the familiar scene – Mrs. Goldberg meticulously picking out a shade of lavender, two teenage girls giggling over a shared bottle of glitter polish, Mr. Ramirez patiently waiting for his usual trim. 

Today, though, felt different. A nervous energy thrummed beneath the surface of the usual routine. Lily Beatrice could feel it in the pit of her stomach, a prickle of anticipation that she couldn’t quite place.  A bell chimed above the door as a new customer entered, bringing with them an unseen ripple of change.
