The Arizona sun beat down on Elias "Eli" Blackwood's neck, turning the sweat clinging to his skin into a shimmering mirage. Dust devils danced across the barren landscape, swirling reddish-brown against the bleached blue sky. Eli squinted through the haze, gripping the controls of his Caterpillar 349 excavator with calloused hands. The engine hummed beneath him, a deep, steady rhythm that mirrored the relentless thrumming in his chest.

He was carving out a foundation for a new solar farm, a project designed to bring clean energy to this thirsty corner of the state. But Eli couldn't shake the feeling that something else was being unearthed alongside the dirt and rock - a history buried deep beneath these unforgiving sands, whispering secrets on the desert wind.
