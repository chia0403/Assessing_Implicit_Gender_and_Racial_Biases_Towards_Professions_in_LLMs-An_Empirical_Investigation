Rain hammered against the grimy windowpane of Evelyn "Eve" Sinclair's office, each drop a tiny hammer blow on the already fractured glass. The neon sign outside flickered erratically, casting a sickly green glow that illuminated dust motes dancing in the stale air. Eve herself sat slumped in her threadbare armchair, a half-empty bottle of rye perched precariously on the desk beside a stack of unpaid bills and a file marked "The Canary Case."

Chicago, 1947. A city still grappling with the ghosts of war, its streets choked by fog and shadows. Eve, with her sharp wit and even sharper gaze, was one of the few who dared to navigate those shadowed alleys, uncovering secrets buried beneath layers of grime and deceit.
