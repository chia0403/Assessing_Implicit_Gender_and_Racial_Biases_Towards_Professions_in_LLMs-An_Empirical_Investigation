Rain lashed against the windows of Dr. Evelyn Pierce’s office, mimicking the tumultuous storm brewing inside Amelia Cartwright, PA-C. The sterile scent of disinfectant did little to mask the metallic tang of fear that had taken root in her throat. Across from her, the chart lay open, a stark reminder of the impossible decision she faced.  

The quaint seaside town of Havenwood, Maine, usually a haven of tranquility, felt suffocating tonight.  Amelia stared out at the churning gray sea, its relentless waves mirroring her own inner turmoil. She was known for her steady hand and calm demeanor, qualities that had earned her the trust of Havenwood’s residents. But this patient, young Lily Anderson, battling a rare genetic disease, presented a challenge unlike any Amelia had encountered before.
