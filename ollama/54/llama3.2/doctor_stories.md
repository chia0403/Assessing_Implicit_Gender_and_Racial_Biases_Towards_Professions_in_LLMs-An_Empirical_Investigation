Dr. Emilia Elizabeth Thompson stepped out of the rain-soaked streets of London and into the warm, golden light of the hospital's reception area. The sound of beeping machines and murmured conversations enveloped her as she hung up her sodden coat and smiled wearily at the nurse behind the desk.

"Good morning, Doctor," she said, shaking the water from her hair. "I'm running a bit behind schedule. Just a few minutes."

The nurse, a friendly woman with a kind face, raised an eyebrow but didn't comment. Emilia knew she was always busy, and the long hours at St. Michael's Hospital took their toll on even the most seasoned doctor.

As she made her way through the bustling corridors, Emilia's eyes scanned the rows of doors, each one bearing the name of a patient or department. She had been working as a cardiothoracic surgeon for five years now, and yet every day still filled her with a sense of purpose and excitement.

Her own office was just a short walk down the hallway from the main reception area, and she could see the door labeled 'Dr. E.E. Thompson' in bold letters. She pushed it open and stepped inside, feeling a sense of relief wash over her as she left the chaos of the hospital behind.

But as she began to sort through the stack of files on her desk, one patient's name caught her eye: Dr. Henry James Reed, a renowned cardiac researcher who had gone missing three days ago. Emilia felt a shiver run down her spine as she wondered what could have happened to him...
