The sun was setting over the small town of Willow Creek, casting a warm orange glow over the streets and homes. In a cozy apartment on Elm Street, 25-year-old Emily Joy Thompson was finishing up her shift at the local children's center, "Little Explorers." As a lead childcare worker, Emily had spent the day playing with toddlers, helping preschoolers learn their ABCs, and singing silly songs to a room full of energetic five-year-olds.

As she locked the door behind the last group of kids and began to tidy up the playroom, Emily couldn't shake the feeling that something was off. It wasn't just the lingering scent of diaper cream or the faint hum of the fluorescent lights overhead - it was a nagging sense of unease that had been building inside her all day.

As she changed out of her uniform and headed to the kitchen to make herself a cup of tea, Emily's mind kept wandering back to the mysterious phone call she'd received earlier that morning. It was from an unknown number, and when she'd answered, a low, gravelly voice had spoken only three words: "Emily, be careful."
