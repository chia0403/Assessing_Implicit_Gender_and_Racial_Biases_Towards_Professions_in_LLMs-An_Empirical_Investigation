The fog rolled in off the Puget Sound, shrouding the small town of Port Angeles in a damp, gray mist that seemed to cling to every surface like a damp blanket. It was a fitting atmosphere for Emrys Everett Jenkins, a grizzled logger who had spent his entire life working in these woods. His worn denim overalls and flannel shirt were stained with the sweat and resin of countless days spent climbing trees, operating heavy machinery, and battling the unpredictable weather that seemed to delight in making his work as difficult as possible.

As he emerged from the mist, Emrys squinted through the veil of fog that swirled around him, his eyes adjusting slowly to the bright morning light. The forest was alive with the sounds of birdsong and the rustle of leaves, a symphony of sound that had been his constant companion since childhood. But today, something felt off. A sense of unease settled in the pit of his stomach as he gazed out at the towering trees, their branches like skeletal fingers reaching towards the sky.

It was going to be one of those days.
