The fluorescent lights above the hospital ward flickered, casting an eerie glow over the rows of beige-gray cots. The smell of antiseptic and sweat hung heavy in the air, mingling with the hum of beeping machines and the soft murmur of anxious conversations. It was 10:30 PM on a Tuesday, and the night shift at Mercy Hospital was in full swing.

Amidst the chaos, Phoebe Alexandra Thompson scrubbed out of her gloves, her hands cradling a cup of lukewarm coffee as she gazed down at the clipboard in front of her. As a phlebotomist on the pediatric ward, Phoebe had seen it all - from tearful tantrums to triumphant smiles, and everything in between. Her eyes, a deep shade of brown that seemed to hold a world of compassion, scanned the list of patients she was scheduled to visit next.

At 23 years old, Phoebe was already an experienced phlebotomist, having started her career at Mercy as soon as she finished her associate's degree in healthcare. She took pride in her work, knowing that every successful draw could mean the difference between life and death for a child fighting an illness. As she began to make her way down the row of beds, Phoebe felt a sense of calm wash over her - this was where she belonged, surrounded by the quiet determination of the hospital's youngest patients.
