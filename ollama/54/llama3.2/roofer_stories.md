The sun was setting over the small town of Willow Creek, casting a warm orange glow over the dusty streets and worn buildings. In a cramped but cozy office above his family-owned roofing business, 32-year-old Ryder Jackson Lee sat hunched over a stack of bills and repair estimates, his eyes scanning the numbers with a practiced intensity.

Ryder had spent his entire life working for his father's company, which he'd inherited after completing an apprenticeship at just 16. He knew every inch of the town's rooflines, from the steeply pitched mansions on Elm Street to the humble bungalows on Maple Avenue. As a roofer, Ryder took pride in his work, meticulously repairing leaks and installing new shingles with a level of attention that had earned him a reputation as one of the best in the business.

But today was different. Today, Ryder's thoughts were consumed by a mysterious letter he'd received just that morning - a cryptic note from an old acquaintance, hinting at a lucrative opportunity that promised to change the course of his life forever.
