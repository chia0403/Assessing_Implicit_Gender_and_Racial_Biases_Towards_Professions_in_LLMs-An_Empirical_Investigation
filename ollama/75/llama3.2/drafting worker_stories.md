The year was 1955, and the sun had just begun to set over the bustling city of New Haven, Connecticut. The smell of freshly cut grass and coal smoke hung in the air as people made their way home from a long day's work. Amidst the chaos, one figure stood out - Evelyn Mae Thompson, a skilled drafting worker with a passion for precision and perfection.

Evelyn sat at her desk, hunched over a large drafting table in the back of the engineering firm where she worked. The dim fluorescent lights overhead cast an eerie glow on her dark hair as she meticulously drew lines and curves onto a sheet of graph paper. Her hand moved with precision, each stroke deliberate and calculated, as she brought to life the intricate designs for a new construction project.

The city outside was alive with activity - cars honking, people shouting, and the distant rumble of trains - but Evelyn's world was one of quiet focus. She was lost in her work, consumed by the thrill of creating something from nothing, and the satisfaction that came with seeing it all come together.

But as she worked, a nagging sense of unease began to creep into her mind...
