The sun was setting over the ancient city of Petra, casting a warm orange glow over the narrow canyon known as the Siq. Tourist groups milled about, their guides leading them through the winding path in a constant stream of noise and camera shutters. Amidst the chaos, Aurora "Rory" Thompson stood out - not just because of her striking emerald eyes and wild curly brown hair, but also due to her effortless command of the tour.

As one of Petra's most popular and respected guides, Rory had spent years honing her knowledge of the city's intricate history and hidden secrets. She was a native Jordanian, born and raised in the small town that lay just outside the ancient ruins. Her family had lived here for generations, and she took pride in sharing their stories with visitors from all over the world.

Rory's group, a rowdy bunch of teenagers on school field trip, lagged behind as they made their way deeper into the Siq. She smiled wryly to herself - it was going to be one of those days. The heat was sweltering, and she could already feel the first signs of dehydration creeping in. But she pushed on, leading her group through the narrow passage as the stars began to twinkle overhead.

"Okay, kids," Rory said, her voice carrying above the din. "We're approaching the famous Treasury building now. It's one of Petra's most iconic landmarks, and it's a must-see for any visitor." She paused for dramatic effect, surveying her group with a practiced eye. "But don't get too close - we need to respect the site."

As she began to lead her group towards the entrance, Rory couldn't shake the feeling that something was off. A sense of unease had been growing inside her all day, and she couldn't quite put her finger on what it was. But as they stepped into the cool darkness of the Treasury's facade, she felt a shiver run down her spine.

And that was when she saw it - a small, cryptic message scrawled across the wall in a language no one seemed to understand.
