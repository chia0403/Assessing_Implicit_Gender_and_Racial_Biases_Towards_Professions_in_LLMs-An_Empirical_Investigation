The misty dawn broke over the sleepy town of Ravenswood, casting a mystical spell over its residents as they began their daily routines. Amidst the quiet streets and quaint Victorian homes, one household stirred to life: Willow May Fletcher, a diligent and unassuming housekeeper who had been employed by the reclusive Mr. Edward Blackstone for nearly five years.

Willow, a tall and slender woman in her mid-thirties with long, dark hair tied back in a neat bun, expertly navigated the labyrinthine corridors of Blackstone Manor, her worn leather clogs making barely a sound on the polished wooden floorboards. She had grown accustomed to the eerie silence that pervaded the manor, where the only sounds were the soft ticking of clocks and the occasional distant rumble of thunder in the nearby mountains.

As she made her way through the kitchen, the scent of freshly brewed coffee wafted from the stove, mingling with the faint aroma of old books and dust. Willow's eyes sparkled with a quiet satisfaction as she poured herself a steaming cup, her mind already focused on the tasks ahead: polishing the silverware, cleaning the library shelves, and preparing the afternoon tea.

But amidst the familiar routine, a hint of unease tickled at the edges of Willow's consciousness. It was a feeling she couldn't quite shake, like the faint scent of smoke on a summer breeze. A sense that something was amiss in the once-peaceful manor, waiting to be uncovered...
