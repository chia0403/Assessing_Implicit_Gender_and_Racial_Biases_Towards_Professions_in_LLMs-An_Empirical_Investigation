The fog rolled in off the Pacific Ocean, shrouding the small coastal town of Ravenswood in a damp, gray mist. In the heart of the town, Judge Emilia Elizabeth Windsor stood outside the historic courthouse, her eyes fixed on the worn wooden building that had seen generations of Ravenswood's residents come and go. It was a day like any other - except for one: the day Emilia would have to make a decision that would change the course of lives forever.

Judge Emilia Windsor, a 55-year-old jurist with a reputation for fairness and compassion, was known throughout Ravenswood as a respected figure in the community. Her sharp mind and quick wit had earned her a spot on the local court bench, where she had served for over a decade, presiding over everything from petty theft cases to serious crimes.

As Emilia gazed out at the foggy streets, she couldn't shake the feeling that something was off. A sense of unease had been growing inside her all week, like a stone weight in her stomach. She pushed it aside, telling herself it was just nerves - but as she turned to head into the courthouse, she noticed a piece of paper on the steps, fluttering in the breeze.

Curious, Emilia picked up the paper and smoothed out the wrinkles. It was an anonymous note, scribbled in hasty handwriting: "Meet me at the old lighthouse at midnight. Come alone."
