The scent of hyacinths and lavender hung heavy in the air, mingling with the earthy aroma of freshly turned soil. Sunlight streamed through the arched windows of "Petals & Promises," bathing everything in a warm, golden glow.  

Inside, Amelia Rose Hawthorne meticulously arranged lilies in a crystal vase, their creamy white petals unfurling like delicate ballerina skirts. Her nimble fingers worked with practiced ease, weaving stems together and tucking in sprigs of fragrant rosemary. Outside, the cobblestone streets of Willow Creek buzzed with the usual morning commotion: children chattering on their way to school, bakers pulling trays of warm bread from ovens, and dogs barking playfully at each other across the street. 

Amelia loved this time of day, before the shop filled with eager customers seeking bouquets for every occasion – birthdays, anniversaries, condolences. This quiet hour was hers alone, a sanctuary where she could lose herself in the artistry of flowers.  But today, something felt different. A nervous flutter danced beneath her ribs, a tremor that had nothing to do with the gentle sway of the wind chimes hanging from the shop's awning. 


A single, crimson rose lay abandoned on the counter, its velvety petals unblemished. It was unlike any she’d seen before – a deep, almost blood red, and radiating an unnatural heat.  Amelia reached out to touch it cautiously, her heart pounding with a strange mix of curiosity and apprehension.
