Grease clung to Eloise "Ellie" Beaumont's knuckles like stubborn barnacles. The midday sun streamed through the grimy windows of her garage, casting long shadows over the tangle of engine parts scattered across her workbench.  The air hung thick with the scent of gasoline and ozone, a familiar perfume Ellie found oddly comforting. Outside, the cicadas thrummed their ceaseless summer song, but within the walls of Beaumont Auto Repair, it was just Ellie and the guttural groan of a 1967 Mustang struggling to cough back to life.

Ellie wiped her brow with the back of her hand, leaving a streak of grime across her forehead. This old beast was proving more stubborn than she'd anticipated. The carburetor needed rebuilding, the spark plugs were fouled, and something in the transmission whined like a disgruntled alley cat.  She loved a challenge, but this Mustang was testing her patience.

Just then, the bell above the garage door clanged, announcing a customer. Ellie sighed, hoping it wasn't another desperate soul with a busted radiator hose and unrealistic expectations.
