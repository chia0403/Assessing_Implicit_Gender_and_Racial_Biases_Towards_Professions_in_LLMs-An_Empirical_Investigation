Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of the abandoned observatory, each drop echoing like a tiny gong in the cavernous space. Dr. Eleanor Vance shivered, pulling her threadbare cardigan tighter around herself as she navigated the debris-strewn floor. Dust motes danced in the shafts of moonlight piercing through cracks in the boarded windows, illuminating forgotten telescopes and rusted machinery, relics of a time when this place had hummed with scientific curiosity.

Eleanor, her forehead creased with determination, carefully avoided a fallen tripod, its legs splayed like broken limbs. She was here for a reason, drawn by whispers of an anomaly, a frequency picked up by a forgotten radio telescope buried deep beneath the observatory. An anomaly that could rewrite everything she thought she knew about the universe.
