Rain lashed against the arched windows of the Grand Blackwood Library, blurring the view of the cobblestone street outside into an impressionistic smear of grey and yellow. Inside, the air smelled of aged paper, leather bindings, and a faint hint of lavender from the sachets tucked amongst the ancient tomes on the highest shelves.  Eleanor Thistlewick, her silver hair pulled back in a severe bun, adjusted her spectacles and peered over the top of a towering stack of returned books. 

A low rumble of thunder echoed through the library's cavernous hall, momentarily silencing the soft murmur of turning pages and whispered conversations. Eleanor sighed, pushing her glasses further up her nose. Another day, another deluge in Blackwood Hollow. It was enough to make anyone crave a good mystery novel and a steaming cup of tea.
