Smoke curled from the gutted building like a vengeful spirit, licking at the bruised twilight sky. The acrid scent stung Elias Blackwood’s nostrils, even through his soot-stained mask. Sirens wailed in the distance, their mournful song a counterpoint to the crackle and hiss of dying flames. Elias tightened his grip on the hose, the icy water a sharp contrast against his gloved hand. He scanned the building's facade, searching for signs of life amidst the charred debris. This wasn't just another routine fire; something felt different, colder, more insidious about this blaze. 

Elias Blackwood had been a firefighter in the city of Newhaven for ten years, and he’d seen his fair share of devastation. Yet, tonight, a shiver ran down his spine as he stared at the skeletal remains of what was once a bustling bakery. The air felt heavy, pregnant with an unseen menace that whispered through the charred timbers.
