Rain lashed against the glass doors of the Helios Clinic, blurring the neon "OPEN" sign into an indecipherable smear. Inside, Amelia Cartwright tapped her manicured nails on the polished granite counter, the monotonous rhythm echoing through the cavernous waiting room. The fluorescent lights hummed overhead, casting a sterile glow on the worn leather chairs and outdated magazines scattered across low tables.

Amelia sighed, straightening a pile of patient intake forms. It was a Wednesday afternoon, typically the slowest time of the week at Helios. Yet, today felt different. A prickling unease settled in her stomach, as though the storm brewing outside mirrored something brewing within the clinic walls.
