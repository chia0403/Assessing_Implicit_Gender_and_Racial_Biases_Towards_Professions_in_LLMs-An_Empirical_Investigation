The air in Ms. Delacroix’s office was thick with anticipation and the scent of stale coffee. Amelia Rose Blackwood, her fingers drumming a nervous tattoo on the mahogany desk, watched as Ms. Delacroix, their esteemed legal eagle, paced before the floor-to-ceiling windows overlooking the bustling New York City skyline. The Empire State Building shimmered like a silver spear piercing the bruised twilight sky, casting long shadows across the manicured expanse of Central Park.

Amelia, a paralegal fresh out of law school and eager to prove herself, felt the weight of responsibility pressing down on her shoulders.  Ms. Delacroix's latest case – defending a renowned architect accused of embezzlement - was high-profile and complex, its outcome potentially altering the trajectory of both men’s lives. Amelia, tasked with meticulously combing through mountains of financial documents, knew this was her chance to shine.


But beneath the city's glittering facade, something felt off. A prickle of unease ran down Amelia’s spine, whispering of shadows lurking in the corners of this seemingly glamorous world.
