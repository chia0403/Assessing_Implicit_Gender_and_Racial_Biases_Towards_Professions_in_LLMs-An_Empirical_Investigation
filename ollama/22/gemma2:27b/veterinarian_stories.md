The air hung thick with the scent of hay and antiseptic, a familiar symphony to Dr. Evelyn Thorne’s nostrils. Sunlight streamed through the dusty windows of her rural veterinary clinic, illuminating motes dancing in the air and casting long shadows from the antique surgical instruments lining the wall. Outside, cicadas thrummed their relentless summer song, while a rooster crowed impatiently from the neighboring farm. Evelyn sighed, running a hand through her already disheveled auburn braid. It was going to be another long day. 


She glanced at the clock – nearly 8 am.  The first patient of the day, a skittish terrier named Pip with a suspected ear infection, whimpered softly from his carrier in the corner. Evelyn took a deep breath, steeling herself for the inevitable flurry of barks, meows, and worried owners that awaited her. Today, like every day, she would be their beacon of hope, a lifeline for those furry (and sometimes feathered) friends who couldn't speak for themselves.
