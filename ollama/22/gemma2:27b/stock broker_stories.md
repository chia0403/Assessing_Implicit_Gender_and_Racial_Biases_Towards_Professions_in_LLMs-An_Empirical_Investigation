Rain hammered against the panoramic windows of Alistair Finchley's corner office, blurring the glittering cityscape below into an impressionist painting of light and shadow. The incessant drumming mirrored the unease churning in Alistair's gut. He hadn't slept properly in days, his mind perpetually replaying the disastrous trades that had cost his clients millions, and consequently, him, his reputation. The sleek mahogany desk, usually a battlefield of charts and graphs meticulously dissected for profit margins, lay bare, a chilling testament to his paralysis. His phone, normally buzzing with eager investors, sat silent, a metallic tombstone marking his downfall. Alistair Finchley, once the golden boy of Wall Street, was drowning in the treacherous currents of his own ambition.
