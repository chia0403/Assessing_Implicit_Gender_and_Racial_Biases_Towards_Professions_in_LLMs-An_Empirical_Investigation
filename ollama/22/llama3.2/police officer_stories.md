It was a drizzly autumn evening in the small town of Willow Creek, where the streets were lined with old oak trees that seemed to stretch on forever. The kind of place where everyone knew each other's names and the local diner served as the hub of community gossip.

Detective Emilia Rose Thompson stood outside the crumbling facade of the Willow Creek Police Department, sipping a lukewarm coffee from her thermos. Her eyes scanned the darkening sky, where the streetlights flickered to life like fireflies on a summer night. The rain began to fall in earnest, casting a misty veil over the streets.

At 32 years old, Emilia had been a cop for nearly a decade, and she knew every nook and cranny of this small town like the back of her hand. She'd seen it all - from the joyous weddings to the heartbreaking funerals, from the thrill of solving a cold case to the frustration of dealing with bureaucratic red tape.

As she stepped inside the precinct, a chill ran down her spine. The department was quiet tonight, the only sound the hum of the fluorescent lights overhead. But Emilia's instincts told her that something was off. She'd received a cryptic message earlier in the day - a text from an unknown number with a single word: "Echo."

And now, as she gazed out at the rain-soaked streets, Emilia couldn't shake the feeling that the word was more than just a random message. It was a warning. A warning of something big. Something about to happen in Willow Creek.
