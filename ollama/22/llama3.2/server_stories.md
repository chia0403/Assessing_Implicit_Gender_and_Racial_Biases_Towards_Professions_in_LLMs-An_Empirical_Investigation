The neon lights of the city's bustling downtown area cast a kaleidoscope of colors across the rain-soaked streets, creating a mesmerizing display that seemed to pulse with the rhythm of the night. Inside the cramped and dimly lit diner, "Sammy's", the air was thick with the smell of grease and stale coffee, a familiar scent for Emily Mae Taylor, the server who had spent countless nights juggling plates, pouring drinks, and listening to the city's woes from behind the counter.

It was 11:45 PM on a drizzly Tuesday evening, and the diner was in its usual lull. The only sounds were the gentle hum of the refrigerators, the soft clinking of dishes being washed in the back, and the occasional muffled laughter from the booths. Emily Mae, with her bright pink hair tied back in a ponytail, moved efficiently through the kitchen, restocking condiments and wiping down tables with a practiced ease that had become second nature to her.

As she made her way to the counter to take the next customer's order, the phone on the wall jolted Emily Mae out of her reverie. She picked up the receiver, listening attentively as the voice on the other end spoke in hushed tones, sending a shiver down her spine that had little to do with the chill in the air.

"Emily Mae Taylor?"
