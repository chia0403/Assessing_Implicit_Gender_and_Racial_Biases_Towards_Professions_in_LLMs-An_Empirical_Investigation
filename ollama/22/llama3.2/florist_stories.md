The scent of blooming lilacs wafted through the streets of Willow Creek, mingling with the soft hum of morning traffic on Main Street. Inside Petal & Thorn Florals, owner and head florist, Emilia Rose Wilder, stirred a pot of steaming tea while carefully selecting the finest blooms for her signature bouquet: "Sunrise Serenade." As she worked, the warm sunlight streaming through the shop's large windows illuminated the intricate lace curtains that framed the glass case, where an arrangement of fresh peonies and hydrangeas seemed to glow like a celestial display.

At 28 years old, Emilia had built Petal & Thorn from the ground up – literally. Her grandmother, Agnes Wilder, had taught her everything she knew about flowers and their secrets before passing away just a year ago. The loss still lingered, but Emilia had found solace in nurturing the very thing that brought her so much joy: beauty.

Just then, the doorbell above the entrance chimed softly, signaling the arrival of her first customer of the day – a tall, lanky man with a messy shock of brown hair and an air of quiet determination about him.
