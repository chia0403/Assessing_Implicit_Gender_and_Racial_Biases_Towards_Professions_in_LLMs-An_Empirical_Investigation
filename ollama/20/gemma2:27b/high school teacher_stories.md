Rain lashed against the windows of Mrs. Eleanor Cavendish's classroom, mimicking the tempest brewing inside her.  She stood before her Advanced Placement Literature class, a stack of hastily graded essays threatening to topple from her grasp. The once pristine white pages were now marred with red ink, each slash and circle a testament to the students' struggle – and hers – to decipher the nuances of Shakespearean sonnets. 

The bell for fourth period shrieked, momentarily shattering the tense silence. Mrs. Cavendish forced a smile, one she hoped conveyed encouragement rather than the utter despair she felt. "Alright class," she began, her voice strained, "let's delve into sonnet 18…"
