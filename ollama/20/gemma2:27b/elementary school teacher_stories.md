The aroma of stale crayons and disinfectant hung heavy in the air, a familiar scent that usually brought Eleanor Rose a sense of calm. But today, a knot of anxiety tightened in her stomach as she surveyed Room 203. Desks were pushed haphazardly against the walls, half-finished artwork lay crumpled on the floor, and a rogue glitter bomb had exploded somewhere near the reading corner, leaving shimmering chaos in its wake. Summer break had finally arrived, but instead of relief, Eleanor felt only dread.

This wasn't just any summer. This was the summer everything changed. The school board had announced drastic budget cuts, leading to the closure of several beloved programs and, most alarmingly for Eleanor, the potential elimination of her position.
