The rusted gate creaked like an arthritic knee as Thaddeus Finch pushed it open. The morning mist still clung to the overgrown lawn, making the ancient oak trees look like specters rising from the dew-kissed grass. A symphony of birdsong filled the air, punctuated by the insistent chirping of crickets hiding amongst the ivy that snaked its way up the crumbling stone walls. Thaddeus inhaled deeply, the scent of damp earth and blooming honeysuckle a familiar comfort.

He'd been groundskeeper at Blackwood Manor for twenty years, ever since his grandfather, a man whose hands were as gnarled as the ancient oaks he tended, had passed away. The manor itself was a hulking Victorian monstrosity, its windows dark and vacant like empty eyes staring out onto the sprawling grounds. It had stood deserted for decades, whispered to be haunted by the ghost of its former owner, a reclusive inventor who'd vanished without a trace.

Thaddeus always scoffed at the ghost stories. For him, Blackwood Manor was simply a place of peace and solitude, a sanctuary where he could lose himself in the rhythms of nature. But today, something felt different. The silence seemed heavier, the air thicker with unspoken secrets. As Thaddeus surveyed the overgrown rose garden, a flicker of movement caught his eye. A sliver of white vanished amongst the thorns. Curiosity piqued, he cautiously approached...
