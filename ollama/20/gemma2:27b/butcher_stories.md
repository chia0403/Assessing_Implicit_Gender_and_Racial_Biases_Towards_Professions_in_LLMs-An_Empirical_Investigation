The air in The Butcher's Block hung thick with the scent of iron and rosemary. Rain hammered against the shop window, blurring the already-dim streetlights outside into shimmering halos.  Edgar Bartholomew Thornton III surveyed the scene with a sigh, his thick forearms rippling as he wiped down the chipped marble counter.

He wasn't afraid of hard work, not after decades spent carving flesh and bone in this very shop. But lately, something felt off. The rhythm of the butchering life – the early mornings, the steady clang of cleavers, the regulars with their specific orders – it all seemed a little… dull. 


Edgar longed for something more, something beyond the predictable routine of sausages and sirloins.  As he reached for another slab of beef, a sudden gust of wind rattled the windowpane, sending a shiver down his spine. He glanced up, catching a flicker of movement in the rain-streaked reflection. For a fleeting moment, he could have sworn he saw a pair of glowing eyes staring back at him from the shadows.
