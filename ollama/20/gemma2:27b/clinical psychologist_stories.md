Rain lashed against the windowpanes of Dr. Eleanor Vance's office, mimicking the tempest brewing within her own chest.  The scent of lavender from her diffuser battled valiantly against the metallic tang of anxiety that permeated the air. Outside, Boston throbbed with its usual frenetic energy, oblivious to the emotional hurricane threatening to engulf Eleanor in her sixth-floor sanctuary. 

Her phone vibrated insistently on the mahogany desk, a jarring interruption to the tense silence. The caller ID flashed "Mother." Eleanor hesitated, her finger hovering over the green button.  It was never good news when her mother called during work hours.  With a sigh, she pressed answer, bracing herself for whatever familial drama awaited.
