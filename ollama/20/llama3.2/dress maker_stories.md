The sun had barely risen over the small town of Willowdale, casting a warm glow over the cobblestone streets and half-timbered buildings. In a tiny shop on High Street, Emilia Rose Windsor stirred the embers of last night's candle to life, coaxing them into a gentle flame that danced across the wooden floor.

Emilia, a skilled dress maker with hands as nimble as a butterfly's wings, sat at her worktable, surrounded by scraps of silk and cotton, threads of every hue, and the soft hum of sewing machines. She was known throughout Willowdale for her exquisite creations - dresses that made women feel like princesses, and children feel like fairies. But as she gazed out the window, watching the town come to life, Emilia's thoughts were not on fabric or fashion, but on the mysterious letter that had arrived at her shop earlier that morning.

The envelope was plain, with no return address and only a single sentence scrawled in elegant script: "Meet me at the old oak tree in the woods at midnight. Come alone."
