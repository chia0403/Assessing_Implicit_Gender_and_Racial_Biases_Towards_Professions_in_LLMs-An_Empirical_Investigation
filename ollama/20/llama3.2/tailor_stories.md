In the narrow streets of 19th century Paris, where the Seine River flowed gently through the heart of the city, and the smell of freshly baked bread wafted from the boulangeries, the small atelier of Aurélien Dumont stood like a beacon on Rue de la Chanvré. For twenty years, Aurélien had spent his days mastering the art of tailoring, passing down techniques learned from his father and grandfather before him to a new generation of clients. His hands were deft and precise, able to coax even the most intricate designs from the finest silks and velvets.

As the sun set over the rooftops, casting long shadows across the cobblestones, Aurélien sat at his workbench, surrounded by scraps of fabric and half-finished garments, his eyes fixed on the latest commission from the Duchesse de Montmorency: a bespoke wedding gown for her daughter's nuptials in just six weeks' time. The pressure was mounting, but Aurélien's fingers moved with a quiet confidence, as if the very essence of his craft had been distilled into these final hours before dawn.

Little did he know, however, that this would be no ordinary wedding gown...
