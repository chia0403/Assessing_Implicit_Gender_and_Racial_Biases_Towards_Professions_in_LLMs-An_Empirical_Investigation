The mist-shrouded streets of New Haven City were always at their most unforgiving on nights like these. The fog rolled in off the river, casting a damp, impenetrable veil over the city's towering skyscrapers and cramped alleyways. It was the kind of night that made you want to stay indoors with a hot cup of coffee and pretend the world outside didn't exist.

But Kaidën Rylan Wynter was no ordinary citizen. As a member of the New Haven City Guard, he had sworn an oath to protect this city from the shadows, and tonight was no exception. His eyes narrowed as he watched the crowds of revelers spill out of the bars and clubs on 5th Street, their laughter and music carrying into the fog like a siren's call.

Kaidën's gaze lingered on one figure in particular - a young woman with piercing green eyes and hair as black as the night. She was a stranger in town, or at least she'd been until tonight. Kaidën had seen her flitting around the edges of the city's nightlife scene for weeks now, always just out of reach.

And he knew that she was trouble.

As a member of the guard, it was his duty to keep this city safe - but sometimes, the line between duty and instinct got blurred. And Kaidën had a feeling that tonight was going to be one of those nights...
