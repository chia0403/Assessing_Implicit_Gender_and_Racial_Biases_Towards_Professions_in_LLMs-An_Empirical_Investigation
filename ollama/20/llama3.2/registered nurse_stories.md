The mist-shrouded streets of Ashwood were always at their most unforgiving on mornings like this. Registered Nurse Emilia Rose Taylor stepped out of her small cottage and into the damp air, the chill of the autumnal dawn biting at her skin. She pulled her fleece-lined jacket tighter around her frame, the familiar weight of it a comfort as she began her daily routine.

Emilia had lived in Ashwood for five years now, ever since she'd left behind the bustling city to find solace in the quiet rhythms of rural life. As a registered nurse, she'd worked in various hospitals and clinics before settling into the local health center, where she'd found a sense of belonging among the community's gentlefolk.

As she made her way down the winding street, Emilia's mind was already focused on the day ahead. The clinic was gearing up for its annual flu vaccination drive, and she had a team to assemble, along with hundreds of patients to care for. But amidst the chaos of the upcoming event, one patient stood out in her mind - an elderly woman named Agnes, who'd been struggling with chronic illness since her husband's passing just last month.

The wind carried Emilia back into her thoughts, carrying the faint scent of damp earth and decaying leaves. It was a smell that always brought to mind the long walks she took through Ashwood's woods on crisp mornings like this - a time for reflection and solitude before the day's work began. Little did she know, today would be no ordinary day in Ashwood...
