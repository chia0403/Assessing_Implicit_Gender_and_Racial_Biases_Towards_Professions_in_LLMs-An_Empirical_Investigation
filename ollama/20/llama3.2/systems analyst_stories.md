It was a drizzly Friday evening in late October, and the city was bathed in the warm glow of streetlights casting long shadows across the wet pavement. The rain-soaked streets of downtown Seattle were eerily quiet, save for the distant hum of traffic on I-5.

In a small, cluttered office high above the city streets, systems analyst Emilia Victoria Reed sat hunched over her computer screen, her eyes scanning lines of code with a practiced intensity. Her dark hair was tied back in a messy bun, and a faint smudge of coffee stain marred the corner of her left eye.

Emilia had spent the day debugging a particularly recalcitrant database system, and now she was trying to make sense of a cryptic error message that refused to yield its secrets. As she worked, the sounds of her coworkers' afternoon chatter and the hum of the office fluorescent lights created a soothing background melody, punctuated only by the occasional clunk of a keyboard or the rustle of paper as someone turned a page.

As the clock ticked closer to 5 PM, Emilia's mind began to wander from the code in front of her. She was thinking about the weekend ahead, and the plans she had made with her best friend, Rachel. They were going to try out that new brewery on Capitol Hill, and Emilia was looking forward to a night of craft beers and good conversation.

But as she gazed blankly at her screen, a nagging feeling began to gnaw at the back of her mind. Something didn't feel right. And then, just as she was about to pack up and head out for the evening, Emilia's eyes landed on something that made her heart skip a beat...
