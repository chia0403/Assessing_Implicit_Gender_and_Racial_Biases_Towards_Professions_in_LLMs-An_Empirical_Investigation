The scent of aged paper and leather clung to Amelia Beatrice Blackwood like a second skin. It was a familiar comfort, a lullaby whispered by countless untold stories within the hallowed halls of Oakhaven Public Library.  

Amelia, with her unruly auburn curls perpetually escaping their braid and spectacles perched precariously on her nose, surveyed the hushed domain with an affection bordering on possessiveness. Sunlight streamed through stained-glass windows, casting rainbows across the worn wooden floors and illuminating dust motes dancing in the air. It was a haven, a sanctuary from the relentless clamor of the outside world, and Amelia, as its guardian, found solace within its silence.


But today, the peace was shattered. A frantic young woman burst through the oak doors, her eyes wide with terror, her voice a breathless whisper. "I need your help," she gasped, clutching a worn leather-bound book to her chest.  "They're coming for it."
