The air hung thick with the smell of disinfectant and stale coffee in the Emergency Room at St. Jude's Hospital. 

Eleanor "Ellie" Davenport wiped a smear of blood from her forehead, the sting barely registering against the backdrop of chaos that was the Tuesday night shift. A symphony of beeps, wails, and hurried whispers filled the space, punctuated by the barked orders of Dr. Ramirez. Ellie adjusted her stethoscope, its weight familiar against her chest, and surveyed the scene before her: a young girl clutching her arm, a man with a bandaged head muttering incoherently, a paramedic frantically trying to calm a frantic mother. 

Ellie took a deep breath, steeling herself for the night ahead. It was going to be a long one.
