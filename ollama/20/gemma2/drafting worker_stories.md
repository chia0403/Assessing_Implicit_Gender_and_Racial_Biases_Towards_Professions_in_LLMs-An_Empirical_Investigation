The fluorescent hum of the drafting room buzzed in Amelia Beatrice Cartwright's ears, a constant companion to the scratch of her mechanical pencil against the vellum paper.  Outside, the late summer sun cast long shadows across the dusty streets of Detroit. Inside, Amelia hunched over her board, the blueprints for the Ford Motor Company's latest prototype sprawled before her like a complex puzzle. 

Sweat beaded on her forehead, blurring the lines etched in graphite. It was nearing five o'clock, and the air in the room, thick with the scent of stale coffee and printer ink, felt heavy with anticipation. The new Mustang was due tomorrow, and Amelia, one of the youngest drafters in the entire company, was responsible for finalizing the design for the rear axle housing. Pressure mounted with every tick of the clock on the wall, a relentless metronome counting down to her deadline.
