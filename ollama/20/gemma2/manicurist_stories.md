The air hung thick with the scent of acetone and cherry blossom nail polish as Beatrice Lavender meticulously filed down a chipped pink nail.  Sunlight streamed through the dusty window of "Bea's Nail Nook," illuminating the worn linoleum floor and rows of colorful bottles lining the shelves. The bell above the door tinkled, announcing a new customer. Beatrice glanced up, her hazel eyes peering over her thick-rimmed glasses.

A young woman with unruly brown hair and vibrant green eyes stood hesitantly in the doorway. She wore faded jeans and a band t-shirt, a stark contrast to the prim clientele Bea usually catered to in this quiet suburban corner of Willow Creek.  


"Welcome!" Beatrice chirped, her smile genuine despite the slight apprehension prickling at the back of her neck. "What can I do for you?"
