The air in Anya Petrova's studio hung thick with the scent of burnt coffee and frustrated sighs.  A single lamp cast a harsh glow on her workspace, illuminating a mountain of crumpled sketches and discarded digital files. Outside, the Parisian drizzle lashed against the windowpanes, mimicking the chaotic rhythm of Anya’s thoughts. She rubbed her tired eyes, trying to decipher the jumbled mess of colour swatches and typography samples before her. Her latest project – a rebranding campaign for a trendy vegan bakery – was due tomorrow, and it was, to put it mildly, a disaster. 

Anya's signature bold aesthetic had fallen flat. The playful fonts felt childish, the vibrant colours jarring against the rustic charm of the bakery’s interior, and the overall message lacked the warmth and authenticity that her client desperately sought. She slumped back in her chair, defeated.  Maybe she wasn't cut out for this anymore.


This story is set in a small, cluttered graphic design studio tucked away on a cobblestone street in Montmartre, Paris.
