The air in the showroom hung heavy with the scent of fresh leather and desperation. Sunlight slanted through the panoramic windows, illuminating dust motes dancing in the stale air. Amelia "Amy" Beatrice Hawthorne, her heels clicking against the marble floor like a metronome marking time's relentless march, adjusted the lapel of her power suit. Another day, another car left unsold. 

The dealership, aptly named "Auto Haven," was anything but. Its gleaming facade couldn't mask the creeping decay beneath - chipped paint on the entrance pillars, faded brochures gathering dust in the corner, and a dwindling customer base that seemed to vanish as quickly as it arrived. Amy knew this wasn't just about selling cars; it was about survival.  


This wasn't how she envisioned her life after graduating top of her class from the prestigious Wharton School of Business. But here she was, battling against sinking sales figures and a looming sense of dread that gnawed at her ambition like rust on steel.
