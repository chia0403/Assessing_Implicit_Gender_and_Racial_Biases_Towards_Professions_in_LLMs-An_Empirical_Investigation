The air in Dr. Amelia Beatrice Hawthorne's office was thick with the scent of disinfectant and unspoken anxieties. A single shaft of late afternoon sun sliced through the dusty window, illuminating motes of dust dancing in the stillness. Outside, the city of Newhaven hummed with its usual frenetic energy, a stark contrast to the hushed tension within these four walls. Amelia, her usually vibrant auburn hair pulled back into a severe bun, sat at her desk, her brow furrowed as she studied a sheaf of x-rays. Her patient, a young woman named Eleanor Bellweather, had arrived just minutes ago, clutching a crumpled note from her doctor and the fear etched onto her face was palpable. 

"It says it's something inoperable," Eleanor whispered, her voice trembling, "but I don't understand...I feel fine."  


Amelia looked up, her gaze piercing but kind. The weight of Eleanor's uncertainty pressed down on Amelia, a familiar burden she carried every day. It was moments like these that made her career, this seemingly impossible dance between hope and despair, both exhilarating and utterly exhausting.
