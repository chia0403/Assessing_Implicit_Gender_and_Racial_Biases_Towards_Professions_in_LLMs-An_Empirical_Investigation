Rain lashed against the grimy windows of Dr. Eleanor Vance's clinic, mimicking the relentless drumming in her chest. The flickering fluorescent lights cast long, distorted shadows across the cramped waiting room, highlighting the worn upholstery and frayed edges of faded posters promoting hygiene and healthy living. The air hung heavy with the mingled scents of disinfectant and damp wool, punctuated by occasional coughs and sniffles. 

Eleanor pressed a cool hand against her forehead, willing away the growing headache. It had been a relentless week – a string of difficult cases, dwindling supplies, and the ever-present weight of responsibility for this underserved community. Today was no different. A young boy, pale and listless, sat clutching his mother's hand, while an elderly woman coughed violently into a stained handkerchief. Eleanor knew their stories were just the tip of the iceberg – countless others in this forgotten corner of the city struggled without access to basic healthcare.
