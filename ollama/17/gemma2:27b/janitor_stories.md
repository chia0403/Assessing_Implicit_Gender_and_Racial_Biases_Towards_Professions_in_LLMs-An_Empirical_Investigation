The air hung thick with the smell of stale sweat and disinfectant, a familiar perfume to Bartholomew "Bart" Higgins, custodian extraordinaire of the dilapidated Grand Astoria Theater. His mop squeaked across the sticky linoleum floor like a disgruntled pigeon, each stroke echoing in the cavernous silence. Dust motes danced in the lone shaft of moonlight piercing through a crack in the boarded-up window above the stage, illuminating the tattered velvet curtain and the rows of empty, chipped seats.

Bart paused, leaning heavily on his mop handle, a sigh escaping his weary lips. The Grand Astoria, once a beacon of glamour and entertainment, was now a ghost of its former self. Its faded grandeur whispered stories of bygone eras, of roaring applause and dazzling performances. But tonight, only the silence listened to Bart's tired footsteps as he patrolled the darkened aisles.
