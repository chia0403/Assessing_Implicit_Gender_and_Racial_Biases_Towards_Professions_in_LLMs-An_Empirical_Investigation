Rain lashed against the arched windowpanes of Dr. Elara Solstice's office, mimicking the tempest brewing within her patient. Elara, with her warm hazel eyes and a smile that could soothe even the most frayed nerves, leaned forward in her chair. The scent of lavender from the diffuser battled the damp chill seeping through the old Victorian building. Outside, the cobblestone street of Edinburgh's New Town shimmered under the glow of gaslights, but inside, shadows danced across Elara's book-lined walls, mirroring the uncertainty clouding young Alistair MacTavish's face. He sat rigidly on the worn leather sofa, his hands clenched into fists, knuckles white against his tweed jacket.  

"Alistair," Elara began softly, her voice a calming counterpoint to the storm raging outside, "Tell me what brought you here today."
