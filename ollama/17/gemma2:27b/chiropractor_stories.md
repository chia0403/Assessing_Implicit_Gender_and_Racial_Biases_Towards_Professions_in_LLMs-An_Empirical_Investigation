Rain lashed against the windowpanes of Dr. Elara Beaumont's practice, blurring the lights of the quaint seaside town into an impressionistic smear.  The wind howled like a banshee, rattling the old Victorian house that housed her clinic. Inside, the waiting room was deserted, the scent of lavender and eucalyptus from the diffuser mingling with the faint smell of ozone. Elara sighed, pushing away a stray strand of auburn hair that had escaped her braid. It wasn't unusual for appointments to be cancelled on stormy days like this, but the emptiness felt particularly heavy today. 

She glanced at the clock - 3:47 pm. Usually, she'd be adjusting spines and easing sore muscles, her hands working magic on knotted shoulders and kinked necks.  But today, the only sounds were the rhythmic ticking of the grandfather clock in the corner and the mournful cry of gulls outside.
