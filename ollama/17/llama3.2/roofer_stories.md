The sun was just starting to set over the small town of Willow Creek, casting a warm orange glow over the rows of quaint suburban homes. In one of those homes, on Elm Street, 32-year-old Jack Ethan Thompson was making his way up the ladder to the roof of the Smith's house. As a seasoned roofer with years of experience under his belt, Jack had seen it all - from leaky faucets to shattered skylights, and from crumbling chimneys to missing shingles.

Jack had grown up in Willow Creek, the son of a local hardware store owner who taught him the ins and outs of fixing just about anything. After completing his apprenticeship as a young man, Jack had struck out on his own, starting his own roofing business with a small loan from his father. Over the years, he'd built a reputation for himself as one of the best roofers in town - reliable, efficient, and always willing to lend a helping hand.

As he began to inspect the Smith's house, Jack couldn't shake the feeling that something was off. The family had called him out to fix a small leak on their porch roof, but when he arrived at the job site earlier that day, he'd noticed that there were signs of more extensive damage - cracks in the siding, missing shingles, and a general air of neglect about the entire place.

Now, as he stood on the Smith's roof, Jack couldn't help but wonder what was really going on behind those closed doors. And just then, his phone buzzed with an incoming text from an unknown number...
