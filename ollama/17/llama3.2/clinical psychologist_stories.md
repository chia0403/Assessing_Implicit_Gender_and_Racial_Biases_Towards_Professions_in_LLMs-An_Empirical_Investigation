Dr. Alexandra "Lexi" Thompson stepped out of her worn leather satchel and onto the dew-kissed grass of the old university quad, the early morning sunlight casting long shadows across the weathered brick buildings. As a clinical psychologist with a specialty in trauma and anxiety disorders, Lexi had spent countless hours studying these very same walls, listening to the stories of students and faculty members who had come to her for help.

The University of Michigan, Ann Arbor campus was a place where the past and present collided - where academic rigor and intellectual curiosity met the vibrant cultural landscape of a small Midwestern town. And Lexi, with her sharp mind and compassionate heart, was no exception. She had grown up in this very city, spending countless hours exploring its hidden corners and listening to the whispers of those who needed someone to hear them.

As she took a deep breath of the crisp spring air, Lexi felt a sense of unease settle over her like a shroud. It wasn't the first time she'd felt this way - a nagging sense that something was off, that the fragile balance of her life was about to be disrupted in ways she couldn't yet imagine.

And then, just as she was starting to feel like she could shake off the feeling, Lexi's phone buzzed with an incoming text from her best friend and colleague, Dr. Rachel Patel. The message was brief - a single sentence that sent a shiver down Lexi's spine: "Meet me in my office ASAP. I need to talk about something."
