Dr. Emilia Rose Wilson stepped out of her small apartment, gazing up at the mist-shrouded streets of Seattle. The gray dawn was slowly giving way to a pale blue sky, a stark contrast to the darkness that had been lurking in her mind since she arrived at the hospital earlier that morning. As a forensic psychologist with the city's most notorious crime unit, Emilia had seen her fair share of horrors - the faces of victims etched into her memory like scars.

She took a deep breath of the damp air, feeling the weight of her latest case settle upon her shoulders. The 17-year-old girl who had been found brutally murdered in an abandoned warehouse on the outskirts of town was still a blur in Emilia's mind - but one thing was certain: she would be haunted by this case for a long time to come.

Emilia checked her watch, noting that she had just enough time to grab a coffee before her meeting with Detective James "Jim" Thompson, the lead investigator on the case. As she walked towards the hospital, the sound of sirens and murmured conversations filled the air - a cacophony that seemed all too familiar in this city where darkness lurked around every corner.

She pushed open the door to the hospital's forensic unit, a small, cluttered room filled with the detritus of humanity's worst excesses. The smell of disinfectant and despair hung heavy over everything, a constant reminder of the work that lay ahead.
