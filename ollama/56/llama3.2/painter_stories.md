The sun had long since set over the small town of Ashwood, casting a warm orange glow over the quaint streets and homes. In a cozy studio above his family's antique shop, Emilia Rose Fothergill stood at her easel, brush poised mid-air as she gazed out the window at the fading light. The smell of turpentine and linseed oil wafted through the air, mingling with the scent of old books and dusty trinkets that filled the shop below.

As a painter, Emilia had always found solace in the quiet evenings spent lost in her art. Her latest piece, a moody still life of wildflowers and twilight skies, seemed to be coming alive on the canvas before her eyes. But as she turned back to her work, her mind kept wandering to the letter she'd received earlier that day from her estranged grandmother, which had set her thoughts spinning with questions about their family's past.

With a sigh, Emilia dipped her brush into a vibrant shade of indigo and began to block out the shapes of the wildflowers, letting the colors blend together in a swirl of blues and purples. As she worked, the world outside her window grew darker and more still, but inside, a storm was brewing.
