The sun had just begun to set over the bustling city of New Haven, casting a warm orange glow over the crumbling brick buildings and rusty factories that lined the streets. Amidst the hum of traffic and chatter of pedestrians, a lone figure emerged from the shadows of a dilapidated warehouse on 5th Street.

Ethan James Thompson, a ruggedly handsome drafting worker with a wild shock of curly brown hair and a scruffy beard to match, stepped out into the fading light. His worn denim jeans and faded plaid shirt seemed out of place amidst the steel giants and concrete canyons that dominated the city's industrial landscape.

As he lit a cigarette, the smell of smoke and grease wafted up from his fingers, mingling with the acrid tang of machine oil and the distant thrum of traffic. Ethan took a long drag, feeling the familiar burn in his lungs as he gazed out over the city he knew like the back of his hand.

It was a city that had seen its fair share of ups and downs – from the boom years of the 1920s to the crash of '29, and everything in between. But for Ethan, this gritty industrial town was home. He'd grown up on its streets, learned to fix machines and draw blueprints by the time he was a teenager. Now, at 25, he was making a name for himself as one of New Haven's top drafting workers – but with a secret that could change everything.

As he exhaled, a small piece of paper fluttered out of his pocket and drifted to the ground, carrying a message scrawled in hasty handwriting: "Meet me at the old clock tower at midnight. Come alone."
