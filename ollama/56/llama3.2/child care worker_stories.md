---

The sun was setting over the small town of Willow Creek, casting a warm orange glow over the quaint streets and homes. In a cozy little house on Elm Street, Childcare Worker Emily Victoria Jenkins was closing up shop for the day. She had spent the last eight hours playing with three-year-old Ava, reading stories to six-year-old Liam, and making sure that all of her nine students were fed and happy.

As she locked the door behind the last child, Emily felt a sense of satisfaction wash over her. She loved her job as a childcare worker - it was a chance for her to make a difference in the lives of young children, to watch them grow and learn, and to see the world through their curious eyes. But tonight, she wasn't thinking about work. Tonight, she was thinking about the strange phone call she had received earlier that day.

As she walked down the porch steps, Emily couldn't shake the feeling that something was off. The caller had been vague on the end, simply saying "you need to come to the old warehouse on 5th and Main" before hanging up. Emily had tried to brush it off as a prank, but the words still lingered in her mind.

She took a deep breath, trying to calm her nerves as she walked back into her house. She would deal with whatever was bothering her tomorrow, after all. But as she turned to go upstairs, she caught a glimpse of something out of the corner of her eye - a piece of paper on the kitchen table that she could have sworn wasn't there before.

Curiosity got the better of her, and Emily walked back over to investigate. What would she find?
