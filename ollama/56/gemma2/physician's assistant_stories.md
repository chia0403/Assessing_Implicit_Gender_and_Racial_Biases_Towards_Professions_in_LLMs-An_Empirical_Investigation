The humid Georgia air hung thick with the scent of honeysuckle and impending rain.  Margaret "Maggie" Hayes, her stethoscope hanging around her neck like a worn badge of honor, squinted through the grime-coated windshield of her aging pickup truck. The clinic loomed ahead, a faded brick building that had seen better days, much like the small town it served nestled in the foothills of the Appalachian Mountains.

Inside, the waiting room buzzed with a familiar symphony of coughs, sniffles, and whispered anxieties.  Maggie knew each patient by name, their stories woven into the fabric of her life here in Pine Ridge. She’d been Dr. Miller's right hand for five years now, navigating everything from sprained ankles to heart conditions, always striving to bring a sliver of hope to this forgotten corner of the world. 


But today felt different. A prickle of unease ran down Maggie's spine as she glanced at the clock – almost noon, and Dr. Miller was still nowhere to be seen.  Something wasn't right.
