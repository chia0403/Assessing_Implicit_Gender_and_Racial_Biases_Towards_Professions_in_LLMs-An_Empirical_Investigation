Rain hammered against the dusty skylight of Amelia Rose Cartwright's Brooklyn loft, each drop echoing through the cavernous space like a drumbeat. The rhythmic patter matched the frantic tempo of her heart. Spread across her worn drafting table were sketches, storyboards, and crumpled pages of script - all bearing the faint scent of stale coffee and desperation. Her latest project, "Ephemeral Echoes," a neo-noir thriller set in the shadowy underbelly of 1940s Los Angeles, was hemorrhaging money faster than she could secure funding.

Amelia, with her fiery red hair perpetually escaping its messy bun and smudged kohl lining her emerald eyes, stared at the screen of her ancient laptop. The spreadsheet glared back at her, a stark reminder of the ever-widening chasm between her vision and reality. She needed a miracle.
