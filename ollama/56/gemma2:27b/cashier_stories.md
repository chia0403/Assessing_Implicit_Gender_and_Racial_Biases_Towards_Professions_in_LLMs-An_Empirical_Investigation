The flickering fluorescent lights hummed overhead, casting a sickly yellow pallor over the aisles of "Bargain Bonanza." Emilia Rose Hawthorne sighed, her breath fogging the glass sneeze guard momentarily before dissipating into the stale air. Another Tuesday night, another endless stream of weary customers shuffling through her line, their faces etched with exhaustion and resignation. The scent of microwaved hotdogs and day-old coffee hung heavy in the air, a grim reminder of the monotonous existence Emilia found herself trapped in. Tonight, however, felt different. A prickling sensation danced across her skin, a whisper of anticipation that she couldn't quite place. As the bell above the entrance chimed, announcing the arrival of a new customer, Emilia straightened her apron and braced herself for the unknown.
