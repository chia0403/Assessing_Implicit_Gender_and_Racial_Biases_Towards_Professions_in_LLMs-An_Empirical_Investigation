The fluorescent lights buzzed impatiently overhead, casting a sickly yellow glow across the cramped waiting room. Evelyn Rose Cartwright sighed, pushing a stray strand of auburn hair behind her ear as she surveyed the scene before her. Five chairs were occupied: a young boy nervously fidgeting with his Spiderman action figure, an elderly woman clutching a worn handbag and muttering prayers under her breath, a businessman scrolling endlessly through his phone, oblivious to the world around him, a couple holding hands and exchanging worried glances, and a teenager slumped in the corner, headphones blasting music loud enough for Evelyn to feel the bass vibrating through the floor.

It was going to be another long day. 

As a phlebotomist at Mercy General Hospital, Evelyn was accustomed to dealing with all sorts of patients - from those who bravely faced their needles with stoicism to those who whimpered and trembled at the mere sight of her lab coat. Today felt different, though. A prickling unease settled in her stomach as she glanced at the overflowing biohazard bin, a testament to the relentless stream of blood tests that had already flowed through her hands this morning.
