Rain lashed against the grimy windowpanes of Thaddeus Bartholomew Quill III's office, blurring the already bleak cityscape beyond.  The air hung heavy with the scent of stale coffee and desperation. Thaddeus, a man whose once-sharp suits were now fraying at the seams, sighed heavily, running a hand through his thinning hair. Another rejection. This time, Mrs. Henderson had deemed his offer on her late husband's life insurance policy "insultingly low."

He couldn't blame her, really. The Bartholomew Insurance Agency wasn't exactly thriving these days. In fact, it was teetering precariously on the edge of bankruptcy, much like the rickety building it occupied. Thaddeus glanced at the peeling paint on the ceiling, a testament to years of neglect and dwindling clientele. 


He needed a win. A big one. Something to turn the tide and remind him why he'd chosen this career path in the first place – to help people navigate life's uncertainties, to offer them a safety net when the world seemed determined to tear it away.

But hope was a dwindling commodity in Thaddeus Bartholomew Quill III's world. Until a peculiar letter arrived, addressed in elegant calligraphy and sealed with crimson wax. It promised an opportunity unlike any other, one that could change everything…
