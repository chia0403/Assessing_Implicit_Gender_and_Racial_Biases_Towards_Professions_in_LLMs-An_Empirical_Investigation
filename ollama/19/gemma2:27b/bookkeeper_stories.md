Rain lashed against the grimy windowpane of Alistair Finch's office, blurring the already faded cityscape beyond.  The incessant drumming mirrored the rhythm of unease tapping in Alistair's chest. He chewed nervously on the end of his fountain pen, the acrid ink tasting bitter on his tongue. Spread out before him were the ledgers, their neat columns and carefully tallied figures suddenly seeming like a labyrinthine maze. Something was amiss.

Alistair Finch, a man whose life revolved around meticulous order and precise calculations, prided himself on being the unsung hero of Thornhill & Sons Import-Export. For twenty years he had balanced their books with unwavering accuracy, his ledger entries as predictable as the sunrise. But this new shipment, from the enigmatic "M. Lazarus" in Tangier, was throwing everything off kilter. The numbers simply didn't add up, and Alistair, for the first time in his career, couldn't decipher the anomaly.
