The humid air hung heavy in the makeshift clinic, thick with the scent of antiseptic and overripe mangoes. Sweat beaded on Eliza Ramirez's brow as she peered through her stethoscope at Mrs. Okeke's chest, listening for the telltale wheezing that indicated pneumonia. Flies buzzed against the flimsy plastic windowpane, a relentless soundtrack to the constant stream of patients that shuffled in, seeking relief from the tropical maladies that plagued their village.

Eliza, despite the sweltering heat and the exhaustion gnawing at her bones, felt a surge of satisfaction. This was why she'd left the sterile comfort of a bustling city hospital for this dusty outpost: to make a tangible difference, one patient at a time.
