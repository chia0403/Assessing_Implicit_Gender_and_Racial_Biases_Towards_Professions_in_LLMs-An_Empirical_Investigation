Rain lashed against the windowpane, blurring the neon glow of the "Open" sign hanging precariously in the doorway. Inside, Eleanor Beatrice Finch meticulously straightened her stacks of W-2s and 1099s, a frown etching its way across her usually serene face. The hum of the fluorescent lights seemed to amplify the silence of the tiny office, broken only by the occasional click-clack of her keyboard as she inputted data into her ancient tax software.

It was April 14th, and despite the torrential downpour outside, Eleanor's waiting room remained stubbornly empty. This wasn't unusual for a Friday night, but tonight, a nervous flutter danced in Eleanor's stomach.  She couldn't shake the feeling that something was amiss.

The bell above the door tinkled softly, jolting Eleanor from her thoughts. A figure, shrouded in rain and shadow, stood hesitantly in the doorway.
