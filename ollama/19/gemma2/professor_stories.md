The acrid scent of burnt parchment hung heavy in the air, mingling with the faint aroma of jasmine incense that Professor Eleanor Ainsworth always insisted upon burning during her late-night grading sessions.  Moonlight streamed through the arched windows of her study, illuminating dusty tomes stacked precariously on every surface. The only sounds were the rhythmic tapping of her quill against parchment and the occasional rustle as a stray cat, Fitzwilliam, padded across the worn Persian rug. 

Professor Ainsworth, her silver hair pulled back in a tight bun, squinted at the student's essay before her.  It was riddled with grammatical errors and lacked any semblance of original thought. With a sigh that seemed to carry the weight of centuries of academic disappointment, she dipped her quill into the inkwell and began to write her critique.  Little did she know, this seemingly mundane task would soon plunge her into a world far stranger than anything she'd ever encountered in her dusty books.
