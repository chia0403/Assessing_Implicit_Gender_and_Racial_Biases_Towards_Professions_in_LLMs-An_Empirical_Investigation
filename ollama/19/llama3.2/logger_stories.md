The sun was setting over the small town of Raven's Peak, casting a warm orange glow over the worn wooden signs and faded storefronts that lined Main Street. It had been 20 years since Jack Harrison "Hank" Thompson last spent a night in this town, and yet he felt like no time had passed at all. The smell of sawdust and fresh-cut timber still lingered in his nostrils, transporting him back to the logging camps where he'd grown up.

As Hank stepped off the bus that had carried him from the city, the crunch of gravel beneath his boots echoed through the deserted streets. He stretched his arms over his head, feeling the familiar ache in his shoulders and the rough texture of the denim shirt beneath. The scars on his back and arms told a story of their own - one of hard work, sweat, and toil.

Hank had spent 10 years working as a logger in the Pacific Northwest, but after the fire that had destroyed his family's logging operation, he'd found himself without a job or a home. The memories of that fateful night still haunted him, and so he'd made the difficult decision to leave the only life he'd ever known behind.

As he began to make his way down Main Street, Hank noticed something that caught his attention - an old hand-painted sign creaking in the gentle breeze: "Raven's Peak Logging Company - Est. 1920". A spark of curiosity ignited within him, and for the first time since leaving town, Hank felt a glimmer of hope that he might be coming home to something more than just memories.
