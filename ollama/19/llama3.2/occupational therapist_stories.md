Dr. Alexandra "Lexi" Thompson stepped out of her car and onto the cracked asphalt of the rehabilitation center's parking lot, surveying the familiar landscape with a mix of exhaustion and determination. As an occupational therapist specializing in neurological rehabilitation, Lexi had seen her fair share of injuries and disabilities - but none as challenging as the one she was about to tackle.

The Rehabilitation Center for Traumatic Brain Injuries (RBTBI) was a state-of-the-art facility located on the outskirts of New Orleans, surrounded by the lush greenery of the city's famous Garden District. It was here that Lexi had spent countless hours working with patients, helping them relearn basic skills and adapt to their new realities.

As she walked towards the entrance, the warm Louisiana air enveloped her, carrying the scent of magnolias and a hint of humidity. The sound of birds chirping and the hum of activity from within the center's walls created a symphony of sounds that Lexi had grown accustomed to over the years.

Lexi's eyes scanned the waiting area, where several patients were seated with family members or caregivers, their faces filled with worry and hope. She spotted her patient, a young woman named Sarah, sitting in a wheelchair, her eyes fixed intently on a puzzle she was attempting to complete. Lexi exchanged a warm smile with Sarah's mother, before nodding at the receptionist as she made her way towards Sarah.

"Good morning, everyone," Lexi said, her voice firm and confident. "Let's get started today!"

Little did Lexi know, this session would be unlike any other - it would push her to her limits, test her skills, and force her to confront the darker corners of her own past...
