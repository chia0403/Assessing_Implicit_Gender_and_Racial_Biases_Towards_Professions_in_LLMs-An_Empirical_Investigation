The sun was just starting to rise over the small town of Willow Creek, casting a warm orange glow over the sleepy streets and homes. The air was crisp and cool, carrying the scent of damp earth and fresh-cut grass from the gardens of the local estates.

In a small cottage on Elmwood Drive, groundskeeper Emmet Fletcher stirred in his bed, rubbing the sleep from his eyes. He yawned, feeling the familiar ache in his back from years of bending over pruning shears and hauling heavy loads of mulch. Despite the early hour, Emmet was already up and getting dressed, eager to get a head start on his day's work at Oakwood Manor.

As he made his way downstairs, the sounds of the manor's interior staff stirring grew louder - the clinking of dishes in the kitchen, the murmur of hushed conversations from the bedrooms above. Emmet smiled wryly to himself; he'd been working at Oakwood for over ten years now, and he knew every nook and cranny of the sprawling estate like the back of his hand.

With a cup of steaming tea in hand, Emmet set off towards the gardens, ready to tackle whatever tasks lay ahead - from pruning the roses to fixing the broken birdbath. Little did he know that today would be different. Today, something was off.
