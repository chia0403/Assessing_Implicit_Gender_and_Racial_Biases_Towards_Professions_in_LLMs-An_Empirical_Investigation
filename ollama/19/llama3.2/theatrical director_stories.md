The curtains were still warm from the final dress rehearsal when Alexandra "Lexi" Thompson stepped out of the dimly lit backstage area and into the bright lights of the city. The scent of greasepaint and sweat hung heavy in the air, mingling with the sounds of honking cars and chatter from the crowd outside the theater.

Lexi, a seasoned theatrical director in her mid-thirties, gazed out at the bustling streets of New York City's Theater District. Her own theater, the small but beloved "Red Door Repertory," was nestled between a vintage clothing store and a 24-hour diner on West 42nd Street. As she breathed in the familiar sights and sounds, Lexi felt a thrill of excitement - her new production, a gritty drama about the rise and fall of an iconic American rock band, was finally set to open.

With a deep breath, Lexi began to mentally prep for the long day ahead. Rehearsals had been grueling, but she knew that every moment was worth it - this was the culmination of months of tireless planning and collaboration with her talented cast and crew. As the director's instincts told her, there was something special about this production. She could feel it in her bones.

As she made her way to the theater's office to review the day's schedule, Lexi's mind began to wander back to the mysterious letters that had started arriving at Red Door Repertory a few weeks ago - letters with no return address, containing cryptic messages and warnings about the production. Lexi had tried to brush them off as mere pranks or overzealous fans, but the nagging feeling in her gut told her there was more to it than that...

And with that thought, the bright lights of New York City seemed to grow a little brighter, casting a spotlight on the tangled web of secrets and lies that Lexi's production was about to unravel.
