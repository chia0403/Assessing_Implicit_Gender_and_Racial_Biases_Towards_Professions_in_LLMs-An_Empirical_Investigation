The fluorescent lights hummed a monotonous tune over Amelia Beatrice Harding’s head, casting a sickly yellow glow over the spreadsheets on her screen. Outside the window of her sixth-floor office, Chicago shimmered under the hazy afternoon sun, a cityscape teeming with life that felt worlds away from the sterile confines of her cubicle. Amelia stared at the numbers, trying to reconcile them, but her mind was elsewhere. It had been six months since she’d moved back home after her divorce, and six months of burying herself in work, hoping to drown out the echoes of a failed marriage and a shattered dream. 

Her once vibrant auburn hair now hung limply around her face, her usual enthusiasm replaced with a weary cynicism. The only solace she found was in the predictable rhythm of numbers, the cold comfort of logic amidst the chaos of her personal life.  But today, something felt different. A restlessness pulsed beneath Amelia’s skin, a yearning for something more than the endless columns and rows before her.
