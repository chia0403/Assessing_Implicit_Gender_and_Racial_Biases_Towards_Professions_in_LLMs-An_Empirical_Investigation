The storm of the century was brewing over the sleepy coastal town of Ravenswood, its dark clouds gathering like an ominous omen over the quaint harbor. Inside the small, cluttered office of Electrician Edwin Fletcher III, the wind howled and the lights flickered, threatening to short circuit at any moment.

Edwin, a ruggedly handsome man in his mid-40s with a thick head of curly brown hair and a bushy beard, stood hunched over his workbench, his eyes fixed on the scattered array of wires, circuit breakers, and electrical diagrams spread out before him. He was known around town for his exceptional skills as an electrician, and had spent years building a reputation that earned him a loyal clientele among Ravenswood's residents.

As he pondered the peculiar problem at hand – a mysterious fault in the town's power grid that seemed to defy explanation – Edwin felt a shiver run down his spine. Something was off about this storm, something that didn't feel right. The air seemed charged with an electric tension, and he couldn't shake the feeling that tonight was going to be more than just another typical summer evening.

And then, as if on cue, the lights in Edwin's office went out, plunging him into darkness.
