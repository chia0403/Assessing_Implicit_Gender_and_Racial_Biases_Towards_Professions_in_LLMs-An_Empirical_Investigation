It was a drizzly autumn evening in Portland, Oregon, and the fog rolled off the Willamette River like a damp shroud, casting a misty veil over the city. Inside Mac's Tavern, where Emilia Mae "Mae" Thompson polished glasses with a white cloth, the air was thick with the smell of stale beer and yesterday's cigarettes. The neon sign outside flickered and cast an eerie glow on the faces of the patrons, who huddled in booths or stood at the bar, nursing their drinks.

Mae wiped down the counter for what felt like the hundredth time that night, her eyes scanning the room with a practiced air of detachment. She'd been tending bar here since college, and she knew every regular by name - there was Mike, the truck driver who always ordered a whiskey on the rocks; Rachel, the artist who sipped wine in silence while sketching in her notebook; and Tony, the local musician who played his guitar during happy hour.

As Mae poured another round of beers, the door swung open and a tall, dark-haired man strode in, shaking the rain off his jacket. He scanned the room, his eyes locking onto Mae with an intensity that made her heart skip a beat. For a moment, they just stared at each other - Mae frozen behind the bar, the stranger standing out like a sore thumb in the dimly lit tavern.

"Welcome to Mac's," Mae said finally, trying to sound nonchalant despite the flutter in her chest. "What can I get for you?"

The stranger smiled, and Mae felt like she was drowning in those piercing green eyes.
