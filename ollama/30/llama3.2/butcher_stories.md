The fog rolled in off the canal, shrouding the narrow streets of Aldgate in a damp, grey mist that seemed to cling to every surface like a damp cloth. In a small, cramped shop on Brick Lane, Emilia Helena Fletcher wrapped her hands around the worn wooden handle of her meat cleaver, the familiar weight of it a comforting presence as she prepared for another day of work.

As a third-generation butcher, Emilia had grown up surrounded by the sights and smells of flesh and blood - not just the customers who came to buy their meat, but also the endless parade of sausages, steaks, and roasts that lined her father's shelves. It was a trade she knew intimately, one that required a mix of skill, knowledge, and intuition that only came with years of practice.

Outside, the sounds of the market drifted in - the clanging of pots from the street vendors, the chatter of the shoppers haggling over prices, and the wail of sirens in the distance. It was just another day on Brick Lane, where Emilia's shop stood like a beacon among the rows of colorful stalls, its faded sign creaking in the breeze: "Fletcher's Fine Cuts" - a name that had been passed down through generations, and one that Emilia vowed to uphold with every cut of meat she sold.

Little did Emilia know, however, that this particular day would be different from all the others. As she began to prep for the lunchtime rush, a mysterious package arrived at her doorstep, wrapped in brown paper and twine, with no return address and no indication of who might have sent it...
