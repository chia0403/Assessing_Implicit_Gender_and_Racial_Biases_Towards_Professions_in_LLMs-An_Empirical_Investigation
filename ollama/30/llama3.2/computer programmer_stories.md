The rain-soaked streets of Seattle glistened like polished onyx as Evelyn Jane Thompson stepped out of her small apartment and onto the worn sidewalk. A fresh pot of coffee still lingered in her cup, its warmth seeping into her chilled bones as she wrapped her scarf tighter around her neck. The neon lights of the Space Needle flickered to life in the distance, casting a colorful glow over the grey urban landscape.

Evelyn took a deep breath, feeling the damp air fill her lungs as she gazed out at the drizzly morning. It was days like this that made her want to stay inside and code all day – the kind of foggy mornings where words felt lost in the void, but code seemed to flow from her fingers like magic.

As a junior software engineer at a mid-sized tech firm, Evelyn spent most of her waking hours staring at screens, wrestling with bugs, and wondering if it was all worth it. But when she wasn't trapped behind a keyboard, she loved to escape into the city's hidden corners – secret alleys, abandoned buildings, and underground art scenes that only revealed themselves on days like this.

Little did Evelyn know, today would be different. Today, her mundane routine would take an unexpected turn, one that would challenge everything she thought she knew about herself, her job, and the very code that flowed from her fingers like blood from a wounded heart.
