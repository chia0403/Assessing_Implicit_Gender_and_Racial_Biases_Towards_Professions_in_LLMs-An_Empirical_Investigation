The air hung heavy with the scent of antiseptic and lingering eucalyptus as Amelia "Amy" Hayes pushed open the door to her clinic. Sunlight streamed through the large windows, painting long stripes across the polished wooden floor. Her office, nestled in the heart of bustling downtown San Francisco, was a haven of calm amidst the city's frenetic energy.  

Amy adjusted her stethoscope around her neck, feeling the familiar weight of responsibility settle upon her shoulders. Today was another day to help people regain their mobility, ease their pain, and find strength where they thought they had none. 


Her first patient, a young woman named Sarah struggling with post-surgery recovery from a fractured hip, sat nervously on the examination table, biting her lip. Amy took a deep breath, ready to offer her expertise and compassion.
