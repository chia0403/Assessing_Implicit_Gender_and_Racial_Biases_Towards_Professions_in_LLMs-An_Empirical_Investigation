The stale scent of aging paper hung heavy in the air, mingling with the faint aroma of coffee that perpetually clung to Mildred Beatrice Periwinkle's cardigan. Her tiny office was tucked away in the back corner of Mr. Fitzwilliam's antique bookstore, a haven of dusty tomes and forgotten whispers. Sunlight streamed through the grimy windowpanes, casting long shadows across the worn ledger she meticulously balanced. 

Mildred, with her perpetually furrowed brow and spectacles perched precariously on the bridge of her nose, was a woman who thrived in order and routine. Every transaction, every receipt, found its rightful place within the rigid framework of her ledgers. Yet, today, something felt different. A prickle of unease danced along her spine, as if the usual comforting rhythm of numbers had been subtly disrupted. As she reached for another invoice, a stray feather drifted down from the ceiling, landing with a soft whisper on the open page before her.
