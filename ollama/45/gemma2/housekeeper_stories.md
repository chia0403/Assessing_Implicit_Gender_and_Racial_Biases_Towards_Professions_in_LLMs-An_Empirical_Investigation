The humid Georgia air hung heavy, thick with the scent of honeysuckle and jasmine. Cicadas thrummed a relentless symphony in the ancient oak trees that lined Magnolia Lane, their buzzing a constant companion to Amelia Rose Hawthorne's work. She moved through the grand old house, her worn linen duster trailing behind her like a ghost, her steps silent on the polished hardwood floors. Dust motes danced in the shafts of sunlight filtering through the lace curtains, illuminating the faded grandeur of the place - once vibrant with laughter and music, now cloaked in a stillness that felt almost oppressive. 


Amelia paused, tilting her head to listen.  There was a faint scratching from upstairs, followed by a rustle. She frowned, clutching the feather duster tighter. This house had secrets, whispered on the wind through the overgrown gardens and echoed in the creaking floorboards. Secrets she wasn't supposed to hear, wasn't meant to see. But tonight, something felt different. Tonight, those secrets seemed to be calling her name.
