Dr. Alexander Ryder Thompson stood at the edge of his grandfather's old wooden desk, running his fingers over the intricate carvings that adorned the surface. It was a tradition he'd carried on for years - every morning before starting his day as a chiropractor, he'd sit down in his grandfather's chair and go over the day's schedule one last time.

The sun-drenched room of Thompson Chiropractic was a relic of a bygone era, with its worn leather couches and vintage medical equipment that had been lovingly restored. It was a place where Alexander felt most at home - surrounded by the familiar scents of old books and liniment, he could shut out the chaos of modern life and focus on helping his patients find relief from their aches and pains.

Outside, the early morning mist hung low over the quiet streets of Oakdale, casting a mystical spell over the small town. It was a place where everyone knew each other's names, and Alexander felt grateful to be a part of it. As he sat down in the creaky old chair, he couldn't shake the feeling that something was off - a nagging sense of unease that had been growing inside him for weeks.

"Today's schedule?" he asked himself aloud, as if seeking guidance from his grandfather's watchful spirit.

He took out his trusty clipboard and began to scan through the day's appointments. There were three new patients scheduled for check-ups, including a young mother with a toddler-sized limp, a middle-aged businessman complaining of lower back pain, and an elderly woman struggling with chronic migraines.

As he delved into the details of each appointment, Alexander couldn't help but feel that this was going to be a day like no other...
