His day had been long and physically demanding, from dawn's early light until now, but it had also been rewarding. The crops were thriving, and the animals were healthy and content.

Elijah's eyes wandered to the old oak tree standing sentinel at the edge of his property. He had planted that tree himself when he was just a boy, and it had grown into a symbol of strength and resilience for the Jenkins family farm. Elijah felt a sense of nostalgia wash over him as he remembered the countless hours he'd spent playing beneath its branches, learning the ways of farming from his father and grandfather before him.

Just as Elijah was about to head back to the farmhouse, his phone buzzed with an incoming call. He hesitated for a moment, wondering who could be calling at such an hour, but something in his gut told him it was someone important.
