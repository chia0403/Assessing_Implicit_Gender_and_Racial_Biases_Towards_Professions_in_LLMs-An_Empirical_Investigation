The fog rolled in off the harbor, shrouding the streets of Ravenswood in a damp, grey mist. It was a typical morning in late November, the kind of day where the only sound was the distant hum of the city and the soft gurgle of water from the pipes that plumber Jack Edward Thompson relied on to keep his business afloat.

Jack, or Eddie as his friends called him, trudged through the misty streets, his worn boots squelching in the wet pavement. He had been up since 4am, already checked on the pipes at the local elementary school and was waiting for the call that would start his day - which usually came around lunchtime.

As he turned the corner onto Elm Street, he spotted a small note taped to the window of the old Victorian across from him. The words were scribbled in haste: "Eddie, please fix the leak at 345 Oak St ASAP." Jack's eyes narrowed as he recognized the address - it belonged to his old neighbor, Mrs. Jenkins, who was notorious for her cantankerous attitude and love of complaints.

Jack pulled out his phone and dialed the number on the note, already mentally preparing himself for a long day ahead. Little did he know, this would be just the beginning of a mystery that would lead him down a rabbit hole of pipes, secrets, and lies in the very heart of Ravenswood...
