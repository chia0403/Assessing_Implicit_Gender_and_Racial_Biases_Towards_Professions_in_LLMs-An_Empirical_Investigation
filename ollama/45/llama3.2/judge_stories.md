The sun had long since set over the bustling streets of Tokyo, casting a warm orange glow over the neon lights of the city. In a small, unassuming office nestled between towering skyscrapers in the heart of Shinjuku, Judge Kaito Yamada sat behind his desk, sipping a cup of bitter green tea.

At 55, Kaito was a respected figure in Tokyo's judicial community, known for his fair and impartial decisions. His years of experience had taken their toll, however, and the weight of the world seemed to bear down on him as he stared out the window at the city below. The hum of the traffic, the chatter of pedestrians on the street outside, it all blended together into a cacophony of sound that Kaito couldn't shake.

As he lost himself in thought, his mind wandered back to the case that had brought him to this moment - the trial of the wealthy businessman who stood accused of embezzling millions from his company. It was a high-profile case, one that had garnered widespread media attention and sparked heated debate throughout the city. And now, as Kaito pored over the evidence in front of him, he couldn't help but wonder if he had made the right decision.

The sound of his assistant's knock on the door broke the silence, jolting Kaito back to reality. "Judge Yamada?" a soft voice called out. "There is someone here to see you."

Kaito set down his tea cup and rose from his seat, his eyes narrowing slightly as he wondered who could be visiting him at such late hour.
