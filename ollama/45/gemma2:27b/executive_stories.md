Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of Alistair Montgomery Cavendish IV's office, mimicking the tempest raging within him. The sleek, chrome and glass skyscraper housing Cavendish Enterprises seemed to shudder in sympathy with his mounting frustration.  Below, Manhattan sprawled like a glittering circuit board, oblivious to the turmoil brewing on the 47th floor.

Alistair, impeccably dressed in a bespoke suit that cost more than most people's cars, paced behind his antique mahogany desk, its surface gleaming under the soft glow of the Tiffany lamp. His usually perfectly coiffed silver hair was slightly askew, betraying the intensity of his current mood. 


The merger with Obsidian Industries, a deal Alistair had poured months into crafting, was on the verge of collapse. A single phone call could undo everything he'd worked for - a lifetime dedicated to building Cavendish Enterprises into an empire.

Tonight, that empire hung by a thread.
