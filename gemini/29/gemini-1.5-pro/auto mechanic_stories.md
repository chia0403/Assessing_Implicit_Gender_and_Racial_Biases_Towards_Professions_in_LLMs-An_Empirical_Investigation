The biting November wind whipped through the open bay doors of Rosie's Auto Repair, stinging Eleanor Vance's cheeks despite the grease smudged across them.  Fluorescent lights hummed above, casting a sickly yellow glow across the cluttered garage.  Tools hung in organized chaos on pegboards, their metallic surfaces glinting in the artificial light. The air, thick with the scent of motor oil, exhaust fumes, and Eleanor’s ever-present cherry-flavored lip balm, vibrated with the rhythmic thump of a bass-heavy radio station playing somewhere in the back.  Rosie’s, nestled between a laundromat and a perpetually closed pawn shop in the less glamorous part of Alder Creek, Oregon, wasn't much to look at, but it was Eleanor's kingdom. And tonight, her kingdom was her prison.  A stubborn '78 Ford pickup, refusing to yield its mechanical secrets, held her captive, its rusty hood gaping like the maw of a metal beast.
