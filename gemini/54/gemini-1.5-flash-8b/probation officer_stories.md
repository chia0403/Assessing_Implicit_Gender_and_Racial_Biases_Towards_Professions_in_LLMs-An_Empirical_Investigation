The biting November wind whipped Amelia Hayes's trench coat around her legs as she hurried down the cracked asphalt path.  The air, thick with the scent of damp earth and decaying leaves, mirrored the oppressive atmosphere clinging to the small town of Havenwood.  Dust motes danced in the weak sunlight filtering through the skeletal branches of the ancient oaks lining the street.  Havenwood, nestled in the foothills of the Appalachians, had a reputation for quiet desperation, a place where secrets buried themselves deep as the roots of the ancient trees and whispered their dark tales to the wind.  Amelia, Probation Officer Amelia Hayes, was a stark contrast to the hushed, muted tones of the town.  Her dark hair, pulled tightly back from her face, and sharp, observant eyes suggested a resilience born of years spent navigating the jagged edges of troubled lives.  Today, that resilience was needed more than ever.  A new case, a case that smelled faintly of something rotten, had landed on her desk.  A seventeen-year-old boy, accused of a crime that, if proven, would shatter the delicate fabric of Havenwood's already fragile composure.
