The chipped porcelain sink, stained a faded rose by years of spilled polish and forgotten dreams, reflected Amelia Blackwood's anxious face.  Her manicured fingers, usually adept at coaxing life into brittle nails, trembled as she scrubbed at a stubborn smudge of dark crimson.  The tiny, sterile space of "Amelia's Polished Perfection," her small salon tucked between a bakery overflowing with the scent of cinnamon and a bustling antique shop filled with whispered histories, felt claustrophobic.  The rhythmic clatter of pots and pans from the bakery next door amplified the thump-thump of her own heart.  It was Wednesday, the day she was supposed to meet him, the day she had meticulously planned for the past three months.
