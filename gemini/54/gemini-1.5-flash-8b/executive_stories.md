Rain hammered against the panoramic windows of Alistair Finch-Hayden's office, mirroring the relentless drumming in his temples.  The view, usually a calming panorama of the city skyline, now seemed a menacing, grey blur.  He sat hunched over his mahogany desk, the polished surface reflecting the flickering fluorescent lights above.  Papers, reports, and memos—mountains of them—were piled haphazardly around him, testament to the relentless demands of his position as Executive Vice President of Global Acquisitions for ZenithCorp.  The air, thick with the scent of stale coffee and impending crisis, clung to him like a shroud.  He chewed on the end of his pen, his usually impeccable suit slightly rumpled, a stark contrast to the pristine, corporate sterility of the office.  The storm outside was more than just meteorological; it was a microcosm of the turmoil brewing within the company, and within him.
