The flickering neon sign of "The Rusty Mug" cast a sickly yellow glow over the rain-slicked cobblestones of Alderbrook.  The air, thick with the scent of roasted coffee beans and damp earth, hung heavy, mirroring the mood of Amelia "Amy" Butterfield.  Amy, a server at The Rusty Mug for the past three years, wished she could trade her worn, starched uniform for a cloak of invisibility. Tonight was the annual Alderbrook Festival, and the tiny, historic tavern was packed.  Every table was occupied, a symphony of clattering cutlery and hushed conversations battling with the rhythmic thump-thump-thump of a nearby street band.  A bead of sweat trickled down Amy's temple, pooling on her meticulously-applied lipstick.  Her perfectly-braided hair, usually a beacon of order, felt like a tangled nest.
