The flickering neon sign of "Wanderlust Wonders" cast a sickly green glow on Amelia Petrova, illuminating the dust motes dancing in the air.  Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of the travel agency, mimicking the relentless drumming of Amelia’s thoughts.  Orders were piling up on her desk—a flurry of crumpled itineraries, passport photos the size of postage stamps, and hotel reservation confirmations the colour of faded parchment.  Tonight, however, the rhythm of the office felt different, off-key. A knot of anxiety tightened in her stomach, a sensation as unfamiliar as a flight to the moon.

Amelia, with her perpetually rumpled trench coat and eyes the colour of a stormy sea, was a seasoned travel agent, a whirlwind of efficiency and enthusiasm for destinations far-flung and exotic.  She’d brokered trips to the Serengeti, charted cruises through the fjords, and orchestrated treks through the Himalayas.  But tonight, a whisper of something unknown, something unsettling, threatened to dismantle the carefully crafted facade of her profession.  The rain outside mirrored the chaos brewing inside her, a tempest of unspoken worries and mounting doubt about a single, crucial booking.
