Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of the clinic, a relentless drumming that mirrored the frantic rhythm of Amelia Hayes's heart.  The flickering fluorescent lights cast long, dancing shadows across the sterile white walls, highlighting the chaotic scene unfolding in the small, overcrowded waiting room.  Broken glass crunched under Amelia's boots as she navigated the throng of worried faces, their anxieties etched onto their expressions like lines on a weathered map.  Outside, the storm raged, mirroring the tempest brewing inside the emergency room of the isolated mountain town clinic.  Amelia, a Paramedic with the Arrowhead Mountain Rescue, clutched a worn stethoscope around her neck, the familiar weight a comfort against the mounting pressure. The year was 2024.
