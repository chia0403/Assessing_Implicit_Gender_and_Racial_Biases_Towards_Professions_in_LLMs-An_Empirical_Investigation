The scent of sawdust and linseed oil clung to the air like a persistent fog, swirling around Elias Thorne, a carpenter of considerable renown in the cobbled alleyways of Blackwood.  Blackwood, nestled between the towering spires of the Cathedral Quarter and the shadowed depths of the Whispering Woods, was a city built on secrets and whispered promises.  Its narrow, winding streets were a labyrinth of timber-framed houses, each with its own unique story etched into the weathered wood of its eaves and the intricate carvings adorning its window frames.  Elias, with his calloused hands and eyes that held the wisdom of a thousand planed boards, moved with the practiced ease of a man who had walked these same alleyways for a lifetime.  Today, however, a different sort of tension hung in the air.  It wasn't the usual hum of hammers and the rhythmic sawing that Elias was accustomed to.  This was a quiet, expectant hush, a precursor to something... different.
