The biting wind, a relentless, icy claw, whipped across the exposed steel skeleton of the new skyscraper, rattling the scaffolding and stinging Elias Thorne’s cheeks.  He hunched deeper into his insulated jacket, the breath fogging before him in a white cloud that quickly dissipated into the grey-blue expanse of the January sky.  Below, the city sprawled out like a fractured tapestry of glass and steel, a million tiny lights twinkling in the gloom.  This was his world, the dizzying heights of the construction site, where steel became skin and concrete became muscle.  Elias, a seasoned bricklayer with calloused hands and eyes that held the unwavering gaze of the desert sun, knew every nuance of this particular project – the intricate angles, the precise measurements, the relentless pressure to meet the deadline.  He had been a part of this city's growth for years, and now, this skyscraper, his current project, was the pinnacle of his ambition.  His days were measured not in hours, but in the progress of the building; in the way steel beams connected to form a new floor; in the weight of the concrete he meticulously laid.
