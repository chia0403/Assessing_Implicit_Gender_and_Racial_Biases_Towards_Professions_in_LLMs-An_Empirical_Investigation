The greasy haze hung heavy in the air of "Rusty Wrench," a small, cluttered auto repair shop nestled in the shadow of the towering, rust-eaten factory chimneys that lined the industrial heart of Steelport.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of sunlight piercing the grime-coated windows, illuminating a tangle of wrenches, sparking tools, and the perpetually-smoldering remains of a forgotten bonfire in the corner.  This was the domain of  Elias Thorne, a man whose hands, hardened by years of wrestling with engines and transmissions, held the key to the city's sputtering vehicles.  Elias, a man with eyes the color of polished steel and a laugh that often swallowed its own volume, was a master mechanic, his reputation as legendary as the city's own hard-won strength. But today, a different kind of repair awaited him, one that went far beyond the confines of rusted metal and fractured plastic.
