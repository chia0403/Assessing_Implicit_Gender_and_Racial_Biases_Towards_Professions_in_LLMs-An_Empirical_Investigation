The biting London wind whipped Amelia Ainsworth's crimson coat around her legs, stinging her cheeks as she hurried down the cobbled alleyway.  Rain, a relentless, pewter-coloured curtain, plastered the brickwork to a glistening sheen.  The air, thick with the smell of damp earth and something acrid, something indefinably *wrong*, hung heavy.  This was the infamous alley behind the Spitalfields Market, a labyrinth of shadowed doorways and forgotten courtyards, a place where whispers of the city's darker secrets clung to the air like the persistent drizzle.  Amelia, a forensic psychologist with sharp, intelligent eyes and a pragmatic approach to the chaotic, rarely felt truly safe, not even in the heart of the bustling metropolis.  Tonight, however, the unease was a physical presence, pressing against her, whispering tales of a crime more profound than any she had encountered.  Her notepad, clutched tightly in her gloved hand, felt strangely heavy, its pages awaiting the meticulous recounting of what she knew, or what she was about to discover.
