The chipped Formica countertop gleamed under the harsh fluorescent lights of the "Apex Strategies" reception desk.  Dust motes danced in the solitary shaft of sunlight that pierced the gloom, illuminating a single, half-eaten granola bar nestled amongst a mountain of promotional brochures and crumpled receipts.  Amelia "Amy"  Fitzwilliam, perpetually battling a caffeine-induced tremor, tapped a delicate, silver-plated pen against her notepad.  Another Tuesday. Another deluge of potential clients, each more demanding and eccentric than the last.  Today, the waiting room, usually a chaotic swirl of nervous energy, was oddly quiet.  A single, oversized armchair, upholstered in a faded floral print, sat forlornly in the corner, practically begging for a drama queen to slump into it.

The office building itself, a stark concrete monolith rising from the asphalt canyons of downtown Metropolis, seemed to breathe in the oppressive city heat.  Steel and glass reflected the unrelenting glare of the afternoon sun, creating shimmering distortions of the already stressful cityscape.  The air, thick with the scent of exhaust fumes and stale coffee, hung heavy, mirroring the weight of Amy's own anxieties.  Her tiny, almost invisible, desk, crammed with phone messages and overflowing in-trays, was a miniature microcosm of the chaotic world she navigated each day.  She longed for the quiet rustling of leaves and the scent of freshly cut grass, a world entirely absent from the sterile, unforgiving environment of her professional life.
