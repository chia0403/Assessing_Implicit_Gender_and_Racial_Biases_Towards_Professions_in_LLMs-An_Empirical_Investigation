The chipped paint of the easel, a faded ochre that mirrored the dust motes dancing in the lone shaft of sunlight slicing through the grime-coated window, was the only thing that felt solid in Amelia Seraphina Blackwood's world.  The room, crammed with canvases in various stages of completion – a swirling vortex of blues and greys, a chaotic symphony of charcoal and ochre – was her sanctuary, her prison.  Outside, the city of Veridia thrummed with the relentless heartbeat of industry, a symphony of honking taxis and the clang of factory machinery.  Inside, Amelia, her brow furrowed in concentration, struggled to capture the ephemeral essence of a dream on stretched linen, an elusive image that refused to fully materialize.  She stared at the blank canvas before her, a vast, unyielding expanse mirroring the emptiness she felt within.
