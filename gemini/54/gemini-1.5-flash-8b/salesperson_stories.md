Rain lashed against the grimy windows of the "Metropolitan Motor Mart," each drop a tiny, insistent hammer blow on Amelia Petrova's nerves.  The fluorescent lights buzzed, casting a sickly yellow glow over the showroom, highlighting the dust motes dancing in the air.  Today, despite the downpour, the lot was eerily empty.  Amelia, a sales representative with more experience than any of the newer faces in the showroom, sat hunched behind her desk, a half-eaten pastry crumpled in her hand.  The stale scent of car wax and disappointment hung heavy in the air, a palpable weight mirroring the quiet desperation she felt.  It was a Tuesday, a day that always seemed to bring a chill to her soul; a day often marked by the sting of rejection and the hollow echo of "no."
