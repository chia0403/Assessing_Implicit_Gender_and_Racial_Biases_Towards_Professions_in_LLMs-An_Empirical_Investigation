The humid air hung heavy in the drafting room, thick with the scent of old paper and stale coffee.  Sunlight, fractured by the grimy windowpanes, cast elongated shadows across the meticulously arranged drawing tables.  Amelia Petrova, a young woman with restless energy perpetually battling the oppressive heat, meticulously traced a blueprint of a bridge, her brow furrowed in concentration.  The rhythmic scratch of her pencil against the paper was the only sound besides the distant hum of the air conditioning, a barely perceptible sigh in the suffocating stillness.  Amelia, a drafting worker at the sprawling, century-old firm of  Architects & Engineers, Inc., was a vital, if often overlooked, cog in the intricate machine of urban development.
