The fluorescent lights of the accounting firm, "Numbers & Nonsense," hummed a monotonous tune, a soundtrack to the relentless clicking of keyboards and the occasional exasperated sigh.  Rain hammered against the grimy windows of the third-floor office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside Amelia Butterfield, a meticulous accountant with a penchant for spreadsheets and a soul yearning for something more.  Amelia Butterfield, twenty-eight years old, meticulously documented every transaction, every invoice, every penny, in the quiet solitude of her workstation.  Her small, cluttered office smelled faintly of stale coffee and old paper, a comforting aroma in its own way, but one that failed to mask the growing unease within her.  This week, the numbers seemed to be whispering secrets she wasn't quite ready to hear.  The numbers, she knew, were never wrong.  It was the people behind them that held the true mysteries.
