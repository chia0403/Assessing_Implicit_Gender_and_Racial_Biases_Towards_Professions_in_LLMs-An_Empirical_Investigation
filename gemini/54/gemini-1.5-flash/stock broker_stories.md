The rain lashed against the panoramic windows of Avery Sterling's office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside him.  Avery James Sterling, a name whispered with reverence – and sometimes, fear – in the hallowed halls of the New York Stock Exchange, stared out at the city lights blurring into streaks of neon and grey. Below, the concrete canyons churned with the relentless pulse of Wall Street, a symphony of ambition and anxiety that thrummed in his own veins. His penthouse office, perched atop the gleaming spire of the Sterling & Sterling building – a testament to his family's legacy and his own ruthless climb to the top – offered a breathtaking view, but tonight, it felt like a gilded cage. The air hung thick with the scent of expensive leather and impending doom.  The flickering screen of his Bloomberg terminal displayed a cascade of red, a chilling prelude to the chaos that was about to unfold.
