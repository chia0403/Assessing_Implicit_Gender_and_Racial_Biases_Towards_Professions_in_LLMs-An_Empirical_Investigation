The scent of vanilla and burnt sugar hung heavy in the air, a comforting perfume that usually calmed Isabelle “Izzy” Moreau’s nerves.  But today, the cloying sweetness did little to soothe the knot of anxiety tightening in her stomach.  Izzy, owner and sole operator of "Izzy's Indulgences," a quaint, sun-drenched salon nestled on a cobblestone side street in the historic district of Charleston, South Carolina, stared out the window at the relentless downpour.  The rain hammered against the antique windowpanes, mirroring the chaotic rhythm of her heart.  Charleston’s charm, usually a comforting backdrop to her days of pampering clients, felt oppressive, the Spanish moss dripping from the ancient oaks like mournful tears.  Today, the allure of the city’s pastel-colored houses and wrought-iron balconies was lost beneath a grey, foreboding sky.  Her usual cheerful salon, with its vintage hair dryers and plush velvet chairs, felt strangely empty, the only sound the rhythmic tick-tock of a grandfather clock in the corner, counting down to a moment Izzy dreaded.
