The acrid tang of antiseptic hung heavy in the air, a familiar perfume to Dr. Eleanor Vance.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of her office, blurring the already fading twilight over the San Francisco Bay.  Eleanor, perched on a stool, meticulously cleaned her instruments – a gleaming array of probes, forceps, and miniature saws reflecting the city lights like a scattered constellation.  The rhythmic click of her tools against the stainless steel tray was the only sound besides the relentless drumming of the rain, a counterpoint to the quiet hum of anticipation that always preceded a night clinic.  Tonight, however, the anticipation felt sharper, edged with a prickle of unease that had nothing to do with the usual pre-procedure jitters.  Her last patient of the day, a Mr. Silas Blackwood, had called with a rather… unusual complaint.
