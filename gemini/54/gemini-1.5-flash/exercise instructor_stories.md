The chipped turquoise paint of the "Sunrise Fitness" sign flaked onto the cracked asphalt of the parking lot, mirroring the slow, agonizing decay of Amelia Grace Hawthorne's enthusiasm.  Amelia, a woman whose carefully sculpted physique belied the weariness etched around her eyes, stared at the deserted aerobics studio.  Dawn painted the sky in bruised purples and hesitant oranges, a stark contrast to the vibrant, energetic colours she usually projected.  Inside, the mirrored walls reflected the emptiness, mocking the usually pulsating rhythm of thumping bass and panting bodies.  The only sound was the mournful creak of the rusty swing door, a soundtrack to Amelia's dwindling hope.  For the first time in fifteen years, her Tuesday morning spin class was completely empty.
