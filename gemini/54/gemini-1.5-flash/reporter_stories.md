The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Hernandez’s hands, a meager comfort against the November chill that clawed its way through the drafty windows of her cramped office. Rain lashed against the grimy panes, mirroring the turmoil churning in her gut.  Amelia, a seasoned reporter for the struggling *San Francisco Chronicle*, stared out at the city sprawling below, a labyrinth of glistening lights and shadowy alleys.  The Golden Gate Bridge, usually a beacon of hope, was swallowed by a swirling fog, mirroring the uncertainty that clung to her latest assignment – a seemingly innocuous missing person's case that smelled suspiciously like something far more sinister.  The city, usually vibrant and alive, felt heavy tonight, burdened by secrets hidden beneath its glittering facade.
