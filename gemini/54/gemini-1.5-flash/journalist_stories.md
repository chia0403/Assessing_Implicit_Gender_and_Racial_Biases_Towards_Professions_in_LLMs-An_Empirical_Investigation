The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Hathaway’s hair across her face, stinging her cheeks a vibrant crimson that clashed spectacularly with the drab grey of the Edinburgh cityscape.  Rain, a fine, persistent drizzle, plastered her trench coat to her back, the weight of it a physical manifestation of the heavy story she was chasing.  The Royal Mile, usually teeming with tourists snapping photos of its ancient, crooked buildings, was deserted save for the occasional hunched figure hurrying towards the refuge of a pub.  Only the mournful cry of a gull, lost and lonely above the grey stone, broke the silence, a sound that mirrored the desolate feeling churning in Amelia’s gut.  Tonight, the legendary beauty of Edinburgh felt bleak, a fitting backdrop to the secrets she was determined to unearth.
