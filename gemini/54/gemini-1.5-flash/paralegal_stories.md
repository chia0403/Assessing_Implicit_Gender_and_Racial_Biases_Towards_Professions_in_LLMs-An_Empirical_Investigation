The flickering neon sign of Benny’s Bar & Grill cast a lurid green glow across the rain-slicked asphalt of Bleecker Street.  Rain lashed against the windowpanes of the small, cramped office, a relentless rhythm that mirrored the frantic pounding of Amelia Hernandez’s heart.  The city outside, a symphony of sirens and shouting, felt a world away from the stuffy, dimly lit room where Amelia, a paralegal with a reputation for meticulous organization and an even more meticulous avoidance of drama, stared at the single, crumpled photograph on her desk.  It depicted a smiling young man with kind eyes and a mischievous grin – a man who, just twenty-four hours ago, had been alive.  Now, he was the subject of a murder investigation that threatened to unravel everything Amelia thought she knew about her carefully constructed life.
