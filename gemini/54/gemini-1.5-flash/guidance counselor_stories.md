The chipped paint of the "Guidance" sign above Ms. Eleanor Vance's door seemed to mirror the cracks forming in the foundation of her carefully constructed composure.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of Northwood High, blurring the already indistinct cityscape into a watercolor wash of grey.  Inside, the air hung thick with the scent of old coffee and teenage angst, a familiar perfume Eleanor had grown accustomed to over her twenty years at the school.  The fluorescent lights hummed a monotonous tune, a counterpoint to the frantic tapping of her pen against the overflowing in-tray – a testament to the anxieties and uncertainties of the students she was sworn to shepherd.  Today, however, the anxieties felt particularly acute, even for a woman who’d seen it all, or so she thought.  A crumpled note lay on top of the pile, its hastily scrawled message promising to unravel the already precarious calm of her Tuesday afternoon.
