The fluorescent lights of Beatrice "Bea" McMillan's office hummed a monotonous tune, a counterpoint to the frantic tapping of her keyboard.  Dust motes danced in the pale light, illuminating the stacks of tax forms that threatened to topple like precarious Jenga towers.  February in Syracuse meant a blizzard outside – a swirling chaos of white mirroring the organized, yet equally overwhelming, chaos within Bea's small, cramped office above Luigi's Pizza.  The aroma of garlic and oregano, a constant companion throughout the tax season, mingled with the scent of stale coffee and desperation – the latter emanating mostly from Bea herself.  This year, the mountain of paperwork seemed higher, the deadlines closer, and the clients… well, the clients were always a mixed bag. But this year, they felt particularly… volatile.
