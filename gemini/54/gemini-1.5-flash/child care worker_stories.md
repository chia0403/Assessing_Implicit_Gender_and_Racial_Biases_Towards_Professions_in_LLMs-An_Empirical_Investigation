The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s hair across her face as she hurried down Maple Street, the scent of woodsmoke and damp earth clinging to the air.  Her breath plumed out in frosty clouds, each exhale a small, white testament to the frigid early morning.  Overhead, the skeletal branches of ancient oak trees clawed at the bruised purple sky, their silhouettes stark against the weak, watery sunlight.  Amelia, bundled in a thick, navy-blue coat that swallowed her slight frame, clutched a steaming mug of coffee in one hand and a worn, leather-bound book of children’s rhymes in the other.  Her destination: "Sunshine Valley" Daycare, a rambling Victorian house perched precariously on the edge of Blackwood Forest, its paint peeling like sunburnt skin and its windows like vacant eyes staring out at the encroaching woods.  Inside, a cacophony of small voices – some cheerful, some shrill – waited for her, a daily symphony she both cherished and dreaded.
