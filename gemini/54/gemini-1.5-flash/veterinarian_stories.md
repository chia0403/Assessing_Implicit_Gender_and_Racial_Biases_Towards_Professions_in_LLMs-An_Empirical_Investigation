The biting wind whipped Dr. Evelyn Reed’s auburn braid across her face as she wrestled the recalcitrant gate open.  It groaned a protest, rusty hinges screeching a mournful counterpoint to the mournful bleating echoing from within the dilapidated barn.  The air hung thick with the smells of damp earth, decaying hay, and something else… something acrid and unsettling, like burnt sugar mixed with fear.  This wasn't a typical farm call; the sprawling, neglected property of Blackwood Manor, perched precariously on the edge of the windswept moors of Cornwall, was known for its eccentric owner and even more eccentric livestock. Evelyn tugged her thick wool coat tighter, the setting sun casting long, skeletal shadows from the gnarled oak trees that clawed at the stormy sky.  Tonight, however, the unease went beyond the usual quirks of the Cornish countryside.  Something felt profoundly wrong.
