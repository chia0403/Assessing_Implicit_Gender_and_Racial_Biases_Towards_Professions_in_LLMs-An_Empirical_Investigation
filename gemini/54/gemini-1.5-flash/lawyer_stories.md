The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s perfectly tailored wool coat around her legs as she hurried across the cobblestones of Chancery Lane.  Rain, a relentless, icy drizzle, plastered her dark hair to her scalp and threatened to ruin the pristine white of her blouse.  The scent of damp earth and woodsmoke, usually comforting, felt oppressive tonight, clinging to her like the chill that seeped into her bones.  London, usually a city that pulsed with a vibrant, almost aggressive energy, felt subdued, its usual cacophony muted by the relentless downpour.  Amelia, however, felt anything but subdued.  A knot of anxiety, tight and cold as the London night, coiled in her stomach.  The flickering gaslights of Lincoln’s Inn cast long, distorted shadows, transforming the familiar streets into a labyrinth of uncertainty.  Tonight, her meticulously constructed world of legal precision and controlled chaos teetered on the edge of something far more unpredictable – and potentially devastating.
