The chipped paint of the windowsill was cold against Dr. Eleanor Vance’s cheek as she watched the October wind whip fallen leaves into frenzied spirals across the Oakhaven High School quadrangle.  A lone crow perched on the weather vane of the bell tower, its obsidian eyes glinting like polished stones.  The late afternoon sun cast long, skeletal shadows from the gothic architecture, imbuing the normally bustling school with an unsettling stillness. It was the kind of stillness that preceded a storm, both meteorological and otherwise.  Eleanor, a school psychologist with a penchant for tweed jackets and a weariness etched deep into her expressive hazel eyes, knew this silence all too well.  It was the prelude to another crisis, another unraveling thread in the complex tapestry of teenage life within Oakhaven's hallowed – and increasingly troubled – halls.
