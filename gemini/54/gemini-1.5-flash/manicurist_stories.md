The scent of acetone and lavender hung thick in the air, a familiar perfume to Evangeline "Evie" Moreau.  Her small, brightly painted salon, "Nails of the Nile," nestled incongruously between a tire shop and a pawnbroker on the grittier edge of downtown Charleston, buzzed with the low hum of conversation and the whirring of the electric nail file.  Outside, the Charleston heat shimmered off the asphalt, turning the cobblestone streets into wavering mirages.  But inside, Evie's world was a sanctuary of calm, a riot of colour and meticulously organized polish bottles, a testament to her methodical nature.  Today, however, the usual tranquility was threatened by a storm brewing in the form of a chipped, crimson nail belonging to Mrs. Vandergelt, a woman whose social standing was as formidable as her perfectly sculpted coiff.  Evie braced herself, knowing that a simple nail repair was about to become anything but.
