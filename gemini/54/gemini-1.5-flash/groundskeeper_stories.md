The rusty hinges of the gate groaned a mournful protest as Elias Thorne pushed it open, the sound swallowed almost instantly by the vastness of Blackwood Manor's grounds.  A thick, clinging mist, the breath of the approaching autumn, clung to the ancient oaks, their skeletal branches clawing at the bruised purple sky.  Elias, his weathered face etched with the map of countless seasons spent tending this sprawling estate, adjusted the worn leather strap of his shears.  The air, sharp with the scent of decaying leaves and damp earth, held a stillness that was almost oppressive, broken only by the occasional rustle of unseen creatures in the overgrown shrubbery.  Blackwood Manor itself, a gothic behemoth of grey stone looming in the distance, seemed to watch him with its myriad empty windows, silent and watchful as a brooding owl.
