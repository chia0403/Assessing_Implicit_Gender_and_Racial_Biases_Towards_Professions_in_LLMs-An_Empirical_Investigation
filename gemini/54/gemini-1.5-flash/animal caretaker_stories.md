The biting November wind whipped Amelia Katherine Holloway’s hair across her face as she wrestled a recalcitrant bale of hay.  The air, thick with the scent of damp earth and woodsmoke, carried the faint, sweeter aroma of pine needles and something musky – likely the resident badger, Bartholomew, asserting his territorial claim once more.  Amelia, bundled in layers of wool beneath her mud-caked overalls, muttered a string of frustrated curses under her breath.  The setting sun cast long, skeletal shadows across the sprawling grounds of Blackwood Manor, a crumbling Victorian edifice that served as both her workplace and her solitary refuge.  Its gothic grandeur, once a symbol of opulence, now leaned precariously towards decay, mirroring the state of its neglected menagerie – a collection of rescued and abandoned animals that Amelia, with unwavering devotion, had taken under her wing.
