The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Katherine Finch's hands, a meager counterpoint to the November chill seeping through the drafty window of her office.  Rain lashed against the panes, a relentless drumming that mirrored the persistent thrum of anxiety in her chest.  Amelia, a bookkeeper of impeccable – if slightly obsessive – habits, stared out at the sodden alleyway behind "Finch & Sons, Chartered Accountants," the family business she’d inherited along with a mountain of debt and a lingering suspicion her late father hadn't been as meticulously honest as his ledger books suggested. The air hung thick with the smell of damp wool and the faint, metallic tang of rust – a scent that, inexplicably, always seemed to precede trouble.  Tonight, trouble smelled particularly potent.
