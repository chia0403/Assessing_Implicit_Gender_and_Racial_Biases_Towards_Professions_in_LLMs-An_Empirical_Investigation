The acrid bite of woodsmoke clung to Captain Elias Thorne's throat, a familiar discomfort that did little to dampen the adrenaline surging through him.  Flames, a hungry orange maw, devoured the dry pines of the Redwood National Park with terrifying speed, their crackle a monstrous symphony against the backdrop of the howling wind.  The air itself shimmered with heat, a hazy orange-red curtain obscuring the already fading twilight.  Elias, sweat plastering his firefighting gear to his broad shoulders, squinted through the smoke, the orange glow reflecting in his weary, but determined, blue eyes.  His battered helmet, scorched and scarred from countless battles with fire, provided little respite from the relentless heat.  This wasn't just another wildfire; this was a beast, a raging inferno fueled by a week of unrelenting drought and driven by winds that felt like the breath of a vengeful god.
