The humid air hung heavy, thick with the scent of frying plantains and simmering sofrito.  Cicadas buzzed a relentless rhythm against the backdrop of salsa music spilling from a nearby bodega.  Sweat beaded on Chef Aurelia “Rella” Hernandez’s brow as she surveyed the chaotic yet organized ballet of her kitchen.  Rella, a woman whose fiery spirit matched the habanero peppers she wielded with such skill, stood amidst a flurry of stainless steel, the rhythmic clang of pots and pans a familiar lullaby.  Her restaurant, *La Cocina de Abuela*, nestled on a cobbled street in the heart of  Old San Juan, Puerto Rico, was a vibrant tapestry of family recipes, island spices, and Rella's own fiercely independent spirit. Tonight, however, the usual comforting chaos felt charged, pregnant with an unspoken tension that even the intoxicating aroma of her mofongo couldn't quite mask.
