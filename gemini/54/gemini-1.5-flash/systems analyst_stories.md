The stale air of the server room hung heavy with the scent of ozone and desperation.  Fluorescent lights hummed a discordant symphony above Amelia Hernandez, their flickering glow reflecting in the sweat beading on her forehead.  Amelia, a systems analyst for NovaTech Solutions, hadn't slept properly in three days.  The rhythmic whirring of hard drives and the frantic blink of indicator lights were the soundtrack to her current existential crisis.  It wasn't just another software glitch; this was a full-blown, company-crippling catastrophe, and Amelia, with her caffeine-fueled resolve and a rapidly depleting supply of energy bars, was the only one standing between NovaTech and utter digital annihilation.  The air conditioning, predictably, had given up the ghost hours ago, adding a layer of oppressive heat to the already fraught atmosphere.
