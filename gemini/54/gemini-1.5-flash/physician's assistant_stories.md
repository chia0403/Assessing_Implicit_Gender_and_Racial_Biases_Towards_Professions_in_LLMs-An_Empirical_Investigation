The biting November wind whipped Amelia Katherine Hayes’s hair across her face as she hurried down the cobblestone street, the collar of her worn wool coat pulled high.  The air, thick with the scent of woodsmoke and brine, carried the mournful cry of gulls circling the harbour, a sound that somehow felt strangely fitting for the grim, grey day.  Above, the skeletal branches of the ancient oak trees clawing at the bruised purple sky mirrored the turmoil brewing within her.  Amelia, a Physician’s Assistant at the small, underfunded St. Jude’s Clinic nestled on the windswept cliffs overlooking the turbulent waters of the North Sea, was late.  Excruciatingly, unforgivably late.  And the patient waiting for her, a grizzled fisherman named Silas Blackwood, wasn't known for his patience.  The rhythmic crash of waves against the rocks below was a constant, almost hypnotic reminder of the unforgiving nature of both the sea and the life it sustained – a life Amelia, despite her exhaustion, was sworn to protect.
