The chipped mug warmed Amelia Hernandez’s hands, the lukewarm chamomile doing little to soothe the icy dread churning in her stomach. Rain lashed against the panoramic window of her Soho apartment, blurring the already hazy cityscape into a watercolour wash of greys and blacks.  Amelia, a graphic designer with a penchant for bold colours and even bolder fonts, felt a stark contrast between the vibrant chaos of her digital world and the muted, melancholic reality pressing down on her.  Her deadline for the Blackwood & Sons rebranding campaign loomed – a campaign that, if she failed, could sink her small freelance business faster than a lead balloon. The scent of burnt coffee – a testament to her all-nighter – hung heavy in the air, a pungent reminder of the pressure mounting with each tick of the rain-streaked clock.
