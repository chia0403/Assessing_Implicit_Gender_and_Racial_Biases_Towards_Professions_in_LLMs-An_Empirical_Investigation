The sterile scent of antiseptic hung heavy in the air, a stark contrast to the humid, almost cloying perfume of the night-blooming jasmine clinging to the wrought-iron balconies outside.  Dr. Elias Thorne, his hands scrubbed raw and his brow slick with perspiration, felt the familiar tremor of adrenaline coursing through his veins.  He wasn't in a gleaming, modern operating theatre; instead, he was hunched over a makeshift operating table fashioned from a sturdy wooden crate, the faint moonlight filtering through the cracked panes of a window illuminating the grim scene before him.  The air, thick with the scent of woodsmoke and something else… something metallic and sickeningly sweet… spoke of a desperate situation far removed from the polished halls of the city hospital he usually called home.  He was in the heart of the Haitian countryside, a world away from everything he knew.
