The chipped ceramic mug warmed Amelia Katherine Finch’s fingers, a small comfort against the biting November wind howling outside the window of her cramped office.  Rain lashed against the grimy panes, mirroring the storm brewing inside her.  Amelia, a meticulous accountant with a reputation for unflappable precision, felt anything but precise.  Her usually pristine desk, a testament to her ordered mind, was a battlefield of scattered papers, each bearing the crimson stamp of the Blackwood Group, a corporation as notoriously ruthless as it was obscenely wealthy.  The air hung thick with the scent of stale coffee and the impending dread of a looming deadline – a deadline that threatened not only her bonus, but possibly her entire career.  The flickering fluorescent lights above hummed a discordant tune to the drumming rain, a soundtrack to Amelia's escalating anxiety. This wasn't just any audit; this was the Blackwood Group. And something was deeply, unsettlingly wrong.
