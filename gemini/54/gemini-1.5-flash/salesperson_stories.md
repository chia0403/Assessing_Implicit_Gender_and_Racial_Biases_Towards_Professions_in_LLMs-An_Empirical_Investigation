The humid Louisiana air hung heavy, thick with the scent of honeysuckle and impending rain.  Cicadas buzzed a relentless, almost frantic symphony, a soundtrack to the simmering frustration bubbling inside Evangeline “Evie” Dubois.  Evie, a seasoned sales representative for "Cajun Creations," a purveyor of handcrafted spice blends and gourmet jellies, slumped onto the cracked, paint-peeling porch swing of her latest prospect, Mrs. Mathilde Broussard.  The screen door slammed shut behind her with a weary sigh, echoing the weariness in Evie’s own soul.  This was her fifth “no” of the day, and the sun hadn’t even begun its descent.  Mrs. Broussard's sprawling, moss-draped antebellum home, beautiful as it was, felt more like a gilded cage than a potential customer.  The air conditioning hummed a condescending tune within its thick walls, a stark contrast to the stifling heat Evie was enduring outside.
