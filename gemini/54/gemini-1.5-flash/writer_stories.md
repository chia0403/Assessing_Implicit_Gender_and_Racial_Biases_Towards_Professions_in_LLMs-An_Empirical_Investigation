The chipped paint of the windowsill dug into Elara Jean Blackwood's elbows as she stared out at the churning grey sea.  November’s wind, a snarling beast with teeth of icy rain, clawed at the cottage’s weathered timbers, rattling the panes like skeletal fingers.  Elara, her auburn hair escaping its messy bun to whip around her face, shivered, not entirely from the cold.  The cottage, perched precariously on the windswept cliffs of Cornwall’s dramatic southern coast, was as isolated as her mood.  Inside, the only sounds louder than the gale were the rhythmic tick-tock of a grandfather clock in the hall and the frantic scratching of her pen, a desperate attempt to wrestle words from the turbulent landscape mirroring her own internal chaos.  Her deadline loomed, a monstrous wave threatening to consume her, and the blank page mocked her with its pristine emptiness.
