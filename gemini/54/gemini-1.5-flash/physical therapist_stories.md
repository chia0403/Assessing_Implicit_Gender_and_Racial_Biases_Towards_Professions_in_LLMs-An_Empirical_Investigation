The salt spray stung Dr. Eleanor Vance’s face as she wrestled the stubborn fishing boat door inward.  The air, thick with the smell of brine and decaying seaweed, hung heavy and damp.  The tiny harbor of Port Blossom, nestled on the rugged, windswept coast of Maine, was a picturesque postcard come to life – if postcards featured peeling paint, rusting lobster traps stacked haphazardly, and the ever-present threat of rogue waves crashing against the weathered granite cliffs.  Eleanor, however, wasn't here for the postcard scenery.  She was here for Silas Blackwood, a fisherman with a reputation as stubborn as the gnarled oak trees clinging to the cliffside, and a knee that stubbornly refused to heal.
