The fluorescent lights of Hemlock & Thorne hummed a monotonous tune, a counterpoint to the staccato clatter of Penelope Anne Featherstonehaugh's perfectly manicured nails against the ivory keys of her typewriter.  Rain lashed against the tall, gothic windows of the imposing law firm, blurring the already hazy cityscape of London into a watercolour smear of grey and black.  Penelope, however, remained unmoved, her gaze fixed on the meticulously typed document before her – a contract so dense with legal jargon it could have suffocated a lesser woman.  The air in the office hung thick with the scent of old paper, expensive leather, and a faint, underlying aroma of desperation, a fragrance Penelope had become intimately familiar with during her five years at Hemlock & Thorne.  Tonight, however, the desperation felt sharper, more… tangible.  A shiver, unrelated to the chill seeping in through the drafty window, traced its way down her spine.  Something was about to happen.
