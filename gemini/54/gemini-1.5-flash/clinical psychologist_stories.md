The chipped porcelain teacup warmed Dr. Eleanor Vance’s hands, a fragile comfort against the November chill seeping through the tall, gothic windows of her office. Rain lashed against the panes, mirroring the turmoil she sensed brewing within her latest patient.  Her office, nestled on the third floor of a converted Victorian townhouse in the heart of Charleston, South Carolina, was a sanctuary of calm – or at least, it was meant to be.  The scent of jasmine and sandalwood incense hung heavy in the air, a deliberate counterpoint to the damp, salty breeze that often found its way through the inadequately sealed windows. Outside, the ancient oaks of the city's historic French Quarter swayed dramatically in the wind, their branches like skeletal fingers scratching at the twilight sky.  Tonight, however, the usual charm felt oppressive, a gothic backdrop to a story Eleanor suspected she wasn’t quite prepared to hear.
