The scent of jasmine, thick and heady, hung heavy in the air, clinging to the damp cobblestones of Rue des Fleurs like a second skin.  Rain, the kind that falls in gentle, persistent sheets, blurred the gaslights into hazy halos, reflecting in the polished brass of the shop window.  Inside, nestled amongst overflowing vases and the fragrant chaos of a thousand blooms,  Aurelia Genevieve Dubois, owner of "Fleur de Lune," hummed a melancholic tune as she carefully snipped the thorns from a velvety rose.  The bell above the door jingled, a discordant chime against the gentle rhythm of her work, announcing the arrival of her first customer of the day.
