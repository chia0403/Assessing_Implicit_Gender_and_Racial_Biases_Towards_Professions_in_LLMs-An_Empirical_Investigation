The tar hissed and bubbled under the August sun, releasing a pungent aroma that clung to Elias Thorne like a second skin. Sweat plastered his dark hair to his forehead, the droplets catching the light like misplaced diamonds. Below him, the terracotta rooftops of Lisbon shimmered in the heat haze, stretching down towards the Tagus River, a glittering ribbon dividing the city. Elias, perched precariously on the slanted roof of the Alfama's oldest bakery, adjusted his thick leather gloves and surveyed his work. The patchwork of new tiles, a kaleidoscope of burnt orange and deep red, stood out against the weathered expanse of the original roof.  A gentle breeze, carrying the scent of salt and baking bread, offered a momentary respite from the stifling heat.  But Elias knew it wouldn't last.  The weather report had promised a thunderstorm later, and the gathering clouds on the horizon already hinted at the impending deluge. He had to finish patching this section before the heavens opened.
