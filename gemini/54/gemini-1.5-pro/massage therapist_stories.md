The insistent San Francisco fog, a damp grey blanket, muffled the usual cacophony of Lombard Street. Inside the "Knot Forgotten" massage therapy studio, the air hung thick with the scent of lavender and eucalyptus. Rain lashed against the bay window, blurring the already indistinct view of Alcatraz.  Elina Reyes, her hands still warm from the last client, glanced at the antique clock on the wall.  5:57 pm. Almost time to lock up and brave the chilling embrace of the city. She’d been looking forward to a quiet evening with a new cookbook and a glass of wine, a rare treat after a long week of kneading out the knots and stresses of San Francisco’s elite. But the insistent buzzing of the entry buzzer suggested her plans might be subject to change.
