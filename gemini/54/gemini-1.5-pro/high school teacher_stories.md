The biting November wind whipped at Amelia Reyes's ankles as she hurried across the parking lot of Northwood High, her arms laden with graded essays and a thermos of lukewarm coffee.  The sky, the color of a bruised plum, threatened snow.  Northwood, a sprawling brick behemoth nestled amidst the rolling hills of Vermont, looked particularly bleak under the oppressive grey.  Inside, the fluorescent lights hummed a monotonous tune, a stark contrast to the quiet rustle of the dying leaves outside. Amelia, a petite woman with perpetually worried brown eyes and a messy bun that threatened to unravel at any moment, braced herself for the onslaught of teenage energy that awaited her in room 214, otherwise known as the crucible of American Literature.  She had a sinking feeling this particular Tuesday was going to be a long one.
