The flickering fluorescent lights of the Kressler & Sons Law Offices buzzed in Amelia Hayes' ears, a soundtrack as familiar as her own heartbeat. Outside, the Chicago wind howled, whipping snowflakes against the forty-second-story windows and painting the cityscape in a hazy, swirling grey.  It was late, the kind of late where the only other signs of life in the towering steel and glass behemoth were the faint hum of the cleaning crew's vacuum cleaner a few floors down and the security guard's silhouette patrolling the lobby, visible from Amelia's perch.  She hunched over a mountain of depositions, the crisp white pages illuminated by the harsh glare of her desk lamp, a half-eaten container of lukewarm Thai takeout beside her.  The deadline for the Peterson vs. Hartman case loomed like a guillotine, and Amelia, fueled by lukewarm Pad See Ew and a potent blend of caffeine and anxiety, was determined to meet it, even if it meant another all-nighter. The scent of stale coffee and printer ink hung heavy in the air, a testament to the countless hours she’d already poured into this seemingly endless litigation.  Tonight, however, something felt different.  A prickly unease settled in the pit of her stomach, a feeling she couldn’t shake, a feeling that had nothing to do with deadlines and everything to do with the file sitting open on her desk - the Peterson file.
