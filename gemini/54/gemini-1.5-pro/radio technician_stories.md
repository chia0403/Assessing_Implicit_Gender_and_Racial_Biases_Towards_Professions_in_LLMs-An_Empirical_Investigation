The biting wind whipped across the desolate, rust-colored plains of Mars, rattling the corrugated iron walls of Galileo Base. Inside, Amelia “Amy” Chen, her breath fogging the air in front of her, hunched over a tangle of wires and circuit boards.  Fluorescent lights hummed overhead, casting a sickly yellow glow across the cramped comms room, the only source of warmth aside from the sputtering heater in the corner.  It was sol 472 of her mission, and the silence from Earth was starting to feel less like a technical difficulty and more like an ominous premonition. The red dust that perpetually coated everything, from the equipment to the cracked linoleum floor, seemed to symbolize the creeping sense of isolation that threatened to engulf her.
