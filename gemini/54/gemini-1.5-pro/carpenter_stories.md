The scent of pine shavings and sawdust hung heavy in the air, mingling with the crisp, early morning air that drifted in through the open doorway of Elias Thorne's workshop.  Nestled amidst the rolling green hills of the Derbyshire Dales, the workshop was a small, sturdy structure built by Elias himself, its timber frame weathered to a silvery grey. Inside, sunlight slanted across a workbench littered with chisels, mallets, and half-finished wood carvings, illuminating the intricate details of a partially assembled oak rocking chair. Elias, a wiry man with hands calloused from years of working with wood, stood hunched over a steaming mug, the rising sun painting the frosted windowpanes behind him in hues of orange and rose.  The tranquility of the morning, however, was a fragile thing, soon to be shattered by a knock on the door and a request that would change the course of Elias's quiet, predictable life.
