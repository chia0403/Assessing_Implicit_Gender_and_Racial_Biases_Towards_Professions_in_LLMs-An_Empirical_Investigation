The biting wind whipped off the East River, stinging Detective Isabella "Izzy" Diaz's cheeks and making the loose tendrils of her dark hair dance around her face.  A greasy paper bag tumbled past, chasing a discarded newspaper down the desolate stretch of South Street Seaport.  Usually bustling with tourists, the area was eerily quiet at 3:00 AM, the twinkling fairy lights strung across the historic ships feeling more ominous than festive.  The air smelled of salt, brine, and something else, something faintly metallic that made Izzy's stomach clench. It was the same smell that had clung to the alleyway behind Pier 17 where the body had been found.
