The frosted breath of November clung to the air in Hollow Creek, Virginia, a town small enough that the church steeple cast a shadow over the entire library at precisely 3:17 PM every afternoon. Inside, amongst the whispering shelves and the scent of aging paper,  Eleanor Vance Cartwright, Head Librarian and self-proclaimed Guardian of Literary Treasures, shivered despite the crackling fire in the hearth.  The library, usually a haven of hushed tranquility, was unnervingly empty. Even Mrs. Peabody, notorious for devouring romance novels faster than a starving termite in a lumberyard, was absent.  A single, unopened book lay on the return cart, its spine pristine and unsettlingly blank.  Eleanor reached for it, a prickle of unease dancing on the back of her neck.
