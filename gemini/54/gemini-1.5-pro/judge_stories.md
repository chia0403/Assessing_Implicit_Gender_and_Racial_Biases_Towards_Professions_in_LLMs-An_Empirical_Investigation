Rain lashed against the arched windows of Judge Elias Vance's chambers, mirroring the tempest brewing inside him.  The mahogany desk, usually a comforting symbol of order and justice, felt like a barricade against the encroaching chaos.  Greystone, a city built on coal and steel in the heart of Pennsylvania's anthracite region, was a place where justice had always been a hard-won commodity.  Tonight, the lines were blurring, and Elias, a man who prided himself on impartiality, felt the cold hand of doubt gripping his heart.  The anonymous letter lay before him, the ink bleeding slightly in the damp, accusing him of the very corruption he had sworn to eradicate.
