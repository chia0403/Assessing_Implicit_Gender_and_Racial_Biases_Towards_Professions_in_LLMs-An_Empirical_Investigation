The aroma of burnt sugar and almonds hung heavy in the air, a bittersweet perfume that clung to the brick walls of Buttercup Bakery like a second skin.  Rain hammered against the large, paned windows, blurring the already fading light of the late November afternoon. Inside, Eleanor Ainsworth, her flour-dusted apron askew and a streak of chocolate smudged across her cheek, stared into the gaping maw of the ancient oven.  The almond biscotti, usually a golden triumph, were charred beyond redemption. Eleanor sighed, the sound swallowed by the rhythmic drumming of the rain and the low hum of the refrigerator in the corner of her small, but otherwise charming, bakery nestled in the heart of Oakhaven, a town that smelled perpetually of pine needles and woodsmoke.
