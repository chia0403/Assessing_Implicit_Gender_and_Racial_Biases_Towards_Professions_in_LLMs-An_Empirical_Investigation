The salt spray kissed Amelia "Amy" Hawthorne's face as she leaned against the weathered railing of her seaside cottage.  Windswept Pointe, a jagged finger of land jutting into the churning Atlantic off the coast of Maine, was both her refuge and her prison.  Three years ago, after her breakout novel, *The Clockwork Heart*, had catapulted her to unexpected literary fame, Amy had bought the isolated cottage, seeking solace from the sudden glare of the public eye.  Now, with a looming deadline for her difficult second novel and a crippling case of writer's block, the charming isolation had begun to feel more like exile. The cottage, dubbed "Inkwell" by a local fisherman, was a charming two-story affair, all shingle siding and wide paned windows, but the cozy interior, usually filled with the comforting scent of woodsmoke and brewing tea, felt stifling today.  The rhythmic crash of waves against the rocks below, once a soothing lullaby, now seemed to mock her lack of progress.  A single, half-written page sat forlornly in the typewriter on her desk, a stark testament to the demons that whispered insidious doubts in her ear.
