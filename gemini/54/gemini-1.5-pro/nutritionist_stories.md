The crisp October air, scented with woodsmoke and decaying leaves, whipped Amelia Reyes's dark curls around her face as she navigated the cobblestone streets of Wickenden, Rhode Island.  Her breath puffed out in small white clouds, mirroring the steam rising from the pumpkin spice latte clutched in her gloved hand. Amelia, a registered dietitian with a penchant for oversized scarves and a genuine love for helping people achieve healthier lifestyles, was on her way to a particularly challenging consultation. The quaint, seaside town of Wickenden, nestled amongst rolling hills and the churning Atlantic, was known for its fiercely independent fishermen, its abundance of fried seafood restaurants, and its general resistance to anything remotely resembling dietary restraint.  This morning's appointment, with the notoriously stubborn Captain Elias "Stormin'" Norcross, promised to be a true test of Amelia's professional mettle.
