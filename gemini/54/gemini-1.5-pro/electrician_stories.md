The biting November wind whipped around Elias Thorne, stinging his cheeks as he wrestled with the rusted hinges of Mrs. Petrov's electrical box.  Grey, sleeting rain plastered his dark hair to his forehead, and his thick work gloves were already soaked through.  The small town of Havenwood, nestled deep in the Appalachian Mountains of Virginia, was usually picturesque this time of year, all fiery foliage and crisp air. But this year, winter had arrived early, bringing with it a sullen grey sky and a bone-chilling dampness that seeped into everything.  The flickering porch light above Elias cast long, distorted shadows that danced across the overgrown lawn, giving the already dilapidated Victorian house an almost sinister air.  Mrs. Petrov, a woman known throughout Havenwood for her eccentric habits and rumored ability to speak with squirrels, had called him in a panic, claiming her electricity was possessed. Elias, a practical man, suspected a faulty wire or a blown fuse, but in Havenwood, one never truly knew.
