The flickering fluorescent lights of the Zenith Corporation Research and Development wing buzzed like trapped insects, casting long, skeletal shadows across the polished linoleum floor. Elias Reyes, his name tag slightly askew on his faded blue uniform, pushed his janitorial cart along the deserted corridor. Outside, the Chicago wind howled a mournful tune, rattling the oversized windows that offered a panoramic view of the city lights twinkling far below. It was 2:17 am, the quietest hour in the Zenith tower, a time when the secrets whispered between the sterile walls felt most potent.  Elias, however, wasn't privy to these secrets.  He was simply there to mop up spilled coffee, empty overflowing bins, and ensure the gleaming facade of scientific progress remained untarnished by the mundane messiness of human existence.  Tonight, though, something felt different.  A low hum, distinct from the usual electrical thrum of the building, vibrated beneath his feet, and the air crackled with an unfamiliar energy.
