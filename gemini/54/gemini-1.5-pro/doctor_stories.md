The biting wind whipped off the North Sea, rattling the frosted windowpanes of Dr. Elias Thorne's surgery.  The salt spray, frozen mid-air, dusted the cobbled lane outside like icing sugar.  Greyhaven, a small fishing village clinging to the rugged Yorkshire coast, was shrouded in a pre-Christmas gloom. Inside, a single lamp illuminated the cluttered room, casting long shadows across the worn medical texts and gleaming instruments.  Elias, his dark hair streaked with silver at the temples, stared out at the churning grey expanse, a frown etching lines across his usually kind face.  The storm mirrored the turmoil within him, a storm of worry for a patient he feared he couldn't save.
