The biting wind whipped off the Tyrrhenian Sea, carrying the scent of salt and something else, something acrid, like iron rusting.  Eamon Finnigan, his calloused hands shoved deep into the pockets of his worn leather apron, squinted at the sky. Grey clouds, heavy with the promise of rain, scudded low over the jagged peaks of the Apuan Alps. Below him, nestled in the folds of the mountains like a forgotten child's toy, lay the town of Colonnata, its white marble quarries gleaming like open wounds in the earth.  Eamon, a stone cutter for three generations, felt a familiar ache in his bones, a premonition as cold and sharp as the chisel he wielded. It wasn't the coming storm that bothered him, but the silence from the quarry. A silence that had stretched on for an unnatural three days now, broken only by the whisper of the wind and the unsettling flutter of carrion crows circling high above.
