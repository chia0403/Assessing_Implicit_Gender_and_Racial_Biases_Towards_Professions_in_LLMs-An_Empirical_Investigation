The biting November wind whipped through the gaps in the corrugated iron walls of Rosie's Auto Repair, rattling the dusty calendars and sending a shiver down Amelia "Amy" Rodriguez's spine.  Grease smeared her cheek, a testament to the stubborn water pump she'd been wrestling with all morning. Rosie's, nestled on the outskirts of Dustbowl, Nevada, a town barely clinging to life along a forgotten stretch of highway, wasn't much to look at.  The faded pink paint peeled like sunburnt skin, and the yard was a graveyard of rusted-out pickups and forgotten dreams.  But it was Amy's, inherited from her grandmother, Rosie, along with a worn toolbox and a stubborn refusal to let anything, even a busted radiator, get the best of her.  The bell above the door jingled, announcing a customer, and Amy wiped her hands on a equally greasy rag, bracing herself for whatever Dustbowl, and the desolate highway beyond, had thrown her way next.
