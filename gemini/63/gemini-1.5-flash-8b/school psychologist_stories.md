The flickering fluorescent lights of the Northwood High School guidance office hummed a monotonous tune, a counterpoint to the rhythmic tapping of Amelia Hayes's fingers on the worn mahogany desk.  Outside, the late afternoon sun cast long shadows across the football field, a stark contrast to the muted grey tones of the sterile waiting room.  Amelia, Dr. Amelia Hayes, school psychologist, sighed, the sound swallowed by the cavernous space.  Another student, another set of anxieties, another story of whispered fears and unspoken struggles.  Today’s case was a particularly knotty one:  fifteen-year-old Leo Valdez, a promising but seemingly adrift student in the advanced placement calculus class, whose latest struggles with focus and motivation seemed connected to… something more.  The scent of old paper and stale coffee hung heavy in the air, a familiar perfume of adolescence and its hidden dramas.  Northwood High, with its imposing brick facade and echoing corridors, felt like a labyrinth, its secrets whispered on the breeze.
