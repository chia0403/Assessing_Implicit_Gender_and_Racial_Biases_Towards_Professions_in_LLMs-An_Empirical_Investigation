The air, thick with the smell of graphite and stale coffee, felt strangely still, as if holding its breath in anticipation of the next design challenge.  Elias, 32 with a face etched with the lines of countless hours spent hunched over drawing tables, felt a familiar prickle of anxiety. A single, misplaced line could mean catastrophe.
