The scent of cinnamon and freshly baked bread hung thick in the air, a comforting blanket draped over the cobbled streets of Oakhaven.  Rain, the constant companion of the small, perpetually grey town, hammered against the slate rooftops, a rhythmic counterpoint to the gentle whirring of the old-fashioned bakery oven.  Elias Thorne, a baker with flour dusted hands and eyes that held the wisdom of countless loaves, carefully placed a final swirl of icing on a delicate lavender-sugar cake.  The aroma of the rising dough filled his small, but perfectly ordered, bakery, a testament to his meticulous nature.  Outside, the rain-slicked cobblestones reflected the single flickering gas lamp, casting long shadows that danced with the steam rising from the warm loaves.
