The chipped porcelain sink, stained a perpetual, muted grey from decades of spilled chamomile tea and the inevitable stray strands of dyed auburn, reflected Amelia Nightingale's worried frown.  Sunlight, fractured by the dusty windowpanes of "Scissors & Smiles," sliced across the small, cluttered salon.  A single, wilting geranium in a cracked terracotta pot sat forlornly on the counter, mirroring Amelia's own wilting spirits.  It was a Tuesday, the slowest day of the week, and the rhythmic *clink* of the bell above the door had been absent for hours.  Amelia ran a hand through her own honey-colored hair, a familiar comfort despite the knot of anxiety tightening in her stomach.  The salon, once buzzing with the animated chatter of clients and the rhythmic snip-snip of shears, was now a mausoleum of silence.  This town, nestled in the heart of the rolling hills of the Vermont countryside, seemed to be holding its breath, a palpable chill in the air, a quiet anticipation that felt as ominous as a gathering storm.
