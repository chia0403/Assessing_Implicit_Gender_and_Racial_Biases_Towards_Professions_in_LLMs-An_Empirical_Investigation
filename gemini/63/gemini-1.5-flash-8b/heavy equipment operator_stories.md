The biting wind whipped across the desolate expanse of the Wyoming badlands, stinging Amelia "Amy"  Harding's cheeks and rattling the rusted metal of the bulldozer's cab.  Dust devils, the colour of burnt umber, danced across the cracked earth, swirling around the hulking machine like wraiths.  Amy, her face weathered like the stone formations that surrounded her, squinted through the grime-coated window.  The sun, a pale disc sinking towards the horizon, cast long, skeletal shadows that stretched across the eroded canyons, mimicking the impossible angles of the land itself.  This was the Lost Valley, a place swallowed by time and drought, a place where the whispers of the wind were the only company.  Amy, a heavy equipment operator with calloused hands and eyes that held the sharp focus of a hawk, was alone, wrestling with a mountain of stubborn rock.  The bulldozer, a beast she knew intimately, groaned and shuddered under the relentless pressure of the day's work.
