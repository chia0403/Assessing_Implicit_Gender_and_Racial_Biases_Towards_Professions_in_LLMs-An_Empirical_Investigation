The chipped Formica countertop gleamed under the harsh fluorescent lights of "Snip-Snap Scissors," a salon more renowned for its questionable air freshener than its sophisticated atmosphere.  Dust motes danced in the solitary shaft of sunlight slicing through the grime-coated window, illuminating a single figure hunched over a client’s head.  This was  Amelia "Amy"  Featherstone, a hair cutter with hands as steady as granite and a heart that held secrets as tangled as the unruly knots she frequently untangled.  Her apron, stained a faded, almost-lavender hue from countless perms and highlights, was a testament to her tireless dedication.  Despite the chaotic symphony of buzzing clippers and the occasional exasperated sigh, Amy worked with a quiet focus, a focused calmness that belied the turmoil simmering beneath the surface.  The salon's scent of cheap shampoo and desperation clung to her like a second skin.  Today, however, it felt…different. A faint, metallic tang, mingled with the familiar floral perfume, drifted in the air, a warning that something more than a simple trim was about to unfold.
