The biting wind whipped rain-slicked strands of Amelia "Amy" Hernandez's hair across her face, stinging her eyes.  The flickering sodium lights of the deserted street cast long, distorted shadows that danced with the swirling mist.  Rain hammered against the corrugated metal roof of the precinct station, a relentless tattoo that echoed the frantic rhythm pounding in Amy's temples.  She was knee-deep in a case that defied logic, a case that smelled faintly of desperation and something far darker.  The crime scene, a cramped, cluttered apartment on the edge of the impoverished Southside, was a vortex of chaos.  Broken glass littered the floor like shattered dreams, and the acrid smell of burnt coffee clung to the air, strangely juxtaposed with the faint, metallic tang of blood.  Amy's partner, Diaz, stood rigidly in the doorway, his usual easygoing demeanor replaced with grim concentration.  The air crackled with unspoken questions and growing unease.  The city, a concrete jungle draped in a shroud of rain, seemed to hold its breath, waiting for the answers Amy desperately sought.
