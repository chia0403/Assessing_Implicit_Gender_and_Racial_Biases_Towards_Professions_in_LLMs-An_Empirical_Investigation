The biting November wind whipped rain against the grimy storefront windows of the "Corner Grocer," the only building visible through the skeletal branches of the ancient oaks lining Elm Street.  Rain slicked the cobblestones, reflecting the dim, yellow light spilling from the bodega's single, flickering bulb.  Detective Inspector Amelia "Amy" Hawthorne, Probation Officer for the 14th Precinct, adjusted her worn trench coat, the collar pulling tight around her neck.  The scent of damp earth and stale coffee hung heavy in the air, a familiar aroma of late-night patrols and strained patience.  She traced the outline of the chipped paint on the boarded-up windows of the adjacent building, a silent testament to the city's persistent struggle with neglect.  Tonight, the struggle felt personal.  Her latest case, a young man named Marcus 'Mac'  Delacroix, was proving particularly frustrating.
