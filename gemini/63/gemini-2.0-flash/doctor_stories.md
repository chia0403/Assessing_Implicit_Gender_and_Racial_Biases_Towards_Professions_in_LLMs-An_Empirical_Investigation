The stale, antiseptic smell clung to Dr. Eleanor Vance's nostrils, a familiar comfort in the chaos that was her life. Rain lashed against the panoramic window of the observation deck at the Northwood Research Facility, blurring the already indistinct lights of Anchorage, Alaska. Below, the facility stretched like a sprawling, concrete octopus, its tentacles probing deep into the frozen tundra. Tonight, Eleanor wasn't observing research. She was waiting. Waiting for news, and bracing herself for the inevitable. The news was rarely good at Northwood, especially when it came to Project Chimera.
