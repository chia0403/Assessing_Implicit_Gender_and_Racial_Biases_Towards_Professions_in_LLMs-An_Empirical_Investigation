The scent of buttercream and vanilla hung thick in the air, a sweet, comforting fog that both soothed and stressed Cordelia Beatrice Plumtree. Outside, a slate-grey London sky threatened rain, pressing down on the city like a poorly-risen sponge cake. Inside "Cordelia's Confections," her small but fiercely-loved bakery in Notting Hill, the atmosphere was a flurry of quiet chaos. Cordelia, her hands dusted white with powdered sugar, surveyed the nearly completed three-tiered masterpiece before her, her brow furrowed in concentration. It was for the wedding of Lady Penelope Worthingham, a notoriously picky socialite, and everything had to be absolutely, unequivocally perfect.
