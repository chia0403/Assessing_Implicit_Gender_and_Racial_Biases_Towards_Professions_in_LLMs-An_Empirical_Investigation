The biting wind whipped across the desolate practice field, carrying the scent of damp earth and the faint echo of a distant train whistle.  Floodlights cast long, skeletal shadows of the bleachers across the cracked asphalt, illuminating a lone figure huddled deeper into his threadbare windbreaker.  Coach Elias "Eli" Thorne, his breath clouding in the frigid November air, watched his team – the Jericho High Cyclones – stumble through another lackluster drill.  Jericho, a once-proud mill town nestled in the rusting heart of Pennsylvania, had seen better days, and the Cyclones, reflecting the town's fortunes, hadn't won a game in two seasons.  Tonight, with the playoffs a distant, impossible dream, Eli felt the weight of the losing streak settle on his shoulders like a physical burden.  He knew this wasn't just about football; it was about a town searching for something to believe in, a town that, like its high school football team, had seemingly forgotten how to win.
