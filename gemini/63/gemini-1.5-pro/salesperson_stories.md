Rain lashed against the grime-streaked windows of The Rusty Mug diner, blurring the neon "Open" sign into a hazy smear of pink. Inside, the air hung thick with the smell of stale coffee and desperation.  Eleanor Vance, a travelling saleswoman for the "Miracle Mop" company, huddled deeper into her booth, the worn vinyl clinging uncomfortably to her damp trench coat.  Her sample case, emblazoned with a cartoon housewife beaming maniacally at a sparkling clean floor, sat at her feet like a loyal, if somewhat embarrassing, pet.  Ashville, North Carolina, population barely clinging to five thousand, hadn't exactly been a highlight of her sales route. Three days she'd been here, and the only things she'd managed to shift were a couple of lukewarm cups of coffee and the pervasive sense of gloom that seemed to have settled over the town like a shroud.  Eleanor sighed, picking at a loose thread on her glove.  This trip was proving to be a bust. Just like the last one. And the one before that.
