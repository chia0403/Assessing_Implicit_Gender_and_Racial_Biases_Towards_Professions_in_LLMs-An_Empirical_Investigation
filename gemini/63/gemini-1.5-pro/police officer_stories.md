The biting November wind whipped off Lake Michigan, carrying icy spray that stung Detective Isabella "Izzy" Diaz's face and froze the ends of her dark, curly hair.  The sky, a bruised purple-grey, mirrored the churning waters below.  Two squad cars, their emergency lights flashing like deranged Christmas ornaments, illuminated a small section of North Avenue Beach, usually bustling with sunbathers and volleyball players, but now deserted except for a handful of uniformed officers and a white forensics tent that billowed ominously in the wind.  Izzy pulled her collar higher, the damp wool scratching against her skin.  This was her fourth homicide in as many weeks, and the bleak Chicago landscape seemed to reflect the growing unease in her gut.  This one felt different.  Colder.
