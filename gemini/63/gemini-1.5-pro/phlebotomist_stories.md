The fluorescent lights of St. Jude's Hospital hummed a monotonous B-flat, a soundtrack Amelia Reyes had grown accustomed to over her five years as a phlebotomist. The pre-dawn quiet of the lab, usually a sanctuary of sorts, felt oppressive this morning.  Outside, a late-October wind rattled the skeletal branches of the sycamore trees lining the parking lot, their leaves swirling like discarded confetti.  Inside, the air hung heavy with the metallic tang of antiseptic and the low thrum of the centrifuge. Amelia, bundled in a lilac cardigan despite the stuffy lab’s temperature, meticulously labeled vials of blood, her movements precise and practiced.  This morning, however, a nervous tremor ran beneath her practiced calm, a prickle of unease she couldn't quite place. Something felt… off.
