The flickering neon sign of the "Binary Bean" cast a sickly green glow across Amelia "Mia" Rodriguez's rain-streaked glasses.  Seattle's perpetual drizzle had transitioned into a full-blown downpour, mirroring the storm brewing inside her.  The cafe, usually buzzing with the low hum of conversation and the clatter of keyboards, was eerily quiet at 2:17 am.  Empty coffee cups littered the tables like fallen soldiers, and the lone barista, a young man with a constellation of piercings, stared blankly at a TikTok on his phone. Mia, huddled in a corner booth, hunched over her laptop, the glow of the screen illuminating the exhaustion etched onto her face.  Lines of code scrolled past, cryptic symbols in a language only she and a select few truly understood.  Tonight, however, even that familiar language seemed alien.  The project, a revolutionary new encryption algorithm she'd been working on for months, was stalling. A single, stubborn bug, hidden deep within the digital labyrinth, was mocking her, whispering secrets she couldn't decipher.  Outside, the wind howled, a mournful cry that echoed the rising panic in Mia's chest. This wasn't just a bug; it felt like a warning.
