Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of the 47th floor, blurring the already fading lights of downtown Chicago.  Inside, the air buzzed with the low hum of servers and the frantic click-clack of keyboards.  Eleanor Vance, a systems analyst with a reputation for both brilliance and a caffeine dependency that rivaled a small coffee plantation, stared intently at the scrolling lines of code on her monitor. Her reflection, pale and drawn, shimmered in the glass, framed by the flickering glow.  It was 2:17 AM, a Tuesday, and the entire trading floor of the prestigious investment firm, Blackwood & Sterling, was in lockdown. A virus, unlike anything Eleanor had ever encountered, was snaking its way through their meticulously crafted security systems, threatening to unravel years of carefully guarded data.
