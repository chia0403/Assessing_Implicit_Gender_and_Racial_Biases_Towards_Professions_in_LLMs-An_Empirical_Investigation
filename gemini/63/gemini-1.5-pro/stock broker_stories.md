The rain hammered against the thirty-foot windows of Elias Thorne’s office, a percussive counterpoint to the frantic clicking of keyboards on the trading floor below.  Elias, his tie loosened and his normally impeccable dark hair ruffled, stared out at the deluge sweeping across the Manhattan skyline.  Fifty stories below, the yellow cabs looked like frantic beetles scrambling through the downpour.  His office, a sanctuary of polished mahogany and muted leather, felt miles removed from the frenetic energy of Thorne & Sterling Investments.  It was 6:58 AM, two minutes before the opening bell, and Elias already felt the familiar tightening in his chest, the adrenaline-fueled anticipation that was both exhilarating and exhausting. Today, however, the usual thrill was edged with a cold dread. He had a feeling this wasn’t going to be just another Tuesday.
