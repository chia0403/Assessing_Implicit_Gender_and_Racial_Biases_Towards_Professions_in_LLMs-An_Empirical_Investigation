The biting wind whipped off the North Sea, carrying the scent of salt and something faintly, unsettlingly metallic.  Dr. Elias Thorne pulled his threadbare coat tighter around himself, the damp chill seeping into his bones despite the thick wool.  The flickering gas lamps of Grimsby Docks cast long, dancing shadows that seemed to mock the hurried steps of the few souls braving the late November night.  Elias’s boots echoed on the cobblestones, the rhythmic sound swallowed by the groans of moored fishing trawlers straining against their ropes.  He was headed towards the flickering lamplight spilling from the doorway of The Mariner’s Rest, a pub known more for its potent grog and rough clientele than its hospitality.  Tonight, however, it wasn’t the warmth of the fire or the promise of a stiff drink that drew him in.  A crumpled note, delivered by a shivering urchin just an hour before, contained a single, chilling sentence:  "They've brought something back from the deep, Doctor, and it ain't fish."
