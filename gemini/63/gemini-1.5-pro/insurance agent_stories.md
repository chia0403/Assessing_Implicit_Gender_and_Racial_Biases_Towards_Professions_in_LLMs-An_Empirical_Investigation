Rain lashed against the windows of Abigail "Abby" Rose Hawthorne's cramped office, mimicking the frantic drumming of her fingers on the chipped Formica desk.  Fluorescent lights buzzed overhead, casting a sickly yellow glow over the cluttered space.  Half-eaten takeout containers littered one corner, a testament to late nights spent chasing deadlines and appeasing demanding clients.  Abby's office, sandwiched between a laundromat and a perpetually closed bakery on Bleaker Street in the perpetually grey city of Grimsborough, perfectly reflected her current state of mind: overwhelmed, underappreciated, and just a little bit soggy.  This Tuesday, however, promised to be different.  This Tuesday, Abby was finally meeting with Mr. Silas Blackwood, a recluse rumored to be both fabulously wealthy and profoundly eccentric, and the holder of a life insurance policy so large it could single-handedly save Abby's struggling agency.
