Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of Amelia Vivienne Hawthorne's 42nd-floor office, mirroring the tempest brewing within her.  The cityscape of Chicago, usually a glittering tapestry of lights, was obscured by a grey, swirling mist.  Gargoyles perched on the gothic revival architecture of the Tribune Tower across the street seemed to leer at her, mocking the knot tightening in her stomach. Amelia, Chief Operating Officer of Hawthorne Enterprises, a multinational conglomerate built on the bones of her great-grandfather's steel empire, tapped a manicured nail against the polished mahogany of her desk.  The air in the room, normally crisp with the scent of lilies and ambition, was thick with the metallic tang of fear.  The quarterly reports lay open before her, a stark white battlefield littered with the red ink of devastating losses. Something was rotten within the company, something more insidious than a simple market downturn, and Amelia was determined to find out what.
