The rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of the Salty Siren Diner, a rhythm that usually lulled Eleanor Vance into a meditative state as she wiped down the sticky Formica tables.  Not tonight. Tonight, the relentless drumming mirrored the anxiety churning in her stomach.  The Siren, nestled on a lonely stretch of Highway 1, just south of the Oregon-California border, was usually quiet on a Tuesday night.  But tonight, a thick fog had rolled in off the Pacific, swallowing the highway and stranding a motley crew of travelers inside.  Fluorescent lights buzzed overhead, casting a sickly yellow glow on the chrome and vinyl booths. The air hung heavy with the smell of stale coffee, frying onions, and something else… something Eleanor couldn’t quite place, but that prickled the hairs on the back of her neck.
