The biting November wind whipped around Eleanor Vance as she wrestled a bolt of emerald green velvet out of her vintage Porsche, the fabric billowing like a rogue sail.  Frost glittered on the browning ivy that clambered up the imposing brick walls of Blackwood Manor, a grand, if slightly dilapidated, Georgian mansion nestled deep in the Somerset countryside. Eleanor, her breath puffing out white clouds in the frigid air, tucked a stray strand of auburn hair behind her ear and surveyed the scene.  The manor, despite its current state of gentle disrepair, held an undeniable charm.  Stained glass windows winked in the afternoon sun, hinting at the opulence within, while the overgrown gardens promised hidden pathways and forgotten fountains.  This was her latest project, and Eleanor, ever the optimist, saw not a crumbling relic, but a sleeping beauty waiting to be awakened.
