The insistent London drizzle seemed to seep into Eleanor Vance’s bones, a damp chill mirroring the creeping dread in her stomach.  Her flat, usually a vibrant explosion of colour and carefully curated chaos reflecting her work as a graphic designer, felt oppressive this evening.  Empty Chinese takeaway containers littered the coffee table, remnants of a solitary dinner, and the glow from her iMac cast long, distorted shadows across the room, turning familiar objects into menacing shapes.  Rain lashed against the single window overlooking the narrow alleyway below, a rhythmic percussion that underscored the unsettling silence of her phone.  It had been three days since the email – the one with the cryptic subject line: “We know what you saw.”  Three days since the world outside her vibrant designs had turned a shade of unsettling grey.
