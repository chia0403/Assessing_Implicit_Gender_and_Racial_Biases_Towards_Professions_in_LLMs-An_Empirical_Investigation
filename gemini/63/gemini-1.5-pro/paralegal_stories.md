Rain lashed against the grimy windows of the Kressler & Blake building, mirroring the tempest brewing inside Amelia Hayes. Amelia, a paralegal with a penchant for cardigans and a vocabulary rivalling a seasoned barrister, tapped a chipped crimson nail against her desk.  The mahogany surface, usually gleaming under the fluorescent office lights, was obscured by scattered files relating to the Peterson case – a messy divorce involving a philandering CEO, a scorned socialite, and enough dirty laundry to fill a stadium.  The air in her small, windowless office, nestled deep within the labyrinthine corridors of the prestigious Chicago law firm, was thick with the scent of stale coffee and impending doom.  The doom, in this case, took the form of Mr. Kressler himself, whose booming voice echoed from the hallway, demanding her presence and the Peterson file… *immediately*.
