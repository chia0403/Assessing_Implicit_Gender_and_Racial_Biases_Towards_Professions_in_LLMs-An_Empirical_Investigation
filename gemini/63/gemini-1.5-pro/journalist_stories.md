The rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of the 'Lucky Panda' takeaway, a relentless metallic drumming that mirrored the anxiety churning in Cassandra "Cassie" Bellweather's stomach. Neon signs flickered erratically through the greasy windowpane, casting a lurid, otherworldly glow across the chipped Formica table where she sat. Kowloon Walled City, a claustrophobic labyrinth of interconnected high-rises clinging to the edge of Hong Kong, pressed in on all sides, a concrete jungle teeming with secrets. Cassie, a freelance journalist with a nose for trouble and a penchant for cheap noodles, had come here chasing a story, a whisper of a triad power struggle brewing beneath the city's chaotic surface. The air hung thick with the mingled scents of soy sauce, exhaust fumes, and something indefinably sinister. Her contact, a shifty-eyed man known only as "Mr. Wong," was late. Very late.
