The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Mia” Hawthorne’s auburn braid across her face as she wrestled a recalcitrant bale of hay.  The air, thick with the scent of damp earth and woodsmoke, carried the mournful lowing of cattle from the distant pastures of the Blackwood Animal Sanctuary.  Mia, bundled in layers of worn wool against the chill, ignored the stinging icy rain that plastered her eyelashes.  Blackwood, nestled deep within the rolling hills of the Scottish Highlands, was a sprawling haven for rescued and abandoned animals – a place of both profound beauty and quiet desperation, mirroring Mia's own complicated heart.  The sanctuary itself was a haphazard collection of repurposed barns, rambling Victorian outbuildings, and makeshift shelters, all clinging precariously to the steep hillside overlooking a turbulent loch.  Tonight, however, even the usual comforting chaos felt overshadowed by a palpable sense of unease.  Something was wrong.  Very wrong.
