The salt spray stung Officer Amelia “Amy” Hernandez’s face as the November wind whipped around her, carrying the mournful cry of gulls.  Rain, a relentless drizzle that had been falling for three days, plastered her dark hair to her scalp.  She pulled her collar higher, the damp wool of her uniform offering little comfort against the biting chill. Below, the churning grey expanse of the Atlantic gnawed at the jagged cliffs of Cornwall, the waves a furious, frothing testament to the storm that had been raging for the past 24 hours.  The small, picturesque fishing village of Portwenn, usually a postcard-perfect scene, was transformed into a windswept, rain-lashed battleground.  Amy shivered, not entirely from the cold.  The body, half-submerged in the churning surf below, was a grim reminder that even in this seemingly idyllic corner of the world, darkness lurked beneath the surface.
