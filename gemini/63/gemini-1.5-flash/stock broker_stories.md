The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Mia” Davenport’s tailored coat around her legs as she hurried down Wall Street, the canyons of steel and glass looming like indifferent giants.  The air, thick with the scent of exhaust fumes and expensive coffee, vibrated with a nervous energy that was as familiar to Mia as the rhythm of her own heartbeat.  Rain, a fine, icy drizzle, plastered her dark hair to her forehead, mirroring the tension tightening in her jaw.  She glanced at her reflection in a polished skyscraper window, catching sight of her own determined expression – a stark contrast to the panicked faces hurrying past her. The Dow was tanking, and the whispers of a looming recession were no longer whispers, but a deafening roar echoing through the financial district.  Mia, a rising star at Davenport & Co. (a firm bearing a surprisingly coincidental family name), knew the storm was about to break, and she was bracing herself for the deluge.
