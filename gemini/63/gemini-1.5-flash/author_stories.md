The salt spray stung Amelia Katherine Blackwood’s face as she wrestled the stubborn fishing boat’s tiller.  The churning grey expanse of the Atlantic stretched before her, a vast, unforgiving canvas reflecting the stormy sky.  Seabirds, their cries lost in the wind’s howl, wheeled overhead, their silhouettes stark against the bruised purple and slate-grey clouds.  Amelia, renowned author of the bestselling “Whispers from the Deep” trilogy, usually found her inspiration in quiet corners of dusty libraries, but today, inspiration felt less like a muse and more like a rogue wave threatening to capsize her small vessel.  The remote, windswept island of Inishmore, shrouded in mist and legend, loomed ahead, its jagged cliffs a stark promise of solitude – and perhaps, the answer to a question that had haunted her for years.
