The scent of lavender and old silk hung heavy in the air, a comforting perfume that clung to the velvet curtains and the dust motes dancing in the afternoon sunbeams slicing through the arched window of "Stitches in Time."  Elara Genevieve Thorne, her fingers stained a delicate purple from the dye she'd been using earlier, carefully pinned a cascade of hand-stitched roses onto the ivory silk of a wedding gown.  The atelier, nestled on a cobbled side street in the heart of Prague's Old Town, was a haven of quiet creativity, a stark contrast to the bustling market square just beyond its weathered oak door.  Outside, the clatter of horse-drawn carriages and the cheerful chatter of tourists were muted whispers, barely disturbing the serene hum of Elara's sewing machine.  But today, the usual tranquility was fractured by a nervous tremor in her usually steady hands.  This wasn't just any wedding gown; this was for the daughter of the Count, and the pressure, as always with high society clientele, was suffocating.
