The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Mia” Holloway’s auburn hair across her face as she hurried down Bleecker Street, the scent of roasting chestnuts and damp leaves clinging to the chill air.  Rain, a fine, persistent drizzle, plastered her trench coat to her slender frame.  Above, the cast-iron buildings of Greenwich Village loomed, their brick facades darkened by the approaching twilight, a gothic backdrop to the frenetic energy of the city.  Mia clutched a steaming takeaway coffee, the warmth a meager comfort against the November gloom.  Inside her battered leather satchel, nestled amongst crumpled manuscripts and rejection slips, lay the final edits of “The Serpent’s Kiss,” a historical romance she’d poured her heart and soul into, a book she desperately hoped would be the breakout success her publishing house, Blackwood & Crane, so desperately needed.
