The diesel engine coughed, a throaty rumble that vibrated through Amelia “Amy” Stone’s worn work boots and up into her spine.  Dawn painted the eastern sky in bruised purples and oranges, bleeding across the flat expanse of the Nevada desert.  Dust, fine as talcum powder, hung in the air, illuminated by the harsh glare of the rising sun, clinging to everything – the hulking yellow excavator Amy was perched inside, the scattered pre-fab buildings of the mining operation, even the sweat beading on her brow.  The air itself tasted of grit and diesel, a familiar flavor after twenty years spent wrestling behemoths of steel across this unforgiving landscape.  Today’s task was particularly daunting: clearing a newly discovered fault line before the midday heat warped the already unstable earth.  Amy adjusted her hardhat, the familiar weight a comforting presence amidst the vast, silent immensity of the desert.  This wasn't just a job; it was a battle, and Amy Stone, seasoned operator of the monstrous "Bite-Me 3000," was ready to fight.
