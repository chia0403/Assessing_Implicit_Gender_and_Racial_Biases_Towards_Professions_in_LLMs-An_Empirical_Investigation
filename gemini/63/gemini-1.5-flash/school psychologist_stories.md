The stale scent of old textbooks and nervous sweat hung heavy in the air, a familiar perfume to Dr. Eleanor Vance.  Rain lashed against the tall, gothic windows of Northwood High, each drop a tiny hammer blow against the already frayed nerves of the students huddled inside.  Eleanor, a school psychologist with a penchant for sensible cardigans and an uncanny ability to decipher teenage angst, sat at her desk, the chipped paint a testament to countless troubled confessions whispered across its surface.  Outside, the November wind howled like a banshee, mirroring the turmoil she knew was brewing within the school's walls.  This wasn't just another Monday at Northwood; this was the day the whispers had finally turned into screams.
