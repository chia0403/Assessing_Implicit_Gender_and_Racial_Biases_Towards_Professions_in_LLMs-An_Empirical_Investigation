The salt spray kissed Elias Thorne's weathered face as he hauled the last of the rough-hewn planks onto the rickety wharf.  Seagulls, fat and impudent, squawked overhead, their cries a counterpoint to the rhythmic crash of waves against the pilings of Port Blossom.  The air, thick with the smell of brine and drying fish, carried the faint, sweeter scent of pine from the lumber piled high in Elias's small workshop, a ramshackle structure perched precariously on the edge of the cliffs overlooking the churning grey sea.  The setting sun cast long, dramatic shadows across the cobbled streets of the fishing village, painting the scene in hues of orange and bruised purple, a fitting backdrop to the secrets Port Blossom held close, secrets Elias, with his calloused hands and keen eyes, was about to uncover.
