The salt spray stung Dr. Eleanor Vance’s face as she wrestled with the stubborn knot in the fishing line.  The late afternoon sun, a bruised plum in the sky, cast long shadows across the rocky outcrop where she’d parked her beat-up Jeep Cherokee.  Below, the churning grey waters of the Atlantic pounded against the jagged Maine coastline, a relentless rhythm that mirrored the persistent ache in her own shoulders.  The air, thick with the scent of brine and pine, offered little solace.  Eleanor, usually so adept at untangling physical knots in her patients' bodies, found herself hopelessly snagged by this metaphorical one, the frustration mirroring the tangled fishing line.  Her small, weathered cottage, barely visible through the wind-whipped pines perched precariously on the cliff's edge, felt a million miles away.  This was her sanctuary, her escape, but today, even the raw beauty of the Maine coast couldn't soothe the turmoil within.
