The humid Charleston air hung heavy, thick with the scent of jasmine and impending rain.  Cicadas buzzed a relentless, almost frantic rhythm against the backdrop of Amelia Katherine "Amy" Blackwood's perfectly manicured lawn.  Amy, a real estate agent whose tailored linen suit somehow managed to remain crisp despite the oppressive heat, adjusted the tasteful "For Sale" sign planted firmly in the meticulously kept earth.  The antebellum mansion behind her, a testament to Charleston's opulent past, loomed, a gothic masterpiece of weathered grey wood and crumbling grandeur, its shadowed windows hinting at secrets both charming and chilling.  This wasn't just another property; this was the Blackwood Estate, and selling it felt less like a transaction and more like a betrayal.
