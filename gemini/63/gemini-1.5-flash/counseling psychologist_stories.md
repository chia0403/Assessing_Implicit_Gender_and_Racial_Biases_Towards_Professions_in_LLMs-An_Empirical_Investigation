The chipped porcelain mug warmed Dr. Eleanor Vance’s hands, the lukewarm chamomile tea doing little to soothe the icy knot of anxiety tightening in her stomach.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of her office, blurring the already hazy cityscape of Seattle.  Below, the Space Needle, usually a beacon of optimistic futurism, was swallowed by the grey, almost malevolent, gloom mirroring the mood inside.  Eleanor, a counseling psychologist specializing in trauma and grief, stared out at the tempest, her reflection a pale ghost in the swirling water-streaked glass.  The worn leather of her armchair creaked softly as she shifted, the sound a tiny counterpoint to the thunder rumbling overhead – a prelude, perhaps, to the storm brewing within her next client.
