The scent of coconut and vanilla hung heavy in the air, a cloying sweetness that usually soothed Seraphina “Sera” Dubois.  But tonight, the aroma only served to amplify the prickling unease crawling up her spine.  Rain lashed against the oversized windows of her salon, “Serafina’s Styles,” blurring the neon glow of the city lights into hazy streaks of crimson and emerald.  Inside, the usually vibrant space was deserted, the usually sparkling chrome and glass surfaces gleaming under the muted emergency lights.  A single, flickering candle sat atop the mahogany manicure station, casting long, dancing shadows that distorted the elegant curves of the antique chaise lounge. The only sounds were the rhythmic drumming of the rain and the low hum of the refrigerator in the back room, a lonely soundtrack to Sera's growing apprehension.
