The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Amy” Hernandez’s hair across her face as she wrestled the heavy toolbox from the back of her beat-up Ford Ranger.  The salt spray from the churning Atlantic Ocean, a constant companion to the residents of Seabrook Island, stung her cheeks and carried the sharp tang of seaweed and diesel.  Above, the bruised purple sky threatened another downpour, mirroring the stormy mood brewing inside her.  Amy, a journeyman electrician with calloused hands and eyes that held the weary wisdom of a woman twice her age, wasn't known for her patience, especially when faced with a flickering light bulb in a beachfront mansion owned by someone who clearly thought money could buy them immunity to the laws of electricity and basic human decency.  The salty air hung heavy with the promise of another long, frustrating night.
