The humid air hung heavy, thick with the scent of frying plantains and diesel fumes.  A lone stray dog, ribs showing beneath its matted fur, picked its way through the overflowing bins lining the narrow alleyway.  Rain threatened, the sky a bruised purple mirroring the anxieties churning in Isabella “Izzy” Diaz’s stomach.  Izzy, a cub reporter for the *San Juan Sentinel*, adjusted the worn strap of her camera bag, its weight a familiar comfort in the chaotic symphony of sounds emanating from the San Juan marketplace.  The vibrant chaos – the shouts of vendors hawking their wares, the rhythmic clang of metal on metal from a nearby workshop, the murmur of a thousand conversations – usually invigorated her. Tonight, it only amplified the unease that had settled in her bones.  She was chasing a story that smelled far worse than the overflowing trash, a story that could unravel the fragile peace of the city, and possibly, her own career.
