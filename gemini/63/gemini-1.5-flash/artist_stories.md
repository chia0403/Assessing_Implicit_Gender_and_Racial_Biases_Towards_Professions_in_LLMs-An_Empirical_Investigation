The salt spray stung Elias Thorne's face, a familiar kiss from the unforgiving Atlantic.  He stood perched on the jagged rocks of the Cornish coast, the wind whipping his unruly brown hair across his eyes.  Below him, the waves crashed against the cliffs with a thunderous roar, their frothy white crests mirroring the turbulent emotions churning within him.  The easel, stubbornly planted in the wind-battered earth, held a canvas still stark white, a mocking testament to his creative drought.  Elias, a landscape artist renowned for his dramatic seascapes, felt strangely devoid of inspiration.  The very ocean that usually fueled his passion now felt like a cold, indifferent giant, reflecting back only the emptiness he carried within. The scent of brine and seaweed hung heavy in the air, a pungent perfume to accompany the grey, brooding sky that mirrored the turmoil in his soul.
