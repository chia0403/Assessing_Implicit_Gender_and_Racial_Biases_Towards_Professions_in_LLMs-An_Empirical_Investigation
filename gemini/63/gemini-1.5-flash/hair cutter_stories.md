The scent of lavender and burnt sugar hung heavy in the air, a peculiar perfume unique to "Shear Genius,"  a barbershop tucked away on a cobbled side street in the ancient city of Prague.  Rain, a relentless drizzle typical of a late autumn evening, plastered the medieval rooftops outside, mirroring the dampness clinging to the worn leather barber's chair.  Inside, however, a single, warm lamp cast a golden glow upon the mahogany surfaces and the tools of the trade meticulously arranged on a small, cluttered counter.  Behind the counter, meticulously trimming a client’s unruly beard with silver shears that gleamed like polished moonstones, sat Antonín "Toni"  Prokop, a master barber whose skill with scissors was only surpassed by his knack for listening to the woes of the city's inhabitants.
