The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Mia” Hernandez’s hair across her face, stinging her cheeks a vibrant, angry red.  She tugged her worn wool scarf tighter, the rough texture a welcome contrast to the icy air.  The pre-dawn gloom clung to the cobbled streets of Prague, a heavy blanket woven from shadows and the faintest hint of woodsmoke.  Gas lamps, their flickering flames battling the encroaching darkness, cast an ethereal glow on the ancient architecture, highlighting the intricate carvings on the gothic buildings that clawed at the inky sky. Mia, bundled in layers against the chill, adjusted the strap of her battered camera bag, the weight of her equipment a familiar comfort in the solitude of the deserted city.  Her breath plumed out in white clouds, momentarily visible before vanishing into the damp, cold air.  Tonight, she wasn't just capturing images; tonight, she was hunting for a ghost.
