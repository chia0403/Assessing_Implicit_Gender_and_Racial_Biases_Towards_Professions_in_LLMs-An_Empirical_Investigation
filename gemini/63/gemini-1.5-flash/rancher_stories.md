The wind, a rasping, unforgiving hand, clawed at Silas Gage’s weathered face as he surveyed his land.  Dust devils, miniature tornadoes of red earth, danced across the parched expanse of the Broken Spur Ranch, their erratic movements mirroring the turmoil churning in Silas’s gut.  The sun, a malevolent eye in the brassy sky, beat down mercilessly on the cracked soil, baking the already brittle grass to a crisp.  Cacti, gnarled and tenacious, stood sentinel against the relentless assault, their spines gleaming like tiny, wicked swords.  The air hung thick and heavy with the scent of sagebrush and the faint, metallic tang of sweat and desperation.  For three months, the rain had stayed away, a cruel mistress withholding her life-giving embrace, threatening to swallow Silas’s legacy whole.  The Broken Spur, passed down through generations of his family, was withering, and with it, Silas’s hope.
