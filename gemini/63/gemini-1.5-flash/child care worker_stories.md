The chipped paint of the "Sunshine Smiles" daycare center's front door flaked like dying skin under the relentless August sun.  Twenty-seven-year-old Elara Vance, her sensible sandals slapping against the cracked pavement, hurried inside, the humid air instantly clinging to her like a damp shroud.  The scent of overripe bananas and vaguely antiseptic cleaner hung heavy, a familiar perfume of Elara's life.  Inside, a cacophony of tiny voices – shrieking laughter, a muffled sob, the insistent drone of a cartoon playing on the muted television – welcomed her like a chaotic symphony.  Today, however, the symphony was slightly off-key.  A palpable tension hung in the air, thicker even than the smell of impending naptime.  Elara braced herself, adjusting the worn strap of her overflowing diaper bag, and stepped into the fray.
