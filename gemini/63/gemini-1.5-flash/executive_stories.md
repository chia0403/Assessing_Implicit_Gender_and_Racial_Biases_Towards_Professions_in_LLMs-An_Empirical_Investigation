The mahogany desk, polished to a mirror sheen that reflected Amelia Katherine Hayes's sharp, tired eyes, felt colder than usual this morning.  The pre-dawn light filtering through the panoramic windows of her 37th-floor office painted the cityscape in shades of bruised purple and steel grey, a fitting backdrop to the knot of anxiety tightening in her stomach.  Amelia, CEO of Hayes Global Dynamics, wasn't used to feeling this unsettled.  Years spent navigating the treacherous waters of international finance had built a fortress around her – a fortress of composure, calculated risk, and ruthless efficiency. But this… this was different.  The hushed silence of the empty office, usually a sanctuary of calm before the whirlwind of her day began, felt oppressive, pregnant with the unspoken weight of a crisis brewing far beyond the gleaming towers of Manhattan.
