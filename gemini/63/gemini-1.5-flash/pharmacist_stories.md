The chipped porcelain of the apothecary jar felt cool against Dr. Elara Vance's fingertips.  Dust motes, illuminated by the single, sputtering gas lamp hanging above the counter of Vance's Pharmacy, danced in the stagnant air.  Outside, the relentless November wind howled a mournful dirge, rattling the ancient, storm-beaten windows of the shop nestled on the edge of Oakhaven, a town clinging precariously to the cliff face overlooking the churning grey sea. The scent of dried herbs – rosemary, lavender, and something faintly acrid she couldn't quite place – mingled with the more familiar aroma of crushed pills and potent tinctures, a comforting, if slightly melancholic, perfume that had permeated Elara's life since she'd inherited the pharmacy from her grandmother ten years ago.  Tonight, however, the usual comforting familiarity felt heavy, oppressive, mirroring the storm raging both outside and within her.
