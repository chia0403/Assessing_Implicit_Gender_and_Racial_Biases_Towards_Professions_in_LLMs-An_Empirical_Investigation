The salt-laced wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s hair across her face, stinging her cheeks with icy spray.  She pulled her charcoal grey wool coat tighter, the stiff fabric offering little solace against the November gale that howled across the craggy cliffs overlooking the churning Atlantic. Below, the waves crashed against the jagged rocks with a ferocity that mirrored the turmoil brewing inside her.  The air smelled of brine, seaweed, and something else… something faintly metallic, clinging to the back of her throat like the taste of old blood.  Amelia, a lawyer known for her sharp intellect and even sharper wit in the bustling city of Boston, found herself utterly out of her element on the windswept shores of Cape Ann, Massachusetts, a place steeped in legend and, she suspected, far more secrets than the charming seaside town let on.
