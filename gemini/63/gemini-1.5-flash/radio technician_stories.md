The static crackled, a malevolent whisper in the otherwise silent control room.  Amelia "Amy"  Jeanette Cartwright, her brow furrowed in concentration, adjusted the gain knob on the battered console.  Dust motes, illuminated by the sickly green glow of the oscilloscope, danced in the air of the abandoned radio station, clinging to the cobwebs that draped like macabre lace across the antiquated equipment.  Outside, the wind howled a mournful dirge through the broken panes of the north window, rattling the already precarious structure of WCRL, once a beacon of local news and music, now a mausoleum of forgotten broadcasts.  The air hung heavy with the scent of damp earth and decaying timber, punctuated by the metallic tang of rust and the faint, lingering smell of ozone from a recent electrical surge. Amy, armed with a soldering iron and a toolbox overflowing with spare parts, felt utterly alone in the heart of this crumbling edifice, a lone sentinel against the encroaching silence.
