The fluorescent lights of the Zenith Corporation hummed a monotonous tune, a counterpoint to the incessant clatter of Agnes Periwinkle’s high heels against the polished linoleum floor.  Agnes, whose name always felt slightly too grand for her life spent fielding calls from irate clients and lost packages, tapped a perfectly manicured nail against the mahogany reception desk.  The air, thick with the scent of stale coffee and expensive aftershave, hung heavy in the cavernous lobby.  Outside, the Chicago wind howled a mournful ballad, rattling the enormous glass windows that overlooked the city’s glittering skyline – a skyline Agnes rarely had time to appreciate.  Today, however, felt different.  A peculiar unease, a tremor of something unexpected, vibrated in the usually predictable rhythm of her workday.
