The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Katherine Finch's hands, the lukewarm tea doing little to soothe the icy dread creeping up her spine.  Rain lashed against the tall, gothic windows of her office, each gust a frantic percussion against the ancient, oak-paneled walls.  Amelia, a meticulous accountant with a penchant for order – a trait sorely tested by the current state of her employer's books – stared out at the storm-tossed cityscape of Blackwood, a brooding Victorian metropolis where shadows lingered long after dusk and secrets seemed woven into the very fabric of the cobbled streets.  The flickering gaslight cast long, dancing fingers across her cluttered desk, illuminating the mountain of ledgers and invoices that represented not just numbers, but the potential unraveling of a century-old family business.  Tonight, however, Amelia suspected the unraveling might be something far more sinister.
