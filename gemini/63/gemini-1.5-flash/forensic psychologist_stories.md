The biting November wind whipped at Dr. Eleanor Vance’s tailored coat as she navigated the uneven cobblestones leading to Blackwood Manor.  Rain, a relentless drizzle that had been falling since dawn, plastered her dark hair to her scalp, and the chill seeped deep into her bones despite the multiple layers beneath her wool. Blackwood Manor, perched precariously on a windswept cliff overlooking the churning grey expanse of the North Sea, was a gothic monstrosity, its darkened windows like vacant eyes staring out at the storm.  Ivy, thick and gnarled, clawed at its decaying stonework, mirroring the tenacious grip of the secrets Eleanor suspected lay hidden within. The air hung heavy with the scent of damp earth and something else… something acrid and metallic, clinging to the very fabric of the place like a lingering death rattle.  This was her third visit, and each time, the unsettling feeling intensified, a palpable sense of unease that even her years of experience as a forensic psychologist couldn't quite explain.
