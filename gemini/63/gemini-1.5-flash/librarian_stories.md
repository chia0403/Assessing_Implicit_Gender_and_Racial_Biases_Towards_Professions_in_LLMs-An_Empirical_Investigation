The scent of aged paper and leather hung heavy in the air, a comforting aroma that usually soothed Eleanor Vance's soul.  But tonight, the musty perfume of the Ashworth Memorial Library felt oppressive, clinging to her like the damp chill that seeped in from the ancient stone walls.  Rain lashed against the arched windows of the reading room, a relentless drumming that mirrored the frantic beat of Eleanor's heart. Outside, the wind howled a mournful tune, rattling the stained-glass panels depicting long-dead scholars and mythical beasts.  Inside, however, a different kind of beast was stirring – a sinister secret hidden within the library’s seemingly innocuous collection, a secret Eleanor was about to uncover.
