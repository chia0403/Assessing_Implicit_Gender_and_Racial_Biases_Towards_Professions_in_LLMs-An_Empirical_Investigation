The flickering neon sign of "Serenity Springs Massage" cast a sickly yellow glow on the rain-slicked street outside.  Inside, the air hung thick with the scent of lavender and sandalwood, a comforting counterpoint to the relentless drumming of rain against the storefront.  This was Amelia Nightingale's sanctuary, a place where the anxieties of the outside world were left to simmer on the city's grey canvas. Amelia, a massage therapist with hands as steady as the ancient stones of a mountain range, felt the familiar pull of exhaustion, a persistent ache that mirrored the throbbing heads and tense shoulders of her clients.  Tonight, though, the quiet hum of the shop felt different, disturbed by an unusual tremor of unspoken tension. Tonight, she felt it too.
