The chipped porcelain sink, stained a sickly yellow by years of forgotten lemon peels and spilled nail polish, reflected Amelia Nightingale's face.  Her emerald green eyes, usually sparkling with manicured wit, were clouded with a dull exhaustion.  The fluorescent lights of "Nail Nirvana," her tiny salon nestled between a used bookstore and a perpetually-crowded bakery, buzzed like angry bees.  Amelia, a manicurist of uncommon skill and even more uncommon quiet desperation, stared at her reflection.  The meticulous, almost obsessive, lines of her own perfectly sculpted nails seemed to mock the frayed edges of her life.  Today, another client, a woman named Mrs. Periwinkle with a penchant for the dramatic, was due, and Amelia knew the floral-themed designs she'd meticulously prepared would have to hold up against the swirling anxieties threatening to consume her.  Outside, the city of Veridia, a sprawling metropolis steeped in the scent of exhaust fumes and cheap coffee, thrummed with a life Amelia, for now, couldn't quite participate in.  The salon, a haven of fleeting beauty and whispered secrets, was the only place she felt remotely connected.
