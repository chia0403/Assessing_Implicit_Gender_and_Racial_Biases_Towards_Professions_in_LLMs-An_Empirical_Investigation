The chill wind, biting like a starved wolf, whipped across the jagged peaks of the Grey Mountains, carrying with it the faint scent of pine needles and the echoing, rhythmic clang of hammer on stone.  High above the village of Stonehaven, nestled in a valley choked by ancient trees, Elias Thorne, a stone cutter of unyielding resolve and calloused hands, worked.  The sun, a pale disc struggling to pierce the swirling mist, cast long, skeletal shadows across the towering face of the mountain.  Elias, his breath puffing out in white clouds, focused on the task before him – a colossal carving of a winged stag, destined to adorn the newly-constructed façade of the Temple of the Dawn.  He was halfway through the stag's intricate antler structure, the sculpted form already hinting at the creature's graceful power.  But something about the stone itself felt wrong, a whisper of resistance beneath the chisel that tugged at his focus.
