The fluorescent lights of the emergency room hummed, a relentless, almost mocking soundtrack to the chaotic symphony of sirens, beeping machines, and the ragged breaths of the patients sprawled across the examination tables.  Rain hammered against the grimy windowpanes of St. Jude's, mirroring the tempest brewing inside Amelia "Amy"  Fitzgerald, a registered nurse with five years' experience at the hospital.  Tonight, however, felt different.  The usual adrenaline rush, the familiar dance with death, was replaced by a cold dread, a chilling unease that settled deep in her gut.  The air, thick with the scent of antiseptic and fear, felt heavy with unspoken anxieties.  Amy glanced at the clock: 2:17 AM.  Another long night, another mountain of suffering.  The emergency room, a chaotic battlefield, was her current domain.
