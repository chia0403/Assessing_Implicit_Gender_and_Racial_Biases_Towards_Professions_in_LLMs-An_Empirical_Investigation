The biting November wind whipped through the skeletal branches of the ancient oaks, rattling the glass of the aviary.  Inside, Amelia Blackwood, Animal Caretaker Extraordinaire at the Blackwood Sanctuary for Endangered Species, meticulously cleaned the parrot enclosure.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of sunlight piercing the gloom, illuminating the vibrant plumage of the macaws, their squawks echoing through the cavernous space.  Amelia, her dark hair pulled back in a practical braid, knelt beside a nesting box, her gloved hands deftly arranging fresh sprigs of eucalyptus.  The sanctuary, nestled deep within the whispering woods of the Cotswolds, was a haven for creatures both large and small, a testament to Amelia's lifelong dedication to preserving biodiversity.  The air, thick with the scent of pine and damp earth, held a peculiar quiet that belied the ceaseless activity beneath the surface.  Today, however, a different kind of quiet settled over her, a quiet that felt…unnerving.  She'd seen nothing but the usual routine in the last week, except… something felt off.
