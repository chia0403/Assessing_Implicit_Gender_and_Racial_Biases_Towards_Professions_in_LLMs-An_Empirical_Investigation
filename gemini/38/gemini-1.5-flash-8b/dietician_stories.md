The humid air hung heavy, thick with the scent of jasmine and simmering spices.  Sunlight, fractured by the dense foliage of the mango trees, dappled the tiled courtyard of the clinic.  Dr. Isabella "Izzy" Ramirez, meticulously organized and always a few steps ahead, adjusted her crisp white uniform, a small frown etching itself onto her brow.  The clinic, nestled in the heart of the vibrant, bustling neighbourhood of Barrio San Miguel, pulsed with a rhythm of hushed conversations, the rhythmic clatter of pots and pans, and the distant cries of street vendors.  Izzy, a registered dietician with a passion for holistic nutrition, was currently overseeing a nutrition education workshop for local mothers.  Her meticulously prepared presentation on balanced meals and portion control lay neatly on her table, ready for the arrival of her eager students.  But a sense of unease, a persistent tremor at the edge of her calm exterior, nagged at her.
