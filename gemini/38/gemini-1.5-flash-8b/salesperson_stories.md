The fluorescent lights of the "Everything You Need, Guaranteed!" store hummed, a soundtrack to Amelia Butterfield's quiet desperation.  Dust motes danced in the harsh glare, reflecting off the pristine, almost sterile displays of gardening tools, from miniature trowels to oversized digging forks.  Amelia, a whirlwind of nervous energy contained within a meticulously tailored navy blazer, stood behind the "Trellis and Twine" section, a small, almost hidden alcove tucked between the compost bins and the self-checkout kiosks.  Each meticulously arranged trellis, each lovingly wrapped vine, felt like a silent accusation.  Today, sales were as dry as the desert succulents lining the shelves.  And Amelia, despite her unwavering belief in the restorative power of well-placed greenery, was beginning to feel a little withered herself.  It was a Tuesday, the slowest day of the week, and the only customers seemed to be lost souls searching for the "perfect" potting soil.
