The fluorescent lights of the "Sweat Equity" gym hummed, a relentless soundtrack to Amelia "Sparky"  Fitzwilliam's daily routine.  Dust motes danced in the shafts of sunlight piercing the grime-coated windows, illuminating the polished floors reflecting a thousand tiny, determined faces.  Sparky, her signature bright pink sweatband pulled tightly, surveyed the room, her gaze sharp and analytical.  Rows of treadmills groaned under the weight of runners, a symphony of rhythmic thump-thumps accompanying the rhythmic beeps and whistles of the cardio equipment.  The air, thick with the scent of ozone and sweat, felt electric.  This was her kingdom, her battleground, where she sculpted bodies and forged will power, one rep at a time.
