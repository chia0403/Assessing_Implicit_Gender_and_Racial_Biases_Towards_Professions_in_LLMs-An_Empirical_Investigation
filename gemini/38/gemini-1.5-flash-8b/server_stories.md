The flickering neon sign of "The Rusty Mug" cast a sickly yellow glow over Elara Blackwood, illuminating the grease smudges on her apron and the weariness in her eyes.  Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof, a relentless counterpoint to the clinking of mugs and the low hum of conversation inside.  The small diner, nestled between a sprawling junkyard and a surprisingly vibrant flower shop, was a haven of greasy spoons and whispered secrets in the heart of the forgotten corner of Havenwood.  Elara, a server with a name that whispered of dark secrets and a past she preferred to leave behind, felt the familiar sting of loneliness prickle at her skin, as persistent as the persistent smell of frying onions.  Tonight, however, a new face was making her heart flutter in a way that went beyond the usual flutter of customer complaints.  A young man, with eyes as dark as the shadowed corners of the diner, was lingering near the counter, meticulously examining the menu.
