The biting wind of the Outer Hebrides whipped Amelia Blackwood's scarf tighter around her neck, stinging her cheeks.  Grey, relentless clouds hung low over the jagged peaks of the Isle of Harris, mirroring the storm brewing within her.  Rain, the constant companion of this remote outpost of Scotland, hammered against the battered corrugated iron roof of the local community centre, a place more accustomed to the hushed murmurs of islanders than the cacophony of a newsroom.  Amelia, a freelance reporter for the *Glasgow Gazette*, huddled deeper into her worn-out tweed coat, her breath clouding the air.  The flickering fluorescent light above cast long, dancing shadows across the cramped, sparsely furnished room.  This wasn't the glamorous world of city news; this was the gritty reality of a place where whispers travelled faster than the ferries, and truth was often as elusive as the elusive local legend of the Storr Ghoul.  Outside, the tempest raged, mirroring the turmoil in her mind.  The local fishing community had vanished.  Not disappeared, not moved. Vanished.  And Amelia was tasked with uncovering why.
