Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of the dilapidated clinic, a relentless percussion mirroring the frantic thoughts swirling in Dr. Eleanor Vance's mind.  The air, thick with the smell of mildew and disinfectant, hung heavy in the sterile, yet somehow suffocating, examination room.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of light filtering through a grimy window, illuminating the faded floral wallpaper and casting long, skeletal shadows across the cluttered desk.  This wasn't the pristine, technologically advanced lab of the Metropolitan Police Department; this was the ramshackle, almost forgotten, forensic psychology clinic tucked away in the shadowed alleys of the city's underbelly.  Eleanor, a forensic psychologist with a reputation for unraveling the darkest corners of the human mind, was about to confront a case unlike any other she'd encountered.  The victim, a renowned artist named Isabella Rossi, had been found, brutally murdered, in her secluded studio, her masterpiece, a canvas of vibrant chaos, inexplicably untouched.  The only clue: a single, crimson feather, lodged deep within the victim's outstretched hand.
