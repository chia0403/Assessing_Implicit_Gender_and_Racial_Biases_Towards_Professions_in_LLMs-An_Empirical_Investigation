The biting November wind whipped Amelia Rose Hawthorne's trench coat around her legs as she hurried down the cobbled street.  Leaden clouds hung low over the ancient city of  Aethelgard, casting long, skeletal shadows that stretched like grasping fingers from the half-timbered buildings.  The scent of woodsmoke mingled with the crisp, metallic tang of rain, a familiar perfume of the late autumn season.  Aethelgard, nestled in a valley surrounded by towering, forested hills, pulsed with a quiet energy, a subtle hum of activity beneath the cloak of twilight.  Amelia, a chiropractor with a practice nestled within the heart of the old town, knew this city intimately.  Its narrow alleyways whispered stories of generations past, each stone a silent witness to the ebb and flow of life.  Tonight, however, a different, more urgent hum resonated within her.
