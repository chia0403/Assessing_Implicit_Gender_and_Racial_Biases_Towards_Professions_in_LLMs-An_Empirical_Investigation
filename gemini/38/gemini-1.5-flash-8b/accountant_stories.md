The fluorescent lights of the accounting firm, "Numbers & Nuggets," hummed a monotonous tune, a soundtrack to the rhythmic clack of Amelia Peabody's keyboard.  Amelia, a precise accountant with a penchant for perfectly organized files and meticulously balanced ledgers, stared at the screen, her brow furrowed in concentration.  The numbers danced before her eyes, a dizzying ballet of debits and credits, figures swirling like smoke in a perpetual accounting audit.  Outside, the grey London sky mirrored the relentless gloom of the spreadsheets she was wrestling with.  It was a Tuesday, and the damp chill seeped into the cavernous office, clinging to the worn leather chairs and the forgotten mugs of lukewarm coffee.  The air was thick with the scent of stale paper and the faintly metallic tang of fear, a subtle pressure on Amelia's chest.  A pending audit of the prestigious Blackwood Industries, a company with a history as shadowy as its financial dealings, loomed large.
