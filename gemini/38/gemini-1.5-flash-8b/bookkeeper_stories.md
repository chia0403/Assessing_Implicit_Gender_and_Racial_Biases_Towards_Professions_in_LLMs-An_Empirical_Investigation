The chipped Formica countertop gleamed under the harsh fluorescent lights of "Numbers N' Things," a tiny accounting office tucked away on a side street off the main thoroughfare.  Dust motes danced in the solitary shaft of afternoon sun slicing through the grime-coated window, illuminating a scene of organized chaos.  Stacks of invoices, receipts, and tax forms, piled high like miniature mountains, surrounded Amelia Cavendish, Bookkeeper Extraordinaire.  Amelia, a woman of thirty-two with perpetually tired eyes and a surprising amount of determination etched onto her face, was wrestling with a particularly unruly ledger, its pages filled with figures both familiar and maddeningly inscrutable. The air hung thick with the scent of stale coffee and desperation, a scent Amelia had come to know all too well.  Today, however, the usual frenetic energy of the office was subdued, a strange calm before the storm of the quarterly audit that was about to descend.
