The flickering fluorescent lights of the D&M Architectural firm hummed a monotonous tune, punctuated by the rhythmic scratch of pencils on vellum. Amelia "Milly" Reyes, her dark hair pulled back in a tight bun, leaned over her drafting table, the cool metal pressing against her forearms. Outside, the Chicago wind howled a mournful song against the glass windows of the skyscraper, hinting at the approaching November chill.  Her workspace, tucked away in a corner cubicle on the 27th floor, felt miles away from the bustling city below.  Blueprints, tracing paper, and mechanical pencils formed a fortress around her, a familiar sanctuary where she translated the dreams of architects into the precise language of lines and angles. This evening, however, the familiar comfort was replaced with a gnawing unease.  The blueprints spread before her depicted not just another high-rise condo, but the demolition of the very building she occupied, a project slated to make way for something bigger, something shinier.
