Rain lashed against the window of Amelia Grace Holloway's cramped office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her.  Tax season was reaching its crescendo, a symphony of ringing phones, frantic clients, and the relentless whir of the printer churning out form after form.  Amelia's office, nestled above a laundromat in the perpetually damp basement of a faded brick building on Bleecker Street in Greenwich Village, wasn't glamorous, but it was hers.  Every chipped mug, every overflowing file folder, every slightly-too-sweet air freshener spoke to her years of tireless work building her practice. This year, however, felt different. A knot of unease tightened in Amelia’s stomach. It wasn't just the usual pre-deadline stress. Something felt…off.
