The humid air hung heavy, thick with the scent of plumeria and exhaust fumes.  Agnes Periwinkle, her name as whimsical as her floral-print sundresses, adjusted the microphone clipped to her collar.  A bead of sweat trickled down her temple, threatening to smudge the meticulously applied mascara she considered essential battlefield gear in the war against Bangkok humidity.  Around her, the twenty-odd members of her "Hidden Gems of Bangkok" tour group shifted restlessly in the shade of a giant banyan tree, its roots snaking across the cracked pavement like slumbering pythons.  Wat Arun, the Temple of Dawn, shimmered across the Chao Phraya River, its porcelain-encrusted spires catching the late afternoon sun, a dazzling beacon against a sky bruised with the promise of a monsoon.  Agnes, despite having seen this view a thousand times, felt a familiar flutter of awe. This was her city, her stage, and these wide-eyed tourists, her captive audience.  "Right then, lovelies," she announced, her voice amplified and slightly tinny through the speaker system, "Ready to delve into the secrets this magnificent temple holds?"
