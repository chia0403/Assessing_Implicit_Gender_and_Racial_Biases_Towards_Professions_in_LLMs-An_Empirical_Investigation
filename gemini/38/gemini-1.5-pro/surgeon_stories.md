The biting November wind whipped around Dr. Elias Thorne as he hurried across the cobblestone courtyard of St. Jude's Hospital, the gothic spires of the ancient building looming against the bruised London sky.  Rain, a near-constant companion in this city, began to fall, slicking the stones beneath his worn leather shoes. The glow from the arched windows of the operating theatre spilled out onto the courtyard, a beacon in the encroaching gloom.  Inside, a life hung in the balance, and Elias, his heart pounding a frantic rhythm against his ribs, knew he had precious little time to spare.
