The biting November wind whipped around Dr. Eleanor Vance as she hurried across the sprawling, leaf-strewn campus of Ashwood Preparatory Academy.  Grey stone buildings, gothic in style and imposing in size, loomed over her, their leaded windows reflecting the steely sky. Ashwood, nestled deep in the Connecticut woods, prided itself on its rigorous academics and its cultivation of "well-rounded" students.  Eleanor, however, had learned in her three years at the school that beneath the veneer of manicured lawns and perfectly pressed uniforms, a darker, more complicated ecosystem thrived.  This morning's summons from Headmaster Thorne, delivered via a curt email with the subject line "Urgent - Student Incident," suggested a particularly thorny specimen had sprouted.
