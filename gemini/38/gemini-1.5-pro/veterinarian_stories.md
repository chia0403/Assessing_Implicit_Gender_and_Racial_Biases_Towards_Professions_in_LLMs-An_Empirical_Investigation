The biting wind whipped across the desolate plains of Wyoming, carrying with it the scent of sagebrush and the low moan of a distant train.  Dr. Elias Vance, his breath clouding in the frigid air, squinted at the dilapidated ranch house silhouetted against the bruised twilight sky.  Broken-down corrals leaned precariously, and a rust-eaten windmill creaked a mournful song.  This was the furthest call he'd received yet, a desperate plea from a rancher named Jedediah who claimed his prize bull was on the verge of death. Elias, bundled in his thickest parka and armed with his well-worn medical bag, trudged through the knee-high snow drifts, the crunch under his boots the only sound breaking the vast, lonely silence of the high plains.
