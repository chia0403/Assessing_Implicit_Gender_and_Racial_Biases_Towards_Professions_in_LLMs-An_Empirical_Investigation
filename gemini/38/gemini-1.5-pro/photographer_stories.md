The salt spray kissed Amelia Reyes's face, a familiar, gritty welcome.  The rusty tang of the ocean filled the air, mixing with the sweet, decaying scent of kelp piled high on the shore.  Grey clouds, heavy with the promise of rain, clung low to the jagged peaks of the Dragon's Tooth Mountains, their shadows darkening the already twilight sky of the Isle of Aisling. Amelia, perched precariously on a wind-battered outcrop overlooking the churning North Atlantic, adjusted the tripod of her battered Hasselblad, the cold metal biting into her gloved fingers.  She was searching for the elusive "green flash," that fleeting burst of emerald light sometimes seen at the moment the sun dips below the horizon.  It was a fool's errand, most likely, given the impending storm, but Amelia, ever the optimist when it came to capturing ephemeral beauty, remained steadfast, her dark eyes fixed on the fiery sliver of sun rapidly sinking towards the turbulent waves.
