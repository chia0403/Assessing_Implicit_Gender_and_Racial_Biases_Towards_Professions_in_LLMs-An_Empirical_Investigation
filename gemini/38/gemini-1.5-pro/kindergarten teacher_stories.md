The chipped ceramic frog perched on Ms. Eleanor Vance's desk stared blankly at the swirling chaos of glitter, construction paper scraps, and half-eaten glue sticks.  Room 2B of Sunny Meadow Elementary, usually a vibrant kaleidoscope of childish energy, was strangely silent. The late afternoon sun, filtering through the large window overlooking the playground, cast long shadows across the miniature tables and chairs, painting the room in a melancholic gold.  Eleanor, her usually bright blue eyes clouded with worry, knelt beside a lopsided tower of brightly coloured blocks, a single, red Lego brick clutched in her hand.  It was the only clue left behind after little Sammy Peterson vanished, seemingly into thin air, during naptime.
