Rain lashed against the grimy windows of Silas Finch's office, each drop a staccato note in the symphony of the city. Neon signs reflected in the slick streets of downtown New Eden, casting an eerie glow on the waterlogged pavements below. Silas Blackwood, his trench coat still dripping from the downpour, sat hunched over his desk, the only light source a flickering desk lamp that cast elongated shadows across the cramped room.  Cigarette smoke, thick and cloying, hung heavy in the air, mingling with the scent of stale coffee and cheap whiskey. The city, usually a cacophony of noise, was muffled by the storm, creating an unsettling sense of quiet punctuated by the rhythmic drumming of the rain.  A single photograph, a faded image of a smiling woman with auburn hair, lay face down on the otherwise cluttered desk.
