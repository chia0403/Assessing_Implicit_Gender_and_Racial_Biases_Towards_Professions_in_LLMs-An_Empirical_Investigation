The bell above the door of "Petal & Plume" tinkled a melancholic two-note chime, the sound swallowed almost immediately by the humid Charleston air.  Elara Josephine Beaumont, her fingers stained a delicate shade of lilac from arranging hyacinths, glanced up from her worktable. Outside, the Spanish moss hanging from the live oaks swayed lazily in the afternoon heat, casting dappled shadows across the cobblestone street.  Inside, the small shop was a riot of color and fragrance: buckets overflowing with sunflowers, roses, and lilies, garlands of eucalyptus and ivy draped artfully across the walls, and the sweet, earthy scent of freshly-cut stems permeating every corner. Elara sighed, pushing a stray strand of auburn hair from her face. Business had been slow, and the air conditioner, a wheezing behemoth in the corner, seemed to be losing its battle against the South Carolina summer.
