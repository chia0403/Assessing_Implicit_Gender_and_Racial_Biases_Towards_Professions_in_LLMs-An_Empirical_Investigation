The clatter of horse-drawn carriages against cobblestones was a constant lullaby in the bustling port city of Aethelgard. The year was 1888, and a perpetual haze of coal smoke clung to the narrow, winding streets, softening the sharp edges of the gaslight glow.  Nestled between a boisterous pub and a fishmonger with a perpetually runny nose, sat the small but meticulously organized shop of Elias Finch, Tailor.  Elias, whose full name was Elias Peregrine Finch, a name whispered to be as fanciful as his buttonhole stitching, was hunched over his workbench, the warm glow of a kerosene lamp illuminating the fine weave of emerald green silk draped across his lap. Outside, the autumn wind howled, rattling the panes of his shop window, but within, the air was still and quiet, save for the rhythmic snip-snip-snip of his shears.
