The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Amy” Reyes’s hair across her face as she hurried across the cobbled courtyard of Elderwood Manor.  Flakes of snow, fat and lazy, began to drift down, catching in the gnarled branches of the ancient oak that dominated the center of the courtyard. Elderwood, a sprawling Victorian mansion converted into a rehabilitation center nestled in the Catskill Mountains of upstate New York, usually exuded a comforting, old-world charm.  But today, a distinct chill permeated the air, a chill that seemed to seep deeper than the dropping temperature. Amy clutched her satchel tighter, a knot of unease tightening in her stomach. This was only her second week working at Elderwood, and while the staff and patients had been welcoming, there was an undeniable air of mystery clinging to the place, like the faint scent of woodsmoke that lingered even when the fireplaces were cold.
