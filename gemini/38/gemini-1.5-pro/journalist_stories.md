Rain lashed against the grimy windows of The Daily Clarion’s office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside Amelia “Amy” Reyes.  Fluorescent lights hummed overhead, casting a sickly yellow glow on the cluttered desks and overflowing ashtrays. Cigarette smoke clung to the air like a shroud, a testament to the late hour and the frayed nerves of the remaining staff. Amy, her dark hair pulled back into a tight bun, tapped a chewed-up pen against her teeth, staring at the blinking cursor on her battered laptop screen.  It was almost 3 am in the forgotten town of Havenwood, Pennsylvania, a place where the only breaking news was usually the annual bake sale or the occasional escaped goat from Old Man Hemlock's farm.  Tonight, however, felt different.  A low thrum of unease vibrated through the usually sleepy town, a feeling Amy couldn't quite shake off, and it had nothing to do with deadlines or the lukewarm coffee staining her mug.
