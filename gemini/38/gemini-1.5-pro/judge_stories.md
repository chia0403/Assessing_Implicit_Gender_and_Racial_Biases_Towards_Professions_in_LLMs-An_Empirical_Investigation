The Honorable Seraphina Beatrice Moreau tapped a long, polished fingernail against the worn leather of her desk, the rhythmic click echoing in the otherwise silent chambers. Rain lashed against the arched windows of her office, a relentless drumming that mirrored the tension coiling in her gut.  Outside, the gaslights of Old Haven flickered and swayed in the wind, casting long, dancing shadows across the cobbled streets below.  Old Haven, a city built on whispers and secrets, clung to the edge of the Whispering Cliffs, a place where the sea gnawed relentlessly at the stone, much like the tide of corruption that seemed to be eroding the city's foundations from within.  Tonight, that tide threatened to drown Judge Moreau in its depths.
