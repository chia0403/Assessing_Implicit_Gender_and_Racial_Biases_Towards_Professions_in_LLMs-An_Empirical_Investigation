The crisp October air, scented with woodsmoke and decaying leaves, whipped around Eleanor Vance’s ankles as she hurried across the cobbled courtyard of Eventide Manor.  Gargoyles leered from the eaves of the imposing Victorian mansion, their granite faces slick with rain.  Built in 1889 by a wealthy industrialist with a penchant for the macabre, Eventide now served as a secluded wellness retreat nestled deep in the Vermont woods. Eleanor, a registered dietitian with a reputation for transforming the diets and lives of even the most stubborn clients, had arrived only an hour earlier.  Her breath puffed out in white clouds as she clutched her briefcase tighter, the polished wood a reassuring weight against the creeping unease that settled in her stomach. This retreat, she had been assured, catered to a very… *exclusive* clientele. But the locked gates, the unnervingly silent staff, and the sheer gothic opulence of the place hinted at something beyond mere exclusivity. Something Eleanor couldn't quite put her finger on.
