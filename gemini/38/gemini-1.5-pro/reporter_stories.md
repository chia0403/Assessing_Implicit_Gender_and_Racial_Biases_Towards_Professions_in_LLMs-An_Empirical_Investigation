Rain lashed against the grimy windows of The Daily Clarion, mimicking the tempest brewing inside Amelia "Amy" Reyes.  The flickering fluorescent lights of the newsroom buzzed overhead, casting a sickly yellow glow on the scattered desks and overflowing ashtrays. Cigarette smoke hung thick in the air, a stale perfume mingling with the bitter tang of cheap coffee. It was 1957, and McCarthyism’s chill still clung to Philadelphia like the damp November air. Amy, her dark hair pulled back in a tight bun, tapped a stubby pencil against her teeth, staring at the blank page in her typewriter.  Her editor, a gruff man named O’Malley with a face like a crumpled newspaper, had just assigned her the fluff piece of the century: a profile on the local butcher’s prize-winning sausage.  Amy, however, craved a story with teeth, something that would bite back at the apathy and fear that seemed to have choked the city.  Something real.  Something dangerous.
