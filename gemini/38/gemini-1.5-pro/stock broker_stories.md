The rain hammered against the forty-foot windows of Elias Thorne's office, a relentless percussive rhythm mirroring the anxiety churning in his gut.  Fifty-two stories above the glistening, rain-slicked streets of Manhattan, the city lights blurred into a hazy, impressionistic painting.  Elias, whose full name was Elias Vance Thorne III, a legacy he carried with a mixture of pride and suffocating pressure, stared out at the view.  His office, a testament to success sculpted from polished mahogany and brushed steel, felt less like a sanctuary and more like a gilded cage.  The air hung thick with the scent of expensive leather and impending doom.  It was October 29th, 2008, and the whispers of a market crash had grown into a deafening roar.
