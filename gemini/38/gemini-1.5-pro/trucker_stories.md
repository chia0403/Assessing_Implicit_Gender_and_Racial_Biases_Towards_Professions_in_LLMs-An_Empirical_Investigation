The rusty diner sign creaked in the relentless Nevada wind, its neon "Starlight Oasis" flickering like a dying firefly. Inside, the air hung thick with the aroma of stale coffee and frying spam.  Dust motes danced in the single beam of sunlight slicing through the grimy window, illuminating a lone figure hunched over a mug.  This was Amelia "Amy" Rose Calloway's regular pitstop on her long haul from Reno to Albuquerque, a desolate haven in a sea of endless asphalt and shimmering mirages.  Outside, her rig, a battered Kenworth W900 she'd affectionately nicknamed "The Wanderer," growled impatiently, its chrome gleaming under the harsh desert sun.  Amy, with her calloused hands wrapped around the warm ceramic, stared out at the vast expanse of nothingness, the setting sun painting the sky in hues of fiery orange and bruised purple.  This was her life: the open road, the hum of the engine, and the quiet solitude that stretched for miles in every direction. But tonight, something felt different.  A low thrumming vibration in the air, like a distant heartbeat, seemed to echo the growing unease in her gut.
