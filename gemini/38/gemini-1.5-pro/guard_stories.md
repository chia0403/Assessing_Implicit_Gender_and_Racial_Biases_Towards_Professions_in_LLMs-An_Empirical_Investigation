The biting wind whipped across the Obsidian Cliffs, carrying with it the scent of brine and the mournful cries of gulls.  Far below, the slate-grey waves of the Iron Sea crashed against the jagged rocks, a ceaseless symphony of fury.  Atop the highest point of the Cliffs, perched precariously on the edge of a crumbling watchtower, stood Elias Thorne, his breath misting in the frigid air. Elias, a veteran guard of the Citadel of Whispers, shifted his weight, the leather of his greaves creaking beneath the thick wool of his cloak. For five years he had held this lonely vigil, his eyes constantly scanning the horizon, searching for any sign of the dreaded Reavers who haunted the sea lanes.  The watchtower, once a proud edifice of black stone, now bore the scars of time and weather, its walls etched with runes of protection that seemed to offer little comfort against the ever-present chill. Tonight, however, the chill felt different, deeper, and a prickling unease ran down Elias's spine. The air held a stillness that was more menacing than any storm.
