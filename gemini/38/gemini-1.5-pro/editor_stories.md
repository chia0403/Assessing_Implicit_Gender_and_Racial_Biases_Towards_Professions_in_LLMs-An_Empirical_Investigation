Rain lashed against the attic window of Amelia “Mia” Thorne’s cramped office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her.  Loose manuscripts, their pages curling in the damp, threatened to avalanche off the precarious stacks lining the walls.  Outside, the gothic spires of Oxford University pressed against the bruised twilight sky, their silhouettes stark against the lingering lavender hues. Mia, hunched over a particularly turgid academic paper on the socio-economic implications of medieval beekeeping, chewed on the end of her pen, the bitter ink tasting almost as bad as the prose she was editing.  Her office, nestled in the eaves of a converted Victorian house that doubled as the offices of "Scholarly Pursuits" publishing house, offered stunning views of the dreaming spires, but right now, all Mia could see was a swarm of misplaced commas and a distinct lack of coherent argument.
