The crisp October air, scented with woodsmoke and decaying leaves, nipped at Dr. Elias Thorne's cheeks as he hurried across the cobblestone courtyard of Alderwood Manor.  The grand Victorian mansion, once a private residence, now served as a rehabilitation center tucked away in the Catskill Mountains.  Its sprawling grounds, ablaze with the fiery hues of autumn, offered a tranquil backdrop to the often arduous journeys of recovery his patients embarked upon. Elias pulled his tweed jacket tighter around him, the setting sun casting long shadows across the manicured lawns.  Inside, the warm glow of lamps spilled from the tall, arched windows, promising the comfort of a roaring fire and the quiet rustle of turning pages in the library. But tonight, tranquility seemed a distant prospect.  A new patient, one with a particularly complex case, was due to arrive, and a knot of apprehension tightened in Elias's stomach.
