The flickering gaslight cast long, dancing shadows across Amelia Prudence Hawthorne's meticulously organized desk.  Ledgers, bound in faded green leather and filled with her precise script, lay open beneath its warm glow. Outside, the November wind howled down the narrow cobbled streets of Hollow Creek, rattling the windowpanes of her small office above the bustling O’Malley’s General Store.  The year was 1923, and Hollow Creek, nestled deep in the Appalachian Mountains of North Carolina, was a town steeped in secrets as old and deep as the surrounding hollows themselves. Amelia, with her wire-rimmed spectacles perched precariously on her nose, preferred the quiet company of numbers to the whispers and rumors that permeated the town like the ever-present mountain mist. Tonight, however, the silence was broken by a sharp rap at the door, a sound as unexpected as a bloom in winter.
