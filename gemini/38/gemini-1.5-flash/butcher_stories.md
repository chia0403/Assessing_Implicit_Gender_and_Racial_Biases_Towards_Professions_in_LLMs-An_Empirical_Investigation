The air hung thick and heavy with the scent of blood and sawdust, a familiar perfume to Bartholomew "Barty" Butterfield.  Barty, a man whose hands were as calloused as oak bark and whose apron bore the stains of a thousand carcasses, surveyed his domain: Butterfield's Meats, a butcher shop wedged between a sputtering bakery and a perpetually-empty antique store on the cobbled, rain-slicked streets of Grimsborough.  The shop itself was a testament to time; dark wood paneling groaned under the weight of years, the floorboards creaked a mournful tune underfoot, and the chill seeped from the aged stone walls, a stark contrast to the warm, crimson glow emanating from the hanging sides of beef.  Outside, a relentless November drizzle mirrored the grim mood settling over the town, a mood Barty felt keenly, even before the discovery.
