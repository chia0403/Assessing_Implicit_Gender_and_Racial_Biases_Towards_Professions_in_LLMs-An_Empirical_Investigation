The humid Charleston air hung heavy, thick with the scent of magnolias and something faintly…rotten.  Dr. Eleanor Vance, registered dietician and owner of the surprisingly successful "Nourish Charleston" practice, wiped a bead of sweat from her brow, her crisp white linen shirt clinging uncomfortably.  The midday sun beat down on her as she navigated the cobblestone alleyway, the sound of her sensible heels clicking a staccato rhythm against the uneven surface.  Overgrown ivy clawed at crumbling brick walls, threatening to swallow the already shadowed passage whole.  This wasn't the picturesque postcard Charleston; this was the Charleston hidden behind the glossy brochures, a city of secrets whispered amongst crumbling foundations and forgotten stories – and today, Eleanor was about to uncover one.
