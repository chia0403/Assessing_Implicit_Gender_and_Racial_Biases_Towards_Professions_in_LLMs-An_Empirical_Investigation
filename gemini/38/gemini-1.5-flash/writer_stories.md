The chipped mug warmed Amelia Blackwood’s fingers, the lukewarm tea doing little to thaw the icy grip of November settling over the Scottish Highlands.  Rain lashed against the single-paned window of her writing cabin, a converted shepherd’s hut perched precariously on the windswept hillside overlooking Loch Ness.  Inside, the air hung thick with the scent of peat smoke and old paper, a comforting aroma that did little to soothe the gnawing anxiety churning in Amelia’s stomach.  Her deadline loomed, a monstrous, shadowy figure threatening to swallow her whole, and the blank page on her laptop screen mocked her with its pristine emptiness.  The only sound louder than the relentless drumming of the rain was the frantic tap-tap-tap of her own increasingly desperate thoughts.
