The biting November wind whipped Amelia Katherine Hayes's auburn hair across her face as she stepped out of the chauffeured limousine.  Rain, a fine, icy drizzle, plastered her impeccably tailored Burberry coat to her frame.  Amelia, Executive Vice President of Hayes & Sterling Industries, barely registered the chill.  Her gaze was fixed on the imposing structure looming before her: Blackwood Manor, a gothic monstrosity perched precariously on a windswept cliff overlooking the turbulent Atlantic.  Its darkened windows stared out like empty eyes, reflecting the stormy sky and mirroring the turmoil churning within her own meticulously ordered world.  This wasn't a business meeting; it was a family summons, and Amelia knew, with a certainty that chilled her more than the wind, that it would be anything but pleasant.
