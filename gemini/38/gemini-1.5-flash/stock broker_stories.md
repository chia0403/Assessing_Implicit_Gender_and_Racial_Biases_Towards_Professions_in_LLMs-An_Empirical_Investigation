The biting November wind whipped Amelia Katherine Holloway’s perfectly tailored wool coat around her legs as she hurried across the plaza, the mirrored glass towers of the Financial District reflecting the bruised, twilight sky.  Rain, a fine, icy drizzle, plastered her auburn hair to her forehead, blurring the already indistinct cityscape.  Amelia, a woman whose life was usually measured in precise trades and calculated risks, felt a prickle of unease that had nothing to do with the volatile Dow Jones. It wasn't the market that had her on edge tonight; it was the cryptic message waiting in her locked office, a message that suggested her meticulously built world was about to crumble.  The message, a single, blood-red playing card – the Queen of Spades – lay nestled amongst the crisp, white reports and the meticulously organized spreadsheets, a stark contrast to the order she so carefully cultivated.
