The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia “Mia” Holloway’s hands, a small comfort against the biting November wind that howled outside the dusty windows of her office.  Rain lashed against the panes, mimicking the relentless drumming of her headache.  Her office, a cramped space above a failing bakery in the perpetually overcast town of Port Blossom, smelled faintly of stale sugar and damp wool.  A single, flickering fluorescent light buzzed overhead, illuminating a chaotic desk piled high with insurance forms, half-eaten candy bars, and the ever-present scent of impending doom – the aroma, Mia often joked, of her profession.  The town itself, nestled between a brooding mountain range and a perpetually stormy sea, seemed to reflect the melancholy that had settled over her lately.  Even the cheerful, slightly off-kilter Christmas decorations the bakery owner had haphazardly strung across the street couldn't quite pierce the gloom. This wasn't just any rainy Tuesday in Port Blossom; this was the day Mia Holloway's meticulously ordered life threatened to unravel completely.
