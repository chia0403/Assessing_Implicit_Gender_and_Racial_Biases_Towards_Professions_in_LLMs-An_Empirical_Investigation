The scent of aged paper and leather hung heavy in the air, a comforting, familiar perfume to Eleanor Vance.  Dust motes, illuminated by the late afternoon sun slanting through the arched windows of the Grandchester Public Library, danced in the stillness.  Eleanor, meticulously straightening a row of oversized folios on the lower shelves, paused, her fingers tracing the embossed gold lettering on a particularly worn volume: *A Treatise on the Properties of Obscure Minerals*.  Outside, the wind howled a mournful tune, rattling the ancient panes, a stark contrast to the hushed reverence within the library’s hallowed walls.  The Grandchester Public Library, a gothic masterpiece of grey stone and towering spires, stood sentinel over the town, its secrets as numerous and well-preserved as the countless volumes within its embrace.  Tonight, however, a different kind of secret was about to unfold, a secret that would reach far beyond the dusty shelves and into the shadowy corners of Eleanor’s own carefully ordered life.
