The fluorescent lights of Miller & Zois hummed a monotonous tune, a counterpoint to the frantic tap-tap-tapping of Amelia “Amy” Hernandez’s fingers across her keyboard.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of their twenty-second-floor office, blurring the already hazy cityscape of Chicago into a watercolor wash of grey and black.  Amy, a paralegal with a caffeine addiction rivaling the city's population, squinted at the mountain of case files stacked precariously on her desk – a testament to the ongoing class-action lawsuit against OmniCorp, a seemingly benevolent tech giant with a suspiciously dark underbelly.  The air hung heavy with the scent of stale coffee and impending deadlines, a familiar aroma in the life of a paralegal wrestling with a behemoth of a case.
