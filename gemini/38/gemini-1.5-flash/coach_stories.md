The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Amy” Hernandez’s hair across her face as she surveyed the skeletal remains of the Northwood High School football field.  Rows of empty bleachers, bleached bone-white against the bruised purple twilight, creaked mournfully in the gusts.  The scent of damp earth and decaying leaves hung heavy in the air, a stark contrast to the vibrant green of the manicured lawns surrounding the school.  Amy shivered, pulling her worn, navy-blue Northwood Wildcats jacket tighter around her.  It wasn't just the cold; a deeper chill, a familiar pre-season dread, settled in her gut. This year felt different.  Heavier.  The weight of expectation, and the ghost of last season's crushing defeat, pressed down on her like the relentless November wind.  The Wildcats needed a miracle, and Amy, their head coach, was running out of time.
