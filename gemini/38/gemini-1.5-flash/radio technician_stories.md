The static crackled, a relentless, whispering tide against the rhythmic thump of Amelia "Amy" Blackwood's soldering iron.  Rain lashed against the corrugated iron roof of the KGRA radio station, a relentless drumming that mirrored the frantic beat of her own heart.  The air hung thick with the smell of ozone and old coffee, a familiar aroma in the cramped, cluttered control room perched precariously on the edge of the desolate Mojave Desert. Dust motes, illuminated by the harsh glare of the single work lamp, danced in the swirling air currents escaping the faulty ventilation system. Outside, the wind howled a mournful ballad, threatening to rip the antenna clean from its moorings. Amy, her brow furrowed in concentration, tightened a nut on a frayed connection, her fingers nimble and sure despite the pressure mounting with every passing second. The emergency broadcast system was down, and the storm was only just beginning.
