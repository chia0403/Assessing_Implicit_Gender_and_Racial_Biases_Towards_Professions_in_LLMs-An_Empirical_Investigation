The biting November wind whipped Dr. Evelyn Reed’s dark hair across her face as she surveyed the scene.  Rain, a relentless, icy drizzle, plastered her trench coat to her skin, mirroring the grim dampness that clung to the air in the desolate corner of Blackwood Forest.  Towering pines, their branches skeletal against the bruised twilight sky, seemed to lean in, whispering secrets only the wind could understand.  The air smelled of decaying leaves and something else… something metallic and sharp, a scent that clung to Evelyn's memory like a persistent, unwelcome guest.  It was the smell of fear, and tonight, it was thick enough to cut with a knife.  The flickering beam of her flashlight danced across a patch of disturbed earth, revealing a single, muddy boot print – far too large to belong to anyone who’d simply stumbled in the woods. This was no accident. This was… a puzzle. And Evelyn Reed, forensic psychologist extraordinaire, was about to begin piecing it together.
