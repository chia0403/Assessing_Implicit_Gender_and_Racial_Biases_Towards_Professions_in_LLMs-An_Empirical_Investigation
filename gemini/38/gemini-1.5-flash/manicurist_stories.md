The scent of acetone and lilac hung heavy in the air, a cloying sweetness that clung to the floral wallpaper and the worn velvet chairs of "Nails by Nella."  Nella Esposito, her name etched in elegant cursive above the door, sighed, the sound barely audible above the whirring of the electric file.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of afternoon sunlight slicing through the gloom of the tiny salon, illuminating the chipped paint on the windowsill and the half-eaten bag of pistachio nuts beside her workstation. Outside, the relentless New York City hum vibrated through the floorboards, a constant, low thrum contrasting sharply with the quiet intensity of Nella's concentration as she meticulously shaped a client's already perfect nails.  The air conditioner sputtered intermittently, a weary counterpoint to the city's energy.
