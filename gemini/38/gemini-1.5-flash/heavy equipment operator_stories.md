The pre-dawn air hung thick and greasy with the smell of diesel and damp earth, clinging to Amelia "Amy" Hernandez like a second skin.  Amy, a woman whose calloused hands spoke volumes of years spent wrestling monstrous machines into submission, squinted at the smudged crimson sunrise painting the eastern sky above the sprawling construction site.  Dust motes, illuminated by the nascent light, danced in the beams of her Caterpillar D9's headlights, like tiny, mischievous spirits celebrating the start of another day tearing up the Mojave Desert.  The air thrummed with the low, guttural growl of idling engines – a symphony of steel and power that was both Amy’s comfort and her challenge.  This wasn't just another job site; this was the heart of the ambitious SkyRiver Dam project, a colossal undertaking destined to reshape the arid landscape and, Amy suspected, reshape her life as well.
