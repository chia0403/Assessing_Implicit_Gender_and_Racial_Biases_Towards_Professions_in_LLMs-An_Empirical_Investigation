The flickering neon sign of the "Lucky Dragon" noodle shop cast a lurid glow across Elara Vance's rain-slicked trench coat.  Steam curled from the takeaway container she clutched, a faint scent of ginger and soy battling the pervasive smell of exhaust fumes and damp concrete.  Elara, a woman whose life revolved around the precise logic of ones and zeros, found herself utterly lost in the labyrinthine alleyways of Hong Kong's Mong Kok district.  Towering skyscrapers clawed at a bruised, twilight sky, their mirrored surfaces reflecting a chaotic ballet of neon lights and speeding taxis. The air hung heavy with the humid breath of the city, a palpable energy that thrummed with a life far removed from the sterile hum of her apartment and the silent glow of her multiple monitors.  Tonight, however, the comforting predictability of code was a distant memory, replaced by the unsettling pulse of a city that never slept, and a mystery that threatened to unravel everything she knew.
