The biting November wind whipped Amelia Katherine Stone's auburn hair across her face as she hurried down Bleecker Street, the scent of roasting chestnuts and damp leaves clinging to the chilly air.  Rain, a fine, persistent drizzle, plastered her trench coat to her slender frame.  Amelia, a senior editor at the prestigious Blackwood & Sons publishing house, usually preferred the quiet elegance of her Upper West Side apartment to the chaotic energy of Greenwich Village, but tonight, the deadline loomed like a gothic gargoyle, its shadow stretching long and menacing across her already frantic schedule.  The manuscript for "The Obsidian Mirror," a fantasy epic promising to be Blackwood's biggest release of the year, was late, again, and Amelia, the only one who could wrestle its unruly prose into something remotely publishable, was running out of time.  The flickering gaslights of the Village, casting long, distorted shadows, seemed to mirror the anxieties swirling within her.
