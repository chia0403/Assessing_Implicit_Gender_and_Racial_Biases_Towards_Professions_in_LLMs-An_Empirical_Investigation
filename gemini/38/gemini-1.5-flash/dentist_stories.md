The biting November wind howled a mournful tune around the craggy peaks of the Scottish Highlands, rattling the rain-streaked windows of Dr. Alistair Finch’s dental surgery.  Inside, the air was a comforting contrast – warm, smelling faintly of antiseptic and cinnamon from the spiced tea stewing on the radiator.  The surgery itself was a curious blend of modern convenience and rustic charm.  Sleek, stainless steel equipment gleamed under the fluorescent lights, juxtaposed against the rough-hewn stone walls, their age evident in the deep fissures and the occasional patch where the plaster had crumbled away, revealing the ancient rock beneath.  Outside, the grey landscape was almost entirely swallowed by the encroaching dusk; within, Alistair Finch, a man whose life was as meticulously ordered as his dental instruments, prepared for his last patient of the day.
