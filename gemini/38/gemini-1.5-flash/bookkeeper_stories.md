The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Beatrice Finch's hands, the lukewarm tea doing little to soothe the icy dread creeping up her spine.  Rain lashed against the single, grimy window of her office, a relentless drumming that mirrored the frantic beat of her heart.  Amelia, a bookkeeper whose life was usually as orderly as the meticulously balanced ledgers on her desk, found herself staring at a set of figures that defied all logic, all reason, and possibly, all the laws of accounting.  The cramped, cluttered office, smelling faintly of damp paper and stale biscuits, felt suddenly oppressive, the walls closing in on her like the suffocating weight of the impossible sum before her.  Outside, the gas lamps of Grimsborough flickered in the downpour, casting long, distorted shadows that danced menacingly across the cobblestones.  It was a night for secrets, and Amelia Beatrice Finch was about to uncover one.
