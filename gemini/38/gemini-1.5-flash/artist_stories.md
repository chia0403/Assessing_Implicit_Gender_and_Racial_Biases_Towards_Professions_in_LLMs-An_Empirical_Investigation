The biting November wind whipped Amelia Katherine Blackwood’s auburn hair across her face as she leaned precariously over the crumbling stone wall, sketchbook clutched tight in gloved hands.  Below, the churning grey sea gnawed relentlessly at the cliffs of Cornwall, its spray painting ephemeral masterpieces on the jagged rocks.  The air smelled of brine and something earthy, a scent that clung to the ancient, windswept landscape like a second skin.  Amelia, despite the chill that seeped into her bones, felt a familiar thrill course through her.  This raw, untamed beauty, this relentless dance between land and sea – it was the very essence of her art.  The small fishing village of Portwenn huddled in the valley below, its lights twinkling like scattered diamonds against the encroaching darkness, offered a stark contrast to the untamed power of the ocean before her.  Tonight, however, Amelia wasn't interested in capturing the picturesque charm of the village; she was hunting for something far more elusive – the heart of the storm.
