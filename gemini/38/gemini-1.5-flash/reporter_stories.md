The salt spray stung Amelia Hernandez’s face, a familiar discomfort she barely registered anymore.  The relentless Pacific wind howled a mournful tune around the skeletal remains of the abandoned lighthouse, whipping her already wind-chapped hair into a frenzy. Below, the churning grey expanse of the ocean swallowed the setting sun, leaving behind a sky the colour of bruised plums.  Amelia, a seasoned reporter for the *San Francisco Chronicle*, perched precariously on a crumbling ledge, her notepad secured in her teeth as she wrestled with her battered camera.  The lighthouse, perched dramatically on the craggy cliffs of Point Reyes, was her current obsession, a silent witness to a mystery that had captivated – and frustrated – her for the past three months.  The disappearance of Elias Thorne, a renowned marine biologist, had left behind more questions than answers, and Amelia, driven by a hunch and a stubborn refusal to let go, was determined to find them.
