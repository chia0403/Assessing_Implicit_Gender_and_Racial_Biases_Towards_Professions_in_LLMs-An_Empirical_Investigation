The chipped paint on the windowsill, a faded robin's egg blue, perfectly encapsulated the melancholic spirit of the entire house.  Dust motes danced in the slivers of sunlight piercing the gloom, illuminating the thick layer of grime clinging to the ornate, yet utterly neglected, Victorian wallpaper.  Isabelle “Izzy” Moreau, renowned for her ability to coax beauty from even the most desolate spaces, sighed. This wasn't just a renovation; it was an exorcism.  The air hung heavy with the ghosts of past occupants, a palpable sense of sadness clinging to the faded grandeur of the Blackwood Manor, a sprawling monstrosity perched precariously on the cliff edge overlooking the churning grey expanse of the North Sea.  The salty wind howled a mournful tune outside, mirroring the turmoil Izzy felt churning within her own heart. This project, more than any other, felt… personal.
