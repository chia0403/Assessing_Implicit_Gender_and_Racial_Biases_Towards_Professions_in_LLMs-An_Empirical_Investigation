The chipped paint of Dr. Eleanor Vance’s office door seemed to mirror the cracks appearing in her carefully constructed composure.  Rain lashed against the tall, gothic windows of the Blackwood Clinic, each gust a mournful sigh echoing the turmoil brewing within.  The air hung thick with the scent of old paper and something faintly metallic – the lingering aroma, perhaps, of a particularly fraught therapy session.  Outside, the wind howled a lonely tune through the skeletal branches of the ancient oaks that guarded the clinic, their gnarled fingers clawing at the stormy sky.  Inside, Eleanor, a clinical psychologist with a reputation for her unwavering calm and sharp intellect, felt the tremors of a case that threatened to unravel her meticulously ordered world.
