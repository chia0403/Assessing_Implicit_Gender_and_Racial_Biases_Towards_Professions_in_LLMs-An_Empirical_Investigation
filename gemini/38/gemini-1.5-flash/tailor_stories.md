The scent of woodsmoke and damp wool hung heavy in the air, clinging to the cobblestones like a second skin.  Rain, a relentless drizzle that had begun at dawn, slicked the narrow alleyways of Oakhaven, turning the already uneven paving into a treacherous maze.  Gas lamps flickered weakly against the gloom, casting long, distorted shadows that danced with the swaying branches of the ancient oak tree that lent the town its name.  Inside his tiny shop, tucked away from the worst of the weather, Elias Thorne, tailor extraordinaire, meticulously stitched a button onto a richly embroidered waistcoat. The needle moved with a practiced grace, a silent counterpoint to the rhythmic drip, drip, drip of the rain against the windowpane.  Elias hummed a low, melancholic tune, his brow furrowed in concentration, his world reduced to the fine silk threads and the steady rhythm of his work.
