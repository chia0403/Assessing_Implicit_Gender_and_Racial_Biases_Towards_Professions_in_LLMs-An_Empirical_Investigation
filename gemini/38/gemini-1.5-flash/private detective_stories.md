The rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of Jack Stratton's office, a rhythm as relentless and unforgiving as the city itself.  He sat hunched over a chipped Formica desk, the weak light of a single bare bulb illuminating the swirling dust motes dancing in the air.  The scent of stale coffee and something vaguely resembling despair hung heavy, a familiar perfume in the cramped, cluttered space that served as both his headquarters and his sanctuary – if sanctuary could ever truly be found in a back alley off Bleecker Street in the less-than-glamorous heart of New York City.  Jack Stratton, Private Investigator, stared at the faded photograph on his desk – a woman with eyes that held a storm of their own, a storm he was about to wade into.
