The scent of lavender and aged linen hung heavy in the air, a comforting shroud against the relentless November drizzle lashing against the leaded windows of "Stitches & Dreams."  Inside, amidst bolts of silk the colour of a twilight sky and spools of thread shimmering like captured rainbows, sat Aurelia Genevieve Beaumont, her nimble fingers coaxing life into a cascade of ivory lace.  Rain hammered a frantic rhythm against the ancient stone of her workshop, a small, crooked building tucked away on a cobbled lane in the heart of the fictional, perpetually rain-swept town of Porthaven.  Aurelia, with her fiery red hair pulled back in a messy bun and a smudge of charcoal dust on her cheek, barely registered the storm. Her world was contained within the delicate fabric she manipulated, a world far removed from the gossip and intrigue simmering just beyond her rain-streaked windowpanes.
