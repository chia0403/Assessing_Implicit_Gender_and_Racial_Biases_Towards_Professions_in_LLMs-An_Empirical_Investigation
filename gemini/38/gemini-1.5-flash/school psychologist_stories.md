The chipped paint of the windowsill felt cold beneath Dr. Eleanor Vance's elbows as she leaned out, the late afternoon sun casting long shadows across the manicured lawns of Oakhaven High.  October's breath, crisp and carrying the scent of decaying leaves, ruffled her auburn hair.  Oakhaven, nestled in the heart of a deceptively idyllic New England town called Havenwood, was anything but idyllic behind its ivy-covered walls.  Eleanor, school psychologist for the past five years, had witnessed enough teenage angst, parental dysfunction, and simmering resentment to fill a dozen case files.  But even she was unprepared for the strange, unsettling occurrences that had begun to plague the school in the weeks leading up to Halloween.  It started subtly – whispers in the hallways, unsettling artwork appearing in the art room, a pervasive sense of unease that seemed to cling to the very air itself.  Tonight, however, felt different.  Tonight, the unease had solidified into something more tangible, something… malevolent.
