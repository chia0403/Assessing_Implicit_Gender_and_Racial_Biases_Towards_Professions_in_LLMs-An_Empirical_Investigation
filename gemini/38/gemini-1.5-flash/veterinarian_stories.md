The biting wind whipped Dr. Evelyn Reed’s auburn braid across her face as she wrestled the recalcitrant gate open.  Salt spray, thick as pea soup, stung her eyes, the taste of it acrid on her tongue.  Behind her, the battered Land Rover, affectionately nicknamed “Betsy,” groaned in protest, its engine sputtering a final, defiant cough before falling silent.  The setting sun bled across the churning grey sea, painting the sky in bruised purples and angry oranges, a dramatic backdrop to the desolate beauty of the Isle of Skye.  Evelyn shivered, pulling her thick wool coat tighter, the chill seeping deep into her bones despite the layers.  She was alone, miles from the nearest village, and the only thing between her and the raging Atlantic was a crumbling stone wall and the increasingly insistent bleating of a distressed sheep.  Tonight, even Betsy’s stubborn reliability had failed her, and Evelyn knew this wouldn’t be just another routine farm call.
