Rain lashed against the grimy windows of the converted warehouse, mimicking the storm brewing inside Amelia Grace Hawthorne.  The fluorescent lights of  Goldman & Levy, Attorneys at Law, buzzed overhead, a discordant soundtrack to the silent fury simmering in her.  It was 7:47 pm on a Friday, and Amelia, a paralegal with the firm for five years, was still chained to her desk, buried under a mountain of discovery documents for the Peterson vs. Oscorp case.  The air in the converted warehouse, once a bustling textile factory in the heart of Brooklyn’s DUMBO neighborhood, was thick with the smell of stale coffee and desperation. Brick walls, stripped bare and painted a sterile white, offered no comfort. Outside, the Manhattan skyline, usually a glittering spectacle, was swallowed by the oppressive grey. Amelia’s normally meticulous bun had begun to unravel, mirroring the fraying edges of her patience.
