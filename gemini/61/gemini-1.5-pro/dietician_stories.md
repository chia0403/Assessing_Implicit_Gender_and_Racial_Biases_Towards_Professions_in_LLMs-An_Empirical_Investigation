The relentless London drizzle hammered against the arched window of  "Nourish," Amelia Grace Holloway's tiny but fiercely independent dietetics practice. Rainwater cascaded down the glass, blurring the neon glow of the Tesco Express across the street in Notting Hill. Inside, the air hummed with the quiet gurgle of Amelia's vintage kettle, perched precariously atop a stack of cookbooks. The room, crammed with pamphlets on healthy eating and adorned with anatomical charts of the digestive system, felt both chaotic and comforting.  A half-eaten bowl of overnight oats sat forgotten on her desk, a testament to the frantic morning rush.  It was a Tuesday in late November, the sky the colour of dishwater, and Amelia, at the ripe old age of twenty-seven, felt older than the damp, crumbling Victorian building that housed her dreams. This morning, however, held a particular kind of heavy anticipation.  A new client, a notoriously difficult celebrity chef, was due any minute, and Amelia, armed with her nutritional knowledge and a healthy dose of apprehension, waited.
