The rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of The Rusty Nail, a rhythm Amelia “Amy” Hawthorne had come to find oddly comforting. Inside, the only sounds were the gentle clinking of ice against glass as she polished a tumbler, and the occasional sigh of the ancient refrigerator struggling to maintain a chilly interior against the oppressive Louisiana humidity. The bar, nestled between a dilapidated bait shop and a perpetually closed fortune teller's caravan on the outskirts of Thermidor Parish, wasn't much to look at.  Faded Mardi Gras beads draped limply over dusty liquor bottles, and the once-vibrant murals depicting swamp scenes were now peeling and obscured by years of cigarette smoke. But for Amy, it was sanctuary.  And tonight, with the storm raging outside and only one other patron nursing a lonely beer in the corner booth, it felt more like a refuge than ever.
