Rain lashed against the windows of the "Golden Kettle" diner, blurring the neon sign's cheery glow into a hazy smear of orange. Inside, the air hung thick with the smell of stale coffee and frying bacon, a scent Amelia Hayes usually found comforting.  Not today. Today, the greasy air felt heavy, clinging to her like the anxieties churning in her stomach.  Amelia, her full name Amelia Rose Hayes, tapped a manicured nail against the worn Formica tabletop, her gaze fixed on the swirling grey skies outside.  This small town of Harmony Creek, nestled deep in the Appalachian foothills of West Virginia, was the last stop on her sales route.  The last stop and, if she didn't close this deal, possibly the last stop of her career.  She clutched the handle of her briefcase, the smooth leather offering little comfort.  The Killian family owned the largest orchard in the county, and their order of industrial-grade apple peelers could save her quota, her job, maybe even her sanity.  But old man Killian was notoriously tight-fisted, and Amelia hadn't even managed to get past his secretary yet.
