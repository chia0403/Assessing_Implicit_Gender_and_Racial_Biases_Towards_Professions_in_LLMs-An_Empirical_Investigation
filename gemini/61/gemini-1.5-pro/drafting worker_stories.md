The flickering fluorescent lights of the D&M Architectural firm buzzed in counterpoint to the rhythmic *scratch-scratch* of Eliza Jane Holloway's pencil as it danced across the vellum.  Outside, the Detroit skyline, a jagged silhouette against the bruised purple of a pre-dawn sky, offered a stark contrast to the meticulously rendered lines taking shape beneath her hand.  It was 5:30 AM, and Eliza was already three hours into her workday, the only occupant of the cavernous drafting room, save for the rustling blueprints and the occasional sigh of the ancient radiator in the corner.  The air hung thick with the scent of dust and stale coffee, a familiar aroma that clung to the very fabric of the building, a testament to generations of draftsmen who had toiled within its walls.  Eliza, however, barely noticed. Lost in a world of precise measurements and meticulous detailing, she was focused on perfecting the curve of a grand staircase for a new civic center, a project that held the promise of revitalizing a neglected corner of the city she loved.
