The biting November wind whipped around Dr. Elias Thorne, stinging his eyes as he hurried across the cobbled courtyard of St. Jude's Hospital.  Gargoyles perched high on the gothic facade leered down at him, their stone faces slick with a mixture of rain and soot. The hospital, a hulking edifice of Victorian brick and shadowed archways, clung to the edge of the bleak Yorkshire moors like a grim fortress.  Inside, warmth and the sterile scent of antiseptic battled the lingering damp. Elias, his breath fogging in the chilled air, pushed through the heavy oak doors, the weight of the coming night – and the surgery that awaited him – settling heavily on his shoulders.
