The relentless Nevada sun beat down on the dusty asphalt of Harmony Creek, baking the air to a shimmering haze.  Inside Harmony Creek Pharmacy, the air conditioning hummed a steady, reassuring tune, a stark contrast to the scorching world outside.  Eleanor Vance, PharmD, adjusted her crisp white coat, the name tag pinned neatly above her left pocket, and surveyed the quiet interior.  Prescription bottles lined the shelves like multicoloured soldiers, each containing a promise of relief or a maintenance of fragile normalcy.  The scent of antiseptic mingled with the faint sweetness of the sugar-free cough drops displayed near the register. It was a Tuesday afternoon, typically slow, but Eleanor sensed a peculiar stillness in the air, a precursor to something about to disrupt the sleepy rhythm of this desert town.
