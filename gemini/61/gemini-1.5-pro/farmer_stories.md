The biting wind whipped across the frost-rimed fields of Athelney Farm, stinging Elias Thorne's cheeks and rattling the brittle stalks of last season's corn.  A smear of grey light clung stubbornly to the eastern horizon, promising a sunrise that felt unlikely to deliver much warmth.  November in the Somerset Levels was a cruel mistress, beautiful in her starkness, but unforgiving to those who toiled under her gaze. Elias, his breath puffing out in white clouds, surveyed his land, a patchwork of brown and ochre stretching as far as the eye could see, hemmed in by the distant, mist-shrouded Quantock Hills.  This land, passed down through generations of Thornes, was both his legacy and his burden. This year, the burden felt heavier than ever.
