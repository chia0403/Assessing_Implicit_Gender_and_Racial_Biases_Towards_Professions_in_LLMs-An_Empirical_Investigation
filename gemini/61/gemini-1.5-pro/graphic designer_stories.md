Rain lashed against the oversized windows of Eleanor Vance's studio apartment, blurring the already fading neon glow of the Hong Kong skyline.  The rhythmic tapping, usually a soothing white noise for her creative process, tonight felt like an insistent, accusatory drumbeat.  Scattered across her worn drafting table, amidst a kaleidoscope of Pantone swatches and half-finished logo sketches, lay the crumpled rejection letter from Golden Gate Graphics.  Eleanor, her dark hair pulled back in a messy bun, stared at it with the weary resignation of a seasoned marathon runner facing yet another uphill climb. This tiny, cluttered space in the heart of Kowloon, usually buzzing with the energy of her latest project, felt suffocating.  The air hung heavy with the scent of instant ramen and dashed hopes.
