The rusty wrench slipped in Elias Thorne's grease-stained hand, the clang echoing in the cavernous, damp basement of the Grand Majestic Hotel.  Dust motes, disturbed by the sudden noise, danced in the single, bare bulb hanging precariously from a cracked rafter.  Elias, his brow furrowed in concentration, muttered a curse under his breath.  Built in the opulent roaring twenties, the Grand Majestic was a crumbling monument to faded glory, its once-gleaming brass fixtures now tarnished, its marble floors cracked and chipped.  Tonight, it was Elias's job to wrestle with the ancient plumbing system that lay snarled beneath the hotel like a metal serpent, the source of the persistent dripping that plagued the honeymoon suite on the fifth floor. The air hung thick with the smell of mildew and something else, something faintly metallic and unsettling, that Elias couldn't quite place.
