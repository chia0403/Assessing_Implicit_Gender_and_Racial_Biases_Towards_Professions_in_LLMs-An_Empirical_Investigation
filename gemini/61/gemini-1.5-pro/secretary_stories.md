Rain lashed against the Art Deco windows of the Grimshaw Building, each drop a frantic drumbeat against the glass. Inside, the air hung thick with the scent of stale coffee and impending deadlines.  Eleanor Vance, fingers hovering over the keys of her vintage Underwood typewriter, shivered despite the warmth radiating from the ancient radiator hissing in the corner. Her office, tucked away on the twenty-seventh floor, usually offered a breathtaking view of the Manhattan skyline. Today, however, it was shrouded in a swirling grey mist, mirroring the anxiety churning in Eleanor's stomach.  Mr. Grimshaw, the notoriously demanding owner of Grimshaw and Sons Importers, was due back from his trip to Brazil any minute, and his desk, usually a model of imposing order, currently resembled a paper-strewn battlefield.  A half-eaten sandwich languished on a chipped china plate, and a single, crimson orchid, a gift from a hopeful client, drooped dramatically from its crystal vase. Eleanor knew that the state of his office was a direct reflection of her perceived competence, and today, she felt anything but competent.
