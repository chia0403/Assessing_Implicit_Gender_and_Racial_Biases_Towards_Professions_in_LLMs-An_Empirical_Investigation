Rain lashed against the arched window of Amelia Hawthorne's attic office, mimicking the relentless drumming of a deadline in her head.  Loose papers, covered in Amelia's spidery scrawl of edits, fluttered precariously on the wide, oak desk.  Outside, the slate-grey sky hung low over the crooked rooftops of Prague, the ancient city a maze of cobbled streets and shadowed alleyways.  Inside, the only light came from a flickering gas lamp that cast long, dancing shadows across the cramped space, illuminating dusty stacks of manuscripts and half-empty teacups.  The air was thick with the scent of old paper, printer's ink, and the faintest hint of woodsmoke drifting in from the chimney.  Amelia, her dark hair pulled back in a messy bun, chewed on the end of a quill, her brow furrowed in concentration as she wrestled with the particularly purple prose of a fledgling romantic novelist.
