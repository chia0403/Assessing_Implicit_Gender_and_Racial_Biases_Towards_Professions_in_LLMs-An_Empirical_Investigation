The flickering fluorescent lights of Dr. Alistair Finch's dental practice buzzed like trapped insects, a sound Amelia Hayes usually tuned out.  This Tuesday, however, the buzzing seemed to amplify the thrumming anxiety in her chest. Outside, the November rain lashed against the large bay window, blurring the view of the usually bustling Market Street in Ashworth, a small coastal town in Oregon.  The waiting room, typically filled with nervous patients leafing through outdated magazines, was eerily empty.  Amelia, perched behind her polished oak reception desk, tapped a crimson-painted nail against the worn copy of "Good Housekeeping" she'd read cover to cover at least twice that day.  The clock on the wall ticked with exaggerated slowness, each second a hammer blow against the fragile calm she was trying to maintain.  Something felt off, deeply off, and it wasn't just the quiet.
