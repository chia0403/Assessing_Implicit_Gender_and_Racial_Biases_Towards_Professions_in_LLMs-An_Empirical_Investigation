The aroma of burnt sugar hung heavy in the air, a bittersweet perfume that clung to the brick walls of Rosalie "Rosie" Cartwright's bakery.  The Buttercup Bake Shop, nestled between a dusty antique store and a perpetually bustling laundromat on Sycamore Street, usually smelled of warm yeast and vanilla.  This morning, however, disaster had struck in the form of a caramel catastrophe.  Outside, the pre-dawn sky above the small Oregon town of Havenwood was just beginning to blush with the promise of sunrise, painting the rain-slicked streets in hues of lavender and rose. Inside, Rosie, her apron dusted with flour and her fiery red hair escaping its messy bun, frantically scraped a blackened pan, her brow furrowed in concentration.
