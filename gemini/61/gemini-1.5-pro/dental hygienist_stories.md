The insistent Vermont wind rattled the leaded panes of Dr. Albright’s Family Dentistry, a sound Amelia Grace Hawthorne had come to associate with the smell of antiseptic and the whine of high-speed drills.  Snow, fine and relentless, obscured the view across Lake Champlain, usually a breathtaking vista from her operatory window.  Today, however, Amelia saw only swirling white, a fitting reflection of the swirling anxiety in her stomach.  It wasn't the blizzard; she was used to those.  It was the small, antique wooden box tucked away in her locker, a box she’d promised herself she wouldn't open until after her last patient. A box that contained the key, quite literally, to her past.  And possibly, her future.
