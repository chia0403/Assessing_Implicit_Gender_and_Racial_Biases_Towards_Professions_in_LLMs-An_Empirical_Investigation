The flickering fluorescent lights of the server room buzzed in counterpoint to the rhythmic hum of the cooling fans.  Cables, thick as pythons, snaked across the floor, disappearing into the blinking, metallic behemoths that held the digital lifeblood of Vanderlyn Industries.  Dust motes danced in the pale light, illuminated like tiny galaxies.  Eleanor Vance, a systems analyst with a perpetually furrowed brow and a penchant for oversized cardigans, stood amidst the controlled chaos, her breath fogging slightly in the chilled air.  It was 3:17 am on a Tuesday, and Eleanor, normally tucked away in her ergonomically optimized cubicle, was hunting a ghost in the machine.  A ghost that was slowly, systematically, devouring Vanderlyn's data.
