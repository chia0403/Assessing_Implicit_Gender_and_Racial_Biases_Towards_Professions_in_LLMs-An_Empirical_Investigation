The biting wind whipped across the desolate expanse of the Colorado Plateau, stinging Amelia "Ames" Stone's cheeks.  Dust devils, miniature tornadoes of ochre and rust, danced across the cracked earth, swirling around the massive Caterpillar D9R, its hydraulics humming a low, rhythmic song of power.  Ames, her face smeared with grime, wrestled the joystick, her knuckles white against the worn leather.  The sun, a molten disc sinking towards the jagged peaks of the San Juan Mountains, cast long, skeletal shadows that stretched and writhed like tormented spirits.  This wasn't the picturesque landscape postcard-producers painted; this was the brutal, unforgiving heartland of the Grand Canyon's eastern edge, where silence was as deafening as the roar of the desert wind.  This was her domain, the place where she, a heavy equipment operator for the  Canyon View Reclamation Project, was shaping the very earth itself.
