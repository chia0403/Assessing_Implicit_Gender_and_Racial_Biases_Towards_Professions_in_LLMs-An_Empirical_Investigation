The flickering neon sign of the "Rusty Mug" cast a sickly yellow glow across the rain-slicked streets of New Prague, a city clinging to the fading grandeur of its industrial past.  Rain hammered against the corrugated iron awnings, mirroring the relentless rhythm of Amelia "Amy" Stone's thoughts.  Amy Stone, a film director known more for her audacious concepts than her box office successes, hunched over a steaming mug of lukewarm coffee in the dimly lit diner.  Outside, the city sighed and groaned with the relentless downpour.  Inside, Amy's mind, a volatile mix of adrenaline and anxiety, churned over the next shot of her ambitious, and potentially disastrous, new film.  "Echoes of the Ironclad," a historical epic about a forgotten rebellion in the late 19th century, was already a month behind schedule and over budget.
