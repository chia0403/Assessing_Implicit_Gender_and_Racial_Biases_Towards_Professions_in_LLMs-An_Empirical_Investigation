The biting wind whipped Amelia Hayes's reporter's notebook into a frenzy, scattering half-formed notes across the cobblestones of the ancient marketplace.  Dust motes danced in the pale afternoon sun filtering through the skeletal remains of a once-grand archway.  Amelia, a seasoned investigative reporter for the "Everglen Gazette," hunched deeper into her worn leather coat, the scent of roasted chestnuts and acrid smoke swirling around her.  The air, thick with the unspoken anxieties of the city, clung to her like a shroud.  Everglen, nestled between the towering Serpent's Spine mountains and the whispering Whispering Woods, was a city steeped in history, yet shrouded in a recent, unsettling silence.

**Setting:** Everglen, a sprawling, historically significant city nestled between the towering Serpent's Spine mountains and the whispering Whispering Woods.  The marketplace, a hub of activity in normal times, now felt desolate and eerie.  The air hung heavy with a palpable sense of unease, a contrast to the usually vibrant aroma of the city's bustling food stalls.
