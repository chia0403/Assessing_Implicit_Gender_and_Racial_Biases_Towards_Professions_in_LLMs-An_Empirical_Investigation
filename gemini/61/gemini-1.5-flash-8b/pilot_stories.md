The biting wind whipped Amelia "Storm" Blackwood's crimson scarf around her face, stinging her cheeks like a thousand tiny needles.  Below, the jagged peaks of the Himalayas clawed at the bruised twilight sky.  Dust devils, born from the relentless sun's kiss on the parched earth, danced across the barren plateau.  Her cockpit, a claustrophobic steel cocoon, offered little respite from the unforgiving elements.  Amelia, pilot of the Zephyr 7, had chosen this desolate, high-altitude training run not for the scenery, but for the raw, unforgiving challenge it presented.  She needed this.  Needed the bone-jarring turbulence, the pressure on her muscles, the almost palpable threat of the unforgiving terrain that surrounded her.  Her mission: to push the limits of both herself and the sleek, powerful aircraft that had become an extension of her very being.
