The biting November wind whipped across the skeletal frame of the unfinished skyscraper, rattling the scaffolding like a cage of agitated birds.  Rain, the colour of pewter, hammered against the corrugated metal sheeting, blurring the already hazy cityscape of grey concrete and steel.  This was the domain of Silas Blackwood, a construction worker with calloused hands and eyes that held the unwavering gaze of a seasoned hawk.  Silas, a man built like a sturdy oak, moved with practiced grace amidst the chaos, his movements rhythmic and precise.  He was at the summit of the building, several floors above any safety net or possible rescue, a testament to the impossible heights humanity could reach.  He was fixing a loose bolt holding a critical structural beam.  His breath fogged in the icy air.
