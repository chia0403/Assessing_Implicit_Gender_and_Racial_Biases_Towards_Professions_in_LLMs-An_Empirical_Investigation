The flickering neon sign of "Sparky's Electrical Repairs" cast a sickly yellow glow across the rain-slicked alley.  Water pooled in the uneven cobblestones, reflecting the greasy grime clinging to the brick walls.  Elias Thorne, a seasoned electrician with calloused hands and eyes that saw more than just wires, crouched beneath a leaking fire escape.  The rhythmic drip, drip, drip was a familiar soundtrack to his life.  Tonight, however, the sound was punctuated by a frantic, high-pitched whine, coming from the shadowed depths of the building above. It wasn't the usual hum of faulty wiring; this was... different.
