The biting wind, a relentless clawing beast, whipped at Elias Thorne's worn leather jacket, stinging his cheeks and whistling through the gaps in the dilapidated scaffolding.  Below, the sprawling city of Veridia, a tapestry woven with gleaming skyscrapers and shadowed alleyways, stretched out like a monstrous, intricate beast, indifferent to the struggles of the men clinging to its skeletal frame.  Elias, a roofer for the last fifteen years, felt a familiar knot tighten in his stomach.  The precarious wooden planks groaned beneath his weight, a symphony of impending disaster.  Rain, a relentless curtain, hammered against the corrugated iron roof, blurring the already hazy cityscape into an almost abstract masterpiece of grey and gloom.  He was working on the spire of the recently-renovated "Skyreach" building, a testament to the city's relentless upward climb, a climb that often left those on the ground feeling hopelessly dwarfed.  Today, however, Elias felt more than just dwarfed; he felt forgotten.
