The flickering fluorescent lights of the "Tally Ho" accounting office hummed a monotonous tune, a soundtrack to Amelia Finch's meticulously precise life.  Rows of filing cabinets, each labelled with painstakingly neat, handwritten index cards, stretched into the gloom.  Dust motes danced in the lone beam of sunlight slicing through a grimy window overlooking a bustling city street, a stark contrast to the quiet, almost oppressive, atmosphere within. Amelia, a bookkeeper of twenty-seven years standing, hunched over her desk, a stack of invoices and ledgers towering before her.  The air, thick with the scent of old paper and stale coffee, clung to her, a constant reminder of the relentless rhythm of numbers and deadlines that governed her existence.  Her neatly-organized workspace, a testament to her methodical nature, was the only constant in a world that seemed increasingly chaotic.
