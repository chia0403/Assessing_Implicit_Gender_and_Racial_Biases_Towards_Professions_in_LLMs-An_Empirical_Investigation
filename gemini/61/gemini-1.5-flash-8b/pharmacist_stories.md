The chipped porcelain sink, stained a dull, perpetual grey, reflected the flickering gaslight above Amelia Blackwood's head.  Dust motes danced in the single beam of light, illuminating the intricate swirls of dried herbs and spices lining the shelves of her apothecary, "The Gentle Remedy."  A persistent, almost mournful hum emanated from the ancient copper still in the corner, a sound Amelia, despite her twelve years tending it, could no longer ignore.  Rain hammered against the single, leaded window pane, mimicking the frantic rhythm of her own heartbeat.

The year was 1888.  Fog, thick and cloying, clung to the cobbled streets of Grimsby, a port city choked by the stench of coal smoke and the relentless ebb and flow of the North Sea.  The houses, huddled together like shivering sheep, seemed to lean into the perpetual gloom, their windows like vacant eyes staring out at the oppressive world.  Grimsby was a place where shadows held dominion, and whispered secrets clung to the damp brickwork.  Amelia, with her spectacles perched precariously on her nose, felt the weight of that unspoken history pressing down on her.  Not just the history of the city, but her own.  Tonight, it felt like a particularly suffocating shroud.
