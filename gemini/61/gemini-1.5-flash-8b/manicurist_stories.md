The scent of lilies and antiseptic hung heavy in the air, a familiar perfume to Amelia Nightingale.  Her tiny, manicured hands moved with practiced grace across a client’s perfectly sculpted nails, each swipe a silent ballet of precision.  Sunlight, filtered through the dusty rose-colored drapes of "The Polished Petal," cast long shadows across the small salon, highlighting the chipped porcelain doll figurines perched on every available surface.  The soft, repetitive hum of a vintage fan was the only other sound, a constant companion to the rhythmic click-clack of Amelia’s tools.  Today, however, the quiet was punctuated by the insistent, almost frantic tapping of a pen against a notepad.  Across from her, perched on a plush velvet armchair, sat Mrs. Eleanor Vance, her face etched with a thousand unspoken stories, her gaze fixated on the swirling patterns of crimson paint Amelia was applying to her pinky nail.
