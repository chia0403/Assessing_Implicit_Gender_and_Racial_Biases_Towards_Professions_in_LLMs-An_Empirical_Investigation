The fluorescent lights of the Zenith Insurance office hummed, a monotonous soundtrack to Elara Nightingale's day.  Dust motes danced in the meager afternoon sunlight filtering through the grimy windowpanes, illuminating the swirling patterns of chipped varnish on the reception desk.  A faint aroma of stale coffee and desperation clung to the air, a scent Elara had grown intimately familiar with over her three years at Zenith.  Paperclips jingled, a tiny metallic percussion punctuating the silence between the occasional click of a keyboard and the rhythmic thump of a weary foot on the worn linoleum floor.  Elara, a woman of thirty-two with eyes that held the quiet weariness of a seasoned librarian, meticulously alphabetized the overflowing stack of claim forms, the faint click of the stapler a small comfort in the otherwise relentless tedium.  Today, however, a new, unsettling element had been introduced.
