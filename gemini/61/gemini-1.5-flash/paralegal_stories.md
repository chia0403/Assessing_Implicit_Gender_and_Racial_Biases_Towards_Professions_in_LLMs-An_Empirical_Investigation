The fluorescent lights of Miller & Zois hummed a monotonous tune, a soundtrack to Amelia “Mia” Hernandez’s increasingly frayed nerves.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of their twenty-second-floor office, blurring the already hazy cityscape of Chicago into a watercolor of grey and black.  Inside, the air hung thick with the scent of stale coffee and simmering tension. Mia, a paralegal with Miller & Zois for the past five years, stared at the mountain of paperwork on her desk – a chaotic landscape of deposition transcripts, legal briefs, and the ever-present threat of yet another last-minute request from Mr. Zois himself, a man whose temper could curdle milk at fifty paces.  Tonight, however, it wasn't the usual workload that gnawed at her; it was the unsettling feeling that something was profoundly, irrevocably wrong.
