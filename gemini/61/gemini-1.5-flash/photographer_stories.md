The biting November wind whipped Amelia Katherine Hayes's hair across her face, stinging her cheeks a vibrant crimson that clashed wildly with the stark, monochrome landscape before her.  Iceland.  Specifically, the desolate black sand beach of Reynisfjara, where the basalt columns marched rigidly towards a churning, grey ocean, each wave a hungry beast threatening to swallow the land whole.  Amelia, bundled in layers of wool and waterproofs, fought against the gusts, her breath misting in the frigid air.  Her tripod, a sturdy, loyal companion, trembled slightly under the assault, but she held firm, her gloved fingers adjusting the focus of her Hasselblad 500C/M, the familiar weight grounding her amidst the elemental chaos.  The light, however, was breathtaking – a stark, almost ethereal quality that transformed the familiar harshness of the landscape into something both beautiful and terrifying. This was exactly what she’d been searching for.  This was the perfect storm.
