The scent of lavender and sandalwood hung heavy in the air, a comforting balm against the relentless November chill that clawed at the windows of Serenity Now Massage Therapy.  Rain lashed against the panes, mirroring the tempest brewing inside Elara Vance.  Elara, a woman whose hands possessed the magic to melt away tension with the deftest of strokes, found herself utterly knotted with a problem that no amount of kneading could unravel.  Her small, cozy studio, nestled in the heart of a quiet, cobbled street in the historic district of Charleston, South Carolina, usually offered a sanctuary of peace. Tonight, however, it felt like a cage. The flickering gas lamp outside cast long, dancing shadows across the plush massage table, highlighting the unsettling emptiness where her next appointment should have been.
