The fluorescent lights of Northwood High hummed a monotonous tune, a counterpoint to the frantic tapping of Dr. Eleanor Vance’s pen against her notepad.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of her office, blurring the already indistinct outline of the football field below.  Inside, the air hung thick with the scent of stale coffee and adolescent angst – a familiar perfume to Eleanor, who had inhaled it almost daily for the past fifteen years.  Today, however, the usual anxieties felt amplified, the whispers from the hallway echoing louder, more insistent, like the relentless drumming of the storm outside.  A chipped mug, lukewarm and forgotten, sat on her desk, a testament to the interrupted flow of her morning. The case file before her, labelled simply “Ethan Blackwood,” promised another tangled knot to unravel in the already complex tapestry of teenage turmoil at Northwood High.
