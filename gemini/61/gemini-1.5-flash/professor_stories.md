Professor Augustine Blackwood adjusted his tweed jacket, the worn fabric whispering against the crisp October air as he ascended the granite steps of Blackwood Manor.  The wind, a mischievous sprite, whipped strands of his silver hair across his face, carrying with it the scent of damp earth and decaying leaves.  Blackwood Manor, perched precariously on a windswept cliff overlooking the churning grey expanse of the North Sea, was as imposing and unpredictable as the elements themselves.  Its gothic architecture, a testament to generations of Blackwood ancestors, seemed to groan under the weight of centuries, its shadowy gargoyles leering down at the churning waters below.  Inside, however, a fire crackled merrily in the vast hearth of the library, a stark contrast to the tempest raging outside, promising a night of both warmth and unsettling secrets.
