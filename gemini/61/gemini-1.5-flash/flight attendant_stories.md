The recycled air hung thick and faintly floral in the cabin of Flight BA249, a scent that, to Amelia Katherine Davies, smelled suspiciously like desperation and cheap perfume.  Amelia, perched precariously on the fold-down jump seat, nursed a lukewarm cup of instant coffee that tasted alarmingly like sadness. Outside, the London skyline bled into a bruised purple twilight, the city lights twinkling like spilled diamonds on a velvet cloth.  Rain lashed against the fuselage, a relentless drumming that mirrored the chaotic thump-thump-thumping of her own heart.  Tonight wasn't just another red-eye to Hong Kong; tonight felt like a flight to the edge of the world, and Amelia, with her perfectly coiffed bun threatening to unravel and her smile firmly plastered in place, was its increasingly anxious navigator.
