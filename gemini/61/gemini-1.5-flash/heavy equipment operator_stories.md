The diesel engine coughed, a throaty rumble that vibrated through Amelia "Amy" Blackwood's worn work boots and up into her chest.  Dawn painted the eastern sky in bruised purples and angry oranges, reflecting off the polished steel of the massive Caterpillar D11T she commanded.  Amy, her face smudged with grease and her auburn braid escaping its haphazard bun, gripped the joystick with calloused hands.  Around her, the vast expanse of the Nevada desert stretched, a seemingly endless tapestry of sun-baked earth, scrub brush, and the skeletal remains of long-dead mines.  The air hung thick and heavy with the scent of dust and diesel, a perfume Amy had come to know intimately over the fifteen years she’d spent carving a living from this unforgiving landscape.  Today’s task: clearing a path for the new solar farm, a project both monumental in scale and utterly insignificant compared to the ancient geological forces that had shaped the land around her.
