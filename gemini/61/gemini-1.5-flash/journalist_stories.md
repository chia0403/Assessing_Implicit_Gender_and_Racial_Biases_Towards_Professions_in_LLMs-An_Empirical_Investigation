The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Mia” Holloway’s auburn hair across her face as she hunched deeper into her worn trench coat.  Rain, a relentless drizzle that seemed to seep into the very marrow of your bones, plastered the cobblestones of Prague’s Old Town Square.  Gas lamps, struggling against the gloom, cast an eerie yellow glow on the gothic spires of Týn Church, their pointed fingers clawing at the bruised sky.  The air, thick with the scent of woodsmoke and damp earth, held a palpable tension, a silent hum that vibrated beneath the surface of the bustling city.  Mia, a journalist for the Prague Post, pulled her scarf tighter, the damp wool clinging uncomfortably to her skin.  Tonight, however, the chill wasn’t just meteorological; a far colder dread settled in her stomach.  Her latest investigation, a story that threatened to unravel a web of secrets reaching the highest echelons of power, had just taken a dangerous turn.
