The stale scent of sweat and cheap disinfectant hung heavy in the air, a familiar perfume to Amelia "Amy" Hernandez.  Sweat beaded on her forehead, not from exertion – she'd finished her 6 a.m. spin class an hour ago – but from the nervous energy that pulsed through her like a second heartbeat.  The fluorescent lights of "BodyBoost Fitness," a cramped but surprisingly popular gym nestled in the shadow of the Brooklyn Bridge, hummed a monotonous tune.  Outside, the pre-dawn sky was a bruised purple, hinting at the day’s promise of relentless heat.  Amy, however, wasn't focused on the rising sun.  Her gaze was fixed on the flickering neon sign outside, its promise of "BodyBoost" feeling cruelly ironic right now.  For the first time in her five years leading classes at BodyBoost, Amy was facing a challenge that threatened not just her reputation, but the very survival of her beloved, albeit slightly dilapidated, fitness haven.
