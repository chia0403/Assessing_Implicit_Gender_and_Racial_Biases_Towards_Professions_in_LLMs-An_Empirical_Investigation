The acrid tang of ozone hung heavy in the air, a metallic counterpoint to the cloying sweetness of lilies Dr. Eleanor Vance hated.  Eleanor, a woman whose precision extended from the meticulous placement of a dental dam to the perfectly aligned buttons on her crisp white coat, stared out the panoramic window of her practice, "Vance & Vance Dental," overlooking the perpetually overcast skies of Seattle. Rain, a relentless, grey curtain, shrouded the city below, mirroring the grim expression etched onto her face.  The sleek, modern office, designed with calming blues and strategically placed succulents, felt colder than usual this morning, a chill that had nothing to do with the November damp seeping through the double-paned glass.  It was a chill that emanated from the ominous manila envelope sitting unopened on her pristine, mahogany desk.
