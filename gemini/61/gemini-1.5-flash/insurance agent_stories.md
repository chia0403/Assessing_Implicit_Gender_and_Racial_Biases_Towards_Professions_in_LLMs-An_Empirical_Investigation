The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Hernandez's hands, a stark contrast to the November chill biting at the exposed skin of her wrists.  Rain lashed against the single-pane windows of her small, cluttered office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her.  The scent of stale coffee and damp wool hung heavy in the air, a fitting aroma for the day.  Amelia, a licensed insurance agent with Hernandez & Associates – a "firm" consisting solely of herself and a perpetually malfunctioning fax machine – stared out at the grey expanse of the Manchester waterfront. The skeletal remains of a derelict fishing trawler, half-submerged and listing precariously, seemed to mock her own precarious financial situation.  Today's appointment, a notoriously difficult client known only as "Mr. Silas," held the promise of a desperately needed commission, or the very real possibility of yet another rejected claim form adding to the growing pile on her desk.
