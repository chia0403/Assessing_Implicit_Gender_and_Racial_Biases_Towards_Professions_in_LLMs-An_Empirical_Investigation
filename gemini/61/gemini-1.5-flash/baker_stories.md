The pre-dawn chill clung to Amelia Hawthorne like a damp linen shroud as she unlocked the bakery doors.  Dust motes, disturbed by the faintest crackle of the newly-lit gas lamps, danced in the pale, lemon-hued light spilling through the grimy windowpanes.  The air, thick with the scent of yeasty warmth and faintly acrid flour, was a familiar comfort, a silent promise of the day's chaotic symphony of kneading dough, the hiss of steam, and the satisfying crackle of crusts browning in the oven.  Amelia's bakery, "The Golden Crust," nestled on the cobbled lane of Whisperwind Alley, was a haven of flour-dusted calm in the bustling heart of the ever-restless city of Aethelburg.  Outside, the first tentative rays of sun were painting the sky a bruised purple, hinting at the day's relentless energy about to spill into the alley's narrow confines.
