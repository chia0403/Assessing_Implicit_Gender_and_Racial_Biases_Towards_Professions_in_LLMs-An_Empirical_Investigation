The scent of aged paper and leather hung heavy in the air, a comforting blanket against the relentless November chill that clawed at the stained-glass windows of the Blackwood Public Library.  Rain lashed against the panes, a steady rhythm accompanying the quiet shushing of Amelia Beatrice Hawthorne’s fingertips as she meticulously catalogued a first edition of *Wuthering Heights*.  Amelia, or Amy as she preferred, wasn’t just a librarian; she was the custodian of Blackwood’s collective memory, a silent observer of generations who passed through those hallowed halls, their lives whispered between the pages of countless borrowed books.  Tonight, however, the usual peaceful solitude was fractured by an unnerving tremor that ran through the ancient oak floorboards, a vibration that felt less like a gust of wind and more like…a heartbeat.
