The chipped paint of the school hallway seemed to mirror the cracks in Eleanor Vance's composure.  Rain lashed against the tall, gothic windows of Northwood High, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her.  It was a Tuesday, the kind of Tuesday that smelled faintly of stale coffee and teenage angst, a scent Eleanor Vance, Northwood's resident guidance counselor, had become intimately familiar with over her fifteen years at the school. The fluorescent lights hummed a monotonous tune above her as she navigated the cluttered labyrinth of lockers, the metal echoing the relentless drumming of the rain.  Each slam of a locker door felt like another tiny hammer blow against her already frayed nerves.  Today, however, was different.  Today, the storm wasn't just outside; it was threatening to engulf her completely.
