The chipped porcelain teacup trembled in Dr. Evelyn Reed’s hand, the floral pattern a stark contrast to the grim tableau unfolding before her. Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of her office, blurring the already indistinct cityscape of Manchester, a city currently holding its breath, waiting for answers only she could possibly provide.  Evelyn, her dark hair pulled back in a severe bun, stared at the grainy photograph spread across her desk – the close-up of a hand, delicate and manicured, clutching a single, blood-soaked playing card: the Queen of Spades.  The card, grotesquely beautiful, was the only clue left behind in the meticulously staged murder of Lord Ashworth, a reclusive aristocrat whose sprawling estate, Blackwood Manor, stood shrouded in mist and steeped in a history far darker than the already grim circumstances of his death.
