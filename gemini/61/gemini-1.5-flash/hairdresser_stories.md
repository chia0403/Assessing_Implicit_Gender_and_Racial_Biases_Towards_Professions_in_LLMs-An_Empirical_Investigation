The scent of burnt sugar and rosemary hung heavy in the air, a bizarre perfume clinging to the damp chill of a November evening in the seaside town of Port Blossom.  Rain lashed against the multi-paned windows of “Shear Indulgence,” blurring the already indistinct gaslight glow of the cobbled street outside. Inside, amongst the comforting chaos of hair dryers humming a low, monotonous tune and the clinking of scissors,  Aurelia Genevieve Thorne, owner and sole stylist of the salon, sighed, the sound swallowed by the rhythmic whirring.  Her fingers, nimble and stained with a rainbow of semi-permanent dyes, meticulously untangled a particularly stubborn knot in a client’s platinum blonde hair, a frown etched on her usually cheerful face.  The storm outside mirrored the tempest brewing within.
