The stale air of the server room hung heavy with the scent of ozone and desperation.  Fluorescent lights hummed a monotonous dirge, reflecting off the rows of blinking, whirring machines that thrummed with the lifeblood of Global Dynamics, a lifeblood Elias Thorne, Systems Analyst, was desperately trying to stanch.  Elias, a man whose neatly trimmed beard and perpetually tired eyes hinted at far too many nights spent wrestling with cryptic error codes, stared at the blinking red light on rack 7B, a single malevolent eye glaring back at him from the heart of the digital beast.  The rhythmic beep-beep-beep of a failing hard drive punctuated the silence, a morbid metronome counting down to a catastrophic system failure. Tonight, the fate of Global Dynamics, and perhaps Elias's career, rested on his weary shoulders.
