The biting November wind whipped Dr. Eleanor Vance’s auburn hair across her face as she hurried down the cobblestone street, the collar of her thick wool coat pulled high.  Rain, sleeting now, plastered the ancient, gas-lamp-lit buildings of Oakhaven into a gothic tableau.  Water cascaded from the eaves of crooked, half-timbered houses, forming miniature waterfalls that snaked their way down the uneven pavement. The air hung thick with the smell of woodsmoke and damp earth, a scent both comforting and melancholic, fitting for the brooding town that had become her home – and perhaps, her prison.  Inside her small but warmly lit practice, "Vance Chiropractic," the scent of pine and chamomile essential oils battled valiantly against the chill seeping in through the old window frames.  Tonight, however, the usual comforting aroma was overshadowed by a more pungent, unsettling fragrance – the metallic tang of blood.
