The acrid bite of woodsmoke stung Lieutenant Elias Thorne’s nostrils, a familiar companion on nights like these.  He squinted through the swirling orange haze, the flickering flames illuminating the skeletal remains of what had once been a proud old oak, its branches now twisted, charred fingers reaching for the inky sky.  Around him, the relentless crackle and pop of the fire were a counterpoint to the rhythmic thud of his own boots on the parched earth.  The air hung heavy with the scent of burning pine and something else, something faintly metallic and sickeningly sweet, that sent a prickle of unease down his spine. This wasn't just another brush fire; something felt…off.  The blaze, nestled deep within the whispering pines of the Redwood National Park, was a malevolent beast, its hungry maw consuming everything in its path, a hungry monster Elias Thorne and his team were tasked with slaying.
