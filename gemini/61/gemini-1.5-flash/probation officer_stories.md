The biting November wind whipped Amelia Katherine Hayes’s dark hair across her face as she wrestled the battered file folder into her briefcase.  Rain, the kind that seeped into your bones and chilled you to the marrow, lashed against the grimy windows of the probation office overlooking the perpetually grey industrial sprawl of Grimsborough.  The city, a monument to rusted ambition and forgotten dreams, mirrored the lives of many of the people Amelia supervised.  Inside, the fluorescent lights hummed a monotonous tune, a stark contrast to the cacophony of sirens wailing in the distance – a soundtrack to her perpetually stressful workday. Tonight, however, was different. Tonight, Amelia felt a prickle of unease that transcended the usual anxieties of her job.  The case file in her hand, thick with grim details and ominous photographs, felt heavier than usual, the weight of another life hanging precariously in the balance.  This was more than just another probation check-in; this was Elias Thorne, and Amelia had a deeply unsettling feeling that tonight, everything would change.
