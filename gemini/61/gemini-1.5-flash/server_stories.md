The chipped Formica tabletop felt sticky under Elara Jean Blackwood’s fingertips.  Elara, or “Ellie” to the regulars who knew her better than they should, sighed, the sound swallowed by the cacophony of clattering plates, sizzling bacon, and the low hum of conversation that permeated the perpetually-busy diner, The Rusty Spoon.  Steam rose from her coffee cup, a small island of warmth in the pre-dawn chill that clung to the air even inside the brightly-lit establishment.  Outside, the neon sign flickered intermittently, casting a lurid pink and orange glow on the rain-slicked streets of Havenwood, a town whose charm was as faded as the diner’s upholstery.  This was Ellie's kingdom, a greasy spoon battlefield where she navigated mountains of dirty dishes, demanding customers, and the ever-present threat of spilled coffee.  But it was her kingdom, and tonight, she sensed something was different. The usual Friday night rush felt… sharper, somehow.  More electric.
