The sterile scent of antiseptic hung heavy in the air, a familiar perfume to Dr. Evelyn Reed, a perfume that tonight clung to her like a shroud.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of her penthouse apartment, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her.  The city of Chicago sprawled beneath her, a glittering tapestry of lights that offered no solace to the turmoil in her chest.  Each flickering window seemed to represent a life she couldn't save, a life she'd lost, a life she desperately wished she could reclaim.  The elegantly furnished room, with its plush Persian rug and abstract art, felt cold and empty despite the warmth of the crackling fireplace.  Tonight, even the carefully chosen comfort of her home couldn't mask the gnawing emptiness.
