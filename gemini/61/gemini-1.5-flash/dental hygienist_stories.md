The sterile scent of antiseptic hung heavy in the air, a familiar perfume to Amelia Grace Hawthorne.  Fluorescent lights hummed a monotonous tune above her as she meticulously polished the already gleaming headrest of chair number three, the faint whir of the sterilization machine a counterpoint to the rhythmic tapping of her fingernails against the smooth enamel.  Amelia, a dental hygienist with a reputation for both meticulous cleaning and an unnervingly calm demeanor, surveyed her domain: Dr. Albright’s impeccably clean, if slightly sterile, dental practice nestled on the third floor of a converted brownstone in the heart of Brooklyn’s Park Slope.  Rain lashed against the tall, arched windows, a relentless drumming that mirrored the anxiety thrumming beneath Amelia's usually placid exterior.  Today wasn't just another Tuesday; today, Mr. Silas Blackwood, a notoriously difficult and eccentric patient, was scheduled for his six-month checkup.
