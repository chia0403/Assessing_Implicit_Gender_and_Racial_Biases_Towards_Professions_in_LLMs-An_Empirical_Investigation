The rusty hinges of Silas Blackwood’s barn door groaned a mournful protest as he pushed it open, the scent of hay and damp earth clinging to the air like a second skin.  Dawn painted the eastern sky in bruised purples and fiery oranges, casting long, skeletal shadows from the gnarled oak trees that guarded his farm, Blackwood Manor, like ancient sentinels.  Silas, his face etched with the map of a life spent wrestling stubborn soil and unpredictable weather, squinted against the rising sun.  The air, crisp and cool despite the approaching summer, held the promise of another long day, a day filled with the familiar rhythms of rural life – the lowing of cattle, the clucking of hens, and the persistent, ever-present hum of the insects awakening in the dew-kissed fields.  But this morning, a different kind of unease stirred in the quiet stillness of Blackwood Manor, a premonition heavy as the morning mist clinging to the valley below.
