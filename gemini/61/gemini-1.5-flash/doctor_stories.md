The biting wind whipped Dr. Eleanor Vance’s dark hair across her face as she wrestled the heavy oak door of Blackwood Manor open.  Gusts of icy air, scented with damp earth and decaying leaves, rushed in, swirling around her and carrying with them the faint, almost imperceptible, aroma of woodsmoke and something else… something subtly metallic and unsettling. Blackwood Manor, perched precariously on the windswept cliffs overlooking the churning grey expanse of the North Sea, loomed before her, a gothic behemoth silhouetted against the bruised purple of the twilight sky. Rain lashed against the leaded windows, a relentless percussion against the mournful sigh of the wind.  Inside, the only light came from a single flickering lamp in the distance, a tiny, defiant spark against the encroaching darkness.  Eleanor shivered, pulling her worn wool coat tighter, not entirely from the cold.  This was no ordinary house call.
