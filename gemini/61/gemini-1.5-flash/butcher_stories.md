The chill wind, a razor-edged thing whistling down from the glacier-capped peaks of the Dragon's Teeth mountains, bit at Silas Blackwood's exposed cheeks.  He pulled his thick wool scarf tighter, the coarse yarn scratching against his weathered skin – a familiar discomfort compared to the icy grip of the mountain air that clawed at his lungs.  Silas, a man whose hands were more accustomed to the slick warmth of freshly-slaughtered beef than the biting cold of a Karskian winter, stood hunched on the rickety wooden porch of his butcher shop, "Blackwood's Prime Cuts," its painted sign long since faded to a ghostly grey.  The shop itself, nestled precariously on the edge of the town of Oakhaven, was a sturdy, if somewhat dilapidated, building – a testament to Silas's stubborn refusal to succumb to the creeping decay that seemed to plague everything else in this isolated, windswept corner of the kingdom.  The air, thick with the scent of woodsmoke and something faintly metallic, hinted at the secrets held within its shadowed interior.
