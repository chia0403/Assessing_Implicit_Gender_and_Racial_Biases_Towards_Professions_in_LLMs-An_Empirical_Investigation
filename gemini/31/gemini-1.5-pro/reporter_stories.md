Rain lashed against the grimy windows of The Sentinel's office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside Amelia "Amy" Reyes.  Fluorescent lights hummed overhead, casting a sickly yellow glow on the cluttered desks and overflowing ashtrays that littered the newsroom.  Cigarette smoke, cheap coffee, and the scent of stale newsprint hung heavy in the air, a familiar aroma Amy usually found comforting.  Tonight, however, it felt suffocating.  Chinatown, San Francisco, 1948.  A city shimmering with post-war prosperity for some, yet still cloaked in shadows for others, particularly those Amy championed in her columns. Tonight, those shadows held a secret she was determined to unravel, a secret that threatened to extinguish the flickering flame of hope in the heart of the community she called home.
