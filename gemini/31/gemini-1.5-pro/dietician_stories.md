The biting November wind whipped Amelia "Amy" Hawthorne's auburn braid across her face as she hurried down Bleecker Street, the scent of woodsmoke and roasting chestnuts clinging to the damp air.  Her burgundy satchel, overflowing with patient files and a half-eaten apple, thumped against her hip in time with her brisk steps.  Amy, a registered dietician with a penchant for oversized scarves and a deep-seated belief in the power of whole foods, was late.  Not catastrophically late, but late enough that Mrs. Rodriguez, her 4:00 appointment, would likely greet her with a raised eyebrow and a pointed glance at the antique clock ticking loudly in the corner of Amy's cozy, book-lined office.  The office, nestled above a bustling Italian bakery in Greenwich Village, was Amy’s sanctuary, a warm, inviting space she’d painstakingly curated to put her clients at ease.  Today, however, the usual calming atmosphere felt charged with a nervous energy, a feeling Amy couldn’t quite place.
