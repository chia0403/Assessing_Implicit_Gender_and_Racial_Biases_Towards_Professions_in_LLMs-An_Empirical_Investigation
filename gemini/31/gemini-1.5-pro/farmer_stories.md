The biting wind whipped across the frost-kissed fields of Hemlock Hollow, stinging Elias Thorne's cheeks raw even through his thick beard.  Dawn was a hesitant smudge of grey in the east, barely illuminating the rolling expanse of his farm. The farmhouse, a sturdy two-story structure of weathered grey stone, hunched against the chill, smoke curling invitingly from its chimney. Elias, his breath misting in the frigid air, adjusted the worn leather straps of his overalls and surveyed his domain.  A thin layer of ice crackled under his heavy boots as he walked, the frozen earth groaning beneath the weight of the coming winter.  This year, the frost had arrived early, a cruel harbinger whispering of lean times ahead for Elias and the small community nestled in the isolated valley.
