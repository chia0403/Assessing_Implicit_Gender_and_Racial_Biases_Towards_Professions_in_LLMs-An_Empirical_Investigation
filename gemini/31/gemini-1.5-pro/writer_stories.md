Rain lashed against the attic window, a relentless drumming that mirrored the headache pounding behind Amelia Thorne's eyes.  The single bare bulb hanging precariously from the rafter cast long, distorted shadows across the cramped space, illuminating dust motes dancing in the air and the overflowing stacks of manuscripts and notebooks that threatened to spill onto the threadbare rug.  Amelia, perched on a rickety stool at her ancient typewriter, chewed on the end of a chipped mug, the dregs of lukewarm coffee doing little to soothe her frayed nerves.  The attic of her grandmother's dilapidated Victorian house in Hollow Creek, Maine, was supposed to be her sanctuary, a place where inspiration flowed as freely as the nearby Whispering River.  Tonight, however, the only thing flowing freely was a deep, unsettling sense of dread.
