The biting November wind whipped around Amelia "Mia" Rodriguez, stinging her cheeks and tugging at the loose strands of hair escaping her helmet.  Flakes of snow, fat and wet, began to plaster themselves to the brick facades of the old brownstones lining Bleecker Street in Greenwich Village. The air crackled with a nervous energy, a palpable tension that always preceded a major incident.  Mia, a five-year veteran of Ladder 8, adjusted her turnout gear and squinted at the swirling smoke billowing from the fourth floor of a narrow, five-story building. The pungent smell of burning wood and plastic filled the air, a grim perfume announcing another battle against the ever-hungry flames.  Christmas lights, strung haphazardly across the fire escape, flickered erratically, casting dancing shadows against the backdrop of the inferno. The ladder truck's engine rumbled behind her, a mechanical beast ready to roar into action.  Tonight, Mia knew, could be a long one.
