The biting November wind whipped at Elias Thorne's cheeks, turning them the same shade as the crimson maple leaves he was raking into a neat pile.  Frost crunched beneath his worn work boots as he surveyed the sprawling grounds of Eventide Manor, a Victorian behemoth perched on a bluff overlooking the churning grey expanse of the Atlantic. The salt spray kissed the air, carrying with it the mournful cry of gulls and the distant clang of a buoy. Eventide, with its wraparound porch and turret capped with wrought iron, had seen better days.  The gardens, once meticulously manicured, were now overgrown and choked with weeds, a reflection of the reclusive owner's dwindling fortune.  Elias, however, saw potential, not decay. He saw the ghost of vibrant rose bushes in the tangled thorns, the spectral elegance of forgotten hydrangeas beneath the blanket of fallen leaves.  This wasn't just a job for him; it was a resurrection.
