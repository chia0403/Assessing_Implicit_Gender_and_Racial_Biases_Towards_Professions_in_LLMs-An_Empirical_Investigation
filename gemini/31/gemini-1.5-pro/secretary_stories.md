Rain lashed against the ornate, leaded windows of the Grimshaw Building, a gothic monstrosity that clawed its way into the perpetually overcast London sky.  Gargoyles, slick with rainwater, seemed to leer down at the scurrying figures below. Inside, on the eighteenth floor, Eleanor Vance, secretary to the enigmatic Mr. Silas Blackwood, shivered despite the humming radiator in the corner. The Art Deco office, usually a sanctuary of polished mahogany and muted elegance, felt oppressive under the storm's relentless drumming.  A single lamp burned low on her desk, casting long, dancing shadows that played across the overflowing in-tray.  The air hung heavy with the scent of old paper and something else, something faintly metallic, that Eleanor couldn't quite place.
