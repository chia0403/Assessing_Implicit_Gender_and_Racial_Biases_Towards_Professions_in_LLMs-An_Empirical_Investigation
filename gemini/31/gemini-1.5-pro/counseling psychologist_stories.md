The rain hammered against the leaded glass windows of Dr. Elias Thorne's office, mimicking the persistent ache in his own chest.  His consulting room, nestled on the second floor of a converted Victorian townhouse in the heart of fog-drenched Sausalito, California, usually felt warm and inviting, a sanctuary from the bluster of the outside world.  Tonight, however, even the flickering gas fireplace and the scent of jasmine tea couldn't dispel the oppressive gloom that had settled over the town, and over Elias himself.  The ornate clock on the mantelpiece chimed seven times, a sharp counterpoint to the rhythmic drumming of the rain, and Elias glanced at the empty leather armchair opposite his desk. His last client of the day, a troubled artist grappling with a creative block, had cancelled at the last minute, leaving Elias with an unexpected expanse of quiet and a growing sense of unease.
