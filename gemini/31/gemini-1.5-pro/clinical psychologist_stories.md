The rain hammered against the bay window of Dr. Eleanor Vance's office, mimicking the relentless drumming in her head.  Her leather armchair, usually a haven of calm contemplation, felt more like a damp, sinking raft tonight. The gothic spires of St. Jude's Church, barely visible through the downpour, loomed across the street like skeletal fingers reaching for the stormy sky.  Eleanor, nestled in the heart of historic Charleston, South Carolina, usually found solace in the antiquated charm of her surroundings. But not tonight. Tonight, the cobblestone streets and gas lamps whispered of secrets, and the humid air hung thick with a premonition she couldn't shake.  The session with her last patient, a troubled artist named Silas, had left her unsettled, a knot of unease tightening in her stomach.  His parting words, "You can't hide from the shadows, Doctor," echoed in the silence between the thunderclaps.
