The biting February wind whipped around Dr. Elias Thorne as he hurried across the cobbled courtyard of St. Jude's Hospital, the gothic spires looming against the bruised twilight sky of Edinburgh. Snow, fine and insistent, dusted the ancient stonework, transforming the gargoyles perched high above into spectral sentinels.  Elias pulled his threadbare scarf tighter around his neck, the chill seeping through his thick wool overcoat. He was late. Again. The insistent buzzing of his pager, clipped to his belt, echoed the frantic fluttering in his stomach. It was Mrs. MacKay's heart, a fragile thing held together by hope and surgical wire, demanding his attention.  The operating theatre, a beacon of sterile light on the third floor, awaited him, and with it, another battle against the relentless march of time and decay.
