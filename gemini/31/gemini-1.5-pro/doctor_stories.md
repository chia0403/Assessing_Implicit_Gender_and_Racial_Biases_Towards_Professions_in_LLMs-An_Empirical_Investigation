The biting November wind whipped around Dr. Elias Thorne's ankles as he hurried across the cobbled courtyard of St. Jude's Hospital, the ancient stone buildings looming like benevolent giants against the bruised twilight sky.  Tucked away in the heart of the Cotswolds, St. Jude's was a sanctuary, a place where time seemed to slow its relentless march.  Ivy, thick and green even in late autumn, clung to the walls, softening the harsh lines of the architecture.  Inside, warm light spilled from mullioned windows, promising refuge from the encroaching cold.  Elias, his breath puffing out white clouds in the frigid air, clutched his worn leather satchel tighter, the weight of the day's burdens settling heavy in his shoulders.  Tonight, the quiet sanctuary held a secret, a disquiet that hummed beneath the surface of the usual hushed routines, and Elias knew, with a chilling certainty, that he was at its heart.
