Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of the Kestrel Corporation building, blurring the Seattle skyline into a hazy tapestry of grey and neon.  Inside, the air buzzed with a low hum of fluorescent lights and the quiet tap-tap-tap of keyboards.  Elara Vance, a systems analyst with a penchant for oversized cardigans and an even greater penchant for dissecting complex code, hunched over her monitor, her brow furrowed in concentration.  Lines of cryptic script scrolled across the screen, a digital hieroglyphic puzzle she’d been wrestling with for the past three days.  The problem wasn't just the intermittent system failures plaguing Kestrel’s new logistics platform; it was the unsettling lack of any discernible pattern to them. They were random, sporadic, almost...malicious.  And Elara, fueled by lukewarm coffee and an unshakeable belief in logical explanations, was determined to find out why.
