The biting wind whipped Amelia “Amy” Rodriguez’s dark braid across her face as she wrestled with the rusted service panel on the side of the dilapidated Victorian.  The late November air in Port Blossom, Oregon, carried the scent of salt and decaying leaves, a peculiar but familiar perfume to Amy.  Grey clouds, heavy with the promise of rain, pressed down on the town, muting the usually vibrant colors of the seaside cottages that lined the cliffs overlooking the turbulent Pacific. This particular house, however, perched precariously on a windswept bluff, seemed to embrace the gloom.  Its peeling paint, boarded-up windows, and overgrown garden hinted at a long-forgotten story, a story Amy was about to become a part of as she braced herself for whatever electrical horrors lay within.
