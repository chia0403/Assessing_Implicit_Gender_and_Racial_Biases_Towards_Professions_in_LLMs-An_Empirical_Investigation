The biting November wind whipped around Professor Elias Thorne's ankles as he hurried across the cobbled quadrangle of St. Jude's College, Oxford. Gargoyles, slick with rain, leered down from the medieval stonework, their expressions mirroring his own grim mood.  A thin scrim of frost clung to the manicured lawns, crunching faintly under his worn leather shoes. The buttery glow from the arched windows of the library offered a tempting refuge, but Elias pressed on towards the looming silhouette of the Radcliffe Camera, its dome a dark crown against the bruised twilight sky.  He was late, unforgivably late, for the meeting of the Bibliophilic Society, a transgression he knew wouldn't be easily forgiven, particularly by its notoriously prickly chairman.  Tonight's topic – the provenance of a recently discovered illuminated manuscript – was one Elias couldn’t afford to miss. Not if his suspicions were correct.
