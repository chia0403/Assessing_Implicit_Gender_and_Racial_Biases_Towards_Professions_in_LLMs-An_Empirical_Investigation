The biting wind whipped across the desolate expanse of the Bonneville Salt Flats, stinging Amelia “Milly” Reyes’s cheeks as she adjusted the antenna atop her battered Ford Econoline van.  The setting sun painted the vast, cracked salt pan in hues of orange and purple, casting long, distorted shadows that danced like phantoms across the blinding white surface.  Inside the van, a chaotic tangle of wires, oscilloscopes, and soldering irons sprawled across the workbench, illuminated by the flickering glow of a kerosene lamp.  Milly, her dark hair pulled back in a messy bun, hunched over a vintage Hallifax receiver, the faint static hiss filling the small space. Tonight, she was determined to catch a signal, a whisper from the cosmos, a voice beyond the mundane chatter of earthly broadcasts.  Miles from the nearest town, nestled in the heart of this otherworldly landscape, Milly and her mobile radio lab were alone, poised on the edge of the listening unknown.
