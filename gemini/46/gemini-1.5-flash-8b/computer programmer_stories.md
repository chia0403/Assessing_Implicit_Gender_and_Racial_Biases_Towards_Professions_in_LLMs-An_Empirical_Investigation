The flickering fluorescent lights of the "Code Crafters" office hummed a discordant tune, mirroring the chaotic symphony of keyboard clicks and mumbled pronouncements echoing from the cramped, windowless space.  Rain hammered against the grimy exterior windows, blurring the city lights into a swirling kaleidoscope outside.  Elias Thorne, a thirty-two-year-old computer programmer with perpetually tired eyes and a perpetually messy mop of brown hair, hunched over his battered laptop.  A half-eaten takeout container of ramen sat beside him, a testament to the all-nighters he'd become accustomed to.  Coffee, lukewarm and bitter, sat in a chipped mug, a faded symbol of an earlier, more productive era.  The air, thick with the scent of stale coffee and recycled air, felt almost tangible, pressing down on him. This was his life, his sanctuary, and his purgatory all rolled into one.  The project, codenamed "Project Chimera," was due in five hours, and Elias was nowhere near finished.
