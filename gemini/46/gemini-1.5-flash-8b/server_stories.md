The chipped Formica counter, stained a perpetual beige from spilled coffee and lukewarm tea, reflected Amelia "Amy" Butterfield's weary face.  The fluorescent lights of the diner, "The Rusty Spoon," hummed a monotonous tune, a soundtrack to the rhythmic clatter of silverware and the low murmur of conversations.  Rain hammered against the grimy windows, blurring the already faded floral wallpaper and painting streaks of grey across the pale yellow walls.  Amy, her name tag slightly askew,  adjusted the overflowing stack of menus, the paper crinkling with every shift of her weight.  It was a Tuesday night, the slowest night of the week, but the gnawing emptiness in the pit of her stomach mirrored the almost silent emptiness of the diner.
