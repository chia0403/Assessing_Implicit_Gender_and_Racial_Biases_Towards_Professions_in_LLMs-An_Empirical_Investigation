The chipped porcelain sink, stained a perpetual blush of faded rose, reflected Amelia Blackwood's tired eyes.  Dust motes danced in the afternoon sun filtering through the grimy windowpanes of "The Gilded Curl," her tiny salon tucked away on a side street off of Elm.  The scent of lavender and ozone, a familiar comfort, clung to the air, mingling with the faint aroma of burnt coffee.  Amelia, a cosmetologist with fingers that had sculpted countless transformations, traced the faint line of a client's eyebrow with a practiced hand.  But today, even the rhythmic click of her clippers felt oddly hollow.  Outside, the relentless rhythm of the city – cars honking, children laughing, distant sirens – seemed to mock the quiet desperation that settled in her chest.  Amelia knew, with a chilling certainty, that today was the day things were going to change – or break.
