The biting wind whipped Dr. Eleanor Vance's crimson scarf around her neck, stinging her cheeks like a thousand tiny needles.  Snow, the consistency of powdered sugar, blanketed the cobblestone streets of Prague, obscuring the already dim light filtering through the ancient, gabled roofs.  The air, thick with the scent of pine and something indefinably…metallic, hung heavy in the winter air.  Eleanor, Professor of Medieval History at the University of Oxford, felt a familiar tremor of unease.  It wasn't the cold, though that always intensified her apprehension. It was the city itself, a living tapestry woven from centuries of whispered secrets and forgotten histories, that felt… expectant.  She was here, on this freezing, shrouded night, seeking answers within the shadowed halls of the National Museum, a quest driven by a cryptic message found tucked within a centuries-old manuscript, a message speaking of a hidden chamber and a forgotten relic.
