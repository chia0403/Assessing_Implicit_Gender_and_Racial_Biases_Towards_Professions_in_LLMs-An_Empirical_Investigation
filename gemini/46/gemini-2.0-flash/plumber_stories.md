The screech of rusty hinges echoed through the cathedral-like quiet of Mrs. Havercroft's basement. Bartholomew "Bart" Higgins, his name embroidered on the breast pocket of his faded blue work shirt, wiped a bead of sweat from his brow with the back of his hand, leaving a smudge of grime. He hated basements, especially old ones. This one, however, was on a whole other level of creepy. 

The setting was the sprawling, Victorian-era Havercroft Manor, perched on a windswept bluff overlooking the churning Atlantic Ocean just outside the sleepy town of Port Blossom, Maine. It was a house that whispered stories of fortunes made and lost, of generations born and buried within its weathered granite walls. Sunlight struggled to penetrate the grimy, arched windows set high in the foundation, leaving the vast basement perpetually shrouded in a damp, cool gloom. Cobwebs hung like macabre chandeliers from the exposed pipes and beams, and the air was thick with the scent of mildew and something indefinably...old. The only light came from Bart's halogen work lamp, which cast long, dancing shadows that seemed to writhe with a life of their own. He could almost feel the house watching him, judging him, waiting for him to slip up. And Bart, armed with nothing but his trusty wrench and a healthy dose of skepticism, had a sinking feeling that today, he might.
