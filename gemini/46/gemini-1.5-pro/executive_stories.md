Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of Amelia "Amy" Hawthorne's forty-second-floor office, blurring the already fading London skyline into a watercolor wash of grey and ochre. The Shard, usually a gleaming spire of defiance against the elements, was tonight a subdued giant, its tip swallowed by the swirling clouds. Inside, the air crackled with a different kind of electricity. Amy, the newly appointed Chief Operating Officer of  Stratton-Reese International, a global investment firm, tapped a manicured nail against the polished mahogany of her desk, the rhythmic click a counterpoint to the relentless drumming of the rain.  Scattered across its surface were confidential documents, each bearing the stark red stamp: "Project Nightingale." This was not the celebratory welcome she had envisioned.  Her first week had been a whirlwind of closed-door meetings, hushed phone calls, and a pervading sense of unease she couldn't quite put her finger on.  Something was rotten in the heart of Stratton-Reese, and Amy, with her reputation for unwavering integrity and laser-sharp focus, was tasked with finding out what.
