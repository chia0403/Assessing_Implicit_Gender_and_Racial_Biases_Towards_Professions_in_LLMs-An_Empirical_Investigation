The recycled air hummed a monotonous C-sharp, a lullaby only Amelia Grace Hawthorne could truly appreciate.  Outside, the bruised purple of a pre-dawn sky bled into the inky blackness clinging to the horizon.  Flight 427 from JFK to Heathrow was almost empty, a ghost plane ferrying a handful of souls across the Atlantic. Amelia, her navy blue uniform crisp and uncreased despite the ungodly hour, moved through the cabin, checking seatbelts and offering lukewarm smiles.  The faint smell of stale coffee and airplane disinfectant clung to the air, a familiar perfume she'd long grown accustomed to.  The cabin lights, dimmed low, cast long shadows that danced with the gentle rocking of the 747, creating the illusion of a slow, ethereal waltz. This was Amelia's domain, a metal tube suspended between two worlds, where the ordinary rules of time and space seemed to bend at the whim of the jet stream. Tonight, however, a strange disquiet hung in the air, a subtle shift in the usual symphony of flight that prickled the hairs on the back of her neck.
