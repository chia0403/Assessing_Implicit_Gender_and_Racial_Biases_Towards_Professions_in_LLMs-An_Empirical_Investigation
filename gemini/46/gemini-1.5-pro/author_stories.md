The salt spray stung Amelia Iris Thorne's face as she leaned against the railing of her rented cottage, the wind whipping her dark hair around her shoulders. The cottage, perched precariously on the edge of a cliff overlooking the turbulent Irish Sea, was supposed to be her sanctuary, a place where she could finally finish her long-overdue novel.  Below, the waves crashed against the jagged rocks at the base of the cliff, a constant, rhythmic percussion that echoed the unsettling rhythm of her writer's block.  The Isle of Aran, with its stark beauty and isolated charm, had seemed the perfect escape from the pressures of London literary society. But after three weeks, the only words Amelia had managed to produce were grocery lists and increasingly frantic emails to her editor.  The looming deadline hung over her like the perpetually grey sky, a constant reminder of her failing inspiration. The wild landscape, once a source of awe, now felt like a reflection of the chaotic emptiness in her mind.
