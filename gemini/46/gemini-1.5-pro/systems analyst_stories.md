The flickering fluorescent lights of the server room hummed a monotonous C sharp, a soundtrack to Amelia Reyes’s mounting frustration.  Cable ties lay scattered across the raised floor like discarded exoskeletons, and the air, thick with the metallic scent of overheating electronics, clung to her like a second skin.  It was 3:17 AM on a Tuesday, the witching hour for rogue code and failing hardware, and Amelia, a systems analyst for the sprawling Metropolis Transit Authority, was trapped in the belly of the beast.  Outside, the sprawling metropolis of New Cascadia glittered with a million electric promises, oblivious to the digital Jenga tower threatening to collapse beneath its feet.  The MTA's entire network – the subway lines, the buses, the commuter rails – relied on these temperamental servers, and tonight, one of them was determined to bring the whole system grinding to a halt.
