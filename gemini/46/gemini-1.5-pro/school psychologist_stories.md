The biting November wind whipped Dr. Eleanor Vance's auburn hair across her face as she hurried across the cracked asphalt of the Northwood High School parking lot.  Frosted leaves, the color of bruised plums, skittered around her ankles. The sky, a steely grey canvas, threatened snow. Inside her battered leather satchel, Eleanor carried the weight of a dozen student files, each one a small universe of anxieties, hopes, and hidden struggles. Northwood High, a sprawling brick behemoth perched on the edge of the decaying mill town of Havenwood, Pennsylvania, wasn't just a school; it was a microcosm of the town's struggles, reflecting its economic decline and the quiet desperation clinging to its inhabitants like coal dust. Today, however, a new and unsettling tension hung in the air, a palpable unease that went beyond the usual Monday morning blues.
