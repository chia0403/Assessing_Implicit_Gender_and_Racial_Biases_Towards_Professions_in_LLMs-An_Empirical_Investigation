The biting November wind whipped around Professor Alistair Finch, tugging at the edges of his tweed coat as he hurried across the cobbled quadrangle of St. Jude's College, Oxford. Gargoyles leered down from the honey-coloured stone walls, their expressions mirroring the grim set of Alistair's jaw.  A thin layer of frost crunched under his worn leather shoes, the sound swallowed by the looming silence of the ancient buildings.  It was barely past six in the morning, the sky a bruised purple, and the only other sign of life was a faint light flickering from the window of his office in the New Quad, a starkly modern addition that always felt jarringly out of place.  Inside that brightly lit room lay the manuscript, the very reason for his sleeplessness and the source of a growing unease that was slowly turning into full-blown dread.
