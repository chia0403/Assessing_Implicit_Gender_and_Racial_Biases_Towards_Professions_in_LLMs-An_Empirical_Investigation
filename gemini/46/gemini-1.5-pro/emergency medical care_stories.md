The siren screamed, a high-pitched wail slicing through the thick, humid Charleston air.  Amelia “Amy” Reyes gripped the steering wheel of the ambulance tighter, her knuckles white against the worn rubber. Rain lashed against the windshield, blurring the already fading neon lights of the bars and restaurants lining King Street.  It was 2:17 AM, a prime time for bad decisions and even worse outcomes.  Amy, a seasoned Emergency Medical Technician with Charleston County EMS, braced herself.  Tonight, the city was exhaling a chaotic energy, and she knew, with a grim certainty, that her shift was far from over.
