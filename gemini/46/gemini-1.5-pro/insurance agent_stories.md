Rain lashed against the window of Elias Thorne's office, mimicking the relentless drumming of his headache.  The flickering neon sign of the "Lucky Clover Pawn" across the street cast a sickly green glow on the stacks of claim forms piled haphazardly on his desk.  Elias Thorne, a man whose crisp suit and polished shoes belied the deepening weariness in his eyes, was an insurance agent in the perpetually damp and perpetually grey town of Havenwood, Oregon.  Havenwood, nestled between the brooding Cascade Mountains and the churning Pacific, was a place where misfortune seemed to linger like the sea fog, clinging to the clapboard houses and seeping into the very bones of its inhabitants. This was a town where Elias made his living, one unfortunate incident at a time.
