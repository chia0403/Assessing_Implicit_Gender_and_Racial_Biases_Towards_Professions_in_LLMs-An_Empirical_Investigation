The biting November wind whipped across the desolate expanse of the Black Ridge power substation, stinging Elias Thorne's cheeks even through his thick wool beard.  Rust-colored flakes of paint peeled from the towering transformers, humming a low, ominous song in the gathering twilight. Elias, his breath clouding the frigid air, adjusted his hard hat and squinted at the intricate web of wires overhead.  Below, the small, frozen town of Havenwood, nestled in the valley, glittered with a fragile constellation of lights.  One of those lights – the one belonging to the old church on Elm Street – flickered erratically, the source of Elias’s current misery and the reason he was out in this sub-zero wasteland instead of enjoying a warm fire and a hot cup of coffee.  He knew, with a certainty honed from years of wrestling with faulty circuits, that somewhere within this labyrinth of steel and wire lay the answer, and tonight, Havenwood depended on him finding it.
