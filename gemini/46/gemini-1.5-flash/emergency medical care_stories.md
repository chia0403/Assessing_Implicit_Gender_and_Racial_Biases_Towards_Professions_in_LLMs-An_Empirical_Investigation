The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Amy” Hernandez’s hair across her face as she wrestled the stretcher into the back of the ambulance.  The flickering sodium vapor streetlights cast long, distorted shadows across the slick cobblestones of Prague’s Old Town Square, illuminating the scene with a sickly yellow glow.  Atop the ancient paving stones, a young man lay crumpled, his breath shallow and ragged, a crimson stain blossoming on his crisp white shirt. The scent of roasted chestnuts and spiced wine, usually so comforting in the pre-Christmas air, was now overwhelmed by the metallic tang of blood. Amy, a paramedic with Prague Emergency Medical Services, felt the familiar knot of adrenaline tighten in her chest. Tonight, the fairytale city held a different kind of magic, a grim and urgent magic that demanded her unwavering attention.
