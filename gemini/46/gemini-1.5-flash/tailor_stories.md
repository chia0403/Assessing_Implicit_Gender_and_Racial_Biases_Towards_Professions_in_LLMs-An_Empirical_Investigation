The scent of beeswax and aged linen hung heavy in the air, a familiar perfume to Elias Thorne, master tailor of Threadhaven.  Dust motes, illuminated by the single, guttering gas lamp above his workbench, danced in the twilight that had already settled over the cobbled streets of Oakhaven.  Outside, the wind howled a mournful tune, rattling the aged windowpanes of his tiny shop, a sound Elias had grown accustomed to over his fifty-two years of stitching and snipping within these four walls.  Oakhaven, nestled deep within a valley shrouded by ancient, whispering oaks, was a town steeped in secrets and shadows, a place where the past clung stubbornly to the present, much like the threads Elias painstakingly coaxed into life with his needle. Tonight, however, a new secret, sharp and unexpected, was about to be woven into the fabric of his life.
