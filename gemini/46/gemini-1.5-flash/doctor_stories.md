The biting wind whipped Dr. Eleanor Vance’s auburn braid across her face as she navigated the treacherous, ice-slicked path leading to the isolated cottage.  Snow, thick and unforgiving, piled high against the crooked stone walls, almost swallowing the single, flickering lamplight visible from within.  The air hung heavy with the scent of pine and something else… something metallic and faintly sickening, clinging to the frigid breeze like a shroud.  This wasn't the picturesque winter wonderland depicted on the glossy brochures advertising the remote Highland village of Glencoe; this was a scene straight from a gothic nightmare, and Eleanor, despite her years of experience in emergency medicine, felt a prickle of unease crawl down her spine.  The cottage, nestled deep within a grove of skeletal, snow-laden trees, seemed to hold its breath, waiting.
