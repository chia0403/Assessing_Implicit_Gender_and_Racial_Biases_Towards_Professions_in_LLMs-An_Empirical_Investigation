The salt spray stung Dr. Eleanor Vance's face as she navigated the rickety wooden walkway, the rhythmic creak of the planks a counterpoint to the relentless roar of the Atlantic.  Seabirds wheeled overhead, their cries lost in the wind that whipped strands of her auburn braid across her cheeks.  She clutched her worn leather satchel tighter, its contents – a collection of specialized tools and carefully chosen exercises – jostling against her side.  The air, thick with the scent of brine and seaweed, carried a hint of woodsmoke from the distant cottages clinging to the cliffs of the isolated Cornish village of Porthcurno.  Eleanor, a physical therapist renowned for her unconventional methods and unwavering dedication, was not here for a holiday.  She’d been summoned, under rather mysterious circumstances, to the clifftop mansion of Lord Ashworth, a reclusive nobleman rumored to be afflicted by a debilitating and utterly baffling ailment.
