The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Katherine Finch's hands, doing little to thaw the icy grip of the November wind whistling through the gaps in the window frame of her office.  Amelia, or Amy as most people called her – though she preferred the formality in professional settings – stared out at the churning grey expanse of the North Sea, its waves mirroring the tempestuous turmoil currently brewing in her own life.  Her tiny, almost comically inadequate, insurance agency, "Finch & Finch Adjusters" (a name inherited from her late father, a much more successful man than she), clung precariously to the windswept clifftop overlooking the town of Seabrook, a picturesque but economically struggling fishing village on the rugged Northumberland coast.  The smell of salt and seaweed, usually a comfort, tonight felt like a suffocating blanket, heavy with the weight of unpaid bills and looming deadlines.
