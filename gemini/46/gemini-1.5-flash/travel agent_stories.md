The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Hernandez’s hands, the lukewarm chamomile tea doing little to soothe the icy dread gripping her stomach.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of her office, blurring the already hazy cityscape of San Francisco sprawling below.  Amelia, a travel agent with a penchant for meticulously organized spreadsheets and a deep-seated fear of spontaneity, stared out at the tempest.  Her meticulously organized desk, usually a testament to her efficient nature, was a chaotic mess of crumpled brochures, half-written itineraries, and a single, wilting orchid – a pathetic reminder of her last, disastrously planned, romantic getaway to Bali.  The air hung thick with the scent of stale coffee and impending doom, the latter courtesy of Mr. Fitzwilliam’s impending arrival – a notoriously demanding client with a penchant for exotic locations and even more exotic demands.
