The biting November wind whipped at Elias Thorne’s weathered face, stinging his eyes and tugging at the brim of his worn baseball cap.  Below, the city of Portland sprawled, a tapestry of grey rooftops stitched together by a relentless drizzle.  From his precarious perch atop the gabled roof of a Victorian mansion, Elias surveyed his kingdom – a chaotic landscape of decaying shingles, warped wood, and treacherous ice patches.  The air, thick with the smell of damp wood and exhaust fumes, carried the faint, distant aroma of roasting coffee, a cruel reminder of the warmth he was desperately trying to maintain against the onslaught of the elements.  This wasn't just a job; it was a battle against the relentless Pacific Northwest winter, a fight Elias Thorne, roofer extraordinaire, was determined to win.
