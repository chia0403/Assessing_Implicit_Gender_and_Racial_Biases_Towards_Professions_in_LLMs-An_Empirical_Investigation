The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Amy” Hernandez’s hair across her face as she hunched deeper into her threadbare coat.  Rain, a fine, icy drizzle, plastered her already damp newspaper to her chest, the headline – *Mayor’s Scandal Rocks City Hall* – mockingly bold against the grey, unforgiving sky.  Amy shivered, not entirely from the cold.  The air itself seemed thick with the unspoken anxieties of Havenwood, a town clinging precariously to the edge of the Appalachian Mountains, its charm gradually eroding under a veneer of political corruption and simmering resentment.  The ramshackle Victorian houses, once proud and stately, now wore the weary expression of a town holding its breath, waiting for the inevitable collapse.  Amy adjusted the worn leather strap of her reporter's bag, the weight of her notepad and half-eaten granola bar a familiar comfort in the face of the unfolding chaos.  This wasn’t just another story; this was Havenwood’s reckoning.
