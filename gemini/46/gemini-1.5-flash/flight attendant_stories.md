The recycled air hung thick with the scent of cheap perfume and stale coffee, a familiar olfactory cocktail for Seraphina “Sera” Dubois.  Outside, the pre-dawn sky over Anchorage was a bruised purple, the faintest blush of sunrise struggling to break through the persistent gloom.  Sera, perched precariously on the edge of a fold-out seat in the nearly deserted crew lounge of Ted Stevens Anchorage International Airport, meticulously polished her already gleaming name tag.  The polished brass reflected the weary lines etched around her eyes, lines earned not from age, but from years of navigating screaming babies, demanding passengers, and the unrelenting tyranny of airport delays.  Tonight, however, the usual pre-flight jitters were overshadowed by a deeper unease, a prickling sensation at the back of her neck that hinted at something far more unsettling than a delayed connection.
