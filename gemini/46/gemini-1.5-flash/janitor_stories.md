The biting November wind howled a mournful dirge through the cracked windowpanes of the Blackwood Academy for the Gifted and Talented, rattling the ancient, oak-framed portraits that lined the echoing hallway.  Dust motes, illuminated by the single flickering fluorescent light above, danced in the chilly air like mischievous spirits.  Elias Thorne, his name a stark contrast to the grim setting, hunched deeper into his worn, navy blue janitor's uniform.  The scent of stale polish and damp stone clung to him like a second skin, a familiar aroma in the grand, yet decaying, halls of the academy.  Elias, a man whose life was measured in the rhythmic swish of his mop and the quiet whisper of the vacuum cleaner, was about to have his routine – and perhaps his life – irrevocably disrupted.
