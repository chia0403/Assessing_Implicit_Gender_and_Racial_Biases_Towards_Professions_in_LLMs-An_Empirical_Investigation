The wind, a rasping, gravelly voice, whispered secrets across the parched earth of the Broken Bow Ranch.  Dust devils, miniature tornadoes of red dirt and grit, danced across the withered grass, mimicking the restless energy churning inside Silas “Hawk” Hawkins.  The sun, a malevolent eye in the brassy sky, beat down on the sprawling ranch, baking the already cracked soil until it resembled a mosaic of ancient, forgotten pottery.  Hawk squinted, his weathered face a roadmap of years spent under this unforgiving Texas sun, the brim of his Stetson casting a shadow that did little to alleviate the oppressive heat.  The air hung heavy, thick with the scent of dry earth and the faint, metallic tang of blood – not a fresh wound, but the lingering aroma of a recent coyote skirmish near the dwindling water trough. This was his life, his kingdom, and it was slowly, relentlessly dying.
