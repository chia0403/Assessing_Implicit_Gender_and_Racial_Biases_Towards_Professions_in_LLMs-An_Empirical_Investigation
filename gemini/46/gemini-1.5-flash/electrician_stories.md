The biting November wind howled a mournful tune around Elias Thorne's ears as he wrestled the recalcitrant conduit pipe into place.  His breath plumed out in frosty clouds, instantly vanishing into the grey, swirling air above the half-constructed skyscraper.  Below, the city of Chicago sprawled like a concrete and steel tapestry, its myriad lights already twinkling into life against the rapidly descending dusk. Elias, his face smudged with grease and his thick work gloves stiff with cold, adjusted his hard hat, the fluorescent lamp strapped to his forehead casting a stark, pale circle on the unfinished concrete floor.  The air thrummed with the low hum of power tools and the shouts of other workers, a chaotic symphony against the backdrop of the city's evening roar. He was perched precariously on a scaffolding platform, sixty stories above the ground, a tiny human speck against the colossal, skeletal frame of the building, tasked with wiring the city's newest, and arguably most ambitious, luxury condominium.
