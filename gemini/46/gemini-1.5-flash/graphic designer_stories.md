The biting November wind whipped Amelia Hernandez’s auburn hair across her face as she hurried down Bleecker Street, the collar of her vintage wool coat pulled high.  The scent of roasting chestnuts and damp leaves mingled in the air, a bittersweet perfume that perfectly mirrored her mood.  Rain threatened, a grey, brooding sky mirroring the turmoil brewing inside her.  Amelia, a graphic designer with a penchant for bold typography and a secret longing for watercolor landscapes, was late.  Terribly late.  Not just late for a meeting – a *pivotal* meeting with Mr. Silas Blackwood, the notoriously demanding head of Blackwood & Sons Publishing, a company whose logo – a stark, elegant raven – she'd spent the last three weeks painstakingly perfecting.  The raven, perched on a gnarled branch, stared back at her from her overflowing tote bag, a silent, feathered judge of her impending tardiness.  The pressure was immense, the stakes impossibly high: this was her chance, her big break.  And she was about to blow it.
