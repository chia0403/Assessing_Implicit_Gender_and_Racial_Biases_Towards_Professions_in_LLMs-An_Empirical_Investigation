The humid Louisiana air hung heavy, thick and sweet with the scent of honeysuckle and decay.  Cicadas buzzed a relentless, hypnotic drone, a soundtrack to the oppressive heat that clung to Eleanor Vance, RN, like a second skin.  Sweat beaded on her forehead, despite the crisp white uniform clinging to her.  Eleanor adjusted the stethoscope around her neck, the cool metal a small comfort against the oppressive warmth as she navigated the cluttered, dimly lit hallway of St. Jude's Parish Hospital.  The ancient building, a relic of a bygone era, groaned under the weight of years and the ever-present humidity, its peeling paint and chipped plaster a testament to a dwindling budget and a chronic shortage of staff. Tonight, however, the usual quiet exhaustion of the night shift was replaced by a palpable tension, a nervous energy that vibrated in the very floorboards beneath her worn leather shoes.  Something was different.  Something was wrong.
