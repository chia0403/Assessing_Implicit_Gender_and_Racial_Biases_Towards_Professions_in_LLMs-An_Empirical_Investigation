The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s fingers, a meager comfort against the November chill that seeped through the drafty windows of Blackwood & Sons Accounting.  Rain lashed against the grime-coated panes, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her.  Amelia, a secretary of impeccable efficiency and perpetually suppressed sighs, stared at the overflowing inbox on her computer screen, a digital Everest of invoices, memos, and the dreaded “urgent” emails that always seemed to arrive just as she was about to leave for the day. The air hung thick with the scent of old paper, stale coffee, and the faint, unsettling aroma of desperation that clung to the firm like a persistent cough.  Outside, the city of Grimsborough hummed a low, melancholic tune, its skyscrapers piercing the twilight like skeletal fingers reaching for a perpetually overcast sky.  Tonight, however, the usual urban symphony felt amplified, each car horn a sharp stab of anxiety echoing the chaos on Amelia’s desk.
