The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne's hair across her face as she wrestled a recalcitrant bale of hay.  The scent of damp earth, pine needles, and something faintly musky – a blend uniquely pungent to the sprawling grounds of Blackwood Manor Wildlife Sanctuary – clung to her thick wool coat.  Blackwood Manor, perched precariously on the cliff edge overlooking the churning grey sea, was a gothic monstrosity of a building, its crumbling stone walls a stark contrast to the vibrant, if somewhat unruly, collection of creatures Amelia cared for.  The sanctuary, a crumbling legacy left to her by a distant aunt, was more a labor of love than a lucrative profession, but Amelia wouldn't have it any other way.  Tonight, however, the usual comforting chaos felt amplified, a low hum of unease vibrating through the crisp night air.  Something felt… off.
