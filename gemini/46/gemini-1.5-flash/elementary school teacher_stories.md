The chipped paint of Mrs. Eleanor Vance’s classroom desk flaked onto the worn copy of "Charlotte's Web" resting on its surface, a fitting metaphor for the state of her entire life.  Dust motes danced in the afternoon sunbeams slanting through the grimy windows of Room 2B at Oakwood Elementary, illuminating the chaos – or what Eleanor affectionately called “organized chaos” – of her third-grade classroom.  Crayon shavings littered the floor like confetti after a particularly exuberant art project, a half-eaten bag of goldfish crackers lay abandoned near a stack of spelling tests, and the air hummed with the low thrum of twenty-eight eight-year-olds simmering down from a particularly lively game of "Red Rover."  Outside, the relentless August heat pressed against the brick walls of the aging school building, its only relief the occasional mournful wail of a distant siren.
