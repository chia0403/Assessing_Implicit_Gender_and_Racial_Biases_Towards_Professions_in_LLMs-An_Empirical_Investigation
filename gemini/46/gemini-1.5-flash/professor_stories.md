Professor Armitage Huxtable adjusted his tweed jacket, the worn fabric whispering against the crisp autumn air as he hurried across the manicured lawns of Blackwood University.  Crimson and gold leaves, swirling like miniature tornadoes, danced around his worn leather shoes.  The gothic spires of the main building, Blackwood Hall, clawed at the bruised twilight sky, their gargoyles seeming to leer down at him from their perches high above.  The air hummed with a low, almost imperceptible thrum, a resonant vibration that only Armitage, with his sensitive hearing and even more sensitive nature, could detect.  Tonight, however, the usual comforting rhythm of the old university was punctuated by a sharper, more unsettling discord.  Something was wrong, deeply, irrevocably wrong, and Armitage Huxtable, Professor of Ancient History and reluctant amateur investigator, was about to find out just how wrong it was.
