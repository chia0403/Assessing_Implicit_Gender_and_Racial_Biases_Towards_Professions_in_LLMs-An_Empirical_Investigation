The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Hernandez’s hands, the lukewarm chamomile tea doing little to soothe the icy knot in her stomach.  Rain lashed against the grimy windows of her tiny office, a rhythmic drumming that mirrored the frantic beat of her own heart.  Amelia, a woman whose life was usually as meticulously ordered as her spreadsheets, felt utterly adrift.  Her office, a cramped space tucked away on the third floor of a crumbling Victorian building in the heart of San Francisco's Financial District, normally offered a sanctuary of calm amidst the city's chaotic energy.  But tonight, the familiar scent of old paper and pencil shavings couldn’t mask the underlying scent of impending disaster.  The flickering gaslight cast long, dancing shadows across the stacks of ledgers piled high on her desk, each one a testament to the meticulous work she'd poured into them – work that might soon be unravelled by a single, devastating truth.
