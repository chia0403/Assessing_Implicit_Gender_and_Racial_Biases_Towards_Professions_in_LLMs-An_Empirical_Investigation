The salt spray stung Aurelia Blackwood's face, a familiar kiss from the unforgiving Atlantic.  The wind, a howling banshee, whipped her auburn hair across her paint-splattered overalls as she wrestled with her easel, its legs threatening to succumb to the gusts.  Before her, the churning grey canvas of the ocean met the bruised purple and fiery orange of a bruised sunset, a spectacle both terrifying and breathtakingly beautiful.  Aurelia, her fingers numb with cold, struggled to capture the raw, untamed energy of the scene, the tempestuous dance between sky and sea mirroring the turmoil brewing within her own heart. She perched on the windswept cliffs of Cornwall, the jagged rocks a stark counterpoint to the soft curve of the distant coastline, a solitary figure battling both the elements and the elusive nature of artistic inspiration.  The air hung thick with the scent of brine and seaweed, a pungent perfume that permeated her very being, becoming almost a part of her palette.
