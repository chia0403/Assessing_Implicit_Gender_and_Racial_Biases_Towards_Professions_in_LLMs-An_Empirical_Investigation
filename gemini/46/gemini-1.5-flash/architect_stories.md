The salt spray stung Amelia Hernandez's face as she leaned against the railing of the ferry, the wind whipping her dark hair across her cheek.  Below, the churning grey waters of the Puget Sound separated Bainbridge Island from the Seattle skyline, a jagged silhouette against a bruised, twilight sky.  The city, normally a breathtaking display of glass and steel, looked almost hostile tonight, its lights flickering like the nervous pulse of a giant, slumbering beast. Amelia, a renowned architect known for her innovative and sustainable designs, usually found solace in the city's dynamic energy.  Tonight, however, the city's vibrant pulse felt more like a thrumming pressure against her chest, a premonition mirroring the unsettled feeling that had plagued her since receiving the enigmatic commission.  The commission for the Blackwood Project.
