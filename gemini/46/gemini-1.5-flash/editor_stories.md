The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia “Mia” Holloway’s hands, the lukewarm tea doing little to soothe the icy dread creeping up her spine.  Rain lashed against the tall, gothic windows of her office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her.  The gargoyles perched on the rooftop of Blackwood Publishing House leered down, their stone faces seemingly mirroring her own grim expression.  Blackwood, a Victorian monstrosity of dark brick and echoing hallways, hunched over the cobbled street like a brooding giant, its imposing presence a constant reminder of the weight of her responsibility.  Tonight, that weight felt heavier than ever.  The manuscript lay open on her desk, its pages illuminated by the flickering gaslight, a stark contrast to the shadowy corners of the room where centuries of forgotten stories seemed to whisper secrets.  The deadline loomed, a monstrous shadow in itself, and Mia knew that this was more than just another edit; it was a fight for survival, both hers and the book's.
