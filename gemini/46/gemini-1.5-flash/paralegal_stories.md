The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Hernandez’s hands, a meager comfort against the November chill that seeped through the drafty windows of the law office.  Rain lashed against the panes, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her.  Grey light, weak and watery, barely penetrated the gloom of the shared office space, illuminating dust motes dancing in the stale air.  The scent of old paper, cheap coffee, and lingering desperation clung to everything like a shroud.  Amelia, a paralegal with a reputation for sharp eyes and an even sharper tongue, pushed aside the mountain of case files threatening to engulf her desk.  Outside, the city of San Francisco howled; inside, the quiet hum of the overworked office air conditioner was the only sound louder than the frantic thump-thump-thump of her own heart.  Tonight, Amelia Hernandez wasn't just facing another mountain of paperwork – she was facing a truth that could shatter her carefully constructed world.
