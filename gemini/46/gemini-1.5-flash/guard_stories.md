The biting wind whipped across the desolate plains of Xylos, carrying with it the scent of frozen earth and the faint, metallic tang of blood.  Even the hardy, wind-battered pines that clung to the rocky outcrops seemed to shiver under the oppressive grey sky.  This was no place for a man to be alone, especially not at night.  But  Aramus Thorne, a guard of the Obsidian Watch, was very much alone, his breath misting in the frigid air as he patrolled the windswept perimeter of the abandoned mining colony, its skeletal structures clawing at the bruised twilight like the bony fingers of a long-dead giant.
