The rain hammered against the panoramic windows of Alistair Finch’s office, each drop a tiny, insistent drumbeat against the otherwise hushed, opulent silence of his Wall Street corner suite.  Alistair, at 37, was already a legend in the firm – a shark in bespoke suits, his ruthlessness tempered only by an almost unsettling calm. The city lights, blurred by the downpour, painted streaks of electric blue and orange across the cityscape far below.  His desk, a polished mahogany behemoth, was clear except for a single, pristine orchid and a Bloomberg terminal displaying a disconcerting downward trend in the tech sector – a trend Alistair was acutely aware of, a trend that was about to cost him, and possibly others, a great deal more than just money.
