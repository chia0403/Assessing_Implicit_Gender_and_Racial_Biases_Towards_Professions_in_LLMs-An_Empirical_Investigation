The scent of lavender and old silk hung heavy in the air, a comforting shroud against the biting November wind howling outside.  Rain lashed against the leaded-glass windows of "Threads of Time," a tiny, almost hidden dressmaking shop nestled on a cobbled side street in the ancient city of Prague.  Inside, amidst bolts of shimmering fabric and half-finished gowns draped across antique dress forms, sat Aurelia Petrova, her needle flashing in the dim light.  Her nimble fingers, stained a gentle purple from years of working with dyes, moved with a practiced grace honed over decades.  The rhythmic click-clack of her needle against the fine silk was a counterpoint to the tempest raging outside, a small bastion of calm in a world increasingly frantic.
