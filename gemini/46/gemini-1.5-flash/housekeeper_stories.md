The chipped porcelain sink groaned under the weight of Elara Vance's scrubbing.  Rain lashed against the leaded glass windows of Blackwood Manor, each gust a mournful sigh echoing the desolate feeling that had settled deep in Elara's bones.  Blackwood Manor itself, perched precariously on a windswept cliff overlooking the churning grey expanse of the North Sea, seemed to hold its breath, waiting for the storm to swallow it whole.  Its once-proud façade, now scarred by time and neglect, was a reflection of the family it housed: the eccentric, reclusive Blackwood clan, shrouded in whispers and shadowed by a history as stormy as the sea outside. Elara, her hands chapped and raw from years of battling grime and decay, was the only constant in their chaotic lives – a silent observer, a keeper of secrets, and the only one who truly understood the manor's crumbling heart.
