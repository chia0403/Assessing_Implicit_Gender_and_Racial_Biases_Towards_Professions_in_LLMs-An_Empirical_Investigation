Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of Miss Eleanor Vance's classroom, a relentless percussion that echoed the frantic rhythm of her thoughts.  The fluorescent lights flickered, casting long, dancing shadows across the rows of empty desks.  It was the Friday before spring break, a day usually buzzing with the final flurry of assignments and whispered goodbyes.  Instead, the cavernous room felt suffocatingly still, a stark contrast to the usual boisterous energy.  Dust motes danced in the pale light, mirroring the swirling anxieties in Miss Vance's chest.  She was a high school English teacher, a woman of quiet intensity, whose meticulously organized classroom was tonight a battlefield of unanswered questions and unspoken fears.  The rhythmic drumming of the rain was the only constant.
