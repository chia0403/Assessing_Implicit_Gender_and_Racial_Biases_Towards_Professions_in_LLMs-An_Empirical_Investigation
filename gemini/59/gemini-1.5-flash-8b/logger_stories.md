The biting wind whipped around Elias Thorne, a veteran logger, stinging his cheeks and clawing at the ragged edges of his worn flannel shirt.  He squinted against the swirling snow, the timber-laden peaks of the Cascade Mountains a ghostly white blur against the bruised twilight sky.  The air, thick with the scent of pine and damp earth, carried the faint, chilling whisper of the approaching blizzard. Elias, his calloused hands gripping the worn leather of his axe sheath, stood alone at the edge of the timberline, the silence of the forest broken only by the frantic screech of unseen ravens.  This was his domain, the unforgiving wilderness of the Washington State mountains, a place where the trees stood taller than any man's ambition, and where survival was a daily, brutal dance with the elements.  His small, hand-hewn cabin, nestled deep within the trees, was a mere speck against the encroaching storm, a fortress against the world's relentless fury.
