The biting London wind whipped Amelia Cavendish's crimson coat around her legs as she surveyed the sprawling, grey expanse of Regent's Park.  Fog, thick and clinging, hung low, obscuring the towering Georgian houses that lined the park's perimeter.  The only discernible light came from the gas lamps, casting long, skeletal shadows that danced with the mist.  It was a night perfect for whispers and secrets, and Amelia, concierge at the opulent Grosvenor House, was well acquainted with both.

The house, a monolithic edifice of stone and glass, shimmered faintly in the gloom.  Inside, the grand halls echoed with the low murmur of conversation and the clinking of crystal.  But Amelia, perched on a low stone wall, felt a profound sense of quiet isolation.  Tonight, however, was different.  Tonight, the whispers weren't just whispers in the fog; they were demands.
