The fluorescent hum of the law firm, Perkins, Powell, and Prescott, grated on Amelia "Amy" Hawthorne's nerves.  Every keystroke echoed in the cavernous space, a rhythmic counterpoint to the low murmur of hushed conversations and the occasional, sharp intake of breath from someone deep in legal paperwork.  Amy, a paralegal with a penchant for meticulous organization, sat hunched over her laptop, the sterile white walls pressing in around her.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of afternoon sunlight piercing the gloom of the high-ceilinged office, illuminating the scattered files and overflowing recycling bins.  It was a typical Wednesday in the sprawling downtown law firm, a day that promised to be filled with the same relentless grind of legal minutiae.  Her meticulously maintained schedule, meticulously laid out, promised nothing extraordinary. But today felt different. Something was stirring, beneath the surface of the routine.
