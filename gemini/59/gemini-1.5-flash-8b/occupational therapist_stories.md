The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Petrova's hands, the faint scent of Earl Grey clinging to the air.  Sunlight, filtered through the dusty panes of the window overlooking the bustling, chaotic market square, cast long shadows across the small, cluttered office.  Amelia, Occupational Therapist at the Community Rehabilitation Centre, leaned back in her worn leather chair, a sigh escaping her lips.  Another day, another flurry of paperwork, another patient with a story as complex and layered as the tapestry woven in the market stalls below.  The rhythmic clatter of carts and the hawkers' cries, usually a vibrant soundtrack to her day, felt like a cacophony today.  A restless energy pulsed beneath the surface of the quiet, a nervous tremor that mirrored the anxiety she felt about the latest referral.
