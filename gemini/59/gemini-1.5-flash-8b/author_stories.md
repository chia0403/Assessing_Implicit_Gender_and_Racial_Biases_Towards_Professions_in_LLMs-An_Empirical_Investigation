Rain hammered against the leaded windows of Amelia Blackwood’s study, a relentless percussion that mirrored the frantic rhythm of her thoughts.  The scent of damp earth and aged paper hung thick in the air, a familiar comfort in the suffocating silence of the Blackwood Manor library.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of pale sunlight slicing through the gloom, illuminating a towering stack of notebooks, each one a testament to Amelia’s relentless pursuit of the perfect sentence, the elusive narrative voice.  Amelia Blackwood, author of the critically acclaimed *Echoes of the Obsidian Heart*, was trapped in a vortex of writer’s block, a cruel mistress who'd seized her muse and left her stranded in a sea of blank pages.  The once vibrant hues of her imagination had dulled to a muted grey, mirroring the dreary November sky outside.
