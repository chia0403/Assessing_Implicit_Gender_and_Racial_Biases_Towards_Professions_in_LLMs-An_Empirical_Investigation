The chipped paint of the "Welcome to Whispering Pines Elementary" sign, faded a sickly yellow-green, barely reflected the bruised twilight sky.  Rain, the persistent, unwelcome soundtrack of a late September evening, drummed a relentless rhythm against the corrugated metal awning.  Inside, nestled amongst the overflowing bins of misplaced art projects and the faint smell of old glue and damp earth,  Ms.  Elara Hawthorne surveyed the classroom.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of light filtering through the grimy window, highlighting the scattered remnants of a day’s lessons.  Fifth grade.  A symphony of scraped knees, whispered secrets, and the occasional eruption of boisterous laughter.  It had been a particularly trying day; a day of misbehaving monsters, fractured friendships, and a paper airplane that had narrowly missed the principal’s prized geranium.  The air was thick with unspoken anxieties, the kind that clung to the walls like mildew.  Tonight, though, the silence was heavy, punctuated only by the rhythmic drip-drip-drip of the leaky faucet in the adjacent bathroom.
