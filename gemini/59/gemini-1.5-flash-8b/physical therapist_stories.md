The chipped paint of the examination table reflected the harsh fluorescent light, a stark contrast to the muted, almost grey palette of the physiotherapy clinic.  Rain hammered against the single, small window, a relentless rhythm that mirrored the rhythmic thrum of Amelia Hayes's thoughts.  Amelia, a physical therapist with a name whispered in hushed tones amongst her colleagues for her meticulous approach and unwavering empathy, stared at the x-ray.  A fractured ulna.  A simple break, the report stated.  Simple.  But the patient, a young woman named Elara, hadn't just broken her arm.  She'd broken something far more fragile.


**Setting:**  A small, unassuming physiotherapy clinic nestled in the shadow of a towering, industrial complex on the outskirts of a bustling city.  The building, painted a tired cream, struggled to maintain its luster despite the constant effort of the perpetually-tired cleaning crew.  The air, heavy with the scent of antiseptic and lingering dampness, suggested a constant struggle against the city's relentless urban sprawl.
