The aroma of simmering thyme and garlic hung heavy in the air, a fragrant curtain shielding the chipped paint of the "Golden Spoon" kitchen.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of afternoon sun slicing through the grime-streaked window.  Amelia Josephine "Amy" Cartwright, her apron stained a deep, almost mahogany, brown from the day's hearty stew, meticulously checked the bubbling cauldron.  Twenty-three years old, with eyes that held the same fiery glint as the hearth's blaze, she judged the stew's readiness not by time but by the subtle shift in its colour, the perfect melding of rich broth and browned vegetables.  The Golden Spoon, a small, ramshackle inn nestled on the edge of the Whispering Woods, was her sanctuary, and her kitchen her battlefield.
