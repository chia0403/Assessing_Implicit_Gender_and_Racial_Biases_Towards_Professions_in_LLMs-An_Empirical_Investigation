The biting wind, a relentless, icy shard, whipped across the manicured lawns of Blackwood Manor, stinging Amelia Featherstonehaugh's cheeks.  The wrought-iron gates, normally gleaming with a polished sheen, were now coated in a fine layer of frost, their intricate designs obscured by a hazy, white veil.  Amelia, wrapped in a thick wool scarf that muffled her breath into a soft, white mist, wrestled with a stubborn patch of frozen ground near the rose garden.  The air, thick with the scent of pine and damp earth, was punctuated by the occasional, sharp caw of a crow circling overhead.  She was the groundskeeper for Blackwood Manor, a sprawling estate shrouded in whispered rumours and shrouded even deeper in the gloom of a long-forgotten tragedy.
