The flickering neon sign of the "Cosmic Chatterbox" cast a sickly yellow glow across Elias Thorne's grease-stained overalls.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of moonlight slicing through the grime-coated window of the cramped workshop.  Inside, the air hung thick with the smell of solder and ozone, a constant hum of static a soundtrack to Elias's meticulous work.  His calloused fingers, expert at coaxing whispers from the silent ether, painstakingly adjusted a tuning knob on a battered radio, its wooden casing worn smooth by decades of whispered secrets. The Cosmic Chatterbox, a legendary radio shop in the heart of the sprawling, forgotten port city of Aethelgard, was his life, his sanctuary, and his battlefield against the encroaching silence of the dying age of electronics.  Elias, a radio technician of renown, or perhaps infamy, depending on who you asked, was about to make a discovery that would shake the foundations of Aethelgard, and perhaps the world.
