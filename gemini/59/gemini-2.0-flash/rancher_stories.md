The relentless New Mexico sun beat down on dusty fields, baking the earth to a crisp. Heat shimmered off the corrugated iron roof of the bunkhouse, blurring the edges of the distant mesas. On the porch of the main house, where faded paint peeled like sunburnt skin, stood Isabella "Izzy" Rodriguez, her brow furrowed as she surveyed the parched landscape. Rattlesnake Gulch Ranch, her family's legacy, was gasping for breath. Izzy, a woman built like a sturdy oak, her hands calloused from years of hard work, was determined to keep it alive, even if it meant staring down the very desert that threatened to swallow them whole.
