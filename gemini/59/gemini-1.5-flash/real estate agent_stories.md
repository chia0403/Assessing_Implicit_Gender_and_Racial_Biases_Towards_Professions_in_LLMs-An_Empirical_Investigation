The salt spray kissed Amelia "Mia" Kensington's face as she wrestled the stubborn “For Sale” sign into the unforgiving wind.  The wind, a constant companion on this windswept promontory overlooking the churning grey Atlantic, howled a mournful tune that mirrored the mood of the property itself – a crumbling Victorian mansion named Seacliff Manor, perched precariously on the cliff's edge.  Seacliff's once-proud façade was now a patchwork of decaying paint and shattered windows, its skeletal frame clawing at the stormy sky like a desperate hand.  The air hung heavy with the scent of brine and decay, a stark contrast to the usually vibrant, sun-drenched coastal town of Port Blossom, nestled a mile or so down the winding, cliff-hugging road.  This wasn't Mia's typical beachfront bungalow; this was a challenge, a behemoth of a project that smelled suspiciously of secrets, and she, Amelia Kensington, was ready to unravel them.
