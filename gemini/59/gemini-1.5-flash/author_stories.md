The chipped paint of the windowsill was cold against Amelia Katherine Blackwood’s cheek.  October’s relentless rain hammered against the panes of her writing room, a relentless rhythm mimicking the frantic beat of her own heart.  The air hung thick with the scent of damp earth and woodsmoke, a familiar aroma that usually soothed her, but tonight, it only served to amplify the suffocating claustrophobia creeping in from the storm and, she suspected, from within. Amelia, better known in literary circles as simply “K. Blackwood,” stared out at the swirling grey of the Cornish coastline, the wild Atlantic a churning, unforgiving beast mirroring the turmoil in her own creative well.  Her quaint, ivy-covered cottage, usually a sanctuary, felt like a prison tonight, its walls closing in on her as she wrestled with the crippling blankness of a writer’s block far more severe than any she’d ever experienced.  The deadline loomed, a monstrous shadow hanging over the flickering candlelight on her cluttered desk.
