The dawn mist clung to the manicured lawns of Willow Creek Estates like a shroud, obscuring the meticulously placed rose bushes and sculpted hedges until the sun, a hesitant orb peeking over the distant pines, finally burned it away.  Dew-kissed spiderwebs shimmered like tiny, fragile diamonds, catching the first rays of light.  Amelia Jean Blackwood, owner and sole operator of Blackwood Botanicals, sighed, the chill air a welcome contrast to the simmering frustration building within her.  Her breath plumed white against the pre-dawn grey as she surveyed her day’s work – a sprawling, impeccably designed landscape awaiting her touch, a landscape that held a secret, buried somewhere beneath the perfectly arranged azaleas and the meticulously trimmed boxwoods.
