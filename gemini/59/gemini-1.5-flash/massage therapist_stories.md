The aroma of lavender and sandalwood hung heavy in the air, a comforting counterpoint to the relentless drumming of rain against the leaded-glass windows of Serenity Now Massage Therapy.  Water cascaded down the ivy-covered brick walls of the quaint Victorian building, blurring the already indistinct gaslight glow of the cobblestone street outside.  Inside, however, the only visible light emanated from a single Himalayan salt lamp, casting a warm, amber hue across the meticulously clean treatment room.  Eleanor Vance, her fingers still faintly scented with the geranium-infused massage oil she'd used on her last client, meticulously folded a fresh set of linen sheets.  The rhythmic squeak of her leather-soled slippers on the polished wooden floor was the only sound besides the rain, a soothing soundtrack to the quiet solitude she craved after a long day of untangling the knots of other people's lives.
