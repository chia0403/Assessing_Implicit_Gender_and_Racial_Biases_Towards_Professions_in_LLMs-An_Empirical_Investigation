The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Mia” Hernandez’s hair across her face as she navigated the crumbling steps of the Riverbend Housing Projects.  The air, thick with the smell of woodsmoke and something indefinably acrid, clung to the back of her throat.  Above, the skeletal branches of bare sycamores clawed at the bruised twilight sky, their silhouettes stark against the bruised orange glow of the setting sun.  Riverbend wasn't just a housing project; it was a labyrinth of despair, a concrete jungle where hope withered and shadows held secrets heavier than any brick.  Mia, a probation officer with the City of Oakland, was intimately familiar with those secrets.  Tonight, she was hunting for one in particular, a whisper of a rumor that had slithered its way into her already overflowing caseload, a rumor about a missing girl named Lily Carter, and a potential violation of parole by someone Mia knew all too well – her client,  Marcus “Mac” Riley.
