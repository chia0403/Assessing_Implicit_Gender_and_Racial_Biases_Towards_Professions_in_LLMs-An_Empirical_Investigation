The midday sun beat down on the cracked asphalt of Oakhaven’s Main Street, baking the already stifling air.  Cicadas buzzed a relentless, high-pitched symphony, their song a soundtrack to Dr. Eleanor Vance’s simmering frustration.  Eleanor, a registered dietician with a reputation for both her cutting-edge nutritional plans and her equally sharp tongue, adjusted the strap of her worn leather satchel, the weight of client files a physical manifestation of her mounting anxieties.  Oakhaven, nestled in the sun-drenched heart of California’s Central Valley, was picturesque, undeniably so. But its charm was a thin veneer, barely concealing a community grappling with a devastating surge in obesity and related illnesses.  Today, Eleanor was facing her most challenging case yet:  the enigmatic and increasingly unwell mayor, a man whose political power seemed to be inversely proportional to his commitment to his own health.  The air hung heavy, not just with heat, but with the unspoken weight of a town teetering on the precipice of a health crisis.
