The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Amy” Hernandez’s dark hair across her face as she hurried down Bleecker Street, the scent of roasting chestnuts and damp asphalt battling for dominance.  Rain, a fine, persistent drizzle, clung to her coat, mirroring the persistent drizzle of anxieties currently clogging her brain. Amy, a systems analyst for a mid-sized financial firm called Sterling & Thorne, hadn't slept properly in three days.  The looming deadline for the new client-facing software, codenamed “Project Nightingale,” was a guillotine hanging over her head, its blade sharpened by the ever-present threat of a catastrophic system failure.  The flickering neon signs of Greenwich Village, usually a comforting kaleidoscope of colour, felt harsh and accusing tonight, each one a stark reminder of the pressure mounting within her.
