The fluorescent lights of the Zenith Corporation hummed a monotonous tune, a counterpoint to the rhythmic tap-tap-tap of Penelope Anne Featherstonehaugh's perfectly manicured fingernails against the polished mahogany of her desk.  Penelope, or Penny as she preferred (though few dared use it outside of her immediate family), surveyed her kingdom: a small, impeccably organized space nestled in the hushed lobby of a gleaming skyscraper that pierced the perpetually overcast London sky.  The air smelled faintly of expensive coffee and simmering resentment, a scent as familiar to her as her own reflection in the highly polished brass nameplate that read, in elegant script:  "Penelope Featherstonehaugh - Reception."  Outside, the relentless London rain hammered against the vast panoramic windows, mirroring the storm brewing inside Zenith Corporation, a storm Penny knew, with chilling certainty, she was about to be caught in the middle of.
