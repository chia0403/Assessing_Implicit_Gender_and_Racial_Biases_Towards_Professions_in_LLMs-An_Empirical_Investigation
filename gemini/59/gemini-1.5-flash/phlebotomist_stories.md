The fluorescent lights of the Meadowbrook Medical Center hummed a monotonous tune, a counterpoint to the frantic beeping of heart monitors echoing down the sterile hallway.  The air, thick with the scent of antiseptic and something faintly floral – probably the desperate attempt at cheerfulness via air freshener – clung to everything, including the crisp white coat worn by Elias Thorne, a phlebotomist whose patience was currently being tested to its absolute limit.  It was 7:17 AM, officially seventeen minutes past the time Elias had expected to be enjoying his second cup of coffee in the blessed quiet of his own kitchen, instead of facing the chaotic pre-dawn rush of the outpatient blood draw clinic.  Outside, the pre-dawn sky was a bruised purple, a stark contrast to the clinical brightness of the room where Elias stood, already bracing himself for the day's inevitable onslaught of needle-phobic patients and overflowing requisition slips.
