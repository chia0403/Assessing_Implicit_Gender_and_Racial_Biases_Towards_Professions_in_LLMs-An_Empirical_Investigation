The biting November wind whipped Amelia Katherine Davies's auburn braid across her face as she hurried down Elm Street, the scent of woodsmoke and damp leaves clinging to the chill air.  Oakhaven Elementary School loomed ahead, a squat, brick behemoth hunched against the gathering twilight.  Its windows, glowing a warm amber against the darkening sky, offered a promise of warmth and the comforting chaos of twenty-five five-year-olds.  Amelia shivered, pulling her worn wool scarf tighter.  It wasn't just the cold that sent a tremor through her; it was the knot of apprehension tightening in her stomach.  Tonight was parent-teacher night, and this year felt different, heavier than usual.  The whispers about little Timmy Miller's increasingly erratic behavior had reached even her ears, and the shadow of something unspoken hung over the usually jovial atmosphere of Room 12.
