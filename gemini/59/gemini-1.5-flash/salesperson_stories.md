The air hung thick and humid, a clinging blanket woven from the scent of frying plantains and diesel fumes.  Sweat beaded on the forehead of Amelia Hernandez, the dampness clinging to the crisp white collar of her blouse like a stubborn shadow.  She adjusted her uncomfortable, yet meticulously chosen, power suit – a pale lilac that clashed spectacularly with the vibrant chaos of the Santo Domingo marketplace.  The cacophony of hawkers' cries, the insistent bleating of goats, and the rhythmic clang of a blacksmith's hammer formed a relentless soundtrack to her day.  Amelia, senior account executive at 'Sol y Mar Imports,' was on a mission – and it was going to take more than a little heat and noise to deter her.  Her briefcase, surprisingly lightweight despite its professional demeanor, contained the future of her quarterly bonus, and potentially, her career.
