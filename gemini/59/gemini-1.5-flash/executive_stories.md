The mahogany desk, polished to a mirror sheen, reflected Amelia Katherine Holloway's grim expression back at her tenfold.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of her 47th-floor office, blurring the city lights into a hazy, impressionistic painting of relentless grey and sickly yellow.  Below, the relentless thrum of New York City – a symphony of sirens, car horns, and the muffled roar of a million hurried footsteps – seemed to mirror the turmoil churning within her.  Amelia, Executive Vice President of Holloway & Sons, Inc.,  had spent the last twelve hours staring at the same damning email, a digital viper coiled ready to strike at the very heart of her family's legacy.  The scent of expensive, brooding incense – sandalwood and something darker, almost metallic – did little to soothe her frayed nerves. Tonight, the empire her grandfather had built stood precariously balanced on the edge of a knife.
