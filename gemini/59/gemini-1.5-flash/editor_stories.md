The rain hammered against the attic windowpanes of Blackwood Manor, each drop a tiny percussionist in the symphony of the storm.  Dust motes, illuminated by the single bare bulb hanging precariously from the ceiling, danced in the swirling air, mirroring the chaos in Amelia “Mia” Hawthorne’s life.  Mia, a woman whose sharp wit was only surpassed by her even sharper eye for a poorly constructed sentence, slumped in a creaky armchair, surrounded by piles of manuscripts that threatened to engulf her entirely. The air hung heavy with the scent of aged paper and damp earth, a peculiar perfume only an attic could provide.  Blackwood Manor itself, a gothic monstrosity perched precariously on a windswept cliff overlooking the turbulent North Sea, seemed to sigh with the wind, a silent accomplice to the storm raging both outside and within Mia’s cluttered sanctuary.  The deadline for the anthology, *Whispers from the Deep*, loomed, and Mia was drowning in a sea of purple prose and grammatical atrocities.
