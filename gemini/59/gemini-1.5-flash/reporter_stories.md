The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Mia” Holloway’s hair across her face, stinging her cheeks a fiery red.  She tugged her worn wool coat tighter, the collar scratching against her already chapped skin. Rain, a relentless drizzle that had been falling since dawn, plastered her dark curls to her forehead.  The flickering neon sign of the Rusty Mug tavern cast a sickly yellow glow on the slick cobblestones of Hemlock Alley, barely illuminating the overflowing bins and the shadowed doorways that whispered of secrets best left undisturbed.  This wasn't exactly the picturesque postcard view of the quaint coastal town of Port Blossom that the tourist brochures boasted, but Mia Holloway, investigative reporter for the *Port Blossom Gazette*, knew the true beauty – and the ugliness – lay hidden beneath the surface. Tonight, she suspected, the ugliness was about to rise.
