The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s hair across her face as she hurried down the cobbled street, the scent of woodsmoke and brine heavy in the air.  Salt spray, flung from the churning grey sea just beyond the cliff edge, kissed her cheeks.  Above, the sky was a bruised purple, mirroring the anxieties churning in her own stomach.  Amelia, a physician’s assistant at the small, wind-battered clinic perched precariously on the edge of Port Blossom, wasn’t accustomed to this level of unease.  Usually, her worries revolved around dwindling supplies of penicillin or the erratic temper of old Mr. Fitzwilliam, not the frantic, blood-soaked message clutched in her gloved hand.  The message, scrawled in a shaky hand, promised a night she wouldn't soon forget, and not for the reasons associated with a quiet coastal town.
