The smell of old paper and dust motes dancing in the single shaft of afternoon sunlight were the familiar companions of Elara Vance, bookkeeper extraordinaire.  Elara, a woman whose life revolved around meticulously balanced ledgers and the satisfying *snap* of a perfectly filed receipt, sat in her tiny, cluttered office above Mrs. Periwinkle’s Emporium of Curiosities.  The emporium itself, a warren of dusty shelves overflowing with taxidermied badgers, chipped porcelain dolls, and ominously silent music boxes, was a sensory assault; but Elara's office, tucked away in the attic, was her sanctuary – a haven of order in a world of chaotic charm.  Rain lashed against the single, grimy windowpane, a rhythmic counterpoint to the scratching of her pen across the ledger page.  Outside, the cobblestones of Ashwood Lane glistened, reflecting the bruised purple of the twilight sky, a sky that mirrored the increasingly unsettling entries in Mrs. Periwinkle’s accounts.
