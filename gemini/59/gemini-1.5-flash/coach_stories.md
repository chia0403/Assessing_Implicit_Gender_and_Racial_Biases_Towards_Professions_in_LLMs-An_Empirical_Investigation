The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Amy” Hawthorne’s hair across her face as she surveyed the desolate soccer field.  Fallen leaves, the color of rust and decay, clung stubbornly to the damp grass, mirroring the gnawing anxiety in her gut.  Floodlights hummed a mournful tune, their pale beams struggling to pierce the encroaching darkness that mirrored the looming shadow of the upcoming championship game.  The air hung thick with the smell of damp earth and woodsmoke drifting from the distant houses clinging to the edge of the bleak, windswept moorland.  This wasn't the manicured perfection of the elite academies Amy had coached at in the past; this was Ashwood United, a ragtag team of teenagers fueled by grit and a desperate hunger for victory – and Amy, their beleaguered but fiercely loyal coach, was their only hope.
