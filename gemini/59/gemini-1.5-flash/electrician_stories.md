The biting November wind howled a mournful dirge through the skeletal branches of the ancient oaks lining Hemlock Lane, rattling the loose windowpanes of the dilapidated Victorian mansion before Elias Thorne.  Elias, a man whose hands were as calloused as the worn leather of his tool belt, adjusted his worn tweed cap, the brim pulled low against the icy drizzle.  The flickering gaslight in his hand cast long, skeletal shadows that danced with the wind-whipped leaves.  Hemlock Manor, they called it – a place whispered about in hushed tones by the locals, a place steeped in history and, according to rumour, something far more sinister.  Elias, however, cared little for local legends.  He was an electrician, not an exorcist, and his only concern was getting the antiquated wiring of this decaying behemoth up to code – or at least functional enough to satisfy his demanding client, the reclusive Ms. Agatha Blackwood.
