The salt spray stung Elara Vance’s face, a familiar kiss from the unforgiving Atlantic.  She gripped her battered Leica M6, its worn leather cool against her gloved hand, and squinted through the viewfinder.  The pre-dawn light, a bruised purple bleeding into a hesitant grey, painted the jagged cliffs of Cornwall in shades of melancholic beauty.  Below, the churning sea roared a ceaseless symphony, its whitecaps frothing against the black rocks like the teeth of some ancient beast.  Elara, a freelance photographer known for her evocative landscapes and haunting seascapes, had come to this remote stretch of coast seeking something elusive, something beyond the breathtaking scenery itself – a reflection of the storm raging within her own soul.  The wind whipped her thick auburn hair across her face, a chaotic contrast to the meticulous precision she employed behind the lens. This was her sanctuary, this wild, untamed corner of the world; a place where she hoped to find both solace and the perfect shot.
