The scent of burnt sugar and ozone hung heavy in the air, a peculiar perfume only a barbershop could generate.  Dust motes, illuminated by the single, bare bulb swinging above the cracked linoleum floor, danced in the late afternoon sun slicing through the grimy window of "Shear Perfection."  This wasn't the sleek, chrome-and-glass affair one might find downtown; this was a relic, a testament to a bygone era, tucked away on a forgotten corner of Maple Street.  Inside, amidst the faded posters of forgotten rock stars and the collection of chipped mugs lining the shelves, sat Elias Thorne, his scissors poised mid-snip above a customer's head, his brow furrowed in intense concentration.  The whirring of his ancient clippers provided a rhythmic counterpoint to the low hum of the city outside, a city Elias had long ago decided to leave to its own chaotic devices.
