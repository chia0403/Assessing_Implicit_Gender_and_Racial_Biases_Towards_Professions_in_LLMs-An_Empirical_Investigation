The humid air hung heavy, thick with the scent of frangipani and impending rain.  Cicadas buzzed a relentless, rhythmic chorus from the emerald canopy overhead, their song a counterpoint to the rhythmic slap of Amelia Hernandez’s sandals against the ancient cobblestones.  Amelia, a woman whose smile lines spoke of years spent under the relentless Caribbean sun, adjusted the brim of her straw hat, her eyes scanning the small, expectant group gathered before her.  The crumbling ochre walls of the San Juan Gate, a testament to centuries of history, formed a dramatic backdrop to their impending journey through Old San Juan, a journey she was about to expertly – and hopefully entertainingly – orchestrate.
