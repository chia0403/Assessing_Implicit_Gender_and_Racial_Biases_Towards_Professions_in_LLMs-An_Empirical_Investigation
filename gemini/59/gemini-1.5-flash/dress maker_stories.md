The scent of lavender and old linen hung heavy in the air, a comforting blanket against the biting November wind rattling the windowpanes of  "Stitches & Dreams," Aurelia  Rosemont's atelier.  Rain lashed against the leaded glass, blurring the gaslight-illuminated cobblestones of  Cobblestone Lane in the heart of  York, England.  Inside, Aurelia, her nimble fingers flying over a cascade of emerald silk, hummed a melancholic tune, the rhythmic snip of her shears a counterpoint to the storm raging outside.  Her workshop, a chaotic haven of fabrics – bolts of velvet, lace, and brocade piled high, overflowing onto every available surface – was a reflection of her own vibrant, if slightly cluttered, spirit.
