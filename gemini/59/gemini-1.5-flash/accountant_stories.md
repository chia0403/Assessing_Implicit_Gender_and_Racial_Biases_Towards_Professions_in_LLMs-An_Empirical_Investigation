The fluorescent lights of the Blackwood & Finch accounting firm hummed a monotonous tune, a counterpoint to the staccato click-clack of Amelia Jean Hawthorne's keyboard.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of her 27th-floor office, blurring the already indistinct cityscape of Chicago into a hazy, grey watercolor.  Amelia, a woman whose neatly-braided auburn hair mirrored the precision of her spreadsheets, sighed, the sound barely audible above the rhythmic drumming of the storm.  The deadline for the quarterly reports loomed, a monolithic pressure that threatened to crush her under its weight.  Outside, the wind howled like a banshee, mirroring the turmoil brewing within her own meticulously ordered world.  Tonight, however, it wasn't just the impending deadline that unsettled her. Tonight, a cryptic, anonymous package had arrived, addressed simply to "A.J. Hawthorne, Knower of Secrets."
