The bell above the door of "Petal & Plume" tinkled, a fragile counterpoint to the rumble of a passing lorry on cobbled Steep Street. Rain lashed against the large front window, blurring the vibrant colours of the tulips and hyacinths displayed within. Inside, Elsie Amelia Hawthorne, her hands dusted with pollen, hummed along to a scratchy Billie Holiday record as she arranged a cascade of ivory roses for a society wedding. Petal & Plume, nestled in the heart of Hayford-on-Wye, a small, book-loving town tucked away in the Welsh Marches, was Elsie's sanctuary, a riot of fragrant blooms and verdant foliage in stark contrast to the grey skies that frequently cloaked the surrounding hills.  The air within was thick with the perfume of lilies and damp earth, a comforting, familiar scent that always seemed to chase away Elsie’s lingering anxieties.  This afternoon, however, a different kind of unease prickled at the edges of her calm.  The bell had tinkled, yet no customer had appeared…
