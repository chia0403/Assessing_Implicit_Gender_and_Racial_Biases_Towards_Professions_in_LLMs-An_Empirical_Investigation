The biting November wind whipped off the Puget Sound, carrying the scent of brine and pine needles, and rattling the mismatched windows of The Salty Spoon. Inside, the air was thick with the aroma of simmering cioppino and the nervous energy of Eleanor Ainsworth, its head chef and sole proprietor. Eleanor, with her fiery red hair perpetually escaping its bun and flour dusting her chapped cheeks, paced the tiny galley kitchen.  The Salty Spoon, nestled in the heart of the sleepy, seaside town of Port Blossom, Washington, was her life's work, a testament to years of scrimping, saving, and perfecting her grandmother's secret clam chowder recipe. Tonight, however, wasn't about chowder. Tonight was the night a renowned food critic, the notoriously sharp-tongued Bartholomew Crane, was coming to dinner.  And Eleanor, despite all her bravado and culinary prowess, was terrified.
