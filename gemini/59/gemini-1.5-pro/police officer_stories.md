The relentless Michigan wind whipped at Detective Isabella "Izzy" Diaz’s trench coat, plastering it against her legs as she stood on the edge of Lake St. Clair.  Ice floes, like shattered glass, jostled against the shoreline, the sound a brittle counterpoint to the mournful cry of gulls overhead.  The sky, a bruised purple-grey, threatened snow.  It was late February in Port Huron, a time of year when the picturesque beauty of the Great Lakes was overshadowed by a bone-chilling dampness that seeped into everything – your clothes, your bones, your very soul.  This bleak landscape served as a fitting backdrop for the scene before her:  a lone figure, slumped against a weathered breakwater, draped in a tarp that billowed in the wind like a discarded sail.
