The sawdust swirled around Elias Thorne like a miniature galaxy, illuminated by the single bare bulb hanging precariously from the rafters of his workshop.  The air, thick with the scent of pine and cedar, clung to his clothes and hair, a familiar embrace. Outside, the relentless October rain lashed against the corrugated iron roof of the small outbuilding, a counterpoint to the rhythmic scrape of Elias's plane against a length of oak.  His workshop, nestled at the edge of the whispering woods bordering the tiny village of Oakhaven, was his sanctuary, a place where the chaos of the world faded into the background, replaced by the quiet satisfaction of creation.  Tonight, however, the usual peace was punctuated by a persistent gnawing worry. The commission for the new church doors, due in just two weeks, was proving more complex than he'd anticipated, and the oak, though beautiful, was fighting him every step of the way.
