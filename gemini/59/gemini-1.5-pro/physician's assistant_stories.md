The biting November wind whipped around Elias Thorne, PA-C, as he hurried across the cobblestone courtyard of St. Jude's Community Hospital.  The wrought iron gates, usually imposing in their dark grandeur, offered little protection from the icy gusts that seemed intent on infiltrating every layer of his thick wool coat.  The sky above the gothic spires of the hospital was a bruised purple, hinting at the early nightfall that was already descending upon the remote coastal town of Port Blossom, Maine.  The rhythmic crash of waves against the rocky shore, usually a soothing backdrop to the town's quiet charm, tonight sounded ominous, a warning of the approaching storm.  Elias pulled his scarf tighter around his neck, the scent of brine and woodsmoke clinging to the air, and reached for the heavy oak door that led to the bustling warmth of the emergency room, his shift about to begin.
