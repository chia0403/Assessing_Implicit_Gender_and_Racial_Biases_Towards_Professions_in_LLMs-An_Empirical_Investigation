Rain lashed against the grimy windows of the "Daily Clarion" office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside Amelia "Amy" Reyes.  Fluorescent lights buzzed overhead, casting a sickly yellow glow on the cluttered desks and overflowing ashtrays. Cigarette smoke, stale coffee, and the faint scent of printer ink hung heavy in the air.  Asherton, Pennsylvania, a once-booming coal town, was now a skeleton of its former self, its streets pockmarked with boarded-up businesses and its spirit crushed beneath the weight of economic hardship.  Amy, her dark hair pulled back in a messy bun, tapped a chipped mug against her teeth, her gaze fixed on the blinking cursor of her ancient computer screen.  The deadline for her piece on the closing of the last remaining mine was looming, and she had nothing.  Nothing but a gnawing feeling that there was more to the story than just another casualty of progress.
