The pungent scent of acetone hung heavy in the air, mingling with the sweet aroma of hairspray and the low thrum of eighties power ballads. Rain lashed against the large plate glass window of "Shear Radiance," obscuring the already fading neon sign advertising perms and highlights. Inside, the salon buzzed with the usual Friday afternoon frenzy.  Amelia "Amy" Rose Hawthorne, her name embroidered in looping cursive on her crisp white smock, surveyed the scene with a practiced eye. Her fiery red hair, a stark contrast to her pale complexion, was pulled back in a neat French twist, not a single strand out of place. She adjusted the tortoiseshell comb perched behind her ear and sighed, a tiny puff of air escaping her perfectly glossed lips. This was her kingdom, a haven of hairspray and gossip nestled in the heart of the sleepy town of Havenwood, Illinois.  Today, however, a different kind of storm was brewing, one that threatened to upend Amy's carefully coiffed world.
