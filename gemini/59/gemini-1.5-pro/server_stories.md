The flickering neon sign of the Starlight Diner cast a greasy, iridescent glow across the rain-slicked asphalt of Bleecker Street.  Inside, a haze of cigarette smoke mingled with the aroma of frying onions and cheap coffee.  Eleanor Vance, her name tag slightly askew on her starched white uniform, wiped down the sticky Formica countertop, her mind miles away from the clatter of plates and the incessant drone of the late-night radio.  It was 3:17 AM, the witching hour in the city that never sleeps, and the Starlight was populated by its usual cast of insomniacs, dreamers, and the just plain lost.  Tonight, however, Eleanor sensed something different, a prickle of anticipation in the air, like the quiet before a summer storm.
