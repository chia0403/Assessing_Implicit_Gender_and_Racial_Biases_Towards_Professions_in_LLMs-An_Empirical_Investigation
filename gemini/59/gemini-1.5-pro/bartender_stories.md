Rain lashed against the stained-glass window depicting St. Jude, patron saint of lost causes, a fitting emblem for The Rusty Mug.  Inside, the air hung thick with the scent of stale beer, damp wool, and desperation.  Amelia "Amy" Reyes, her name embroidered in looping cursive on her black apron, polished the already gleaming mahogany bar top with a practiced hand.  The Mug, nestled in a forgotten corner of Brooklyn where brownstones met crumbling warehouses, was her domain.  It was a haven for the city's castaways, the ones who'd slipped through the cracks, and Amy, with her sharp wit and even sharper eye, presided over them all.  Tonight, though, the usual Friday night buzz was replaced by a low hum of anticipation, a charged silence broken only by the rhythmic clinking of ice against glass as Amy prepared for the storm she knew was brewing.
