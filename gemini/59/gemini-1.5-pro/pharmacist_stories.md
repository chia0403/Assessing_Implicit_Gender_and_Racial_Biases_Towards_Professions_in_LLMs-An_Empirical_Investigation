The relentless Nevada sun beat down on the dusty windows of Yucca Springs Pharmacy, baking the "World Famous Cactus Remedies" sign until the painted spines seemed to shimmer in the heat. Inside,  Eleanor Vance Holloway, PharmD, meticulously counted out metformin tablets, the rhythmic click of the pill counter a soothing counterpoint to the insistent buzzing of flies trapped against the glass.  Yucca Springs, a town clinging precariously to the edge of civilization and the Mojave Desert, wasn't exactly bustling, but it was Eleanor's home, and the pharmacy, a quirky blend of modern medicine and desert folklore, was her domain. This Tuesday, however, felt different. A low hum of unease vibrated in the dry desert air, a feeling Eleanor couldn't quite put her finger on, but one that prickled her skin like a cholla spine.
