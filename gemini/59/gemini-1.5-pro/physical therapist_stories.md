The biting November wind whipped off Lake Michigan, carrying the scent of frozen pines and the faint, metallic tang of snow.  Inside "Kinetic Care," the fluorescent lights hummed a monotonous tune against the backdrop of soft jazz playing on the radio.  Amelia Grace Hawthorne, PT, DPT, adjusted the thermostat, chasing away the lingering chill that clung to the brick walls of her small, private practice.  She’d chosen the location in Evanston, Illinois, specifically for the view – large, sun-drenched windows offered a panoramic vista of the restless lake, a calming influence for both her patients and herself.  Today, however, the grey expanse churning beyond the glass seemed to mirror the unease simmering in Amelia’s stomach.  Her first appointment of the day, a new patient with a particularly complex case, was due any minute, and something in his file had prickled her intuition, a feeling she’d learned never to ignore.
