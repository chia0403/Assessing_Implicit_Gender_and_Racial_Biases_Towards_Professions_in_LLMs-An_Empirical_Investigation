Rain lashed against the Art Deco windows of the Chrysler Building, a furious counterpoint to the rhythmic clatter of Amelia Grace Holloway’s typewriter.  The Manhattan skyline, usually a breathtaking panorama from her 75th-floor office, was obscured by a swirling grey fog.  The only illumination came from the sickly green glow of the desk lamp highlighting the typed page and the steady, rhythmic movements of Amelia’s slender fingers.  It was 7:17 pm, well past quitting time, but Amelia wasn’t going anywhere until Mr. Henderson’s presentation was flawless.  He was notoriously particular, and a misplaced comma could ruin her week.  The air hung thick with the scent of stale coffee and Mr. Henderson’s preferred brand of Cuban cigars, a lingering aroma Amelia had come to associate with impending deadlines and simmering anxiety.
