The salt spray kissed Aurelia Celeste Moreau's face as she perched precariously on the cliff edge, her easel braced against a gust of wind that threatened to rip her canvas away. Below, the turquoise waters of the Aegean Sea crashed against the black volcanic rock of Santorini, sending white plumes skyward. The whitewashed village of Oia clung to the caldera’s rim like a cascade of sugar cubes, its blue-domed churches glinting under the late afternoon sun. Aurelia’s fingers, stained with cobalt and crimson, tightened around her palette knife, her gaze fixed on the shifting light that painted the sea in a thousand shades of blue.  This volatile beauty, this precarious balance of light and shadow, was what she longed to capture, to translate onto the blank expanse before her. This was her obsession, her torment, her life.
