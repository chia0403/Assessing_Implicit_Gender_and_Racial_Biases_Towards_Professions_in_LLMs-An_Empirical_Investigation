Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of the Kestrel Corporation headquarters, blurring the already fading Seattle skyline into a watercolour wash of grey and neon. Inside, the air buzzed with a low-level hum of anxiety.  Fluorescent lights flickered overhead, casting a sickly yellow glow on the rows of empty desks.  Eleanor Vance, Systems Analyst Level 3, huddled deeper into her worn cardigan, the only splash of colour in the sterile office a chipped mug of lukewarm tea cradled in her hands. It was 2:17 AM, well past quitting time for any sane individual.  But Eleanor wasn't exactly sane, not anymore. Not since the glitches started.
