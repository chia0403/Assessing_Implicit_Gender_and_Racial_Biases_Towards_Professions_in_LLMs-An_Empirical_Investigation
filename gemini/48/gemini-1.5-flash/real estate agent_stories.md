The salt spray kissed Amelia Katherine Reed's face as she wrestled the stubborn "For Sale" sign into the wind-battered earth.  The relentless Atlantic gale howled a mournful dirge around the crumbling, grey-shingled cottage perched precariously on the cliff edge overlooking the churning grey sea.  Seagulls wheeled overhead, their cries a harsh counterpoint to the rhythmic crash of waves against the rocks below.  This wasn't your typical picturesque coastal property;  the realtor brochure would politely omit the precarious foundation, the persistent damp, and the unnerving legend whispered by locals about the previous owner's… untimely demise.  But Amelia, a woman who’d sold beachfront bungalows in hurricane zones and fixer-uppers haunted by generations of disgruntled squirrels, wasn’t easily deterred.  This was Widow’s Point, and Amelia Katherine Reed was determined to sell it.
