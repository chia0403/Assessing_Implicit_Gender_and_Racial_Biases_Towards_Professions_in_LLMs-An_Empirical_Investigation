The biting November wind whipped Amelia Hernandez’s dark hair across her face as she hurried across the cobblestone courtyard of St. Jude’s Hospital.  The ancient stone buildings, hunched under a bruised, twilight sky, seemed to exhale a chill that seeped deep into her bones, a chilling counterpoint to the warm, sterile environment she was leaving behind. Rain, a relentless drizzle that had plagued Dublin all day, plastered her tailored coat to her back, mirroring the weight of the long, grueling operation she'd just completed.  The faint scent of antiseptic still clung to her, a ghostly reminder of the life she’d fought to save – and the one she’d almost lost.  Inside the imposing, gothic edifice, the rhythmic beeping of heart monitors pulsed like a morbid heartbeat, a constant soundtrack to Dr. Amelia Hernandez’s life. Tonight, however, the symphony felt particularly discordant.
