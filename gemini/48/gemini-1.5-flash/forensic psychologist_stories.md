The biting November wind whipped Dr. Eleanor Vance’s trench coat around her legs as she navigated the uneven cobblestones of the lane.  Grey, bruised clouds hung low over Blackwood Manor, clinging to the gothic turrets like shrouds.  Rain, a fine, persistent drizzle, plastered her dark hair to her forehead, mirroring the grim determination etched on her face.  Blackwood Manor, a brooding silhouette against the stormy sky, wasn’t just old; it felt ancient, its very stones whispering tales of secrets buried deep within its decaying walls.  The air hung heavy with the scent of damp earth and something else…something metallic, faintly sweet, that clung to the back of her throat like the taste of blood.  This wasn't her first foray into the macabre, but even Eleanor, a forensic psychologist hardened by years spent deciphering the minds of killers, felt a prickle of unease settle deep in her bones. Tonight, the darkness felt…different. More knowing.
