The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Katherine Finch’s hands, a meager comfort against the November chill that seeped through the single-pane windows of her office.  Rain lashed against the glass, blurring the already indistinct view of the grey, slate-roofed houses huddled on the opposite side of Willow Creek.  Her office, tucked away above a dusty antique shop on the edge of Ashworth, was as charmingly dilapidated as the town itself – a fact Amelia, a resolutely practical woman and insurance agent of fifteen years, found simultaneously endearing and infuriating.  The charming part was undeniable; the crooked floorboards, the scent of old paper and damp earth, the creak of the building settling with each gust of wind. The infuriating part?  The constant battle against leaks, drafts, and the occasional errant mouse. Today, however, the mouse was the least of her worries.  A particularly hefty file, bulging with paperwork relating to a claim that smelled suspiciously of arson, sat heavy on her desk.
