The humid air hung heavy, thick with the scent of frangipani and impending rain.  Cicadas buzzed a relentless symphony from the emerald canopy that draped over the ancient Mayan ruins of Uxmal.  Sweat beaded on Amelia Hernandez's forehead, despite the gentle breeze that rustled the leaves of a nearby ceiba tree.  Amelia, a woman whose smile lines spoke of years spent under the scorching Yucatan sun, adjusted the brim of her wide-brimmed hat and consulted her watch.  Another tour group was due, another chance to weave the captivating tapestry of Mayan history for a fresh batch of eager faces.  The shadows stretched long and dramatic across the crumbling stone temples, hinting at the secrets they held within, secrets Amelia was more than happy to reveal.
