The scent of sandalwood and chamomile hung heavy in the air, a fragrant counterpoint to the relentless drumming of rain against the leaded-glass windows of Serenity Now Massage Therapy.  Water cascaded down the ivy-covered brick walls of the Victorian building, blurring the gaslight glow into hazy pools of amber.  Inside, however, the atmosphere was a haven of controlled warmth, punctuated only by the soft crackle of the fireplace and the rhythmic whisper of Amelia Grace Hawthorne's skilled hands working the knotted muscles of her client.  Amelia, with her nimble fingers and a gaze as calming as the lavender-infused oil she used, was known throughout the city of Oakhaven for her uncanny ability to melt away stress, both physical and emotional.  Tonight, however, even her practiced serenity felt threatened by a storm brewing far more potent than the one raging outside.
