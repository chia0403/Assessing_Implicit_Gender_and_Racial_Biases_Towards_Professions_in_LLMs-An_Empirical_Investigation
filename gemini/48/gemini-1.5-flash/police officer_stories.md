The salt spray stung Detective Inspector Alistair Finch's face as the wind howled a mournful dirge across the desolate expanse of Saltfleet Sands.  November’s teeth, sharp and unrelenting, gnawed at the exposed skin of his neck, already reddened from the relentless wind.  The air hung thick with the smell of brine and decaying seaweed, a pungent perfume that clung to everything, even the worn leather of his worn police-issue jacket.  Before him, the vast, grey North Sea churned restlessly, its waves crashing against the jagged, black rocks that lined the beach like a skeletal grin.  A single, flickering lantern cast long, distorted shadows, barely illuminating the scene – a scene that Alistair knew, with a chilling certainty, held the key to a tragedy unfolding.
