The salt spray kissed Dr. Eleanor Vance's face as she wrestled the stubborn fishing net onto the weathered wooden dock.  The late afternoon sun, a bruised plum sinking below the horizon, cast long shadows across the churning grey water of the Atlantic.  Seagulls cried overhead, their calls a melancholic counterpoint to the rhythmic slap of waves against the pilings.  Eleanor, her dark hair escaping its braid, pulled with a grunt, her muscles protesting the unaccustomed exertion.  This wasn't exactly the typical post-work routine for a chiropractor in the bustling city of Portland, Maine, but then again, nothing about life on Monhegan Island was typical.  The scent of brine and seaweed hung heavy in the air, a stark contrast to the antiseptic smell of her usually sterile clinic.  Tonight, however, the only patient Eleanor expected was the unpredictable ocean.
