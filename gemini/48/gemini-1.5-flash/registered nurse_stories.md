The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s dark hair across her face as she hurried down the cobbled street, the scent of woodsmoke and damp earth clinging to the chill air.  Rain, a relentless drizzle that had been falling since dawn, plastered her already sodden trench coat to her body.  Ahead, the gas lamps of Oakhaven, a town clinging stubbornly to the edge of the brooding Northumberland moors, cast a sickly yellow glow on the rain-slicked stones.  Amelia, a registered nurse at the small, understaffed Oakhaven General Hospital, pulled her collar higher, her breath misting in the frigid air.  Tonight, however, the usual grim weariness that shadowed her after a long shift was overshadowed by a deeper, more unsettling unease.  Something felt…off.  The silence, broken only by the rhythmic whisper of the wind and the distant, mournful cry of a curlew, felt heavier than usual, pregnant with a foreboding she couldn't quite place.
