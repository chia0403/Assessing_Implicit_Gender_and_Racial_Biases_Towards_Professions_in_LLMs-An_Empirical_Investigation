The biting November wind howled a mournful tune down the narrow cobblestone alley, whistling through gaps in the ancient brickwork of Grimsborough.  Rain, a relentless, icy drizzle, plastered Amelia Grace Hawthorne's already damp hair to her forehead.  Amelia, or "Amy" to those who knew her well – and even to some who didn't – shivered, pulling her threadbare work jacket tighter around her. The pungent aroma of damp earth and decaying leaves fought with the surprisingly sweet scent of honeysuckle clinging stubbornly to a half-dead vine clawing up the wall of a crumbling building. This was Amy’s office, or at least, it often felt like it.  Tonight, it was the location of her latest, and decidedly less-than-glamorous, emergency plumbing call.  The flickering gas lamp above cast long, dancing shadows that seemed to writhe with the same urgency as the leaky pipe she was about to confront.
