The stale scent of disinfectant clung to Elias Thorne like a second skin, a familiar perfume in the echoing silence of the Blackwood Academy for the Gifted.  Three a.m. found Elias, a man whose life was measured not in accomplishments, but in the meticulous swipe of his mop across polished terrazzo floors, pushing his cleaning cart through the deserted halls. Moonlight, fractured by the gothic architecture's stained-glass windows, painted the corridors in shifting hues of ruby and sapphire, illuminating dust motes dancing in the stillness. The air, usually vibrant with the chaotic energy of the students – prodigies in mathematics, arcane arts, and everything in between – was heavy with the quietude of sleep, broken only by the rhythmic squeak of Elias's worn-out shoes and the faint ticking of a grandfather clock somewhere deep within the ancient building.  Tonight, however, the silence felt… different.  A prickling unease, sharper than the usual chill of the drafty hallways, settled on his skin.
