The flickering neon sign of the "Lucky Dragon" noodle bar cast an oily, orange glow on the rain-slicked streets of Shinjuku.  Rain hammered against the cheap, single-pane windows of Apartment 3B, each drop a tiny percussion beat against the relentless rhythm of Anya Sharma's typing.  Anya, a coding prodigy with a caffeine addiction that rivaled her talent, hunched over her keyboard, the glow of multiple monitors illuminating her face, making her appear almost ethereal against the backdrop of the stormy night.  Empty ramen containers and energy drink cans littered her desk, a testament to the all-nighter she was currently pulling.  The air hung thick with the scent of instant noodles and the faint, electric hum of her powerful custom-built PC, a machine as temperamental and brilliant as its owner.  Tonight, however, even Anya's formidable skills were being pushed to their absolute limit. The deadline loomed, a digital dragon breathing fire in the form of a demanding client and a mountain of unfinished code.
