The wind, a relentless sculptor of the Wyoming plains, whipped Amelia “Amy” Blackwood’s hair across her face as she squinted at the horizon.  Dust devils, miniature tornadoes of red earth, danced a chaotic jig across the parched land, mimicking the turmoil in her gut.  Her ranch, Blackwood Creek, sprawled before her – a tapestry of weathered barns, struggling alfalfa fields, and a herd of skittish Angus cattle huddled nervously near the dwindling creek bed.  The relentless sun beat down, turning the already arid landscape into a shimmering mirage, a cruel mockery of the lush pastures she remembered from her childhood.  This summer, the drought was relentless, a suffocating blanket threatening to smother everything she held dear.  The air, thick with the scent of dry grass and impending desperation, hung heavy and still between the infrequent gusts.
