Dr. Eleanor Vance adjusted the antique brass magnifying glass, its cool weight a familiar comfort in the clammy grip of her hand.  The rain hammered against the leaded-glass windows of her office, a relentless rhythm mirroring the turmoil she suspected lay hidden behind the closed door.  Her office, tucked away on the third floor of a rambling Victorian house in the quiet coastal town of Seabrook, smelled faintly of old books and woodsmoke.  The walls, painted a calming shade of seafoam green, were lined with overflowing bookshelves crammed with volumes on everything from Jungian archetypes to the healing power of nature.  Tonight, however, the comforting familiarity felt strained, the silence heavy with the unspoken anxieties of the patient waiting beyond the threshold.  The flickering gaslight cast long shadows that danced across the Persian rug, a restless ballet hinting at the emotional tempest brewing just a few feet away.
