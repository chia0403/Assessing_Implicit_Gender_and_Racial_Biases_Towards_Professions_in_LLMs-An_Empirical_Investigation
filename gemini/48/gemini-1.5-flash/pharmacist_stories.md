The chipped porcelain of the apothecary jars hummed a faint, almost imperceptible vibration under Elara Vance's fingertips.  Dust motes, illuminated by the single, flickering gas lamp above, danced in the stagnant air of Vance's Pharmacy, a space clinging stubbornly to the fading grandeur of the Victorian era.  Outside, the November wind howled a mournful dirge through the skeletal branches of the ancient oak that scraped against the stained-glass window depicting a rather jaundiced-looking St. Fiacre, the patron saint of gardeners – a cruel irony given the state of the overgrown yard.  Inside, the air was thick with the scent of dried herbs, aged leather-bound books, and something else… something subtly metallic and unsettling, a scent Elara couldn’t quite place, but which prickled at the edges of her awareness, a warning whispered on the wind.
