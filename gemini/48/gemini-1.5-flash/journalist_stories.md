The salt spray stung Amelia Hernandez's face, a familiar kiss from the unforgiving Pacific.  The relentless wind howled a mournful tune through the skeletal remains of the pier, whistling a counterpoint to the rhythmic crash of waves against the pilings.  Amelia, a seasoned investigative journalist for the *San Francisco Chronicle*, shivered, pulling her worn wool coat tighter.  The air hung thick with the scent of brine and decay, a fitting atmosphere for the story she’d come to uncover – the disappearance of renowned marine biologist, Dr. Elias Thorne, and the unsettling whispers surrounding his last research project, codenamed: Project Leviathan.  The crumbling, almost forgotten fishing village of Seabrook clung precariously to the cliff edge, its weathered houses huddled together like frightened sheep against the stormy night.  It was a place where secrets, like barnacles, clung tenaciously to the rocks, and Amelia intended to pry them loose.
