The chipped porcelain teacup rattled in Elara Vance’s hand, mirroring the tremor in her own heart.  Dust motes, illuminated by the weak winter sun slanting through the grimy windowpanes of Blackwood Manor, danced in the stagnant air like tiny, mocking spirits.  Elara, her usually meticulous bun askew, stared out at the sprawling, neglected gardens, a landscape mirroring the decaying grandeur of the house itself.  Blackwood Manor, perched precariously on a windswept cliff overlooking the turbulent grey sea, had seen better days – centuries of them, in fact. Now, it whispered secrets of forgotten grandeur and simmering resentments, secrets that clung to the faded tapestries and the dust-laden furniture like a persistent, suffocating damp.  Elara, housekeeper to the eccentric and reclusive Lord Ashworth for the past ten years, felt the weight of those secrets pressing down on her, heavier than any feather duster she'd ever wielded.
