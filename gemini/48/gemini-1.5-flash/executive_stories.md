The polished mahogany desk gleamed under the harsh fluorescent lights, reflecting Amelia Hernandez’s sharp, tired eyes.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of her 57th-floor office, blurring the glittering cityscape of Chicago into a hazy, impressionistic painting.  Amelia, Executive Vice President of Global Operations for Zenith Industries, stared at the overflowing inbox on her sleek, minimalist computer screen, the unread emails a digital Everest she’d have to conquer before the day was out. The air hung thick with the scent of expensive leather and brewing anxiety – the aroma of a thousand looming deadlines and the pressure of a billion-dollar corporation resting squarely on her capable, yet weary, shoulders.  Tonight, however, the weight felt heavier than usual.  The whispers about the impending merger with Kestrel Dynamics had intensified, their threat as palpable as the chill wind whistling through the unseen cracks in the skyscraper’s glass facade.
