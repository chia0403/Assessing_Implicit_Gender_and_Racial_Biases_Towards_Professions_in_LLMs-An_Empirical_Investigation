The scent of old paper and exotic spices hung heavy in the air, a familiar perfume to Amelia Grace Hawthorne. Amelia, owner and sole operator of "Wanderlust & Co.," a travel agency tucked away on a cobbled side street in Prague's Old Town, sighed contentedly, the aroma a comforting balm after a particularly frantic morning. Rain lashed against the leaded-glass windows of her tiny office, mirroring the tempest brewing in her inbox – a flurry of emails from disgruntled clients whose flights had been canceled due to a volcanic eruption in Iceland.  The flickering gas lamp above her desk cast dancing shadows on the antique globe, its worn surface testament to countless journeys planned and dreams realized within those four walls.  Outside, the Charles Bridge hummed with the low thrum of tourist traffic, a stark contrast to the quiet intensity Amelia was experiencing as she wrestled with a particularly stubborn airline’s customer service line.
