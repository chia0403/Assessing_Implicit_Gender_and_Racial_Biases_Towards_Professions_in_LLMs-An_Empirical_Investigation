The biting November wind whipped Amelia Blackwood’s auburn hair across her face as she wrestled with the recalcitrant stage door.  Rust groaned in protest with every heave, a sound oddly fitting for the crumbling grandeur of the Blackwood Theatre.  Gargoyles, chipped and stained with decades of London grime, leered down from the eaves, silent witnesses to countless triumphs and disasters – a fitting backdrop for Amelia’s current predicament.  Inside, the theatre itself was a cavern of shadowed velvet and faded gilt, smelling faintly of dust and forgotten dreams.  A single bare bulb swung precariously above, casting long, dancing shadows that seemed to mock her efforts.  Tonight, the premiere of her ambitious new production of "King Lear" hung precariously, not just on the creaking hinges of the stage door, but also on the frayed nerves of its increasingly erratic lead actor.
