The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne's hair across her face as she hurried down the slick cobblestone street, the collar of her worn peacoat pulled high.  The air, thick with the scent of woodsmoke and brine, carried the mournful cry of gulls overhead.  She tugged her gloves tighter, the leather creaking a counterpoint to the rhythmic slap of her boots against the damp stones.  This was Oakhaven, a remote fishing village clinging precariously to the jagged cliffs of the Maine coast, a place where the relentless ocean dictated the rhythm of life – and death.  Amelia, a physician's assistant at the small, understaffed Oakhaven Community Clinic, had grown accustomed to the harsh beauty and the even harsher realities of her isolated existence.  Tonight, however, a storm was brewing, and not just the one raging outside.  A call had come in – a frantic plea for help – and Amelia knew, with a sinking feeling in her stomach, that this one might be different.
