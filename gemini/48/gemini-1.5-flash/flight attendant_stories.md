The recycled air hung thick and stale, a miasma of cheap perfume, stale coffee, and the faint, metallic tang of fear.  Amelia Grace Hawthorne, a veteran flight attendant with eyes the color of a stormy Atlantic and hair the shade of burnt umber, gripped her tray of lukewarm chamomile tea with a practiced calm that belied the tremor in her hand.  Flight UA873 from London Heathrow to New York JFK was, to put it mildly, not going according to plan.  Outside, the inky blackness of the night was punctuated only by the erratic flicker of the wingtip lights, painting frantic, strobe-like patterns on the cloud cover below.  The usual low hum of the engines had devolved into a disconcerting series of groans and shuddering sighs, a mechanical dirge that seemed to mimic the growing unease rippling through the cabin.  Amelia's carefully constructed professional facade threatened to crack; tonight, the turbulence wasn't just meteorological.
