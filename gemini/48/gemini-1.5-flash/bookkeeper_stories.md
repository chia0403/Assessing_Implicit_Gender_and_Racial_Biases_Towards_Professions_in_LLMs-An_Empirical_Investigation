The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s fingers, the lukewarm tea doing little to soothe the icy dread that had settled in her stomach.  Rain lashed against the leaded-glass windows of her office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her.  The air hung thick with the scent of damp wool and old paper – the familiar, comforting aroma of  "Hawthorne & Sons, Chartered Accountants," a firm as steeped in tradition as it was in dust.  Amelia, perched on her worn leather stool, stared at the meticulously organized ledger before her, the neat columns of numbers mocking her inability to reconcile the discrepancies plaguing the Blackwood Estate account.  The Blackwood Estate, with its sprawling mansion overlooking the turbulent North Sea, and its reclusive, enigmatic owner, was proving to be a far more complex puzzle than any she’d encountered in her twenty years as a bookkeeper.
