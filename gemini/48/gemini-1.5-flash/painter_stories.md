The salt spray stung Aurelia Blackwood's face, a familiar kiss from the unforgiving Atlantic.  The wind, a relentless sculptor, whipped her auburn hair across her canvas, threatening to smear the vibrant blues and greens she'd painstakingly blended to capture the tumultuous sea churning against the jagged cliffs of Cornwall.  Perched precariously on a windswept outcrop, Aurelia, a woman whose spirit mirrored the wild beauty of the landscape around her, battled both the elements and her own internal tempest.  Her easel, secured with ropes and stubborn determination, swayed slightly, a testament to the raw power of the ocean that both inspired and challenged her.  The dramatic, almost violent, beauty of the Cornish coast had become her muse, her sanctuary, and her tormentor.
