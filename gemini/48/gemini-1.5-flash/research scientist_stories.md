The biting Antarctic wind howled a mournful song around Dr. Evelyn Grace Holloway as she wrestled the recalcitrant satellite dish into place.  Ice crystals, sharp as shards of glass, stung her exposed cheeks, and the breath she exhaled instantly frosted in the frigid air.  The desolate, snow-swept landscape of the McMurdo Dry Valleys stretched before her, a stark and beautiful wasteland under the pale, unforgiving sun.  Evelyn, bundled in layers of thermal gear that felt as restrictive as a straitjacket, fought a losing battle against the elements, her fingers numb despite the heated gloves.  This remote research station, perched precariously on the edge of a glacial lake, was her sanctuary, her prison, and the only place on Earth where she dared to hope she might uncover the secrets hidden beneath the ancient ice.  The success, or failure, of her five-year project – a desperate search for evidence of extraterrestrial life – rested on this single, perilous moment.
