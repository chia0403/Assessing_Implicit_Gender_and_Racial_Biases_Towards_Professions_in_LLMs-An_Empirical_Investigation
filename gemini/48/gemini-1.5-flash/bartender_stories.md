The chipped Formica bar top gleamed under the sickly yellow light of the "Rusty Mug," reflecting the weary face of Elias Thorne.  Elias, a man whose age was as difficult to pinpoint as the origin of the mysterious stain blooming on the ceiling above the jukebox, meticulously polished a glass, the rhythmic swish of the cloth a counterpoint to the low hum of conversation and the clinking of ice.  Rain lashed against the grimy windows of the bar, blurring the neon glow of the liquor store across the street into a hazy, orange smear. The air hung thick with the scent of stale beer, cheap whiskey, and desperation, a familiar perfume in this forgotten corner of Oakhaven, a town clinging to life on the edge of a dying industrial wasteland.
