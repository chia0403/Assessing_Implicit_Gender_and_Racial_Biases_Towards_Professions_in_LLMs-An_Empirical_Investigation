The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Mia” Hernandez’s auburn hair across her face as she hurried down Bleecker Street, the scent of roasting chestnuts and damp pavement a strangely comforting cocktail.  Rain, a fine, persistent drizzle, plastered her charcoal grey coat to her slender frame.  Overhead, the pre-Thanksgiving bustle of Greenwich Village swirled around her like a chaotic watercolor painting – a vibrant, almost overwhelming mix of hurried footsteps, honking taxis, and the cheerful chatter spilling from overflowing cafes.  Mia, however, saw only muted tones, shades of grey and brown punctuated by the occasional splash of scarlet from a passing bus.  Her mind, usually a kaleidoscope of fonts and color palettes, was stubbornly stuck on a single, insistent shade of dull beige – the color of creative blockage.  Tonight's deadline loomed, a monstrous, pixelated beast threatening to devour her sanity, and the only thing she could visualize was the stark white rectangle of her blank computer screen.
