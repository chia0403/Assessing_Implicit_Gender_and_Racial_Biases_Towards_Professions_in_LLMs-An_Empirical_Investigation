The air hung thick and sweet in “Sugar Rush,” a bakery so small it barely qualified as a storefront, more a cozy cupboard overflowing with sugary delights.  The scent of vanilla bean clung to everything – the worn wooden counters, the chipped porcelain mugs hanging from a rack above, and even Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s buttercream-stained apron.  Amelia, a woman whose life revolved around the precise swirl of a piping bag and the perfect bloom of a rose made from fondant, found herself staring at a disaster.  Not a collapsed meringue, nor a burnt ganache, but a three-tiered wedding cake, its pristine white frosting marred by a jagged, angry gash running down its side.  The offending weapon lay discarded nearby: a rogue spatula, abandoned mid-catastrophe.
