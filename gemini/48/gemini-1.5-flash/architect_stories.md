The biting November wind whipped Amelia Hernandez’s charcoal-grey coat around her legs as she surveyed the skeletal remains of the Blackwood Manor.  Rain, a relentless drizzle that had been falling for three days, plastered her dark hair to her forehead.  Around her, the sprawling Victorian mansion stood as a testament to both glorious decay and stubborn resilience.  Jagged, broken gables clawed at the bruised sky, their once-elegant lines now fractured and softened by time and neglect.  The air hung thick with the scent of damp earth and decaying wood, punctuated by the sharp, metallic tang of rusting ironwork.  This wasn’t the romantic ruin of a gothic novel; this was a festering wound on the landscape, a project that tested the limits of even Amelia’s considerable architectural prowess.  Her breath plumed out in ghostly white clouds, mirroring the wispy tendrils of smoke escaping from a derelict chimney, a final defiant gasp from a dying giant.
