The chipped porcelain teacup warmed Mrs. Eleanor Abigail Humphrey’s hands as she stood at the large, smudged window, watching the late afternoon mist roll in from the moor. It clung to the gnarled branches of the ancient oak trees that surrounded Blackwood Manor, turning them into ghostly sentinels. Blackwood Manor, a sprawling, gothic monstrosity of grey stone and shadowed turrets, had been Eleanor’s workplace, and in a way, her home, for the past fifteen years. The silence, usually a comforting blanket woven from the house's aged bones, felt particularly oppressive this afternoon. Even the rhythmic tick-tock of the grandfather clock in the hall seemed to hold its breath, waiting for something.
