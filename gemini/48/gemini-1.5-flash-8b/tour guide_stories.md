The biting wind whipped through the cobbled alleyways of San Gimignano, stinging Elara Seraphina’s cheeks.  Dust motes, illuminated by the dying light of the Tuscan sun, danced in the air, swirling around the terracotta rooftops and medieval towers that formed the city’s dramatic silhouette.  Elara, her vibrant scarlet shawl pulled tighter around her, adjusted the worn leather satchel slung over her shoulder.  Her nameplate, delicately engraved with "Seraphina Elara, Licensed Tour Guide," jingled softly.  Today, just two hours before the official closing, she was alone, her usual flock of tourists having vanished like the last rays of the sun.  The air, thick with the scent of rosemary and woodsmoke, smelled of forgotten stories, of secrets whispered by the wind through the centuries.
