The flickering fluorescent lights of the third-floor law office hummed, a monotonous soundtrack to Amelia Blackwood's meticulously organized workday.  Stacks of legal documents, each one a miniature universe of potential legal battles, towered around her.  Amelia, a paralegal at the prestigious firm of Sterling & Finch, meticulously tracked every filing, every deposition, every tiny detail that could potentially swing a case one way or another.  Her office, a small, almost claustrophobic space filled with the scent of stale coffee and aged paper, mirrored the precise, almost sterile nature of her job.  Outside, the city of Meridian thrummed with its own relentless energy, oblivious to the quiet, dedicated work taking place within.  Amelia, however, was acutely aware of the delicate balance of power and the subtle threads that connected every legal case to the larger tapestry of human affairs.  A single misplaced comma could change everything.
