The aroma of freshly baked sourdough, sharp and tangy, clung to the air in the narrow alleyway, a comforting blanket against the biting November wind.  Rain lashed against the corrugated iron awning above, a relentless drumming that echoed the frantic rhythm of Amelia Blackwood's heart.  Amelia, a baker with flour-dusted hands and eyes that held the fire of a thousand sunsets, stared at the overflowing basket of bread loaves.  Each one, a testament to her skill, a whisper of her grandmother's secrets, a promise of warmth and sustenance.  But today, the promise felt fragile, threatened by the relentless grey of the storm and the growing emptiness in her bakery, the "Golden Crust," tucked away in the shadowed heart of the cobbled city of Aethelgard.
