The chipped porcelain sink, stained a perpetual, murky grey by decades of lather and the occasional rogue hair, reflected the flickering gaslight above.  Bartholomew "Barty" Butterfield, a barber with hands as deft as they were calloused, meticulously trimmed the unruly moustache of a burly merchant, the scent of pipe tobacco and sweat thick in the air.  Dust motes danced in the single beam of light piercing the gloom of his tiny shop, "The Clipper's Cut," nestled on a cobbled alleyway in the heart of the sprawling, fog-shrouded city of Aethelgard.  Rain lashed against the grimy windowpanes, a relentless rhythm that echoed the steady snip-snip-snip of Barty's shears.  The year was 1887.
