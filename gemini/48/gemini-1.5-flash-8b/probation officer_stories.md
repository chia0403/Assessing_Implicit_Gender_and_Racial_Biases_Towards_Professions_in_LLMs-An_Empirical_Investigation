Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of the probation office, a relentless percussion that mirrored the rhythmic thump of Amelia "Amy" Sterling’s frustration.  The fluorescent lights hummed, casting a sickly yellow glow over the cluttered desk, piled high with paperwork – case files, court orders, and the ever-present mountain of forms for her latest batch of charges.  The stale smell of coffee and desperation hung heavy in the air, a familiar companion in this corner of the city's justice system.  Amy, her dark hair pulled back in a tight bun, stared at the computer screen, the cursor blinking impatiently.  Outside, the city, a tapestry of grey and gloom, reflected the stormy weather.  Amy's worn leather jacket felt like another layer of cold weight against her shoulders.  She was facing a new case, a young man named Leo, with a past as tangled and tempestuous as the weather outside.
