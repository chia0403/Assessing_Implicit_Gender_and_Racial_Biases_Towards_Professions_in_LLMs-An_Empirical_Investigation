The chipped Formica countertop gleamed under the harsh fluorescent lights of Ms. Eleanor Vance's guidance office, reflecting the frantic energy of a thousand adolescent anxieties.  Crumbs of what looked suspiciously like pizza crust clung to the edges, a testament to the late-night study sessions and frantic phone calls that populated this particular corner of the sprawling high school.  Eleanor, a woman whose grey hair was the colour of storm clouds on a summer day and whose eyes held the weary wisdom of countless whispered secrets, sighed.  Another student, another crisis, another carefully crafted plan for navigating the treacherous landscape of teenage existence.  Outside, the late afternoon sun cast long shadows across the football field, a stark contrast to the relentless fluorescent glow within.  It was a Tuesday, and the air hummed with the low thrum of anticipation for the impending weekend, a weekend that felt like a distant, shimmering mirage to many of the students she saw.
