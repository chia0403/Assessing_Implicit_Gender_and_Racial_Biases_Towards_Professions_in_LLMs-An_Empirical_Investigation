The flickering neon sign of "The Rusty Mug" cast a sickly yellow glow across the rain-slicked cobblestones.  The air, thick with the scent of brewing coffee and stale beer, hung heavy in the alleyway.  Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof, a relentless percussion that echoed the frantic rhythm of Amelia "Em"  Fitzwilliam's heart.

Em, a server at the perpetually crowded pub, hunched over a stack of overflowing trays, her fingers stained crimson from the spilled tomato sauce of the night's penultimate order.  Tonight, more than usual, the air crackled with an unsettling energy.  The regulars, usually boisterous, were unusually subdued, their whispers and hushed conversations a stark contrast to the usual cacophony.  A chill wind whipped through the alleyway, carrying with it the faint scent of something…metallic.  Em shivered, not just from the cold.  Tonight felt different.  The air itself seemed to hold its breath, anticipating something…
