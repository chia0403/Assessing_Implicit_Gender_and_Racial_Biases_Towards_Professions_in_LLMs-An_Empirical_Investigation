The biting November wind whipped off the Puget Sound, carrying the scent of brine and damp earth into the open kitchen of "The Wandering Spoon."  Rain lashed against the large, west-facing windows, blurring the view of the Olympic Mountains usually visible across the water. Inside, the warmth from the six-burner stove radiated outwards, battling the chill.  Copper pots gleamed under the low-hanging lights, casting a warm glow on the stainless steel counters.  Isabelle "Izzy" Dubois, her auburn braid tucked into her chef’s toque, stirred a bubbling pot of bouillabaisse, her brow furrowed in concentration.  A faint line of worry etched itself between her eyebrows, a stark contrast to the usually playful sparkle in her hazel eyes.  Tonight was the biggest night of her career – the night her restaurant, nestled in the heart of Port Townsend, Washington, would either cement its reputation as a culinary gem or sink faster than a poorly made soufflé.
