The biting November wind whipped off the slate-grey Thames, tugging at Dr. Eleanor Vance’s auburn braid as she crossed Waterloo Bridge.  London sprawled beneath her, a glittering tapestry woven with the amber glow of streetlights and the sapphire trails of late-night traffic.  Eleanor pulled her wool coat tighter, the damp chill seeping into her bones despite the multiple layers. It had been a grueling day of testimony at the Old Bailey, dissecting the fractured psyche of a man accused of unspeakable acts. The weight of his crimes, the echoes of his confessions, still clung to her like a shroud.  Her destination, a converted Victorian warehouse on the South Bank, offered little respite.  The stark, minimalist lines of the Metropolitan Police’s Forensic Psychology Unit, with its sterile white walls and hushed atmosphere, was more often a reflection of the darkness it contained than a haven from it. Tonight, however, a new case awaited, one that promised to be even more perplexing than the last.
