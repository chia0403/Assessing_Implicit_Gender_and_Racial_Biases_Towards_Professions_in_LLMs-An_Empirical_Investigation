Rain lashed against the arched windows of Amelia Hayes' office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her.  The mahogany furniture, usually gleaming under the warm glow of the antique lamp, was dull and shadowed in the gloom of the late November afternoon. Outside, the Chicago skyline was swallowed by fog, the usually vibrant city lights reduced to blurry halos. Amelia Catherine Hayes, Esq., a rising star in corporate law at the prestigious firm of Miller, Davies & Sterling, tapped a perfectly manicured nail against her mahogany desk, a stack of meticulously organized case files forgotten before her.  She stared at the blinking cursor on her computer screen, the blank document mocking her writer's block. This wasn't just another contract dispute or a hostile takeover.  This was personal.  The email sitting in her inbox, subject line simply "Regarding your father," had thrown her carefully constructed world into disarray.
