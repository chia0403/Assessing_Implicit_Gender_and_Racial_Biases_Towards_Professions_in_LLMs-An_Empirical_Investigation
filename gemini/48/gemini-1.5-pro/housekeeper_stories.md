The biting November wind howled a mournful tune around the eaves of Blackwood Manor, rattling the ancient windowpanes and sending shivers down Eliza Jane Holloway's spine.  Dead leaves, brittle and brown, swirled across the gravel drive like tiny, frantic dancers.  Eliza Jane, her hands tucked deep into the pockets of her worn wool coat, paused at the foot of the imposing stone steps, her breath puffing out in ghostly white clouds. The manor loomed above her, a gothic behemoth of dark stone and shadowed windows, its silhouette stark against the bruised twilight sky.  It had been Eliza Jane's domain, her prison and her sanctuary, for the past five years, ever since she'd arrived in the small, isolated village of Hollow Creek with nothing but a battered suitcase and a heart full of secrets.  The secrets, like the dust that perpetually settled on the manor's antique furniture, were something Eliza Jane worked tirelessly to keep hidden.
