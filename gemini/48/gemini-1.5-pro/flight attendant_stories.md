The stale, recycled air of the Boeing 747 hung heavy with the scent of lukewarm coffee and someone's aggressively floral perfume.  Amelia “Mia” Rodriguez smoothed down her navy skirt, the fabric whispering against her tights, and plastered a smile on her face.  Outside, the Chicago O'Hare International Airport glittered beneath a bruised, pre-dawn sky. Rain lashed against the windows, blurring the city lights into a shimmering, impressionistic painting.  Flight 42 to Tokyo, delayed by two hours and counting due to the inclement weather, was buzzing with the low thrum of restless passengers.  Mia, bracing herself for another round of complaints about the weather – something demonstrably outside her control – adjusted the silk scarf at her neck.  Little did she know, this seemingly ordinary delay was about to unravel into something far more extraordinary than a few grumpy travellers and a missed connection.
