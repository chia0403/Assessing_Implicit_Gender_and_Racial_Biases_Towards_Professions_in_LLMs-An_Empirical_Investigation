The biting November wind whipped Amelia Hayes’ auburn braid across her face as she hurried across the frost-covered playground of Maplewood Elementary. The air, sharp with the scent of woodsmoke and damp leaves, tugged at the corners of the colourful “Kindness Matters” banner strung across the brick facade of the school.  Maplewood, nestled in the heart of the sleepy, forested town of Oakhaven, Vermont, was usually a picture of quaint tranquility. This morning, however, a palpable tension hung in the air, thicker than the swirling mist rising from the nearby creek.  Amelia, the school psychologist, had a feeling her usual Tuesday morning of standardized testing and scheduled counseling sessions was about to take an unexpected turn.  A cluster of teachers huddled near the main entrance, their voices hushed and urgent, their breath clouding the frigid air. Something was definitely wrong.
