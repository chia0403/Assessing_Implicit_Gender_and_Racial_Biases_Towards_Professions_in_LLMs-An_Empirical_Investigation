The sterile scent of antiseptic hung heavy in the pre-dawn air, clinging to Dr. Elias Thorne like a second skin.  He stood at the window of his office on the 12th floor of St. Jude's Hospital, the sprawling city of Chicago a glittering grid below.  The first blush of sunrise painted the steel and glass canyons in hues of rose and gold, a stark contrast to the sterile white and clinical green that encompassed him.  Elias, still in his scrubs from a grueling overnight surgery, rubbed the weariness from his eyes.  A single, insistent beep from his pager broke the quiet contemplation.  It was going to be another long day.
