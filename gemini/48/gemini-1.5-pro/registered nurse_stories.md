The fluorescent lights of St. Jude's Hospital buzzed overhead, casting a sterile, almost alien glow on the freshly polished linoleum floor. Rain lashed against the windows of the Emergency Room, mirroring the frantic pace within.  Eleanor Vance, RN, paused for a moment at the nurses' station, the crisp white collar of her uniform stark against the vibrant chaos swirling around her. A gurney rattled past, carrying a man clutching his chest, his face contorted in pain.  A child wailed in the arms of her mother, her small hand pressed against a swollen cheek.  The air crackled with tension, a familiar symphony of human suffering and resilience that Eleanor had come to know intimately over her five years at St. Jude's.  Tonight, however, something felt different. A prickle of unease danced on her skin, a premonition of a night unlike any other.
