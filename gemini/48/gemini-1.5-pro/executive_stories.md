Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of Cassandra "Cassie" Moreau's forty-second-floor office, blurring the already fading twilight view of the Chicago skyline.  Inside, the air was crisp and cool, a stark contrast to the stifling humidity that had plagued the city all day.  Cassie, CEO of Moreau Industries, a rapidly expanding tech company specializing in sustainable energy solutions, tapped a restless finger against the polished mahogany of her desk.  Scattered across its surface were blueprints, financial reports, and a half-eaten container of takeout sushi – testament to the eighteen-hour workday she’d just endured.  The rhythmic drumming of the rain was momentarily drowned out by a shrill, insistent ringing. Cassie’s sleek, rose-gold phone pulsed with an incoming call from a blocked number. A flicker of unease danced in her hazel eyes.  This was the third such call this week.
