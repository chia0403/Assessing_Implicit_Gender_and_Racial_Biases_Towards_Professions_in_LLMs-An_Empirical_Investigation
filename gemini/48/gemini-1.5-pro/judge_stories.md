The mahogany desk, polished to a mirror sheen, reflected the flickering gaslight back into the stern face of Judge Elias Thorne. Rain lashed against the tall, arched windows of his chambers, a relentless drumming that echoed the disquiet in his heart.  It was late, past midnight, in the shadowed city of New Birmingham, a sprawling metropolis built upon the ruins of old Birmingham, now choked with industry and secrets.  The air hung thick with coal smoke and the metallic tang of the nearby factories, a constant reminder of the city's relentless pursuit of progress, a progress Judge Thorne often found morally bankrupt.  Tonight, however, the weight pressing on him wasn't the usual ethical dilemma presented by patent disputes and industrial sabotage cases.  Tonight, it was something far more personal, something that threatened to unravel the carefully constructed life he'd built within these soot-stained walls. A single, unopened letter lay on the gleaming mahogany, its stark white envelope a jarring contrast to the surrounding gloom.  The elegant script of the sender's name sent a shiver down his spine:  Seraphina Blackwood.
