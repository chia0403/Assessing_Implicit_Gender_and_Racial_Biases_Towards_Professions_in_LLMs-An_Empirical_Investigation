The aroma of burnt sugar and cardamom hung heavy in the air, a bittersweet perfume clinging to the brick walls of Buttercup Bakery.  Amelia "Millie" Hawthorne, her auburn braids dusted with powdered sugar, sighed, pulling the blackened tray of kouign amann from the oven with a clang.  Dawn painted the cobbled streets of Port Blossom, Maine, in hues of rose and gold, but inside the bakery, Millie’s mood felt as grey as the encroaching fog rolling in from the harbour.  Port Blossom, nestled between the rocky coastline and whispering pine forests, was usually a picture of quaint, seaside charm. Today, however, it felt like a postcard faded by disappointment. The annual Blueberry Festival, the biggest event of the year, was just three days away, and Millie’s signature creation, the blueberry-lemon meringue pie that usually won blue ribbons and rave reviews, was proving to be a fickle beast this year.
