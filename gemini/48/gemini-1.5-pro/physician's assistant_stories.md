The biting wind whipped Amelia Reyes's dark braid across her face as she hurried across the slick, cobbled courtyard of St. Jude's Memorial Hospital.  The November sky over Baltimore pressed down, a bruised purple heavy with the promise of snow.  The ancient hospital, a gothic behemoth of grey stone and stained glass, seemed to hunker down against the coming storm as well.  Amelia, her breath puffing white clouds in the frigid air, clutched her thermos of coffee tighter, a small comfort against the gnawing anxiety that had taken root in her stomach since the frantic pre-dawn call.  A rare and aggressive strain of influenza was tearing through the city, and St. Jude's, already stretched thin by the usual winter ailments, was rapidly becoming ground zero.  Tonight, Amelia was the senior physician's assistant on duty, meaning the weight of dozens of lives rested, precariously, in her capable, but increasingly weary, hands.  The ornate wooden doors of the hospital loomed before her, offering a deceptive sense of sanctuary.  Inside, Amelia knew, chaos reigned.
