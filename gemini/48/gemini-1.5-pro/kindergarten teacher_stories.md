The scent of Elmer's glue and impending naptime hung thick in the air of Mrs. Eleanor Vance's kindergarten classroom.  Sunlight, filtered through the vibrant construction paper leaves clinging to the windowpanes, cast dappled patterns across the worn, alphabet-bordered rug.  Rainbow Daycare, nestled between a bustling laundromat and a perpetually closed antique store in the sleepy town of Harmony Glade, Oregon, wasn't much to look at from the outside. Its beige stucco walls and faded plastic playset hinted at a place long past its prime. But inside, within the brightly painted walls of Mrs. Vance's classroom, magic – of the glitter-glue and finger-paint variety – happened daily.  This Tuesday, however, felt different.  A disquiet hummed beneath the usual cheerful chaos, a sense of anticipation that prickled even Mrs. Vance's usually unflappable demeanor.
