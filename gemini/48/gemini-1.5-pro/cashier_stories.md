The flickering fluorescent lights of the Quick Stop buzzed like trapped insects, casting a sickly yellow glow over Aislinn "Ace" O'Malley and her tiny kingdom of chewing gum, lottery tickets, and slightly-too-warm bottled beverages.  It was 2:17 AM in Harmony Creek, Oregon, a town where the most exciting event in the past decade was the grand re-opening of the bowling alley after a rogue raccoon caused a minor fire in the snack bar.  Outside, a lone dog barked mournfully at the indifferent moon, while inside, Ace meticulously organized a display of beef jerky, her mind as numb as the stale air conditioning. The digital clock above the cigarette display ticked off another second, each one an agonizing reminder of the glacial pace of time in the graveyard shift.
