Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of the Zenith Tower, blurring the already fading cityscape of Neo-Kyoto into a watercolour of neon and grey. Inside, the air thrummed with the low hum of servers, a constant, reassuring presence against the storm raging outside.  A half-eaten bowl of synthetic ramen sat forgotten on Amelia “Amy” Sharma’s desk, its bioluminescent broth dimming in protest.  Amy, her dark hair pulled back in a messy bun, hunched over her workstation, fingers flying across the holographic keyboard projected above her wrists. Lines of code scrolled across her augmented reality contact lenses, a dizzying stream of emerald green against the backdrop of the storm.  She was chasing a ghost in the system, a phantom error that had been plaguing Zenith Corp’s new operating system, Osiris, for weeks.  Tonight, she was determined to exorcise it.
