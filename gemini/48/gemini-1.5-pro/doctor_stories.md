The biting November wind whipped around Dr. Eleanor Vance, tugging at the edges of her thick wool coat as she hurried across the cobbled courtyard of St. Jude's Hospital.  Gargoyles perched on the gothic building’s eaves leered down at her, their stony faces slick with rain.  Built in the 17th century, the hospital clung to the hillside overlooking the churning grey waters of the North Sea in the remote Scottish fishing village of Portwenn.  The air, thick with the scent of brine and woodsmoke, did little to soothe the gnawing unease in Eleanor’s stomach. Tonight, the perpetual gloom that shrouded the village seemed to have seeped into the very bones of the old hospital, a premonition of the difficult night that lay ahead.
