The biting November wind whipped around Coach Elias Thorne as he stood on the cracked asphalt of the St. Jude's High School basketball court.  Floodlights cast long, skeletal shadows of the chain-link fence across the deserted bleachers, the metal groaning in protest against the gusts.  Above, a bruised, indigo sky threatened snow.  Elias, his breath clouding the air in front of him, pulled his threadbare Eagles varsity jacket tighter around his thin frame.  St. Jude's, nestled in the forgotten corner of Ironwood, Michigan, a town clinging to the shores of Lake Superior like a barnacle to a ship's hull, hadn't seen a winning season in over a decade.  Elias, in his first year as head coach, was determined to change that, even if it meant facing down the ghosts of seasons past and the icy indifference of a town that had long given up on its team. The squeak of sneakers on the asphalt announced the arrival of his first player, a lone figure emerging from the swirling snow flurries.
