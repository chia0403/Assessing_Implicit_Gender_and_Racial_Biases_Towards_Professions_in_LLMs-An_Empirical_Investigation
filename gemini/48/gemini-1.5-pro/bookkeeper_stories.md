The rhythmic clatter of the Underwood typewriter, punctuated by the occasional *ding* of the bell, filled the otherwise silent office.  Agnes Periwinkle, her spectacles perched precariously on her nose, meticulously entered figures into the ledger for Henderson's Haberdashery.  Outside, the cobbled streets of Oakhaven, Massachusetts shimmered under the oppressive July heat. The year was 1923, and even the usually bustling harbor seemed to hold its breath, waiting for the inevitable afternoon thunderstorm.  Agnes, however, remained oblivious to the stifling atmosphere, lost in a world of debits, credits, and the satisfying balance of a perfectly reconciled account. Little did she know, the meticulous order of her world was about to be irrevocably disrupted by a single misplaced comma.
