The rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of The Rusty Mug, a rhythm that mirrored the thrumming anxiety in Amelia “Amy” Hawthorne’s chest.  Neon signs advertising long-dead beer brands flickered intermittently in the grimy windows, casting a sickly green glow across the mostly empty bar.  Dust motes danced in the beams, illuminated like tiny, frantic fireflies. Located on the edge of the desolate town of Harmony Creek, Nevada, where the desert met the mountains in a jagged, unforgiving embrace, The Rusty Mug was less a watering hole and more a last resort.  Tonight, even the usual handful of regulars were absent, leaving Amy alone with the ghosts of miners past and the unsettling premonition that something was about to change.
