Rain lashed against the grimy windows of Cassandra “Cassie” Moreau’s office, mimicking the relentless drumming in her head.  Neon light from the pawn shop across the street reflected in greasy puddles on the cracked linoleum floor, painting the space in alternating hues of emerald and ruby. Her office, sandwiched between a laundromat and a perpetually closed fortune teller’s parlor on Bleecker Street, smelled of stale coffee and desperation, a scent Cassie had come to associate with a typical Tuesday in Greenwich Village.  A single bare bulb illuminated the room, casting long shadows that danced with the flickering light.  Cassandra, perched precariously on a rolling office chair that squeaked with every movement, tapped a chewed-down pencil against a stack of unpaid bills.  The case files stacked haphazardly on her desk seemed to mock her with their silent promise of unfulfilled potential, while the insistent ringing of the rotary phone on the corner pierced the monotonous rhythm of the rain.
