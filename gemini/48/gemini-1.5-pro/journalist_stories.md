Rain lashed against the grimy windows of The Daily Clarion's office, mimicking the tempest brewing inside Amelia “Mia” Rodriguez.  The flickering fluorescent lights buzzed overhead, casting a sickly yellow glow over the cluttered room, a stark contrast to the vibrant city lights usually visible through the rain-streaked glass.  Charleston, South Carolina, normally a city bursting with charm and history, tonight felt oppressive, shrouded in a damp, clinging darkness. Mia, hunched over her battered laptop, chewed on the end of a pen, her brow furrowed in concentration. The deadline for her piece on the controversial redevelopment of the historic Gullah district loomed, and she was still missing a key piece of information, a piece that could make or break the entire story.  The air hung heavy with the scent of stale coffee and desperation, the only sounds the rhythmic clatter of keys and the relentless drumming of the rain.
