The biting November wind whipped Amelia Reyes's dark hair across her face as she hurried down Bleecker Street, the scent of roasted chestnuts battling with the exhaust fumes for dominance.  Amelia, a registered occupational therapist at the Greenwich Village Rehabilitation Center, clutched her worn leather satchel tighter, the chill seeping through her wool coat.  The sky, a bruised purple, hinted at an early snowfall.  Number 147 Bleecker, a narrow brownstone sandwiched between a bustling trattoria and a dusty antique shop, was her destination.  Its peeling paint and perpetually darkened windows gave it an air of mystery, a stark contrast to the vibrant life pulsing around it. Inside, awaited her newest, and most enigmatic, patient.
