The rusty scissors whispered against Elias Thorne's calloused thumb. Outside, the neon glow of "Shear Perfection" bled onto the rain-slicked streets of Port Blossom, casting the whole block in a lurid, artificial twilight.  The town, nestled on the edge of the Whispering Pines forest in Oregon, normally thrived on the lumber trade and the occasional tourist drawn to its quirky shops and rumored connection to cryptid sightings. But tonight, a Tuesday, and a particularly dreary one at that, the only sound besides the rhythmic *snip-snip* of Elias's scissors was the relentless drumming of the rain against the large, single-pane window.  The air inside the small shop, thick with the scent of hairspray and sandalwood, felt heavy with an unspoken anticipation, a feeling Elias knew all too well whenever the wind howled down from the pines like this.
