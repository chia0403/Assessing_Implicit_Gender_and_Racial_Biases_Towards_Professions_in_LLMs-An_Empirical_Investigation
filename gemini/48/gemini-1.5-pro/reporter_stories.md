Rain lashed against the grimy windows of The Daily Clarion, blurring the neon glow of Benny's Diner across the street.  Amelia "Amy" Reyes shivered, pulling her threadbare cardigan tighter around her. The newsroom, usually buzzing with the clatter of keyboards and the shouts of editors, was eerily quiet.  It was 2:17 am, a time usually reserved for janitors and insomniac reporters chasing down dead-end leads. Amy, however, wasn't chasing anything tonight. She was waiting.  Her chipped mug of lukewarm coffee sat beside a crumpled pack of Marlboros, untouched.  Ashbury, Ohio, a town built on the crumbling bones of the coal industry, wasn't exactly a hotbed of breaking news.  But something felt different tonight. A tension hung in the air, thick and heavy, like the coal dust that still choked the lungs of this dying town.  Amy could feel it in the way the wind howled outside, in the persistent drip-drip-drip of the leaky roof above her head. Something was about to happen.
