The biting November wind whipped around Professor Alistair Finch, tugging at the edges of his tweed overcoat as he hurried across the cobbled quadrangle of St. Jude's College, Oxford.  Gargoyles leered down from the honey-coloured stone walls, their grotesque features softened by a dusting of snow.  The air, thick with the scent of woodsmoke and damp earth, carried the distant chime of the university clock tower, announcing the quarter hour.  Alistair clutched a leather-bound volume of Chaucer tighter to his chest, his breath misting in the frigid air.  His destination was the Bodleian Library, a sanctuary of warmth and knowledge, a refuge from the encroaching darkness of the winter afternoon, and, more importantly, a hiding place from the insistent questions of his increasingly suspicious colleagues.
