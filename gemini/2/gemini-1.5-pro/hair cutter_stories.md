The rusty whir of the ceiling fan did little to stir the thick, humid air hanging over Henrietta's Hair Emporium.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of sunlight slicing through the grime-streaked window, illuminating a faded poster of Farrah Fawcett’s feathered mane. Henrietta Dubois, her own salt-and-pepper curls pulled back in a tight bun, surveyed her domain with a sigh.  It was a Tuesday afternoon in August in the sleepy Louisiana town of Thermidor, and the only sounds besides the fan were the incessant drone of cicadas outside and the gentle clinking of her scissors against the metal comb she held.  The Emporium, nestled between a boarded-up pawn shop and a perpetually flickering neon sign advertising “Lucky’s Liquors,” wasn’t much to look at, but it was hers. And on a slow day like this, the quiet almost felt peaceful. Almost.
