The chipped Formica countertop reflected Elara Vance's weary face, illuminated only by the harsh fluorescent lights of the guidance office.  Dust motes danced in the lone shaft of afternoon sunlight slicing through the grimy window overlooking the cracked asphalt playground.  Eleventh-grade angst, meticulously transcribed onto spiral notebooks and typed onto word documents, piled precariously on the table beside her overflowing coffee mug.  Elara, a guidance counselor at Oakhaven High, a school with a reputation for failing as much as it did for graduating, felt the familiar weight of her role settling heavily on her chest.  Another Tuesday, another cycle of whispered anxieties, unspoken fears, and the relentless churn of teenage desires and anxieties.  Today, however, felt different.  A note, tucked beneath a stack of transcripts, possessed a haunting simplicity.  It was addressed to her.
