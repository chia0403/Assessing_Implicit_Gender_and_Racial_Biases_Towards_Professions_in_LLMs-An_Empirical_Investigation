The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Blackwood's hands, the faint scent of Earl Grey clinging to the air like a forgotten memory.  Sunlight, filtered through the dusty windowpanes of her tiny attic apartment, painted the worn wooden floor in shifting patterns of gold and shadow.  A scattering of manuscripts, each one a testament to untold hours of toil, lay open on the cluttered desk, their pages filled with the spidery script of a thousand imagined worlds.  Amelia Blackwood, a novelist struggling to find her footing in the unforgiving landscape of literary ambition, stared out at the cityscape of faded grey buildings and the relentless drizzle that clung to the cobblestone streets below.  It was a Tuesday, the kind that seemed to swallow every other day whole, leaving behind only a dull ache in the soul.
