The chipped Formica countertop gleamed under the harsh fluorescent lights of the "Numbers & Nibs" accounting firm.  Rain hammered against the grimy windowpanes, a relentless rhythm mirroring the steady, rhythmic clack of Amelia Peabody's keyboard.  Amelia, a meticulous accountant with a penchant for spreadsheets and sharp pencils, stared at the ever-growing mountain of invoices, a testament to the relentless demands of the season.  The air, thick with the smell of stale coffee and desperation, clung to her like a second skin.  Tonight, the pressure felt particularly intense.  The annual audit for Blackwood Industries, a prestigious but notoriously demanding client, was looming, and Amelia was lagging behind.  Her meticulously organized binder, usually a source of comfort, felt like a suffocating weight.  Outside, the city thrummed with a life she rarely felt part of, a stark contrast to the quiet, calculated world of numbers she inhabited.
