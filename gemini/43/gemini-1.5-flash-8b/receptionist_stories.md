The fluorescent lights of the Sterling & Stone law firm hummed, a relentless, almost hypnotic thrum that resonated with the rhythmic *tap-tap-tap* of Amelia Hayes’s fingers on the polished reception desk.  Dust motes danced in the afternoon sun, slicing through the pale beige of the waiting room, illuminating the pristine leather armchairs, each one unoccupied save for the faint imprint of a departed client.  Amelia, with her perpetually neat auburn bob and a smile that clung to her lips as if sewn on, felt the familiar weight of the office's unspoken expectations.  She was the first face, the first voice, the silent guardian of the towering edifice of legalities that resided behind the etched glass doors.  Amelia Hayes, receptionist extraordinaire, was the custodian of secrets, the unwitting chronicler of fortunes, and today, the potential harbinger of something…different.
