The fluorescent lights of Ms. Eleanor Vance's classroom hummed, a monotonous soundtrack to the rhythmic tap-tap-tap of pencils against paper.  Dust motes danced in the sliver of afternoon sun piercing the grime-coated windowpanes of Room 214 at Northwood High.  Posters of famous mathematicians, their faces etched with a determined intensity, stared down at the sea of teenage heads hunched over their algebra worksheets.  The air, thick with the scent of stale coffee and cheap printer ink, felt heavy with the weight of unspoken anxieties and the near-unbearable boredom of a Tuesday afternoon.  Eleanor, a history teacher, was, as always, a little too early.
