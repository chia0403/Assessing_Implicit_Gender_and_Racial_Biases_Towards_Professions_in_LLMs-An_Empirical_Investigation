The chipped paint on the wrought-iron gates of Blackwood Manor whispered tales of neglect, mirroring the muted hues of the sprawling, overgrown gardens beyond.  Dust motes danced in the pale September sunlight, illuminating a landscape choked with weeds and forgotten roses.  This was the domain of Bartholomew "Barty" Finch, groundskeeper, and today, the whispers seemed to carry a particularly unsettling rhythm.  Barty, a man weathered by years of battling thorny bushes and stubborn soil, adjusted his worn gardening gloves, his calloused hands already feeling the weight of the day.  Blackwood Manor, a stately Victorian gothic pile, loomed over him, its many windows like vacant eyes staring out into the melancholic landscape.  The air, thick with the scent of damp earth and decaying leaves, seemed to hold its breath, anticipating something.
