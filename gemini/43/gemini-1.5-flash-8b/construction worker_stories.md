The biting wind whipped around Amelia "Amy" Rodriguez, stinging her cheeks and rattling the flimsy plastic sheeting draped precariously over the half-finished skyscraper.  She adjusted the thick wool scarf around her neck, her breath fogging in the frigid air, and peered up at the sheer, dizzying height.  The skeletal structure of the building, a monument to human ambition, seemed to mock the small, huddled figures below, lost in the oppressive grey of the late November Chicago sky.  Construction debris littered the scaffolding, a chaotic symphony of lumber, metal, and plaster dust.  The rhythmic clang of a distant jackhammer was the only sound that dared to compete with the relentless howl of the wind.  Amy, her calloused hands gripping the metal railing, felt a familiar tremor of exhaustion, a constant companion in this unforgiving city. The year was 2024, and the city's relentless expansion was relentlessly demanding.
