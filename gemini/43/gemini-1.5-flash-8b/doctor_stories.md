The biting wind whipped Dr. Eleanor Vance's lab coat around her legs as she hurried across the deserted university campus.  Snow, thick and unforgiving, crunched under her boots, a stark contrast to the vibrant, almost incandescent glow emanating from the lab windows of the Cryogenics Institute.  Inside, a silent symphony of humming machinery and the rhythmic *click* of automated systems filled the air, a discordant counterpoint to the howling blizzard raging outside.  The year was 2247, and the Institute, nestled deep within the sprawling, snow-capped peaks of the newly-reclaimed Appalachian Mountains, was at the heart of a crisis that transcended the boundaries of scientific progress.
