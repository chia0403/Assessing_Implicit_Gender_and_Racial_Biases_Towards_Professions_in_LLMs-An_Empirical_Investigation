The chipped porcelain sink, stained a perpetual, murky grey from decades of lukewarm water and countless washes of delicate silks, reflected Amelia Nightingale's anxious face.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of afternoon sun that pierced the grime-coated windowpanes of her tiny workshop, illuminating the meticulously pinned patterns scattered across her worn oak table.  Amelia Nightingale, a dressmaker renowned for her exquisite gowns in the quiet, cobbled streets of Ashwood, felt a knot of dread tighten in her stomach.  The order, whispered on the wind by the gossiping seamstresses of the marketplace, the one that had arrived folded in a worn, cream-coloured handkerchief, was different.  Unusual.  And alarmingly, utterly unlike anything she had ever attempted.  A gown, not for a debutante or a duchess, but for a... phantom.
