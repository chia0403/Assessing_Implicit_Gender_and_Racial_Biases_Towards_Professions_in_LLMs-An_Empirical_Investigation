The chipped Formica countertop gleamed under the harsh fluorescent lights of the accounting firm, reflecting Amelia Blackwood’s tired eyes.  Stacks of invoices, meticulously organized by client, towered around her, a silent testament to the relentless rhythm of numbers.  The air hung thick with the scent of stale coffee and desperation, a symphony of the unsung heroes of finance.  Amelia, a bookkeeper at Zenith Accounts, had seen it all: the ecstatic highs of successful startups and the crushing lows of bankruptcies.  Today, it was the latter.  The towering stacks of paperwork seemed to lean in closer, whispering stories of missed deadlines, unpaid bills, and the bitter taste of failure.  The flickering neon sign outside, advertising "Zenith Accounts – Your Financial Fortress," felt mocking in its false promise.  The setting was the heart of a bustling, yet ultimately grey, city, where skyscrapers pierced the overcast sky, their glass facades a stark reflection of the financial turmoil below.
