The wind, a constant companion on the plains, whipped Amelia "Milly" Jane Blackwood's long braid across her face as she surveyed the parched landscape.  Dust devils danced on the horizon, swirling red earth against a mercilessly blue sky.  Her ranch, the Rocking B, sprawled beneath her gaze, a patchwork of stubborn green pasture clinging to life amidst the encroaching brown of drought-stricken land.  The skeletal remains of a windmill creaked a mournful song, a rusty counterpoint to the dry rattle of the cottonwoods lining the creek bed, which had been bone dry for five months.  July in west Texas was unforgiving, and this year, it seemed determined to break even the most resilient spirits, including Milly's.
