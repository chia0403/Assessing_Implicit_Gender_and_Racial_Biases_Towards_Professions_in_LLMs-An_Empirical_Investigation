Rain lashed against the leaded windows of Bartholomew Quill's office, each drop a tiny, insistent hammer against the glass.  The wind howled a mournful tune down the narrow chimneys of Grimsby Crescent, a street of tall, soot-stained townhouses clinging precariously to the cliffside overlooking the churning grey North Sea.  Inside, illuminated by the flickering gaslight above his mahogany desk, Bartholomew Quill meticulously entered figures into his ledger.  The rhythmic scratch of his pen was the only sound that dared compete with the tempest outside.  His office, a small room tucked away at the back of Grimshaw & Sons Shipping Company, was a comforting sanctuary of order and predictability in a world that often seemed determined to be neither.  Tonight, however, even the neatly arranged stacks of invoices and the precisely sharpened pencils couldn't fully dispel the unsettling feeling that crept into the corners of the room with the drafts.  Something was amiss.  The numbers, usually his steadfast companions, whispered a disquieting story of discrepancies and imbalances.
