Rain lashed against the grimy windows of The Daily Chronicle's office, mimicking the tempest brewing inside Amelia "Mia" Rodriguez.  Fluorescent lights hummed overhead, casting a sickly yellow glow over the cluttered newsroom. Stacks of newspapers teetered precariously on desks, interspersed with half-eaten takeout containers and overflowing ashtrays. The air hung thick with the smell of stale coffee and deadline pressure. Mia, her dark curls pulled back in a messy bun, tapped a frantic rhythm on her chipped keyboard, the glow of the screen illuminating the fierce determination in her hazel eyes. Ashworth, Indiana, a town more renowned for its annual pie-eating contest than hard-hitting journalism, was about to become the center of a story that could make Mia's career.  A story she wasn't even supposed to have.
