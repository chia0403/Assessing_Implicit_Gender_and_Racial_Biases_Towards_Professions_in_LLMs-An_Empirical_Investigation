The biting Antarctic wind whipped at Dr. Elias Thorne's parka, stinging his exposed cheeks despite the thick layers of protective gear.  The perpetual twilight of early spring cast long, distorted shadows across the desolate, snow-covered landscape.  Beneath the crunching of his boots on the packed ice, Elias could hear the low, guttural groans of the Ross Ice Shelf, a constant reminder of the immense, shifting power beneath his feet.  He was miles from McMurdo Station, his temporary home, a solitary figure at Site Zeta-9, a clandestine research outpost dedicated to the study of a recently discovered anomaly buried deep within the ice.  The anomaly, codenamed "Glacius," emitted a strange energy signature unlike anything Elias had encountered in his years as a glaciologist and geophysicist.  And it was getting stronger.
