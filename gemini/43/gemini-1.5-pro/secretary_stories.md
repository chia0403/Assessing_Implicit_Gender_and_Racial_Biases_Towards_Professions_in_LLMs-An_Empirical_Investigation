The rhythmic clatter of typewriter keys filled the small, stuffy office, punctuated by the occasional *ding* of the bell announcing the end of a line.  Dust motes danced in the lone shaft of sunlight slicing through the grimy window, illuminating a chipped porcelain doll perched precariously on a filing cabinet. This was the domain of Miss Esmeralda Penelope Higgins, secretary to Mr. Bartholomew P. Grimshaw, Esquire, Attorney at Law, and it was, to put it mildly, underwhelming. The peeling floral wallpaper, the perpetually drooping philodendron in the corner, and the faint aroma of stale pipe tobacco all conspired to create an atmosphere of faded grandeur and quiet desperation. Outside, the bustling sounds of 1940s New York City – car horns, newsboys hawking papers, the rumble of the elevated train – seemed a world away.  Esmeralda, her spectacles perched on the bridge of her nose, paused mid-sentence, her fingers hovering over the keys.  A strange premonition, a prickly feeling at the back of her neck, told her that this particular Tuesday was about to become anything but ordinary.
