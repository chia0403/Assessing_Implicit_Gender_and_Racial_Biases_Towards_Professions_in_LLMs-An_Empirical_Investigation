The insistent Florida sun beat down on the turquoise awning of "Knead to Relax," baking the faux-bamboo wind chimes into a state of near-silent stillness.  Inside, the air hung thick with the scent of lavender and coconut oil, a carefully curated olfactory oasis against the humid assault of the outside world.  Eleanor Vance, her dark hair pulled back in a neat bun, wiped down the massage table with a practiced hand, the rhythmic swipes a familiar prelude to the day's appointments.  Her small, sun-drenched studio, nestled between a laundromat and a struggling Cuban bakery in Key Largo, wasn't glamorous, but it was hers.  And today, a Monday notorious for its slow start, held a surprising appointment: a new client, a Mr. Silas Blackwood, booked for a deep tissue massage, with a cryptic note requesting "extra pressure."
