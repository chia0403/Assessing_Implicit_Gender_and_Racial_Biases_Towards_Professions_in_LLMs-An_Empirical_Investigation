Rain lashed against the forty-foot windows of Elias Thorne's office, blurring the already fading twilight view of the Manhattan skyline.  Elias, his full name Elias Vance Thorne III, tapped a manicured fingernail against the polished mahogany of his desk, the rhythmic click a counterpoint to the relentless drumming of the storm. His office, perched high on the 57th floor of the prestigious Thorne & Sterling Investments building, usually exuded an aura of calm, controlled power. Tonight, however, a palpable tension hung in the air, thick and suffocating like the humid July air outside.  The blinking red light on his Bloomberg terminal pulsed like a malevolent eye, reflecting in the deep worry lines etched around Elias's usually composed features.  The market was crashing, and for the first time in his illustrious career, Elias wasn't sure he could stop it.
