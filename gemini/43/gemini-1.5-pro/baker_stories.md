The aroma of burnt sugar and cardamom hung heavy in the air, a not entirely unpleasant scent that clung to Elara Thistlewick like a second skin.  Dawn was just beginning to paint the cobblestone streets of Hollow Creek a soft, pearly grey, but within Thistlewick's Treats, nestled between a dusty apothecary and a perpetually closed haberdashery, the ovens were already roaring. Elara, her chestnut braid dusted with flour, hummed a lilting tune as she wrestled a particularly stubborn batch of sourdough from its proving basket. Hollow Creek, a town perpetually shrouded in mist and whispering secrets nestled deep within the Whispering Pines, was a place where magic simmered beneath the surface, woven into the very fabric of everyday life. And Elara, though she wouldn't admit it aloud, was as much a part of that magic as the whispering pines themselves, her baked goods imbued with a charm that went beyond mere sweetness.
