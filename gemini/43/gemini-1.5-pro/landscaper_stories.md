The biting October wind whipped at Eliza Jane Wilder's cheeks, turning them the same shade as the crimson maple leaves swirling around her work boots.  Frost crunched underfoot as she surveyed the sprawling grounds of Alder House, a Victorian mansion perched on a hill overlooking the sleepy town of Oakhaven, Vermont.  A thick blanket of grey clouds pressed low in the sky, threatening snow.  Eliza adjusted the brim of her worn baseball cap, a stray strand of auburn hair escaping to dance in the wind.  This was her last job of the season, winterizing the gardens of the notoriously reclusive Mr. Silas Blackwood.  A shiver, not entirely from the cold, ran down her spine.  Alder House had a reputation, a whisper of strange occurrences and an unsettling stillness that hung heavy in the air.  Eliza gripped the handle of her pruning shears, a familiar comfort in the face of the creeping unease.
