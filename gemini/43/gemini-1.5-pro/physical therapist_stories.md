The biting November wind whipped around Dr. Eleanor Vance as she hurried across the cobbled courtyard of the Alderney Manor Rehabilitation Center. Rain, mixed with sleet, plastered her dark hair to her forehead, and her breath puffed out in small, white clouds.  Alderney Manor, a sprawling, ivy-covered Victorian mansion perched on a bluff overlooking the churning grey Atlantic, was both grand and imposing. Its Gothic architecture, complete with gargoyles and arched windows, hinted at stories whispered within its thick stone walls.  Eleanor, despite having worked there for three years, still felt a prickle of unease whenever she approached the heavy oak doors. This morning, the feeling was particularly acute, a sense of foreboding that went beyond the dismal weather.  She had just received a call from the administrator about a new patient, a John Doe with a mysterious past and a baffling injury, and Eleanor, known for her calm demeanor and unflappable professionalism, found herself oddly unsettled.
