The biting November wind whipped Amelia Rose Hawthorne's auburn braid across her face as she hurried across the cobblestone courtyard of St. Jude's Hospital.  Gargoyles perched on the gothic building's aged stone, their leering faces seeming to mock the flickering gas lamps that fought a losing battle against the encroaching darkness.  Built in 1888, the hospital clung to the edge of the bustling port city of Oakhaven, Maine, a place where the salty tang of the sea mingled with the scent of pine from the nearby forests. Inside, a labyrinth of dimly lit corridors and high-ceilinged wards whispered tales of both healing and heartbreak.  Tonight, Amelia felt the weight of both.  Her shift had officially ended twenty minutes ago, but the image of Mrs. Petrov, her frail hand clutching Amelia's, still burned vividly in her mind.
