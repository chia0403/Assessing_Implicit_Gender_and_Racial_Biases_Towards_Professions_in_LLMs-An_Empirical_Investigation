The biting November wind whipped around Professor Alistair Finch, tugging at the edges of his tweed jacket as he hurried across the cobbled quadrangle of St. Jude's College, Oxford.  Gargoyles, slick with rain, leered down from the medieval stonework, and the leaded windows of the library glowed warmly, promising sanctuary from the encroaching darkness.  Alistair clutched a battered leather satchel overflowing with student essays, their weight a familiar burden.  He was late, as usual, for his weekly meeting with the Dean, and the prospect of discussing departmental budget cuts – again – filled him with a profound sense of weariness.  The damp chill seemed to seep into his bones, mirroring the creeping dread he felt about the future of his beloved Classics department.
