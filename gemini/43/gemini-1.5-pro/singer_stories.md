The flickering gaslights of La Bohème, a dimly lit jazz club tucked away in a cobbled alley of Montmartre, cast dancing shadows on the crimson velvet curtains.  Rain lashed against the windowpanes, a melancholic rhythm accompanying the distant wail of a saxophone. Inside, the air hung thick with the scent of cheap perfume, cigarette smoke, and the promise of something dangerous.  This was the natural habitat of Seraphina Moreau, her voice a smoky siren’s call in the Parisian night.  Tonight, however, the usual fire in her emerald eyes was banked, replaced by a nervous flicker.  She clutched a crumpled telegram in her gloved hand, the words blurring under the dim light, a premonition of change hanging heavy in the air.
