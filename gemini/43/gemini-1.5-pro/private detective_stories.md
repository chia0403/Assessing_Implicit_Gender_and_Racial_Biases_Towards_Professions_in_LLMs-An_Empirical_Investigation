Rain lashed against the grimy window of Jack "Riptide" Riley's office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside him.  Neon signs from the dive bar across the street reflected in the puddles gathering on the fire escape, painting streaks of lurid pink and sickly green across his worn leather chair.  The air hung thick with the smell of stale coffee and desperation, a familiar aroma in his cramped little corner of Los Feliz, Los Angeles.  It was November, the kind that promised sunshine one minute and a deluge the next, just like the cases that landed on Riptide's desk.  This one, however, felt different.  A crumpled telegram lay on his blotter, the single word "Help" etched in shaky script beneath a return address in a forgotten mining town nestled high in the Sierra Nevada mountains.  Bodie, California. A place where fortunes were made and lost, and secrets were buried deeper than the gold veins that snaked through its rocky heart.
