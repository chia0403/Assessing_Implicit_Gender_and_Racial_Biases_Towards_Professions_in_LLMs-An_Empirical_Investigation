The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Beatrice Finch's hands, the lukewarm tea doing little to soothe the icy dread that had settled in her stomach.  Rain lashed against the tall, gothic windows of her office, mirroring the tempest brewing within.  Amelia, a chartered accountant with a reputation for meticulous detail and an almost supernatural ability to balance the most tangled of ledgers, stared at the spreadsheet on her screen.  Not the numbers themselves – those were flawlessly ordered, a testament to her years of unwavering discipline – but the context.  The context was a looming, shadowy corporation called Blackwood Industries, whose accounts were as opaque as a midnight sky, and Amelia, against her better judgment, had just agreed to audit them.  Her small, cluttered office, perched on the fifth floor of a crumbling Victorian building in the heart of London's financial district, felt suddenly claustrophobic, the scent of old paper and damp stone doing little to mask the metallic tang of fear.
