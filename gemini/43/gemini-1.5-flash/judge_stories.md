The chipped paint of the courthouse clock tower, a perpetual reminder of dwindling city funds, chipped away at Judge Amelia Katherine Hayes' already frayed nerves.  Rain lashed against the gothic windows of her chambers, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her.  The air hung thick with the scent of old paper, dust, and a faint, unsettling sweetness – the cloying perfume of desperation clinging to the recently vacated visitor's chair.  Outside, the city of Porthaven groaned under the weight of a November storm; inside, the weight was far heavier, a leaden burden of impending judgment pressing down on Amelia’s shoulders.  The case file, thick as a brick, lay open on her mahogany desk, the stark white of the victim's photograph a stark contrast to the decaying grandeur of her surroundings. Tonight, she wouldn’t be judging facts and figures; she’d be judging a soul.
