The late afternoon sun, a bruised plum colour, bled across the terracotta roofs of Santa Barbara.  Palm fronds swayed languidly in the offshore breeze, their whispers a counterpoint to the rhythmic crash of the Pacific against the cliffs below.  From her perch on the balcony of the sprawling Spanish-style mansion, Amelia Katherine “Amy” Hernandez surveyed her kingdom – or at least, the lucrative sliver of it she was currently tasked with selling.  The scent of jasmine and salt hung heavy in the air, a fragrant backdrop to the subtle anxiety that gnawed at her.  This wasn't just another million-dollar listing; this was *the* listing, the one that could make or break her fledgling real estate career.  The pressure, like the Santa Barbara fog, was rolling in.
