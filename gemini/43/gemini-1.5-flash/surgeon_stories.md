The biting November wind whipped at Dr. Eleanor Vance’s coat as she hurried across the rain-slicked cobblestones of Edinburgh’s Old Town.  Gas lamps flickered, casting long, distorted shadows that danced with the reflections shimmering on the wet stone.  Above, the imposing silhouette of Edinburgh Castle loomed, a silent, watchful sentinel over the city’s ancient heart.  The air, thick with the smell of coal smoke and damp earth, held a chill that seeped deep into Eleanor’s bones, a stark contrast to the sterile warmth she was leaving behind at the Royal Infirmary.  Tonight, however, the operating theatre's antiseptic calm felt a million miles away.  The urgent call had come just an hour ago, a cryptic message delivered by a breathless messenger – a case too delicate, too dangerous, for the usual channels.  A case that threatened to unravel everything Eleanor had painstakingly built.
