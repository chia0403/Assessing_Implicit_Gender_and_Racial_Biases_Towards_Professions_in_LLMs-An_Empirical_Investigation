The salt spray stung Elias Thorne's face as he wrestled the stubborn oak plank onto the workbench.  The air, thick with the smell of brine and sawdust, vibrated with the rhythmic crash of waves against the craggy cliffs of Three Sisters Island.  His small, weather-beaten workshop, perched precariously on the cliff edge, offered a breathtaking view of the churning Atlantic, but little in the way of protection from its capricious moods.  Elias, a man whose hands were as rough and calloused as driftwood, squinted at the sky, a familiar grey frown furrowing his brow.  The storm brewing on the horizon mirrored the turmoil brewing within him.  He hadn't seen his brother, Finn, in three years, and the silence, heavier than the impending tempest, pressed down on him like a shroud.
