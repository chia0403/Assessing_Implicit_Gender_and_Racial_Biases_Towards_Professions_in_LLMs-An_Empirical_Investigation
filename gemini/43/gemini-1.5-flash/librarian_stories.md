The scent of aged paper and leather, a perfume both comforting and slightly melancholic, hung heavy in the air of the Grand Archives of Alexandria – or what remained of them.  Dust motes, illuminated by the weak afternoon sun filtering through the arched windows, danced in the stillness.  Elara Vance, a woman whose life had been quietly dedicated to the preservation of forgotten stories, traced a finger along the spine of a crumbling papyrus scroll, its hieroglyphs barely visible beneath centuries of accumulated grime.  Outside, the relentless desert wind howled a mournful dirge, a stark contrast to the hushed reverence within the crumbling stone walls.  Elara, however, felt no such reverence today.  A creeping dread, cold and sharp as the desert night, had settled in her bones, a feeling far more unsettling than the usual anxieties of a librarian safeguarding irreplaceable artifacts.  For the Serpent’s Eye, a legendary amulet rumoured to grant its owner unimaginable power, had been stolen. And Elara knew, with a chilling certainty, that she was the only one who could find it.
