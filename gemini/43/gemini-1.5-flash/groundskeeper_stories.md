The biting November wind whipped across the manicured lawns of Blackwood Manor, carrying with it the scent of damp earth and decaying leaves.  Fallen branches, slick with rain, littered the meticulously gravelled pathways, a testament to the recent storm.  Elias Thorne, his breath misting in the frigid air, surveyed the damage with a practiced eye.  His thick woolen scarf barely kept the chill from creeping into his bones, but the rhythmic crunch of his boots on the gravel was a comforting counterpoint to the howling wind.  Blackwood Manor, a gothic behemoth of grey stone and shadowed turrets, loomed over him, a silent, watchful presence in the deepening twilight.  Elias, groundskeeper for the estate for the past fifteen years, knew every twist of its ancient pathways, every gnarled branch of its ancient oaks, every secret whispered by the wind through its overgrown hedges.  Tonight, however, even the familiar comfort of his work felt shadowed by an unusual unease.
