The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s hair across her face, stinging her cheeks with icy rain.  She tugged her worn wool coat tighter, the collar scratching against her already chapped skin.  The scent of brine and decaying seaweed hung heavy in the air, a familiar perfume in the perpetually overcast coastal town of Port Blossom, Maine.  Amelia shivered, not entirely from the cold.  A tremor of unease ran deeper, a subtle discordance that vibrated beneath the rhythmic crash of waves against the jagged rocks lining the shore.  She clutched the worn leather notepad in her gloved hand, its pages filled with the frantic scrawl of interview notes, half-formed theories, and the chillingly cryptic last words of Elias Thorne: "The lighthouse… knows…"
