The chipped paint of the windowsill dug into Dr. Eleanor Vance’s elbow as she stared out at the relentless drizzle blanketing Ashwood High.  November in Oregon was always a damp, grey affair, but this year felt particularly oppressive, mirroring the unsettling weight settling in her stomach.  The air hung heavy with the scent of damp earth and something else… something faintly metallic, like old blood.  Ashwood High, a sprawling brick behemoth clinging precariously to the hillside overlooking the churning Pacific, wasn't just soaked; it seemed to be drowning in unspoken anxieties. Eleanor, a school psychologist with ten years’ experience navigating the turbulent waters of adolescent emotions, felt the undercurrent of this particular storm more acutely than usual.  It wasn't the usual teenage angst; this was something deeper, a collective unease that vibrated in the very foundations of the building.
