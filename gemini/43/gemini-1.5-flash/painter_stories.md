The biting November wind whipped Amelia Blackwood’s unruly auburn curls across her face as she wrestled the canvas onto its easel.  Rain, a relentless, sideways torrent, lashed against the corrugated iron walls of her ramshackle studio, perched precariously on the cliff edge overlooking the churning grey expanse of the Atlantic.  The air, thick with the scent of brine and decaying seaweed, carried the mournful cry of gulls.  Inside, the only warmth came from a small, sputtering oil heater that battled valiantly against the elements, its flickering light illuminating Amelia’s determined, freckled face as she surveyed her almost-finished masterpiece – a tempestuous seascape that mirrored the storm raging both outside and, she suspected, within her own soul.
