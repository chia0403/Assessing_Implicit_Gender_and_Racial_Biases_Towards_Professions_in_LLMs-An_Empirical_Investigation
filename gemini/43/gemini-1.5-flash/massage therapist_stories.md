The rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of Serenity's Touch, a rhythm that mirrored the frantic beat of Elara Vance's heart.  Elara, a massage therapist with hands as deft as a surgeon's and a spirit as weathered as the ancient redwood dominating the vista outside her tiny coastal Oregon studio, stared out at the churning grey Pacific.  The wind howled a mournful counterpoint to the drumming rain, whipping sprays of saltwater against the panoramic window, blurring the already indistinct outline of the jagged cliffs. Inside, the air hung thick with the scent of lavender and cedarwood, a stark contrast to the wild tempest raging outside.  The only light came from a single, flickering oil lamp on the reception desk, casting long, dancing shadows across the polished wooden floor.  Tonight, however, the usual calming atmosphere felt oppressive, a suffocating blanket woven from the storm and a growing unease that settled deep in Elara's bones.
