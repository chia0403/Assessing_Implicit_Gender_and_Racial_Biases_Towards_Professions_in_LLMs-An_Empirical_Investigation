The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Mia” Hawthorne’s hair across her face, stinging her cheeks a vibrant crimson that clashed spectacularly with the muted greys of the Cornish coastline.  Salt spray, thick as pea soup, plastered itself to her already damp parka as she wrestled with her tripod, its legs sinking slightly into the sodden earth.  Below her, the churning Atlantic roared against the jagged teeth of the rocks, a tempestuous symphony accompanying the mournful cry of gulls circling overhead.  The dramatic sky, a canvas smeared with bruised purples and angry oranges, promised a breathtaking sunset, but the conditions were brutal, even for a seasoned photographer like Mia.  This was Porthcurno, famed for its turquoise waters and tranquil beaches in summer, but tonight, it was a wild, untamed beast, and Mia intended to capture its untamed beauty.
