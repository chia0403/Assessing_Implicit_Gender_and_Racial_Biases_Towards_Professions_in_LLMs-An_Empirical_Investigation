The brass nameplate on the mahogany desk gleamed under the soft, amber light of the Grand Majestic Hotel lobby:  *Augustine “Gus” Moreau, Concierge*.  Rain lashed against the arched windows, a relentless drumming that mirrored the persistent thrum of anxiety in Gus’s chest.  Outside, the Parisian night was a swirling canvas of black and grey, the Eiffel Tower a distant, shadowy silhouette against the tempest. Inside, the Grand Majestic offered a haven of opulent calm; plush carpets muffled footsteps, the scent of lilies and old leather hung heavy in the air, and a crackling fire in the hearth cast flickering shadows on the priceless tapestries that adorned the walls.  But Gus felt anything but calm. Tonight, the usual requests for opera tickets and Michelin-starred reservations were overshadowed by a far more unsettling matter.  A crumpled, anonymous note lay on his desk, its edges damp from the rain, bearing a single, chilling sentence scrawled in uneven script: *The raven remembers.*
