Professor Augustine Rhys-Davies shivered, despite the thick tweed of his jacket.  The November wind, a raw, biting thing that clawed at the exposed stonework of Blackwood Manor, whipped around him, carrying the scent of damp earth and decaying leaves.  Rain, a relentless, icy drizzle, plastered his unruly grey hair to his scalp.  Blackwood Manor, perched precariously on a windswept cliff overlooking the churning grey expanse of the North Sea, was, to put it mildly, unwelcoming.  Even the gargoyles perched on the parapets seemed to sneer at the approaching figure, their stone faces slick with moisture and gloom.  Tonight, however, Professor Rhys-Davies felt a thrill that superseded the chill. Tonight, he was finally going to uncover the truth behind the Blackwood Curse.
