The chipped paint of the guidance counselor's office door flaked like disappointed dreams.  Inside, Eleanor Vance, M.Ed., sat perched on the edge of her worn, teal chair, the faint scent of stale coffee and teenage angst clinging to the air like a stubborn fog.  Sunlight, weak and watery from the November sky, barely penetrated the dusty blinds, casting long shadows across the room.  The walls, once a cheerful yellow, were now a muted ochre, mirroring the subdued energy of Northwood High, a place where hope often seemed as fragile as a butterfly's wing.  Eleanor sighed, the sound a tiny tremor in the otherwise silent room, and tapped a pen against the overflowing in-tray.  Another Monday. Another mountain of anxieties, academic struggles, and first-love heartbreaks to navigate.  This was her life, and, truth be told, despite the chaos, she wouldn't have it any other way.
