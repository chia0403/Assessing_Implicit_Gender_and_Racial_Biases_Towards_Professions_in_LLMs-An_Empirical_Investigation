The chipped porcelain mug warmed Dr. Eleanor Vance’s hands, the lukewarm chamomile tea doing little to soothe the icy dread that had settled in her stomach.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of her office, blurring the already indistinct cityscape of Seattle.  Grey, bruised clouds hung low, mirroring the emotional storm brewing within her latest patient, a young woman named Clara whose fragmented memories painted a picture far darker than the Seattle drizzle outside.  Eleanor’s office, perched on the 27th floor of the Rainier Tower, usually offered a breathtaking view, a calming expanse of water and mountains. Tonight, however, the vista felt oppressive, a vast, indifferent witness to the human suffering unfolding before her.  The only sound louder than the tempestuous rain was the rhythmic ticking of the grandfather clock in the corner, each second a tiny hammer blow against Eleanor’s growing unease.
