The biting November wind whipped Amelia "Mia" Hawthorne’s auburn braid across her face as she wrestled a recalcitrant bale of hay.  The scent of damp earth and pine needles hung heavy in the air, mingling with the sharper tang of woodsmoke drifting from the distant farmhouse chimneys.  Mia, head zookeeper at the sprawling, slightly ramshackle Blackwood Wildlife Sanctuary, was already battling a losing war against the encroaching twilight.  The sanctuary, nestled deep within the whispering pines of the Oregon Cascades, was a breathtakingly beautiful yet unforgiving place, a testament to both nature's grandeur and its relentless, unpredictable power. Tonight, that power felt particularly potent. The first snowflakes of the season, tiny, hesitant crystals, danced in the dying light, promising a long, harsh winter ahead – a winter that would test both Mia and the animals under her care to their limits.
