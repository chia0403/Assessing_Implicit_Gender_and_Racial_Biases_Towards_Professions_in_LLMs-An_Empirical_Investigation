The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Holloway’s dark hair across her face as she wrestled the stretcher into the back of the ambulance.  Sirens wailed a mournful counterpoint to the city’s usual cacophony – a symphony of screeching brakes, distant shouting, and the rhythmic thump of a thousand hurried footsteps. Rain, a relentless grey curtain, plastered itself against the already grimy windows of the emergency vehicle, blurring the already chaotic cityscape of Chicago into a smear of neon and shadow.  The air, thick with the metallic tang of exhaust fumes and the acrid bite of something burning a few blocks over, tasted of adrenaline and impending doom.  Amelia, her breath misting in the frigid air, adjusted her already damp gloves and checked her gear one last time, the practiced movements honed by years spent navigating the unforgiving streets and the even more unforgiving realities of emergency medical care in the heart of the Windy City.  Tonight, however, felt different.  Heavier.  The usual frantic energy that fuelled her work felt… muted, replaced by a prickling sense of unease that settled deep in her bones.
