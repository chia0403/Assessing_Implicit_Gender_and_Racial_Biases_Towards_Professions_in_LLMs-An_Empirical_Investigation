The biting wind howled a mournful tune through the skeletal branches of the ancient Douglas firs, their needles dusted with a fresh layer of snow that crunched under Silas Blackwood's heavy boots.  Silas, a man carved from the same granite as the mountains he worked, felt the familiar ache in his joints as he hefted his axe, the steel gleaming faintly in the weak, pre-dawn light.  The air, thick with the scent of pine and damp earth, held a preternatural stillness, broken only by the occasional snap of a twig under the weight of the snow and the far-off, lonely cry of a raven.  He stood at the edge of the Blackwood Timber Reserve, a sprawling expanse of forest that had been his family's livelihood for generations, a legacy etched into the very grain of the towering trees.  This morning, however, felt different. A cold dread, sharper than the winter wind, clung to him, whispering of a change, a shadow lurking in the deep woods beyond.
