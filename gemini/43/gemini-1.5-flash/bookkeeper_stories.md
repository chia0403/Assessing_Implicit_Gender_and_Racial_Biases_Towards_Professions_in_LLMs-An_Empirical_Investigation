The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Katherine Finch's fingers, the lukewarm tea doing little to soothe the icy dread that had settled in her stomach.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of afternoon sunlight slicing through the grimy window of her office, illuminating the swirling chaos of papers on her desk – invoices, receipts, ledgers piled haphazardly like fallen dominoes.  Amelia, a bookkeeper by trade and a worrier by nature, stared at the mountain of paperwork, the rhythmic tick-tock of the ancient grandfather clock in the corner a mocking counterpoint to the frantic thump-thump of her own heart.  Her tiny office, tucked away in the shadowy basement of Blackwood & Sons Auction House, smelled of old paper, damp earth, and the faint, lingering scent of something vaguely floral – possibly lilies, or perhaps the equally potent aroma of impending doom.
