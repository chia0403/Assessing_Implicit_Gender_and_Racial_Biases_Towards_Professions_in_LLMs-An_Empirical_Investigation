The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s hair across her face as she hurried down the cobbled street, the scent of woodsmoke and roasting chestnuts doing little to alleviate the chill that seeped into her bones.  Rain, a relentless drizzle that had been falling for days, plastered her dark coat to her back.  She pulled her worn leather satchel tighter, its contents – a battered copy of *Wuthering Heights*, a half-eaten apple, and a slightly crumpled schedule – jostling against her ribs.  The gas lamps flickered weakly, casting long, distorted shadows that danced across the rain-slicked streets of  Penhaligon, a town clinging stubbornly to the cliffs overlooking the tempestuous Irish Sea.  Amelia, a registered nurse at St. Jude’s Hospital,  pulled her collar higher, the faint glow of the hospital’s windows a beacon in the encroaching darkness.  Tonight, however, the warmth promised within those walls felt a long way off.  The storm brewing outside mirrored the one raging inside her.
