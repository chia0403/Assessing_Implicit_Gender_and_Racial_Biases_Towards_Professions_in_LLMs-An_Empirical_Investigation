The dawn mist clung to the manicured lawns of Willow Creek Estates like a shroud, dampening Elias Thorne’s already grumpy mood.  Elias, a man whose hands were more accustomed to the feel of loam than silk, squinted at the pre-dawn gloom.  His battered pickup truck, affectionately nicknamed "The Green Machine," rumbled to a halt at the entrance to the exclusive development, its headlights cutting through the clinging fog.  Willow Creek Estates wasn't just another job; it was a gilded cage of perfectly pruned hedges and impossibly green grass, a stark contrast to the rough-and-tumble landscape of Elias's own life.  He wrestled his thermos of lukewarm coffee from the passenger seat, the condensation already slick on its surface, mirroring the beads of sweat forming on his forehead despite the chill.  Today's task:  the infamous Peterson estate, a sprawling monstrosity that demanded meticulous attention to detail – and, Elias suspected, a hefty dose of patience.
