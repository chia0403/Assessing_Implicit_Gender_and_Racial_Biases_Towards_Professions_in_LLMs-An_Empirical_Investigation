The air hung thick and sweet with the cloying scent of jasmine and exhaust fumes, a fragrance uniquely Hanoi.  Thirty-degree heat shimmered off the cracked asphalt of Hang Bac Street, turning the already chaotic traffic into a blurry, impressionistic painting of motorbikes, cyclo-rickshaws, and the occasional lumbering ox-cart.  Sweat beaded on the forehead of Nguyen Thi Lan Anh, clinging to the fine strands of her black hair plastered to her neck.  Lan Anh adjusted the meticulously knotted silk scarf around her throat, a small act of defiance against the oppressive humidity and the even more oppressive weight of her sales quota.  This month, the target was particularly brutal, and the looming deadline pressed down on her like the humid air itself.  Her briefcase, a battered but sturdy leather number, felt heavier than usual, its contents – a collection of exquisite hand-crafted silver jewelry – representing not just potential profit, but her family's livelihood.
