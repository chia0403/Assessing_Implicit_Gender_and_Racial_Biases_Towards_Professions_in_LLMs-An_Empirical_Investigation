The chipped paint of the fire escape flaked onto Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s worn leather notepad, a tiny, rust-colored shower in the oppressive July humidity. Below, the streets of New Orleans pulsed with a syncopated rhythm of car horns, jazz spilling from open doorways, and the insistent drone of cicadas – a soundtrack to the simmering heat and Amelia’s simmering frustration.  The air hung thick and heavy, laden with the scent of beignets and something else… something acrid, metallic, that clung to the back of her throat like the taste of cheap bourbon.  This wasn't the romantic, storybook New Orleans of tourist brochures; this was the city’s underbelly, where secrets festered in the shadows of wrought-iron balconies and the decaying grandeur of antebellum mansions.  And Amelia, a reporter for the *New Orleans Gazette*, was about to delve right into it.
