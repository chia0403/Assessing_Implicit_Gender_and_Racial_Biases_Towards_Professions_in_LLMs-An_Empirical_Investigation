The air hung thick and humid, a clinging blanket woven from the scent of jasmine and exhaust fumes.  Neon signs, fractured and flickering, cast a lurid glow on the rain-slicked streets of Ho Chi Minh City.  Inside, however, the air conditioning hummed a soothing counterpoint to the urban chaos.  Nguyen Thi Phuong Anh, or Phuong Anh as she preferred, sat hunched over her keyboard, the glow of three monitors illuminating her face, a landscape of sharp angles and determined concentration.  Her small apartment, perched precariously on the seventh floor of a crumbling building, was a stark contrast to the vibrant, chaotic city below.  Empty ramen containers and half-drunk cans of energy drink littered her desk, silent witnesses to countless hours spent wrestling with lines of code. Tonight, however, the struggle was different. Tonight, the code fought back.
