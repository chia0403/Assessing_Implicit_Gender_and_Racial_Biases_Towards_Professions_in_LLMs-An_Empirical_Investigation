The chipped porcelain teacup warmed Amelia Katherine Finch’s hands, a stark contrast to the November chill seeping through the drafty windows of her office. Rain lashed against the panes, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her.  Amelia, a woman whose sensible beige cardigan and sensible brown shoes perfectly matched her sensible life as an insurance agent for “Finch & Finch, Protecting Your Future,” was anything but sensible right now.  Her office, tucked away on the third floor of a crumbling Victorian building in the perpetually damp coastal town of Seabrook, usually smelled of old paper and faintly of lemon polish. Today, however, the air hung thick with the scent of impending doom – or at least, a very, very large insurance claim.  The swirling grey sky outside seemed to press down on the already claustrophobic space, filled as it was with towering stacks of files detailing the anxieties and misfortunes of Seabrook’s inhabitants.  Tonight, Amelia suspected, she would be adding another hefty file to the precarious tower.
