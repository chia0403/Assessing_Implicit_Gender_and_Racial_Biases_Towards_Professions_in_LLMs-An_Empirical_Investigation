The chipped ceramic mug warmed Amelia Katherine Worthington’s hands, the lukewarm tea doing little to soothe the icy dread that had settled in her stomach.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of Mr. Finch’s office, blurring the already indistinct cityscape of London.  Amelia, a secretary whose meticulous nature was only surpassed by her unwavering loyalty to the eccentric, if undeniably brilliant, Professor Finch, stared out at the tempest.  The air in the office, normally thick with the scent of old paper and pipe tobacco, was strangely still, heavy with an unspoken tension that prickled at her skin.  This wasn't just the usual pre-storm anxiety; this felt different.  This felt… ominous.  The grandfather clock in the corner ticked with exaggerated loudness, each chime a hammer blow against the already fragile calm.  Outside, a particularly ferocious gust of wind howled, a mournful cry that mirrored the unease twisting in Amelia's gut.  Tonight, something was wrong, terribly wrong.
