The scent of old paper and simmering cardamom hung heavy in the air, a peculiar perfume for a travel agency, but then, Anya Petrova’s “Wanderlust Voyages” wasn't your average brochure-slinging operation.  Dust motes danced in the afternoon sunbeams slanting through the arched windows of her shop, illuminating shelves crammed with antique globes, faded maps rolled tighter than ancient scrolls, and a disconcerting number of miniature ceramic elephants.  Outside, the cobblestones of Prague's Old Town shimmered under a relentless summer heat, the clatter of horse-drawn carriages a rhythmic counterpoint to the gentle whir of Anya's ancient, but perfectly functioning, typewriter.  Today, however, the usual peaceful rhythm of her workday was shattered by a frantic ringing of the telephone, a jarring peal that echoed the turmoil brewing in Anya's own surprisingly restless heart.
