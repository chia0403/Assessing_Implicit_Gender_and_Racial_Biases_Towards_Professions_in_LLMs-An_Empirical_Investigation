The chipped paint of the probation office's waiting room smelled faintly of stale coffee and simmering resentment.  Rain lashed against the grimy windows of the building, mirroring the tempest brewing inside Amelia Hernandez.  It was a Thursday, the day her caseload always felt the heaviest, a day she usually spent juggling paperwork and the precarious balance of keeping her clients on the straight and narrow.  But today, something felt different.  A low hum of anxiety vibrated beneath the surface of the usual chaotic quiet, a discordant note that even the rhythmic tick-tock of the ancient wall clock couldn't mask.  Amelia, a woman whose weary eyes held the weight of a thousand broken promises and second chances, straightened her already impeccable blazer and steeled herself.  Another day, another battle in the never-ending war against recidivism.  The first client was already late.
