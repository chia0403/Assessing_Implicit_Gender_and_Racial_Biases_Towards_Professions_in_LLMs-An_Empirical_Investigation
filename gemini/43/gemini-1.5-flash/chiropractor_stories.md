The salt spray stung Dr. Eleanor Vance's face as she wrestled the stubborn fishing net onto the weathered wooden dock.  The air, thick with the smell of brine and seaweed, whipped her dark braid across her cheek.  It was a far cry from the sterile, calming scent of her chiropractic clinic back in bustling Seaside, Oregon, a place where the loudest sound was usually the gentle whir of the electric massage table.  Here, on the windswept islet of Three Sisters, the only sounds were the relentless crash of waves against the rocks and the mournful cry of gulls overhead.  The tiny, ramshackle cottage she'd rented for the summer, perched precariously on the cliff edge, offered a breathtaking, if slightly terrifying, view of the churning Pacific.  This wasn't a vacation; this was a desperate attempt to escape the mounting pressure of her practice, the endless back pain and the crushing weight of responsibility, all while trying to reconnect with a long-lost part of herself.
