The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Mia” Holloway’s auburn hair across her face as she hurried down Bleecker Street, the scent of roasting chestnuts and damp asphalt clinging to the chill air.  Rain, a relentless drizzle that had been falling since dawn, plastered her trench coat to her slender frame.  Overhead, the gas lamps of Greenwich Village cast a hazy, orange glow on the cobblestones, barely piercing the pre-winter gloom. Mia, senior editor at the prestigious literary magazine *The Gilded Quill*, pulled her scarf tighter, the wool scratching against her already chapped skin.  The deadline for the December issue loomed, a monstrous, paper-cut-inflicted beast gnawing at her composure.  And tonight, of all nights, she was late.  Terribly, unforgivably late.  The manuscript she’d been agonizing over for the past three months – a sprawling, gothic novel promising both literary brilliance and commercial success – was waiting for her, nestled amongst the overflowing piles of rejected submissions on her desk in the magazine's cramped, attic office.
