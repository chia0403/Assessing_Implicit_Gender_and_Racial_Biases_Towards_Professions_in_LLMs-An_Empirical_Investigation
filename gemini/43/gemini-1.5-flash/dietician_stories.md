The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Hernandez’s hands, the lukewarm chamomile tea doing little to soothe the icy dread creeping up her spine.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of her office, blurring the already hazy cityscape of Seattle sprawled beneath.  Amelia, a registered dietician with a penchant for meticulously organized spreadsheets and a growing aversion to kale smoothies, stared out at the tempestuous grey.  Her office, nestled on the 27th floor of the Emerald Tower, usually offered breathtaking views of Puget Sound – today, it felt more like a prison cell overlooking a churning, unforgiving sea.  The storm mirrored the turmoil brewing inside her.  It wasn't the cancelled client appointments, nor the overflowing inbox, that plagued her; it was the cryptic, anonymous note delivered that morning, its message scrawled in what looked suspiciously like dried blood:  "Your secrets are not safe."
