The scent of old paper and lemon polish hung heavy in the air, a comforting aroma to Amelia Beatrice Hawthorne. Sunlight, filtered through the towering Gothic windows of the Blackwood Library, painted stripes across the mahogany shelves, illuminating dust motes dancing in the stillness. Outside, the October wind howled a mournful song around the eaves of the centuries-old building, a sound that usually brought a shiver of delightful spookiness to Amelia's spine. Today, however, it felt more like a warning.

Amelia Beatrice Hawthorne, Head Librarian of the esteemed Blackwood Library, stood perched precariously on a rolling ladder, her sensible brown Oxfords gripping the rungs. She was reaching for a particularly large and ornately bound tome on the top shelf of the Restricted Section - a collection of texts so ancient and potentially volatile, they required special permission to access, even for her. The book's title, barely discernible beneath layers of grime and the faded gilt lettering, seemed to writhe in the dim light: *Liber Umbrarum*. Book of Shadows. The very name sent a prickle of unease down her neck, a feeling amplified by the sudden, uncharacteristic silence outside. The wind had stopped as abruptly as a slammed door.
