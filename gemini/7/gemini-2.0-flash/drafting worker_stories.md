**Setting:**

The year is 1954. Silas works in the sprawling, somewhat dilapidated drafting room of "Abernathey and Sons Engineering," a company specializing in the design and construction of bridges and other public works projects. The room itself is a cavernous space in an old brick building downtown, a relic from a bygone era of industry. Rows of sturdy oak drafting tables, each illuminated by a gooseneck lamp with a slightly flickering bulb, dominate the space. Rolled-up blueprints, secured with faded rubber bands, are stacked haphazardly in corners. Shelves crammed with technical manuals, slide rules, and well-worn textbooks line the walls. The air is thick with the smell of paper, ink, and the aforementioned ammonia from the blueprint copier. It's a place of meticulous detail, of precise lines and calculations, but also a place steeped in a comfortable, almost nostalgic, stagnation. A large, panoramic window at the far end of the room offers a view of the grimy cityscape, dominated by the skeletal frame of a new skyscraper slowly rising from the ground, a symbol of progress that feels both exciting and unsettling to Silas.

**Character Introduction:**

**Silas Bartholomew Finch** was a man of meticulous habits and quiet observation. At 32, he was a mid-level drafting worker at Abernathey and Sons.  He wasn't brilliant, but he was dependable, a master of the T-square and triangle. He was comfortable in his routine, finding a quiet satisfaction in the precise execution of his drawings, in translating the bold ideas of the engineers into concrete plans. He wasn't ambitious, content to blend into the background, a cog in the machine of progress. But beneath his unassuming exterior lay a sharp mind and a keen eye for detail, qualities that had often gone unnoticed but that would soon be put to the test. He usually keeps to himself, but beneath his quiet exterior lurked a keen intelligence and a moral compass that had always pointed true north, a compass that was about to spin wildly out of control.
