**Main Character:** Bartholomew "Bart" Thistlewick

**Opening:**

The bell above the door of Thistlewick & Son jingled a mournful tune, a sound as familiar to Bartholomew "Bart" Thistlewick as the prick of a needle or the rustle of fine silk. He didn’t even bother looking up from the intricate embroidery he was painstakingly adding to the sleeve of a velvet smoking jacket – crimson roses, each petal painstakingly shaded. It was a commission for Lord Ashworth, a man whose only discernible talent was accruing debt and demanding increasingly outlandish embellishments for his already extravagant wardrobe. Bart sighed, pulling the thread taut. The Ashworth account was lucrative, yes, but draining. It was hard to remember why he'd taken over the family business, which his late father had intended as Thistlewick & *Sons*, a dream quickly dashed when Bart discovered a talent for textiles and his brother for the bottle. He was a master craftsman, undeniably, but sometimes he felt like a gilded cage canary, stitching his life away in threads of silk and societal expectation. Today, the air hung heavy with the scent of beeswax, old wool, and a lingering hint of rain – a perfect brewing ground for discontent. Little did he know, a storm of a different kind was about to walk through that door, one that had nothing to do with damp weather.

**Setting:**

Thistlewick & Son, established in 1888, occupies a prime location on Willow Street in the bustling heart of Oakhaven, a fictional city nestled in the rolling hills of the English countryside. The shop itself is a study in quiet elegance, a testament to the enduring quality of bespoke tailoring. Dark mahogany shelves lined the walls, overflowing with bolts of fabric: tweeds from Scotland, silks from the Orient, linens from Ireland, and velvets in every shade imaginable. Mannequins draped in partially finished garments stand like silent observers, their stoic expressions reflecting the shop’s hushed atmosphere. Sunlight filters through the large window at the front, illuminating dust motes dancing in the air and highlighting the gleaming surfaces of antique sewing machines, passed down through generations. In the back room, Bart’s workspace is a whirlwind of organized chaos: spools of thread scattered across his workbench, patterns pinned to corkboards, and half-finished projects draped over chairs. The aroma of beeswax and lanolin hangs in the air, a constant reminder of the meticulous process of creating clothing that is both beautiful and functional. Oakhaven itself is a city of quiet charm, a place where tradition and modernity mingle. Grand estates overlook cobbled streets, and the sounds of horse-drawn carriages mix with the hum of early automobiles. Thistlewick & Son is a fixture in the community, a place where gentlemen and ladies alike come to be fitted for garments that reflect their status and personality.
