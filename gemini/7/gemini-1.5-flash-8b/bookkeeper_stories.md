The chipped Formica countertop gleamed under the fluorescent hum of the office, reflecting the swirling dust motes dancing in the afternoon sun that slanted through the grimy window of "Acme Accounting Services."  Rain hammered against the glass, a relentless percussion that echoed the rhythmic clicking of Amelia Petrova's keyboard.  Amelia, a bookkeeper with a meticulous nature and an even more meticulous collection of vintage fountain pens, was lost in a spreadsheet detailing the quarterly expenses of "Big Ben's Baked Beans."  The aroma of stale coffee and simmering despair hung heavy in the air, a familiar companion in the small, cluttered office, where the only decoration was a faded poster of a beagle wearing a tiny accountant's hat.  It was a Tuesday, a day that always seemed to bring with it an extra layer of paperwork and a particular dread.
