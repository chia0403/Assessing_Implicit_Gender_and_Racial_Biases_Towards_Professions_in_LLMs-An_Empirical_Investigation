The biting wind whipped Amelia "Am" Cavendish's scarlet scarf around her neck, stinging her cheeks like a thousand tiny needles.  The crisp autumn air, thick with the scent of pine and damp earth, hung heavy over the desolate expanse of the Blackwood Forest.  Jagged peaks of the Razorback Mountains pierced the bruised sky, a stark backdrop to the lone figure huddled beside the crumbling stone ruins of the old Gryphon's Keep.  Am, her breath puffing out in white clouds, tightened her grip on the worn leather-bound journal nestled in her satchel.  This was not a place for pleasant walks; it was a place for answers.  The faded, almost illegible script within the journal held the only clue she had left to track down the lost members of the Phoenix Flyers, her elite team of aerial acrobats.  Am, a woman hardened by years of demanding training regimes and the unpredictable nature of the soaring sport, refused to surrender to the encroaching gloom.  She needed answers.  And the Blackwood Forest was determined to provide them, one chilling whisper at a time.
