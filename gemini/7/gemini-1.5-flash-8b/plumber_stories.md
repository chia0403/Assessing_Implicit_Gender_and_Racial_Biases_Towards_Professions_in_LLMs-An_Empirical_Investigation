The flickering neon sign of "Plumbers' Paradise" cast a sickly yellow glow over the rain-slicked alleyway.  A single, sputtering streetlamp illuminated the grime-caked brick walls, highlighting the overflowing dumpster and the general squalor of the backstreets surrounding the bustling downtown of Port Blossom.  This was the domain of Bartholomew "Barty" Butterfield, master plumber and unlikely hero of the city's forgotten corners.  Barty, a man whose grease-stained overalls seemed a permanent fixture, surveyed the scene from beneath the awning of his tiny shop.  A persistent drip, a sound that echoed the relentless rhythm of his life, emanated from the shadowed entrance of the building opposite.
