The chipped paint of the gazebo, peeling like sunburnt skin, reflected the bruised purple of the late afternoon sky.  A lone robin, its breast a flash of crimson against the encroaching gloom, hopped from weathered stone to weathered stone, oblivious to the quiet drama unfolding beneath the faded floral carvings.  This was Barnaby "Barty" Butterfield's domain, the sprawling, meticulously tended gardens of Blackwood Manor.  Barty, a man whose hands, gnarled and calloused from years of coaxing life from stubborn soil, spoke volumes more eloquently than his hesitant mouth, sighed.  Another day done.  Another storm brewing, not just in the sky, but within the ancient walls of the manor itself.
