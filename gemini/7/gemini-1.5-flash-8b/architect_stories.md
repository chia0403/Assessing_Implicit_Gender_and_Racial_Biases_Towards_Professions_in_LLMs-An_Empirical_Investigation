The biting wind whipped through the skeletal branches of the ancient oaks, rattling the corrugated iron shed that housed Elara Vance's architectural practice.  Dust motes danced in the lone shaft of afternoon sun slicing through the gloom, illuminating the scattered blueprints strewn across a makeshift table fashioned from a salvaged wooden pallet.  Elara Vance, her fingers stained a muted ochre from the aged paper, traced a delicate line with a worn graphite pencil.  The year was 1937.  The crumbling, red-brick buildings of the small, forgotten town of Ashhaven, nestled in the foothills of the Spine Mountains, looked up at the grey sky, waiting for her touch, her vision.  Elara, barely twenty-five, possessed a fiery passion for design, a skill forged in the crucible of her isolated upbringing.  Ashhaven, a place where time had seemingly stopped, was her canvas.
