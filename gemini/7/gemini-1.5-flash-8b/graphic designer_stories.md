Rain lashed against the windows of Elara Vance's studio, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her.  The grey light, perpetually filtered through the overcast London sky, cast long, melancholic shadows across the sprawling workspace.  Stacks of meticulously organized prints, each one a testament to a client's fleeting vision, were scattered around her.  Empty coffee cups, each a miniature tragedy of half-finished ideas, sat beside crumpled sketches and discarded mood boards. Elara, a graphic designer with a penchant for bold typography and a heart etched with artistic frustration, stared at the blank canvas of her laptop screen.  The project, a rebranding campaign for a struggling artisanal bakery, felt like a looming mountain, its peak shrouded in a perpetual fog of doubt.  She traced the chipped paint on her desk with a finger, the familiar texture a stark contrast to the vibrant designs she so painstakingly created.
