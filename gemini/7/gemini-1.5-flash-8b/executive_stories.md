The fluorescent lights of the executive boardroom hummed, a monotonous soundtrack to Amelia Sterling's mounting frustration.  Empty coffee cups, half-eaten pastries, and the scattered papers of a failed quarterly report littered the mahogany table, a grim testament to the morning's futility.  Outside, the grey London sky mirrored the leaden mood in the room.  Rain lashed against the windows of the sprawling, glass-fronted headquarters of "Global Synergies," a multinational conglomerate that Amelia, despite her anxieties, held a significant stake in.  She was, after all, the Chief Operations Officer.  But today, the future felt as bleak and damp as the weather.
