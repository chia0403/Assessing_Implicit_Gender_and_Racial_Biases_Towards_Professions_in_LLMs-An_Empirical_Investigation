The chipped porcelain sink, a faded robin's egg blue, reflected the harsh fluorescent lights of "Nails & Nonsense," casting an unnatural glow on Amelia Blackwood’s manicured hands.  Fragrant, yet somehow faintly synthetic, nail polish fumes hung heavy in the air, mingling with the faint scent of burnt coffee from the perpetually overflowing pot on the counter.  Amelia, her name a whispered secret in the hushed salon, meticulously sculpted a delicate French tip on a client’s nail, the rhythmic click of her tools the only sound louder than the hum of the refrigerator.  It was a Tuesday afternoon, the kind of day that bled into itself, grey and unremarkable, a canvas waiting for the strokes of something extraordinary.  Outside, the relentless London drizzle hammered against the shop’s frosted windows, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her.
