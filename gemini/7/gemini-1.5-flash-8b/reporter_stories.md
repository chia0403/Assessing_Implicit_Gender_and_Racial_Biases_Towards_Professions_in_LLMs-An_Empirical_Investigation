The biting November wind whipped Amelia Hayes's reporter's notebook into a frenzy against her chest, a frantic dance mirroring the chaos unfolding within the derelict factory on the edge of Blackwood.  Rust-eaten brick walls, scarred by graffiti and time, clawed at the grey sky.  Broken windows stared out like hollow eyes, reflecting the fractured light of the dying sun.  Amelia, a seasoned reporter for the Blackwood Gazette, huddled deeper into her threadbare coat, her breath misting in the frigid air.  This wasn't her usual assignment, covering town hall meetings or local bake sales.  This was the Whispering Walls, a place whispered about in hushed tones, a place where rumour and truth blurred into an unsettling fog.  The rumours had centred around a missing artist, Elias Thorne, who had vanished without a trace after working on a secretive mural within these very walls.  Amelia, determined to uncover the truth, was now knee-deep in the labyrinthine corridors of the abandoned factory, the metallic tang of decay clinging to her clothes.
