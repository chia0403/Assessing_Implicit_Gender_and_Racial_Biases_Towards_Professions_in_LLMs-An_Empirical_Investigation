The flickering neon sign of "Serenity's Touch" cast a sickly green glow on the rain-slicked cobblestones.  Water pooled in the narrow alleyway, reflecting the garish colours of the posters advertising cheap jewellery and dubious fortunes.  It was a Tuesday night, the kind of night when the city seemed to exhale a sigh of relief, and the only sounds were the rhythmic drip, drip, drip of the leaky gutter and the distant wail of a siren.  This was the perfect backdrop for Isolde Blackwood, massage therapist extraordinaire, to begin another evening of easing away the tensions of the city's weary souls.  Isolde, her name whispered with reverence among a select few, had a reputation for wielding her hands like instruments of healing, for coaxing peace from the most tempestuous souls. Tonight, though, a different kind of tension hung in the air, a tension she felt deep within herself, mirroring the palpable unease of the city.
