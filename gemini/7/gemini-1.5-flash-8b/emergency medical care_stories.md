The fluorescent hum of the emergency room thrummed beneath Amelia “Em” Carmichael’s skin, a constant, low-level vibration that mirrored the frantic energy swirling around her.  The air, thick with the smell of antiseptic and fear, clung to her like a second skin.  Outside, the city of Veridian, a sprawling metropolis clinging precariously to the slopes of the Cascade Mountains, roared with a life she often felt detached from.  Tonight, however, that roaring was amplified, translated into a cacophony of sirens echoing through the steel and glass corridors of St. Jude's Hospital.  Em, a third-year paramedic with a sharp, almost clinical, grace in her movements, surveyed the scene before her. A young man, no older than twenty, lay crumpled on a gurney, his face pale and etched with pain.  A deep gash bisected his forehead, oozing a dark crimson that stained the white sheets. A frantic tremor pulsed through his body.
