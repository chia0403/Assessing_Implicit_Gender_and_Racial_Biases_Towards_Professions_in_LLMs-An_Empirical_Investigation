The biting November wind whipped Amelia Blackwood's scarf around her neck, stinging her cheeks and threatening to tear the worn leather of her worn satchel.  Rain, the relentless grey-black kind that clung to everything, plastered the cobblestones of the Rue de l'Étoile in Paris against her boots.  The scent of damp earth and something indefinably smoky hung heavy in the air, a peculiar blend that always seemed to thicken in the city's oldest quarters.  Amelia, a journalist for the *Parisian Chronicle*, hunched deeper into her worn trench coat, searching for a flicker of warmth, or perhaps, a story.  The crumbling facade of the Hôtel des Marais, a building whispered to hold secrets as old as the city itself, loomed before her, its shadowed windows like vacant eyes staring out at the unending Parisian drizzle.  This was the heart of the Quartier Latin, a neighbourhood that breathed history and held the ghosts of countless stories, some whispered, some screamed into the centuries. Amelia knew, with a certainty that settled deep in her bones, that this particular story was waiting to be unearthed.
