The flickering fluorescent lights of the abandoned radio station hummed a mournful tune, a counterpoint to the static crackle that filled Elias Thorne's ears.  Dust motes danced in the lone shaft of sunlight slicing through the grimy window, illuminating the jumbled mess of wires, tubes, and ancient consoles that made up the station's heart.  Elias Thorne, a radio technician with calloused hands and eyes that seemed to absorb the faintest radio signals, meticulously adjusted a faded dial.  The year was 2042, and the only whispers of the outside world came through the crackling airwaves.  The sprawling metropolis of Neo-Kyoto, once a vibrant hub of communication, was now a ghost city, cloaked in silence, save for the occasional, cryptic transmissions Elias intercepted on his antiquated equipment.  His purpose, his only tether to what was once normal, was to keep the faintest echoes of communication alive, to maintain a thread of connection in a world gone silent.
