The salt spray kissed Elara Aurelia Thorne's face as she perched precariously on the cliff edge, her easel braced against a gust of wind that threatened to send her tumbling into the churning turquoise waters below. The Isle of Aisling, a jagged emerald claw reaching out from the wild Atlantic, was her muse, her prison, and her sanctuary all at once.  Grey, stone cottages clung to the lower slopes, huddled together as if for warmth against the ceaseless wind, while above them, the cliffs soared, crowned with a tangle of sea thrift and heather. Today, the light was particularly dramatic, the usual pearly grey sky slashed with streaks of violet and bruised orange, promising a storm. Elara, unfazed, dipped her brush into a swirl of cerulean blue, her intent gaze fixed on the tempestuous meeting of sky and sea.
