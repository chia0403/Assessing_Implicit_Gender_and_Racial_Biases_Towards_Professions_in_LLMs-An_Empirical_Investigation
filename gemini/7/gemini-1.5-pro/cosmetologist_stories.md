The pungent aroma of perm solution hung heavy in the air, mingling with the sweet scent of hairspray and the faint, metallic tang of freshly sharpened scissors. Rain lashed against the large window of "Curl Up & Dye," obscuring the view of the usually bustling Main Street of Harmony Creek, Georgia.  Inside, the salon was a vibrant splash of color against the grey afternoon, a kaleidoscope of turquoise chairs, flamingo pink dryers, and lime green retail shelves. Eleanor Rose Cartwright, her auburn hair pulled back in a messy bun secured with a leopard-print scrunchie, sighed and tapped a perfectly manicured nail against the worn countertop.  Business had been slow all day, slower even than usual for a Tuesday, and Eleanor was beginning to think the only curls happening today would be the ones forming in her stomach from hunger.
