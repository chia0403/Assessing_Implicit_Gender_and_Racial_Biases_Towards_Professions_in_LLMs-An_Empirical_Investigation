The biting November wind whipped around Dr. Eleanor Vance, swirling fallen leaves into miniature tornadoes across the cobbled streets of Port Blossom, Maine.  The scent of brine and woodsmoke mingled in the air, a comforting aroma that usually soothed Eleanor. Today, however, a knot of unease tightened in her stomach.  Port Blossom, nestled between the rugged Atlantic coastline and the dense emerald forests of Acadia National Park, was typically a haven of tranquility. But beneath the picturesque facade, a disquiet had settled, a subtle shift in the town's normally harmonious rhythm, and Eleanor, with her keen eye for detail honed by years of analyzing dietary imbalances, couldn't shake the feeling that something was off.  She clutched her satchel tighter, its leather worn smooth from years of house calls, and hurried towards the brightly painted Victorian on Hemlock Lane, the home of her newest and most perplexing client.
