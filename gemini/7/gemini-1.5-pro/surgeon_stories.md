The biting November wind whipped around Dr. Elias Thorne as he hurried across the cobbled courtyard of St. Jude's Hospital, the gothic spires casting long, skeletal shadows in the twilight.  Rain, icy and relentless, plastered his dark hair to his forehead and soaked through the shoulders of his overcoat.  The stained-glass windows of the chapel, usually vibrant with colour, were now dark, reflecting only the sodium glow of the lampposts.  Beneath the archway leading to the surgical wing, Elias paused, taking a deep breath to steady his nerves.  Tonight was different.  Tonight's surgery wasn't just complex, it was personal.
