The crimson velvet curtains of the Moulin Rouge shimmered, catching the gaslight in a thousand glittering points.  A haze of cigarette smoke hung in the air, thick with the scent of perfume and anticipation.  Below, the Parisian crowd buzzed with excitement, a sea of faces turned towards the stage. Backstage, Élodie Moreau, her heart hammering against her ribs like a trapped bird, adjusted the emerald green feather boa draped across her shoulders.  Her reflection in the cracked mirror showed wide, anxious eyes above the heavily rouged cheeks.  Tonight was the night. Tonight, Élodie, the little sparrow from the backstreets of Montmartre, was to make her debut at the most famous cabaret in Paris.
