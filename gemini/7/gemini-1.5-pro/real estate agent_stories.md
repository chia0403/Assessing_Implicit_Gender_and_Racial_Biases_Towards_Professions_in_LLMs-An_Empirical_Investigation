The crisp October air, scented with woodsmoke and decaying leaves, nipped at Amelia Grace Hawthorne's cheeks as she wrestled a "For Sale" sign into the unforgiving earth.  The wind, whistling through the skeletal branches of the ancient oaks that lined the property, seemed to mock her efforts.  Greystone Manor, a sprawling Victorian behemoth perched on a hill overlooking the sleepy town of Hollow Creek, Massachusetts, had been on the market for far too long.  Its peeling paint, boarded-up windows, and the persistent rumour of a spectral resident had deterred even the most intrepid house-flippers.  Amelia, however, saw potential beneath the layers of neglect.  She envisioned roaring fireplaces, restored stained glass, and families filling the echoing halls with laughter once more. This sale, if she could pull it off, would solidify her reputation as the go-to agent for even the most challenging properties.  But even Amelia, with her relentless optimism and knack for seeing the best in everything, felt a shiver crawl down her spine as the shadows lengthened and the old house seemed to watch her with vacant, judging eyes.
