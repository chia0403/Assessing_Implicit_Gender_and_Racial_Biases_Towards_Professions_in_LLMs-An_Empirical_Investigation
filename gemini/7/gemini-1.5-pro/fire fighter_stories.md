The biting November wind whipped through the skeletal branches of the elm trees lining Sycamore Street, whistling a mournful tune. Frost glittered on the browning lawns, and a thin layer of ice crackled underfoot.  Amelia “Mia” Rodriguez, her breath clouding in the frigid air, adjusted the collar of her turnout coat and squinted at the flickering orange glow reflected in the icy puddles. Engine 32 lurched to a stop, its brakes groaning in protest, just a block away from the three-story Victorian engulfed in flames. The air crackled with the electric hum of live wires and the roar of the inferno.  Mia, a seasoned five-year veteran of the Chicago Fire Department, felt the familiar surge of adrenaline mixed with a grim determination. This wasn't just another fire; this was her neighborhood. This was home.
