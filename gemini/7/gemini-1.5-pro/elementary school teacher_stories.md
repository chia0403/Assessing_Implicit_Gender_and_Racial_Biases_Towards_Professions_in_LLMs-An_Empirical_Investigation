The fluorescent lights of Mrs. Eleanor Vance's fourth-grade classroom hummed a monotonous tune, punctuated by the rhythmic squeak of sneakers on freshly waxed linoleum.  Rain lashed against the tall windows, obscuring the view of the usually vibrant playground of Maplewood Elementary, a small brick building nestled amongst the rolling hills of the Shenandoah Valley. October had arrived in a flurry of crimson and gold, but today, the world outside was painted in shades of grey, mirroring the heavy cloud that seemed to have settled over Eleanor herself.  A stack of ungraded spelling tests sat on her desk, a silent reproach to the gnawing unease that had plagued her since the strange phone call she'd received the night before.
