The biting wind whipped off the Bering Sea, rattling the antenna bolted precariously to the roof of the KODK shack.  Inside, amidst the comforting hum of vacuum tubes and the crackle of static, Elias Thorne, his fingers stained with soldering resin, hunched over a recalcitrant transmitter.  King Island, Alaska, a rugged fist of rock jutting from the steel-grey waters, was home to barely a hundred souls, and tonight, those souls depended on Elias. The annual storm, christened “The Widowmaker” by the locals, was howling at the door, and the only link to the mainland – KODK, 50 kilowatts of sputtering hope – threatened to go silent.
