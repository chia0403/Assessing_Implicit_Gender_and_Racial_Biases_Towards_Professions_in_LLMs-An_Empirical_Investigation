The aroma of burnt sugar hung heavy in the air, a bittersweet symphony that only Eleanor Ainsworth, proprietor of "The Whispering Oven," could truly appreciate.  Dawn painted the cobblestone streets of Oakhaven a soft lavender, the gaslights flickering their last breaths against the encroaching light. Nestled between a dusty antique shop and a perpetually bustling fishmonger, Eleanor’s bakery was a beacon of warmth in the pre-dawn chill.  Inside, the brick oven, Eleanor’s faithful companion, radiated a comforting heat, its curved belly glowing like a welcoming hearth.  Oakhaven, a small seaside town clinging to the craggy cliffs of the Cornish coast, was slowly waking, but Eleanor had been up for hours, her hands already dusted with flour, her heart full of the quiet joy of creation.
