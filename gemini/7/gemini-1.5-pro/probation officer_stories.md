The biting wind whipped off Lake Michigan, carrying a spray of icy rain that plastered Amelia “Amy” Rodriguez’s hair to her face.  She shoved her damp strands behind her ear, tucking them under the brim of her worn Chicago Cubs baseball cap. The sky, a bruised purple-grey, reflected in the grimy puddles that pocked the cracked asphalt of the parking lot.  Amy stood outside the dilapidated brick building that housed the South Side Community Center, a place that, despite its noble name, felt more like a holding pen for broken dreams. The air hung thick with the smell of stale cigarette smoke and despair, a scent Amy had grown accustomed to in her five years as a Cook County probation officer.  Tonight’s scheduled check-in with one of her most volatile clients, sixteen-year-old Marco “Razor” Ramirez, promised to be anything but routine.
