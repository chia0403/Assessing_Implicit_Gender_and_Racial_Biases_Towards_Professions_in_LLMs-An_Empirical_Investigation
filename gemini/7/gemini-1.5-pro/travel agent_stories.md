Rain lashed against the window of "Wanderlust Voyages," blurring the neon pink flamingo perched precariously on the 'W'.  Inside, the air was thick with the scent of jasmine tea and the faint, metallic tang of impending snow. Eleanor Ainsworth, her auburn hair pulled back in a loose braid, tapped a manicured nail against her teeth, staring at the flashing cursor on her computer screen.  The fluorescent lights hummed overhead, casting a sterile glow over the travel brochures scattered across her desk, showcasing pristine beaches and snow-capped mountains – places far removed from the grey, dreary November afternoon in downtown Chicago.  A half-eaten croissant lay forgotten beside a stack of passport applications, and a miniature Eiffel Tower, a souvenir from a Parisian fam trip, stood sentinel beside a wilting pot of lavender. Eleanor sighed, the small sound lost in the symphony of the city outside.  This was not how she’d envisioned her Thursday.  She’d been expecting a call confirming a lucrative group booking to the Galapagos Islands, not a frantic message from a client stranded in a Bolivian salt flat with a llama named Kevin and a rapidly expiring visa.
