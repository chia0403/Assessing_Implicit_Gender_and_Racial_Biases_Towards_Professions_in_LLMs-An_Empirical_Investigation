The biting wind whipped off the jagged peaks of the Dragon's Tooth mountains, carrying with it the scent of pine and the fine dust of granite.  The sun, a pale disc in the iron-grey sky, barely managed to illuminate the small, isolated village of Quarry Hollow, nestled precariously on a ledge overlooking a sheer drop. Here, amidst the constant symphony of hammer against stone, lived Elias Thorne, a stone cutter renowned throughout the valley for his steady hand and his eye for the hidden beauty within the unforgiving rock.  His workshop, a simple structure built into the side of the mountain, was cluttered with chisels, mallets, and half-finished sculptures, each a testament to his dedication to his craft.  This morning, however, a different kind of tension hung in the air, thicker than the usual dust, a premonition of something about to shatter the quiet routine of Quarry Hollow.  A raven, black as the approaching storm clouds, perched on the lintel of Elias's workshop, its obsidian eyes fixed on him with an unnerving intensity.
