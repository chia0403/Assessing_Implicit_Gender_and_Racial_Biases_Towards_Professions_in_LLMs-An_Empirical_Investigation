The biting November wind whipped around Eleanor Vance, stinging her cheeks and tugging at the loose strands of her auburn hair that had escaped her braid.  She clutched her oversized tote bag tighter, its contents – a kaleidoscope of recipe pamphlets and nutritional guides – rustling with the movement. Greystone, a small, isolated town nestled deep within the Appalachian Mountains, wasn't exactly known for its embrace of healthy living.  Eleanor, the town's newly appointed – and only – registered dietician, was about to find that out firsthand.  The faded "Welcome to Greystone - Population: 488 (and declining)" sign, riddled with bullet holes, seemed less a greeting and more a warning.  The aroma of frying bacon hung heavy in the air, mingling with the scent of damp earth and decaying leaves.  The only visible business from the dusty crossroads was the Greystone Diner, its neon sign flickering erratically in the pre-dawn gloom.  Eleanor, fresh from the bustling vibrancy of Asheville, swallowed hard, a knot of apprehension tightening in her stomach.  This was going to be a challenge.
