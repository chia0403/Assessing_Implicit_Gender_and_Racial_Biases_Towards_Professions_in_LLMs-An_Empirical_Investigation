Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of the 47th floor, blurring the already fading lights of downtown Chicago. Inside, the air buzzed with a low, persistent hum – the collective breath of a thousand servers housed in the climate-controlled heart of Thompson & Reed Financial.  Eleanor Vance, Systems Analyst, hunched over her monitor, the glow reflecting in her tired, hazel eyes.  Lines of code scrolled past, a digital waterfall she’d navigated a thousand times before.  Tonight, however, something felt different.  A subtle anomaly, a flicker in the system's heartbeat, had caught her attention, and a knot of unease tightened in her stomach.  It was almost 2:00 AM, and the rest of the floor was deserted, leaving Eleanor alone with the restless machines and a growing suspicion that something was very, very wrong.
