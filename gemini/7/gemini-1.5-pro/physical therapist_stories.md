The biting November wind whipped off Lake Michigan, rattling the paned windows of Eleanor Vance Physical Therapy and Wellness. Inside, the air was thick with the scent of antiseptic and the faint, metallic tang of sweat.  Eleanor Vance herself, a woman built like a willow tree – slender but strong – adjusted the heat lamp over the treatment table. Snow dusted the windowsills, a stark contrast to the vibrant green of the climbing pothos that snaked its way across the exposed brick wall. Eleanor's clinic, nestled in a revitalized warehouse district of Milwaukee, Wisconsin, was her sanctuary, a place where broken bodies found hope and stiff joints regained their swing.  This morning, however, a different kind of tension hung in the air, thicker than the lingering aroma of peppermint oil. Eleanor had a new patient arriving, one whose case file hinted at something far beyond a simple rotator cuff tear.
