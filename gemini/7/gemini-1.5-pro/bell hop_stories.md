The revolving doors of the Grand Majestic Hotel whispered a sigh of cool air as Elias Finch, barely seventeen and swimming in a burgundy uniform two sizes too large, stepped back into the gilded lobby.  Rain lashed against the arched windows, blurring the neon glow of Times Square beyond. It was 1947, a year when optimism hung heavy in the air like cigarette smoke, yet inside the Majestic, a different kind of tension simmered.  Beneath the crystal chandeliers and amongst the plush velvet furniture, secrets whispered from shadowed corners, carried on the rustle of silk gowns and the clinking of ice in crystal glasses. Elias, with his shock of unruly brown hair perpetually escaping his cap and a perpetually nervous smile, was privy to them all, a silent observer in this opulent theater of human drama. Tonight, however, the drama was about to become much more than a whispered secret. Tonight, it was going to explode.
