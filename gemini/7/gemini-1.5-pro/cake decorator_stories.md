The scent of burnt sugar hung heavy in the air, a stark contrast to the usually saccharine atmosphere of Buttercup Bakery.  Amelia "Amy" Piper, her brow furrowed in concentration, stared intently at the towering five-tiered wedding cake before her. It was a monstrosity of white fondant, intricately adorned with sugar-paste peonies and cascading ribbons – a masterpiece in progress, or at least, it was supposed to be.  The bakery, nestled on a quaint cobblestone street in the heart of Willow Creek, a town perpetually smelling of vanilla and cinnamon, was usually Amy’s sanctuary. But this morning, with the bridezilla’s mother due any minute and a rogue caramelized sugar plume threatening to sabotage the entire confection, sanctuary felt more like a pressure cooker.  The clock above the oven ticked ominously, each second a hammer blow against Amy’s dwindling composure.
