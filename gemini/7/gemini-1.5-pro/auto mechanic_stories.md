The rusty tang of brake dust and the sharp scent of engine degreaser hung thick in the air, a familiar perfume to Elias Vance. His garage, Vance Auto Repair, sat hunched like a tired metal beast on the edge of Harmony Creek, a blink-and-you'll-miss-it town nestled in the foothills of the Appalachian Mountains. The November wind whistled through the gaps in the corrugated iron siding, carrying with it the brittle scent of dying leaves and the promise of an early winter.  Harmony Creek wasn't much – a gas station, a diner that closed at 7pm sharp, and a handful of houses clinging to the winding mountain road – but it was home, and Elias, with grease ingrained in his knuckles and a wrench permanently imprinted on his palm, was its reluctant king.
