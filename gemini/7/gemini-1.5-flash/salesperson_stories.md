The stale air of the showroom hung heavy with the scent of new car leather and desperation.  Dust motes, illuminated by the harsh fluorescent lights, danced in the silent expanse between rows of gleaming, yet somehow lifeless, vehicles.  Amelia Jean Hawthorne, her sensible heels clicking faintly on the polished concrete floor, adjusted the crisp collar of her cream-colored blouse.  The silence, usually a comfortable backdrop to her practiced charm, felt oppressive today. It had been a week since her last sale, a week too long in the cutthroat world of Prestige Motors, a week that threatened to push her dangerously close to the edge of her manager's already dwindling patience.  The hum of the distant highway, a constant reminder of the traffic she'd rather be stuck in than facing another empty sales day, seemed to mock her.
