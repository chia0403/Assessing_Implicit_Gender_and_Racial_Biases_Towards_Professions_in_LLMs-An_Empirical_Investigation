The chipped porcelain teacup warmed Amelia Grace Hawthorne's fingers, a meager comfort against the November chill seeping through the single-paned window of her office.  Rain lashed against the glass, blurring the already indistinct gaslight glow of the cobbled street below.  Amelia, a woman whose life was as meticulously ordered as the ledgers she balanced, found herself oddly unsettled.  The usual rhythmic scratch of her quill across parchment was absent; instead, an unnerving silence hung in the air, broken only by the mournful sigh of the wind whistling through the gaps in the aged, oak-framed building that housed Hawthorne & Sons Accounting. This wasn't just any accounting firm; it was *her* family's legacy, a legacy shrouded in a mystery she was only now beginning to unravel.
