The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Amy” Hernandez’s trench coat around her legs as she hunched deeper into the shadowed alleyway.  Rain, a relentless, icy drizzle, plastered her dark hair to her forehead, blurring the already indistinct shapes of overflowing dumpster bins and graffiti-scarred brick walls.  The air hung thick with the smells of rotting garbage and something else, something metallic and faintly sweet that curdled in Amy's stomach.  This wasn't the charming, postcard-perfect San Francisco she’d grown up reading about in tourist brochures; this was the city’s underbelly, a place where shadows stretched long and secrets festered in the dampness.  It was a place where, tonight, Amy Hernandez, investigative reporter for the *San Francisco Chronicle*, hoped to find the truth.
