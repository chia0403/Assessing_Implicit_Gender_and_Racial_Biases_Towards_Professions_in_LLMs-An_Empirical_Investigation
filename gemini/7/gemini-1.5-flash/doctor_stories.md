The salt spray stung Dr. Eleanor Vance’s face as she wrestled the stubborn fishing boat's tiller.  The late afternoon sun, a bruised plum sinking below the choppy waters of the Irish Sea, cast long, skeletal shadows across the deck.  Around her, the tiny island of Inisheer dwindled to a smudge of green and grey against the darkening horizon, its solitary lighthouse a flickering eye in the gathering gloom.  The air, thick with the scent of brine and seaweed, held a chilling dampness that seeped into her bones despite the thick wool of her Aran sweater.  She’d been promising herself a peaceful week of escape from the relentless pressure of her London practice, a chance to reconnect with her roots and the rhythm of the sea.  Instead, the radio crackled with a distress call just as she rounded the treacherous Carragh Point, a call that threatened to shatter the fragile peace she'd so desperately sought.
