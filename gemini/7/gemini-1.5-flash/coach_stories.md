The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Amy” Hernandez’s hair across her face as she surveyed the skeletal remains of the high school football field.  Stark, grey clouds bled into the bruised purple of the twilight sky, mirroring the bruised purple of the hefty bruise blossoming on Kevin O’Malley’s forearm.  The air hung heavy with the scent of damp earth and the faint, metallic tang of blood.  Amy, head coach of the Westview High Wildcats, shivered, not entirely from the cold.  The chilling silence pressing down on the deserted field was far more unsettling than the wind.  It was a silence born not of peaceful slumber, but of simmering resentment, of simmering… betrayal.  Tonight, the whispers in the locker room felt louder than any cheerleading squad ever could.  The upcoming championship game felt miles away, replaced by a far more immediate and dangerous challenge.
