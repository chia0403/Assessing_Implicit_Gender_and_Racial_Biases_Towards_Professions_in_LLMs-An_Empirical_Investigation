The flickering neon sign of the "Golden Dragon" restaurant cast a lurid glow across the rain-slicked streets of Chinatown, reflecting in the puddles that mirrored the city's weary sigh.  A biting November wind whipped Amelia Hernandez’s hair across her face as she hurried along, the collar of her worn wool coat pulled high.  The scent of ginger and something vaguely medicinal hung heavy in the air, a stark contrast to the metallic tang of blood that clung to the alleyway she’d just navigated.  Amelia, a paralegal with a penchant for trouble and an even stronger penchant for justice, tucked a stray strand behind her ear and adjusted the heavy briefcase she carried.  Inside, nestled amongst legal documents and a half-eaten bag of seaweed crisps, lay something far more unsettling than any contract: a crumpled photograph showing a man with a broken nose and a very familiar, very dead, look in his eyes.
