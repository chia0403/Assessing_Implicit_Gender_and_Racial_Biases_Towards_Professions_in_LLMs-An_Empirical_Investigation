The scent of old paper and exotic spices hung heavy in the air, a peculiar perfume unique to Amelia Rose Hathaway’s travel agency, “Wanderlust & Co.”  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of sunlight slicing through the grimy window of her tiny shop, nestled incongruously between a bustling laundromat and a pawnbroker on Bleecker Street in Greenwich Village.  Amelia, a woman whose spirit was as boundless as the destinations she booked, tapped a manicured fingernail against a faded map of the world pinned to the wall, its vibrant hues dulled by years of dreaming and scheming.  Outside, the cacophony of New York City – the honking taxis, the chattering crowds, the insistent rhythm of the street – faded into a muted hum, lost in the quiet sanctuary of her meticulously cluttered office.  Today, however, the usual comforting chaos felt different; a tremor of unease vibrated beneath the surface, as unsettling as the sudden crackle of static on an old radio.
