The chipped porcelain mug warmed Dr. Eleanor Vance’s hands, a meager counterpoint to the biting November wind rattling the windowpanes of her office.  Rain, a relentless, grey curtain, obscured the usually vibrant cityscape of Seattle, transforming the Emerald City into a monochrome watercolour of muted greens and greys.  Her office, nestled on the top floor of a converted warehouse in the Belltown district, offered a panoramic view, normally a source of quiet contemplation. Tonight, however, the swirling storm mirrored the turmoil brewing within her latest patient’s mind, a turmoil that had begun to seep into Eleanor’s own carefully constructed calm. The scent of damp earth and pine – a familiar Seattle aroma – did little to soothe the growing unease in her chest.  The flickering gas lamp on her desk cast long, dancing shadows, accentuating the worn leather of her armchair, a silent witness to countless hours of shared anxieties and whispered secrets.  This case, however, felt different.  This case felt…personal.
