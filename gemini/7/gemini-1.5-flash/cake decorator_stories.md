The scent of vanilla bean hung heavy in the air, a comforting blanket against the November chill seeping through the cracked windowpane of “Sweet Surrender Cakes.”  Rain lashed against the panes, mirroring the tempest brewing inside Amelia Grace Hawthorne.  Amelia, or Amy as most people called her – though she loathed the diminutive –  stood amidst a sugary battlefield.  Fallen fondant soldiers lay scattered across the stainless steel counter, casualties of a particularly aggressive buttercream rose.  Her masterpiece, a five-tiered wedding cake designed to resemble a snow-covered castle, was currently more reminiscent of a crumbling fortress after a siege.  The deadline loomed – a mere six hours until the bride, notorious for her icy demeanor and even icier demands, would arrive.  And Amy, despite her considerable talent, felt a wave of panic threatening to drown her in a sea of sugar paste.
