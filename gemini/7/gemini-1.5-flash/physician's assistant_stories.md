The biting November wind whipped Amelia Hernandez’s charcoal grey coat around her legs as she hurried across the deserted parking lot of the Redwood County General Hospital.  The sky, a bruised purple bleeding into a sickly yellow, mirrored the unsettling unease churning in her stomach.  The fluorescent lights of the ER cast a sickly glare on the slick, rain-streaked pavement, highlighting the scattered debris – a crumpled newspaper, a discarded coffee cup, and the unsettling glint of something metallic half-buried in the gravel.  Amelia, a physician’s assistant with five years of experience under her belt, had seen a lot in her time at Redwood County, but the gnawing feeling in her gut told her this night was different.  This night promised something far more… sinister.
