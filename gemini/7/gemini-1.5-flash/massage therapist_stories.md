The scent of lavender and sandalwood hung heavy in the air, a comforting balm against the biting November wind howling outside.  Rain lashed against the leaded-glass windows of Serenity Spa, each gust a frantic percussion against the otherwise peaceful interior.  Inside, nestled amongst plush, earth-toned fabrics and the soft glow of Himalayan salt lamps, sat Anya Petrova, a massage therapist whose hands possessed a magic all their own.  Anya, with her dark braid cascading down her back and eyes the color of melted chocolate, was meticulously arranging warm basalt stones on a heated massage table, the smooth, dark rocks reflecting the flickering candlelight.  The spa, tucked away on a cobbled side street in the historic district of Prague, was usually a haven of tranquility, but tonight, a nervous energy crackled in the air, a palpable tension that even Anya’s practiced calm couldn’t entirely dissipate.
