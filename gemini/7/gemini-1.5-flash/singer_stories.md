The chipped porcelain sink was stained a permanent, unsettling shade of rust, mirroring the bruised purple blooming beneath Seraphina “Sera” Nightingale’s left eye.  The air in the cramped, windowless bathroom of the "Rusty Mug" dive bar hung thick with the cloying scent of stale beer and desperation, a perfume Sera had grown intimately familiar with over the last six months.  Rain lashed against the corrugated iron roof above, a relentless percussion accompanying the throbbing ache in her head.  Tonight, however, the usual melancholic thrum of the city was overwhelmed by a different kind of anxiety – the kind that clawed at her throat, tightening its grip with every tick of the cheap clock on the wall.  Her gig started in fifteen minutes, and she still hadn't decided what to sing.
