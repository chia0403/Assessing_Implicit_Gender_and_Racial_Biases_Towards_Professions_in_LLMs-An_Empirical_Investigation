The salt spray stung Detective Inspector Ava Thorne's face as the November wind howled a mournful dirge across the desolate stretch of Whitby beach.  The sky, a bruised purple mirroring the churning grey sea, offered no comfort.  Rain, driven sideways by the gale, plastered her dark hair to her forehead, blurring the already indistinct outlines of the crumpled figure lying half-submerged in the sand.  The air, thick with the smell of brine and something else – something acrid and unsettling – clung to Ava’s throat, a bitter taste mirroring the grim reality she knew awaited her.  The flickering beam of her torch picked out scattered fragments of broken wood and a glint of something metallic, half-buried in the wet sand, adding to the unsettling tableau.  This wasn’t just another drunken stumble onto the rocks; this felt… deliberate.
