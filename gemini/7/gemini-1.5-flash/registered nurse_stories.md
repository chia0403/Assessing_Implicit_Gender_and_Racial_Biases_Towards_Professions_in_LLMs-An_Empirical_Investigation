The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s hair across her face as she hurried down the cobbled street, the collar of her worn wool coat pulled high.  Rain, sleeting with a vicious determination, plastered her dark curls to her forehead, obscuring the already faint streetlights of Oakhaven.  Oakhaven, a town clinging stubbornly to the cliff edge overlooking the turbulent North Sea, was a place of stark beauty and hidden secrets, a place Amelia had called home, begrudgingly, for the past five years.  The only light visible in the otherwise gloomy landscape came from the tall, gothic windows of St. Jude’s Hospital, its imposing silhouette a stark reminder of the relentless demands of her job.  Tonight, however, the familiar comfort of the hospital felt miles away, a distant beacon in a storm that mirrored the turmoil brewing inside her.
