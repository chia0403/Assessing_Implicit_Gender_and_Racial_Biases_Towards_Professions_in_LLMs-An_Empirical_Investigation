The chainsaw’s whine was a mournful song, swallowed by the cathedral-like hush of the old-growth Douglas fir forest.  Mist, thick as a shroud, clung to the moss-draped branches, obscuring the sun and lending an ethereal quality to the towering trees.  Each giant felt ancient, a silent sentinel watching over centuries of whispered secrets.  Elias Thorne, his face grimy with sawdust and sweat, barely registered the beauty.  His focus was laser-sharp, honed by years spent wrestling with these titans, a dance of man and machine against the raw, untamed power of nature.  The air, heavy with the scent of pine and damp earth, carried the faint, underlying tang of something else… something metallic, almost… blood.
