The recycled air hung thick and sweet with the cloying scent of cheap perfume and stale coffee, a familiar aroma to Seraphina "Sera" Moreau.  She perched on the jump seat at the back of the nearly empty Boeing 737, the rhythmic hum of the engines a lullaby she'd heard a thousand times.  Outside, the pre-dawn sky over the Alaskan wilderness bled from bruised purple to hesitant rose, a breathtaking canvas completely ignored by the handful of bleary-eyed passengers still straggling onboard.  Anchorage, Alaska, was still hours away, a pinprick of light against the vast, inky expanse, but Sera already felt the pull of its rugged beauty, a stark contrast to the sterile confines of the cabin.  This wasn't her usual route – the glittering casinos of Vegas or the sun-drenched beaches of Hawaii – but a last-minute assignment she'd snapped up, craving a break from the relentless cycle of demanding passengers and tight schedules.  The Alaskan wilderness, however, promised a different kind of challenge, one she felt a peculiar, almost unsettling anticipation for.
