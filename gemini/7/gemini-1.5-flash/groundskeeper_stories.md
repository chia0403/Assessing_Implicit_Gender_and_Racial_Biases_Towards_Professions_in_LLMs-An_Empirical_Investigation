The biting November wind whipped Elias Thorne’s already-chapped lips raw as he wrestled with a stubborn oak branch, its leaves a brittle, skeletal tapestry against the bruised purple sky.  Elias, groundskeeper of Blackwood Manor for the past fifteen years, had seen countless autumns come and go, but this one felt different.  A chill deeper than the November air settled over the sprawling estate, clinging to the ancient stone walls and whispering through the skeletal branches of the ancient trees like a forgotten secret. Blackwood Manor, perched precariously on a windswept cliff overlooking the turbulent Atlantic, was beautiful, yes, but tonight, its beauty held a sinister edge, a promise of something… unsettling.  The manor itself, a gothic behemoth of grey stone and shadowed windows, loomed behind him, a silent, watchful sentinel against the encroaching darkness.
