The aroma of woodsmoke and roasting chestnuts hung heavy in the crisp November air, a stark contrast to the sterile scent of antiseptic that clung stubbornly to Dr. Eleanor Vance's tweed coat.  Eleanor, a nutritionist whose practice was usually confined to the brightly lit, minimalist confines of her clinic in downtown Edinburgh, found herself standing on the windswept shores of Loch Ness, the legendary monster a distant, almost laughable, thought in the face of her current predicament.  Grey skies wept a fine drizzle over the inky waters, reflecting the troubled expression on her face. The ancient, gnarled branches of a Scots pine clawed at the stormy clouds, mirroring the turmoil within her.  This wasn't a consultation she'd signed up for; this was a rescue mission, fueled by a frantic phone call and a whispered plea for help that had led her far from her carefully controlled world of balanced meals and healthy eating plans.
