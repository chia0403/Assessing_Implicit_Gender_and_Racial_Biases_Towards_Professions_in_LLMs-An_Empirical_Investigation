Outside, the relentless London drizzle hammered against the corrugated iron roof, a percussive rhythm that mirrored the frantic beat of Amelia’s own heart.  This wasn't the charming, bucolic setting she'd envisioned for her latest historical romance, a tale of passionate dukes and daring heiresses. No, this cramped, leaky attic, smelling faintly of damp wool and forgotten dreams, was the stark reality of Amelia Beatrice Worthington, author of precisely zero bestsellers.  The manuscript, titled "The Duke's Unexpected Duchess," lay abandoned on the desk, a testament to her current creative drought, its pages as blank as the future she feared lay ahead.
