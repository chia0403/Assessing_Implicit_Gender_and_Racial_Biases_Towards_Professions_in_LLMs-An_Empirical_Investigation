The air hung thick and sweet with the scent of sandalwood and burnt sugar, a perfume unique to Madame Evangeline Moreau's salon.  Evangeline, her fingers nimble as a spider weaving its web, snipped a stray lock of auburn hair from her client's perfectly sculpted bob.  The salon itself was a marvel of Art Deco extravagance, tucked away on a cobbled side street in the Marais district of Paris.  Sunlight, filtered through stained-glass windows depicting stylized lilies, cast kaleidoscopic patterns across the plush velvet chairs and gleaming brass fixtures.  The low hum of conversation, the snip-snip-snip of scissors, and the gentle whir of the hairdryer created a symphony of calm amidst the bustling Parisian city outside.  Today, however, a different kind of tension crackled in the air, a silent current that ran deeper than the usual pre-appointment jitters.  A certain Monsieur Dubois, a man known for his volatile temper and even more volatile investments, was waiting.  Evangeline, ever the master of her craft and her composure, placed the finished cut delicately on her client's shoulder, a silent promise of perfection.  The true artistry, however, was about to begin.
