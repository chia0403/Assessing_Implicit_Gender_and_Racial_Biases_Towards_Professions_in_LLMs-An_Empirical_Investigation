The mahogany desk, polished to a mirror sheen reflecting the harsh glare of the Chicago skyline, felt strangely cold beneath Amelia Katherine Blackwood’s fingertips.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of her 37th-floor office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her.  Amelia, Executive Vice President of Blackwood Industries – a title that once filled her with a potent cocktail of pride and ambition – now tasted like ash in her mouth. The city, usually a vibrant backdrop to her relentless climb, felt suffocating, a concrete cage confining her to a battle she wasn't sure she could win.  The scent of expensive leather and old money hung heavy in the air, a stark contrast to the bitter metallic tang of fear prickling at the edges of her consciousness.  Tonight, the stakes were higher than ever before.  Her carefully constructed empire, built brick by painstaking brick over two decades, was teetering on the precipice.
