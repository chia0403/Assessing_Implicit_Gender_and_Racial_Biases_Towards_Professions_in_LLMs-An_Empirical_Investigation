The scent of aged leather and expensive perfume hung heavy in the air, a familiar and comforting aroma to Evangeline "Evie" Moreau.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of the opulent Zenith Tower, blurring the glittering cityscape of Neo-Kyoto into a hazy, impressionistic masterpiece.  Evie, her impeccably tailored charcoal suit barely ruffled, adjusted the small, antique silver pen tucked behind her ear.  From her post behind the polished mahogany desk in the Zenith’s exclusive concierge lounge, she surveyed the scene: a nervous-looking businessman pacing by the koi pond, a glamorous woman meticulously applying lipstick, and a group of giggling teenagers huddled over holographic game consoles.  This was her domain, the heart of luxury, a place where dreams – and occasionally nightmares – were meticulously orchestrated.  Tonight, however, felt different.  A prickling unease, as subtle as the tremor in the city’s ancient foundations, vibrated beneath the veneer of effortless elegance.
