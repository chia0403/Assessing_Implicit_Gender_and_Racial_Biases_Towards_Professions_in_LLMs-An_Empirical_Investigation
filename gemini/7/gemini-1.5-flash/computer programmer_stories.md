The rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of Elara’s apartment, a relentless percussion echoing the frantic rhythm of her typing.  Elara Vance, a woman whose life revolved around the precise logic of code, found herself utterly adrift in the chaotic storm brewing inside her.  Her tiny, cluttered apartment in the perpetually damp backstreets of Seattle, overlooked by towering skyscrapers that seemed to sneer at her cramped existence, felt smaller than ever.  The air hung thick with the scent of stale coffee and the faintly sweet, metallic tang of ozone, a smell that always preceded a power outage – and tonight, the city hummed with a nervous energy that mirrored her own.  Empty ramen containers and discarded energy drink cans littered her desk, a testament to the marathon coding session that had consumed her for the past thirty-six hours.  The glow of three monitors bathed her face in an ethereal, sickly green light, illuminating the tense set of her jaw and the dark circles under her eyes.  Tonight, the lines of code weren't just lines of code; they were a lifeline.
