The biting November wind whipped Amelia Hernandez’s hair across her face as she wrestled the heavy tarp off the enclosure.  Frozen droplets clung to her eyelashes, blurring the already fading light.  The air, thick with the scent of damp earth and pine, carried the faint, musky aroma of the animals she tended – a comforting familiarity after a long, grueling day at the Whispering Pines Wildlife Sanctuary.  The sanctuary, nestled deep within the Redwood National Park, was a sprawling collection of enclosures, each designed to mimic the natural habitats of its inhabitants – from the soaring redwood cages housing majestic bald eagles to the meticulously crafted burrows sheltering shy badgers.  Tonight, however, Amelia was focused on the newest arrival: a young, orphaned grey wolf pup, barely bigger than a house cat, named Luna.
