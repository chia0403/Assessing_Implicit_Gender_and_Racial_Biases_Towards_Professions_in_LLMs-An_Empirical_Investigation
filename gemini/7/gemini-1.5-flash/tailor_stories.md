The scent of woodsmoke and damp wool hung heavy in the air, clinging to the cobblestones like a second skin.  Rain, a fine, persistent drizzle, slicked the narrow, twisting streets of Oakhaven, mirroring the sheen on the polished brass buttons of Elias Thorne's meticulously crafted waistcoat. Elias, a man whose life was measured in the precise stitch and the perfectly pressed seam, stood hunched beneath the awning of his shop, "Thorne's Tailoring," his gaze fixed on the swirling grey mist that obscured the distant hills.  The flickering gaslight above cast a jaundiced glow on the faded sign, its once-proud lettering now softened by years of relentless weather and the quieter wear of time.  Inside, the air was warmer, fragrant with beeswax and lavender, a stark contrast to the damp chill that permeated the ancient town.  A half-finished coat, a rich crimson velvet, lay abandoned on his workbench, a testament to the interruption that had shattered the rhythm of his day.
