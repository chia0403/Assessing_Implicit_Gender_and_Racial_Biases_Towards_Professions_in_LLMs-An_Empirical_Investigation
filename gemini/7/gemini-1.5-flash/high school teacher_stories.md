The chipped paint of the classroom clock, a relentless metronome ticking away the last vestiges of a Friday afternoon, mocked Ms. Eleanor Vance’s attempts at maintaining order.  Dust motes, illuminated by the slanting afternoon sun, danced in the single shaft of light piercing the gloom of Northwood High’s perpetually underfunded Room 207.  The air hung thick with the scent of stale coffee and adolescent angst – a familiar perfume Eleanor had come to tolerate, if not exactly enjoy, over her fifteen years teaching English literature.  Outside, the manicured lawns of the school grounds were beginning to glow with the promise of a late summer evening, a promise cruelly juxtaposed with the relentless drone of teenage boredom emanating from within her classroom.  Eleanor, perched precariously on the edge of her desk,  tapped a worn copy of *Hamlet* against her knuckles, a silent plea for attention from the sea of apathetic faces before her.
