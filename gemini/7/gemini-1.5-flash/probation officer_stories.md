The biting November wind whipped Amelia Stone’s hair across her face as she navigated the uneven pavement of Grimsborough’s industrial estate.  Rust-eaten shipping containers, their paint peeling like sunburnt skin, lined the narrow street, casting long, skeletal shadows in the fading light.  The air hung thick with the metallic tang of decay and the acrid smell of something burning – probably more scrap metal, she thought grimly, a familiar olfactory marker of her probationary rounds.  Amelia, a probation officer for the city’s notoriously unforgiving justice system, pulled her worn wool coat tighter, her breath misting in the frigid air.  She checked her watch; already ten minutes late for her meeting with  Kenneth “Kenny”  Fitzwilliam, a convicted arsonist whose penchant for pyromania, even in its most minor manifestations, seemed to be as inextinguishable as the fires he started.
