The scent of jasmine and lilies hung heavy in the humid air, a cloying sweetness that clung to Amelia Katherine Hawthorne like a second skin.  Amelia, or Amy as most people called her – though she preferred the formality of her full name when dealing with particularly demanding clients – wiped a stray petal of lavender from her cheek with the back of her hand, the dust motes dancing in the shafts of afternoon sunlight slanting through the stained-glass windows of "Petal & Thorn," her quaint little flower shop nestled on a cobbled side street in the ancient town of Oakhaven.  Oakhaven itself was a place steeped in history, its crooked, half-timbered houses leaning against each other like gossiping old women, their eaves almost touching above the narrow passageways.  The air hummed with the quiet murmur of daily life, a counterpoint to the vibrant, almost frantic energy that thrummed within the walls of Petal & Thorn, where Amy wrestled with unruly roses and the delicate dance of arranging perfect bouquets.
