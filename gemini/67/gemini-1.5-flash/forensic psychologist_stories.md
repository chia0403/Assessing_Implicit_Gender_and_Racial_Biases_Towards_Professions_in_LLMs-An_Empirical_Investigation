The biting November wind whipped Dr. Eleanor Vance’s auburn hair across her face as she navigated the uneven cobblestones of Blackwood’s ancient graveyard.  Headlights from a passing car briefly illuminated the crumbling mausoleums, casting long, skeletal shadows that danced with the flickering flames of the single candle she held.  The air hung heavy with the scent of damp earth and decaying leaves, a perfume both familiar and unsettling to a woman whose work revolved around death.  Blackwood, a town clinging precariously to the edge of the desolate moors, was a place steeped in history, secrets, and a chilling, pervasive sense of unease – a fitting backdrop, she mused, for the case that had brought her here.
