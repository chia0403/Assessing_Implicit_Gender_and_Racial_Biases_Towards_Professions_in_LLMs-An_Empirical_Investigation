The sterile scent of ozone hung heavy in the air, a familiar perfume to Anya Petrova.  It clung to her scrubs, to the polished chrome of the instruments neatly arrayed on her tray, even seemed to permeate the worn linoleum floor of Dr. Albright's dental practice.  Outside, the October wind howled a mournful tune against the rain-lashed windows of the small, brick building nestled on the edge of Willow Creek, a town so quiet it practically held its breath between heartbeats.  Anya, meticulously checking her suction tip for any lingering debris, barely registered the storm.  Her focus, as always, was absolute.  Today's patient, Mrs. Henderson, had a reputation for a particularly stubborn layer of plaque, and Anya was determined to conquer it.
