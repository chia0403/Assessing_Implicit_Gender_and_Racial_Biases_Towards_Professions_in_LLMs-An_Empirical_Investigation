The chipped porcelain sink groaned under the weight of Seraphina “Sera” Nightingale’s elbows as she stared into its murky depths.  The reflection staring back was pale, framed by tangled auburn curls escaping their haphazard bun.  Her usually vibrant green eyes were shadowed, mirroring the gloom of the cramped, damp bathroom in the back of "The Rusty Mug," a dive bar clinging precariously to the edge of Grimsborough's forgotten waterfront.  The air hung thick with the smell of stale beer, cheap bourbon, and the lingering scent of saltwater tainting the perpetually damp brick walls.  Outside, the wind howled a mournful ballad, a fitting soundtrack to Sera's current state of mind – a melancholic symphony of missed opportunities and shattered dreams.  Tonight, however, the mournful wind was also carrying the faint, tantalizing aroma of roasting chestnuts from a vendor down the street, a stark contrast to the harsh reality of her existence.
