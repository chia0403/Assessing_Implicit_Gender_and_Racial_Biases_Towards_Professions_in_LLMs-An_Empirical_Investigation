The salt spray stung Elias Thorne's face as he wrestled the recalcitrant antenna into place.  The wind, a howling banshee straight off the churning grey expanse of the North Atlantic, threatened to rip the whole contraption from the skeletal tower perched precariously on the cliff edge. Below, the waves crashed against the jagged rocks with a ferocity that mirrored the tempest brewing inside Elias.  He wasn’t just battling the elements; he was fighting a deadline, a failing signal, and the gnawing suspicion that something far more sinister was interfering with the lonely little radio station clinging to the windswept precipice of Ballynahown Head, County Donegal.  The air, thick with the scent of brine and the metallic tang of the storm, vibrated with the lonely crackle of static – a constant, unsettling hum that seemed to echo the turmoil in Elias's own heart.
