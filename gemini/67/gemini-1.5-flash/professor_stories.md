The biting November wind whipped Professor Eleanor Vance's grey wool coat around her legs as she hurried across the windswept quad of Blackwood University.  Fallen leaves, the colour of dried blood and rust, skittered across the cobblestones, swirling into miniature, ephemeral tornadoes.  The gothic spires of Blackwood Hall, its stone a bruised purple under the bruised sky, loomed before her, a silent sentinel guarding secrets older than the university itself.  The air hung heavy with the scent of damp earth and woodsmoke, a fragrance both comforting and unsettling, mirroring the strange mixture of anticipation and dread churning in Eleanor's stomach.  She was late, terribly late, for a meeting she hadn't even wanted to attend.  But the cryptic note, tucked into her mailbox just hours ago, had left her with no choice.  The note, penned in spidery script on aged parchment, promised answers to questions she hadn't even dared to formulate.
