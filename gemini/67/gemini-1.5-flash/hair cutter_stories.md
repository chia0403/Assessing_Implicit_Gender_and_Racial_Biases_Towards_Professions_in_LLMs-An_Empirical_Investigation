The scent of lavender and burnt sugar hung heavy in the air, a peculiar perfume only Aurelia “Reli” Blackwood’s salon could produce.  Reli, with her perpetually smudged charcoal eyeliner and a cascade of auburn curls escaping her messy bun, was perched on a stool, meticulously snipping at a particularly stubborn cowlick.  Her tiny, cluttered salon, "The Snip & Sip," nestled between a failing bakery and a surprisingly successful taxidermist, was a haven of mismatched furniture and forgotten dreams. Sunlight streamed through the dusty windowpanes, illuminating motes of dust dancing in the air like tiny, glittering fairies.  Outside, the relentless grey of a Manchester drizzle obscured the cobbled street, a perfect counterpoint to the vibrant chaos within.
