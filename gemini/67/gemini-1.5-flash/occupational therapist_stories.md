The biting November wind whipped Amelia Katherine Hayes's auburn hair across her face as she hurried down Elm Street, the collar of her tweed coat pulled high.  The scent of woodsmoke and roasting chestnuts battled with the less pleasant aroma of damp leaves and exhaust fumes, a familiar autumnal cocktail in the historic, slightly decaying charm of Oakhaven, Vermont.  Oakhaven, nestled in a valley cradled by the Green Mountains, was a town steeped in history, its quaint New England architecture a stark contrast to the modern challenges faced by its inhabitants, challenges Amelia, a highly-regarded occupational therapist, was intimately familiar with. This morning, however, her focus wasn’t on the town’s elderly population struggling with arthritis or the young children battling developmental delays.  This morning, her appointment was of a different kind entirely.  It was a visit that promised to unearth secrets buried deep beneath the town’s picturesque facade.
