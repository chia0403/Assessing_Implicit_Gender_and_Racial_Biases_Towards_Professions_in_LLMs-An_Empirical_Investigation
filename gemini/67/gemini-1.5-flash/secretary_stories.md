The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Grace Hathaway's hands, a small comfort against the biting November wind whistling through the gaps in the ancient windowpanes of Blackwood & Sons.  Rain lashed against the leaded glass, blurring the already hazy cityscape of London sprawled beyond.  Amelia, a secretary of impeccable efficiency and decidedly unimpressive stature, hunched deeper into her worn armchair, the scent of damp wool and stale coffee clinging to her like a second skin.  The office, a cavernous space dominated by a mahogany desk that could comfortably house a small family, was steeped in the hushed, expectant silence that always preceded Mr. Blackwood's arrival.  The only sound, aside from the relentless drumming of rain, was the rhythmic tick-tock of the grandfather clock in the corner, each second a tiny hammer blow against the quiet anticipation.
