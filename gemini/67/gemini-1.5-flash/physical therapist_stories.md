The salt spray stung Dr. Eleanor Vance's face as she wrestled the stubborn fishing net from its tangled mooring on the weathered pier.  Seagulls shrieked overhead, their cries a discordant counterpoint to the rhythmic crash of waves against the pilings.  The air, thick with the briny scent of the ocean and the pungent aroma of decaying kelp, was a far cry from the sterile, antiseptic smell of the physiotherapy clinic she usually inhabited.  But then again, Eleanor Vance, Doctor of Physical Therapy, wasn't exactly inhabiting her usual self today.  This remote, windswept corner of the Maine coastline, nestled between craggy cliffs and a churning, grey sea, was a world away from her meticulously ordered life in Portland. And the reason for her sudden, unplanned escape was as tangled and knotted as that blasted fishing net.
