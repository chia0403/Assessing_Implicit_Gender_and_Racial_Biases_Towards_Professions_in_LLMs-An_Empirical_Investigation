The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Hernandez’s hands, a meager comfort against the November chill that seeped through the drafty window of her travel agency.  Rain lashed against the panes, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her.  “Wanderlust & Co.” – the name painted above the door in a faded, optimistic script – felt more like a cruel joke tonight.  The once vibrant office, usually a kaleidoscope of brochures depicting sun-drenched beaches and snow-capped mountains, now seemed muted, overshadowed by the gloom of a particularly slow season and a mountain of unpaid invoices.  Outside, the cobblestone streets of Edinburgh’s Old Town glistened under the relentless rain, a far cry from the exotic locales Amelia dreamt of, and, more importantly, the ones she usually helped others experience.
