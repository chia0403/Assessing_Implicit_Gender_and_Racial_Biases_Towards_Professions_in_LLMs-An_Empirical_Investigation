The scent of rosemary and burnt sugar hung heavy in the air, a peculiar perfume that clung to the velvet curtains and the chipped porcelain sink in Aurelia “Rella” Davenport’s salon. Rella herself, a whirlwind of auburn curls and sharp wit contained within a worn denim apron, was wrestling a particularly stubborn tangle from the back of Mrs. Periwinkle’s platinum blonde bouffant.  Rain lashed against the single, misted window of “Shear Delight,” a quaint, if slightly dilapidated, establishment tucked away on a cobbled side street in the ancient, rain-soaked town of Port Blossom. The relentless downpour mirrored the tempest brewing inside Mrs. Periwinkle, whose anxieties about her upcoming bridge tournament were as tightly wound as the aforementioned hair.  Outside, the gas lamps flickered, casting long, dancing shadows that seemed to mimic the nervous twitch in Mrs. Periwinkle’s left eye.
