The chipped paint of the fire escape flaked onto Amelia Grace Hawthorne's worn leather boots as she perched precariously on the rusted metal, a half-empty cup of lukewarm coffee trembling in her hand.  Below, the relentless pulse of New York City throbbed – a symphony of car horns, sirens, and the rhythmic clang of the subway – a soundtrack to the city's relentless, unforgiving churn.  The air, thick with the scent of exhaust fumes and roasting chestnuts from a vendor's cart across the street, hung heavy with the impending November chill.  Amelia, bundled in a threadbare wool coat several sizes too large, ignored it all, her gaze fixed on the flickering gaslight illuminating the grimy window of the apartment across the alley.  It was a window that held a secret, a story she was determined to uncover, even if it meant risking more than just a few scrapes on her already battered boots.
