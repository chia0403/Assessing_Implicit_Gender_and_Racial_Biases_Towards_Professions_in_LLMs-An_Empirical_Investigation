The fluorescent lights of "Quick Stop" hummed a monotonous tune, a soundtrack to Amelia Hernandez's weary existence.  Amelia, a twenty-three-year-old with eyes that mirrored the perpetually overcast sky of Seattle, meticulously arranged a stack of discounted candy bars, their brightly colored wrappers mocking the drabness of her surroundings. Rain lashed against the grimy windows, blurring the already indistinct neon signs of the neighboring businesses into a hazy, watercolor wash.  The air inside the convenience store clung thick and damp, a miasma of stale coffee, spilled soda, and the faintly metallic scent of old pennies.  Outside, the relentless Seattle drizzle seemed to seep into the very bones of the building, a constant reminder of the grey, unending day.
