The polished brass of the elevator doors reflected Aurelia Genevieve Dubois’s weary face, the city lights blurring into streaks of emerald and sapphire beyond.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of the Zenith Tower, a relentless rhythm that mirrored the drumming in Aurelia’s head.  The Zenith, a shimmering obsidian spire that pierced the Manhattan skyline, was her domain.  For the past ten years, Aurelia had reigned as the concierge, the silent guardian of its glittering secrets, the keeper of its gilded keys, the unseen hand that smoothed the path for its ultra-wealthy residents.  Tonight, however, the usual hum of privilege felt more like a suffocating pressure.  The scent of expensive perfume and desperation clung to the air, a potent cocktail that hinted at the storm brewing within the building's meticulously crafted facade.
