The salt spray stung Eleanor Vance’s face as she wrestled the stubborn sailboat's mainsail.  The late afternoon sun, a bruised plum sinking below the churning grey-green expanse of the Atlantic, cast long shadows across the deck of the *Sea Serpent*, a thirty-foot sloop more temperamental than a prima donna.  Eleanor, a writer of gothic thrillers whose success was as unpredictable as the ocean's moods, hadn't expected the sea trial of her newly acquired vessel to be quite so… turbulent.  The air, thick with the smell of brine and diesel, carried the mournful cry of gulls overhead, a fitting soundtrack to the gnawing unease that had settled in her stomach since leaving the bustling port of Provincetown several hours ago.  She gripped the worn wooden helm, her knuckles white, the rhythmic slap of waves against the hull a constant, unsettling reminder of her isolation.  This solitary voyage, intended as a restorative escape, felt less like a retreat and more like a confrontation with something… unseen.
