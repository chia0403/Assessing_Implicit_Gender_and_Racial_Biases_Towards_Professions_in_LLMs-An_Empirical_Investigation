The fluorescent hum of the office lights was the only soundtrack to Amelia Katherine Finch’s meticulous work.  Amelia, a woman whose life was governed by debit and credit, by perfectly aligned spreadsheets and the satisfying *click-clack* of her mechanical keyboard, found herself staring blankly at the figures on her screen.  The air in the accounting firm of Peterson, Parker, and Phelps hung thick and stale, smelling faintly of old coffee and desperation, a scent that seemed to mirror the state of Amelia’s own finances.  Outside, the relentless Chicago wind howled a mournful tune against the towering glass and steel skyscrapers, a fitting counterpoint to the inner turmoil churning within her. This wasn't just another quarterly report; this was the audit for Blackwood Industries, a notoriously ruthless conglomerate, and Amelia suspected something was seriously amiss.  The numbers weren't just off; they were deliberately obscured, hidden behind a veneer of perfectly legitimate-looking transactions.  And Amelia, despite her meticulous nature, was starting to feel the icy grip of fear.
