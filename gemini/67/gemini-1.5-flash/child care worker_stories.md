The chipped paint of the "Sunny Days Child Care" sign flaked onto the already overflowing flowerbeds, a testament to the relentless summer sun beating down on Willow Creek.  Twenty-seven-year-old Elara Vance, her dark hair pulled back in a practical braid escaping the relentless humidity, adjusted the strap of her worn canvas tote bag, the weight of juice boxes and half-eaten goldfish crackers a familiar burden.  The air hung thick and sweet, a cloying mix of honeysuckle and the slightly acrid tang of sunscreen, amplified by the shrieks of delight and the occasional wail of frustration emanating from the brightly painted playground just beyond the daycare's double doors.  Inside, the promise of sticky fingers, crayon masterpieces, and the endless, endearing chaos of five to seven-year-olds awaited.
