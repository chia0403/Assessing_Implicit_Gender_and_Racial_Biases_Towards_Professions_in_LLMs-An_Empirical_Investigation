The chipped mug warmed Amelia Hernandez’s hands, the lukewarm tea doing little to thaw the icy dread that had settled in her stomach.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of the Peterson & Yates law firm, blurring the already hazy cityscape of Chicago sprawled below.  Amelia, a paralegal with a reputation for meticulous detail and an uncanny ability to sniff out inconsistencies, stared at the mountain of legal documents piled on her desk – a testament to the impending deposition of Arthur Blackwood, a notorious real estate magnate accused of fraudulent land acquisitions.  The air in the office, normally buzzing with the frenetic energy of a high-powered legal team, hung heavy with a suffocating silence, broken only by the rhythmic drumming of the rain and the occasional frustrated sigh from her colleague in the adjacent cubicle.  The Blackwood case, Amelia knew, was far from ordinary; it smelled of something far more sinister than just shady business dealings.
