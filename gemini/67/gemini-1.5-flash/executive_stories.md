The mahogany desk, polished to a mirror sheen, reflected Amelia Grace Hawthorne's sharp, determined gaze.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of her 57th-floor office, blurring the already hazy cityscape of Hong Kong into a watercolour wash of grey and black.  The scent of jasmine, usually a comforting fragrance drifting in from the Kowloon side, was overpowered tonight by the metallic tang of ozone and the low hum of a city holding its breath under the onslaught of a typhoon.  Amelia, CEO of Hawthorne Global Holdings,  didn't flinch.  Typhoons were merely inconveniences, weather events to be navigated like any other complex deal. Tonight, however, something far more disruptive than wind and rain threatened to unravel her meticulously crafted world.
