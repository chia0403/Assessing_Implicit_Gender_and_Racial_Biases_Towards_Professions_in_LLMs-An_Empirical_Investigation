The fluorescent hum of the "Pixel Pushers" co-working space vibrated through Elara Blackwood's worn-out sneakers.  Dust motes danced in the afternoon sun, filtering weakly through the grimy windows overlooking the bustling, exhaust-choked city streets of Neo-Veridia.  Elara, a graphic designer with a penchant for vintage typefaces and a chaotic mind brimming with conceptual ideas, hunched over her custom-built desk, the clatter of keyboards and muffled conversations forming a low-level symphony around her.  A half-eaten croissant, abandoned amidst a mountain of sketches and crumpled papers, sat beside a chipped mug filled with lukewarm chamomile tea.  The air was thick with the aroma of burnt coffee and desperation, a familiar scent in her world.  Her latest project – a rebranding campaign for the struggling "Sunstone Organics" – was stalling.  The client, a perpetually harried, silver-haired woman named Seraphina, seemed increasingly resistant to her concepts. Elara felt a familiar knot tightening in her stomach.  Another potential missed deadline loomed.
