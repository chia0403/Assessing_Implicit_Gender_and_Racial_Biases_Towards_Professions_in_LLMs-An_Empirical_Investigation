Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of the Willow Creek Rehabilitation Center, a rhythmic counterpoint to the steady hum of the ventilation system.  Inside, Amelia Blackwood, Occupational Therapist, adjusted the grip on a brightly colored wooden puzzle.  The chipped paint and faded images of tropical birds belied the painstaking care she poured into each piece.  Across the room, a young woman named Maya, her face etched with frustration, stared blankly at a stack of brightly colored fabric swatches.  The scent of antiseptic clung to the air, a familiar, if slightly oppressive, aroma of the place Amelia had called home for the past five years.  Today, however, that scent seemed to carry a heavier, almost melancholic weight.
