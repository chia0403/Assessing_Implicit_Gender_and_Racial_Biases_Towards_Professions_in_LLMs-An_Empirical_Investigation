Rain hammered against the leaded windows of Amelia Blackwood's study, a relentless percussion that mirrored the storm brewing inside her.  The flickering gaslight cast long, dancing shadows across the room, illuminating stacks of manuscripts, half-finished poems scribbled on scraps of parchment, and overflowing inkwells.  Amelia Blackwood, author of the lauded "Chronicles of the Whispering Woods" and the soon-to-be-published "Ode to the Obsidian Moon,"  found herself, once again, staring into the abyss of writer's block.  The grand Victorian house,  Oakhaven Manor, stood sentinel on a windswept hill overlooking the town of Alderbrook, a place Amelia had loved since childhood, yet now felt suffocatingly familiar.  The oppressive silence of the house, broken only by the relentless rain, felt heavier than the lead weight in her chest.
