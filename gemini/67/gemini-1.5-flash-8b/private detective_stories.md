Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of the ramshackle building, a rhythmic tattoo that echoed the frantic thumping of Amelia “Ace” Cavendish’s heart.  The air, thick with the scent of damp earth and mildew, hung heavy in the dimly lit office, the only illumination provided by a flickering fluorescent bulb that cast long, dancing shadows on the cluttered desk.  Empty coffee cups, half-eaten sandwiches, and crumpled newspaper clippings littered the surface, a testament to Ace’s relentless pursuit of the truth.  Ace Cavendish, private investigator extraordinaire (or at least, that’s what her business cards claimed),  was crouched over a worn leather-bound notebook, the faded gold lettering barely visible in the gloom.

The setting was the gritty underbelly of Blackwood, a sprawling industrial town nestled amongst the bleak, windswept hills of the Midlands.  Blackwood had a reputation, a reputation that clung to the town like the constant drizzle.  Rumors whispered of hidden tunnels, illicit dealings, and shadowy figures who moved in the dead of night.  This particular case, this desperate plea from a distraught widow named Eleanor Vance, was about to plunge Ace into the heart of Blackwood's darkest secrets.
