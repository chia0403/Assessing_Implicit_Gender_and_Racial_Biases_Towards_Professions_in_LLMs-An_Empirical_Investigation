The biting wind, a relentless prairie wolf, howled across the cracked earth, whipping dust devils into swirling motes of grey.  The sun, a pale disc sinking below the horizon, cast long, skeletal shadows across the sprawling expanse of the Red River Valley.  This was the domain of Jedediah "Jed" Cartwright, and tonight, the valley held its breath, a nervous anticipation coiled in the silence of the approaching storm.  Jed Cartwright, weathered rancher of the Cartwright Ranch, felt the familiar tremor in the very ground beneath his boots.  He wasn't afraid, not in the way fear gnawed at the weak.  He was wary, a seasoned tracker recognizing the language of the plains.  Tonight, something wasn't right.
