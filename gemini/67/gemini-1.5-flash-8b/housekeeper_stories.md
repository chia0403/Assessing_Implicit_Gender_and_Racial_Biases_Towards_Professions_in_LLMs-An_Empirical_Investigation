The chipped porcelain sink, stained a dull, milky yellow, reflected Elara Nightingale's face back at her.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of sunlight piercing the gloom of the sprawling, neglected manor.  The air, thick with the scent of old books and decaying wood, hung heavy in the oppressive silence of Blackwood Manor.  Elara, a wisp of a woman with eyes the colour of faded amethyst and hair like spun moonlight, adjusted the worn apron tied around her waist.  This was her third day, her third solitary breakfast of lukewarm tea and stale bread, and the oppressive atmosphere was beginning to seep into her bones.  Blackwood Manor, a monument to forgotten grandeur and festering decay, was slowly consuming her.
