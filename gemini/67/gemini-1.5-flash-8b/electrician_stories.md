The flickering fluorescent lights of the abandoned factory hummed a mournful dirge, mirroring the rhythm of Elias Thorne’s heart.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of sunlight piercing the grime-coated window, illuminating the tangled mess of wires and half-finished circuits that littered the floor.  Elias Thorne, a journeyman electrician with calloused hands and eyes that had seen more shadowed corners than most, knelt amidst the debris, his flashlight beam tracing a frayed copper cable.  This was the 'Phoenix Foundry,' shuttered for five years now, a testament to the city's relentless march forward, leaving the obsolete behind.  The air, thick with the smell of ozone and forgotten machinery, hung heavy, a suffocating blanket over the silent factory.  He was here not for the money, not for the challenge – but for something else, something buried deep within the rusted heart of this forgotten place.
