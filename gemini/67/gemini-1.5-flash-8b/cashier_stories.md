The fluorescent lights of the "Everyday Essentials" grocery store hummed, a relentless, low thrum that vibrated through Amelia  Rosewater's tired bones.  Dust motes danced in the sickly yellow glow, mimicking the blur of the afternoon rush.  Rows of brightly colored cereal boxes, pre-packaged salads, and oddly-shaped fruits lined the aisles, a testament to the relentless march of consumerism.  Amelia, stationed behind register 3, felt like a tiny, insignificant cog in a vast, impersonal machine.  Today, the machine was particularly grinding.
