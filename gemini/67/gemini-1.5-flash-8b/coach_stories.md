The biting wind whipped Amelia "Amy" Cartwright's crimson scarf around her neck, stinging her cheeks as she surveyed the frozen expanse of the Glacier Heights ski slope.  Early morning light, fractured by the crystalline shards of ice, painted the peaks in hues of silver and rose.  Below, the groomed trails snaked like sculpted ribbons, a silent testament to the meticulous work of the snowmaking crew.  Amy, her breath puffing out in visible clouds, adjusted the oversized fleece of her ski-team jacket.  This was her sanctuary, her battlefield, her laboratory, where the artistry of skiing met the science of human potential. Glacier Heights was more than just a mountain; it was a metaphor for the relentless pursuit of excellence she instilled in her athletes.  It was 6:00 AM on a crisp, January morning, and the air hummed with the unspoken energy of a new season.  Her team, the Glacier Glaciers, were about to begin their grueling, weeks-long training camp.
