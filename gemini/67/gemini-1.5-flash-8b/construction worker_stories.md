The biting wind howled a mournful dirge against the skeletal frame of the skyscraper, a symphony of whistling steel and groaning concrete.  Rain lashed against the exposed girders, blurring the already dim light filtering through the grey clouds.  This was Elias Thorne’s world – a world of towering cranes, the metallic clang of welding torches, and the earthy scent of damp concrete.  Elias Thorne, a seasoned construction worker with calloused hands and eyes that held the enduring weariness of a thousand sunrises and sunsets on building sites, surveyed the scene from the precarious perch of a scaffolding platform.  Below him, the city sprawled, a maze of muted colours under the relentless downpour.  Thirty floors up, on the nascent skeleton of "Apex Tower," Elias felt the familiar knot of apprehension tighten in his chest.  This project, this monumental testament to human ambition, felt heavier than any other he’d overseen, a weight he carried on his broad shoulders not just physically, but emotionally.
