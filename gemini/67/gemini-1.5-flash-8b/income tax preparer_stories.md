The fluorescent lights of the "Tax Relief Experts" office hummed, a monotonous counterpoint to the rhythmic clatter of Amelia Peabody's keyboard.  Tax season was in full swing, a relentless tide of paperwork and furrowed brows washing over her.  Amelia, a meticulous income tax preparer with a perpetually tired smile and eyes that held the weary wisdom of countless 1099s and W-2s, stared at the screen.  Another Schedule C.  Another small business owner, a nervous wreck of a human trying to navigate a labyrinth of numbers and regulations.  Her small office, tucked away on a quiet side street of the bustling city, was a testament to the organized chaos of tax season.  Stacks of manila folders, overflowing with documents, stood sentinel against the walls.  The faint scent of stale coffee and desperation hung in the air, a palpable aroma of the season.  A tiny, chipped mug sat on her desk, a comforting reminder of the sugary elixir she'd consumed earlier, and which had only marginally masked the stress that was clinging to her like a damp shroud.
