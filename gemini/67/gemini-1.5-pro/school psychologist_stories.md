The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Amy” Carter’s auburn braid across her face as she hurried across the cracked asphalt of the Northwood High School parking lot. Tiny pellets of sleet, precursors to the promised snowstorm, stung her cheeks.  Nestled against the foothills of the Catskill Mountains in upstate New York, Northwood was a small town where everyone knew everyone, and the high school, a sprawling brick building that had seen better days, was the heart of the community. Amy, Northwood's sole school psychologist, clutched a thermos of lukewarm coffee in one hand and a bulging file folder in the other. The file contained the case notes of a student, Jason Bellweather, who'd been exhibiting increasingly erratic behavior. This morning, Jason had reportedly arrived at school wearing a full suit of armor fashioned from cardboard boxes and duct tape, claiming to be a knight on a quest to "slay the apathy dragon." Amy sighed.  It was going to be a long day.
