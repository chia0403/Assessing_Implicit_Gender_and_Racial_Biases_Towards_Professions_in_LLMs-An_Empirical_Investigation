The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne's auburn braid across her face as she hurried across the cobbled courtyard of Alderbrooke Manor.  The grand, albeit slightly dilapidated, Victorian mansion perched on a bluff overlooking the churning grey waters of the North Atlantic.  Built in 1889 by a shipping magnate, it now served as a rehabilitation center, its once opulent ballroom converted into a state-of-the-art physiotherapy gym, and the ornate bedrooms housing patients recovering from strokes, accidents, and surgeries.  Amelia, wrapped in a thick wool coat against the sea-borne chill, clutched her satchel tighter, a faint unease fluttering in her stomach. It wasn't just the unsettling atmosphere of the old manor, or the relentless wind; it was the new patient arriving today, Mr. Silas Blackwood, a recluse with a shrouded past and a reputation that preceded him like a storm cloud.
