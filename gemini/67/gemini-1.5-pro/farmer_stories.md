The wind, sharp and smelling of approaching rain, whipped across the parched fields of Abalone Farm, tugging at the brim of Jedediah "Jed" Higgins' worn straw hat. Jed, his face weathered like the oak fence posts lining his property, squinted at the bruised purple sky. It was late July in the Missouri Ozarks, and the promised rain had been a fickle mistress all summer. The creek bed that snaked through his lower forty was bone dry, and the corn stalks in the north field were rustling like brittle paper.  Abalone Farm, carved out of the wilderness generations ago by Jed's ancestors, was clinging to life by a thread, much like the hopes in Jed's weary heart.
