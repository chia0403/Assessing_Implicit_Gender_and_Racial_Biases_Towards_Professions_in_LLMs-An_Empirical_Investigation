The biting November wind whipped around Professor Alistair Finch, tugging at the edges of his tweed overcoat as he hurried across the cobbled quadrangle of St. Jude's College, Oxford.  Gargoyles, slick with rain, leered down from the medieval stonework, and the gas lamps flickered, casting long, dancing shadows that seemed to mimic his own hurried gait.  Alistair clutched a battered leather satchel overflowing with student essays, the weight a familiar comfort against his side.  Tonight, however, the familiar rhythm of academic life felt different, charged with a nervous energy that had nothing to do with impending deadlines.  He was on his way to a meeting, a clandestine gathering in the dusty archives beneath the Bodleian Library, a meeting that promised to unravel a centuries-old mystery, a mystery that Alistair, in his quiet, scholarly way, had stumbled upon amidst the yellowed pages of a forgotten manuscript.
