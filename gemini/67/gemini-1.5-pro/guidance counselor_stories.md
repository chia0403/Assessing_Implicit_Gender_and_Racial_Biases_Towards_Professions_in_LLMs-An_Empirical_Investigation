The fluorescent lights of Northwood High hummed a monotonous tune, a soundtrack to the quiet chaos of Mrs. Eleanor Vance's office.  Rain lashed against the windowpane, blurring the already dreary November landscape of bare trees and sodden playing fields. Inside, the air was thick with the scent of stale coffee and the faint, lingering perfume of a distraught student who'd just fled, leaving behind a crumpled tissue damp with tears. Eleanor, her auburn hair pulled back in a loose bun, sighed and rubbed the bridge of her nose. The clock on the wall ticked with agonizing slowness, each second a reminder of the mountain of paperwork and the ever-growing list of student anxieties piled on her desk.  Northwood High, nestled in the seemingly idyllic town of Havenwood, Oregon, was a pressure cooker of adolescent angst, and Eleanor Vance was its designated release valve.
