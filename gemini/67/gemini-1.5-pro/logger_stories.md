The biting wind whipped through the skeletal branches of the pines, clawing at Elias Blackwood's thick flannel shirt as he surveyed the stand. Dawn was barely a smudge of grey on the eastern horizon, the air still crisp with the promise of frost. Towering Douglas Firs, their bark rough and furrowed like ancient faces, stood sentinel around him, their scent a heady mix of resin and damp earth. This was Elias's domain, the Blackwood Timber Tract, nestled deep in the Cascade Mountains of Oregon. Generations of Blackwoods had worked this land, felling giants and shaping the landscape, and Elias, with his calloused hands and the scent of sawdust permanently ingrained in his skin, was no different. Today, however, felt different.  A low, guttural growl echoed from the shadowed depths of the woods, a sound that resonated with a primal unease Elias had never experienced before.
