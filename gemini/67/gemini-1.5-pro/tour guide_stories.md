The humid air hung thick and heavy, smelling of plumeria and exhaust fumes, as Isabella "Izzy" Ramirez adjusted the microphone clipped to her coral-pink linen shirt.  The sun, a molten orb sinking towards the jade-green peaks of the Na Pali Coast, painted the sky in fiery hues of orange and violet.  Her fifteen tourists, a motley crew ranging from sunburned honeymooners to a trio of boisterous college students, milled around the weathered stone wall overlooking Hanalei Bay on the Hawaiian island of Kauai.  Izzy, her dark curls escaping her braid in the gentle trade winds, gestured dramatically towards the crescent of golden sand below. "Aloha, everyone, and welcome to what Mark Twain himself called 'the most beautiful place on earth'."
