The biting wind whipped off Lake Michigan, stinging Amelia Reyes's cheeks as she braced herself against the ambulance door.  Chicago, in all its brutal, January glory, sprawled before her.  The city lights shimmered off the slick, black ice coating the streets, turning the urban landscape into a treacherous, glittering maze.  Sirens wailed in the distance, a mournful counterpoint to the rhythmic thump of the bass emanating from a nearby nightclub.  Tonight, however, Amelia wasn't responding to the usual weekend revelry-gone-wrong. Dispatch had crackled over the radio with a chillingly vague report: "Possible multiple casualties. Unknown origin. Abandoned warehouse district, Pier 81."  Amelia, a seasoned paramedic with five years on the Chicago Fire Department, felt a knot of unease tighten in her stomach. Pier 81 was a desolate stretch of crumbling brick and broken concrete, a place where the city's forgotten stories gathered like dust. As her partner, a rookie named Kevin O'Malley, wrestled a stretcher out of the back, Amelia took a deep breath of the frigid air, steeling herself for whatever awaited them in the shadows.
