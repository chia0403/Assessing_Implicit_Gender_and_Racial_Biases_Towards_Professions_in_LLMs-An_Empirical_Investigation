The biting November wind whipped around Eleanor Grace Hawthorne’s ankles as she hurried across the deserted parking lot of St. Jude’s Memorial Hospital.  Fluorescent lights buzzed from the Emergency Room entrance, casting a sickly yellow glow on the swirling snow.  Pine trees, strung with twinkling Christmas lights that felt incongruously cheerful against the bleak backdrop, lined the perimeter of the lot. Eleanor tucked a stray strand of auburn hair behind her ear, the icy air stinging her cheeks. It was 6:58 am, two minutes before the start of her shift, and already the gnawing premonition that clung to her since she woke hadn’t abated. This wasn’t the usual Monday morning dread; it was something heavier, a sense of foreboding that settled deep in her stomach like a stone.  St. Jude’s, perched on a hill overlooking the small, isolated town of Havenwood, Maine, usually felt like a sanctuary, but this morning, it felt like something else entirely – a fortress bracing itself for a siege.
