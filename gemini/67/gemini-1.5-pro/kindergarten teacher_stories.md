The sticky, sweet scent of spilled apple juice hung in the air, mingling with the faint aroma of finger paint and the slightly more pungent odor of unwashed nap mats.  Sunlight streamed through the large windows of Mrs. Eleanor Rose Hawthorne's kindergarten classroom, illuminating a kaleidoscope of brightly colored construction paper, mismatched plastic blocks, and tiny handprints smeared across nearly every surface.  Outside, the crisp October air rustled the leaves of the ancient oak tree that stood sentinel over the playground of Maplewood Elementary, hinting at the approaching chill of winter.  Inside, however, the atmosphere was a humid microcosm of youthful energy, barely contained within the four pastel-painted walls.  Mrs. Hawthorne, her auburn hair pulled back in a practical braid, knelt amidst the chaos, patiently mediating a dispute over a particularly glittery purple crayon.
