The aroma of cardamom and burnt sugar hung heavy in the air, a familiar perfume that clung to Elara Thistlewick like flour dust.  Dawn painted the cobblestone streets of Oakhaven a soft lavender, its light filtering through the leaded glass windows of Thistlewick’s Treats, illuminating the rows of cooling pastries.  Elara, her auburn braid already coming undone, hummed a wordless tune as she dusted powdered sugar over a mountain of blackberry scones.  Oakhaven, nestled deep in the Whispering Pines Valley, was a town where gossip travelled faster than the river that snaked through its heart, and the secrets held within its quaint timber-framed houses were as rich and layered as Elara’s spiced apple cake.  This morning, however, a different kind of secret simmered beneath the surface, a secret carried on the crisp autumn wind that rattled the shop’s front door.
