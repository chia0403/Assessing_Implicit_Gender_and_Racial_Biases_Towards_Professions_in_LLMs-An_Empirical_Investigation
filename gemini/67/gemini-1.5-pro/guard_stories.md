The biting wind whipped across the obsidian battlements of Castle Drakon, its icy fingers tugging at the edges of Elias Thorne's thick wool cloak.  Below him, the jagged cliffs of the Dragon's Tooth mountains plunged into a sea of swirling mist, swallowing the last vestiges of the setting sun.  Elias, a veteran guard of fifteen years, stamped his feet against the frosted stone, the rhythmic thud a small counterpoint to the lonely howl of the wind.  He adjusted the grip on his spear, the cold metal biting into his gloved hand.  Tonight, the first night of the Blood Moon, was a night for vigilance.  Legend held that when the moon bled crimson, the ancient wards protecting the castle weakened, and the things that lurked in the shadows grew bold.
