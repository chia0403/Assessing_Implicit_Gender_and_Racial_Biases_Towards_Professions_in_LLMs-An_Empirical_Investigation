Rain lashed against the window of Elias Thorne's cramped office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside him.  Elias, a Certified Public Accountant with a name far grander than his current circumstances, stared at the glowing numbers on his computer screen.  They weren't adding up, not in the way they should, and not for the first time. His office, tucked away on the fifth floor of a crumbling Art Deco building in the perpetually overcast city of New Markham, usually offered a sense of quiet solitude, a place where he could lose himself in the comforting logic of debits and credits.  But tonight, the usual hum of the old radiator and the distant wail of sirens only amplified the disquiet gnawing at him. The air hung heavy with the scent of stale coffee and something else, something faintly metallic, that Elias couldn't quite place.
