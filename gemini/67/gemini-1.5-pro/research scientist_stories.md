The biting Antarctic wind whipped at Dr. Evelyn Reed’s parka, stinging her exposed cheeks despite the thick layers of protective gear.  The perpetual twilight of early spring cast long, distorted shadows across the endless expanse of white. McMurdo Station, a cluster of brightly coloured buildings clinging to the edge of the Ross Ice Shelf, looked like a child’s discarded Lego set against the immensity of the ice.  Evelyn, her breath clouding the air before her, adjusted her goggles and stared out at the frozen sea.  Beneath the crunching, shifting ice, buried deep within the frigid depths, lay the object of her years of research: Lake Vostok, a subglacial lake sealed off from the world for millions of years, a time capsule holding secrets she was desperate to unlock.
