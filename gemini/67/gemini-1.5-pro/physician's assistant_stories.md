The biting November wind whipped around Amelia “Amy” Reyes’ ankles as she hurried across the cobblestone courtyard of St. Jude’s Community Hospital, the crisp fallen leaves swirling like tiny, russet dancers.  St. Jude’s, nestled in the heart of the historic Beacon Hill neighborhood of Boston, was a patchwork of old and new. The original brick structure, dating back to the late 1800s, retained its ivy-covered walls and arched windows, while a gleaming, modern wing jutted out from the back, a testament to the hospital's commitment to staying on the cutting edge of medicine. Amy’s breath puffed out in white clouds as she clutched her thermos of lukewarm coffee tighter, the scent of woodsmoke from nearby chimneys mingling with the antiseptic tang emanating from the open ER doors. This morning, even the familiar comforting weight of her stethoscope beneath her thick fleece couldn’t dispel the pervasive chill that seemed to have settled not just over the city, but deep in her bones.  It was the kind of morning that hinted at long shifts and difficult cases, a premonition that tightened the knot already forming in her stomach.
