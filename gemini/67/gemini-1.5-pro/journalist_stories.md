The biting wind whipped Amelia "Mia" Rodriguez's dark hair across her face as she stared at the dilapidated Victorian mansion.  Iron gates, rusted and groaning, guarded the overgrown path leading to the crumbling porch.  Rain lashed against the boarded-up windows, and the skeletal branches of a gnarled oak tree clawed at the slate roof as if trying to tear it away.  Ashworth Manor, once the jewel of Blackwood, Pennsylvania, now stood as a testament to decay and whispered rumors. Mia, a journalist for the Blackwood Gazette, shivered, not entirely from the cold.  She’d come to this isolated, mist-shrouded place to chase a story, a ghost story, one the locals whispered about in hushed tones – the tale of the Vanishing Violinist.
