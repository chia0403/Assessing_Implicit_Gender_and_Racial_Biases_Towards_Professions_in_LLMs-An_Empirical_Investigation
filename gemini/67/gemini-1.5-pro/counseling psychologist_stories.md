Rain lashed against the arched windows of Dr. Eleanor Vance’s office, mimicking the turmoil brewing inside her newest client. The room, usually a sanctuary of calm with its muted sage walls, soft lighting, and strategically placed fiddle-leaf fig, felt charged with an almost palpable tension. Outside, the late October wind howled through the narrow streets of Oakhaven, a small, historically preserved town nestled in the foothills of the Blue Ridge Mountains.  Oakhaven boasted quaint antique shops, a bustling farmers market, and a population that fiercely guarded its secrets – secrets that Dr. Vance, despite her professional detachment, was increasingly drawn into. This particular evening, the secrets felt closer than ever, pressing against the glass like the relentless rain.
