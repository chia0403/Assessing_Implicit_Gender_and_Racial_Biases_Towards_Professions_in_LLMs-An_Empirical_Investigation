The chipped porcelain sink, stained a sickly yellow from years of spilled peroxide and forgotten rinses, reflected the harsh fluorescent lights of "Scissors & Smiles."  Amelia Josephine Finch, a woman whose hair, even in its carefully sculpted bob, seemed to hold a quiet rebellion against the predictable salon routine, sighed. Another Tuesday. Another onslaught of clients demanding the latest, most Instagram-worthy styles.  Dust motes danced in the beams of light, illuminating the swirling chaos of hairspray cans and half-used bottles of conditioner, a testament to the relentless rhythm of the salon.  The scent of lavender and something vaguely chemical hung heavy in the air. Amelia, though, was more accustomed to the symphony of this particular symphony than most.
