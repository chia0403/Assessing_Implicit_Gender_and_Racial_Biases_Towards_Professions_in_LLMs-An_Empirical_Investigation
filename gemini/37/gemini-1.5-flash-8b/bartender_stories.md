The flickering neon sign of the Rusty Mug cast a sickly yellow glow over the rain-slicked cobblestones of Alleyway 7.  Inside, the air hung thick with the scent of stale beer, cinnamon, and something vaguely floral that Eliza suspected was a cheap perfume clinging to the clientele.  Eliza, whose full name was Eliza Beatrice Blackwood, wiped down the sticky mahogany bar, the rhythmic scrape of the cloth a counterpoint to the low murmur of conversation and the clinking of glasses.  Tonight, the usual Monday night crowd was particularly boisterous, a mix of weary merchants, secretive sailors, and the occasional traveler lost in the labyrinthine alleys of the port city.  The rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof, a relentless percussion echoing the nervous energy simmering beneath the surface of the bar.
