The biting November wind whipped Amelia Blackwood's trench coat around her legs as she hurried down the cobbled lane.  Rain, a relentless, icy curtain, plastered the ancient stone houses to her vision.  Fog, thick and clinging, obscured the already dim afternoon light, shrouding the picturesque but undeniably melancholic village of Aldercroft in a perpetual twilight.  Amelia, a seasoned real estate agent with a sharp eye for potential and a heart that ached for forgotten stories, adjusted her spectacles.  Aldercroft, nestled deep within the Derbyshire Peak District, was known for its stunning views, its quiet charm, and its deeply rooted history, but recent years had seen a decline in both fortunes and families.  The houses, though beautiful, often carried the weight of decades of unspoken secrets. Amelia was tasked with selling the sprawling, ivy-covered manor house on the edge of the village, a property steeped in legend and rumored to be haunted.  It was a challenge, even for someone as driven as she was.
