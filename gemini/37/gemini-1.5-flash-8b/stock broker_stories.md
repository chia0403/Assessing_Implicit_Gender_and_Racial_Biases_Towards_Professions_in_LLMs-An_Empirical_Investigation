The flickering fluorescent lights of the trading floor hummed a relentless, monotonous tune, a soundtrack to the cacophony of ringing phones and the frantic typing of Amelia Hayes, Stock Broker.  The air, thick with the smell of stale coffee and expensive cologne, hung heavy, mirroring the oppressive weight of the market downturn.  Outside, the city of New Zenith, a metropolis built on gleaming skyscrapers and whispered anxieties, swirled in a grey haze, a tangible reflection of the market's volatile mood.  Amelia, a whirlwind of nervous energy in her tailored suit, navigated the labyrinthine aisles, her movements a blur against the backdrop of ceaseless activity.  Each ticker tape update, each whispered rumour, sent shivers down her spine.  The relentless pressure to perform, to maintain the meticulously constructed facade of calm professionalism, was a constant companion.  Today, it felt like a vise.
