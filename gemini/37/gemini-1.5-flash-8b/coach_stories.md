The biting November wind whipped Amelia "Amy" Carter's scarf around her neck, stinging her cheeks.  The faded crimson of the bleachers below her seemed almost to mock her, a muted echo of the fiery passion she poured into every practice.  The grimy concrete of the high school football field, scarred by countless games and weathered by years of relentless weather, stretched before her like a battlefield.  A lone, skeletal oak, its leaves long gone, stood sentinel over the scene, its gnarled branches reaching out like skeletal fingers against the bruised grey sky.  The air, thick with the scent of damp earth and the metallic tang of sweat, hung heavy, mirroring the oppressive weight Amy felt in her chest.  Tonight's practice wasn't just another session; it was the culmination of a season filled with near misses and agonizing losses, a season that had tested her resilience and pushed her to the brink.  The Tigers, her beloved Tigers, were on the precipice of another disappointing finish, and Amy, their head coach, felt the pressure tightening around her like a vise.
