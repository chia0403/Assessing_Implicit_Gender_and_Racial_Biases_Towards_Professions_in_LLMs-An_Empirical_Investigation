The biting wind, a relentless Siberian serpent, whipped across the desolate landscape, stinging Elias Thorne's exposed cheeks.  Dust devils, miniature tornadoes of ochre and grey, danced across the cracked earth, swirling around the hulking silhouette of the Caterpillar 992H.  Elias, a heavy equipment operator with calloused hands and eyes the color of a stormy sea, felt the machine beneath his weathered palms vibrate in rhythmic counterpoint to the wind's relentless howls.  He was alone, miles from the nearest outpost of the Northerner's Mining Company, in the heart of the frozen tundra, deep within the perpetually shadowed valleys of the Kolyma Mountains.  The year was 1937.
