The chipped paint of the classroom walls, a faded, sickly yellow, seemed to absorb the late afternoon sun, casting long, skeletal shadows across the rows of desks.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of light piercing the gloom, illuminating the intricate, almost obsessive, patterns carved into the worn wooden surface of Miss Eleanor Vance's desk.  Eleanor Vance, a third-grade teacher at Oakhaven Elementary, sighed, the sound barely audible above the soft rustle of papers and the muffled murmur of children playing hopscotch in the deserted playground beyond the classroom window.  It was the quiet before the storm, the pre-twilight lull before the inevitable flood of homework papers, report cards, and parent-teacher conferences that always seemed to descend upon her like a particularly heavy rain.  Today, though, the quiet held a different kind of weight, a palpable tension that had nothing to do with the impending end of the school day.  It had everything to do with the mysterious note tucked beneath a stack of overdue library books.
