The flickering fluorescent lights of the "Numbers & Nonsense" accounting firm cast long, distorted shadows across the rows of beige cubicles.  Dust motes danced in the meagre afternoon sunlight that slanted through the grimy windowpanes, illuminating a faint layer of grime on everything.  Amelia Penelope Featherstone, bookkeeper extraordinaire (and self-proclaimed champion of the semicolon), hunched over her battered laptop, the rhythmic clack of her keyboard a lonely counterpoint to the hum of the air conditioning and the occasional muffled groan from a colleague struggling with a spreadsheet gone rogue.  Outside, the city thrummed with a life Amelia rarely touched.  Her world existed within the sterile confines of this office building, a concrete fortress housing the financial lives of countless others.  Today, however, the numbers seemed to whisper secrets she couldn't quite decipher.
