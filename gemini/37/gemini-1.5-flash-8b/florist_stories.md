The biting London wind whipped Amelia Nightingale's crimson coat around her legs as she hurried down the cobbled alleyway.  Grey skies, perpetually draped over the city, mirrored the gloom that had settled over her flower shop, "Bloom & Thistle," for the past fortnight.  The scent of damp earth and decaying leaves clung to the air, a stark contrast to the vibrant floral bouquets she meticulously crafted.  Amelia, a wisp of a woman with eyes the colour of forget-me-nots, pulled her thick scarf tighter, her breath misting in the frigid air.  Despite the chill, a flicker of determination remained within her, a stubborn flame against the encroaching despair.  She'd been meticulously arranging a shipment of snowdrops, a delicate dance of white petals, a silent promise of spring in the heart of winter, when the sudden, guttural bellow of a passing lorry made her flinch.  A single, enormous black rose, torn from a nearby bush by a cruel hand, lay crushed at her feet.
