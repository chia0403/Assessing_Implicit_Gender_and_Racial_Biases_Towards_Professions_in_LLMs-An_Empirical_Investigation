The flickering neon sign of the "Rusty Mug" cast a sickly yellow glow on Amelia Hayes, her face a mask of weary determination.  Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof, a relentless percussion echoing the frantic rhythm of her thoughts.  Outside, the city sprawled beneath a bruised twilight sky, the skeletal remains of skyscrapers piercing the gloom like skeletal fingers.  Amelia, a seasoned reporter for the *Crimson Chronicle*, hunched over a chipped Formica table, the greasy remnants of a lukewarm stew clinging to the surface.  Across from her, a grimy window offered a bleak view of the decaying industrial district, a place where forgotten dreams and whispered secrets clung to the damp brick walls like cobwebs.  Tonight, Amelia felt like a spider, trapped in the ever-widening web of a story that refused to unravel. Her name was Amelia Hayes, and this was not just another assignment. This was something more insidious, more disturbing.  This was the Whisperwind case.
