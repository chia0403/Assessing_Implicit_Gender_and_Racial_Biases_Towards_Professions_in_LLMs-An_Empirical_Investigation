The chipped Formica countertop gleamed under the fluorescent hum of the law firm, a dull counterpoint to the frantic tapping of Amelia Petrova's fingers on the keyboard.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of afternoon sunlight slicing through the grimy window, illuminating the overflowing stacks of legal documents that seemed to reach for the fluorescent ceiling like grasping skeletal hands.  Amelia, a paralegal at the prestigious yet perpetually understaffed firm of Sterling & Stone, was buried in a mountain of paperwork for the upcoming Smith-Jones corporation antitrust trial.  The air, thick with the scent of stale coffee and desperation, hung heavy in the sterile cubicles.  Across from her, the rhythmic whir of the photocopying machine punctuated the quiet clatter of the office. This was her life, a life of late nights, endless redaction requests, and whispered anxieties about deadlines looming like a storm on the horizon.
