The flickering neon sign of "Sparky's Electric Fix-It" cast a sickly yellow glow across the rain-slicked alley.  Rain hammered against the corrugated metal roof, a relentless drumming that echoed the frantic rhythm in Elias Thorne's chest.  He, Elias Thorne, master electrician and self-proclaimed "Sparky" to the local clientele, crouched beneath a rusted fire escape, his head bent over a tangle of wires thicker than his forearm.  The air, already thick with the smell of ozone and damp earth, was punctuated by the metallic tang of something burning.  This wasn't just a simple short circuit; this was a symphony of electrical chaos, a symphony he felt personally responsible for conducting.  Above him, a third-floor window glowed with a disconcerting, almost feverish, light.  The source of the problem, and the source of his growing unease, was inside.
