The biting wind, a relentless sculptor itself, whipped across the exposed slopes of the Grey Peaks, stinging Elara Stonehand's cheeks.  Dust, the fine, grey grit of centuries, swirled around her like a phantom army, obscuring the jagged peaks and the valley far below.  She was perched precariously on a narrow ledge, the only visible part of her being the hunched figure and the glint of her steel chisel in the fading afternoon sun.  Her name, whispered by the wind, was more than just a label; it was a testament to the life carved into her very bones. Elara Stonehand, daughter of the late Master Stonemason Theron, worked the quarries of the Shadowfell, a harsh and isolated region famed for its resilient, obsidian-black stone, a stone that held the secrets of ages past.
