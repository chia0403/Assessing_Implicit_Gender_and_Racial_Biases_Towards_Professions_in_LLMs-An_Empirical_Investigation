The chipped ceramic mug warmed Amelia “Mia” Hernandez’s hands, the lukewarm chamomile tea doing little to soothe the icy dread that had settled in her stomach. Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of her travel agency, "Wanderlust Expeditions," blurring the already hazy cityscape of Seattle.  Inside, the office was a chaotic symphony of half-packed suitcases, brochures scattered like fallen autumn leaves, and the faint, lingering scent of exotic spices – a testament to Mia's recent, and wildly successful, trip to Morocco.  But the vibrant tapestry of her usual professional life felt muted, overshadowed by the looming shadow of her latest client:  a notoriously demanding billionaire, known only as Mr. Blackwood, who had booked a highly secretive, last-minute expedition to the remote Amazon rainforest.  The itinerary was sparse, the payment exorbitant, and the sheer unpredictability of it all had Mia's usually unflappable spirit teetering on the brink of panic.
