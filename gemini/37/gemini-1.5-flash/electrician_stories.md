The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Amy” Hernandez’s hair across her face as she wrestled with a recalcitrant junction box.  Frozen fingers fumbled with the corroded screws, the metallic tang of salt spray clinging to everything in the desolate, windswept lighthouse.  Perched precariously on the craggy cliffs overlooking the churning grey expanse of the Atlantic, the structure groaned under the relentless assault of the storm, its whitewashed walls battered and scarred by decades of relentless sea winds.  Amy, a seasoned electrician known for her ability to tame even the most temperamental of electrical systems, found herself facing a challenge far beyond faulty wiring; the lighthouse’s ancient generator was sputtering its last breaths, threatening to plunge the entire coastline into darkness.
