The salt spray stung Amelia “Mia” Hernandez’s face as she wrestled the stubborn yacht door open.  The wind, a raw, relentless Pacific beast, howled a mournful dirge around the *Seraphina*, its canvas sails flapping like tattered flags in surrender.  Below, the churning grey water of the Monterey Bay clawed at the hull, a hungry, unforgiving beast mirroring the turmoil in Mia’s own gut.  The air, thick with the scent of brine and impending rain, offered little comfort.  This wasn't the picturesque coastline of brochures; this was a scene of desperate urgency, and Mia, a high-powered maritime lawyer with a reputation for ruthlessness, was about to become intimately involved.
