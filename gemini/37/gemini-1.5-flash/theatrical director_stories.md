The biting November wind whipped Amelia Hawthorne's auburn hair across her face as she wrestled with a recalcitrant stage door.  Rust groaned a mournful protest with each heave, mirroring the turmoil churning within her.  Behind the crumbling brick façade of the Grand Majestic Theatre, a symphony of chaos reigned.  Hammers pounded, saws shrieked, and the air hung thick with the scent of sawdust, stale beer, and a faint, lingering perfume of desperation – the signature aroma of a production teetering on the brink of collapse.  Tonight, Amelia, the formidable director of the Grand Majestic's ambitious revival of "The Tempest," needed a miracle.  Or at least, a functioning stage door.
