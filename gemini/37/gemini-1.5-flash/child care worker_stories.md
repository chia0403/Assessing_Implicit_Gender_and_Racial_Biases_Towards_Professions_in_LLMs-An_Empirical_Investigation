The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s hair across her face as she hurried down Sycamore Street, the collar of her worn peacoat pulled high.  The scent of woodsmoke and damp leaves clung to the air, a familiar autumnal perfume that usually soothed her, but tonight, it only served to heighten the gnawing anxiety in her stomach.  Sycamore Street, nestled in the shadow of the imposing Blackwood Hills, was usually a picture of quiet suburban charm – neat brick houses, well-manicured lawns, the occasional mischievous squirrel darting across a path. Tonight, however, an unnerving stillness hung over it, broken only by the rhythmic crunch of her boots on the frosted pavement.  Amelia's destination:  the brightly lit windows of "Little Wonders Daycare," where the muffled sounds of children’s laughter, usually a comforting balm, now felt strangely distant and muted.
