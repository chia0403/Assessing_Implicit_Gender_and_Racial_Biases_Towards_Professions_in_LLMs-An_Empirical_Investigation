The scent of lavender and beeswax hung heavy in the air, a fragrant counterpoint to the damp chill seeping in through the cracked windowpanes of Elias Thorne's tailoring shop.  Rain lashed against the cobblestones of  Bleak Street, a relentless drumming that echoed the frantic rhythm of Elias's needle as he stitched a fine silken seam.  His shop, nestled between a crumbling apothecary and a perpetually shuttered bakery, was a haven of quiet industry, a tiny island of warmth and order in the swirling chaos of the ever-growing city of Porthaven.  The gaslight sputtering above his workbench cast long, dancing shadows across the bolts of richly coloured cloth piled high against the walls, their vibrant hues a stark contrast to the greyness of the storm raging outside. Elias, his brow furrowed in concentration, paused only momentarily to wipe a bead of sweat from his forehead with the back of his hand, the faintest trace of chalk dust clinging to his fingertips.
