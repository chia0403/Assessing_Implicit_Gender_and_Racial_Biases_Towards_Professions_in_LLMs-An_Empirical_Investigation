The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia “Mia” Holloway’s hands, the lukewarm tea doing little to soothe the icy dread that had settled in her stomach.  Rain lashed against the tall, gothic windows of her attic office, each gust a frantic fingertip tapping against the dusty panes.  From her perch amongst towering stacks of manuscripts – some pristine, others dog-eared and stained with coffee – she could see the sprawl of London below, a hazy, grey ocean punctuated by the skeletal silhouettes of church steeples.  The city, usually a vibrant tapestry of life, felt tonight like a brooding, watchful beast, mirroring the turmoil brewing within her.  Her deadline for the prestigious Blackwood Literary Prize loomed, a monstrous shadow threatening to consume her entirely.  And the final manuscript, the one that held the potential to either elevate her career to dazzling heights or send it plummeting into oblivion, lay unopened on her desk, a stark white rectangle mocking her procrastination.
