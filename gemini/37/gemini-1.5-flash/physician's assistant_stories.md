The biting November wind whipped Amelia "Amy" Hernandez's hair across her face as she hurried down the cobbled street, the scent of woodsmoke and roasting chestnuts doing little to alleviate the chill that seeped into her bones.  Above, the gothic spires of St. Jude's Cathedral pierced the bruised purple sky, their gargoyles leering down at the bustling marketplace below.  Amy pulled her worn wool coat tighter, the collar scratching against her already chapped cheeks.  She wasn't used to this kind of weather;  she’d traded the sun-drenched beaches of her native California for the grey, melancholic beauty of Edinburgh barely six months ago, lured by a position at the renowned – and notoriously demanding – Blackwood Clinic.  Tonight, however, the clinic's reputation wasn't what weighed on her mind.  Tonight, it was the gnawing anxiety twisting in her gut, a feeling that something was terribly wrong.
