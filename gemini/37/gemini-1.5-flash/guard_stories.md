The biting wind howled a mournful song across the jagged peaks of the Dragon's Teeth mountains, whipping snow into frenzied spirals that stung Elias Thorne's exposed cheeks.  Elias, a veteran guard of the Obsidian Citadel, shifted his weight from one frostbitten foot to the other, the rough wool of his cloak offering little respite from the relentless cold.  The Citadel itself, a monolithic fortress of black basalt clinging precariously to the cliff face, loomed above him, a silent, brooding giant against the bruised purple of the twilight sky.  Below, the chasm yawned, a dizzying drop into the unforgiving wilderness, where rumour whispered of monstrous creatures and forgotten magic.  Tonight, however, Elias’s concern wasn’t mythical beasts, but the creeping unease that had settled over the Citadel like a shroud of ice.  Something was wrong, he could feel it in the bone-deep chill that permeated even the thickest stone walls.
