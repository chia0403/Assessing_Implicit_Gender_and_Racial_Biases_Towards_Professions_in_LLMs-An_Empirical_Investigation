The salt spray stung Detective Inspector Ava Thorne's face as the November wind howled a mournful dirge across the desolate expanse of Blackpool beach.  The skeletal remains of the Pleasure Beach rollercoaster loomed against the bruised, twilight sky, their rusted carriages resembling the claws of some gargantuan, metal beast.  Ava pulled her collar tighter, the thin material offering little protection against the biting wind that whipped sand into her already weary eyes. The air, thick with the smell of brine and something else… something metallic and faintly sickening, clung to her like a shroud. This was it, the scene of the latest – and most baffling – disappearance to plague Blackpool’s usually boisterous seaside charm.  Three young women, all vanished within a month, leaving behind only whispers, anxieties, and a growing sense of dread that gnawed at the edges of the town's festive veneer.
