The humid air hung heavy, thick with the scent of frying plantains and diesel fumes.  Cicadas buzzed a relentless, rhythmic chorus that competed with the sporadic blasts of car horns battling for dominance on the potholed streets of Santo Domingo.  Amelia Hernandez Reyes, a journalist with ink permanently stained under her fingernails and a weariness etched deep into her usually bright eyes, adjusted the strap of her worn leather satchel.  The midday sun beat down relentlessly, turning the vibrant colours of the colonial buildings into shimmering mirages.  Amelia squinted, her gaze fixed on the crumbling façade of the Palacio de los Alcazares Coloniales, its history a silent witness to the unfolding drama that had brought her here – a drama she was about to unravel, one thread at a time.
