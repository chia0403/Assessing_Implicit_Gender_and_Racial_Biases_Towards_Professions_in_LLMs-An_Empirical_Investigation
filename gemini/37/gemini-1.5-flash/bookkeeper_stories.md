The chipped porcelain teacup warmed Amelia Katherine Finch's fingers, a meager comfort against the November chill seeping through the drafty window of her office.  Rain lashed against the leaded panes, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her.  Amelia, a bookkeeper of meticulous habits and even more meticulous accounts, stared at the ledger spread before her, its neat columns of figures mocking her current state of disarray.  The air hung thick with the scent of old paper, dust motes dancing in the weak light filtering through the grime-coated window of Finch & Finch Accountants, a business as old and creaky as the Victorian townhouse it occupied on the rain-soaked cobblestone street of Grimsborough Lane.  Outside, the gas lamps flickered, casting long, distorted shadows that seemed to writhe with the city's secrets.  Inside, Amelia wrestled with a secret of her own, one far more complicated than any balance sheet.
