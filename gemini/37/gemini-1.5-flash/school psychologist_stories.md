The flickering fluorescent lights of Northwood High hummed a discordant lullaby, a counterpoint to the frantic scratching of Dr. Eleanor Vance’s pen across her notepad.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of her office, blurring the already indistinct cityscape into a grey, impressionistic smear.  The scent of stale coffee and something vaguely floral – probably the air freshener attempting, and failing, to mask the underlying aroma of teenage angst – hung heavy in the air.  Eleanor, her auburn braid loosened and escaping its clasp, sighed, the sound swallowed by the relentless drumming of the storm outside.  Another student crisis, another late night wrestling with the tangled emotions of adolescents grappling with a world far too complex for their still-developing minds.  Tonight's subject:  a seemingly quiet, withdrawn girl named Chloe Miller, whose descent into silence had become alarmingly rapid.
