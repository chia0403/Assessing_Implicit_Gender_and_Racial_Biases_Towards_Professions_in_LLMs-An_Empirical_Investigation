The scent of sandalwood and chamomile hung heavy in the air, a comforting blanket against the relentless November chill that clawed at the windows of Serenity Spa.  Rain lashed against the frosted panes, each drop a tiny percussion against the otherwise peaceful hush of the late afternoon.  Inside, the only sound was the rhythmic, almost hypnotic,  drip-drip-drip of a leaky faucet in the unused restroom down the hall.  Elara Genevieve Thorne, a woman whose hands possessed the magic to melt away tension and unlock buried knots, sat at her small, cluttered desk, meticulously arranging the smooth, warm stones for her next client.  The dim, amber lighting reflected in the polished surface of a jade roller, mirroring the quiet determination in her amber eyes.  Outside, the storm raged, but within the walls of Serenity Spa, Elara held a small, carefully cultivated sanctuary of calm.
