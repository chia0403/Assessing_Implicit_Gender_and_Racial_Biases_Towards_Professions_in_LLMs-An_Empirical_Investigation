The biting November wind whipped Dr. Eleanor Vance’s already-frazzled nerves into a frenzy.  Rain, the kind that lashed against the ancient stone of St. Jude’s Hospital like a vengeful spirit, plastered her dark hair to her forehead.  She pulled her collar tighter against the chill, the damp seeping through the thin fabric of her coat, mirroring the icy dread that had settled in her stomach.  St. Jude’s, perched precariously on a windswept cliff overlooking the churning grey expanse of the Atlantic, was a gothic masterpiece – beautiful, imposing, and utterly isolated.  Tonight, that isolation felt less like a picturesque setting and more like a prison. The emergency lights pulsed a sickly yellow against the rain-slicked stone, highlighting the hospital's ominous silhouette against the stormy sky, a backdrop to the silent battle unfolding within its walls.  Eleanor’s pager vibrated against her ribs, a jarring counterpoint to the rhythmic thump-thump-thump of her own racing heart.  Another trauma case.  And judging by the urgency of the alert, this one might just break her.
