The salt spray stung Eleanor Vance's face as she wrestled the stubborn fishing net from its tangled heap on the deck of the "Sea Serpent."  The late afternoon sun, a bruised plum sinking towards the turbulent Atlantic, cast long shadows across the weathered wood.  The air, thick with the scent of brine and diesel, was far removed from the sterile, antiseptic smell of the rehabilitation clinic where Eleanor usually spent her days.  This, however, wasn't just any Tuesday afternoon.  This was the culmination of a year-long, painstakingly planned adventure, a desperate gamble to help a patient far beyond the reach of conventional occupational therapy.  The craggy cliffs of the Isle of Skye loomed before her, a formidable silhouette against the fiery sky, promising both breathtaking beauty and a challenge far more daunting than any she'd faced in her twenty years as an occupational therapist.
