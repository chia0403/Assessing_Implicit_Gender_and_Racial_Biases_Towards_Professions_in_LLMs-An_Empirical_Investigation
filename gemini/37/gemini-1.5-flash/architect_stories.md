The biting November wind whipped Amelia Kensington’s charcoal-grey coat around her legs as she stood on the precipice of the cliff, the spray from the churning Atlantic a cold kiss on her face.  Below, the jagged rocks clawed at the turbulent sea, a stark contrast to the seemingly serene, sprawling Victorian mansion perched precariously on the clifftop.  Its once-proud façade, now scarred by decades of neglect and the relentless assault of the elements, was a testament to both enduring beauty and heartbreaking decay.  Amelia, a renowned architect with a reputation for breathing life back into crumbling structures, felt a familiar thrill – a blend of excitement and apprehension – tighten in her chest. This wasn't just another renovation; this was Blackwood Manor, and its secrets ran deeper than the ocean itself.
