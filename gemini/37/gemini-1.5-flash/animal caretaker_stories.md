The humid air hung heavy, thick with the scent of decaying leaves and something subtly…musky.  Cicadas buzzed a relentless, hypnotic rhythm against the backdrop of rustling bamboo.  Amelia Katherine "Amy" Holloway, her khaki shirt damp with sweat, leaned against the chipped concrete wall of the enclosure, a worn leather notepad clutched in her hand.  Before her sprawled the meticulously maintained habitat of Rajah, a magnificent Sumatran orangutan, his rusty-red fur gleaming in the dappled sunlight filtering through the canopy above.  The Kuala Lumpur Orangutan Sanctuary, nestled deep within the Malaysian rainforest, was Amy’s world, a symphony of chirps, howls, and the constant, earthy pulse of the jungle itself.  But today, the familiar sounds were overshadowed by a growing unease, a prickling at the back of her neck that whispered of something wrong.
