The chipped paint of the "Guidance Counselor" sign on Ms. Eleanor Vance's door seemed to mirror the cracks in the foundation of Northwood High.  Rain lashed against the warped windowpanes, a relentless percussion accompanying the muffled shouts emanating from the hallway – the soundtrack to another Tuesday at a school teetering on the brink of chaos.  Inside, the air hung thick with the scent of stale coffee and teenage angst, a familiar aroma that clung to Eleanor’s cardigan like a second skin.  The fluorescent lights hummed a discordant tune, flickering intermittently as if mimicking the uncertain futures of the students who would soon fill her cramped office, seeking solace, advice, or just a momentary reprieve from the relentless pressure cooker that was their high school existence.  Eleanor, perched behind her desk, a mountain of student files threatening to topple, sighed.  Another day, another deluge of adolescent drama.
