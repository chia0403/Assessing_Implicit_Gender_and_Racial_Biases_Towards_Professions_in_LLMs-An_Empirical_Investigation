The biting November wind whipped Dr. Eleanor Vance’s auburn hair across her face as she surveyed the scene.  Rain, a relentless, icy drizzle, plastered her trench coat to her frame, mirroring the grim dampness clinging to the derelict warehouse before her.  Rust-eaten corrugated iron groaned under the gusts, a mournful counterpoint to the muted sirens wailing in the distance.  Inside, the air hung thick with the metallic tang of blood, a scent that, despite years spent navigating the darkest corners of the human psyche, still managed to prickle at the edges of Eleanor's carefully cultivated composure.  This wasn’t just another crime scene; the palpable sense of violence, of raw, unchecked rage, vibrated through the very foundations of the decaying building, a silent scream trapped within crumbling brick and mortar.  Tonight, the city of Ashwood held its breath, waiting for Eleanor Vance to unravel its secrets.
