The scent of aged paper and leather hung heavy in the air, a comforting blanket against the relentless November chill that gnawed at the stone walls of the Blackwood Library.  Rain lashed against the tall, gothic windows, each gust a mournful sigh echoing the emptiness Amelia Katherine Thistlewick felt in her own heart.  Amelia, or Millie as she preferred, meticulously straightened a row of first editions, her fingers tracing the gilded lettering with a practiced ease born from years spent amongst these silent sentinels of history.  The library, a sprawling labyrinth of towering shelves and hushed alcoves, was her sanctuary, her kingdom, a world away from the relentless anxieties that clawed at the edges of her carefully constructed routine. Tonight, however, even the comforting weight of centuries of knowledge couldn't quite quell the unsettling tremor that had begun in the pit of her stomach hours ago, a premonition as sharp and cold as the wind howling outside.
