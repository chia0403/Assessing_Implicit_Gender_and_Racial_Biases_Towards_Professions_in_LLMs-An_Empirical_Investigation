The fluorescent lights of the Zenith Corporation hummed a monotonous tune, a soundtrack to Amelia Katherine Hayes's otherwise silent existence.  Amelia, or Amy as most people – those few who bothered to remember her name – called her, sat perched on a stool behind the reception desk, a polished mahogany beast that had probably seen more executive meltdowns than actual executives.  The air hung thick with the scent of stale coffee and desperation, a familiar perfume in the sterile, glass-walled lobby of Zenith's downtown headquarters.  Outside, the relentless Chicago wind howled, a stark contrast to the oppressive calm within, a calm that Amy knew was only the eye of the corporate storm.  This particular Monday morning, however, the storm felt particularly ominous.  A low, persistent thrumming vibrated through the floor, a sensation that had nothing to do with the city's usual subterranean rumble and everything to do with the palpable tension radiating from the closed-door executive suite.
