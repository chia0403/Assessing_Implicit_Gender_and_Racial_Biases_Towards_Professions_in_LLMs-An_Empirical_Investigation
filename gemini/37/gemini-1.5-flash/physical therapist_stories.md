The salt spray stung Dr. Eleanor Vance's face as she wrestled the stubborn fishing net from its tangle of seaweed.  The late afternoon sun, a bruised plum sinking below the churning grey-green waters of the Atlantic, cast long shadows across the rocky outcrop where she stood.  The air, thick with the scent of brine and decaying kelp, carried the mournful cry of gulls overhead.  This wasn't exactly the typical setting for a physical therapist, but then, Eleanor Vance had never been typical.  Her small, seaside clinic, perched precariously on the cliffs overlooking the unforgiving ocean, mirrored her own life – a blend of rugged beauty and unpredictable challenges.  Today’s challenge, however, involved a recalcitrant fishing net and a rapidly approaching tide.
