The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Amy” Hernandez’s hair across her face as she surveyed the desolate practice field.  Frozen clumps of mud clung to the worn cleats of the empty goalposts, mirroring the icy knot of apprehension tightening in her stomach.  The air, thick with the scent of damp earth and decaying leaves, carried the mournful sigh of a lone, wind-battered oak tree at the edge of the field.  This wasn't the sun-drenched California paradise she'd envisioned when she’d accepted the coaching position at Northwood High; this was a bleak, windswept corner of rural Oregon, a place where the relentless rain seemed to seep into your bones and the perpetual gloom threatened to extinguish even the fiercest spirit.  And tonight, the gloom felt particularly heavy.  The upcoming regional championship game loomed, and her team, the Northwood Wildcats, were facing a monumental challenge.  Their morale was lower than the already-sub-zero temperature, and Amy knew that even her legendary motivational speeches might not be enough to turn the tide.
