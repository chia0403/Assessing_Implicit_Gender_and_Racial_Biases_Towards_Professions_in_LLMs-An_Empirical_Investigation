The wind, a rasping, icy hand, clawed at Elias Thorne's weathered face as he surveyed his fields.  Dawn painted the sky in bruised purples and angry oranges, a stark contrast to the blanket of snow that had fallen relentlessly for the past three days, burying Thorne's livelihood under a suffocating white shroud.  His farm, Oakhaven, nestled in the valley below the brooding silhouette of Mount Cinder, usually a picture of rustic charm, was now a landscape of frozen stillness, the skeletal branches of apple trees etched against the pale canvas of the snow.  The air, thick with the scent of pine and the biting chill of the approaching winter storm, carried the distant mournful cry of a hawk, a sound that mirrored the despair churning in Elias's heart.  This year, the harvest had been meager, and this relentless snow threatened to wipe out what little remained.
