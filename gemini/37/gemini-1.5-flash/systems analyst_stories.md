The flickering fluorescent lights of the server room hummed a discordant symphony, a counterpoint to the rhythmic *click-clack* of Amelia “Amy” Hernandez’s keyboard.  Dust motes, illuminated by the pale glow, danced in the stale air, a silent ballet performed for an audience of humming hard drives and blinking indicator lights.  Amy, a systems analyst for the sprawling, slightly chaotic tech firm known as NovaTech Solutions, squinted at the screen.  Lines of code scrolled past, a relentless river of digital information, and a knot of frustration tightened in her stomach.  The deadline for the Peterson Group project loomed – a week away, and the database was acting up again, spitting out cryptic error messages like a disgruntled oracle.  The air conditioning, usually a merciful balm against the oppressive heat generated by the server racks, had sputtered out hours ago, leaving Amy drenched in a fine sheen of sweat.  Tonight, it wasn't just the system that was overheating.
