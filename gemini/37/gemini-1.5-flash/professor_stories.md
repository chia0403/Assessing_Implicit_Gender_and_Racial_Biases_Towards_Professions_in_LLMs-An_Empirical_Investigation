Professor Augustine Blackwood adjusted his spectacles, the faint scent of old paper and pipe tobacco clinging to his tweed jacket.  Rain lashed against the tall, gothic windows of his study, a relentless drumming that mirrored the turmoil in his own mind.  The storm, however, was nothing compared to the tempest brewing within the hallowed halls of Blackwood Manor, a sprawling Victorian estate perched precariously on the windswept cliffs overlooking the churning grey expanse of the North Sea.  The manor, once a beacon of scholarly pursuit, now felt more like a mausoleum, its shadowed corners whispering secrets Augustine desperately wished remained buried.  Tonight, those secrets threatened to claw their way to the surface.
