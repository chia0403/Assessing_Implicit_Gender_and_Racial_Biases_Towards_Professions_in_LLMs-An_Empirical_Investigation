The scent of rosemary and vanilla hung heavy in the air, a comforting blanket against the relentless November chill biting at the windowpanes of "Shear Perfection."  Rain lashed against the glass, blurring the already fading twilight into a watercolor smudge of grey and purple. Inside, however, the salon was a warm haven, illuminated by the soft glow of strategically placed lamps that highlighted the sleek, minimalist décor.  Twenty-seven-year-old Seraphina “Sera” Bellweather, her fingers nimble and swift, worked with practiced ease, sculpting a cascade of auburn curls around her client’s face.  The rhythmic snip-snip of her shears was a counterpoint to the low hum of conversation drifting from the other styling stations, a familiar symphony that had been the soundtrack to Sera’s life for as long as she could remember.  Tonight, however, the usual quiet rhythm felt punctuated by an unsettling unease, a prickling sensation at the back of her neck that had nothing to do with the stray strands of hair clinging to her dark turtleneck.
