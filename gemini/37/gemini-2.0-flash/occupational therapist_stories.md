The chipped ceramic mug warmed Eleanor Vivian Ramsey’s hands, its familiar weight a small comfort against the bone-chilling rain lashing against the panoramic windows of her office. She watched the grey sky bleed into the grey Sound, the occasional ferry a ghostly silhouette in the gloom. It was the kind of late October day in Seattle that made you want to burrow under a pile of blankets and never leave the house. But Eleanor, like most days, was at Evergreen Senior Living, a sprawling complex perched on a bluff overlooking Puget Sound. It was a place of stunning views and, she’d learned over the last five years, equally stunning stories etched onto the faces of its residents.
