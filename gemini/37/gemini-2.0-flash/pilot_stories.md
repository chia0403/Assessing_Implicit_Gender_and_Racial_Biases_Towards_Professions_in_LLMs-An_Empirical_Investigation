The biting wind howled like a banshee, rattling the corrugated iron of the hangar and whipping snow into swirling devils across the frozen tundra. Inside, huddled against the chill of the Alaskan winter, Captain Anya Petrova shivered, despite the thick wool layers beneath her flight suit. The air hung thick with the smell of oil and the metallic tang of a working machine. Anya, a pilot with Icefield Airways, ran a gloved hand along the smooth fuselage of her beloved de Havilland Beaver, the "Spirit of the Yukon." Outside, the endless white expanse of the Wrangell-St. Elias National Park and Preserve stretched as far as the eye could see, a brutal, beautiful landscape that demanded respect. And respect, Anya knew, was earned one flight at a time.
