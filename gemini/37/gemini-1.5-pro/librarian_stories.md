The frosted breath of November clawed at the leaded glass windows of the Athelney Public Library, swirling the dust motes dancing in the weak afternoon sun. Inside, Elias Thorne, the head librarian, meticulously reshelved a returned copy of "A Shropshire Lad."  Athelney, a sleepy village nestled in the folds of the Vermont hills, was a place where time seemed to move at the pace of a browning leaf spiraling to the ground. The library, with its oak-paneled walls, the scent of aged paper and beeswax, and the rhythmic tick-tock of the grandfather clock in the corner, was its very heart.  It was a sanctuary Elias cherished, a world ordered and predictable, a stark contrast to the turbulent currents swirling beneath the placid surface of his own life.
