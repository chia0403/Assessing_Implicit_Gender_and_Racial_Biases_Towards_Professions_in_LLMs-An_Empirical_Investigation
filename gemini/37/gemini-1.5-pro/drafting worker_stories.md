The flickering fluorescent lights of Darrow & Sons Architectural Design hummed a monotonous tune, a soundtrack to Amelia Rose Hawthorne's life.  It was a Tuesday, much like any other Tuesday in the stifling summer of 1957.  The air in the small, windowless drafting room was thick with the scent of pencil shavings, stale coffee, and the faint chemical tang of blueprint paper. Amelia, perched on her stool, meticulously inked in the details of a sprawling ranch house destined for a sun-drenched lot in the burgeoning suburbs of Phoenix, Arizona.  Outside, the dry heat shimmered off the asphalt, baking the city, but in here, beneath the buzzing lights, Amelia’s world was one of precise lines, measured angles, and the quiet satisfaction of bringing someone else’s dream home to life, one careful stroke at a time.
