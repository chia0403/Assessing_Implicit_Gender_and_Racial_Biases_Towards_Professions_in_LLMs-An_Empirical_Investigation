The biting wind whipped across the frost-covered fields of Thistlebrook Farm, stinging Elias Thorne's cheeks raw. Dawn hadn't yet broken over the rolling hills of the Derbyshire Dales, but Elias, bundled in a thick wool coat and sturdy boots, was already out tending to his flock.  A thick blanket of fog clung to the valley, muting the usual sounds of the awakening countryside, leaving only the soft bleating of sheep and the crunch of Elias' boots on the frozen ground. Thistlebrook, nestled in a small, sheltered valley, had been in the Thorne family for generations. Its stone farmhouse, with smoke curling lazily from its chimney, was a beacon of warmth and familiarity in the bleak midwinter landscape.  This morning, however, the familiar comfort felt fragile, a thin veil over a growing unease that settled in Elias' stomach like a stone.
