The fluorescent lights of Northwood High hummed a monotonous B-flat, a soundtrack to Amelia Reyes’s Tuesday morning.  Rain lashed against the window of her cramped office, blurring the already faded view of the football field.  A chipped ceramic mug, proclaiming "World's Best Guidance Counselor" (a gift from a student who'd later dropped out to join a travelling circus), sat steaming on her desk amidst a precarious stack of college applications and aptitude tests. Amelia, her dark hair pulled back in a practical bun, tapped a chewed-down pen against a student file labelled “Jackson, Peter.”  Peter Jackson was a bright kid, but lately, his spark had dimmed, replaced by a listlessness that worried Amelia.  Northwood, nestled in the heart of the Oregon wilderness, was a small town where secrets festered like moss on fallen logs, and Amelia feared Peter’s was about to sprout.
