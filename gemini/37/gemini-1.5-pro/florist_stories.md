The bell above the door of "Petal & Stem" tinkled a melancholic two-note tune, the sound swallowed almost immediately by the humid air thick with the scent of lilies and damp earth.  Rain lashed against the large front window, blurring the view of the cobbled streets of Port Blossom, Oregon.  Elara Thistlewick, her fingers stained green from trimming stems, sighed and pushed a stray strand of auburn hair behind her ear.  It was the Tuesday after Memorial Day, typically a slow day, but the relentless downpour had made it practically desolate.  The only other occupant of the small shop was Jasper, a ginger cat who regarded the falling rain with haughty indifference from his perch atop a stack of terracotta pots.
