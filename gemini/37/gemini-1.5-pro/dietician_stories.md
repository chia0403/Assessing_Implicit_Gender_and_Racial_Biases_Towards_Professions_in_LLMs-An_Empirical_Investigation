The fluorescent lights of the "Nourish to Flourish" clinic hummed a low, almost apologetic tune, casting a sterile glow over the pale green walls. Rain lashed against the large window overlooking Elm Street, blurring the already grey November afternoon in Portland, Oregon.  Inside, Amelia Grace Hawthorne, RD, tapped a pen against her teeth, staring at the almost blank meal plan in front of her. Her client, a perpetually exhausted marathon runner, had just left, confessing a deep-seated and somewhat bewildering addiction to pickled beets. Amelia, a dietician known for her calm demeanor and creative solutions, found herself stumped.  Pickled beets? This was a new one.  The scent of antiseptic hand sanitizer mingled with the faint, earthy aroma of damp wool clinging to the empty chair opposite her desk.  Outside, the wind howled, a mournful soundtrack to Amelia's beet-induced predicament.
