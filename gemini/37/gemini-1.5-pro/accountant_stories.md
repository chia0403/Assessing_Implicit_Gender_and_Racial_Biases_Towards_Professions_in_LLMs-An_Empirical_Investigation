The rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of Archibald Finch's rented office, a relentless metallic drumming that mirrored the anxiety churning in his stomach.  Archibald, a Certified Public Accountant with a neatly trimmed mustache and a penchant for perfectly pressed trousers, stared out the grime-streaked window at the bleak November landscape of Grimsby, Lincolnshire.  Fishing trawlers, usually bustling in the harbor, were huddled together for shelter, their masts swaying like skeletal fingers against the bruised, grey sky. The air inside the small, stuffy office, heated by a single, wheezing electric radiator, smelled faintly of damp cardboard and despair. This was Archibald's third year in business for himself, and despite his meticulous bookkeeping and unwavering dedication to Generally Accepted Accounting Principles, the clients were few and far between. Today, however, was different. Today, a potential client was due to arrive, a client who could make or break Archibald Finch, CPA.
