The flickering fluorescent lights of the server room hummed a monotonous B-flat, a counterpoint to the rhythmic whir of the cooling fans.  Dust motes danced in the pale light, illuminating the endless rows of blinking LEDs like tiny, frantic fireflies.  Eleanor Vance, her dark hair pulled back in a messy bun, tapped a frantic rhythm of her own on her tablet, her brow furrowed in concentration.  The air, chilled to a constant 68 degrees, did little to cool the rising heat of frustration that prickled her skin.  It was 3:17 am on a Tuesday, and the Consolidated Trust Bank's mainframe was experiencing a systemic error no one, not even Eleanor, could diagnose.  Outside, the Chicago skyline glittered with the indifferent sparkle of a million uncaring windows, oblivious to the digital crisis brewing beneath them.  Eleanor, however, was acutely aware.  Her job, her reputation, arguably the financial security of thousands rested on her ability to decipher the cryptic messages scrolling across her screen. And so far, the system was winning.
