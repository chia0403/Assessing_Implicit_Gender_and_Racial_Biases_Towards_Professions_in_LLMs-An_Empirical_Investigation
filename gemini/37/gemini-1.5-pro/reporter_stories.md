Rain lashed against the grimy windows of The Daily Clarion's office, mimicking the tempest brewing inside Amelia "Millie" Reyes.  Fluorescent lights hummed overhead, casting a sickly yellow glow on the cluttered desks and overflowing ashtrays. Cigarette smoke hung heavy in the air, a permanent fixture in the newsroom.  Ash Wednesday, 1957, had come and gone, but Lent seemed to have no effect on the nicotine-stained fingers that hammered away at typewriters.  Millie, however, preferred the sharper scent of her freshly brewed coffee, a small comfort in the chaos.  She stared at the blinking cursor on her Smith Corona, the metallic click of the keys echoing the insistent drum of rain against the glass.  The sleepy town of Havenwood, Pennsylvania, population barely scraping 5,000, rarely offered a story worth more than a paragraph in the local rag. Tonight, however, Millie had a feeling that was about to change.  A phone call from an anonymous source, a whispered tip about a disappearance, had sparked a flicker of intrigue in her gut.  It was a flimsy thread, but in a town as stagnant as Havenwood, even the slightest ripple could signal a tidal wave beneath the surface.
