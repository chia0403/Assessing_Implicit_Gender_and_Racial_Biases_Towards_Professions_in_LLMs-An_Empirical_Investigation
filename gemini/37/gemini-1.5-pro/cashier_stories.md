The flickering fluorescent lights of the Quick Stop buzzed in a rhythm only they seemed to appreciate.  Rain lashed against the grimy windows, mirroring the dreary November mood that clung to everything in the small Ohio town of Harmony Creek.  Inside, Evelyn Beatrice Reed, her nametag slightly askew on her faded blue uniform shirt, sighed and methodically counted the crumpled bills a farmer, smelling faintly of hay and manure, had slapped down on the counter for his pack of Marlboros and a lukewarm bottle of Mountain Dew.  Harmony Creek boasted a population of 482 (483 if you counted Mrs. Higgins' new baby), and Evelyn knew every single one of their purchasing habits.
