Rain lashed against the grimy windows of Silas Blackwood's office, each drop a staccato note in a symphony of urban misery.  Neon signs from the strip club across the street bled lurid colours onto the rain-slicked asphalt, painting the scene in hues of sickly green and bruised purple. Silas Finch, his trench coat still dripping onto the threadbare rug, sat slumped behind his desk, the single bare bulb above casting harsh shadows that accentuated the hollows under his eyes.  His office, nestled above a laundromat on the less glamorous end of Bleecker Street in Manhattan, was cramped and smelled perpetually of stale cigarette smoke and cheap instant coffee. Tonight, the aroma of desperation hung heavier than usual.
