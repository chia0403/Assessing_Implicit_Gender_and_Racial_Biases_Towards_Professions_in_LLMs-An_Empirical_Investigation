The biting November wind whipped through the gaping maw of Miller's Auto Repair, carrying the scent of burnt motor oil and decaying leaves.  Fluorescent lights buzzed overhead, casting a sickly yellow glow across the concrete floor, littered with discarded wrenches, crumpled cigarette packs, and the ghostly outlines of grease stains.  Eliza Jane McAlister, her knuckles raw and bleeding from wrestling a stubborn alternator, wiped a streak of grime across her forehead with the back of her equally grimy hand. The garage, nestled between a pawn shop and a perpetually closed laundromat on the outskirts of bleak, forgotten Harmony Creek, Indiana, was her sanctuary, her kingdom, and her prison, all rolled into one.
