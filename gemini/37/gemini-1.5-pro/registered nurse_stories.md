The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne's auburn braid across her face as she hurried across the deserted parking lot of St. Jude's Memorial Hospital.  Fluorescent lights spilled from the towering brick building, painting the swirling snow a sickly yellow.  The clock tower chimed midnight, its mournful clang echoing the hollowness in Amelia's chest.  Tonight, the normally bustling Emergency Room, a place Amelia usually found invigorating, felt more like a tomb.  The hushed silence was broken only by the distant rhythmic beep of a heart monitor and the rhythmic swish of the automatic doors as she pushed through them, the warmth of the lobby offering little comfort against the chill that had settled deep in her bones.
