The biting wind whipped across the desolate plains of Wyoming, carrying the scent of sagebrush and snow.  The sun, a pale disc in the iron-grey sky, offered little warmth against the encroaching January dusk. Dr. Elias Thorne, his breath clouding before him, slammed the tailgate of his battered pickup truck shut, the metallic clang echoing across the frozen landscape.  His clinic, a squat, single-story building with a faded "Thorne Animal Clinic" sign above the door, stood solitary against the vast expanse, a beacon of fluorescent light in the gathering gloom.  Inside, a nervous whimper punctuated the rhythmic tick-tock of the grandfather clock in the waiting room, a harbinger of the long night ahead.
