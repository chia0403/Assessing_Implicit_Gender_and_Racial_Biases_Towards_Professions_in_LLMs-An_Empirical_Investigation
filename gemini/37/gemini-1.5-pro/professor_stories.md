The biting November wind whipped around Professor Elias Thorne, stinging his cheeks and tugging at the edges of his tweed overcoat.  Fallen leaves, the color of burnt umber and ochre, swirled around his ankles as he hurried across the cobbled quadrangle of St. Jude’s University.  Gargoyles perched atop the gothic buildings leered down at him, their stony faces seemingly etched with an amusement that mirrored the unsettling feeling growing in Elias’s gut.  The ancient clock tower, a looming presence at the heart of the campus, chimed six times, its mournful toll echoing across the deserted grounds.  Elias usually relished the solitude of this hour, the quiet hum of academia settling after the day’s intellectual fervor. But tonight, the silence felt oppressive, charged with an unspoken anticipation. Tonight, the air itself seemed to vibrate with a secret, and Elias feared he was about to become privy to it.
