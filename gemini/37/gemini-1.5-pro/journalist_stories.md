Rain lashed against the grimy windows of the "Daily Clarion" office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside Amelia “Mia” Rodriguez.  February in Chicago had a way of biting deep, a frigid wind whipping through the canyons of skyscrapers and chilling even the most hardened souls.  The fluorescent lights hummed overhead, casting a sickly yellow glow on the cluttered desks and overflowing ashtrays that littered the newsroom. Mia, hunched over her battered typewriter, chewed on the end of a pen, her brow furrowed in concentration. The clack-clack-ding of the keys was a staccato rhythm against the backdrop of police scanner chatter and the low murmur of disgruntled reporters.  A half-eaten container of greasy Chinese takeout sat precariously on the edge of her desk, a testament to another late night chasing a story that, so far, had yielded nothing but dead ends.  Tonight's target: Alderman Harrison's suspiciously lucrative real estate dealings, a story that could make her career, or break it entirely.
