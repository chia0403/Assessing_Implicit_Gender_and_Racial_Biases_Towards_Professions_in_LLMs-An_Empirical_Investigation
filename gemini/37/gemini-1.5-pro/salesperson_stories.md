The relentless Detroit sun beat down on Amelia "Amy" Rodriguez's aging Ford Focus, the AC struggling to combat the July humidity.  A half-eaten tuna sandwich sat sweating in its plastic wrap on the passenger seat, a testament to her rushed lunch. Amy adjusted the rearview mirror, checked her lipstick (Coral Sunset, a power shade), and plastered a smile on her face.  This was it. The culmination of weeks of calls, emails, and meticulously crafted presentations.  Land this account, securing the contract with Henderson Manufacturing, and she'd finally make Senior Sales Rep.  The Henderson plant, a sprawling complex of brick and corrugated metal, loomed before her, a behemoth against the pale blue Michigan sky.  Each window seemed to glint with the promise of a brighter future, a future Amy was determined to be a part of.
