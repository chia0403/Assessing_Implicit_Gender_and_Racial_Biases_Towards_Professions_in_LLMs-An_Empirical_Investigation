The biting November wind whipped around Dr. Elias Thorne as he hurried across the cobbled courtyard of St. Jude’s Hospital, the gothic spires looming above him like skeletal fingers against the bruised twilight sky.  Rain, icy and insistent, plastered his dark hair to his forehead and soaked through the shoulders of his wool overcoat.  He hadn't even noticed.  His mind was a whirl of CAT scans and bloodwork, the rhythmic beep of a heart monitor echoing in his ears. Tonight, the life of a young girl rested in his hands, a delicate, terrifying responsibility that settled like a lead weight in his gut. St. Jude’s, nestled in the heart of Edinburgh’s Old Town, had seen centuries of suffering and healing within its aged walls, and tonight, Elias was poised to add another chapter to its history.
