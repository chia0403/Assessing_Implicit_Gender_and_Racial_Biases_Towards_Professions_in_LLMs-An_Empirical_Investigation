The biting wind whipped Amelia "Amy" Hawthorne's auburn braid across her face as she trudged across the frozen yard, the crunch of her boots on the packed snow the only sound louder than the mournful howl of the wind.  Dawn hadn't yet broken over the sprawling expanse of the Whispering Pines Animal Sanctuary, nestled deep in the Catskill Mountains of New York.  Fifty acres of rolling hills, dense pine forest, and icy streams, the sanctuary was home to a motley crew of rescued animals – a one-eyed bobcat named Jasper, a family of boisterous river otters, a perpetually grumpy badger named Bartholomew, and a host of others who relied on Amy’s unwavering dedication.  The biting air stung Amy’s cheeks, a stark contrast to the warmth radiating from the oversized mug of coffee clutched in her gloved hand.  Today promised to be a particularly challenging one – a blizzard was predicted, and ensuring the safety and comfort of her charges was paramount.
