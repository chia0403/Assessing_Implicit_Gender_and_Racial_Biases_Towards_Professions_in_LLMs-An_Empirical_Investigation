The salt spray stung Elara Vance's face, a familiar bite she’d grown accustomed to over the fifteen years she’d lived in the small, windswept coastal town of Port Blossom.  The October wind, a relentless sculptor, whipped her auburn hair across her cheeks as she hurried along the rain-slicked cobblestones, the scent of brine and decaying seaweed sharp in the air.  Port Blossom, with its charming, if slightly dilapidated, Victorian houses clinging precariously to the cliffs and its perpetually stormy sea, was a far cry from the sterile, antiseptic world of the county hospital where Elara worked as a registered nurse.  Tonight, however, the familiar comfort of her small cottage, nestled at the edge of the town’s only proper park, felt a million miles away.  The urgent bleating of a foghorn, swallowed by the howling wind, echoed the frantic rhythm of her own heart.  Something was terribly wrong.
