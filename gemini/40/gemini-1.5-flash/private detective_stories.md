The chipped porcelain of the teacup warmed Amelia “Mia” Blackwood’s fingers, a paltry comfort against the November chill seeping through the drafty window of her office.  Rain lashed against the grimy panes, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her.  Her office, a cramped space above a failing bakery on Bleecker Street in New York City, smelled faintly of stale croissants and despair – a fitting aroma for a private investigator whose latest case had her more tangled than a ball of Christmas lights after a toddler's visit.  The flickering gas lamp cast long shadows across the worn Persian rug, obscuring the dust motes dancing in the weak light.  Outside, the city roared – a symphony of car horns, distant sirens, and the rhythmic thud of hurried footsteps – but inside, only the rhythmic tick-tock of a grandfather clock, its pendulum swaying like a metronome of impending doom, broke the silence.
