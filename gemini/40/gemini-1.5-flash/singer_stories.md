The stale air hung thick with the scent of cheap beer and desperation, clinging to the peeling paint of the Blue Moon Saloon like a second skin.  Dust motes, illuminated by the single, flickering bulb above the bar, danced in the sluggish currents stirred by the occasional shuffling foot.  Tonight, however, the usual melancholic hum of the Blue Moon was punctuated by a nervous energy, a collective holding of breath.  For tonight, Seraphina “Sera” Nightingale, the most captivating, and arguably most tragic, voice to ever grace this forgotten corner of Dustbowl City, was taking the stage.
