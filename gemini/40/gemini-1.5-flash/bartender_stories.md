The chipped porcelain sink groaned under the weight of Amelia Grace Hawthorne's elbow as she scrubbed at a stubborn ring of something vaguely resembling margarita – or perhaps a particularly aggressive cocktail involving tequila and regret – from the bar top.  Rain lashed against the grimy windows of “The Rusty Mug,” a dive bar clinging precariously to the edge of the docks in the perpetually twilight town of Port Blossom. The air hung thick with the scent of brine, stale beer, and the faint, unsettling sweetness of decaying fish.  Outside, the wind howled a mournful tune, punctuated by the rhythmic clang of dockside cranes wrestling with cargo containers.  Inside, the only sound louder than the storm was the low hum of the refrigerator and the occasional, weary sigh escaping Amelia's lips.  Tonight, however, felt different.  Even for Port Blossom, the air crackled with an unusual, almost palpable tension.
