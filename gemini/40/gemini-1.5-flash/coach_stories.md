The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Amy” Hawthorne’s auburn braid across her face as she surveyed the desolate practice field.  Fallen leaves, the color of burnt umber and rust, clung stubbornly to the muddy turf, mirroring the grim determination etched on the faces of her players.  The skeletal branches of the ancient oaks clawing at the bruised twilight sky seemed to mock their dwindling hopes.  This wasn't the sun-drenched California paradise depicted in the glossy brochures that had lured them to West Valley High; it was a bleak, windswept battleground in the heart of rural Oregon, where dreams came to die, frozen solid under a blanket of relentless rain.  The air hung thick with the smell of damp earth and the faint, metallic tang of blood – a small cut on one player’s knee, perhaps, but Amy knew that the real wounds ran far deeper.  Their season was on the brink of collapse, and Amy, the newly appointed head coach of the West Valley Wildcats girls’ soccer team, felt the weight of their failing campaign pressing down on her like a physical burden.
