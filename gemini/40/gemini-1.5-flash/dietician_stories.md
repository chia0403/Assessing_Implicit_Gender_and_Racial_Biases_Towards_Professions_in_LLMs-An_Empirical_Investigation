The salt spray stung Dr. Eleanor Vance's face as she wrestled the stubborn lobster trap onto the deck of the *Sea Serpent*.  The late afternoon sun, a molten orange orb sinking towards the jagged teeth of the Maine coastline, cast long shadows across the pitching boat.  The air, thick with the scent of brine and pine, carried the mournful cry of gulls overhead. Eleanor, her sensible navy raincoat plastered to her frame, shivered, not entirely from the cold.  This wasn't the sterile, controlled environment of her Boston practice; this was Monhegan Island, a place where the relentless rhythm of the ocean dictated life, and where the hearty, sea-weathered residents clung to traditions as fiercely as they clung to the rocky cliffs.  And it was here, amongst the craggy beauty and stubbornly independent islanders, that Eleanor Vance, Registered Dietician, found herself facing her most challenging case yet.
