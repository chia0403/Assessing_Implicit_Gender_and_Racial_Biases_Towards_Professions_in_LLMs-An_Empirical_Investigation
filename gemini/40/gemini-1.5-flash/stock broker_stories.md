The stale scent of burnt coffee and desperation clung to the air of the brokerage firm like a persistent cough.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of the 47th floor, blurring the already indistinct cityscape into a blurry watercolor of grey and black.  Amelia Katherine “Amy” Davies, her usually sharp crimson lipstick smudged slightly, stared at the relentless plummet of the Dow Jones Industrial Average on her monitor.  The numbers, once a source of exhilaration and profit, now felt like icy daggers, each downward tick a tiny death knell for her meticulously crafted portfolio, and perhaps, her career.  The hum of frantic phone calls and the rhythmic clatter of keyboards formed a discordant symphony of impending doom in the otherwise opulent, if slightly threadbare, office of Davies & Davies Securities.
