The humid air hung heavy, thick with the scent of jasmine and exhaust fumes, a peculiar perfume clinging to the neon-slicked streets of Ho Chi Minh City.  Rain, a sudden and violent downpour, lashed against the corrugated iron roofs of the tiny shops lining Nguyen Hue Street, each hammering drop a percussive punctuation to the relentless drone of motorbikes.  Amelia "Mia" Hernandez, a journalist for the *Southeast Asia Chronicle*, hunched deeper into the doorway of a crumbling colonial building, the damp seeping through her thin cotton shirt.  Her notepad, a sodden mess, lay useless in her lap, its pages blurring into an indistinguishable smudge of ink and water.  The assignment – a seemingly innocuous piece on the city's burgeoning coffee trade – had taken a decidedly unexpected turn.
