The stale scent of sawdust and forgotten dreams hung heavy in the air, clinging to the faded velvet curtains and the chipped paint of the proscenium arch.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of sunlight slicing through the grime-coated window of the dilapidated theatre, illuminating the swirling chaos that was, for the moment, the life of  Seraphina Evangeline Blackwood.  Seraphina, or "Sera" as everyone – except her demanding mother – called her, ran a hand through her already unruly cascade of auburn curls, a sigh escaping her lips that sounded more like the rusty groan of a theatre door swinging in the wind.  The Grand Majestic Theatre, once a jewel of the city, now resembled a forgotten tomb, waiting for the resurrection only Sera’s unwavering – some might say foolhardy – ambition could possibly bring.
