The wind, a rasping, gritty hand, clawed at Elias Thorne's weathered face as he squinted out at the darkening sky.  Dust devils, miniature tornadoes of ochre and brown, danced across the parched earth of the Triple X Ranch, swirling like malevolent spirits across the seemingly endless expanse of Texas plains.  The setting sun bled crimson and orange across the horizon, painting the skeletal silhouettes of mesquite trees against a canvas of bruised purple.  The air, thick with the scent of dry grass and the impending storm, crackled with a tension Elias felt deep in his bones, a tension that had nothing to do with the weather.  For three generations, the Thorne family had bled and toiled on this land, and tonight, it felt as though the land itself was holding its breath, waiting for something – or someone – to break.
