The scent of almond extract hung heavy and sweet in the air, a cloying perfume that clung to Amelia Grace Hawthorne like a second skin.  Amelia, or Amy as most people – thankfully – called her, wiped flour dust from her cheek with the back of her hand, leaving a faint white smudge against the already-powdered complexion.  Outside, the relentless November rain lashed against the windows of "Sugar Rush," her tiny, perpetually cluttered cake decorating shop nestled on a cobbled side street in the heart of historic Charleston, South Carolina.  The gas lamps flickered, casting an amber glow on the rows of gleaming cake stands, each displaying a miniature masterpiece – a testament to Amy’s talent, though right now, she felt far from masterful.  A half-finished wedding cake, a towering confection of ivory fondant and cascading sugar roses, sat unfinished on the turntable, a silent witness to her current bout of creative paralysis.
