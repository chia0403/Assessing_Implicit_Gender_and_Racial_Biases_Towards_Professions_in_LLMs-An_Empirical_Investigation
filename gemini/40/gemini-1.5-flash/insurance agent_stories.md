The chipped porcelain teacup rattled in Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s hand, the tremor barely perceptible against the floral china.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of her office, blurring the already hazy cityscape of Portland, Oregon.  The grey light mirrored the grim expression etched onto Amelia's face, a stark contrast to the cheerful, sun-drenched landscape painted on the wall behind her – a cruel joke played by the interior decorator, she thought, considering the deluge and the particularly nasty case file sitting open on her mahogany desk.  The file, thick with paperwork smelling faintly of desperation and damp cardboard, detailed the claim of a disgruntled fisherman who’d lost his entire catch, and possibly his sanity, to a rogue kraken –  or at least, that’s what he claimed.  Amelia, a woman whose life revolved around actuarial tables and risk assessments, sighed.  This was not how she envisioned spending her Tuesday.
