The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Mia” Hernandez’s hair across her face as she hurried across the deserted school parking lot.  Fallen leaves, the color of bruised plums and decaying oranges, crunched under her worn leather boots.  Lincoln High, a sprawling brick behemoth that had seen better decades, loomed before her, its gothic architecture softened only slightly by the flickering orange glow spilling from the few windows still alight.  Inside, the ghosts of a thousand Friday night football games and whispered teenage secrets clung to the polished floors and echoing hallways.  Mia, a veteran English teacher with a perpetually tired smile and eyes that held the weary wisdom of someone twice her age, shoved her hands deeper into the pockets of her threadbare tweed coat, bracing herself for another weekend of grading papers and wrestling with the complexities of seventeen-year-old angst.  The scent of damp earth and woodsmoke – a familiar autumnal aroma – hung heavy in the air, a stark contrast to the sterile smell of disinfectant and chalk dust that awaited her within those imposing walls.
