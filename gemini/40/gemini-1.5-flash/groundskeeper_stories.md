The biting November wind whipped Elias Thorne's already-chapped lips raw as he wrestled a recalcitrant oak branch into submission.  Dead leaves, brittle as parchment, skittered across the manicured lawns of Blackwood Manor, swirling in miniature tornadoes around his worn work boots.  Blackwood Manor itself, a gothic behemoth of grey stone and brooding gargoyles, loomed over him like a silent judge, its countless windows reflecting the bruised purple of the twilight sky.  Elias, his hands rough and calloused from years spent battling the relentless encroachment of nature, paused his work, the scent of damp earth and decaying leaves filling his nostrils.  The air hung heavy with the unspoken secrets the ancient estate held within its walls, secrets Elias, the groundskeeper, had felt the weight of for the past twenty years.
