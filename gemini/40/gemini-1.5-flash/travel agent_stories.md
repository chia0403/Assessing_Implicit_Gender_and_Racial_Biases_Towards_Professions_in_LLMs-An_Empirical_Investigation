The chipped paint of Amelia Hernandez’s desk was a testament to years spent wrestling with unruly airline schedules and the temperamental whims of capricious clients.  Rain lashed against the window of her small, cluttered travel agency, "Wanderlust Escapes," blurring the already fading neon sign that hung precariously above the doorway.  The air inside smelled faintly of stale coffee and the exotic perfume samples she kept scattered amongst brochures showcasing sun-drenched beaches and snow-capped mountains – a fragrant cocktail of escape for both her and her clients.  Tonight, however, Amelia felt far from escaping.  The rhythmic drumming of the rain mirrored the anxious thump-thump-thump of her own heart.  Outside, the usually vibrant streets of San Francisco were shrouded in a gloomy twilight, and inside, the only light came from the flickering lamp on her desk, illuminating the single, unopened envelope sitting squarely in its centre.
