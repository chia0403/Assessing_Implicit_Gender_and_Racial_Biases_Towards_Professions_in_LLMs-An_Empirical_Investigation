The humid Louisiana air hung heavy, thick and sweet with the scent of honeysuckle and decaying magnolia blossoms.  Cicadas buzzed a relentless, rhythmic chorus that vibrated through Dr. Eleanor Vance’s bones, a counterpoint to the persistent throb in her own aching right shoulder.  Sweat beaded on her forehead as she wrestled with the stubborn padlock on the back door of Vance Chiropractic, her family practice nestled incongruously between a neon-lit pawn shop and a crumbling Victorian house rumored to be haunted.  The late afternoon sun cast long shadows across the cracked asphalt of Bayou Road, painting the scene in shades of orange and bruised purple, a fitting backdrop for the turmoil brewing within her.  Eleanor Vance, D.C.,  had a bad feeling, a prickling unease that settled deep in her gut, far lower than any spinal misalignment she’d ever treated.  And tonight, that feeling felt awfully close to dread.
