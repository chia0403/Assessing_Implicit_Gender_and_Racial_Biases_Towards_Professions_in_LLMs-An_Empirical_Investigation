The fluorescent lights of Sterling & Steele hummed a monotonous tune, a soundtrack to Amelia Grace Hawthorne's carefully organized existence.  Dust motes, illuminated like tiny galaxies, danced in the single shaft of afternoon sunlight slicing through the tall, gothic windows of the imposing downtown office building.  Amelia, impeccably dressed in a charcoal grey pantsuit that spoke volumes about her dedication to efficiency, tapped a perfectly manicured nail against the polished mahogany of her desk.  The scent of old paper, expensive leather, and faintly bitter coffee hung in the air, a familiar perfume to her years of service within the hallowed halls of the prestigious law firm.  Outside, the city roared, a chaotic counterpoint to the silent, controlled world she inhabited within those four walls.  But today, the usual hum of controlled chaos felt different.  A prickle of unease, sharp and unexpected, snaked its way up her spine.  Something was amiss, a discordant note in her meticulously orchestrated symphony of a workday.
