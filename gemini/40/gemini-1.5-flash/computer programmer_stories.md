The flickering neon sign of the "Lucky Dragon" noodle bar cast a lurid orange glow across Elias Thorne's rain-slicked trench coat.  Water, the kind that chilled you to the bone despite the late August humidity of Hong Kong, dripped from the brim of his hat, obscuring the city's chaotic tapestry of skyscrapers and hanging laundry.  Elias, a computer programmer whose expertise lay in the murky world of cybersecurity, hunched deeper into his collar, the scent of simmering soy sauce and exhaust fumes a familiar backdrop to his nocturnal existence.  He tugged his worn leather satchel closer, its contents – a battered laptop and a half-eaten bag of prawn crackers – representing the sum total of his immediate worldly possessions, aside from the burning question that had brought him to this rain-lashed alleyway:  was the Serpent's Tooth really as impenetrable as everyone said?
