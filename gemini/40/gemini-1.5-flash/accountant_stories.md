The fluorescent lights of the Miller & Sons accounting firm hummed a monotonous tune, a soundtrack to Amelia Grace Hawthorne's meticulously organized life.  Amelia, a woman whose precision extended to the perfectly aligned pencils on her desk and the precisely timed tea breaks, found herself staring blankly at a spreadsheet titled "Q3 Projections – PetroChem Industries."  The numbers swam before her tired eyes, blurring into a meaningless jumble, a stark contrast to the usual satisfying order she found in her work. Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of her 27th-floor office, mirroring the storm brewing inside her.  The city of Chicago sprawled beneath her, a glittering tapestry of lights and shadows, but tonight, it offered no comfort.  The deadline loomed, a monstrous, deadline-shaped kraken threatening to drag her under.  And it wasn't just the deadline; something about the PetroChem figures felt…off.  Deeply, unsettlingly off.
