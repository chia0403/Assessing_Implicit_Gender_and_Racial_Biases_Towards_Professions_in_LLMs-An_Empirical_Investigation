The chipped porcelain mug warmed Dr. Eleanor Vance's hands, a meager comfort against the November chill that seeped through the drafty windows of her office.  Rain lashed against the panes, mimicking the turbulent emotions she’d been wrestling with all afternoon.  Her office, nestled on the third floor of a rambling Victorian building in the heart of Oakhaven, was usually a sanctuary – a space of calm amidst the chaos of others' lives.  But today, even the comforting scent of sandalwood incense couldn't quite dispel the lingering unease.  The worn Persian rug underfoot felt oddly cold, and the usually soothing ticking of the grandfather clock in the corner sounded like a relentless drumbeat against the silence that followed her last client's tearful departure.  Outside, the skeletal branches of the ancient oak tree that gave the town its name clawed at the stormy sky, mirroring the thorny complexities of the human heart she dedicated her life to untangling.
