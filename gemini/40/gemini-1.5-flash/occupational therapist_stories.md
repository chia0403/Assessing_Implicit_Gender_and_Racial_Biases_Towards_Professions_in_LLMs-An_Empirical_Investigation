The salt spray stung Eleanor Vance’s face as she wrestled the stubborn fishing net from its tangle on the weathered wooden deck.  Gulls cried overhead, their calls a raucous soundtrack to the churning grey sea that slapped against the hull of the *Sea Serpent*, a small, battered research vessel bobbing precariously in the choppy waters off the coast of Newfoundland.  The biting wind whipped at her already wind-chapped lips, a stark contrast to the sterile, controlled environment of her usual workplace – the brightly lit rehabilitation centre in St. John’s.  Eleanor, a registered occupational therapist with a specialty in neurological rehabilitation, wasn't exactly used to battling the Atlantic for her next client.  But then again, this wasn't exactly your average patient.
