The chipped porcelain mug warmed Dr. Eleanor Vance's hands, a meager defense against the November chill that seeped through the drafty windows of her office. Rain lashed against the panes, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her.  Her office, nestled in the attic of a rambling Victorian house on the edge of Blackwood, Oregon, was a peculiar space.  Dust motes danced in the slant of afternoon sunlight that pierced the gloom, illuminating antique furniture shrouded in velvet covers and overflowing bookshelves groaning under the weight of decades' worth of case studies and forgotten theories.  The air hung heavy with the scent of old paper and something else… something faintly metallic, like dried blood.  It wasn’t the first time she’d noticed it, but tonight, it felt particularly pronounced, a grim prelude to the appointment that awaited her.
