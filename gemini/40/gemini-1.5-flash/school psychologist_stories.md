The biting November wind whipped Dr. Eleanor Vance’s auburn hair across her face as she hurried across the rain-slicked quad of Northwood High.  Fallen leaves, the color of bruised plums and decaying sunsets, clung stubbornly to the damp asphalt, swirling around her worn leather boots.  Northwood, a sprawling gothic monstrosity of grey stone and looming gargoyles, seemed to cast a perpetual gloom even on the brightest days, but today, the oppressive sky matched the weight in Eleanor’s stomach.  The scent of woodsmoke and damp earth hung heavy in the air, a stark contrast to the sterile, antiseptic smell she’d left behind in her office – the smell of another failed intervention, another student lost in the labyrinthine corridors of teenage angst.  This wasn't just another day at the office; this was the day the unsettling whispers about the Blackwood Room finally stopped being whispers.
