The biting November wind whipped Amelia Katherine Hayes’s perfectly coiffed auburn hair across her face as she stepped from the chauffeured limousine.  Rain, a fine, persistent drizzle, plastered her tailored Burberry trench coat to her body.  The air hung thick with the scent of damp earth and exhaust fumes, a stark contrast to the sterile, antiseptic aroma of the boardroom she’d just left.  Amelia, CEO of Hayes Global Industries, stood on the precipice of a crumbling industrial estate in the shadow of Manchester’s once-proud cotton mills, now a skeletal reminder of a bygone era.  Rust-eaten cranes clawed at the bruised sky, their skeletal arms mimicking the grasping desperation she felt in the pit of her stomach.  This wasn't the polished veneer of her London headquarters; this was the raw, unforgiving heart of a deal that could make or break her – and possibly the entire company.
