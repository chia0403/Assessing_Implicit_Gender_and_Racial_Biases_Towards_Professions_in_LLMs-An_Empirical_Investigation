The air hung thick and heavy with the scent of frying onions and desperation, a familiar perfume in the perpetually twilight-drenched depths of "The Rusty Mug."  Rain lashed against the grimy windows, blurring the neon glow of the "Liquor & Cigarettes" sign across the street.  Twenty-two-year-old Amelia Grace Hawthorne, her name embroidered in a slightly faded script on her worn-out apron, weaved through the cramped space, a practiced grace in her movements that belied the chaos surrounding her.  Empty plates piled precariously on her tray threatened a catastrophic avalanche; the clinking of glasses, the raucous laughter of a tipsy bachelorette party, and the low growl of the jukebox all blended into the incessant soundtrack of her evening.  Tonight, like every other night, felt like a marathon she'd already lost.
