The stale air of the server room hung heavy with the scent of ozone and desperation.  Fluorescent lights hummed a monotonous dirge above Amelia Hernandez, their flickering reflection dancing in the sweat beading on her forehead.  Amelia, a systems analyst whose caffeine intake rivaled that of a small coffee plantation, stared at the blinking red light on the mainframe with a mixture of grim determination and simmering annoyance.  It was 3:17 AM, the air conditioning was struggling against the heat generated by thousands of humming hard drives, and the city outside, normally a vibrant tapestry of lights, was reduced to a smudge of indistinct grey through the grimy window. This was not how Amelia envisioned spending her Friday night.  The impending deadline for the Peterson Group’s rollout loomed, a monstrous shadow threatening to consume the next 72 hours of her life.  The red light, however, was a far more immediate concern.  It spoke of impending failure, of catastrophic data loss, and of Amelia’s rapidly diminishing supply of emergency dark chocolate.
