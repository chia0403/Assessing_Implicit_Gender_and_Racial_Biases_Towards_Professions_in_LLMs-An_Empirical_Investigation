The scent of lavender and burnt sugar hung heavy in the air, a peculiar perfume only Aurelia "Rel" Hawthorne truly appreciated.  It was the aroma of a day's work at "Shear Perfection," her tiny, sun-drenched salon tucked away on a cobbled side street in the ancient, whimsical town of Port Blossom.  Port Blossom, where the tide whispered secrets to the crooked houses clinging to the cliff edge, and where the air itself seemed to hum with a quiet, magical energy, was the perfect backdrop for Rel's uniquely creative approach to hairdressing.  Today, however, the usual calming hum was punctuated by the frantic ringing of the bell above the door, a sound that promised anything but perfection.
