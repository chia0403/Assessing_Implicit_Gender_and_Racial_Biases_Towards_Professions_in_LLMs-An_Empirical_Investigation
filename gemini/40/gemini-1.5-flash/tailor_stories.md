The scent of beeswax and lavender hung heavy in the air, a comforting blanket against the biting November wind that rattled the single pane windows of Elias Thorne's tailor shop.  Elias, a man whose life was measured in the precise fall of a stitch and the subtle drape of a fine cloth, sat hunched over his workbench, the muted glow of a single gas lamp illuminating the intricate needlework in his hands.  His shop, nestled on a cobbled side street in the shadowed heart of Prague, was a haven of quiet industry, a stark contrast to the bustling, boisterous city that thrummed just beyond its weathered oak door.  The walls, lined with bolts of rich velvets, shimmering silks, and sturdy tweeds, whispered tales of countless clients and bespoke creations, each garment a testament to Elias's unparalleled skill.  Tonight, however, the usual calm was disrupted by a frantic rapping at the door, a sound that promised anything but the measured rhythm of Elias's life.
