The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia “Mia” Hernandez’s hands, a meager comfort against the biting November wind whistling through the gaps in the dilapidated storefront’s window frames.  Rain lashed against the glass, blurring the already indistinct view of the deserted street.  Inside, the air hung thick with the scent of stale coffee and desperation – a familiar aroma to Mia, a seasoned salesperson who’d seen more than her fair share of struggling businesses and even more struggling entrepreneurs. Her reflection in the grimy window showed a woman etched with the weariness of countless pitches and the faint, hopeful glint of someone clinging to the belief that even the most stubbornly closed doors could be pried open with enough persistence. This wasn't just any storefront; this was her last chance.  The fate of her fledgling company, "Hernandez & Co. Solutions," hung precariously in the balance, resting on the success – or failure – of this one crucial meeting.
