The biting wind whipped Amelia “Mia” Hernandez’s dark braid across her face as she hurried down the snow-packed street of Barrow, Alaska.  The air, thick with the scent of frozen fish and woodsmoke, stung her lungs with each ragged breath.  Streetlights cast a weak, flickering glow on the icy sidewalks, barely piercing the pre-dawn gloom that clung stubbornly to the small, isolated town. The only sound louder than the wind was the rhythmic crunch of her boots on the compacted snow, a stark counterpoint to the muffled anxieties churning in her stomach.  Mia, a physician's assistant at the North Slope Borough Medical Center, had just received a frantic call – a potential medical emergency miles out on the frozen tundra, a location notoriously difficult to reach, even in the best of conditions. And these were far from the best of conditions.  The blizzard threatening to engulf the town was already starting to show its teeth.
