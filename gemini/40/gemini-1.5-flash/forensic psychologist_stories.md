The chipped porcelain teacup trembled in Dr. Eleanor Vance's hand, the floral pattern blurring slightly as she stared out the rain-streaked window of the interrogation room.  Grey November clung to the city of Portland like a shroud, the relentless drizzle mirroring the persistent gloom settling over her.  Across the scarred metal table sat Marcus Thorne, his eyes, the colour of faded denim, fixed on a point somewhere beyond her shoulder.  The air hung thick with the metallic tang of blood – a phantom scent, lingering from the previous occupant, a suspect whose confession had been as chilling as the November air itself.  The precinct, a Brutalist behemoth of concrete and steel, hummed with a low, subterranean thrum – the collective heartbeat of a city grappling with a series of increasingly brutal murders.  Eleanor adjusted her charcoal grey cardigan, the worn fabric offering little comfort against the chill that seeped not just from the window, but from the unsettling case itself.
