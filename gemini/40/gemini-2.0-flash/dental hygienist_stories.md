The harsh fluorescent lights of the Smile Bright Dental Clinic hummed, a monotonous soundtrack to Amelia Hawthorne's Tuesday morning. Outside, the January wind whipped through the bare branches of the elms lining Elm Street, promising another day of icy pavements and chapped lips. Inside, the air was thick with the sterile scent of bleach and the lingering sweetness of bubblegum-flavored fluoride. Amelia, a meticulously organized woman with a penchant for sensible cardigans and an unwavering belief in the power of proper flossing, straightened the stack of patient files on her desk, a sigh escaping her lips. Today was going to be another marathon of teeth.
