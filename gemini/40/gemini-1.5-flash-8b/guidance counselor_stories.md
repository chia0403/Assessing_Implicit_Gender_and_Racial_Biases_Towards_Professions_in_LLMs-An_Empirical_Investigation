The fluorescent lights of the high school hummed, a monotonous drone that echoed the quiet desperation in Amelia Hernandez's chest.  October rain hammered against the grimy windows of the guidance office, mirroring the relentless anxiety that clung to the air.  Amelia, Guidance Counselor Hernandez, stared at the overflowing stack of student files, each one a miniature universe of hopes, fears, and unspoken dramas.  The chipped Formica desk, scarred from countless whispered anxieties and spilled coffee, felt like a battlefield.  Today, it seemed particularly besieged.  The scent of stale coffee and old textbooks, a familiar comfort, felt almost suffocating.  The school, a sprawling brick behemoth looming over the small, working-class town of Oakhaven, felt like a cage, and she felt trapped inside it.  This was her fifth year in this particular office, and the heavy weight of unspoken frustrations, of broken promises, of lives spiralling out of control, pressed down on her.
