The chill of the November air, thick with the scent of damp earth and woodsmoke, clung to Elias Thorne, Butcher of the Hollow, as he hauled a hefty hindquarter of venison onto the butcher block.  The flickering candlelight cast long, dancing shadows across the blood-stained floorboards, highlighting the glistening crimson smears that marked the very heart of his little shop.  The Hollow, a cluster of weathered houses nestled deep within the whispering woods, was a place where the veil between the living and the dead seemed thinner than usual, a place where the smell of roasting meat mingled with the murmur of ancient stories whispered in the leaves.  Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof, a relentless drumming counterpoint to the rhythmic thud of Elias's cleaver.  Tonight, a gathering of the Hollow's most seasoned hunters was expected, men whose faces held stories as old as the forest itself, and whose pockets promised a bounty of game.  Elias, a man carved from the same stubborn oak as the trees surrounding his shop, was ready.
