The flickering fluorescent lights of the Carnegie Library cast long, skeletal shadows across the worn wooden shelves, highlighting the dust motes dancing in the air.  Rain lashed against the library's arched windows, a relentless percussion that mirrored the frantic rhythm in Elara Nightingale's chest.  Elara Nightingale, a librarian of impeccable composure and quiet dedication at the sprawling, historic Oakhaven Library, was experiencing something decidedly un-Nightingale-like:  she was terrified.  Not of the usual, quiet terrors of a librarian—the fear of misplaced books or the phantom whispers of overdue fines—but a cold, gnawing fear that whispered of something beyond the library's well-ordered pages.  Tonight, the whispers were amplified by a peculiar, faint humming emanating from the stacks.
