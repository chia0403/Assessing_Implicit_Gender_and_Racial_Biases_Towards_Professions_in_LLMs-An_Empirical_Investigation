The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Blackwood's hands as she surveyed the sprawling estate of Blackwood Manor.  Sunlight, fractured by the ancient oaks that lined the meticulously manicured paths, dappled the manicured lawns.  A faint scent of honeysuckle, thick with the promise of summer, hung heavy in the air.  Amelia, a groundskeeper for the last fifteen years, knew this estate as well as the lines on her own weathered hands.  Every blade of grass, every petal of a rose, every shadowed corner held a secret whispered to her by the wind.  This morning, though, a whisper carried a different tone.  It was urgent, almost desperate.
