Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of the "Eagle's Nest" gym, a cavernous space echoing with the ghosts of countless basketballs and the guttural cries of teenage angst.  The flickering fluorescent lights cast long, distorted shadows over Amelia  "Ace"  Abernathy, her figure hunched over a worn clipboard, meticulously scribbling notes.  Outside, the wind howled, a mournful dirge accompanying the relentless downpour.  Ace, a seasoned high school basketball coach with a reputation as sharp as her wit, knew this wasn't just any practice. This was the make-or-break moment for the Eagles' season.  They were down by a crucial three points with under a minute to go in the state championship game.  The opposing team, the formidable "Iron Wolverines," had them cornered. The pressure was palpable, a thick blanket suffocating the players and coaches alike.
