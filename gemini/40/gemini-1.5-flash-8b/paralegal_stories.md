The fluorescent lights of the law firm hummed, a monotonous soundtrack to Amelia "Em"  Everhart's workday.  Dust motes danced in the sterile, beige-toned air, mirroring the swirling chaos in her mind.  Another deadline loomed, another stack of legal documents threatened to bury her alive.  Outside, the city of Veridia thrummed with a life she rarely experienced, a life filled with vibrant colors and unpredictable movements, a stark contrast to the predictable, paper-laden monotony of her office on the 12th floor of the Zenith Tower.  Veridia was a city of towering skyscrapers that pierced the clouds, its intricate web of concrete and glass a testament to human ambition, and a world Em often felt excluded from.  She was, quite literally, trapped in a paper labyrinth, her days spent meticulously organizing and deciphering the complex jargon of contracts, wills, and depositions. Em, a paralegal at the prestigious firm of Sterling,  Andrews & Davies, felt her own life beginning to resemble the tangled mess of paperwork that filled her workspace.
