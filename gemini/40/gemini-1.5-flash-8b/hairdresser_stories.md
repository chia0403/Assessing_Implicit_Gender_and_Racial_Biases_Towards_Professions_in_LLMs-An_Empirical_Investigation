The flickering neon sign of "Scissors & Smiles" cast a lurid, magenta glow across the rain-slicked cobblestones of the Lower East Side.  Inside, the air hung thick with the scent of rosemary, lavender, and something vaguely metallic, like old pennies and desperation.  This was Elara Nightingale's domain, a sanctuary tucked away beneath a sprawling tenement building, a haven from the relentless grey of the city.  Elara, a woman with eyes the color of stormy seas and hair the shade of raven's wings, meticulously sculpted a cascade of auburn curls around the shoulders of her client, a young woman named Anya.  Tonight's appointment was unusually tense. The air crackled with unspoken anxieties, a palpable current between the two, as if the city's anxieties were concentrated within the confines of the small salon.
