The fluorescent lights of Ms. Amelia Hawthorne's classroom hummed a monotonous tune, a counterpoint to the frantic buzzing of a hundred tiny bodies.  Crayon shavings littered the floor like fallen confetti, and the air, thick with the scent of construction paper and unidentifiable preschool snacks, hung heavy with unspoken demands and burgeoning creativity.  This was Amelia's third year teaching kindergarten at the Sunbeam Academy, and even after countless iterations of the alphabet song and the intricacies of finger painting, the chaos still felt exhilarating, almost chaotic.  Today, though, a different kind of energy thrummed beneath the surface, a quiet hum of anticipation that resonated with Amelia's own unsettled feelings.
