The biting November wind whipped around Dr. Eleanor Vance as she hurried across the cobbled courtyard of St. Jude's Hospital, the hem of her long coat snapping against her ankles.  Gargoyles perched on the aged stone walls leered down at her, their frozen expressions mirroring the grim set of her own jaw.  St. Jude's, nestled deep in the heart of the ancient, sprawling city of Aethelburg, had stood for centuries, a bastion against illness and injury within a labyrinth of winding alleys and shadowed squares.  Tonight, however, the usual comforting weight of history felt oppressive, a premonition of the long, difficult night ahead.  The flickering gas lamps illuminating the courtyard cast long, distorted shadows that danced with the swirling snow, creating an unsettling, almost theatrical atmosphere.  Eleanor pulled her collar higher, the chill seeping into her bones despite the thick wool.  She knew, with a certainty that settled like ice in her stomach, that something was deeply wrong.
