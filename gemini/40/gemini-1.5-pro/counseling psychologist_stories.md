Rain lashed against the mullioned windows of Dr. Elias Thorne's office, mimicking the tempest brewing inside his newest patient.  The room, nestled on the second floor of a converted Victorian house in the heart of Ashworth, Oregon, usually exuded a calming presence.  Tonight, however, the flickering gas fireplace and the scent of sandalwood incense seemed to do little to dispel the tension clinging to the air like a shroud.  Outside, the skeletal branches of a centuries-old oak tree clawed at the darkening sky, their silhouettes projected onto the sheer curtains like grasping, spectral fingers. Elias, his brow furrowed with concern, leaned forward in his wingback chair, the soft glow of the desk lamp illuminating his kind, yet weary, features.
