Rain lashed against the windows of Eleanor Vance's small office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her.  The rhythmic drumming of the downpour on the glass was the only sound besides the low hum of the refrigerator in the corner, stocked with educational pamphlets on healthy eating and samples of sugar-free protein bars. Her office, nestled within the Community Wellness Center of the sleepy coastal town of Port Blossom, Maine, was usually a haven of calm, smelling faintly of herbal tea and optimism.  But today, the scent of impending change hung thick in the air. Eleanor, a Registered Dietitian with a heart as big as her meticulously organized filing cabinet, had just received a phone call that threatened to upend the carefully balanced life she'd built.
