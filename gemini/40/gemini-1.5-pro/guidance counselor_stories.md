The fluorescent lights of Ms. Eleanor Vance's office hummed a monotonous tune, a soundtrack to the anxieties and triumphs that unfolded within its pale yellow walls.  Grimshaw High, a sprawling brick behemoth perched precariously on a hill overlooking the steel mills of Allegheny, Pennsylvania, wasn't exactly a beacon of hope for most of its students.  The air, thick with the scent of rust and despair from the valley below, seemed to permeate even the freshly waxed linoleum floors.  October had arrived, painting the hillsides in vibrant hues of orange and red, a stark contrast to the gray pallor that clung to the town.  But inside Eleanor’s office, nestled between a struggling ficus tree and a poster proclaiming "Reach for the Stars," a different kind of season was dawning – college application season.  And Eleanor, armed with her overflowing filing cabinets, an endless supply of highlighters, and an unwavering belief in the potential of every student who walked through her door, was ready for battle.
