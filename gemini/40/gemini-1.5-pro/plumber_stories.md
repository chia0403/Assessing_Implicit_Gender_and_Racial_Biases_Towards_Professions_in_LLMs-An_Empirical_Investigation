The rusty wrench slipped in Elias Finch's grease-stained hand, sending a bolt pinging off the chipped porcelain of the claw-foot tub.  He muttered a curse under his breath, the sound swallowed by the cavernous silence of the abandoned Blackwood Manor.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of sunlight filtering through a boarded-up window, illuminating the peeling wallpaper and the cobwebs draped like macabre lace across the ornate mirror.  Elias, his weathered face etched with years of late nights and stubborn pipes, pushed back his sweat-dampened cap and surveyed the scene.  Hired to simply assess the plumbing of the derelict estate before its auction, he found himself increasingly uneasy.  The air hung heavy with a stillness that went beyond simple disuse; it was as though the very walls were holding their breath.  This was more than just a plumbing job; Elias could feel it in his bones.
