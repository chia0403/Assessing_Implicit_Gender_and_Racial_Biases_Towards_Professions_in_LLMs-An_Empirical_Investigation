Rain lashed against the stained-glass windows of The Gilded Lily, blurring the neon glow of the "Open" sign into a hazy halo. Inside, the air hung thick with the smell of stale beer, cheap whiskey, and a lingering hint of jasmine – Amelia “Mia” Rodriguez’s signature perfume.  Mia, perched on the worn stool behind the mahogany bar, polished a glass with a practiced hand, her gaze fixed on the empty street outside. The Lily, nestled between a pawn shop and a fortune teller’s parlor on the rain-slicked cobbles of Grimsborough Lane, was usually bustling on a Friday night. Tonight, however, the only company she had was the rhythmic drip from a leaky tap and the ghostly hum of the jukebox in the corner.  Grimsborough Lane, a narrow alleyway branching off the more respectable streets of New Birmingham, had a reputation. A reputation that kept most folks away after dark, unless, of course, they were looking for something they couldn’t find anywhere else. And tonight, it seemed, even *they* were staying home.
