The biting November wind whipped around Professor Alistair Finch, tugging at the edges of his tweed jacket as he hurried across the cobbled quadrangle of St. Jude's College, Oxford.  Gargoyles leered down from the honey-coloured stone, their expressions seeming to mock his haste.  Frost crunched under his worn leather shoes, the sound sharp against the hushed reverence of the ancient buildings.  The light was fading rapidly, the late afternoon sky a bruised purple bleeding into the deep grey of the coming night.  Alistair clutched a battered leather satchel overflowing with student essays, his breath puffing out in white clouds as he made his way towards the warmth and flickering gaslight of his rooms in the Old Quad, a haven he rarely left before midnight.  Tonight, however, a summons from the college Dean, delivered with an unsettling air of urgency, had disrupted his usual routine and filled him with a gnawing sense of foreboding.
