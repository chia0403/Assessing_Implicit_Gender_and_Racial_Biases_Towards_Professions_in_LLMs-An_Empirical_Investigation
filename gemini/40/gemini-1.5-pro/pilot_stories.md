The salt spray stung Amelia "Ace" Reyes's face as she wrestled the De Havilland Beaver towards the churning grey expanse of the Hecate Strait. Rain lashed against the cockpit windows, blurring the already indistinct line between sky and sea. The wind howled like a banshee, buffeting the small floatplane and making every manoeuvre a battle against the elements.  Ace tightened her grip on the yoke, her knuckles white against the worn leather. Below, the jagged, pine-clad peaks of the Haida Gwaii archipelago, shrouded in mist and rain, offered little comfort.  This remote corner of British Columbia, a place of wild beauty and ancient secrets, was Ace's domain.  It was also, this tempestuous afternoon, proving to be a particularly unforgiving one.
