The rain lashed against the arched windows of Amelia Grace Holloway’s office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her.  Her mahogany desk, usually a testament to order and precision, was currently awash in files pertaining to the Pendlebrook Estate case – a tangled mess of contested wills, missing beneficiaries, and whispered accusations of foul play.  Amelia, her tailored blazer damp from the downpour that had greeted her arrival, pushed a stray curl from her face and stared out at the grey expanse of Lake Michigan.  Her office, perched high on the twenty-seventh floor of the Dearborn Tower in Chicago, usually afforded her a breathtaking view of the city's skyline. Tonight, however, the swirling fog obscured everything but the nearest skyscrapers, lending an air of unsettling isolation. The air crackled with unspoken tension, a premonition that this case, more than any other in her career, was about to drag Amelia into depths she hadn't anticipated.
