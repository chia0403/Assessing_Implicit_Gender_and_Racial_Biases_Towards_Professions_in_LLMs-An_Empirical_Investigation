The late afternoon sun, filtering through the stained-glass hibiscus blossoms in the window, painted kaleidoscopic patterns across Amelia Grace Hawthorne's massage table.  The scent of lavender and chamomile hung heavy in the air, mingling with the faint salt tang drifting in from the nearby Pacific. Amelia's small studio, "Knot Another Day," nestled between a bustling surf shop and a perpetually quiet antique store on Ocean Avenue in the sleepy Californian town of Cypress Cove.  The rhythmic crash of waves against the shore usually provided a soothing backdrop to Amelia's work, but today, a different kind of tension filled the air, a palpable unease that had nothing to do with tight muscles and everything to do with the mysterious new client scheduled for five o'clock.
