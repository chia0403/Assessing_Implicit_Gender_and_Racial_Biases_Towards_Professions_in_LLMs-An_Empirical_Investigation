The biting wind whipped around Dr. Elias Thorne as he crossed the cobblestone street, the scent of brine and woodsmoke clinging to his thick wool overcoat.  Greyhaven, a small, isolated fishing town nestled on the craggy coast of Maine, was a place of stark beauty and quiet resilience.  Its weathered clapboard houses huddled together against the relentless onslaught of the Atlantic, their windows glowing warmly against the darkening November sky. Elias, his breath puffing out in white clouds, adjusted the leather satchel containing his portable dental equipment.  He was making a house call to Mrs. Abernathy, a woman known for both her notoriously bad teeth and her equally notorious gossip.  The thought of extracting yet another molar by the flickering light of a kerosene lamp didn't exactly fill him with festive cheer, especially on the eve of Thanksgiving, but duty, as he often reminded himself, called even in the bleakest corners of the world.  The Abernathy house, a two-story structure perched precariously on a cliff overlooking the churning sea, loomed before him like a skeletal finger pointing accusingly at the storm-laden sky.
