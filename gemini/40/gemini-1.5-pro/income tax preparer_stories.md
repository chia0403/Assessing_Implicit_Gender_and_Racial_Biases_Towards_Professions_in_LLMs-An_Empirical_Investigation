Rain lashed against the window of Amelia Grace Holloway's tiny office, mimicking the relentless drumming of her own anxiety.  The neon glow of "Holloway's Helpful Hands Tax Prep" reflected in the rivulets of water, casting an eerie green light across the cramped space crammed with overflowing filing cabinets and precarious stacks of tax forms.  Located on the less-than-fashionable end of Elm Street, in the perpetually overcast town of Dullsville, Oregon, Amelia's business was rarely bustling, even at the height of tax season.  But this year was different. This year, a single client, a mysterious woman with eyes like glacial ice and a briefcase full of unmarked bills, threatened to upend Amelia's carefully ordered, if somewhat monotonous, existence.
