The mahogany desk, polished to a mirror sheen, reflected the flickering gaslight back into Judge Elias Thorne's weary eyes. Dust motes danced in the golden glow, illuminating the stacks of parchment piled precariously around the edges of the blotter.  The air in his chambers, thick with the scent of stale pipe tobacco and old leather-bound books, clung to him like a second skin. Outside, the relentless November rain lashed against the leaded glass windows of the courthouse, a mournful soundtrack to the late hour. In the shadowed corners of the room, grotesque gargoyles perched atop the bookshelves seemed to leer at him, their stone faces mocking his fatigue. Greyhaven, a town clinging to the windswept cliffs of the Cornish coast, was shrouded in a perpetual mist, much like the secrets its inhabitants desperately tried to keep hidden beneath its swirling grey cloak.  Tonight, Elias knew, another one of those secrets was about to be laid bare before him.
