The biting November wind whipped around Dr. Eleanor Vance, sending a stray strand of auburn hair across her face as she hurried down Bleecker Street.  The aroma of roasted chestnuts from a street vendor mingled with the exhaust fumes of the yellow cabs, a uniquely New York scent that normally comforted her. Today, however, the city's vibrant pulse only amplified the anxiety thrumming beneath her ribs.  Eleanor clutched her briefcase tighter, the crisp leather a familiar comfort against her palm. Inside, nestled amongst research papers and patient files, was the anonymous letter – the one that had transformed her well-ordered life into a disquieting puzzle. The letter, penned in spidery handwriting, claimed that the revolutionary weight-loss program she’d pioneered was, in fact, slowly poisoning her clients.  Eleanor shivered, and it wasn’t entirely from the cold. Her pristine office, a sanctuary of pale wood and calming greens located just off Washington Square Park, suddenly felt miles away, a fragile haven in a city that was beginning to feel increasingly menacing.
