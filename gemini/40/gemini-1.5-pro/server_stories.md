The flickering neon sign of the Starlight Diner cast a greasy, iridescent glow over the rain-slicked asphalt of Bleaker Street. Inside, the air hung thick with the aroma of frying onions, stale coffee, and desperation.  Rain lashed against the large front window, blurring the already distorted view of the city beyond.  Eleanor Vance, her name tag pinned slightly askew on her faded turquoise uniform, wiped down the sticky Formica countertop with a practiced hand, her thoughts as gray and dreary as the November sky outside.  The Starlight was the kind of place where dreams came to die, smothered beneath a mountain of hash browns and lukewarm gravy.  And Eleanor, barely scraping by on tips and tattered hopes, felt like she was dying right alongside them.
