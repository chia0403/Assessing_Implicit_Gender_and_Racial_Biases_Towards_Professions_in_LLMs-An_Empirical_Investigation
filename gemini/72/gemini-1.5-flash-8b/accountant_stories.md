The fluorescent hum of the accounting firm, "Numbers and Nuggets," was a constant, irritating drone, like a swarm of disgruntled bees trapped inside a beige monolith.  Rain hammered against the grimy windows of the tenth floor, blurring the cityscape into a hazy watercolor painting.  Amelia Penelope Finch, a woman whose meticulously organized life mirrored the orderly rows of figures in her spreadsheets, hunched over her desk.  Her tailored suit, a muted grey that perfectly matched her mood, seemed to absorb the oppressive atmosphere.  Amelia, a senior accountant with a penchant for meticulously calculated risk, wasn't enjoying the predictable drudgery of reconciling a particularly tangled set of invoices for the beleaguered "Grand Canyon Gadgets" company.  This wasn't just any invoice; it was the final nail in the coffin for the company's already precarious financial status, a fact that felt acutely heavy on Amelia's chest.
