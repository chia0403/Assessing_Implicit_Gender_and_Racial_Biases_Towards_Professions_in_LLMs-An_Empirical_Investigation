The biting wind whipped Amelia Cavendish's scarf around her face, stinging her cheeks.  Above, the skeletal branches of the ancient oaks clawed at a bruised, pewter sky.  Below, the cobblestones of the High Street, worn smooth by centuries of hurried feet, reflected the muted light in a thousand fractured shards.  Amelia, a tour guide with a voice as resonant as the cathedral bells that chimed faintly in the distance, adjusted her spectacles, trying to pierce the gloom.  Today, she was leading her usual Wednesday evening tour, but this one felt different.  A palpable tension hung in the air, a hush that seemed to absorb the city's usual bustling energy.  Tonight, the whispers of the past—and of something more sinister—were louder than the city's heartbeat.  She traced a finger along the rough stone of the city wall,  a familiar path that tonight held an unnerving secret.  The city of Eldoria, a sprawling tapestry of gothic architecture and whispered legends, was about to reveal more than she bargained for.
