The scent of beeswax and fine wool hung heavy in the air, a comforting blanket around Elias Thorne, Master Tailor.  His workshop, tucked away on a cobbled alley in the heart of the burgeoning city of Aethelgard, was a haven of hushed whispers and rhythmic snips. Sunlight, fractured by the towering, stone-faced buildings, slanted across the intricately-folded fabrics displayed on the long tables.  Dust motes danced in the pale beams, illuminating the intricate patterns embroidered onto silks from distant lands.  Elias, his brow furrowed in concentration, meticulously measured a length of deep crimson velvet, the material whispering softly against his calloused fingertips.  He was working on a commission for Lady Isolde of the Blackwood Manor, a commission that felt heavy with unspoken anxieties.  It was a custom-made dress, not just for a princess, but for a princess in desperate need of a carefully crafted secret.
