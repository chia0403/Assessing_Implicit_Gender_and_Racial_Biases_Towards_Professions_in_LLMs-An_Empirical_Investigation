The chipped Formica countertop gleamed under the harsh fluorescent lights of the "Numbers Nook" accounting office.  Dust motes danced in the pale beams, illuminating the meticulously organized rows of receipts, invoices, and tax forms that filled Amelia Penelope Featherstone's workspace.  Amelia, a bookkeeper with a precision that bordered on the obsessive, meticulously tallied figures into her worn, leather-bound ledger.  The air, thick with the scent of old paper and stale coffee, clung to her like a second skin.  Today, however, the usual quiet hum of the office was punctuated by an unusual clatter.  Outside, the relentless city of New Corinth, a sprawling metropolis built on the concrete dreams of the past century, thundered with its ceaseless rhythm.  Inside, Amelia felt a tremor in the air, a disturbance in the meticulously balanced ledger of her life.  She was certain something was amiss.
