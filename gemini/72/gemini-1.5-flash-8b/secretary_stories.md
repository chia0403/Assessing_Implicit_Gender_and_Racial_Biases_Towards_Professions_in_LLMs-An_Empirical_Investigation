The chipped Formica countertop gleamed under the harsh fluorescent lights of the third-floor office suite, reflecting the swirling dust motes that danced in the afternoon sun slicing through the grimy window.  Rain hammered against the glass, a relentless percussion that echoed the frantic rhythm in Amelia Blackwood's chest.  She, Amelia Blackwood, executive secretary to Mr. Silas Thorne, the notoriously demanding head of Thorne Industries, was staring at the overflowing inbox.  Paper, yellowed with age and laced with the cryptic scribbles of clients and colleagues, threatened to topple the precarious stacks.  A half-eaten granola bar, abandoned mid-munch, lay forlornly beside a pile of urgent memos.  The air, thick with the scent of stale coffee and desperation, seemed to press down on her, threatening to suffocate her.  This was her reality:  the quiet, unseen battlefield of a high-powered executive's office.
