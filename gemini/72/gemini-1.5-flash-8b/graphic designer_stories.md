The chipped Formica countertop reflected the fluorescent hum of the design studio, a pale, sickly yellow that seemed to leech the color from everything around it.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of afternoon sun slicing through the grimy window, illuminating a chaotic landscape of crumpled paper, half-eaten takeout containers, and the omnipresent tangle of wires emanating from Amelia "Amy" Carmichael's computer.  Amy, a graphic designer with a penchant for bold typefaces and a perpetually dishevelled bun, stared blankly at the screen.  The logo for "Evergreen Estates," a new housing development, glared back at her, a stark and lifeless rectangle of pastel greens and beige, utterly lacking the vibrant spark she'd envisioned.  The deadline loomed, a looming, concrete beast, and the project felt like a stubborn knot in her stomach, tightening with each passing minute.
