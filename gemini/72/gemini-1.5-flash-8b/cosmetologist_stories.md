The scent of lavender and burnt sugar hung heavy in the air of "Whispers of Beauty," a salon tucked away on a cobbled side street in the heart of the perpetually rain-slicked city of Aethelgard.  Rain hammered against the ornate, leaded-glass windows, mirroring the frantic drumming of Elara Seraphina Blackwood's heart.  Elara, a cosmetologist with eyes the color of storm clouds and hair the shade of midnight, stared at the reflection of her client, a woman named Lady Isolde, in the oversized vanity mirror.  Isolde, usually a radiant bloom of crimson silk and laughter, was today a pale, trembling whisper of her former self.  A deep frown etched her features, and a single tear traced a path down her cheek.  The room, usually buzzing with the soft murmur of conversation and the whir of hair dryers, was eerily silent.  The only sounds were the insistent rhythm of the rain and the frantic, almost imperceptible, clicking of Elara's fingers against the cool surface of the marble countertop.  Elara knew, with a sickening certainty, that something was very wrong.
