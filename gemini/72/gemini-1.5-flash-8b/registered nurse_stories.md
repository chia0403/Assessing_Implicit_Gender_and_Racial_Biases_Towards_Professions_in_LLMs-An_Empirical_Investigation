The fluorescent lights of the emergency room hummed, a monotonous soundtrack to the cacophony of beeping machines and the urgent whispers of doctors.  Rain hammered against the grimy windows of St. Jude's Hospital, mirroring the relentless drumbeat of Amelia Grayson's heart.  She, a Registered Nurse with five years of experience, stood amidst a flurry of activity, her name badge practically vibrating with the unspoken tension in the air.  The aroma of antiseptic and fear hung heavy in the stale air, a potent cocktail she’d become all too familiar with.  It was a Tuesday night, a night like any other, yet tonight felt different.  A different kind of sick.  A different kind of fear.  This one felt… personal.
