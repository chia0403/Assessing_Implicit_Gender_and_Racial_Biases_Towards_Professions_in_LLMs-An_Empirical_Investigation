The chipped Formica countertop gleamed under the harsh fluorescent lights of "Scissors & Smiles," a salon more accustomed to the murmur of whispered conversations than the thrum of a busy city street.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of sunlight piercing the grime-coated window, illuminating a faded poster advertising a "Spring into Style" promotion from three years past.  This was the domain of  Anya Petrova, a hair cutter with hands as sure and swift as a practiced surgeon's, and a soul as enigmatic as the tangled knots she effortlessly untangled.  Her latest client, a nervous young woman named  Clara, sat rigidly in the uncomfortable, but surprisingly sturdy, vinyl chair, her gaze fixed on the vibrant crimson streaks that snaked through the faded floral wallpaper.  The air hung heavy with the scent of cheap hairspray and something indefinably metallic, perhaps the remnants of a forgotten lunch.  Anya, humming a tuneless melody under her breath, carefully selected a comb, her fingers already anticipating the contours of Clara's head.
