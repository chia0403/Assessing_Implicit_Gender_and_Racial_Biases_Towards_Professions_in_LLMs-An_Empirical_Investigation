Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of the "Whispering Pines" motel, a rhythmic counterpoint to the frantic tapping of Amelia Ashworth’s keyboard.  The flickering fluorescent light above cast long, dancing shadows across the dusty room, highlighting the scattered papers and half-empty coffee cups that littered her makeshift workspace.  Amelia, a seasoned investigative journalist for the *Emerald County Chronicle*, was deep in research for her next exposé.  The air hung thick with the scent of stale cigarettes and damp wood, a familiar aroma of the remote mountain town of Blackwood, nestled in the shadow of the jagged peaks of the Spine Range.  Blackwood, a place where secrets whispered in the wind and the past clung to the cobblestone streets like tenacious ivy, was her current battleground.  This time, it was the disappearance of local artist, Elara Vance, that had drawn her to this seemingly ordinary town.
