The aroma of cinnamon and freshly baked bread hung thick in the air, a comforting blanket against the biting November wind whistling through the gap in the cobbled alleyway.  Rain slicked the worn flagstones, mirroring the sheen of Amelia Nightingale's sweat as she meticulously arranged a dozen pain au chocolat on a warming tray.  Her tiny, sun-drenched bakery, "The Golden Crust," nestled between a butcher shop with a perpetually pungent smell and a bustling apothecary, was her sanctuary, her world.  Every morning, the first rays of dawn found her already stirring the dough, her small, nimble fingers dancing with a practiced grace that spoke volumes of years spent kneading love into every loaf.
