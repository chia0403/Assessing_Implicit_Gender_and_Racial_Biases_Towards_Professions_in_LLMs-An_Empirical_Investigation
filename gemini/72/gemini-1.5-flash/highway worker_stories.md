The biting wind whipped across the desolate expanse of the Nevada desert, stinging Elias Thorne's cheeks raw.  He hunched deeper into his worn, orange safety jacket, the fabric offering little protection against the relentless January chill.  The sun, a pale disc hanging low in the hazy sky, cast long, skeletal shadows from the towering mesas that bordered Interstate 15.  Elias, his calloused hands gripping a steaming mug of lukewarm coffee, watched a lone pickup truck, barely visible as a speck of dust on the horizon, shrink further into the vastness.  Another hour before the next passing vehicle, another hour of solitude punctuated only by the rhythmic whine of the distant highway patrol siren and the rasping sigh of the desert wind.  For Elias Thorne, a veteran highway maintenance worker for the Nevada Department of Transportation, this was just another Tuesday.
