The biting November wind whipped Amelia Katherine “Amy” Holloway’s auburn hair across her face as she hunched deeper into her worn, paint-splattered coat.  The skeletal branches of the ancient oaks clawed at the bruised twilight sky above the cobbled lane, their silhouettes stark against the rapidly fading light.  Amy shivered, less from the cold and more from a nervous energy that thrummed beneath her skin.  Before her, nestled between two crumbling Georgian townhouses in the heart of the forgotten artists’ quarter of Ashworth, stood Number 13 Grimstone Lane – her new studio.  The building groaned under the weight of years and neglect, its once-grand façade marred by peeling paint, broken windowpanes, and a persistent, earthy smell that hinted at dampness and decay – a smell, Amy decided with a thrill of anticipation, that perfectly suited her current artistic phase.
