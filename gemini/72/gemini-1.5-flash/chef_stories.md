The air hung thick and humid, a clinging blanket woven from the scent of frying garlic, simmering ginger, and the faint, metallic tang of blood.  Chef Aurelia “Rella” Moreau wiped a bead of sweat from her brow, the motion as practiced and precise as the julienne of carrots she'd been meticulously preparing for the last hour.  Her tiny, cluttered kitchen, nestled in the heart of the bustling, neon-drenched night market of Bangkok's Chinatown, was a symphony of controlled chaos.  Woks clanged, rice steamed, and the rhythmic thud of a mortar and pestle punctuated the constant hum of conversation drifting in from the crowded stalls outside.  Rella, her face smudged with turmeric and chili oil, worked with the effortless grace of a seasoned dancer, a whirlwind of controlled energy amidst the fragrant pandemonium.  Tonight, however, the usual comforting rhythm of her kitchen felt off-kilter, a dissonant note in the otherwise harmonious clatter.  A premonition, sharp and unsettling, pricked at the edges of her mind.
