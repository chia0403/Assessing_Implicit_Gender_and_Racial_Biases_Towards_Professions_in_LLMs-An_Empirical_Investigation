The salt spray stung Elias Thorne's face as he wrestled the stubborn oak plank onto the small, pitching deck of the *Sea Serpent*.  The late afternoon sun, a bruised plum in the churning grey sky, cast long shadows across the chaotic scene of his workshop – a converted fishing trawler bobbing precariously in the harbour of Port Blossom, a town clinging precariously to the jagged cliffs of the Cornish coast.  The air hung thick with the smells of sawdust, tar, and the ever-present tang of the sea.  Around him, tools lay scattered – chisels gleaming dully, mallets worn smooth by years of faithful service, and the rhythmic rasp of his plane, the only constant sound amidst the cries of gulls and the creak of the ship's timbers.  Elias, his hands rough and calloused, his face tanned and lined by sun and sea, paused, wiping a bead of sweat from his brow.  This wasn't just any carpentry job; this was his masterpiece, a vessel that held more than just wood and nails. It held his heart.
