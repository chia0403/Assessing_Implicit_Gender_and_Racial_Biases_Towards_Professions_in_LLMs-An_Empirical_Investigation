The rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of Jack Stratton's office, a relentless rhythm that mirrored the throbbing headache behind his eyes.  The air hung thick and sour with the smell of stale coffee and damp wool, a fitting aroma for the grimy, forgotten corner of Chinatown where his agency, Stratton Investigations, resided.  Neon signs flickered intermittently, casting a sickly yellow glow on peeling paint and overflowing bins.  A stray cat, sleek and black as midnight, stalked across the alleyway outside, its eyes twin emeralds in the gloom.  Jack, nursing a lukewarm cup of the aforementioned coffee, stared out at the deluge, the city’s relentless downpour a perfect metaphor for the case that had landed on his desk – a disappearance as murky and unpredictable as the Shanghai monsoon.
