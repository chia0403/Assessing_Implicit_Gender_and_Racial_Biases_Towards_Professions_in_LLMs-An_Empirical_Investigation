The biting November wind whipped Amelia Katherine Blackwood's auburn hair across her face as she wrestled the heavy easel onto the cobblestone street.  Salt spray, thick as a shroud, clung to everything, clinging especially stubbornly to the thick, oil-soaked canvas she'd painstakingly prepared.  Before her, the churning grey of the North Sea met the bruised purple of a bruised sky, a tempestuous ballet of nature only a fool, or a hopelessly romantic artist like Amelia, would attempt to capture. The air tasted of brine and impending rain, a familiar scent that permeated the tiny, windswept fishing village of Port Blossom, nestled precariously on the jagged cliffs of Cornwall.  Amelia shivered, pulling her worn wool scarf tighter, but a stubborn glint of excitement shone in her emerald eyes. This storm, this chaotic beauty, was precisely what she needed.
