The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Hernandez’s hands, doing little to thaw the icy grip of the November wind whipping through the gaps in the boarded-up windows of her office.  Rain lashed against the single pane, blurring the already indistinct view of the decaying waterfront warehouses across the street.  Amelia, a woman whose meticulously coiffed auburn hair and sharp tailored suit belied a weariness that settled deep in her bones, sighed, the sound swallowed by the relentless drumming of the storm.  Her office, perched precariously on the third floor of a building that had seen better centuries, smelled faintly of damp plaster and the lingering scent of cheap coffee.  Tonight, the aroma was heavily underscored by the acrid tang of fear.  The flickering gas lamp cast long, dancing shadows, making the already cramped space feel claustrophobic.  Amelia, insurance agent for Hernandez & Sons, knew this fear. She dealt in it every day.  Tonight, however, it felt… closer.
