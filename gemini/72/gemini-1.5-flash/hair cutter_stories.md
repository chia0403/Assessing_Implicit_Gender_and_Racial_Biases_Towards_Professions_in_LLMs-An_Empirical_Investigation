The scent of ozone hung heavy in the air, a metallic tang clinging to the damp, sea-sprayed strands of seaweed that clung stubbornly to the weathered wooden planks of the pier.  Gulls shrieked overhead, their cries swallowed by the relentless roar of the Atlantic as it crashed against the jagged rocks below.  This wasn’t exactly the typical salon location, but then again, Aurelia “Rel” Blackwood wasn’t your typical hair cutter.  Her salon, "Shear Madness," was a repurposed fishing shack perched precariously on the edge of the rugged Cornish coast, its paint chipped and faded, a testament to the relentless battering of wind and tide.  Inside, the air was thick with the fragrance of lavender and the sharp bite of sea salt, a peculiar but strangely comforting mix that mirrored Rel’s own eclectic personality.
