The chipped porcelain of the apothecary's mortar felt cool beneath Elias Thorne's fingertips, a familiar comfort in the stifling July heat.  Sweat beaded on his forehead, blurring the already smudged labels of the countless jars lining the shelves of Thorne's Apothecary.  Dust motes danced in the lone sunbeam slicing through the gloom of the shop, illuminating the intricate carvings on the mahogany counter, a relic from a time when apothecaries were revered, not just tolerated. Outside, the cobblestones of Bellweather Lane shimmered under the relentless sun, the air thick with the scent of roasting chestnuts and the cloying sweetness of honeysuckle, a stark contrast to the earthy, herbal aromas that permeated his shop.  Elias, a man whose life was measured in milliliters and milligrams, found himself strangely unsettled by the oppressive stillness of the afternoon, a quiet that felt heavy with unspoken anxieties.
