The fluorescent lights of Sterling & Steele hummed a monotonous tune, a soundtrack to Amelia Katherine Hayes’s otherwise silent existence.  Amelia, or Amy as everyone except the perpetually disgruntled Mr. Fitzwilliam insisted on calling her, sat perched on her ergonomically designed but utterly uncomfortable chair, the faint scent of lemon-scented disinfectant battling with the lingering aroma of yesterday's lukewarm coffee. Outside, the relentless Chicago drizzle mirrored the grey monotony of the towering skyscraper that housed Sterling & Steele, a prestigious – and decidedly stuffy – law firm.  The only splash of colour in Amy’s meticulously organised reception area was the vibrant, almost aggressively cheerful, bouquet of sunflowers someone had sent earlier, ironically addressed to the notoriously grim Mr. Fitzwilliam himself.  The sunflowers, Amy mused, seemed to mock the oppressive atmosphere with their relentless optimism. Today, however, even their sunny disposition couldn’t quite pierce the gloom.  A particularly brutal storm was brewing, both outside and, Amy suspected, within the firm's polished mahogany doors.
