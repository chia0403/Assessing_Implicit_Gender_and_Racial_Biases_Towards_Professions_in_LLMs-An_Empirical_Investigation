The salt spray stung Dr. Eleanor Vance’s face as she wrestled the stubborn canvas awning back into place.  The wind, a relentless Pacific beast, howled off the churning grey sea, threatening to rip the whole thing from its moorings.  Her small, ocean-view chiropractic clinic, "Seabreeze Spine & Wellness," perched precariously on the cliff edge overlooking the turbulent waters of the Oregon coast, felt more like a kite than a building in this gale.  Inside, the comforting scent of essential oils battled valiantly against the pervasive tang of brine, a daily contest won sometimes by one, sometimes by the other.  The rhythmic crash of waves against the rocks below provided a constant, if somewhat unnerving, soundtrack to her solitary existence.  Today, however, the rhythm was particularly ferocious, mirroring the storm brewing inside her.  The cancellation notice from her last patient, a lumberjack with a chronic back problem named "Big" Gus, hadn’t just been a disruption to her schedule; it was a crack in the carefully constructed façade of her carefully controlled life.
