The biting November wind whipped Amelia Hernandez’s auburn hair across her face as she hunched deeper into her threadbare coat, the collar scratching at her already chapped cheeks.  Rain, a relentless, sideways drizzle, plastered her trench coat to her body, clinging like a second skin.  She stood on the precipice of the crumbling Blackwood Pier, the skeletal remains of the once-grand structure groaning under the weight of the unforgiving sea.  The air tasted of salt and decay, a fitting aroma for the story Amelia was about to uncover.  Blackwood, a town clinging precariously to the edge of the Maine coastline, was known for its harsh winters and even harsher secrets, and Amelia, a reporter for the Portland Gazette, was determined to unearth one buried deep beneath the town’s weathered facade.
