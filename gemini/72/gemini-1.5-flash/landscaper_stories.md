The dawn mist clung to the manicured lawns of Willow Creek Estates like a reluctant shroud, each blade of grass shimmering with a thousand tiny diamonds.  Amelia “Mia” Hawthorne, her breath puffing white clouds in the crisp October air, adjusted the strap of her worn leather gardening gloves.  The scent of damp earth and decaying leaves, a familiar and comforting perfume, filled her lungs.  Willow Creek, with its ostentatious mansions and meticulously planned gardens, was a far cry from the wildflower meadows of her childhood, but it paid the bills, and Mia had bills – a mountain of them, threatening to bury her under an avalanche of overdue notices.  Today, however, was a particularly promising day.  Mrs. Vandergelt, the resident with the notoriously temperamental hydrangeas, had requested an emergency consultation – and Mia, with her green thumb and uncanny ability to coax life from even the most neglected specimens, was just the woman for the job.
