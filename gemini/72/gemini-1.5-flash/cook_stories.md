The salt spray stung Amelia “Amy” Hawthorne’s face as she hauled the last crate of ice onto the deck of the *Sea Serpent*.  The late afternoon sun, a bruised plum sinking behind the jagged teeth of the Shetland Islands, cast long shadows across the churning grey water.  Amy, her flour-dusted apron clinging damply to her sturdy frame, surveyed her domain: a galley smaller than most London kitchens, yet somehow holding the chaotic symphony of a professional cook’s life.  The air, thick with the smells of brine, smoked fish, and simmering something vaguely resembling stew, was punctuated by the rhythmic clang of the ship's engine and the cries of gulls overhead.  This wasn't some glamorous cruise; the *Sea Serpent* was a fishing trawler, and Amy, despite her culinary aspirations, was currently its sole chef, caterer, and, on occasion, reluctant deckhand.
