The biting November wind howled a mournful dirge around the granite facade of St. Jude's Hospital, rattling the ancient oak trees lining the cobblestone drive.  Inside, however, Dr. Elias Thorne, a man whose hands were as deft as a concert pianist's but whose heart held the weary weight of a thousand surgeries, felt a stillness that bordered on the sepulchral. The operating theatre lights, usually a harsh, sterile beacon, seemed dimmed tonight, their glow reflecting off the polished steel instruments laid out on the stainless-steel table like a macabre silver service. Elias, his face etched with the fine lines of exhaustion and the faintest shadow of a beard, stared at them, his breath fogging in the frigid air.  The case tonight was different; this wasn't just another life hanging in the balance, it was a life intricately woven into the fabric of his own.
