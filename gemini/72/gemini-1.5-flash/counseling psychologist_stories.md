The chipped porcelain mug warmed Dr. Eleanor Vance’s hands, the lukewarm chamomile tea doing little to soothe the icy knot tightening in her stomach.  Rain lashed against the panoramic window of her office, blurring the usually vibrant cityscape of Seattle into a hazy, grey impression.  Inside, the room was a carefully curated sanctuary – warm, neutral tones, a plush rug muffling the storm’s fury, and shelves overflowing with carefully selected books, a silent testament to years spent navigating the complexities of the human psyche.  Tonight, however, the carefully constructed calm felt fragile, threatened by the looming presence of the empty chair opposite her desk.  It was a chair that, in just under an hour, would be occupied by a client whose case file promised to be the most challenging of her career – a man claiming to be haunted by a ghost, not of the metaphorical kind, but a literal, spectral presence that only he could see.
