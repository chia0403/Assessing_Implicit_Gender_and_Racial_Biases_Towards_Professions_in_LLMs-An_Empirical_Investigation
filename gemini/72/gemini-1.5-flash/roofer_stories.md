The wind, a relentless sculptor, carved icy fingers across the slate tiles of Blackwood Manor.  A biting November wind whipped Elias Thorne’s already chapped lips raw as he wrestled a length of heavy-gauge copper flashing into place.  Below him, the grounds of the sprawling Victorian mansion were a muted tapestry of greys and browns, the skeletal branches of ancient oaks clawing at the bruised twilight sky.  Elias, his breath clouding in the frigid air, clung precariously to the steeply pitched roof, the only sound louder than the wind being the rhythmic clang of his hammer against the metal.  The job was nearly finished, another day’s brutal work etching itself into his calloused hands and aching back, but the unsettling feeling that clung to Blackwood Manor like the clinging ivy on its walls remained. This wasn’t just another leaky roof; this was a story waiting to be told, one Elias Thorne, seasoned roofer and reluctant investigator, was about to uncover.
