The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Hawthorne’s hands, the lukewarm chamomile tea doing little to soothe the icy dread that had settled in her gut.  Rain lashed against the tall, Gothic windows of her office, each gust a mournful sigh echoing the turmoil within her.  Outside, the gaslights of Bloomsbury cast a sickly yellow glow on the slick cobblestones, transforming the usually charming streets into a labyrinth of shadowy corners.  Amelia, editor-in-chief of the prestigious *Literary Gazette*, stared out at the storm, the manuscript of "The Obsidian Mirror" lying forgotten on her desk – a stark white rectangle against the dark mahogany. It wasn't the manuscript that troubled her, though.  It was the cryptic note, tucked inside its pages, a single sentence scrawled in a spidery hand:  *The truth is buried deeper than you think.*
