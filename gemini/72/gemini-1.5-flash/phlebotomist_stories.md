The fluorescent lights of the Meadowbrook Medical Center hummed a monotonous tune, a counterpoint to the rhythmic *thump-thump-thump* of Amelia Katherine “Amy” Finch’s own pulse.  Amy, a phlebotomist with a perpetually weary smile and eyes that had seen too much morning sickness, sat perched on a rolling stool, her gaze fixed on the overflowing sharps container.  It bulged, a grim testament to another busy morning in the bustling outpatient clinic.  The air hung heavy with the scent of antiseptic and something faintly floral – probably the desperate attempt of a nervous patient to mask their fear with perfume.  Outside, the late October wind rattled the windows, a stark contrast to the sterile, controlled environment within.  Rain lashed against the glass, mirroring the storm brewing inside Amy, a storm of exhaustion and simmering resentment that had been building for weeks.
