The salt spray kissed Dr. Eleanor Vance's face as she navigated the rickety wooden dock, the scent of brine and decaying seaweed clinging to the humid air.  Above, the bruised purple of a twilight sky bled into the inky black of the approaching night, mirroring the storm brewing inside her.  She clutched her worn leather satchel, its contents – meticulously organized meal plans and nutritional supplements – a stark contrast to the chaotic beauty of the Isle of Skye.  The wind howled a mournful Gaelic song, whipping her auburn hair around her face as she reached the small, weather-beaten cottage that was her temporary haven, a place as far removed from the sterile environment of her London practice as she could manage.  This wasn't a holiday; Eleanor had come seeking answers, not relaxation.  Answers about the baffling illness that had struck the island's tightly-knit community, an illness that defied all conventional dietary explanations.
