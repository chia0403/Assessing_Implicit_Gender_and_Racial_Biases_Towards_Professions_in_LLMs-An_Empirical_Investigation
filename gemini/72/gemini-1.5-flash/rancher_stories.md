The wind, a relentless sculptor, carved patterns into the snowdrifts that choked the valley.  Each gust howled a mournful dirge across the frozen expanse of the Bar None Ranch, a symphony only broken by the occasional crackle of ice under the weight of a burdened pine branch.  The biting air stung Elias Thorne's cheeks, a familiar discomfort that did little to dampen the grim determination etched onto his weathered face.  His breath plumed out in ragged white clouds, mirroring the swirling chaos of the blizzard that had besieged the ranch for three days.  The ranch house, a squat, sturdy affair of weathered wood and stone, hunkered down against the onslaught, a solitary sentinel against the encroaching wilderness.  Elias, bundled in layers of thick wool and leather, squinted into the maelstrom, the worry lines etched deep into his forehead a stark contrast to the defiant glint in his grey eyes.  Tonight, the fate of the Bar None, and perhaps even his own, hung precariously in the balance.
