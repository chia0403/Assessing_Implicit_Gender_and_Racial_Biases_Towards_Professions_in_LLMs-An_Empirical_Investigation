The salt-laced wind whipped Amelia Jean Hawthorne’s perfectly coiffed auburn bob across her face as she wrestled the flimsy "For Sale" sign into the frozen ground.  The January air bit with a ferocity that rivaled the gnawing anxiety in her gut.  Amelia Jean, or AJ as she preferred – professionally, at least –  hadn't sold a single property in three months.  This ramshackle Cape Cod on the windswept cliffs of Seabrook, Maine, represented her last, desperate hope before the bank came knocking.  The ocean roared a symphony of discontent in the distance, mirroring the turmoil brewing within her meticulously organized, yet increasingly precarious, life.  The house itself, perched precariously on the edge of a crumbling cliff, seemed to lean as far into the stormy sea as Amelia Jean felt she was leaning towards financial ruin.
