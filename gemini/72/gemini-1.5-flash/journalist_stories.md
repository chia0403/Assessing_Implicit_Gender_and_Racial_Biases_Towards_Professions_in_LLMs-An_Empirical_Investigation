The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Holloway’s trench coat around her legs as she hurried across the rain-slicked cobblestones of Prague’s Old Town Square.  Gas lamps, casting a sickly yellow glow on the ancient architecture, struggled to pierce the gloom.  Above, the spires of Týn Church clawed at the bruised purple sky, their gothic silhouettes stark against the gathering storm.  Amelia, her breath misting in the frigid air, pulled her scarf tighter, the worn leather chafing against her already chapped cheeks. The scent of roasted chestnuts and woodsmoke, usually comforting, was tonight overwhelmed by a sharper, metallic tang – the faint, lingering smell of blood.  This wasn't the charming fairytale Prague tourists flocked to; this was the city's underbelly, and Amelia was about to delve right in.
