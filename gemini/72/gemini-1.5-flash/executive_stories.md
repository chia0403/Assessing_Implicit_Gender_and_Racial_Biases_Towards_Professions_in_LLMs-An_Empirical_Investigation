The stale air of the executive suite hung heavy with the scent of old leather and impending doom.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of the 57th floor, mirroring the tempest brewing inside Amelia “Mia” Hawthorne, CEO of Hawthorne Industries.  The city of Chicago sprawled beneath her, a glittering tapestry of light and shadow, utterly indifferent to the precarious position of its most prominent – and possibly soon-to-be-former – daughter.  Mia, impeccably dressed in a charcoal grey power suit that did little to hide the tremor in her hand, stared at the flickering screen of her Bloomberg terminal.  The stock ticker, once a comforting friend, now felt like a malevolent judge, each downward tick a hammer blow to her carefully constructed empire.  Tonight, everything hung in the balance.
