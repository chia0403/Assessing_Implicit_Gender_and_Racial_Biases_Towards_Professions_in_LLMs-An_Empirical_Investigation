The stale air hung thick with the scent of cheap beer and desperation, clinging to the chipped paint of the Melody Lounge like a second skin.  Rain lashed against the grimy windows, mirroring the tempest brewing inside Seraphina “Sera” Nightingale.  Her emerald eyes, usually sparkling with mischievous light, were shadowed with fatigue, reflecting the flickering neon sign outside that promised "Live Music & Cold Drinks" but delivered only the former, and that sparingly.  Tonight, the promise of a full house had evaporated faster than the condensation on a lukewarm glass, leaving Sera with a near-empty bar and the gnawing emptiness of a singer's unsung song. The only other occupants were a grumpy bartender polishing glasses with excessive vigour, and a lone, hunched figure nursing a whiskey in the darkest corner, their face obscured by a wide-brimmed hat.  This wasn’t the glamorous life Sera had envisioned when she first arrived in the smoky haze of New Orleans, but it was all she had for now.
