The salt spray stung Amelia Hernandez’s face as she leaned against the railing of the ferry, the wind whipping her dark hair across her cheek.  The churning grey waters of Puget Sound separated her from Bainbridge Island, a smudge of emerald green against the brooding sky.  Rain, a constant companion in this corner of Washington state, threatened to unleash itself in earnest.  Amelia, however, barely registered it.  Her gaze was fixed on the sprawling, half-finished structure dominating the island's shoreline – a behemoth of concrete and glass, her own ambitious, and increasingly problematic, masterpiece: the Seaglass Pavilion.  The project, intended as a testament to sustainable architecture and breathtaking coastal views, was currently teetering on the brink of disaster, a precarious balance mirroring the turbulent waters below.
