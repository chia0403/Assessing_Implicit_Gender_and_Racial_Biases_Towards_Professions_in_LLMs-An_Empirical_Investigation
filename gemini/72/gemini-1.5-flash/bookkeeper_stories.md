The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Beatrice Finch's hands, the lukewarm tea doing little to chase away the November chill that seeped into the bones in Blackwood, a town where shadows lingered longer than the sun.  Rain hammered against the leaded panes of her office window, a relentless rhythm mirroring the frantic beat of her own heart.  Amelia, a bookkeeper with ink-stained fingers and a mind sharper than any audit, sat hunched over a ledger, the flickering gaslight casting long, dancing shadows across the meticulously organized rows of figures.  The air smelled faintly of dust, old paper, and the lingering scent of pipe tobacco, a testament to the decades her grandfather had spent in this very office, a legacy she now inherited, along with the peculiar, and increasingly unsettling, mysteries that seemed to follow the town's finances like a persistent cough.
