The scent of lavender and old silk hung heavy in the air, a comforting perfume that clung to the velvet curtains and the worn wooden floorboards of "Stitches in Time," Aurelia Clementine Hawthorne's dressmaking establishment.  Dust motes, illuminated by the afternoon sun slanting through the tall, arched windows, danced in the quiet space, momentarily eclipsing the vibrant colours of the bolts of fabric stacked neatly against the walls.  Aurelia, a woman whose age was etched gracefully onto her face like the delicate embroidery on a fine handkerchief, hummed a low, melancholic tune as she meticulously hand-stitched a delicate rose onto a pale blue gown.  Outside, the bustling streets of Edwardian London thrummed with life, a stark contrast to the peaceful sanctuary she'd created within her small shop.  But today, the usual calm was shattered by a frantic rapping at the door, a sound that promised anything but tranquility.
