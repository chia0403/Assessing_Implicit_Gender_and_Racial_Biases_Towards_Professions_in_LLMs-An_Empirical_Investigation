The scent of sandalwood and chamomile hung heavy in the air, a fragrant curtain drawn against the relentless November drizzle lashing against the leaded windows of Serenity Spa.  Rain hammered a steady rhythm against the slate roof, a counterpoint to the soft, melodic chimes that tinkled every time the door opened.  Inside, however, a stillness reigned, broken only by the low hum of the aromatherapy diffuser and the gentle squeak of Eleanor Vance's worn leather massage chair.  Eleanor, a woman whose hands possessed the magic to melt away tension with the lightest of touches, leaned back, sipping lukewarm chamomile tea and regarding the empty appointment book with a sigh that was more of a weary acceptance than despair.  The late autumn slump had hit Serenity Spa hard, leaving Eleanor, its sole proprietor, with an unsettling amount of free time and a growing stack of overdue bills.
