The flickering neon sign of the "Lucky Dragon" noodle shop cast a lurid green glow across Amelia Hernandez's rain-slicked trench coat.  Raindrops, fat and insistent, plastered her dark hair to her forehead as she hurried down the nearly deserted street, the rhythmic drumming on the pavement a counterpoint to the frantic hammering in her chest.  Amelia, a systems analyst for the city's notoriously unreliable public transportation authority, wasn't known for her penchant for late nights, but tonight was different.  Tonight, the entire city's subway system – a sprawling, groaning beast of steel and electricity she knew intimately – was on the brink of complete collapse, and the only person who could possibly fix it was her. The air hung heavy with the smell of exhaust fumes and cheap ramen, a strangely fitting aroma for the impending technological apocalypse she was about to face.
