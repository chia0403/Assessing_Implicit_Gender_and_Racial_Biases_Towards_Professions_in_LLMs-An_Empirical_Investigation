The scent of woodsmoke and damp wool hung heavy in the air, a familiar perfume to Elias Thorne, tailor to the gentry and the desperately poor alike.  His shop, a cramped but cherished space wedged between a fishmonger’s and a bustling bakery on Cobblestone Lane in the perpetually overcast town of Oakhaven, hummed with a quiet industry.  The rhythmic snip-snip of his shears, a counterpoint to the rhythmic clang of the blacksmith’s hammer a few doors down, was the soundtrack to Elias’s life.  Dust motes, illuminated by the weak winter sun struggling through the grimy windowpanes, danced in the shafts of light that sliced through the gloom.  On the workbench, half-finished garments lay draped across headless mannequins, their expectant forms mirroring the hopes and anxieties of their future owners.  Today, the air was thick with the promise of a particularly challenging commission – a wedding gown, intricate and breathtakingly beautiful, for Miss Amelia Tremaine, the Mayor's daughter.  Elias adjusted his spectacles, the faint scent of lavender from his handkerchief a small comfort against the looming pressure.
