The biting November wind whipped around Ms. Eleanor Vance as she hurried across the parking lot of Northwood High, clutching her worn leather satchel tighter against her side. Flakes of snow, the first of the season, danced in the twilight, dusting the hoods of the cars and the skeletal branches of the elm trees lining the perimeter.  Northwood, a sprawling brick edifice that had seen better days, hunched against the darkening sky like a tired giant. Inside, the fluorescent lights hummed their monotonous tune, illuminating mostly empty classrooms and hallways that still echoed with the cacophony of the recently dismissed student body. Eleanor, head of the English department and a veteran of fifteen years at Northwood, felt a familiar pang of loneliness settle in her chest as the quiet descended. This time of day, the in-between time, always amplified the quiet melancholy that seemed to permeate the aging school.  Tonight, however, it felt heavier, more ominous, like the stillness before a storm.
