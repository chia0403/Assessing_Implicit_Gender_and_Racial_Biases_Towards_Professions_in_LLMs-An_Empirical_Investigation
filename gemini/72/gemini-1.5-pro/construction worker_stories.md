The biting November wind whipped across the half-finished skeleton of the skyscraper, rattling the scaffolding like a nervous skeleton itself.  Fifty stories below, the Chicago streets were a blur of grey slush and yellow cabs.  Up here, Amelia “Milly” Hernandez, her hard hat pulled low over her thick braid, wrestled with a length of rebar, the frozen metal biting into her gloved hands.  The sky, a bruised purple, threatened snow.  The wind howled again, carrying the faint, metallic tang of the lake and the distant, rhythmic clang of another crane, a lonely giant in the gathering gloom.  This was Milly's office, her kingdom, her challenge.  And today, it felt like the whole damned world was against her.
