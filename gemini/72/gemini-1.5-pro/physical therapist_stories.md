The biting November wind whipped around Eleanor Vance Holloway's ankles as she hurried across the cobblestone courtyard of the Griffin Rehabilitation Center, her breath puffing out in white plumes.  Gargoyles perched on the gothic building's eaves seemed to leer down at her, their frozen expressions mirroring the tight knot in her stomach. The Center, a converted 19th-century monastery nestled in the Catskill Mountains of upstate New York, boasted stunning views and state-of-the-art facilities. But today, its aged stone walls seemed less like a sanctuary and more like a prison holding a secret Eleanor was desperate to unlock.  The fluorescent lights humming in the hallway cast long, eerie shadows as she approached Room 304, the source of her unease.
