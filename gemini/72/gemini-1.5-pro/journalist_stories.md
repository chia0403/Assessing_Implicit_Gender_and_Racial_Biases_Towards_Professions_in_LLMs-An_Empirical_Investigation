Rain lashed against the grimy windows of The Mariner's Rest, blurring the already faded neon sign advertising "Rooms & Grub."  Inside, the air hung thick with the smell of stale beer and frying fish, a pungent aroma that clung to everything in the small, seaside town of Port Whisper.  Eleanor Vance, a journalist for the Boston Chronicle, hunched deeper into her threadbare coat, the damp chill seeping into her bones.  She stirred the lukewarm coffee in her chipped mug, her gaze fixed on the churning grey expanse of the Atlantic visible beyond the rain-streaked glass.  She'd arrived in Port Whisper three days ago, chasing a story about a missing fishing trawler, the 'Sea Serpent,' and its seven-man crew.  So far, she'd found nothing but closed lips and suspicious glances.  The town, nestled between craggy cliffs and the unforgiving ocean, seemed to be guarding its secrets closely, and Eleanor, despite her reputation for tenacity, was starting to feel the oppressive weight of its silence.
