The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Amy” Hawthorne’s auburn braid across her face as she trudged across the frosted cobblestones of the Sanctuary.  Dawn hadn't yet broken over the sprawling grounds of the Whispering Pines Animal Rescue, leaving the towering pines shrouded in pre-dawn gloom.  Frost crunched under Amy's worn work boots, the only sound besides the distant, mournful howl of a lone wolf housed in the sanctuary’s far northern enclosure.  The air, thick with the scent of pine needles and damp earth, held a promise of snow.  Whispering Pines, nestled deep in the Catskill Mountains of New York, was a haven for creatures great and small, a place where Amy had found her calling, mending broken wings and soothing frightened souls. This morning, however, a different kind of unease settled in Amy’s stomach, a feeling that went beyond the usual pre-winter anxieties. Something felt… off.
