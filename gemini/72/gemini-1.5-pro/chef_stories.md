The clatter of pots and pans echoed through the cramped galley kitchen of "The Salty Spoon," a brightly painted food truck parked permanently on the windswept cliffs overlooking the Oregon coast. Rain lashed against the corrugated metal walls, a familiar soundtrack to Elias Thorne’s culinary creations. Elias, his dark hair perpetually escaping the confines of his chef's hat, hummed a melancholic sea shanty as he expertly flipped a Dungeness crab cake.  The air, thick with the aroma of frying garlic and the briny tang of the ocean, offered a stark contrast to the grey, storm-churned vista visible through the serving hatch. Today’s special, scrawled on a chalkboard propped against a stack of lobster traps: "Crab Cakes with Coastal Slaw and a Side of Seaside Sorrow."  It wasn't a marketing gimmick. Elias, for all his culinary talent, carried a weight in his heart as heavy as the anchor of a sunken ship.
