The salt spray stung Amelia Zephyr Thorne's face as she perched precariously on the windswept cliff edge, her easel braced against a gust that threatened to snatch it and send it tumbling into the churning turquoise waters below.  The air, thick with the scent of brine and blooming sea lavender, vibrated with the cries of gulls wheeling overhead.  Below her, the tiny fishing village of Port Blossom clung to the rocky coastline of Cornwall, its whitewashed cottages huddled together like sheep against the elements.  Amelia, her fingers already stained with cobalt and crimson, squinted at the dramatic scene before her, trying to capture the wild beauty of the Cornish coast on her canvas. This was her third attempt to paint the crashing waves against the setting sun, and frustration, a familiar companion, gnawed at her.  She knew, with a certainty that settled deep in her bones, that this landscape held the key to unlocking the creative block that had plagued her for months. She just had to find the right way in.
