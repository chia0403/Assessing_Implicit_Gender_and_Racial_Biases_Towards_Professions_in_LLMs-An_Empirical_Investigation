The rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of The Rusty Mug, a rhythm Amelia "Mia" Rodriguez knew by heart.  It was a lullaby of sorts in the isolated mining town of Dust Devil Gulch, Nevada.  Inside, the air hung thick with the scent of stale beer, cheap whiskey, and the faint, lingering aroma of chili from the previous night's special.  Dust motes danced in the single beam of sunlight stubbornly pushing through a grimy window, illuminating the rows of dusty bottles behind the bar where Mia polished a glass, her movements slow and deliberate.  Dust Devil Gulch was a town forgotten by time, a place where dreams went to die and secrets went to be buried under the shifting sands of the desolate landscape.  And Mia, with her sharp eyes and even sharper tongue, was the keeper of those secrets.
