Rain lashed against the arched windows of Dr. Elias Thorne's office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside his newest client.  The room, nestled on the second floor of a converted Victorian house in the sleepy, seaside town of Port Blossom, Maine, usually exuded a calming atmosphere.  The walls were painted a gentle seafoam green, a plush, teal rug softened the polished hardwood floor, and a small, crackling fireplace fought a valiant battle against the November chill.  But tonight, the flickering flames seemed only to highlight the tension etched on the young woman's face as she nervously twisted the silver ring on her thumb.  Outside, the wind howled a mournful dirge, punctuated by the rhythmic crash of waves against the rocky shore, a soundtrack that seemed perfectly suited to the anxieties Dr. Thorne was about to unravel.
