The flickering fluorescent lights of the Zenith Laboratories sub-basement buzzed like trapped insects, casting long, skeletal shadows from the overflowing bins lining the corridor.  Amelia "Amy" Rodriguez, her name tag slightly askew on her faded blue uniform, sighed, the sound swallowed by the constant thrum of the building's ventilation system.  It was 2:17 am, the quietest hour in Zenith, when even the nocturnal lab rats seemed to doze.  Tonight, however, Amy's usual routine of mopping, emptying bins, and polishing the impossibly shiny chrome of the forgotten machinery was punctuated by a low, rhythmic thumping coming from behind the heavy steel door marked "Storage - Authorized Personnel Only." A door she'd been specifically instructed never to open.
