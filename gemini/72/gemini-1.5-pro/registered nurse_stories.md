The biting wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne's dark hair across her face as she hurried across the deserted parking lot of St. Jude's Memorial Hospital.  The neon "Emergency" sign buzzed faintly, casting a sickly green glow over the freshly fallen snow. The clock on the dashboard of her aging Corolla blinked 11:58 pm.  Almost shift change.  Amelia pulled her threadbare wool coat tighter around herself, the damp chill seeping into her bones despite the layers.  St. Jude's was perched on a bluff overlooking the turbulent waters of Lake Superior, and in late November, the wind off the lake carried the sharp, metallic tang of ice and impending winter storms.  Tonight, the usually vibrant Duluth cityscape across the bay was shrouded in a thick, swirling fog, the distant lights blurring into a hazy, ethereal glow.  Amelia shivered, not entirely from the cold, but from a premonition of unease that had been gnawing at her since the start of her shift.
