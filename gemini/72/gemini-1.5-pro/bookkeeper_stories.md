The insistent drumming of rain against the corrugated iron roof of Barnaby & Fitch Accounting mirrored the relentless thrumming in Beatrice Amelia Finch's temples. November in the small town of Hollow Creek, Pennsylvania, always brought a particular brand of gloom, a damp chill that seeped into the very bones of the buildings and its inhabitants.  The two-story brick building, sandwiched between Mrs. Higgins' perpetually fragrant bakery and the ominously silent Hollow Creek Funeral Home, felt smaller than usual, suffocating almost, under the weight of the grey sky. Beatrice, perched on her creaky stool behind a mahogany desk older than she was, meticulously entered figures into the thick ledger, her brow furrowed in concentration. The only other sound besides the rain and the rhythmic scratching of her pen was the ticking of the grandfather clock in the corner, each tick a stark reminder of the rapidly approaching deadline for the town's annual audit.
