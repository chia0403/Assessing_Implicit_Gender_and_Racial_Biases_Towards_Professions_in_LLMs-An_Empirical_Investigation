The chipped paint on the walls of Mrs. Eleanor Vance's kindergarten classroom was a testament to the boundless energy that flowed through its doors each morning.  Butterflies, lopsided suns, and finger-painted rainbows, all slightly sticky to the touch, adorned every available surface. Outside, the October wind whipped through the branches of the ancient oak that sheltered the Maplewood Elementary playground, scattering crimson and gold leaves across the worn wooden climbing frame.  Inside, however, the air was thick with the scent of Elmer's glue and anticipation.  Mrs. Vance, her auburn hair pulled back in a practical bun, knelt amidst a flurry of construction paper scraps, her kind eyes twinkling behind her wire-rimmed glasses.  Today was Halloween costume parade day, and the excitement was palpable.
