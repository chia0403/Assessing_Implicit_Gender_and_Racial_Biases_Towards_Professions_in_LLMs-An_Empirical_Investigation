The insistent Florida sun beat down on the turquoise awning of "Curl Up & Dye," baking the sidewalk outside to a shimmering haze.  Inside, the air thrummed with the low hum of hair dryers and the rhythmic snip of scissors.  Eleanor Vance, her name embroidered in a looping script on her crisp white smock, expertly wielded a curling iron, coaxing a cascade of auburn ringlets to frame Mrs. Henderson's beaming face.  The salon, nestled between a laundromat and a pawn shop in the heart of sleepy Cypress Creek, was Eleanor's kingdom, a fragrant oasis of hairspray and hope where transformations unfolded daily. This particular Tuesday, however, held a different kind of transformation in store for Eleanor, one that had nothing to do with perms or highlights.
