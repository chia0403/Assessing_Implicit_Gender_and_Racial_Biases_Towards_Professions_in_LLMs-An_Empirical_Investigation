Rain lashed against the attic window of Elias Thorne's apartment, mimicking the drumming anxiety in his chest. Elias, his full name Elias Humphrey Thorne, sat hunched over his battered oak desk, a single lamp illuminating the chaotic landscape of manuscripts, galleys, and half-empty coffee cups that constituted his workspace.  Loose papers, covered in his spidery scrawl, threatened to take flight with every gust that rattled the panes.  His cramped apartment, tucked away in the eaves of a crumbling brownstone in Greenwich Village, usually served as a comforting sanctuary from the bustling energy of New York City. Tonight, however, the low, slanted ceiling seemed to press down on him, mirroring the weight of the deadline looming over his head.  The manuscript for "The Obsidian Heart," the debut novel of a supposedly literary genius, lay open before him, its pages filled with prose that Elias suspected was less brilliant than baffling.
