The wind, a relentless sculptor, carved patterns into the snowdrifts that piled high against the weathered fence posts of the Bar None Ranch.  A biting January wind whipped across the expanse of the Wyoming plains, stinging the exposed skin of Elias Thorne, Jr., as he surveyed his kingdom.  Elias, his face etched with the map of a life spent battling the elements and the unforgiving land, squinted against the blinding sun reflecting off the pristine snow.  The air, thin and crisp, held the scent of pine and the faint, metallic tang of frozen earth.  Miles stretched before him, a vast, silent tableau of snow-covered mesas and skeletal trees, broken only by the occasional huddled shape of a juniper bush fighting for survival.  His breath plumed out in ragged white puffs, momentarily visible against the stark, winter landscape.  This year, the winter had come early and with a vengeance, threatening to bury the Bar None under a suffocating blanket of white.
