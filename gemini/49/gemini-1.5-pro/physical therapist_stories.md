The biting wind whipped off the Puget Sound, carrying the scent of salt and pine through the open window of Olympia Physical Therapy. Rain lashed against the glass, mirroring the persistent ache in Dr. Elias Thorne's lower back.  He rubbed at it absently, his gaze fixed on the ferry churning its way across the choppy water towards Bainbridge Island. His clinic, nestled in a quaint, cedar-shingled building on the Olympia waterfront, was usually a haven of calm, a place where he helped people reclaim their mobility and their lives. But today, the restless energy of the storm seemed to have seeped into the very walls, mirroring the unease churning within him.  He had a new patient arriving any minute, one with a particularly complex case, and Elias, despite his years of experience, felt a knot of apprehension tighten in his stomach.  This patient, he knew, would challenge him in ways he hadn't been challenged before.
