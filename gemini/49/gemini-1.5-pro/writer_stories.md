The salt spray kissed Amelia "Mia" Reyes's face as she leaned against the railing of her porch, the rusted metal cool against her palms. Her weathered notebook, filled with half-formed sentences and frantic scribbles, lay open on the small, round table beside her, abandoned.  Her gaze was fixed on the churning grey expanse of the North Atlantic, the waves a restless counterpoint to the quiet, creeping dread that had settled in her chest.  Mia’s cottage, perched precariously on the windswept cliffs of Inis Mór, the largest of the Aran Islands, had once been a sanctuary, a place where words flowed freely, inspired by the raw, untamed beauty that surrounded her. Now, the silence of the island felt oppressive, the isolation suffocating. The deadline for her next novel loomed, a menacing shadow stretching across the wildflower-dotted landscape, and the blank page mocked her with its endless possibilities, none of which she could seem to grasp.  The wind howled, a lonely, mournful sound that mirrored the emptiness within her.
