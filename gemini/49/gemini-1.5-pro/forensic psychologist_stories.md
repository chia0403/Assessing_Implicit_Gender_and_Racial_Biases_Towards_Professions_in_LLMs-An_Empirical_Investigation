Rain lashed against the grimy windows of the Seattle Police Department's interrogation room, mirroring the tempest brewing inside Dr. Eleanor Vance.  The flickering fluorescent lights overhead cast a sickly yellow glow on the sterile, grey walls, adding to the oppressive atmosphere.  Eleanor, her auburn hair pulled back in a tight bun, adjusted her rimless glasses and studied the man seated across the table. He was a study in contradictions: impeccably dressed in a charcoal suit, yet his hands trembled uncontrollably.  His eyes, a startling shade of ice blue, darted around the room, refusing to meet hers. This was her third hour with Thomas Reed, the prime suspect in the brutal murder of a prominent art dealer, and so far, he'd offered nothing but strained silence and a disconcerting smile. Eleanor knew, with the chilling certainty that came from years of dissecting the darkest corners of the human mind, that beneath that carefully constructed facade lay a storm far more dangerous than the one raging outside.
