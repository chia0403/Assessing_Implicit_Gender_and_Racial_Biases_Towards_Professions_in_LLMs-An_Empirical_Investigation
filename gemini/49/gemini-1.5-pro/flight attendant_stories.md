The stale recirculated air hung heavy with the scent of lukewarm coffee and someone's overly-enthusiastic application of duty-free perfume.  Amelia "Mia" Rodriguez smoothed down her navy blue uniform skirt, the fabric whispering against her tights.  Outside, the tarmac of Charles de Gaulle Airport glittered under the harsh sodium lights, reflecting a pre-dawn Parisian sky just beginning to blush with the promise of a cold January sunrise.  Flight AF008 to New York City was delayed. Again. Mia sighed, expertly maneuvering her trolley past a cluster of disgruntled passengers huddled around a flickering departure board.  She plastered a smile on her face, one she’d perfected over five years of navigating cramped cabins and temperamental travellers, and announced, "Refreshments, anyone?"  But beneath the practiced cheer, a prickle of unease danced on her skin. This delay wasn’t just weather related.  Something felt…off.
