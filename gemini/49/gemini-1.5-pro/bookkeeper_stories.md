The rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of Barnaby & Sons General Store, a sound that usually soothed Amelia Grace Holloway.  Today, however, it mirrored the frantic thumping in her chest.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of sunlight slicing through the grimy windowpane, illuminating columns of meticulously kept ledgers stacked on Amelia's rolltop desk.  Crowsfoot, Kentucky, population 384 (not counting the livestock), nestled deep in the Appalachian Mountains, rarely saw excitement.  But excitement, in the form of a discrepancy of precisely $17,312.48 in Barnaby & Sons' accounts, had found Amelia.  And it wasn't going away.
