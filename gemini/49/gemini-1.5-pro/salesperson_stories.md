Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of the Skytop Diner, blurring the already fading neon glow of the Ferris wheel on the pier below.  Inside, the air hung thick with the aroma of stale coffee and frying onions.  Eleanor Vance, a pharmaceutical sales representative for MedTech Solutions, tapped a perfectly manicured nail against the laminated tabletop, her gaze fixed on the swirling grey expanse of the Atlantic.  Atlantic City in November wasn't exactly a tropical paradise, and this particular Tuesday morning was proving to be even drearier than usual.  Her meeting with Dr. Alistair Finch, a notoriously difficult cardiologist, was already running forty-five minutes late, and the lukewarm coffee in front of her was doing little to soothe her frayed nerves.  This sale, the largest potential deal of her career, hung precariously in the balance, and Eleanor couldn't shake the feeling that the churning storm outside mirrored the turbulent uncertainty in her own gut.
