The biting wind whipped off the East River, carrying the metallic tang of rain and the distant shriek of a ferry horn.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of Amelia “Amy” Reyes’s Brooklyn loft, blurring the already fading twilight view of the Manhattan skyline. Inside, the only sounds were the rhythmic tap of her stylus against her tablet and the low hum of the space heater battling the encroaching November chill. Amy, her dark hair pulled back in a messy bun, traced a finger across the glowing blueprint of the "Chrysalis," a daringly innovative community center she'd designed for the Lower East Side – a neighborhood she knew intimately, having grown up amidst its vibrant chaos. The building, a swirling edifice of reclaimed brick and glass, was her passion project, a chance to give back to the community that had shaped her, and tonight, the eve of the final proposal, a knot of anxiety tightened in her stomach.
