The biting November wind whipped around Dr. Elias Thorne, stinging his cheeks as he hurried down Bleecker Street, the scent of woodsmoke and roasted chestnuts battling for dominance in the frigid air.  His brown leather satchel, heavy with the day's compounding supplies, thumped against his hip in time with his brisk steps.  Elias, head tucked against the wind, squinted through the gathering gloom towards the warm glow emanating from Thorne Apothecary, a quaint, ivy-clad building nestled between a bustling Italian deli and a dusty antique shop.  For generations, the Thorne family had served Greenwich Village's medicinal needs from within its aged brick walls, and Elias, the latest in a long line of Thorne pharmacists, felt the weight of that legacy every time he turned the key in the heavy oak door.  Tonight, however, something felt different. A strange, unsettling quiet hung in the air, a stark contrast to the usual symphony of city noises that serenaded his nightly walk home.
