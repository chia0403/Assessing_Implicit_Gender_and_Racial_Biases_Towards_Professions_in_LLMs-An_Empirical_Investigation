The crimson velvet curtain, heavy with gold tassels, shimmered under the single spotlight.  A hushed expectancy, thick as the Parisian fog clinging to the Seine outside, filled the intimate cabaret, Le Chat Noir.  Rain hammered against the arched windows, a rhythmic counterpoint to the nervous tapping of a polished heel against the worn wooden floorboards backstage.  Ariadne Celeste Dubois, her throat tight with a mixture of anticipation and terror, adjusted the silver dragonfly clasp at her throat.  This was it.  Her debut.  Tonight, she would either soar, a phoenix rising from the ashes of obscurity, or crash and burn, another forgotten spark in the city of lights.
