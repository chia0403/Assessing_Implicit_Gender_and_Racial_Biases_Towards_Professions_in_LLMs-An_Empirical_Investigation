The rain hammered against the gothic windows of Dr. Eleanor Vance's office, each drop a tiny explosion against the leaded glass. Gargoyles perched on the roofline seemed to leer down at the sodden cobblestone streets of Arkham, Massachusetts, a town steeped in a chilling history of strange occurrences and unsettling whispers. Inside, the only sound competing with the downpour was the rhythmic tick-tock of a grandfather clock in the corner, its brass pendulum swinging like a hypnotist's watch. Eleanor, her dark hair pulled back in a severe bun that did little to soften the sharp angles of her face, sat at her mahogany desk, the dim light of a Tiffany lamp casting long shadows across the room.  She tapped a pen against a leather-bound notepad, her brow furrowed in concentration as she reviewed the notes from her latest patient, a man plagued by dreams of impossible geometries and whispering voices from beyond the veil.  The scent of old books and woodsmoke hung heavy in the air, a comforting aroma that usually soothed her. Tonight, however, a prickle of unease danced on the back of her neck, a feeling that she was not alone, despite the empty waiting room beyond the heavy oak door.
