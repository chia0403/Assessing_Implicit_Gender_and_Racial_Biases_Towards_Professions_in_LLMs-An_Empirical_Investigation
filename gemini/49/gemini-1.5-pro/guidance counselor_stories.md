The chipped porcelain mug, stained faintly brown from years of reheated coffee, warmed Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s hands as she stared out the window of her office.  Frost feathered across the glass, obscuring the already muted colours of a November morning in Ashwood, Vermont. Ashwood High, a sprawling brick building hunched low against the wind, seemed to hold its breath, waiting for the inevitable onslaught of winter.  Inside, the air buzzed with the low hum of adolescent energy, a current that Amelia, after fifteen years as the school’s guidance counselor, could feel vibrating even through the closed door of her small, book-lined sanctuary. Today, however, that hum felt different, charged with a nervous anticipation that pricked at the edges of Amelia's awareness.
