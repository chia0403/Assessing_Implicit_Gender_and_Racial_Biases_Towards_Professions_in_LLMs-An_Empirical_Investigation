The fluorescent lights of Aspire Dental hummed a monotonous tune, reflecting off the polished chrome of the instruments Dr. Ramirez favored.  A thin film of November rain slicked the windows, blurring the view of the bustling Seattle street below.  Inside, the air smelled faintly of mint and antiseptic, a scent Amelia Grace Hawthorne had come to associate with both dread and a strange sense of calm. Amelia, meticulously clad in her teal scrubs, adjusted the tiny magnifying glass perched on her nose and sighed. It was a Tuesday, which meant Mrs. Periwinkle, notorious for her elaborate tales of Siamese cats and her equally elaborate bridgework, was due any minute.  Amelia glanced at the clock. 2:58 pm.  Two minutes.  Just enough time to brace herself.
