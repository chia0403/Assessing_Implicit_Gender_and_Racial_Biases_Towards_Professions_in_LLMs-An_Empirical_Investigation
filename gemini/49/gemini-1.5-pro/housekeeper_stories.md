The biting November wind whipped around Clementine Agatha Holloway's ankles as she wrestled the heavy oak door of Eventide Manor closed.  Built into the cliff face overlooking the churning grey expanse of the North Sea, the manor seemed to groan and creak in sympathy with the gale.  Inside, the air was thick with the scent of beeswax and the lingering ghost of woodsmoke from the seldom-used fireplaces.  Dust motes danced in the sliver of sunlight that pierced through the tall, arched windows of the grand hall, illuminating the faded grandeur of the tapestry-hung walls and the vast, empty space.  Clementine, her hands raw and red from the cold, adjusted the starched white apron over her worn grey dress and surveyed her domain.  Eventide, once a bustling family home, was now her responsibility, a silent, echoing testament to a bygone era, and to the secrets it still held.
