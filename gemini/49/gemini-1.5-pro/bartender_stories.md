Rain lashed against the stained-glass window depicting Saint Jude, patron saint of lost causes, a fitting image for The Rusty Mug. Inside, the air hung thick with the smell of stale beer, cheap whiskey, and desperation.  A single flickering gas lamp cast long, dancing shadows across the worn wooden bar, illuminating the dust motes swirling in the smoky haze.  Behind the bar, Elias Thorne, his face etched with the weariness of a thousand late nights and whispered confessions, polished a glass with a practiced hand, his gaze fixed on the relentless downpour outside. This was his domain, a haven tucked away in the labyrinthine alleyways of Lower Grimsborough, a city perpetually cloaked in fog and secrets.
