The flickering fluorescent lights of St. Jude's Hospital Emergency Room cast a sterile, pale glow on Amelia Reyes, RN.  The air, thick with the metallic tang of blood and the antiseptic bite of disinfectant, buzzed with a nervous energy.  It was 2:17 am on a Tuesday, the kind of hour that swallowed sound and amplified the whispers of suffering. Outside, a relentless Chicago wind howled, rattling the aging windows, mirroring the turmoil brewing inside Amelia’s chest. Tonight, the usual controlled chaos of the ER felt different, heavier.  A premonition, cold and clammy, clung to her skin, a feeling she’d learned to trust over her ten years on the job.  Something bad was coming.
