The biting wind whipped off Lake Superior, carrying the scent of pine and iron ore dust, stinging Amelia “Amy” Reyes’s cheeks as she wrestled the antenna into place.  Dusk bled across the sky, painting the towering grain elevators of Duluth, Minnesota, in hues of bruised purple and fading gold.  Amy, perched precariously on the metal roof of the KDWZ radio station, tightened the last bolt, the frigid metal biting through her gloves. Below, nestled between a dilapidated tavern and a laundromat that smelled perpetually of burnt fabric softener, the small brick building that housed KDWZ hummed with the low thrum of a thousand untold stories waiting to be broadcast.  It was December 1988, and static crackled in the air, both literally and figuratively, as a blizzard threatened to swallow the city whole.
