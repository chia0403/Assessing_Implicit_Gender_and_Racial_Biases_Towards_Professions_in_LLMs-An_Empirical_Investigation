The biting wind whipped across the desolate expanse of the Nevada desert, stinging Elias Thorne’s cheeks raw.  Dust, fine as powdered sugar, swirled around his worn work boots, clinging to the already grime-covered overalls he’d been wearing for three days straight.  The sun, a malevolent eye in the pale sky, beat down relentlessly on the endless ribbon of asphalt that stretched before him like a shimmering mirage.  Elias squinted, shielding his eyes with a calloused hand, as he surveyed the freshly-laid stretch of Highway 50, his trusty shovel leaning against a battered pickup truck parked haphazardly a hundred yards away.  The air hung heavy with the smell of hot tar and exhaust fumes, a familiar perfume to Elias, a constant companion for the past fifteen years he’d spent patching up this lonely stretch of road.  Today, however, the familiar discomfort was amplified by a gnawing unease, a feeling that something was profoundly…off.
