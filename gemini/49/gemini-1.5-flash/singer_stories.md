The stale air hung thick with the scent of cheap beer and desperation, clinging to the peeling paint of the Blue Moon Saloon like a second skin.  Dust motes, illuminated by the single, flickering bulb above the bar, danced in the sluggish currents stirred by the occasional shuffling foot.  Tonight, however, the usual languid atmosphere of the Blue Moon was punctuated by a nervous energy.  For tonight, Seraphina Evangeline Blackwood, a woman whose voice could soothe a rabid dog and shatter glass with equal ease, was singing.  The chipped, mahogany stage, usually occupied by a dusty piano and a lone, wilted fern, was bathed in the weak glow of a single spotlight, waiting for the queen of this forgotten corner of Dustbowl City to take her throne.
