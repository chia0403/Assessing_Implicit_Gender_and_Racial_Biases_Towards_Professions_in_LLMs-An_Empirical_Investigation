The biting November wind whipped Amelia "Mia" Worthington's auburn hair across her face as she hurried down Wall Street, the relentless rhythm of her heels a counterpoint to the city's frantic heartbeat.  Skyscrapers clawed at the bruised twilight sky, their glass and steel faces reflecting the flickering neon signs of bars already brimming with the weary and the wildly celebratory.  The scent of roasted chestnuts and exhaust fumes mingled in the air, a potent cocktail unique to this concrete jungle.  Mia, however, barely registered it.  Her mind, a whirlwind of stock tickers and market fluctuations, was already preoccupied with the impending close of trading and the volatile performance of her most significant client’s portfolio – a portfolio whose value hung precariously close to the precipice of ruin.
