The aroma of cardamom and roasted almonds hung heavy in the air, a fragrant counterpoint to the biting November wind whistling through the gaps in the old, wooden shutters of "The Gilded Whisk."  Inside, Amelia Grace Hawthorne, her flour-dusted apron a testament to a morning already spent wrestling with a particularly stubborn batch of croissant dough, hummed a jaunty tune completely at odds with the grim weather outside.  Rain lashed against the windowpanes of her tiny bakery, blurring the already hazy view of the cobbled street in the heart of  Port Blossom, a quaint coastal town where the salty tang of the sea mingled perpetually with the sweet scent of baking bread.  The flickering gaslight above Amelia's worktable cast long, dancing shadows across the shelves lined with jars of colorful candies, glistening preserves, and rows upon rows of perfectly formed gingerbread men, each one meticulously iced with a different, whimsical expression.
