The salt spray stung Eleanor Vance's face as she wrestled the stubborn fishing net onto the deck of the "Sea Serpent."  Gulls cried overhead, their calls a harsh counterpoint to the rhythmic creak of the aged wooden hull.  The air, thick with the smell of brine and diesel, carried the faint, sweet scent of seaweed clinging to the rocks just below the cliff face.  Eleanor, a registered occupational therapist with a penchant for the unpredictable, wasn't supposed to be here, hauling in lobster pots on the rugged coast of Maine.  Her usual workspace was a sterile, brightly-lit clinic, not the pitching deck of a small fishing boat, miles from the nearest paved road.  But then again, this wasn't a usual case.  This was Silas Blackwood.  And Silas, a man broken not by age, but by a relentless, unseen enemy, needed more than physiotherapy; he needed a miracle.
