The salt spray stung Amelia “Mia” Hernandez’s face as she wrestled the stubborn For Sale sign from its rusted bracket.  The wind, a relentless Pacific gale, howled a mournful song around the crumbling Victorian perched precariously on the cliff overlooking the churning grey sea.  Seagulls shrieked overhead, their cries echoing the desolate beauty of the landscape. This wasn't your typical beachfront property;  'Ocean's Wrath,' as the locals jokingly called it, was more accurately described as a monument to neglect, its once-grand facade now scarred by time and the unforgiving elements.  The house, or what remained of it, clung to the cliff edge like a stubborn barnacle, a testament to the capricious nature of both the ocean and its human inhabitants.  Mia, however, saw potential, or at least, the possibility of a hefty commission.  The sky, a bruised purple canvas streaked with angry orange, mirrored the turmoil brewing inside her as she finally pried the sign free.  This sale, she knew, would be anything but straightforward.
