The biting November wind whipped around Dr. Elias Thorne’s ankles as he hurried across the cobbled courtyard of St. Jude’s Hospital.  Rain, a relentless grey curtain, plastered his dark hair to his forehead. The ancient stone buildings, their gothic arches shadowed and slick, seemed to lean in on him, their age a palpable weight pressing down on the already heavy burden in his satchel: the notes from the pre-op consultation for the Pfeiffer case.  The Pfeiffer case, which threatened to unravel everything Elias had worked for, everything he believed in.  He pulled his worn leather coat tighter, the chill seeping into his bones, mirroring the icy dread that had settled in his gut.  The air, thick with the smell of antiseptic and woodsmoke from a nearby chimney, did little to alleviate the growing sense of foreboding.  Inside, the sterile fluorescent lights of the operating theatre awaited, a stark contrast to the melancholic beauty of the storm raging outside.
