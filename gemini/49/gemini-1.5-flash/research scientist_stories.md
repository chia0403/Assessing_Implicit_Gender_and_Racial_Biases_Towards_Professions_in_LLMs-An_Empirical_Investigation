The biting Antarctic wind howled a mournful symphony around Dr. Evelyn Grace Holloway, whipping her already frost-stiffened parka tighter against her skin.  Ice crystals, sharp as shards of glass, stung her exposed cheeks despite the heavy balaclava.  She hunched deeper into her insulated suit, the rhythmic thump of her heart a counterpoint to the howling wind.  Ahead, the vast, unforgiving expanse of the McMurdo Ice Shelf stretched to the horizon, a blinding white canvas under the bruised purple of the twilight sky.  For six weeks, Evelyn had been stationed at the remote research outpost, a tiny, prefabricated structure clinging precariously to the ice, its only companions the relentless wind and the echoing silence of the Antarctic wilderness.  Her mission: to analyze a newly discovered subglacial lake, a body of water untouched by the outside world for millennia, potentially holding secrets that could rewrite the history of life on Earth.  But the secrets the lake held weren't the only ones that threatened to surface.
