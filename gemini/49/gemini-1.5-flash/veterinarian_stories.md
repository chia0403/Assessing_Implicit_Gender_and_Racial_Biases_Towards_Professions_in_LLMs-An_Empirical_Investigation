The biting November wind whipped Dr. Eleanor Vance's auburn braid across her face as she wrestled the reluctant Shetland pony, aptly named Stormy, into the confines of the examination stall.  The air hung thick with the smell of damp earth and woodsmoke, a familiar perfume in the backcountry of rural Vermont.  Beyond the weathered clapboard walls of her clinic, nestled between a gnarled maple and a rambling stone wall, the late afternoon sun cast long shadows across the snow-dusted fields.  The only other sounds besides Stormy's frustrated snorts were the rhythmic creak of the old barn and the distant, mournful call of a lone crow.  This wasn't exactly the glamorous veterinary practice Eleanor had envisioned after graduating top of her class at Cornell, but it was hers, and in the quiet solitude of the Vermont hills, she was finally finding a sense of peace she hadn't expected.
