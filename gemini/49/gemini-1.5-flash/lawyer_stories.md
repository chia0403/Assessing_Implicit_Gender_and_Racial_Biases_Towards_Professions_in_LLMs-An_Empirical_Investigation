The chipped paint of Amelia Grace Hawthorne's office window framed a spectacular, if somewhat unsettling, view.  Below, the relentless grey expanse of the Thames churned under a sky the colour of bruised plums.  Rain lashed against the glass, each drop a tiny, insistent drumbeat against the almost unbearable silence that had settled over the otherwise bustling legal district of London.  Amelia, a solicitor with a reputation for sharp wit and sharper legal acumen, stared out at the storm, the flickering gaslight on her mahogany desk casting long, dancing shadows that seemed to mock her current state of frustrated contemplation.  The case of *Rex v. Finchley*, a seemingly straightforward embezzlement, had taken a bewildering turn, leaving her drowning in a sea of contradictory evidence and increasingly frantic phone calls.  The weight of it all pressed down on her, heavy as the sodden London air.
