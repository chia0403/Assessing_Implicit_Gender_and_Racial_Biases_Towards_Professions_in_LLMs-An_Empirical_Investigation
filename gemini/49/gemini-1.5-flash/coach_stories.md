The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Amy” Hawthorne’s hair across her face as she surveyed the desolate practice field.  Fallen leaves, the color of bruised plums and rusting iron, crunched under her worn leather boots.  The air hung thick with the scent of damp earth and woodsmoke drifting from distant chimneys, a stark contrast to the sterile chlorine smell that usually clung to her – the lingering aroma of the indoor pool she usually commanded.  This, however, was no ordinary practice.  Tonight, under the skeletal branches of ancient oaks silhouetted against a bruised twilight sky, Amy wasn't coaching synchronized swimmers; she was coaching a team far more…unconventional.  The flickering gaslight of the nearby abandoned farmhouse cast long, dancing shadows that mimicked the anxious fidgeting of her charges, huddled around a makeshift bonfire.
