The biting November wind howled a mournful tune around Elias Thorne’s ears, rattling the loose windowpanes of the abandoned mill.  Dust motes, illuminated by his headlamp, danced in the skeletal beams of the vast, cavernous space.  Elias, a man whose calloused hands spoke more eloquently of years spent wrestling with wires than any flowery prose ever could, adjusted his worn leather work gloves.  The air hung thick with the scent of damp earth and decaying timber, a perfume that, to most, would be unpleasant, but to Elias, was simply the familiar aroma of a job well-underway.  This wasn't just any job, though; this was the restoration of Blackwood Mill, a local landmark whispered to be haunted, a project that promised to be as challenging as it was… intriguing.
