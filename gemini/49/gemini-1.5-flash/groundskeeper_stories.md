The biting November wind whipped Elias Thorne's already-chapped lips raw as he wrestled with a stubborn oak branch, its leaves clinging stubbornly despite the approaching winter.  Elias, a man whose life was as weathered and dependable as the ancient elms he tended, hunched deeper into his worn tweed jacket.  The grounds of Blackwood Manor, his domain for the past twenty years, stretched before him, a tapestry of fading autumnal colours – russet, gold, and the deep, somber green of the evergreens – now softened by the first dusting of snow.  The manor itself, a gothic behemoth of grey stone, loomed in the distance, its many windows like dark, watchful eyes against the twilight sky.  The air, thick with the scent of damp earth and decaying leaves, carried a faint, almost imperceptible undercurrent of something else… something unsettling.
