The scent of almond extract hung heavy in the air, a sugary counterpoint to the metallic tang of rain lashing against the windowpanes of "The Sweet Surrender," Amelia Rose Hawthorne's cake decorating studio.  Rain, a relentless, grey curtain, had been falling since dawn, mirroring the tempest brewing inside Amelia herself.  Her meticulously organized workspace – usually a haven of pastel perfection – was a chaotic swirl of buttercream roses, toppled fondant castles, and a half-finished, three-tiered masterpiece representing a despondent-looking unicorn that seemed to weep iridescent tears of frosting.  Amelia, her usually vibrant red hair dampened and plastered to her cheeks, stared at the unicorn with a sigh that could wilt a bouquet of lilies.  The deadline for Lord Ashworth's extravagant 70th birthday cake loomed – and Amelia was spectacularly behind.
