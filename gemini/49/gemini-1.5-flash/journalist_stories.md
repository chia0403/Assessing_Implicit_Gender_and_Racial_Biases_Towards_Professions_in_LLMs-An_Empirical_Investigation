The humid air hung heavy, thick with the scent of rotting mangoes and diesel fumes.  Cicadas shrieked a relentless chorus from the overgrown bougainvillea that clawed at the crumbling walls of the Hotel Casablanca, a once-grand establishment now clinging precariously to its faded glory like a forgotten film star.  Amelia Grace Hernandez, a journalist with ink-stained fingers and a thirst for stories that simmered just below the surface of her quiet exterior, sat on the cracked marble of her balcony, a half-empty glass of lukewarm rum sweating beside her notepad.  The city of Veracruz, a symphony of chaotic beauty and simmering secrets, sprawled before her, a labyrinth of narrow streets and vibrant markets shrouded in the gathering twilight.  Tonight, however, the usual cacophony was muted, replaced by an unsettling stillness that prickled her skin.  Amelia knew, with a certainty that chilled her despite the oppressive heat, that something was about to break.
