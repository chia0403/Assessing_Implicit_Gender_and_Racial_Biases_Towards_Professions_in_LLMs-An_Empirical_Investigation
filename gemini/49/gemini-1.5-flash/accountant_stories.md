The fluorescent lights of the Alder & Birch Accounting firm hummed a monotonous tune, a counterpoint to the frantic tapping of Amelia Katherine Finch's fingers on her keyboard.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows overlooking the grey, brooding cityscape of Manchester, each drop a tiny, insistent percussion against the glass.  Amelia, a woman whose life was meticulously organized down to the last decimal point, found herself staring blankly at a spreadsheet filled with figures that refused to reconcile.  The usual soothing rhythm of numbers, a comfort in her otherwise chaotic world, had been replaced by a dissonant jangle, a discordant symphony of errors that mirrored the storm raging outside.  The air in the office, usually sterile and smelling faintly of old paper and desperation, was thick with the scent of brewing coffee and a growing sense of unease.  Tonight, however, the usually predictable routine felt ominously off-kilter.
