The stale scent of chalk dust and simmering resentment hung heavy in the air of Northwood High's Room 302, clinging to the chipped paintwork like a persistent cough.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows, blurring the already fading autumnal colours of the sprawling campus beyond.  Eleanor Vance, a woman whose auburn braid usually held a meticulous order mirroring her classroom routines, felt the familiar knot of frustration tighten in her stomach.  The crumpled lesson plan on her desk, detailing the intricacies of 18th-century English literature, seemed a distant, irrelevant dream compared to the throbbing headache threatening to derail her already precarious equilibrium.  Outside, the wind howled a mournful dirge, a fitting soundtrack to the chaos brewing within the walls of Northwood High.
