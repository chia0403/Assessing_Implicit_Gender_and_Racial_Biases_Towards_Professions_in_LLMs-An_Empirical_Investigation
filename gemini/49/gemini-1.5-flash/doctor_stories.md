The salt spray stung Dr. Eleanor Vance's face as the ferry bucked violently against the churning grey sea.  Rain lashed down, blurring the already indistinct outline of the Isle of Skye, a jagged silhouette against the bruised purple sky.  November’s grip held the island in an icy embrace, the wind a howling banshee tearing at the frayed edges of her waterproof coat.  Eleanor shivered, not entirely from the cold.  The message she’d received – a cryptic plea for help from a remote croft, delivered by a breathless, tear-streaked messenger – had left a knot of unease tightening in her stomach that no amount of Highland weather could dispel.  This wasn't a routine house call; this felt…different.  The air crackled with a palpable sense of foreboding, as heavy and damp as the mist clinging to the cliffs.
