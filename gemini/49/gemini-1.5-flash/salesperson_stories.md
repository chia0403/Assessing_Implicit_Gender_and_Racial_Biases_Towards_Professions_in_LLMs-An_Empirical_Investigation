The stale air of the showroom hung heavy with the scent of new car leather and desperation.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of afternoon sunlight slicing through the grime-coated windows of Prestige Motors.  Amelia Grace Hawthorne, her name as polished and gleaming – or at least, she tried to keep it that way – as the cherry-red convertible she was desperately attempting to sell, sighed.  Another Saturday, another slow day.  Another chance for her carefully constructed façade of cheerful competence to crumble under the weight of unsold inventory and mounting commission pressure.  The silence was broken only by the rhythmic tick-tock of the grandfather clock in the corner, each second a hammer blow against her dwindling hope of meeting her quota.
