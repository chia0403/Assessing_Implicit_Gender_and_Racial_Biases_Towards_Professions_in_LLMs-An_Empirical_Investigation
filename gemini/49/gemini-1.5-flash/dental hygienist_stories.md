The sterile scent of ozone hung heavy in the air, a familiar perfume to Amelia Grace Hawthorne, RDH.  Fluorescent lights hummed a monotonous tune above her, reflecting harshly off the polished stainless steel of the dental chair.  Rain lashed against the panoramic window of Dr. Albright’s opulent dental practice, blurring the cityscape of Portland, Oregon, into a hazy watercolour.  Amelia, meticulously polishing a particularly stubborn stain from a patient’s molar, barely registered the storm.  Years of cleaning teeth had honed her focus to a razor’s edge, a skill that served her well, both professionally and personally.  Tonight, however, even her usually unflappable concentration wavered.  The patient, a nervous, fidgety man in his late thirties with eyes that held a disconcerting amount of unshed worry, had just confessed to something that shattered the mundane rhythm of her evening.
