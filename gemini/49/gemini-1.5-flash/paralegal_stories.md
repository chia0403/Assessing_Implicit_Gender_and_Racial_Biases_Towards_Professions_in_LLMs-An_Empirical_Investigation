The fluorescent lights of the Peterson & Yates law firm hummed a monotonous tune, a counterpoint to the frantic tapping of Amelia Katherine “Amy” Reed’s keyboard.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of their downtown Chicago office, blurring the already hazy cityscape into a watercolor of grey and black. Amy, a paralegal with five years of experience and a caffeine dependency bordering on the pathological, wrestled with a mountain of discovery documents – thousands of pages detailing a complex corporate fraud case involving a mysteriously bankrupt tech startup,  “Innovate or Die,” and allegations of insider trading so convoluted they made her head spin.  The air hung heavy with the scent of stale coffee and impending deadlines, a familiar aroma in the life of a paralegal on the verge of a very long night.
