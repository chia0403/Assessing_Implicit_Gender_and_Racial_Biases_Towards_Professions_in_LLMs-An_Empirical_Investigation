The biting November wind whipped Amelia Katherine Blackwood’s unruly auburn curls across her face as she wrestled the canvas onto the easel.  The skeletal branches of the ancient oak trees clawing at the bruised twilight sky formed a stark, gothic backdrop to the ramshackle fisherman’s shack perched precariously on the cliff edge.  Below, the churning grey expanse of the Atlantic roared its unending symphony, a relentless rhythm that echoed the tempestuous emotions swirling within Amelia herself.  Her small, rented cottage, barely visible behind the weathered timbers of the shack, offered little respite from the raw, unforgiving beauty of the Cornish coast.  It was a beauty she both worshipped and feared, a muse that both inspired and threatened to consume her.  Tonight, it was threatening to win.
