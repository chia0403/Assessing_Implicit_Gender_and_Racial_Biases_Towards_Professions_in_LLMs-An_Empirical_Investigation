The humid air hung heavy, thick with the scent of frangipani and impending rain.  Cicadas buzzed a relentless chorus from the emerald canopy that draped over the crumbling Mayan ruins of Ek' Balam.  Sweat trickled down Amelia Hernandez’s spine, blurring the already smudged map clutched in her hand.  She adjusted the brim of her wide-brimmed straw hat, its faded red a stark contrast to the ochre stone surrounding her.  This wasn't how she'd envisioned her Tuesday afternoon.  The scheduled tour group, a boisterous bunch of Canadians with an insatiable appetite for selfies, were nowhere to be seen.  Only the ghosts of past civilizations and the unnerving silence of the Yucatán jungle kept her company.
