The chipped paint of the windowsill was cold against Dr. Eleanor Vance’s cheek as she stared out at the relentless November rain lashing against the brick facade of Northwood High.  Each drop seemed to mirror the relentless anxieties swirling within the walls of the building itself, a chaotic symphony of teenage angst and adolescent turmoil she knew all too well.  The air hung heavy with the scent of damp wool coats and the faint, underlying aroma of stale coffee from the faculty lounge, a smell that, to Eleanor, had become inextricably linked to the weight of a thousand untold stories.  Northwood High, nestled in the perpetually overcast town of Havenwood, Oregon, was her battlefield – and today, it felt particularly besieged.
