The fluorescent hum of the hospital cafeteria buzzed a discordant counterpoint to the rhythmic squeak of Amelia Jeannette Finch's shoes on the linoleum.  Amelia, a nutritionist with a perpetually tired smile and eyes that held the weight of a thousand questionable food choices, pushed a trolley piled high with suspiciously vibrant green smoothies and suspiciously beige trays of meticulously portioned chicken breast.  The air hung thick with the cloying sweetness of over-ripe fruit and the faintly metallic tang of antiseptic, a scent as familiar to her as her own reflection.  This wasn't just any hospital; it was St. Jude's, a sprawling behemoth of pale brick and hushed anxieties nestled in the heart of a city that never slept, but certainly seemed to subsist on a diet of processed carbohydrates and caffeine.  Today, Amelia was facing her toughest challenge yet:  convincing a family of five, clinging fiercely to the comfort of mac and cheese, to embrace the kale.
