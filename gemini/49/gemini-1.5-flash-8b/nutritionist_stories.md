The aroma of roasted turmeric and ginger hung heavy in the air, mingling with the faint scent of jasmine tea that perpetually clung to Dr. Amelia Petrova's cluttered office.  Sunlight, fractured by the elaborate stained glass window depicting a vibrant tapestry of fruits and vegetables, painted shifting patterns across the meticulously organized (yet slightly chaotic) workspace.  Stacks of research papers, overflowing recipe journals, and vibrant, hand-drawn charts of nutritional profiles cluttered her desk.  Amelia, a nutritionist with a passion as fiery as the cayenne pepper she often sprinkled into her turmeric smoothies, sighed.  Another late night.  Another mountain of patient data to analyze.  Today's case, a young man named Leo who insisted his body was "fighting him," was proving particularly frustrating.
