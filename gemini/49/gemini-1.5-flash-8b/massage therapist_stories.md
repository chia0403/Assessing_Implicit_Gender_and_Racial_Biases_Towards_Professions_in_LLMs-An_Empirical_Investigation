Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of "The Tranquil Touch," a small, unassuming massage parlour nestled in the shadowed alleyways between the towering apartment blocks of the city’s industrial heart.  The air hung thick and damp, the scent of jasmine from a neglected plant outside mingling with the metallic tang of the nearby factory.  Inside, bathed in the soft glow of warm amber lights, lay Amelia Nightingale, her brow furrowed in concentration.  She was a massage therapist with a reputation for precision, a touch as gentle as a falling feather, yet as firm as a sculptor’s hand. Tonight, though, the rhythm of the rain outside echoed a different kind of storm within her.
