The flickering fluorescent lights of the "Apothecary's Corner" cast long, skeletal shadows across the shelves crammed with jars, vials, and boxes.  Dust motes danced in the solitary shaft of afternoon sunlight that pierced the grime-coated windowpane, illuminating a faint sheen of moisture clinging to the polished counter.  This was Amelia Nightingale's sanctuary, her battlefield, and her meticulously organized chaos.  Amelia Nightingale, proprietor and pharmacist of the unassuming, yet surprisingly popular, little shop nestled on the backstreets of Blackwood, sighed, pushing a stray strand of auburn hair behind her ear.  The rhythmic *clink* of small metal tools on glass mingled with the low hum of the ancient refrigeration unit, a soundtrack to her daily life.  Blackwood was a city steeped in whispers and shadows, a place where the boundaries between reality and the surreal seemed remarkably thin.  A single, almost imperceptible chill seeped from the corner near the back, a constant reminder of the lingering cold that seemed to cling to the cobbled alleyways, despite the late-spring sun.  It was a chill that always seemed to accompany the murmurings of the city's unusual clientele.
