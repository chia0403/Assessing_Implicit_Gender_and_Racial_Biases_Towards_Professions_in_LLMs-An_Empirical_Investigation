The chipped Formica table, stained a dull beige from years of spilled coffee and anxiety-induced tears, felt oddly cold beneath Dr. Eleanor Vance’s hand.  Sunlight, fractured by the grime-coated windowpanes of the cramped office, cast long, dancing shadows across the room, highlighting the dust motes swirling in the air.  This was her sanctuary, or at least, it had been.  Now, the quiet hum of the fluorescent lights seemed to mock her, a relentless metronome counting down to something… she couldn’t quite name.  Outside, the bustling city of New Verona thrummed with a restless energy, a stark contrast to the stagnant feeling that clung to the air within her small, second-floor office.  Eleanor, a counseling psychologist with a reputation for understanding the intricate dance of human emotion, felt the familiar knot tighten in her stomach. Today's session with 17-year-old Leo Romero was shaping up to be anything but routine.
