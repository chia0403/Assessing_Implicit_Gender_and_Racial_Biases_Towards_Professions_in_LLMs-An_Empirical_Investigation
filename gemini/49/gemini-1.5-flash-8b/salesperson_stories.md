The fluorescent lights of the "Everything You Need for a Perfect Patio" store hummed, a relentless counterpoint to the rhythmic click-clack of Amelia Finch's high heels on the polished concrete floor.  Dust motes danced in the artificial sunlight, illuminating the meticulously arranged display of Adirondack chairs, fire pits, and outdoor rugs.  Amelia, a seasoned salesperson with a smile that could charm a hummingbird out of a hibiscus flower, surveyed the store with practiced efficiency.  Today, the quiet hum of the afternoon was punctuated by the insistent buzz of a particularly frantic bee trapped inside a glass terrarium filled with exotic orchids, a tiny prisoner amongst the exotic flora and fauna of the patio section.
