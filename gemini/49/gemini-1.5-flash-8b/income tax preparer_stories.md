The fluorescent lights of the "Tax Relief Express" hummed, a monotonous soundtrack to Amelia Butterfield's life.  Paperclips clicked, pens scratched across tax forms, and the muffled murmur of frustrated clients drifted through the air, a familiar symphony of late April.  Amelia, a tax preparer with a perpetually tired smile and eyes that held the weariness of a thousand 1040s, hunched over her desk.  Outside, the spring rain lashed against the grimy storefront windows, mirroring the deluge of paperwork on her desk.  Her tiny office, a cramped space nestled within the bustling downtown corridor, reeked of stale coffee and desperation.  Empty pizza boxes and discarded receipts littered the corners, testament to countless late nights spent deciphering complex deductions and soothing stressed-out taxpayers. This was the rhythm of Amelia's life: a relentless dance with tax code, a steady march towards the impending deadline.  Today, however, a new rhythm was beginning, one that felt less predictable, and far more...urgent.
