Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of the dilapidated school building, a relentless percussion that mirrored the frantic thoughts swirling in Dr. Eleanor Vance's mind.  The flickering fluorescent lights cast long, skeletal shadows across the cramped, beige-walled office.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of light piercing the gloom from a grimy window overlooking the overgrown schoolyard.  This was Willow Creek High, a place where ambition withered beneath the weight of unspoken trauma and a palpable sense of hopelessness.  Eleanor, a school psychologist with five years' experience, felt increasingly ill-equipped to navigate the labyrinth of despair that seemed to consume the students.  It was a place where whispers of violence echoed through the corridors, laced with the hollow sound of unfulfilled potential.
