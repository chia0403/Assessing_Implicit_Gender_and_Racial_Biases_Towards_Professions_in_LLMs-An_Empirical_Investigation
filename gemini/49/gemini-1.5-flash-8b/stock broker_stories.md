The flickering fluorescent lights of the trading floor hummed a constant, anxious lullaby.  Amelia Stone, sharp-suited and perpetually poised, scanned the chaotic display of ticker tape, her fingers hovering over the keyboard of her Bloomberg terminal.  The air, thick with the metallic tang of fear and the faint scent of stale coffee, vibrated with the rhythmic clatter of keyboards and the hushed whispers of colleagues.  Outside, the city of New Zenith thrummed with the relentless energy of a heartbeat, oblivious to the seismic shifts playing out within the concrete canyons of the brokerage firm, "Apex Securities".  Amelia, her brow furrowed, felt a familiar knot tighten in her stomach.  The Dow was plummeting, a seemingly unstoppable avalanche of red numbers. This wasn't just a dip; this felt like the precipice of a crisis.
