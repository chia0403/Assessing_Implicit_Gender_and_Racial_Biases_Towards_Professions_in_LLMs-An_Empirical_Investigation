The chipped Formica countertop, stained a perpetual beige from spilled coffee and countless forgotten crumbs, reflected the fluorescent hum of the Occupational Therapy department at Oakhaven Rehabilitation Center.  Rain hammered against the windows, a relentless percussion that echoed the rhythmic clicking of Amelia "Amy"  Rosenthal's keyboard as she documented her latest patient's progress.  Amy, 32, with a tangle of dark brown hair perpetually escaping its usual neat braid, hunched over the file, a tired furrow etched between her brows. The scent of antiseptic and faint lavender clung to the air, a comforting, yet somewhat suffocating, aroma in this sterile haven for healing.  Outside, the grey sky mirrored the dull ache in her own weary shoulders.  Today's session with Mr. Henderson had been particularly challenging.
