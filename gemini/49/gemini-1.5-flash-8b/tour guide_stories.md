The humid air hung heavy over the cobbled streets of Seville, clinging to the ornate balconies overflowing with bougainvillea and the scent of roasting chestnuts.  Dust motes danced in the weak afternoon sun, illuminating the intricate carvings of the Alcázar's facade.  Isabella "Isa" Rodriguez, her voice a melodic counterpoint to the city’s vibrant pulse, adjusted her worn leather satchel.  She surveyed the small, expectant group gathered around her – a mix of tourists, their faces a tapestry of bewildered wonder and slightly bored detachment.  Isa, a Seville native with a passion for her city that burned brighter than the flamenco dancer's castanets, was on the cusp of a new tour.  This one, she felt, would be different.  This one would reveal a hidden heart.  This one would be worth the faded rose-colored memories she'd carried since the day her grandmother had whispered stories of the Alcázar's secret passageways to her as a child.
