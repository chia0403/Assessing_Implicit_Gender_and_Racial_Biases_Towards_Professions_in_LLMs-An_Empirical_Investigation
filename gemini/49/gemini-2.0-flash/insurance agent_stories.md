The fluorescent lights of the Miller & Sons Insurance Agency hummed a monotonous tune, a soundtrack to the life of Bartholomew "Bart" Finch. Bart adjusted his tie, a paisley disaster his Aunt Mildred swore was "making a comeback," and peered through the dusty blinds. Outside, the Tuesday morning drizzle had begun its relentless assault on Harmony Creek, Ohio, turning Main Street into a shimmering, grey river. The bell above the door jingled, signaling a customer, and Bart pasted on his most reassuring smile, ready to face another day of mitigating risks and selling peace of mind, one policy at a time.
