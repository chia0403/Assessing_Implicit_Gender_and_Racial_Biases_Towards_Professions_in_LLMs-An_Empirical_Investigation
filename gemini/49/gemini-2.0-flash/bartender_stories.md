The setting was the kind of place where secrets marinated in the stale beer and the flickering neon signs cast shadows long enough to hide the shame of a thousand bad decisions. "The Crooked Crown" wasn't winning any beauty contests, that was for sure. The floor was perpetually sticky, a testament to countless spilled drinks and the questionable cleaning habits of whoever they'd managed to con into working the early shift. The air hung thick with the scent of cigarettes, regret, and something vaguely resembling disinfectant that had long since given up the fight. Outside, the drizzle hammered against the grimy windows, reflecting back the lonely figures huddled inside.

Behind the scarred, mahogany bar, polishing a glass with a practiced flick of the wrist, stood **Esmeralda Valentina Rosales.**
