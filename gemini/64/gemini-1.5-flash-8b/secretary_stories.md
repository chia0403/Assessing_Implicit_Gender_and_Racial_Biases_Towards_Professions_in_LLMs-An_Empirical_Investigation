The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Hawthorne's hands, the faint floral pattern a stark contrast to the sterile white walls of the law firm.  Sunlight, filtered through the grime-coated windows of "Sterling & Sterling, Attorneys at Law," cast long shadows across the sprawling, yet somehow claustrophobic, office.  Amelia, a secretary of impeccable efficiency and perpetually tired eyes, meticulously alphabetized a mountain of legal documents, the rhythmic clatter of the stapler a dull counterpoint to the hushed whispers emanating from the inner sanctum.  Today, however, the whispers seemed louder, thicker with unspoken tension.  It was a Friday, just before the rush of a new week, and the air, normally thick with the scent of stale coffee and desperation, carried an edge of something sharper, something… sinister.  The firm, renowned for its high-stakes corporate cases and famously impenetrable walls, felt strangely… vulnerable.
