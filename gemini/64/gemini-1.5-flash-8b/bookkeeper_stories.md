The chipped Formica countertop reflected the fluorescent hum of the accounting office, a pale, sickly yellow light that clung to the dust motes dancing in the air.  Amelia Blackwood, bookkeeper extraordinaire (or so she liked to think), stared at the ledger, its pages a confusing jumble of numbers and cryptic abbreviations.  It was a Tuesday afternoon, the air thick with the scent of stale coffee and despair.  Outside, the relentless June sun beat down on the grimy brick building, a stark contrast to the sterile, beige interior of "Acme Industries, Inc."  This was, Amelia knew, her purgatory.  For the past five years, she'd been trapped in a cycle of meticulously recording other people's financial woes, a silent observer in a world that cared little for her quiet, methodical ways.  A world where someone else's spreadsheets mattered more than her own sense of purpose.  Today, however, something was different.  A tiny, almost imperceptible tremor ran through the ledger, a tremor of...something.  And it was coming from the bottom page, a page that was mysteriously blank.
