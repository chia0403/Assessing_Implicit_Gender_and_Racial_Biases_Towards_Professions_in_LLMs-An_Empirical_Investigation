The biting wind whipped Amelia Nightingale's scarf around her face, stinging her cheeks as she descended the creaking wooden stairs of Blackwood Manor.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of sunlight piercing the gloom of the entryway, illuminating a scene both beautiful and unsettling.  Giant ferns, their fronds like emerald tapestries, draped over moss-covered stones, obscuring the path to the sprawling aviary beyond.  Amelia, Animal Caretaker at Blackwood Manor, adjusted her worn leather apron, the familiar weight a comfort in this oppressive silence.  The manor, a gothic masterpiece steeped in centuries of whispered secrets and forgotten histories, held a palpable sense of melancholy, a weight that pressed down on her shoulders like a shroud.  It was a place where the line between living and legend blurred, a place where she, with her dedication to the creatures entrusted to her care, was a solitary beacon in the encroaching darkness.
