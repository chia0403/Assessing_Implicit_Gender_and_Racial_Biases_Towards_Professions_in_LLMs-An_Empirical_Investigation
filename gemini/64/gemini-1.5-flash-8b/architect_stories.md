Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of the dilapidated workshop, a relentless percussion that echoed the frantic rhythm in Elara Blackwood’s head.  The air, thick with the smell of damp earth and linseed oil, clung to her like a second skin.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of light slicing through the gloom, illuminating the skeletal remains of a half-finished model – a sprawling, modernist apartment complex planned for the edge of the city’s forgotten district, a place known only as “The Hollows.”  Elara, architect extraordinaire and resident enigma of the city’s burgeoning architectural scene, traced a weathered finger across the intricate framework of the model.  The Hollows, once a thriving hub of industry, now stood as a silent testament to the city's relentless march forward – a space left behind, forgotten, waiting to be reimagined.
