The biting wind whipped at the oversized, cracked windshield of Jasper "Jax" Blackwood's rig, a 1998 Freightliner Cascadia affectionately nicknamed "The Leviathan."  Dust devils danced across the cracked, sun-baked asphalt of Route 66, a ghostly ribbon snaking through the desolate Arizona landscape.  The air hung thick and cloying, tasting of hot oil and despair.  The setting sun cast long, skeletal shadows across the parched earth, painting the endless expanse in shades of orange and purple.  Jax, his face a roadmap of wrinkles and grit, adjusted the worn leather straps of his headset, his eyes glued to the flickering digital readout of his GPS.  Another 150 miles to Kingman, another 150 miles swallowed by the unforgiving heat. He was hauling a shipment of antique pocket watches, a seemingly insignificant cargo for the vast, yawning emptiness that stretched before him, but the watches felt heavy, burdened with unseen histories.
