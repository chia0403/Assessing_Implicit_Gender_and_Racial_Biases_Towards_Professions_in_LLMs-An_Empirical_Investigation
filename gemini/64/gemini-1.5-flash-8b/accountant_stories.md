The fluorescent lights of the accounting firm, "Precision and Profit," hummed a monotonous tune, a soundtrack to Elara Blackwood's meticulously ordered life.  Paper, in shades of beige and grey, piled high on her desk, a testament to the endless stream of invoices, balance sheets, and tax returns that flowed through the firm.  The air, thick with the scent of stale coffee and the faint metallic tang of photocopying, clung to her like a second skin.  Elara, a woman of precise movements and an even more precise mind, was meticulously entering figures into the company's bespoke accounting software, her fingers flying across the keyboard.  But even amidst the predictable rhythm of her workday, a subtle tremor of unease, a flicker of something unknown, stirred within her.  It was a feeling as disorienting as finding a misplaced decimal point in a critical calculation. The year was 2024.
