The biting wind whipped Amelia Blackwood's tailored trench coat around her legs as she hurried across the cobbled streets of  Old Town.  Crimson leaves, shredded by the relentless autumnal gale, swirled like confetti around her ankles, painting the narrow alleyways in hues of burnt orange and russet.  The scent of roasting chestnuts, a comforting counterpoint to the chill, drifted from a nearby stall, mingling with the pungent aroma of aged wood and damp earth that clung to the ancient stone buildings.  Amelia, a seasoned real estate agent with a reputation for turning even the most dilapidated properties into coveted homes, found herself increasingly frustrated by the lack of available sunlight.  The perpetually overcast sky cast a dreary pall over the historic district, mirroring the mood of the potential buyer she was desperately trying to impress.  This particular property, a 17th-century manor house rumored to be haunted, was proving to be, as usual, a challenge.
