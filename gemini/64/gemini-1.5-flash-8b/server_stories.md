The flickering neon sign of the Rusty Mug cast a sickly yellow glow on Amelia Blackwood's tired face.  Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof, a relentless percussion against the already tense hum of the diner.  Inside, the air hung thick with the smells of stale coffee, burnt sugar, and desperation.  Amelia, a server at the Rusty Mug for the past three years, knew every creak of the ancient wooden floorboards, every chipped ceramic mug, every whispered complaint from the regulars.  Tonight, however, felt different.  A palpable tension hung in the air, heavier than the usual Friday night traffic.  Tonight, something was brewing, and Amelia, with her perpetually weary eyes and her practiced smile, felt it stirring within her.
