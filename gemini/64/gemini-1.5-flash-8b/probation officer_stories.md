Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of the probation office, a relentless drumming that mirrored the rhythm of Amelia Hayes's thoughts.  The flickering fluorescent lights cast long, unnerving shadows across the worn, linoleum floor, highlighting the chipped paint and the general air of muted desperation that clung to the room like damp laundry.  Amelia, Probation Officer Amelia Hayes, stared out the grimy window at the skeletal branches of the ancient oak tree, its gnarled fingers clawing at the grey sky.  Outside, the world was a monochrome canvas of rain and gloom, a fitting backdrop for the case file spread open before her.  Today, it was fifteen-year-old Leo Miller, a boy with eyes that held a chilling emptiness despite their vibrant blue, who was making her head spin.
