The scent of burnt sugar and rosemary hung heavy in the air, a bizarre perfume clinging to the damp November chill that seeped through the cracked windowpanes of "Shear Perfection."  Rain lashed against the glass, blurring the already indistinct neon sign sputtering above the doorway. Inside, amidst the clutter of half-used hairspray cans, stray bobby pins, and the ghostly scent of a thousand different shampoos, sat Seraphina "Sera"  Delacroix, her fingers delicately working through a tangled mess of auburn curls.  Sera, with her own raven hair pulled back in a severe bun that betrayed the vibrant chaos of her salon, sighed. This wasn't the glamorous life of a high-end stylist she'd envisioned when she'd graduated top of her class at the Vidal Sassoon Academy.  No, Shear Perfection, nestled in the forgotten corner of a rain-soaked alleyway in Grimsborough, was far from perfection, but it was hers. And tonight, it was almost empty.
