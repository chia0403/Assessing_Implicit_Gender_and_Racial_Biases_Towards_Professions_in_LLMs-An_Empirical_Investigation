The biting Alaskan wind howled a mournful song, whipping snow into frenzied spirals around Captain Evelyn "Evie" Reed's battered Cessna 185.  The skeletal branches of the spruce trees clawed at the bruised twilight sky, their silhouettes stark against the bruised purple and fading orange of the setting sun. Below, the frozen expanse of Lake Iliamna stretched out like a fractured mirror, reflecting the stormy heavens with an icy sheen.  Evie shivered, not entirely from the cold. A knot of unease, tighter than the straps of her worn leather flight jacket, constricted her chest. This wasn't just another charter flight; this was a rescue mission, and the clock was ticking down faster than the mercury in her altimeter.  The radio crackled with static, a jarring counterpoint to the rhythmic thrum of the single engine, a desperate heartbeat in the vast, unforgiving wilderness.
