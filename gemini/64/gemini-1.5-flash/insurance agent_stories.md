The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Hernandez’s hands, a meager comfort against the November chill that seeped through the drafty windows of her office.  Rain lashed against the panes, mirroring the turmoil churning inside her.  Amelia, a woman whose life was usually meticulously organized – down to the color-coded files lining her shelves – felt utterly adrift.  Her small, independent insurance agency, “Hernandez & Associates,” nestled on the second floor of a crumbling Victorian building on the edge of Oakhaven’s historic district, usually hummed with a quiet efficiency. Today, however, it was eerily silent, the only sound the relentless drumming of rain and the low thrum of her anxieties.  The scent of stale coffee and old paper hung heavy in the air, a fitting backdrop to the looming shadow of her biggest challenge yet: a claim so bizarre, so utterly unbelievable, it threatened to unravel everything she’d worked for.
