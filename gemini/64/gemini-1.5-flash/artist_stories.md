The salt spray stung Elara Vance's face, a familiar kiss from the unforgiving Atlantic.  Her easel, perched precariously on the wind-battered cliffs of Cornwall, groaned a mournful protest against the gale.  The canvas, a stark white rectangle against the tumultuous grey of the ocean, seemed to mock her with its blankness.  Elara, however, felt no frustration, only a deep, simmering anticipation.  The relentless waves crashing against the jagged rocks below weren't chaos; they were a symphony of colour waiting to be captured, a tempestuous ballet she intended to translate onto her canvas, a testament to the untamed beauty of the Cornish coast.  The air, thick with the scent of brine and seaweed, vibrated with the raw energy of the storm, mirroring the tempestuous emotions churning within her own soul.
