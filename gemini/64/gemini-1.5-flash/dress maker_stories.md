The scent of lavender and aged linen hung heavy in the air, a familiar perfume that clung to the dusty velvet curtains and the worn wooden floors of  "Stitches & Dreams," a quaint dressmaking shop nestled on a cobbled side street in the heart of Prague's Old Town.  Rain lashed against the leaded panes, mirroring the tempest brewing inside  Magdalena Kovářová.  Magdalena, with her nimble fingers stained a delicate lilac from years spent coaxing silk and satin into breathtaking gowns, stared at the half-finished wedding dress draped across her tailor's dummy.  It was a masterpiece, a cascade of ivory lace and shimmering pearls, but the intricate beadwork felt as heavy as the storm outside, a stark contrast to the lightness of the bride's hopeful heart – a heart Magdalena feared might soon be broken.
