The biting November wind whipped Dr. Eleanor Vance’s auburn hair across her face as she surveyed the scene.  Rain, a relentless, icy drizzle, plastered her trench coat to her skin, mirroring the grim dampness clinging to the sprawling Victorian mansion before her.  Blackwell Manor, they called it – a gothic monstrosity perched precariously on a cliff overlooking the churning grey expanse of the North Sea.  Its once-proud turrets were now shrouded in mist, their gables skeletal against the bruised twilight sky.  The air hung heavy with the scent of brine and something else… something subtly acrid, like burnt sugar and decay, that snagged at the back of Eleanor’s throat.  This wasn't just a crime scene; it was a mausoleum waiting to exhale its secrets.
