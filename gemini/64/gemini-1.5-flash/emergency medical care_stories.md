The biting November wind whipped Amelia Hernandez's dark hair across her face as she wrestled the heavy stretcher into the back of the ambulance. Rain, slick and relentless, plastered her already damp scrubs to her skin.  The flickering neon sign of the "Rusty Mug" tavern cast a lurid glow on the glistening street, reflecting in the puddles that mirrored the city’s grim underbelly.  This wasn’t the sterile, controlled environment of County General; this was the wild, unpredictable frontier of downtown Providence's emergency medical services, a place where the sirens were a constant, mournful soundtrack to the city's suffering. Tonight, the symphony was particularly chaotic.
