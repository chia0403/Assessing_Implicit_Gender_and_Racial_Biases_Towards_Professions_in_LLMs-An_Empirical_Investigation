The salt spray stung Amelia Hernandez's face as she wrestled the stubborn sailboat's tiller.  The late afternoon sun, a bruised plum sinking towards the horizon, cast long shadows across the choppy waters of the San Juan Bay.  Amelia, a seasoned occupational therapist with calloused hands that belied her otherwise delicate features, ignored the aching in her arms.  The rhythmic creak of the mast and the slap of waves against the hull were a welcome counterpoint to the relentless internal monologue that had plagued her all day.  The case of eight-year-old Mateo Rodriguez, with his bafflingly slow progress and increasingly withdrawn demeanor, had left her feeling as adrift as the little sailboat she was currently navigating. The air, thick with the scent of sea-salt and hibiscus, offered little solace.  Tonight, she needed the vast, indifferent ocean more than the sterile, predictable confines of her clinic.
