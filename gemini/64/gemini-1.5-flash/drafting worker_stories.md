The biting November wind whipped Amelia Katherine Finch's hair across her face as she hurried down the cobbled street, the scent of woodsmoke and damp earth clinging to her threadbare coat.  Rain threatened, a grey, greasy smear across the bruised purple sky mirroring the exhaustion etched onto Amelia's own face.  Her destination: the sprawling, soot-stained brick edifice of  Messrs. Grimshaw & Sons Architectural Drafting, a place where dreams were meticulously rendered in ink and tracing paper, and where Amelia, a highly skilled but perpetually underpaid drafting worker, spent twelve grueling hours a day chasing those dreams into tangible form.  The gas lamps flickered feebly, casting long, dancing shadows that seemed to mock her weary gait as she wrestled the heavy portfolio under her arm, its contents – meticulously drawn plans for a new orphanage – precious cargo in the relentless city of Birmingham, 1888.
