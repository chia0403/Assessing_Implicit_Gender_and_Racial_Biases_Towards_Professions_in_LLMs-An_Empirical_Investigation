The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Holloway’s auburn braid across her face as she hurried down the rain-slicked street.  Water streamed from the brim of her worn, navy blue raincoat, pooling in the puddles that reflected the blurry neon glow of the city's nightlife.  The air hung heavy with the scent of exhaust fumes and damp concrete, a familiar olfactory landscape to Amelia, who had navigated these streets countless times in her twelve years as a registered nurse at St. Jude’s Memorial Hospital.  Tonight, however, the usual urban symphony felt amplified, each car horn and distant siren a jarring note in the symphony of her already frayed nerves.  She was late, dreadfully late, and the gnawing anxiety in her stomach told her this wasn't just a case of misjudged traffic.  Something felt… wrong.  The usual comforting hum of the city’s rhythm had been replaced by an unsettling, almost imperceptible tremor.
