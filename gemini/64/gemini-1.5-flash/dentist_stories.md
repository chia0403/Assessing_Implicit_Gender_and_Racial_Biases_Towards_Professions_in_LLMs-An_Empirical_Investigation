The biting November wind whipped Dr. Eleanor Vance’s dark hair across her face as she wrestled the recalcitrant key into the lock of her clinic.  The small, brick building, nestled incongruously between a bustling bakery and a dilapidated antique shop on Bleecker Street, groaned a protest under the gusts.  Inside, the air hung heavy with the scent of antiseptic and something faintly floral, a desperate attempt to mask the lingering aroma of coffee and fear that clung to the waiting room’s plush, but worn, emerald green chairs.  Eleanor shivered, pulling her worn wool coat tighter.  It wasn't just the cold; a nervous energy, a prickling premonition, settled on her like the late afternoon gloom gathering outside. This wasn't just another Tuesday at Vance Dental.  This was the day Mr. Silas Blackwood's appointment had finally arrived.
