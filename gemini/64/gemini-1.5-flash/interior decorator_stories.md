The scent of aged wood and dust motes dancing in the afternoon sun hung heavy in the air, a stark contrast to the vibrant fuchsia bougainvillea spilling over the crumbling wrought-iron balcony.  Isabelle Moreau, renowned for her ability to coax magic from even the most dilapidated spaces, stood poised on the threshold of Villa Paradiso, a once-grand Italianate mansion now sadly succumbing to the relentless march of time.  Ivy, thick as a man's arm, clawed at the faded stucco, obscuring intricate carvings that whispered of a bygone era of opulence.  The air thrummed with the cicadas' insistent song, a soundtrack to Isabelle's silent assessment.  This wasn't just a renovation; it was a resurrection.
