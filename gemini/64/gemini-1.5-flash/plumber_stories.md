The wrench slipped, a metallic shriek echoing through the oppressive silence of the abandoned Blackwood Manor.  Dust motes, disturbed by the jarring sound, danced in the single shaft of moonlight slicing through a grimy windowpane.  A chill, far colder than the autumn night outside, snaked down Archibald "Archie" Fitzwilliam's spine.  Archie, a plumber whose clientele usually consisted of disgruntled housewives with leaky faucets, not haunted mansions, adjusted his headlamp, the beam cutting a swathe through the cobweb-draped grandeur of the hallway.  The air hung thick with the scent of damp earth and something else… something acrid, metallic, and undeniably…wrong.
