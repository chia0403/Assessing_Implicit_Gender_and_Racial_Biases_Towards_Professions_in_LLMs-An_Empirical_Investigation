The chipped paint of the windowsill dug into Dr. Eleanor Vance’s elbow as she watched the late afternoon sun bleed crimson and gold across the manicured lawns of Northwood High.  October in Ashwood, Oregon, always held a melancholic beauty, a stark contrast to the frenetic energy simmering just beyond the glass – the chaotic ebb and flow of teenagers navigating the hallways, their anxieties as palpable as the crisp autumn air.  Inside, the fluorescent lights hummed a dull counterpoint to the rustling whispers and the occasional, muffled shout that drifted from the crowded corridors.  Eleanor, a school psychologist with twenty years of experience etched onto her face in the fine lines around her eyes, sighed. Another day, another deluge of adolescent turmoil.  Tonight, however, felt different. A low thrum of unease vibrated through the usually predictable rhythm of Northwood High, a discordant note in the familiar symphony of teenage angst.
