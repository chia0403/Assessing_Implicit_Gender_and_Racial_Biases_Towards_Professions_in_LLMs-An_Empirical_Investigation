The chipped ceramic mug warmed Eleanor Vance’s hands, the lukewarm chamomile tea doing little to soothe the knot of anxiety tightening in her stomach.  Rain lashed against the panoramic window of her office, blurring the already hazy view of the sprawling Redwood High campus.  Each gust of wind seemed to howl a mournful symphony, mirroring the silent cries for help she often heard, but couldn't always decipher, behind the closed doors of her small, cluttered office.  The air hung heavy with the scent of old textbooks and the lingering ghost of teenage angst – a familiar perfume in the hallowed halls of the guidance counselor's suite.  Today, however, the usual hum of adolescent drama felt amplified, charged with a palpable sense of unease that even the comforting aroma of her lavender-scented desk blotter couldn't quite dispel.
