The sterile scent of ozone hung heavy in the air, a familiar perfume to Amelia Grace Holloway, though tonight it did little to mask the metallic tang of fear clinging to the polished chrome of the dental chair.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of Aspen Dental, blurring the already indistinct cityscape of Denver into a hazy watercolour.  Amelia, usually a picture of calm efficiency in her crisp white scrubs, felt a prickle of unease crawl up her spine.  It wasn't the late hour – she'd volunteered for the extra shift to catch up on paperwork – nor the relentless drumming of the rain.  It was the patient.  Mr. Silas Blackwood, scheduled for a routine cleaning, had arrived an hour late, his face obscured by the shadow of a wide-brimmed hat, his eyes – the only visible feature – burning with an unsettling intensity.
