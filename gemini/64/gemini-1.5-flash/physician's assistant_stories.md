The biting November wind whipped Amelia Hernandez’s dark hair across her face as she hurried down the slick cobblestone street.  The scent of woodsmoke and roasting chestnuts, usually comforting, did little to alleviate the icy knot of anxiety tightening in her stomach. Rain, a relentless drizzle that had been falling for days, plastered her charcoal-grey coat to her back. Above, the gothic spires of St. Jude’s Cathedral loomed, their stone faces etched with the shadows of centuries, mirroring the grim determination etched on Amelia’s own.  She was late, hopelessly late, for her shift at the city’s oldest and most notorious hospital, St. Jude’s itself, a place where the line between life and death felt alarmingly thin, especially on a night like this.  The flickering gas lamps cast long, dancing shadows, transforming the familiar streets of Old Edinburgh into a labyrinth of foreboding.
