The biting wind whipped at Elias Thorne's thick wool coat, carrying the scent of pine and damp earth.  It was the kind of wind that cut through layers, finding purchase in the marrow of your bones. Elias, his face weathered like ancient bark itself, squinted against the grey, pre-dawn light filtering through the towering Douglas firs of the Cascade Mountains.  His breath plumed out in ragged white clouds, quickly dissipating into the frigid air.  The only sound louder than the wind was the rhythmic crunch of his boots on the frozen snow, each step a measured beat against the symphony of the wilderness.  He was deep in the heart of the Gifford Pinchot National Forest, miles from the nearest road, a solitary figure swallowed by the immensity of the ancient woods, ready to begin another day's work felling giants.
