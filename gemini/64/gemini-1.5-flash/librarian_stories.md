The scent of aging paper and leather, a perfume both comforting and faintly melancholic, clung to the air in the hushed stacks of the Grandchester Public Library.  Dust motes, illuminated by the afternoon sun slanting through the tall, arched windows, danced in the stillness.  Eleanor Vance, a woman whose life had been quietly unfolding amidst these very shelves for the last twenty-five years, meticulously straightened a row of oversized folios on the bottom shelf, her fingers tracing the raised lettering of their gilded spines. The silence, usually her solace, felt heavy today, pregnant with an unspoken tension that vibrated from the very floorboards beneath her worn leather shoes.  Outside, a storm was brewing, the wind rattling the ancient panes of glass like a restless spirit, mirroring the unease that had settled deep within Eleanor's own heart.
