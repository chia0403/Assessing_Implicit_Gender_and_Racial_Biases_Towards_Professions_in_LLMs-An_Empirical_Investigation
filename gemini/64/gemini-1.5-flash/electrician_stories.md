The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Amy” Hernandez’s hair across her face as she wrestled with a stubborn conduit fitting.  Her gloved fingers, already stiff with cold, fumbled with the wrench, the metallic tang of rain mixing with the acrid smell of ozone in the air.  Above, the skeletal frame of the unfinished lighthouse loomed against a bruised, twilight sky, its unfinished stone a stark contrast to the churning grey sea that crashed against the rocky outcrop below.  Amy, perched precariously on a rickety scaffolding platform bolted to the exterior wall, cursed softly under her breath.  The salty spray stung her eyes, blurring her vision as she attempted, once more, to secure the conduit.  This was no ordinary electrical job; this was the final, treacherous phase of electrifying the infamous Widow's Peak Lighthouse, a structure notorious for swallowing both men and their dreams whole.
