The rain hammered against the panoramic windows of Alistair Finch's office, a relentless percussion accompanying the even more relentless drip, drip, drip of his dwindling optimism.  Alistair Finch, Jr.,  a man whose tailored suits usually reflected the bullish market he navigated with ruthless efficiency, felt the damp chill seeping into him, mirroring the icy dread that had settled in his gut.  From his 47th-floor perch in the gleaming glass skyscraper of the Century Tower, the city sprawled below, a glittering labyrinth of ambition and avarice.  Tonight, however, the usual hypnotic beauty of Manhattan was lost in the blurry grey wash of the storm, a fitting backdrop to the tempest brewing in Alistair's meticulously organized life.  His mahogany desk, usually cluttered with financial reports, sat surprisingly clear, save for a single, wilting orchid and a half-empty glass of amber liquid that smelled suspiciously of desperation.
