The chipped porcelain sink wept rusty water as Seraphina "Sera" Nightingale rinsed the last of the blackberry stain from her fingers.  The air in her tiny, attic apartment hung thick with the scent of damp plaster, old paint, and the faint, lingering sweetness of the jam she’d been making – a desperate attempt to distract herself from the gnawing anxiety that had settled in her stomach like a lead weight.  Rain lashed against the single, grime-streaked windowpane, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her.  Outside, the cobbled streets of Prague’s Žižkov district echoed with the muffled sounds of a city waking, but Sera felt utterly isolated, a lone star extinguished in a vast, indifferent sky.  Her upcoming audition – a chance to sing at the prestigious Rudolfinum – loomed, a monstrous shadow threatening to swallow her whole.
