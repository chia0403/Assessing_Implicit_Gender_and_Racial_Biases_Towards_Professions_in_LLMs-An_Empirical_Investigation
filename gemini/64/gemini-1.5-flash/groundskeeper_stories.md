The first frost of autumn had kissed the manicured lawns of Blackwood Manor, transforming the emerald blades into a shimmering tapestry of jade and silver.  A thin veil of mist clung to the ancient oaks, their leaves a fiery mosaic of red and gold, their branches gnarled and reaching like skeletal fingers towards a pewter sky.  Elias Thorne, Blackwood Manor's groundskeeper, breathed it in, the crisp air stinging his lungs with a familiar comfort.  For forty years, Elias had watched the seasons unfurl and retreat across this sprawling estate, a silent observer to the comings and goings of its inhabitants, their triumphs and tragedies reflected in the changing hues of the landscape. Today, however, felt different. A subtle unease, like the premonition of a storm, settled in his bones, as heavy and chilling as the morning mist itself.
