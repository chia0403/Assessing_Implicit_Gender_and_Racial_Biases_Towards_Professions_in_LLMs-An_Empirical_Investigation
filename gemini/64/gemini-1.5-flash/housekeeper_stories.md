The chipped porcelain sink groaned under the weight of Elara Vance's elbows as she scrubbed at the stubborn crust of burnt sugar clinging to the bottom of Lord Ashworth's favorite casserole dish.  Elara, a woman whose life was measured in the rhythmic swish of a mop and the satisfying clink of polished silverware, sighed. The air in Ashworth Manor hung thick and heavy with the scent of damp stone and woodsmoke, a familiar perfume that had permeated her senses for the past fifteen years.  Outside, the November wind howled a mournful ballad against the ancient, ivy-clad walls, rattling the leaded panes of the kitchen windows.  Rain lashed against the glass, mirroring the relentless rhythm of her scrubbing, a constant reminder of the ceaseless tasks that defined her existence within these hallowed, yet increasingly unsettling, halls.
