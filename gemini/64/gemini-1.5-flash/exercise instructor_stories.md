The stale air hung thick with the scent of sweat and desperation, a familiar perfume to Seraphina “Sera” Bellweather.  Fluorescent lights hummed a monotonous tune above the mirrored walls of "Body Blitz," a gym tucked away in the grittier side of downtown Oakland.  Rain lashed against the grime-coated windows, a relentless percussion accompanying the rhythmic thud of twenty-something bodies relentlessly attacking treadmills and stationary bikes.  Sera, perched on a high stool near the entrance, surveyed her domain with a practiced eye, her sleek, dark ponytail swinging in time with the relentless beat of the pop music blasting from the gym's speakers.  Tonight's 7pm Body Sculpt class was particularly brutal – a mix of Pilates, strength training, and cardio designed to push even the most seasoned fitness enthusiasts to their limits. And tonight, they were all about to find out just how far that limit truly stretched.
