The salt spray stung Aurelia Blackwood's face, a familiar kiss from the unforgiving Atlantic.  She perched precariously on the jagged rocks, the wind whipping her crimson scarf around her like a frantic bird. Below, the churning grey sea gnawed at the cliffs of Cornwall, a relentless sculptor forever reshaping the coastline.  The air, thick with the scent of brine and seaweed, carried the mournful cry of gulls.  Aurelia, however, was oblivious to the elemental drama unfolding around her; her eyes, the same shade of stormy grey as the ocean, were fixed on the canvas propped against a weathered boulder.  The half-finished painting, a chaotic swirl of blues and whites capturing the tempestuous energy of the sea, reflected the turmoil within her own soul.  This secluded cove, usually a haven of peace, felt anything but today.  The weight of a long-unspoken truth pressed down on her, heavy as the granite cliffs themselves.
