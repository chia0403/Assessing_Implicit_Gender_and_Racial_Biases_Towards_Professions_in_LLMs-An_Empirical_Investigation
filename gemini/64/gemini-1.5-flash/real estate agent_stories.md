The salt-laced wind whipped Amelia “Mia” Hernandez’s auburn hair across her face as she wrestled the “For Sale” sign into the frozen earth.  The late November sun, a pale disc sinking behind the jagged peaks of the Olympic Mountains, cast long, skeletal shadows across the windswept bluff overlooking the churning grey expanse of the Pacific Ocean.  This wasn’t exactly the postcard-perfect view that usually graced her brochures, but then again, this wasn’t exactly your typical beachfront property.  The ramshackle cabin, perched precariously on the cliff edge, looked more like it belonged in a haunted house movie than a high-end real estate listing.  But Mia Hernandez, owner and operator of Hernandez Coastal Properties, had a feeling this weathered piece of Pacific Northwest history held a secret – a secret that might just make her the most successful real estate agent in Port Angeles.
