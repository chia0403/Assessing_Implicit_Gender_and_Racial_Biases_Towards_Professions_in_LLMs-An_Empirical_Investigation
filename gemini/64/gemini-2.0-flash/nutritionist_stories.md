The aroma of roasted squash and cinnamon hung thick in the air, a stark contrast to the dreary November drizzle clinging to the windows of "Nourish & Flourish," Amelia Eleanor Hawthorne's cozy nutrition practice nestled in the heart of Beacon Hill, Boston. Sunlight, weak and watery, barely penetrated the antique lace curtains, casting long, dancing shadows across the exposed brick wall adorned with framed botanical prints. Amelia, perched on a worn velvet chaise lounge, sipped lukewarm chamomile tea, the mug warming her numb fingers. She reread the intake form clutched in her hand, the scrawled answers barely decipherable, a familiar pang of apprehension twisting in her stomach. Her last appointment of the day, a Mr. Bartholomew Finch, was, according to the secretary's notes, "a bit...eccentric."
