The biting November wind whipped around Eleanor Vance as she hurried across the cracked asphalt of the Northwood High parking lot, her arms laden with graded essays and a thermos of lukewarm tea.  Sleet, the unwelcome harbinger of a Buffalo winter, began to prick her cheeks. Above her, the school loomed, a hulking brick edifice that had seen better days.  Ivy, tenacious and clinging, crawled up its walls, a verdant contrast to the gray sky and the worn, rust-colored window frames.  Inside, the fluorescent lights hummed a tired song, a soundtrack to the daily grind of teenage angst and algebraic equations.  Eleanor, head of the English department and a veteran of fifteen years at Northwood, braced herself. Another Monday.
