Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of Amelia Vivienne Thorne's 48th-floor office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her.  The cityscape of Chicago glittered below, a chaotic tapestry of blurred red and gold lights reflected in the slick, black surface of her desk.  Amelia, CEO of Thorne Industries, a multinational conglomerate built on her grandfather's legacy of steel and her own sharp business acumen, tapped a perfectly manicured nail against a report detailing a disastrous quarterly loss. The air in her minimalist, steel-and-glass office, usually humming with the low thrum of success, crackled with a dangerous energy.  The crimson leather of her Eames chair creaked as she shifted, her gaze fixed on the storm raging outside, a perfect metaphor for the battle about to begin within the boardroom.
