Rain lashed against the grimy windows of The Daily Clarion's office, mimicking the tempest brewing in Amelia "Amy" Reyes's gut.  The flickering fluorescent lights above buzzed in a counterpoint to the rhythmic drumming of the downpour, casting long, distorted shadows across the cluttered room. Stacks of newspapers, yellowed with age and curling at the edges, threatened to topple from overflowing filing cabinets.  A half-eaten tuna sandwich sat forlornly on Amy's desk, next to a chipped mug that proclaimed "World's Okayest Reporter."  Triton City, New Jersey, a once-bustling fishing port now clinging to the tattered edges of its former glory, offered little in the way of glamorous assignments. Tonight, however, was different.  A cryptic, unsigned note had landed on Amy's desk that afternoon, promising a story that would "shake Triton to its core."  And Amy, despite the gnawing unease, couldn't resist a good scoop.
