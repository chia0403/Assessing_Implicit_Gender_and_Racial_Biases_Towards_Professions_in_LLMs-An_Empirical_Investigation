The insistent chirping of the intercom, a shrill counterpoint to the rhythmic whir of the air conditioning, pulled Amelia Grace Hawthorne from her reverie.  She blinked, the hazy image of a sun-drenched Tuscan villa, meticulously clipped hedges and all, dissolving back into the familiar beige monotony of Dr. Alistair Finch’s waiting room.  Sunlight, weak and filtered through the dusty blinds, cast long stripes across the worn floral carpet.  It was a Tuesday in late November in Ashworth, Oregon, a town where the perpetually overcast sky seemed to mirror the perpetually neutral expressions of its inhabitants.  Amelia, however, couldn’t entirely fault them.  Five years spent behind the laminated oak reception desk of Ashworth Family Dentistry had instilled in her a deep appreciation for the calming power of beige and the soothing predictability of routine.  Today, however, something felt…off.
