The pungent aroma of perm solution mingled with the sweet scent of hairspray, creating a unique olfactory cocktail that only a seasoned cosmetologist could appreciate.  Rain lashed against the large picture window of "Shear Perfection," blurring the neon glow of the "Open" sign reflected in the slick asphalt of Magnolia Street. Inside, the salon hummed with the low drone of hair dryers and the rhythmic snip of scissors.  Tuesday afternoons were usually slow, but today, even the regulars seemed to be deterred by the downpour.  Eloise Clementine Dubois, her name embroidered in elegant cursive on her crisp white smock, tapped a perfectly manicured fingernail against the counter, watching the rivulets of water race each other down the glass.  She was contemplating closing early when the bell above the door chimed, announcing a customer braving the storm.
