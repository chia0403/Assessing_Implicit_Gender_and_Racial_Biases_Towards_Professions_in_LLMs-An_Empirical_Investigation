The biting November wind whipped off the East River, stinging Amelia "Amy" Reyes's cheeks as she hurried across the Brooklyn Bridge.  Her laptop bag, heavy with mock-ups for the rebranding of a failing artisanal pickle company (yes, really), thumped against her hip with each stride.  The city lights, a hazy, glittering smear against the bruised twilight sky, normally soothed her, but tonight, they pulsed with the same frenetic energy that thrummed in her own chest.  Amy had exactly two hours to get to DUMBO, finalize the presentation, and somehow convince three vinegar-obsessed entrepreneurs that lime green and millennial pink were the keys to their salvation.  Her studio, a converted spice factory overlooking the Manhattan skyline, was usually a haven of creative chaos, but tonight, it felt less like a sanctuary and more like a pressure cooker.  The scent of stale coffee and desperation hung thick in the air, punctuated by the rhythmic click-clack of her assistant, Mateo, frantically typing away in the adjacent room.
