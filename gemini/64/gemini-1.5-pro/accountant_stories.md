The rain lashed against the window of Amelia Grace Hawthorne's tiny office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her.  Her office, tucked away on the third floor of a squat, brick building overlooking the perpetually grey Manchester Canal, was usually a haven of order.  Neat stacks of invoices lined her desk, meticulously organized by date and client.  Her favourite mug, adorned with a cartoon calculator, sat steaming with Earl Grey tea.  But tonight, even the comforting aroma of bergamot couldn't dispel the gnawing unease that had settled in her stomach.  The rhythmic tap-tap-tapping of the rain against the glass was less a lullaby and more a relentless drumbeat, echoing the unsettling discrepancies she'd discovered buried deep within the accounts of Harrison & Sons Textiles.
