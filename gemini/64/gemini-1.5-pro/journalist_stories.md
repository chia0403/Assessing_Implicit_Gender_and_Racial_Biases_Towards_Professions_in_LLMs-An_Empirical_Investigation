Rain lashed against the grimy windows of The Daily Clarion's office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside Amelia “Mia” Rodriguez.  The flickering fluorescent lights buzzed overhead, casting a sickly yellow glow across the cluttered room.  Stacks of newspapers teetered precariously on every surface, interspersed with half-empty coffee cups and crumpled notes.  Ashton, Kentucky, a town clinging to the banks of the Ohio River like a rusty barge, rarely saw breaking news.  But tonight, Mia, hunched over her battered laptop, felt the tremors of a story that could shake Ashton to its core, and possibly her career along with it.  The anonymous email sitting open on her screen spoke of corruption, cover-ups, and a conspiracy that reached the highest echelons of the town's seemingly benign leadership.
