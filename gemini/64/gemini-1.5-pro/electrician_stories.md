The biting wind whipped across the desolate expanse of the Bonneville Salt Flats, stinging Amelia “Amy” Reyes’s cheeks despite the layers she wore beneath her Carhartt jacket.  The sodium crust crunched under her heavy work boots as she trudged towards the flickering lights of the research station, a lonely outpost in the blinding white.  It was 3:17 am, the sky a bruised purple just hinting at the dawn that was stubbornly refusing to break. Amy, clutching her thermos of lukewarm coffee, cursed the faulty generator that had summoned her out here, miles from civilization, in the pre-dawn chill. The Bonneville Salt Flats International Speedway, usually bustling with speed-hungry racers during the day, was now eerily silent, a landscape of stark beauty and biting cold. Inside the station, a crucial experiment hung in the balance, dependent on the skills of one very tired electrician.
