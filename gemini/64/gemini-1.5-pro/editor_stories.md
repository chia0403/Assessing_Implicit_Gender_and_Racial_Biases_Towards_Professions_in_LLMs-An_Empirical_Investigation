Rain lashed against the attic window, a relentless drumming that mirrored the headache pounding behind Amelia Grace Hawthorne's eyes.  Loose plaster dust sifted down from the ceiling, coating the worn, oak desk like a fine, grey snow.  The only light in the cramped space came from a battered brass lamp, its weak glow barely illuminating the stacks of manuscripts piled haphazardly around her. The air smelled of damp paper, stale coffee, and the faintest hint of lavender, Amelia’s futile attempt to combat the musty odor. Outside, the wind howled through the eaves of the old Victorian house that served as both her home and the headquarters of "Hawthorne House Editing," a small, independent press struggling to stay afloat in the digital age.  Amelia, perched on a creaking chair, chewed on the end of a red pen, staring at the manuscript currently claiming her attention – a sprawling, self-published fantasy epic titled "Chronicles of the Obsidian Wyrm, Part One: The Egg's Awakening."  It was, to put it mildly, a challenge.
