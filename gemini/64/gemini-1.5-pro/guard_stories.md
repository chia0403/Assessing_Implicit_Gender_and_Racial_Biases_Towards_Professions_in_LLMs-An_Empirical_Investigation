The biting wind whipped across the obsidian cliffs of the Nightwatch Citadel, stinging Elias Thorne's exposed cheeks.  He pulled his thick wool cloak tighter around him, the rough fabric scratching against his chainmail. Below, the churning Grey Sea gnawed at the cliff base, a restless, hungry beast.  Elias, his full name Elias Thorne Blackwood, had been standing his lonely vigil for three hours, the only sound the cry of gulls overhead and the rhythmic clang of the Citadel’s weather-beaten bell buoy.  He was one of the Night’s Watch, the last line of defense against the shadows that lurked beyond the known world, and tonight, the shadows felt particularly close.  The air crackled with an unnatural stillness, a silence that spoke volumes of a coming storm, one far more ominous than the gathering clouds on the horizon.
