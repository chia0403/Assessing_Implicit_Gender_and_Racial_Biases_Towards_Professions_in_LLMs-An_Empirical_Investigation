The salt-laced wind whipped Amelia Zephyr Thorne's hair across her face as she stared out at the churning grey expanse of the North Sea.  Her small cottage, perched precariously on the cliffs overlooking the fishing village of Port Blossom, creaked and groaned in sympathy with the gale.  Inside, a half-finished manuscript lay open on her battered writing desk, the words blurring in the flickering lamplight.  November had descended on the Yorkshire coast with a vengeance, bringing with it not just the biting cold but also a stubborn writer's block that had plagued Amelia for weeks.  Port Blossom, usually a haven of inspiration with its quirky inhabitants and dramatic seascapes, felt this year like a cage.  The rhythmic crash of the waves against the rocks, once a comforting lullaby, now echoed the relentless pounding of her own frustrated thoughts.
