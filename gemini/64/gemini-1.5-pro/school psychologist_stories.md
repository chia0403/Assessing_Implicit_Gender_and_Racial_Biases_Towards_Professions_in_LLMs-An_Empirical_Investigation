The biting November wind whipped around Dr. Eleanor Vance as she hurried across the cracked asphalt of the Northwood High School parking lot.  Dead leaves, the color of rust and old pennies, swirled around her ankles.  The sky, a bruised purple-grey, threatened snow.  Inside, the fluorescent lights of the main office buzzed, a stark contrast to the gathering gloom outside. Eleanor, her breath clouding in the sudden warmth, pushed a stray strand of auburn hair from her face and smiled at Mrs. Higgins, the perpetually frazzled school secretary.  Northwood High, a sprawling brick behemoth nestled in the struggling, once-thriving mill town of Havenwood, Pennsylvania, was rarely a beacon of calm, but today, a palpable tension hung in the air, thicker than the smell of stale coffee and disinfectant that usually permeated the office.
