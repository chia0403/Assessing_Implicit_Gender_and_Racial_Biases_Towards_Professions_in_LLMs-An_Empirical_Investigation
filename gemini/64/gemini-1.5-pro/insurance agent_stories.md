Rain lashed against the window of Elias Thorne's cramped office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside him.  Elias Vance Thorne, a man whose meticulously knotted tie and polished shoes belied the gnawing anxiety he carried, stared at the flashing red light on his answering machine.  It blinked accusingly, each pulse a reminder of the mounting missed calls from his increasingly desperate clients.  Outside, the neon glow of the Lucky Clover Pawn Shop across the street cast an eerie green light across the water-streaked asphalt of Elm Street, the main drag of the desolate town of Havenwood, Pennsylvania.  A town that, like Elias's career, seemed perpetually on the verge of going under.  The air hung thick with the smell of wet pavement and despair, a fitting aroma for a town built on the crumbling foundations of a defunct coal mine.
