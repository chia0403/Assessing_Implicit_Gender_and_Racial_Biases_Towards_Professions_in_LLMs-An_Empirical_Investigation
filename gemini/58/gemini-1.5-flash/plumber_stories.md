The biting November wind howled a mournful tune around the crumbling Victorian eaves of 13 Grimalkin Lane, rattling the loose panes of glass in their lead frames like skeletal fingers.  Rain, thick and relentless, lashed against the brickwork, turning the cobblestones into treacherous, glistening rivers.  Inside, the air hung heavy with the cloying sweetness of damp plaster and something else… something vaguely metallic, almost…blood-like.  Bartholomew "Barty" Butterfield, a plumber whose girth suggested a lifelong fondness for sausage rolls and whose face bore the permanent imprint of a perpetually furrowed brow, wrestled a stubborn drainpipe free from its rusted moorings.  The flickering gaslight cast long, dancing shadows that seemed to writhe and twist in the corners of the dilapidated attic, adding to the already unsettling atmosphere.
