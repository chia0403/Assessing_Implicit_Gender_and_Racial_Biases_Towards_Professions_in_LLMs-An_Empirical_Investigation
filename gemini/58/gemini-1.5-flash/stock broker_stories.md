The rain hammered against the panoramic windows of Avery Sterling's office, a relentless percussion accompanying the even more relentless thrum of the city below.  Avery, full name Avarice Sterling – a name his grandmother had insisted on, much to his lifelong chagrin – stared out at the blurred cityscape, a miniature Manhattan sculpted from grey and black.  His 72nd floor perch in the gleaming glass tower of Zenith Financial offered an unparalleled view, a perspective that, ironically, felt increasingly claustrophobic.  The air hung thick with the scent of expensive coffee and simmering anxiety, a familiar perfume in the high-stakes world of Wall Street.  Tonight, however, the anxiety felt particularly sharp, a serrated edge against the polished veneer of his usually unflappable demeanor. The deal with PetroNova hung in the balance, a billion-dollar gamble that could make or break his career, and the storm raging outside seemed to mirror the tempest brewing within.
