The chipped porcelain teacup trembled in Amelia Hernandez's hand, mirroring the tremor of anxiety in her chest.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of her office, blurring the already hazy cityscape of Seattle sprawling below.  Grey, a color synonymous with the perpetual drizzle and the damp chill that seemed to seep into the very bones of the Emerald City, dominated the scene. Amelia, a meticulous woman who preferred crisp lines and vibrant colours – reflected in her impeccably tailored suit – found herself increasingly at odds with her surroundings.  Her office, usually a sanctuary of organized chaos, felt oppressive tonight, the scent of damp wool clinging to the air like a shroud.  The flickering neon sign of the "Salty Siren" seafood restaurant across the street cast an eerie, pulsating glow on the rain-streaked glass, adding to the unsettling atmosphere.  Tonight, the usual hum of Seattle's night life felt muted, almost held its breath, waiting.  And Amelia Hernandez, insurance agent extraordinaire, was about to find out why.
