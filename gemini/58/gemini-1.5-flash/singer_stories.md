The chipped porcelain teacup warmed Amelia Blackwood’s fingers, a fragile counterpoint to the icy November wind rattling the single-pane windows of her tiny, attic apartment.  Rain lashed against the slates, a relentless percussion mirroring the frantic rhythm of her heart.  From her perch overlooking the cobbled streets of Prague’s Old Town, the city sprawled beneath her like a dark, glittering tapestry, its ancient spires and onion domes barely visible through the driving storm.  Amelia, a mezzo-soprano with a voice that could shatter glass and mend broken hearts in equal measure, was not known for her composure.  Tonight, however, even her usually formidable self-control threatened to crumble. The premiere of her new opera, *The Obsidian Mirror*, was only hours away, and a creeping dread, cold and sharp as a shard of ice, had taken root in her soul.
