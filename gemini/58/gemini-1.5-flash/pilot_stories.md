The biting Alaskan wind howled a mournful song across the desolate, snow-swept expanse of Denali National Park, whipping Captain Evelyn "Evie" Reed's dark hair across her face as she wrestled the controls of her trusty De Havilland Beaver.  Below, the jagged peaks of the Alaska Range clawed at the bruised twilight sky, their snow-laden slopes a treacherous labyrinth of ice and rock.  The air, thin and frigid, bit at the exposed skin on her cheeks, a familiar sting that spoke volumes about the unforgiving beauty of her workplace.  Evie, however, felt none of the usual thrill.  Tonight, the majestic wilderness felt less like a challenge and more like a vast, indifferent tomb.  The radio crackled with static, a harsh counterpoint to the rhythmic thrum of the engine, and the weight of her unspoken dread pressed down on her heavier than the accumulating snow.
