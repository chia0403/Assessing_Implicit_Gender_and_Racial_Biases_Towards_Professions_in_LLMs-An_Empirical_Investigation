The chipped ceramic mug warmed Amelia Hernandez’s hands, the lukewarm tea doing little to soothe the icy dread that had settled in her stomach.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of her downtown Chicago office, blurring the glittering cityscape into a hazy, watercolour impression.  From her 27th-floor perch, Amelia, a meticulous accountant with a reputation for unwavering precision, could usually find solace in the ordered rhythm of the city lights. Tonight, however, the rhythmic pulse felt more like a frantic heartbeat, mirroring the frantic calculations spinning in her head.  The tax season deadline loomed, a monstrous beast breathing down her neck, but it wasn’t the usual pressure that gnawed at her.  This was different.  This felt…wrong.  A shiver, unrelated to the damp Chicago night, ran down her spine.  A subtle discrepancy, a tiny anomaly in a mountain of meticulously organized data, had sparked a wildfire of suspicion in her usually unflappable mind.  And Amelia Hernandez knew, with a certainty that chilled her to the bone, that something was terribly, terribly wrong.
