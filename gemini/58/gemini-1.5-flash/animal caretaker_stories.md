The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne's hair across her face as she wrestled a recalcitrant bale of hay into the goat pen.  Frozen droplets clung to her eyelashes, blurring the already fading light.  The air hung thick with the scent of damp earth, woodsmoke, and the surprisingly sweet musk of the herd of pygmy goats huddled together for warmth at the far end of the enclosure.  Around her, the sprawling grounds of Whispering Pines Animal Sanctuary, usually a haven of vibrant life, were cloaked in a somber twilight.  The skeletal branches of ancient oaks clawed at the bruised purple sky, their silhouettes etched starkly against the last remnants of the setting sun.  Tonight, the sanctuary felt less like a refuge and more like a lonely, windswept island in a sea of encroaching darkness.
