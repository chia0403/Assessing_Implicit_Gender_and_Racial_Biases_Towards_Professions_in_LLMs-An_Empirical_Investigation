The salt spray stung Aurelia Blackwood’s face as she wrestled the easel into position, the wind threatening to topple it over the cliff edge.  Below, the churning turquoise water of the Atlantic gnawed at the jagged Cornish coastline, a relentless symphony of crashing waves that mirrored the tempest brewing inside her.  The air, thick with the scent of brine and wild heather, carried the mournful cry of gulls, a fitting accompaniment to the desolate beauty of Porthcurno’s hidden cove.  Aurelia, her fiery red hair whipping wildly around her, ignored the discomfort, her gaze fixed on the dramatic interplay of light and shadow on the rocky outcrop before her. This was it, the perfect canvas, waiting to be captured, a raw, untamed masterpiece mirroring the turmoil in her own soul.
