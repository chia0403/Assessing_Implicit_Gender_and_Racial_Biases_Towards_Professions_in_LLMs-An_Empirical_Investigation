The chipped paint of the "Calm Corner" – a misnomer if ever there was one – flaked onto the worn, floral carpet.  Dr. Eleanor Vance, school psychologist for Ashwood Middle, sighed, the sound swallowed by the oppressive silence of the otherwise empty room.  Outside, the cacophony of the school bell's shrill ring echoed, a stark contrast to the hushed, almost suffocating quiet within.  Ashwood Middle, nestled in the perpetually overcast town of Havenwood, Oregon, was a building that seemed to absorb the grey of the sky into its very brickwork.  Even on the rare sunny days, a pervasive gloom clung to the hallways, a palpable sense of adolescent angst and simmering drama.  Eleanor traced a finger along the edge of a wobbly, child-sized chair, its plastic already scratched and marred by countless tiny hands. Another day, another crisis, she thought, already bracing herself for the inevitable parade of troubled teens and overwrought parents.
