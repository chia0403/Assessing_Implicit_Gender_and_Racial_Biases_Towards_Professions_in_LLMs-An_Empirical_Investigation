The salt spray stung Amelia "Mia" Hernandez's face as she wrestled the stubborn tiller of her small sailboat, the "Sea Serpent," through the churning grey waters of Puget Sound.  Rain, a relentless drizzle that had been falling since dawn, plastered her dark hair to her forehead, obscuring the already limited visibility.  Ahead, the jagged silhouette of Bainbridge Island, shrouded in mist, promised little comfort.  Mia, a seasoned maritime lawyer with a reputation for both fierce intellect and a stubborn refusal to back down from a fight, found herself battling not only the elements but also the gnawing feeling that this particular case—the convoluted ownership dispute over a derelict fishing trawler—was more than just another knotty legal tangle.  It smelled of something darker, something rotten, lurking beneath the surface, as murky and unforgiving as the Sound itself.
