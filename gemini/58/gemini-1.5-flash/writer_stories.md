The chipped paint of the windowsill dug into Elara Vance’s elbows as she stared out at the relentless grey of the North Sea.  November clung to the coast of Northumberland with the tenacity of a starving dog, its icy fingers wrapping around the fishing village of Craster in a suffocating grip.  The wind, a banshee wail that ripped across the cobbled streets, rattled the single-paned glass, a counterpoint to the rhythmic thump-thump-thump of the waves against the ancient harbour wall.  Elara, a writer whose words usually flowed as effortlessly as the Tyne, found her pen frozen, the blank page mocking her with its pristine whiteness.  The deadline for her historical fiction novel, *The Serpent’s Kiss*, loomed, a monstrous shadow in the corner of her already cluttered study, a study that smelled faintly of brine, old books, and the lingering ghost of a forgotten peat fire.
