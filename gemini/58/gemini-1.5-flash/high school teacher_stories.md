The fluorescent lights of Northwood High buzzed a monotonous counterpoint to the rhythmic tap-tap-tap of Ms. Eleanor Vance's pen against her grading rubric.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of her classroom, blurring the already indistinct cityscape into a watercolour smudge of grey and black.  Empty desks, still bearing the faint scent of teenage angst and cheap cologne, stretched out before her like a battlefield after a particularly messy skirmish.  Eleanor sighed, the sound swallowed by the relentless downpour. Another Friday night, another mountain of essays on the existential dread of Shakespeare’s Hamlet – a mountain she was slowly, painstakingly, chipping away at with the dull but necessary instrument of her red pen.  Outside, the wind howled like a banshee, a fitting soundtrack to the turbulent emotions she suspected were brewing beneath the surface of her students’ carefully crafted prose.
