The chipped mug warmed Amelia Hawthorne’s fingers, the lukewarm chamomile doing little to soothe the jagged edges of her frustration.  Rain lashed against the panoramic window of her Soho loft, blurring the already hazy cityscape into a watercolour smear of greys and muted oranges.  Empty ramen containers and crumpled sketches littered the vast, otherwise minimalist workspace, a testament to the all-nighter she’d just pulled wrestling with the latest iteration of the “Vibrance” campaign for Stellar Cosmetics.  The deadline loomed, a monstrous, fuchsia-pink deadline, mocking her from the corner of her iMac screen in the form of a flashing digital clock.  The air hung thick with the scent of burnt coffee and simmering resentment towards a client who clearly valued trend over substance.
