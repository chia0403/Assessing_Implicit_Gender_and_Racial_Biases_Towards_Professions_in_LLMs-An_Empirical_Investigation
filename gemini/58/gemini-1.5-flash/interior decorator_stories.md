The chipped paint on the windowsill of the abandoned Victorian mansion felt brittle under Seraphina Evangeline Bellweather's gloved fingers.  Dust motes, disturbed by the faintest tremor of wind whistling through a shattered pane, danced in the slivers of afternoon sunlight slicing through the gloom.  Seraphina, a woman whose life revolved around the calculated placement of a throw pillow and the perfect shade of Farrow & Ball, found herself utterly at sea in this ocean of decay.  The air hung heavy with the scent of damp earth and forgotten dreams, a stark contrast to the crisp, clean fragrance of lavender and beeswax she usually carried with her.  This wasn't a charmingly rustic cottage in need of a makeover; this was a mausoleum, whispering secrets in the rustling of its neglected ivy and the groan of its settling timbers.  And Seraphina, despite her initial reservations, had been hired to resurrect it.
