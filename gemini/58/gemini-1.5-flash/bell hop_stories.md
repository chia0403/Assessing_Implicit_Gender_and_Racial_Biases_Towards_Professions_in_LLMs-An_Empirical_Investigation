The brass spittoon gleamed under the flickering gaslight, reflecting the weary slump of Bartholomew "Barty" Butterfield's shoulders.  Barty, a bellhop at the Grand Majestic Hotel, a crumbling monument to a bygone Gilded Age, sighed a sound like escaping steam.  The air hung thick with the scent of stale cigar smoke, lavender-scented polish, and a faint, unsettling undercurrent of desperation – a perfume only truly appreciated by those who spent their nights navigating the hotel's labyrinthine corridors and its even more labyrinthine clientele.  Rain lashed against the tall, gothic windows, mirroring the turmoil brewing in Barty's own chest.  He clutched his worn leather-bound tip book, its pages stiff with the accumulated grease of countless nervous hands and the weight of a thousand unspoken stories. Tonight, however, felt different. Tonight, something felt…wrong.
