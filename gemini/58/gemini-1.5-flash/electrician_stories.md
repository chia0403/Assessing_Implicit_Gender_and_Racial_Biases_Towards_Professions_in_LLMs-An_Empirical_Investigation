The salt spray stung Elias Thorne's face as he wrestled the heavy coil of armored cable onto the swaying platform.  Below him, the churning grey expanse of the Atlantic roared its displeasure at the encroaching storm.  Above, the skeletal remains of the old lighthouse, its once-proud lantern a gaping maw of rust and decay, clawed at the bruised twilight sky.  Elias, a man whose hands were as familiar with frayed wires as they were with calloused knuckles, squinted against the wind-whipped rain.  He adjusted the worn leather strap of his tool bag, a comforting weight against his hip, and muttered a silent prayer to whatever deity might be listening amidst the tempest. This wasn't just any job; this was a battle against the elements, a fight to restore light to a forgotten sentinel of the coast.
