The wind, a rasping, gravelly voice, whispered secrets across the parched earth of the Painted Desert.  Dust devils, miniature tornadoes of red and ochre, danced across the cracked plains, leaving Amelia Jane "Amy" Blackwood's ranch, the sprawling Blackwood spread, looking even more desolate than usual.  Amy, her weathered face etched with the map of a life spent under the unforgiving Arizona sun, squinted at the horizon, the setting sun painting the sky in hues of blood orange and bruised purple.  The air, thick with the scent of dry sagebrush and impending rain, hung heavy and expectant.  Three hundred head of cattle, lean and thirsty, grazed listlessly on the sparse, brittle grass, their low moans a mournful chorus against the dying light.  The silence, broken only by the wind and the cattle, was oppressive, a stark contrast to the usual lively chaos of the ranch.  Something was wrong.  Amy could feel it in the bones of her tired body, a primal unease that tightened her grip on the reins of her trusty paint mare, Star.
