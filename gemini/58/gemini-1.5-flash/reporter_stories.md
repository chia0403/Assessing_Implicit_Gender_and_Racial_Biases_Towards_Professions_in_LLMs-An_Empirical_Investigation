The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Mia” Hernandez’s trench coat around her legs as she huddled deeper into the shadowed alleyway.  Rain, the kind that chilled you to the bone before it even touched your skin, plastered her dark hair to her forehead.  The air hung thick with the smells of rotting garbage, damp brick, and something else… something metallic and faintly sweet, like old blood.  This wasn't the charming, postcard-perfect San Francisco she'd read about in tourist brochures; this was the city’s underbelly, a labyrinth of forgotten corners and whispered secrets, and tonight, Mia was diving headfirst into its murky depths.  Her battered notepad, clutched in her gloved hand, felt heavy with the weight of the story she was chasing – a story that promised to unravel a web of deceit far more tangled than any she'd encountered in her five years as a reporter for the *San Francisco Chronicle*.
