The salt-laced wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne's hair across her face as she navigated the uneven cobblestones of the old port.  The grey, brooding sky threatened rain over the quaint, almost comically picturesque village of Port Blossom, nestled on the rugged Cornish coast.  Fishing boats bobbed gently in the harbour, their masts like skeletal fingers against the turbulent sea.  Amelia, bundled in a thick wool coat despite the relatively mild October air, pulled her scarf tighter.  The scent of brine and seaweed hung heavy in the air, a familiar aroma that usually soothed her; today, however, it did little to ease the knot of tension in her stomach.  Her appointment with Mr. Silas Penhaligon, a renowned but notoriously cantankerous fisherman, was in fifteen minutes, and the only thing more unpredictable than the Cornish weather was the temper of a stubborn old sea dog.
