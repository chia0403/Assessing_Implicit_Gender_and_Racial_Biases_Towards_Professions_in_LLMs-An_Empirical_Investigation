The biting wind whipped across the desolate expanse of the Alberta plains, stinging Amelia “Amy” Blackwood’s cheeks raw.  It howled a mournful song through the skeletal branches of the few hardy pines clinging to the frozen earth, a counterpoint to the rhythmic rumble of her Caterpillar D6T.  Amy gripped the controls, her gloved hands steady despite the numbing cold.  The massive bulldozer, its tracks leaving deep furrows in the snow-packed soil, was her kingdom, a steel behemoth she commanded with effortless grace.  The setting sun cast long, skeletal shadows, painting the already stark landscape in shades of bruised purple and fading orange.  Ahead, the vast, snow-covered expanse of the abandoned potash mine stretched out like a frozen scar on the earth, a silent testament to the boom and bust cycles that had shaped this harsh, unforgiving land.  Tonight, Amy was alone with the ghosts of industry past, and the gnawing suspicion that this job, reclaiming this land, might be her last.
