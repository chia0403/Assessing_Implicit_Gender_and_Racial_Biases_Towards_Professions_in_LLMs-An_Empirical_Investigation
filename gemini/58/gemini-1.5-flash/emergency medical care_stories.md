The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Mia” Hernandez’s dark hair across her face as she wrestled the stretcher door closed.  The harsh glare of the sodium vapor streetlights reflected off the slick, rain-lashed pavement of Chicago’s South Side.  Sirens wailed in the distance, a familiar, mournful symphony that underscored the city's relentless pulse.  Mia, a veteran paramedic for Chicago EMS, shivered, not entirely from the cold.  The adrenaline, a constant companion in her line of work, still thrummed beneath her skin, leaving a faint tremor in her hands even after delivering the unresponsive young man to County General.  His pale face, etched with the grim signature of an opioid overdose, haunted the edges of her vision. Another night, another life hanging in the balance. Another reminder that in the heart of this sprawling metropolis, death and despair were as pervasive as the ever-present grit and grime.
