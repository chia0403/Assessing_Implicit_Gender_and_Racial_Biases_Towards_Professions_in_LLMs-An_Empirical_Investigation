The biting wind whipped Dr. Eleanor Vance’s hair across her face as she wrestled the heavy oak door of the Blackwood Manor open.  December’s icy breath snaked through the gaping cracks in the aged stonework, carrying with it the scent of damp earth and something else… something acrid, almost metallic, that prickled the back of her throat.  Blackwood Manor, perched precariously on a windswept cliff overlooking the churning grey expanse of the North Sea, was a gothic masterpiece of decay.  Ivy, thick as a man’s arm, clawed at the crumbling facade, while broken panes of glass stared out like vacant eyes. The only sound louder than the wind was the mournful groan of the house itself, settling deeper into the cliff face with every gust.  This was not a house call; it was a summons to a battlefield, and Eleanor knew, with a sinking feeling in her stomach, that the fight had already begun.
