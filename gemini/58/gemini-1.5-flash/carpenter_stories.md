The salt spray stung Elias Thorne's face as he wrestled the stubborn oak plank into place.  He gripped the rough-hewn wood, its splinters biting into his calloused hands, a familiar discomfort that spoke of years spent battling the unforgiving elements and the even more unforgiving nature of timber.  The air hung thick with the scent of brine and pine, a constant companion in the isolated fishing village of Port Blossom, nestled precariously on the craggy cliffs overlooking the tempestuous North Sea.  The wind, a relentless sculptor, howled a mournful song through the gaps in the weathered clapboard houses, a soundtrack to Elias’s solitary work on the dilapidated fishing boat, *The Wanderer*, its hull scarred and patched like a well-loved, though weary, friend.  The grey sky threatened another downpour, mirroring the turmoil brewing within Elias’s own heart.
