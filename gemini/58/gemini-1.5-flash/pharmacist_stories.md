The chipped porcelain of the mortar felt cool beneath Dr. Elara Vance’s fingertips, a familiar comfort in the oppressive July heat clinging to the air of Blackwood Apothecary.  Dust motes, illuminated by the single shaft of sunlight slicing through the grimy window, danced in the stillness of the almost-empty shop.  The scent of dried chamomile and aged oak hung heavy, a far cry from the sterile, clinical smell of the modern pharmacies Elara had briefly worked in before inheriting this relic from her grandmother.  Blackwood, a town clinging stubbornly to its past, nestled in the shadowed valley where the whispering pines met the churning grey of the Blackwood River, was a place where time seemed to move at a slower, more deliberate pace – a pace that often tested Elara's patience but strangely, also soothed her soul.
