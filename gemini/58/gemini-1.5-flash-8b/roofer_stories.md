The biting wind whipped at Elias Thorne's worn leather jacket, stinging his cheeks and rattling the loose shingles he held.  Below, the cobbled streets of Old St. Augustine, Florida, shimmered under a relentless midday sun, the air thick with the scent of salt and aged stucco.  He was perched precariously on the rusted ironwork of the Casa de la Luz, a crumbling Spanish Colonial mansion that had seen generations of sunrises and sunsets, storms and celebrations.  For Elias, a roofer with calloused hands and eyes that held the ancient secrets of the city's architecture, this was merely another day on the job.  But today, something felt different.  A persistent, almost tangible unease clung to the air, a feeling that whispered of hidden histories and unanswered questions, and something, or someone, was definitely watching from within the shadows of the sprawling estate.
