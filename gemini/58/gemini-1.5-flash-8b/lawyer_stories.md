The biting November wind whipped Amelia "Amy" Cartwright's trench coat around her legs as she hurried down the cobbled alleyway, the scent of damp earth and decaying leaves clinging to the air like a shroud.  Above, the skeletal branches of ancient London plane trees clawed at the bruised, grey sky.  Rain, a persistent drizzle, slicked the cobblestones, mirroring the sheen of apprehension clinging to her.  She clutched her worn leather briefcase, its contents—vital documents detailing the intricate case of the Blackwood Estate—feeling as heavy as the leaden atmosphere around her.  London, in all its brooding majesty, seemed to hold its breath, mirroring the suffocating weight of the impending court hearing.

Amy Cartwright, a sharp, thirty-five-year-old lawyer specializing in property law, felt the familiar knot tightening in her stomach.  The Blackwood Estate, a sprawling, centuries-old manor house tucked away in the Surrey hills, had been the source of whispered anxieties and simmering resentments for years.  Now, the estate's future—and perhaps the fate of several families—rested in her hands.
