The fluorescent lights of the high school cafeteria hummed, a monotonous soundtrack to the clatter of trays and the low murmur of teenage conversations.  Amelia Grace Sterling, school psychologist, sighed, pushing a stray strand of auburn hair behind her ear.  The aroma of mystery meat and stale french fries hung heavy in the air, a familiar battlefield scent in this particular corner of the world.  She sat hunched over a half-eaten salad, her eyes scanning the room, searching for a particular constellation of fidgeting limbs and downcast eyes.  This was her patrol route, a daily ritual in the labyrinthine hallways and echoing classrooms of Northwood High, a place where anxieties were as common as lockers and whispered secrets as plentiful as graffiti tags.  Her name, though, carried a subtle weight, a reminder of the quiet strength she tried so hard to project.  Northwood High, a sprawling brick building nestled in the heart of a town that seemed to hold its breath, was her microcosm, her battleground, her stage.
