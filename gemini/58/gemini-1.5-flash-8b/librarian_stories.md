The flickering fluorescent lights of the Archibald County Library cast a pale, ghostly glow on the worn, oak shelves.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of afternoon sun piercing the grimy window, illuminating a meticulously organized section of first editions.  Amelia Seraphina Blackwood, her fingers tracing the embossed lettering of a leather-bound volume, sighed.  The air, thick with the scent of aged paper and stale coffee, hung heavy with the quiet hum of the library, a symphony of hushed whispers and the rhythmic tap-tap-tap of a typewriter in the back.  It was a Tuesday, a day Amelia, a librarian with a mind as sharp and precise as the Dewey Decimal System itself, loathed.  Tuesdays were for returns, a deluge of overdue books, misplaced bookmarks, and the inevitable, frustrating explanations.  Today, however, a particular return held an unusual weight, a piece of mystery that had been lurking beneath the surface of the library's quiet life for weeks.
