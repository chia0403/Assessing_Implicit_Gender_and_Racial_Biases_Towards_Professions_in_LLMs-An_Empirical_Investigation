The biting London wind whipped Amelia Blackwood's tailored coat around her legs as she hurried across the cobblestones of St. James's Park.  Rain, a persistent drizzle, slicked the ancient stonework, reflecting the flickering gaslight lamps like a thousand miniature moons.  She clutched her briefcase, its leather cool and reassuring against her gloved hand.  The scent of damp earth and distant roasting chestnuts filled the air, a stark contrast to the sterile, polished perfection of the boardroom she'd left behind just hours before.  Amelia, Head of Strategic Partnerships at Blackwood Industries, felt the familiar weight of expectation pressing down on her shoulders, a tangible force even as the wind threatened to tear her from her path.  Above the hushed murmur of the park, the rhythmic clang of the nearby church bells echoed a reminder of the relentless march of time, and of the crucial decisions that lay ahead.
