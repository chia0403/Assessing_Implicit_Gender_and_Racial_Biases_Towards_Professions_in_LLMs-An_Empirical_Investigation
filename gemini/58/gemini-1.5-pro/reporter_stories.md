Rain lashed against the grimy windows of the *Daily Clarion* office, mimicking the relentless drumming of Amelia “Milly” Reyes’s headache. Fluorescent lights buzzed overhead, casting a sickly yellow glow over the cluttered newsroom.  Half-eaten takeout containers and crumpled newspapers littered the desks, testament to a late night spent chasing a story that ultimately went nowhere.  Milly, her dark curls pulled back in a messy bun, stared at her flickering computer screen, the cursor blinking mockingly in the empty document.  It was 2:00 AM in the perpetually grey city of New Aldersburgh, a place where ambition went to die and cynicism thrived. The kind of city that chewed up bright-eyed reporters like Milly and spat them out as hardened shadows of their former selves. Tonight, Milly felt closer to being swallowed whole than ever before.
