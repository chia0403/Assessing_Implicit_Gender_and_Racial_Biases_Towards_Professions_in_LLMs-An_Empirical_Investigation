The biting November wind whipped through the skeletal branches of the towering pines, whistling a mournful tune that echoed through the Clearwater Valley.  Frost crunched underfoot as Jedediah "Jed" Blackwood adjusted the earflaps of his worn leather cap.  Dawn hadn't yet broken over the Idaho mountains, but Jed, his breath puffing out in ghostly plumes, was already making his way through the pre-dawn gloom to the logging camp.  Camp Five, nestled deep in the heart of the Clearwater National Forest, was a ramshackle collection of prefab cabins and canvas tents, clinging precariously to the steep mountainside like a bird's nest.  The aroma of woodsmoke and brewing coffee, a beacon in the frigid air, promised a brief respite from the relentless cold before another day of hard work felling giants began.
