The flickering gaslight cast elongated shadows across Amelia Hawthorne's mahogany desk, illuminating columns of figures meticulously inked onto thick ledger paper. Outside, the November wind howled through the narrow cobbled streets of Grimsborough, a small, perpetually fog-shrouded town nestled on the Yorkshire moors. Amelia, a woman of precise habits and even more precise accounting, shivered despite the crackling fire in the hearth.  It wasn't just the chill seeping in through the ill-fitting window frames; a different kind of coldness, a premonition of unease, had settled in her bones ever since the arrival of the new client, Mr. Silas Blackwood.
