The biting wind whipped off the North Atlantic, carrying the scent of salt and something faintly, unsettlingly fishy, straight into Dr. Elspeth "Ellie" Cartwright's face.  Grey cliffs, streaked with white guano, loomed over the tiny, wind-battered village of Port Blossom, nestled precariously on the easternmost edge of Newfoundland.  December had descended with a vengeance, coating the rugged landscape in a thick layer of frost and silencing the usual cheerful chatter of the fishing boats that dotted the harbor.  Ellie, bundled in a thick parka and fur-lined boots, trudged towards the flickering lights of the O'Malley General Store, the only source of supplies – and human contact – for miles.  Her breath puffed out in white clouds, mirroring the turbulent waves crashing against the rocky shore. This was her third winter in Port Blossom, a far cry from the bustling veterinary clinics of St. John's where she'd trained, but a place that had, surprisingly, burrowed its way into her heart.
