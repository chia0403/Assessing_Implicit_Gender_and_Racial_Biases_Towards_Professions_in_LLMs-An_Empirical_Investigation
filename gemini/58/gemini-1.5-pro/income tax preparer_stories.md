Rain lashed against the window of Amelia Prudence Higgins's tiny office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her.  Tax season was officially over, the final frantic filings sent off into the digital ether just before midnight.  Yet, the scent of stale coffee and desperation still clung to the air, permeating the cramped space above O’Malley’s Pub in downtown Scranton, Pennsylvania.  Amelia, surrounded by stacks of now useless 1040 forms and half-eaten boxes of stale crackers, stared out at the desolate Lackawanna Avenue, a single streetlight casting a halo on the downpour.  Her normally meticulous desk was a chaotic landscape of discarded paperclips, highlighter pens, and a lone, forgotten tax return for a Mr. Bartholomew "Bart" Simpson – a name that, for some reason, now filled her with a strange unease.
