The wind, a raw-boned thing gnawing at the eaves of the ranch house, carried the scent of impending snow.  Amelia Jane Cartwright, her face weathered like the saddle leather she worked, squinted at the bruised purple sky above the Big Horn Mountains.  Her ranch, the Crooked C, sprawled across a vast swathe of Wyoming grassland, rolling away from the house in a tapestry of ochre and fading green.  The land, once a shimmering sea of prairie grass, was now dotted with the restless dark shapes of her Hereford cattle, their breath puffing white clouds in the frigid air. Winter was coming, earlier than usual, and Amelia Jane, alone save for her aging Blue Heeler, Gus, knew it would be a hard one.
