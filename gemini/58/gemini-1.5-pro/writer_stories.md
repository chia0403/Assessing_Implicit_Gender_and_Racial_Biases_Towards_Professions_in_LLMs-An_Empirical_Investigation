The salt spray kissed Amelia "Mia" Reyes's face as she leaned against the weathered railing of her balcony, the rusted metal cool beneath her fingertips.  Her small cottage, perched on the cliffs overlooking the churning grey expanse of the North Atlantic, was usually her sanctuary. Today, however, the rhythmic crash of the waves against the rocks below felt less like a lullaby and more like an insistent drumbeat, mirroring the anxious thump-thump-thump in her chest.  The wind, a near-constant companion on this remote stretch of the Irish coast, whipped strands of her dark hair across her face, obscuring the view of Skellig Michael, a jagged, mystical island rising from the mist several miles offshore.  It was a view that had inspired countless words, filling notebooks and spilling onto the pages of her half-finished manuscript, but today, the muse was stubbornly silent.  The deadline loomed, a menacing shadow stretching across the wild beauty of County Kerry, and Mia, for the first time in her writing career, was facing a terrifying blank page.
