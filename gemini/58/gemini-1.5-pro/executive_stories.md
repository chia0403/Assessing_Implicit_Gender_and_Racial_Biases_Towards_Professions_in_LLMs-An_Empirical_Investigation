Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of Elias Thorne’s forty-second-floor office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside him. The cityscape of Chicago, normally a glittering tapestry of light and steel, was obscured by a grey, swirling curtain.  Elias, Chief Operating Officer of Thorne & Blackwood Investments, gripped the cool aluminum frame of his window, his knuckles bone-white. The mahogany desk behind him, usually a testament to order and control, was strewn with spreadsheets, market analysis reports, and – most unsettling of all – a single, cream-colored envelope.  Its elegant script mocked his carefully constructed world, a single name scrawled across its surface:  "Cassandra."  A name he hadn’t heard, a name he’d buried, for twenty years.
