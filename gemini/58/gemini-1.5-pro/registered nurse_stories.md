The biting November wind whipped around Eleanor Vance, RN, as she hurried across the deserted parking lot of St. Jude’s Community Hospital.  The sodium lamps cast an eerie orange glow on the swirling snow, turning the familiar brick building into something vaguely sinister.  Eleanor tugged her woolen scarf tighter around her neck, the icy air stinging her cheeks.  It was well past midnight, and the only sounds were the mournful sigh of the wind and the distant rumble of a snowplow. St. Jude’s, nestled deep in the Catskill Mountains of upstate New York, usually felt like a sanctuary.  Tonight, however, a palpable unease hung heavy in the air, prickling Eleanor's skin like static electricity.  Something felt wrong, deeply wrong.  And for the first time in her ten years at St. Jude's, Eleanor felt a flicker of genuine fear.
