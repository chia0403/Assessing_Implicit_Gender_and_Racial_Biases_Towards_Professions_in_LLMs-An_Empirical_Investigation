The biting wind whipped down Butcher Street, stinging Elias Thorne's cheeks raw above his thick, woolen scarf.  Frost crunched under his heavy boots as he trudged towards his shop, "Thorne's Fine Meats," its gaslights casting a warm, flickering glow against the pre-dawn gloom of Port Blossom, Maine.  The salty tang of the nearby harbor mingled with the metallic scent of sawdust and the faint, sweet aroma of woodsmoke curling from the chimneys of the clapboard houses huddled against the relentless January cold.  Port Blossom, a town clinging to the rugged coastline like a stubborn barnacle, was a place where secrets festered beneath a veneer of quiet respectability, and Elias, with his keen butcher's eye for dissecting truth from bone, knew them all.
