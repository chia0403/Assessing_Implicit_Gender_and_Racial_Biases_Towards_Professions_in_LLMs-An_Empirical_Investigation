The biting November wind whipped around Eleanor Vance, PA-C, as she hurried across the cobblestone courtyard of St. Jude's Memorial Hospital.  The ancient clock tower, a gothic monstrosity looming over the sprawling complex, chimed six times, the mournful sound swallowed by the wind.  Nestled in the heart of the historic French Quarter of New Orleans, St. Jude's had served the city for nearly two centuries.  Its aging walls held stories of yellow fever epidemics, hurricanes, and countless human dramas, each whispering in the shadows as Eleanor rushed towards the brightly lit emergency room entrance.  Tonight, the city's humid air carried a different kind of tension, a palpable unease that prickled her skin even beneath her thick wool coat.  It was the kind of night that bred strange cases, the kind Eleanor, despite her five years of experience, still wasn't quite used to.
