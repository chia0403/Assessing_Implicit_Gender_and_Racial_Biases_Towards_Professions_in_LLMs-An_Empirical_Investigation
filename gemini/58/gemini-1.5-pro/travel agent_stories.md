Rain lashed against the window of "Wanderlust Voyages," blurring the neon pink flamingo perched precariously on the sill.  Inside, Amelia Zephyr Thorne, her name as whimsical as the destinations she peddled, tapped a chipped coral-pink nail against her mahogany desk.  The air in the small, cluttered agency hung thick with the scent of jasmine tea and desperation.  Outside, the relentless Seattle downpour mirrored the state of Amelia’s business.  October in the Pacific Northwest was usually bustling with last-minute sun-seekers, desperate for an escape before winter’s icy grip tightened.  This year, however, the only thing escaping was Amelia’s hope of making rent. The travel posters plastered across the walls, depicting turquoise waters and white sand beaches, seemed to mock her with their vibrant unreality.
