The biting wind whipped across the desolate practice field, carrying with it the scent of damp earth and the faint echo of shouting.  Floodlights cast long, skeletal shadows of the bleachers onto the churned mud, illuminating the lone figure pacing the sidelines.  Amelia "Amy" Chen, head coach of the Northwood High Timberwolves, pulled her threadbare beanie further down over her ears, a grimace etched on her face as she watched her team stumble through another botched drill. This wasn't the crisp, precise execution she'd drilled into them for weeks, and with the regional championships looming just two weeks away, the pressure was mounting like a dark thundercloud on the horizon.  Northwood, a small, forgotten town nestled in the heart of Oregon's Willamette Valley, had pinned its hopes on these kids, on Amy, to break their twenty-year losing streak.  And tonight, beneath the flickering sodium lamps, Amy felt the weight of that expectation settle heavy on her shoulders.
