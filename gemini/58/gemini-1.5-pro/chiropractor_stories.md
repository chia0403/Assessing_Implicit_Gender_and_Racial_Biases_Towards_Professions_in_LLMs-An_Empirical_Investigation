The biting November wind whipped around Dr. Elias Thorne as he locked up his clinic, Thorne Chiropractic & Wellness, nestled between a laundromat and a perpetually empty storefront promising "Authentic Vietnamese Cuisine Coming Soon."  The neon "Open" sign buzzed faintly as he flipped the switch, casting a sickly green glow over the frosted windows.  Ashworth, Pennsylvania, a town whose heyday had long since passed, was settling in for another long, cold night.  The skeletal trees lining Main Street rattled their bare branches like arthritic fingers, a sound Elias, with his intimate knowledge of the human skeleton, found strangely unsettling. He pulled his collar tighter around his neck and shoved his hands deep into the pockets of his worn tweed jacket.  Tonight, the chill seemed to seep deeper than bone.  He had a feeling it wasn't just the weather.
