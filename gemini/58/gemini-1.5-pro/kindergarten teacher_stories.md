The sticky, sweet scent of spilled juice and the cacophony of tiny voices arguing over the ownership of a single, glittery purple crayon filled the air.  Amelia Rose Hawthorne, her auburn hair pulled back in a messy bun, surveyed the chaos of her kindergarten classroom with a weary but affectionate smile.  Sunlight streamed through the large windows of the "Rainbow Room" at Maplewood Elementary, illuminating the miniature tables and chairs, the brightly colored alphabet rug, and the finger-painted masterpieces adorning every available surface.  Outside, the first fat snowflakes of the season began to drift down, transforming the playground into a swirling winter wonderland.  Little did Amelia know, this seemingly ordinary December morning was about to become anything but.
