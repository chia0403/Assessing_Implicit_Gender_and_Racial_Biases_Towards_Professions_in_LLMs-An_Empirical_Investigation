The damp chill of a London November seeped through the ill-fitting window frame, raising gooseflesh on Amelia Thistlewick's arms.  Amelia, whose full name was Amelia Prudence Thistlewick, and whose life was as meticulously ordered as the columns in her ledgers, shivered and pulled her shawl tighter around her shoulders. Her small office, perched above Mr. Henderson's Haberdashery on Threadneedle Street, was cramped and perpetually dusted with fine particles of wool and linen.  Gaslight flickered against the rows of neatly stacked journals, casting long, dancing shadows that seemed to mock the unwavering precision of her work.  Outside, the fog hung thick and heavy, muffling the usual cacophony of the street below, creating an unusual, almost unsettling quiet. It was the kind of quiet that made Amelia feel as though something was about to happen. And she, Amelia Prudence Thistlewick, did not like things that happened unexpectedly.
