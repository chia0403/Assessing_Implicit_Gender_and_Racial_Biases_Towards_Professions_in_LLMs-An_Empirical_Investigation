The siren’s wail was a banshee’s scream, tearing through the thick, humid New Orleans night.  Rain lashed against the windshield of Medic One, blurring the already distorted glow of neon signs advertising voodoo shops and daiquiri bars on Bourbon Street. Inside, Amelia “Mia”  Rodriguez, a paramedic with five years on the job and an even longer lineage of New Orleans natives, gripped the handrail as the ambulance lurched around a corner, its tires spitting up a spray of grimy water. The radio crackled to life, dispatch relaying the fragmented, panicked call: multiple victims, possible stabbing, outside The Cat’s Meow, a notorious dive bar nestled deep in the French Quarter's labyrinthine alleys.  The air inside the ambulance was thick with the metallic tang of anticipated blood and the ever-present scent of stale beignets that clung to the city like a second skin. Mia’s stomach churned, not with fear, but with the familiar, grim anticipation of the unknown. Tonight, the heart of the city was bleeding, and she was racing towards the wound.
