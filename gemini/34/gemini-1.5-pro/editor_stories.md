The chipped porcelain mug, stained ochre from years of lukewarm Earl Grey, warmed Amelia Thorne's hand as she stared out the grime-streaked window of her attic office.  Rain lashed against the glass, mirroring the tempest brewing within her. Below, the city of Arkham, Massachusetts, cloaked in a perpetual twilight of mist and looming Victorian architecture, seemed to exhale a collective sigh.  It was late October, the kind of evening that smelled of damp leaves and woodsmoke, a scent that usually brought Amelia comfort, but tonight, it only amplified the gnawing unease in her stomach.  A manuscript, its pages thick and unsettling, lay open on her desk, the source of her disquiet.  Amelia Thorne, editor for the obscure and increasingly infamous publishing house, Blackwood Press, had just read something that made her question her already fragile grip on reality.
