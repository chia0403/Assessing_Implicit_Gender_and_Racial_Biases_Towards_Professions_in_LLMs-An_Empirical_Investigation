The biting November wind whipped around Professor Alistair Finch, tugging at the edges of his tweed overcoat as he hurried across the cobbled quadrangle of St. Jude's College, Oxford.  Gargoyles leered down from the honey-coloured stone, their grimaces seemingly amplified by the swirling mist that clung to the ancient buildings.  Alistair clutched a stack of essays, their weight a familiar comfort against the gnawing unease that had settled in his stomach since the discovery in the Bodleian Library that morning.  It wasn't the text itself – a 15th-century treatise on alchemy, hardly unusual in his line of work – but the small, intricately carved wooden box tucked inside its pages that had set his teeth on edge.  The box was locked, and Alistair had the unsettling feeling that whatever lay within held secrets best left undisturbed.
