The flickering fluorescent lights of Rosie's Auto Repair hummed a monotonous tune, punctuated by the rhythmic clang of metal on metal. Grease stained the concrete floor like abstract art, and the air hung thick with the scent of motor oil, burnt rubber, and something vaguely sweet, likely Mrs. Rodriguez's perpetually baking pan dulce from the bakery next door.  Eleanor "Ellie" Vasquez, her dark hair pulled back in a tight bun, wiped a bead of sweat from her brow with the back of her grimy hand.  It was a sweltering July afternoon in the small, dusty town of Harmony Creek, Arizona, a place where the sun beat down relentlessly and secrets simmered just below the surface like the asphalt on Main Street.  Ellie, perched on a rolling creeper, slid out from under a cherry-red '67 Mustang, her face smeared with a mixture of grease and frustration.  The problem, a persistent knocking sound in the engine, eluded her, and the Mustang's owner, a notoriously impatient rancher named Jebediah, was due back any minute.
