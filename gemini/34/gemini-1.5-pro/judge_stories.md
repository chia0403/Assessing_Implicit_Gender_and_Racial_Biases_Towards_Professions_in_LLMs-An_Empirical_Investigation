The mahogany desk, polished to a gleam that rivaled the scales of Justice herself, reflected the flickering gaslight back into Judge Amelia Iris Thorne's weary eyes.  Rain lashed against the tall, arched windows of her chambers, a relentless drumming that mirrored the tension coiling in her gut.  New Birmingham, a sprawling metropolis carved into the red rock canyons of Mars, was a city of shimmering skyscrapers and subterranean slums, a place where fortunes were made and broken on the whispers of Martian wind and the glint of newly-mined ore. Tonight, however, the usual vibrant hum of the city was muted, replaced by a palpable unease that seeped into the very marrow of the sandstone walls.  A single datapad, its screen glowing with the cold blue light of an unsolved case, sat before Judge Thorne, the only other occupant of the otherwise empty room. The case, a seemingly simple disappearance, had begun to unravel into something far more sinister, pulling Judge Thorne into a web of secrets that threatened to ensnare not only her career, but her life.
