The biting November wind whipped around Elias Thorne, stinging his cheeks and tugging at the brim of his worn baseball cap.  He squinted at the dilapidated Victorian mansion looming before him, its gingerbread trim peeling like sunburnt skin.  Perched atop a windswept hill overlooking the bleak, slate-grey expanse of the North Sea, Eventide Manor looked more like a haunted house in a penny dreadful than a viable wiring job.  Elias, his breath clouding in the frigid air, hefted his toolbox, the metal cold against his gloved hand.  The call had come in late last night, a frantic woman’s voice crackling over the line about flickering lights and a persistent burning smell. He’d been the only electrician in the small coastal town of Port Blossom brave – or foolish – enough to answer.
