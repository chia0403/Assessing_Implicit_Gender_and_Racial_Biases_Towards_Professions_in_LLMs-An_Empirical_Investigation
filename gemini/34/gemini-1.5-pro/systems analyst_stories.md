Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of the Zenith Tower, blurring the already fading twilight into a swirling grey soup.  Forty-two stories below, the Chicago streets glittered with reflected neon, a kaleidoscope fractured by the downpour.  Inside, the air buzzed with the low hum of servers and the insistent chirping of Eleanor Vance's neglected phone.  Eleanor, a systems analyst with the impossibly long title of Senior Network Architecture and Optimization Specialist, hunched deeper into her ergonomic chair, her brow furrowed in concentration.  Lines of code scrolled across her dual monitors, a cryptic language she navigated with practiced ease.  Tonight, however, the familiar comfort of the digital world offered little solace. A rogue process, a digital phantom, was wreaking havoc within Zenith's internal network, and Eleanor, despite her considerable skills, was drawing a blank.  The problem wasn't just technical; it was personal. This wasn't just a glitch; it felt like a deliberate attack. And Eleanor had a sinking suspicion she knew who was behind it.
