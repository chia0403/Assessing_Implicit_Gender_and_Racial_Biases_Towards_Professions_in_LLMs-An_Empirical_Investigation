The scent of eucalyptus and bergamot hung heavy in the air, a fragrant shroud against the relentless Seattle drizzle.  Rain lashed against the large, arched window of Serenity Now Massage Therapy, blurring the emerald green of the Olympic Mountains in the distance.  Inside, the warm glow of salt lamps cast flickering shadows across the exposed brick walls, creating a haven of calm amidst the storm.  Eleanor Vivienne Ainsworth, her dark hair pulled back in a loose braid, adjusted the thermostat a fraction of a degree higher.  She’d learned early in her career that a truly relaxing massage required more than just skilled hands; it demanded a perfectly orchestrated environment.  Today, however, the tension humming beneath the surface of her carefully curated calm felt as palpable as the humidity clinging to her skin.  Her first client of the day, a regular known for his cryptic pronouncements and unnerving stillness, was due any minute, and something – a prickle at the back of her neck, a sense of unease she couldn’t quite place – told her this session would be anything but ordinary.
