Rain lashed against the grimy windows of The Daily Clarion, blurring the neon glow of "Joe's Eats" across the street. Inside, the air hung thick with the smell of stale coffee and deadline pressure.  Eleanor Vance Holloway, her trench coat dripping onto the threadbare carpet, tapped a frantic rhythm on her chipped mug. The clock above the water cooler blinked 11:57 pm. Three minutes. Three minutes until her editor, a man whose veins seemed to run on printer ink and cynicism, would bellow for her copy.  Ashworth, Indiana, population dwindling, morale lower, was not known for its thrilling news stories.  Tonight, however, Eleanor had a feeling that was about to change.  A crumpled flyer on her desk, advertising a travelling circus newly arrived on the outskirts of town, depicted a clown with eyes that seemed to follow her every move.  Beneath the garish makeup, something about that painted smile chilled her to the bone.
