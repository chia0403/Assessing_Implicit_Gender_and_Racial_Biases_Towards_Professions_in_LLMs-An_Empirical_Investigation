The biting October wind whipped at Amelia “Amy” Hawthorne’s cheeks, turning them the same shade as the crimson maple leaves swirling around her work boots.  Frost crunched underfoot as she surveyed the sprawling grounds of Eventide Manor, a gothic behemoth perched precariously on the edge of the cliff overlooking the churning grey waters of the North Sea.  Gargoyles, eroded by centuries of salt spray, leered down at her from the parapets. Eventide had been empty for nearly a decade, its once glorious gardens now a tangle of overgrown ivy, thorny rose bushes, and weeds that stretched towards the sky like skeletal fingers. Amy, clad in her usual worn overalls and a thick fleece, had landed the contract to tame this wilderness, a daunting task even for her capable hands. But the solitude, the wild beauty of the place, and the unsettling whispers of Eventide’s history, drew her in like a moth to a flickering flame.  This wasn't just a job; it felt like a calling.  The sea roared below, a constant, ominous soundtrack to her solitary task.
