The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne's auburn hair across her face as she hurried across the deserted parking lot of St. Jude's Memorial Hospital.  Fluorescent lights spilled from the Emergency Room entrance, painting the swirling snow a sickly yellow.  Inside, Amelia knew, chaos reigned. It was always like this on a Friday night, but the first blizzard of the season had amplified the usual frenzy tenfold. The air crackled with a strange mixture of anticipation and dread, a feeling Amelia had come to associate with the unpredictable dance of life and death played out nightly within those sterile walls.  St. Jude's, a sprawling brick behemoth perched on a hill overlooking the bleak industrial landscape of Ashworth, Pennsylvania, had been Amelia's second home for the past five years.  Tonight, it felt more like a battlefield.
