The fluorescent lights of the "Everyday Essentials" store hummed, a monotonous drone that seemed to vibrate through Amelia Grace Blackwood's very bones.  Dust motes danced in the pale light, reflecting off the chipped enamel of the register, a stark contrast to the vibrant colours of the colourful, yet ultimately mundane, products lining the shelves.  Amelia, a cashier with a perpetual frown etched into her teenage face, stared blankly at the overflowing basket of a customer:  a basket overflowing with an improbable collection of brightly coloured plastic containers, questionable-looking cleaning supplies, and a single, unusually large, fuzzy yellow sponge.  The customer, a woman with eyes that seemed to hold the weight of the world, sighed loudly.  The store, a sprawling, utilitarian space with the faint smell of disinfectant and stale coffee, felt, as always, like a suffocating cage.
