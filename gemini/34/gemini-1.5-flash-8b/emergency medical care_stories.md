Rain lashed against the corrugated iron roof of the clinic, mimicking the frantic rhythm of Dr. Eleanor Vance's heart.  The flickering fluorescent lights cast long, distorted shadows across the cluttered examination room, highlighting the spatter of dried blood on the linoleum floor.  The air hung thick with the metallic tang of antiseptic and something else, something acrid and faintly sweet, like burning sugar.  Outside, the storm raged, a terrifying counterpoint to the quiet desperation that had settled over the small town of Havenwood.  Eleanor, her name etched into the fabric of this emergency room, knew this was no ordinary night.  This was Havenwood's night.
