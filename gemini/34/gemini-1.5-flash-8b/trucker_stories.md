The cracked asphalt shimmered under the relentless Arizona sun, a shimmering mirage mocking the sweat dripping down Bartholomew "Barty"  Buckley's face.  Dust devils danced across the vast expanse of the I-40, swirling around the cab of his beat-up, but loyal, Freightliner.  Barty, a seasoned trucker with calloused hands and eyes that held the weariness of a thousand miles, gripped the worn steering wheel.  His destination: the ghost town of Harmony Gulch, a place whispered about in hushed tones by other drivers, a place rumored to hold secrets older than the desert itself.  He'd been hired to deliver a crate, its contents a mystery even to him.  The only clue was a faded, almost illegible, note tucked inside the contract: "Deliver to the Widow's Well."
