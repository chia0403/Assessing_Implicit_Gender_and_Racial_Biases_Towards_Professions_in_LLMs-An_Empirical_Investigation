The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Blackwood's hands, the faint scent of Earl Grey clinging to the steam.  From her window seat at "Wanderlust Voyages," the meticulously curated travel agency nestled on a cobbled street in the heart of Edinburgh, she gazed out at the relentless November rain.  Grey skies mirrored the mood in her office, a space crammed with travel brochures, meticulously pinned maps, and overflowing boxes of postcards depicting exotic locales.  Amelia, a woman with eyes that held the shimmering allure of distant horizons and a smile that could melt the iciest glacial fjord, sighed softly.  Today, the phone had been stubbornly silent.  A symphony of disappointed sighs had replaced the usual bustling chatter of clients, eager to escape the mundane realities of everyday life.  The usual rush of exotic itineraries and whispered travel secrets was muted, a stark contrast to the cheerful holiday bustle that usually filled the air.  Outside, the rain hammered against the windowpanes, a relentless counterpoint to her quiet desperation.
