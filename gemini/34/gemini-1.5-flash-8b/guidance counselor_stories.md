Rain lashed against the grimy windows of the high school, mirroring the tempest brewing inside Amelia "Amy" Cartwright.  The fluorescent lights hummed a monotonous tune, a soundtrack to the mounting anxieties swirling around her.  Amy, a guidance counselor at Northwood High for the past seven years, stared at the overflowing inbox of student concerns on her computer screen.  The usual mix of relationship drama, academic struggles, and existential angst was amplified by the palpable tension hanging over the school like a humid shroud.  A recent string of vandalism, culminating in the defacing of the school's iconic oak tree – a beloved landmark for generations – had chipped away at the already fragile sense of community.  The town of Northwood, nestled between the towering pines and the whispering streams of the Whispering Woods, felt like it was holding its breath.  Amy knew the unspoken fear: what if this was the beginning of something bigger, something darker? The scent of damp earth and pine needles clung to the air, a stark contrast to the sterile environment of the guidance office.  Her worn leather chair seemed to absorb the weight of her worries, the same way the quiet hallways of the school absorbed the hushed whispers of rumors.
