The biting November wind whipped Amelia Hayes's cashmere scarf around her neck, a futile attempt to ward off the chill seeping into her bones.  The glass skyscrapers of the New York City skyline, usually a dazzling spectacle, looked like jagged teeth against the bruised purple sky.  She hurried through the throngs of bundled-up pedestrians, her high-heeled shoes clicking impatiently on the slick pavement.  Inside the imposing marble entrance of  "Apex Industries," the city's undisputed financial behemoth, the hushed murmur of a thousand conversations, a symphony of ambition and anxiety, awaited her.  Amelia, Executive Vice President of Apex's burgeoning Fintech division, was already running late.  Again.
