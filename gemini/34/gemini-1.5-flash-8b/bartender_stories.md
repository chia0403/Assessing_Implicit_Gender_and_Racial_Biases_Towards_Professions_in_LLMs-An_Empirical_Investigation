The flickering neon sign of the Rusty Mug cast a sickly yellow glow over Amelia Petrova, painting her face in a ghostly pallor.  Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof, a relentless percussion that echoed the frantic rhythm of her own heart.  The small, smoky bar was a refuge, a sanctuary from the tempest brewing outside, but inside, the storm was no less fierce.  Empty glasses, etched with condensation, mirrored the emptiness swirling in her stomach. Tonight, the silence was deafening, punctuated only by the clinking of ice and the occasional sigh escaping Amelia's lips.  The Rusty Mug, usually a boisterous hub of late-night revelry, was eerily still, a stark contrast to the chaotic world outside.  The air hung thick with the scent of stale beer and desperation.  Amelia, a woman etched with the lines of countless late nights and whispered secrets, wiped a stray tear from her cheek with a practiced hand. Tonight, however, the secret tasted more bitter than any drink on her menu.
