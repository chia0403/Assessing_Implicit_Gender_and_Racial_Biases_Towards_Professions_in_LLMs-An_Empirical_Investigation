The fluorescent lights of the Sterling & Stone law firm hummed, a monotonous drone that mirrored the slow, deliberate pace of life within.  Dust motes danced in the afternoon sun slicing through the grimy windowpanes, illuminating a cloud of faint cigarette smoke clinging to the air, a testament to the late-afternoon rituals of the legal eagles.  Behind the polished mahogany reception desk, Amelia Hawthorne, a young woman with eyes the colour of stormy seas, meticulously filed a stack of legal documents.  Her fingers, stained a faint shade of sepia from years of handling paper, moved with practiced precision.
