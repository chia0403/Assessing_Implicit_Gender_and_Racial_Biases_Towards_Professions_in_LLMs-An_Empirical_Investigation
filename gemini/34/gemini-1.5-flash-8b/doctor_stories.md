The biting wind whipped at the eaves of the dilapidated clinic, rattling the ancient, copper-framed windows.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of sunlight piercing the gloom, illuminating a small, cluttered examination room.  Dr. Elara Vance, her auburn hair pulled back tightly in a braid, hunched over a patient slumped on a chipped wooden table.  The air hung heavy with the metallic tang of antiseptic and the faint, cloying sweetness of desperation.  Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof, a relentless percussion mirroring the frantic rhythm of Elara's own heart.  Outside, the sprawling, impoverished district of Oakhaven sprawled in a blanket of grey, the skeletal remains of forgotten industries hinting at a history both vibrant and broken.
