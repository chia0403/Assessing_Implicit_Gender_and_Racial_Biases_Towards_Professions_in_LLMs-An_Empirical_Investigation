The fluorescent lights of the "Chronicles of the Cosmos" editorial office hummed, a monotonous soundtrack to Amelia Petrova's relentless typing.  Dust motes danced in the lone shaft of sunlight piercing the grimy window, illuminating the swirling vortex of coffee stains and crumpled manuscripts that littered her small, cluttered desk.  Amelia, a senior editor at the prestigious but increasingly obscure science fiction magazine, "Cosmic Horizons," stared blankly at the glowing screen, the words blurring into a meaningless jumble.  The deadline loomed, a concrete beast threatening to crush her under its weight.  Outside, the city of Neo-Veridia thrummed with the frantic energy of a dying star, oblivious to the quiet crisis brewing within the magazine's hallowed walls.
