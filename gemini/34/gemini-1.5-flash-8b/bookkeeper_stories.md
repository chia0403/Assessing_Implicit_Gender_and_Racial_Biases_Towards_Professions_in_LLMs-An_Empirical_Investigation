The chipped Formica countertop, stained a dull beige from countless spilled coffees and the inevitable crumbs of Amelia "Amy" Cartwright's meticulously organized life, reflected the flickering fluorescent lights of the "Numbers & Nibbles" accounting office.  Dust motes danced in the meagre rays, illuminating a sea of meticulously filed invoices, tax forms, and receipts that formed the backbone of Amy's existence.  The air, thick with the scent of stale coffee and the faint, cloying sweetness of cheap office pastries, hung heavy in the air, a constant reminder of the nine-to-five grind.  Amy, a bookkeeper with a penchant for precision and an even greater fondness for the quiet hum of numbers, was hunched over her ledger, a fountain pen poised above a line of figures that seemed to stretch into infinity.  Outside, the city thrummed with a life she barely registered. Her world was contained within these four walls, these spreadsheets and balance sheets, and for the moment, it felt entirely satisfactory.
