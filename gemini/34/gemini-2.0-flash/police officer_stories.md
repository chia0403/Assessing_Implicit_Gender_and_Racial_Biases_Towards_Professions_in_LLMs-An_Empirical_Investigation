The cloying sweetness of honeysuckle, thick and suffocating, did little to mask the metallic tang of blood in the humid air. Detective Isabella “Izzy” Diaz, her navy blue uniform sticking uncomfortably to her skin, pushed past the yellow police tape, the insistent buzz of cicadas a frantic counterpoint to the scene before her. The small, whitewashed house, usually brimming with the laughter of children according to the neighbors, now stood silent, a stark contrast to the vibrant bougainvillea climbing its porch. This was Magnolia Street in Bayou Blanc, Louisiana, a place where secrets festered beneath the surface of southern hospitality, and Izzy knew, with a sinking feeling in her gut, this particular secret was about to unravel in the most brutal way imaginable.
