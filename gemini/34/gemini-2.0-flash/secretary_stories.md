The aroma of burnt coffee hung thick in the air of the "Executive Solutions" office, clinging to the drab, beige carpet and the motivational posters no one actually read. Outside, the Chicago wind howled a mournful tune against the towering skyscrapers, a stark contrast to the muted hum of fluorescent lights buzzing overhead.

For Penelope Astrid Bellweather, it was just another Tuesday. Penelope, or Penny as she preferred to be called, surveyed the chaos of her desk: a precariously stacked mountain of invoices, a half-eaten bagel slowly hardening under a Post-it note graveyard, and a ringing phone that she desperately hoped would stop before her sanity completely unraveled.

"Executive Solutions, this is Penelope speaking, how can I direct your call?" she asked, the practiced cheerfulness in her voice a carefully constructed mask over the low-grade dread that had become her constant companion.
