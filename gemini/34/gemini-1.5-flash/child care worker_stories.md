The chipped paint of the "Sunshine Smiles Daycare" sign flaked onto the overgrown ivy clinging to the brick wall, mirroring the slow decay of Elara Jean Montgomery's optimism.  It was a damp Tuesday morning in Oakhaven, a town so perpetually overcast it felt as though the sun had simply forgotten its way back.  The air hung heavy with the scent of wet leaves and the faint, underlying tang of old crayons – a perfume Elara had come to associate intimately with the chaotic symphony of childhood that awaited her inside.  The rusted swing set groaned a mournful tune in the wind, a soundtrack to Elara’s own quiet anxieties.  Today, however, felt different.  A particularly unsettling premonition clung to her like the damp chill of the November air.
