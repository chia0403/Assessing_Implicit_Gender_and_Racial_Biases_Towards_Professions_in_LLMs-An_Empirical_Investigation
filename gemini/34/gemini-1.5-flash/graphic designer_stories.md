The chipped ceramic mug warmed Amelia Hernandez’s hands, the lukewarm chamomile doing little to soothe the simmering frustration brewing inside her.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of her studio apartment, a relentless drumming that mirrored the chaotic rhythm of unfinished projects scattered across her desk.  Empty coffee cups, crumpled sketches, and a half-eaten bag of sourdough pretzels formed a precarious landscape around her MacBook, its screen displaying a rejected logo design – a vibrant, swirling phoenix that had, apparently, looked “too aggressive” for the client’s new line of organic baby food.  Amelia sighed, the scent of rain and petrichor doing little to alleviate the sting of yet another creative rejection.  Her studio, a converted attic space in a bustling Brooklyn brownstone, usually felt inspiring, a haven of creativity. Today, however, it felt like a cage.
