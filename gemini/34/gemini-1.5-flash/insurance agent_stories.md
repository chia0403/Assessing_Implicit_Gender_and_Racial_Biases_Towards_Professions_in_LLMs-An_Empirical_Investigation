The humid Louisiana air hung thick and heavy, clinging to Amelia Jean Dubois like a second skin.  Cicadas buzzed a relentless, maddening chorus from the cypress trees that lined the cracked asphalt road leading to Bayou Boeuf.  Amelia Jean, a woman whose sensible beige suit clashed spectacularly with the vibrant, almost violent, green of the surrounding swamp, adjusted her collar, a small bead of sweat tracing a path down her meticulously applied makeup.  Her sensible, sensible shoes sank slightly into the soft shoulder of the road as she navigated the potholes, the battered Ford Focus she drove bouncing with every impact. This wasn’t exactly the type of clientele she was used to – high-powered executives in downtown New Orleans – but a hefty commission was a hefty commission, and old Madame Evangeline’s peculiar insurance needs were proving surprisingly lucrative, if also profoundly unsettling.
