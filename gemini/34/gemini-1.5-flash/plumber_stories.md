The biting November wind whipped Amelia "Amy" Hernandez's already-chapped lips as she wrestled the heavy toolbox from the back of her beat-up Ford Transit van.  Rust-colored leaves swirled around her worn work boots, clinging to the damp concrete of the alleyway behind Mrs. Periwinkle's prize-winning petunia patch.  The air hung thick with the smell of damp earth and something vaguely…sulfurous.  Amy grimaced. This wasn't just a clogged drain; this smelled like a sewer kraken had decided to stage a revolt.  The address, scrawled messily on the service request – 13 Grimalkin Lane – only added to the ominous feeling that clung to the air like the lingering scent of cheap cologne.
