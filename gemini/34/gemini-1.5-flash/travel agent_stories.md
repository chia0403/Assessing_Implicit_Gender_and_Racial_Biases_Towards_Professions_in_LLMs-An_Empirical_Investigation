The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Katherine “Amy” Finch’s hands, the lukewarm tea doing little to dispel the chill that had settled deep in her bones, a chill far colder than the November wind rattling the windowpanes of her tiny travel agency.  Rain lashed against the glass, blurring the already indistinct view of cobbled St. Giles Street in Oxford.  Amy, her auburn hair pulled back in a practical braid, sighed, the sound swallowed by the rhythmic drumming of the rain and the low hum of her ancient computer.  "Wanderlust World Tours," the faded sign above the door proclaimed, a promise whispered to the empty street.  Inside, the air smelled faintly of old paper, coffee, and the lingering scent of exotic spices from a forgotten shipment of brochures – a heady mix that perfectly captured the essence of Amy's life, a constant blend of the familiar and the far-flung, the mundane and the magical.
