Professor Evelyn Beatrice Ainsworth adjusted her spectacles, the faint scent of old paper and pipe tobacco clinging to the lenses.  Rain lashed against the leaded-glass windows of her study, a frantic tattoo against the ancient oak paneling.  Outside, the grounds of Blackwood Manor – a sprawling Victorian behemoth perched precariously on the windswept cliffs overlooking the turbulent North Sea – were shrouded in a swirling mist that swallowed the skeletal branches of the ancient yew trees whole.  The only light came from the flickering flames in the hearth, casting long, dancing shadows that writhed across the densely packed bookshelves that lined every wall, their spines a testament to decades of scholarly pursuits.  Tonight, however, Evelyn felt the comforting weight of her research was no match for the chilling premonition that had settled over her like the encroaching fog.  Something was wrong.  Terribly wrong.
