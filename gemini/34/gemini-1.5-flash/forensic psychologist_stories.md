The biting November wind whipped Dr. Eleanor Vance’s auburn hair across her face as she surveyed the scene.  Rain, a relentless, sideways deluge, plastered her trench coat to her skin, mirroring the grim dampness clinging to the sprawling Victorian mansion before her.  Greystone Manor, they called it.  More accurately, it should have been called Greystone Morbidity, considering the events that had unfolded within its decaying walls.  The air hung heavy with the scent of wet earth and something else, something acrid and faintly metallic that clung to the back of Eleanor’s throat – the lingering ghost of violence.  The skeletal branches of ancient oaks clawed at the bruised sky, their silhouettes stark against the gathering gloom, mirroring the twisted wreckage of lives inside.
