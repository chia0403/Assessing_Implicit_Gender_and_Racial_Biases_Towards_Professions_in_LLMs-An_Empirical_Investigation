The salt spray stung Elias Thorne's face as he wrestled the stubborn oak plank onto the scaffolding.  Below him, the churning grey Atlantic gnawed at the jagged rocks of the Cornish coast, a relentless rhythm accompanying the rhythmic thud of his hammer.  The air, thick with the scent of brine and freshly sawn timber, carried the mournful cry of gulls overhead.  Elias, his weathered hands calloused and strong, paused, wiping a streak of sweat from his brow with the back of his hand. The half-built lighthouse, a skeletal giant against the turbulent sky, loomed above him, a testament to both his skill and the unforgiving nature of the sea.  This wasn't just another job; it was a battle against the elements, a race against the coming storm.
