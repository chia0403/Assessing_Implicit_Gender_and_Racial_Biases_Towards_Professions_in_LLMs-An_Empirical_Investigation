The rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of Elias Thorne’s shed, a relentless percussion accompanying the rhythmic tap-tap-tap of his fingers on the keyboard.  Inside, the air hung thick with the scent of stale coffee and ozone, a familiar perfume to Elias, a programmer who preferred the company of flickering screens and whirring hard drives to the unpredictable chaos of human interaction.  His shed, perched precariously on the edge of a windswept Cornish cliff, overlooked a churning grey sea that mirrored the tempestuous code wrestling on his monitor.  The only light came from the dual monitors illuminating his face, casting his features in a harsh, flickering glow, highlighting the dark circles under his bloodshot eyes – testament to a sleepless night spent chasing a particularly elusive bug.  Tonight, however, the bug wasn’t in his code.  Tonight, it seemed, the bug was outside, lurking in the storm.
