The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Mia” Hernandez’s hair across her face as she navigated the crumbling steps of the Carver Projects.  Grey skies bled into a bruised purple horizon, mirroring the bleakness clinging to the air thicker than the exhaust fumes choking the narrow streets.  Each chipped brick and graffiti-tagged wall whispered tales of hardship and despair – a backdrop perfectly suited to Mia’s profession.  She adjusted the worn leather satchel slung across her shoulder, its contents – a well-thumbed copy of the city’s probation guidelines, a half-eaten granola bar, and a flickering hope – representing her arsenal in this urban battlefield.  Tonight, her target was apartment 3B, home to a repeat offender with a penchant for petty theft and an even stronger aversion to authority.  The flickering porch light above the door cast long, dancing shadows that seemed to mock her already dwindling optimism.
