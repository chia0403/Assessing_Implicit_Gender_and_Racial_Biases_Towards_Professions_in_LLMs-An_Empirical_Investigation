The scent of argan oil and burnt sugar hung heavy in the air, a familiar perfume to Seraphina "Sera" Moreau.  It clung to the plush velvet chairs of her salon, "Shear Genius," a tiny oasis of calm nestled incongruously between a boisterous taco truck and a perpetually-under-renovation laundromat on the grittier edge of Belleville, New Jersey.  Rain lashed against the large, slightly grimy windowpanes, mirroring the tempest brewing inside Sera herself.  Tonight, the usual soothing rhythm of clippers and the gentle hum of the hair dryers was absent, replaced by the frantic clicking of her long, scarlet nails as she tapped impatiently on her phone.  A crucial appointment, one that could catapult her fledgling business into the stratosphere – or send it crashing down in a spectacular, financially crippling mess – hung precariously in the balance.
