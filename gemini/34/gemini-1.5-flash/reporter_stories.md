The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s trench coat around her legs as she hunched deeper into the shadows of the crumbling Victorian mansion.  Rain, icy and relentless, plastered her already damp auburn hair to her forehead.  Ivy, thick as pythons, clawed at the decaying stonework of Blackwood Manor, its gnarled fingers seemingly reaching out to grasp her.  The air hung heavy with the scent of damp earth and something else… something subtly metallic, like old blood.  Amelia adjusted the worn leather strap of her reporter’s bag, the weight of her notepad and half-empty flask a small comfort against the pervasive chill, both physical and otherwise. Tonight, Blackwood Manor wasn't just a haunted house whispered about in hushed tones by locals; tonight, it was the scene of her next story.  A story that smelled decidedly of murder.
