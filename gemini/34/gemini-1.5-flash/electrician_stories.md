The salt spray stung Amelia Hernandez's face as she wrestled the heavy coil of marine-grade cable onto the groaning deck of the *Seraphina*.  The late afternoon sun, a bruised plum against the churning grey Atlantic, cast long shadows that danced across the rust-eaten hull.  Amelia, a woman whose calloused hands belied a sharp mind and even sharper wit, adjusted her safety glasses, the wind whipping strands of her dark braid across her cheek. The air hung thick with the smell of brine, diesel, and the faint, metallic tang of ozone – a familiar perfume to any seasoned electrician, and particularly to Amelia, who was currently battling a short in the *Seraphina’s* ancient electrical system somewhere deep within its bowels.  The fishing trawler, a relic of a bygone era, groaned and creaked around her like a wounded beast, its aged timbers protesting every movement.
