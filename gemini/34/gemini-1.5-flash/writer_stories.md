The chipped mug warmed Amelia Katherine Blackwood’s hands, the lukewarm tea doing little to soothe the icy dread that had settled in her bones.  Rain lashed against the leaded-glass windows of her writing cottage, a relentless drumming that mirrored the frantic tattoo of her own heart.  Nestled deep within the ancient, windswept cliffs of Cornwall, overlooking a churning, grey Atlantic, the cottage, known locally as "Seawrack," was usually a sanctuary. Tonight, it felt like a prison. The scent of brine and woodsmoke, normally comforting, was overwhelmed by the metallic tang of fear.  Amelia stared out at the storm, the rhythmic crash of waves a counterpoint to the unsettling silence within the cottage, a silence broken only by the erratic tick-tock of a grandfather clock in the hall.  The manuscript lay abandoned on her desk, its pages fluttering slightly in the draught, a stark testament to the story she was failing to write – a story that was now, terrifyingly, unfolding around her.
