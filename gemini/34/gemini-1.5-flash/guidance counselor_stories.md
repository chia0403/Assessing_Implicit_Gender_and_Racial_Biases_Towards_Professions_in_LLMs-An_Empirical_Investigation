The chipped paint of the "Guidance Counselor" door seemed to mirror the cracks appearing in Elara Vance's meticulously organized life.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of Northwood High, blurring the already-somber November landscape into a watercolour wash of grey and brown. Inside, the air hung thick with the scent of old coffee and simmering teenage anxieties. Elara, a woman whose tailored suits usually projected an image of calm competence, felt anything but.  The overflowing inbox on her computer hummed a discordant tune to the rhythmic drumming of the rain, a symphony of impending deadlines and unresolved crises.  Today, however, was different. Today, the weight of a specific, looming problem threatened to unravel even her usually impeccable composure.  The crumpled note, still clutched in her hand, felt like a burning brand.  It read simply: "Help me."
