The salt spray stung Amelia Hernandez’s face as the ferry churned through the choppy waters of Puget Sound.  The late afternoon sun, a bruised plum in the rapidly darkening sky, cast long shadows across the Olympic Mountains, their snow-capped peaks shimmering like distant, ethereal cities.  Amelia, a litigator for the prestigious Seattle firm of Blackwood & Thorne, usually preferred the sterile, air-conditioned calm of her office.  But this case, a tangled web of maritime law and alleged corporate malfeasance involving the failing King Salmon Cannery, demanded a different approach.  The air smelled of brine and diesel, a stark contrast to the polished mahogany and leather she was accustomed to. Tonight, she wasn’t just arguing a case; she was breathing it in.
