The scent of sandalwood and chamomile hung heavy in the air, a comforting balm against the relentless drumming of the Seattle rain against the frosted-glass windows of Serenity Spa.  Rain, Elias Thorne knew, was the soundtrack to life in this city, a constant, low hum beneath the city’s vibrant pulse.  He adjusted the warm, fluffy towel draped over the massage table, the soft fabric a stark contrast to the chilling dampness seeping in from the outside.  Tonight, however, the usual soothing rhythm of the rain was punctuated by a different kind of tension – a knot of anxiety that Elias felt tightening in his own chest, mirroring the one he suspected awaited him beneath the shoulders of his next client, Ms. Evangeline Bellweather.
