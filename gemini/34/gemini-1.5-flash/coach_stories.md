The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Amy” Hernandez’s hair across her face as she surveyed the sodden soccer field.  Floodlights cut through the swirling rain, illuminating the slick, muddy expanse and the skeletal remains of the goalposts, bent at unnatural angles from the recent storm.  The air hung heavy with the scent of damp earth and the faint, lingering aroma of hotdogs from the concession stand – a stark contrast to the chilling reality of the situation.  Amy, head coach of the struggling Westside Wildcats Under-16 girls' soccer team, shivered, not entirely from the cold.  The Wildcats were on a losing streak that threatened to derail their season, and the upcoming match against the undefeated Northwood Eagles loomed like a particularly menacing storm cloud.  The pressure was immense, a suffocating weight pressing down on her already weary shoulders.  Tonight, more than just a game hung in the balance; it was the fragile hope of a team, and perhaps, Amy’s own future as a coach.
