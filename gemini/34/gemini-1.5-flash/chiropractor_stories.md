The salt spray stung Dr. Eleanor Vance's face as she wrestled the stubborn fishing net onto the weathered wooden dock.  The air, thick with the scent of brine and decaying seaweed, whipped her dark hair across her eyes.  She squinted, the setting sun painting the sky in fiery hues of orange and purple over the choppy waters of the Atlantic.  This wasn't exactly the typical setting for a chiropractor, but then again, Eleanor Vance wasn't your typical chiropractor.  Her practice, "Coastal Chiropractic," nestled precariously on stilts above the rocky shoreline of  Port Blossom, Maine, was as much a part of the rugged landscape as the craggy cliffs and the ever-present gulls.  The rhythmic crash of waves against the pilings provided a constant, almost hypnotic, soundtrack to her day.  Tonight, however, the usual serenity was fractured by a growing unease, a prickling sensation at the back of her neck that had nothing to do with a slipped vertebra.
