The salt spray stung Amelia Katherine Blackwood's face as she leaned against the railing of the *Sea Serpent*, the fishing trawler pitching violently in the churning North Sea.  Amelia, or Amy as she preferred, usually found inspiration in the quiet solitude of her Cornish cottage, the rhythmic tap-tap-tap of rain against the windowpanes a far more agreeable soundtrack than the current cacophony of gulls and groaning timbers.  But this – this raw, untamed expanse of grey water, whipped into a frenzy by a November gale – was precisely what her new novel demanded.  She needed the visceral, the untamed, the desperate fight for survival mirrored in the tumultuous lives of her characters.  The manuscript, titled *The Kraken's Grip*, lay unfinished in her satchel, a mocking testament to her writer's block, a block only the relentless, unforgiving ocean seemed capable of shattering.
