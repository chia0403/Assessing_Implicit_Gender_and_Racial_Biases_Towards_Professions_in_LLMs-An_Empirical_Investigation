The chipped paint of Judge Abigail "Abi" Hawthorne's chambers reflected the bruised twilight sky outside.  Rain lashed against the leaded-glass windows of the old courthouse, a relentless rhythm mirroring the relentless ache in her shoulder, a souvenir from a particularly aggressive suspect's attempt at a courtroom escape three months prior.  The air hung thick with the scent of old books and something faintly metallic, a lingering ghost of the day's proceedings: a bitter custody battle that had left everyone feeling raw and depleted.  Beyond the rain-streaked panes, the city of Oakhaven sprawled, a tapestry woven with glittering lights and shadowed alleys, a place where justice, Abi often mused, was as elusive as a summer breeze.  Tonight, however, the only breeze she felt was the chill that crept from the damp stone walls of the building that had been her professional home, and increasingly, her prison, for the last twenty years.
