The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Katherine Finch’s hands, a meager comfort against the November chill that seeped through the single-paned window of her office.  Dust motes, illuminated by the weak afternoon sun, danced in the shafts of light slicing through the gloom of Finch & Finch Accounting.  The air hung thick with the scent of aged paper, ink, and something vaguely resembling despair – a scent Amelia had come to associate intimately with the last decade of her life spent hunched over ledgers in this very room, a room inherited, along with the business, from her late, eccentric Uncle Silas.  Outside, the wind howled a mournful tune, rattling the loose windowpanes and mimicking the frantic scribbling of her pen as she meticulously reconciled a particularly stubborn account for Mr. Grimshaw’s failing haberdashery.  The rhythmic tap-tap-tap of her adding machine provided a counterpoint to the storm’s fury, a small, predictable beat in the otherwise chaotic symphony of impending winter and dwindling clients.
