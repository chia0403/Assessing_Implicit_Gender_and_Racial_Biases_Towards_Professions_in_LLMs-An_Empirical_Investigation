The scent of acetone and lilac hung heavy in the air, a familiar perfume to Seraphina “Sera” Nightingale.  Sera, whose nimble fingers could coax a chipped nail back to perfection in minutes, sighed, the sound swallowed by the low hum of the salon’s ventilation system.  Outside, the relentless Chicago wind howled a mournful tune against the plate-glass windows of "Nails & Nibblers," a tiny oasis of calm nestled amongst the bustling chaos of Michigan Avenue.  Rain lashed against the glass, blurring the glittering cityscape into a hazy impressionist painting.  Inside, the only sounds were the gentle whir of the electric file in Sera's hand and the soft crackle of the fireplace, its flames licking at a meticulously arranged stack of perfectly polished magazines.  Tonight, however, the usual comforting rhythm of her work was fractured by a gnawing unease, a feeling as sharp and unexpected as a rogue splinter under a freshly manicured nail.
