The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s auburn hair across her face as she hurried down Bleecker Street, the scent of roasting chestnuts and damp earth clinging to the crisp air.  Rain, a fine, persistent drizzle, slicked the cobblestones, reflecting the hazy glow of the streetlights in shimmering puddles.  Amelia, a nutritionist with a practice nestled above a bustling Italian deli on the same street, tugged her worn cashmere scarf tighter, the familiar weight a small comfort against the chill.  The aroma of oregano and garlic, potent even through the rain, promised a welcome warmth inside, but tonight, even the promise of pasta couldn’t fully chase away the unsettling feeling that had settled in her stomach – a feeling far more profound than the usual pre-holiday rush.
