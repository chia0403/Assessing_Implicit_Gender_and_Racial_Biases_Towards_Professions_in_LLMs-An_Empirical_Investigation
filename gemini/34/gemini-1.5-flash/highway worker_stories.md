The sun beat down on Elias Thorne’s neck, baking the sweat already clinging to his grime-stained denim shirt.  He squinted, shielding his eyes with a calloused hand as he surveyed the shimmering asphalt ribbon of Highway 17, a ribbon that snaked its way through the unforgiving heart of the Mojave Desert.  Dust devils, miniature tornadoes of grit and sand, danced a chaotic ballet across the cracked earth alongside the road, their movements mirroring the restless churn in Elias’s own gut.  The air hung thick and heavy, the silence broken only by the distant drone of a semi-truck and the rhythmic thump-thump-thump of his own heart, a relentless percussion against the backdrop of the vast, empty landscape.  Today's task: patching a particularly nasty pothole – a crater, really – that threatened to swallow a car whole.  But Elias wasn't just worried about the pothole; a growing unease, sharper than the desert sun, gnawed at him. Something felt…off.
