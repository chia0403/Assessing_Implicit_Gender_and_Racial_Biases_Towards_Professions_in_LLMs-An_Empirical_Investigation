The mahogany desk, polished to a mirror sheen, reflected Amelia “Mia” Hernandez’s sharp, tired features.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of her 47th-floor office, blurring the cityscape of Chicago into a hazy, impressionistic painting.  The city, usually a source of exhilarating energy, tonight felt like a suffocating blanket, mirroring the weight pressing down on Mia’s shoulders.  She hadn’t slept properly in three days, the relentless demands of her position as Executive Vice President of Global Operations at Kestrel Industries leaving her feeling more depleted than a squeezed lemon.  The scent of expensive, but ultimately uncomforting, leather clung to her tailored suit; a stark contrast to the earthy aroma of the single, wilting orchid stubbornly clinging to life in a crystal vase on her desk.  Tonight, however, even the orchid seemed to droop under the weight of the impending crisis.
