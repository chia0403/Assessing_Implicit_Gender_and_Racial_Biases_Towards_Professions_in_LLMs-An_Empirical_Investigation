The insistent drumming of rain against the arched windowpane echoed the frantic tapping of Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s manicured nails on the mahogany desk.  Outside, the London fog, thick as pea soup, swallowed the neo-Gothic spires of St. Pancras station, reducing the usually bustling cityscape to a muted watercolour. Inside, the air hung heavy with the scent of stale pipe tobacco and the faint, metallic tang of fear. Amelia, secretary to the notoriously eccentric inventor, Professor Alistair Finch, adjusted the tortoiseshell frames of her spectacles and glanced nervously at the ornate grandfather clock ticking relentlessly in the corner. It was half-past midnight, three and a half hours past Professor Finch’s expected return, and the unnerving silence of his usually chaotic workshop, located just beyond the heavy oak door beside her desk, was starting to fray her nerves.
