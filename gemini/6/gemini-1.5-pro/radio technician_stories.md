The biting November wind whipped around Elias Thorne's ankles, swirling fallen leaves into miniature cyclones against the brick walls of the Wessex County Broadcasting Corporation building.  Built in the 1920s, the squat, three-story structure exuded an air of faded grandeur, like a dowager duchess clinging to the last vestiges of her youth. Inside, however, nestled amidst a chaotic sprawl of wires, vacuum tubes, and dusty equipment, was Elias's sanctuary – the radio repair room.  The year was 1958, rock 'n' roll was sweeping the nation, and Elias, with his calloused fingers and encyclopedic knowledge of radio frequencies, was the man who kept WCBC on the air. The flickering fluorescent lights buzzed overhead, casting long, distorted shadows that danced with the ghostly crackle of static emanating from a nearby receiver.  Tonight, however, the static was different.  It was more insistent, almost… intelligent.
