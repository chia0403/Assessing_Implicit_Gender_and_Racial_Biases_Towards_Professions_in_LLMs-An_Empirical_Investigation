The biting wind whipped Amelia “Amy” Reyes’s hair across her face as she hurried across the cobbled courtyard of Elderwood Manor.  Built in the late 1800s, the sprawling Victorian mansion had been converted into an assisted living facility decades ago, retaining its original charm – stained glass windows, ornate cornices, and a wraparound porch that creaked ominously in the gusts. Amy clutched her thermos of coffee tighter, the warm ceramic a small comfort against the November chill.  Inside, the grand oak doors awaited, promising a haven of warmth and the familiar, comforting scent of cinnamon and old books. Today, however, Amy felt a knot of unease tighten in her stomach. Mr. Henderson, a new resident and a renowned but reclusive astrophysicist, had refused to participate in any therapy sessions. His room, at the far end of the west wing, was shrouded in a persistent silence that unnerved even the seasoned staff. Amy took a deep breath, the icy air stinging her lungs. Today, she was determined to break through.
