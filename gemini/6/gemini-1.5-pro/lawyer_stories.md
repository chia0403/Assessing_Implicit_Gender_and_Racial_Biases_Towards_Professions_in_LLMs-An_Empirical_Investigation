Rain lashed against the arched windows of Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s office, mimicking the tempest brewing inside her.  The ornate clock on the mahogany bookshelf chimed seven times, each chime a hammer blow against the fragile peace she’d managed to construct after a grueling sixteen-hour workday.  Her office, perched high on the fifteenth floor of the prestigious Hawthorne & Blackwood building in downtown Chicago, usually offered a breathtaking panorama of the city skyline, glittering like a fallen constellation. Tonight, however, the city was shrouded in a thick, oppressive fog, mirroring the uncertainty swirling around her latest case.  The air was thick with the scent of old leather and Amelia’s signature Earl Grey tea, long since gone cold in a chipped porcelain mug.  Files, stacked precariously on every available surface, threatened to topple like dominoes, a physical manifestation of the mounting pressure she felt. This wasn't just any case; this was the one that could make or break her career, and perhaps, even her life.
