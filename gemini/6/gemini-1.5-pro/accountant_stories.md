Rain lashed against the leaded windows of Amelia Grace Holloway's office, a rhythmic drumming that mirrored the anxiety churning in her stomach.  The air in the small, cluttered room, usually stuffy with the scent of old paper and leather-bound ledgers, was thick with the metallic tang of ozone. Outside, the gothic spires of St. Jude's Church were swallowed by the swirling grey mist that clung to the cobbled streets of Arkham, Massachusetts. Amelia, her brow furrowed in concentration, tapped a manicured nail against a stack of invoices.  The neat columns of figures usually soothed her, a predictable order in a chaotic world, but tonight, they offered no comfort. The discrepancy, a mere $4.37 missing from the Cartwright Estate accounts, gnawed at her.  It was a negligible sum, easily explained by a misplaced decimal or a smudged receipt. Yet,  in a town like Arkham, where shadows stretched longer than reason and whispers held more weight than facts, even the smallest imbalance could herald something far more sinister.
