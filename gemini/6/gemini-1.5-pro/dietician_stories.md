The crisp October air, scented with woodsmoke and decaying leaves, nipped at Eleanor Vance's cheeks as she hurried down Sycamore Street.  Her breath puffed out in small white clouds, momentarily obscuring the wrought-iron gates of Ashworth Manor, her destination. The Manor, a sprawling Victorian edifice of faded grandeur, sat perched on a small hill overlooking the quaint, if somewhat sleepy, town of Alder Creek. Eleanor, a registered dietician with a penchant for sensible shoes and an even more pronounced aversion to tardiness, clutched her briefcase tighter, its contents – meal plans, nutritional charts, and a half-eaten granola bar – rustling with her quickened pace. She was five minutes behind schedule, a cardinal sin in her meticulously organized world, and the looming silhouette of Ashworth Manor, with its darkened windows and overgrown ivy, did little to soothe her anxieties.  She was here to revolutionize the dietary habits of its eccentric inhabitants, a task that seemed increasingly daunting with each step she took toward the imposing front door.
