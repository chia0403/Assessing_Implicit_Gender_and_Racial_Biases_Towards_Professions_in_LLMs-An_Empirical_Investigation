The rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of The Rusty Mug, a rhythm that only amplified the silence inside. Amelia “Amy” Dubois, her elbows propped on the sticky mahogany bar, watched the rivulets race each other down the grime-streaked window.  Nestled between a pawn shop and a laundromat on the fringes of downtown New Carthage, Louisiana, The Rusty Mug wasn't much to look at.  Its neon sign flickered erratically, promising "Coldest Beer in Town," a claim dubious at best.  The air inside hung thick with the scent of stale beer, cheap whiskey, and regret, a perfume Amy had grown accustomed to over her five years tending bar there. Tonight, however, even the usual low hum of loneliness seemed amplified by the storm raging outside.  She polished a glass with a practiced hand, the only sound the rhythmic squeak of the cloth against crystal, and waited.  Something was about to happen. She could feel it in the humid, charged air.
