The crisp October air, scented with woodsmoke and decaying leaves, nipped at Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s cheeks as she wrestled a “For Sale” sign into the frozen earth.  The wind, whipping down from the Shawangunk Ridge, plastered strands of her auburn hair across her face, momentarily obscuring the breathtaking view of the Hudson Valley spread out below.  This property, nestled on five acres just outside the quaint town of Cold Spring, New York, was a tough sell.  A fixer-upper, to put it mildly.  The once-grand Victorian mansion, now weathered and grey, slumped on its foundations like a tired dowager.  Its paint peeled, its gingerbread trim sagged, and several windows gaped like empty sockets. But Amelia, ever the optimist, saw potential where others saw only decay. This wasn't just a house; it was a story waiting to be rewritten.  And she, Amelia Grace Hawthorne, was just the real estate agent to help tell it.
