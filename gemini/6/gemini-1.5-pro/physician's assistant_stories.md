The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Amy” Reyes’s dark hair across her face as she hurried across the cobbled courtyard of St. Jude’s Community Hospital.  The ancient stone buildings, huddled together against the grey sky, seemed to exude a chill that seeped deeper than the mere weather.  St. Jude’s, nestled in the heart of the remote fishing village of Port Blossom, Maine, was a place of contrasts – modern medical equipment humming within centuries-old walls, the scent of antiseptic battling the briny tang of the nearby Atlantic. Amy tightened her fleece jacket, the embroidered "Physician Assistant" patch a small beacon of warmth against the gathering gloom.  The pager clipped to her hip buzzed insistently, its insistent tone a stark reminder that even in this isolated corner of the world, emergencies waited for no one.
