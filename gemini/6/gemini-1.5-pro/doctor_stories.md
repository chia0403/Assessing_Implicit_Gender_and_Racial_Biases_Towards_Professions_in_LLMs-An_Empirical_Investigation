The biting November wind whipped around Dr. Elias Thorne as he hurried across the cobbled courtyard of St. Jude's Hospital, the hem of his long coat snapping against his legs.  Gas lamps flickered feebly against the encroaching dusk, casting long, distorted shadows that danced across the aged brick walls.  Built in the 17th century, the hospital clung to the edge of the moor overlooking the churning grey sea, a solitary beacon against the desolate landscape of the Cornish coast.  Inside, a labyrinth of dimly lit corridors and wards whispered with the hushed anxieties of the sick and dying, a constant, low hum that Elias had long since grown accustomed to. Tonight, however, the air felt different, thicker with a palpable unease that prickled at the back of his neck.
