The fluorescent lights of the All-Brite Fluorescent Bulb Emporium hummed a monotonous B-flat, a counterpoint to the rhythmic squeak of Amelia "Amy" Cartwright's sensible pumps as she paced the linoleum floor. Outside, the Nevada sun beat down on the dusty asphalt of the Reno highway, baking the air to a shimmering mirage.  Inside, the air conditioning battled valiantly, but Amy could still feel a trickle of sweat tracing a path down her spine beneath her crisp, powder-blue pantsuit. It was the slowest Tuesday in the history of slow Tuesdays, and Amy, Reno's self-proclaimed "Fluorescent Queen," was beginning to feel the oppressive weight of unsold lumens.
