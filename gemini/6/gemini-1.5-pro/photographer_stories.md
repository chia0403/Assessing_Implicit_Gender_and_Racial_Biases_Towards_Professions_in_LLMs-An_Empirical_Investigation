The biting wind whipped strands of Amelia Reyes's dark hair across her face as she adjusted the tripod on the precipice of Blackheart Cliff.  Below, the churning slate-grey waters of the North Atlantic crashed against the jagged rocks, sending plumes of spray skyward.  The desolate beauty of the Isle of Skye, with its dramatic, cloud-shrouded mountains and windswept moorland, had drawn Amelia for years. This particular spot, notorious amongst the islanders for its unpredictable weather and treacherous footing, was where she hoped to capture the perfect storm photograph, one that would finally solidify her place in the prestigious world of nature photography.  The air crackled with an electric tension, the scent of rain and brine sharp in her nostrils. The light, a bruised purple hue, was fading fast, and Amelia knew she had perhaps an hour, maybe less, before the full fury of the approaching storm broke.
