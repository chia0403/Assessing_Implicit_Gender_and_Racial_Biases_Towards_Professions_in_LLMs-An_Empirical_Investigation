The biting wind whipped around Dr. Elias Thorne's ankles as he hurried across the rooftop helipad of St. Jude's Memorial Hospital.  Below, the sprawling metropolis of Chicago glittered, a cold, diamond-dusted tapestry against the inky pre-dawn sky.  Steam plumed from the ventilation shafts, ghosting into the swirling snow flurries.  Elias adjusted the collar of his coat, the raw December air a stark contrast to the sterile, controlled environment he was accustomed to.  His pager vibrated insistently against his hip, a shrill reminder of the urgency waiting for him inside - a heart procured across state lines, now racing against the clock, destined for the chest of a six-year-old girl.
