The crisp October air, scented with woodsmoke and decaying leaves, nipped at Amelia Rose Hawthorne’s cheeks as she unlocked the brightly painted door of Room 2B.  Sunbeams, fractured by the multi-coloured panes of the whimsical stained-glass window depicting a friendly caterpillar, danced across the miniature tables and chairs scattered around the room.  Glitter clung stubbornly to every surface, a testament to yesterday's enthusiastic art project.  Rainbow Ridge Kindergarten, nestled amidst the rolling hills of Vermont, was Amelia’s haven, a place where imagination reigned supreme and finger-painted masterpieces were celebrated with the same fervor as Renaissance art. This morning, however, a peculiar stillness hung in the air, a stark contrast to the usual pre-school cacophony.  Even Mr. Snuggles, the class guinea pig, sat unusually still in his cage, his tiny nose twitching nervously.
