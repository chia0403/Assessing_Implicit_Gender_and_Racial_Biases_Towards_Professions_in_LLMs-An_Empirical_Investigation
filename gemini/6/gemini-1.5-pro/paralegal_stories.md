Rain lashed against the grimy windows of the converted warehouse, mimicking the relentless drumming in Amelia Hayes's head.  Fluorescent lights hummed overhead, casting a sterile glow over the stacks of cardboard boxes crammed into the temporary office space.  Amelia, her full name Amelia Rose Hayes, tucked a stray strand of auburn hair behind her ear and sighed, rubbing the tension from her neck. The aroma of stale coffee and desperation hung heavy in the air.  They were three weeks behind schedule on the Peterson case, a sprawling class-action lawsuit against a pharmaceutical giant, and this makeshift document review center in the industrial district of Baltimore was starting to feel less like a temporary inconvenience and more like a purgatory. Outside, the November wind howled, a fitting soundtrack to the chaotic energy swirling around her as paralegals and junior associates scrambled to meet the impending deadline.  Amelia glanced at the clock: 11:47 pm. Another long night.  Another mountain of documents. Another reason to seriously reconsider her career choice.
