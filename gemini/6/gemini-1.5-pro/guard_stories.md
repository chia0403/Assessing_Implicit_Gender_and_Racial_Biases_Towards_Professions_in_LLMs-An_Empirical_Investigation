The biting wind whipped across the obsidian ramparts of Castle Drakon, stinging Elias Thorne's exposed cheeks.  He tugged his thick wool scarf tighter, the rough fabric scratching against his beard. Below him, the valley spread like a crumpled velvet cloak, the scattered lights of Oakhaven village twinkling feebly against the encroaching dusk.  Elias, Captain of the Night Guard for the past fifteen years, knew every shadowed alley and flickering lamp in that village as well as he knew the worn grooves in the stone beneath his boots.  Tonight, however, something felt different.  A prickle of unease danced on his skin, a sensation he hadn't experienced since the Goblin Wars fifteen years prior.  The air itself seemed to hum with a low, almost imperceptible vibration, and the usual comforting chorus of crickets was strangely silent.
