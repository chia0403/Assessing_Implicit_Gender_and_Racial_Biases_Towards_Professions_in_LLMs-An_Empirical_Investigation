The scent of beeswax and lavender hung heavy in the air, a comforting blanket against the chill November wind rattling the single pane windows of Elias Thorne's tailoring shop. Elias, a man whose fingers were as nimble as a hummingbird's wings and whose eyes held the patient wisdom of a seasoned craftsman, meticulously stitched a fine silk lining into a midnight-blue velvet coat.  The shop itself, nestled on a cobbled side street in the heart of the ancient city of Porthaven, was a chaotic haven of bolts of vibrant fabric, overflowing drawers brimming with buttons and threads, and half-finished garments draped artistically over every available surface.  Dust motes danced in the lone shaft of sunlight slicing through the grime on the window, illuminating a world of quiet industry and untold stories whispered in the rustling silk.
