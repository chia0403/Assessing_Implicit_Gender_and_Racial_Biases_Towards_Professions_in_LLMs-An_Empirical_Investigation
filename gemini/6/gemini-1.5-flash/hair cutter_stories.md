The scent of rosemary and sandalwood hung heavy in the air, a deliberate counterpoint to the slightly acrid tang of bleach that clung stubbornly to the worn velvet curtains.  Rain lashed against the leaded-glass windows of "Shear Perfection," blurring the already indistinct gaslight glow of the cobbled street outside.  Inside, amidst the comforting chaos of hairbrushes, combs, and half-empty bottles of brightly colored dyes, sat Aurelia Blackwood, her fingers deftly weaving through a tangled mess of auburn hair.  Her shears, gleaming under the single overhead lamp, moved with the practiced grace of a conductor leading an orchestra, each snip precise and deliberate.  The small salon, tucked away on a quiet side street in the heart of Victorian London's Spitalfields district, was her sanctuary, a refuge from the relentless grey of the city and the even greyer lives of her clientele.
