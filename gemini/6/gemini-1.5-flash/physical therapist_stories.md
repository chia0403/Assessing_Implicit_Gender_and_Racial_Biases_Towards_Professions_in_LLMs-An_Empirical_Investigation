The salt spray stung Dr. Eleanor Vance's face as she wrestled the stubborn kayak onto the rocky shore.  The late afternoon sun, a bruised plum sinking towards the churning grey Atlantic, cast long shadows across the jagged teeth of the Maine coastline.  Seabirds wheeled overhead, their cries a lonely counterpoint to the rhythmic crash of waves against the granite cliffs.  Eleanor, her PT uniform – usually crisp and immaculate – now damp and sand-strewn, hauled the kayak the final few yards towards her small, weather-beaten cottage, its windows gleaming like watchful eyes in the fading light.  The air, thick with the scent of brine and pine, carried a hint of woodsmoke, a comforting aroma promising warmth and the quiet solitude she craved after a particularly grueling day at the clinic.
