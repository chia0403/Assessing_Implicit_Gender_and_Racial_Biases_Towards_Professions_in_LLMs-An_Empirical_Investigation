The air hung thick and cloying, a humid blanket woven with the scent of sawdust, blood, and something indefinably *old*, like a forgotten cellar.  It clung to everything in Bartholomew "Barty" Croft's butcher shop, from the gleaming stainless steel cleavers hanging neatly on the wall to the worn wooden countertop where Barty himself stood, his strong hands meticulously trimming the fat from a plump leg of lamb.  Barty's wasn't just any butcher shop; it was a cornerstone of Oakhaven, a village nestled deep within the whispering woods of Devon, where time seemed to move at a slower, more deliberate pace, a pace dictated by the seasons and the rhythm of the livestock market. The shop itself, a quaint building with a sloping roof and a window displaying a colourful array of sausages, had stood for generations, its age etched into the weathered stonework.  Outside, the first hesitant rays of a dawn sun struggled to penetrate the dense canopy of ancient oaks, casting long, skeletal shadows across the cobblestone street.
