The wind, a rasping whisper against the granite cliffs, carried the scent of brine and something else, something acrid and unfamiliar.  Elias Thorne, his calloused hands stained the ochre of the sunset, paused his work.  The chisel, a familiar weight in his grip, lay abandoned beside the half-finished gargoyle perched precariously on the scaffolding.  Elias, his grey hair whipping around his weathered face, squinted at the hazy horizon where the churning sea met the bruised purple of the twilight sky.  He’d lived his entire fifty-three years in the shadow of these cliffs, carving figures from the stubborn stone, a life as unchanging and enduring as the rock itself. But tonight, something felt…different. The air hummed with a low, almost imperceptible thrum, a vibration that seemed to emanate from the very heart of the earth.  He was perched high above the village of Port Blossom, its twinkling lights a scattered constellation against the gathering gloom, a world seemingly oblivious to the unsettling change that hung heavy in the air.
