The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne's auburn hair across her face as she hurried down the cobbled street, the scent of woodsmoke and damp earth clinging to the air.  Rain, a relentless drizzle that had been falling since dawn, plastered her trench coat to her back.  She pulled the collar higher, her breath misting in the cold.  Amelia, an occupational therapist with a reputation for her innovative, almost unorthodox methods, was late, a fact that gnawed at her usually meticulous nature.  Her destination: Oakhaven Manor, a rambling Victorian house perched precariously on the cliff overlooking the churning grey expanse of the North Sea.  The manor, shrouded in mist and mystery, was home to its latest resident, a young woman grappling with the devastating aftermath of a car accident, and Amelia was determined to help her find her way back to life.
