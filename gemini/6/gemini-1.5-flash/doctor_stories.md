The biting wind whipped Dr. Eleanor Vance’s hair across her face as she wrestled the heavy oak door of Blackwood Manor open.  Gusts, smelling faintly of brine and decay, clawed at the ancient stonework, threatening to tear the groaning hinges from their sockets.  Blackwood Manor, perched precariously on the windswept cliffs overlooking the churning grey expanse of the North Sea, was a gothic monstrosity of crumbling turrets and shadowed windows.  Its silhouette, jagged against the bruised twilight sky, looked less like a dwelling and more like a skeletal hand reaching out from the sea to claim its next victim.  Inside, the air hung thick with the scent of damp earth and something else… something acrid and subtly metallic, that prickled at the back of Eleanor’s throat.  This was no ordinary house call.
