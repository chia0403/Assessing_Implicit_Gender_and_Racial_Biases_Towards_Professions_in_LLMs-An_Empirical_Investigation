The chipped porcelain cherub, its once-bright paint now flaking like sunburnt skin, stared accusingly at Seraphina “Sera” Moreau from atop a precarious pile of dusty antique furniture.  Dust motes, disturbed by the single shaft of sunlight slicing through the grimy window of the derelict Victorian mansion, danced in the air like mischievous sprites.  The air itself hung heavy with the scent of damp plaster, decaying wood, and a faint, unsettling sweetness – the ghostly perfume of a forgotten era.  Sera, a woman whose life was normally a kaleidoscope of vibrant fabrics and perfectly curated spaces, felt a shiver crawl down her spine. This wasn’t just a renovation project; it was an exorcism waiting to happen.  The grand, decaying Blackwood Manor, perched precariously on the windswept cliffs overlooking the turbulent Atlantic, was about to become her most challenging – and possibly her most haunted – undertaking.
