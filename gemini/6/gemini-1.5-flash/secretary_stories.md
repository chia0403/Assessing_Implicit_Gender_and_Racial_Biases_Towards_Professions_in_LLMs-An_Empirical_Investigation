The chipped ceramic mug warmed Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s fingers, a small comfort against the November chill that seeped through the drafty windows of Blackwood & Sons’ accounting firm.  Rain lashed against the panes, blurring the already grimy cityscape of Manchester into a watercolor wash of grey and black.  Amelia, perched on her worn, vinyl-covered chair, stared out at the downpour, the rhythmic tap-tap-tap of the rain a counterpoint to the relentless clatter of her typewriter.  The air hung heavy with the scent of old paper, stale coffee, and the faint, underlying aroma of desperation – a scent Amelia had grown accustomed to during her five years at Blackwood & Sons, a scent that seemed to permeate the very fabric of the building itself.  Tonight, however, the desperation felt different, sharper, laced with a chilling undercurrent of something…unseen.
