The air hung thick and cloying, a humid blanket woven from the scent of frying plantains and exhaust fumes.  Cicadas buzzed a relentless, hypnotic rhythm against the backdrop of car horns battling for dominance on Avenida Revolución.  Sweat beaded on the forehead of Elias Thorne, Jr., as he wiped it away with the back of his hand, leaving a smudge of dust on his already-rumpled linen suit.  His briefcase, a battered Samsonite that had seen better days – and far fewer humid Mexican afternoons – thumped softly against his leg with each weary step. Elias, a salesman of artisanal tequila with a reputation as sharp as the agave spines he occasionally found embedded in his shoes, knew this wasn't just another Tuesday.  This was the day he either clinched the biggest deal of his career, or watched his commission dreams evaporate like morning mist in the scorching sun.
