The pre-dawn chill nipped at Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s cheeks as she trudged through the dew-kissed grass of the Whispering Pines Wildlife Sanctuary.  Mist, thick as spun sugar, clung to the ancient pines that ringed the sprawling enclosure, their silhouettes stark against the bruised purple of the approaching sunrise.  The air, still and quiet save for the rustle of unseen creatures in the undergrowth, held the scent of damp earth and pine needles, a familiar balm to Amelia’s weary soul.  This was her sanctuary, her refuge, a place where the wild things thrived, and where, for the past ten years, she'd dedicated her life to their care.  Today, however, felt different. A heavy unease, a prickling at the back of her neck, hinted at something amiss in the tranquil beauty of Whispering Pines.
