The salt spray stung Elias Thorne's face as he wrestled the last stubborn plank into place.  The wind, a relentless, howling banshee, clawed at the half-finished structure, threatening to send his carefully measured work tumbling into the churning grey maw of the Atlantic.  Below him, the jagged teeth of the Black Rock cliffs plunged into the ocean, a constant reminder of the unforgiving nature of this remote corner of Cornwall.  Elias, his weathered hands calloused and strong, hammered the final nail home with a satisfying *thunk*, the sound almost swallowed by the roar of the sea.  The small, ramshackle lighthouse, perched precariously on the edge of the world, was finally taking shape, a defiant beacon against the encroaching storm.
