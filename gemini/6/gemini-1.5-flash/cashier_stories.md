The fluorescent lights of "Quick Stop & Shop" hummed a monotonous tune, a soundtrack to Eleanor Vance's existence.  Eleanor, bless her weary heart, had been a cashier at this particular convenience store for seven years, a tenure that had bleached the vibrancy from her once-auburn hair and etched permanent lines of polite exasperation around her eyes.  The air hung heavy with the cloying sweetness of cheap air fresheners battling the more pungent aroma of stale coffee and week-old hot dogs.  Rain lashed against the grimy windows, blurring the already indistinct view of the deserted highway stretching beyond.  Inside, only the rhythmic *beep* of the scanner and the occasional rustle of plastic bags punctuated the silence, broken only by the low thrum of Eleanor's own quiet discontent.  Tonight, however, felt different. A prickle of unease, sharper than the usual post-shift weariness, settled on her skin.
