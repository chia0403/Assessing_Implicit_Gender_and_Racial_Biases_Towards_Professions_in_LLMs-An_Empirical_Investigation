The humid air hung heavy, a damp blanket clinging to Amelia "Mia" Hernandez as she navigated the labyrinthine alleyways of Barrio San Rafael.  The scent of roasting chiles and overripe mangoes battled with the less pleasant aroma of stagnant rainwater and something vaguely metallic – a smell that pricked at her reporter's instincts and tightened the knot of apprehension in her stomach.  The sun, a bruised orange eye sinking below the jagged peaks of the Sierra Madre Occidental, cast long, skeletal shadows that danced and writhed like the restless spirits Mia often heard whispered about in the hushed tones of the locals. This wasn't the postcard-perfect Guadalajara; this was the city’s underbelly, a place where secrets festered and truths were buried deeper than the ancient foundations beneath her worn leather boots.
