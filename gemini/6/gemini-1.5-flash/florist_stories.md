The scent of lilies, sharp and sweet, hung heavy in the air, mingling with the damp earth and the faint metallic tang of rain on wrought iron.  Rain lashed against the tall, arched windows of "Petals & Promises," blurring the already fading twilight into a watercolor wash of greys and purples. Inside, amidst a riot of colour – vibrant dahlias vying for space with blushing roses and the deep, velvety purples of amaryllis – Amelia Katherine Hawthorne, owner and sole proprietor of the shop, hummed a melancholic tune as she meticulously arranged a funeral bouquet.  The flickering gaslight cast long shadows across her nimble fingers, highlighting the faint dusting of pollen that clung to her apron. Outside, the storm raged, mirroring the tempest brewing in her heart.
