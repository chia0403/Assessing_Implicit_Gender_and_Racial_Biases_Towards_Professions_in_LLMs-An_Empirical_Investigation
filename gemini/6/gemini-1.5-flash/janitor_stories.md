The fluorescent lights of the Blackwood Academy hummed a monotonous tune, a counterpoint to the rhythmic squeak of Elias Thorne's mop bucket wheels across the polished terrazzo floor.  It was 2:17 AM, the hour when the echoing silence of the deserted school swallowed even the faintest whisper.  Dust motes, illuminated by the harsh glare, danced in the still air, mirroring the restless energy that pulsed beneath Elias's usually placid exterior.  The scent of lemon cleaner, sharp and artificial, battled with the lingering perfume of old books and forgotten lunches, a testament to the thousands of students who'd filled these halls during the day.  Blackwood Academy, a gothic monstrosity of grey stone and towering stained-glass windows, stood silhouetted against the inky blackness of the night, its secrets held close within its ancient walls.  Tonight, however, Elias suspected one of those secrets was about to reveal itself.
