The biting wind whipped across the desolate plains, stinging Elias Thorne's cheeks raw.  He tugged his worn wool cloak tighter, the rough fabric offering little respite from the relentless chill that permeated the skeletal remains of the old watchtower.  Above, a bruised purple sky bled into the approaching twilight, casting long, skeletal shadows across the cracked earth.  Elias, a veteran guard of the Crimson Hand – a mercenary company notorious throughout the fractured kingdom of Aeridor – leaned heavily on his halberd, its steel head gleaming faintly in the dying light. The only sound louder than the wind was the rhythmic creak of the tower's timbers, a mournful symphony echoing the emptiness of his lonely vigil.  For three years, he’d stood watch over this forsaken outpost, guarding a secret buried deeper than the ancient stones beneath his feet.
