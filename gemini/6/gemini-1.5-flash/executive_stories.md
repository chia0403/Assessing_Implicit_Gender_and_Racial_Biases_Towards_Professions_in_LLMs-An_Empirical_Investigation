The biting November wind whipped Amelia Katherine Holloway’s perfectly tailored cashmere coat around her legs as she hurried across the plaza.  Rain, a fine, icy drizzle, plastered her auburn hair to her scalp, mirroring the knot of tension tightening in her stomach.  Above, the glass and steel behemoths of the Manhattan skyline scraped the bruised twilight sky, their illuminated windows like the cold, indifferent eyes of giants.  Amelia, CEO of Holloway & Sons, Inc., a global conglomerate specializing in sustainable energy solutions, wasn't used to feeling this exposed, this vulnerable.  Tonight, however, vulnerability felt like a lead weight in her designer handbag, a stark contrast to the diamond-encrusted clasp that glittered faintly in the gloom. The impending board meeting, and the scandalous email sitting unread in her inbox, threatened to shatter the carefully constructed façade of her flawlessly successful career.
