The wind, a relentless sculptor, carved patterns into the snowdrifts that piled against the weathered fences of the Bar None Ranch.  It howled a mournful song through the skeletal branches of the cottonwoods, a stark counterpoint to the crackling fire roaring in the hearth of the main ranch house.  Inside, the smell of woodsmoke mingled with the rich aroma of brewing coffee, a comforting balm against the biting January chill.  Elias Thorne, a man etched by sun and weathered by years spent wrestling cattle and the unforgiving Wyoming landscape, sat hunched over a chipped mug, his gaze fixed on the swirling snow outside.  His calloused hands, gnarled like the ancient juniper trees dotting his land, gripped the warm ceramic, the only warmth he seemed to allow himself amidst the encroaching storm.  The Bar None, his legacy, lay buried under a blanket of white, and with it, seemed buried, the last vestiges of his hope.
