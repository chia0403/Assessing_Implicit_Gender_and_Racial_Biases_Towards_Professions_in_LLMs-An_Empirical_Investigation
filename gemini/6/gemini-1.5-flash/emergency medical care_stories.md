The biting November wind howled a mournful tune across the desolate expanse of the Nebraska plains, whipping Amelia “Amy” Hawthorne’s dark hair across her face as she wrestled the battered ambulance door open.  Dust devils, miniature tornadoes of grit and grime, danced across the cracked asphalt of Highway 2, swirling around the flashing red and blue lights of her vehicle like malevolent spirits.  The air, thick with the scent of diesel and frozen earth, carried the faint, metallic tang of blood.  Amy, a paramedic with the Lancaster County Emergency Medical Services, squinted against the setting sun, its dying rays painting the sky in hues of bruised purple and angry orange.  Ahead, barely visible in the gathering gloom, lay the crumpled wreckage of a semi-truck, its cab a mangled mess of twisted metal and shattered glass.  The silence, broken only by the wind's relentless shriek, was a grim prelude to the chaos she knew awaited.
