The wind, a snarling beast with icy claws, ripped at Elias Thorne's jacket as he clung to the dizzying peak of the St. Jude's Cathedral.  Below, the cobbled streets of Prague twisted like petrified rivers, the ancient buildings huddled together for warmth against the November chill.  Gargoyles, frozen in mid-snarl, leered down at him from their lofty perches, silent witnesses to his precarious dance with gravity.  Elias, a roofer by trade and a daredevil by nature, adjusted the worn leather strap of his tool bag, the scent of damp slate and old mortar filling his nostrils.  The setting sun cast long, skeletal shadows across the city, painting the scene in hues of blood orange and bruised purple, a fitting backdrop for the perilous task ahead: repairing the cathedral's crumbling spire before the next storm hit.
