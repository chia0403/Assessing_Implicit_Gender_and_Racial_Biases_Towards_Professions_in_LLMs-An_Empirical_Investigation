The chipped paint of Amelia Grace Hawthorne's studio apartment windowsill was a perfect, albeit unintentional, match for the Pantone 18-1664 TPX she’d been wrestling with all afternoon.  Dust motes, illuminated by the harsh glare of the late afternoon sun, danced in the air above her cluttered desk, a chaotic ballet mirroring the turmoil in her own mind.  Empty coffee cups, discarded sketches, and overflowing recycling bins formed a haphazard landscape around her, each object a silent testament to the relentless pressure of her latest freelance project: redesigning the logo for a failing artisanal pickle company.  Outside, the relentless hum of New York City throbbed, a counterpoint to the quiet click-clack of her keyboard as she frantically tweaked the kerning of the word "Dill-icious."  Amelia sighed, the sound swallowed by the city’s omnipresent noise.  This wasn't the glamorous life of a graphic designer she'd envisioned; it was more like a gritty, caffeine-fueled survival game.
