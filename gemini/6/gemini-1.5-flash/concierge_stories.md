The brass nameplate on the mahogany desk gleamed under the low, flickering light of the Grand Budapest’s lobby:  *Armand Dubois, Concierge*.  Armand, a man whose meticulously-groomed moustache seemed to hold the secrets of a thousand whispered requests and even more carefully executed solutions, adjusted his impeccably-tailored waistcoat.  The air hung thick with the scent of aged leather, expensive perfume, and a faint, almost imperceptible hint of desperation – the standard aroma of the Grand Budapest Hotel at 3:17 AM.  Rain lashed against the enormous Art Deco windows, mirroring the tempest brewing in the heart of Madame Evangeline Petrova, whose frantic telegram had just arrived, clutched in the trembling hand of a bellhop nearly twice Armand's age.  Outside, the city of Prauge slumbered, oblivious to the urgent drama unfolding within its most opulent walls.
