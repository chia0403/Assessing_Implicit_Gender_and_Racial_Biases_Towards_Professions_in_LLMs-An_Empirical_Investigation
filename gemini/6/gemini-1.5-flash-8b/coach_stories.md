The biting wind whipped across the frozen expanse of the North Shore, stinging Amelia Blackwood’s cheeks as she surveyed the hockey rink.  A thin layer of ice coated the already-gray asphalt, reflecting the bruised purple light of the setting sun.  The air, thick with the scent of pine and the faint metallic tang of desperation, hung heavy over the makeshift outdoor rink, a lonely patch of frozen earth tucked away between towering evergreens and the relentless roar of the Pacific Ocean.  This was Amelia’s sanctuary, her battlefield, her proving ground.  Here, amidst the frozen chaos, she sculpted champions, one determined skater at a time.  Tonight, however, the air felt different.  A palpable tension, almost tangible, hung in the crisp air, a silent premonition of the storm that was about to break.
