The biting wind whipped through the cobbled streets of Veridia, stinging Amelia Blackwood, Judge of the Eastern District, in the face.  Rain, a relentless grey curtain, blurred the already dim gaslight lamps, casting long, skeletal shadows that danced around the ornate, stone-faced buildings.  Veridia, a city built upon centuries of whispered secrets and forgotten tragedies, held its breath, awaiting the dawn and the inevitable pronouncements that would ripple through its heart.  Amelia, her scarlet cloak billowing around her, hurried towards the grand, iron-gated entrance of the District Court. The scent of wet earth and decaying leaves clung to the air, a pungent reminder of the city's melancholic beauty and the weight of justice that lay before her.  Inside, the hushed whispers of anticipation already filled the cavernous courtroom, a symphony of nervous anxieties and suppressed hopes.
