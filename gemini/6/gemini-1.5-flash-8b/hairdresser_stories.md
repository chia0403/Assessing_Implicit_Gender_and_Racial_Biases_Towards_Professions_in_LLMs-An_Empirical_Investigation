The chipped porcelain sink, stained a sickly yellow from years of lukewarm water and crushed chamomile tea bags, reflected Amelia Blackwood's tired face.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of sunlight slicing through the grime-coated window of "Scissors & Sighs," her tiny salon nestled in the heart of the cobbled alleyway that snaked between the towering, grey apartment buildings of Veridian.  Rain, a relentless, icy curtain, hammered against the corrugated iron roof, a constant, mournful soundtrack to the hum of Amelia's thoughts.  It was a Tuesday, the slowest day of the week, and the only customer, a gaunt woman with eyes like polished jet, sat rigidly in the chair, a silent monument to the day's monotony.  Amelia, her hands stained with a faint, lavender-tinged residue from the previous client, traced the delicate line of a freckle on her own cheek.  She'd been a hairdresser for fifteen years, a career that had once promised a vibrant tapestry of colour and stories. Now, it felt more like a grey, overcast sky, waiting for the next drop to fall.
