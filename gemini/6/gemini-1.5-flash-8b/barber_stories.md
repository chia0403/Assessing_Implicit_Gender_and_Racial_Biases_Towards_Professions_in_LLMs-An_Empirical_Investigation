The scent of freshly cut hair, a heady mix of ozone and something vaguely floral, hung heavy in the air of "The Clipper's Edge."  Barnaby "Barty" Butterfield, a man whose meticulously trimmed beard seemed to whisper tales of forgotten empires, expertly guided the gleaming steel of his razor.  His tiny shop, tucked away on a cobbled street in the heart of the decaying but undeniably charming district of  Aetherium, was a haven of quiet precision.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of sunlight that pierced the gloom, illuminating the intricate patterns etched into the worn wooden counter.  Aetherium, once a bustling hub of trade and magic, now felt as if it had lost its spark.  The only fire seemed to be the one Barty kept stoked, literally and metaphorically, within his small, surprisingly vibrant shop.
