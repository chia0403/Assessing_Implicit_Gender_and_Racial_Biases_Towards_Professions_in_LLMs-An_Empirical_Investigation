Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of the probation office, mimicking the relentless rhythm of Amelia "Amy"  Charleston's thoughts.  The fluorescent lights hummed, a sterile counterpoint to the simmering anxiety in her gut.  The small, cramped office, barely larger than a walk-in closet, smelled faintly of stale coffee and despair.  Empty takeout containers – evidence of late-night ramen and whispered conversations – littered the overflowing file cabinet.  Outside, the city of Ashwood, a sprawling metropolis choked by industry and obscured by perpetually overcast skies, grumbled and groaned with the storm.  Amy, a probation officer with three years under her belt, stared at the crumpled manila folder in her hands.  Inside, the case file of eighteen-year-old Leo Vance – a volatile young man with a past that seemed to coil around her like a venomous snake – lay waiting to be dissected.  Her task: to untangle the threads of his youthful recklessness and try, desperately, to find a thread of redemption amidst the storm.
