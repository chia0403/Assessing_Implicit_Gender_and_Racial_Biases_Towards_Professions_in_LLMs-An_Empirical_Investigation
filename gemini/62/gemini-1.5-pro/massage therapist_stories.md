The neon "Open" sign buzzed erratically in the window of Kneadful Things Massage Therapy, casting a flickering, sickly green light across the rain-slicked street. Inside, Elara Juniper Finch meticulously wiped down the massage table, the scent of lavender and eucalyptus hanging heavy in the air.  Chinatown, San Francisco, was usually alive with the clang of cable cars and the fragrant steam of dumplings, but tonight, a thick fog had rolled in, muffling the city's usual symphony and leaving an eerie quiet in its wake.  Elara glanced at the antique clock on the wall – 11:57 pm.  Almost closing time.  She’d had exactly one client all day, a tense stockbroker who’d spent the entire hour complaining about Bitcoin.  Elara sighed, rubbing the tension from her own shoulders.  She was about to switch off the buzzing neon sign when the bell above the door chimed, announcing a late arrival.
