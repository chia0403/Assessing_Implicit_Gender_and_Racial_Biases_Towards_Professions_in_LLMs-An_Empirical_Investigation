Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of Amelia Thorne's forty-second-floor office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her. The cityscape of Chicago, normally a glittering tapestry of light and steel, was obscured by a curtain of grey.  Inside, the sleek, minimalist space, usually a sanctuary of calm, felt oppressive.  Amelia, CEO of Thorne Industries, a multinational tech company she’d inherited from her father, tapped a manicured nail against the polished ebony of her desk. The silence was broken only by the rhythmic drumming of the rain and the quiet hum of the state-of-the-art air purifier.  A single email, glowing ominously on her oversized monitor, threatened to unravel everything she’d worked so tirelessly to build.  The subject line simply read: "We know."
