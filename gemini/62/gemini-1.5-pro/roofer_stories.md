The tar hissed and bubbled under the August sun, the smell thick and acrid in the air. Elijah "Eli" Blackwood, his boots planted firmly on the scalding shingles, squinted at the shimmering horizon of Milwaukee.  From his vantage point atop the three-story Victorian, the city sprawled beneath him like a rusty patchwork quilt, the glint of Lake Michigan a sliver of cool blue in the distance. Eli wiped the sweat from his brow with the back of his calloused hand, leaving a streak of black tar across his forehead. The air hung heavy and still, the kind of oppressive heat that promised a thunderstorm later, the kind that made the old houses in this neighborhood creak and groan under the strain. This particular house, with its peeling paint and overgrown ivy, whispered secrets Eli wasn't sure he wanted to hear.
