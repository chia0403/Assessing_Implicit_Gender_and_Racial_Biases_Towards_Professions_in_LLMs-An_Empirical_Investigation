The flickering fluorescent lights of the Zenith BioTech labs hummed a monotonous B flat, the only sound echoing through the deserted hallways.  Outside, the Denver skyline shimmered with the first hesitant rays of dawn, painting the snow-capped Rockies a pale rose. Inside, Elias Thorne, his name tag slightly askew on his faded blue uniform, pushed his cleaning cart with a sigh. The squeak of its wheels was a familiar counterpoint to the buzzing lights, a soundtrack to his solitary existence within these sterile walls.  Zenith BioTech, a gleaming monument to scientific progress, was a labyrinth of gleaming chrome, pristine white walls, and the faint, metallic tang of something Elias couldn't quite place.  Tonight, however, something felt different. A subtle shift in the air, a prickle of unease that had nothing to do with the usual Monday morning dread.
