The biting wind whipped at Elias Thorne's cloak, the frozen rain clinging to the thick wool like icy claws. Above him, the obsidian spires of Veritas Citadel scraped against the bruised, twilight sky. Built on the precipice of the Whispering Cliffs, the Citadel seemed to defy gravity, its foundations anchored to the very edge of the world.  Elias, a man weathered by decades of harsh mountain winters and the even harsher realities of his duty as a Citadel Guard, stamped his feet to ward off the creeping numbness.  For seventeen years, he had patrolled this exposed section of the outer wall, a lonely vigil against threats both real and imagined. Tonight, however, the icy air held a different kind of chill, a prickling unease that settled deep in his bones.  The wind whispered secrets, and Elias, a man who had long learned to listen to the mountain's voice, felt a tremor of fear he hadn't known since he was a boy.
