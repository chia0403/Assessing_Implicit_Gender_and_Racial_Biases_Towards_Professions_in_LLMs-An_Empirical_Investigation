Rain lashed against the attic window of Amelia Grace Holloway's tiny Edinburgh flat, mimicking the tempest brewing inside her.  Loose manuscript pages, escaped from their binder, danced across the worn wooden floor like frantic moths. Outside, the wind howled a mournful ballad around the chimney pots, a soundtrack to Amelia’s despair. Her cluttered desk, usually a comforting chaos of dictionaries, red pens, and half-eaten biscuits, tonight felt like a monument to her failure.  The glow of her laptop screen illuminated the rejection email, its words sharp and cold against the warm flicker of the gas fire struggling to combat the November chill.  For the third time, her painstakingly crafted edit of Professor Alistair Finch’s magnum opus, "A Comprehensive History of Haggis," had been deemed "too… enthusiastic."  Amelia, arguably the most enthusiastic editor north of Hadrian’s Wall, was beginning to suspect the literary world might just be conspiring against her.
