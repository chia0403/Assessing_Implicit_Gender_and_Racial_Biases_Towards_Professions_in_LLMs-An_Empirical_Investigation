The biting November wind whipped off the Puget Sound, carrying the scent of brine and pine needles into the open kitchen window of "The Salty Spoon."  Flakes of snow, fat and lazy, began to drift down, dusting the cobblestone alleyway outside. Inside, the air crackled with a different kind of energy – the controlled chaos of a Friday night dinner rush.  Amelia "Amy" Rose Hawthorne, her auburn braid plastered to her cheek with sweat, barked orders with the precision of a drill sergeant and the warmth of a seasoned hostess. "Two halibut specials, firing! Scallops need two more minutes! And for heaven's sake, someone get that bread out of the oven before it becomes charcoal!"  The Salty Spoon, nestled in the heart of Pike Place Market in Seattle, was Amy’s sanctuary, a testament to her lifelong dream, and tonight, it was buzzing with a life of its own.
