The biting November wind whipped off Lake Michigan, sending icy tendrils down LaSalle Street, Chicago.  Flakes of snow, fat and wet, began to plaster themselves against the towering windows of Mercy General Hospital. Inside, the Emergency Room throbbed with a low hum of controlled chaos.  Eleanor Vance, RN, adjusted the stethoscope around her neck, her breath fogging slightly in the cool air. The fluorescent lights overhead buzzed incessantly, a counterpoint to the rhythmic beeping of monitors and the hushed murmur of worried voices. Tonight, like many nights, Eleanor braced herself for whatever the revolving doors of the ER would bring. The city outside, a glittering, indifferent beast, held its breath, waiting with her.
