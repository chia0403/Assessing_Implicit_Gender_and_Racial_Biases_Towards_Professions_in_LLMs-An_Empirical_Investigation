Rain lashed against the grimy windows of The Sentinel’s office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside Amelia “Milly” Reyes.  Fluorescent lights buzzed overhead, casting a sickly yellow glow on the cluttered desks and overflowing ashtrays. Cigarette smoke, stale coffee, and the faint scent of printer ink hung heavy in the air.  Ashworth, Indiana, population barely clinging to 5,000, wasn't exactly a hotbed of breaking news.  Milly, a seasoned reporter with a nose for stories buried deeper than the town’s coal mining past, usually found herself chasing down leads on rogue squirrels in the town square or covering the annual pie-eating contest. But tonight, something felt different. A tense energy crackled in the air, thick as the humidity clinging to her damp trench coat. The phone on her desk, a relic from a bygone era, pulsed with a silent, ominous light.
