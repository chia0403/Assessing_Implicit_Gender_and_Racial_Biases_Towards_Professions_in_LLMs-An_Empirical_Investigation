The biting November wind whipped off Lake Michigan, stinging Amelia Reyes’ cheeks as she hurried across the near-deserted parking lot of the Mercy Clinic North.  Flu season had officially hit Chicago, and Amelia, a Physician’s Assistant in the bustling clinic, was already running two hours behind. The sky, a bruised purple-grey, mirrored the exhaustion settling deep in her bones.  Inside, the fluorescent lights hummed a monotonous tune, a soundtrack to the cacophony of coughing, sniffling, and the occasional wail of a feverish child.  The waiting room, usually sterile and smelling faintly of disinfectant, now carried the heavy, cloying scent of sickness.  A discarded tissue lay crumpled by the overflowing trash can, a small, crumpled flag of surrender in the ongoing battle against the germs.
