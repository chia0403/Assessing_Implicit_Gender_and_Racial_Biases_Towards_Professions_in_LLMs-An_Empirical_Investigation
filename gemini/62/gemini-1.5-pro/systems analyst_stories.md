The flickering fluorescent lights of the server room buzzed like angry wasps, a counterpoint to the rhythmic hum of the cooling fans.  Dust motes danced in the pale light, illuminating the endless rows of blinking servers that held the digital lifeblood of Caduceus Pharmaceuticals.  Elara Vance, her name tag slightly askew on her charcoal cardigan, shivered despite the stifling heat. It was 2:17 am on a Tuesday, and she was the only living soul in this subterranean world. Elara, a systems analyst with a reputation for untangling the most Gordian of digital knots, had been summoned to diagnose a particularly pernicious server issue that threatened to halt production of Caduceus's flagship antibiotic. Outside, the Indianapolis skyline glittered indifferently, oblivious to the digital crisis unfolding beneath its twinkling lights.
