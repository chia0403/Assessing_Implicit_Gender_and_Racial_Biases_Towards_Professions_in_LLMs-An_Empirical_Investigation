The biting wind whipped Amelia "Mia" Reyes's dark hair across her face, stinging her cheeks raw despite the thick wool scarf wrapped around her neck.  She crouched low behind a crumbling drystone wall, the rough granite digging into her gloved hands. Below her, the valley of Glencoe unfolded in a tapestry of muted browns and greens, the snow-capped peaks of the Three Sisters looming like watchful giants.  Mia shivered, not entirely from the cold.  This was it. This was the shot she'd been chasing for months: the elusive, ephemeral moment when the low winter sun painted the ancient landscape with an almost otherworldly light.  Her Nikon D850, heavy and familiar in her grip, was her only solace in the vast, unforgiving beauty of the Scottish Highlands.
