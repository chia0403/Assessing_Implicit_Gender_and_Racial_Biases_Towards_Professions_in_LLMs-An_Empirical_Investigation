The flickering neon sign of "Rosie's Rods & Repairs" buzzed erratically, casting a sickly yellow glow across the rain-slicked asphalt of Highway 17. Inside, grease-stained concrete floors mirrored the bruised twilight sky, reflecting off the chrome bumpers and dented hoods of a dozen ailing vehicles.  The air, thick with the aroma of motor oil, gasoline, and stale coffee, hung heavy around Eliza Jane Holloway.  Her hands, calloused and scarred from years of wrestling with stubborn bolts and busted engines, were currently buried deep inside the guts of a '67 Mustang, its heart a tangled mess of wires and hoses.  Rain lashed against the corrugated iron roof, a relentless drumbeat that echoed the thrumming headache building behind Eliza's eyes.  This stretch of Arizona highway, a lonely ribbon bisecting the vast, unforgiving desert, rarely offered up anything but trouble, and tonight, trouble had rolled in on four flat tires and a prayer.
