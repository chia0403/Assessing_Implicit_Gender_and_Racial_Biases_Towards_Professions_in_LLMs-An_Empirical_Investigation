The biting November wind whipped around Professor Alistair Finch, tugging at the edges of his tweed jacket as he hurried across the cobbled quadrangle of St. Jude's College, Oxford. Gargoyles leered down from the honey-coloured stone buildings, their expressions mirroring the anxiety churning in Alistair's stomach.  The low, grey sky threatened snow, and the skeletal branches of the ancient oak in the center of the quad rattled like dry bones.  It was almost five o'clock, and the buttery's warm glow beckoned, promising a refuge from the encroaching cold and, more importantly, a stiff drink.  But Alistair couldn't afford the distraction.  Tonight was the unveiling of the Codex Lumina, a medieval manuscript he'd spent the last decade painstakingly deciphering, and a creeping dread, far colder than the November air, whispered that something was terribly wrong.
