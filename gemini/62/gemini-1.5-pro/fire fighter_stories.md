The biting November wind whipped at Amelia “Mia” Rodriguez’s turnout coat as she stood on the rooftop of the abandoned textile mill, embers swirling around her like angry fireflies.  Below, the once-proud brick edifice of the Lowell Manufacturing Company was a charred skeleton, flames still licking greedily at the remaining wooden beams. The acrid smell of burning cotton and wool clung to the air, a ghostly reminder of the mill's bustling past.  Lowell, Massachusetts, had seen its share of mill fires over the centuries, but this one, fueled by decades of accumulated dust and neglect, was proving particularly stubborn. The flickering orange glow painted Mia’s face in a demonic light, reflecting not the hellish scene before her, but the fierce determination burning in her eyes.
