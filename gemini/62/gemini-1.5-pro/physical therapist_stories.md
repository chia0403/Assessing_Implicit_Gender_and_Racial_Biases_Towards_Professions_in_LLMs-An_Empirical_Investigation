The biting November wind whipped off Lake Michigan, rattling the oversized windows of "Kinetic Rehab," frosting them with a delicate layer of condensation.  Inside, the air buzzed with the low hum of exercise equipment and the rhythmic squeak of sneakers on polished hardwood.  Eleanor Vance, PT, DPT, adjusted the resistance on a leg press machine for Mrs. Petrov, a sprightly octogenarian determined to reclaim her ballroom dancing prowess after a nasty fall. Eleanor, with her auburn hair pulled back in a practical braid and a smile that could thaw even a Chicago winter, offered words of encouragement, her voice a calming counterpoint to the blustery symphony outside.  Little did she know, the calm of her carefully constructed world was about to be shattered by a patient unlike any she had encountered before.
