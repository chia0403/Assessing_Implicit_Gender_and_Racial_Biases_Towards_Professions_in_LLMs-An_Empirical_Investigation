The biting November wind whipped around Dr. Eleanor Vance as she hurried across the cobbled courtyard of Ashworth Manor, her breath clouding the air.  Gargoyles perched on the aged stone walls seemed to leer at her through the swirling mist, their expressions mirroring the gnawing unease in her stomach. Ashworth Manor, a sprawling gothic behemoth overlooking the churning grey expanse of the North Sea, had been converted into a rehabilitation centre for patients with severe neurological conditions. Eleanor, newly appointed Head of Occupational Therapy, clutched her satchel tighter, the leather cold against her gloved hand.  Inside, the warm glow emanating from the arched windows promised refuge from the elemental fury outside, but Eleanor knew the real storm was brewing within the walls of Ashworth. The whispers about the Manor's history – tales of eccentric patients, tragic accidents, and unexplained disappearances – had been dismissed as local folklore by her colleagues, but Eleanor couldn't shake the feeling that something unsettling lurked beneath the veneer of clinical professionalism.
