The late afternoon sun, a bruised plum in the November sky, cast long shadows across the cracked asphalt of the Northwood High parking lot.  Frost crunched under Amelia Grace Holloway's sensible brown boots as she made her way to her battered Volvo, a stack of graded essays clutched in her mittened hand.  The air, sharp with the scent of pine and impending snow, bit at her exposed cheeks. Northwood, nestled in the foothills of the Adirondacks, was a town that wore its winters like a badge of honor, and this year promised to be a particularly harsh one. Amelia, however, preferred the cold.  It offered a stark contrast to the simmering tensions that always seemed to permeate the hallways of Northwood High, a pressure cooker of teenage angst and dwindling budgets.  She tucked a loose strand of her auburn hair behind her ear, her breath puffing out in white clouds, and fumbled with her keys, already anticipating the warmth of her small cottage and the comforting silence within.  Little did she know, the silence wouldn't last.
