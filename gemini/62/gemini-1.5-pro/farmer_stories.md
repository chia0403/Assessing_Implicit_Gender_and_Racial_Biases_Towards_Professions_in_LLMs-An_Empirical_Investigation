The wind, sharp and smelling of impending snow, whipped across the frost-covered fields of Hemlock Hollow, tugging at the brim of Elias Finch's worn felt hat. Elias, his breath clouding in the frigid air, squinted at the bruised purple sky above the skeletal branches of the ancient oak that marked the western edge of his property.  Hemlock Hollow, nestled deep in the Appalachian foothills of western North Carolina, was a place of quiet beauty, a patchwork of rolling farmland and thick forest, steeped in the lore of generations past.  But on this late November afternoon, a palpable unease hung in the air, heavier than the gathering storm clouds. Elias, a man usually as stoic and dependable as the granite peaks that loomed in the distance, felt a shiver crawl down his spine, a premonition of something amiss settling in his gut.
