The flickering fluorescent lights of the Quick Stop hummed a monotonous tune, a soundtrack to Amelia Reyes' life.  Outside, the relentless New Mexico sun beat down on the cracked asphalt of the parking lot, baking the air to a shimmering haze. Inside, the air conditioning battled valiantly, but the small convenience store still held a stuffy warmth, scented with a peculiar mix of stale coffee, hot dogs rotating on metal rollers, and the faint, lingering perfume of Mrs. Henderson, who religiously bought a single lottery ticket every Tuesday and Thursday. Amelia, her name tag slightly askew on her faded blue polo shirt, punched the keys of the ancient cash register, the rhythmic beeping a counterpoint to the hum of the lights. It was 2:17 pm on a Wednesday, statistically the slowest time of the week, and Amelia was already counting down the minutes until her shift ended and she could escape to the cool sanctuary of her tiny apartment, where a stack of library books and a purring calico cat awaited her.
