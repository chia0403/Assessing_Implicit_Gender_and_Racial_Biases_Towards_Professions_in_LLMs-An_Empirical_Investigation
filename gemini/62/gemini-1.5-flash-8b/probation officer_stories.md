Rain hammered against the grimy windows of the probation office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside Amelia Kensington.  The chipped Formica desk, scarred with the countless fingerprints of desperation and despair, reflected the flickering fluorescent lights, casting long, distorted shadows that danced with the rhythmic drumming on the glass.  The air, thick with the scent of stale coffee and fear, hung heavy in the small, cluttered room.  Amelia, a probation officer with five years under her belt, stared at the latest case file, a manila folder bulging with the grim details of another young life spiralling out of control.  Outside, the city thrummed with a nocturnal energy, oblivious to the quiet storm raging within the walls of her office in the heart of the gritty, industrial district of  Eastport, a place where shadows stretched long and the future seemed as uncertain as the weather.
