The crisp autumn air, thick with the scent of fallen leaves and woodsmoke, snaked its way into the tiny, sun-drenched kitchen of Dr. Eleanor Ainsworth, Nutritionist.  Dust motes danced in the lone shaft of sunlight piercing the gloom of the overgrown ivy that clung to the ancient stone walls.  Empty teacups, speckled with dried chamomile blossoms, sat haphazardly on the chipped wooden table beside overflowing notebooks filled with meticulously crafted meal plans and handwritten nutritional analyses.  Eleanor, her name embroidered in faded gold thread on a worn linen apron, hunched over a steaming pot of lentil soup, her brow furrowed in concentration.  Outside, the wind howled a mournful tune, echoing the quiet desperation in her heart.  It was a Tuesday, the day she’d designated for soul-searching and, more importantly, for confronting the gnawing anxiety that had been gnawing at her since the city health department’s latest report. The report, seemingly damning, indicated a disturbing spike in cases of nutrient deficiency in the Lower East Side community, a community she had dedicated her life to serving.
