The biting wind whipped at Bartholomew "Barty" Butterfield's worn leather jacket, stinging his cheeks and tugging at the frayed edges of his hard hat.  He squinted, tracing the precarious line of the roof with a calloused finger.  Below, the bustling city of Aethelgard, a sprawling metropolis built on the jagged peaks of the Veridian Mountains, was a blur of grey and steel.  Towering skyscrapers, their windows like tiny, glittering eyes, pressed against the bruised twilight sky.  Barty, perched precariously on the narrow ridge of a twenty-story building, felt the familiar tremor of the ancient stone beneath his feet, a tremor that had echoed through generations of roofers before him. This was his domain, his stage, his life: the perilous expanse between the earth and the clouds. Aethelgard, in all its breathtaking and threatening glory, was his canvas.
