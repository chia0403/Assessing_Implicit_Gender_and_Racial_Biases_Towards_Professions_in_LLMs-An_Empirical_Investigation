The biting wind, a razor-sharp whisper against the frosted glass of the Citadel's obsidian walls, whipped through the courtyard.  Snow, a relentless, silent foe, coated everything in a layer of pristine white.  The air, thick with the scent of pine and impending frost, hung heavy, mirroring the weight of responsibility pressing down on Sergeant Jasper Blackwood.  He stood at the obsidian archway, the cold seeping into his very bones, a sentinel against the encroaching darkness, a guard against the unknown.  The Citadel, a towering fortress of polished stone and shadowed spires, held the secrets of the Whispering Woods, and Jasper was its last line of defense.
