The fluorescent lights of the Sterling & Stone law firm hummed, a monotonous drone that echoed Amelia Cavendish's own internal monologue.  Eight AM, and already the phone was ringing, a relentless symphony of hurried voices and urgent pleas.  Her small, cramped office, tucked between a conference room and the overflowing mailroom, smelled faintly of stale coffee and legal documents.  Sunlight, weak and filtered through the grime-coated windows, cast long, wavering shadows across the polished mahogany desk, highlighting the countless pens, paperclips, and stray sticky notes that cluttered its surface. Amelia, a petite woman with perpetually tired eyes and a perpetually strained smile, adjusted her perfectly-pressed floral blouse.  Another day, another barrage of lawsuits, divorce proceedings, and whispered secrets. This morning, however, felt different. A tremor of anticipation, almost palpable, ran through her. Today, something might crack.
