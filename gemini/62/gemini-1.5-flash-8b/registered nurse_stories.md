The fluorescent hum of the emergency room was a constant, almost imperceptible thrumming beneath the cacophony of sirens, beeping machines, and the frantic whispers of staff.  Rain hammered against the windows of St. Jude's General, blurring the city lights into a shimmering, oily sheen.  Outside, the world was a chaotic wash of grey, but inside, in the sterile white of room 304, everything was sharp, clinical, and undeniably urgent.  Amelia Grace Carter, a registered nurse with ten years of experience, stood poised at the head of the bed, her hands hovering over the patient's chart.  The air, thick with the metallic tang of blood and antiseptic, clung to her like a second skin.
