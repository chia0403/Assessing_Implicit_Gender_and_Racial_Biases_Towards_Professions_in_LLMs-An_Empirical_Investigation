The flickering fluorescent lights of Dr. Eleanor Vance's dental office hummed a monotonous tune, a counterpoint to the rhythmic *tap-tap-tap* of her tools against the enamel.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of sunlight piercing the grime-coated window, illuminating a chipped porcelain doll nestled amongst a jumble of dental instruments and overflowing patient charts.  The air, thick with the antiseptic tang of bleach and the faint, cloying sweetness of mint gum, hung heavy in the small, cluttered space.  Eleanor, her brow furrowed in concentration, meticulously filed down a particularly stubborn cavity.  Outside, the city thrummed with its own relentless energy, oblivious to the quiet struggle unfolding within the confines of her cramped practice on the bustling, yet surprisingly quiet, corner of Elm Street and Maple Avenue.
