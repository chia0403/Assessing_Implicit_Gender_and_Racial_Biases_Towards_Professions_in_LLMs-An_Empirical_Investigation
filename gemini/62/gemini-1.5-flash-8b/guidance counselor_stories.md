The chipped Formica countertop felt strangely cold beneath Amelia Hayes’s fingertips as she traced the faded outline of a mug.  The aroma of burnt coffee hung heavy in the air, a familiar companion in the small, windowless guidance office at Westview High.  Outside, the November rain hammered against the corrugated metal roof, a relentless percussion mirroring the anxieties swirling inside the teenagers who, throughout the day, had sought refuge – and often, a distraction – within her cluttered space.  Amelia, a guidance counselor with a perpetually tired smile and eyes that had seen far more than the average 20-something, sighed.  Another day, another mountain of academic struggles, relationship woes, and existential dread to navigate.  Empty coffee cups piled precariously beside a stack of student files, whispering tales of unfulfilled dreams and uncharted futures.  The flickering fluorescent lights cast long, distorted shadows, emphasizing the quiet desperation that permeated the room.
