The biting wind, a relentless, icy hand, clawed at Dr. Eleanor Vance’s face as she squinted through the swirling snow.  The clinic, nestled deep within the jagged peaks of the Alaskan range, was a small, weathered wooden structure clinging precariously to the mountainside.  Snow piled high against the windows, obscuring the flickering gas lamps inside.  A lone, mournful howl echoed across the valley, punctuated by the rhythmic clack of Eleanor’s boots on the frozen ground.  This was the desolate beauty of the Alaskan wilderness, a place where the wild held dominion, and where the fate of creatures both great and small rested in the hands of a woman who considered herself little more than a reluctant guardian.
