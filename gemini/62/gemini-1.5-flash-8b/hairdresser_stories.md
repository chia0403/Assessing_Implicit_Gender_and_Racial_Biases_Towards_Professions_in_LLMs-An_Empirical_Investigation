The chipped porcelain sink, stained a perpetual blush pink from decades of spilled dye and the occasional rogue shampoo, gurgled softly.  Sunlight, fractured by the grime-coated windowpanes, cast elongated shadows across the tiny salon, "Scissors & Smiles," owned and operated by  Anya Petrova.  Anya, a woman whose sharp, intelligent eyes seemed to have absorbed the hues of every colour imaginable, meticulously snipped a wisp of auburn hair, the rhythmic snip-snip-snip echoing in the quiet space.  Outside, the cacophony of the bustling city of Veridia – a metropolis built on shimmering skyscrapers and forgotten alleyways – faded into a muted hum.  Today, however, only the faint scent of lavender and fear clung to the air.
