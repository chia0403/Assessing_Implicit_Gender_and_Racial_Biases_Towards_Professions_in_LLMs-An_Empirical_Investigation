The flickering gaslight cast long, dancing shadows across the cobblestones of the Grand Imperial Hotel, illuminating dust motes swirling in the humid air of late summer.  Rain, a welcome but unpredictable visitor to the city, had stopped only moments before, leaving behind a sheen of dampness on the ornate facade of the hotel.  Inside, amidst the hushed whispers of patrons and the clinking of silverware from the dining rooms, stood Bartholomew "Barty" Butterfield, bellhop extraordinaire.  Barty, a young man with eyes the colour of polished mahogany and a perpetually surprised expression, meticulously polished a brass tray, his movements practiced and precise.  He was a master of the seemingly mundane, a quiet observer in a world of boisterous personalities, and today, a particularly unusual request had landed squarely in his carefully crafted routine.
