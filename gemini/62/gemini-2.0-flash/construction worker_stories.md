The air hung thick and heavy, smelling of hot asphalt and diesel fumes. Sunrise was still a good hour away, but the site was already a hive of activity, a metal and concrete symphony of clanging, shouting, and the relentless thrum of generators. Dust motes danced in the harsh glare of temporary floodlights, painting the half-finished skeletal frame of the new skyscraper in a ghostly, almost ethereal glow. On the tenth floor, a lone figure surveyed the scene, his silhouette stark against the pre-dawn sky. That figure was **Joseph "Joey" Anthony Mancini**, and for him, this wasn't just a construction site; it was his canvas.
