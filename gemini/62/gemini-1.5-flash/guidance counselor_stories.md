The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Hernandez's hands, the lukewarm chamomile tea doing little to soothe the knot of anxiety tightening in her stomach.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of her office, blurring the already hazy cityscape of Portland, Oregon.  Grey, a color that seemed to have seeped into every fiber of the day, reflected in the polished wood of her desk, mirroring the muted despair she often felt as she navigated the turbulent emotional seas of her students.  Today, however, the usual adolescent angst felt amplified, a crescendo building to a storm she wasn’t entirely sure she could weather.  The scent of damp earth and petrichor, usually a comfort, only served to heighten the oppressive atmosphere, a fitting prelude to the meeting she was about to have with sixteen-year-old Liam O’Connell.  His file, a testament to escalating disciplinary actions and plummeting grades, sat squarely on her desk, a silent, ominous warning.
