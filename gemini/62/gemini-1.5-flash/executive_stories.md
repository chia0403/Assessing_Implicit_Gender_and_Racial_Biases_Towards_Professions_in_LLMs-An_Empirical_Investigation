The mahogany desk gleamed under the harsh fluorescent lights, a stark contrast to the bruised purple of the twilight sky bleeding through the panoramic windows of Amelia Hernandez’s office.  Rain lashed against the glass, each drop a tiny, furious percussionist in the symphony of the approaching storm.  Below, the city of Chicago sprawled, a glittering tapestry of lights battling the encroaching darkness, mirroring the turmoil brewing inside Amelia herself.  Amelia Katherine Hernandez, CEO of Hernandez Industries, stared out at the cityscape, the weight of a billion-dollar company – and a crumbling personal life – pressing down on her slender shoulders.  The scent of expensive leather and brewing coffee did little to mask the bitter taste of regret clinging to the air.  Tonight, however, the storm outside felt strangely comforting, a perfect reflection of the tempest raging within.
