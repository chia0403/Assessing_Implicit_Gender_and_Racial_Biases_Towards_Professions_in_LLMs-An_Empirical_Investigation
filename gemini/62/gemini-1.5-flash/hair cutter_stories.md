The air hung thick and sweet with the scent of lavender and something faintly metallic, a smell Aurelia Blackwood, owner and sole proprietor of "Snip & Stitch," knew intimately.  It clung to her apron, to the worn wooden floorboards beneath her feet, even to the chipped porcelain mugs warming on the radiator.  Rain lashed against the single, grimy window of her tiny salon, tucked away on a cobbled side street in the perpetually twilight district of Grimsborough.  The streetlights, weak and flickering, cast long, distorted shadows that danced with the frantic rhythm of the downpour, mirroring the restless energy coiled within Aurelia herself.  Tonight, however, the usual comforting hum of her clippers was silent.  The only sound was the relentless drumming of rain and the nervous tremor in her own hand as she gripped a pair of shears, their polished steel gleaming under the dim light.
