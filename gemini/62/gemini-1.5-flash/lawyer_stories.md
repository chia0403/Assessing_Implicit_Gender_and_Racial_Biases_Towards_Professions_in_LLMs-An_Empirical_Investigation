The salt spray stung Amelia Holloway’s face as she wrestled the stubborn yacht door open.  The November wind, a razor-edged thing, howled around the sleek, black hull of the *Seraphina*, threatening to rip the expensive silk scarf from her neck.  Below, the churning grey waters of the Irish Sea clawed at the rocky coastline of Anglesey, a symphony of crashing waves and mournful cries from unseen gulls.  Amelia, a solicitor with a reputation for sharp wit and sharper legal skills, shivered, pulling her cashmere coat tighter.  The air, thick with the scent of brine and something faintly metallic, held a promise of trouble – a promise she’d come a considerable distance, and braved a violently treacherous crossing, to keep.
