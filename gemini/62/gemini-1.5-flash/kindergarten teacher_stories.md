The chipped paint of the “Welcome to Ms. Eleanor Vance’s Class!” sign flaked onto the vibrant, slightly sticky, floor like a miniature, colorful snowstorm.  Dust motes, illuminated by the morning sun slanting through the grimy windowpanes, danced in the air above the miniature plastic dinosaurs scattered across the carpet – casualties of a particularly energetic game of “Jurassic Park” the previous afternoon.  The scent of crayons, slightly sour milk, and something vaguely resembling overcooked broccoli hung heavy in the air, a perfume peculiar only to a kindergarten classroom.  Eleanor Vance, whose auburn braid was already coming loose from its meticulously crafted bun, sighed contentedly. This was her kingdom, chaotic and beautiful in equal measure, and despite the early hour, she felt utterly at home.
