The aroma of cardamom and burnt sugar hung thick in the air, a comforting blanket against the November chill that gnawed at the cobblestones of Prague's Old Town.  Rain, a relentless drizzle that had been falling since dawn, plastered Amelia Katarina Novotná's dark hair to her forehead.  She stood hunched over the steaming copper cauldron, stirring a rich plum jam with a wooden spoon, her brow furrowed in concentration.  Inside her tiny bakery, "Naše Pekárna" (Our Bakery), the single gas lamp cast long shadows, dancing with the flickering candlelight on the shelves laden with crusty rye loaves, honey cakes, and kolaches glistening with apricot glaze.  Amelia, despite the damp and the relentless rhythm of her stirring, felt a familiar warmth bloom in her chest – the warmth only the comforting embrace of her beloved oven and the sweet scent of baking could provide.
