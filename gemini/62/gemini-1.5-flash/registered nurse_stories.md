The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s dark hair across her face as she hurried down the rain-slicked street, the collar of her worn peacoat pulled high.  The scent of woodsmoke and damp earth hung heavy in the air, a stark contrast to the antiseptic smell that clung to her, a permanent reminder of her twelve-hour shift at St. Jude’s Memorial Hospital.  Rain lashed against the ancient cobblestones of Ashwood, a town clinging precariously to the edge of the brooding Blackwood Forest, its gaslights casting a feeble glow against the encroaching darkness.  Amelia shivered, not entirely from the cold.  A sense of unease, sharp and prickly, had settled in her gut, a feeling that had begun the moment she'd left the hospital, a feeling that intensified with each creaking step she took closer to her cottage, nestled deep within the shadows of the ancient trees.
