The air hung thick and humid, a clinging shroud woven from the scent of frying plantains and exhaust fumes.  Cicadas buzzed a relentless, high-pitched symphony, punctuated by the sporadic blare of car horns battling for dominance on the congested streets of Port-au-Prince.  Rain, a threat perpetually looming, gathered in bruised purple clouds on the horizon, promising a downpour that would transform the already chaotic city into a churning, muddy river.  Isabelle “Izzy” Dubois, a reporter for the Haitian Times,  squeezed the sweat from her brow, the dampness clinging to her already wrinkled cotton shirt.  Her notepad, tucked into the worn leather satchel slung across her shoulder, felt heavy with the weight of unanswered questions, the simmering unease of a story that refused to reveal itself completely.
