The chipped porcelain sink groaned under the weight of Elara Vance's elbows as she scrubbed at the stubborn crust of burnt sugar clinging to the bottom of Lord Ashworth’s copper saucepan.  Dust motes, illuminated by the weak winter sun slanting through the grimy kitchen window of Blackwood Manor, danced in the air like tiny, mischievous sprites.  Blackwood Manor itself, a sprawling Gothic monstrosity perched precariously on a windswept cliff overlooking the turbulent North Sea, seemed to sigh with the damp breath of the approaching storm.  The air hung thick with the scent of woodsmoke, brine, and something faintly… unsettling.  Elara, her knuckles white against the cold porcelain, knew that something unsettling was the manor's default setting.
