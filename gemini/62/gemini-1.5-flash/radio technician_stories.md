The static crackled, a familiar hiss that usually soothed Elias Thorne, but tonight it grated on his nerves like sandpaper on bone.  Elias, a man whose life revolved around the precise calibration of frequencies and the comforting hum of perfectly tuned circuits, felt a prickling unease crawl up his spine.  The air in his workshop, normally thick with the scent of solder and ozone, now carried a metallic tang, tinged with something else... something akin to fear.  Outside, the relentless wind howled a mournful dirge against the corrugated iron walls of his shack, perched precariously on the windswept cliffs overlooking the churning grey expanse of the North Sea. The only illumination came from the flickering neon sign of "Thorne's Radio Repairs," its light barely piercing the oppressive gloom of the approaching storm.
