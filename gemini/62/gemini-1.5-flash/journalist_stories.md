The air hung thick and heavy with the scent of brine and decay, a miasma clinging to the cobbled streets of Port Blossom like a shroud.  Seagulls, fat and brazen, squawked incessantly overhead, their cries a discordant soundtrack to the rhythmic slap of waves against the weathered pilings of the harbour.  Rain, a relentless drizzle that had been falling for days, slicked the already precarious alleyways, turning them into treacherous channels of mud and refuse.  Amelia “Mia” Hawthorne, a journalist with ink-stained fingers and a perpetually weary expression, pulled her threadbare trench coat tighter around her as she navigated the labyrinthine streets, the collar pulled high to shield her face from the persistent downpour.  Her destination: the dilapidated waterfront tavern known as The Drowning Mermaid, a place whispered about in hushed tones even by the city's hardened sailors.
