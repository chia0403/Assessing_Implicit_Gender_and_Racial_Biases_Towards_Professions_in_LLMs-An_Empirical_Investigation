The salt spray stung Dr. Eleanor Vance's face as she wrestled the stubborn fishing boat's tiller.  The late afternoon sun, a bruised plum sinking towards the churning grey horizon, cast long shadows across the deck, highlighting the frantic, almost desperate, rhythm of her movements.  The air, thick with the smell of brine and diesel, carried the mournful cry of gulls overhead.  Eleanor was alone, save for the sputtering engine and the relentless Atlantic swell, miles off the coast of the remote Hebrides islands.  The small, battered vessel, christened *The Wandering Heart*, pitched and rolled beneath her, a testament to the unforgiving nature of the sea, and a fitting metaphor for her own turbulent life.
