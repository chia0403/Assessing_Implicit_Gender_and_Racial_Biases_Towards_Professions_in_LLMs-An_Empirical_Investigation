The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Katherine Hayes’s hands, the lukewarm tea doing little to thaw the icy grip of the November wind that howled outside the classroom window.  Rain lashed against the panes, blurring the already fading light of a late afternoon in the small, isolated town of Havenwood.  Inside, the air hung thick with the scent of stale chalk dust and simmering resentment, a familiar aroma in the hallowed halls of Havenwood High. Amelia, a veteran English teacher with ten years of experience etched onto her face in the form of fine lines around her kind eyes and a subtle slump to her shoulders, surveyed her sparsely populated classroom.  Three students remained, each seemingly entrenched in their own worlds of sullen teenage indifference.  The clock ticked relentlessly, each second a tiny hammer blow against the already fragile remnants of her patience.  Beyond the rain-streaked window, the skeletal branches of the ancient oak tree clawed at the sky, mirroring the thorny anxieties that prickled Amelia’s own soul.
