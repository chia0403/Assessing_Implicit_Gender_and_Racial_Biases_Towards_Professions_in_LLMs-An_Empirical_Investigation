The wind, a rasping whisper that carried the scent of sagebrush and dust, whipped across the plains, tugging at the brim of Jebediah "Jeb" Stone's weathered Stetson.  Jeb, owner and operator of the sprawling Stone Ranch, squinted at the bruised purple horizon.  The sky, usually a vast, unblemished canvas of Arizona blue, was choked with a churning mass of ominous, charcoal clouds.  His boots, worn smooth by years of traversing the parched earth, crunched on the gravel driveway leading to the rambling, adobe ranch house, its low-slung silhouette barely visible against the gathering gloom.  The air hung heavy with the promise of a storm, not just of rain, but something far more violent, something that threatened to unravel the fragile peace of his life, and the very fabric of the Stone Ranch itself.
