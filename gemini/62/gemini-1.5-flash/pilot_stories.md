The biting Alaskan wind howled a mournful song around Captain Elara Vance as she wrestled the controls of her de Havilland Beaver.  Below, the Chugach Mountains, cloaked in a thick blanket of freshly fallen snow, clawed at the bruised grey sky.  Visibility was down to near zero, the swirling blizzard a churning white chaos that threatened to swallow her whole.  The radio crackled with static, a mocking counterpoint to the rhythmic thrum of the single engine, its insistent pulse the only reliable companion in this unforgiving wilderness.  Her destination: a remote research station clinging precariously to the side of a glacier, a lifeline dependent on her arrival.  The weight of that responsibility, heavier than the snow piling on the wings, settled in Elara's stomach.  This wasn't just another flight; it was a fight for survival.
