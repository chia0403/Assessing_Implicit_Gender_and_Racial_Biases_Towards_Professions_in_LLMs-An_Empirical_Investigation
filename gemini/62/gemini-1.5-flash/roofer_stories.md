The biting November wind whipped at Elias Thorne’s thick woolen scarf, threatening to tug him from his precarious perch atop the gabled roof of the Blackwood Manor.  Below, the sprawling estate, a gothic monstrosity clinging to the cliff edge, plunged dramatically towards the churning grey sea.  The air tasted of salt and impending rain, a familiar symphony to Elias, a seasoned roofer who’d braved far worse storms than this.  But even he had to admit, Blackwood Manor held a certain…unease.  The shadows stretching long and skeletal from the ancient oaks that guarded the property seemed to writhe with an unnatural energy, and the mournful cry of gulls overhead sounded less like a call to the sea and more like a lament for something lost – or something waiting.
