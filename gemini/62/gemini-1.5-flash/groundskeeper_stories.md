The biting November wind whipped Elias Thorne's already-chapped lips raw as he wrestled with the recalcitrant branches of a rogue oak.  Leaves, the colour of burnt umber and decaying plums, clung stubbornly to their skeletal frames, resisting his efforts to clear the path leading to Blackwood Manor.  Blackwood Manor itself stood silhouetted against the bruised twilight sky – a gothic monstrosity of grey stone, its myriad windows like vacant eyes staring out across the sprawling, neglected grounds. Elias, groundskeeper and sole custodian of this sprawling estate for the past fifteen years, shivered, pulling his worn tweed cap lower over his ears.  The air, thick with the scent of damp earth and decaying vegetation, held a peculiar stillness, a premonition of something…unsettling.  Tonight, the usual quiet solitude of Blackwood felt heavier, more oppressive than usual.  The silence itself seemed to hum with an unseen energy.
