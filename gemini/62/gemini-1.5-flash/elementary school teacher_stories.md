The chipped paint of Room 2B peeled like sunburnt skin, mirroring the exhaustion etched onto Ms. Eleanor Vance’s face.  The air hung thick with the cloying sweetness of Crayola markers and the faint, lingering scent of yesterday's spilled milk.  Sunlight, fractured by the dusty windowpanes, illuminated motes of dust dancing in the otherwise still classroom.  Outside, the relentless July heat shimmered above the asphalt playground of Oakwood Elementary, a silent testament to the long, sweltering summer that lay ahead – a summer Ms. Vance desperately needed, but one that felt impossibly far away.  The last bell had rung an hour ago, but the quiet was deceptive.  The echoes of twenty-five vibrant, energetic eight-year-olds still reverberated in the lingering chaos of scattered worksheets, half-finished art projects, and the persistent, barely audible hum of the ancient overhead projector.
