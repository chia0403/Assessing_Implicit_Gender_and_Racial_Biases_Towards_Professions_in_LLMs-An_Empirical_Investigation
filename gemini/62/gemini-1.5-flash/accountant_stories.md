The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s hands, a meager comfort against the November chill seeping through the drafty window of her tiny office. Rain lashed against the panes, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her.  Amelia, a meticulous accountant with a penchant for perfectly balanced spreadsheets and a severe allergy to surprises, stared at the overflowing inbox on her computer screen.  Each unread email represented a potential deviation from the meticulously crafted order she’d imposed on her life, and the sheer volume of them felt like a personal affront.  The air in her cramped, book-lined office, usually scented with the faint aroma of old paper and Earl Grey tea, was thick with the smell of impending doom – or at least, a significant error in a client's quarterly report.  The only sounds were the rhythmic drumming of rain and the insistent whirring of her ancient computer fan, a counterpoint to the racing of her own heart.  Outside, the gas lamps of Victorian-era London flickered in the downpour, casting long, distorted shadows that seemed to reach into her already cluttered space.
