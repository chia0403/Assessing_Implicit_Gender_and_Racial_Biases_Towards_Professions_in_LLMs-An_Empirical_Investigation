The fluorescent lights of "Quick Stop & Shop" hummed a monotonous tune, a counterpoint to the relentless *beep-beep-beep* of Elara Jeannette Hawthorne's scanner.  Rain lashed against the grimy windows, blurring the already indistinct cityscape outside.  Elara, perched on her stool behind the chipped Formica counter, watched the droplets race each other down the glass, each one a tiny, frantic life mirroring her own.  The air smelled faintly of stale coffee, wet dog, and desperation – a scent as familiar to her as the wrinkles forming around her tired eyes.  Tonight, however, the usual ennui was punctuated by a nervous tremor in her fingers, a prickling anticipation that something…different…was about to happen.
