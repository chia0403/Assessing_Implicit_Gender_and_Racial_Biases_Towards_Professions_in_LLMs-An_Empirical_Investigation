The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Katherine Finch’s hands, the lukewarm tea doing little to soothe the icy tremor that ran through her.  Rain lashed against the grime-coated windows of Finch & Finch Accountants, a relentless drumming that mirrored the frantic beat of her own heart.  The air hung thick with the scent of old paper, damp wool, and the faint, metallic tang of fear.  Outside, the gas lamps of Grimsborough cast a sickly yellow glow on the slick cobblestones, barely piercing the swirling November fog that clung to the narrow alleyways like a shroud.  Amelia, perched on her high stool, surveyed the overflowing ledger before her – a chaotic landscape of meticulously inked entries that, tonight, felt less like a record of transactions and more like a cryptic map leading to something… terrible.
