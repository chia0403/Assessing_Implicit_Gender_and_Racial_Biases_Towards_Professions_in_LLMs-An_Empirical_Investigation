The biting November wind howled a mournful tune through the skeletal branches of the ancient oaks lining Blackwood Lane, whipping Amelia “Amy” Hernandez’s thick, dark hair across her face.  Amy, a journeywoman electrician with calloused hands and eyes that had seen more than their fair share of flickering fluorescent lights and frayed wiring, shivered, pulling her worn leather jacket tighter.  The air smelled of damp earth and decaying leaves, a scent she usually found comforting, but tonight, it only served to amplify the unsettling quiet that had settled over the sprawling Victorian mansion looming before her – Blackwood Manor.  Its darkened windows stared out like vacant eyes, promising secrets within its crumbling walls, secrets Amy was about to uncover.
