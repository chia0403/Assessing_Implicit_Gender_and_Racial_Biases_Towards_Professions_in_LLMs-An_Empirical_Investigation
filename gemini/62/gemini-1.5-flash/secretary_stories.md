The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Grace Hathaway’s hands, a meager comfort against the bone-chilling November wind rattling the single-pane windows of her office.  Rain lashed against the glass, blurring the already dismal view of the alleyway behind Grimshaw & Sons Accounting.  Amelia, a secretary whose meticulous nature was only surpassed by her quiet desperation, sighed, the sound swallowed by the rhythmic clatter of the ancient typewriter across the room.  The air hung thick with the scent of stale coffee, dust motes dancing in the weak light filtering through the grime-coated window, a fitting atmosphere for a firm whose future, like Amelia’s own, seemed increasingly uncertain.  The only bright spot in the room was the vibrant, almost defiant, splash of geraniums struggling to survive in a chipped terracotta pot on her desk.
