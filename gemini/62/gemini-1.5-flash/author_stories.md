The salt spray stung Amelia Katherine “Kit” Blackwood’s face as she wrestled the stubborn sailboat’s tiller.  The late afternoon sun, a bruised plum sinking towards the turbulent Atlantic, cast long shadows across the deck of the *Sea Serpent*, painting the churning waves in hues of angry purple and bruised orange.  Kit, a woman whose weathered face spoke of sun-drenched summers and tempestuous winters spent chasing stories across the globe, squinted against the glare.  Her usual haven, the cozy writer's cottage nestled amidst the rolling hills of Cornwall, felt a million miles away.  This tiny, battered vessel, however, was currently her office – a floating sanctuary where the relentless rhythm of the waves provided a more compelling muse than any quiet countryside ever could.  The manuscript for her next novel, a gripping tale of piracy and betrayal set against the backdrop of the 17th-century Caribbean, lay unfinished in her cabin, waiting impatiently for its completion.  But inspiration, like the wind, was fickle, and tonight, it seemed to have abandoned her completely.
