The biting November wind howled a mournful tune around the gothic spires of Blackwood Academy, rattling the ancient stained-glass windows and whistling through cracks in the crumbling stonework.  Inside, a far less romantic symphony played – the rhythmic squeak of mop against polished linoleum, punctuated by the clatter of a metal bucket.  This was the nightly soundtrack to Elias Thorne’s existence.  Elias, a man whose life was as meticulously organized as the rows of lockers he polished to a mirror sheen, found a peculiar comfort in the quiet solitude of Blackwood after the students had departed for their weekend escapades.  The academy, a sprawling labyrinth of echoing corridors and shadowy corners, was his kingdom, and tonight, it was particularly silent, a silence so profound it felt almost… expectant.
