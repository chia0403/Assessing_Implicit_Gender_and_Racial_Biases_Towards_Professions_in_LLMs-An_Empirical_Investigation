The scent of old paper, dried lavender, and just a hint of Earl Grey hung heavy in the air of the Willow Creek Lending Library. Outside, the late afternoon sun painted the cobbled streets of the village in a honeyed glow, but within the library's stone walls, time seemed to slow, the rhythmic tick-tock of the grandfather clock in the corner its only measure.

Primrose "Posy" Abigail Bloom, Head Librarian and keeper of Willow Creek's literary secrets, adjusted her spectacles, which had a tendency to slide down her nose, and peered over the top of her enormous, leather-bound copy of "The Collected Works of Edgar Allan Poe." The sound of a chair scraping against the floorboards near the poetry section had startled her. It was nearly closing time, and she thought she was alone.
