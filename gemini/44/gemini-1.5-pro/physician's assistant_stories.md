The biting November wind whipped around Amelia Reyes, PA-C, as she hurried across the cobblestone courtyard of St. Jude's Community Hospital, her breath clouding the frosty air. The ancient clock tower, a gothic monstrosity perched atop the hospital's oldest wing, chimed six times, its melancholy tones echoing through the gathering dusk.  St. Jude's, nestled in the heart of the perpetually overcast town of Port Blossom, Maine, was a place of stark contrasts. Its weathered stone facade hinted at a bygone era, while the gleaming, modern glass atrium jutted out from one side like a futuristic afterthought. Inside, the warmth beckoned, promising a temporary reprieve from the relentless coastal chill.  Amelia pulled her thick wool scarf tighter around her neck, the rough fabric scratching against her skin, and pushed through the heavy oak doors, the scent of antiseptic and brewing coffee washing over her.  Tonight was the first night of her new shift in the Emergency Department, and a knot of apprehension tightened in her stomach. Port Blossom was known for its eccentric inhabitants and the unpredictable nature of its emergencies, and Amelia, fresh from her rotations in bustling Boston, was about to get her first taste.
