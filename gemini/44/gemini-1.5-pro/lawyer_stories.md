Rain lashed against the gothic windows of Cassandra Ainsworth's office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her.  Gargoyles perched on the building’s edge seemed to leer down at the bustling city of New Corinth, their stony grins reflecting the cynical twist Cassandra felt tightening her stomach.  Her office, a sanctuary of rich mahogany and leather-bound law books, usually offered a sense of calm amidst the chaos of her profession, but tonight, the scent of old paper and beeswax did little to soothe her fraying nerves. The emerald green clock on the mantelpiece chimed nine times, each chime a sharp punctuation mark in the silence broken only by the drumming rain.  Cassandra Ainsworth, Esq., a woman known for her sharp intellect and even sharper tongue, stared out at the blurred cityscape, a half-empty glass of amber liquid warming in her hand.  A case file lay open on her desk, the details of the “Harrington Inheritance” illuminated by a single, brass desk lamp, its golden light casting long, dancing shadows across the room.  Something about this case felt wrong, and Cassandra, a woman with an impeccable record of winning even the most improbable of legal battles, couldn't shake the premonition that this time, she was walking into a trap.
