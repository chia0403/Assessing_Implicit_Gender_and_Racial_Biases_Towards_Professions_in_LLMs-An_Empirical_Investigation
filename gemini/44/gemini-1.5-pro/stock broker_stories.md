Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of Amelia Hayes's thirty-second-floor office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her.  The Chicago skyline, normally a glittering tapestry of steel and glass, was obscured by a swirling grey fog.  Inside, the air crackled with a nervous energy that had nothing to do with the approaching storm.  Amelia Hayes, Senior Portfolio Manager at Blackwood & Thorne Investments, tapped a manicured nail against her mahogany desk, the rhythmic click a counterpoint to the frantic ringing of phones and the hushed anxieties echoing through the open-plan office.  The market was tanking, and Amelia, usually a beacon of cool-headed rationality in the turbulent world of high finance, was starting to feel the pressure.  Today wasn't just any Tuesday; it was the day her biggest client, the notoriously volatile tech mogul, Victor Sterling, was due to review his portfolio.  And if the market didn't correct itself soon, Amelia was about to deliver him some very bad news.
