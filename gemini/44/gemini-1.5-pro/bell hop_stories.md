The revolving doors of the Grand Majestic Hotel sighed open, exhaling a gust of frigid Chicago air that chased away the scent of lilies and old money.  Jasper Finch, his pillbox hat perched precariously on his head, shivered and tugged his burgundy uniform coat tighter.  Snow fell in thick, lazy flakes, transforming the bustling Michigan Avenue into a muted, Impressionist painting.  Inside, the lobby glowed with warmth and the murmur of hushed conversations. Crystal chandeliers cast dancing light on the polished marble floor, reflecting in the gleaming brass luggage carts Jasper maneuvered with practiced ease.  He'd been a bellhop at the Grand Majestic for five years, long enough to know the regular guests' preferred newspapers and the precise angle at which Mrs. Vandergelt liked her sherry served.  He knew the secrets whispered behind cupped hands in the dimly lit corners, the clandestine meetings that took place in the opulent suites, the hushed heartbreak that sometimes echoed in the cavernous halls.  Tonight, however, felt different.  A strange tension hummed in the air, a premonition that whispered of something more than the usual holiday rush.
