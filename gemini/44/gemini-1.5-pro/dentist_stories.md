The salt spray kissed Dr. Elias Thorne's cheeks as he leaned against the railing of the ferry, the "Salty Siren," churning its way across the choppy waters of the Penobscot Bay.  The air, thick with the scent of brine and pine, invigorated him after the stuffy confines of his Boston practice.  Elias loosened the knot of his tie, his gaze fixed on the hazy outline of Monhegan Island, a solitary sentinel on the horizon. He wasn't coming to this remote, windswept isle for a vacation. He was inheriting a dental practice, and a whole lot more than he bargained for.
