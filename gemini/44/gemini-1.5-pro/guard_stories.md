The biting wind whipped across the desolate plains of Aeridor, carrying with it the scent of snow and the low growl of distant thunder.  Jagged peaks, capped with a precariously balanced layer of white, clawed at the iron-grey sky. Nestled between two of these behemoths lay the fortress of Duskwatch, a solitary sentinel against the encroaching darkness. Its walls, built from obsidian stone, seemed to absorb the meager light, giving it an air of perpetual twilight.  Here, on the westernmost rampart, stood Elias Thorne, his breath misting in the frigid air.  Clad in thick furs and steel plate, his hand resting on the hilt of his frost-rimed sword, Elias scanned the horizon, his eyes narrowed against the swirling snow. He was a veteran of the Duskwatch Guard, his face weathered and lined, a testament to years spent battling the creatures that roamed the frozen wastes beyond the wall. Tonight, however, a different kind of chill settled in his bones, a premonition of something far more sinister than the usual blizzard-borne beasts.
