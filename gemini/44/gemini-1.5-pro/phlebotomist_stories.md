The fluorescent lights of St. Jude's Memorial Hospital hummed a monotonous B flat, a soundtrack Amelia Reyes was intimately familiar with.  The pre-dawn chill still clung to the air in the phlebotomy lab, even though the radiators hissed valiantly against it. Outside, the Chicago skyline was just beginning to blush a pale pink, the skyscrapers jagged teeth against the lightening sky. Amelia, still rubbing sleep from her eyes, pulled on her lavender gloves, the color a small rebellion against the sterile white of the lab.  She adjusted the name tag pinned to her scrubs - *Amelia Reyes, CPT* - and surveyed the neatly arranged trays of needles, tourniquets, and vials, waiting like soldiers for the morning's onslaught. Today was Wednesday, which meant oncology first, followed by maternity, then a mixed bag of general patients.  Amelia took a deep breath, the faint metallic scent of disinfectant filling her lungs.  She was ready.
