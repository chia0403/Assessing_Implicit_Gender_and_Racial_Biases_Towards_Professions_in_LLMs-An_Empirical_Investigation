The fluorescent lights of Mr. Alistair Finch's classroom hummed a monotonous B-flat, a counterpoint to the frantic scratching of pencils against paper as his eleventh-grade English class wrestled with the symbolism in *The Great Gatsby*.  Rain lashed against the tall, arched windows, blurring the view of the sprawling, manicured lawns of Crestview Academy, a prestigious boarding school nestled in the rolling hills of the Berkshire Mountains. October had arrived, cloaked in mist and a melancholic chill that seeped even through the thick stone walls of the venerable institution.  Alistair, his tweed jacket pulled tight against the draft, paced between the rows of mahogany desks, a faint scent of pipe tobacco clinging to his clothes. He paused, adjusting his spectacles, and peered over the shoulder of a student struggling with the concept of the green light. Outside, the wind howled like a banshee, a stark contrast to the hushed intensity of the classroom, a precursor to something unsettling on the horizon.
