The Himalayan pink salt lamp cast a warm, rosy glow across Dr. Elias Thorne's cluttered desk, illuminating stacks of research papers on the anti-inflammatory properties of turmeric and the dubious benefits of detox teas. Rain lashed against the windowpane of his small, second-floor office overlooking the bustling Pike Place Market in Seattle, Washington. The air, thick with the aroma of freshly brewed coffee from the stall below, mingled with the faint scent of jasmine tea Elias perpetually kept steeping on his electric burner.  It was a Friday evening, the kind of evening most people relished as the gateway to the weekend.  But for Elias, Fridays were often his busiest, filled with last-minute appointments from clients desperately seeking nutritional guidance before succumbing to the siren song of weekend indulgences.
