The biting wind whipped Amelia "Amy" Reyes's braid across her face as she surveyed the overgrown monstrosity before her. Brambles clawed at the wrought iron gates, obscuring the peeling paint of the once-grand Victorian house. Ivy, thick as pythons, snaked up the brick walls, swallowing windows whole. The air, heavy with the scent of damp earth and decaying leaves, hung still in the neglected gardens of Eventide Manor, nestled deep in the fog-choked valleys of the Oregon Coast Range. Amy, her hands shoved deep in the pockets of her worn Carhartt jacket, let out a low whistle. This wasn't just a landscaping job; it was an archaeological dig.  This was her biggest challenge yet, and the potential, buried beneath years of neglect, shimmered like a hidden emerald in the grey light.
