The rusty bell above the door of "Shear Perfection" jingled merrily, announcing the arrival of Mrs. Henderson, a woman whose blue rinse could rival the intensity of a summer sky.  Amelia Rose Hawthorne, proprietor and sole stylist, sighed internally. Mrs. Henderson's weekly appointment was less a haircut and more a therapy session, punctuated by the snip of scissors.  Shear Perfection wasn't exactly on the cutting edge of fashion – situated as it was between a laundromat and a pet supplies store in the sleepy, seaside town of Port Blossom, Maine. Salt air perpetually hung heavy, leaving a faint, briny scent clinging to everything, including Amelia's meticulously crafted updo. The salon itself was a cozy space, crammed with vintage styling chairs and framed photos of Amelia’s award-winning poodle cuts from her younger, more ambitious days. Outside, the fog was rolling in, obscuring the usually picturesque view of the harbor, mirroring the vague sense of unease that had settled over Amelia in recent weeks.  Something was brewing in Port Blossom, something more than just the usual sea mist and gossip, and she couldn't quite put her finger on it.
