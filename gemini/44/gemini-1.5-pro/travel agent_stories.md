Rain lashed against the window of "Away With You Travel," blurring the neon pink flamingo perched precariously on the sill. Inside, Amelia "Amy"  Rose Hawthorne sighed, the rhythmic drumming of the downpour a counterpoint to the insistent buzzing of her computer.  The small, cluttered agency, nestled between a laundromat and a perpetually empty storefront in the sleepy seaside town of Port Blossom, Oregon, usually exuded a cheery, escapist vibe.  Tonight, however, the vibrant travel posters and meticulously crafted miniature hot air balloons hanging from the ceiling felt less like promises of adventure and more like mocking reminders of a world beyond the dreary grey confines of her reality.  It was a Tuesday night in November, the off-season had sunk its teeth in deep, and Amy hadn't booked a single trip in over a week.
