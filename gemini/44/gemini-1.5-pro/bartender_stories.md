The rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of The Rusty Mug, a rhythm that usually lulled Adeline "Addie" Bellweather into a meditative state as she polished glasses.  Tonight, however, the relentless drumming echoed the frantic beat of her own heart. The Mug, nestled between a pawn shop and a perpetually closed fortune teller's parlor on the outskirts of Dustbowl, Nevada, was usually a haven from the biting desert wind and the even more biting loneliness of the town. But tonight, the air crackled with a tension thicker than the dust devils that danced across the deserted main street.  The only light came from the flickering neon sign outside that buzzed like a trapped insect, and the single bare bulb hanging precariously above the worn mahogany bar. Addie wiped down the sticky counter, her gaze fixed on the door, waiting.
