The air in the Bumblebrook Daycare Center buzzed with the chaotic energy only a room full of toddlers could generate.  Finger-painted masterpieces dripped from the walls, a tower of brightly colored blocks swayed precariously in the corner, and the rhythmic thump of tiny feet on linoleum accompanied a chorus of shrieks and giggles. Amidst this vibrant pandemonium, Amelia Grace Hawthorne, her auburn hair pulled back in a practical braid, knelt patiently, helping two-year-old Leo navigate the treacherous terrain of a particularly stubborn zipper on his miniature firetruck coat. Outside, fat snowflakes began to fall, blanketing the sleepy town of Oakhaven, Connecticut, in a soft layer of white, transforming the bustling daycare into a cozy haven against the encroaching winter chill.
