The bell above the door of "Petal & Stem" chimed a melancholic two-note tune, the sound swallowed by the thick scent of lilies and damp earth. Rain lashed against the large front window, blurring the view of the deserted cobblestone street in the small, coastal town of Port Blossom, Oregon.  Eleanor Ainsworth, her fingers stained a delicate shade of violet from arranging irises, sighed and brushed a stray strand of auburn hair from her freckled forehead.  November had arrived with its usual dreary fanfare, casting a pall over the usually vibrant little shop.  It was the slowest time of year for a florist, especially one tucked away on a side street, a fact that was beginning to weigh heavily on Eleanor's mind as she tallied the meager earnings of the day.
