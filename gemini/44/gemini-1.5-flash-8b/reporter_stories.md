The flickering neon sign of the "Golden Galleon" cast a sickly yellow glow on Amelia Petrova's rain-slicked trench coat.  She shivered, not just from the biting wind whipping across the deserted docks of Port Leviathan, but from the gnawing premonition that clung to her like the clinging kelp on the pilings.  The air, thick with the smell of brine and something indefinably acrid, hung heavy, mirroring the knot in her stomach.  Port Leviathan, a sprawling, forgotten industrial hub nestled between the jagged teeth of the coastal cliffs, pulsed with a hushed, almost spectral energy.  Rusty cranes, skeletal fingers reaching for a sky choked with smog, dominated the landscape.  Amelia, a seasoned reporter for the *Coastal Chronicle*, had come seeking answers, but the city whispered secrets only the wind seemed keen to carry.
