The humid air hung thick and heavy, a suffocating blanket draped over the bustling city of Rio de Janeiro.  Sunlight, filtered through the dense canopy of the Tijuca National Park, dappled the cobblestone patio of "Academia da Serra," casting long shadows that danced with the sweat glistening on Amelia Rodriguez's brow.  Amelia, a petite woman with fiery red hair perpetually escaping its messy braid, surveyed the group of twenty-something trainees.  Their faces, flushed and determined, reflected the intense workout they'd just completed.  Today's session focused on the demanding "Serra Sprint," a circuit designed to push the limits of physical and mental endurance.
