Rain hammered against the grimy windows of the dilapidated law office, mirroring the relentless drumming in Elara Blackwood's head.  The flickering fluorescent lights cast long, distorted shadows across the cluttered space, highlighting the mountains of legal documents piled high on every available surface.  Dust motes danced in the feeble light, swirling around the chipped porcelain mug of lukewarm coffee Elara clutched, its contents long since forgotten.  Outside, the city of Aethelgard, choked by perpetual smog, grumbled and groaned in the downpour.  Aethelgard, a city built on the ruins of forgotten empires and whispered promises, was a city of shadows, and tonight, Elara Blackwood, sharp-tongued and fiercely independent lawyer, felt swallowed by them.  The case, a labyrinthine tangle of corporate deceit and political maneuvering, had consumed her for months, and she felt the gnawing dread of imminent failure tightening around her heart. The faded wallpaper, peeling in places to reveal the damp plaster beneath, seemed to mock her with its unchanging backdrop of despair.
