Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of the dilapidated "Hawks' Nest" sports complex, mirroring the storm brewing inside Amelia "Ames" Carmichael.  The air, thick with the smell of damp earth and stale sweat, hung heavy in the dimly lit weight room.  Broken-down equipment cast long, skeletal shadows across the space, mocking the half-hearted attempts at lifting weights by the undernourished athletes scattered about.  This was Ames's third season coaching the Hawks, a struggling high school team perpetually on the brink of collapse, and each season felt more daunting than the last.  The team's star quarterback, a promising but volatile senior named Leo, was visibly frustrated, slumping over a bench, his jaw clenched. The weight of expectations, both his and Ames's, was crushing.
