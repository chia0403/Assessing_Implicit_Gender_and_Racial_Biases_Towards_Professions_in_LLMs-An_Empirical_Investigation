The fluorescent hum of the lab was a constant, low thrum against Elara Nightingale's ears, a soundtrack to her life.  Empty vials lined the shelves like skeletal fingers, their sterile tips pointing towards the sterile white walls of the BioMed Diagnostics lab in the bustling city of Atheria.  Rain hammered against the grimy windows, mirroring the relentless rhythm of the automated machines humming in the background.  Elara, a phlebotomist with the quiet efficiency of a well-oiled machine herself, meticulously prepared a tray of sterile needles and antiseptic wipes.  The day had just begun, and already the lab, a concrete fortress in the heart of the city, felt like a mausoleum of human biology.
