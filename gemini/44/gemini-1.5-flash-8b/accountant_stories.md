The fluorescent lights of the accounting firm, "Numbers & Nuggets," hummed a monotonous tune, a soundtrack to Amelia Butterfield's daily grind.  Amelia, a meticulously organized accountant with a penchant for spreadsheets and perfectly sharpened pencils, stared at the towering stack of invoices.  They were a mountain, a daunting Everest of paperwork, threatening to bury her under a deluge of figures.  The air in the small, windowless office hung thick with the scent of stale coffee and the quiet, almost oppressive, concentration of dozens of other accountants hunched over their own individual battles with spreadsheets.  Clocks ticked, relentlessly marking the passage of time, each second a potential pitfall in the labyrinthine world of financial statements.  This was Tuesday, the worst day of the week, and the start of Amelia's relentless, quarterly tax season marathon.
