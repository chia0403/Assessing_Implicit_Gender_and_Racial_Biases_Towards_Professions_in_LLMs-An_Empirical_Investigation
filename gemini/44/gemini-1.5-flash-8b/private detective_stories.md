The rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of the "Grey Hound" diner, a rhythmic tattoo that mirrored the frantic beat of Amelia "Ace" Hawthorne's heart.  The air inside was thick with the smell of stale coffee and desperation.  Rain-slicked streets reflected the flickering neon sign, casting long, distorted shadows across the Formica tables.  Ace, a private investigator with eyes the color of storm clouds and a reputation as sharp as a broken shard of glass, hunched over a chipped mug, the chipped ceramic a stark contrast to the expensive, custom-made trench coat she wore.  Outside, the city of Ashwood, a sprawling metropolis choked by smog and simmering tensions, was a swirling vortex of grey and gloom.  The year was 1947.  Ace's phone, a bulky, antique device, lay silent on the counter, its coiled cord a useless gesture in this tempest of bad news.  A case, a seemingly insignificant case involving a missing actress and a mysterious benefactor, had landed in her lap and, like the relentless downpour, had rapidly escalated into something far more sinister, something that threatened to engulf the city in a web of deceit and violence.
