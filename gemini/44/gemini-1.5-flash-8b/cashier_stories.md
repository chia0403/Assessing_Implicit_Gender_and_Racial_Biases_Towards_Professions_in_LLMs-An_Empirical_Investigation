The fluorescent lights of the "Everything You Need, Under One Roof" mega-mart hummed, a monotonous soundtrack to Amelia Butterfield's life.  A life, she mused, largely defined by the rhythmic clang of the cash register, the predictable shuffle of shoppers, and the faint, sickly sweet smell of discounted candy corn wafting from the aisle behind her.  Amelia, a twenty-two-year-old cashier, felt a persistent ache in her left wrist, a familiar companion these days.  The oversized shopping carts, filled with everything from broccoli to inflatable unicorns, seemed to weigh down the very air around her, not just her wrists.  Today, however, something shifted.  A man, tall and gaunt, with eyes that seemed to hold a lifetime of unspoken stories, was staring at her.  He wasn't buying anything.  He was just standing there, a silent observer in the brightly lit chaos, his gaze lingering on the chipped paint of the register counter.
