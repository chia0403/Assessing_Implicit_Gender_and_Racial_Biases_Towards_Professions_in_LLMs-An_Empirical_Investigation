The fluorescent lights of the "Tax-Free Zone" hummed, a relentless, almost hypnotic drone that accompanied Amelia Thistlewick's workday.  Rain hammered against the grimy storefront windows, mirroring the relentless deluge of paperwork piled high on her desk.  Her tiny office, tucked away on a side street in the perpetually overcast city of Ashwood, was a symphony of rustling papers, the occasional exasperated sigh, and the faint scent of stale coffee.  Tax season was in full swing, and Amelia, a meticulous income tax preparer with a sharp eye for detail and an even sharper wit, was battling the storm, both literal and metaphorical.  Today, a particularly confusing set of deductions from a client named Bartholomew "Barty" Butterfield threatened to unravel her carefully constructed morning routine.  Barty Butterfield, a flamboyant antique dealer with a penchant for elaborate tax strategies (and even more elaborate hats), was already a thorn in her side.
