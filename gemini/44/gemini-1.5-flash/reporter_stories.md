The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Holloway’s trench coat around her legs as she hunched deeper into the shadows of the crumbling Victorian mansion.  Rain, a relentless, icy drizzle, plastered her already damp auburn hair to her forehead.  Above, the skeletal branches of ancient oaks clawed at the bruised, twilight sky, their silhouettes a grotesque parody of welcoming arms.  This wasn’t the charming, historic district the tourist brochures promised; this was Blackwood Manor, infamous for its eccentric owner, its rumoured hauntings, and, most recently, its string of unexplained disappearances. Amelia, a reporter for the *Kingston Chronicle*, shivered, not entirely from the cold.  The air hung heavy with a silence more unnerving than any shriek, a silence broken only by the rhythmic drip, drip, drip of water leaking from a broken gargoyle perched high above.  Tonight, she was determined to uncover the truth behind the whispers, even if it meant facing the shadows lurking within Blackwood’s decaying walls.
