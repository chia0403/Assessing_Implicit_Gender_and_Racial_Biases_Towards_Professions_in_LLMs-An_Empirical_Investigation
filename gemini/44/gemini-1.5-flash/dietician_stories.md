The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s sensible, oatmeal-colored coat around her legs as she hurried down Bleecker Street.  Rain, the kind that clings to you like a second skin, plastered her dark hair to her forehead.  The air, thick with the scent of roasting chestnuts and damp brick, did little to alleviate the gnawing anxiety in her stomach.  It wasn’t the pre-Thanksgiving rush that usually sent a shiver down her spine – Amelia, a registered dietician with a thriving practice nestled above a charming Italian deli on the same street, was used to the chaos.  No, this was different.  This was the kind of anxiety that tasted like cold, leftover kale chips and tasted like impending doom.  It stemmed from the cryptic message scrawled on the napkin she'd found tucked into the delivery of organic quinoa earlier that morning:  *The truth lies buried beneath the kale.*
