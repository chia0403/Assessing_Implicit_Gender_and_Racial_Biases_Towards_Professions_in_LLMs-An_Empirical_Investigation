The air hung thick and humid, a clinging blanket woven from the scent of jasmine and exhaust fumes.  Cicadas buzzed a relentless, rhythmic counterpoint to the insistent thrum of traffic snaking its way through the neon-drenched streets of Ho Chi Minh City.  Rain threatened, the sky a bruised purple bruise, mirroring the exhaustion etched onto the face of Anya Sharma.  Anya, a whiz-kid coder with a reputation for turning impossible deadlines into improbable triumphs, slumped deeper into her worn leather armchair, the glow of her multiple monitor setup illuminating the dark circles under her eyes.  Empty energy drink cans littered the desk like fallen soldiers after a particularly brutal coding war.  Her apartment, a cramped but stylish space nestled high above the city's clamor, offered a stunning view, but Anya barely registered it.  Tonight, the panoramic vista of glittering skyscrapers held no allure; only the flickering cursor on her screen held her captive.  The deadline for Project Nightingale loomed, and Anya was running out of time.
