The salt spray stung Amelia Katherine Stone's face as she wrestled the stubborn fishing net from its tangle on the deck of the *Sea Serpent*.  The late afternoon sun, a bruised plum sinking below the jagged peaks of the Outer Hebrides, cast long shadows across the churning grey water.  The air, thick with the smell of brine and peat smoke, carried the mournful cry of gulls overhead.  Amelia, her tailored charcoal suit incongruous against the rugged backdrop, gritted her teeth, her perfectly manicured nails already showing the first signs of wear.  This wasn't exactly the kind of pre-trial relaxation her high-powered London firm had envisioned for her, but then again, this wasn't exactly the kind of case she usually handled.  This was Skye, and this was personal.
