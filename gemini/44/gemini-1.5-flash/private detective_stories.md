The rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of Isabella “Izzy” Diaz’s office, a rhythm that mirrored the relentless pounding in her head.  The scent of stale coffee and cheap cigarettes hung heavy in the air, a familiar perfume in the cramped, dimly lit space above a laundromat on the grittier side of San Francisco's Mission District.  A flickering neon sign outside cast a sickly yellow glow on the peeling paint and mismatched furniture – a battered desk piled high with case files, a threadbare armchair where Izzy usually collapsed after a long day, and a chipped ceramic mug perpetually filled with lukewarm java.  The only window offered a view of a fire escape rusting elegantly into oblivion, a fitting metaphor for her current mood.  Izzy, a woman whose sharp eyes and even sharper wit were usually her greatest assets, felt thoroughly defeated.  The case of the missing manuscript, a seemingly simple matter initially, had unravelled into a tangled web of deceit and danger, leaving her more lost than she cared to admit.
