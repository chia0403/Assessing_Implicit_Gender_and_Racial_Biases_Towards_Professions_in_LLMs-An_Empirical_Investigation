The chipped Formica tabletop felt slick beneath Amelia Hernandez's fingertips as she wiped it down for the tenth time that hour.  The air in Rosie's Diner hung thick with the scent of frying bacon and simmering coffee, a greasy perfume that clung to everything, including Amelia herself.  Fluorescent lights buzzed overhead, casting a harsh, yellow glow on the faded checkered floor and the worn vinyl booths that lined the walls.  Rosie’s, nestled on the edge of Highway 17, just outside the sleepy town of Harmony Creek, was a place where time seemed to slow to a crawl, punctuated only by the rhythmic clatter of cutlery and the occasional disgruntled sigh from a truck driver nursing a lukewarm cup of joe.  Outside, the relentless summer sun beat down on the cracked asphalt, promising another scorcher of a day.  Amelia sighed, the weight of another double shift pressing down on her like the humid air.  This wasn't the life she'd envisioned, not by a long shot, but rent was due, and Rosie’s, despite its flaws, paid the bills.
