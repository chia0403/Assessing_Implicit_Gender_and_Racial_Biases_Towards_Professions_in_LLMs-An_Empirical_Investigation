The biting November wind whipped Dr. Eleanor Vance's auburn hair across her face as she hurried down the cobbled lane, the scent of woodsmoke and brine clinging to the damp air.  Salt spray, whipped into a frenzy by the relentless gale, stung her cheeks.  She pulled her worn wool coat tighter, the collar scratching against her neck, a small discomfort she barely registered.  The tiny fishing village of Portwenn, nestled precariously between the churning Atlantic and the brooding cliffs of Cornwall, was beautiful, even in its stormy moods, but tonight, its beauty felt almost menacing.  The single gas lamp casting a feeble glow at the entrance to her surgery did little to dispel the pervasive gloom. Inside, the warmth of the waiting room, and the promise of a hot cup of tea, felt a million miles away.
