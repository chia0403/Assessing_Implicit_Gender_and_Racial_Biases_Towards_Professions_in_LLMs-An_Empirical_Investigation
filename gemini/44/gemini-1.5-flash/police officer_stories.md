The salt spray stung Officer Elias Thorne's face as he wrestled the battered patrol car onto the narrow, cliff-side road.  Below, the churning grey expanse of the Atlantic roared its displeasure at the relentless November wind, the waves crashing against jagged rocks with the sound of a thousand bones breaking.  The air, thick with the smell of brine and something vaguely metallic – the lingering scent of blood, perhaps? – bit through his thick wool jacket.  This wasn't the picturesque coastal town advertised in the brochures; this was Craghaven, a place where the sea’s fury was mirrored in the hearts of its inhabitants, a place where secrets clung to the windswept cliffs like barnacles to a hull. Tonight, Elias Thorne was about to dredge up one of those secrets.
