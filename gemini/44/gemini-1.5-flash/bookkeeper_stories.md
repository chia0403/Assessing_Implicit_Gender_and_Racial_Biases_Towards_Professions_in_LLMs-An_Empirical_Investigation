The chipped porcelain teacup warmed Amelia Beatrice Finch’s fingers, a meager comfort against the November chill that seeped through the single-paned window of her office.  Rain lashed against the glass, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her.  Amelia, a bookkeeper of meticulous habit and even more meticulous accounting, stared at the ledger sprawled across her desk, a chaotic mess of crimson ink splatters marring the usually pristine columns of figures.  The air in her small, cluttered office on the third floor of the crumbling Blackwood Building smelled of damp wool, old paper, and the faint, lingering scent of lavender – a futile attempt to mask the pervasive odour of desperation clinging to the unsorted paperwork.  Outside, the gas lamps flickered weakly in the downpour, casting long, distorted shadows that danced across the cobblestones of Grimsborough’s forgotten corner of Market Street.  Tonight, however, the shadows felt less atmospheric and more menacing.  Tonight, Amelia Beatrice Finch’s carefully ordered world was about to unravel.
