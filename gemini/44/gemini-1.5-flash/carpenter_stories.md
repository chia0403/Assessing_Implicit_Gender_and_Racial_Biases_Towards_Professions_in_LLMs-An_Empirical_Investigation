The salt spray stung Elias Thorne's face as he wrestled the stubborn oak plank into place.  The air, thick with the smell of brine and drying fish, whipped through the skeletal frame of the boathouse, clinging to his calloused hands.  Seagulls shrieked overhead, their cries a counterpoint to the rhythmic clang of his hammer against iron.  Elias, a man whose life was as weathered and dependable as the ancient timbers he worked, squinted at the swirling grey expanse of the North Sea, a restless giant churning just beyond the decaying pilings.  The boathouse itself, perched precariously on the cliff edge overlooking the churning water in the small, windswept village of Port Blossom, was as ancient and gnarled as Elias himself, its timbers groaning under the relentless assault of the elements.
