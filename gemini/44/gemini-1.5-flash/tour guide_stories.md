The humid air hung heavy, thick with the scent of frangipani and impending rain.  Cicadas buzzed a relentless symphony from the emerald canopy that draped over the ancient Mayan ruins of Tikal, their song a counterpoint to the rhythmic thud of Amelia Hernandez's sandals on the uneven stone path.  Amelia, a woman whose smile lines spoke of years spent under the relentless Guatemalan sun, adjusted the brim of her wide-brimmed hat, her dark eyes scanning the faces of the tourists trailing behind her.  Their anticipation, a palpable energy that crackled in the stifling heat, was a familiar comfort, a welcome distraction from the gnawing unease that had settled in her stomach since dawn.  Today wasn't just another tour; today, Amelia felt, held a different kind of history waiting to be unearthed, a secret whispered on the wind rustling through the colossal jungle trees.
