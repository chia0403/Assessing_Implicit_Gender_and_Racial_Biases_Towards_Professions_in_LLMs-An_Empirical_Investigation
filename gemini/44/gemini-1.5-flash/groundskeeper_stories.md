The biting November wind whipped around Elias Thorne, stinging his cheeks and tugging at the worn brim of his cap.  He hunched deeper into his threadbare jacket, the chill seeping through the multiple layers as he surveyed the skeletal remains of the sprawling Blackwood Manor gardens.  Fallen leaves, the colour of dried blood and rust, crunched under his heavy boots, a melancholic symphony accompanying the mournful sigh of the wind through the bare branches of ancient oaks.  Blackwood Manor itself, a gothic behemoth of grey stone, loomed over him, its many windows like vacant eyes staring out at the decaying beauty of its neglected grounds.  Elias, groundskeeper for the manor for the past twenty years, felt the familiar pang of responsibility, a weight heavier than the overflowing wheelbarrow beside him, filled with the debris of a bygone summer.
