The scent of aged paper and leather, a perfume both comforting and slightly melancholic, clung to Elara Genevieve Hawthorne like a second skin.  Dust motes, illuminated by the single shaft of afternoon sunlight slicing through the arched window of the Blackwood Library's Rare Books Room, danced in the air like tiny, mischievous spirits.  Elara, her dark hair pulled back in a severe bun that betrayed neither her age nor her inherent calm, adjusted her spectacles, the faint click a tiny counterpoint to the rhythmic tick-tock of the grandfather clock in the corner.  Outside, the wind howled a mournful ballad against the ancient stone walls, a stark contrast to the hushed reverence within.  This was her sanctuary, this labyrinth of knowledge, and tonight, it held a secret she wasn't sure she was ready to uncover.
