The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne's hair across her face as she hurried down the rain-slicked street, the collar of her worn wool coat pulled high.  The scent of woodsmoke and damp earth clung to the air, a familiar fragrance in the quaint, if somewhat dilapidated, coastal town of Port Blossom.  Amelia, a registered nurse at the small, understaffed St. Jude's Hospital, pulled her damp gloves tighter, the chill seeping through the thin fabric.  The hospital, a gingerbread-trimmed Victorian monstrosity perched precariously on a cliff overlooking the turbulent ocean, was visible in the distance, its yellow glow a beacon against the gathering gloom. Tonight, however, the comforting light felt less like a promise of warmth and more like a warning signal, a silent invitation to another long, grueling shift.
