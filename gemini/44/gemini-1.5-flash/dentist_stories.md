The biting wind whipped Dr. Eleanor Vance’s tailored coat around her legs as she hurried across the deserted town square.  Snow, the kind that clung with icy determination, dusted the cobblestone streets of Havenwood, transforming the quaint New England village into a postcard illustration of a winter’s slumber.  The only sound louder than the wind’s mournful howl was the rhythmic crunch of her worn leather boots against the frozen ground.  Havenwood, population 1,287 (give or take a few snowbirds), was usually quiet, but tonight, even its usual hushed serenity felt…off. A palpable tension hung in the air, thick and heavy as the falling snow, and Eleanor Vance, despite her years spent calmly navigating the anxieties of patients in her dental chair, felt an unfamiliar prickle of unease crawl up her spine.
