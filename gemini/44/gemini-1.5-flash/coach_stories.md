The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Amy” Hernandez’s hair across her face as she surveyed the desolate soccer field.  Fallen leaves, the color of rusting metal, clung to the damp grass, swirling in miniature tornadoes around the lone, skeletal goalposts.  The air hung heavy with the scent of damp earth and woodsmoke from distant chimneys.  This wasn't the manicured green of the prestigious academy she'd left behind; this was the cracked, uneven pitch of Westview High, a school clinging precariously to the edge of the sprawling city, a place where hope often felt as fragile as the thin layer of ice forming on the puddles.  Amy, head coach of the Westview Wildcats girls' soccer team, pulled her worn, navy blue jacket tighter, a familiar knot of apprehension tightening in her stomach.  Tonight, more than ever, the weight of expectation felt like a physical burden.  This was their playoff game, and the Wildcats were facing a team whose reputation for ruthlessness preceded them like a cold front.
