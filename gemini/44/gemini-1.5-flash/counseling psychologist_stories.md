The chipped paint of Dr. Eleanor Vance’s office window frame mirrored the cracks in the plaster of the ceiling, a testament to the building's age and, perhaps, its neglect.  Rain lashed against the glass, a relentless drumming that mirrored the anxieties churning within Eleanor herself.  She sat at her antique desk, the mahogany polished to a deep, lustrous sheen that belied the worn leather of her chair – a comfortable perch from which she’d witnessed countless tears, confessions, and breakthroughs over the twenty years she’d practiced counseling psychology in this quiet, unassuming corner of Willow Creek.  Tonight, however, the silence felt heavy, suffocating even, punctuated only by the rhythmic thrum of the rain and the faint ticking of a grandfather clock in the hallway, its pendulum a metronome counting down to an unknown future.  The air hung thick with the scent of old books and damp earth, a fragrance both comforting and unsettling, mirroring the complex emotions Eleanor felt as she waited.
