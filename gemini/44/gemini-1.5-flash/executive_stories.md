The stale scent of burnt coffee and desperation clung to the air in Amelia “Mia” Hernandez’s mahogany-paneled office, a stark contrast to the pristine view of the Manhattan skyline sprawling outside her floor-to-ceiling windows.  Rain lashed against the glass, mirroring the tempest brewing inside the forty-year-old CEO of Hernandez Global Dynamics.  Mia, her usually impeccable chignon loosened and several strands escaping to frame a face etched with fatigue, stared at the blinking cursor on her laptop screen.  The deadline for the Peterson acquisition – the deal that could make or break her company – loomed like a guillotine, its blade poised to fall at precisely 11:59 PM.  The plush, Italian leather of her chair offered little comfort; its luxurious embrace felt more like a gilded cage. The only sound besides the relentless drumming of rain was the low hum of the city, a relentless, indifferent symphony playing out against the backdrop of her personal crisis.
