The chipped paint of the probation office’s waiting room peeled like sunburnt skin, mirroring the frayed edges of Elias Thorne’s patience.  A half-eaten bag of stale donuts sat on the counter, a testament to the early morning struggles of his colleague, a woman whose name he could never quite remember.  The air hung thick with the scent of cheap coffee and desperation; a familiar perfume in the sterile, fluorescent-lit space of the Department of Corrections, Branch 7, nestled on the grittier side of Hemlock County, Oregon.  Outside, the relentless Pacific rain hammered against the grimy windows, a rhythm as monotonous and predictable as the lives of many of the individuals Elias supervised.  This morning, however, felt different.  A nervous energy thrummed beneath Elias’s usual weary calm, a premonition whispering of something about to shatter the tedious routine.  His next appointment, a young man named Caleb Finch, had a history as unpredictable as the Oregon weather.
