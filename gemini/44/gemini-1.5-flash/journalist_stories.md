The biting November wind whipped Amelia Katherine "Amy" Holloway's hair across her face, stinging her cheeks a vibrant crimson that clashed spectacularly with the somber grey of the Edinburgh sky.  Rain, a relentless drizzle that seemed woven into the very fabric of the city, plastered her trench coat to her back.  She hunched deeper into the collar, the damp chill seeping into her bones despite the layers of wool and cashmere.  Around her, the Royal Mile, usually teeming with tourists, was eerily deserted, the ancient stone buildings looming like brooding sentinels under the oppressive sky.  Only the rhythmic slap of her worn leather boots on the slick cobblestones punctuated the silence, a counterpoint to the mournful moan of the wind whistling through the narrow wynds.  Amy, a journalist for the Edinburgh Evening Chronicle, pulled her worn notepad from her bag, the dampness already softening the paper.  Tonight, the city held a secret, and she intended to find it.
