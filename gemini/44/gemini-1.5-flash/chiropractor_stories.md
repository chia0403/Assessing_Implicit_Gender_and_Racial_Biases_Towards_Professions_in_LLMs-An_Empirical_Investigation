The salt spray stung Dr. Eleanor Vance’s face as she wrestled the stubborn fishing net onto the deck of her small boat, the *Sea Serpent*.  The late afternoon sun, a bruised plum against the churning grey Atlantic, cast long shadows across the weathered wood.  The air, thick with the scent of brine and seaweed, carried the mournful cry of gulls overhead.  Eleanor, her dark hair plastered to her forehead, gritted her teeth against the wind.  This wasn't exactly the idyllic seaside practice she'd envisioned when she'd traded her bustling city clinic for the remote, windswept island of Aisling.  But then again,  Aisling, with its rugged beauty and fiercely independent inhabitants, had a way of surprising you – usually in ways that involved tangled fishing nets and aching backs.
