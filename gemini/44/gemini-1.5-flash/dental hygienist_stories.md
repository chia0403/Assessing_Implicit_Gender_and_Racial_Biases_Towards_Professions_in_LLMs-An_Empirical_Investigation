The sterile scent of antiseptic and the low hum of the suction machine were the soundtrack to Amelia Hernandez's life.  Not that she minded; Amelia found a peculiar comfort in the predictable rhythm of the dental surgery.  This particular Tuesday morning, however, felt different.  A palpable tension hung in the air of Dr. Albright's pristine office, a tension thick enough to cut with the diamond burr currently vibrating in the dentist’s hand. The polished chrome of the equipment gleamed under the fluorescent lights, reflecting the anxious faces of the waiting patients huddled in the pastel-colored chairs. Outside, the relentless Los Angeles sun beat down on the palm trees lining Wilshire Boulevard, a stark contrast to the cool, controlled environment within. Amelia, already scrubbed and gloved, adjusted the tiny mirror in her hand, her dark eyes scanning the chart for Patient 3B's dental history – a history that, she suspected, was about to become considerably more interesting.
