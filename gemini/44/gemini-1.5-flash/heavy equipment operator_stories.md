The biting wind whipped across the desolate expanse of the Alberta tar sands, stinging Amelia “Amy” Blackwood’s cheeks raw.  Her breath plumed out in frosty clouds, quickly swallowed by the frigid air.  The roar of her Caterpillar D11T, a behemoth of steel and grit, was a comforting counterpoint to the howling wind, a familiar lullaby in this brutal landscape.  Amy, her face partially obscured by a worn, orange hardhat, gripped the joystick with gloved hands, her knuckles white against the worn leather.  The massive blade of the bulldozer sliced through the thick, black earth, a precise, powerful dance under her expert control.  The sun, a pale disc barely visible through the swirling grey sky, cast long, skeletal shadows from the towering oil extraction towers that dotted the horizon like rusty, metallic sentinels.  This was her kingdom, a kingdom of mud, machinery, and the relentless pursuit of black gold.
