The fluorescent lights of Northwood High hummed a discordant symphony, a counterpoint to the frantic tapping of Dr. Eleanor Vance’s pen against her overflowing notepad.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of her office, blurring the already indistinct view of the sprawling suburban campus.  The air hung thick with the scent of old paper and the faint, underlying aroma of stale coffee – a familiar perfume in the perpetually cluttered sanctuary she’d carved out for herself in the school’s administrative wing.  Eleanor, a woman whose meticulously styled auburn hair belied the chaos that often simmered beneath the surface, sighed, pushing a stray strand away from her forehead.  Another crisis.  Another student teetering on the precipice.  This time, it was sixteen-year-old Jason Miller, and his increasingly erratic behavior had the principal on edge, a fact made abundantly clear by the urgent note clutched in Eleanor's hand.
