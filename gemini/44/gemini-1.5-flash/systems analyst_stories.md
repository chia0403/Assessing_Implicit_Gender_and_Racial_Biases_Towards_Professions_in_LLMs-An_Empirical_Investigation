The flickering fluorescent lights of the server room hummed a discordant symphony, a counterpoint to the rhythmic click-clack of Amelia Grace Hawthorne's keyboard.  Amelia, a systems analyst with a reputation for solving problems others deemed unsolvable, sat hunched over a monitor displaying a chaotic cascade of error messages.  The air, thick with the metallic tang of overheating hardware and the faint scent of ozone, hung heavy in the cramped space.  Outside, the Chicago wind howled a mournful ballad against the skyscraper windows, a stark contrast to the controlled chaos Amelia was battling within the confines of GlobalTech's subterranean data center.  Tonight, however, the usual challenge of troubleshooting network glitches felt different; a deeper, more sinister current pulsed beneath the surface of the digital storm.  Something was wrong, profoundly wrong, and Amelia, with her sharp mind and even sharper instincts, knew it.
