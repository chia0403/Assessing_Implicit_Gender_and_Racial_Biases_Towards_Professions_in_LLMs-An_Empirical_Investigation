The salt spray stung Dr. Eleanor Vance’s face as she wrestled the stubborn fishing net onto the dilapidated dock.  Gulls wheeled overhead, their cries a discordant soundtrack to the rhythmic crash of waves against the barnacle-encrusted pilings.  The air, thick with the smell of brine and decaying seaweed, carried the faint, almost imperceptible scent of woodsmoke – a comforting counterpoint to the relentless chill that clung to the tiny island of Aisling.  This wasn't exactly the picturesque coastal retreat Eleanor had envisioned when she'd accepted the temporary occupational therapy position at the Aisling Island Community Center, but the breathtaking, rugged beauty of the place, despite its isolation, was undeniable.  The ramshackle cottages clinging to the cliffs, the relentless, grey-green ocean, and the imposing silhouette of the ancient lighthouse all contributed to a sense of both wild freedom and utter remoteness.  Her assignment: to help rebuild the lives of the island’s residents, fractured after a devastating storm six months prior.  She just hoped she could rebuild her own fractured sense of purpose, too.
