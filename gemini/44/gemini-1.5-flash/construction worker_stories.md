The biting November wind whipped Amelia "Amy" Hernandez's hair across her face as she wrestled a length of rebar into place.  Above, the skeletal frame of the new skyscraper clawed at the bruised, grey sky, a metal monster rising from the heart of downtown Chicago.  Below, the city hummed with a low, relentless thrum – the sound of a million lives unfolding, oblivious to the precarious dance Amy and her crew were performing hundreds of feet in the air.  The scent of cold steel, fresh concrete, and exhaust fumes hung heavy, a familiar perfume that clung to her like a second skin after fifteen years on the job.  Today, however, the usual symphony of construction was punctuated by an unsettling silence – a silence that prickled at Amy's nerves more than the icy wind.
