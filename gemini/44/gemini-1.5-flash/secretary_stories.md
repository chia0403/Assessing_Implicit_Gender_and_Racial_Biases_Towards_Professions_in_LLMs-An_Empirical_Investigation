The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Worthington’s hands, the lukewarm tea doing little to soothe the icy dread that had settled in her stomach.  Rain lashed against the tall, gothic windows of Blackwood & Sons, the rhythmic drumming a counterpoint to the frantic tap-tap-tapping of her own fingers on the mahogany desk.  Outside, the London November sky was the colour of bruised plums, mirroring the state of Amelia’s own bruised spirit.  The air in the office, thick with the scent of old paper and something vaguely floral that was meant to mask the damp, felt suffocating.  Amelia, Senior Secretary to the notoriously enigmatic Mr. Silas Blackwood,  had a feeling this particular Tuesday wouldn't just be busy; it would be catastrophic.
