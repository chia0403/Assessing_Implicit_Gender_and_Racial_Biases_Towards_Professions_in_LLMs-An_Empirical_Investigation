The scent of acetone and almond hung heavy in the air, a familiar perfume to Seraphina "Sera" Bellweather.  Sera, a manicurist with hands as nimble as a hummingbird's wings and a heart perpetually teetering on the edge of chaos, sighed, the sound lost in the rhythmic whir of the electric file.  It was a Tuesday, a day usually reserved for the predictable parade of chipped polish and hurried appointments at "Nails by Sera," her tiny salon nestled in the cobbled alleyway behind the bustling marketplace of Oakhaven. Oakhaven itself was a quaint, if slightly faded, seaside town, where the salty tang of the ocean perpetually battled the sweet aroma of lavender from Mrs. Gable's overflowing garden next door.  Today, however, the usual Tuesday calm was shattered. A storm, both literal and figurative, was brewing.  The wind howled outside, rattling the windowpanes, mirroring the tempestuous energy that had settled uneasily on Sera's shoulders.
