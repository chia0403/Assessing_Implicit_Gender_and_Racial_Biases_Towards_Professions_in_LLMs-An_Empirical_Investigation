Rain lashed against the grimy windows of the Rossi & Bellows Legal Offices, mirroring the tempest brewing inside Amelia Grace Hawthorne.  The flickering fluorescent lights of the cramped paralegal bullpen cast long, skeletal shadows across overflowing filing cabinets and desks cluttered with half-eaten takeout containers. The air hung thick with the scent of stale coffee and desperation, a familiar aroma for Amelia after six years toiling in the trenches of personal injury law.  Cincinnati, Ohio, wasn’t exactly a hotbed of glamorous legal battles, but tonight, even the usual fender-benders and slip-and-falls felt like insurmountable Everest expeditions.  A deadline loomed – midnight – and the crucial document she needed, a signed affidavit from a notoriously elusive witness, was still MIA.
