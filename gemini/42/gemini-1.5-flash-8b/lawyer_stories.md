Rain lashed against the grimy windows of the "Ironclad" building, mirroring the tempest brewing inside Amelia Blackwood, Senior Partner at Blackwood & Bone, a law firm notorious for its cutthroat deals and even more cutthroat lawyers.  The year was 1932, and the city of New York seethed with the anxieties of the Depression.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of light piercing the gloom of Amelia's opulent, yet somehow sterile, office.  Across her mahogany desk, a single, wilting orchid sat, a stark contrast to the piles of legal documents that suffocated the space. Amelia, a woman sculpted from steely resolve and a sharp intellect, stared at the swirling chaos of paperwork.  Her tailored suit, the color of a bruised plum, held the weight of countless lives, fortunes, and legal battles, all demanding her attention.  Outside, the city roared, a symphony of desperation and determination.  The fate of a struggling garment factory, a powerful syndicate, and perhaps even an innocent man hung precariously in the balance, all tethered to the decisions she would make that day.
