The biting wind whipped across the desolate, grey plains of Aeridor, stinging Elara Stonehand's cheeks.  Dust devils danced across the cracked earth, swirling fragments of shattered obsidian and chipped granite into miniature tornadoes.  Above, the bruised sky, a canvas of leaden clouds, threatened rain that never came.  This was the northern outcrop of the Obsidian Mountains, a place where the harsh beauty of the earth clashed with the quiet desperation of the human heart.  Elara, her name echoing the unyielding stone she carved, pulled her worn leather cloak tighter around her.  A single flickering torch, held precariously in her calloused hand, cast long, dancing shadows across the rough-hewn stone pit that served as her workshop.  She was, as always, alone.  The scent of flint and damp earth hung heavy in the air, the only companions to the rhythmic clang of her chisel against the obsidian, a sound that echoed the solitary pulse of her life.
