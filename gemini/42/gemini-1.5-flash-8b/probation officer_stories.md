Rain lashed against the grimy windows of the Probation Department, mimicking the tempest brewing inside Amelia "Amy" Hawthorne.  The fluorescent lights hummed, a sterile counterpoint to the raw emotions swirling around her.  The year was 2024, and the city of New Haven, Connecticut, felt choked by a perpetual twilight, a grey smog clinging to the towering brick buildings and the overflowing dumpsters lining the alleyways.  Amy, a probation officer with five years under her belt and a weariness etched into her prematurely lined face, stared at the file in front of her.  Inside, the details of Jackson "Jax"  Rivera's latest infraction –  a reckless endangerment charge stemming from a near-fatal car accident – glared back at her, a stark testament to the failures she felt were built into the system, and the ones she carried within herself.
