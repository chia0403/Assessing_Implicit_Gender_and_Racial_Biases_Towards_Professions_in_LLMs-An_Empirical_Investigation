The flickering fluorescent lights of the "Wanderlust Wonders" travel agency hummed, a counterpoint to the incessant clicking of Amelia Butterfield's laptop keys.  Dust motes danced in the lone shaft of afternoon sun slicing through the grime-coated window, illuminating a map of Southeast Asia pinned precariously to a corkboard overflowing with crumpled brochures and hastily scribbled notes.  Amelia, a whirlwind of perpetually rumpled clothes and nervous energy, was drowning in a sea of exotic destinations.  Tomorrow was the deadline for the meticulously crafted "Spice Route Safari" tour, a bespoke experience promising a journey through ancient temples, bustling markets, and serene rice paddies.  But the safari was proving elusive, haunted by a series of improbable delays and cancellations that threatened to unravel her carefully constructed itinerary.  The air in the small, cluttered office was thick with the scent of stale coffee and impending disaster.  Outside, the relentless rhythm of city traffic pounded against the window, a soundtrack to Amelia's escalating anxiety.
