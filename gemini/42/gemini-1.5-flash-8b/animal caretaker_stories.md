The biting wind whipped Amelia Blackwood's auburn hair across her face as she surveyed the sprawling aviary.  Dust motes danced in the afternoon sun, illuminating the intricate latticework of the enclosure.  Sunlight, filtered through the emerald canopy of the towering trees, cast long shadows that stretched and contorted the shapes of the exotic birds nestled amongst the branches.  Amelia, Keeper of the Avian Wonders at Blackwood Manor, adjusted the worn leather strap of her satchel.  It held not paperwork or tools, but a carefully curated selection of specially formulated birdseed, each blend meticulously tailored to the specific needs of the individual species within her care.  Blackwood Manor, a sprawling estate nestled deep within the whispering woods of the Cotswolds, held a secret.  It wasn't just the ancient oaks that whispered secrets to the wind, but the very air, saturated with the melodies of unseen creatures.  Amelia knew that today, as always, the whispers held a particular urgency.  The air hummed with an unfamiliar energy, a feeling akin to a symphony of anticipation, and she felt a tremor of unease ripple through her, just beneath the surface of her practiced calm.  She had always loved these creatures, these feathered wonders, each with their own unique personalities, their own silent dramas.  But today, something felt different.
