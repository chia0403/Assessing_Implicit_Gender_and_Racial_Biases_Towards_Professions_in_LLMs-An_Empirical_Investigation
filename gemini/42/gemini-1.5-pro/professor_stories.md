The biting November wind whipped around Professor Alistair Finch, tugging at the edges of his tweed jacket as he hurried across the cobbled quadrangle of St. Jude's College, Oxford.  Gargoyles leered down from the honey-coloured stone walls, their grotesque faces slick with rain.  The ancient clock tower, a gothic spear piercing the bruised twilight sky, began to chime six. Alistair clutched his briefcase tighter, the leather cold against his gloved hand.  He was late.  Again.  The weekly meeting of the Bibliophilic Society, a gathering he usually cherished, had become a source of anxiety lately. Not because of the books, of course, but because of the unsettling discovery he'd made in the library’s restricted section – a discovery he hadn't dared share with anyone, not even his closest colleague.  A discovery that made the shadows in the ancient library seem deeper, the whispers of turning pages more sinister.
