The biting November wind whipped around Dr. Elias Thorne's ankles as he crossed the cobblestone courtyard of St. Jude's Hospital, the gothic arches of the building looming over him like skeletal fingers. Gargoyles perched atop the rain-slicked stone, their granite faces seeming to sneer down at the bustling figures below. St. Jude's, a venerable institution nestled in the heart of Edinburgh, was a place steeped in both healing and hushed whispers of its long history.  Inside its walls, Elias, a renowned cardiothoracic surgeon, navigated a world of gleaming steel, pulsing life, and the constant, low hum of machinery battling against the encroaching tide of mortality. Tonight, that battle felt particularly personal.
