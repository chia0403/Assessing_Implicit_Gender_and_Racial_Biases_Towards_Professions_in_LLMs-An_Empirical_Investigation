The biting wind whipped across Lake Michigan, sending icy sprays against the windows of Mercy Hospital's emergency room.  Inside, the air crackled with a different kind of chill, the tense hum of controlled chaos that Eleanor Vance, RN, recognized all too well.  Fluorescent lights buzzed overhead, casting a sterile glow on the bustling scene.  The triage nurse's voice boomed across the room, announcing a fresh wave of patients from a multi-car pileup on the icy I-94.  Eleanor, her auburn hair pulled back in a tight bun, braced herself, the adrenaline already surging through her veins.  Tonight was going to be a long one.
