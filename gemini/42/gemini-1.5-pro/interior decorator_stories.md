The biting November wind whipped around Eleanor Vance, plastering stray strands of auburn hair across her face. She tugged her cashmere scarf tighter, its emerald green a stark contrast to the grey stone of the Aldridge Manor.  Gargoyles perched on the eaves leered down at her, their expressions mirroring her own apprehension.  Built in the late 1800s, the manor sat perched on a windswept bluff overlooking the churning Atlantic, its grandeur faded, its opulence tarnished by decades of neglect. Eleanor, renowned interior decorator to Boston’s elite, usually relished a challenge, but this…this felt different.  This felt less like restoring a home and more like awakening a sleeping giant.  The heavy oak door, studded with iron rivets, creaked open as Mrs. Albright, the manor's caretaker, a wizened woman with eyes as sharp as shards of ice, beckoned her inside.  The air within smelled of dust and old secrets, a scent that clung to the antique tapestries and threadbare Persian rugs like a shroud.
