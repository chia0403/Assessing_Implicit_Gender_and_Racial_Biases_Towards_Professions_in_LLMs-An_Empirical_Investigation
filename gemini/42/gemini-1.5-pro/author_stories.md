The salt-laced wind whipped Amelia "Amy" Thorne's auburn braid across her face, stinging her cheeks as she stared out at the churning slate-grey sea.  Her fingers, stiff and aching from the cold, clutched a chipped enamel mug of lukewarm tea.  Perched precariously on the weather-beaten porch of Seascape Cottage, a ramshackle dwelling clinging to the cliffs of the remote Cornish village of Porthleven, Amy felt a familiar knot of anxiety tighten in her chest.  The deadline for her third novel, a gothic mystery set in the very village she now inhabited, loomed like the storm clouds gathering on the horizon.  The words, however, remained elusive, trapped somewhere between the crashing waves and the echoing emptiness of her writer's block.  Seascape Cottage, intended as a writer's retreat, had become instead a prison of her own making.
