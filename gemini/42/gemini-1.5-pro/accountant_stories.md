Rain lashed against the leaded windows of Bartholomew "Barty" Finch's office, mimicking the frantic drumming of his fingers against the mahogany desk. Outside, the gaslights of Grimshaw Lane flickered, casting long, skeletal shadows across the cobbled streets of Victorian London.  November, 1888, had descended upon the city like a shroud, bringing with it not only the biting cold but also a chilling fear that gripped the hearts of its inhabitants. Barty, a meticulously organized accountant known for his crisp suits and even crisper bookkeeping, usually found solace in the predictable rhythm of numbers. Tonight, however, even the perfectly balanced columns of his ledger couldn't quell the unease that gnawed at him. The unsettling quiet that had fallen over Whitechapel in recent weeks, punctuated only by whispered rumors and the mournful cries of newspaper boys, was far more disturbing than any miscalculated sum.
