The sterile scent of disinfectant mingled with the faint, metallic tang of blood.  Eleanor Vance, RDH, adjusted the overhead lamp, its harsh white light illuminating the next patient’s nervously smiling face. Outside, the late afternoon sun cast long shadows across the brick facade of the Seabreeze Dental Clinic, perched precariously on a bluff overlooking the churning grey expanse of the North Atlantic.  The wind howled a mournful tune, rattling the windowpanes and momentarily distracting Eleanor from the chipped molar she was about to scale. This isolated clinic, nestled in the remote fishing village of Port Blossom, Maine, wasn’t exactly where Eleanor envisioned herself after graduating top of her class. But life, as she was quickly learning, rarely adhered to plans.  And Port Blossom, with its eccentric inhabitants and unsettling secrets, was proving to be anything but ordinary.
