The biting wind, scented with pine and snow, whipped at Jedediah "Jed" Blackwood's thick beard as he surveyed the stand of Douglas firs. Dawn, a hesitant blush on the eastern horizon, barely pierced the dense canopy of the Cascade Mountains in Washington State.  Frost crunched under his heavy boots, a familiar rhythm accompanying the distant drone of the Snoqualmie River.  Jed, his calloused hands tucked deep into sheepskin-lined gloves, adjusted the brim of his worn Stetson. This particular grove, dubbed "The Cathedral" by the older loggers, held trees older than Jed's grandfather, their towering trunks a testament to centuries of enduring the harsh mountain winters. Today, they would fall.
