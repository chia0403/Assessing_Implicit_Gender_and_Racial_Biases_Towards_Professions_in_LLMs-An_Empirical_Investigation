Rain lashed against the grimy windows of The Daily Clarion's office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside Amelia “Mia” Rodriguez.  Fluorescent lights hummed overhead, casting a sickly yellow glow on the cluttered desks and overflowing ashtrays. Cigarette smoke hung thick in the air, mingling with the bitter aroma of stale coffee.  1948 in Los Angeles was a city of stark contrasts: glittering Hollywood glamour masked a seedy underbelly of corruption, and Mia, a seasoned reporter with a nose for trouble and a penchant for whiskey, found herself smack-dab in the middle of it.  Tonight, however, the story wasn't about a crooked cop or a mobster's mistress. Tonight, it was personal. A crumpled telegram lay on her desk, the ink blurred by a single teardrop she hadn't bothered to wipe away. It announced the death of her estranged father, a man she hadn't seen or spoken to in ten years, a man who now lay cold in the dusty town of Redemption, Arizona.
