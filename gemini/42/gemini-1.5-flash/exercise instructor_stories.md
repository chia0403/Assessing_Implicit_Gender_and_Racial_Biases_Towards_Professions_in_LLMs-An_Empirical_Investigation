The stale scent of sweat and cheap disinfectant clung to the air in Fitness First, a gym so aggressively cheerful it bordered on sinister.  Fluorescent lights hummed a monotonous tune above rows of gleaming, unused treadmills, their digital displays blinking a mocking invitation to a healthier lifestyle that most of Amelia Grace Hawthorne's clients seemed determined to avoid. Amelia, or Amy as she preferred, adjusted the strap of her neon-pink sports bra, the vibrant colour a stark contrast to the muted grey of the pre-dawn gloom outside.  The only sounds were the rhythmic whirring of the ventilation system and the faint, almost imperceptible thump-thump-thump of her own heart, a steady beat against the backdrop of impending chaos.  Her 6 a.m. Spin class was about to begin, and today, Amy had a feeling, it would be anything but ordinary.
