The air hung thick and heavy with the scent of frying onions and desperation.  Amelia Hernandez, a name whispered on the breath of regulars as much for her lightning-fast service as for her uncanny ability to remember orders and life stories alike, weaved expertly through the crowded tables of "The Rusty Mug."  The Mug, a greasy spoon diner clinging precariously to the edge of a crumbling dockyard in Gloucester, Massachusetts, was a symphony of clattering plates, sputtering coffee pots, and the low hum of disgruntled seagulls circling overhead.  Rain lashed against the grimy windows, mirroring the tempest brewing inside Amelia, a storm of unpaid bills and a looming rent increase threatening to capsize her already fragile life.
