The scent of acetone and lavender hung heavy in the air, a cloying perfume that clung to the plush velvet armchairs and the chipped porcelain sink of Serenity Nails.  Rain lashed against the windowpanes of the tiny salon, blurring the neon glow of the "Open" sign into a hazy halo.  Inside, amidst the glitter and the hushed chatter of gossip, sat Seraphina "Sera" Delacroix, her nimble fingers working magic on a client's already flawlessly manicured nails.  Sera, with her own nails painted a daring shade of amethyst that matched the flickering amethyst pendant around her neck, wasn't just a manicurist; she was a sculptor of tiny canvases, a confidante, and, unbeknownst to most, a reluctant detective.  Tonight, however, the usual hum of the salon felt strained, replaced by a nervous energy that prickled at the back of her neck, a premonition as sharp as the edges of the nail file nestled in her hand.
