The scent of rosemary and burnt sugar hung heavy in the air, a peculiar perfume that clung to the faded velvet curtains and the chipped porcelain sink in Aurelia “Rella” Blackwood’s salon.  Rella herself, a woman whose age was as carefully concealed as the grey hairs she expertly plucked from her clients' heads, hummed a discordant tune as she swept stray strands of auburn hair from the worn wooden floorboards.  Outside, the November wind howled a mournful dirge against the crooked panes of glass, rattling the already precarious sign that proclaimed "Rella's Restyled" to the dreary street.  The only light in the small, almost claustrophobic space came from a single, flickering bulb hanging precariously above her styling chair, casting long, dancing shadows that played tricks on the already somewhat chaotic arrangement of hair products, curling irons, and forgotten appointment books.
