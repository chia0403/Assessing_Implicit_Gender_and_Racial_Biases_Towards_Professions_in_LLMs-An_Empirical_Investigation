The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne's dark hair across her face as she hurried down the rain-slicked cobblestones of Castle Quay.  The gaslights, struggling against the relentless downpour, cast a jaundiced glow on the ancient, leaning buildings of Edinburgh’s Old Town.  The scent of peat smoke and damp earth hung heavy in the air, a familiar perfume that usually soothed Amelia, but tonight, it only served to amplify the tremor in her hands.  Her worn leather satchel, heavy with her nursing kit and a half-eaten apple, swung against her leg, a dull rhythm against the frantic beat of her heart.  The Royal Infirmary loomed ahead, its imposing stone facade a stark contrast to the chaotic beauty of the city surrounding it. Inside, another battle raged – one Amelia, a registered nurse with seven years’ experience and a heart overflowing with both compassion and weariness, was bracing herself to face.
