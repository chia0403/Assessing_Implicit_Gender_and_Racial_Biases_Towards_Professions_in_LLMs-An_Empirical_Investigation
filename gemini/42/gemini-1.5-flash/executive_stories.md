The mahogany desk, polished to a mirror sheen, reflected Amelia Katherine Holloway’s grim expression.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of her 57th-floor office, blurring the glittering cityscape of Hong Kong into a hazy, impressionistic painting.  Below, the neon signs of Kowloon flickered like frantic fireflies, a stark contrast to the sterile calm of her meticulously organized workspace.  Amelia, CEO of Holloway Global Investments,  wasn’t known for her calm.  Tonight, however, the usually iron-clad control she projected was fraying at the edges, the meticulously applied lipstick a fragile mask barely concealing the simmering fury within.  The email, still open on her computer screen, pulsed with a malevolent glow, a digital harbinger of the storm about to break.
