The biting March wind whipped Amelia Hernandez’s sensible brown coat around her legs as she hurried down Elm Street, the scent of thawing snow and impending spring battling it out in the crisp air.  Her breath plumed out in white puffs, mirroring the anxiety churning in her stomach.  Amelia wasn't afraid of the tax season rush; she'd faced down snarling clients, baffling deductions, and the IRS itself for fifteen years.  No, this wasn't about the paperwork.  This was about the overflowing mailbox waiting for her at Hernandez & Hernandez Tax Services – a mailbox promising a deluge of 1040s, W-2s, and enough Schedule Cs to make her head spin before the first cup of coffee was even brewed.  The small, brick building housing her business – a testament to her hard work and unwavering dedication – stood before her, a haven from the unforgiving wind, yet a battlefield she was about to enter.
