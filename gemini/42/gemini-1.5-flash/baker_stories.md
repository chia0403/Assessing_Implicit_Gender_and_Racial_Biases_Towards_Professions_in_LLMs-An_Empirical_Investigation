The aroma of cardamom and warm yeast hung heavy in the air, a comforting blanket against the biting November wind howling outside.  Rain lashed against the small-paned windows of "The Flour Child," blurring the already dim gaslight glow of the cobbled street beyond.  Inside, however, a warm, golden light bathed Amelia Katherine Finch, her flour-dusted apron a stark contrast to the fiery red of her hair, as she meticulously shaped a croissant, her nimble fingers working with a practiced grace that belied the turmoil brewing within her.  The bakery, tucked away on a quiet side street in the ancient, atmospheric town of Oakhaven, was usually a haven of peaceful routine.  But tonight, a storm was gathering, and it wasn't just the one raging outside.
