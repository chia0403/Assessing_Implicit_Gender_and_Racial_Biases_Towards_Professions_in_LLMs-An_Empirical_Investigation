The humid Louisiana air hung heavy, thick with the scent of honeysuckle and impending rain.  Cicadas buzzed a relentless symphony, their drone a counterpoint to the rhythmic slap of Judge Evangeline “Evie” Dubois’s sandals against the worn cypress planks of her porch.  Sweat beaded on her forehead, clinging to the fine strands of her dark hair pulled back in a practical braid.  Before her, the sprawling bayou stretched, a tapestry of emerald green and murky brown, reflecting the bruised purple of the twilight sky.  The air crackled with the unspoken anxieties of the community;  the simmering tensions that always seemed to boil just beneath the surface of her small, isolated town of Lacassine. Tonight, however, felt different.  Even the usually boisterous frogs seemed subdued, their croaks muted by a palpable sense of foreboding.
