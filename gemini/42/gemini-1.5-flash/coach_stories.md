The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Amy” Hernandez’s hair across her face as she surveyed the desolate soccer field.  Fallen leaves, the color of rust and decay, clung stubbornly to the damp grass, mirroring the state of her own spirits.  The stadium lights, skeletal fingers against the bruised purple twilight, cast long, mournful shadows across the empty bleachers.  This wasn't the vibrant, teeming spectacle she was used to; the roar of the crowd, the clash of cleats, the sharp crack of the ball against boot – all absent, replaced by a gnawing silence that felt heavier than the impending snow.  The air hung thick with the scent of damp earth and the faint, lingering aroma of hotdogs from a long-gone game, a ghostly reminder of better times.  Amy, head coach of the struggling Westside Wildcats, pulled her worn, navy blue tracksuit tighter around her, a futile attempt to ward off the chill that seeped deeper than the November air. Tonight, more than the game, she felt the weight of her team’s failing season pressing down on her.
