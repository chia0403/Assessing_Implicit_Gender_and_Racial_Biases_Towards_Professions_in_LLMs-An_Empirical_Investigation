The scent of ozone hung heavy in the air, a metallic tang clinging to the damp strands of seaweed clinging to the pilings beneath the boardwalk.  Seagulls wheeled overhead, their cries a discordant soundtrack to the rhythmic *snip-snip-snip* of scissors.  Amelia “Mia” Rodriguez, owner and operator of “Shear Madness,” a tiny, brightly painted salon perched precariously at the edge of the Pacific Ocean in the quirky seaside town of Seabreeze, California, sighed contentedly.  The salt spray kissed her face, a welcome contrast to the lingering scent of lavender and coconut from her client's hair.  The ocean’s roar was a constant companion, a comforting rumble that drowned out the petty anxieties of everyday life – or at least, it usually did.  Today, however, a different kind of unease prickled at the edges of her normally tranquil existence.  A storm, both literal and metaphorical, was brewing.
