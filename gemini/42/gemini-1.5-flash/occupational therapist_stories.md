The salt spray stung Dr. Eleanor Vance's face as she wrestled the stubborn fishing net free from a tangle of kelp.  The late afternoon sun, a bruised plum in the churning grey sky, cast long shadows across the jagged rocks of the Maine coast.  The air, thick with the scent of brine and decaying seaweed, was bracingly cold, a stark contrast to the sterile, antiseptic smell she was usually surrounded by at the Coastal Pines Rehabilitation Center.  Eleanor, however, found the raw energy of the ocean far more therapeutic than any carefully calibrated exercise regimen.  Today, though, the ocean was proving a rather recalcitrant patient itself.
