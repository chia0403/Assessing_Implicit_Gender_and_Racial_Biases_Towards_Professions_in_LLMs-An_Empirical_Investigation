The air hung thick and heavy with the scent of blood and iron, a perfume as familiar to Bartholomew "Barty" Butterfield as the smell of his own skin.  It clung to him, a damp shroud woven from years spent behind the counter of Butterfield's Prime Meats, a butcher shop nestled on the cobbled side street of Oakhaven, a town so quaint it bordered on cliché. Oakhaven, with its perpetually rosy-cheeked children, its gossiping housewives clustered around the well, and its ancient oak tree that seemed to watch over everything with knowing, gnarled branches, was a place where nothing ever truly changed – except, perhaps, in Barty's own life, which was about to be cleaved in two, as neatly as he cleaved a sirloin.
