The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Hernandez’s hands, a small comfort against the November chill that seeped through the drafty windows of the Legal Eagles office. Rain lashed against the glass, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her.  Outside, the city of Portland, Oregon, hunched under a sky the color of bruised plums, its iconic bridges shrouded in mist.  Inside, the air hung thick with the scent of stale coffee and simmering resentment. Amelia, a paralegal with a reputation for both meticulous organization and a biting wit that could curdle milk, stared at the mountain of files stacked on her desk, each one a testament to the ceaseless churn of human drama unfolding within the city's legal system.  Tonight, however, it was a single, deceptively slim file that held her captive.  The case of *State v. Silas Blackwood* – a name that sent a shiver down her spine despite her years of experience – promised to be anything but straightforward.
