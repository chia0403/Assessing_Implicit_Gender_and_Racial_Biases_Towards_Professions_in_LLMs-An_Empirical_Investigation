The flickering gaslight cast long, skeletal shadows across the worn oak table, highlighting the intricate whorls of Dr. Eleanor Vance’s fingerprints as she steepled her fingers.  Rain lashed against the tall, gothic windows of her office, a relentless percussion accompanying the rhythmic tick-tock of a grandfather clock in the corner.  The air hung heavy with the scent of old books and something else… something faintly metallic, a subtle undercurrent that pricked at the edges of her senses.  It was a scent she recognized, a grim olfactory marker that whispered of violence, a scent that clung to the worn leather armchair opposite her, the one where her latest client, a man named Arthur Blackwood, had just sat, his eyes wide with a chilling blend of fear and… something else, something akin to grim satisfaction.  Outside, the wind howled a mournful dirge, a fitting soundtrack to the chilling tale Blackwood had just begun to unravel.  The city of Veridia, nestled deep within a rain-soaked valley, held its breath, unaware of the darkness that Dr. Eleanor Vance was about to confront.
