The biting Antarctic wind howled a mournful song around Dr. Aris Thorne's research station, a relentless, icy counterpoint to the rhythmic whirring of the geothermal generators within.  Snow, driven by gusts that threatened to peel the corrugated iron siding from the prefabricated buildings, piled against the reinforced windows of his lab, obscuring the already limited visibility.  Inside, the air hung thick with the scent of ozone and something else, something faintly metallic and unsettling, that Aris couldn't quite place.  It emanated from the newly-discovered cave system, a network of frozen tunnels snaking beneath the glacier, a system that had yielded its secrets – and perhaps a far greater, far more dangerous mystery – only hours before.
