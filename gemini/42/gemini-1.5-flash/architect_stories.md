The biting November wind whipped Amelia Katherine Reed's charcoal-grey coat around her legs as she stood perched precariously on the scaffolding, the city of Prague sprawling beneath her like a meticulously crafted model.  Gargoyles leered down from the ancient rooftops, their stone faces etched with centuries of secrets.  Below, the Vltava River snaked its way through the heart of the old town, reflecting the fiery hues of the setting sun.  Amelia, a renowned architect known for her audacious designs and even more audacious spirit, inhaled the crisp air, the scent of woodsmoke and damp cobblestones filling her lungs.  This wasn't just any project; this was the restoration of the crumbling Baroque masterpiece, the Church of Saint Nicholas, a commission that both thrilled and terrified her in equal measure.  The weight of history, the fragility of the structure, and the city's unwavering expectation pressed down on her like the centuries-old stones themselves.
