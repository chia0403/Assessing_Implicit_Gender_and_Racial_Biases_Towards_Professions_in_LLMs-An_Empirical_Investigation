The chipped paint of the "Sunshine Smiles Daycare" sign flaked onto the already overgrown lawn like a slow, melancholic snowfall.  A rusty swing set groaned a mournful tune in the stiff autumn breeze, a counterpoint to the chirping crickets that seemed to mock the creeping twilight.  Inside, twenty-three pairs of small eyes, some bright with mischief, others heavy with the exhaustion of a long day, stared at twenty-three separate points on the wall.  Amelia Grace Hawthorne, a woman whose smile lines betrayed a weariness far beyond her twenty-four years, surveyed her charges with a practiced calm that masked a rapidly depleting well of patience. The air hung thick with the sweet, cloying scent of crayons and impending bedtime tantrums.
