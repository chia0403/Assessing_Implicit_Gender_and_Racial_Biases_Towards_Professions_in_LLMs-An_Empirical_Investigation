The scent of Bay Rum and impending rain hung heavy in the air, a familiar perfume to Bartholomew "Barty" Butterfield, owner and sole operator of Butterfield's Barbershop.  Barty, a man whose hands moved with the practiced grace of a seasoned surgeon, yet whose laugh could shake the dust from a forgotten attic, sat perched on his worn leather barber's chair, the afternoon sun slanting through the dusty windowpane and illuminating motes of dust dancing in the air.  Rain lashed against the single pane of glass, a rhythmic counterpoint to the rhythmic snip-snip-snip of his shears, a sound as comforting and familiar to the residents of Oakhaven as the church bells on Sunday morning. Oakhaven itself, a town nestled deep in the heart of the Appalachian Mountains, was a place where time seemed to move at a slower pace, where secrets were whispered on the wind, and where the most dramatic events usually involved a runaway goat or a particularly stubborn mule.  But today, Barty suspected, might be different.
