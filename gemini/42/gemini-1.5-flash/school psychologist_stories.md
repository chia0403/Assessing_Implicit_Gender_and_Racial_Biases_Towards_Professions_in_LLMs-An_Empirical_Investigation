The chipped paint of the windowsill felt cold beneath Dr. Eleanor Vance's fingertips.  October in Ashwood, Oregon, clung to the air like damp wool, a persistent grey clinging to the towering pines that surrounded Ashwood Elementary.  Rain, a fine, insistent drizzle, hammered against the glass, blurring the already muted colours of the playground into a watercolour wash of muted greens and browns.  Inside, the air hung heavy with the scent of old carpet and something vaguely floral, a desperate attempt to mask the underlying aroma of nervous sweat and slightly sour milk.  Eleanor sighed, the sound swallowed by the rhythmic drumming of the rain.  Another day, another child wrestling with something unseen, something she hoped to help unravel before it tangled them too tightly.
