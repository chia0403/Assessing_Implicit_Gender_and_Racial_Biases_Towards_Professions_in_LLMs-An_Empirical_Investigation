The rain hammered against the panoramic windows of Avery Sterling’s office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside him.  Avery, a name whispered with both awe and apprehension in the hallowed halls of Sterling & Vance, stared out at the glittering, rain-slicked cityscape of Manhattan. Below, yellow cabs weaved like frantic ants through the concrete canyons, their horns a discordant symphony to the rhythmic drumming of the storm.  His mahogany desk, usually a testament to controlled chaos – neatly stacked financial reports punctuated by the glint of a silver pen – was strewn with crumpled printouts, each a testament to the disastrous day unfolding. The air hung thick with the scent of expensive leather and the faint, metallic tang of fear.
