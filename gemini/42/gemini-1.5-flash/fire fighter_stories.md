The acrid bite of woodsmoke stung Lieutenant Elias Thorne's nostrils, a familiar companion on nights like this.  He squinted through the swirling orange haze, the flames a malevolent ballet against the inky black of the Montana pines.  The air itself crackled with the energy of the fire, a beast they’d been battling for three agonizing days, its hungry maw consuming acres of parched forest with terrifying efficiency.  The flickering light painted Thorne's face in shifting shadows, highlighting the grime caked beneath his eyes and the weariness etched into the lines around his mouth.  He adjusted his helmet, the weight of it a comforting solidity in the chaos, and barked orders into his radio, his voice roughened by the smoke and the strain.  This wasn’t just another wildfire; this was personal.  This fire threatened everything Elias held dear.
