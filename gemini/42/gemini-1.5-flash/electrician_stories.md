The humid Louisiana air hung heavy, thick and sweet with the scent of honeysuckle and impending rain.  Cicadas buzzed a relentless, almost hypnotic rhythm against the backdrop of distant thunder.  Sweat beaded on Elias Thorne's brow, plastering the already damp strands of his dark hair to his forehead.  He wiped it away with the back of his calloused hand, the grime from years of wrestling with electrical wiring clinging stubbornly to his skin.  Elias, a man whose life revolved around the dance of electrons and the hum of transformers, found himself perched precariously on a rickety ladder in the crumbling attic of Madame Evangeline’s antique shop, a place steeped in more mystery than any wiring he’d ever encountered.  The air itself seemed to crackle with a strange energy, a palpable hum that had nothing to do with the ancient, overloaded circuits that sparked and sputtered around him.
