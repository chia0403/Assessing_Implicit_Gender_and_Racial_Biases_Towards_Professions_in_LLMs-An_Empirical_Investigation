The fluorescent lights of "Sterling & Steele, Attorneys at Law" hummed a monotonous tune, a counterpoint to the frantic tap-tap-tap of Penelope Anne Featherstonehaugh's perfectly manicured fingernails against the highly polished mahogany desk.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows overlooking the grey expanse of the Chicago River, each gust a mournful sigh echoing the dreary mood settling over Penelope's usually bright disposition.  The aroma of stale coffee and simmering resentment hung heavy in the air, a familiar scent in the otherwise impeccably clean reception area.  It was a Tuesday, and Tuesday, Penelope had come to learn, was the day the universe conspired to test her patience to its absolute limit.
