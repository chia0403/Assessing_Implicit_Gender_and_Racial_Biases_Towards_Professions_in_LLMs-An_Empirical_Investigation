## Story Opening and Setting:

**Setting:** The story opens on a crisp autumn evening in the small, picturesque town of Havenwood, nestled amongst the rolling hills of Vermont. The air is thick with the scent of burning leaves and woodsmoke, carried on a gentle breeze that rustles through the vibrant tapestry of red, orange, and gold foliage. Havenwood, despite its idyllic charm, possesses a quiet melancholy, a feeling of secrets held close, whispered amongst the ancient maples and forgotten gravestones in the town's oldest cemetery.

**Opening:**

The flickering gaslight cast long, dancing shadows across the polished mahogany shelves of the Havenwood Public Library as Eleanor Abigail Hawthorne, a librarian with a penchant for cardigans and a soul brimming with untold stories, surveyed her domain. The late afternoon sun, now dipping below the horizon, painted the stained-glass windows in the reading room with hues of amber and rose, illuminating the dust motes dancing in the air. A familiar comfort settled over Eleanor, a feeling of belonging and purpose that only the hushed whispers of turning pages and the comforting weight of history could provide.

Tonight, however, that solace was thin. A chill deeper than the autumn air seeped into the library, settling in the pit of Eleanor's stomach. The clock above the circulation desk chimed six, its resonant tones echoing in the otherwise silent space. She was alone, the last employee to leave. Yet, she felt a presence, an unseen observer lingering in the stacks, watching, waiting. Her usually organized shelves seemed to lean inwards, their leather-bound treasures hiding something more sinister than just a forgotten due date.
