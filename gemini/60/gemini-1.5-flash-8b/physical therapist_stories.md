Rain lashed against the grimy windows of the small physiotherapy clinic, mirroring the tempest brewing inside Amelia Hawthorne.  The flickering fluorescent lights cast long, unsteady shadows across the sterile, yet somehow depressing, waiting room.  A single, forlorn-looking potted fern slumped in a corner, its once vibrant green leaves now muted and lifeless, a poignant symbol of the stagnation that had settled over Amelia's life.  She was a physical therapist, specialising in sports injuries, yet the vibrant energy of the athletes she used to rehabilitate felt a world away.  Her own body, once a powerhouse of agility, now ached with a dull, persistent ache she couldn't quite place.  The constant, almost unbearable pressure to perform, to excel, had quietly eroded her.  Outside, the relentless downpour continued, a soundtrack to the muted desperation echoing within her.
