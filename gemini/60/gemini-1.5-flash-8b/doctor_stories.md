Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of the clinic, a relentless percussion that mirrored the frantic rhythm of Dr. Elara Nightingale's heart.  The air, thick with the smell of damp earth and antiseptic, hung heavy in the small, sparsely furnished room.  Outside, the jungle, a vibrant tapestry of emerald green, pressed close against the crumbling walls, its unseen creatures stirred by the storm.  The clinic, nestled precariously on the edge of the remote village of Avani, was a testament to Dr. Nightingale's unwavering dedication.  It was a place where the whispers of the jungle, and the urgent cries of its inhabitants, often mingled with the clatter of medical instruments. Today, however, the whispers were louder, more urgent than usual.
