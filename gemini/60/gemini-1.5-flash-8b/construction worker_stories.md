The biting wind whipped across the desolate expanse of the Arizona desert, stinging Elias Thorne's cheeks and tugging at the frayed edges of his worn hard hat.  Dust devils danced like miniature tornadoes across the cracked, sun-baked earth, swirling the fine ochre particles into the already hazy air.  He squinted, his gaze fixed on the towering, skeletal frame of the new hydroelectric dam, a monument to human ambition against the backdrop of a relentlessly harsh landscape.  The air hung thick and heavy, a suffocating mix of concrete dust and the metallic tang of rust.  This was the forgotten corner of the world, the final frontier for a construction project that had swallowed dreams and spit out nightmares.
