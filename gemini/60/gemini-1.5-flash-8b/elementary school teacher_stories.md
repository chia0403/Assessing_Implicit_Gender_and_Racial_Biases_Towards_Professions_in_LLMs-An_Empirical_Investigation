Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of the dilapidated school building, a relentless percussion that mirrored the frantic rhythm in Amelia Blackwood's chest.  The air, thick with the smell of damp earth and mildew, hung heavy in the small, sparsely-furnished classroom.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of light slicing through the grimy window, illuminating a scene of quiet chaos.  Crayon shavings littered the floor, mingling with stray pieces of construction paper; a half-finished diorama of a volcano stood precariously balanced on a wobbly desk.  Amelia, a third-grade teacher at the perpetually underfunded Willow Creek Elementary, ran a hand through her tangled brown hair, trying to calm the storm brewing inside her.  It was the first day of the spring semester, and already, the challenges felt insurmountable.
