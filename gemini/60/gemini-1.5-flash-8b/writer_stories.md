The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Blackwood's hands, the faint scent of Earl Grey clinging to the steam.  Sunlight, filtered through the dusty lace curtains of her tiny attic room, painted the worn wooden desk in shifting patterns of gold and ochre.  Dust motes danced in the air, swirling like microscopic, ethereal butterflies.  Amelia, a writer of gothic tales and brooding romances, sighed, the sound swallowed by the quiet of the old house.  Outside, the wind howled a mournful tune, rattling the ancient windows like restless spirits.  This was her sanctuary, her workshop, her prison.  The year was 1928.  Rain lashed against the lead panes of the enormous, imposing Victorian home on the edge of Blackwood Manor, a place shrouded in secrets and shadows, as old and as ominous as the whispered tales Amelia so readily spun.  Her latest manuscript, a collection of chilling short stories tentatively titled "Whispers from the Manor," lay open before her, its yellowed pages hinting at the dark narratives she was weaving within.  The characters lived and breathed within those pages, more real to her than the world outside.  This, she mused, was the perfect setting for a truly haunting tale.  But what horror could she conjure, from these echoing walls and whispering shadows?
