The chipped porcelain sink, stained a perpetual, sickly yellow, reflected the harsh fluorescent lights of "Nail Nirvana."  Dust motes danced in the meagre shafts of light, illuminating the intricate patterns of chipped polish swirling in the drain.  Amelia Genevieve "Amy"  Ashworth, her own nails painted a vibrant, almost shocking, turquoise,  hunched over a client.  The scent of artificial lavender hung heavy in the air, battling with the faint, metallic tang of the freshly-buffed cuticle.  Outside, the relentless percussion of city traffic blurred into a low, droning hum.  Amy sighed, pushing a stray strand of auburn hair behind her ear.  The tiny room, crammed with a bewildering array of nail colours and tools, felt like a suffocating cage.  Her client, a woman named Beatrice, meticulously examining her reflection, was oblivious to the turmoil churning beneath Amy's carefully curated facade.  Amy had a feeling this was going to be a long afternoon.
