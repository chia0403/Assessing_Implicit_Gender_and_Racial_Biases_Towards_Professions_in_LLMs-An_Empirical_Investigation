The scent of freshly cut hair, a heady mix of lavender and something vaguely metallic, clung to the air in Barnaby "Barty" Butterfield's barbershop.  Sunlight, fractured by the ornate stained-glass window depicting a triumphant barber slaying a monstrous head-lice, spilled across the worn mahogany chairs.  Dust motes danced in the beams, illuminating the intricate carvings on the antique wooden shelves lined with gleaming razors and vintage combs.  Barty Butterfield, a man whose hands, though calloused from years of wielding the clippers, retained a surprising delicacy, meticulously trimmed the fringe of a young woman named Elara.  Elara, a wisp of a thing with eyes the colour of stormy seas, fidgeted nervously, her gaze darting around the dimly lit shop as if expecting a shadow to leap from the shadows.  The shop, "The Razor's Edge," nestled in the cobbled alleyways of the sprawling, medieval city of Aethelgard, hummed with the quiet murmur of the city outside.  Carriages rattled past, hawkers' cries drifted in through the open doorway, and the distant chime of the city bell announced the passing of another hour.  Barty, his eyes focused on his task, was oblivious to the turmoil brewing within Elara's mind.  He was, as always, lost in the rhythm of the clippers, the snip of the shears, the quiet whisper of the razor’s passage.  Today, however, there was something different, something that hinted at the unraveling of a story that would intertwine with the lives of both the barber and the restless maiden…
