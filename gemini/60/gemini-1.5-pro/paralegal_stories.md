Rain lashed against the grimy windows of the converted warehouse, mimicking the churning in Amelia “Amy” Reyes’s stomach. The fluorescent lights of Rothman & Blackwood, Attorneys at Law, buzzed overhead, casting a sickly yellow glow over the rows of overflowing filing cabinets and precarious stacks of legal briefs.  It was 7:48 pm on a Friday, and Amy, the only paralegal foolish enough – or dedicated enough, depending on who you asked – to still be at the office, was drowning in discovery documents for the Peterson vs. OmniCorp case. The air hung thick with the scent of stale coffee and desperation, a familiar aroma in this particular corner of Baltimore's legal district.  A single, flickering bulb illuminated the peeling paint of the wall above her desk, casting an elongated, distorted shadow that danced with every gust of wind that rattled the ill-fitting window pane.  The Peterson case was a behemoth, a David-and-Goliath struggle against a corporate titan that seemed determined to bury the small, family-owned bakery under a mountain of paperwork. And Amy, armed with highlighters and an iron will, was the only thing standing between them.
