Rain lashed against the grimy windows of The Daily Clarion, blurring the already fading neon sign advertising "Truth for the People!"  Inside, the air hung thick with the smell of stale coffee and deadline pressure.  Amelia "Mia" Rodriguez, her dark hair pulled back in a messy bun, tapped a chipped mug against her teeth, the caffeine doing little to combat the gnawing exhaustion. The clock above the cluttered newsroom ticked towards 3 AM, each click an accusation against her unfinished article.  Port Blossom, Oregon, population 8,412, wasn't exactly known for breaking news, but tonight, something felt different.  A storm, both literal and metaphorical, was brewing, and Mia had a feeling she was about to be swept up in it.
