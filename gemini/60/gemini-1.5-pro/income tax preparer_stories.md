The flickering neon sign of "A-Plus Tax Solutions" buzzed erratically, casting a sickly yellow light onto the rain-slicked streets of Grimsborough. Inside, Amelia Iris Thorne, a woman whose life was as meticulously organized as the 1040 forms she handled, shivered despite the muggy April air.  Her small office, crammed between a laundromat and a perpetually closed fortune teller's parlor, reeked of stale coffee and the faint, metallic tang of anxiety that seemed to cling to every tax season.  Rain lashed against the single, grimy window, mirroring the turbulent emotions churning within Amelia as she stared at the cryptic message on her answering machine, a message that promised to turn her orderly world upside down.
