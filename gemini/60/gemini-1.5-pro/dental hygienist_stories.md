The relentless New Orleans humidity clung to Amelia Claire Dubois like a damp towel, even in the crisp, air-conditioned sanctuary of "Smile Designers," a dental practice nestled between a po'boy shop and a voodoo apothecary on Magazine Street.  Amelia, in her seafoam green scrubs, adjusted the tiny magnifying glasses perched on her nose and sighed.  It was a Tuesday, which meant Mrs. Periwinkle, with her intricate bridgework and penchant for gossip about the Garden District book club, was due any minute. Outside, the rhythmic clang of the streetcar punctuated the sultry air, a familiar soundtrack to Amelia's meticulously ordered life.  Little did she know, the order was about to be delightfully, and perhaps disastrously, disrupted.
