The crimson velvet curtain, heavy with the weight of anticipation, shimmered under the lone spotlight.  A hush, thick and expectant, blanketed the opulent auditorium of the Palais de la Musique.  The air, perfumed with the faint scent of lilies and old money, crackled with unspoken excitement.  Tonight, after years of toiling in smoky jazz clubs and dimly lit cabarets, Isabelle "Izzy" Moreau was finally making her debut at the most prestigious venue in Paris.  Her heart hammered against her ribs, a frantic drumbeat against the silence, as the announcer’s voice, rich and resonant, echoed through the hall, “…and now, Mesdames et Messieurs, the chanteuse you’ve all been waiting for… Mademoiselle Isabelle Moreau!”
