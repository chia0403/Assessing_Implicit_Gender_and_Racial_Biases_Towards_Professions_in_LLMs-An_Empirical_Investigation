The year is 1923.  Gaslights hissed and flickered, casting long, dancing shadows across the cobbled lanes of Spitalfields, London.  The air, thick with the scent of coal smoke and damp wool, clung to  Eliza Rose Cartwright as she hurried along, the hem of her own midnight-blue skirt brushing against the uneven pavement.  Her destination, nestled between a butcher shop and a pub with frosted windows, was a small, brightly lit storefront.  Gold lettering above the door proclaimed it "Cartwright Creations: Dressmaking & Alterations."  Inside, amidst a flurry of silk, satin, and the rhythmic whir of a Singer sewing machine, Eliza reigned supreme over her own little kingdom of fabric and dreams.
