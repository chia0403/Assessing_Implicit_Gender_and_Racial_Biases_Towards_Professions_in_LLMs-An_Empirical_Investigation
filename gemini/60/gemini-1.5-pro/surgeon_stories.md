The biting November wind whipped across the East River, stinging Dr. Eleanor Vance's exposed cheeks as she hurried across the Roosevelt Island Tram platform.  Below, the city lights glittered like a scattered handful of jewels against the inky canvas of the night. Eleanor, her dark hair escaping from beneath her surgical cap, clutched her worn leather satchel tighter, the weight of the day pressing down on her as heavily as the bag itself. The tram car swayed precariously as it ascended, mirroring the unsettling flutter in her stomach. Tonight wasn't just another late night at the prestigious Lenox Hill Hospital on Manhattan Island. Tonight, she was operating on the Mayor's son.
