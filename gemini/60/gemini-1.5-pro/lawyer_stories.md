The biting wind whipped off Lake Michigan, carrying with it the scent of frozen fish and the mournful cries of gulls.  Flakes of snow, fat and wet, plastered themselves to the windows of Amelia Grace Holloway’s office, obscuring the already grey, late-afternoon light. Inside, the only illumination came from a single desk lamp, casting a warm circle on the mahogany surface where Amelia sat, hunched over a stack of documents. The papers detailed the messy divorce of a prominent Chicago socialite, a case so steeped in bitterness and recrimination it could curdle milk.  Her office, perched high on the twenty-seventh floor of the Prudential Plaza, usually offered a breathtaking view of the city’s skyline, but today, it felt more like a cage, the swirling snow a constant reminder of the icy complexities of human relationships.  The insistent buzzing of her intercom broke the silence.
