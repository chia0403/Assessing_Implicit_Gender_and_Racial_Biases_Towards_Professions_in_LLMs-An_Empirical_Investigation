The biting November wind whipped across the desolate practice field, carrying the scent of damp earth and decaying leaves. Floodlights cast long, skeletal shadows of the goalposts across the churned mud, illuminating the solitary figure of Elias "Eli" Thorne, head coach of the Ashwood Ravens.  Ashwood, a small, forgotten town nestled in the Pennsylvania coal country, was a place where dreams went to die, buried beneath the slag heaps and the weight of generations past.  Eli, his breath misting in the frigid air, watched the empty field with a familiar ache in his chest.  The Ravens hadn't won a game in two years, a streak that mirrored the town's slow decline. Tonight, however, something felt different.  A faint, almost imperceptible shift in the air, like the quiet rumble before a storm.  He just didn't know if it promised destruction or deliverance.
