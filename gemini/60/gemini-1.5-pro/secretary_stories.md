The rhythmic clatter of typewriter keys was the soundtrack to Amelia Grace Hawthorne's existence.  The small, stuffy office of Grimshaw, Grimshaw, and Fitch, Attorneys at Law, smelled perpetually of old paper, stale coffee, and Mr. Grimshaw Senior's pipe tobacco, a scent Amelia alternately found comforting and nauseating.  Sunlight, filtered through the grime-streaked window overlooking Bleecker Street in Greenwich Village, cast long shadows across the room, illuminating dust motes dancing in the perpetual twilight. It was a Tuesday in October, 1957, and the wind, carrying the first crisp bite of autumn, rattled the windowpanes, a stark counterpoint to the humid air trapped within. Amelia, her dark hair pulled back in a severe bun, adjusted her cat-eye glasses and sighed, another perfectly typed letter added to the ever-growing pile on her desk.  Little did she know, this particular Tuesday held more in store for her than just legal jargon and filing.
