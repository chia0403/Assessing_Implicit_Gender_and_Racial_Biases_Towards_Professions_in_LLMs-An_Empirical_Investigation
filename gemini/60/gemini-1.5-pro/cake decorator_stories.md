The scent of burnt sugar hung heavy in the air, a bitter counterpoint to the usual vanilla-laced sweetness that permeated Genevieve "Gen" Dupres' bakery, "The Flourish & Swirl." Rain lashed against the large front windows, blurring the neon "Open" sign and reflecting the flickering gaslight streetlamps of cobbled Thistlewick Lane.  Gen, her auburn braid coming undone, frantically fanned a smoking tiered cake with a tea towel, her brow furrowed in concentration.  This six-tiered monstrosity, adorned with hand-crafted sugar peonies and intricate buttercream latticework, was due for a society wedding at the Grand Thistlewick Hotel in precisely three hours, and the caramelized top tier was threatening to derail the entire affair. Thistlewick, a quaint town nestled in the heart of the English countryside, prided itself on tradition, and a burnt wedding cake simply wouldn't do.
