The relentless Nevada sun beat down on the cracked asphalt of the Desert Bloom Hospital parking lot, baking the air to a shimmering haze.  Inside, the stale, antiseptic scent battled with the faint aroma of burnt coffee drifting from the breakroom. Amelia “Amy” Reyes, RN, leaned against a faded vending machine, the cool metal a welcome respite against her aching back.  Her twelve-hour shift had just ended, but the tension still knotted her shoulders, a familiar companion after years working in the understaffed and perpetually overwhelmed emergency room.  She closed her eyes, picturing the turquoise waters of Lake Mead, a shimmering oasis she desperately longed to escape to.  But escape wasn't an option. Not tonight. Not with the cryptic voicemail her brother had left just hours before, a message filled with panicked whispers and a chilling plea for help.
