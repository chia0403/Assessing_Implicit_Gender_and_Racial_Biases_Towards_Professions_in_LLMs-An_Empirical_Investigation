The biting November wind whipped around Dr. Elias Thorne as he hurried across the cobbled courtyard of St. Jude's Hospital, the gothic spires of the building looming over him like skeletal fingers.  Gargoyles perched precariously on the rain-slicked stone, their grimaces mirroring his own internal anxiety. St. Jude's, nestled in the heart of the perpetually overcast city of Aethelburg, had a reputation as a place of last resort, a sanctuary for the medically unusual and the inexplicably ill. The air itself hummed with a low, unsettling thrum, a constant reminder that within these walls, the boundaries of conventional medicine were often blurred.  Tonight, that unsettling hum felt amplified, resonating with an urgency that made the hairs on the back of Elias's neck prickle. He knew, with a certainty that chilled him to the bone, that tonight was going to be different.
