Rain lashed against the corrugated iron roof of the abandoned cannery, a rhythmic drumming that mirrored the anxiety churning in Dr. Elias Thorne's stomach.  The air, thick with the metallic tang of rust and decay, clung to him like a shroud.  Seagulls cried overhead, their mournful calls echoing through the cavernous space, punctuated by the distant, rhythmic crash of waves against the rocky Oregon coastline. Elias, his breath misting in the frigid air, adjusted the beam of his powerful flashlight, its cone illuminating a gruesome tableau.  A single, bare lightbulb, swaying precariously from a rusted hook, cast long, dancing shadows that distorted the already horrific scene.  This wasn't the sterile environment of his Portland office; this was where the darkness resided, and Elias, armed with his training and a grim determination, had stepped right into its heart.
