The flickering fluorescent lights of the St. Jude's Hospital basement buzzed like trapped insects, their sickly yellow glow barely penetrating the corners where dust bunnies gathered like clandestine meetings. The air, thick with the smell of disinfectant and something vaguely metallic, clung to Elias Finch like a damp shroud.  Elias, his name tag pinned slightly askew on his faded blue uniform, pushed his mop bucket across the slick concrete floor, leaving a gleaming trail in its wake.  It was 3:17 am, the quietest hour in the hospital, a time when even the ghosts seemed to sleep. Outside, a nascent dawn painted the high basement windows a bruised purple, barely hinting at the new day struggling to be born.  This was Elias's kingdom, a realm of echoing silence and forgotten things. And tonight, something was different.
