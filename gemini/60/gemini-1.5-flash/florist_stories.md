The scent of lilies, sharp and sweet, battled with the grit of the London rain lashing against the windowpanes of "Petals & Thorns."  Inside, amidst a riot of colour and fragrance – vibrant sunflowers jostling for space with delicate orchids, and deep crimson roses vying with fragrant lavender –  Amelia Rose Hawthorne carefully pruned a dying hydrangea, her brow furrowed in concentration.  Rain hammered a relentless rhythm against the aged glass, mirroring the erratic beat of her own heart.  The shop, tucked away on a cobbled side street in the shadow of St. Paul's Cathedral, usually buzzed with the cheerful chaos of customers, but tonight, it was eerily quiet, the only sounds the mournful drip of a leaky roof and the frantic scratching of a stray cat outside, desperate for shelter.  Amelia sighed, the damp chill seeping into her bones as much as into the already sodden earth of her potted plants.  The storm outside reflected a storm brewing within her.
