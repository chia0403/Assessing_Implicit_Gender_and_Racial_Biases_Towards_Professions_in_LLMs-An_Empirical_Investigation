The mahogany desk, polished to a mirror sheen, reflected Amelia “Amy” Davenport’s sharp, tired eyes.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of her 37th-floor office, blurring the cityscape of Chicago into a shimmering, grey watercolor.  The city, usually a vibrant tapestry of ambition and energy, felt muted tonight, mirroring the gnawing unease in Amy’s gut.  At 42, Amy Davenport, Senior Vice President of Strategic Acquisitions for North American Industries, had conquered more boardrooms than she could count, navigated countless corporate power plays, and closed deals that made headlines.  But the looming shadow of the Blackwood acquisition, a deal so complex and potentially lucrative it could make or break her career, hung heavier than any storm cloud.  The scent of expensive leather and brewing anxiety filled the air, a potent cocktail that hinted at the long night ahead.
