The biting November wind whipped Dr. Elias Thorne’s charcoal coat around him as he hurried across the cobbled courtyard of St. Jude’s Hospital.  Rain, the kind that seemed to seep into your bones, plastered his dark hair to his forehead.  St. Jude’s, a gothic monstrosity of a building perched precariously on a cliff overlooking the churning grey expanse of the North Sea, was more than just a hospital; it was a character in itself, its aged stone sighing under the relentless assault of the elements, mirroring the weight of responsibility that pressed down on Elias’s shoulders.  Inside, the sterile scent of antiseptic couldn't quite mask the underlying tang of salt and sea spray that permeated everything, a constant reminder of the isolation of their windswept island location.  Tonight, that isolation felt heavier than usual, a suffocating blanket woven from the storm and the gravity of the case awaiting him.
