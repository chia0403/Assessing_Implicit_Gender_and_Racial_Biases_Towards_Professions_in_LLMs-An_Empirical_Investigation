The chipped paint of the "Welcome to Northwood High" sign flaked onto the already littered asphalt like a premature snowfall.  A damp, November chill clung to everything, seeping into the bones and making even the most optimistic soul consider hibernating until spring.  Inside, however, the air was thick with the cloying scent of stale coffee and teenage angst.  Eleanor Vance, a woman whose meticulously-coiffed auburn bun belied a heart weary from grading essays about the existential dread of Hamlet, sighed.  Her breath puffed out a small, white cloud in the dimly lit hallway, mirroring the fog of impending deadlines and parent-teacher conferences that loomed before her like a particularly malevolent storm.
