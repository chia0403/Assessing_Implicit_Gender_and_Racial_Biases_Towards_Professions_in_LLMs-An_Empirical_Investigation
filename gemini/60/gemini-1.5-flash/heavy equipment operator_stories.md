The diesel engine coughed, a throaty rumble that vibrated through Amelia “Amy” Hernandez’s bones.  Dawn painted the eastern sky in bruised purples and angry oranges, a stark contrast to the calm, grey expanse of the Alberta tar sands stretching before her.  Amy, perched high in the cab of her Caterpillar D11T, felt the familiar weight of responsibility settle on her shoulders, heavier than any load she’d ever hauled.  The air hung thick with the scent of petroleum and the metallic tang of freshly turned earth, a smell she’d come to associate not with disgust, but with the quiet, brutal power she wielded over this unforgiving landscape.  The biting wind whipped around the massive machine, a constant reminder of the unforgiving climate and the monumental task ahead: carving a new path through the heart of the oil sands, one colossal scoop at a time.
