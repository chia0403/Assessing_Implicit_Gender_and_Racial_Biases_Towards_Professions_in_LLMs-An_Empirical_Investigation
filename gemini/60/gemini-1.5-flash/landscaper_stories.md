The humid Louisiana air hung heavy, thick with the scent of decaying cypress and the promise of an afternoon thunderstorm.  Cicadas buzzed a relentless, hypnotic rhythm, a soundtrack to Elias Thorne's weary afternoon.  Sweat plastered his faded green work shirt to his back, the damp material clinging to him like a second skin.  Elias, a man whose hands were as rough as bark and whose tan spoke volumes of sun-drenched days spent coaxing life from the stubborn Louisiana soil, squinted at the sprawling expanse of overgrown azaleas before him.  The azaleas, once a vibrant tapestry of color, were now a tangled mess, choked by weeds and shadowed by the oppressive weight of neglect.  This wasn't just a garden; it was a battlefield, and Elias Thorne, landscaper extraordinaire, was ready for the fight.
