The biting November wind whipped Amelia "Mia" Hernandez's dark hair across her face as she wrestled the heavy canvas door of the ambulance open.  The fluorescent lights of the emergency bay at St. Jude's Hospital cast a harsh glare on the slick, rain-streaked tarmac.  Sirens, a constant thrumming undercurrent to city life, were momentarily silenced as Mia, a seasoned Emergency Medical Technician with five years under her belt, took a deep breath, the cold air stinging her lungs. The smell of antiseptic and something vaguely metallic – the lingering scent of a previous trauma – hung heavy in the air. Tonight, the usual chaotic symphony of the ER was punctuated by a chilling stillness, a pregnant pause before the next emergency exploded onto the scene. The city, draped in a shroud of low-hanging clouds, seemed to hold its breath alongside her.
