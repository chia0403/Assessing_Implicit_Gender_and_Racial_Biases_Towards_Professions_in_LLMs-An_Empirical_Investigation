The greasy spoon diner hummed with the low thrum of conversation and the clatter of cutlery, a soundtrack to the pre-dawn chill clinging to the air outside.  Steam rose from Amelia "Amy" Jean Blackwood's coffee cup, mirroring the plume of exhaust that still lingered from her eighteen-wheeler, the *Iron Maiden*, parked just across the street.  The neon sign of the "Roadside Rest" flickered intermittently, casting a sickly yellow glow on the rain-slicked asphalt of Highway 17, a ribbon of black stretching towards the looming, snow-capped peaks of the Cascade Mountains.  Amy, her face etched with the weariness of a thousand sleepless nights behind the wheel, traced circles in the condensation on her mug, her gaze fixed on the endless expanse of highway, a familiar loneliness settling in her bones.  The smell of burnt coffee and bacon hung heavy in the air, a familiar comfort in the vast, unpredictable landscape of her life.
