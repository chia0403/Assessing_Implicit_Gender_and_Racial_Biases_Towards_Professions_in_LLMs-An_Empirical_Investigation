The chipped teal paint of the “Polish Perfection” sign peeled back like sunburnt skin, mirroring the frayed edges of Seraphina “Sera” Bellweather’s patience.  Dust motes, illuminated by the weak afternoon sun struggling through the grimy window of her tiny salon, danced in the air like restless spirits. Sera, her own perfectly manicured hands stained a vibrant crimson from a particularly stubborn client's gel removal, sighed, the sound swallowed by the hum of the refrigerator in the back room – its mournful whir a constant companion to the rhythmic snip-snip of her nail clippers.  The air hung thick with the sweet, cloying scent of acetone and the faint, melancholic perfume of lilies, a floral arrangement long past its prime wilting forlornly on the counter.  Outside, the relentless Chicago wind howled a lonely tune, rattling the already shaky windowpanes of the shop nestled on the edge of a rapidly gentrifying neighborhood – a stubbornly clinging island of faded glamour in a sea of gleaming new construction.
