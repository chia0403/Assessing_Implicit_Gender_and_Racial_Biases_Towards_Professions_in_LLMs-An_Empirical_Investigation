The salt spray stung Amelia Grace Hawthorne's face as she wrestled the stubborn sail.  The wind, a snarling beast from the north, whipped her auburn braid across her cheek, carrying with it the scent of brine and something else… something acrid, metallic, that prickled her nostrils and set her teeth on edge.  Amelia, a writer of acclaimed historical mysteries, usually found her most thrilling adventures confined to the pages of her manuscripts.  But the churning grey waters of the North Sea, off the rugged coast of Shetland, were proving far more compelling – and considerably less forgiving – than any fictional landscape she'd ever conjured.  The small, battered fishing boat, *The Wanderer*, pitched violently, threatening to swallow her whole.  The looming cliffs of Foula, shrouded in mist and legend, loomed like the shadowy figures from one of her own novels.  This was no research trip; this was a desperate escape.
