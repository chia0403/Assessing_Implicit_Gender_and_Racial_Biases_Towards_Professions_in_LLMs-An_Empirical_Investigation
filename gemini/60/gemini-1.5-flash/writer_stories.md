The chipped paint of Amelia Katherine Blackwood’s writing desk flaked onto the worn Persian rug, a miniature snowstorm mirroring the tempest brewing inside her.  Rain lashed against the leaded-glass windows of her attic study, a relentless rhythm that echoed the frantic tapping of her fingers against the keys of her ancient typewriter.  The air hung thick with the scent of old paper, damp wool, and the faint, metallic tang of fear.  Outside, the wind howled like a banshee, rattling the loose slates on the roof of her rambling Victorian house perched precariously on the windswept cliffs overlooking the churning grey expanse of the North Sea. This was her sanctuary, her prison, her battleground – a place where words flowed like blood, and inspiration struck like lightning.  Tonight, however, the lightning was threatening to strike her down.
