The chipped porcelain of Judge Evelyn Beatrice Ainsworth's teacup trembled in her hand, mirroring the tremor of unease that ran through her.  Rain lashed against the tall, gothic windows of her chambers, each gust a mournful sigh against the ancient stone of Blackwood Manor.  The manor, a brooding testament to centuries of family history, loomed over the sleepy village of Ashworth like a gothic gargoyle, its shadowed eaves whispering secrets only the wind could understand.  Inside, however, the air hung thick with the scent of woodsmoke and simmering resentment, a far cry from the sterile impartiality Evelyn strived to maintain in her courtroom. Tonight, the courtroom was irrelevant. Tonight, the judgment would be far more personal.
