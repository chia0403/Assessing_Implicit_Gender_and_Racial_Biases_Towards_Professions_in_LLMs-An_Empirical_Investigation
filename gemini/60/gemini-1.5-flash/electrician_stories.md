The salt spray stung Elias Thorne's face as he wrestled the heavy coil of armored cable onto the swaying platform.  Below him, the churning grey expanse of the North Sea roared its displeasure at the encroaching wind turbines, a symphony of metallic groans and crashing waves.  The wind, a relentless, icy hand, clawed at his oilskins, threatening to rip them from his frame. Elias, a man whose life was measured in volts and amps, felt a different kind of current now – a prickling unease that had nothing to do with faulty wiring.  This wasn't just another offshore job; the brooding, storm-wracked island of St. Kilda, usually deserted except for the seabirds, felt…wrong.  Even the usually predictable rhythm of the North Sea seemed disturbed, its pulse erratic and violent.
