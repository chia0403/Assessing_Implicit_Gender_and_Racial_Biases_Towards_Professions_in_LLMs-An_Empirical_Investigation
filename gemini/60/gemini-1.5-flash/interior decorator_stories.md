The scent of old varnish and forgotten dreams hung heavy in the air, a cloying sweetness that clung to the velvet curtains and the dust motes dancing in the slivers of afternoon sun.  Amelia Worthington-Smith, a woman whose life was a carefully curated palette of creams, taupes, and precisely chosen accents, wrinkled her nose.  This wasn't the kind of elegant decay that inspired; this was simply… neglected.  The grand Victorian mansion, perched precariously on a cliff overlooking the churning grey sea of the Cornish coast, groaned under the weight of its own history, its once-proud façade now scarred by wind and time.  Salt spray, a constant companion to the inhabitants of this remote peninsula, had etched its way into the very stonework, leaving behind a patina of melancholic beauty that Amelia found both captivating and intensely challenging.  This was her latest project, and arguably, her most daunting yet.
