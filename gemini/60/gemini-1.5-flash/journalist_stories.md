The biting November wind whipped Amelia Katherine “Amy” Holloway’s trench coat around her legs as she hunched deeper into the shadow of the crumbling brick tenement building.  Rain, a relentless, icy drizzle, plastered her auburn hair to her forehead, blurring the already indistinct gaslight glow illuminating the cobbled alleyway.  The air hung thick with the smells of coal smoke, damp earth, and something else… something faintly metallic, like old blood. This was the kind of night that whispered secrets, the kind of night that swallowed whole the lives of those who weren't careful.  And Amy Holloway, seasoned journalist for the *New York Chronicle*, intended to hear every whispered word.  Her worn leather notebook felt reassuringly heavy in her gloved hand, ready to record the city's darkest confessions.
