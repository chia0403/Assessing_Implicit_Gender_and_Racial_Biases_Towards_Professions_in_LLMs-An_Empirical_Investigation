The stale air of the showroom hung heavy with the scent of new car leather and desperation.  Dust motes, illuminated by the harsh fluorescent lights, danced in the silent space between rows of gleaming, yet somehow lifeless, vehicles.  Amelia Hernandez, her crimson lipstick a defiant splash of color against the muted tones of the dealership, adjusted the tiny pearl earrings that dangled from her lobes.  Another Tuesday, another slow day at Prestige Motors, and another mountain of unsold inventory looming before her like a concrete monolith.  The silence was broken only by the rhythmic tick-tock of a grandfather clock in the corner, each second a tiny hammer blow against Amelia's dwindling commission.
