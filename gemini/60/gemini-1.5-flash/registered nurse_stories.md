The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne's auburn braid across her face as she hurried down the rain-slicked cobblestone street.  The air, thick with the scent of woodsmoke and damp earth, clung to her like a shroud.  Gaslights flickered weakly against the backdrop of looming, gothic architecture, casting long, distorted shadows that danced and writhed with the gusts.  This wasn't the sterile, brightly lit efficiency of the city hospital where Amelia spent her days; this was Blackwood, a town steeped in history, superstition, and a chilling secret that Amelia, a registered nurse with a penchant for unraveling mysteries, was about to uncover.  The imposing silhouette of Blackwood Manor loomed before her, its darkened windows like vacant eyes staring into the stormy night.  It was here, in the decaying grandeur of this ancestral home, that her latest patient, a reclusive heiress named Eleanor Vance, awaited.
