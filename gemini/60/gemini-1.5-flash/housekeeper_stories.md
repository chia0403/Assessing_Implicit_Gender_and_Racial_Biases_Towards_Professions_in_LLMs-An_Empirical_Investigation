The chipped porcelain sink groaned under the weight of Elara Vance's scrubbing.  Steam, thick and fragrant with lemon and pine, clung to the air in the cavernous kitchen of Blackwood Manor.  Outside, the wind howled a mournful tune, rattling the ancient leaded-glass windows and whispering secrets only the centuries-old oak trees could understand.  Rain lashed against the gothic gables, blurring the already indistinct landscape into a canvas of greys and brooding blacks.  Elara, her hands chapped and red despite the thick rubber gloves, paused, the rhythmic squeak of the sponge ceasing abruptly.  The silence that followed was broken only by the relentless drumming of the rain and the distant, almost imperceptible creak of the house settling into its age-old slumber. Blackwood Manor wasn't just a house; it was a living, breathing entity, full of whispers and shadows, and Elara, its housekeeper for the past five years, knew its moods as intimately as she knew the layout of its labyrinthine corridors.
