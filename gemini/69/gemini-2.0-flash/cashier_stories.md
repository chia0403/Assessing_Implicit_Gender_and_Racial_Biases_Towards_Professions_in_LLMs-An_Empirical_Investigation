The harsh fluorescent lights of the Quik-Stop hummed a monotonous tune, a soundtrack to the humdrum life of Beatrice Eleanor Ainsworth. Beatrice stood behind the chipped Formica counter, a monument to routine and lukewarm coffee. The setting sun cast long, angular shadows through the dusty windows, painting the aisles in a sickly orange glow. Outside, the endless flat expanse of the Nevada desert stretched towards the horizon, a stark reminder of the nowhere-ville that was Harmony Creek. Inside, Beatrice dreamt of escaping.
