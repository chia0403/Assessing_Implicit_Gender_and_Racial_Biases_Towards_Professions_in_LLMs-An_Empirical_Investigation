**Story Opening:**

The smell of linseed oil and dust motes dancing in the weak afternoon light were as familiar to Eleanor Vance as her own heartbeat. She paused, brush hovering over the almost-finished canvas, a sigh escaping her lips. Outside, the insistent honking of a taxi fought a losing battle against the rhythmic crash of the waves, a constant soundtrack to her life in this rickety, forgotten corner of Brighton. Eleanor knew she should add a touch more cerulean to the churning water, a hint of the anger she felt brewing beneath the surface of her calm facade, but the anger felt…stale, predictable. Just like her.

**Setting:**

Eleanor's studio is located on the third floor of a dilapidated Victorian building overlooking the Brighton seafront. The building itself is a faded glory, its once-grand facade now crumbling, paint peeling, and windows rattling in their frames. Inside the studio, exposed brick walls are splattered with years of paint, canvases are stacked haphazardly against one another, and brushes overflow from chipped ceramic mugs. Sunlight streams through the large, arched windows, illuminating dust motes dancing in the air. A worn, overstuffed armchair sits in one corner, draped with a paint-stained throw blanket, a testament to long nights spent lost in thought and creation. The air is thick with the scent of oil paints, turpentine, and a faint undercurrent of salt from the nearby sea. It’s a space of comfortable chaos, a haven where Eleanor can lose herself in her art and forget the world outside.
