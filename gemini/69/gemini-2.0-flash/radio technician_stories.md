The biting wind howled around the abandoned lighthouse, a lonely symphony of salt and rust. Waves crashed against the jagged rocks below, each surge threatening to swallow the crumbling stone foundation. Inside, battling a chill that seemed to seep into his bones, Elias Theodore Finch adjusted the dial on the ancient radio transmitter. He cursed under his breath, the static crackling back at him in mocking response. This damn thing hadn't worked properly since before he was born, and the Maritime Authority expected him, Elias Finch – their most expendable and, arguably, most stubborn technician – to bring it back to life before the next gale ripped through. He ran a hand through his already disheveled hair, leaving a greasy smudge of machine oil on his forehead. "Just another Tuesday," he muttered to the empty, echoing room.
