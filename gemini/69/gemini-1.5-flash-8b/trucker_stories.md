The biting wind whipped across the desolate plains, stinging Elias Thorne's cheeks and rattling the windows of his 18-wheeler, the "Ironclad."  Dust devils danced across the cracked earth, swirling into miniature tornadoes that obscured the already hazy horizon.  The sun, a pale disc sinking below the jagged rim of the Rockies, cast long, skeletal shadows that stretched like grasping fingers across the endless highway.  This was the heart of the American West, a place where silence stretched for miles and the only company came from the rhythmic groan of the truck's engine and the occasional howl of a coyote.  Elias, a veteran trucker with calloused hands and eyes that held the wisdom of a thousand miles, gripped the steering wheel, the familiar weight grounding him in the loneliness. The worn leather of the steering wheel felt like a second skin, whispering tales of countless journeys. He was on his way to the dusty town of Harmony, a town whispered to hold the key to a mystery that had dogged him for years.
