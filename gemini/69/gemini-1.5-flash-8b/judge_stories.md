The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Blackwood's gloved hands, the faint scent of Earl Grey clinging to the steam.  Outside, the grey London drizzle, a constant companion to the city, hammered against the tall, gothic windows of the Old Bailey.  Rain-slicked cobblestones reflected the flickering gaslight, casting long, distorted shadows across the ancient courtroom.  Amelia, Judge Amelia Blackwood, QC,  was alone, the silence punctuated only by the rhythmic drip of water from the leaky gutter outside.  Her meticulous, almost forensic, attention was focused on the meticulously arranged documents spread across her oak desk: a complex case of corporate espionage, involving a multinational tech giant and a shadowy network of disgruntled employees.  The air, thick with the lingering aroma of ink and anticipation, felt heavy with the weight of justice.
