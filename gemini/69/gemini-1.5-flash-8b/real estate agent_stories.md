The flickering neon sign of the "Golden Gate Realty" cast a sickly yellow glow on Amelia Petrova's face as she stared out at the rain-slicked streets of Seattle.  The city, a tapestry of towering skyscrapers and emerald-green hills, seemed to mock the relentless downpour.  Amelia, a seasoned real estate agent with a reputation for navigating the often-turbulent waters of the Pacific Northwest market, felt a familiar knot of anxiety tighten in her stomach.  Today was supposed to be the day she closed the deal on the century-old Victorian mansion, a property whispered about in hushed tones across the real estate circles:  "The Blackwood Manor."
