The fluorescent lights of the "Everything You Could Ever Want" warehouse hummed a monotonous tune, a soundtrack to Amelia Butterfield's daily existential crisis.  Dust motes danced in the harsh glare, reflecting off the towering stacks of generic-brand widgets, brightly colored gizmos, and questionable kitchen appliances.  Amelia, a seasoned salesperson with ten years under her belt, meticulously wiped down a display of novelty stress balls, each one sculpted into a bizarre animal or abstract form.  Each day felt like wading through a sea of indistinguishable items, a sensory overload of pointless objects.  Today, the sea was particularly turbulent.  The new shipment of "Mood-Boosting Magnetic Maracas" had arrived, and Amelia was tasked with the monumental task of selling them to a clientele who clearly needed something more substantial than rhythmic clanging to elevate their spirits.
