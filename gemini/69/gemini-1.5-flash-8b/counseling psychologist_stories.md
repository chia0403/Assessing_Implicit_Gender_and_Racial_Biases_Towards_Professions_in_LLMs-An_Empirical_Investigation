Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of the "Willow Creek Counseling Center," a building swallowed by the relentless green canopy of the Pacific Northwest.  Dust motes danced in the fractured sunlight filtering through the gaps in the leaves, illuminating the cluttered office of Dr. Eleanor Vance, a counseling psychologist with a weathered face and eyes that held the weight of countless stories.  Today, however, a quiet desperation settled over her, a feeling as thick and heavy as the damp air.  The chipped mug of lukewarm chamomile tea sat forgotten on her desk, beside a stack of overflowing client files.  Across the room, a single, unblinking eye stared from the window, a reflection of the stormy weather outside, mirroring the storm brewing within her.
