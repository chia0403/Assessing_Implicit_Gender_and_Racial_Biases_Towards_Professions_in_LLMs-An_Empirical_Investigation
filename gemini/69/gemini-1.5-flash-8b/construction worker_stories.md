The biting wind whipped Amelia "Izzy"  Fitzgerald's hardhat around her head, stinging her cheeks with icy pellets.  Dust, the colour of dried blood, swirled around her boots as she surveyed the half-finished skyscraper.  It was a monument to ambition, a jagged scar against the bruised grey sky of  Dustbowl City.  This was the 37th floor, the "apex," as the foreman liked to call it, although Izzy felt it was more akin to a jagged tooth threatening to snag the clouds.  October.  The wind carried the scent of rusting metal and the faint, acrid tang of the nearby chemical plant.  Construction was a brutal mistress, and tonight, she felt her grip tightening.
