The fluorescent lights of the "Apex Research Institute" hummed, a monotonous drone that echoed through the sterile hallways of the facility.  Dust motes, illuminated by the harsh glare, danced in the air, oblivious to the quiet drama unfolding in the bowels of the building.  This was the domain of Elias Thorne, head janitor, and tonight, the quiet hum was more oppressive than usual.  The air, usually thick with the scent of disinfectant, held a metallic tang, a subtle hint of something...unnatural.  Elias, a man whose life revolved around the rhythmic squeak of his mop and the rhythmic clatter of his cleaning cart, felt a prickle of unease.
