The fluorescent lights of the "Super Saver Mart" hummed a monotonous tune, a soundtrack to Amelia Butterfield's life.  Dust motes danced in the pale glow, illuminating the chipped Formica counter and the rows of meticulously organized canned goods.  Amelia, with her perpetually tired eyes and a floral print dress that had seen better days, was the quiet, unassuming cashier, practically invisible behind the plexiglass barrier.  Today, however, a tremor of something other than the usual weekday monotony ran through her.  It wasn't the usual rush of customers, nor the stale scent of yesterday's bakery goods. It was the presence of the small, leather-bound book tucked beneath the overflowing stack of lottery tickets.  The book, with its faded gold lettering, pulsed with a faint, inner light, a whisper of ancient secrets.  The Super Saver Mart, usually a microcosm of mundane existence, held a subtle, unsettling change, a hint of the extraordinary amidst the ordinary.
