The chipped porcelain sink, stained a perpetual latte-brown from years of lukewarm water and spilled coffee, reflected Amelia Kensington’s weary face.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of sunlight piercing the grime-coated window of "Scissors & Smiles," a salon that had seen better days, and smelled, well, decidedly less than fresh.  Amelia, a woman whose own hair was a vibrant, almost shocking shade of crimson, ran a hand through the tangled mess of a client's chestnut curls, a sigh escaping her lips.  Today’s appointment was Mr. Alistair Finch, a retired accountant famed (or perhaps infamous) for his precise, almost clinical, demands about the exact length of his sideburns.  Amelia had already nearly lost a fingernail to his obsessive precision in the past.
