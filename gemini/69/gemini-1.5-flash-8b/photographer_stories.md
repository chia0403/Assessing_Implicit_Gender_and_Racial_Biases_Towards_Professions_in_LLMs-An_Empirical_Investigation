The biting wind whipped Amelia Petrova's long auburn hair across her face, stinging her cheeks.  She hunched deeper into her thick wool coat, the icy breath misting in front of her as she squinted through the lens of her battered Leica.  The desolate, windswept moor stretched before her, a tapestry of bruised purple heather and skeletal, gnarled trees clawing at a bruised grey sky.  This wasn't the picturesque Scottish highlands brochure promised. This was raw, untamed, and utterly captivating.  Amelia, a freelance photographer renowned for her evocative landscapes,  had arrived at the remote village of Glencoe, drawn not by its supposed beauty, but by a cryptic, almost menacing whisper in the back pages of an antique magazine—a whisper of something extraordinary hidden within the heart of the moor.  The magazine article spoke of an ancient stone circle, shrouded in myth and legend, said to possess a power that both protected and threatened.  Amelia’s instincts, honed by years of seeking the extraordinary in the ordinary, were screaming at her to press on.
