Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of the "Hope's Harbor Insurance Agency," a building more reminiscent of a weathered barn than a modern office.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of light piercing the gloom, illuminating a figure hunched over a worn mahogany desk.  Amelia Grace Harding, a woman whose carefully sculpted auburn hair was now plastered to her forehead, stared at the swirling ink on a stack of claims forms.  The humid air hung heavy with the scent of damp earth and something vaguely sweet, like overripe mangoes.  Outside, the rhythmic drumming of the rain matched the frantic thumping of Amelia's heart.  The storm wasn't just lashing the coast; it was lashing her.  She had just received a notification that the latest claim, for a sprawling, centuries-old plantation house nestled deep within the rain-lashed mangrove swamps, had been escalated to the highest level of investigation.  This was no ordinary claim.  This was the Blackwood Estate, a name whispered with both awe and dread in the tight-knit community.
