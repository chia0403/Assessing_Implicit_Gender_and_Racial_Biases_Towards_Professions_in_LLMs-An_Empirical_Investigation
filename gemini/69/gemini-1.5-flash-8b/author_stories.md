The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Ashworth's hands, the steam curling around her face like a reluctant ghost.  Rain hammered against the leaded-glass windows of her tiny, cluttered attic office, a relentless rhythm that mirrored the frantic beat of her own thoughts.  Outside, the cobbled streets of the ancient, decaying town of Oakhaven whispered secrets only the wind seemed to hear.  Oakhaven, nestled deep within the emerald embrace of the Whispering Woods, was a place where the past clung to the present like cobwebs, and stories whispered on the breeze seemed to writhe with a life of their own.  Amelia, a self-proclaimed chronicler of forgotten tales, felt the weight of a new story pressing down on her, a story whispered in the rustle of autumn leaves and the mournful cry of the ravens circling the church spire.  She was tasked with unearthing the truth behind the town's most enduring legend: the disappearance of the Blackwood family, a mystery shrouded in centuries of hushed whispers and spectral sightings.  This was not a story she had volunteered for; it was one that had found her. And Amelia, armed with her worn leather-bound journal, a well-worn copy of "Poetic License," and a stubborn refusal to yield to fear, was ready to begin.
