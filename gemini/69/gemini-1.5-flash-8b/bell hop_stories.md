The humid air hung heavy over the grand lobby of the Imperial Palace Hotel, clinging to the polished mahogany floors and the ornate, gilded furniture like a damp shroud.  Dust motes danced in the shafts of sunlight piercing the towering stained-glass windows, illuminating a kaleidoscope of emerald greens and sapphire blues.  This was August, and the city of Veridia sweltered under a relentless heatwave, but inside the Imperial Palace, a cool, almost glacial elegance prevailed.  Barnaby "Barney" Butterfield, bellhop extraordinaire, adjusted the worn leather of his uniform, feeling the faint sheen of sweat beading on his brow.  He sighed, the sound swallowed by the murmur of voices, the clinking of silverware, and the distant strains of a grand piano.  His workday, as usual, was about to begin.
