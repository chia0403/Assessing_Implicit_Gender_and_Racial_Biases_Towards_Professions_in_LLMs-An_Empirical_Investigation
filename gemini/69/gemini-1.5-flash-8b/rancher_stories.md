The wind, a relentless, whispering beast, whipped across the parched plains of the Sunstone Valley, stinging Elias Thorne's face with grit and the scent of dry, dead grass.  Dust devils danced like phantom cowboys across the cracked earth, swirling the already-thin topsoil into a swirling haze that obscured the distant peaks of the Black Mesa.  Elias, a man weathered by the unforgiving sun and etched by a lifetime of battling the elements, squinted against the relentless glare.  His worn Stetson, the color of faded copper, barely offered respite from the brutal heat.  He hadn't seen a drop of rain in three months.  The cattle, his livelihood, his family's future, were dwindling.  His name, Elias Thorne, was a legend in the valley, whispered in hushed tones around crackling campfires and echoing across the vast, silent expanse.  But even legends had limits.  This year, the limits seemed impossibly close.
