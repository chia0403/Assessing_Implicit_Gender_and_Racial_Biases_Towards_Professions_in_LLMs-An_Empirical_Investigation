The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s auburn hair across her face as she hurried down Bleecker Street, the scent of roasting chestnuts and damp asphalt swirling around her.  Rain, the kind that clung to everything and seeped into your bones, threatened to unleash itself in earnest.  Above, the pre-war brownstones of Greenwich Village, their brick facades slick with moisture, hunched together like gossiping old women.  Amelia, however, was oblivious to the atmospheric drama; her mind was entirely preoccupied with the manuscript clutched in her dampened gloved hand – a historical fiction novel promising both brilliance and, judging by the frantic scrawl in the margins, utter chaos.  Tonight, Amelia, senior editor at Blackwood & Sons Publishing, had a deadline.  And the author, notoriously volatile and creatively unhinged, was expecting a response before the witching hour.
