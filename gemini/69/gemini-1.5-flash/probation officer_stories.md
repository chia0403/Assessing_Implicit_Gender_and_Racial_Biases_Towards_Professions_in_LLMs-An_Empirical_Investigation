The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Amy” Hernandez’s dark hair across her face as she navigated the crumbling steps leading to the dilapidated apartment building.  Number 4B.  Another Tuesday, another check-in.  The chipped paint of the building mirrored the state of many of Amy’s probationers, she thought grimly, pulling her worn wool coat tighter around her.  The air hung heavy with the scent of stale cigarettes and something vaguely greasy – a smell that seemed permanently etched into the fabric of this forgotten corner of East Oakland.  Above, a single bare bulb flickered weakly in the hallway, casting long, distorted shadows that danced with the dust motes swirling in the weak light.  Amy adjusted the small, worn leather-bound notebook in her hand, its pages filled with the names and stories – some hopeful, most harrowing – of the individuals under her supervision. Tonight, it was Marco Ramirez’s turn.  She just hoped he was home.
