The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s hair across her face as she hurried down Elm Street, the collar of her worn wool coat pulled high.  The scent of woodsmoke and damp earth hung heavy in the air, a familiar autumnal perfume that usually calmed her, but tonight, it only served to amplify the gnawing anxiety in her stomach.  Rainbow Daycare, nestled between a dilapidated bakery and a surprisingly vibrant flower shop, glowed a comforting yellow under the bruised twilight sky.  Inside, three small, brightly painted rooms hummed with the muffled sounds of children’s laughter and the rhythmic thud of tiny feet.  Tonight, however, the usual comforting symphony felt discordant, tinged with an unsettling quiet that prickled Amelia's skin.  She pushed open the heavy oak door, the bell above jingling a shrill, unwelcome announcement of her arrival.
