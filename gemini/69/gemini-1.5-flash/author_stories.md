The salt spray stung Amelia Blackwood’s face as she wrestled the stubborn mainsail.  The November wind howled a mournful ballad across the churning grey waters of the North Sea, whipping her already tangled auburn hair into a frenzy.  Amelia, author of the wildly successful "Whispers of the Kraken" trilogy, had traded the quiet solitude of her Cornish cottage for the unpredictable chaos of a chartered fishing trawler, a decision she was beginning to regret.  The pitching deck groaned beneath her worn leather boots, the smell of brine and diesel heavy in the air.  Ahead, the stark, windswept silhouette of the Shetland Islands loomed, promising refuge – and perhaps, inspiration – but only if she survived the journey.
