The salt spray stung Elias Thorne's face as he wrestled the stubborn oak beam into place.  The air, thick with the scent of brine and sawdust, whipped his dark hair across his eyes. Below him, the churning grey expanse of the North Sea gnawed at the craggy cliffs of Cornwall, its relentless roar a constant companion to the rhythmic thud of Elias's hammer. The half-finished boathouse, perched precariously on the cliff edge, swayed slightly with the wind, a testament to both Elias's skill and the unforgiving nature of the location.  He paused, wiping sweat from his brow, the raw, wind-bitten landscape a stark contrast to the warmth of the sun glinting on the newly-planed wood.  This wasn't just a boathouse; it was a refuge, a testament to a promise whispered on a stormy night, a promise Elias was determined to keep.
