The scent of rosemary and sandalwood hung heavy in the air, a fragrant counterpoint to the metallic tang of spilled bleach clinging stubbornly to the worn linoleum floor.  Rain lashed against the single window of Seraphina "Sera" Bellweather's salon, a rhythmic percussion accompanying the rhythmic snip-snip of her shears.  Sera, her fingers nimble and precise, worked on a cascade of fiery red curls, the client’s head tilted back, eyes closed in blissful surrender.  The salon itself, nestled on a cobbled side street in the perpetually damp, but charming, village of Oakhaven, was a haven of mismatched furniture and overflowing shelves crammed with hair products, each jar and bottle a testament to years of loyal service and accumulated clientele.  Outside, the storm raged, but inside, in Sera’s little sanctuary, a quiet, almost sacred calm reigned.
