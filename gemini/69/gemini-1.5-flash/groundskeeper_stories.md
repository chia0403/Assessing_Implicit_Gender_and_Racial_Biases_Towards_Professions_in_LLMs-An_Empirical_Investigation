The dawn crept over the manicured lawns of Blackwood Manor like a shy lover, painting the dew-kissed grass in shades of amethyst and rose.  A thick fog, clinging to the ancient oaks that ringed the estate, muffled the sounds of the waking world, leaving only the distant caw of a crow and the rhythmic *snip-snip-snip* of shears.  Elias Thorne, his breath puffing out in small white clouds, worked with the quiet diligence of a man who found solace in the meticulous care of his domain.  For Elias, groundskeeper at Blackwood Manor for the past twenty years, the estate wasn't just a job; it was a living tapestry woven from centuries of history and whispered secrets, a place where the weight of the past pressed down as heavily as the morning dew.
