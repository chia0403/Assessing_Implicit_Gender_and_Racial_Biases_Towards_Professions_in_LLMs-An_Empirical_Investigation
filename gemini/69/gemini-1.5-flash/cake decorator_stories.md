The scent of almond extract hung heavy and sweet in the air, a fragrant fog clinging to the chipped Formica countertops and the rainbow splatter of icing on Amelia Grace Worthington’s apron.  Amelia, or Amy as most people called her, sighed, the sigh catching on the wisps of vanilla-scented steam rising from the cooling rack beside her.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of sunlight slicing through the grimy window of "Sweet Surrender," her tiny, cluttered cake decorating studio nestled on a cobbled side street in the perpetually damp town of Seabrook.  Seabrook, where the salty tang of the ocean battled the persistent aroma of baking bread, and where Amy’s extraordinary cakes were the only known bright spots in a town more accustomed to grey skies than celebrations.
