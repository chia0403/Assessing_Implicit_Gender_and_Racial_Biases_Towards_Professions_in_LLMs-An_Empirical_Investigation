The scent of lilies, sharp and sweet, hung heavy in the air, a fragrant counterpoint to the damp chill that clung to the cobblestones of  Cobblestone Lane.  Rain, a relentless drizzle that had been falling since dawn, plastered Amelia Hawthorne's auburn curls to her forehead.  Her breath plumed out in little white clouds as she hurried along, her arms laden with armfuls of vibrant sunflowers, their faces drooping slightly under the weight of the water.  Amelia, owner of "Petals & Promises," a tiny flower shop tucked away at the end of the lane, had a reputation for creating breathtaking arrangements, but even her magic couldn't fully banish the gloom of this particularly dreary October morning. The only light came from the flickering gas lamps, casting long, distorted shadows that danced and writhed with the mist.
