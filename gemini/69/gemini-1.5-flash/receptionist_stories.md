The fluorescent lights of "Sterling & Steele, Attorneys at Law" hummed a monotonous tune, a soundtrack to Amelia Grace Hawthorne's otherwise silent existence.  Amelia, or Millie as she preferred, meticulously straightened the already-perfect row of orchids on the reception desk, their vibrant purple a stark contrast to the muted grey of the office.  The air, thick with the scent of expensive leather and old paper, felt stagnant, even at 8:03 AM.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows overlooking the city, blurring the towering skyscrapers into indistinct, watercolour smudges.  Today promised to be another deluge of disgruntled clients, demanding lawyers, and the ever-present, low-level thrum of legal battle echoing from behind the mahogany doors.  Millie sighed, the sound swallowed by the rhythmic drumming of the rain.  Her day, she suspected, was about to begin.
