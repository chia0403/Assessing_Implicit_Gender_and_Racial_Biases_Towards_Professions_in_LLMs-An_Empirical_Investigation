The salt spray stung Elara Vance's face as she wrestled the stubborn easel into position, its legs sinking slightly into the yielding sand.  The wind, a relentless sculptor itself, whipped her auburn hair across her vision, blurring the already hazy line between the churning grey sea and the bruised purple sky.  Elara, a landscape artist with a reputation for capturing the untamed spirit of the Cornish coast, found herself perched precariously on a jagged outcrop of rock, the relentless Atlantic roaring its disapproval at her intrusion.  Below, the waves crashed against the cliffs with a thunderous rhythm, a symphony of white froth and echoing booms that provided a far more dramatic soundtrack than her usually preferred classical music.  This remote cove, known only to locals and a handful of hardy hikers, was her sanctuary, a canvas waiting to be filled with the raw, untamed beauty that only nature could provide.
