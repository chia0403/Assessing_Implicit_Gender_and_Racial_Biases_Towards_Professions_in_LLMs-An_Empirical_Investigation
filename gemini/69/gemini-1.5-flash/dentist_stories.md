The biting November wind whipped Amelia Katherine Finch's dark hair across her face as she hurried down the cobbled lane, the scent of woodsmoke and brine clinging to the air.  Above, the bruised purple sky threatened more rain onto the already sodden streets of Port Blossom, a picturesque fishing village clinging precariously to the jagged Cornish coastline.  Dr. Amelia Finch, though, was more concerned with the throbbing ache in her own jaw than the impending downpour.  She'd been wrestling with a particularly stubborn impacted molar all afternoon, and the lingering numbness was now accompanied by a dull, insistent throb.  The rhythmic crash of waves against the harbour wall, usually a soothing balm, only served to amplify the discomfort.  Tonight, the usual charm of Port Blossom felt less like a haven and more like a damp, chilly prison.
