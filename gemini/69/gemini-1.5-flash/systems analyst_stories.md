The fluorescent lights of the SysTech data center hummed a monotonous tune, a counterpoint to the frantic clicking of Amelia “Mia” Hernandez’s keyboard.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows overlooking the sprawling cityscape of Seattle, mirroring the storm brewing inside her.  Mia, a systems analyst with a reputation for solving the unsolvable – and a penchant for caffeine-fueled all-nighters – stared at the cascading lines of code on her multiple monitors, a knot of frustration tightening in her stomach.  The new CRM system, touted as a revolutionary leap forward, was behaving less like a revolutionary and more like a particularly stubborn mule, spitting out error messages with the cheerful regularity of a metronome.  Tonight, however, the errors weren't just annoying; they were catastrophic.  The entire system threatened to collapse, taking SysTech's entire client base with it.  And the deadline was in three hours.
