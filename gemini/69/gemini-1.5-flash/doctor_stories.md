The biting wind whipped Dr. Eleanor Vance’s dark hair across her face as she wrestled the heavy oak door of the Blackwood Manor open.  A skeletal hand, carved into the weathered wood above the lintel, seemed to claw at the swirling grey sky.  The air hung thick and cold, carrying the scent of damp earth and something else… something acrid, metallic, that prickled at the back of her throat.  Blackwood Manor, perched precariously on the cliff overlooking the churning, slate-grey sea, was a gothic monstrosity of crumbling stone and shadowed windows, a place whispered about in hushed tones by the villagers of Portwenn.  It had stood empty for decades, a silent testament to a tragedy long buried, until now.  And Eleanor, despite the nagging unease that clawed at her own insides, was here to uncover its secrets.
