The chipped porcelain teacup warmed Amelia “Mia” Hawthorne’s hands, a meager comfort against the November chill seeping through the drafty windows of her office.  Rain lashed against the panes of glass, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her.  Mia, a woman whose meticulously organized spreadsheets and flawlessly tailored suits suggested an unwavering control over her life, felt anything but composed.  Her office, a cramped space above a bakery on the cobbled streets of  Port Blossom, Maine, usually smelled of cinnamon and sugared dreams. Today, however, the scent was overwhelmed by the damp wool of her coat and the lingering aroma of desperation. The flickering gaslight cast long shadows across the cluttered desk, highlighting the overflowing in-tray and the half-finished report on the suspiciously frequent claims filed by Captain Silas Blackwood, owner of the infamous "Sea Serpent" fishing trawler.
