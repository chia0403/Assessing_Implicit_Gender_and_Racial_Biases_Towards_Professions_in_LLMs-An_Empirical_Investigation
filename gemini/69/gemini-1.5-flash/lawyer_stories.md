The biting November wind whipped Amelia Stone’s dark hair across her face as she hurried down Bleecker Street, the scent of roasting chestnuts and damp earth stinging her nostrils.  Rain, a fine, persistent drizzle, plastered the posters advertising obscure bands and lost cats to the brick walls of Greenwich Village.  Amelia, a partner at the prestigious law firm of Harding & Hayes, usually navigated these cobblestone streets with a practiced, almost disdainful grace.  Tonight, however, the usual urban symphony of sirens and hurried footsteps was drowned out by the frantic thumping of her own heart.  The crumpled file clutched in her gloved hand felt heavy, a physical manifestation of the weight pressing down on her – a weight far heavier than any case she’d ever handled.  This wasn’t about corporate mergers or intellectual property rights. This was personal.  This was about Elias Thorne, and the secrets buried beneath the meticulously manicured lawns of his sprawling estate upstate.
