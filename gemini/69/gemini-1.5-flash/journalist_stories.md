The salt spray stung Amelia Grace Blackwood's face as the ferry bucked violently against the churning grey sea.  Rain lashed down, blurring the already indistinct outlines of the craggy, windswept islands that comprised the Outer Hebrides.  Amelia, huddled deeper into her oilskins, clutched her battered notepad to her chest, its pages already damp with the relentless downpour. The air hung thick with the smell of brine and peat smoke, a stark contrast to the sterile, air-conditioned newsrooms she usually inhabited.  This assignment, chasing a rumour of a long-lost manuscript detailing a forgotten clan feud, felt a million miles away from the polished desks of the *Glasgow Herald*.  And Amelia, despite her reputation for chasing hard-hitting stories in the city, felt profoundly out of her depth.  The isolated beauty of the islands was breathtaking, but also unsettling; a raw, untamed wilderness that seemed to hold its secrets close.
