The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Mia” Holloway’s auburn hair across her face as she hurried down Bleecker Street.  Rain, a fine, insistent drizzle, plastered her trench coat to her back, mirroring the weight settling in her stomach.  The air hung heavy with the scent of damp leaves and exhaust fumes, a familiar, almost comforting aroma for someone who’d spent the last fifteen years navigating the chaotic labyrinth of Manhattan’s mental health landscape.  Tonight, however, the familiar felt oppressive, the city’s usual frenetic energy a discordant symphony against the unsettling silence that had fallen between her and Dr. Elias Thorne, her patient – and the man who’d just called, his voice thick with a terror she recognized all too well.  His apartment, a cramped, book-lined space overlooking Washington Square Park, was a mere ten-minute walk away, but it felt like a journey across continents.
