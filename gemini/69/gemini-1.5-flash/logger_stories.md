The biting wind howled a mournful song through the skeletal branches of the ancient Douglas firs, their needles dusted with the first snow of the season.  It whipped around Jedediah “Jed” Stone’s thick wool coat, a familiar chill that burrowed deep into his bones. Jed, his breath puffing out in white clouds, squinted at the vast expanse of the Cascade Mountains, a tapestry of snow-laden peaks and shadowed valleys stretching before him like a sleeping giant.  The air, thin and crisp, carried the scent of pine, damp earth, and the faintly metallic tang of blood – a scent Jed knew all too well.  He stood on the edge of the logging road, his worn leather boots sinking slightly into the frost-covered ground,  the axe leaning against a gnarled stump beside him, its head gleaming dully in the weak winter sun.  This was his kingdom, the unforgiving yet breathtaking wilderness of the Washington backcountry, and for Jed Stone, this was home.
