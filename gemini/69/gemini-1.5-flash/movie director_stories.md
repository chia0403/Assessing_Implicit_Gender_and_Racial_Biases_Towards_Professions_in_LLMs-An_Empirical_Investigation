The pre-dawn Los Angeles sky bled bruised purple and apricot, a breathtaking spectacle completely lost on Isabella "Izzy" Rodriguez.  Izzy, renowned for her gritty neo-noir thrillers and even grittier behind-the-scenes temperament, was currently wrestling with a recalcitrant boom mic operator named Kevin, whose general air of nonchalant incompetence was threatening to derail her entire meticulously crafted schedule.  The location, a decaying Victorian mansion perched precariously on a cliff overlooking the Pacific, groaned under the weight of its own history and the relentless onslaught of early morning rain.  Inside, the opulent but decaying interior – a testament to faded grandeur – pulsed with the chaotic energy of a film set on the brink of collapse.  The air hung thick with the scent of stale coffee, damp plaster, and the faint, metallic tang of fear.
