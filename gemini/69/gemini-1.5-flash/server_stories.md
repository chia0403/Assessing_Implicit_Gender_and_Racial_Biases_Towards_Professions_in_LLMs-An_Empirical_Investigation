The chipped Formica tabletop felt sticky beneath Elara Jean Blackwood's fingertips, a familiar testament to a Friday night rush at The Rusty Mug.  The air hung thick with the cloying sweetness of spilled beer, stale coffee, and desperation – a potent perfume Elara had learned to tolerate, even, in a twisted way, appreciate.  It was the scent of Friday night at The Rusty Mug, a dive bar nestled so deep within the shadowed alleys of Oakhaven that the city lights seemed to forget it existed.  Neon signs flickered intermittently above the rain-streaked windows, casting a sickly yellow glow on the faces of the regulars – a motley crew of disgruntled poets, aging rockabillies, and hopeful hustlers clinging to the dregs of the week.  Tonight, however, the usual Friday night chaos felt different, heavier, charged with an undercurrent of something Elara couldn't quite place, something that prickled at the edges of her awareness like static electricity.
