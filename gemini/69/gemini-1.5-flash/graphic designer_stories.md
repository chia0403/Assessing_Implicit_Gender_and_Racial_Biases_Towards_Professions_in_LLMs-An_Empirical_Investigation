The chipped ceramic mug warmed Amelia Hernandez’s hands, the lukewarm chamomile doing little to soothe the icy dread settling in her stomach.  Rain lashed against the panoramic window of her Soho loft, blurring the already hazy cityscape into a watercolour wash of grey and black.  The rhythmic drumming of the downpour mirrored the frantic tattoo of her own heart.  Amelia, a graphic designer whose reputation rested on clean lines and vibrant colours, felt utterly devoid of both.  Her usually meticulously organized workspace – a testament to her obsessive attention to detail – was a chaotic mess of scattered sketches, crumpled paper, and half-empty coffee cups.  The looming deadline for the Peterson & Sons rebranding project, a contract that could make or break her fledgling design studio, loomed larger than the storm raging outside.  And the creative well, usually overflowing with ideas, had run frustratingly dry.
