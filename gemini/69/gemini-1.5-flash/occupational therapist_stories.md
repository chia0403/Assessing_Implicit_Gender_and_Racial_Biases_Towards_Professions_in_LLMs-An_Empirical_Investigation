The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s auburn braid across her face as she hurried down Elm Street, the collar of her charcoal grey coat pulled high.  Rain, a relentless, sideways drizzle, plastered her already damp hair to her scalp.  The streetlights, struggling against the gloom of the pre-dawn sky, cast long, distorted shadows that danced and writhed like phantoms on the wet pavement.  Ahead, the imposing brick façade of Oakhaven Rehabilitation Center loomed, a gothic monument to healing in the heart of the otherwise charming, if slightly dilapidated, coastal town of Seabrook.  Inside, Amelia knew, a silent battle was being waged – a battle she was uniquely equipped, and perhaps, uniquely burdened, to fight.  Her day, and the lives of her patients, were about to begin.
