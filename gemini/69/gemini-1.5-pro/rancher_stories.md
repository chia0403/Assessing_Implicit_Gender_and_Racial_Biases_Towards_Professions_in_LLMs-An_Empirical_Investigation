The wind, a constant companion in this part of Montana, whipped at Amelia Grace Holloway's weathered face as she surveyed the endless expanse of the Rocking H Ranch.  The sky, a bruised purple and orange canvas, hinted at the coming storm.  The air, thick with the smell of sagebrush and impending rain, crackled with a restless energy.  Beneath her worn leather boots, the dry earth crunched, a counterpoint to the mournful lowing of cattle in the distance. The Rocking H, nestled in a valley carved by the relentless Chisholm River, was Amelia's legacy, a sprawling testament to generations of Holloways who had wrestled a living from this unforgiving, yet breathtaking, land.  This evening, however, the beauty felt ominous, the quiet a tense prelude to something unknown.
