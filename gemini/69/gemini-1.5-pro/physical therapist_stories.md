The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s auburn braid against her cheek as she hurried across the cobblestone courtyard.  Gargoyles perched on the eaves of the imposing, gothic-revival Thornhill Manor seemed to leer down at her, their stony faces slick with rain.  Built in 1888, the manor now housed the Thornhill Rehabilitation Center, a place where Amelia had spent the last five years patching together broken bodies and spirits. The slate-grey sky mirrored the oppressive mood within the manor’s walls, a feeling that had intensified since the arrival of their newest patient, a reclusive and enigmatic author named Mr. Silas Blackwood.
