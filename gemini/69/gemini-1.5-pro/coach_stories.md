The biting wind whipped across the desolate practice field, carrying the scent of damp earth and the faint, metallic tang of old blood. Not literal blood, of course, but the kind spilled in sweat and tears by generations of Wolverines who had toiled on this very ground.  Coach Eleanor "Ellie" Vance shivered, pulling her worn Wolverines windbreaker tighter around her. The floodlights cast long, skeletal shadows from the bleachers, making the empty stadium feel even more cavernous than usual. November in Northwood, Minnesota, was a cruel mistress, and tonight, she was showing no mercy. Ellie’s breath puffed out in ghostly white clouds as she surveyed the muddy field, her gaze fixed on the solitary figure practicing field goals.  This was her domain, her kingdom – a kingdom of broken dreams and flickering hopes, where she reigned supreme, a stoic queen in a faded blue baseball cap.
