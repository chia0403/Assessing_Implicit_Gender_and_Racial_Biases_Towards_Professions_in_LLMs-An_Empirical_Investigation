The biting November wind whipped around Elias Thorne's thick wool coat, stinging his cheeks as he surveyed the half-finished timber frame against the backdrop of the brooding Scottish Highlands. Grey clouds, heavy with the promise of snow, clung to the peaks of Ben Cruachan, and the air tasted of pine needles and damp earth.  Elias, his calloused hands tucked deep in his pockets, adjusted the brim of his worn cap.  Kilchurn Castle, a crumbling monument to a bygone era, stood sentinel across the loch, its reflection shimmering in the steely water.  Here, nestled on a small, sheltered patch of land between the loch and the imposing mountain, Elias was building his future, plank by carefully measured plank.  He was building his home.
