The wind whipped off the jagged peaks of the Dragon's Tooth mountains, carrying the scent of pine and the faint, metallic tang of snowmelt.  Elias Thorne, his calloused fingers already numb despite the thick gloves, squinted at the granite face before him. Dawn was a bruised purple bleeding into the eastern sky, painting the sheer cliff in shades of lavender and grey.  His quarry, nestled high on a precarious ledge overlooking the whispering valley of Whisperwind, was a lonely and unforgiving place.  For generations, the Thorne family had carved their living from this unforgiving landscape, wresting beauty from the cold, hard stone.  Elias, the latest in this long line of stone cutters, was no exception. Today, however, was different. Today, he wasn't carving a gargoyle for a nobleman's estate or a tombstone for the village graveyard. Today, he was carving his masterpiece.
