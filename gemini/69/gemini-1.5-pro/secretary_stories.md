The rhythmic clatter of typewriter keys filled the small, airless office, punctuated by the occasional exasperated sigh of Eleanor Vance.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of sunlight slicing through the grimy windowpane, illuminating the overflowing ashtrays and precarious stacks of paper threatening to topple onto Eleanor's neatly organized desk.  It was a Tuesday in November, 1957, in the unassuming town of Alder’s Bend, Pennsylvania, a place where time seemed to move slower, clinging to the rhythms of a bygone era. Eleanor, secretary to the perpetually flustered Mr. Henderson, owner of Henderson’s Haberdashery, adjusted her cat-eye glasses and blew a stray strand of dark hair from her face.  Little did she know that the seemingly ordinary delivery boy arriving at the door with a package marked “Personal and Confidential” was about to unravel the carefully constructed routine of her life.
