Rain lashed against the windows of Bartholomew "Barty" Higgins' cramped office, mimicking the drumming anxiety in his chest.  Fluorescent lights buzzed overhead, casting a sickly yellow glow over the stacks of claim forms and brochures for "Higgins Haven Insurance: Protecting Your Piece of Paradise."  Paradise, Barty thought wryly, glancing out at the flooded streets of Port Blossom, Oregon.  The picturesque coastal town, usually brimming with the charm of its antique shops and the salty tang of the Pacific, was currently resembling a forgotten bathtub toy left to soak overnight. This was the third major storm in as many months, and Barty knew, with a chilling certainty, that his phone was about to start ringing off the hook.
