The biting wind howled down Market Street, whipping snow into frenzied swirls that stung Anya "Ace" Petrova's exposed cheeks.  Chicago in January was a beast, a concrete jungle clad in ice armour.  The city lights, usually a comforting glow, were diffused and hazy behind the blizzard, casting an eerie, almost otherworldly, light on the scene unfolding before Engine 66.  Flames licked at the night sky from the third floor of a dilapidated brownstone, the hungry orange glow a stark contrast to the swirling white chaos. Ace, her breath clouding the air before her, adjusted the strap of her helmet and exchanged a grim look with Captain Hayes. This wasn't going to be an easy one.
