The frosted breath of November clawed at the leaded windows of the Alderanth Library, sketching ephemeral ferns and forests across the glass. Inside, nestled amongst the hushed whispers of turning pages and the comforting scent of aging paper, Eleanor Vance, the head librarian, adjusted her spectacles and surveyed her domain.  Alderanth, a small, isolated town nestled deep within the whispering pines of the Oregon Cascades, relied on the library as its sole connection to the wider world.  Here, amidst the towering shelves crammed with forgotten lore and half-remembered tales, Eleanor reigned supreme, a quiet guardian of words and the stories they held.  But the tranquility of this literary sanctuary was about to be shattered.  A raven, black as midnight, had taken up residence on the window ledge, its obsidian eyes fixed on Eleanor with an unsettling intelligence, and in its beak, it clutched a single, tarnished silver key.
