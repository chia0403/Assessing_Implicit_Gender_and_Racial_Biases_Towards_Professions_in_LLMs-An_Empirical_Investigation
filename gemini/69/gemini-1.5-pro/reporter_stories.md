The biting November wind whipped Amelia "Amy" Reyes's auburn braid across her face as she stood on the precipice of Blackwood Bluff, overlooking the churning slate-grey waters of the North Atlantic.  Salt spray stung her cheeks, a familiar discomfort she’d grown accustomed to in the five years she'd been reporting for the Port Blossom Gazette.  Below, the small fishing town of Port Blossom, Maine, clung to the rocky coastline like a barnacle, its brightly painted houses a stark contrast to the brooding sky.  Amy pulled her threadbare wool coat tighter, the damp chill seeping through its inadequate protection.  She wasn’t here to admire the view, though. She was here because a fisherman had vanished, swallowed whole by the same unforgiving sea that provided this town’s livelihood, and Amy, ever the tenacious reporter,  was determined to uncover the truth behind his disappearance, even if it meant braving the chilling whispers of a town that held its secrets close.
