Rain lashed against the window of Amelia Grace Holloway's cramped office, a relentless drumming that mirrored the throbbing headache forming behind her eyes. Tax season was reaching its fever pitch, and Amelia's small office, nestled above Mrs. Higgins' perpetually dusty antique shop in the perpetually grey town of Dullsville, Ohio, was overflowing with receipts, W-2s, and the palpable anxiety of last-minute filers. The scent of stale coffee and desperation hung heavy in the air, punctuated by the rhythmic tap-tap-tapping of Amelia's fingers on her calculator.  A half-eaten tuna sandwich sat forlornly on a chipped porcelain plate beside her, a testament to the scarcity of time in these final, frantic weeks before the April 15th deadline.  Outside, the neon sign of the 'Dullsville Diner' flickered erratically, casting a sickly green glow on the rain-slicked streets.  This year, Amelia felt the pressure more than ever.  Mrs. Higgins had raised the rent again, and Amelia, despite working twelve-hour days, was barely keeping afloat.  She desperately needed a miracle, or at least a client who wasn't claiming their pet goldfish as a dependent.
