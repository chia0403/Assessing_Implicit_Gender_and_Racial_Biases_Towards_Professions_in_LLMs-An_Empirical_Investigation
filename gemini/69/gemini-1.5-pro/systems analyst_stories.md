Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of the 47th floor, blurring the already fading London skyline into a watercolour wash of grey and ochre.  Inside, the air thrummed with the low hum of servers and the frantic tapping of keyboards.  Elara Vivienne Sharma, a systems analyst for the notoriously secretive tech firm, Chronos Incorporated, chewed on the end of her pen, her brow furrowed in concentration. Lines of code scrolled across her monitor, a cryptic language only she seemed to fully understand.  The office, a cavernous space of polished concrete and exposed brick, was almost deserted. Most of Chronos' employees adhered religiously to a 9-to-5 schedule, but Elara thrived in the quiet solitude of the after-hours, where the only distractions were the rhythmic clatter of the rain and the persistent, nagging feeling that something within Chronos’ intricate systems wasn’t quite right.
