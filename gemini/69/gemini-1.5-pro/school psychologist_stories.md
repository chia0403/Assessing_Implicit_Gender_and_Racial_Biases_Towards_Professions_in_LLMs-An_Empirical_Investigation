The biting November wind whipped around Dr. Eleanor Vance as she hurried across the frost-covered quad of Crestwood Preparatory Academy.  Gargoyles perched on the gothic stone buildings seemed to leer down at her, their expressions mirroring the knot of unease tightening in her stomach.  Crestwood, nestled in the affluent, rolling hills of the Hudson Valley, was a picture of prestigious academia.  Ivy climbed the walls, whispering promises of bright futures, but beneath the veneer of manicured lawns and legacy admissions lurked a darkness Eleanor was only beginning to understand. This morning, the crisp air carried not just the scent of burning leaves, but a palpable tension, a chilling premonition that something at Crestwood was about to shatter.
