The biting November wind whipped around Elias Finch, tugging at the brim of his tweed cap and stinging his cheeks.  He hunched deeper into his worn canvas jacket, the scent of damp earth and decaying leaves clinging to him like a second skin.  Frost crunched beneath his heavy boots as he traversed the winding path through Blackwood Manor's sprawling gardens.  The once vibrant flowerbeds now lay dormant beneath a blanket of browning leaves, their skeletal remains a stark reminder of summer's fleeting glory.  The Manor itself loomed over him, a gothic monstrosity of grey stone and leaded glass windows, its silhouette stark against the bruised purple sky.  Elias, Blackwood's groundskeeper for the past twenty years, knew every inch of this place, every gnarled branch, every hidden alcove, every whispering secret the wind carried through the ancient trees.  This morning, however, a different kind of whisper hung in the air, a chilling unease that settled deep in his bones.
