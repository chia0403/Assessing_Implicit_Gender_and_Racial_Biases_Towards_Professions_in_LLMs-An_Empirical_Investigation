The scent of burnt sugar hung heavy in the air, a stark contrast to the usual buttercream-and-rose perfume that permeated Eleanor Ainsworth's bakery, "Sugar & Bloom."  Rain lashed against the large Victorian windows, blurring the view of the bustling market square in the quaint coastal town of Port Blossom, Maine. Eleanor, her brow furrowed in concentration, scraped a charred attempt at caramel off the bottom of a copper pot. It was the third batch she'd ruined that evening, and the grand opening of her bakery was just two days away.  The pressure was mounting like the storm clouds gathering over the harbor.  Each flicker of the gas lamps cast long, dancing shadows across the buttercream-colored walls, adorned with framed botanical prints and vintage cake decorating tools.  The usually cheerful space felt oppressive, heavy with the weight of Eleanor's anxieties.
