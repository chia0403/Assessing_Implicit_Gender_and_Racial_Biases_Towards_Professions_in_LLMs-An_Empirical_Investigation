The scent of stale crayons and freshly sharpened pencils hung in the air, a familiar perfume that both soothed and slightly nauseated Miss Penelope Amelia Hawthorne. Room 204 at Meadow Creek Elementary School was her domain, a riot of colorful construction paper, alphabet charts, and finger-painted masterpieces that clung precariously to the walls. Sunlight, filtered through the dusty blinds, striped the worn linoleum floor. Outside, the shrieks and laughter of children at recess echoed, a vibrant counterpoint to the quiet industry she was attempting to foster in her third-grade classroom. Most of her students were glued to their multiplication worksheets, their brows furrowed in concentration. Except for Billy Miller, who was currently attempting to fashion a paper airplane out of his math test. Penelope sighed inwardly. It was going to be a long day.
