The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Hathaway’s hair across her face, stinging her cheeks a vibrant, angry red.  She pulled her worn wool coat tighter, the collar scratching at her already chapped neck.  The air hung thick with the smell of coal smoke and brine, a familiar aroma clinging to the cobbled streets of Port Blossom, a picturesque fishing village clinging precariously to the jagged cliffs of the Cornish coast.  Amelia, a journalist for the struggling *Cornwall Chronicle*, hunched deeper into her coat, the damp chill seeping into her bones.  The flickering gaslights cast long, skeletal shadows that danced with the restless sea, reflecting in the slick, dark surface of the harbour below. Tonight, however, the usual melancholic beauty of Port Blossom felt overshadowed by a palpable unease, a sense of something dark and hidden lurking beneath the surface – much like the secrets Amelia suspected this town was fiercely guarding.
