The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne's auburn braid across her face as she hurried down the rain-slicked street, the collar of her worn raincoat pulled high.  Grey skies wept a relentless drizzle over the cobbled lanes of St. Kilda, mirroring the turmoil brewing inside her.  The scent of brine and decaying seaweed hung heavy in the air, a familiar perfume in this seaside town nestled on the rugged Scottish coast.  Amelia, a registered nurse at the understaffed and perpetually overwhelmed St. Jude's Hospital, pulled her damp gloves tighter.  She’d just finished a grueling twelve-hour shift, the memory of Mrs. MacLeod's labored breaths and the sterile tang of antiseptic still clinging to her like a second skin.  Tonight, however, the exhaustion wasn’t the worst of it. Tonight, the shadows held something far more sinister.
