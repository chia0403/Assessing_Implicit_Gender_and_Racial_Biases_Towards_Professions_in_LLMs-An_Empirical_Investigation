The air hung thick and sweet with the scent of rosemary and burnt sugar, a peculiar perfume only found in the heart of Old Town, nestled between the crumbling brick walls of a forgotten alleyway and the boisterous laughter spilling from the Blue Parrot Tavern.  Rain, a relentless drizzle that had been falling since dawn, slicked the cobblestones to a gleaming sheen, reflecting the neon glow of a crooked “Barbershop” sign hanging precariously above the doorway. Inside, amidst the whirring of clippers and the snip-snip of scissors, sat Aurelia Genevieve Bellweather, her nimble fingers working magic on a head of unruly, copper-red hair.  The shop, a cozy haven of vintage barber chairs and faded photographs, was her kingdom, and the scent of her unique blend of pomades and tonics, her royal decree.
