The salt spray stung Amelia Katherine Blackwood's face as she wrestled the easel into position, the wind threatening to send her palette and brushes tumbling into the churning grey sea below.  The cliffs of Cornwall, a jagged, unforgiving spine of granite, rose behind her, their wind-whipped grasses a dull emerald against the bruised purple of the gathering storm.  The air, thick with the scent of brine and seaweed, carried the mournful cry of gulls, a counterpoint to the rhythmic crash of waves against the rocks. Amelia, her oils already slick with rain, squinted at the turbulent expanse of ocean before her, its wild beauty both terrifying and inspiring.  This tempest, she knew, would be her masterpiece.
