The biting wind whipped across the desolate expanse of the Alberta plains, stinging Amelia "Amy" Blackwood's cheeks raw.  Dust devils, miniature tornadoes of ochre and brown, danced across the frozen ground, swirling around the massive, slumbering behemoth that was her Caterpillar D11T dozer.  Amy, bundled in layers of thermal underwear, a thick fleece jacket, and a bright orange parka, sat in the heated cab, the scent of diesel and cold metal clinging to her like a second skin.  The pre-dawn sky bled shades of bruised purple and steely grey, promising another day of relentless cold and even more relentless work on the Trans-Canada Highway expansion project.  The only sound louder than the wind was the low hum of the dozer's engine, a comforting thrum that vibrated through the steel frame and into Amy's very bones. This was her sanctuary, this machine, and the frozen wasteland her kingdom.
