The biting November wind howled a mournful tune through the gaps in the boarded-up windows of Blackwood Manor, rattling the ancient panes like skeletal fingers.  Inside, the air hung thick with the scent of damp earth and something else… something faintly metallic and disturbingly sweet.  This wasn’t the usual aroma of a leaky pipe, thought Archibald "Archie" Butterfield III, as he wrestled a stubborn toolbox onto the sodden flagstones of the overgrown courtyard.  Archie, a plumber whose clientele ranged from the perpetually blocked drains of suburban housewives to the occasionally haunted plumbing systems of eccentric aristocrats, found himself distinctly out of his depth.  Blackwood Manor wasn't just old; it felt ancient, a place where shadows lingered longer than they should and secrets were buried deeper than any sewer line.
