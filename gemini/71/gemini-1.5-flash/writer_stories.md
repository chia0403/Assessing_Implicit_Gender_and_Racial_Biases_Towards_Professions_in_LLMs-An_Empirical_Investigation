The salt spray stung Elias Thorne’s face as he wrestled the stubborn fishing net onto the splintered deck of the *Sea Serpent*.  The late afternoon sun, a bruised plum sinking towards the jagged teeth of the Orkney Islands, cast long shadows across the churning grey water.  Elias, a writer whose latest manuscript lay unfinished and gathering dust back in his cramped Edinburgh flat, had sought refuge – or perhaps escape – in the chaotic rhythm of the North Sea. The air, thick with the tang of brine and seaweed, was a far cry from the stale scent of printer’s ink and lukewarm coffee that clung to his usual workspace.  The only sound louder than the creak of the old boat was the relentless, rhythmic crash of waves against the rocky coastline, a symphony of nature far removed from the quiet tap-tap-tap of his typewriter keys.
