The flickering neon sign of "Benny's Bagels" cast a sickly yellow glow on the rain-slicked streets of Chicago.  Water cascaded from the eaves of the ancient brick building, mirroring the relentless torrent of paperwork threatening to engulf Amelia Grace Hawthorne's desk.  Amelia, a paralegal with a perpetually weary sigh etched into her face, stared at the mountain of files threatening to topple onto the already precarious stack of half-eaten bagels and lukewarm coffee cups. The air hung thick with the scent of stale coffee, cheap printer toner, and the lingering aroma of burnt sugar from Benny's disastrous attempt at a cinnamon roll earlier that morning – a fitting metaphor for Amelia’s current mood.  Outside, the city roared, a symphony of sirens and honking horns, a stark contrast to the suffocating silence of her cramped office, punctuated only by the rhythmic tap-tap-tap of her fingers on the keyboard. Tonight, however, the silence felt particularly heavy, pregnant with the impending storm of a case that threatened to consume her – and possibly her sanity – entirely.
