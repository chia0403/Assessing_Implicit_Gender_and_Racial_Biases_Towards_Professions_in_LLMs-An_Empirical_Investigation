The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Hernandez's fingers, the lukewarm chamomile tea doing little to soothe the icy knot in her stomach.  Rain lashed against the panoramic window of her Brooklyn loft, blurring the already hazy cityscape into a watercolour wash of greys and inky blues.  Amelia, a graphic designer whose portfolio boasted vibrant, almost aggressively optimistic designs for tech startups, felt anything but optimistic tonight.  The deadline for the Peterson & Sons rebranding loomed, a monstrous, pixelated beast gnawing at her already frayed nerves.  The loft, usually a haven of creative chaos – a meticulously arranged chaos, mind you – felt suffocating, the half-finished mockups scattered across her desk mocking her stalled progress.  The rhythmic drumming of the rain outside was a morbid counterpoint to the frantic tap-tap-tap of her fingers against the worn wooden surface of her desk, a frantic rhythm that mirrored the frantic beat of her heart.
