The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Mia” Hernandez’s hair across her face as she wrestled the battered metal door of the abandoned textile mill open.  Rust groaned like a dying beast under the strain, showering her with flakes of orange dust.  Inside, the air hung thick and heavy, a miasma of damp wool and decay.  Moonlight, fractured by grimy windows, cast long, skeletal shadows across the cavernous space, illuminating looms long silent and spinning jennies frozen mid-cycle, like some macabre museum of forgotten industry.  The only sound was the rhythmic drip, drip, drip of water somewhere deep within the labyrinthine structure, a relentless counterpoint to the hammering of Mia’s own anxious heart.  She was on the trail of a story that could make her career, or break her – the disappearance of textile magnate, Silas Blackwood, a man whose wealth was as legendary as his ruthlessness.  And she had a feeling this crumbling mill held the key.
