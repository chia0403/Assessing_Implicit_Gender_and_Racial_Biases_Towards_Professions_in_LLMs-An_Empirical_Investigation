The biting wind howled a mournful dirge across the jagged peaks of the Dragon's Tooth mountains, whipping snow into frenzied dervishes that stung the exposed skin of Sergeant Valerius Thorne.  Valerius, his breath misting in the frigid air, gripped the shaft of his halberd tighter, the cold seeping into his very bones.  He stood atop the windswept battlements of Fort Bastion, a grim, grey fortress clinging precariously to a sheer cliff face, its stone teeth bared against the unforgiving elements.  Below, the chasm yawned, a bottomless abyss swallowed by swirling shadows.  For centuries, Fort Bastion had stood guard against the savage tribes that roamed the shadowed valleys, their eyes always hungry, their intentions always dark. Tonight, however, the usual quiet unease was amplified by an unnatural stillness, a silence heavier than the falling snow.
