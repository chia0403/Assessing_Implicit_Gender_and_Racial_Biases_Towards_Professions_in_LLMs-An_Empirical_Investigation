The scent of acetone and lilac hung heavy in the air, a perfume as familiar to Seraphina “Sera” Dubois as her own skin.  Fluorescent lights hummed a monotonous tune above the chipped Formica countertop of “Sera’s Snips & Polish,” a nail salon tucked away on a quiet side street in the perpetually twilight-drenched town of Oakhaven.  Rain lashed against the single, grime-streaked window, mirroring the stormy grey of the sky and somehow matching the tempestuous mood of Mrs. Gable, currently undergoing a decidedly aggressive French manicure.  Sera, her own fingers stained a kaleidoscope of colours from a long day, sighed, the sound almost swallowed by the rhythmic whir of the electric file.  Outside, the wind howled a mournful ballad, a fitting soundtrack to the simmering drama unfolding before her.
