The chipped porcelain sink groaned under the weight of Elara Vance's elbows as she scrubbed at the stubborn crust of burnt sugar clinging to the bottom of Lord Ashworth's prized copper casserole dish.  Dust motes, disturbed by her movements, danced in the weak afternoon light filtering through the grime-coated leaded glass windows of the sprawling, gothic kitchen.  Outside, the wind howled a mournful ballad around Blackwood Manor, its icy breath rattling the ancient timbers of the house like rattling bones. Elara, her knuckles raw and red, sighed.  Another day, another mountain of chores in a house that seemed to actively resist cleanliness, a house that whispered secrets in the shadows and held its breath in the silence between the wind's cries.
