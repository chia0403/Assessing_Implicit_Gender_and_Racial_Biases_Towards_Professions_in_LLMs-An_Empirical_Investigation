The biting November wind whipped Dr. Eleanor Vance’s charcoal grey coat around her legs as she navigated the uneven cobblestones of Blackwood Lane.  Overhead, the skeletal branches of ancient oaks clawed at the bruised twilight sky, their silhouettes etched sharply against the bruised orange and violet hues of the setting sun.  Blackwood, a town clinging stubbornly to the edge of the brooding Peak District, was a place where secrets burrowed deep into the very earth, a place where the past refused to stay buried.  Eleanor, a forensic psychologist with a reputation for cracking even the most hardened of criminal minds, found herself drawn to its melancholic charm – and its unsettling mysteries. Tonight, however, the charm felt thin, replaced by a palpable sense of unease that prickled at the back of her neck, a sensation amplified by the mournful wail of a distant church bell.  The flickering gaslights cast long, dancing shadows that seemed to writhe and twist, mirroring the turmoil brewing within her.  She was here to assess a suspect, but she sensed this case was far more complex than the standard profile.
