The fluorescent lights of Northwood High hummed a monotonous tune, a soundtrack to Dr. Eleanor Vance's perpetually overflowing inbox.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of her office, blurring the already muted autumn colours of the sprawling campus beyond.  The air, thick with the scent of stale coffee and teenage angst, hung heavy even after she'd cracked open the window a sliver.  Eleanor sighed, the sound barely audible above the rhythmic tap-tap-tap of her pen against the overflowing file on her desk – a testament to the simmering anxieties, bubbling frustrations, and outright meltdowns that characterized the lives of her students.  Today, however, felt different.  Heavier.  A premonition, perhaps, that nestled in the pit of her stomach, cold and clammy, like the damp October air pressing against the glass.  The email subject line – "Urgent – Student Incident" – only served to deepen the unsettling feeling.
