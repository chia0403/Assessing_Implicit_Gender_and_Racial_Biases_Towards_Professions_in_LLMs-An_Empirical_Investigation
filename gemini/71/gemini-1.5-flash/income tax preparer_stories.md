The fluorescent lights of Millie’s Tax Services hummed a monotonous tune, a soundtrack to the chaos unfolding around Beatrice “Bea” Mallory.  It was April 14th, the dreaded eve of the tax deadline, and Bea’s small, cluttered office in the back of a strip mall in Anytown, USA, resembled a war zone after a particularly messy battle.  Empty coffee cups formed a precarious mountain range on her desk, amidst a sea of crumpled papers, half-eaten granola bars, and the faint scent of desperation and stale printer ink.  Outside, the late afternoon sun cast long shadows across the parking lot, highlighting the desperate scrambles of last-minute filers rushing to meet the impending deadline.  Inside, Bea, armed with her trusty calculator, a well-worn copy of the Internal Revenue Code, and an almost superhuman capacity for caffeine, braced herself for the final onslaught.
