The salt spray kissed Amelia Hernandez’s face as she wrestled the stubborn “SOLD” sign into the parched earth of the beachfront lot.  The wind, a relentless Pacific gale, threatened to rip the flimsy cardboard from its stake, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her.  Amelia, a real estate agent with a reputation for closing even the most impossible deals, felt a familiar knot tighten in her stomach.  This wasn't about the commission, hefty as it was; this was about the house – or rather, the absence of one – perched precariously on the cliff edge overlooking the churning ocean in the exclusive enclave of Seacliff Heights.  The sky, a bruised purple and angry orange, reflected the turmoil in her heart, a perfect backdrop to the looming tragedy she felt, even before the official call had come.
