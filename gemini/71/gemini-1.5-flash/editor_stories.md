The air hung thick and heavy with the scent of old paper and brewing coffee, a familiar perfume to Aurelia “Rel” Blackwood.  Dust motes, illuminated by the single, harsh bulb dangling precariously above her desk, danced in the stagnant air of her cramped office.  Rel, a woman whose sharp wit was only surpassed by her even sharper eye for detail, leaned back in her creaking chair, a half-eaten granola bar perched precariously on the stack of manuscripts threatening to topple from its precarious perch.  The walls of Blackwood & Quill Publishing, a once-grand building now showing its age through peeling paint and stained carpets, seemed to whisper tales of bygone literary glories, a stark contrast to the financial woes plaguing the company, and, consequently, Rel herself.  Outside, the relentless London drizzle mirrored the relentless pressure she felt to save her family's legacy, one rejected manuscript at a time.
