The chipped paint of Bartholomew "Barty" Butterfield's desk matched the chipped optimism in his soul.  Rain lashed against the grimy windows of his office, a rhythm mimicking the relentless drumming of anxiety in his chest.  He sat hunched over a stack of unpaid premiums, the aroma of stale coffee and impending doom clinging to the air like a persistent cough.  Barty, a man whose life insurance sales were as consistently underwhelming as his hairline, stared out at the sodden streets of Grimsborough, a town where the only things thriving seemed to be the weeds pushing through the cracks in the pavements and the ever-growing list of his overdue accounts.  The clock ticked, each second a tiny hammer blow against the fragile edifice of his already precarious existence.
