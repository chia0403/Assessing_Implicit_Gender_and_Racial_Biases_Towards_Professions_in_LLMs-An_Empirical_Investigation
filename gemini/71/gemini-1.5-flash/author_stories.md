The salt spray stung Amelia Jane Worthington’s face as she wrestled the stubborn fishing boat’s tiller.  The wind, a snarling banshee, whipped her auburn hair across her vision, momentarily blinding her as she fought to keep the "Sea Serpent," a temperamental vessel inherited from her eccentric grandfather, on course.  The grey, churning waters of the Irish Sea stretched before her, a vast, unforgiving expanse under a sky the colour of bruised plums.  Amelia, a writer of gothic mysteries renowned for their atmospheric detail, found herself strangely inspired by the brutal beauty of the storm, the raw power mirroring the tempest brewing within her own life – a tempest far more treacherous than any sea could ever muster.  She’d come to this remote island, Inisheer, seeking solace, hoping the relentless rhythm of the waves would drown out the deafening silence of her writer’s block, and the even louder clamor of her broken heart. But the island, shrouded in mist and legend, seemed determined to offer only a different kind of torment.
