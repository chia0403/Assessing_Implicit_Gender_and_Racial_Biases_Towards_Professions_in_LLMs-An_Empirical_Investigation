The chipped porcelain of Dr. Eleanor Vance's coffee mug mirrored the state of her own composure.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of her practice, blurring the already hazy cityscape of Seattle.  Grey, a colour Eleanor usually associated with a promising, overcast day ideal for hiking, felt oppressive this morning.  The rhythmic drumming of the storm on the glass was a counterpoint to the frantic tapping of her pen against the meticulously organised appointment book, each tap a tiny hammer blow against the already frayed edges of her patience.  Her usually pristine, white-coated figure was rumpled, her usually perfectly coiffed auburn hair escaping its bun in unruly wisps.  This wasn't just a bad Tuesday; this was a Tuesday that threatened to unravel the carefully constructed fabric of her meticulously ordered life.
