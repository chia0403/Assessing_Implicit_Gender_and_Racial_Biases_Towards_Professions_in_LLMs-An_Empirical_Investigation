The chipped paint of Dr. Eleanor Vance’s waiting room peeled like sunburnt skin, mirroring the fragile state of many who sat upon its uncomfortable floral-patterned couch.  Rain lashed against the single, grime-streaked window of her small, ground-floor office in the heart of Grimsborough, a town clinging stubbornly to its faded Victorian charm.  The air hung heavy with the scent of damp wool and old books, a comforting aroma to Eleanor, a stark contrast to the anxieties that often permeated her practice.  The clock on the wall ticked with a relentless rhythm, each second a reminder of the limited time she had to unravel the tangled threads of her clients' lives, threads often knotted tighter than she’d ever imagined.  Today, however, the knot seemed particularly intractable; a new patient, Mr. Silas Blackwood, waited in the hushed silence, his presence felt more than seen.
