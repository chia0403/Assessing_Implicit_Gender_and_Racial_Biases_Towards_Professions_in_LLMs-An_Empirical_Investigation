The salt spray stung Dr. Eleanor Vance's face as she wrestled the stubborn fishing net free from the barnacles clinging to the weathered pilings.  The late afternoon sun cast long shadows across the craggy coastline of Cape Ann, Massachusetts, painting the turbulent Atlantic in hues of bruised purple and fiery orange.  The air, thick with the scent of brine and seaweed, carried the mournful cry of gulls overhead.  Eleanor, her usually meticulously styled auburn hair escaping its braid in wind-tossed chaos, grunted with exertion.  This wasn't exactly the kind of physiotherapy she'd studied for, but then again, helping old Captain Silas untangle his livelihood was a form of rehabilitation in its own right, she supposed.  The small, weather-beaten fishing village of Rock Harbor, with its jumble of brightly painted houses clinging to the hillside, felt a million miles away from the sterile, brightly lit clinic where she usually spent her days.
