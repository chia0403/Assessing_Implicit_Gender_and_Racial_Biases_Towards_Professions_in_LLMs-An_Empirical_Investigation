The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Hernandez’s hands, a small comfort against the November chill that seeped through the drafty window of her office.  Rain lashed against the panes, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her.  The air in the Northwood High School guidance counselor's office hung thick with the scent of stale coffee and teenage angst, a familiar aroma that Amelia had come to both tolerate and dread in equal measure.  Filing cabinets, overflowing with student transcripts and college brochures, lined the walls, their metal surfaces reflecting the flickering fluorescent lights overhead.  A worn, leather-bound copy of "The Catcher in the Rye" sat precariously on her desk, a silent testament to the endless stream of troubled souls who passed through her doorway.  Today, however, felt different.  A particularly unsettling phone call had just ended, leaving Amelia with a knot of unease tightening in her stomach – a premonition that something far more significant than typical teenage drama was about to unfold.
