The biting November wind whipped around Elias Thorne's thick wool coat, whistling through the skeletal branches of the elms that lined Oakhaven Lane.  Sawdust clung to his eyelashes, the fine powder glittering like frost in the weak afternoon sun.  Elias, his calloused hands tucked deep in his pockets, surveyed the half-finished timber frame house that rose against the backdrop of the grey Welsh mountains.  The air, sharp with the scent of pine and damp earth, held the promise of snow.  Oakhaven, a small village nestled in the Conwy Valley, was preparing for winter, and Elias, its sole carpenter, was working against the encroaching cold to finish the Davies family's new home before the first flakes fell.
