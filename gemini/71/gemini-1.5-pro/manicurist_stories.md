The bell above the door to "Nailed It" tinkled merrily, a sound that usually brought a smile to Seraphina "Sera" Reyes's face.  This Tuesday, however, the cheerful chime grated on her already frayed nerves.  Rain lashed against the large window showcasing her meticulously painted sample nails, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her.  Nailed It, her pride and joy, was nestled between a laundromat and a perpetually closed-down fish and chips shop on Bleecker Street in Greenwich Village.  The scent of lavender hand lotion usually battled valiantly against the lingering odor of stale grease, but today even the lavender seemed to have given up the fight. Sera glanced at the clock – 2:57 pm. Three minutes until Mrs. Higgins, notorious for her exacting standards and gossip-mongering, was due for her weekly gel manicure and pedicure. And Sera still hadn’t figured out how to tell her she was raising her prices.
