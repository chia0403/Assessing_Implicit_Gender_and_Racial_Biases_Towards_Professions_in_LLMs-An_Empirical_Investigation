The biting November wind whipped around Dr. Elias Thorne as he hurried across the cobblestone courtyard of the Griffin Rehabilitation Center.  Grey stone buildings, their windows glowing warmly against the encroaching twilight, huddled around the central fountain, now frozen mid-spurt.  Dead leaves, brittle and brown, skittered across the flagstones, mimicking the nervous energy thrumming beneath Elias's calm exterior.  He was late, again.  Not drastically, but enough to irritate Mrs. Abernathy, the Center's notoriously strict head nurse.  And today, of all days, he couldn't afford to be on her bad side.  Today was the day his most challenging patient, a renowned ballerina struggling to regain mobility after a devastating accident, was scheduled to begin her first tentative steps.
