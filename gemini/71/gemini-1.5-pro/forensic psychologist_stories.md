The biting November wind whipped Dr. Evelyn Reed’s auburn hair across her face as she stepped out of her vintage Jaguar E-Type.  Rain, sharp as needles, stung her cheeks, and the gothic spires of the University of Blackwood seemed to claw at the bruised, twilight sky.  Nestled in the heart of the Scottish Highlands, the university, with its imposing stone buildings and hushed courtyards, exuded an air of both ancient wisdom and unsettling secrecy. Evelyn shivered, not entirely from the cold. She’d been summoned here, to this remote and prestigious institution, to consult on a case unlike any she had encountered in her decade-long career as a forensic psychologist. A case that, according to the cryptic phone call from the university chancellor, threatened to unravel the very fabric of Blackwood's hallowed history.
