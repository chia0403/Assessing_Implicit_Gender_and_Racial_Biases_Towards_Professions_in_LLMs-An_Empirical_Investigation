The biting wind whipped across the Bonneville Salt Flats, stinging Elias Vance's exposed cheeks even through his thick beard.  Sodium dust, fine as powdered sugar, swirled around his worn work boots, clinging to the cuffs of his Carhartt overalls.  The setting sun, a molten orange orb sinking behind the jagged peaks of the Silver Island Mountains, cast long, distorted shadows across the blinding white expanse.  Elias, his toolbox clanking softly against his leg, trudged towards the lonely cluster of prefabricated buildings that housed the pumping station for the Great Salt Lake Mineral Extraction Facility.  Flickering fluorescent lights, buzzing like trapped insects, spilled from the grimy windows, casting an eerie, sickly glow onto the salt-encrusted landscape.  The smell of brine and ozone hung heavy in the air, a constant reminder of the unforgiving environment Elias called his workplace.  Tonight, the hum of the pumps was absent, replaced by an unsettling silence that prickled the hairs on the back of his neck.  Something was wrong.
