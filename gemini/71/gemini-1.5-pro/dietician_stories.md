The crisp October air, scented with woodsmoke and decaying leaves, whipped around Eleanor Vance's ankles as she hurried across the cobbled courtyard of Blackwood Manor.  Gargoyles leered from the eaves, their granite faces slick with rain, and the ivy clinging to the ancient stone walls rustled like whispered secrets. Eleanor, a registered dietician with a penchant for sensible shoes and a deep distrust of anything remotely supernatural, clutched her briefcase tighter, wishing she'd insisted on meeting her new client at her office in the brightly lit, decidedly un-haunted town of Ashworth.  But Mr. Silas Blackwood, the reclusive owner of the manor, had been adamant.  He required a consultation at his residence, and the substantial fee he was offering had, unfortunately, overruled Eleanor's better judgement.
