The biting pre-dawn wind whipped across the Alberta plains, stinging Amelia “Milly” Petrov’s exposed cheeks. Frost crunched under her steel-toed boots as she walked towards the behemoth that was her charge for the day: a Caterpillar D11T dozer, its blade gleaming faintly under the security lights of the Horizon Oil Sands Project.  The sky, a bruised purple edged with the faintest hint of rose, promised a brutally cold day.  Steam billowed from the exhaust stacks of the idling machines scattered across the vast, scarred landscape.  Milly inhaled deeply, the sharp scent of diesel mixing with the metallic tang of the bitumen-rich earth. This was her office, a symphony of growling engines and grinding metal, and she, its conductor.
