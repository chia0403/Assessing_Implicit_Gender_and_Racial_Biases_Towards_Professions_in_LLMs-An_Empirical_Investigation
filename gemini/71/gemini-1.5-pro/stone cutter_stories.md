The wind, sharp and biting as a chisel, whipped across the desolate plains of Eldenhollow, carrying with it the fine dust of granite and the mournful howl of distant wolves.  Elias Thorne, his calloused hands tucked deep within the pockets of his worn leather jerkin, squinted at the setting sun.  It painted the jagged peaks of the Dragon's Tooth mountains a fiery crimson, a stark contrast to the grey monotony of the quarry that dominated the landscape before him. This quarry, a vast, gouged-out scar on the earth, was Elias's life, his livelihood, and his prison. For generations, the Thornes had toiled here, extracting the pale grey Eldenstone, a rock renowned throughout the Seven Kingdoms for its strength and the ethereal light it seemed to capture within its crystalline structure.  The air hung heavy with the scent of stone dust and the faint, metallic tang of iron tools, a smell Elias knew better than any perfume.
