The biting November wind whipped Amelia Reyes's chestnut hair across her face as she hurried down Bleecker Street, the scent of roasting chestnuts warring with the diesel fumes from a passing bus.  Amelia clutched her oversized tote bag, its contents – a kaleidoscope of farmers' market brochures and a half-eaten quinoa salad – threatening to spill onto the rain-slicked pavement.  Above the cacophony of the city, the gargoyles perched atop the crumbling brownstones of the West Village seemed to leer at her hurried progress.  Amelia, a registered dietitian with a penchant for organic produce and a deep-seated distrust of refined sugar, was late.  Very late.  Not for a client appointment, but for a clandestine meeting in a dimly lit speakeasy, tucked away behind a dusty antique bookstore – a meeting that promised to upend the carefully balanced world of healthy eating she had so meticulously constructed.
