Rain lashed against the grimy windows of Anya Sharma’s Ford Focus, blurring the already fading neon glow of the "Lucky Dragon" takeout joint across the street.  The air inside the car was thick with the scent of stale coffee and damp upholstery, a familiar aroma Anya associated with late nights and difficult cases.  She tapped a chipped, lilac-painted nail against the steering wheel, her gaze fixed on the dilapidated two-story building across the street in the seedier part of Bridgeport, Connecticut. Number 42, the last known address of  Marcus “Razor” Riley, one of Anya's most volatile probationers.  Razor was due to check in three days ago, and the gnawing feeling in Anya’s gut told her this wasn’t just a simple missed appointment.
