The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Grace Hawthorne's hands as she stared out the rain-streaked window of the breakroom.  Fluorescent lights hummed overhead, casting a sickly yellow glow on the half-eaten bagels and lukewarm coffee dregs littering the scarred Formica table.  Outside, the Chicago skyline was shrouded in a thick November mist, the towering skyscrapers resembling ghostly sentinels guarding a city steeped in secrets.  Amelia took a sip of her Earl Grey, the bergamot a fleeting comfort against the gnawing unease that had settled in her stomach.  Galweather & Stern, Attorneys at Law, occupied three floors of the imposing granite building, a monument to corporate success built on the backs of overworked paralegals like herself.  Today, however, the usual Monday morning drudgery felt different.  A prickly sense of anticipation, tinged with apprehension, hung in the air, thick as the fog rolling in off Lake Michigan.
