The biting November wind whipped around Eleanor Vance, RN, as she hurried across the deserted parking lot of St. Jude's Memorial Hospital.  The flickering sodium vapor lamps cast long, skeletal shadows that danced with her every step.  Tucked deep within the Appalachian foothills of rural West Virginia, the hospital itself loomed against the inky sky, a monolithic brick structure radiating a faint, sickly yellow glow from its windows.  It was 3:57 AM, just three minutes shy of the start of Eleanor's double shift, and already the familiar knot of dread tightened in her stomach.  St. Jude's was chronically understaffed, perpetually overwhelmed, and whispered to be haunted by the restless spirits of miners lost to the mountain's unforgiving embrace. Tonight, all three felt palpably true.
