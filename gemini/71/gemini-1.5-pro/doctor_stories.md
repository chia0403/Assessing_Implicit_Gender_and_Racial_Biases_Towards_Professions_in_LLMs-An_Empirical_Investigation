The biting wind whipped across the desolate plains of Wyoming, rattling the loose windowpane in Dr. Elias Thorne's cramped office. Snow, already a foot deep, continued to fall in thick, swirling flakes, obscuring the lone gas station across the otherwise empty main street of Harmony Creek.  Inside, the only sounds were the rhythmic tick of a grandfather clock in the corner and the low hum of the kerosene heater struggling to fight back the encroaching cold.  Dr. Thorne, his weathered face illuminated by the flickering lamp on his desk, stared out at the blizzard, a furrow etched deep between his thick, grey eyebrows.  Harmony Creek, population 83, wasn't much, but it was all he had left.  And tonight, something felt different. The wind howled like a mournful banshee, and a strange, disquieting silence hung in the air, broken only by the insistent rapping at his front door.
