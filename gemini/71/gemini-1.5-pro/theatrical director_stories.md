The biting November wind whipped around Beatrice Amelia Thorne as she stood on the fire escape of the dilapidated Grand Majestic Theatre, a cigarette clenched between her teeth. Ash danced on the gusts, mirroring the frantic flutter in her stomach. Below, the neon marquee, flickering and missing half its letters, proclaimed "Coming Soon: A Winter's Tale." Soon. The word echoed mockingly in Beatrice's mind.  The premiere was a mere three weeks away, and the production was, to put it mildly, a disaster.  The Grand Majestic, once a jewel of Chicago's theatre scene, now sported peeling paint, a persistent damp smell, and a resident family of pigeons who seemed determined to sabotage every rehearsal.  Beatrice inhaled deeply, the bitter smoke a meager comfort against the overwhelming sense of impending doom.
