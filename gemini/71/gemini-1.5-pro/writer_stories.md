The salt spray stung Amelia Grace Hawthorne's face as she leaned against the weathered railing of her balcony, the rusty metal cold beneath her fingertips.  The Isle of Skye, usually shrouded in a mystical mist, was bathed in the unusual clarity of a late September afternoon.  The jagged peaks of the Cuillin mountains, normally veiled, stood stark against the sapphire sky, their shadows stretching long fingers across the emerald-green slopes that tumbled down to the churning turquoise sea below.  Amelia’s cottage, perched precariously on a cliff overlooking the Sound of Raasay, was a haven, a self-imposed exile where she’d come seeking solace and inspiration for her next novel. The words, however, remained elusive, trapped somewhere between the crashing waves below and the vast, indifferent sky above.
