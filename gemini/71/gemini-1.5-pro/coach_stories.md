The biting November wind whipped across the desolate practice field, carrying the scent of decaying leaves and the faint echo of a distant train whistle.  Floodlights cast long, skeletal shadows of the bleachers onto the churned mud, illuminating only a small, determined figure in the center circle.  Amelia "Amy" Rodriguez, head coach of the Northwood High Ravens soccer team, hunched deeper into her worn, navy blue windbreaker, her breath clouding the frigid air.  Northwood, a small, forgotten town nestled in the foothills of the Appalachian Mountains, wasn't exactly known for its athletic prowess. In fact, the Ravens hadn't won a single game in three seasons.  But Amy, a former collegiate star with fire in her eyes and a tactical mind sharper than any cleat, was determined to change that, even if it meant facing down the apathy of a town resigned to losing and the ghosts of her own athletic past.
