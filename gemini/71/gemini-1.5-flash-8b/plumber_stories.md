The flickering neon sign of "Plumbers' Paradise" cast a sickly yellow glow over the rain-slicked alleyway.  Water, a relentless, insidious force, dripped from the eaves of the dilapidated buildings, mirroring the constant drip, drip, drip of a leaky pipe that plagued the life of Bartholomew "Barty" Butterfield, third-generation plumber.  Barty, a lanky man with perpetually damp hands and eyes that held the weary wisdom of a thousand leaky faucets, surveyed the scene with a sigh.  The alley, a labyrinth of rusted metal and discarded plumbing parts, was his domain, his office, his battleground.  He was, quite literally, drowning in a sea of plumbing woes.  Tonight, however, the problem wasn't a clogged drain or a burst pipe.  It was a mystery, a puzzle wrapped in a cryptic message delivered by a flustered, drenched squirrel.
