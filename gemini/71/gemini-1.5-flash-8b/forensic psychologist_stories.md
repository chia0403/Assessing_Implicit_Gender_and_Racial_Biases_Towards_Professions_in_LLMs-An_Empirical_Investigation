Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of the abandoned factory, a relentless percussion that mirrored the relentless rhythm of Dr. Eleanor Vance's thoughts.  The air, thick with the smell of mildew and decaying timber, hung heavy in the musty silence.  Broken glass crunched under her boots as she navigated the labyrinthine corridors, the faint glow of her headlamp illuminating the skeletal remains of machinery.  This wasn't a crime scene; it was a tomb, a mausoleum of a forgotten past. Yet, Dr. Eleanor Vance, forensic psychologist, felt the cold certainty of a case unfolding before her, a chilling puzzle hidden within the ruins. The year was 2042.  She was already late.
