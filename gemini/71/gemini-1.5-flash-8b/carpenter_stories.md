The biting wind, a relentless claw, ripped at the edges of Elias Thornewood's worn leather apron.  He hunched deeper into the ramshackle shed, the flickering candlelight casting dancing shadows on the meticulously-hewn planks he was shaping.  Dust motes, illuminated by the flame, swirled in the frigid air.  The small, cobbled village of Oakhaven, nestled deep in the shadowed valleys of the Whispering Woods, was shrouded in a thick, grey mist that clung to the timbers of the houses like a suffocating shroud.  Elias, a carpenter of renowned skill – his hands, weathered and calloused, were as familiar with the grain of wood as the contours of his own face – was trying to salvage a piece of warped oak, destined for the new manor house of Lord Blackwood.  Lord Blackwood.  The very name, whispered with a mixture of awe and apprehension, seemed to carry with it the weight of the entire valley's history.  He knew, with a grim certainty, this piece, if it didn't meet exacting standards, would lead to a most regrettable consequence.
