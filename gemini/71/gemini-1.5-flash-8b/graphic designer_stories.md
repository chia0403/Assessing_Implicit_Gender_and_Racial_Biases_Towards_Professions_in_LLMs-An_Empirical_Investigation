The chipped Formica countertop, stained a dull beige from years of lukewarm coffee and spilled instant ramen, reflected the harsh fluorescent light of the tiny studio.  Rain hammered against the grimy windowpane, a relentless percussion against the quiet hum of Amelia Blackwood's laptop.  Amelia, a graphic designer with a penchant for bold typography and a soul that yearned for something more, stared blankly at the pixelated, almost abstract representation of a storefront sign she'd been meticulously crafting for three hours.  The client, a struggling artisan selling handmade candles, had insisted on a "mystical" aesthetic, and Amelia was frankly running out of mystical.  The only thing mystical about this project was the sheer number of revisions.
