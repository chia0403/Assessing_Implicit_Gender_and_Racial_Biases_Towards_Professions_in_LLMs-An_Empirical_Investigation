The chipped porcelain sink, stained a faded, sickly yellow from decades of lukewarm water and forgotten soaps, reflected Bartholomew "Barty" Butterfield's anxious face.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of afternoon sunlight piercing the gloom of "The Clipper's Edge," a barbershop tucked away on a cobbled side street in the heart of  Old Oakhaven.  The scent of lavender, long faded, clung to the worn, velvet-lined chairs, a ghostly echo of happier days.  Barty, a man whose razor-sharp wit had long since dulled into a perpetual frown, stared at his reflection. Today was the day. The day he was finally taking the plunge, a plunge into the unknown and the potentially catastrophic abyss of running his father's shop alone.  His father, a legendary barber known as "Silas the Swift," had passed away three months prior, leaving behind not only a thriving, but also a complicated, business.  And now Barty, with his perpetually disheveled hair and perpetually worried expression, was his sole heir.
