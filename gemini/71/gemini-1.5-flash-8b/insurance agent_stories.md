Rain lashed against the grimy windows of the "Assured Futures" office, mimicking the relentless drumming of Amelia Blackwood's thoughts.  The fluorescent lights hummed, a monotonous counterpoint to the growing anxiety clawing at her gut.  It was a Tuesday, a day like any other, or so the calendar said. But today felt different. Today, Amelia felt the weight of a thousand whispered promises, unspoken fears, and the ever-present possibility of disaster pressing down on her.  The office, a sterile expanse of beige carpeting and Formica tables, felt suffocating.  Rows of identical file cabinets, their contents holding the hopes and anxieties of countless clients, seemed to loom like silent, judgmental figures.  The aroma of stale coffee and cheap air freshener clung to the air, a familiar, yet unsettling, scent of a life lived on borrowed time.  Amelia, a thirty-five-year-old insurance agent, with the kind of weary resilience that comes from years of navigating the treacherous waters of human misfortune, stared at the blinking cursor on her computer screen.  A new claim had just popped up, and the name at the top of the page, familiar yet chilling, felt like a punch to the gut.  It was a claim concerning the infamous Blackwood Estate, and her stomach lurched.
