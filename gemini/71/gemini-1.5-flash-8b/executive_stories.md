The biting wind whipped Amelia Hawthorne, CEO of Hawthorne Industries, across the cobblestones of the ancient city.  Dust devils danced in the deserted plazas, swirling around the skeletal remains of once-grand buildings.  The air, thick with the scent of ozone and something vaguely metallic, clung to her lungs like a shroud.  This wasn't the gleaming metropolis she'd envisioned when she'd first arrived, the one promised by the meticulously crafted promotional materials of the Terra Nova Consortium.  Instead, it was a desolate, crumbling testament to a forgotten glory, choked by the encroaching sands of time and a lingering, pervasive sense of decay.  Above, the vast, ochre-colored sky seemed to press down, suffocating.  Amelia, her tailored suit straining against the raw elements, felt a cold dread creep into her core, a feeling as tangible as the swirling dust.  This wasn't just a business trip; it was a mission, a desperate gamble to salvage what was left of Project Chimera.  And the odds, she knew, were stacked against her.
