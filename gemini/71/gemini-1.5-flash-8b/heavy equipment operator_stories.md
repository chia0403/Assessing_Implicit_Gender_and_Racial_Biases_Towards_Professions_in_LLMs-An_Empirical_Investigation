The biting wind whipped Amelia "Ace" Rodriguez's hair across her face, stinging her cheeks.  Dust devils danced across the cracked, sun-baked earth of the Mojave Desert, swirling around the massive Caterpillar D9R that groaned beneath her command.  The year was 2042.  The once-fertile valleys of Southern California lay parched and cracked, testament to the relentless drought that had gripped the nation for a decade.  Ace, her calloused hands gripping the levers of the monstrous machine, felt the tremor of the engine resonate through her bones.  She was a lone figure, a solitary point of human industry in a landscape of scorched desolation.  The only sound besides the machine's relentless rumble was the rhythmic clack of her boots on the hard-packed earth, a counterpoint to the vast emptiness surrounding her.  Her mission: to carve a new irrigation canal, a lifeline for a struggling farming community clinging to existence on the edge of the dwindling aquifer.
