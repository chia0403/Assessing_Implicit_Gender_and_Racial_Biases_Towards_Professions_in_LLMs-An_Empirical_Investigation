Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of the probation office, a rhythmic drumming that mirrored the insistent beat of Amelia Hawthorne's thoughts.  The fluorescent lights hummed, casting a sterile, pale glow over the cluttered desk.  Empty coffee cups, overflowing file folders, and half-eaten takeout containers littered the space, a testament to the chaotic ebb and flow of her life – and the lives of the young adults she was charged with supervising.  Outside, the sprawling, grey metropolis of Zenith City stretched out, a labyrinth of towering skyscrapers and shadowed alleys, each one a potential trap or sanctuary, depending on the individual. Amelia, a probation officer for the Zenith City Juvenile Court, felt a knot tightening in her stomach.  Another case file, another risk assessment.  Another chance to make a difference.  Her focus narrowed as she stared at the manila envelope addressed to her:  "Ethan Carter – Case 2739."  A cold dread crept up her spine.  Ethan Carter.  The name whispered a story of defiance, a whisper of darkness that resonated within the oppressive hum of the office.
