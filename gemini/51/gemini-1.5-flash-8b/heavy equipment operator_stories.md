The biting wind whipped across the desolate plains, stinging Amelia "Amy" Blackwood's cheeks as she wrestled the controls of the CAT 390.  Dust devils danced across the cracked earth, swirling into miniature tornadoes that momentarily obscured the vast, ochre landscape.  The sun, a malevolent eye in the bruised sky, beat down mercilessly on the remote drilling site, located somewhere in the heart of the sparsely populated Badlands region of Wyoming.  This was the 17th day of the week-long, relentless drilling operation, a seemingly endless cycle of hammering, vibrating, and the ever-present threat of equipment failure. Amy, 28 years old with calloused hands and eyes hardened by the relentless desert sun, felt a knot of frustration tightening in her chest.
