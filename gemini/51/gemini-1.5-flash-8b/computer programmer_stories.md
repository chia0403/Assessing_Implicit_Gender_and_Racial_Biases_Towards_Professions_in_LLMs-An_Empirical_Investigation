The fluorescent hum of the server room vibrated through Amelia Petrova's worn sneakers, a constant, almost comforting drone in the otherwise silent space.  Rows upon rows of gleaming metal cabinets, stacked high like some futuristic library, held the heart of "Chronos," the global financial network Amelia meticulously coded and maintained.  Dust motes danced in the shafts of pale light filtering through the grimy windows, painting patterns on the concrete floor.  It was a Monday night, the kind of night where the city outside slumbered, oblivious to the intricate dance of electrons happening within the bowels of the data center.  Amelia, however, was far from asleep.  A flickering red light on the console displaying the Chronos core was the only sign of her presence.
