The chipped porcelain sink, stained a perpetual grey-green, reflected Elara Blackwood's weary face.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of afternoon sun slicing through the grimy window of the sprawling, neglected manor.  Elara, a woman whose life was a tapestry woven from quiet routines and the relentless cycle of cleaning, sighed.  Another day, another house, another layer of grime to be peeled away, leaving behind the ghosts of lives lived and lost.  The scent of old books and faded roses, a constant companion in the sprawling Victorian mansion known as Blackwood Manor, hung heavy in the air. This was her 17th house in as many months. Each one whispered a silent tale of its former inhabitants, and Elara, the silent witness, absorbed them all.  Her task, her calling, was to erase those echoes, to return the spaces to a blank canvas – a canvas that, for now, bore the weight of a decade's worth of decay.  The manor’s grand halls, once filled with laughter and music, were now echoing with nothing but the rustling of dried leaves and the creaking of ancient timbers.  Today, the focus was on the library.
