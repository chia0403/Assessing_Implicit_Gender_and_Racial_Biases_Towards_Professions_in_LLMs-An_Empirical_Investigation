The biting wind whipped Amelia Petrova's hard-hatted head, stinging her cheeks with icy rain.  Grey clouds, the colour of bruised plums, hung low over the desolate expanse of the construction site, shrouding the skeletal framework of the new skyscraper in an oppressive gloom.  Scaffolding, rusted and swaying precariously, scraped against the bruised sky like skeletal fingers.  Amelia, her face obscured by the grime of a long day, gripped the frayed rope ladder, her calloused hands clinging desperately to the splintered wood.  Below, the churning concrete mixer coughed and spat its grey slurry into the dark, echoing pit.  The air hung thick with the metallic tang of freshly-cut steel and the cloying scent of diesel fumes.  This was her world, the unforgiving landscape of early morning pre-dawn construction on the outskirts of the sprawling city.
