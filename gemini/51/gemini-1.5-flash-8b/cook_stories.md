The air hung thick and sweet with the scent of cinnamon and roasting cloves.  A low hum, like a contented sigh from a thousand honeybees, vibrated through the stone walls of "The Whispering Hearth," a small, ivy-covered inn nestled deep within the shadowed valleys of the Whisperwind Mountains.  Rain hammered against the slate roof, a relentless percussion that echoed the frantic rhythm in Elara Alderbrook's heart.  Elara, the inn's head cook, a woman with eyes the color of polished amber and hands that seemed to dance across the hearth like fireflies, felt a knot of dread tighten in her stomach.  Tonight, Lord Ashworth, renowned for his ferocious appetite and even more ferocious temper, was expected.  And Elara had failed.  Catastrophically.
