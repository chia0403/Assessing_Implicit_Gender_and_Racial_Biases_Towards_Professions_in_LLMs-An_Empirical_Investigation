The biting wind whipped Amelia Blackwood's scarf around her face, stinging her cheeks as she hunched deeper into the doorway of the abandoned mercantile.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of sunlight piercing the gloom, illuminating a network of cobwebs that stretched like skeletal fingers across the shattered display cases.  The scent of mildew and decay hung heavy in the air, a suffocating blanket over the silent town of Havenwood.  Amelia, a seasoned reporter for the "Crimson Quill," had spent the last three days documenting the town's slow, agonizing death, a death seemingly whispered on the wind and etched into the very landscape.  Havenwood, once a bustling hub of trade and commerce, was now a ghost town, its inhabitants vanished as mysteriously as the golden era it had once represented.  Her notebook, filled with cryptic clues and unanswered questions, rested on her worn leather satchel.
