The biting wind whipped Dr. Anya Petrova's long auburn hair across her face as she peered into the dimly lit examination room.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of light filtering through the grimy window of the dilapidated veterinary clinic, a testament to the harsh realities of the rural town of Oakhaven.  Rust-colored barns, their weathered wood painted in faded hues of red and teal, lined the winding, dirt roads that snaked through the valley.  The air, thick with the scent of pine and damp earth, carried the faint, almost imperceptible whimpers of a creature in distress.  Anya, wrapped in a worn leather coat, felt a familiar pang of dread, a premonition that this particular appointment would be anything but routine.  Today, a small, shivering, feral kitten lay sprawled on a chipped enamel tray, its tiny body trembling uncontrollably, its eyes wide and filled with a nameless terror.
