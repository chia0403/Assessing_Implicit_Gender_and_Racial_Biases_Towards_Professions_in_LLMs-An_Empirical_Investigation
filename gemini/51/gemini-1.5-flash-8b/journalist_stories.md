The biting wind whipped Amelia Blackwood’s scarf around her neck, stinging her cheeks like a thousand tiny needles.  Rain, the persistent, unwelcome guest of the British Isles, plastered her already soaked trench coat to her body.  She hunched deeper into the doorway of the crumbling pub, “The Rusty Mug,” its sign barely clinging to the chipped wooden frame.  The pub, a relic of a bygone era, reeked of stale beer and damp earth.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of light filtering through the grimy window, illuminating a scene more than a century old.  This was the heart of Blackwood, a picturesque, yet forlorn, village nestled deep within the Cotswolds.  Amelia, a seasoned journalist for the London Chronicle, had come seeking the truth behind the local whispers, the tales of an ancient feud and a vanished fortune.  Her notepad, already brimming with scribbled notes, felt heavy in her hand, a tangible weight mirroring the growing unease in her stomach.
