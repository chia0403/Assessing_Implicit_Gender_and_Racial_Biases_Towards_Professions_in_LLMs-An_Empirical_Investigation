The chipped Formica countertop of Amelia Blackwood's tiny, cluttered office reflected the harsh fluorescent light, highlighting the dust motes dancing in the air.  A half-eaten bowl of lukewarm oatmeal sat beside a overflowing recycling bin, its contents a chaotic mix of rejected manuscript pages, crumpled coffee cups, and discarded pens.  Amelia, a Senior Editor at Blackwood & Co. Literary Agency, hunched over her laptop, her brow furrowed in concentration.  Outside, the rhythmic clatter of city life – buses honking, sirens wailing, distant construction – was a constant, intrusive hum.  Amelia, however, was in a different world entirely, wrestling with the intricacies of a manuscript detailing the alleged paranormal activities of a forgotten Victorian-era manor.
