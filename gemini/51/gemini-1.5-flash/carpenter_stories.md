The salt spray stung Elias Thorne's face as he wrestled the stubborn oak plank onto the rickety scaffolding.  Below, the churning grey waters of the Atlantic gnawed at the pilings supporting the dilapidated lighthouse, their rhythmic hiss a counterpoint to the creak of the aged timber under his boots.  The air hung thick with the scent of brine and pine, a familiar perfume to Elias, a carpenter whose life had been as weathered and uncompromising as the ancient sentinel he was tasked with repairing.  He perched precariously, a solitary figure against the vast, unforgiving expanse of the ocean, the wind whipping his dark, sea-tangled hair across his brow.  This wasn't just any lighthouse; it was Widow's Point, notorious for its treacherous currents and the whispers of lost souls clinging to the fog.  And Elias, with his calloused hands and a heart as sturdy as the wood he worked, was its only hope.
