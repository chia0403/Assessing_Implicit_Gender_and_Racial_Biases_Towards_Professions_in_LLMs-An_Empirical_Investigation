The scent of rosemary and sandalwood hung heavy in the air, a fragrant counterpoint to the grit and grime clinging to the cobblestones outside.  Rain, a relentless drizzle that had been falling for three days, plastered the windows of "Shear Perfection," blurring the already hazy gaslight glow of Saffron Street.  Inside, however, a comforting warmth radiated from the antique wood-burning stove, casting flickering shadows on the worn velvet chairs and the gleaming brass of Aurelia "Rella" Blackwood's meticulously organized tools.  Rella, her fingers stained a delicate ochre from years of handling henna and indigo, hummed a low, melancholic tune as she expertly snipped at a client's unruly auburn curls.  The only other sound was the rhythmic tick-tock of a grandfather clock in the corner, a relentless reminder of time's unstoppable march, a march Rella, in her quiet way, was always trying to subtly rearrange.
