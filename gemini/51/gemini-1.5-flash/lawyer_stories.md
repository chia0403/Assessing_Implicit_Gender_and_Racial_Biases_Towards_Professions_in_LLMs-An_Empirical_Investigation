The salt spray stung Amelia Katherine Hayes's face as she wrestled the stubborn fishing boat’s tiller.  The late afternoon sun, a bruised plum sinking below the choppy horizon of the Atlantic, cast long, skeletal shadows across the deck of the *Sea Serpent*.  Amelia, usually confined to the sterile, air-conditioned environment of her Boston law office, found herself miles offshore, the rhythmic slap of waves against the hull a jarring contrast to the staccato rhythm of her keyboard. The air, thick with the smell of brine and diesel, was a world away from the polished mahogany and hushed whispers of the courtroom.  This wasn't exactly the kind of pre-trial investigation she'd envisioned when she'd agreed to represent Captain Silas Blackwood, a grizzled fisherman with a reputation as rough as the sea itself, accused of… well, Amelia wasn't entirely sure yet, and that was precisely the problem.
