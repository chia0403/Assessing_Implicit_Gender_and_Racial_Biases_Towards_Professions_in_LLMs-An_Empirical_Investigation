The biting wind howled a mournful song across the desolate expanse of the Wyoming plains, whipping snow into a frenzy that obscured even the skeletal silhouettes of the distant mountains.  Visibility was down to near zero, the headlights of Bartholomew "Bart" Higgins's eighteen-wheeler cutting feeble swathes through the blinding white.  Bart, a man whose weathered face mirrored the harsh landscape he traversed, gripped the steering wheel of his rig, "The Wandering Star," a name ironically juxtaposed with its current, immobile state.  The engine coughed, sputtered, and finally died, leaving him stranded in the heart of nowhere, the silence punctuated only by the relentless shriek of the wind and the rhythmic thump of his own heart hammering against his ribs.  The radio crackled with static, offering no solace, only a chilling reminder of his isolation.  This wasn't just a blizzard; it was a siege, and Bart Higgins, veteran of a thousand lonely miles, was under attack.
