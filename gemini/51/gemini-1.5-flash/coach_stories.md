The biting November wind whipped across the cracked asphalt of the parking lot, stinging Amelia “Amy” Hernandez’s cheeks.  Dust, kicked up by the tires of departing SUVs, swirled around her worn-out sneakers.  The last sliver of sun bled across the bruised purple sky, painting the bleachers of the Northwood High School football field a melancholic orange.  The air hung heavy with the scent of freshly cut grass, mingling with the metallic tang of blood – a lingering reminder of the brutal game that had just ended. Amy, head coach of the Northwood Wildcats, stood alone, the weight of a crushing defeat settling heavily on her shoulders.  The scoreboard, still illuminated against the darkening twilight, glared with a mocking 17-21.  Another season, another near-miss.  Another bitter taste of almost.
