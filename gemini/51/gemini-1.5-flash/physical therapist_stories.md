The salt spray stung Dr. Eleanor Vance's face as she wrestled the stubborn fishing net free from the barnacle-encrusted pilings.  The late afternoon sun cast long shadows across the weathered planks of the wharf in Port Blossom, a tiny coastal town clinging precariously to the edge of the churning Atlantic.  The air, thick with the smell of brine and decaying seaweed, was surprisingly warm for October, a deceptive calm before the inevitable onslaught of winter storms.  Eleanor, her usually pristine white scrubs now speckled with mud and seaweed, grunted with exertion.  This wasn't exactly the kind of rehabilitation she'd envisioned when she’d left the bustling city for the quiet solitude of Port Blossom – but then, nothing in Port Blossom was exactly what she’d expected.
