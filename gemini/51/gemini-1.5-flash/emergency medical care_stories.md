The biting November wind howled a mournful dirge across the desolate expanse of the salt marshes, whipping Amelia “Mia” Hernandez’s dark hair across her face as she wrestled the heavy EMT bag from the back of the battered ambulance.  The air, thick with the smell of brine and decaying seaweed, stung her nostrils and tasted of impending rain.  The single, flickering sodium-vapor streetlight of the tiny, almost forgotten village of Oakhaven cast long, skeletal shadows that danced with the swaying reeds.  This wasn't the glamorous, fast-paced paramedic life she’d envisioned in med school;  Oakhaven, population 312, mostly consisted of retired fishermen and stubborn, weather-beaten farmers who preferred a shot of whiskey to an IV drip.  But tonight, the quiet solitude was broken by the desperate wail of a siren, a sound that, even in this sleepy corner of the world, meant only one thing: trouble.
