The air hung thick with the scent of frying onions and simmering resentment, a perfume only Amelia Hernandez, server extraordinaire at the Salty Siren Seafood Shack, could truly appreciate.  Amelia, with her perpetually damp forehead and a smile practiced to the point of near-paralysis, navigated the cramped, sawdust-strewn floor with the grace of a seasoned tightrope walker.  The Salty Siren, a ramshackle building clinging precariously to the edge of the perpetually foggy Pacific Coast Highway, was not known for its ambiance, but rather its generous portions of greasy, gloriously unhealthy seafood and its equally generous supply of disgruntled customers. Tonight, the latter was particularly abundant.  Rain lashed against the corrugated iron roof, mimicking the rhythmic clatter of cutlery and the sporadic shouts from the kitchen where Chef "Razor" Ramon was once again engaged in a culinary battle with a recalcitrant crab.
