The mahogany desk, polished to a mirror sheen, reflected Amelia Katherine Holloway's grim expression back at her.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of her 47th-floor office, blurring the already hazy cityscape of Hong Kong into a watercolour wash of grey and black.  The air, thick with the scent of expensive jasmine incense and the underlying tang of ozone, did little to soothe the simmering tension that coiled in Amelia's gut.  Below, the neon chaos of the city pulsed with a frantic energy that mirrored the turmoil within her.  Tonight, the stakes were higher than ever.  The future of Holloway & Sons, a legacy stretching back three generations, hung precariously in the balance.
