The rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of Dr. Eleanor Vance's office, a relentless percussion accompanying the rhythmic ticking of the grandfather clock in the corner.  It was the kind of downpour that swallowed the small coastal town of Port Blossom whole, leaving only the muted glow of streetlights to pierce the oppressive gloom.  The scent of salt and petrichor hung heavy in the air, a familiar fragrance that usually soothed Eleanor, but tonight, it did little to quell the nervous energy that thrummed beneath her skin.  Her worn leather armchair creaked slightly as she shifted, the only sound louder than the drumming rain being the frantic scratching of a pen against her notepad, a list of case notes – names, anxieties, fractured lives – blurring under the dim light of her desk lamp.  Tonight, however, one name stood out, stark and unsettling against the others:  Elias Thorne.
