Professor Augustine Blackwood adjusted his spectacles, the faint scent of old paper and pipe tobacco clinging to the tweed of his elbow patches. Rain lashed against the gothic windows of his study, a relentless drumming that mirrored the tempest brewing within him.  The flickering gaslight cast long, dancing shadows across the meticulously organized chaos of his bookshelves, each crammed with volumes on arcane subjects: forgotten languages, alchemical texts, and the unsettlingly detailed histories of obscure cults.  Outside, the wind howled a mournful dirge through the ancient oaks surrounding Blackwood Manor, its skeletal branches clawing at the stormy sky.  This night, however, felt different, heavier with an unspoken dread that even the professor's decades spent immersed in the shadows of forgotten lore couldn't quite explain.
