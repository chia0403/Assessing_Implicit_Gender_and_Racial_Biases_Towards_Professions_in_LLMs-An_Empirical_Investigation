The rasping cough of the Caterpillar D11T echoed through the pre-dawn chill, a sound as familiar to Amelia “Amy” Hernandez as her own heartbeat.  Dust, the fine, ochre powder of the Arizona desert, swirled around the massive machine like a phantom’s breath, clinging to the already grime-coated treads and settling on the visor of Amy’s hardhat.  The sun, a bruised plum on the horizon, cast long, skeletal shadows from the towering mesas that ringed the open-pit copper mine.  This was her kingdom, a landscape sculpted by dynamite and steel, a kingdom she ruled from the high throne of her earth-moving behemoth.  The air hung thick with the scent of diesel, burnt copper, and the metallic tang of sweat.  Another day, another mountain to move.
