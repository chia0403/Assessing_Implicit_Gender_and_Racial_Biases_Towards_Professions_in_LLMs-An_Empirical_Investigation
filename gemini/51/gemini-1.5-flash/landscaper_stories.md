The dawn broke over Willow Creek, painting the dew-kissed grass a shimmering emerald.  A thin mist clung to the valley, obscuring the distant peaks of the Cascade Mountains, leaving only their shadowed silhouettes visible against the brightening sky.  This was Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s favourite time of day.  The quiet before the clamor of the town began, before the relentless summer sun baked the earth hard and dry.  Amelia, owner and sole operator of “Hawthorne’s Green Thumb,” hummed a cheerful tune as she unlocked the back of her battered pickup truck, the scent of damp soil and freshly cut roses already clinging to her worn denim overalls.  Today’s task: the sprawling, neglected garden of the notoriously eccentric Mrs. Periwinkle.  Amelia adjusted her straw hat and smiled, a thrill of anticipation, mixed with a healthy dose of apprehension, dancing in her heart.
