The biting November wind whipped Dr. Eleanor Vance’s auburn hair across her face as she hurried down the rain-slicked cobblestones of Beacon Hill.  Gas lamps flickered, casting long, skeletal shadows that danced with the reflections in the puddles.  The air, thick with the scent of damp earth and woodsmoke, carried a faint undercurrent of something else… something metallic and unsettling.  Eleanor, bundled in a thick wool coat despite the relative mildness of the Boston evening, tugged her scarf tighter around her neck, her breath misting in the frigid air.  The imposing brick facade of the Eliot House, a crumbling yet undeniably grand Victorian mansion perched precariously on the edge of a steep incline, loomed before her.  Inside, amongst the ornate woodwork and dusty tapestries, waited her newest patient, a man whose shadowed reputation preceded him like a chilling premonition.  And Eleanor, despite her years of experience, felt a prickle of unease she couldn’t quite explain.
