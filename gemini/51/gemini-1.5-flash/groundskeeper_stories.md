The pre-dawn chill clung to Elias Thorne like a damp shroud as he pushed the rusty gates of Blackwood Manor open.  A fine mist, clinging to the ancient oaks that guarded the sprawling estate, muted the already muted colours of the early morning.  Each drop of dew, clinging to the impossibly green lawn, seemed to gleam with an unnatural luminescence in the weak light.  Elias, his breath puffing out in ghostly white clouds, adjusted the worn leather strap of his ancient shears, the metal cold against his calloused palm.  Blackwood Manor wasn't just a house; it was a character in itself, a brooding gothic behemoth that seemed to suck the very light from the sky, a place where secrets were buried deeper than its extensive, meticulously-maintained grounds. And Elias Thorne, its groundskeeper for the past twenty years, knew more of those secrets than anyone dared to imagine.
