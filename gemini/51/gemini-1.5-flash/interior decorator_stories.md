The chipped paint on the windowsill, the colour a faded, almost apologetic rose, mirrored the state of  Seraphina “Sera” Beaumont’s soul. Rain lashed against the panes of the Victorian bay window, blurring the already indistinct view of the overgrown garden below.  Sera, renowned for transforming drab spaces into breathtaking havens, felt utterly and completely defeated.  This wasn't some neglected rental property, or a client's hastily-abandoned renovation project; this was *her* house.  Her sanctuary. And it had become a battlefield of discarded fabrics, half-empty paint cans, and the lingering scent of despair, all culminating in a breathtakingly ugly testament to her recent, spectacularly public, professional failure.
