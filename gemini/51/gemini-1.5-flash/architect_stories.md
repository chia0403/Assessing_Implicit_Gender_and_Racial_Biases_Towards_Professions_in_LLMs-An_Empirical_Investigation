The biting November wind whipped Amelia Hernandez’s auburn hair across her face as she surveyed the skeletal remains of the Blackwood Manor.  Rain, relentless and grey, hammered against her oilskin coat, blurring the already indistinct outlines of the crumbling Victorian mansion.  The skeletal framework of what was once a grand estate, now clawed at the bruised sky like a decaying hand reaching for salvation.  Amelia, a celebrated architect known for her sensitive restorations of historically significant buildings, shivered, not entirely from the cold.  This project, Blackwood Manor, felt different.  It felt… haunted.  The air hung heavy with a palpable sense of sorrow, a silent scream trapped within the decaying brick and mortar.  She pulled her collar higher, the damp chill seeping into her bones, mirroring the unsettling chill that had settled deep within her heart since she'd accepted the commission.
