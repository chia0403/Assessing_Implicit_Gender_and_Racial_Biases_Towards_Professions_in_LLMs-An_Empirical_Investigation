The fluorescent lights of Mildred McMillan’s tax preparation office hummed a monotonous tune, a counterpoint to the frantic tapping of her fingers on the keyboard.  Mildred, a woman whose meticulous nature was only surpassed by her impressive collection of vintage ballpoint pens, wrestled with a particularly thorny Schedule C.  Outside, the February wind howled a mournful dirge through the cracked windowpane of her small, brick storefront in the quiet town of Harmony Creek, Ohio.  The air inside, however, smelled faintly of lemon polish and impending deadlines – a scent Mildred had come to associate with the annual ritual of wrestling with other people's finances.  This year, the scent was particularly potent, amplified by the looming shadow of April 15th and the mountain of paperwork still threatening to engulf her tiny desk.
