The biting November wind whipped Amelia Jean Holloway’s hair across her face, stinging her cheeks a fiery red that clashed spectacularly with the muted greys of the overcast London sky.  Rain, a fine, persistent drizzle, plastered her trench coat to her body, clinging to the worn leather like a second skin.  She pulled her collar higher, the damp chill seeping into her bones despite the layers.  Amelia hunched deeper into the shadowed alcove of a crumbling Victorian building, the chipped stone cold against her back, its dampness a stark contrast to the flickering warmth of the gaslight sputtering overhead. Across the cobbled street, the imposing façade of Blackwood Manor loomed, its gothic architecture a silent sentinel guarding secrets that Amelia Jean Holloway was determined to unearth.  Tonight, she was hunting a ghost story, but she suspected she might find something far more sinister.
