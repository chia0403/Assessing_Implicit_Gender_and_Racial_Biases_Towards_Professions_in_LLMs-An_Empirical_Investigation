The recycled air hung thick and heavy with the scent of stale coffee and anxiety, a familiar perfume to Seraphina "Sera" Moreau.  Outside, the pre-dawn sky over Anchorage, Alaska, bled from bruised purple to hesitant rose, a stark contrast to the sterile fluorescent hum of the crew lounge at Ted Stevens Anchorage International Airport.  Sera, her usually vibrant auburn hair pulled back in a practical bun, nursed a lukewarm cup of something vaguely resembling tea, her gaze fixed on the flickering departure board, its green letters announcing the imminent departure of Flight 172 to Tokyo.  The flight, a red-eye, was already notorious amongst the crew for its notoriously grumpy passengers and even grumpier first-class section.  This wouldn't be just another Tuesday; she could feel it in the icy grip of the Alaskan wind whistling through the cracks in the terminal window, and in the persistent thrum of unease deep within her own chest.
