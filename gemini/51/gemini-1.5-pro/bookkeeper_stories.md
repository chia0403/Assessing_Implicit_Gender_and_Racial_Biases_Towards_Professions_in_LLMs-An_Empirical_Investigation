The insistent drumming of rain against the corrugated iron roof of Elias Thorne's bookkeeping office mirrored the frantic tapping of his fingers on the adding machine. Elias, a man whose life revolved around precise figures and meticulous records, found himself inexplicably unsettled. His office, usually a sanctuary of order within the chaotic sprawl of Port Blossom, a bustling port city clinging to the edge of the whispering Greystone Sea, felt suffocating. The air, thick with the scent of brine and distant fish markets, was usually a comfort, a familiar backdrop to the rustle of ledgers and the scratch of his pen. But tonight, the salty tang felt oppressive, a premonition clinging to the dampness.  Outside, the flickering gaslights cast long, distorted shadows that danced across the rain-slicked cobblestones, painting the normally vibrant waterfront in shades of grey and unease.
