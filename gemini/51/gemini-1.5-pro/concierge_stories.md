Rain lashed against the arched windows of the Grand Majestic Hotel, mirroring the tempest brewing inside Beatrice Eleanor DuPont.  The lobby, usually a symphony of hushed voices and the clinking of fine china, was eerily silent save for the rhythmic swoosh of the automatic doors as a lone guest scurried in, soaked to the bone. Beatrice, perched behind her polished mahogany desk, straightened the already impeccable stack of brochures and forced a welcoming smile.  The Grand Majestic, a bastion of old-world charm nestled in the heart of Vienna, was her domain. For seven years, she had orchestrated the desires of the elite, smoothing ruffled feathers and conjuring miracles from thin air. Tonight, however, the magic felt decidedly absent.  A heavy, antique clock chimed nine times, each clang echoing the unsettling premonition that tonight, her meticulously curated world was about to be irrevocably disrupted.
