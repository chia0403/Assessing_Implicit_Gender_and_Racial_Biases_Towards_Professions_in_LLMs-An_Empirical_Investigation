The insistent Vermont drizzle plastered Amelia Hayes's auburn braid to the collar of her raincoat.  She shivered, pulling the damp fabric tighter around herself as she hurried across the slick cobblestones of Church Street in Burlington. The aroma of roasting coffee beans from Muddy Waters, mingling with the sweet scent of maple creemees from a nearby shop, usually lifted her spirits, but not today.  Today, the grey mirrored the gnawing unease in her stomach. It had been exactly one week since her last patient, Mrs. Abernathy, had vanished without a trace, leaving behind only a faint lavender scent and a half-eaten apple on the little side table in Amelia's operatory.
