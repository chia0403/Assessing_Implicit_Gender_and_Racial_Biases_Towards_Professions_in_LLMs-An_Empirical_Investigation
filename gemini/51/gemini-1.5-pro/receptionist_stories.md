The insistent chirping of the intercom sliced through the quiet hum of the fluorescent lights, announcing another arrival.  Elara Vance sighed, pushing a stray strand of auburn hair behind her ear.  It was barely past nine on a Tuesday morning, and already the lobby of the Vanderlyn Building was bustling.  Sunlight streamed in through the expansive glass doors, glinting off the polished marble floor and illuminating the enormous, abstract sculpture that dominated the center of the space.  This was Elara's domain, a kingdom of ringing phones, scheduled appointments, and the constant, low-level thrum of city life filtering in from the streets below. The Vanderlyn, a sleek, steel-and-glass monolith piercing the Chicago skyline, housed everything from high-powered law firms to tech startups, and Elara, perched behind her sleek, white reception desk, was the gatekeeper to it all.
