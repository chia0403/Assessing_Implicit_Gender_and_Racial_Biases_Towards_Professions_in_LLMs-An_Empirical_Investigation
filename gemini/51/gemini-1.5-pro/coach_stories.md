The biting November wind whipped around Amelia "Amy" Rodriguez as she surveyed the muddy, churned-up field.  Floodlights cast a sickly yellow glow over the uneven ground, illuminating the dejected slump of the Northwood High Ravens. They huddled together like shivering penguins, their breath clouding the frigid air.  Amy, her dark hair plastered to her face by a mixture of rain and sweat, clutched a clipboard to her chest, her usually vibrant green eyes dulled with a weariness that went deeper than the bone-chilling damp. This was their third consecutive loss, and the spirit of this once promising team was sinking faster than a stone in the Blackwater River that snaked its way past the edge of the playing field, separating the town of Northwood from the sprawling, indifferent metropolis beyond. The air crackled with the unspoken tension of a season slipping away, and Amy knew, with a sickening certainty, that something had to change.  And soon.
