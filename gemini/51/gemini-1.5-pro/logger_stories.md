The biting November wind whipped through the towering pines of the Deschutes National Forest, carrying the scent of damp earth and sawdust.  Frost crunched under Elijah "Lije" Blackwood's heavy boots as he trudged through the pre-dawn gloom towards the landing.  A sliver of a moon hung low in the inky sky, offering little comfort against the encroaching cold.  His breath plumed out in white puffs, momentarily obscuring the already limited view. The massive, tracked skidder, affectionately nicknamed "The Beast," sat idling, its diesel engine a rumbling counterpoint to the whispering pines. This was Lije's office, a cathedral of wood and steel nestled deep within the Oregon wilderness, and another day of wrestling giants was about to begin.
