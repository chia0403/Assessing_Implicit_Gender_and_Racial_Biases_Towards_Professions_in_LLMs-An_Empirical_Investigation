The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Amy” Hawthorne’s auburn braid across her face as she wrestled a “For Sale” sign into the frozen ground outside a dilapidated Victorian on Hemlock Lane.  Dustings of early snow clung to the gingerbread trim, highlighting its chipped paint and general air of neglect.  Hemlock Lane, nestled in the shadow of the Catskill Mountains in upstate New York, was typically picturesque, all rolling hills and vibrant foliage.  But now, in the pre-winter gloom, even the usually charming stone walls lining the lane seemed to slump with a weary sigh.  Amy, however, remained stubbornly optimistic, picturing the house restored to its former glory, a warm light glowing in the window, smoke curling invitingly from the chimney.  This house, the biggest challenge of her fledgling real estate career, had the potential to be a masterpiece, and Amy, armed with a thermos of lukewarm coffee and a fiercely determined spirit, intended to prove it.
