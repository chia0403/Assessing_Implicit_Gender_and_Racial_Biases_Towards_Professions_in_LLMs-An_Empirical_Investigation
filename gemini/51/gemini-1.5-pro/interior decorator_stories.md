The biting November wind whipped around Eleanor Vance, plastering strands of her auburn hair across her face as she wrestled a bulky sample book of Italian marble out of her vintage Porsche.  Greystone Manor loomed before her, a gothic monstrosity perched on a windswept cliff overlooking the churning Atlantic. Gargoyles leered from the rain-slicked parapets, and the ivy clinging to the stone walls seemed to writhe in the gale. Eleanor, however, remained undaunted.  This wasn't just another job; this was *the* job.  Eccentric billionaire, Caspian Thorne, had handpicked her, out of hundreds of applicants, to breathe life back into the dusty, decaying grandeur of Greystone. And while the prospect was both thrilling and utterly terrifying, Eleanor Vance wasn't one to back down from a challenge, especially not one with a rumoured seven-figure design budget.
