The biting November wind whipped around Eleanor Vance, her auburn braid snapping against her wool coat like a disgruntled serpent.  Tiny pellets of sleet stung her cheeks as she hurried across the cobbled marketplace of Port Blossom, Maine.  Salty air, thick with the scent of brine and decaying seaweed, clung to everything.  Fishing boats clanged against the docks in the harbor below, their rhythmic percussion a constant soundtrack to life in this small, weathered town. Eleanor clutched her satchel tighter, its contents – a brand new food processor and a stack of meticulously crafted meal plans – bumping against her side. Today was the grand opening of her new practice, "Nourish," nestled between a dusty antique shop and a perpetually bustling fishmonger.  A tremor of nervous excitement danced in her stomach. Port Blossom, with its diet of fried clams and lobster rolls, was a nutritional wasteland in Eleanor's eyes.  She was determined to change that, one kale smoothie at a time.
