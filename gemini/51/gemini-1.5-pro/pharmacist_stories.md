The relentless Arizona sun beat down on the dusty asphalt of Harmony Creek, a town clinging to life on the fringes of the Sonoran Desert.  Inside Harmony Creek Pharmacy, the air conditioning hummed a monotonous tune, battling the oppressive heat.  Eliza Jane Holloway, her name tag pinned neatly to her crisp white coat, meticulously counted out metformin tablets, the rhythmic click echoing in the otherwise quiet store.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of sunlight slicing through the blinds, illuminating a faded poster promoting the annual Harmony Creek Cactus Festival, a reminder of a simpler time before the pharmacy became Eliza Jane's sanctuary, and the town, her reluctant prison.
