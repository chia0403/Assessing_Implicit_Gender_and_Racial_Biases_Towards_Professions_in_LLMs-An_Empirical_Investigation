The biting November wind whipped across the manicured lawns of Eventide Manor, tugging at the brim of Elias Thorne's tweed cap.  Frost crunched beneath his worn leather boots as he surveyed his domain.  Eventide, a sprawling Victorian mansion perched on the cliffs overlooking the churning grey expanse of the North Sea, was his responsibility, his canvas, his life's work.  Twenty years Elias had spent tending to its rose gardens, sculpting its yew hedges, and coaxing life from the stubborn, salt-lashed soil. Twenty years, and the place felt more like home than the cramped cottage in the nearby village where he actually slept.  This morning, however, a strange unease settled over him.  The usual symphony of seabirds was absent, replaced by an unnerving silence. And a single, crimson rose bloomed vibrant and defiant against the frosted earth, out of season, out of place, directly beneath the master bedroom window.
