Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of Cassandra "Cassie" Moreau's 42nd-floor office, blurring the already fading twilight view of the Chicago skyline.  Each drop seemed to echo the relentless pounding in her temples. Cassie, Chief Operating Officer of Helios Technologies, pushed a stray strand of auburn hair behind her ear and stared at the spreadsheet glowing on her sleek, minimalist desk. The numbers, usually a source of comfort and control in her meticulously ordered world, swam before her eyes.  Helios Tower, a gleaming testament to the company's success, felt less like a monument to innovation and more like a gilded cage tonight. The air conditioning hummed, a low, persistent drone that amplified the oppressive silence broken only by the rhythmic drumming of the rain.  She had been working for sixteen hours straight, fueled by lukewarm coffee and the gnawing fear that everything she had built was about to crumble.
