The biting November wind howled a mournful tune around the gables of Oakhaven Manor, rattling the ancient windowpanes like skeletal fingers.  Rain lashed against the stained-glass rose window, blurring the already fading light into a murky twilight. Inside, however, the air was thick with the cloying sweetness of lilies and the acrid tang of something decidedly less pleasant – the unmistakable stench of sewage.  This was precisely where Bartholomew "Barty" Butterfield, a plumber whose calloused hands belied a surprisingly delicate touch with antique fixtures, found himself.  He wrestled with a stubborn pipe, its rusted joints protesting under his wrench, the manor’s shadowy corners seeming to press in on him like unseen guests.
