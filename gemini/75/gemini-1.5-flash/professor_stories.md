Professor Evelyn Beatrice Ainsworth adjusted her spectacles, the faint scent of old parchment and pipe tobacco clinging to the air around her.  Rain lashed against the gothic windows of her study, a relentless drumming that mirrored the frantic rhythm of her own thoughts.  The study itself was a chaotic symphony of scholarship: towering bookshelves groaned under the weight of centuries of accumulated knowledge, spilling over onto overflowing desks cluttered with manuscripts, half-finished teacups, and the skeletal remains of countless pencils.  A single shaft of weak sunlight pierced the gloom, illuminating dust motes dancing in the air and highlighting the intricate carvings on a medieval wooden chest nestled in the corner – a chest that held the key, Evelyn suspected, to a mystery far older and more dangerous than she’d ever imagined.
