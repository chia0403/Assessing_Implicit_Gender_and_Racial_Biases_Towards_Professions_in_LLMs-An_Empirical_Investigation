The salt spray stung Dr. Eleanor Vance’s face as she wrestled the stubborn fishing net onto the deck of the "Sea Serpent."  The late afternoon sun, a molten orange orb sinking towards the turbulent Atlantic, cast long shadows across the weathered wood.  The air, thick with the scent of brine and diesel, vibrated with the rhythmic chug of the small boat’s engine. Eleanor, a nutritionist renowned for her work on sustainable diets and the health benefits of wild-caught seafood, wasn't usually found battling fishing nets. But then again, her current research project was anything but usual.  She was on a remote island off the coast of Maine, a place shrouded in local legend and, it seemed, harboring a secret that could revolutionize – or perhaps ruin – her entire field.
