The scent of woodsmoke and damp wool hung heavy in the air, clinging to the cobblestones like a second skin.  Rain, a relentless drizzle that had been falling for three days, slicked the narrow, twisting lanes of Oakhaven, reflecting the flickering gaslight in shimmering streaks.  Above, the ancient, gnarled branches of the oaks that gave the town its name clawed at the bruised twilight sky.  Inside his tiny, cluttered shop, Elias Thorne, master tailor and purveyor of fine garments, meticulously stitched a button onto a richly embroidered waistcoat.  The needle, a sliver of polished steel, moved with a practiced grace that belied the weariness etched onto his lined face.  The rhythmic click-click-click of the needle against the silk was the only sound besides the relentless drumming of rain on the corrugated iron roof.
