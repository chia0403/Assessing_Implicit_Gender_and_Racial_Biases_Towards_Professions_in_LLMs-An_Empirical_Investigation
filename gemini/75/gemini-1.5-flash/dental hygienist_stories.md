The sterile scent of ozone hung heavy in the air, a familiar perfume to Seraphina “Sera” Moreau.  Fluorescent lights hummed a monotonous tune above her, reflecting off the gleaming chrome of the dental chairs arranged in a neat row.  Outside, the relentless San Francisco fog clung to the steep hills like a damp shroud, muffling the usual city clamor.  But inside Dr. Anya Sharma’s practice, nestled in the quiet, almost forgotten corner of Russian Hill, the only sounds were the rhythmic whir of the suction machine and the occasional sigh escaping Sera's lips as she meticulously polished a molar, her nimble fingers dancing across the pearly surface.  This wasn't just another Tuesday morning; today, Sera was expecting a particular patient, one who had the potential to disrupt the carefully constructed calm of her routine.
