The fluorescent lights of the Zenith Corporation hummed a monotonous tune, a counterpoint to the rhythmic tap-tap-tap of Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s perfectly manicured fingernails against the polished mahogany desk.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows overlooking the city, blurring the cityscape into a hazy watercolour of greys and blues.  Amelia, perched on her ergonomically designed chair – a testament to Zenith’s commitment to employee well-being, or perhaps just a carefully constructed image of such – sighed, the sound barely audible above the incessant drumming of the storm.  Her meticulously organized desk, usually a picture of pristine order, was littered with a slightly chaotic scattering of half-finished paperwork and a lukewarm cup of chamomile tea, a testament to the unexpectedly hectic morning.  The Zenith Corporation, despite its imposing exterior and reputation for ruthless efficiency, was currently experiencing a level of pandemonium that even Amelia, a receptionist of five years, found unsettling.
