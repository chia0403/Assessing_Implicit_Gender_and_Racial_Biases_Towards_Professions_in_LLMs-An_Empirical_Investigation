The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne's auburn hair across her face as she hurried down Elm Street, the collar of her tweed coat pulled high.  The scent of woodsmoke and damp earth hung heavy in the air, a familiar aroma that usually soothed her, but tonight, it only served to amplify the gnawing anxiety in her stomach.  Rain, a fine, persistent drizzle, plastered the already decaying leaves to the cobblestone street, reflecting the hazy glow of the gas lamps in shimmering puddles.  Amelia, an occupational therapist with a practice nestled in the heart of the quaint, if slightly gothic, town of Oakhaven, was late.  Terribly late. And the patient waiting for her wasn't just any patient; it was Mr. Silas Blackwood, a reclusive recluse known throughout Oakhaven for his eccentricities and, more recently, his increasingly erratic behavior.  The old Blackwood manor, looming large and shadowy ahead, seemed to exhale a sigh of foreboding into the already chilly night.
