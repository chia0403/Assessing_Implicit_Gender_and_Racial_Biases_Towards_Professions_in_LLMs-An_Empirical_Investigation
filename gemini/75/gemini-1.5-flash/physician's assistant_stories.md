The biting November wind whipped Amelia Hernandez’s dark hair across her face as she hurried down the cobbled street, the scent of woodsmoke and roasting chestnuts doing little to alleviate the chill that seeped into her bones.  Rain, a relentless drizzle that had been falling since dawn, plastered her already damp coat to her back.  Ahead, the gas lamps of Grimsborough flickered feebly against the encroaching gloom, their pale light barely piercing the dense fog that clung to the narrow alleyways like a shroud.  Amelia, a physician's assistant at Grimsborough General Hospital, tugged her scarf tighter, the worn leather offering scant comfort against the penetrating damp.  Tonight, however, the weather was the least of her worries.  A frantic call from the hospital had summoned her back, a call that spoke of a patient, a young boy, whose condition was deteriorating rapidly.  The urgency in the doctor’s voice had sent a shiver down her spine, a shiver far colder than the November wind.
