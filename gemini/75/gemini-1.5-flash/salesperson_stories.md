The fluorescent lights of the “Bargain Bonanza” hummed a discordant symphony, a soundtrack to Amelia “Amy” Hernandez’s increasingly frantic internal monologue.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of afternoon sunlight slicing through the grimy window, illuminating a chaotic landscape of chipped porcelain dolls, tarnished silver, and mountains of slightly-used Tupperware.  The air hung thick with the cloying sweetness of forgotten potpourri and the faint, metallic tang of disappointment – a familiar perfume in Amy’s world, the scent of unsold inventory.  Amy, a whirlwind of nervous energy encased in a crisp, slightly-too-tight, teal blazer, adjusted her name tag – "Amelia Hernandez, Sales Associate" –  for the thousandth time that day, her reflection a blurry smudge in the dusty glass.  Today was the last day of the quarterly sales contest, and Amy was, to put it mildly, behind.
