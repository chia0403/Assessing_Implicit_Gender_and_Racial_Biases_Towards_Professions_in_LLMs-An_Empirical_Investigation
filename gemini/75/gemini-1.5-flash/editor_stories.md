The chipped mug warmed Amelia “Mia” Hawthorne’s hands, the lukewarm tea doing little to soothe the icy dread that had settled in her stomach.  Rain lashed against the tall, gothic windows of her office, blurring the already indistinct cityscape of Edinburgh sprawling below.  The gargoyle perched on the windowsill seemed to leer at her, its stone face slick with moisture mirroring the sheen of sweat on her own brow.  Mia, senior editor at Blackwood & Sons, a publishing house steeped in history and, lately, in financial peril, stared at the manuscript spread across her desk.  It wasn't the words themselves that chilled her – those were captivating, a gothic thriller that promised to be a bestseller – but the handwritten note tucked inside, a single, blood-red sentence scrawled on a scrap of parchment: *“If you publish this, you die.”*
