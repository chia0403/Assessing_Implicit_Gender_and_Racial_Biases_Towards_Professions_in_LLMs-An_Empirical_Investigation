The rasping cough that shook Elias Thorne’s frame was as familiar as the smell of sun-baked earth clinging to his overalls.  Dust motes, illuminated by the weak winter sun, danced in the shafts of light piercing the gloom of his barn.  The air, thick with the scent of hay and damp wood, carried the faint, metallic tang of blood – a scent Elias knew all too well, having spent fifty-three years tending to both livestock and the stubborn, unforgiving land of the Thorne family farm, nestled deep within the whispering pines of the Blackwood Valley. The valley, usually a tapestry of vibrant green in summer, was now draped in a somber cloak of muted browns and greys, mirroring the heavy burden on Elias's heart.
