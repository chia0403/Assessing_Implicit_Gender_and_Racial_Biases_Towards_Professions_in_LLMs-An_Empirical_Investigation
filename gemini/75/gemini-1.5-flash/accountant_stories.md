The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Katherine Finch’s hands, a small comfort against the pre-dawn chill clinging to the air in her cramped, book-lined office.  Rain lashed against the single, grime-streaked window of her accountancy practice, “Finch & Figures,” a name far grander than the reality of the cramped space suggested.  The building itself, a crumbling Victorian townhouse wedged between a bustling fishmonger and a perpetually-empty solicitor’s office on Grimshaw Street, smelled faintly of brine and despair.  Amelia, however, found a certain grim satisfaction in its dilapidated charm. It was a perfect reflection of her life: meticulously organized, slightly chaotic, and stubbornly persistent in its refusal to crumble entirely.  The rhythmic tap-tap-tap of rain on the glass formed a counterpoint to the frantic ticking of the grandfather clock in the corner, its pendulum swinging with the inexorable march of deadlines.  Tonight, Amelia had a particularly daunting one: the year-end accounts for Grimshaw’s most notorious client, the enigmatic Mr. Silas Blackwood.
