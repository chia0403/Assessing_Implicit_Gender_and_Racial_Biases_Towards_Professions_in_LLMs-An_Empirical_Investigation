The greasy scent of ozone hung heavy in the air, a familiar perfume to Elias Thorne.  Rain lashed against the corrugated iron roof of the abandoned power station, each gust rattling the rusted metal like skeletal fingers.  Inside, the cavernous space was a labyrinth of decaying machinery, colossal turbines standing silent and still like forgotten gods, their once-powerful hearts now cold and inert.  Elias, his brow furrowed in concentration, traced the snaking path of a frayed wire, the beam of his headlamp cutting through the gloom, illuminating dust motes dancing in the air like tiny, ghostly sparks.  The air itself felt charged, not just with electricity, but with a palpable sense of history and impending danger. This wasn't just another job for Elias Thorne; this was a salvage operation of epic proportions, a desperate attempt to reignite a dying giant.
