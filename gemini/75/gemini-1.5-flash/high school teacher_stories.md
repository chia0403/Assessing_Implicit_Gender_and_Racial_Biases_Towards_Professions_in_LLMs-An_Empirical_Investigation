The chipped paint of the "Welcome to Northwood High" sign flaked onto the already littered asphalt like a morbid confetti, mirroring the state of Amelia Beatrice Finch's spirit.  Rain, the kind that soaked to the bone and clung like a second skin, hammered against the windshield of her ancient Corolla, the wipers struggling to keep pace with the deluge.  Northwood High, nestled in the perpetually overcast valley town of Oakhaven, Oregon, wasn't just a school; it was a soggy, grey tapestry woven with the threads of teenage angst, caffeine addiction, and the ever-present scent of stale gym socks.  This particular Monday morning, however, the familiar dampness felt heavier, the air thicker with a sense of foreboding that even Amelia, with her ten years of experience navigating the treacherous waters of adolescent education, couldn't quite shake.
