The chipped porcelain sink, stained a permanent tea-rose pink from years of spilled Earl Grey, felt cold against Elara Genevieve Worthington's knuckles.  Dawn, a bruised purple and hesitant orange, crept through the gaps in the heavy velvet curtains of Blackwood Manor, painting stripes across the dust motes dancing in the air.  The silence, usually a comforting blanket, felt heavy this morning, pregnant with an unspoken tension Elara had learned to recognize over her fifteen years as housekeeper.  The air itself hummed with a low thrum, a disquiet that prickled at the edges of her normally unflappable composure.  Blackwood Manor, a gothic behemoth perched precariously on the windswept cliffs overlooking the churning grey sea, held secrets, and this morning, it felt like those secrets were about to spill.
