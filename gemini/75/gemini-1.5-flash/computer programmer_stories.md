The stale air of the PixelPushers Inc. breakroom hung heavy with the scent of microwaved fish and despair.  Fluorescent lights buzzed overhead, a counterpoint to the low hum of a thousand servers churning away in the bowels of the building.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows, blurring the already indistinct cityscape of Seattle into a grey, impressionistic smear.  Amelia "Mia" Hernandez, a computer programmer with caffeine coursing through her veins and a deadline looming like a digital guillotine, stared blankly at the half-eaten tuna sandwich on her plate.  It had been three days since she’d seen sunlight, two since a proper shower, and one since she’d uttered a coherent sentence not involving debugging code or the expletive-laden description of a particularly stubborn piece of software.  Tonight, however, was different.  Tonight, the code wasn't just stubborn; it was fighting back.
