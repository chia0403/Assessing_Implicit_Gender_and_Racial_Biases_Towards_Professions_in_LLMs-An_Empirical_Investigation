The flickering gaslight cast long, skeletal shadows across the worn Persian rug, highlighting the dust motes dancing in the stagnant air.  Dr. Eleanor Vance, her dark hair pulled severely back from a face etched with the weariness of countless troubled souls, sat behind her imposing mahogany desk.  The scent of old leather and pipe tobacco, a familiar comfort in her otherwise sterile office, hung heavy in the dimly lit room. Rain lashed against the tall, gothic windows of her practice, located in a creaking, century-old building on the edge of Edinburgh's Old Town.  The rhythmic drumming against the glass was a counterpoint to the frantic scratching of her pen against the notepad, where the hastily scribbled notes from her last session threatened to spill onto the pristine white surface.  Tonight, however, the rain seemed to mirror the storm brewing within her newest patient.
