The biting November wind howled a mournful tune around the crumbling brickwork of Radio Station KZZY, its icy fingers rattling the ancient windows like skeletal knuckles.  Inside, the air was thick with the smell of ozone and stale coffee, a familiar aroma that clung to Elias Thorne, like the faint static that always seemed to hum beneath his skin.  Elias, a man whose life revolved around the precise calibration of vacuum tubes and the delicate dance of radio waves, found himself staring at the flickering oscilloscope, its green trace a chaotic heartbeat mirroring the storm raging outside.  The station's aging transmitter, a behemoth of copper and wires affectionately nicknamed "The Beast," sputtered and coughed, threatening to plunge the sleepy town of Havenwood into a silent, information-starved night.
