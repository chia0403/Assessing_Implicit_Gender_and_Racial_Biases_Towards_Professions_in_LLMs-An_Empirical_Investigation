The scent of sandalwood and chamomile hung heavy in the air, a fragrant counterpoint to the relentless drumming of the Pacific Ocean against the cliffs below.  Amelia Grace Hawthorne, her name as calming as the lavender oil she used liberally in her practice, sighed contentedly.  The sun, a molten orange orb sinking towards the horizon, cast long shadows across the polished wooden floor of her seaside studio, "Serenity Shores Massage."  The room itself was a haven of tranquility; soft, muted colours, plush towels folded into perfect rectangles, and the gentle hum of a Himalayan salt lamp creating a warm, ethereal glow.  Outside, the wind howled a mournful ballad, but within these four walls, Amelia found a peace that often eluded her in the tumultuous world beyond. Tonight, however, that peace was about to be shattered.
