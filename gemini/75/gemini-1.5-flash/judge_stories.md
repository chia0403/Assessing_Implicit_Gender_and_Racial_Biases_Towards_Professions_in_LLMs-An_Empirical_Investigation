The salt spray stung Judge Abigail Annelise Thorne's face as she wrestled the stubborn yacht door open.  The wind, a relentless, howling banshee, threatened to rip the canvas awning from its moorings and send it tumbling into the churning grey waters of the Irish Sea. Below, the craggy cliffs of the Blasket Islands clawed at the stormy sky, their silhouettes stark and unforgiving against the bruised purple horizon.  The air hung thick with the scent of brine and impending rain, a fitting backdrop for the grim business that had brought her to this remote, windswept corner of the world.  Her tailored wool coat, usually a symbol of her authority, offered little protection against the raw power of the Atlantic.  This wasn't the polished oak and hushed whispers of her London courtroom; this was a trial by wind and wave, and the defendant remained stubbornly at large.
