The fluorescent lights of the accounting firm, "Numbers & Nuggets," hummed a monotonous tune, a soundtrack to the rhythmic clicking of keyboards and the occasional frustrated sigh.  Rain lashed against the grimy windows of the building, mirroring the tempest brewing inside Amelia Blackwood, a senior accountant with a meticulous eye and a patience bordering on saintly.  Amelia, a woman whose perfectly organized desk was a testament to her unwavering dedication, stared at the spreadsheet on her monitor.  Column after column of figures swam before her, each representing a line item in the latest quarterly audit for "Evergreen Enterprises," a sprawling conglomerate whose CEO was rumored to possess a temper as volatile as the market.  Her stomach churned, a premonition of trouble clinging to her like damp wool.  Today, something felt profoundly *off*.
