The biting wind whipped across the desolate plains of the Texas panhandle, stinging Elias "The Hawk" Hernandez's cheeks.  Dust devils danced across the cracked earth, swirling crimson clouds that mirrored the anger simmering beneath the unforgiving sun.  He gripped the worn leather steering wheel of his rig, the rhythmic groan of the engine a counterpoint to the howling wind.  The year was 2042.  The shimmering mirage on the horizon, a cruel trick of the heat-hazed air, was the only visual respite in the endless expanse of cracked asphalt and parched scrub.  His rig, the "Ironclad," a custom-built rig with a unique, aerodynamic design – a testament to his relentless pursuit of efficiency – lumbered along the deserted highway.  He was on his last leg of a delivery: ten thousand pounds of salvaged palladium – a precious commodity in a world where renewable energy struggled to keep pace with demand, leaving many cities teetering on the brink of blackouts.
