The chipped Formica tabletop reflected Dr. Eleanor Vance’s tired eyes.  Rain hammered against the single pane window of her small, cluttered office, a relentless rhythm mirroring the chaotic thoughts swirling in her head.  The faded floral wallpaper, a testament to a previous tenant’s more optimistic days, seemed to mock the gloom clinging to the room.  Dust motes danced in the lone shaft of afternoon sunlight slanting across the room, illuminating the half-eaten bowl of granola and the overflowing recycling bin.  Outside, the sprawling, grey metropolis of Ashwood throbbed with a relentless energy, indifferent to the quiet storm brewing within Eleanor’s small sanctuary.  She was a clinical psychologist, specializing in trauma, but even her expertise felt inadequate tonight.  Her latest patient, a young woman named Anya Petrova, was a storm in and of herself.
