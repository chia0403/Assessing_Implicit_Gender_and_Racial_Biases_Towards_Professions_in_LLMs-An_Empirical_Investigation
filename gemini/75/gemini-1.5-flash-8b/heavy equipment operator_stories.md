The biting wind, a relentless harbinger of winter, whipped across the desolate plains of the North Dakota badlands.  Dust devils danced across the cracked earth, mimicking the frantic energy simmering beneath Amelia "Milly"  Harding’s skin.  The air, thick with the smell of diesel and pine needles, hung heavy in the cavernous space around the massive Caterpillar 992H.  Milly, her face etched with the grime of a thousand hours in the unforgiving sun, wrestled the joystick, her calloused hands finding a familiar rhythm in the machine’s heartbeat.  This was her world, this expansive landscape of sculpted canyons and eroded hills, where the only sound louder than the growl of her behemoth was the whisper of the wind.  The year was 2042.
