The rhythmic clang of wrenches, a symphony of metal on metal, filled the air of "The Rusty Wrench," a greasy haven nestled between a bustling highway and a forgotten gas station.  Dust motes danced in the afternoon sun filtering through the grime-coated windows, illuminating oily fingerprints smeared across the workbench.  This was the domain of Bartholomew "Barty" Butterfield, auto mechanic extraordinaire, or as the locals whispered, "Barty the Bolt-Breaker."  Barty, a man whose hands spoke a language richer than any textbook, was hunched over a vintage Ford Model T, its engine a silent, ancient beast needing a desperate revival.  Outside, a cacophony of honking taxis and speeding trucks offered a stark contrast to the quiet concentration in the workshop.  The air, thick with the scent of burnt rubber and gasoline, clung to Barty like a second skin.  Today, however, a different scent hung heavy in the air – the sharp tang of something…metallic.
