The fluorescent lights of Ms. Eleanor Vance's classroom hummed, a relentless counterpoint to the rhythmic tapping of her pen against the worn notebook.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of afternoon sunlight slicing through the grimy windowpane, illuminating a scattering of abandoned textbooks and half-eaten granola bars on the cluttered desk.  Outside, the familiar, muted roar of city traffic echoed through the open window, a soundtrack to the quiet desperation of a Tuesday in late October. Ms. Vance, a woman whose auburn hair was perpetually escaping its meticulously crafted bun, stared at the messy page.  A question, a seemingly simple one, stared back at her.


**Setting:**  A cramped, underfunded high school nestled in the shadow of a towering, gleaming skyscraper on the far side of a bustling urban avenue.  The school, a relic of a bygone era, had seen better days.  Cracks spider-webbed across the plaster walls, and chipped paint dulled the once-vibrant hues of the lockers.
