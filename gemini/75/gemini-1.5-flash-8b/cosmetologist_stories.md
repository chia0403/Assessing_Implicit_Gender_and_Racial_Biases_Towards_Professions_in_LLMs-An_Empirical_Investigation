Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of the salon, a relentless percussion that mirrored the frantic rhythm of Amelia "Anya"  Seraphina's heart.  The tiny, almost claustrophobic space, crammed with mismatched chairs and chipped paint, was usually a haven of gentle whispers and the soft hum of conversation. Tonight, however, the atmosphere was thick with unspoken anxieties and the faint, metallic tang of fear.  Anya, a cosmetologist with hands as skilled at sculpting beauty as they were at soothing frayed nerves, stood poised before the chipped mirror, her own reflection blurry in the flickering fluorescent lights.  The salon, nestled in the heart of the gritty, industrial district of Alderwood, was normally a bustling hive of activity, but tonight, only the rhythmic drumming of the rain and the distant rumble of a garbage truck filled the air.  Her clients, the usual mix of factory workers and office clerks, were nowhere to be found.
