The chipped porcelain sink, stained a perpetual, faded rose, reflected the swirling crimson and gold frosting that clung to Amelia Nightingale’s fingertips.  The aroma of burnt sugar hung heavy in the air, a stark contrast to the delicate floral scent clinging to the buttercream roses she’d painstakingly piped earlier.  Her tiny workshop, nestled above a bustling London fish market, was a symphony of clattering pans, the rhythmic clang of metal on metal, and the murmur of a thousand voices – a cacophony that both calmed and fueled her.  Amelia, with her perpetually messy bun and eyes that held the intensity of a thousand sunsets, felt the familiar tremor of frustration ripple through her.  This wasn’t just any cake. This was for the esteemed Lord Ashworth, a man whose reputation for fastidiousness rivaled only the sharpness of his knives.  And this damn cake... it was a disaster.
