The fluorescent hum of the design studio, "Pixel Pushers," vibrated through Elara Vance's tired bones.  The stale aroma of cheap coffee and burnt toast clung to the air, a familiar comfort in the suffocatingly creative atmosphere.  Elara, a graphic designer with the perpetually tired eyes and perpetually messy hair of a caffeine-fueled artist, hunched over her laptop, a half-eaten croissant nestled precariously beside a stack of rejected logo concepts.  Outside, the grey London sky mirrored the muted palette of her current project: a brochure for a boutique cheese shop called "Crumbs & Curds."  It was supposed to be a celebration of the tactile, the rustic charm of artisanal cheeses, but all she saw was beige and brown, a color scheme that was stubbornly refusing to sing.
