Rain hammered against the panoramic windows of the 57th floor, mirroring the relentless drumming in Elias Thorne's head.  The cityscape, usually a dazzling tapestry of lights, was shrouded in a grey, oppressive gloom.  From his perch at the expansive trading desk of Sterling & Archer, Elias surveyed the volatile market, a maelstrom of red and green tickers flashing on the multi-monitor wall.  The air, thick with the scent of burnt coffee and simmering anxiety, hung heavy, a tangible weight pressing down on the steel and glass fortress.  Outside, the city seemed to hold its breath, waiting for the storm to break, and inside, Elias knew, the storm was already raging.  He was drowning in a sea of numbers, and the only compass he had was a gut feeling that felt alarmingly unreliable.
