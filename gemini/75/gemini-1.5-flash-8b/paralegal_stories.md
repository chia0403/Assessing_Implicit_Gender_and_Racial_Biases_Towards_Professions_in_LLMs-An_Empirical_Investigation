Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of the dilapidated courthouse, a relentless drumming that mirrored the relentless pressure building inside Amelia "Amy" Cartwright.  The air in the small, windowless paralegal office hung thick with the scent of stale coffee and desperation.  Outside, the city of Alderhaven, nestled amongst the jagged peaks of the Whisper Mountains, was shrouded in a grey, oppressive gloom.  Amy, a wisp of a woman with perpetually tired eyes, hunched over a mountain of legal documents, the flickering fluorescent light casting long, dancing shadows on the peeling paint.  Her meticulously organized, yet overflowing, desk was a testament to both her dedication and the sheer volume of cases clogging the already overburdened court system.  This particular Tuesday felt like all the past Tuesdays, each one marked by the same anxiety-ridden predictability.
