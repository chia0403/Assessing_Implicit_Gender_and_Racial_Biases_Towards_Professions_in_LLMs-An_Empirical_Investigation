The scent of old money and impending doom hung thicker than the silk drapes Amelia Josephine Blackwood was attempting to wrestle into place. Dust motes danced in the weak afternoon sun slicing through the grime-caked windows of Blackwood Manor, a grand and gloriously dilapidated pile of gothic architecture perched precariously on a windswept cliff overlooking the Cornish coast. Amelia, already sweating in her perfectly tailored linen jumpsuit, let out a frustrated huff. This project, this behemoth, was threatening to be the death of her. Not literally, she hoped, though the rumors swirling around the Blackwood family were enough to give even the most seasoned interior decorator pause.
