The flickering fluorescent lights of St. Jude's Emergency Room cast a sickly yellow glow on the linoleum floor. It was 3:17 a.m., the witching hour, when the veil between sanity and chaos seemed thinnest.  The air crackled with a low hum of nervous energy, punctuated by the rhythmic beeping of monitors and the occasional muffled sob.  Eleanor Vance, RN, pushed a stray strand of auburn hair behind her ear and sighed, the exhaustion settling deep in her bones.  Another double shift. Another night wrestling with the grim reaper, patching up the broken pieces of humanity that stumbled through the ER doors. Tonight, however, the air felt different.  Heavier. Charged.  Eleanor couldn't shake the feeling that something truly unusual was about to happen.
