The biting wind whipped off Lake Superior, rattling the frosted panes of Dr. Alistair Finch's clinic and stinging Amelia Reyes' cheeks as she hurried across the icy parking lot.  December had descended upon the small town of Port Blossom, Minnesota, cloaking everything in a thick blanket of white.  The sky, a bruised purple-grey, threatened another blizzard.  Amelia tugged her teal beanie further down over her ears, the embroidered caduceus barely visible beneath the thick knit.  Inside, the waiting room of the Finch Family Clinic hummed with the low murmur of anxious coughs and the rustling of tissue paper.  The scent of antiseptic and pine disinfectant hung heavy in the air, a strangely comforting blend that Amelia associated with long days, overflowing schedules, and the quiet satisfaction of helping her patients.  This was her fourth winter in Port Blossom, a town she'd initially viewed as a temporary stepping stone, but which had slowly, stubbornly, woven its way into her heart.
