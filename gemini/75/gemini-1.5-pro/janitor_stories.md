The flickering fluorescent lights of the Zenith Bio-Research building hummed a lonely tune, their pallid glow reflecting off the gleaming, sterile floors. It was 2:17 AM, the witching hour for mops and buckets, and Elias Thorne, the night janitor, was making his rounds. Zenith Bio-Research, a gleaming tower of glass and steel piercing the perpetually overcast Seattle sky, was a place of cutting-edge science by day, but by night, it became Elias's silent kingdom.  Tonight, however, the silence was different.  It was thicker, heavier, pressing down on him like the damp Pacific air.  He could feel it in the soles of his worn work boots, a subtle vibration that resonated through the polished linoleum. Something was off.
