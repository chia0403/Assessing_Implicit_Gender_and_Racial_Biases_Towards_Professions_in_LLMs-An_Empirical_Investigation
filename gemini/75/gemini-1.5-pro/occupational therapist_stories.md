The biting wind whipped off Lake Michigan, carrying the scent of pine and damp earth and slapping against Eleanor Vance's cheeks as she hurried along the cobblestone path.  The late October sun, already low in the sky, cast long shadows across the manicured grounds of the Whispering Pines Rehabilitation Center. Nestled amidst towering pines and overlooking the steely grey water, the center appeared serene, a haven of tranquility. But Eleanor knew the currents of worry and hope that ran beneath its placid surface, the silent battles fought within its warm, wood-paneled walls.  She adjusted the strap of her bag, the weight of her patient files a familiar comfort, and pushed open the heavy oak doors, the scent of antiseptic and freshly brewed coffee welcoming her into the hushed interior.
