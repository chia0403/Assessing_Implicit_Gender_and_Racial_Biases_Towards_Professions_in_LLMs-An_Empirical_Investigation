Rain lashed against the arched windows of the Chateau de Valois, blurring the already fading light of the late November afternoon. Inside, Amelia Thorne, her auburn braid draped over one shoulder, surveyed the grand salon with a critical eye.  Dust motes danced in the weak beams filtering through the grimy panes, illuminating the faded glory of the once magnificent room.  Silk tapestries hung limp and lifeless, their intricate patterns obscured by years of neglect.  The ornate furniture, shrouded in white sheets, resembled ghostly apparitions huddled together against the encroaching gloom. Amelia, renowned throughout Paris for her ability to breathe life back into forgotten spaces, had been summoned to the Loire Valley to resurrect this crumbling chateau.  Her client, the eccentric and reclusive Comte de Valois, had given her carte blanche – and a seemingly limitless budget – to transform his ancestral home into a masterpiece worthy of its illustrious past.  The challenge, as Amelia saw it, wasn't the scale of the project, but the weight of history pressing down on every gilded frame and threadbare carpet. This wasn't just interior decoration; it was resuscitation.
