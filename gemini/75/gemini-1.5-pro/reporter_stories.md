Rain lashed against the grimy windows of The Daily Clarion, blurring the neon glow of the Lucky Clover Casino across the street.  Amelia "Mia" Rodriguez shivered, pulling her threadbare cardigan tighter around her. The air in the office hung thick with the scent of stale coffee and impending deadlines, a familiar aroma that usually invigorated her.  Tonight, however, it just added to the general sense of unease that clung to her like the damp chill.  Ashfall, Nevada, a town built on faded dreams and nickel slots, wasn't exactly a hotbed of breaking news.  But tonight, something felt different.  A low hum of anticipation, like the static charge before a desert thunderstorm, prickled the air.  Mia tapped a chipped mug, stained brown from countless refills, against her teeth, her gaze fixed on the flickering casino sign.  It was the kind of night where anything could happen.  And in Ashfall, it usually did.
