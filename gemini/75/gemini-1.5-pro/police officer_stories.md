The biting wind whipped off the East River, carrying the scent of salt and something else, something faintly metallic, that made Detective Isabella "Izzy" Diaz wrinkle her nose.  The sky above Brooklyn was the colour of a bruised plum, threatening snow that hadn't quite committed.  Yellow crime scene tape fluttered around the perimeter of Pier 17, cordoning off the area where a lone figure lay sprawled beneath a sodium vapour lamp, its sickly yellow light painting the scene in an unsettling glow.  Izzy shoved her hands deeper into the pockets of her worn leather jacket, the cold seeping through the thin fabric.  This was her third homicide in as many weeks, and the gnawing feeling in her gut told her this one was going to be particularly messy.
