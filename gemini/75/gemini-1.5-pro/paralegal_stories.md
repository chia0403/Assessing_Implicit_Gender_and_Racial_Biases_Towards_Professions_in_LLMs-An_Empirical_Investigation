Rain lashed against the windows of Amelia Reyes's tiny apartment, mimicking the tempest brewing inside her.  The flickering neon sign of O'Malley's Pub across the street cast a lurid, intermittent glow across her cluttered desk, illuminating stacks of pre-trial discovery documents and half-eaten containers of takeout.  Buffalo, New York, in late November was a brutal mistress, dispensing icy winds and a perpetual gloom that seeped into your bones. Amelia, a paralegal at the prestigious, if somewhat stuffy, law firm of Milbank, Harding & Hayes, usually found solace in the ordered chaos of legal paperwork.  Tonight, however, even the comforting familiarity of billable hours couldn't quell the gnawing unease that had settled in her stomach.  The case she was working on, the defense of a prominent real estate developer accused of bribery, had taken a dark turn.  A key witness had recanted their testimony, and Amelia suspected something, or someone, was pulling strings from the shadows.
