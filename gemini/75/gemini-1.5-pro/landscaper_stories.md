The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Milly” Hernandez’s braid across her face as she surveyed the skeletal remains of Mrs. Hawthorne’s prize-winning rose bushes.  Frost crunched under her steel-toed boots, the sound oddly satisfying in the otherwise silent pre-dawn.  Milly pulled her fleece tighter, the damp chill seeping through her layers despite the thermals. Greystone Manor, an imposing Victorian mansion perched on a bluff overlooking the churning Atlantic, looked particularly gothic in the dim light.  The sprawling grounds, usually vibrant with meticulously planned blooms and sculpted greenery, were now barren and waiting for winter’s embrace.  This was Milly’s canvas, and the coming months her challenge.  She had big plans for Mrs. Hawthorne’s spring garden, plans that involved far more than just roses.
