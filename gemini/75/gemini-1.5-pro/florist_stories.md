The bell above the door of "Petal & Sprig" jingled merrily, a counterpoint to the rhythmic snip-snip-snip of Eliza Jane Wilder's shears.  Outside, the Charleston air hung thick and sweet with the scent of jasmine and impending rain, a familiar summer symphony.  Inside, the small shop, nestled between a historic bookshop and a praline emporium on Church Street, burst with color.  Buckets overflowing with hydrangeas in shades of blush pink and cerulean blue lined the walls, while the delicate fragrance of lilies and freesia mingled with the earthy aroma of freshly watered ferns. Eliza Jane, her auburn braid draped over one shoulder, expertly coaxed a wayward stem of delphinium into place within a cascading bridal bouquet, her brow furrowed in concentration.  The shop, her sanctuary, was a haven of tranquility amidst the bustling tourist-filled streets. But that tranquility was about to be shattered.
