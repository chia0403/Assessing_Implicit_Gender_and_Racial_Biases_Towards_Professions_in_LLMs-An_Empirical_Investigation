The flickering neon sign of the "Last Chance Gas & Grub" buzzed like a trapped insect, casting a sickly green glow over Amelia Reyes's battered Ford pickup. Dust devils danced across the cracked asphalt of Highway 17, swirling tumbleweeds like miniature, frantic ballerinas.  The desolate stretch of New Mexico highway, baking under the relentless July sun, felt less like a road and more like a scar etched across the earth. Inside the truck's cab, Amelia, her brow beaded with sweat, tightened the connection on a jury-rigged antenna.  She was miles from anywhere, the closest town a shimmering mirage on the horizon, and the static crackling from her radio was the only sound besides the low hum of the engine. Her mission: to restore a vital communication link for the isolated research outpost nestled deep in the heart of the Jornada del Muerto desert. A place they called, with a certain chilling accuracy, "The Quiet Zone."
