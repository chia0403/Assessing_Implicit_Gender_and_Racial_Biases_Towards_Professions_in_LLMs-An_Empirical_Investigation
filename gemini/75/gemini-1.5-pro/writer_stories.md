The salt spray stung Amelia Iris Thorne's face as she leaned against the weathered railing of her balcony, the rhythmic crash of waves against the cliffs a constant, almost hypnotic, backdrop to her thoughts. Her cottage, perched precariously on the edge of the Cornish coastline near the tiny village of Portwenn, was a haven, a self-imposed exile from the bustling literary world of London that had both nurtured and nearly devoured her.  Grey clouds scudded across the bruised sky, mirroring the turbulent emotions churning within her.  Three months. Three months she'd been staring at a blank page, the cursor blinking mockingly on her laptop screen, the once-prolific words now trapped somewhere deep within her, refusing to be coaxed out. The deadline for her next novel, a gothic thriller titled *The Shadow of Pendragon*, loomed, a menacing presence as tangible as the approaching storm.
