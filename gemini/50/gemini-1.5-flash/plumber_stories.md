The rusty hinges of the cellar door groaned a mournful protest as Bartholomew "Barty" Butterfield heaved it open, the scent of damp earth and something vaguely metallic – possibly decaying pipe – assaulting his nostrils.  Barty, a man whose hands were perpetually stained a kaleidoscope of rust, grease, and dried-up solder, adjusted the headlamp strapped to his brow. The beam cut through the oppressive darkness of the cellar, revealing cobwebs thick as shrouds draped across ancient stone walls, and a network of pipes that looked as though they’d been installed during the reign of Queen Victoria – and possibly hadn't been touched since.  This wasn't just any basement; this was the infamous Blackwood Manor, perched precariously on the edge of Widow's Creek, a place whispered about in hushed tones by even the most seasoned plumbers in the town of Oakhaven.
