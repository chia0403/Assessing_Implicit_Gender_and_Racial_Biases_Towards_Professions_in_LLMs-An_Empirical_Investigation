The scent of sandalwood and lavender hung heavy in the air, a comforting balm against the relentless Vancouver drizzle lashing against the windowpanes of Serenity Spa.  Rain, a constant companion in this coastal city, drummed a steady rhythm against the glass, a counterpoint to the soothing music drifting from hidden speakers.  Inside, however, a different kind of storm was brewing.  Amelia Katherine Holloway, a massage therapist with hands as deft as a surgeon's and a heart as weary as an old oak, stared at the flickering candle on her massage table, its flame mirroring the turbulent emotions churning within her.  The crisp white sheets were flawlessly smooth, a stark contrast to the knot of anxiety tightening in her stomach.  Tonight, her last client before closing was anything but ordinary.
