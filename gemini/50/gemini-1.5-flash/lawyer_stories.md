The salt spray stung Amelia Hernandez's face as she wrestled the stubborn yacht door open.  The wind, a snarling beast off the Pacific, whipped her dark hair across her eyes, nearly blinding her.  Below, the churning grey water of the Monterey Bay clawed at the hull of the *Seraphina*, a luxury vessel incongruously moored amidst the jagged rocks that jutted out from the craggy coastline like skeletal fingers.  The air, thick with the scent of brine and something else – something metallic and disturbingly familiar – clung to Amelia's tailored suit, a stark contrast to the casual attire of the other people milling about on the smaller boats bobbing nearby, their faces grim with a mixture of curiosity and apprehension.  Amelia, a partner at the prestigious San Francisco firm of Blackwood & Thorne, was used to dealing with high-stakes cases, but this… this felt different. This felt personal.
