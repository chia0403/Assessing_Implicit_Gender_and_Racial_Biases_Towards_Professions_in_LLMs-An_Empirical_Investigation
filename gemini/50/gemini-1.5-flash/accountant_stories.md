The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Katherine Finch's hands, a meager comfort against the pre-dawn chill that seeped through the drafty windows of her office.  Rain lashed against the glass panes of the ten-story building, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her.  Amelia, a meticulous accountant with a reputation for unflappable precision – a reputation she was currently in danger of shattering – stared at the meticulously organized spreadsheets spread across her desk, a stark contrast to the chaotic storm raging outside and within her own heart.  The city of Manchester, usually a muted backdrop to her life of numbers, hummed with a low, anxious thrum this morning, the sounds muffled only slightly by the rhythmic drumming of the rain against the glass.  The air smelled of damp stone and impending deadlines, a scent Amelia had come to know intimately during her eleven years at Blackwood & Thorne, Chartered Accountants.  Tonight, however, the familiar scent felt suffocating, a precursor to something far more significant than a looming audit.
