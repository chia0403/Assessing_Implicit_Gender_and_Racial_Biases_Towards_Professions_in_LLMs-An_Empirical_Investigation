The chipped Formica bar top gleamed under the single, harsh fluorescent light, reflecting the weary sheen of Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s eyes.  Rain lashed against the grimy windows of the Rusty Mug, a sound almost drowned out by the rhythmic clinking of ice and the low hum of the aging refrigerator.  The air hung thick with the scent of stale beer, cheap whiskey, and desperation – a familiar perfume to Amelia, who'd spent the last decade breathing it in, night after night, behind this very bar.  Outside, the neon sign flickered intermittently, casting a weak, sickly yellow glow across the deserted alleyway.  Tonight, the only company Amelia had was the bottle of cheap bourbon she'd been nursing since closing time, and the ghosts of countless broken promises clinging to the worn mahogany wood.
