The salt spray stung Elias Thorne's face as he wrestled the stubborn oak beam into place.  He grunted, the effort drawing a bead of sweat down his temple, and wiped it away with the back of his calloused hand.  The air hung thick with the smell of brine, sawdust, and the peculiar tang of impending storm.  He was perched precariously on the scaffolding, the grey, churning sea a dizzying drop below, hammering away at the half-finished framework of the lighthouse.  The skeletal structure, a defiant silhouette against the bruised purple sky, clung to the jagged edge of Widow's Point, a treacherous finger of rock jutting out into the unforgiving Atlantic.  The wind, a relentless banshee, howled a mournful song around him, promising a night of fury.  Elias, though, remained unfazed; he was used to the tempestuous moods of both the sea and his demanding trade.
