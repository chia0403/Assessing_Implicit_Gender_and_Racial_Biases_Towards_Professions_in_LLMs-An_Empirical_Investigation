The biting wind whipped across the obsidian peaks of the Dragon's Tooth mountains, carrying with it the scent of pine and something else… something metallic and faintly acrid that pricked at Guard Captain Elias Thorne's nostrils.  Elias, his breath misting in the frigid air, tightened his grip on the worn leather of his glaive.  The wind howled a mournful dirge around the crumbling stone walls of Fort Bastion, a lonely sentinel perched precariously on a narrow ledge overlooking a chasm so deep it seemed to swallow the light.  Below, the jagged, snow-covered peaks plunged into a seemingly bottomless abyss, a landscape both breathtakingly beautiful and terrifyingly unforgiving.  For centuries, Fort Bastion had stood guard against the encroaching darkness, a silent testament to the unwavering vigilance of the Order of the Silver Shield.  Tonight, however, the silence felt heavier, more ominous than usual.  A feeling Elias couldn’t shake, a premonition as sharp and cold as the mountain wind itself, settled deep in his bones.
