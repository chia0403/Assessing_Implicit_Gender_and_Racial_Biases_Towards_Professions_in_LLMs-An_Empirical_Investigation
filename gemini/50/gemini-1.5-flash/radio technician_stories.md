The salt spray stung Amelia Grace Hawthorne's face as she wrestled the unwieldy antenna into place.  The wind, a relentless, howling beast off the churning grey expanse of the Atlantic, threatened to rip the entire contraption – her livelihood, practically – from the crumbling clifftop.  Below, the jagged rocks of Widow's Cove gnawed at the churning waves, a constant reminder of the unforgiving nature of this isolated stretch of the Cornish coast.  Amelia, perched precariously on a rusted scaffolding, tightened a bolt, her knuckles white against the cold metal.  The radio shack, a ramshackle wooden hut clinging to the cliff edge like a barnacle, was her kingdom, and the unreliable, temperamental broadcast equipment within, her subjects demanding constant attention and repair.  Tonight, however, the capricious weather was proving a more formidable adversary than any faulty capacitor.
