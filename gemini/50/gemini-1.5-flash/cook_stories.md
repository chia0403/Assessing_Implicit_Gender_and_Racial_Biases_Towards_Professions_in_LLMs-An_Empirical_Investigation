The salt-laced wind whipped Amelia "Mia" Rodriguez's dark braid across her face as she hauled a crate of glistening, emerald-green avocados onto the splintered wooden dock.  The sun, a molten orange orb sinking behind the jagged peaks of the Sierra Madre mountains, cast long shadows across the bustling Puerto Vallarta marketplace.  The air, thick with the aroma of frying fish, roasting chiles, and the sweet, cloying perfume of hibiscus flowers, hummed with the chaotic energy of a thousand conversations, bartering voices, and the rhythmic slap of waves against the hull of nearby fishing boats. Mia, her hands roughened by years of kneading dough and chopping vegetables, wiped sweat from her brow with the back of her forearm, a familiar smile playing on her lips despite the demanding day.  This was her kingdom, this vibrant, chaotic marketplace, and the scent of simmering spices was the soundtrack to her life.
