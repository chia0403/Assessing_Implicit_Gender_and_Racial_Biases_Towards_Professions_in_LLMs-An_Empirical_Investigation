The chipped mug warmed Amelia Stone's hands, the lukewarm chamomile tea doing little to soothe the icy dread creeping up her spine.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of her Soho loft, blurring the already hazy cityscape into a watercolour wash of greys and blacks.  Amelia, a graphic designer with a penchant for vintage typefaces and an aversion to corporate jargon, stared out at the storm, the city lights twinkling like malevolent eyes in the downpour.  Her usually meticulously organized workspace – a testament to her obsessive need for symmetry – was a chaotic mess of scattered sketches, crumpled briefs, and half-empty coffee cups.  The deadline loomed, a monstrous, pixelated shadow hanging over her like the storm clouds outside.  This wasn't just any deadline; this was the *Harbinger Games* rebranding, a project that could catapult her career to dizzying heights – or send it plummeting into the abyss.
