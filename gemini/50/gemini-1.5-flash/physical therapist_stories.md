The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Mia” Hernandez’s auburn hair across her face as she hurried down the cobblestone street, the scent of woodsmoke and roasting chestnuts barely cutting through the damp chill.  Rain, a fine, persistent drizzle, plastered her already-soaked coat to her back.  She pulled her scarf tighter, its worn wool offering little comfort against the penetrating cold.  This was Oakhaven, a picturesque coastal town clinging precariously to the cliffs overlooking the churning grey expanse of the Atlantic, a place of breathtaking beauty and, as Mia was increasingly discovering, equally breathtaking eccentricities.  Her small, newly-opened physiotherapy clinic, nestled amongst antique shops and tea rooms on a narrow side street called Mermaid Lane, felt a world away from the bustling city she'd left behind, a world where the strangest ailment could be a twisted ankle from a rogue cobblestone, or something far, far stranger.
