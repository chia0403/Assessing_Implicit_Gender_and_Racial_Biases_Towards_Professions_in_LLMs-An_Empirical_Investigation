The flickering neon sign of the "CyberByte Cafe" cast a lurid green glow across Elias Thorne's tired face.  Rain lashed against the grimy windows of the all-night diner, mirroring the storm brewing inside him.  Elias, a computer programmer whose caffeine intake rivaled that of a small espresso plantation, hunched over his laptop, the glow of the screen illuminating the frantic tapping of his fingers.  Outside, the city of Neo-Kyoto hummed with a discordant symphony of sirens, speeding hovercars, and the incessant chatter of a million digital conversations.  Inside, the only sound was the rhythmic click-clack of his keyboard, punctuated by the occasional frustrated sigh escaping his lips as he wrestled with a particularly stubborn line of code.  The air hung heavy with the scent of stale coffee and desperation, a familiar aroma in the life of a freelance coder chasing a deadline.
