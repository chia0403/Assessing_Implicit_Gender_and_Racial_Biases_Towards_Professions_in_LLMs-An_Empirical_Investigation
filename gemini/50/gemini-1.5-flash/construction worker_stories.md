The biting November wind whipped at Elias Thorne's already chapped lips as he wrestled a stubborn bolt on the scaffolding.  Below, the half-finished skyscraper clawed at the bruised, grey sky, a skeletal monument to ambition and concrete.  The city of Chicago sprawled around them, a concrete jungle glittering with a million defiant lights against the encroaching dusk.  Elias, his breath misting in the frigid air, could almost taste the grit and grime of the city clinging to the steel girders, a familiar flavour after twenty years spent building its ever-expanding skyline.  The rhythmic clang of steel on steel, the shouts of his fellow workers, and the distant wail of a siren formed the symphony of his life; a harsh, beautiful, and undeniably urban lullaby.
