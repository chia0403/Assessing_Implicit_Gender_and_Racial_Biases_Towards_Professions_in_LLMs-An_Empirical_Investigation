The chipped porcelain sink wept a steady stream of lukewarm water, mirroring the melancholic drip, drip, drip of Elara Vance’s weary heart.  Dust motes, disturbed by the faintest tremor in the ancient oak floorboards beneath her feet, danced in the single shaft of sunlight slicing through the grimy window of the manor's west wing.  This was Blackwood Manor, a gothic behemoth perched precariously on the cliff overlooking the churning grey expanse of the North Sea.  Its stone facade, once proud, was now scarred by time and the relentless assault of the salty wind, a perfect reflection of Elara’s own weathered spirit.  The air hung thick with the scent of damp earth, decaying wood, and the faint, lingering perfume of lilies, a stark contrast to the metallic tang of blood that had clung to the air only hours before.
