The chipped porcelain of the apothecary jars hummed a faint, discordant tune under the flickering gaslight.  Dust motes, caught in the amber glow, danced like tiny, frantic spirits above the cluttered shelves of Blackwood & Sons Pharmacy.  The air hung thick with the scent of dried herbs, sandalwood, and something else, something indefinably acrid, like burnt sugar and regret.  Behind the mahogany counter, meticulously polishing a mortar and pestle, sat Dr. Elara Vance Blackwood.  Her fingers, long and elegant, moved with the practiced grace of a concert pianist, though the melody they played was one of quiet, controlled weariness.  The year was 1902, and the shadowed corners of Blackwood & Sons held secrets older than the peeling paint on the walls.
