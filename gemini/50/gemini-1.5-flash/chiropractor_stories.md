The salt spray stung Dr. Eleanor Vance's face as she wrestled the stubborn fishing net onto the small, rocking boat.  The late afternoon sun, a bruised plum in the rapidly darkening sky, cast long shadows across the churning emerald waters of the Irish Sea.  The air, thick with the scent of brine and seaweed, carried the mournful cry of gulls overhead.  Eleanor, her usually crisp white chiropractic uniform replaced by worn jeans and a waterproof jacket, gritted her teeth against the wind.  This wasn't exactly the kind of patient intake she'd envisioned when she'd traded the sterile environment of her London practice for the rugged beauty of the remote Isle of Skye.  But then again,  Mr. Angus MacLeod's "slipped disc" sounded a whole lot more dramatic than it did on the phone.
