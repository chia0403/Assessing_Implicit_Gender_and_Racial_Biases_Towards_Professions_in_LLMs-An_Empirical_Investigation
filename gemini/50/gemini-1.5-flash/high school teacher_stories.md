The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Katherine Davies’s hands, a meager defense against the November chill that seeped through the drafty windows of her classroom.  Rain lashed against the panes of the aging Northwood High School building, a rhythmic drumming that mirrored the dull ache in her head.  Outside, the skeletal branches of the ancient oak tree clawed at the stormy sky, their silhouettes stark against the bruised purple of the twilight.  Inside, the scent of old textbooks and stale coffee hung heavy in the air, a familiar, if somewhat depressing, aroma that had become the soundtrack to Amelia’s life for the past ten years.  Tonight, however, the usual quiet solitude of her after-school grading was broken only by the mournful wail of the wind and the frantic scratching of her pen across the sea of red-marked essays piled before her.  The essays, on Shakespeare's Hamlet, seemed to mock her with their youthful exuberance, a stark contrast to the weariness that settled deep within her bones.
