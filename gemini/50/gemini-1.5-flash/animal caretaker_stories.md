The biting November wind whipped Amelia Hernandez’s hair across her face as she wrestled a recalcitrant bale of hay.  It smelled, as always, of sun-baked grass and faint, earthy sweetness, a scent that usually calmed her.  But today, the familiar aroma only served to amplify the gnawing anxiety churning in her stomach.  The wind howled a mournful tune across the sprawling expanse of Whispering Pines Animal Sanctuary, a symphony of rustling leaves and the distant, mournful bleating of a goat that felt, in Amelia's gut, uncannily like her own inner turmoil.  Whispering Pines, nestled in the foothills of the Cascade Mountains, was a haven for injured and abandoned animals, a sprawling collection of barns, enclosures, and rambling woodland trails – a place Amelia had dedicated her life to, a sanctuary she now feared was about to be irrevocably broken.
