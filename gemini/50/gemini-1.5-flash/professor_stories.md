The biting November wind whipped Professor Eleanor Vance's auburn hair across her face as she hurried across the quad, the ancient stone buildings of Blackwood University looming darkly against the bruised purple sky.  Rain, a relentless drizzle that had been falling for days, plastered her tweed coat to her thin frame, and the damp chill seeped into her very bones.  The scent of woodsmoke and damp earth hung heavy in the air, a stark contrast to the musty aroma of aging parchment and forgotten knowledge that usually clung to her like a second skin.  Tonight, however, the usual comforting familiarity of Blackwood felt oppressive, mirroring the unsettling weight on her chest.  She was late, terribly late, and the reason for her tardiness was far more disturbing than a missed lecture.
