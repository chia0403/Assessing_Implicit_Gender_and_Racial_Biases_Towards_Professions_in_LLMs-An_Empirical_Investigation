The salt spray kissed Amelia Grace Hawthorne's face as she wrestled the stubborn For Sale sign into the wind-whipped sand.  Amelia, or "Amy" to her clients – though she preferred the formality in business – adjusted her crisp white linen pantsuit, the fabric clinging slightly to her damp skin.  The relentless Atlantic wind howled a mournful tune around her, whipping her auburn hair into a chaotic halo.  Before her stretched the breathtaking, yet unforgiving, coastline of Cape Cod, specifically the windswept cliffs of Eastham.  The sky, a bruised purple and threatening grey, mirrored the turbulent market she'd been navigating for the past six months.  This particular property, a crumbling Victorian perched precariously on the edge of the cliff, was her latest, and possibly her most challenging, listing yet.  The air hung thick with the scent of brine and the faint, almost imperceptible, aroma of decay emanating from the house itself.
