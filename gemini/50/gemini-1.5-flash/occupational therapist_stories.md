The salt spray stung Dr. Eleanor Vance’s face as the ferry churned its way across the churning grey expanse of the Puget Sound.  Rain, a relentless drizzle that had clung to Seattle all morning, now lashed against the windows of the small, overcrowded vessel, blurring the already indistinct outlines of the San Juan Islands.  Eleanor, bundled in a thick wool coat despite the relatively mild October weather, gripped her battered canvas tote bag, its contents – a selection of therapeutic putty, a brightly coloured set of building blocks, and a well-worn copy of "The Sensory Processing Disorder Handbook" – a silent testament to her profession.  Her destination: Lopez Island, a speck of emerald green nestled amidst the archipelago, and the site of her latest, and arguably most challenging, case.  The air hung heavy with the scent of brine and impending storms, mirroring the turmoil Eleanor felt churning within her own stomach.
