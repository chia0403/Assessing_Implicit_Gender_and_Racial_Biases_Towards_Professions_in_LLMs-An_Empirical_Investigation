The biting November wind whipped Elias Thorne’s thick, salt-and-pepper beard across his face as he clung to the steeply pitched roof of the Victorian mansion.  Below, the manicured lawns of Blackwood Manor stretched out like a meticulously woven emerald carpet, dotted with the skeletal remains of summer’s flowerbeds.  Above, a bruised purple sky threatened further rain, mirroring the stormy expression etched onto Elias’s weathered features.  He wasn't afraid of heights – not after thirty years spent battling the elements atop countless rooftops – but the precariousness of this particular job, perched precariously on the edge of a crumbling chimney stack, was even making him, Elias Thorne, feel a prickle of unease. The air tasted of damp earth and decaying leaves, a potent perfume of impending winter.
