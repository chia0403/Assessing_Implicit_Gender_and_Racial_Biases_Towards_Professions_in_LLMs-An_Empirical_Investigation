The humid air hung heavy, thick with the scent of sweat and ozone, clinging to Amelia “Amy” Hernandez like a second skin.  Amy, a whirlwind of tightly-braided brown hair and a perpetually sun-kissed complexion, surveyed her class – a kaleidoscope of huffing, puffing bodies glistening under the harsh fluorescent lights of the "Body Sculpt" gym.  The mirrored walls reflected a chaotic ballet of limbs, each participant grappling with a different stage of exhaustion during their grueling 6 am Zumba session.  Outside, the pre-dawn Los Angeles sky was still a bruised purple, hinting at the fiery sunrise soon to come, a stark contrast to the controlled chaos unfolding within the four walls of Body Sculpt.  The rhythmic throb of Latin beats pulsed from the oversized speakers, a relentless heartbeat driving the class towards its final, fiery crescendo.
