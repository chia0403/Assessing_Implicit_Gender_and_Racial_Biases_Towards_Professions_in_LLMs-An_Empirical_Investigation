The fluorescent lights of Northwood High hummed a monotonous tune, a counterpoint to the frantic tapping of Dr. Eleanor Vance’s pen against her notepad.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of her office, blurring the already muted colours of the autumnal landscape outside.  The air hung heavy with the scent of damp wool and something indefinably teenage – a potent cocktail of Axe body spray, stale coffee, and the faint, underlying aroma of desperation.  Eleanor, a woman whose meticulously-styled auburn bob and crisp, cream-coloured cardigan hinted at a controlled composure that belied the chaotic currents swirling within the school’s walls, sighed.  Another referral.  This one, a scrawled note from Ms. Periwinkle, the notoriously frazzled English teacher, simply read: "Jason Davies.  Urgent.  See attached."  The attached was a crumpled piece of paper, smeared with ink and bearing a single, chillingly artistic rendering of a spider's web.
