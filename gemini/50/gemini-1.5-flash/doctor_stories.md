The biting wind whipped Dr. Eleanor Vance’s auburn braid across her face as she wrestled the heavy oak door of Blackwood Manor open.  The year was 1923, and a late November storm raged around the imposing gothic structure, its shadowy silhouette barely visible through the sheets of driving rain.  Blackwood Manor, perched precariously on a windswept cliff overlooking the churning grey expanse of the North Sea, seemed to breathe a palpable chill, a chill that had nothing to do with the weather.  The air hung heavy with the scent of damp earth and something else… something faintly metallic, like old blood.  Eleanor shivered, pulling her worn wool coat tighter around her as she stepped inside, the heavy door groaning shut behind her like a mournful sigh.
