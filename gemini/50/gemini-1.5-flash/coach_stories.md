The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Amy” Hernandez’s auburn braid across her face as she surveyed the windswept lacrosse field.  Fallen leaves, the color of dried blood, clung stubbornly to the frosted grass, a stark contrast to the vibrant green of the recently-installed synthetic turf that lay mostly unused under a sky the color of pewter.  The air, thick with the scent of damp earth and decaying vegetation, held the faint, lingering aroma of woodsmoke from the distant houses nestled along the edge of the sprawling Northwood High School campus.  This wasn't the idyllic setting Amy had envisioned when she'd accepted the head coaching position, but then again, nothing about Northwood High – or its perpetually underperforming lacrosse team – had been what she expected.
