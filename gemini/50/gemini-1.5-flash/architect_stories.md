The salt spray stung Amelia Hernandez’s face as she leaned against the railing of the ferry, the wind whipping her dark hair across her cheek.  The late afternoon sun, a bruised plum in the rapidly darkening sky, cast long shadows across the churning grey waters of Puget Sound.  Before her, the silhouette of Bainbridge Island rose like a slumbering giant, its forested hills shrouded in mist. Amelia, a renowned architect known for her daring designs and even more daring use of reclaimed materials,  wasn't here for the scenic beauty, though she certainly appreciated it. She was here for a derelict Victorian mansion perched precariously on the island's cliffs, a project that threatened to consume her – both professionally and personally.  The mansion, known locally as Blackwood Manor, whispered promises of both glory and ruin, and Amelia Hernandez, with her sketchbook tucked securely in her bag, was ready to listen.
