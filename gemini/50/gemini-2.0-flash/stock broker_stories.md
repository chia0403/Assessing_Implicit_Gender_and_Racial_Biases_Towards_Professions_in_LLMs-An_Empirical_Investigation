The salt spray kissed the rain-streaked windows of Adrian Michael Davies' corner office on the 47th floor of the Liberty Mutual Tower. Outside, the pre-dawn sky was a bruised purple, threatening another day of relentless downpour over Boston. Inside, Adrian, clad in a perfectly tailored, but decidedly rumpled, navy suit, stared out at the harbor, his fingers drumming a frantic tattoo on the mahogany desk. He hadn't slept properly in three nights, his mind a relentless ticker tape of fluctuating prices, rumored mergers, and the gnawing, persistent fear that he was about to lose everything. He was thirty-five, successful by most metrics, Head of Equity Trading at Hawthorne & Bell, and teetering on the precipice of utter ruin.
