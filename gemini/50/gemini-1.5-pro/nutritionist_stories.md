The crisp October air, scented with woodsmoke and decaying leaves, whipped around Dr. Eleanor Vance as she hurried across the cobbled courtyard of Ashworth Manor.  Her breath plumed before her, a ghostly white against the backdrop of the imposing Gothic mansion.  Gargoyles perched menacingly on the parapets, their stone faces seeming to sneer down at her. Ashworth Manor, nestled deep in the Shropshire countryside, was a place of unsettling beauty. Officially, it was a wellness retreat; unofficially, it was where London’s elite came to discreetly shed pounds, detox from their excesses, and escape the prying eyes of the paparazzi. Eleanor, newly appointed as the manor's head nutritionist, clutched her briefcase tighter, a knot of apprehension tightening in her stomach. She'd dealt with demanding clients before, but something about this place, with its shrouded history and oppressive atmosphere, felt different.  It felt… hungry.
