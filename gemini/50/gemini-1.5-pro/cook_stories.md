The clatter of pots and the sizzle of garlic filled the cramped galley kitchen of the *Wanderlust*, a tramp freighter smelling perpetually of salt, diesel, and whatever spice Elias Thorne happened to be experimenting with that day.  Elias, a man built like a stocky oak tree with a face weathered by sun and sea spray, hummed a jaunty sea shanty as he wrestled a recalcitrant pot of bouillabaisse.  The *Wanderlust* was currently chugging its way through the sapphire waters of the Aegean Sea, halfway between Santorini and Crete, the midday sun beating down on the rusty deck above.  Below, in the sweltering heat of the galley, Elias was creating a culinary miracle, transforming humble ingredients into a feast fit for Poseidon himself.
