The biting November wind whipped Amelia Reyes's auburn braid across her face as she hurried down Bleecker Street, the scent of woodsmoke and roasting chestnuts clinging to the frigid air.  Her burgundy satchel, overflowing with patient files and sugar-free granola bar samples, thumped against her hip in time with her brisk steps. Amelia, a Registered Dietician with a penchant for sensible shoes and an even stronger one for helping people achieve optimal health, was late.  Not disastrously late, but late enough that Mrs. Periwinkle, her 4:00 appointment and a woman with the patience of a starving grizzly bear, would likely greet her with a glare that could curdle almond milk.  Amelia’s office, nestled above a quirky vintage bookstore in the heart of Greenwich Village, was a haven of nutritional charts, motivational posters featuring anthropomorphic vegetables, and the faint, ever-present aroma of peppermint tea.  It was a space she’d meticulously curated, a testament to her dedication to well-being, a dedication that was about to be severely tested by the formidable Mrs. Periwinkle.
