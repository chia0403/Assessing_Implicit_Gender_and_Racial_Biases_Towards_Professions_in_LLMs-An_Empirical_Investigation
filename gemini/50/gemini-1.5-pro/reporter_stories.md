Rain lashed against the grimy windows of The Sentinel's office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside Amelia “Mia” Catherine Rodriguez.  Fluorescent lights buzzed overhead, casting a sickly yellow glow on the cluttered desks and overflowing ashtrays that littered the newsroom. Cigarette smoke hung thick in the air, mingling with the bitter aroma of stale coffee.  Ashfall, Ohio, a once-thriving steel town, now rusted and crumbling like a forgotten relic, was Mia's beat, her prison, and, strangely, her home.  Tonight, however, it felt more like a cage.  The clock ticked relentlessly towards deadline, and Mia, hunched over her battered typewriter, was staring at a blank page, the blinking cursor mocking her writer's block.  The assignment: a puff piece on the upcoming Founder's Day festival, a desperate attempt by the town council to inject some life into the dying community.  But Mia couldn't bring herself to care about parades and pie-eating contests when a much darker story was clawing at her throat, a story that threatened to unravel the carefully constructed facade of Ashfall, a story that no one wanted her to tell.
