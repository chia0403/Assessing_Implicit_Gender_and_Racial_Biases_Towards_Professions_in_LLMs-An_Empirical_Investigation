The biting November wind whipped around Eleanor Vance, making the loose strands of her auburn hair dance like frantic marionettes against her cheek.  She tucked them behind her ear, a familiar gesture, and pulled her worn tweed coat tighter around herself as she climbed the steps to the sprawling Victorian house.  Number 47, Hemlock Lane.  A shiver, unconnected to the cold, traced its way down her spine.  The house loomed over her, a gothic behemoth of peeling paint and stained-glass windows depicting scenes far too macabre for a crisp autumn afternoon. This was the Whittaker Estate, and within its shadowed walls resided her newest, and most enigmatic, patient.
