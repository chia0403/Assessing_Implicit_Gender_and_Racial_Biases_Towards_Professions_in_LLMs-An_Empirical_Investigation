The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Amy” Reyes’s hair across her face as she hurried across the cobblestone courtyard of the historic Eldridge Estate.  Tucked away in the rolling hills of the Hudson Valley, the estate, once a grand manor, was now the Serenity Springs Rehabilitation Center, a place where the wealthy and influential came to recover from everything from skiing accidents to debilitating strokes.  Amy, her breath puffing white clouds in the frigid air, clutched her clipboard tighter, the crisp pages rustling inside.  This morning’s patient was notoriously demanding, a former CEO known for his iron fist and even less forgiving temper.  Amy adjusted the strap of her bag, the weight of her responsibility settling heavy on her shoulders as the imposing oak doors of the main building loomed before her.  The day, she knew, was about to begin, and with it, another battle against pain, frustration, and the ever-present shadow of the past that clung to the Eldridge Estate like the clinging ivy on its weathered stone walls.
