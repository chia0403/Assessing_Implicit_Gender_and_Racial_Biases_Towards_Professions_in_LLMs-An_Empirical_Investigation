The flickering gaslights of the Moulin Rouge cast a hazy glow over the swirling cigarette smoke and the expectant faces below.  Rain lashed against the arched windows, a staccato rhythm against the rising hum of the crowd.  Inside, the air crackled with anticipation. Tonight was the debut of Mademoiselle Céleste Moreau, a name whispered with a mixture of curiosity and skepticism throughout the bohemian corners of Montmartre. Céleste, her heart hammering against her ribs like a trapped bird, adjusted the emerald green feather boa draped around her shoulders.  Paris, 1899 – a city of art, love, and secrets, held its breath.
