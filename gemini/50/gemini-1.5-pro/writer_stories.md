The salt-laced wind whipped at Amelia Hayes's thick wool coat, sending strands of her auburn hair escaping from beneath her knit cap. She braced herself against the gale, her gaze fixed on the churning grey expanse of the North Sea.  The rhythmic crash of waves against the craggy cliffs of Whitby, Yorkshire, usually soothed her writer's soul, but today, it echoed the tempest brewing within her.  Perched precariously on a weathered bench overlooking the harbour, Amelia clutched a lukewarm thermos of tea, her latest manuscript, "The Lighthouse Keeper's Daughter," feeling as cold and lifeless in her hands as the pebbles scattered at her feet.  Three months she'd poured into this story, three months of stolen moments amidst the chaos of single motherhood and freelance writing gigs, and now, staring at the blinking cursor on her laptop screen, she felt nothing but a gnawing emptiness. The deadline loomed, a menacing shadow stretching across the picturesque landscape, and Amelia, for the first time in her writing career, was completely and utterly blocked.
