The rusty wrench slipped in Elias Finch's grease-stained hand, sending a bolt clattering into the murky depths of Mrs. Henderson's overflowing toilet. He sighed, the fluorescent light of the cramped bathroom illuminating the beads of sweat clinging to his forehead. It was a sweltering July afternoon in Harmony Creek, a town so small its only claim to fame was the inexplicably large population of stray cats and the annual pie-eating contest. Elias, Harmony Creek's sole plumber and a man whose physique could best be described as "plump," wasn't a fan of either. The cats, he felt, judged him.  The pies, well, they were a formidable opponent.  He fished around blindly in the porcelain abyss, muttering under his breath, “Just another Tuesday.”
