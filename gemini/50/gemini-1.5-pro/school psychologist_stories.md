The biting November wind whipped around Dr. Eleanor Vance as she hurried across the cracked asphalt of the Northwood High School parking lot. Tiny pellets of sleet stung her cheeks, and her breath puffed out in ghostly white clouds.  The wrought-iron gates of the school loomed before her, stark against the bruised purple of the pre-dawn sky.  Northwood, a sprawling brick behemoth perched on the edge of the affluent, yet strangely isolated, town of Hollow Creek, held a quiet tension this morning, a palpable stillness that hummed beneath the usual pre-bell chaos. Eleanor shivered, pulling her worn peacoat tighter. It wasn't just the cold; a sense of unease, like a low, persistent drone, had settled in her stomach since the anonymous phone call she'd received the previous night.  A whispered warning.  A cryptic message about a student.  And a chilling final sentence: "They're not who you think they are."
