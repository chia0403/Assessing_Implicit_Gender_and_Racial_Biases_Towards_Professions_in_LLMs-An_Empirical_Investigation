The biting November wind whipped around Dr. Elias Thorne as he hurried across the cobbled courtyard of St. Jude's Hospital, the gothic arches looming above him like skeletal fingers against the bruised twilight sky.  Rain, mixed with sleet, stung his face, plastering the dark strands of hair that had escaped his surgical cap to his forehead. He clutched his phone tighter, the screen illuminating the grim message he'd received moments ago: *Trauma One inbound. ETA five minutes. Multiple casualties.* Elias, already weary from a twelve-hour shift, braced himself.  St. Jude's, nestled in the heart of the ancient, labyrinthine city of Prague, had seen its share of tragedies, but something about this message, the terse urgency of it, settled a cold knot in his stomach.  He pushed through the heavy oak doors, the warmth and antiseptic smell of the hospital hitting him like a wave, a stark contrast to the chilling premonition that clung to him like a second skin.
