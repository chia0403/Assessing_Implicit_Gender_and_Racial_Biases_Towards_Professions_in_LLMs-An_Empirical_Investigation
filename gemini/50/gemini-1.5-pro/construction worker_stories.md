The biting November wind whipped through the skeletal steel beams of the half-finished skyscraper, whistling a mournful tune that echoed through the concrete canyons of downtown Chicago.  Fifty stories below, the city hummed with oblivious life, tiny yellow cabs crawling along the grid like ants.  Up here, however, there was only the wind, the groaning metal, and the steady clang of hammer against steel.  Elijah "Eli" James Blackwood, his breath clouding in the frigid air, adjusted his hard hat and tightened his grip on the I-beam, the rough metal cold against his calloused hands.  He squinted at the blueprint flapping precariously in the wind, the thin paper threatening to tear free and become another casualty of the updrafts.  This was the highest point of the Zenith Tower, the soon-to-be tallest building in the city, and Eli, despite his years of experience, felt a prickle of unease looking down at the dizzying drop. Today wasn't just another day on the job; today, they were topping out.
