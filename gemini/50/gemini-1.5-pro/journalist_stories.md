Rain lashed against the grimy windows of The Mariner's Rest, blurring the already faded "Live Music" sign painted on the brick wall opposite. Inside, the air hung thick with the smell of stale beer, damp wool, and a faint, lingering tang of fish.  Eleanor “Ellie” Vance, a journalist for the Boston Chronicle, huddled deeper into her worn peacoat, the collar pulled high to shield her from the November chill that seeped through the ill-fitting window frame.  She nervously tapped her pen against the damp, ring-stained table, its rhythmic clicking a counterpoint to the mournful wail of a distant foghorn. Saltwind, Massachusetts, a small, isolated fishing town clinging precariously to the rocky coastline of Cape Ann, was a far cry from the bustling newsroom she was accustomed to. Ellie had arrived just hours ago, the assignment shoved onto her desk with a gruff "Get down there, Vance. Find out what the hell is going on."  The "what" was the sudden, unexplained disappearance of half a dozen fishing boats and their crews over the past month, a mystery the Coast Guard seemed unable to solve.  The "where" was this desolate, windswept town, shrouded in a fog of secrets as thick as the one rolling in off the Atlantic.
