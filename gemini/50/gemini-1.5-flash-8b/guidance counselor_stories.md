Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of the Southwood Community Center, mimicking the frantic rhythm of Amelia Blackwood's thoughts.  The fluorescent lights flickered, casting an eerie yellow glow over the cramped guidance office.  Empty pizza boxes, crumpled papers, and half-filled mugs littered her desk, a testament to the relentless demands of the high school year.  Outside, the wind howled, whipping the ancient oak trees surrounding the building into a frenzy.  This wasn't just any Tuesday; it was the day before the dreaded college application deadline, and Amelia was drowning in a sea of anxious students and frantic parents.  The air itself seemed thick with the unspoken pressures of futures yet to be written, of choices that felt both monumental and utterly insignificant.  Amelia, a guidance counselor with a decade of experience at Southwood High, knew the weight of these decisions intimately. This night, however, felt heavier than the rest.
