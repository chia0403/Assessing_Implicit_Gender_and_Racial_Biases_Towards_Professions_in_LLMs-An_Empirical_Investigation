Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of the "Rusty Quill" newsstand, a rhythmic tattoo echoing the frantic beat of Amelia "Amy" Carmichael's heart.  The humid air hung heavy, thick with the smell of jasmine and something faintly metallic, like old pennies left out in the monsoon.  Amy, a freelance reporter for the *Echoes of the Mekong*, hunched over a rickety wooden table, the flickering gas lamp casting long, dancing shadows on the stacks of papers and half-eaten bowls of sticky rice.  Outside, the bustling market of Nong Khai, Thailand, had long since subsided, leaving behind a quiet hum of anticipation and the ominous drip-drip-drip of the approaching dawn.  Amy, clutching a worn leather notebook and a chipped porcelain teacup, was about to embark on a story that could either elevate her career or shatter it.  The whispers had begun. They were already a storm.
