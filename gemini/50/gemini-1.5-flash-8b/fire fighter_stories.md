The biting November wind, a relentless, icy claw, ripped at the tattered edges of the firehouse's awning.  Rain, a relentless grey curtain, plastered the grimy brick facade of Engine Company 3 to a slick, unforgiving surface.  Inside, the air hung thick with the smell of burnt coffee and the acrid, lingering scent of smoke, a familiar companion to the crew.  Sergeant Major Isabella "Izzy" Rodriguez, her uniform – a stark, charcoal grey against the muted light – already damp and clinging, paced the cramped break room.  Her boots, scuffed and worn from countless hours on unforgiving pavement, echoed faintly against the linoleum floor.  The city outside was shrouded in a blanket of gloom, a stark contrast to the flickering, ever-present flames of the city's heart.
