The biting wind whipped across the manicured lawns of Blackwood Manor, tugging at Elias Thorne's threadbare tweed jacket.  Dust motes danced in the pale October light, swirling around the sculpted topiary that formed intricate patterns in the overgrown flowerbeds.  Blackwood Manor, a gothic monstrosity perched atop a hill overlooking the town of Ashbrook, was a monument to opulent decay.  Elias, with his weathered hands and eyes that held the melancholy of countless storms, had been its silent guardian for fifteen years, a groundskeeper in a landscape far larger and more complicated than any he could fully comprehend.  His worn boots crunched on fallen leaves as he surveyed the estate, the grand stone walls whispering stories of bygone eras. Today, however, the wind carried a new sound, a faint, metallic clang echoing from the shadowed depths of the conservatory.
