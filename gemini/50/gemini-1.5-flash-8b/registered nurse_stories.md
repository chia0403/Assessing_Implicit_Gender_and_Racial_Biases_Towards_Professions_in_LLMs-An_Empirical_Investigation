The fluorescent lights of the emergency room hummed, a relentless, rhythmic drone that drowned out the panicked whispers and the rhythmic thump-thump-thump of a patient's heart monitor.  Rain lashed against the windowpanes of St. Jude's Hospital, mirroring the tempest brewing inside Amelia Grace Blackwood, a registered nurse with five years of experience navigating the treacherous waters of the ER.  Tonight, though, the waters felt particularly turbulent.  A young man, barely out of his teens, lay unconscious on a gurney, his face pale and etched with a silent scream.  A tangle of wires and tubes snaked across his chest, a grotesque testament to the frantic efforts to save him.  Amelia, her usual calm demeanor fractured, stared at the flickering ECG, her fingers tracing the erratic lines.  The storm outside seemed a distant echo to the storm raging within her.
