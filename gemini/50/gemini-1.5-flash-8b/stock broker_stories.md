The fluorescent lights of the brokerage floor hummed a monotonous lullaby, a counterpoint to the frantic clatter of keyboards and the booming voices of traders.  Rain hammered against the grimy windows of the New York Stock Exchange, mirroring the tempest brewing within Amelia "Amy" Carter.  It was a Tuesday, the kind of Tuesday that felt like the pre-dawn hours before a hurricane, the air thick with unspoken anxieties and the scent of stale coffee and impending doom.  Amy, a junior stockbroker at the prestigious firm of Sterling & Co., meticulously scanned the ticker tape, her fingers flying across the keyboard,  each keystroke a desperate attempt to predict the next move of the market.  The Dow was tumbling, seemingly unstoppable, and Amy felt like she was caught in a churning vortex, her stomach twisting with a mix of fear and mounting pressure.  The hushed conversations around her, normally filled with excited whispers of profit and loss, now carried a sharper edge, a sense of foreboding she couldn't quite place. Her mind was a kaleidoscope of numbers and graphs, of red and green bars flashing across the screen, each one a potential trap or a glimmer of salvation. Tonight, it felt like there was only one way to sleep - in a pile of spreadsheets.
