The biting November wind whipped Amelia Nightingale's apron around her ankles as she hurried down the cobbled lane, the scent of damp earth and decaying leaves clinging to the air like a shroud.  Above, the grey sky pressed down, mirroring the gloom that had settled over the little town of Oakhaven.  Amelia, owner of "Flora's Embrace," a tiny, floral shop tucked away on the edge of the whispering woods, felt a familiar knot of anxiety tightening in her stomach.  It was the day before the annual Oakhaven Harvest Festival, and the market square, usually alive with the bustling energy of the town's residents, was eerily quiet.  This year, the usual vibrant display of handcrafted pottery, woven baskets, and freshly baked bread was absent.  An unsettling stillness hung over the town, broken only by the mournful cry of a lone crow.
