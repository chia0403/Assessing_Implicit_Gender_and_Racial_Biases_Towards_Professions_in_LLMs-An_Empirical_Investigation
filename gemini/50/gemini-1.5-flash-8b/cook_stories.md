The flickering gaslight cast long, dancing shadows across the cramped kitchen of the "Golden Spoon," a greasy spoon diner tucked away on a back street of the bustling port city of Havenwood.  Rain lashed against the grimy windows, mimicking the frantic rhythm of Amelia Blackwood's chopping knife against the onion.  Steam rose from the simmering stewpot, carrying with it the faint aroma of garlic and something vaguely metallic, a lingering whisper of the day's catch.  Amelia, her face streaked with flour and grime, swore under her breath, a low rumble that barely registered above the clatter of dishes and the rhythmic splash of the nearby harbour.  She was behind schedule, as usual.  Tonight's special, a hearty seafood stew, was meant to be ready by six, but the persistent drizzle had slowed deliveries from the docks, leaving her with a mountain of ingredients and a dwindling supply of patience.
