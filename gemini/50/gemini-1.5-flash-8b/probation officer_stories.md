Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of the Probation Office, a relentless drumming that mirrored the rhythm of Amelia Hawthorne’s thoughts.  The air, thick with the smell of damp earth and stale coffee, hung heavy in the small, cramped office.  Sunlight, fractured and weak, struggled to pierce the grime-coated windows, casting long, distorted shadows across the cluttered desk.  Amelia, a probation officer for the city’s notoriously rough Eastside, stared at the file in front of her, the name "Elias Thorne" etched in sharp, stark lettering.  Elias, a 19-year-old with a history as long and complicated as the city’s alleyways, sat before her in a hazy swirl of defiance and quiet desperation.  This was their third meeting.  Her third attempt to unravel the tangled threads of his life.
