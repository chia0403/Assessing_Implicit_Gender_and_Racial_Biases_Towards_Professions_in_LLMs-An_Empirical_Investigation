Rain hammered against the grimy windows of "Pixel Pushers," a cramped, graffiti-scarred studio nestled in the back alleys of the Lower East Side.  The flickering fluorescent lights cast long, distorted shadows across Amelia "Sketch"  Ashworth's cluttered workspace.  Sketch, a graphic designer with a perpetually smudged nose and eyes that held the uncanny ability to see hidden patterns in the chaos around her, hunched over her battered laptop.  Around her, stacks of sketchbooks, overflowing bins of discarded paper, and half-finished designs cluttered every available surface.  The aroma of stale coffee and cheap instant noodles hung heavy in the air, a constant reminder of late-night deadlines and the relentless rhythm of the city that pulsed just outside the dingy windows.
