The humid air hung heavy, thick with the scent of jasmine and the unspoken anxieties of the early morning.  Sweat beaded on Amelia Nightingale's brow, not from the exertion of her morning yoga class, but from the burgeoning dread that clung to her like a second skin.  She adjusted the straps of her vibrant turquoise sports bra, the vibrant color a stark contrast to the grey gloom that seemed to press down on the rooftop patio of the luxurious "Zenith" health spa.  Amelia, a certified exercise physiologist and passionate instructor, felt a familiar knot tighten in her stomach as she surveyed the empty space around her.  This was supposed to be the launchpad for her new, revolutionary fitness program, "Inner Bloom," designed to tap into the interconnectedness of mind and body.  But the first class was a ghost town.
