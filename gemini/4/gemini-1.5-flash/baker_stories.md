The aroma of cardamom and burnt sugar hung heavy in the air, a familiar perfume to Elara Genevieve Hawthorne, but tonight, it felt cloying, almost suffocating.  Elara, flour dusting her eyelashes like a delicate snowfall, kneaded the dough with a practiced rhythm, the rhythmic thud a counterpoint to the frantic clatter of the rain against the leaded glass windows of her bakery, "The Gilded Crust."  The storm, a snarling beast of wind and water, raged outside, mirroring the turmoil brewing within her.  The Gilded Crust, nestled on a cobbled street in the ancient port town of Oakhaven, usually hummed with the comforting sounds of conversation and the cheerful clink of teacups. Tonight, however, it stood silent, a solitary beacon against the encroaching darkness, both literal and metaphorical.
