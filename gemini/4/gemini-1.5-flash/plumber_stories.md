The wrench slipped, a metallic shriek echoing in the claustrophobic darkness of the crawlspace.  Dust motes, disturbed by the tremor, danced in the single, weak beam of Bartholomew "Barty" Butterfield's headlamp.  Barty, a man whose knuckles were perpetually stained the color of rust and whose overalls were perpetually patched, muttered a string of curses under his breath.  The air hung thick and heavy with the scent of damp earth, decaying leaves, and something faintly…rotten.  He was wedged beneath the ancient oak floorboards of the Blackwood Manor, a gothic monstrosity perched precariously on a windswept cliff overlooking the churning grey expanse of the North Sea.  The manor, rumored to be haunted and cursed, was Barty's latest – and possibly his last – plumbing assignment.
