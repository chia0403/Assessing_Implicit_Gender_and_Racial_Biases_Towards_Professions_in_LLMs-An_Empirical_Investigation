The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s fingers, the lukewarm tea doing little to soothe the icy dread that had settled in her stomach.  Rain lashed against the tall, gothic windows of Blackwood & Sons, each gust a mournful sigh echoing the turmoil within her.  The office, a cavernous space dominated by towering mahogany desks and the perpetually flickering fluorescent lights, was usually a haven of organized chaos – a symphony of ringing phones and the rhythmic tap-tap-tap of keyboards.  But tonight, an unnerving silence hung in the air, broken only by the rhythmic drumming of the rain and the erratic ticking of the grandfather clock in the corner, its pendulum swinging with a relentless, almost sinister, precision.  Amelia, normally a picture of efficient composure, felt a prickle of unease crawl up her spine.  The late-night paperwork, usually a mundane chore, felt imbued with a strange significance, as if the very documents held secrets she wasn't meant to uncover.
