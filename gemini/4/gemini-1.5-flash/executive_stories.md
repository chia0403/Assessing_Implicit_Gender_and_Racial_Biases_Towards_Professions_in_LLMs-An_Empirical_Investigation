The mahogany desk, polished to a mirror sheen, reflected Amelia Katherine Hayes's sharp, tired gaze.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of her 72nd-floor office, blurring the cityscape of Hong Kong into a hazy, impressionistic painting.  Each droplet seemed to mimic the relentless pressure Amelia felt, a constant drumming against the glass walls of her meticulously constructed life.  The city, a vibrant tapestry of neon and steel, hummed with a frenetic energy that mirrored the turmoil brewing within her.  Tonight, however, the usual intoxicating buzz felt less like a symphony and more like a cacophony threatening to shatter the fragile calm she'd so painstakingly maintained as Executive Vice President of Pacific Rim Investments.  The scent of jasmine tea, usually a soothing balm, did little to ease the knot of anxiety tightening in her chest.  The late-night email, still glowing accusingly on her screen, was the final, unwelcome note in a crescendo of bad news.
