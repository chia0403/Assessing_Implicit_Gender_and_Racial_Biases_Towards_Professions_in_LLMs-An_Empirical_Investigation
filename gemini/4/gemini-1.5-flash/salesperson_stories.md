The stale scent of lukewarm coffee and desperation clung to the air in the cramped waiting room, a perfume only Amelia “Amy” Hernandez truly appreciated.  Fluorescent lights hummed a monotonous tune above her, mirroring the thrum of anxiety in her chest.  Amy, a sales representative for "GreenThumb Gardening Supplies," adjusted the ill-fitting polyester blazer that felt like a second skin – a skin stretched thin by three months of missed quotas and increasingly pointed glances from her regional manager.  The plush, leather armchairs surrounding her were occupied by a collection of equally nervous souls, all vying for a chance to pitch their wares to Mr. Silas Blackwood, the notoriously capricious owner of Blackwood Manor, a sprawling estate rumored to possess a greenhouse larger than most town halls.  Outside, the late October wind howled a mournful ballad, rattling the single, dusty windowpane, mirroring the tempest brewing within Amy's stomach.  This meeting, she knew, was her last chance.
