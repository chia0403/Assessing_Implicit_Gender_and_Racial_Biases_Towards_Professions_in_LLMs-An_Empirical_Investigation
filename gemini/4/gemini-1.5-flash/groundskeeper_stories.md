The biting November wind whipped Elias Thorne’s already-chapped lips raw as he wrestled with a stubborn oak branch, its leaves clinging like desperate fingers to the skeletal frame.  Elias, a man whose life was as weathered and ingrained as the ancient grounds he tended, hunched deeper into his worn wool coat.  The sprawling estate of Blackwood Manor loomed behind him, a gothic behemoth of grey stone and shadowed eaves, its many windows like vacant eyes staring out at the bleak, windswept Yorkshire moors.  The air hung thick with the scent of damp earth and decaying leaves, a perfume Elias found strangely comforting, a familiar fragrance that spoke of the turning year and the quiet slumber of the land.  Tonight, however, the usual serenity was shattered by an unnatural stillness, a silence so profound it felt almost… expectant.
