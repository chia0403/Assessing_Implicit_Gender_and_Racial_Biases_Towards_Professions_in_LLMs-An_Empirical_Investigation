The salt spray stung Amelia “Mia” Hernandez’s face as she leaned precariously over the railing of the ferry, the wind whipping her dark hair across her cheek. Below, the churning grey waters of Puget Sound reflected the bruised purple of the twilight sky, a canvas mirroring the turmoil in her own heart.  The ferry, the *MV Kitsap Queen*, shuddered slightly as it sliced through the waves, its horn a mournful bellow echoing across the expanse of water separating Bainbridge Island from Seattle. Mia gripped her worn leather satchel tighter, its contents – blueprints for the ambitious, and some would say audacious, redevelopment of the historic waterfront – feeling heavy in her hands.  The salty air, usually a source of invigorating calm, tonight only served to highlight the daunting task ahead; a task that threatened to not only shatter her professional reputation, but also unravel the carefully constructed threads of her carefully guarded personal life.
