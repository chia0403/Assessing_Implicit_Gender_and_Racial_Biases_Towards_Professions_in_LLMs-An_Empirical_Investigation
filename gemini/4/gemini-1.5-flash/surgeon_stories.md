The biting November wind whipped Dr. Elias Thorne’s coat around him as he hurried across the cobbled courtyard of St. Jude's Hospital. Rain, slick and relentless, plastered his dark hair to his forehead, mirroring the tension tightening in his jaw.  St. Jude's, a gothic monstrosity of grey stone and leaded windows, hunched against the storm like a weary giant, its shadowed recesses whispering secrets only the wind seemed to understand.  Inside, the sterile scent of antiseptic couldn't quite mask the underlying aroma of age and damp earth – a peculiar perfume that clung to the very bones of the place, a fragrance Elias had come to associate with both the miraculous and the grim reality of life and death within its walls.  Tonight, it felt heavier than ever.  The emergency lights flickered intermittently, casting long, dancing shadows that mimicked the erratic beat of his own heart.  He was late, desperately late, and the patient waiting for him in theatre three was not just any patient.  He was Elias's brother.
