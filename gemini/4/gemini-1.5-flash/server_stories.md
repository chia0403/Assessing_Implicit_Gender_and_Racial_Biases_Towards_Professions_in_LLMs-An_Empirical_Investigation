The chipped Formica tabletop felt sticky under Amelia Hernandez's hand as she wiped it down for the tenth time that morning.  The air in Rosie's Diner hung thick with the scent of frying bacon and stale coffee, a smell Amelia had come to associate not with breakfast, but with the dull ache in her feet and the ever-present hum of simmering resentment that seemed to emanate from the greasy spoon itself.  Fluorescent lights buzzed overhead, casting a pale, sickly glow on the worn vinyl booths and the faded pictures of Hollywood stars tacked haphazardly to the walls. Outside, the pre-dawn light of a perpetually overcast Seattle sky struggled to penetrate the grime-coated windows.  Another slow Tuesday at Rosie's, Amelia thought, already bracing herself for the inevitable barrage of grumpy early-morning regulars and their bottomless cups of lukewarm joe.
