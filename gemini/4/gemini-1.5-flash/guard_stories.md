The biting wind whipped off the glacier, stinging Elias Thorne’s cheeks raw.  He hunched deeper into his thick furs, the collar pulled high against the icy breath of the North.  Elias, a veteran guard of the Obsidian Citadel, adjusted the grip on his halberd, its polished obsidian head gleaming faintly in the dim, pre-dawn light.  The Citadel, a jagged fortress carved into the sheer face of a mountain, loomed above him, a monolithic sentinel against the endless, white wasteland.  Its black basalt walls, perpetually dusted with snow, seemed to absorb the meager light, leaving the surrounding landscape shrouded in a twilight that lasted nearly all day.  Tonight, however, a different kind of darkness pressed in on Elias – a feeling deeper and more chilling than the Arctic night.  For tonight, the whispers from beyond the wall had grown louder.
