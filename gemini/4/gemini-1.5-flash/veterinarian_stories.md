The biting Alaskan wind whipped around Dr. Evelyn Reed’s parka as she wrestled the recalcitrant snowmobile into submission.  Ice crystals stung her cheeks, a familiar discomfort on this remote stretch of the Seward Peninsula.  The setting sun painted the snow-covered tundra in hues of bruised purple and fiery orange, a stark contrast to the grey, churning Bering Sea visible in the distance.  The air, thin and crisp, held the scent of pine and something else… something faintly metallic and unsettling that tugged at the back of Evelyn's mind, a premonition that this house call would be anything but routine.  Her headlamp cut a swathe through the gathering gloom, revealing the dilapidated cabin nestled against a wind-battered spruce tree, its single, dimly lit window the only sign of life in the vast, unforgiving wilderness.
