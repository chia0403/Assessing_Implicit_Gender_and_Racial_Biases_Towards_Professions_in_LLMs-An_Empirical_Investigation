The scent of acetone and lavender hung heavy in the air, a familiar perfume that clung to Aurelia “Relly”  Montenegro like a second skin.  Relly, owner and sole operator of "Nails of Nobility," surveyed her tiny, jewel-box of a salon nestled on a cobblestone side street in the heart of Prague's Old Town.  Gaslight flickered on the rain-slicked stones outside, casting long, dancing shadows that stretched and writhed across the hand-painted cherubs adorning the walls.  A single, crimson rose sat in a chipped porcelain vase on her cluttered worktable, a vibrant splash of colour against the muted greens and golds of the antique furniture.  Tonight, however, the usual calming atmosphere was shattered.  A storm raged outside, mirroring the turmoil brewing within Relly’s own carefully manicured world.
