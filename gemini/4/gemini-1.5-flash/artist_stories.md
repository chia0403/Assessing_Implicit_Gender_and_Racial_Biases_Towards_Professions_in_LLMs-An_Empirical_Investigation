The salt spray kissed Elara Vance's face, carrying the scent of brine and distant kelp forests.  She perched precariously on a jagged outcrop of rock, the wind whipping her auburn braid across her oilskin jacket.  Below her, the churning grey expanse of the Atlantic roared against the granite cliffs of Cornwall, a dramatic backdrop to the chaotic symphony of gulls overhead. Elara, a landscape artist renowned for her breathtaking depictions of wild, untamed beauty, adjusted her easel, the canvas already a vibrant tapestry of blues, greys, and the angry, frothing white of the waves.  This particular stretch of coast, known locally as Widow's Point, had captivated her for months; its raw, untamed power a constant muse, a challenge she couldn't resist.
