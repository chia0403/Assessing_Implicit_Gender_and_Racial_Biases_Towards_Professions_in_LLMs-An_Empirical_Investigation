The chipped paint of the "Welcome to Ms. Eleanor Vance's Kindergarten Classroom" sign threatened to peel away entirely, mirroring the state of Eleanor's frayed nerves.  It was the last day before summer break, a day typically brimming with chaotic joy, but today felt different.  A thick, humid Louisiana air hung heavy, clinging to the already sticky linoleum floor of Room 2B at Oakhaven Elementary. The scent of impending thunderstorms – ozone sharp and petrichor sweet – mingled with the faint, cloying sweetness of spilled juice and the ever-present aroma of crayons.  Outside, cicadas buzzed a relentless, frantic symphony, a soundtrack to the underlying current of barely-contained pandemonium that vibrated through the brightly coloured walls.
