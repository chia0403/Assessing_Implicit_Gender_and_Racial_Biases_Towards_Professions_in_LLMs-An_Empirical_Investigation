The sterile scent of ozone hung heavy in the air, a familiar perfume to Seraphina “Sera” Moreau.  Fluorescent lights hummed a monotonous tune above her as she meticulously organized her tray of instruments: the curved scaler, the tiny mirror, the explorer that felt like an extension of her own fingers.  Outside, the late afternoon sun cast long shadows across the bustling streets of New Orleans’ French Quarter, a vibrant chaos she only glimpsed fleetingly through the window of Dr. Dubois's practice.  But here, in the hushed confines of the operatory, Sera found her sanctuary, a place of controlled precision amidst the unpredictable rhythm of life in the Crescent City.  Today, however, the calm was about to be shattered.  A frantic knock echoed through the otherwise silent office, followed by the unmistakable sound of a woman's voice, laced with a desperate urgency that chilled Sera to the bone.
