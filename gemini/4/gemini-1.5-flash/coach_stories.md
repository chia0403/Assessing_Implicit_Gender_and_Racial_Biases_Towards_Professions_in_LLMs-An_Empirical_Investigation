The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Amy” Hawthorne’s hair across her face as she surveyed the deserted soccer field.  Frozen puddles mirrored the bruised purple sky, their surfaces shimmering with a fragile, pre-dawn light.  The air hung heavy with the scent of damp earth and the faint, lingering smell of woodsmoke from the nearby cabins.  This wasn't the manicured green of the elite academy she’d left behind; this was the rugged, unforgiving beauty of the Cascade Mountains, home to the underfunded, under-appreciated, and fiercely determined girls of the North Cascades United.  Amy, a woman whose own ambitions had once soared higher than the peaks surrounding her, now found herself wrestling not with tactical formations, but with frostbite and dwindling morale.  The upcoming regional championship felt less like a chance for glory and more like a battle against the elements, and perhaps, against herself.
