The air hung thick and humid, a suffocating blanket woven from the scent of jasmine and exhaust fumes.  Cicadas shrieked their incessant chorus from the mango trees lining the crumbling colonial architecture of Old Havana.  Rain threatened, the sky a bruised purple canvas streaked with angry orange.  Amelia Hernandez-Garcia, a woman whose sharp wit was only surpassed by the unwavering steel in her gaze, adjusted the collar of her crisp white linen shirt.  The humidity clung to her like a second skin, but it couldn't dampen the fire in her belly.  Tonight, she faced her most challenging case yet, one that threatened to unravel not only her reputation, but the delicate balance of power within the city itself.  The flickering neon sign of the *Habana Libre* bar cast an ethereal glow on the cobblestones as she pushed open the heavy, mahogany door, the scent of aged rum and desperation washing over her.
