The humid air hung heavy, thick with the scent of sweat and desperation.  Fluorescent lights buzzed overhead, casting a sickly yellow glow on the mirrored walls of "Body Blitz Fitness."  Sweat dripped from the brow of Amelia "Amy" Hernandez, her dark braid plastered to her neck.  She surveyed the room, a battlefield of panting bodies and straining muscles, each face a testament to the relentless, self-inflicted torture of her 6 a.m. "Boot Camp Inferno" class.  Amy, despite her own exhaustion, felt a surge of grim satisfaction.  They were breaking;  that was the point.  The chipped paint on the wall behind her, a testament to years of grueling workouts, seemed to grin in silent agreement.
