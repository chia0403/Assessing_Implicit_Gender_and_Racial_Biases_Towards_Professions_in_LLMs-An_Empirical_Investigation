The chipped paint of the "Serenity Now" sign hanging askew above the door was a fitting metaphor for Elara Vance's current state of mind.  Elara Vance, MA, Licensed Professional Counselor, stared at the smudge of what she hoped was coffee – but which could also have been old gravy – on her already stained cardigan.  The air in the Northwood High School guidance office hung thick with the scent of stale popcorn and teenage angst, a familiar perfume that clung to the threadbare carpet and the worn-out armchairs like a second skin.  Outside, the late October wind howled a mournful tune, rattling the ancient windows in their frames.  Inside, the muffled sounds of adolescent drama played out – slammed lockers, whispered secrets, and the sporadic eruption of a particularly hormonal shout – provided a fitting soundtrack to Elara’s already chaotic Tuesday.
