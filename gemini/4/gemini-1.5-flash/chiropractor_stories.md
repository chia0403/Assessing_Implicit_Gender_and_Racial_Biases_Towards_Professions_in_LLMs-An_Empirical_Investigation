The salt spray stung Dr. Eleanor Vance's face as she wrestled the stubborn sail.  The late afternoon sun, a molten orange orb sinking towards the churning grey Atlantic, cast long shadows across the deck of the *Sea Serpent*, her small, battered sailboat.  The air, thick with the smell of brine and seaweed, was surprisingly warm for a November evening off the coast of Maine.  Eleanor, her dark hair escaping its braid to whip around her face, gritted her teeth.  Getting the sail properly trimmed was always a battle, but the stubborn wind was a worthy opponent, mirroring the stubborn aches and pains she dealt with daily in her practice back in Bar Harbor.  Tonight, though, the only thing she wanted to wrestle was the unpredictable sea, a welcome distraction from the knot of anxiety tightening in her stomach.  The cryptic message she'd received that morning – a single, faded photograph of a lighthouse and the words, "Find me," scrawled in shaky handwriting –  weighed heavier than any patient's spinal misalignment ever could.
