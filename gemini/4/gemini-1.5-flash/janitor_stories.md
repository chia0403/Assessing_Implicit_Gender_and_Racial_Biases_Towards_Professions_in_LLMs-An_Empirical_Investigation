The stale scent of disinfectant clung to Elias Thorne like a second skin, a familiar perfume in the echoing silence of the Blackwood Academy for the Gifted.  Three a.m. found Elias, a man whose life was measured in the squeak of his mop and the rhythmic swoosh of his broom, hunched over a particularly stubborn stain of what he suspected, but couldn't quite place, near the entrance to the library.  Blackwood, perched precariously on a windswept cliff overlooking the churning grey sea, was a Gothic monstrosity of crumbling stone and leaded windows, a place whispered about in hushed tones, even in the nearby town of Port Blossom.  Inside its walls, prodigies – or so they claimed – honed their arcane abilities, while Elias, armed with nothing but a bucket and his weary cynicism, kept the mess at bay. The moon, a skeletal fingernail in the inky sky, cast long, skeletal shadows across the polished floors, highlighting the dust motes dancing in the weak beams of Elias's flashlight. Tonight, however, the dust seemed to hold a different kind of stillness, a foreboding quiet that prickled at the hairs on the back of his neck.
