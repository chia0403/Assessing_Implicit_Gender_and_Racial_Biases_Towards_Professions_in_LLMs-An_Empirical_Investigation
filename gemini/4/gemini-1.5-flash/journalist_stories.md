The biting November wind whipped Amelia Katherine Holloway’s hair across her face, stinging her cheeks a vibrant crimson that clashed spectacularly with the drab grey of the Glasgow sky.  Rain, a relentless drizzle that seemed to seep into the very marrow of your bones, plastered her trench coat to her back.  Amelia, a seasoned journalist for the Glasgow Chronicle, hunched deeper into her collar, the worn leather offering little comfort against the chill.  She stood before the imposing gothic façade of Blackwood Manor, its stone almost swallowed by the swirling mist that clung to the overgrown grounds like a shroud.  The wrought iron gates, rusted and gnarled with age, groaned a mournful protest as a gust of wind slammed them shut, the sound echoing the unsettling silence that had settled over the estate for the past fifty years – a silence Amelia was determined to break.
