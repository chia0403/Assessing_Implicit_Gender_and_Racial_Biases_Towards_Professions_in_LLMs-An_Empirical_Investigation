The salt spray stung Elara Vance's face, a familiar kiss from the unforgiving Atlantic.  She adjusted the worn leather strap of her Leica M6, the cold metal a stark contrast to the damp warmth clinging to her wool coat.  The wind, a relentless sculptor, whipped her dark hair across her eyes as she perched precariously on the jagged rocks of Cove Head, the relentless roar of the ocean a constant, thrumming bassline to the symphony of the storm.  Grey skies, bruised purple at the horizon, threatened to unleash another deluge.  Below, the churning sea gnawed at the ancient cliffs, a chaotic ballet of foam and fury.  This was her sanctuary, this wild, untamed beauty of Prince Edward Island, and tonight, it held a secret Elara was determined to capture.
