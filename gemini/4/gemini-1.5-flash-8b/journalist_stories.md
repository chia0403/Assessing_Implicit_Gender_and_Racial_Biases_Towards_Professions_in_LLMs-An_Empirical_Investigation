The biting wind of the North Atlantic whipped at Amelia Blackwood's trench coat, stinging her cheeks and rattling the loose papers in her worn leather satchel.  Grey, churning waves crashed against the jagged cliffs of the Outer Hebrides, mirroring the turmoil in her stomach.  The small fishing village of Portree, usually a picturesque huddle of colourful houses clinging to the hillside, felt desolate under the relentless onslaught of the storm.  Amelia, a seasoned investigative journalist for the *Glasgow Gazette*, had come seeking answers, and the tempest seemed to mirror the tempest brewing within her.  The air, thick with the salty tang of the sea and the almost palpable sense of isolation, was a fitting backdrop for the story she was determined to uncover.
