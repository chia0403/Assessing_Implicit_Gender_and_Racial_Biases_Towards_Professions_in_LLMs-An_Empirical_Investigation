The aroma of cinnamon and yeast hung heavy in the air, a comforting blanket draped over the cobbled streets of the walled city of Aethelgard.  Dust motes danced in the sliver of afternoon sun filtering through the tall, arched windows of "The Golden Crust,"  a bakery nestled beside the Guildhall.  This was the realm of Elara Alderwood, a baker with hands as nimble as sparrows' wings and eyes as sharp as a freshly honed knife.  Elara, her apron meticulously dusted with flour, meticulously arranged a batch of freshly baked sourdough loaves on a wooden rack.  A low hum of conversation, the clinking of ceramic mugs, and the rhythmic whir of the hand-cranked grinder created a soothing backdrop to her daily ritual.  But today, a strange tension hung in the air, a palpable shift from the usual morning bustle.  A shiver traced its way down her spine, a feeling she couldn't quite place, despite the warmth of the bakery and the familiar scent of her creation.
