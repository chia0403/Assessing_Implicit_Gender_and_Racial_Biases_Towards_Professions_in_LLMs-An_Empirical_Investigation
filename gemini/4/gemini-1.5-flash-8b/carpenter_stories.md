The biting wind, a relentless, clawing beast, howled across the jagged peaks of the Kestrel Mountains, rattling the single, grimy window of Elias Thorne's workshop.  Dust motes danced in the meagre light filtering through the cracks, illuminating splinters of wood, shavings the colour of dried blood, and a half-finished wooden chest that stubbornly refused to cooperate with Elias's meticulous hands.  Elias Thorne, a carpenter of the unforgiving village of Whisperwind nestled at the foot of the mountains, was wrestling with a stubborn knot in the wood, a knot that seemed to mirror the tightness in his own chest.  He hadn't slept well in weeks.  His wife, Elara, hadn't been herself.  The forest, usually a source of quiet strength, felt suffocating.  A shiver, not from the cold, ran down his spine.  He swore the mountains were whispering secrets to the wind.
