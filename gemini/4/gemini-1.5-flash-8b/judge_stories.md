The biting wind whipped Amelia Blackwood, Judge of the Northern Marches, across the cobblestones of the town square.  Dust devils, kicked up by the unseen hooves of countless beasts, danced in the pale winter light.  Grim stone buildings, each with weathered, scarred facades, pressed in on her, their eaves dripping with an icy slush that mirrored the despair in her heart.  Snow, a perpetual shroud of grey, clung to the skeletal branches of the ancient oaks lining the perimeter of the square. This was Winterhold, a town whispered to be cursed, its inhabitants hardened by hardship and shrouded in suspicion.  Amelia, a woman with eyes the colour of storm clouds and a will forged in the fires of injustice, shivered not from the cold, but from the weight of the case before her.  The trial of the Wandering Witch, Isolde Thorne, was scheduled for dawn.
