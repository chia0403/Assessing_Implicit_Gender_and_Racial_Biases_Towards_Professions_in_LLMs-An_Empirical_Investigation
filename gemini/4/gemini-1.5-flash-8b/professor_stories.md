Professor Eleanor Vance, renowned astrophysicist and meticulous curator of the Blackwood Observatory's historical archive, adjusted her spectacles, the lenses magnifying the dust motes dancing in the afternoon sun streaming through the arched windows.  The air in the dusty, high-ceilinged room hung thick with the scent of aged parchment and forgotten stars.  Sunlight, filtered through layers of stained glass depicting constellations long gone, painted shifting patterns on the rows of ancient astronomical instruments lining the walls.  This was the heart of Blackwood Observatory, a place where the echoes of forgotten discoveries lingered, whispering tales of celestial wonders and human ambition.  For decades, Eleanor had meticulously cataloged every artifact, every observation, every whisper of cosmic history within these walls.  But today, something was different.
