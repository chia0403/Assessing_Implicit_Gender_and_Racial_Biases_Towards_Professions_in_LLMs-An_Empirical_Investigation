Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of the dilapidated gymnasium, mirroring the relentless drumming in Elias Thorne's head.  He stared at the splintered wood floor, the faded basketball hoops, and the peeling paint, each imperfection a scar on the soul of the "Grizzlies," the high school basketball team he'd dedicated the last seven years to.  The small town of Oakhaven, nestled in the foothills of the Cascade Mountains, was a place of quiet desperation, and the Grizzlies, a reflection of that.  Their season had been a string of heartbreaking losses, the players struggling not just with the game, but with the pressures of poverty and fractured families, the same forces that had gnawed at Elias himself.  This wasn't just about basketball; it was about hope.  And tonight, hope felt as fragile as the flickering fluorescent lights above.
