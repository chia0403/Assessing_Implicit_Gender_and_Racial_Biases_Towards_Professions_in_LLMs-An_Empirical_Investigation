The chipped porcelain sink, stained a faded mint green by decades of lukewarm water and spilled cough syrup, hummed faintly under Amelia Evangeline Finch's touch.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of afternoon sunlight slicing through the grime-coated window of "The Wellspring Pharmacy," a testament to a bygone era clinging stubbornly to the heart of Oakhaven.  The air, thick with the scent of old books, powdered sugar, and antiseptic, clung to her like a second skin.  Amelia, a pharmacist of thirty-seven years, surveyed the meticulously organized shelves.  Rows upon rows of bottles, each a miniature universe of remedies, whispered stories of ailments conquered and battles lost, all held within their glass prisons.  Today, however, a new kind of tension hung in the air, a palpable weight settling over the quiet town as if a storm were brewing beneath the surface.
