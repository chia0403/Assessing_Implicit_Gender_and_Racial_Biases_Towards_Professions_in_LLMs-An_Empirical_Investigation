The flickering fluorescent lights of the legal office hummed a monotonous tune, a soundtrack to Amelia Cartwright's growing frustration.  Paper, the bane of paralegals everywhere, piled precariously on her desk, each document a miniature mountain demanding attention.  Outside, the grey London sky mirrored the gloom settling over her.  Rain lashed against the windows, mirroring the torrent of paperwork flooding her inbox and overwhelming her senses.  Amelia, a paralegal at the prestigious firm of Sterling & Thorne, was drowning in a sea of contracts, depositions, and legal briefs.  Her carefully crafted to-do list, painstakingly organized by colour-coded Post-it notes, seemed a futile attempt to conquer the chaos.  Her meticulously organized life, meticulously planned schedule, and rigorously enforced routine were all starting to unravel at the seams.
