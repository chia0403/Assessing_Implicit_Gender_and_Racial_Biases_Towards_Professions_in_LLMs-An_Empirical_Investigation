The scent of burnt sugar hung heavy in the air, a sickly sweet counterpoint to the sharp tang of lemon zest.  Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of the "Rusty Spoon," a bistro tucked away in the cobbled alleyways of Vieux-Port, Marseille.  The rhythmic drumming punctuated the clinking of ceramic plates and the low murmur of voices, a familiar soundtrack to Antoine Dubois’s life.  Antoine, a man whose passion for cuisine burned as fiercely as the flames under his copper pans, stood frozen, a half-formed soufflé collapsing in his hands.  He was a chef, yes, but tonight, even the precise movements that defined his craft felt clumsy, inadequate.
