The crisp October air, scented with woodsmoke and decaying leaves, whipped Amelia “Amy” Rose Hawthorne’s auburn braid across her face as she hurried up the flagstone path.  Her breath puffed white in the gathering twilight, illuminated by the single gas lamp flickering beside the heavy oak door of Blackwood Manor. Perched atop a windswept hill overlooking the churning grey expanse of the North Sea, the manor seemed to hunch against the elements, a brooding silhouette against the bruised purple sky. Amy, her fingers already stiffening in the cold, tugged her woolen gloves tighter and adjusted the heavy medical bag slung over her shoulder.  She was late, and Elias Blackwood, her new, reclusive patient, was not a man known for his patience.
