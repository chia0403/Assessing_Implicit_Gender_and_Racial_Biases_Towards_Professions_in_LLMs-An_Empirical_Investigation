The biting November wind whipped through the scaffolding around the half-finished set, tugging at the edges of Delilah "Del" Moreau's cashmere scarf.  Grey clouds, heavy with unshed rain, pressed down on the desolate stretch of Oregon coastline.  Del, her breath misting in the frigid air, surveyed the scene with a critical eye.  The skeleton of a dilapidated lighthouse clawed at the sky, its skeletal beams a stark contrast against the churning slate-grey ocean. This wasn't the idyllic seaside escape most people pictured; this was the bleak, windswept backdrop for her latest psychological thriller, "The Keeper's Wife," and it was perfect.
