The flickering neon sign of "Shear Radiance" buzzed erratically, casting a purplish glow across the rain-slicked pavement of Clementine Street. Inside, amidst the chemical scent of perm solution and hairspray, Amelia Rose Hawthorne snipped the last frayed ends from Mrs. Henderson's blue rinse, her brow furrowed in concentration.  Amelia, with her name tattooed in elegant script on her inner wrist and a constellation of freckles dusting her nose, wasn't just a hairdresser; she was a sculptor of confidence, a weaver of dreams, at least in the small town of Harmony Glade, Oregon, where gossip travelled faster than the Willamette River and bad hair days were considered a public offense.  Tonight, however, a different kind of storm was brewing, one that had nothing to do with split ends or outgrown roots.  The bell above the door chimed, announcing a new arrival, and Amelia, glancing at the reflection in the salon's wide front window, saw a figure silhouetted against the downpour, a figure that sent a shiver of unease down her spine.
