The scent of old paper and beeswax hung heavy in the air, a comforting perfume to Elias Thorne, Head Librarian of the Athelney Public Library.  Dust motes danced in the slanting rays of the late afternoon sun, illuminating the towering shelves that lined the circular reading room.  Athelney itself was a sleepy, forgotten town nestled in the crook of the River Thorne, a place where the loudest sound was usually the church bells chiming the hour.  But within the library’s thick stone walls, Elias presided over a world of adventure, romance, and intrigue, contained within the countless volumes under his care.  This particular Thursday, however, promised to be anything but ordinary.  A storm was brewing outside, mirroring the unease churning in Elias’s gut.  He'd received a strange package that morning, an unmarked wooden box containing a single, ancient book bound in what looked suspiciously like human skin.
