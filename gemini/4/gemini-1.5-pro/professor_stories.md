The biting November wind whipped around Professor Alistair Finch, rustling the crimson and gold leaves that clung precariously to the ancient oaks lining the quad.  Alistair, his tweed jacket pulled tight against the chill, hurried across the manicured lawn of Crestwood University, a prestigious liberal arts college nestled in the rolling hills of Vermont. The late afternoon sun, already beginning to dip below the horizon, cast long, skeletal shadows from the gothic architecture of the campus buildings.  He clutched a worn leather satchel overflowing with student papers, his brow furrowed in thought.  Tonight was the annual faculty dinner, a stuffy affair Alistair usually dreaded, but this year, something felt different. A strange unease, like a discordant note in a familiar melody, had settled upon him, and it had nothing to do with the prospect of lukewarm hors d'oeuvres and forced conversation.
