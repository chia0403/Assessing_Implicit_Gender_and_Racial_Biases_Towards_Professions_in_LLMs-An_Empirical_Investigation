The biting wind whipped off the Obsidian Sea, stinging Elias Thorne's cheeks and rattling the iron bars of the West Gate.  Jagged peaks, perpetually shrouded in mist, loomed over the fortress-city of Veridia, their shadows painting the cobbled streets in shades of grey and violet. Veridia, carved directly into the mountainside, was a labyrinth of winding alleyways, precarious bridges spanning dizzying chasms, and towering stone structures that seemed to defy gravity itself. Tonight, however, Elias cared little for the breathtaking, if somewhat menacing, beauty of his home. He was bone-tired, the leather of his gauntlets stiff and cold against his skin, and the fifth hour of his watch had just begun.
