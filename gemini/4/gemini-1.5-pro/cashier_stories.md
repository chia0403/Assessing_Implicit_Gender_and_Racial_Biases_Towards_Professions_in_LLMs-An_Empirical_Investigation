The flickering fluorescent lights of the Quick Stop buzzed like trapped insects, casting a sickly yellow glow over the aisles of dusty chips and lukewarm sodas. Rain lashed against the grimy windows, mirroring the tempest brewing inside Amelia Rose Hawthorne.  She tapped a chipped, cherry-red nail against the worn countertop, the only sound besides the rhythmic *beep* of the scanner and the low hum of the refrigerated section in the back.  It was 7:47 PM on a Tuesday, the slowest shift of the week, in the sleepiest corner of Harmony Creek, Indiana.  Amelia, with her nametag slightly askew and a world-weary sigh escaping her lips, braced herself for another long, lonely stretch until closing time.
