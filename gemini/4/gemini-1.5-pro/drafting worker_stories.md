The fluorescent lights of the K&B Architectural firm hummed a monotonous B-flat, a counterpoint to the rhythmic scratch of pencils on vellum. Dust motes, illuminated by the harsh glare, danced a slow waltz in the still air. It was a Tuesday, indistinguishable from any other Tuesday in the last five years for Amelia Grace Hawthorne.  Amelia, a drafting worker with a preternatural eye for detail and a patience honed by years of meticulously rendering blueprints, sat hunched over her drawing board.  Outside, the Chicago wind howled, rattling the large windows that overlooked the grey expanse of Lake Michigan. The lake, usually a calming presence, today mirrored the churning unease in Amelia's stomach. Something felt…off. The usual comforting weight of routine was absent, replaced by a prickling anticipation she couldn’t quite place.  The blueprints before her, detailing the proposed renovations of the historic Drake Hotel, seemed to swim before her eyes, the intricate lines blurring into a meaningless jumble.
