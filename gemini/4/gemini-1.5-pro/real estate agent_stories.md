The salt-laced wind whipped Amelia "Amy" Hawthorne's hair across her face as she wrestled a FOR SALE sign into the unforgiving sand.  The turquoise waters of the Gulf of Mexico glittered under the relentless Florida sun, a stark contrast to the dilapidated beach shack behind her.  Pelican Cove, once a thriving fishing village, was now a patchwork of weathered clapboard houses and ambitious new builds vying for a slice of the revitalized coastline.  This particular property, a ramshackle two-bedroom with a dubious history involving a rumored pirate treasure and a definitely real termite infestation, was Amy's latest challenge.  She smoothed the wrinkles from her crisp white linen pantsuit, a beacon of professionalism against the backdrop of peeling paint and rusting metal, and sighed.  Selling this place was going to take a miracle, the kind that usually only came in the form of a cash buyer with extremely low standards.
