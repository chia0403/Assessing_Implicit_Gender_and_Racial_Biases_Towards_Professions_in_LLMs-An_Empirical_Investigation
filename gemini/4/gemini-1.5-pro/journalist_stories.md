Rain lashed against the grimy windows of The Daily Chronicle's office, mimicking the tempest brewing inside Amelia "Amy" Reyes.  The flickering fluorescent lights buzzed overhead, casting a sickly yellow glow on the cluttered desks and overflowing ashtrays. Cigarette smoke hung thick in the air, mingling with the bitter scent of stale coffee and the faint, underlying odor of desperation that seemed to permeate every inch of the building.  It was 11:57 pm on a Tuesday in November, and Amy, her dark hair plastered to her forehead, stared at the blinking cursor on her computer screen, the deadline for her piece on the city's escalating gang violence looming like a guillotine.  Providence, Rhode Island, once a haven of colonial charm, had become a breeding ground for shadows and secrets, and Amy, more than anyone, knew the truth lurking beneath its cobbled streets.
