Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of the Zenith Tower, blurring the already fading lights of downtown Seattle. Inside, the air buzzed with a nervous energy that had nothing to do with the approaching storm.  Eleanor Vance, a systems analyst with a penchant for cardigans and a preternatural ability to debug code in her sleep, tapped her chipped mug against the scratched mahogany of her desk. The lukewarm remnants of her third coffee of the day did little to soothe the knot tightening in her stomach. Zenith Robotics, usually a hive of quiet, focused work, thrummed with hushed whispers and anxious glances. Tomorrow was the unveiling of Project Chimera, Zenith's foray into true artificial intelligence, and something felt deeply, unsettlingly wrong.
