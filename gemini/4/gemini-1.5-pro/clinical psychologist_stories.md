Rain lashed against the arched windows of Dr. Elias Thorne's office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside his newest patient.  The room, perched high above the fog-choked streets of Victorian London, was usually a sanctuary of quiet contemplation, furnished with worn leather armchairs, shelves overflowing with weighty tomes, and the faint, comforting aroma of pipe tobacco.  Tonight, however, the flickering gaslight cast long, nervous shadows that danced with the anxieties swirling within its walls.  Elias, his dark hair threaded with silver at the temples, observed the young woman seated opposite him, her hands clenched so tightly in her lap that her knuckles were bone-white. Outside, the November wind howled a mournful dirge, a fitting accompaniment to the chilling confession he knew was about to unfold.
