The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne's hair across her face as she hurried across the sprawling parking lot of St. Jude's Memorial Hospital.  Fluorescent lights spilled from the towering windows, casting a sickly yellow glow on the freshly fallen snow. The air crackled with the static electricity of approaching winter, a stark contrast to the controlled, antiseptic environment Amelia knew awaited her inside.  St. Jude's, a behemoth of brick and glass perched on a hill overlooking the small, struggling town of Havenwood, Maine, was Amelia's second home, and tonight, like many nights before, it promised to be anything but peaceful.
