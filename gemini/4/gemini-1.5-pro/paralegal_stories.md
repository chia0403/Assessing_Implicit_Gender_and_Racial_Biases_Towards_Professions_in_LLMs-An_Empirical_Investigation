Rain lashed against the grimy windows of the Legal Aid office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside Amelia Grace Hawthorne.  Fluorescent lights hummed overhead, casting a sterile glow on the overflowing filing cabinets and stacks of dog-eared case files that threatened to engulf her tiny cubicle.  Downtown Baltimore shimmered beyond the rain-streaked glass, a kaleidoscope of blurred lights reflecting off the slick asphalt. It was 5:47 pm on a Friday, and Amelia, normally meticulous and organized, felt the chaos of the week pressing in on her.  The scent of stale coffee and desperation hung heavy in the air, a familiar aroma in this underfunded, overworked corner of the legal world.  Her boss, Mr. Henderson, had just dumped another mountain of paperwork on her desk – the Peterson eviction case, notoriously complex and emotionally charged – with the cheery parting words, "Have a good weekend, Amelia!"
