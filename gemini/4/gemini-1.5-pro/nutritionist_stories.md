The insistent drip from the overflowing sink was the soundtrack to Elias Thorne's mounting frustration.  His small, meticulously organized office, nestled above Mrs. O'Malley's perpetually fragrant bakery in the heart of Willow Creek, Vermont, usually exuded an aura of calm.  Today, however, the usual scent of cinnamon and nutmeg was overpowered by the metallic tang of plumbing disaster.  Elias, a registered dietitian with a reputation for crafting personalized meal plans that could coax even the most stubborn junk food addict towards kale, felt utterly powerless against the steady stream of water invading his meticulously arranged pamphlets on the benefits of omega-3 fatty acids. Outside, a blustery November wind howled down Main Street, rattling the colorful autumn leaves clinging precariously to the ancient maples lining the sidewalk. Inside, Elias, his normally impeccable tweed jacket now sporting a damp patch, braced himself for the inevitable flood.
