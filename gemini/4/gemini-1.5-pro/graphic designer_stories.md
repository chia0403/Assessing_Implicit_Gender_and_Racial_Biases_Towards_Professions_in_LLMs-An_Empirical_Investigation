The biting November wind whipped Amelia "Amy" Reyes's auburn braid across her face as she hurried down Bleecker Street, the scent of roasted chestnuts battling the ever-present aroma of stale pizza from Joe's down the block.  Her gloved fingers, already numb despite the cashmere lining, fumbled with the lock on the heavy iron gate that guarded the entrance to “The Inkwell,” a ramshackle, three-story building sandwiched between a trendy vintage clothing store and a perpetually closed psychic's parlor. Above the gate, a wrought-iron sign depicting a quill and inkpot swayed precariously in the gust. This was her sanctuary, her studio, her escape from the relentless demands of the New York City design scene.  Inside, the warm glow of her iMac beckoned, promising a haven from the encroaching darkness and the unnerving quiet that had settled over the usually bustling Village street.  Tonight, however, even the familiar hum of her computer couldn't quite drown out the unsettling feeling that something was amiss.
