The biting wind whipped Dr. Elias Thorne's dark hair across his face as he hurried across the frozen yard, the scent of woodsmoke and pine sharp in the frigid air.  The sky, a bruise-colored canvas streaked with angry grey, promised snow.  Elias pulled his thick wool coat tighter around him, his breath puffing out in white clouds.  The ramshackle clinic, nestled at the edge of Whisperwood, a small, isolated town nestled in the Catskill Mountains of New York, glowed warmly against the darkening landscape.  Inside, a worried farmer and his prize-winning Holstein, Bess, awaited him.  This was Elias's life, a solitary existence dedicated to the creatures of this forgotten corner of the world.  Tonight, however, felt different.  A sense of unease, a prickling at the back of his neck, accompanied him, as pervasive as the icy wind.
