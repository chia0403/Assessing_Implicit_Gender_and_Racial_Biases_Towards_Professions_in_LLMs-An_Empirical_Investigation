Rain lashed against the stained-glass window depicting St. Jude, patron saint of lost causes, ironically illuminating Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s cramped office.  Outside, the Chicago wind howled a mournful tune, mirroring the sinking feeling in Amelia’s stomach. Her office, tucked between a laundromat with perpetually flickering neon and a suspiciously empty fortune teller's parlor in the less-than-glamorous Humboldt Park neighborhood, usually exuded a certain cozy charm. Today, however, the peeling floral wallpaper and the overflowing file cabinet seemed less charming and more indicative of her stagnant career.  It was a Tuesday, the slowest day of the week, and Amelia, a woman whose optimism usually rivaled a golden retriever’s, found herself staring glumly at a half-eaten tuna sandwich, contemplating the distinct possibility that she’d sold her last insurance policy.
