The biting November wind whipped Amelia "Mia" Rodriguez's dark braid across her face as she surveyed the scene. Jagged shards of what was once a three-story brownstone clawed at the pre-dawn sky, silhouetted against the bruised purple-grey canvas.  Smoke, thick and acrid, curled from the gaping maw where the front door used to be, staining the swirling snow a dirty ochre.  Sirens wailed in the distance, a mournful counterpoint to the crackling inferno that had already devoured the first floor of 187 Bleecker Street, in the heart of New York City's West Village. Mia, a five-year veteran of Ladder 8, braced herself against the chill, the familiar adrenaline thrumming beneath the layers of her turnout gear.  This wasn't going to be an easy one.
