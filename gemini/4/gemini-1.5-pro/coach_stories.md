The biting November wind whipped across the desolate practice field, carrying with it the scent of damp earth and decaying leaves. Floodlights cast long, skeletal shadows of the bleachers onto the cracked asphalt, illuminating the solitary figure of Coach Eleanor Vance.  Eleanor, her breath clouding in the frigid air, pulled her threadbare Green Bay Packers beanie further down over her ears.  Weston, Connecticut, wasn't exactly a football hotbed, especially not for girls' six-a-side flag football, but this ramshackle field behind Weston Middle School was her kingdom, and these girls, huddled in their mismatched parkas on the sidelines, were her army. Tonight, with the first snowflakes of the season beginning to fall, they had a score to settle.  The undefeated Darien Dolphins were coming to town, and Eleanor had a feeling this game was going to be more than just another notch on their perfect record.
