The biting wind whipped Amelia "Amy" Hawthorne's auburn braid across her face as she trudged through the ankle-deep snow towards the primate enclosure.  Dawn was barely a smudge of grey on the horizon, painting the snow-covered peaks of the Cascade Mountains a ghostly lavender.  Hidden Hollow Sanctuary, nestled in a secluded valley in Oregon, was a haven for rescued exotic animals, a place where the roar of a lion could still compete with the whisper of the wind through ancient Douglas firs.  Amy, bundled in a thick parka and snow boots, exhaled a cloud of frosty breath, the scent of pine and damp earth clinging to the air.  This was her domain, her responsibility, a symphony of chirps, growls, and roars that greeted her every morning, and she wouldn't trade it for anything.  Today, however, a certain uneasiness hung in the crisp air, a feeling that something was not quite right.
