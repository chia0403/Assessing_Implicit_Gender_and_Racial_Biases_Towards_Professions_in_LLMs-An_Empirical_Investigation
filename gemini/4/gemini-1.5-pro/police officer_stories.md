The flickering neon sign of the Starlight Diner cast an oily rainbow across the rain-slicked asphalt. Detective Isabella "Izzy" Diaz shivered, pulling her collar tighter against the biting Chicago wind that whipped off Lake Michigan.  It was 2:17 am, the kind of hour that swallowed sound and amplified the lonely hum of the city.  Steam curled from the cracked pavement, a ghostly exhale against the backdrop of darkened storefronts and the rhythmic swoosh of passing cars. Izzy, her dark hair plastered to her forehead, stood on the corner of Clark and Belmont, the flashing blue and red lights of her patrol car painting the scene in alternating hues of emergency.  A crumpled figure lay in the crosswalk, a pool of something dark spreading around them like a morbid halo. The air, thick with the metallic tang of blood, promised a long night.
