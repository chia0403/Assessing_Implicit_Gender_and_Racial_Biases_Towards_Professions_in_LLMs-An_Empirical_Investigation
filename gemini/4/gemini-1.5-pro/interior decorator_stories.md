The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Amy” Hawthorne’s auburn braid across her face as she wrestled a monstrous ficus through the double doors of the Vandergelt mansion.  Gargoyles leered from the eaves of the imposing brownstone, their stone faces slick with rain, and Amy, perched precariously on her four-inch heels, felt a shiver crawl down her spine that had nothing to do with the cold.  The Vandergelt mansion, a gothic behemoth perched on the highest hill overlooking the Hudson River in Sleepy Hollow, New York, had a reputation.  Some called it haunted.  Amy called it a challenging, yet lucrative, commission.  This particular ficus, destined for the cavernous, two-story library, was the centerpiece of her vision for revitalizing the gloomy space. She just hoped she'd survive long enough to see it placed.
