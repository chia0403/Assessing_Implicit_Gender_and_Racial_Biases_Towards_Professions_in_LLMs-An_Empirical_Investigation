The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Grace Hawthorne's fingers, a meager comfort against the pre-dawn chill that clung to the air-conditioned office.  Outside, the skyscrapers of downtown Chicago still slumbered, their glass facades reflecting a bruised, pre-sunrise sky.  Inside, however, the hum of the city was already beginning its relentless crescendo – a low thrum of activity that mirrored the frantic beat of Amelia's own heart.  She hadn't slept well.  The looming deadline for Mr. Fitzwilliam's presentation, coupled with the persistent cough that had plagued her for the past week, had left her feeling frayed and exhausted.  The only light in the cavernous, mostly-empty office came from her desk lamp, its harsh glow highlighting the meticulous order of her workspace – a stark contrast to the chaos simmering just beneath the surface of her carefully composed exterior.
