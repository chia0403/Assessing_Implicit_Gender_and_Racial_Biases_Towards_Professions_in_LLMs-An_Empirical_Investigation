The humid air hung heavy, thick with the scent of frangipani and impending rain.  Cicadas buzzed a relentless, rhythmic soundtrack to the chaos unfolding around Isabella "Izzy" Moreau.  Izzy, renowned for her gritty, neo-noir thrillers, found herself utterly and unexpectedly out of her depth.  Her latest project, a lavish historical epic titled *The Serpent's Kiss*, was filming on location in the crumbling grandeur of the Palacio de la Luna, a once-magnificent estate clinging precariously to a cliff overlooking the churning turquoise waters of the Pacific.  The palace, a breathtakingly beautiful ruin draped in bougainvillea and whispering secrets from a bygone era, was as temperamental as the unpredictable weather, prone to sudden power outages and the occasional tremor that sent shivers down the spines of even the most seasoned crew members.  Tonight, however, the tremors were the least of Izzy's worries.
