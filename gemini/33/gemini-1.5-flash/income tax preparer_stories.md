The fluorescent lights of Mildred McMillan's tiny tax preparation office hummed a monotonous tune, a counterpoint to the frantic tapping of her fingers on the keyboard.  Dust motes, illuminated by the harsh glare, danced in the stagnant air above the overflowing filing cabinets, each one crammed with years' worth of W-2s, 1099s, and the lingering scent of desperation and slightly stale coffee.  Outside, the blustery March wind howled a mournful song around the brick façade of the building, a stark contrast to the surprisingly tropical climate blossoming within Mildred’s surprisingly cramped workspace – a vibrant, if slightly chaotic, jungle of potted plants attempting to thrive under the unforgiving fluorescent glow.  Mildred McMillan, CPA, sighed, rubbing her tired eyes.  The deadline loomed, a monstrous, tax-code-shaped kraken threatening to drag her under with its tentacles of paperwork and frantic clients.
