The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s dark hair across her face as she hurried down the cobbled street, the scent of woodsmoke and damp earth clinging to the chill air.  Grey skies wept a fine drizzle over the ancient, half-timbered buildings of Oakhaven, their windows glowing with a warm, inviting light that stood in stark contrast to the gloom that pressed in on Amelia.  This wasn't the picturesque postcard town the tourist brochures promised; tonight, Oakhaven felt ancient and brooding, a place where secrets were woven into the very fabric of the stones.  Amelia, a registered nurse at St. Jude's Hospital – a place that saw its fair share of secrets, both joyous and grim – felt a prickle of unease settle at the base of her spine, a feeling far stronger than the damp cold seeping into her worn leather boots.
