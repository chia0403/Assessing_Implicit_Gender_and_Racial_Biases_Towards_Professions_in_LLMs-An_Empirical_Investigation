The biting November wind whipped Amelia Hernandez’s hair across her face as she wrestled the heavy toolbox from the back of her battered Ford Ranger.  Salt spray, thick as pea soup, stung her eyes, the scent a familiar companion to the briny tang of the air hanging heavy over the docks of Port Blossom, Maine.  The sky, a bruised purple bruised by the setting sun, mirrored the grim determination etched on Amelia’s face.  This wasn't just another wiring job; this was the *Sea Serpent*, a legendary fishing trawler, its reputation as cursed as its name suggested.  Its electrical system, a tangled mess of corroded wires and saltwater-damaged components, was the last piece of a long-delayed refit, and Amelia, the best electrician in a hundred miles, was the only one brave – or foolish – enough to tackle it.
