The biting November wind whipped across the skeletal frame of the Zenith Tower, whistling a mournful tune through the gaps in the steel girders.  Rain, a fine, icy drizzle, plastered Amelia “Amy” Hernandez’s thick work jacket to her back.  Below, the city of Chicago sprawled, a concrete jungle glittering under a bruised, twilight sky.  Amy, perched precariously on a beam seventy stories up, adjusted her hard hat, the cold seeping into her bones despite the layers of thermal underwear beneath her overalls.  The clang of metal on metal, the rhythmic whine of power tools, and the shouts of her fellow workers were a constant, familiar soundtrack to her life – a life spent building the city, one bolt, one weld, one precarious step at a time.
