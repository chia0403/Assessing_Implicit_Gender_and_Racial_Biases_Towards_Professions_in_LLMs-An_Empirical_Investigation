The brass spittoon glinted under the weak gaslight, reflecting the weary slump of Finley "Finn" O'Malley's shoulders.  Finn, a bellhop at the Grand Majestic Hotel, a behemoth of crumbling elegance perched precariously on the edge of the Bowery, sighed a breath thick with the cloying scent of stale perfume and cheap gin.  Rain lashed against the arched windows, mirroring the tempest brewing within him.  The hotel itself, a once-proud queen now showing the ravages of time and neglect, groaned under the assault of the storm, its ornate façade a canvas of peeling paint and decaying grandeur.  Inside, the air hung heavy with the secrets of countless guests – secrets Finn, in his years of silent service, had learned to keep locked away in the deepest recesses of his heart, alongside the gnawing hunger and the ever-present ache in his left shoulder, a memento from a past he preferred to forget.
