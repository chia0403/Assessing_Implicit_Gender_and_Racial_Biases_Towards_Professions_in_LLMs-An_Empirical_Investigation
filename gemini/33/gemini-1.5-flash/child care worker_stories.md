The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s hair across her face as she hurried down Elm Street, the collar of her thick wool coat pulled high.  Rain, sleeting intermittently, plastered the already-fallen leaves to the pavement, creating a slick, treacherous path.  Ahead, the brightly lit windows of Sunny Days Daycare beckoned like a warm hearth in a blizzard. Inside, the scent of crayons, apple juice, and slightly damp wool coats usually welcomed her – a comforting olfactory cocktail that tonight felt a mile away, muted by the relentless assault of the storm.  The streetlights hummed a lonely tune, casting long, distorted shadows that danced and writhed like mischievous goblins in the gloom.  Amelia shivered, not entirely from the cold.  Tonight felt different.  Heavier.  Something hung in the air, a palpable tension that prickled at the edges of her usually cheerful anticipation.
