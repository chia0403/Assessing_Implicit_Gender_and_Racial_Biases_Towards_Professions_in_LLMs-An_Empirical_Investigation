The salt spray stung Officer Elias Thorne's face as he gripped the railing of the ferry, the churning grey water of the Puget Sound a churning mirror to the turmoil in his gut.  Rain, thick and relentless, lashed down, blurring the already indistinct lights of Bainbridge Island into hazy, shimmering smudges on the horizon.  The air, thick with the scent of brine and diesel fumes, carried a sharper, underlying tang – the metallic bite of blood.  Elias wasn't sure if it was real or just a trick of the wind and the relentless downpour, but the thought clung to him like the damp chill that seeped into his bones, a premonition as cold and heavy as the iron weight of his service revolver.  He’d been on the Seattle PD for ten years, seen his share of grim things, but this… this felt different.  This felt like the beginning of something profoundly wrong.
