The scent of lilies, heady and almost cloying, hung heavy in the air, battling with the sharp, clean aroma of rain-washed pavement outside.  Petals of every imaginable hue – from the deepest crimson of a velvety rose to the palest blush of a baby’s breath – tumbled over each other in overflowing vases and buckets lining the work benches of "Petals & Promises."  Rain lashed against the large, arched windowpanes of Amelia Rose Hawthorne’s flower shop, blurring the already fading light of a late autumn afternoon. Amelia, her hands stained a delicate green from handling hydrangeas, paused her meticulous arrangement of a funeral wreath, a single tear tracing a path through the dusting of pollen on her cheek. The impending storm mirrored the tempest brewing inside her.
