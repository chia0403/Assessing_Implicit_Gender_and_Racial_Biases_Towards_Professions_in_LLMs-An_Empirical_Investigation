The wind howled a mournful tune across the slate rooftops of Abergavenny, whipping Elias Thorne’s thick, dark hair across his face.  He wrestled with a stubborn piece of lead flashing, the biting October air already numbing his fingers despite the thick work gloves. Below, the town huddled in the valley, a patchwork quilt of muted browns and greys, smoke curling lazily from chimneys like whispers against the brooding sky.  The ancient stone houses, clinging precariously to the hillside, looked down on him from their lofty perches, silent witnesses to Elias’s precarious dance with gravity.  This wasn't just a job; it was a conversation with the ages, a testament to enduring craftsmanship against the relentless assault of the elements.  He grunted, finally securing the flashing, and shifted his weight, the creak of the old timber beneath him a familiar soundtrack to his life.
