The biting November wind whipped Dr. Eleanor Vance’s tailored coat around her legs as she hurried across the cobblestone street, the scent of woodsmoke and roasting chestnuts doing little to warm her chilled fingers.  Rain, a relentless drizzle that had been falling since dawn, plastered her dark hair to her scalp. Above, the gothic spires of St. Jude’s Cathedral pierced the grey sky like skeletal fingers, their shadows stretching long and skeletal across the narrow, gaslamp-lit streets of Blackwood.  Blackwood, a town steeped in history, whispers, and a surprisingly high number of patients with inexplicable toothaches – a fact that kept Dr. Eleanor Vance busier than she’d ever imagined when she'd traded the bright lights of London for the brooding charm of this ancient Welsh town.  Tonight, however, the usual hum of her practice felt oddly subdued, replaced by a prickling unease that clung to her like the damp air.
