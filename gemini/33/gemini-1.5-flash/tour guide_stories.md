The humid air hung heavy and sweet, thick with the scent of frangipani and exhaust fumes.  Sweat beaded on the forehead of Elias Thorne, despite the early morning hour.  He adjusted the brim of his khaki hat, its faded emblem – a stylized silhouette of Angkor Wat – barely visible beneath the encroaching jungle green.  He surveyed his small, expectant group, their faces a mixture of anticipation and slightly bewildered heatstroke.  Five tourists, each clutching a water bottle and a map detailing the sprawling, enigmatic ruins of Koh Ker, stood before him, waiting for Elias, the self-proclaimed "Keeper of Khmer Secrets," to unlock the mysteries of this forgotten kingdom.  The sun, already a malevolent eye in the cloudless sky, promised a day of unrelenting heat in the heart of northern Cambodia.
