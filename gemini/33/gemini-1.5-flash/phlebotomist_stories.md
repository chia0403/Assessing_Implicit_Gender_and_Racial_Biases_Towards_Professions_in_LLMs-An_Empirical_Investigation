The fluorescent lights of the Meridian Medical Center hummed a monotonous tune, a counterpoint to the rhythmic *thump-thump-thump* of Amelia Katherine Finch's own heart.  Amelia, or Amy as most people called her, adjusted the tourniquet on the frail wrist of her patient, a woman whose skin was paper-thin and translucent over bone.  The sterile scent of antiseptic battled with the faint, cloying sweetness of lilies – a funeral home’s offering, judging by the hushed sobs emanating from the waiting area just beyond the flimsy curtain.  Outside, the Arizona sun beat down relentlessly, a stark contrast to the cool, clinical atmosphere of the small, understaffed phlebotomy lab.  This wasn't just another Tuesday; this was the kind of Tuesday that made Amy question her career choice, a Tuesday saturated with the quiet desperation of those facing their mortality.
