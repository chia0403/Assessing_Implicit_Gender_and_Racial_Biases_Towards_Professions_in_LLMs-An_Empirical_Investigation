The stale scent of sawdust and forgotten dreams clung to the air of the Blackwood Theatre, a place where ambition had once thrived, now choked by neglect.  Dust motes, illuminated by the single bare bulb hanging precariously above, danced in the shaft of weak sunlight piercing the grimy skylight.  Isabelle “Izzy” Blackwood, great-granddaughter of the theatre's founder and a woman whose fiery spirit burned brighter than any spotlight, ran a calloused hand over the chipped paint of a faded proscenium arch.  The peeling paint mirrored the state of her own life – once vibrant, now crumbling under the weight of mounting debts and a string of disastrous productions.  This time, however, Izzy was determined. This time, the Blackwood Theatre, and perhaps even Izzy herself, would rise from the ashes.
