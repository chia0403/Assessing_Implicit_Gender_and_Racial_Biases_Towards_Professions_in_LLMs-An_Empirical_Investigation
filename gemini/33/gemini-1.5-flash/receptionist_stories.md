The fluorescent lights of Sterling & Steele hummed a monotonous tune, a counterpoint to the incessant tapping of Penelope "Penny" Featherstonehaugh's perfectly manicured fingernails against the polished mahogany of her desk.  Dust motes, illuminated by the harsh glare, danced in the stagnant air of the opulent, if slightly dated, reception area.  Penny, her emerald green dress a vibrant splash of color against the beige and brown monotony of the law firm's interior, sighed.  The scent of old leather and expensive coffee hung heavy, a perfume only slightly less suffocating than the palpable tension that clung to the air like a damp shroud.  Outside, the relentless London rain hammered against the vast Georgian windows, mirroring the tempest brewing within her own breast.  This wasn't just another Monday morning; it was the day the notorious Blackwood case was set to explode, and Penny, unwitting conductor of the impending chaos, braced herself.
