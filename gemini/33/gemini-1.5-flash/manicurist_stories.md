The chipped turquoise paint on the salon door flaked like old nail polish, a fitting metaphor for Evangeline “Evie” Lancaster’s current mood.  Evie, a manicurist with hands as nimble as a hummingbird's wings and a heart currently as heavy as a lead weight, sighed, pushing a stray strand of auburn hair behind her ear.  The air inside “Lacquer & Lore,” her tiny, perpetually under-funded salon nestled on the corner of Bleecker and Grove Streets in the West Village, hummed with the low thrum of a nearly-dead fluorescent light and the faint, sweet scent of acetone.  Rain lashed against the windowpanes, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her.  The only other sound was the rhythmic tick-tock of a vintage clock, counting down the seconds to a deadline Evie wasn't sure she could meet.
