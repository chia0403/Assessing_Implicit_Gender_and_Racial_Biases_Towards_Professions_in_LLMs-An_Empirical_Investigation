The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia “Mia” Hernandez’s hands, the lukewarm chamomile tea doing little to soothe the icy knot in her stomach.  Rain lashed against the panoramic window of her Brooklyn loft, blurring the already hazy cityscape into a watercolour smudge of greys and inky blues.  Empty pizza boxes and discarded sketches littered the worn wooden floorboards, a testament to the all-nighter she’d just pulled wrestling with a particularly recalcitrant logo redesign for a fledgling kombucha company.  The deadline loomed, a monstrous, deadline-shaped kraken threatening to drag her under with its tentacles of unmet expectations and crippling self-doubt.  The city outside mirrored her internal chaos; a tempestuous symphony of sirens and honking horns punctuating the rhythmic drumming of the rain.  Mia sighed, the steam from her tea momentarily obscuring the vibrant, yet unfinished, design mocking her from the glowing screen of her iMac.
