The flickering neon sign of the "Lucky Dragon" noodle bar cast a sickly yellow glow across the rain-slicked alley.  Dr. Evelyn Reed, Ph.D., shivered, pulling her worn trench coat tighter around her.  The air hung heavy with the scent of exhaust fumes, cheap cigarettes, and something else… something metallic and faintly sweet, a scent that pricked at her professional senses even before she saw the body.  It lay sprawled amidst overflowing bins, a stark contrast to the vibrant, chaotic life pulsing just beyond the alley's shadowed maw.  Evelyn's breath hitched. This wasn't a typical late-night call-out; this felt… personal. The rain began to fall harder, mirroring the storm brewing inside her.
