The chipped Formica tabletop felt clammy under Elara Jean Blackwood's fingertips.  Elara, or "Ellie" as the regulars affectionately – and sometimes less affectionately – called her, braced herself against the pre-dinner rush at "The Gilded Lily," a diner clinging precariously to the edge of the crumbling docks in the perpetually twilight city of Porthaven. The air hung thick with the scent of stale coffee, frying onions, and the briny tang of the sea, a perfume as familiar to her as her own skin. Rain lashed against the grimy windows, blurring the already indistinct shapes of the fishing boats bobbing in the harbour, their masts like skeletal fingers against the bruised purple sky.  Inside, the fluorescent lights hummed a discordant counterpoint to the clatter of cutlery and the low murmur of conversations, a symphony of urban fatigue played out nightly in the heart of Porthaven's forgotten corner.
