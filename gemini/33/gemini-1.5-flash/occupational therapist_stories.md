The salt spray stung Eleanor Vance’s face as the ferry churned its way across the churning grey expanse of the Puget Sound.  Rain, a relentless drizzle that seemed woven into the very fabric of the Pacific Northwest, plastered her dark brown hair to her forehead.  Below, the emerald-green islands, shrouded in mist, looked like slumbering giants, their peaks barely visible through the gloom.  Eleanor, a seasoned occupational therapist with ten years under her belt at Seattle Children's Hospital, clutched her worn leather briefcase, its contents – brightly colored therapy tools and a half-eaten granola bar – jostling slightly with the boat’s rocking motion.  She was headed to Bainbridge Island, a picturesque escape for many, but for Eleanor, it was anything but a vacation.  This wasn't a relaxing getaway; it was a mission.  A mission to help a child, a child unlike any she had encountered before.
