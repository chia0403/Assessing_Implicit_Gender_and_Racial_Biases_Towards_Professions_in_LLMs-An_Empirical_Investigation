The wind, a rasping baritone, howled across the desolate expanse of the Broken Tooth Ranch, whipping dust devils into frenzied dances across the parched earth.  The sun, a malevolent eye in the brassy sky, beat down mercilessly on the skeletal remains of long-dead mesquite trees.  Even the vultures circling high above seemed hesitant to descend, the air itself shimmering with the oppressive heat.  This was no place for the faint of heart, and certainly not a place for a man to be alone, especially not a man like Silas "Buck" Blackwood.  Buck, owner and operator of the Broken Tooth, squinted against the glare, his weathered face a roadmap of sun and hardship, etched with the deep lines of a life lived hard against the unforgiving landscape of West Texas.  The air hung thick with the scent of dry earth and impending storm, a fitting atmosphere for the trouble brewing on the horizon.
