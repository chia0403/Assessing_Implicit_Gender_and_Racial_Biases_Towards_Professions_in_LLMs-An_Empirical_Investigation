The rain hammered against the arched windows of the Grand Majestic Hotel, each drop a tiny, insistent drumbeat against the opulent silence of the lobby.  Water cascaded down the sculpted cherubs flanking the entrance, their marble faces gleaming under the subdued light.  Inside, a fire crackled merrily in the vast hearth, casting a warm glow on the polished mahogany furniture and the impeccably dressed guests who flitted about like gilded butterflies.  For Aurelia Genevieve Dubois, however, the comforting warmth was merely a backdrop to the frantic flurry of her evening.  The concierge desk, a gleaming behemoth of polished brass and dark wood, was her battlefield, a strategic command center from which she orchestrated the seemingly impossible, ensuring the smooth sailing of every guest's stay, no matter how demanding.  Tonight, however, the impossible seemed to have multiplied exponentially.
