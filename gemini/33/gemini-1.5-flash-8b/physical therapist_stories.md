Rain lashed against the corrugated iron roof of the clinic, a relentless drumming that echoed the frantic rhythm in Dr. Eleanor Vance's chest.  The small, cluttered physiotherapy practice, nestled in the heart of the windswept coastal town of Seabreeze, usually offered a soothing balm of quiet, a refuge from the tempestuous world outside.  Today, however, the tranquility was shattered.  

The setting was a stark contrast to the calming blue of the ocean visible through the grimy windows.  Dust motes danced in the single, flickering fluorescent light above the reception desk, illuminating the haphazard arrangement of brochures and forgotten appointment slips.  The scent of antiseptic mingled with the salty tang of the sea air, a familiar, yet strangely unsettling combination.  Eleanor, her tailored trousers dampened from the relentless downpour,  paced the cramped space between the treatment tables.  The rhythmic creak of the worn floorboards amplified the turmoil in her mind.
