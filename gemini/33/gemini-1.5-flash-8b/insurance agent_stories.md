The flickering fluorescent lights of the "Reliable Reinsurance" office hummed, a monotonous counterpoint to the rhythmic tapping of Amelia Blackwood's fingers on the worn keyboard.  Dust motes danced in the afternoon sunbeams slicing through the grime-coated windows, illuminating the overflowing file cabinets and the mountain of paperwork that seemed to perpetually climb.  Amelia, a woman whose sharp features were softened by the tired circles under her eyes, stared at the screen, her brow furrowed in concentration.  The case file, labelled "The Copper Kettle Estate", stubbornly resisted analysis.  This wasn't a typical claim.  The property, a sprawling Victorian mansion nestled deep within the mist-shrouded Blackwood Forest, whispered tales of secrets, and the previous owner, a recluse named Alistair Blackwood—Amelia’s great-uncle—had left behind a legacy of cryptic notes and unanswered questions.  It was a job she hadn't anticipated, a case that felt as unsettling as the relentless drizzle that had begun outside, mirroring the storm brewing inside her.
