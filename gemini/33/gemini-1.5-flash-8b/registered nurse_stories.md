The flickering fluorescent lights of the emergency room hummed a monotonous lullaby, a counterpoint to the frantic whispers and the rhythmic thud of a heart monitor.  Rain hammered against the windowpanes of St. Jude's Hospital, mirroring the storm brewing inside Amelia Grace Blackwood, a registered nurse with five years of experience navigating the treacherous terrain of the ER.  The air, thick with the antiseptic smell of disinfectant and the metallic tang of blood, felt heavy, pressing down on her like a suffocating blanket.  Tonight, the pressure felt particularly intense.  Four patients crowded the resuscitation bay, each a microcosm of the city's relentless pulse, each a battle fought in the desperate stillness between life and death.  Amelia, her uniform stained with the faintest traces of crimson, felt a familiar knot of anxiety tighten in her stomach.  She was, as always, a small cog in a vast and terrifying machine.
