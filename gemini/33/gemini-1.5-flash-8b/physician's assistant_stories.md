The humid air hung heavy, thick with the scent of jasmine and simmering street food.  Sweat beaded on Amelia Rodriguez-Garcia's forehead, blurring the already faded paint of the clinic walls.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of sunlight slicing through the dusty window, illuminating a chaotic scene: overflowing medical charts, half-eaten bowls of rice and beans, and the anxious whispers of patients in the waiting area.  This wasn't the sterile, gleaming clinic of her textbooks; this was Dr. Hernandez's mobile clinic in the heart of the burgeoning Barrio Esperanza, a place where poverty and resilience intertwined.  Amelia, a Physician's Assistant with a sharp mind and a compassionate heart, knew every nuance of this clinic, every hurried step and hushed conversation.  Today, though, even the usual cacophony felt amplified, laced with an unusual tension.
