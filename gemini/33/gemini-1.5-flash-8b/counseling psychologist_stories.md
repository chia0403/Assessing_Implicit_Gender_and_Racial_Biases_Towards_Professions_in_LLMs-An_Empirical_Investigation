The chipped Formica countertop gleamed under the harsh fluorescent lights of Dr. Eleanor Vance's office.  A single, wilting geranium sat in a ceramic pot beside a stack of file folders, each one a silent testament to the anxieties and traumas that had passed through these doors.  Eleanor, with her tired but kind eyes and a scattering of grey hairs woven into her neatly braided bun, traced the faint lines etched into the surface of her desk.  Rain lashed against the windowpanes, mirroring the tumultuous emotions churning within her as she waited.  Outside, the city thrummed with a life she felt increasingly disconnected from.  It was a Tuesday, the same as any other, and yet, this one felt pregnant with unspoken dread.  Her meticulously organized life, a fortress built of schedules and structure, seemed to be crumbling around her.  The steady beat of the city was a relentless percussion, a soundtrack to the relentless stream of human suffering she saw in her office.  The scent of antiseptic and stale coffee hung heavy in the air, a subtle perfume of the daily grind.
