Rain hammered against the grimy, soot-stained windows of the small, cramped office, mimicking the relentless pounding in Amelia Blackwood's head.  The fluorescent lights buzzed, casting a sickly yellow glow over the overflowing file cabinets and the scattered legal documents that littered the tiny workspace.  Amelia, a paralegal at the perpetually understaffed Sterling & Stone law firm, hunched over a legal pad, her pen scratching against the paper in a frantic rhythm.  The air was thick with the scent of stale coffee and desperation.  This was not just any Tuesday.  This was Tuesday, the day the crucial evidence for the Peterson v. Amalgamated Industries case was due to be submitted, and Amelia was the only one in the office who seemed to realize the impending deadline was looming. Outside, the cityscape of Gotham City, perpetually shrouded in a thick fog, mirrored the anxiety clouding Amelia's mind.
