The biting wind whipped across the cobbled plaza, stinging Sergeant Alistair Finch's cheeks and rattling the iron bars of the guard post.  Dust devils, stirred up by unseen forces, danced around the crumbling statues of long-dead emperors.  Above, the ochre sun bled into the bruised purple sky, painting the city of Aethelgard in hues of melancholy.  The air, thick with the smell of roasting meats and the lingering scent of something vaguely floral – perhaps jasmine, perhaps something far stranger – hung heavy, hinting at the secrets whispered within the shadowed alleyways that radiated outward from the central square.  Aethelgard, a city built on the ashes of a forgotten war, was a place where shadows held their breath and the past clung to the present like a shroud.  Alistair, a grizzled man of thirty-five with eyes the colour of storm clouds, stared out at the scene, his gaze unreadable, his duty as a night guard a weary burden.  The city was his domain, his responsibility, and tonight, it held its breath, waiting.
