The biting wind whipped across the desolate plains of the Badlands, stinging Elias Thorne's exposed cheeks.  Dust, the color of dried blood, swirled around the rusted behemoth of a bulldozer, the "Ironclad," its tracks gouging grooves into the parched earth.  A lone hawk, its silhouette a razor-edged shadow against the bruised sky, circled overhead, its keen eyes scanning the barren landscape for any sign of movement.  Elias, a man carved from the same unforgiving stone as the land itself, hunched deeper into his worn leather jacket.  The Badlands, a place where time seemed to have lost its grip and nature's fury reigned supreme, was his crucible.  He was a heavy equipment operator, a master of the machines that sculpted this brutal beauty, and today, he faced a task that threatened to consume him.  The sun, a molten orb sinking towards the horizon, painted the sky in fiery hues of orange and purple, casting long, skeletal shadows that danced with the dust devils.  The air hung thick with the metallic tang of oil and the scent of impending storm.
