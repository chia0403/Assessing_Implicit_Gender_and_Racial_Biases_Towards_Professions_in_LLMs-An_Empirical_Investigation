The biting wind whipped Amelia Nightingale's crimson coat around her as she hurried down the cobbled alleyway, the scent of brine and rotting seaweed clinging to the air like a persistent fog.  Above, the grey London sky, perpetually overcast, threatened rain, mirroring the mood settling over the city.  Amelia, proprietor of "Blooms by the Bay," a small, vibrant flower shop nestled at the foot of the treacherous cliff overlooking the churning waters of the Thames, adjusted her spectacles and pulled her scarf tighter around her face.  The shop, a cheerful riot of colour despite the bleakness outside, beckoned her inward with the promise of sunshine and fresh-cut blooms.  Today, however, a different kind of bloom, a disturbing one, was threatening to take root in her heart.
