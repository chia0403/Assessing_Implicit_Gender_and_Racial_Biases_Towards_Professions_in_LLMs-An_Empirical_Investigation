Rain lashed against the grimy windows of the Community Corrections office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside Elias Thorne.  Fluorescent lights buzzed overhead, casting a sickly yellow glow on the overflowing in-trays and chipped Formica desktops. The air hung thick with the scent of stale coffee and desperation, a familiar aroma Elias had come to associate with Monday mornings.  Outside, the dilapidated brick buildings of downtown Port Blossom, Oregon, shimmered under the downpour.  This once-thriving fishing town now clung precariously to its frayed edges, a community battered by economic hardship and the opioid crisis. Elias, his worn leather briefcase clutched tightly in his hand, stared out at the deluge, a knot tightening in his stomach.  He had a full caseload of individuals teetering on the edge, and today, he had to pay a visit to one of his most volatile probationers.
