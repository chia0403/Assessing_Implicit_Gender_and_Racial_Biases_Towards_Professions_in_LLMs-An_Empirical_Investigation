The salt-laced wind whipped Amelia Thorne's auburn braid across her face as she adjusted the easel perched precariously on the cliff edge. Below, the turquoise waters of the Aegean Sea crashed against the volcanic rock of Santorini, sending plumes of white spray into the air.  The stark whitewashed houses of Oia, clinging to the caldera's rim like barnacles to a hull, glowed in the late afternoon sun, their blue domes mirroring the vibrant sky. Amelia, her palette a riot of blues, whites, and ochres, squinted, trying to capture the ephemeral quality of the light, the way it transformed the landscape into something both ethereal and intensely real.  This was her third attempt to capture this particular view, this particular light, and she felt the familiar prickle of frustration beneath her skin.  It was always like chasing a ghost, this painting of light.
