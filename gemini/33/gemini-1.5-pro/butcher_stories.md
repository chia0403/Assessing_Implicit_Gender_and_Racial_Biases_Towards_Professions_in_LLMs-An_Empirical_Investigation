The biting wind whipped down Cobblestone Alley, swirling stray leaves and greasy butcher paper into miniature cyclones.  The flickering gaslights of Grimsborough cast long, skeletal shadows against the slick, rain-washed cobblestones.  Inside "Finnegan's Fine Meats," a warm, savory aroma hung in the air, a stark contrast to the chill outside.  Clementine "Clem" Ainsworth, her auburn hair tucked beneath a stained white cap, sharpened her cleaver with a rhythmic scrape against the steel, her gaze fixed on the carcass of a prize Berkshire hog hanging from a gleaming hook.  It was a Tuesday, typically slow, but tonight, a peculiar disquiet hung in the air, thicker than the sawdust on the floor.
