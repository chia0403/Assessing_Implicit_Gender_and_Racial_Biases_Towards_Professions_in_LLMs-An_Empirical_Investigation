The rain hammered against the leaded glass windows of Dr. Eleanor Vance's office, mimicking the relentless thrum of anxiety she felt tightening in her chest.  Her office, nestled in a converted Victorian house on a quiet, tree-lined street in the historic district of Oakhaven, Illinois, usually offered a sanctuary of calm.  The scent of lavender from the diffuser, the soft glow of the salt lamp, and the comforting weight of her worn leather armchair usually conspired to create a space of tranquility. But tonight, even the crackling fire in the hearth couldn't dispel the unsettling premonition that clung to her like the damp chill seeping in from the outside.  The grandfather clock in the hallway chimed eight times, the sound swallowed by the roar of the storm, just as her new patient, a Mr. Silas Blackwood, was due to arrive.
