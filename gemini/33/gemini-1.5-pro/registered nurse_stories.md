The biting November wind whipped around Amelia Grace Hawthorne's ankles as she hurried across the deserted parking lot of St. Jude's Memorial Hospital.  Fluorescent lights buzzed from the Emergency Room entrance, casting a sickly yellow glow against the freshly fallen snow. St. Jude's, a sprawling brick behemoth nestled in the heart of rural Vermont, was usually a haven of quiet efficiency. Tonight, however, an unsettling tension hung in the air, thick as the swirling snowflakes.  Amelia, her breath misting before her, clutched her thermos of lukewarm coffee tighter, a grim premonition tightening her chest. It wasn't just the weather that felt ominous; it was the unnerving silence that preceded what she instinctively knew would be a long and difficult night.
