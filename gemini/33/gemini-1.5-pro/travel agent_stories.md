The insistent chirping of a gecko, hidden somewhere amidst the overflowing philodendrons, was the only sound that dared interrupt the stillness of the tropical night.  Humidity hung heavy in the air, thick and sweet like overripe mango.  Eliza Jane Holloway, her blonde hair plastered to her forehead with perspiration, sat on the veranda of her small, brightly painted office, overlooking the turquoise waters of the Caribbean Sea.  "Island Escapes Travel," the sign above her door proclaimed in cheerful, hand-painted letters.  Eliza Jane, however, wasn't feeling particularly cheerful.  The idyllic setting of Isla Mujeres, Mexico, usually a balm to her soul, felt oppressive tonight.  The rhythmic crash of waves against the shore, normally a soothing lullaby, sounded like a mournful dirge.  She swirled the melting ice in her glass of tequila, the clinking a sharp counterpoint to the gecko's song.  Her usually brimming appointment book lay open on the wicker table beside her, starkly empty.  Three months. Three months since her last client, and the vibrant colours of her island paradise were starting to fade into a monochrome canvas of worry.
