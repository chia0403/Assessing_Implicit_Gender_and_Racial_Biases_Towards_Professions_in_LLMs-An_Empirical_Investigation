The salt-laced wind whipped Amelia “Mia” Reyes’s charcoal-smudged braid across her face as she perched precariously on the cliff edge. Below, the churning turquoise waters of the Aegean Sea crashed against the volcanic rock of Santorini, the whitewashed village of Oia clinging to the caldera rim like a flock of startled doves. Mia’s canvas, propped against a weathered rock, remained stubbornly blank.  The breathtaking vista, a postcard-perfect panorama of cobalt sky and dazzling white architecture, seemed to mock her artistic block. For weeks, ever since arriving on this idyllic Greek island for her artist residency, the inspiration that usually flowed through her like the island’s famed wine had dried up, leaving her feeling as barren and brittle as the volcanic landscape itself.
