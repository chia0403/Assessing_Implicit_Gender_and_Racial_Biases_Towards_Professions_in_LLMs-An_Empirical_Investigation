Cobwebs, thick as a programmer's beard after a three-day coding sprint, draped from the exposed rafters of the converted workspace. The air hung heavy with the scent of ozone and burnt coffee, the remnants of Amelia's latest all-nighter.  Here, in a forgotten corner of her grandmother's Victorian house in the sleepy coastal town of Port Blossom, Maine, Amelia was building something extraordinary, something that could change everything.  A faint glow emanated from the jury-rigged server rack humming beside her desk, its blinking lights reflecting in the worry lines etched deep into her brow. Outside, the wind howled, mirroring the storm brewing within her code.
