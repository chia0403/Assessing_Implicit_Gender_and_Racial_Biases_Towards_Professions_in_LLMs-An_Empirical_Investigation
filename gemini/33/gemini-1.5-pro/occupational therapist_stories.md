The biting November wind whipped around Eleanor Vance, stinging her cheeks as she hurried across the cobblestone courtyard of the sprawling Alderwood Rehabilitation Center.  Fallen leaves, the color of burnt umber and ochre, swirled around her ankles. The air smelled of woodsmoke and damp earth.  Alderwood, nestled in the foothills of the Berkshire Mountains in western Massachusetts, was a place of quiet serenity, a sanctuary for patients recovering from strokes, spinal cord injuries, and other debilitating conditions.  Eleanor, the center's lead occupational therapist, loved the tranquility of the place, the way the afternoon sun filtered through the towering pines, casting long shadows across the manicured lawns.  But today, a sense of unease prickled at her. It had started with a phone call that morning, a cryptic message from a former patient, and it had clung to her like the damp November air ever since.
