The biting wind whipped across the desolate expanse of the Athabasca oil sands, stinging Rosalind "Roz" Petrova's cheeks even through the thick balaclava she wore under her hard hat.  Dawn barely cracked the horizon, painting the sky in bruised hues of purple and grey.  Giant floodlights cast an eerie glow across the frozen landscape, illuminating the behemoth machines scattered across the site like slumbering metal dinosaurs.  Roz, perched high in the cab of her CAT 797F haul truck, surveyed the scene with a practiced eye.  The air vibrated with the low thrum of idling engines, a constant background noise in this isolated world of steel, diesel, and bitumen.  Today was blast day, and the air crackled with a different kind of anticipation, a mix of excitement and wariness that Roz had come to know well over her fifteen years working these unforgiving sands.
