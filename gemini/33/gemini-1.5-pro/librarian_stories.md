The scent of old paper and beeswax hung heavy in the air, a comforting aroma Eleanor Vance breathed in deeply as she unlocked the heavy oak doors of the Whisperwind Library.  Dawn painted the tall, arched windows a soft lavender, illuminating swirling dust motes that danced in the otherwise still air. Nestled amongst the rolling hills of the sleepy Vermont town of Alderhaven, the library was a sanctuary, a silent sentinel against the relentless march of time.  Eleanor, her silver hair pulled back in a neat bun, stepped inside, the click of her sensible heels echoing on the polished wooden floor.  Outside, the world was waking; birds chirped, a distant rooster crowed, and the gentle rush of the Whisperwind River could be heard meandering through the valley. Inside, however, a different kind of magic stirred. Rows upon rows of books, bound in leather and cloth, stood like watchful guardians, their spines whispering forgotten stories.  Today, Eleanor knew, one of those stories was waiting to be discovered.
