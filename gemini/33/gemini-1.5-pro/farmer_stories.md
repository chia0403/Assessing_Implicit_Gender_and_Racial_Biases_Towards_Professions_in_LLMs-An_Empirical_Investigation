The wind, sharp and smelling of approaching rain, whipped across the parched fields of Abernathy Farm, tugging at the brim of Elias Thatcher's worn straw hat.  Dust devils danced across the cracked earth, swirling past the skeletal remains of withered corn stalks.  Elias, his weathered face etched with lines as deep as the furrows in his fields, squinted at the bruised purple sky.  Abernathy Farm, nestled in the heart of the Oklahoma panhandle, hadn't seen a decent drop of rain in close to four months.  The usually vibrant green landscape had transformed into a muted tapestry of browns and greys, a testament to the relentless sun and the dwindling hope that clung to the air like the dust itself.
