Rain lashed against the grimy windows of The Daily Clarion, blurring the neon glow of Benny's Diner across the street.  Inside, the air hung thick with the smell of stale coffee and deadline desperation.  Amelia "Mia" Rodriguez, her dark curls plastered to her forehead, hunched over a flickering computer screen, chasing a story that felt as elusive as a wisp of smoke.  Asherton, Ohio, a town clinging to the rust belt like a barnacle to a sinking ship, wasn't exactly a hotbed of breaking news.  Tonight, however, something felt different.  A low hum of unease vibrated through the town, a tension Mia could practically taste in the lukewarm coffee she'd been nursing for the past three hours.  The phone on her desk jangled, its shrill ring cutting through the rhythmic clatter of keys.  Sheriff Brody, a man whose voice could gravel a driveway, was on the other end. "Rodriguez," he barked, "Got something you might wanna see down at the old Blackwood Mill."
