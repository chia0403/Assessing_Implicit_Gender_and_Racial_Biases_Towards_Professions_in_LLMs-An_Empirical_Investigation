The opulent lobby of the Hotel Magnifique shimmered, a kaleidoscope of polished marble, cascading crystal chandeliers, and the hushed murmur of affluent guests.  Rain lashed against the arched windows overlooking the Place Vendôme in Paris, reflecting the glittering lights of the square back into the hotel.  A fire crackled merrily in the grand fireplace, casting dancing shadows across the Aubusson rug.  This was Genevieve Dubois’ domain.  From her mahogany-and-brass concierge desk, she orchestrated the desires, whims, and occasional emergencies of the hotel’s discerning clientele.  Tonight, however, a strange stillness hung in the air, a premonition that the storm brewing outside was not the only one about to break.
