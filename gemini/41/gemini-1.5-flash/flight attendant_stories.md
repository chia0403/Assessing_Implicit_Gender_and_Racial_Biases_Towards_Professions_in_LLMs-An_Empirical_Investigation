The recycled air hung thick with the scent of stale coffee and desperation, a familiar perfume in the cramped galley of Flight 872 from Heathrow to Johannesburg.  Rain lashed against the fuselage, blurring the already indistinct lights of the city below.  Amelia Grace Hawthorne, her name a stark contrast to the chaos swirling around her, meticulously polished a already gleaming wine glass.  Her reflection, framed by the tiny galley window, showed a woman barely holding it together behind a facade of professional calm.  The unruly knot of her auburn braid betrayed the tension simmering beneath her crisp, impeccably pressed uniform.  Outside, the storm raged; inside, a different kind of tempest was brewing.  Amelia could feel it, a low hum of unease that vibrated through the cabin, a premonition as sharp and insistent as the rhythmic thump of the plane's engines.
