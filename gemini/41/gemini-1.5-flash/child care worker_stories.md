The chipped paint of the "Sunshine Smiles Daycare" sign flaked onto the overgrown lawn like a melancholic confetti.  A rusty swing set creaked a mournful tune in the damp October air, mirroring the sigh escaping the lips of twenty-two-year-old Elara Genevieve Hayes as she wrestled a recalcitrant toddler into a miniature fireman’s coat.  Inside, the daycare buzzed with a chaotic symphony of tiny voices, the scent of peanut butter and impending naptime thick in the air.  Elara, with her perpetually stained apron and a smile that felt more practiced than genuine these days, surveyed her domain – a slightly dilapidated Victorian house that had seen better days, and housed far too much sugar-fueled energy for its own good.
