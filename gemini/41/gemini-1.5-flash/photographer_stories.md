The salt spray stung Elara Vance's face as she wrestled the tripod into submission, the wind threatening to send her expensive camera hurtling into the churning grey expanse of the Atlantic.  October's fury had descended upon the craggy coast of Cornwall, England, transforming the usually picturesque fishing village of Portwenn into a tempestuous drama unfolding before her very eyes.  Waves the colour of bruised plums crashed against the ancient harbour walls, their roar a deafening counterpoint to the mournful cry of gulls battling the gale.  The air, thick with the scent of brine and seaweed, whipped Elara's dark hair across her cheeks, obscuring her vision momentarily, but she wouldn't flinch.  Tonight, she was determined to capture the untamed beauty of the storm.
