The salt spray stung Eleanor Vance’s face as she wrestled the stubborn lobster trap onto the deck of the *Sea Serpent*.  The late afternoon sun, a bruised plum sinking below the jagged cliffs of the Maine coast, cast long shadows across the churning grey water.  Eleanor, a registered dietician with a penchant for sensible shoes and an even stronger penchant for adventure, grimaced.  The rhythmic creak of the boat and the cries of gulls overhead were a far cry from the sterile, clinical environment of her usual workplace – the bustling city hospital in Portland. This, however, wasn't a vacation.  This was research, of a particularly pungent variety.  The air hung heavy with the smells of brine, seaweed, and something distinctly fishy that hinted at the reason for her unexpectedly rugged fieldwork: a bizarre outbreak of what the local fishermen were calling "the Crabby Cravings."
