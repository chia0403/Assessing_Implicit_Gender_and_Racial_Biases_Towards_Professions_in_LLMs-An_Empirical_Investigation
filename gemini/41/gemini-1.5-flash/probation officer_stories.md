The chipped paint of the probation office’s waiting room peeled like sunburnt skin, mirroring the flaking facade of Elias Thorne’s own carefully constructed composure.  Rain lashed against the grimy windows of the municipal building, a relentless drumming that matched the anxiety thrumming beneath Elias’s tailored suit.  He absently straightened the already immaculate knot of his tie, the mahogany desk looming before him like a judge’s bench.  The air hung thick with the scent of stale coffee and desperation, a familiar perfume for Elias Thorne, Probation Officer, a man who dealt daily in second chances – and the frequent failure thereof.  His current case, a repeat offender named Marcus Bellweather, was proving to be particularly thorny, a weed stubbornly refusing to be uprooted from the fertile soil of recidivism.  The clock ticked, each second a heavy, judgmental beat in the oppressive silence.
