The biting wind whipped Dr. Eleanor Vance’s dark hair across her face as she wrestled the heavy oak door of Blackwood Manor open.  December’s grip on the Scottish Highlands was unforgiving; the air hung thick with the scent of pine and damp earth, a stark contrast to the sterile aroma of the hospital she’d just left. Blackwood Manor loomed before her, a gothic behemoth silhouetted against the bruised purple twilight.  Gargoyles perched menacingly on the eaves, their stone faces seeming to leer in the fading light.  Snow, freshly fallen, dusted the manicured lawns, creating a deceptively peaceful scene that did little to mask the unsettling atmosphere emanating from within the ancient walls.  This wasn't a routine house call; this was a summons steeped in whispered anxieties and a family history as dark as the approaching night.
