The stale air hung thick with the scent of cheap coffee and desperation.  Fluorescent lights hummed a discordant symphony above Amelia “Mia” Hernandez, casting long, skeletal shadows across her cluttered desk.  Empty energy drink cans, discarded takeout containers, and a half-eaten bag of pretzels formed a haphazard barricade against the encroaching chaos of her life, mirroring the state of the ancient, groaning server room that lay just beyond the doorway of her cramped office at DataStream Solutions. Mia, a systems analyst whose caffeine dependency rivaled that of a small nation, rubbed her tired eyes, the harsh glare of the monitor reflecting in their depths.  The flickering cursor on her screen seemed to mock her, a tiny, relentless pulse against the backdrop of a deadline looming larger than the infamous Y2K bug.  Tonight, the fate of DataStream's biggest client, a multinational conglomerate known only as "The Consortium," rested squarely on her weary shoulders.  Outside, the relentless Chicago wind howled a mournful ballad, a fitting soundtrack to the digital apocalypse threatening to unfold within the bowels of DataStream.
