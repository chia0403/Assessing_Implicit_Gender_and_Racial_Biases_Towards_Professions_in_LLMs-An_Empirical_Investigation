The chipped paint of the windowsill was cold against Dr. Eleanor Vance’s cheek.  October’s wind, a spiteful, whispering thing, rattled the ancient panes of the Northwood High School library, a sound that mirrored the nervous tremor in her own hand as she sipped lukewarm coffee.  The library itself, a cavernous space smelling faintly of aging paper and floor polish, was usually a sanctuary.  Today, however, it felt like a pressure cooker, the air thick with unspoken anxieties.  Outside, the late afternoon sun cast long shadows across the manicured lawns, a stark contrast to the storm brewing within the school walls. Eleanor, a school psychologist with ten years of experience navigating the turbulent waters of teenage angst, felt the familiar knot of dread tighten in her stomach. This wasn't just another Tuesday; this felt different.  This felt… ominous.
