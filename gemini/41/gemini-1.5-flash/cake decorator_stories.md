The aroma of almond extract and vanilla hung heavy in the air, a sweet, cloying perfume that clung to Amelia Grace Hawthorne like a second skin.  Dust motes, illuminated by the single bare bulb hanging above her workbench, danced in the late afternoon sun slanting through the grimy window of her tiny shop, "Sweet Surrender."  Amelia, her flour-dusted apron stained with a kaleidoscope of vibrant food coloring, meticulously piped delicate buttercream roses onto a three-tiered wedding cake the size of a small child.  Outside, the relentless rhythm of the city – the blare of horns, the rumble of buses – was a muted counterpoint to the gentle scratch of her piping bag against the smooth fondant.  But even the comforting routine of cake decorating couldn't quite mask the tremor of anxiety that thrummed beneath her fingertips.  Tonight, this cake wasn't just another order; it was a gamble, a potential lifeline in a business teetering on the brink of collapse.
