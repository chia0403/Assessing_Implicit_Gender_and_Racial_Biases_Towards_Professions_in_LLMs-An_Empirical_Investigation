The chipped porcelain teacup warmed Amelia “Mia” Hernandez’s hands, a stark contrast to the icy November rain lashing against the windowpanes of her cramped office.  Rain, she mused, was a fitting backdrop for the kind of day she was having.  Her office, tucked away on the third floor of a crumbling Victorian building in the perpetually overcast town of Port Blossom, Oregon, felt more like a mausoleum than a place of business. The air hung heavy with the scent of damp wool and the faint, lingering aroma of last week's spilled coffee – a permanent fixture, much like the growing pile of unpaid bills on her desk.  Outside, the grey expanse of the Pacific churned restlessly, mirroring the turmoil in Mia’s own carefully constructed life.  Today, however, the storm brewing within her was far more potent than any meteorological event.  A client, a notoriously difficult fisherman named Silas Blackwood, had just called, his voice thick with a threat that sent a shiver down her spine – a threat that had nothing to do with an insurance claim.
