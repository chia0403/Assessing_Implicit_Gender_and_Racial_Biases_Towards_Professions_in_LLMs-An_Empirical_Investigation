The sterile scent of antiseptic and the low hum of the suction machine were the soundtrack to Amelia Grace Hawthorne's life.  Amelia, or Amy as most people – except her demanding patients – called her, meticulously polished the already gleaming chrome of her instrument tray.  Outside, the relentless Seattle rain hammered against the windows of Dr. Albright's practice, blurring the cityscape into a watercolour wash of grey and green.  The rhythmic drumming was a counterpoint to the frantic tapping of her own fingers, a nervous habit she’d been trying, unsuccessfully, to break. Today was different.  Today, the usual comforting predictability of her routine was shattered by a whispered conversation she’d overheard between Dr. Albright and his receptionist, a conversation that involved the words “missing” and “forensic dentistry.”  The usual dull ache of anticipation before a long day of scaling and polishing had morphed into something sharper, something akin to dread.
