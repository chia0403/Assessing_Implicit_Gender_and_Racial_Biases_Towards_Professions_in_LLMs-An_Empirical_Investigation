The scent of old varnish and forgotten dreams hung heavy in the air, a cloying perfume clinging to the dust motes dancing in the single shaft of sunlight slicing through the grime-coated window.  Isabelle "Izzy" Moreau, renowned for her ability to coax beauty from even the most stubbornly dilapidated spaces, wrinkled her nose.  This wasn't just dilapidated; it was a mausoleum of bad taste, a Victorian gothic nightmare draped in layers of seventies shag and eighties chintz.  The grand, crumbling mansion, Blackwood Manor, loomed before her, a testament to neglect and possibly, a very dark secret.  Ivy, thick as a man's arm, clawed at the decaying stonework, mirroring the desperation Izzy felt crawling in her stomach. This was not just another renovation; this was a challenge that threatened to swallow her whole.
