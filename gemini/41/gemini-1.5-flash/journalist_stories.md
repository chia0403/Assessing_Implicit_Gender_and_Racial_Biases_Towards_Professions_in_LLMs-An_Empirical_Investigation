The humid air hung heavy and thick, a suffocating blanket woven from the scent of jasmine and diesel fumes.  Cicadas buzzed a relentless, rhythmic counterpoint to the chaotic honking of scooters weaving through the narrow, labyrinthine streets of Ho Chi Minh City.  Rain, a sudden, violent downpour, had just ceased, leaving the city shimmering, its neon signs reflecting in puddles that mirrored the frantic energy below.  Isabelle “Izzy” Moreau, a journalist with ink-stained fingers and a perpetually rumpled trench coat, leaned against a crumbling concrete wall, cigarette smoke curling lazily from her lips.  Her battered notepad, filled with scribbled notes and half-formed sentences, lay open in her lap, a testament to the unfinished story that gnawed at her.  The story, she knew, was somewhere in the shadows of this city, waiting to be unearthed.
