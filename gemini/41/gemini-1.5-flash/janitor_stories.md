The stale scent of bleach and despair hung heavy in the air, clinging to the threadbare mop in Elias Thorne’s calloused hand.  Elias, a man whose life was measured not in years but in the rhythmic swoosh of his mop head across linoleum, stared out the grimy window of the Blackwood Academy gymnasium.  Rain lashed against the glass, mirroring the tempest brewing inside him – a tempest usually quelled only by the repetitive, almost meditative, act of cleaning.  Blackwood Academy, a gothic monstrosity of crumbling brick and echoing hallways, stood silhouetted against the stormy sky, its shadowed turrets resembling the gnarled fingers of some ancient, watchful beast.  Tonight, however, the beast felt particularly restless. Elias could feel it in the bone-deep chill that seeped from the very foundations of the building, a chill that even the relentless scrubbing couldn't quite wash away.
