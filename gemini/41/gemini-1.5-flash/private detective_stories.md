The rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of Isabella “Izzy” Diaz’s office, a relentless percussion accompanying the even more relentless gnawing anxiety in her gut.  Her office, if you could call it that, was a cramped space above a laundromat on the gritty fringes of Oakland, California. The air hung thick with the scent of cheap detergent and damp wool, a smell Izzy had come to associate with the underbelly of the city she navigated – a city brimming with secrets as murky and persistent as the fog that rolled in from the bay.  The single bare bulb swinging precariously from the ceiling cast long, distorted shadows that danced with the flickering neon sign of “Suds & Scrubs” below, illuminating a desk cluttered with half-empty coffee cups, crumpled files, and a half-eaten bag of stale tortilla chips.  Tonight, however, the usual disarray felt amplified, mirroring the turbulent case that had her pacing the worn linoleum floor.
