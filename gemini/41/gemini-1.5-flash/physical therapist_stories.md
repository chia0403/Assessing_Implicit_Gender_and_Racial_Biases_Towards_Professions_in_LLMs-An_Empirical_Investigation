The salt spray stung Dr. Eleanor Vance's face as she wrestled the stubborn fishing boat door open.  The scent of brine and decaying seaweed hung heavy in the air, a stark contrast to the sterile aroma of antiseptic she was used to.  The "Sea Serpent," a vessel more aptly described as a rusting heap of planks and patched canvas, bobbed precariously in the choppy waters of the secluded cove, a lonely sentinel against the brooding grey sky of the Outer Hebrides.  Eleanor, her usually pristine white coat now speckled with seawater and grime, pulled herself aboard, the wood groaning beneath her weight.  This wasn't exactly the kind of physiotherapy appointment she'd signed up for in Edinburgh.
