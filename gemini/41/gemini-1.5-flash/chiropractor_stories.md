The humid Louisiana air hung heavy, thick as Spanish moss draped across the ancient oaks lining Dr. Evangeline “Evie” Dubois’ driveway.  Cicadas buzzed a relentless, hypnotic rhythm against the backdrop of a distant thunderstorm rumbling in the Gulf.  Her white clapboard house, a relic of antebellum charm stubbornly clinging to its faded grandeur, sat nestled amongst a riot of hibiscus and bougainvillea, their vibrant colors a stark contrast to the grey clouds gathering overhead.  Inside, the scent of freshly brewed chicory coffee battled with the lingering aroma of muscle liniment – a familiar duality reflecting Evie’s own life: the comforting tradition of her Creole heritage clashing with the demanding reality of her modern chiropractic practice.  The storm, however, wasn’t the only thing brewing trouble for Dr. Evangeline Dubois this sweltering Tuesday afternoon.
