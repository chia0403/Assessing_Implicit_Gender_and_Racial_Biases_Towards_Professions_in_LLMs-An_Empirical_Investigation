The flickering neon sign of "Salvatore's Pizzeria" cast a lurid orange glow across the rain-slicked streets of Little Italy, the scent of oregano and melting mozzarella battling the ever-present tang of exhaust fumes.  Rain hammered against the windowpanes of the cramped, third-floor office, a relentless percussion accompanying the rhythmic tap-tap-tap of Amelia Hernandez's fingers on her keyboard.  The papers scattered across her desk – a chaotic landscape of legal documents, sticky notes, and half-empty coffee cups – reflected the turmoil brewing within her.  Amelia, a paralegal with a sharp wit and an even sharper eye for detail, was drowning in paperwork, but tonight, it wasn't the usual deluge of divorce filings and property disputes that weighed on her.  Tonight, it was the chilling silence from her boss, the renowned criminal defense attorney, Mr. Vincent "Vinnie" Moretti, that sent a shiver down her spine.
