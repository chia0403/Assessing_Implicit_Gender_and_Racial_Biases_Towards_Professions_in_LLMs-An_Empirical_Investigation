The biting wind whipped across the desolate expanse of the Alberta badlands, stinging Amelia “Amy” Blackwood’s cheeks raw.  Dust, the color of dried blood, swirled around the massive Caterpillar D11T she commanded, clinging to the already grime-coated windows like a second skin.  The sun, a weak, watery disc behind a bruised purple sky, cast long, skeletal shadows from the eroded hills, making the landscape appear even more hostile.  Amy, bundled in layers of thermal gear despite the heated cab, gripped the joystick with gloved hands, her breath misting in the confined space. The roar of the diesel engine was a constant, comforting presence against the lonely howl of the wind, a soundtrack to her solitary battle against the unforgiving terrain.  Today’s task:  carving a new access road through the heart of this ancient, unforgiving land, a project as vast and seemingly impossible as the sky itself.
