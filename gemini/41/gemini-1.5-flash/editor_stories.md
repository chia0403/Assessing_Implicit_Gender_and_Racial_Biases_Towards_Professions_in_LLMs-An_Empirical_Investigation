The rain hammered against the attic windowpanes of 14 Bleecker Street, a relentless tattoo mirroring the frantic rhythm of Amelia Katherine “Amy” Holloway’s pulse.  Dust motes, illuminated by the single bare bulb hanging precariously from the ceiling, danced in the air like mischievous sprites.  Amy, editor-in-chief of the venerable but perpetually underfunded literary journal *The Quill & Inkpot*, sat surrounded by towering stacks of manuscripts – rejected, accepted, and the agonizingly undecided – their pages yellowed and worn like ancient maps to forgotten lands. The air hung thick with the scent of old paper, damp wool, and the faint, lingering aroma of Earl Grey tea, a testament to countless nights spent wrestling with words and the authors who birthed them.  Outside, the New York City storm raged, a fitting backdrop for the tempest brewing within her.  This deadline, looming like the guillotine of a particularly cruel editor, was threatening to cleave her sanity in two.
