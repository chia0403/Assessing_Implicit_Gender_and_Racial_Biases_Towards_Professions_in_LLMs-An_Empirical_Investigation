The stale scent of antiseptic and despair clung to the air like a persistent cough.  Dr. Eleanor Vance, her name a stark contrast to the muted greys of her office at the Claremont Psychiatric Clinic, sighed, the sound swallowed by the plush, yet somehow unforgiving, carpet.  Rain lashed against the tall, gothic windows, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her latest patient, a young woman named Clara whose eyes held a chilling emptiness that even Eleanor’s years of experience couldn’t quite decipher.  The clock on the wall ticked with a relentless, morbid rhythm, each second a hammer blow against the fragile silence of the room.  Outside, the city of Claremont hummed with a life Eleanor felt increasingly disconnected from, a life she traded for the shadowed corners of the human psyche.
