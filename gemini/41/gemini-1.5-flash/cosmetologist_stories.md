The scent of lavender and burnt sugar hung heavy in the air, a peculiar perfume only Delilah Mae Hawthorne truly appreciated.  Delilah, owner and sole operator of "Shear Brilliance," a salon tucked away on a cobbled side street in the ancient, perpetually twilight-tinged town of Oakhaven, sighed, the sound lost amongst the rhythmic whir of her electric clippers.  Rain lashed against the stained-glass windowpanes depicting fantastical creatures –  a hydra with surprisingly stylish hair and a griffin sporting a rather fetching feathered bob – casting shifting, kaleidoscopic patterns across the worn wooden floor. Outside, Oakhaven huddled under a sky the color of bruised plums, the wind howling a mournful ballad through the crooked chimneys. Inside, only the hum of the clippers and the soft crackle of the antique fireplace offered counterpoint to the storm.  Tonight, however, even the comforting rituals of her profession felt overshadowed by a creeping unease, a premonition as sharp and chilling as the November wind.
