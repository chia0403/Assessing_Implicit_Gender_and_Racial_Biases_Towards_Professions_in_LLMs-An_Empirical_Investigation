The biting wind whipped Professor Eleanor Vance’s auburn braid across her face as she wrestled the ancient, iron gate open.  Rust groaned a mournful protest under her efforts, mirroring the turmoil churning in her stomach.  Above her, the gothic spires of Blackwood Manor clawed at the bruised twilight sky, their silhouettes stark against the bruised purple and blood orange of the setting sun.  Rain, a relentless drizzle that had been falling since dawn, plastered her tweed jacket to her skin, clinging to her like a shroud. The air hung heavy with the scent of damp earth and decaying leaves, a fragrance both familiar and intensely unsettling, especially in the oppressive silence that descended the moment she stepped onto the overgrown gravel drive.  This wasn't the peaceful countryside retreat she’d envisioned; this was a gothic nightmare, ready to unfurl its secrets.
