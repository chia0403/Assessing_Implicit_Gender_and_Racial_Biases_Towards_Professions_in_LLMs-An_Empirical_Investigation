The biting November wind whipped Dr. Evelyn Reed’s auburn braid across her face as she wrestled the stubborn gate open.  Rust groaned a mournful protest from its hinges, mirroring the ache in her own bones.  The air hung thick with the scent of damp earth and decaying leaves, a smell that usually soothed her but tonight only served to amplify the growing unease gnawing at her stomach.  She was far from the sterile, brightly lit comfort of her clinic, “Reed Veterinary Services,” perched on the edge of the prosperous town of Oakhaven.  Now, she stood at the ramshackle entrance to Widow Blackwood’s farm, a sprawling, neglected expanse swallowed by the encroaching darkness of the West Hampshire countryside. The old woman’s frantic phone call, filled with breathless whispers of a “creature unlike any other,” had jolted Evelyn from a much-needed sleep and driven her out into the heart of the storm.
