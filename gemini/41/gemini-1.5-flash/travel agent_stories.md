The scent of old paper and exotic spices hung heavy in the air, a familiar perfume to Amelia Hernandez.  Dust motes, illuminated by the afternoon sun slanting through the grimy window of "Wanderlust Travel," danced in the beams like tiny, restless travellers themselves.  Amelia, perched precariously on a wobbly stool behind a desk piled high with brochures depicting shimmering beaches and snow-capped mountains, sighed.  The once-vibrant travel posters, their colours now faded and slightly curled at the edges, reflected the state of her business – slightly worn, a little dusty, but undeniably full of potential, if only she could find the right key to unlock it.  Outside, the relentless rhythm of New York City – a cacophony of car horns and distant sirens – pulsed, a stark contrast to the tranquil escapes she dreamt of selling.
