The relentless Florida humidity clung to Amelia Grace Holloway like a damp, unwelcome blanket. Even the sterile air of the Bayside Memorial Hospital Emergency Room couldn't fully dispel the stickiness.  Fluorescent lights buzzed overhead, casting a stark, unforgiving glow on the controlled chaos unfolding below. It was a Tuesday night, technically slow by ER standards, but the air crackled with a low-thrumming tension.  Amelia, her dark hair pulled back in a tight bun, adjusted the stethoscope around her neck and glanced at the clock: 11:57 PM. Three minutes to the end of her shift, and all she could think about was the lukewarm coffee waiting for her in the breakroom and the blessed coolness of her car's AC.  Little did she know, those three minutes would change everything.
