The biting November wind whipped around Eleanor Vance's ankles as she adjusted the "For Sale" sign, its cheerful red a stark contrast to the grey, overcast sky.  Rain threatened, spitting icy droplets that clung to the bespoke wool of her camel coat.  She tucked a stray strand of auburn hair behind her ear, surveying the property with a critical eye.  Cedarwood, perched high on a bluff overlooking the churning waters of the Puget Sound, was a tough sell.  Magnificent, yes, with its sweeping views and sprawling grounds, but burdened by a history whispered about in hushed tones by the locals of Port Blossom, Washington.  A history that Eleanor, ever the pragmatist, was determined to overcome.  This sale was crucial, not just for her fledgling real estate career, but for something far more personal.
