The stale air of the District Courthouse basement, thick with the scent of mildew and despair, clung to Clara Evangeline Holloway like a second skin. Fluorescent lights buzzed overhead, casting a sickly yellow glow on the chipped linoleum floor and the rows of mismatched chairs where her clients waited – a motley crew of petty thieves, drug offenders, and those caught in the unforgiving gears of the system. Outside, the October wind howled through the canyons of downtown Chicago, rattling the barred windows and mirroring the turmoil in Clara’s own mind.  This Monday morning was already shaping up to be a long one, and it hadn’t even officially started. Her first appointment, a repeat offender with a history of breaking and entering, was fifteen minutes late, and Clara’s already overflowing caseload felt like a physical weight pressing down on her chest.
