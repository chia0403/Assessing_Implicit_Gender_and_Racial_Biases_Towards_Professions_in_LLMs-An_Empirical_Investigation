The biting November wind whipped around Amelia Grace Hawthorne as she wrestled a stubbornly oversized sample book of Italian silks against her tweed coat.  The cobblestones of Bleecker Street, usually charming, felt treacherous beneath her heeled boots.  Above her, the brownstones of Greenwich Village, dusted with a premature skiff of snow, glowed with warm, inviting light, a stark contrast to the grey, blustery afternoon. Amelia was on her way to meet her most demanding client yet, Mrs. Vandergelt, whose townhouse, rumored to be haunted by a mischievous eighteenth-century spirit, desperately needed Amelia's particular brand of vibrant, modern magic.
