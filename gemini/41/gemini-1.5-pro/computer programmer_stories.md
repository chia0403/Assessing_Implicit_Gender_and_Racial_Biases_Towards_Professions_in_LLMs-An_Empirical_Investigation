Rain lashed against the attic window, mimicking the relentless drumming in Elias Thorne's head.  His fingers, stained a faint yellow from endless nicotine and cheap instant noodles, hovered over the keyboard.  The air in the cramped space, thick with the smell of stale coffee and ozone from his overheating servers, was stifling.  Elias, clad in a faded "World's Okayest Programmer" t-shirt and cargo shorts two sizes too big, squinted at the lines of code scrolling across his monitor.  Outside, the neon glow of downtown Seattle bled through the grimy glass, a stark contrast to the flickering fluorescent bulb illuminating his self-imposed exile. This attic room, above a dilapidated Thai restaurant in the city's less fashionable Ballard district, had been his sanctuary, his prison, and his battlefield for the past six months. He was close, he could feel it, to cracking the code. To exposing the truth.  And to possibly unleashing something he couldn't control.
