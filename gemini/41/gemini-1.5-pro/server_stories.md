The rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of The Rusty Spatula, a diner clinging precariously to the edge of Highway 17 just outside the forgotten town of Dustbowl, Nevada. Inside, the air hung thick with the mingled scents of stale coffee, frying bacon, and despair.  Eleanor "Ellie" Vance, her name tag slightly askew on her faded turquoise uniform, wiped down the sticky Formica countertop with a practiced hand. The only other occupants were a trucker nursing a lukewarm cup of joe and a fly buzzing lazily around a half-eaten slice of apple pie. Outside, the wind howled like a banshee, rattling the dusty windows and promising a long, lonely night.  Ellie sighed, the fluorescent lights casting long shadows on her tired face.  This wasn't exactly the glamorous life she'd envisioned for herself, but rent was due, and tips were tips, even in Dustbowl.
