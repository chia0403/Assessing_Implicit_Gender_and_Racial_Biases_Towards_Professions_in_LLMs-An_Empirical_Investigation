The stale, recycled air hummed a monotonous tune, punctuated by the occasional clatter of a drink cart.  Alexandra "Alex" Moreau, her navy blue uniform crisp despite the fourteen-hour flight from Dubai, smoothed a nonexistent wrinkle from her perfectly tied silk scarf.  Sunrise painted the sky outside with streaks of apricot and lavender, a breathtaking spectacle few of the slumbering passengers aboard Emirates Flight EK203 would witness. They were blissfully unaware of the drama unfolding just behind the galley curtain, a drama that involved a missing sheikh's falcon, a smuggled stash of saffron, and Alex's growing suspicion that she was about to become an unwitting accomplice to something far more serious than a simple customs violation. The Boeing 777 began its descent towards Sydney Kingsford Smith Airport, the sprawling metropolis twinkling below like a scattered handful of diamonds against the velvety pre-dawn darkness.
