Rain lashed against the leaded windows of Judge Amelia Iris Thorne's chambers, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her.  The mahogany desk, usually a comforting island of order in her chaotic world, was awash in case files, each a miniature tragedy demanding her attention.  Outside, the gaslights flickered along the cobbled streets of New Birmingham, casting long, dancing shadows that seemed to mock the solemnity of the High Court building.  It was late October, 1888, and a chill deeper than the autumn air had settled over the city. A string of disappearances, each more baffling than the last, had gripped New Birmingham in a vise of fear, and Judge Thorne, known for her sharp intellect and unwavering commitment to justice, found herself at the heart of the unsettling mystery.
