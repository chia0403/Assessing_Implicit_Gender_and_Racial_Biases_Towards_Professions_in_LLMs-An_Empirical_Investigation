The insistent drumming of rain against the skylight mirrored the thrum of anxiety in Elias Thorne's chest.  He kneaded the tension knot in Mrs. Henderson's shoulder, his strong fingers working with a practiced grace that belied his inner turmoil.  His small massage therapy studio, "Knot Another Day," nestled between a laundromat and a perpetually closed fortune teller's parlor in the fog-draped San Francisco neighborhood of Richmond, usually offered him a sanctuary.  The scent of lavender and sandalwood normally soothed his frayed nerves, but today, even the calming music couldn't drown out the insistent whispers of doubt that plagued him.  The eviction notice, tucked away in his apron pocket, felt like a lead weight pressing against his hip, a constant reminder of the precariousness of his existence.  Outside, the wind howled, a mournful sound that echoed the rising panic within him.
