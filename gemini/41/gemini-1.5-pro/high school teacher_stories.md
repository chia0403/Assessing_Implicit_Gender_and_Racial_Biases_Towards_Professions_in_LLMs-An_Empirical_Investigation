The biting November wind whipped Amelia "Amy" Hawthorne's auburn braid across her face as she hurried across the parking lot of Northwood High.  Frozen puddles, remnants of last night's sleet, reflected the bleak, grey sky like shards of broken mirror. The air, thick with the smell of damp leaves and exhaust fumes, nipped at her exposed skin.  Northwood, nestled in the foothills of the Appalachians in rural West Virginia, was a town where winter descended early and lingered long, clinging to the valley like a persistent cough.  Amy clutched her oversized tote bag, filled with graded papers and lesson plans for her American Literature class, and fumbled for her keys, the metal cold against her gloved fingers.  She was already running late, and the thought of facing a room full of restless teenagers on a Monday morning after a long, dark weekend filled her with a familiar dread. The fluorescent lights of the school building buzzed, a beacon in the pre-dawn gloom, promising warmth but offering little cheer.
