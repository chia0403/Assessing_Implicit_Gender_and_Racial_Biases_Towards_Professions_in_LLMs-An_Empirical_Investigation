The biting November wind whipped across the deserted bleachers of Harmon Field, rattling the metal and sending stray leaves skittering across the cracked asphalt track.  Floodlights cast long, skeletal shadows from the towering oaks that lined the perimeter, illuminating patches of frost clinging stubbornly to the dying grass.  Amelia "Ace" Rodriguez, her breath clouding in the frigid air, jammed her hands deeper into the pockets of her worn varsity jacket.  She stood at the fifty-yard line, the faded paint a ghostly reminder of past glories, and surveyed the empty field.  Harmon Field, once the pride of the small town of Havenwood, now echoed with the silence of forgotten victories and the creeping chill of a looming winter.  This was her kingdom, her battlefield, her purgatory. And tonight, it felt colder and emptier than ever.
