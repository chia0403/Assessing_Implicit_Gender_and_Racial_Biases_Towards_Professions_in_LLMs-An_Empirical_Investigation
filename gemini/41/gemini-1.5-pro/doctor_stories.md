The biting wind whipped off the North Sea, carrying the scent of salt and something faintly metallic.  Dr. Elias Thorne pulled his threadbare coat tighter around him, the collar scratching against his chapped neck.  The flickering gas lamps of Grimsby Docks cast long, skeletal shadows that danced with the swirling snow, illuminating a scene of bleak industry.  Fishing trawlers, caked in ice, groaned against the quayside, their decks slick with a treacherous mix of fish guts and freezing water.  It was December 1888, a brutal winter even for this harsh corner of England, and Elias knew, with a sinking feeling in his gut, that tonight's call wouldn't be a simple case of the chills.
