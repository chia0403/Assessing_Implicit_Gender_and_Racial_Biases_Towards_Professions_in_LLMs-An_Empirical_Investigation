The biting November wind whipped at Dr. Elias Thorne's coattails as he hurried across the cobblestone courtyard of Ashworth Manor, a converted Victorian mansion perched precariously on the cliffs overlooking the churning grey expanse of the North Sea.  Inside, the warmth promised by the flickering gas lamps in the mullioned windows was a welcome illusion. Ashworth wasn't a typical rehabilitation center.  It specialized in long-term care for patients with rare and often baffling neurological conditions, tucked away on this remote stretch of the Yorkshire coast as if hiding its secrets from the world. Elias, only a few months into his tenure, already felt the isolating weight of the place seeping into his bones, a damp chill that no fireplace could dispel. He clutched his satchel tighter, the leather cold against his gloved hand, and braced himself for the mysteries waiting within.
