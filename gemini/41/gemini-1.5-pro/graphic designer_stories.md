The insistent London drizzle seemed to mirror the relentless thrum of anxiety in Amelia Clementine Finch's chest.  Her Shoreditch studio, usually a haven of vibrant colour palettes and bold typography, felt oppressive this Tuesday morning.  Rain hammered against the large, north-facing window, blurring the already grey cityscape into an abstract wash.  Empty coffee cups littered her desk, a testament to the all-nighter she'd pulled trying to crack the rebranding campaign for "Brew & Bloom," a trendy new chain of organic coffee shops. Amelia, usually so confident in her design sensibilities, found herself staring at the screen, a kaleidoscope of swirling logos and taglines blurring before her eyes.  The deadline loomed, a menacing digital clock ticking down in the corner of her screen, and the client's vague brief – "something fresh, modern, but with a touch of vintage charm" – echoed mockingly in her mind.
