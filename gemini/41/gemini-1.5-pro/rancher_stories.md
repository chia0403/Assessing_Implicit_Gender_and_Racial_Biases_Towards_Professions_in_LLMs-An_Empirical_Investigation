The wind, a relentless, chapped-lip wind, whipped across the Montana plains, carrying the scent of sagebrush and the distant lowing of cattle. Dust devils danced across the parched earth, spiralling towards the endless horizon where the burnt orange sky met the undulating silhouette of the Beartooth Mountains.  This was Amelia Rose Blackwood's country, a sprawling ranch called Whispering Pines, inherited from her grandfather and stretching as far as the eye could see.  The main house, a sturdy, weathered structure built from logs and local stone, stood sentinel against the elements, its windows glowing warmly against the encroaching twilight. Inside, Amelia, her boots caked with the dry soil of her land, poured herself a strong coffee, the mug warming her calloused hands.  The radio crackled with a weather report – another week of drought predicted, another week of worry etched onto Amelia’s sun-weathered face.
