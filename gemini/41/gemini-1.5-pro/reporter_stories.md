The biting wind whipped Amelia "Mia" Rodriguez's auburn hair across her face as she leaned against the rusted railing of the ferry, the salt spray a constant, unwelcome companion.  Below her, the churning grey-green waters of the Puget Sound reflected the overcast sky, swallowing the Seattle skyline behind a curtain of mist.  Bainbridge Island, her destination, remained a shadowy, tree-covered hump on the horizon. Mia, a reporter for the small, independent Bainbridge Islander newspaper, pulled her threadbare coat tighter around her, the crumpled ferry schedule crinkling in her pocket.  She was heading back after a whirlwind trip to Seattle, chasing a lead on a story about the city council’s suspiciously quiet rezoning of a waterfront property – a story she suspected had tendrils that reached across the Sound to this seemingly idyllic island community.  The air, thick with the smell of brine and diesel, vibrated with a low, ominous hum that mirrored the uneasy feeling growing in Mia's gut. This wasn't just a zoning issue; something bigger, something darker, was lurking beneath the surface, and she was determined to uncover it.
