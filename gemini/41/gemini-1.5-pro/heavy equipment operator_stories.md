The biting wind whipped across the desolate expanse of the Klondike, stinging Rosalind “Rosie” Cartwright’s cheeks even through the thick layers of fleece she wore beneath her insulated coveralls.  The sky, a bruised purple canvas streaked with angry grey, promised snow.  Around her, the skeletal frames of unfinished buildings clawed at the twilight, casting long, skeletal shadows across the churned-up mud of the construction site.  Rosie sat perched in the heated cab of her D11 dozer, the rhythmic rumble of the engine a comforting counterpoint to the howling wind. Below her, the massive blade glinted dully in the fading light, poised to carve another swathe through the frozen earth.  Gold Rush Estates, the sign at the site entrance proclaimed optimistically.  Rosie snorted.  Right now, it looked more like Gold Rush Wasteland.  This remote corner of the Yukon Territory, still scarred by the frenzied digging of a century past, was being reborn as a luxury resort, and Rosie, with her steady hand and preternatural ability to coax power from a machine, was at the heart of the transformation.  But something about the stillness, the unnatural quiet that had settled over the site, made the hairs on the back of her neck stand on end.
