The chipped porcelain teacup rattled in Amelia Prudence Hawthorne’s hand, a tremor less from the caffeine and more from the stack of invoices quivering on her desk like a nervous chihuahua. Outside, the November wind howled a mournful tune around the eaves of the Grimshaw Building, a gothic monstrosity of brick and gargoyles that loomed over the perpetually grey streets of Hollow Creek.  Inside, the air was thick with the scent of dust, damp wool, and the faint, metallic tang of old ink. Amelia’s office, tucked away in the building's neglected west wing, was a testament to organized chaos.  Ledgers lined the walls, each meticulously labelled and dated.  Stacks of receipts, invoices, and bank statements teetered precariously on every available surface, forming precarious paper canyons between Amelia and the rest of the world.  The only splash of color in the otherwise monochrome room was a single, defiant sunflower, struggling for life in a cracked clay pot on the windowsill, its face stubbornly turned towards the sliver of light filtering through the grime-coated glass. This was Amelia’s domain, her kingdom of numbers, where she reigned supreme, the silent, meticulous queen of Grimshaw Enterprises.  And it was here, amongst the rustle of parchment and the scratch of her pen, that she discovered the discrepancy. A discrepancy that, she suspected, would unravel the carefully woven tapestry of Hollow Creek's respectable facade.
