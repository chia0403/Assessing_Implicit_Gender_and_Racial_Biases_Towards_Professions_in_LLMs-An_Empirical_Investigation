The flickering fluorescent lights of the server room buzzed in Amelia Reyes's ears, a counterpoint to the insistent hum of the cooling fans.  It was 2:17 AM, the air thick with the metallic scent of overworked electronics and Amelia's own rising anxiety. Outside, nestled in the rolling hills of the Napa Valley, the renowned Chateau Bellefleur Vineyard lay blanketed in the inky blackness of a moonless night. Inside, Amelia, a systems analyst with a penchant for cardigans and a deep distrust of anything wireless, hunched over a console, its screen bathing her face in an eerie green glow.  Harvest season was a week away, and the winery's antiquated inventory management system, a Frankensteinian patchwork of code and hardware she'd inherited from a predecessor with a worrying fondness for duct tape, was threatening to collapse under the anticipated load.  A single bead of sweat trickled down her temple.  This wasn't just a system failure; this was a potential vintage disaster.
