Rain lashed against the grimy windows of The Bracknell Bugle's office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside Amelia "Amy" Thorne.  The fluorescent lights buzzed overhead, casting a sickly yellow glow over the cluttered room.  Stacks of precariously balanced newspapers threatened to topple at any moment, a testament to the understaffed and perpetually chaotic state of Bracknell's only remaining local paper. Outside, the bleak November evening had already swallowed the small, perpetually damp town, leaving only the flickering neon signs of kebab shops and off-licences to pierce the gloom. Amy, hunched over her chipped desk, chewed on the end of a biro, the bitter taste of ink mixing with the metallic tang of impending disappointment.  Her editor, a man whose jowls seemed perpetually stained with coffee and cynicism, had just assigned her the "fluff piece" to end all fluff pieces: the annual Bracknell Begonia Bonanza.
