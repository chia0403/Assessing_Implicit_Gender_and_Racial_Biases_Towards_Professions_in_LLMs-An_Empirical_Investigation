The biting wind whipped Amelia Graystone's lab coat around her as she hurried down the deserted hospital corridor.  Fluorescent lights hummed, casting a sterile glow on the polished floors, reflecting the stark white of the walls and the worried faces of the patients she'd seen earlier that day.  The air, thick with the smell of antiseptic and fear, clung to her like a shroud.  It was a Tuesday, the kind of Tuesday that always seemed to bring a fresh wave of emergencies to the understaffed, overworked emergency department of the St. Jude's Regional Hospital, nestled in the shadowed hills of the Allegheny Mountains.  Rain lashed against the large windows, mirroring the tumultuous emotions churning within her.  Amelia, a Physician's Assistant, was already starting to feel the familiar sting of exhaustion, a dull ache that settled deep in her bones, promising a long night of triage, and probably more than a few sleepless hours.
