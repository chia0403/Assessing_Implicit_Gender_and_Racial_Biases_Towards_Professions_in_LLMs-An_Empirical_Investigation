The chipped porcelain sink, stained a sickly yellow-green from years of lukewarm water and forgotten rinses, reflected the harsh fluorescent light buzzing above Amelia "Maisie" Hawthorne's head.  Dust motes danced in the beam, illuminating the faint swirls of lavender paint on the chipped wall, a testament to Maisie's infrequent attempts at sprucing up her cramped salon, "Scissors & Smiles."  The air hung heavy with the scent of rosemary-scented conditioner and something faintly metallic, a lingering echo of the day's last client.  Maisie, hunched over a crimson-haired model, a strand of her own dark brown hair escaping its clip, felt a familiar pang of frustration.  The salon, tucked away on a side street of the bustling city, had seen better days.  Empty chairs whispered stories of lost clients and even more lost dreams.  But Maisie pressed on, the rhythmic snip-snip of the shears her constant companion in this quiet battle against the fading light.
