The fluorescent hum of the server room, a constant, low thrumming that vibrated through Amelia Petrova's worn sneakers, was the only sound besides the rhythmic click-clack of her keyboard.  Rows upon rows of towering server racks, glowing with a thousand tiny LED lights, formed a cathedral of technological devotion.  Dust motes danced in the filtered air, illuminated by the harsh glare of the overhead lights.  Amelia, a systems analyst at the sprawling, multinational conglomerate, ZenithCorp, was lost in the complex, ever-shifting labyrinth of interconnected systems.  Her task: to trace a seemingly inexplicable spike in the company's mainframe processing load.  The anomaly had appeared without warning, disrupting everything from supply chain logistics to customer service portals.  The pressure, palpable in the cool, sterile air, was a palpable weight on her shoulders.  Tonight, the fate of ZenithCorp's smooth operation hung precariously in the balance, and Amelia was the only one she knew who could find the culprit before the whole thing crashed.
