The chipped paint of the kindergarten classroom, a faded robin's egg blue, seemed to sigh with the afternoon sun.  Dust motes danced in the golden light filtering through the grimy windowpanes, illuminating a flurry of colorful drawings scattered across the floor.  This was Ms. Amelia Butterfield's kingdom, a chaotic and vibrant space where the sounds of excited chatter, rhythmic crayon scribbles, and the occasional frustrated wail mingled into a symphony of childhood.  Amelia, with her perpetually stained cardigan and a smile that could melt glaciers, was its benevolent queen.  Today, however, a different kind of quiet hung in the air, a stillness punctuated only by the rhythmic thump-thump of Amelia's own heart.  A lost drawing, inexplicably hidden beneath a mountain of playdough, was the key to this strange unease.
