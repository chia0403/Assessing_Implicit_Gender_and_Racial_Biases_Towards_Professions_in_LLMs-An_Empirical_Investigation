The fluorescent lights of the downtown law firm hummed, a monotonous soundtrack to Amelia Blackwood's relentless typing.  Filing cabinets, groaning under the weight of decades of legal documents, lined the walls like silent, judgmental witnesses.  Dust motes danced in the lone shaft of sunlight piercing the gloom of the reception area, illuminating the chipped porcelain of a forgotten coffee mug.  Amelia, a paralegal with a sharp wit and an even sharper eye for detail, hunched over her keyboard, the rhythmic clack echoing in the otherwise hushed office.  It was a Tuesday, a day much like any other, save for the growing knot of anxiety in her stomach. The impending deadline for the Miller v. Sterling case loomed large, and Amelia suspected a crucial piece of the puzzle was missing, hidden somewhere amongst the voluminous legal files.  She traced the faint smudge of coffee on her worn wristwatch, each tick a metronome counting down the minutes to disaster.
