The fluorescent lights of the accounting firm, "Numbers & Nuggets," hummed a monotonous tune, reflecting off the polished surfaces of the desks scattered across the cavernous office.  Rain lashed against the large windows, blurring the city skyline into a grey watercolor painting.  Amelia Cartwright, a meticulous accountant with a sharp mind and a perpetually tired expression, hunched over her workstation, surrounded by towering stacks of invoices and tax forms.  The year-end audit loomed, a relentless beast threatening to consume every waking hour.  Empty takeout containers and crumpled coffee cups littered her desk, silent witnesses to a relentless battle against deadlines. The air, thick with the scent of stale coffee and the sharp tang of printer toner, was the perfect backdrop for Amelia's quiet, almost desperate, struggle.
