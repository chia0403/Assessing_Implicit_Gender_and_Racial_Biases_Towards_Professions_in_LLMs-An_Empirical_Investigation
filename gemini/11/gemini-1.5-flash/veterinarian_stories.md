The salt spray stung Dr. Eleanor Vance's face as she wrestled the recalcitrant fishing net from the grasp of a particularly indignant-looking harbour seal.  The air hung thick with the smell of brine, kelp, and something vaguely fishy that Eleanor suspected might be the lingering aroma of last night's calamari feast from the Salty Siren Tavern.  She was perched precariously on the slick, barnacle-encrusted rocks of the aptly named Seal Rock Cove, the grey-green waters of the Pacific churning restlessly around her worn leather boots.  The tiny, windswept village of Port Blossom, nestled at the foot of the looming, mist-shrouded mountains, offered little in the way of sophisticated veterinary facilities, but it certainly offered plenty of challenging – and often pungent – cases.
