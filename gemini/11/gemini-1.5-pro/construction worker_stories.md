The biting November wind whipped across the skeletal steel frame of the "Aurora Tower," whistling a mournful tune through the exposed girders.  Fifty stories above the churning grey Hudson, rivets pinged like icy raindrops as Amelia "Mia" Rodriguez tightened her harness, the familiar bite of the straps a comforting pressure against the swirling vertigo.  The city sprawled beneath her, a dizzying tapestry of brick and glass, the yellow cabs crawling along the avenues like tiny beetles.  A thick layer of clouds clung to the skyscrapers, blurring their peaks into the overcast sky, making it feel as though the Aurora Tower was piercing the very heavens. Mia adjusted her hardhat, tucking a stray strand of dark hair beneath it, and glanced across at her foreman, Big Tony, who was gesturing animatedly to a crane operator high above. This was her city. Her job. Her life.  And today, it was about to get a whole lot more complicated.
