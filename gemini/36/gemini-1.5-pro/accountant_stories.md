Rain lashed against the window of Amelia Hayes's tiny office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her.  The fluorescent lights hummed a monotonous tune, a counterpoint to the rhythmic drumming of her fingertips on the worn mahogany desk. Outside, the neon glow of Hong Kong’s Kowloon district bled through the downpour, painting the rain-slicked streets in a lurid, otherworldly light. Amelia, a chartered accountant with a penchant for order and a deep-seated aversion to chaos, stared at the ledger in front of her.  The numbers, usually her comforting companions, swam before her eyes, blurring into an indecipherable mess.  Something wasn’t adding up, and it wasn’t just the figures in the annual report of Leung Industries.  A knot of unease tightened in her stomach.  This wasn't a simple transposition error. This was something far more sinister.
