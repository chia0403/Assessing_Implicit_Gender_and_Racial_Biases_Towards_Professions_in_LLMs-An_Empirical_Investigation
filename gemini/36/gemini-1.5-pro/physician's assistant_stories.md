The biting wind whipped off Lake Michigan, stinging Amelia Reyes's cheeks as she hurried across the deserted parking lot of Mercy Regional Medical Center.  The pre-dawn sky, a bruised purple edged with a sliver of icy blue, offered little warmth.  January in Chicago had a way of clawing its way into your bones, no matter how many layers you wore. Amelia tugged her scarf higher, the wool scratching against her chin, and fumbled for her badge.  Mercy Regional loomed before her, a monolithic structure of brick and glass, radiating a faint, warm glow against the encroaching darkness. Inside, the controlled chaos of the Level One Trauma Center awaited.  Tonight, like every other night,  Amelia knew the thin veil separating life and death would be tested, and she, along with it.
