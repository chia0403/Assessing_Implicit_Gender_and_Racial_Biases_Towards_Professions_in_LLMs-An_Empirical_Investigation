The biting wind whipped off the obsidian cliffs of Raven's Tooth, carrying with it the sharp scent of brine and the mournful cry of gulls.  Far below, the churning grey expanse of the Iron Sea gnawed at the black basalt shore, its relentless waves a constant reminder of the unforgiving nature of this remote corner of the world.  Carved into the cliff face, precarious and seemingly defying gravity, clung the workshop of Elias Thorne, stone cutter.  It was little more than a cave widened by generations of Thorne hands, its mouth a gaping maw shadowed by a makeshift awning of patched sailcloth. Within, the air hung thick with the dust of granite and the metallic tang of chisel against stone, illuminated by the flickering orange glow of a peat fire that battled the encroaching cold. Elias, his face etched with the fine lines of a life spent wrestling beauty from unforgiving stone, squinted at the emerging form beneath his hammer, the rhythmic clang echoing across the empty expanse of the sea.
