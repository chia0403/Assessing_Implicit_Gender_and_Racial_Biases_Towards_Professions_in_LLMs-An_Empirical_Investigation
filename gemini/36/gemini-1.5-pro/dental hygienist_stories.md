The sterile scent of disinfectant battled the faint aroma of Mrs. Higgins' cinnamon gum as Amelia Reyes, a dental hygienist with a smile as bright as her meticulously polished instruments, prepped the exam room. Rain lashed against the large window overlooking Lake Merritt, blurring the Oakland skyline into a hazy watercolour of grey and deep green. The rhythmic swoosh of the windshield wipers mirrored the anxiety thrumming in Amelia’s chest. It wasn't the usual Monday morning jitters; it was the unmarked manila envelope she’d found tucked into her locker before her shift, its weight heavy with an unknown dread.  The small, cramped office of “Pearlescent Smiles” suddenly felt less like a sanctuary of oral health and more like the setting of a low-budget thriller.
