The rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of The Rusty Mug, a sound Amelia "Amy" Hawthorne had come to find both comforting and irritating in equal measure.  It was a Tuesday night in Port Blossom, Oregon, a town clinging to the edge of the Pacific like a barnacle on a whale. The salt spray hung heavy in the air, even this far from the churning grey sea. Inside the bar, a haze of cigarette smoke mingled with the briny tang, illuminated by the flickering neon sign that declared "Cold Beer & Warm Welcome." Amy, with her flame-red hair pulled back in a messy bun and a faded anchor tattoo peeking out from beneath her rolled-up sleeve, wiped down the sticky mahogany counter, her face reflecting the weariness of a long shift just beginning.  The only other occupant was old Silas, perched on his usual stool, nursing a half-empty pint of stout and staring out the rain-streaked window as if expecting the apocalypse to arrive on the back of a fishing trawler.
