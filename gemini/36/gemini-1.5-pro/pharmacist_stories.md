The neon glow of "Apothecary & Elixir," a relic of a bygone era sandwiched between a twenty-four-hour laundromat and a vape shop, cast a strange, medicinal hue across the rain-slicked streets of downtown Port Blossom. Inside, among the mahogany shelves lined with amber-colored bottles and the faint scent of lavender and cloves, Beatrice Eleanor Ainsworth meticulously counted out metformin tablets.  Rain lashed against the large, arched window, blurring the already fading light of the November afternoon.  Port Blossom, a town clinging stubbornly to its Victorian charm despite the steady encroachment of modernity, was Beatrice's world, and the apothecary, her kingdom.  For thirty years, she had dispensed remedies and advice within its hallowed walls, a silent observer of the town's slow, inevitable transformation.  But tonight, something felt different.  A chill deeper than the autumn air settled in her bones, a premonition that the calm of her carefully curated world was about to be shattered.
