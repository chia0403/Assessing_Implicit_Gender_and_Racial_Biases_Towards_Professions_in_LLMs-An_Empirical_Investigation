The biting November wind whipped across the desolate practice field, carrying with it the scent of damp earth and the ghostly echoes of cheers long past.  Floodlights cast an eerie glow on the empty bleachers of Harmon Field, illuminating the swirling dust devils that danced across the cracked asphalt.  Amelia "Amy" Catherine O'Connell, head coach of the Northwood High Ravens, stood alone at the fifty-yard line, her breath clouding the frigid air. The season had ended weeks ago, a crushing defeat in the regional semi-finals still a fresh wound. The silence, usually a welcome respite after a grueling game, now felt heavy, oppressive, a stark reminder of the emptiness that accompanied the off-season.  The once vibrant green turf, now faded and brown, mirrored the hollowness in Amy's chest.  This wasn't just a game to her; it was her life. And right now, life felt a lot like a fourth-and-long with no timeouts left.
