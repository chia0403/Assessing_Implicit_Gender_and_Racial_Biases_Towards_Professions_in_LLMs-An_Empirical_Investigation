The flickering fluorescent lights of St. Jude's Hospital Emergency Room cast a sickly yellow glow on the chaos unfolding before Eleanor Vance.  Rain lashed against the windows, mirroring the frantic pace within. A gurney rattled past, its occupant groaning, followed by a flurry of doctors shouting medical jargon Eleanor understood all too well. The air hung thick with the metallic tang of blood and the antiseptic bite of disinfectant, a familiar cocktail she'd come to associate with the unpredictable dance of life and death.  It was 2:17 am on a Tuesday, and Eleanor, her name tag slightly askew on her starched white uniform, was already on her third cup of lukewarm coffee, bracing herself for whatever the storm – both inside and outside – would bring next.
