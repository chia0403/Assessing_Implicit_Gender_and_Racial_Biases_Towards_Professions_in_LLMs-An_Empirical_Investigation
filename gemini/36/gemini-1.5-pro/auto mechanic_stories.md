The flickering neon sign of "Rosie's Rods and Repairs" buzzed erratically, casting a sickly yellow glow across the rain-slicked asphalt of Highway 17.  Grease stained the concrete apron, reflecting the city lights of Port Blossom, a town clinging precariously to the edge of the Oregon coast. Inside, amidst the cacophony of clanging metal and the acrid scent of burnt oil,  Eleanor "Ellie" Vance wrestled with a stubborn bolt on a '67 Mustang Fastback. Her dark hair, usually tied back in a practical braid, had come loose and strands clung to her forehead, plastered there by a mixture of sweat and grime.  Rain lashed against the corrugated iron roof, mimicking the persistent thrum of Ellie's frustration. This Mustang, a cherry-red beauty, was her current obsession, and it refused to cooperate.  Port Blossom might be a sleepy town, but beneath its calm facade, secrets simmered, and Ellie, with her wrench in hand, was about to be pulled into the heart of one.
