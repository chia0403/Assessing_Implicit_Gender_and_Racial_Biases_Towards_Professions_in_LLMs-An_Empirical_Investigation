The insistent chirp of the intercom cut through Elara Vance’s meticulous arrangement of pastel sticky notes.  It was a Tuesday, the kind that smelled faintly of bleach and desperation, a scent Elara had come to associate with the Grand Majestic Hotel. Built in 1928, the hotel clung to its faded glory like a sequined gown on a dowager. Its lobby, once a marvel of art deco design, now boasted chipped marble floors and a chandelier missing several crystal teardrops, casualties of time and dwindling budgets. Elara, perched behind the mahogany reception desk that was older than she was, smoothed down her navy blazer and pressed the intercom button.  “Grand Majestic, Elara Vance speaking. How may I direct your call?”
