The chipped paint of the courtroom clock – a relentless metronome ticking away the dwindling seconds of Amelia Katherine “Kate” Holloway’s career – seemed to mock her.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of afternoon sun slicing through the grime-coated window, illuminating the suffocating stillness of the almost-deserted chambers.  The air hung heavy with the scent of old paper, stale coffee, and a lingering desperation that Kate knew all too well.  It was the smell of failure, the kind that clung to the threadbare carpet and seeped into the very bones of the building, a crumbling monument to forgotten cases and shattered hopes on the outskirts of Charleston, South Carolina.  The humidity, a thick, unrelenting blanket characteristic of a late summer afternoon in the Lowcountry, pressed down on Kate, mirroring the weight of the impending decision that hung over her like Spanish moss from an ancient oak.
