The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Mia” Hernandez’s charcoal-grey coat around her legs as she stood perched precariously on the scaffolding, the city of Prague sprawling beneath her like a meticulously crafted diorama.  Gargoyles, leering from the rooftops of centuries-old buildings, seemed to watch her with knowing, stony eyes.  Below, the Vltava River snaked its way through the heart of the ancient city, reflecting the bruised purple of the twilight sky.  The air, thick with the scent of woodsmoke and damp cobblestones, carried the faint, distant echo of a church bell.  Mia, a renowned architect with a reputation for both bold designs and meticulous restoration work, shivered, not entirely from the cold.  This wasn’t just any building site; this was the crumbling shell of the historic Vyšehrad Basilica, a project that threatened to consume her both professionally and personally.
