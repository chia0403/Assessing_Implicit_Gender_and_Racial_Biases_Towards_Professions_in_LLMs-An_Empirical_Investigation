The chipped porcelain teacup warmed Amelia Beatrice Finch's fingers, a meager comfort against the November chill seeping through the drafty window of her office.  Rain lashed against the leaded panes, mirroring the turmoil brewing inside her.  Amelia, a bookkeeper of meticulous habits and even more meticulous records, stared at the ledger spread across her cluttered desk, the spidery script a stark contrast to the violent storm raging outside.  Her small, book-lined office, nestled above the dusty antique shop on Bleecker Street in New York City's West Village, usually offered a sanctuary of quiet efficiency.  Today, however, it felt like a cage, the scent of aged paper and pipe tobacco doing little to mask the bitter taste of betrayal clinging to the air.  The ledger, usually a source of calm order, was now a testament to a lie, a carefully constructed fiction that threatened to unravel everything Amelia held dear.
