The stale air of the probation office hung heavy with the scent of cheap coffee and simmering resentment.  Rain lashed against the grimy windows of the municipal building, mirroring the tempest brewing inside Amelia “Mia” Hernandez.  Her mahogany desk, cluttered with half-empty coffee cups and files thick with the lives of others, felt more like a battlefield than a workspace.  Mia, a probation officer with five years and a mountain of paperwork under her belt, stared out at the downpour, the rhythmic drumming a counterpoint to the frantic thump-thump-thump of her own anxious heart.  This wasn’t just another Tuesday; it was the day she was supposed to meet with Javier Rodriguez, a man whose charm was as slick as the oil he’d likely used to grease the wheels of his latest transgression.  And Mia had a feeling this transgression was going to be a big one.
