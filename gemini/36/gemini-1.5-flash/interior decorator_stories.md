The chipped paint on the windowsill flaked like old memories, mirroring the state of Amelia Hawthorne’s current client’s emotional landscape.  Amelia, a woman whose life revolved around meticulously curated spaces, felt a familiar knot of tension tighten in her stomach.  The air hung heavy with the scent of dust and despair in the cavernous Victorian drawing-room, a symphony of faded grandeur and neglected potential.  Sunlight, fractured by the grimy panes, cast long shadows across the threadbare Aubusson rug, illuminating not just the grime but also the heartbreaking evidence of a life lived – and seemingly abandoned – within these walls.  The house itself, a brooding behemoth of grey stone perched precariously on a windswept cliff overlooking the churning grey sea, seemed to sigh with the same melancholic breath as its occupant, Mrs. Eleanor Vance.
