Professor Elara Vance adjusted her spectacles, the thin gold rims catching the weak winter sun slanting through the dusty window of her cluttered study.  Snow, a thick, relentless blanket of the kind only the Scottish Highlands could muster, piled high against the leaded panes, obscuring the already meagre view of the craggy landscape surrounding Blackwood Manor.  The air, thick with the scent of peat smoke and damp earth, hung heavy and still, broken only by the occasional mournful sigh of the wind whistling through the ancient stone walls.  Blackwood Manor, a gothic behemoth perched precariously on the edge of a precipice, had seen centuries of storms, both literal and metaphorical, and tonight, Elara felt the weight of them all pressing down on her.  Her research, a decades-long obsession with the enigmatic Blackwood family and their unsettling legacy, had finally led her here, to this isolated and unsettling place, and a discovery that threatened to unravel everything she thought she knew.
