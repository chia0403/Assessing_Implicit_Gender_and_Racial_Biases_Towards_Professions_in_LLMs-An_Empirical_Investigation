The fluorescent lights of Miller & Zois hummed a monotonous tune, a counterpoint to the frantic clatter of Amelia Katherine Hayes’s keyboard.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of their thirty-second-floor office, blurring the already indistinct cityscape of Chicago into a hazy, grey watercolor.  Amelia, a paralegal with five years of experience and an almost unhealthy obsession with organization, wrestled with a mountain of discovery documents, the paper a chaotic blizzard on her otherwise pristine desk.  Each file, meticulously labelled and colour-coded, represented a sliver of the sprawling, multi-million-dollar class-action lawsuit against OmniCorp, a lawsuit that threatened to consume Amelia – and her already over-caffeinated existence – whole.  Tonight, however, the usual controlled chaos was amplified by a creeping dread that felt far heavier than the weight of the unsorted files.  Something was wrong.  Deeply, fundamentally wrong.
