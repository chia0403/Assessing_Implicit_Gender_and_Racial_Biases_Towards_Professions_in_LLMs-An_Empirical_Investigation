The biting November wind whipped Amelia Thistlewick’s grey hair across her face as she hurried down Cobblestone Lane, the scent of woodsmoke and roasting chestnuts battling with the acrid tang of coal smoke hanging heavy in the air.  Her small shop, “Amelia’s Alterations and Attire,” nestled between a perpetually-grumbling butcher and a brightly painted apothecary, was already casting long shadows in the fading light.  Inside, the air was thick with the comforting aroma of beeswax, lavender, and freshly-pressed linen – a stark contrast to the chill that permeated the cobbled streets of Oakhaven, a town clinging stubbornly to the edge of the whispering Blackmoor Forest.  The rhythmic snip-snip-snip of Amelia's shears, a familiar counterpoint to the town's hushed murmurs, was already beginning to punctuate the quiet evening.
