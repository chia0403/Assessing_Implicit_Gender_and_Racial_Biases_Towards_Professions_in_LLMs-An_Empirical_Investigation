The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Mia” Hernandez’s charcoal grey coat around her legs as she hurried across the windswept expanse of the hospital grounds.  Grey skies mirrored the somber mood settling over her, a familiar pre-storm feeling that had nothing to do with the actual weather.  The imposing brick facade of St. Jude’s Rehabilitation Center loomed before her, its many windows like vacant eyes staring out at the bleak landscape. Inside, however, a vibrant chaos awaited.  The scent of antiseptic and freshly brewed coffee battled it out in the airlock, a fragrant prelude to the symphony of wheelchairs whirring, therapeutic exercises clicking, and the ever-present murmur of human voices, both hopeful and weary.  This was Mia's domain, her battlefield, and today, a particularly challenging patient awaited.
