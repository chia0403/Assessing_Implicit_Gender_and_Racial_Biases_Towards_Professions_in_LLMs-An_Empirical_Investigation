The brass spittoon gleamed under the sickly yellow light of the Crystal Palace Hotel lobby, reflecting the nervous tremor in seventeen-year-old Archibald "Archie" Finch's hand as he adjusted his cap.  The air hung thick and heavy with the cloying sweetness of lilies and the faint, acrid tang of stale cigars.  Outside, the relentless Chicago wind howled a mournful tune, rattling the massive plate-glass windows and promising a night of biting cold.  Inside, the Crystal Palace, all opulent marble and gilded trim, was a haven of artificial warmth, a swirling vortex of wealthy socialites, nervous businessmen, and the ever-present shadow of something illicit simmering just beneath the surface.  Archie, a bellhop barely tall enough to reach the top shelf of the luggage trolley, felt utterly insignificant amidst this grandeur, a tiny cog in the well-oiled machine of the hotel's extravagant, and possibly dangerous, existence.
