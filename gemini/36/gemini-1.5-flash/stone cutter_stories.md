The biting wind whipped across the desolate moor, stinging Elias Thorne's cheeks and carrying with it the scent of brine and peat smoke.  Elias, his breath misting in the frigid air, squinted at the grey expanse of granite before him.  The wind howled a mournful song through the jagged peaks of the Cairngorms, a counterpoint to the rhythmic *thunk-thunk-thunk* of his hammer against chisel.  He was perched precariously on a rocky outcrop, a silhouette against the brooding sky, his only companions the circling ravens and the ancient, unforgiving mountains that had been his livelihood – and his prison – for thirty years.  The quarry, a gaping wound in the earth’s side, stretched behind him, a testament to the relentless work of generations of Thorne men before him, each leaving their mark on the stone, just as Elias was about to leave his own.
