The biting November wind whipped Amelia Hernandez’s dark hair across her face as she hurried down the cobblestone street.  The air, thick with the smell of woodsmoke and roasting chestnuts, did little to soothe the icy dread clenching her gut.  Amelia, a physician’s assistant at the bustling St. Jude’s Community Clinic in the historic district of Charleston, South Carolina, usually found the city’s charm a welcome balm after a long day.  But tonight, the gas lamps casting long, skeletal shadows only amplified the unease that had settled over her since the frantic call from the emergency room.  The rhythmic clang of the wrought-iron gates of the old Huguenot Cemetery, situated just across the street from the clinic, provided an unsettling soundtrack to her hurried steps.  Ahead, the imposing brick façade of St. Jude’s, normally a reassuring sight, seemed to loom, almost menacingly, in the gathering gloom.
