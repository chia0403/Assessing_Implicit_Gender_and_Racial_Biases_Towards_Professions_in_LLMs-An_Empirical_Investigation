The flickering neon sign of the "Lucky Dragon" noodle shop cast a lurid, greasy glow across Elara Vance's rain-slicked trench coat.  Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of the alleyway, a relentless percussion track accompanying the frantic tapping of her fingers on her battered ThinkPad.  The alley itself was a claustrophobic canyon of brick and overflowing bins, the air thick with the smells of soy sauce, diesel fumes, and something vaguely floral that hinted at a nearby florist stubbornly clinging to life amidst the urban decay.  Elara, a freelance computer programmer whose caffeine intake rivaled that of a small espresso bar, hunched deeper into her collar, her brow furrowed in concentration as she wrestled with a particularly stubborn line of code.  The deadline loomed – midnight – and the client, a notorious underworld figure known only as "The Serpent," was not known for his patience.
