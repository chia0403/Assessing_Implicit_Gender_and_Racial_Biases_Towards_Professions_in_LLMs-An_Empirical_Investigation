The chipped paint of the "Serenity Now" mug in Ms. Eleanor Vance's hand mirrored the state of her own composure.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of her office, blurring the already hazy cityscape of Portland, Oregon.  Grey skies matched the grey despair clinging to the air like the damp scent of fir needles after a storm.  Eleanor, a guidance counselor at Northwood High for the past fifteen years, sighed, the sound swallowed by the rhythmic drumming of the rain.  The stack of student files on her desk seemed to grow taller with each passing minute, each file a silent testament to the anxieties and uncertainties of teenagers grappling with a world that felt increasingly chaotic.  Today, however, the weight felt heavier than usual. Today, a particularly thorny case awaited her.
