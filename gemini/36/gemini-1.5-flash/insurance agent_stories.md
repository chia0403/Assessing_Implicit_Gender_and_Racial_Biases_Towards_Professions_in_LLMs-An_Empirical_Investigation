The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Hernandez’s hands, the lukewarm coffee doing little to chase away the pre-dawn chill clinging to the air in her sparsely decorated office.  Rain lashed against the grimy windows of Hernandez & Hernandez Insurance, the rhythmic drumming a counterpoint to the frantic clicking of her computer keyboard.  The tiny office, nestled on the ground floor of a crumbling brick building on the less-than-desirable edge of San Antonio, smelled faintly of stale coffee, damp plaster, and the lingering scent of desperation – a scent Amelia knew all too well.  Outside, the neon sign flickering intermittently above her door cast an anemic glow on the deserted street, its promise of financial security a stark contrast to the reality she faced: a mountain of unpaid bills, a dwindling client list, and a gnawing feeling that her carefully constructed life was about to unravel.
