The rain hammered against the panoramic windows of Amelia Grace Hawthorne's office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her.  Amelia, a woman whose tailored suits and sharp wit were as legendary on the trading floor as her uncanny ability to sniff out a profitable deal, stared out at the rain-slicked cityscape of Chicago.  The glittering towers, usually symbols of ambition and success, felt tonight like menacing monoliths, their reflective surfaces distorting the already fractured image reflected back at her from the glass.  The scent of expensive leather and brewing coffee – usually a comfort – did little to soothe the knot of anxiety tightening in her stomach.  Below, the relentless rhythm of the city thrummed, a relentless heartbeat against the quiet, almost suffocating tension in her otherwise immaculate office.  Tonight, the usual thrill of the market was replaced by a cold, creeping dread, a premonition sharper than any market downturn she'd ever faced.
