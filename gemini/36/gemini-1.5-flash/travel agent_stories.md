The chipped paint of Amelia Hernandez’s desk reflected the sickly yellow glow of the fluorescent lights, a fitting backdrop to the overflowing inbox mocking her from the computer screen.  Rain lashed against the window of her small, cluttered office, a rhythmic drumming that mirrored the relentless pounding in Amelia’s head. The air hung thick with the scent of stale coffee and desperation, a familiar perfume in the almost-bankrupt Travel Dreams agency nestled amongst a dying row of shops in the rain-soaked town of Port Blossom, Oregon.  Outside, the grey Pacific churned restlessly, mirroring the turbulent state of Amelia’s own life.  This wasn't the exotic escape she'd envisioned when she'd hung her shingle— quite the opposite, in fact.
