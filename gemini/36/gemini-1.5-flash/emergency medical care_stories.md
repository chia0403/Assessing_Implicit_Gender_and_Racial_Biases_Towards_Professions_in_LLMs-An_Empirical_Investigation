The biting November wind whipped Amelia Hernandez’s hair across her face as she wrestled the heavy back door of the ambulance open.  The scent of woodsmoke and something acrid, metallic, hung heavy in the air, clinging to the damp chill that seemed to seep from the very bones of the old New England fishing village of Havenwood.  The rhythmic thrum of the ambulance’s idling engine was a stark counterpoint to the eerie silence that pressed in from the surrounding, snow-dusted houses.  Amelia, a seasoned Emergency Medical Technician with the Havenwood Volunteer Ambulance Corps, adjusted her headlamp, the beam cutting through the pre-dawn gloom.  Tonight, the usual quiet hum of the small coastal town had been shattered by a frantic call—a multiple casualty incident at the old Thompson shipyard, a place steeped in local legend and more than its fair share of tragedy.
