The dawn broke over Willow Creek, painting the dew-kissed grass a shimmering emerald.  A thin veil of mist clung to the river, obscuring the ancient willow trees that gave the town its name.  For Elias Thorne, however, the beauty was purely functional.  Elias, a landscaper with hands as rough as bark and eyes the colour of a summer sky, squinted at the rising sun, already calculating the day's workload.  His battered pickup truck, affectionately nicknamed "The Green Machine," rumbled to life, its engine a familiar growl that echoed the quiet hum of activity beginning to stir in the sleepy town.  Today's task: the sprawling, neglected gardens of the Blackwood Manor, a place whispered about in hushed tones, a place Elias both dreaded and, secretly, found utterly fascinating.
