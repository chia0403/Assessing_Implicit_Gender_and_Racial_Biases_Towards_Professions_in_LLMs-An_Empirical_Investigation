The salt spray stung Dr. Eleanor Vance's face as she wrestled the stubborn lobster trap onto the deck of her small fishing boat, the *Seabreeze*.  The late afternoon sun, a bruised plum sinking towards the horizon, cast long shadows across the churning emerald waters of the Maine coast.  The air, thick with the scent of brine and pine, carried the mournful cry of gulls overhead.  Eleanor, her auburn hair plastered to her forehead beneath a worn baseball cap, grunted with exertion.  The lobster trap, heavy with the day's catch, was a far cry from the precision adjustments she made on spines back at her practice in the quaint seaside town of Port Blossom.  But then again, this – the raw, untamed beauty of the ocean – was her sanctuary, a balm for the aches and pains, both physical and emotional, that her patients – and sometimes she herself – carried.
