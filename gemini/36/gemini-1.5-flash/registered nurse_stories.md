The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s hair across her face as she hurried down the cobbled street, the scent of woodsmoke and roasting chestnuts doing little to temper the chill that permeated her bones.  Rain, a relentless drizzle that had been falling for days, slicked the already uneven paving stones, turning the quaint, gaslit streets of Ashworth into a treacherous maze.  Ashworth, nestled deep within the rolling hills of the Yorkshire Dales, was usually a picture postcard of idyllic English countryside, but tonight, under the bruised purple of the twilight sky, it felt bleak and unforgiving, mirroring the turmoil churning within Amelia’s own heart.  The ancient stone buildings, their windows glowing with a warm, inviting light, seemed to mock her with their cozy interiors, a stark contrast to the relentless downpour and the gnawing anxiety that clung to her like the damp air.  Amelia, a registered nurse at the small, but highly-respected, Ashworth General Hospital, pulled her threadbare wool coat tighter around her, her breath misting in the frigid air.  Tonight, she wasn't just battling the elements; she was battling a far more formidable opponent.
