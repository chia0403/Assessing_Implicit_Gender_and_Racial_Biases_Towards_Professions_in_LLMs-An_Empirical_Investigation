The biting wind whipped at Amelia "Ace" Cartwright's worn denim jacket, stinging her cheeks as she peered out at the endless, featureless expanse of the Wyoming plains.  Dust devils danced like malevolent spirits across the cracked earth, mirroring the turmoil simmering within her.  A lone tumbleweed, stubbornly clinging to the fading light, was the only other movement in the desolate landscape.  Ten-Forty-Two, her rig, a battered but loyal workhorse, groaned under the weight of its cargo and the weight of Ace's unspoken anxieties.  The setting sun painted the sky in hues of bruised purple and fiery orange, a stark contrast to the muted gray of the desolate highway stretching before her.  Ace, her fingers wrapped tight around the worn steering wheel, felt utterly alone.  Her destination: a small, forgotten town nestled deep in the heart of the Black Hills, a town that whispered promises of answers she hadn't yet dared to ask.
