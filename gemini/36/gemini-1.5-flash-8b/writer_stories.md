The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Blackwood's hands, the faint steam mirroring the fog clinging to the cobbled streets of Oakhaven.  Rain, a relentless, rhythmic drumming against the narrow, gabled rooftops, punctuated the silence of the pre-dawn hour.  Amelia, a writer haunted by the ghosts of unfinished stories, hunched over her worn, leather-bound journal, the flickering gaslight casting long, dancing shadows on the cluttered desk.  Oakhaven, a town nestled deep within the whispering forests of the Whispering Woods, was a sanctuary for the lost and the lonely, a place where the past clung to the present like moss to ancient stones.  Amelia, with her perpetually smudged ink-stained fingers and her eyes that held the weight of countless untold tales, felt a profound connection to this place, a place where secrets whispered on the wind seemed to weave themselves into the very fabric of existence.  She had come to Oakhaven seeking inspiration, a spark to ignite the dormant embers of her imagination.  But the air hung heavy with an unspoken tension, and Amelia sensed, with a prickle of unease on the back of her neck, that the town held more than just forgotten stories.
