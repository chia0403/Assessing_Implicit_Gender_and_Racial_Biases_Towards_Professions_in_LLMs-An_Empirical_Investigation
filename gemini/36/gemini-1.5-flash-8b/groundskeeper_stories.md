The biting November wind whipped Amelia "Em" Thistlewick's faded green parka around her legs, tugging at the frayed edges of her well-worn gloves.  The ancient oaks of Blackwood Manor, skeletal against the bruised twilight sky, seemed to mock her as she swept fallen leaves into overflowing bins.  The air, thick with the scent of damp earth and decaying leaves, hung heavy, mirroring the oppressive weight of the unspoken secrets whispered within the manor’s imposing stone walls.  Em, a groundskeeper for Blackwood Manor for the past five years, had grown accustomed to the eerie silence, the unsettling stillness that clung to the place like a shroud.  Tonight, however, something felt different.  A low, guttural growl, rising from the shadowed depths of the sprawling gardens, cut through the melancholy.  It wasn’t the usual nocturnal symphony of owls and rustling branches; this was something…else.
