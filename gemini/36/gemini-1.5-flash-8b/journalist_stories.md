The biting wind whipped Amelia Blackwood's trench coat around her legs, stinging her cheeks with icy rain.  The cobbled streets of Prague, normally a symphony of baroque architecture and whispered secrets, were a bleak canvas under the encroaching twilight.  Streetlights flickered erratically, casting long, dancing shadows that seemed to swallow the already sparse pedestrians whole. Amelia, a freelance journalist for the "Eastern Chronicle," hunched deeper into her threadbare coat, her breath puffing white in the frigid air.  Her assignment – to uncover the truth behind the mysterious disappearances plaguing the city's artisan quarter – felt more ominous with each passing hour.  The scent of damp earth and something indefinably metallic hung heavy in the air, a palpable sense of unease clinging to the ancient stone buildings like a shroud.   A lone, flickering gas lamp cast a sickly yellow glow on the intricate carvings of a nearby doorway, the only warmth in the desolate scene.
