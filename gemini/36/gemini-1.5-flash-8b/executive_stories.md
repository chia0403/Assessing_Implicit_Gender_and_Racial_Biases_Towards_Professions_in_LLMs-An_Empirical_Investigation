The flickering fluorescent lights of the executive boardroom cast long, unnerving shadows across Amelia Stonehaven's meticulously organized desk.  Empty coffee cups, each bearing a tiny, chipped inscription ("Amelia's Awesome Afternoon"), littered the surface, a testament to the relentless demands of her position as Vice President of Global Acquisitions at Zenith Industries.  The air, thick with the scent of stale coffee and unspoken anxieties, hung heavy in the room.  Outside, the relentless grey London drizzle mirrored the impending doom Amelia felt clenching in her gut.  The looming deadline for the acquisition of "NovaTech" hung like a dark cloud over the boardroom, threatening to consume everything. Zenith's future, and possibly Amelia's own, depended on it.
