The biting wind whipped Amelia Blackwood's auburn hair across her face, stinging her cheeks.  Rain, a relentless curtain, blurred the already murky reflection in the grimy storefront windows lining the cobbled streets of Port Haven.  The air, thick with the scent of salt and decaying seaweed, hung heavy, mirroring the oppressive mood of the town.  Amelia, a seasoned reporter for the *Haven Herald*, hunched deeper into her worn trench coat, her gaze fixed on the dilapidated fishing pier jutting out into the churning grey sea.  This was the heart of the storm, both literally and figuratively.  The recent string of mysterious disappearances had gripped Port Haven in a fearsome silence, a silence punctuated only by the mournful cries of gulls and the rhythmic slap of waves against the pier's weathered timbers.  Her assignment, to unravel the enigma of the vanishing fishermen, had led her to this desolate outpost, this place where the sea seemed to swallow everything whole.  The *Haven Herald* would break this story, or Amelia Blackwood would break trying.
