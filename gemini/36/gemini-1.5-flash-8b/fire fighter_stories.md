Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of the fire station, a relentless percussion that echoed the frantic thumping in Captain Amelia "Amy"  Rivera's chest.  The flickering fluorescent lights cast long, dancing shadows across the cluttered dispatch room, illuminating the urgent calls scrolling across the monitor.  Tonight, the air hung thick with the smell of ozone and impending doom.  The city of Oakhaven, nestled between towering redwood forests and a restless coastline, was breathing a ragged sigh.  A wildfire, sparked by a lightning strike deep in the Redwood Canyon, was spreading with frightening speed.  Amy, her dark hair plastered to her forehead, stared at the map, the rapidly-expanding orange blotch a stark contrast to the surrounding green.  The call for reinforcements had already been sent, but the canyon was notoriously difficult to navigate, and time was running out for the already stretched resources.  She knew the lives of the residents of the canyon,  the families she'd visited at community events, the faces of the children who'd watched her with wide-eyed wonder.  Their safety was now in her hands. The station, a sturdy but tired-looking building, was a hub of activity.  A knot of nervous firefighters stood in the bay, awaiting orders, the muted sounds of their pre-shift chatter replaced by the growing din of the approaching sirens.  Each breath felt like a heavy weight, the weight of responsibility pressing down on her.
