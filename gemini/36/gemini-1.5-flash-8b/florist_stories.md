The scent of honeysuckle, thick and clinging, hung heavy in the air of Amelia Nightingale's little shop, "Blooms by the Bay."  Sunlight, filtered through the lace-curtained windows, painted the interior in a warm, honeyed light, illuminating the meticulously arranged bouquets.  Amelia, with her perpetually tangled auburn braid and a smile that could coax a smile from a grumpy gnome,  was humming a tuneless melody as she meticulously arranged a cluster of blushing roses.  Outside, the restless murmur of the Pacific Ocean, a constant companion to the shop nestled on the cliff edge, swelled with the approaching tide.  The air, thick with the salty tang of the sea breeze, carried the distant cry of gulls.  It was a Tuesday morning, and the world, as it always seemed to do in this seaside town, was settling into a gentle rhythm.
