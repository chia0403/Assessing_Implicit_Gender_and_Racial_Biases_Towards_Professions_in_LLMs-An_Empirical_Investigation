**Setting:**

The Golden Spoon Diner, nestled in the heart of Harmony Creek, a town seemingly plucked from a Norman Rockwell painting. The diner itself was a monument to simpler times, boasting checkered floors, vinyl booths worn smooth with years of friendly chatter and spilled coffee, and a jukebox permanently stuck on a loop of early rock 'n' roll classics. Sunlight streamed through the large front windows, illuminating swirling dust motes and the chrome gleam of the milkshake machines. The air hung thick with the comforting aroma of frying bacon, brewing coffee, and a hint of cinnamon from the apple pie that always sat cooling on the counter.

**Opening:**

The clatter of plates and the murmur of conversation barely registered with Beatrice Eleanor "Bea" Willow as she expertly navigated the narrow aisle between booths. Her pen, perched behind her ear, bobbed with each step. Bea, a whirlwind of warm smiles and perfectly balanced trays, had worked at The Golden Spoon Diner for fifteen years, practically growing up within its walls. Today, however, the usual rhythm of her workday felt off. A gnawing unease had settled in her stomach, a feeling akin to forgetting to turn off the oven, even though she knew everything was precisely as it should be. Maybe it was the overcast sky finally breaking after a few weeks of brilliant sunshine, maybe it was the fact that Harold, the cook, had burned the toast *again*. Or maybe, Bea thought, sighing as she set down a plate of eggs benedict for Mrs. Abernathy, it was something more.
