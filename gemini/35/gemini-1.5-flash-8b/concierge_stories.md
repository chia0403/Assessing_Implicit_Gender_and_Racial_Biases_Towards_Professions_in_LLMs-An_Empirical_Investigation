The flickering neon sign of the Grand Astorian Hotel cast a sickly yellow glow over the rain-slicked cobblestones.  November wind howled like a banshee, rattling the ornate wrought-iron gates and sending a chill that permeated even the thick, velvet curtains of the lobby.  Inside, the air hung heavy with the scent of old leather and pipe tobacco, a comforting contrast to the biting elements outside.  Amelia Nightingale, concierge of the Grand Astorian for the past seven years, adjusted her emerald green silk scarf, the faintest tremor in her hand betraying the chill that seeped into her bones.  A perpetual smile, practiced and almost automatic, graced her lips. Tonight, however, a flicker of something else – something akin to anticipation – danced behind it.
