The flickering gaslight cast long, dancing shadows across the cramped kitchen of "The Rusty Spatula," a greasy spoon diner clinging to the edge of the shimmering, smog-choked city of Aeridor.  Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof, a relentless percussion that echoed the frantic rhythm of Amelia Blackwood's movements.  Flour dusted her apron, peppered with the faint, acrid scent of burnt sugar.  Amelia, a cook of undeniable talent and fierce determination, was attempting to conjure a feast from ingredients that seemed to conspire against her.  A single, wilted lettuce leaf, stubbornly clinging to a chipped porcelain plate, appeared a monument to the day's culinary failures.  Outside, the city roared, a cacophony of horns, clanking carts, and whispered rumors carried on the gusty wind.  Inside, Amelia battled the clock, the city's relentless hunger, and her own simmering frustration.
