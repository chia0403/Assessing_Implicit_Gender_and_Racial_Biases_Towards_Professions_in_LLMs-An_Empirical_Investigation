The flickering gaslight cast long, skeletal shadows across the cramped rehearsal hall, highlighting the dust motes dancing in the air.  Rain hammered against the single, leaky skylight, mimicking the frantic energy coursing through Isolde de Montaigne, Artistic Director of the Tempest Theatre Company.  The air, thick with the scent of old wood and spilled coffee, vibrated with the almost unbearable tension.  It was Wednesday, a day Isolde hated, the day the set for "The Raven's Shadow" – a play she’d poured her heart and soul into – was finally unveiled, in a state of disarray almost mockingly reminiscent of the story itself.  Her brow was furrowed, and the faintest glimmer of panic shone in her usually steely gray eyes.
