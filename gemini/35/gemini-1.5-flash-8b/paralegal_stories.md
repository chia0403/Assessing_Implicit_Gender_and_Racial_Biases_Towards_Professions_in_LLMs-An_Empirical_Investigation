Rain lashed against the grime-coated windows of the tiny, windowless office, mimicking the relentless rhythm of Amelia Blackwood's pounding heart.  The fluorescent lights buzzed, casting a sickly yellow glow over the overflowing stacks of legal documents that cluttered every available surface.  Amelia, a paralegal at the perpetually understaffed and notoriously cutthroat firm of  Everest & Sterling, hunched over her worn laptop, the faint scent of stale coffee and desperation hanging heavy in the air.  The city outside, a swirling vortex of grey and steel, mirrored the chaos brewing inside her head.  Tonight, the fate of a small family business, the heart-wrenching saga of the Millers and their struggling bakery, "The Golden Croissant," rested precariously on the edge of her keyboard.  Amelia's late nights, the mountains of paperwork, the endless phone calls, all felt utterly meaningless unless she could find the hidden loophole, the elusive sliver of justice, buried somewhere within the labyrinthine legal code.  The Miller's livelihood, their dream, now hung trembling in the balance.
