The wind, a relentless, whispering coyote, whipped across the parched plains of the Sunstone Territory.  Dust devils danced like phantom cowboys, swirling the crimson dirt into the already hazy afternoon sky.  Elias Thorne, a weathered rancher with eyes the color of burnt umber and hands like knotted oak, surveyed his dwindling herd.  The once vibrant green of the grama grass was now a brittle, straw-colored shroud.  A pall of despair hung heavy over the land, mirroring the gnawing emptiness in his stomach.  For three seasons, the rains had been as scarce as the faith of a desert wanderer.  This year, even the ravens had forsaken their circling patrols.
