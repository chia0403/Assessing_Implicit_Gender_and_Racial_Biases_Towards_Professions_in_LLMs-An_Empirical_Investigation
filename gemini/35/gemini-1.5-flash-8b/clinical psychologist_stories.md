Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of Dr. Eleanor Vance's small, cluttered office, a relentless percussion that mirrored the tempest brewing within her.  The air, thick with the scent of damp earth and stale coffee, hung heavy as a shroud.  Eleanor, a clinical psychologist specializing in trauma recovery, hunched over her cluttered desk, the fluorescent lights casting a harsh glare on the scattered papers and half-empty mugs.  Her brow was furrowed, etched with the weariness of countless late nights and the weight of stories whispered in hushed tones.  Outside, the city of Veridian, nestled in the foothills of the Cascade Mountains, was shrouded in a grey, oppressive blanket.  This particular Wednesday, however, felt different.  The familiar roar of the rain seemed to amplify the silence in the room, a silent scream that echoed the mounting unease in her gut.  A knock, hesitant and almost imperceptible, interrupted her thoughts.
