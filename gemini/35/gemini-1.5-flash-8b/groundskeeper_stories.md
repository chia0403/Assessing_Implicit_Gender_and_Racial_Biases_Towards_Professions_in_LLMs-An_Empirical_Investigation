The biting wind whipped through the skeletal branches of the ancient oaks, rattling the wrought-iron gates of Blackwood Manor.  Elias Thorne, groundskeeper for the sprawling estate, shivered, pulling his thick wool coat tighter around him.  Rain, the consistency of fine gravel, hammered against the meticulously manicured lawns, each drop a tiny percussionist in the symphony of the storm.  Blackwood Manor, a gothic masterpiece perched atop a hill overlooking the misty valley, was draped in an oppressive gloom, reflecting the turmoil within its walls.  Elias, his weathered hands calloused from years of tending to the estate's verdant heart, felt a prickle of unease. This wasn't just any storm; this was a storm that seemed to carry a whisper of something ancient, something sinister.
