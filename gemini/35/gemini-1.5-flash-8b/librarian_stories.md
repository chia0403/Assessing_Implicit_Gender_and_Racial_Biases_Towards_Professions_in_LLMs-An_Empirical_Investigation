The flickering fluorescent lights of the Blackwell Public Library hummed a monotonous tune, a counterpoint to the rhythmic rustle of pages and the soft click of the Dewey Decimal System's metal teeth.  Dust motes danced in the pale beams, illuminating Librarian Eleanor Ainsworth's meticulously organized shelves.  Eleanor, a woman of thirty-five with eyes the colour of faded amethyst and a quiet strength that radiated from her, moved with the practiced grace of a seasoned dancer through the labyrinthine stacks.  Her hands, calloused from years of handling aged paper and leather-bound volumes, traced the spines of forgotten histories.  The year was 2042, and the library, a sanctuary amidst the towering chrome and glass skyscrapers of Neo-Manhattan, was more than a repository of knowledge; it was a living testament to the enduring power of the written word.  Outside, the city pulsed with the neon glow of a never-ending night, its ceaseless hum a stark contrast to the quiet, contemplative atmosphere within.
