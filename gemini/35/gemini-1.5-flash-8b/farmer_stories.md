The biting wind, a relentless, icy whisper, clawed at Elias Thorne's weathered face as he surveyed his dwindling fields.  Autumn had arrived in a rush, painting the sky in fiery hues of orange and crimson, but the earth beneath his boots remained a bleak, barren canvas.  Dust devils danced across the parched furrows, mocking the scant harvest, a meager handful of withered pumpkins and bruised turnips.  The year had been unkind, the rains stubbornly absent, the sun a relentless furnace.  He squinted, his gaze tracing the skeletal remains of stalks that had once promised a bountiful yield.  The once vibrant green now lay muted and lifeless, a stark contrast to the vibrant colours above.  He wasn't just losing his crops, he was losing his hope.  His small, sun-baked farm, nestled in the desolate valley of Whisperwind, felt as empty as his soul.  This, in the valley of Whisperwind, was Elias Thorne's world.
