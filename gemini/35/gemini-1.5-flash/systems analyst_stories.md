The fluorescent lights of the server room hummed a monotonous drone, a counterpoint to the frantic tap-tap-tap of Anya Sharma’s fingers on her keyboard.  Anya, a systems analyst for Global Dynamics Incorporated, felt the familiar prickle of caffeine withdrawal battling the adrenaline surge of a looming deadline.  The air, thick with the scent of ozone and stale coffee, clung to her like a second skin. Outside, the Chicago skyline glittered, a breathtaking display of human ingenuity juxtaposed against the chaotic digital landscape Anya navigated within the bowels of GDI's subterranean data center. Tonight, however, the usual hum of efficient processing was punctuated by a series of erratic error messages flashing across her multiple monitors – a digital SOS from a system she'd sworn was impenetrable.
