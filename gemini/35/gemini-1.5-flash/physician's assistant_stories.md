The biting November wind whipped Amelia Hernandez’s hair across her face as she hurried down the cobblestone street, the scent of woodsmoke and roasting chestnuts doing little to soothe the icy chill that had settled deep in her bones.  Grey, slate-colored clouds hung heavy over the ancient, gabled buildings of St. Ives, Cornwall, their weathered stone slick with a fine, persistent rain.  Amelia, a physician's assistant at the small, but renowned, St. Piran’s Hospital, pulled her worn wool coat tighter, the collar scratching against her already chapped skin.  The rhythmic clang of the church bell, a mournful counterpoint to the wind’s howl, marked another hour closer to the relentless storm brewing on the horizon.  Tonight, however, Amelia wasn't concerned about the weather; her worry was far more immediate, and far more human.
