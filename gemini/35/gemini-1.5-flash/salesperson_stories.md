The fluorescent lights of the "Bargain Bonanza" hummed a monotonous tune, a soundtrack to Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s carefully cultivated despair.  Dust motes, illuminated like tiny galaxies, danced in the stale air above the rows of slightly-used, slightly-broken, and thoroughly underwhelming household appliances.  Amelia, a woman whose smile lines hinted at a more glamorous past than her current reality as a floor salesperson for a perpetually bankrupt appliance store, adjusted her name tag –  the cheap plastic already showing signs of wear – and sighed.  The scent of burnt toast and desperation hung heavy in the air, a potent cocktail that perfectly encapsulated her Tuesday morning.  Outside, the relentless Texas sun beat down, promising a day as scorching and unforgiving as the manager’s expectations.
