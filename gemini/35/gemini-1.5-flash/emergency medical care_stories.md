The siren’s wail was a lullaby Elias Thorne had grown accustomed to, a discordant melody that vibrated through his bones and settled into the marrow of his weary soul.  Rain lashed against the windshield of the ambulance, blurring the already indistinct cityscape of Seattle into a hazy watercolor of greys and blacks.  Each drop, Elias thought grimly, was another weight added to the already heavy burden he carried – the weight of life and death, suspended precariously in the balance.  He gripped the steering wheel, knuckles white against the worn leather, his gaze fixed on the flickering red and blue lights reflecting in the rain-slicked street ahead. The air hung thick with the metallic tang of exhaust fumes and the pervasive scent of damp concrete, a familiar aroma that clung to him like a second skin after twelve years as a paramedic with Seattle Emergency Medical Services.  Tonight, however, the usual grim predictability felt heightened, the city itself seeming to hold its breath, anticipating something… more.
