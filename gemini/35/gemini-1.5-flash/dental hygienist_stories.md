The sterile scent of ozone and the low hum of the suction machine were the soundtrack to Amelia Grace Hawthorne's life.  Not that she minded.  Amelia, a dental hygienist with hands as steady as a surgeon's and a smile that could disarm a charging rhinoceros, found a peculiar comfort in the controlled chaos of Dr. Albright's bustling practice.  It was a Tuesday, the kind of Tuesday that started with the familiar metallic tang of coffee and ended with the satisfying click of the autoclave locking shut. Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of the modern, glass-fronted building overlooking the bustling harbor of Seattle, mirroring the tempest brewing inside Amelia's usually unflappable demeanor.  This wasn't just another Tuesday; today, a renowned, and notoriously difficult, periodontal surgeon was scheduled for his six-monthly cleaning.  His name was Dr. Lucian Thorne, and Amelia had heard more whispered horror stories about his temper than she had about the actual intricacies of periodontal surgery.
