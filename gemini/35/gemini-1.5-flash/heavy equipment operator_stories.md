The biting Alaskan wind howled a mournful song across the desolate expanse of the Yukon River, whipping snow into frenzied dervishes that stung Amelia “Amy” Hawthorne’s cheeks.  The air, thick with the scent of pine and frozen earth, frosted her breath instantly.  Amy, bundled in layers of thermal gear that strained even her powerful frame, gripped the controls of the D9 caterpillar bulldozer with gloved hands.  The massive machine, a behemoth of steel and raw power, rumbled beneath her, its treads chewing through the packed snow with a relentless, rhythmic crunch.  Ahead, the frozen river, a fractured tapestry of ice and snow, stretched towards the distant, snow-laden mountains, a landscape both breathtaking and unforgiving. This was her domain, this frozen wilderness where the only sounds louder than the growl of her bulldozer were the shriek of the wind and the occasional crack of shifting ice.
