The fluorescent hum of the office was a dull counterpoint to the frantic thrumming in Amelia Beatrice Finch’s temples.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of her twenty-second-floor office, blurring the already indistinct cityscape of London into a watercolour wash of grey and black.  Amelia, a meticulous accountant for the prestigious firm of Harrington & Croft, usually found solace in the precise order of her spreadsheets, the satisfying click of her keyboard a comforting rhythm. Tonight, however, the numbers swam before her eyes, a chaotic jumble refusing to reconcile.  The deadline for the Blackwood Group audit loomed, a gargantuan, shadowy beast threatening to devour her sanity – and possibly her career.  The air hung thick with the scent of stale coffee and impending doom.
