The stale scent of old textbooks and lukewarm coffee hung heavy in the air, a familiar perfume to Ms. Eleanor Vance.  Rain lashed against the grimy windows of Northwood High's history department, each gust a mournful sigh against the backdrop of flickering fluorescent lights.  Eleanor, perched on the edge of her desk, meticulously graded essays, the scratching of her red pen a counterpoint to the rhythmic drumming of the storm.  Outside, the manicured lawns of the school grounds were being battered into a sodden mess, mirroring, perhaps, the state of her own increasingly frayed nerves.  This wasn't just any Tuesday; it was the Tuesday before the mid-term exams, and the collective anxiety of her students hung thicker than the damp clinging to her cardigan.
