The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia “Mia” Holloway’s hands, doing little to dispel the November chill that seeped through the grimy window of her office. Rain lashed against the panes, mimicking the relentless drumming in her head.  Her office, a cramped space above a perpetually-smelling fishmonger on Bleecker Street in New York City, offered few comforts beyond the aforementioned mug and a perpetually overflowing ashtray.  The air hung thick with the scent of stale coffee, desperation, and the faint, lingering aroma of yesterday's client – a particularly pungent combination that Mia had grown accustomed to, though never quite enjoyed.  Outside, the city roared, a symphony of sirens and shouting lost in the downpour, a fitting backdrop to the tangled web of secrets she was about to unravel.
