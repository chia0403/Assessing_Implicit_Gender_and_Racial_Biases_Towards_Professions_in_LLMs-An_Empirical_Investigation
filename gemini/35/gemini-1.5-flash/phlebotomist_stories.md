The fluorescent lights of the St. Jude's Medical Center phlebotomy lab hummed a monotonous tune, a counterpoint to the frantic rhythm of Amelia Hernandez's heart.  Amelia, a woman whose small stature belied a surprisingly strong grip, stared at the overflowing sharps container, its red lid a stark warning against the day's accumulation of anxieties.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows overlooking the city, mirroring the storm brewing inside her.  The usual pre-dawn calm of the lab, normally punctuated only by the gentle whir of the centrifuge and the soft clicks of computer keyboards, was shattered by the insistent beeping of a malfunctioning blood pressure cuff in the adjacent room.  This wasn't just a bad Tuesday; this felt like the beginning of something far more unsettling.
