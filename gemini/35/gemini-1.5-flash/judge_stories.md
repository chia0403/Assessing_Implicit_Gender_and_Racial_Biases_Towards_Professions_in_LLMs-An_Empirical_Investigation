The chipped paint of the courthouse clock tower, a weary sentinel against the bruised twilight sky, seemed to mirror the weariness in Judge Evelyn Beatrice Ainsworth's soul.  Rain, a relentless, whispering curtain of grey, lashed against the gothic windows of her chambers, each drop a tiny, insistent hammer against the already fractured peace of her life.  Inside, the air hung thick with the scent of old leather and stale coffee, a familiar, almost comforting aroma that did little to soothe the knot of anxiety tightening in her stomach.  This wasn't the usual pre-trial jitters; this was something deeper, a chilling premonition that hung heavier than the storm brewing outside, promising a verdict far more devastating than any she had ever delivered.
