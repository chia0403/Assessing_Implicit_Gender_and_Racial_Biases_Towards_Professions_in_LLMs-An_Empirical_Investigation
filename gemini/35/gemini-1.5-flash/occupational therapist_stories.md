The salt spray kissed Dr. Eleanor Vance's face as she navigated the rickety wooden walkway, the scent of brine and decaying seaweed clinging to the damp air.  The rhythmic crash of waves against the jagged rocks below was a constant, almost hypnotic, soundtrack to her morning.  This wasn't the sterile, brightly lit environment of her usual occupational therapy clinic in the city; this was the remote island of Aisling, a place where the wind howled its own melancholic tunes and the only traffic was the occasional fishing boat.  Aisling, with its stunning, rugged beauty and its isolated community clinging to the cliffs, was her latest, and perhaps most challenging, assignment.  Eleanor adjusted the worn leather strap of her satchel, its contents – a collection of therapeutic tools both conventional and improvised – hinting at the unconventional nature of the work that lay ahead.
