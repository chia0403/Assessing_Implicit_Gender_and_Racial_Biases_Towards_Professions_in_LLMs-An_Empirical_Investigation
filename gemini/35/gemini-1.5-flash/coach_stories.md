The biting November wind whipped across the cracked asphalt of the abandoned airfield, carrying with it the scent of damp earth and decaying leaves.  Coach Elias Thorne, his breath puffing out in ragged white clouds, watched the last sliver of sun bleed beneath the horizon.  The skeletal remains of hangars, their rusted metal groaning in the gale, formed a desolate backdrop to the makeshift practice field he'd carved out of the overgrown wilderness.  This wasn't exactly the manicured turf of a Division I college, but for the ragtag team of underdogs he coached, it was home.  Home, and the only place where their dreams – however improbable – could take flight.
