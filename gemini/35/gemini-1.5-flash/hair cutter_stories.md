The scent of lavender and burnt sugar hung heavy in the air, a peculiar perfume only Amelia "Mia" Hernandez's salon, "Shear Genius," could produce.  Rain lashed against the oversized windowpanes of her tiny, almost-too-cute shop nestled on a cobbled side street in the historic French Quarter of New Orleans.  The wrought-iron balcony above creaked a mournful tune in the wind, a counterpoint to the rhythmic snip-snip-snip of Mia's shears, a sound as familiar to her as her own heartbeat.  Tonight, however, the usual comforting rhythm was off-kilter, a nervous flutter underlying the steady clip.  Across from her in the worn, velvet-upholstered chair sat a woman whose face was veiled in shadow, her only visible feature a pair of luminous, emerald eyes that held a secret older than the city itself.
