The chipped ceramic mug warmed Amelia Hernandez’s hands, the lukewarm chamomile doing little to soothe the icy dread creeping up her spine. Rain lashed against the panoramic window of her office, blurring the already hazy cityscape of San Francisco.  Amelia, owner and sole operator of "Wanderlust & Waypoints Travel," stared out at the storm, the rhythmic drumming a counterpoint to the frantic tapping of her own fingers on the mahogany desk.  Her usually meticulously organized workspace was a battlefield of scattered brochures, crumpled itineraries, and half-eaten boxes of fortune cookies – a testament to the chaotic week that had culminated in this: a last-minute, high-stakes client demanding a near-impossible itinerary to the remote Himalayan region of Mustang.  The air hung heavy with the scent of rain and impending disaster.
