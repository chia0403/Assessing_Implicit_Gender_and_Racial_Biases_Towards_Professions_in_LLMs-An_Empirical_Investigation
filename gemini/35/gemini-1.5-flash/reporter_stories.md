The humid air hung heavy, thick with the scent of frying plantains and diesel fumes.  Cicadas buzzed a relentless, almost maddening soundtrack to the chaos unfolding on the cracked asphalt of Calle Ocho in Little Havana.  Amelia Hernandez-Diaz, her notepad damp with sweat and her sensible flats sinking slightly into the uneven pavement, navigated the throngs of hurried pedestrians.  The air vibrated with the rhythmic pulse of salsa music spilling from open doorways, a stark contrast to the tense silence that clung to the crime scene tape stretched across the entrance to the El Sol Dorado nightclub.  Amelia, a seasoned reporter for the *Miami Herald*, felt the familiar knot of anticipation tighten in her stomach. This wasn't just another late-night brawl; something about the hushed whispers and the grim set of Detective Ramirez's jaw told her this story was going to be far more complicated.
