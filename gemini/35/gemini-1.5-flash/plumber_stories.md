The greasy grime of decades clung to the ancient brickwork of the alley, a testament to the countless leaks and burst pipes that Bartholomew "Barty" Butterfield had wrestled with over his forty years as a plumber.  The air, thick with the smell of damp earth and stale beer, hung heavy and still in the pre-dawn gloom.  A single, flickering gas lamp cast long, skeletal shadows from the overflowing bins, illuminating Barty’s worn leather work boots as he navigated the treacherous path, a battered toolbox swinging rhythmically at his side.  He was headed to 13 Grimalkin Lane, a place whispered about in hushed tones even amongst the seasoned tradesmen of the city – a place known for its impossible plumbing problems and even more impossible tenants.
