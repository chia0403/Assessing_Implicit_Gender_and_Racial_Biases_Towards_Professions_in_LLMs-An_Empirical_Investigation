The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Hernandez’s fingers, the lukewarm tea doing little to soothe the icy dread that had settled in her stomach.  Rain lashed against the grimy windows of her tiny office, a relentless drumming that mirrored the frantic rhythm of her own heart.  Amelia, a woman whose life revolved around meticulously balanced ledgers and the satisfying *click-clack* of her vintage adding machine, found herself staring not at a spreadsheet, but at a single, crumpled envelope bearing the crest of Blackwood & Sons, a law firm renowned for its ruthless efficiency and even more ruthless clients.  The air in the cramped room, usually fragrant with the scent of old paper and slightly burnt coffee, was thick with the metallic tang of fear.  Her office, perched precariously above a bustling fishmonger’s in the perpetually damp alleyways of Dublin’s Temple Bar, felt less like a sanctuary and more like a cage.
