The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Amy” Hernandez’s dark hair across her face as she clung to the gabled roof of the Victorian mansion.  Below, the manicured lawns of the Blackwood estate stretched out like a crumpled emerald carpet, sharply contrasting with the grey, brooding sky above.  Rain, a fine, insistent drizzle, plastered her work gloves to the slick cedar shingles.  The air smelled of damp earth and the faint, almost cloying sweetness of decaying leaves.  Amy, a seasoned roofer with calloused hands and eyes that had seen more than their fair share of treacherous heights, adjusted her grip on the hammer, the steel cold against her palm.  This job, perched precariously high above the seemingly endless wealth of the Blackwood family, was proving to be more challenging than she’d initially anticipated. The old shingles were brittle, the underlayment questionable, and the looming dusk threatened to turn this already precarious situation into a full-blown disaster.
