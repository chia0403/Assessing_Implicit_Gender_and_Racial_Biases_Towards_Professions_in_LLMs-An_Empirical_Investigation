The biting November wind whipped Amelia Stone’s trench coat around her legs as she hunched deeper into the shadowed alcove, the gargoyle above her a silent, stony sentinel.  Rain, a relentless, icy drizzle, plastered her already damp hair to her scalp.  Across the cobbled street, the gaslights of Blackwood Lane flickered, casting long, distorted shadows that danced with the swirling mist.  Blackwood, a town steeped in history and whispered secrets, clung precariously to the cliff edge, its ancient buildings leaning as if in conspiratorial agreement against the unforgiving North Sea. Amelia, a journalist for the *Blackwood Chronicle*, shivered, not entirely from the cold.  The story she was chasing, the one that had led her to this desolate corner of the town at 3 AM, felt colder still – a chilling tale of disappearances, whispers of a forgotten cult, and a legacy of unexplained deaths that stretched back centuries.
