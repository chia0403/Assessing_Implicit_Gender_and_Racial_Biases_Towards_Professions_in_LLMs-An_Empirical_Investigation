The biting November wind whipped at Dr. Evelyn Grace Holloway’s tailored coat as she climbed the crumbling steps of Blackwood Manor.  Rain, the kind that soaked to the bone in minutes, plastered her dark hair to her scalp.  Overhead, the sky was a bruised purple, mirroring the unsettling unease that had settled in her stomach since she'd received the call. Blackwood Manor, perched precariously on a windswept cliff overlooking the churning grey expanse of the North Sea, was less a manor and more a gothic relic, its stone facade scarred by centuries of storms and, she suspected, far darker secrets.  The air hung heavy with the scent of damp earth and something else… something faintly metallic, like old blood.  Evelyn adjusted her grip on her worn leather briefcase, its contents – her meticulously organized notes on the case, a small, ever-present vial of lavender oil, and a slightly battered copy of *The Interpretation of Dreams* –  a bulwark against the chilling atmosphere that threatened to swallow her whole.
