The chipped porcelain sink groaned under the weight of Eleanor Vance's elbows as she scrubbed the stubborn cherry stain from her hands.  The scent of disinfectant, stale coffee, and something vaguely floral – the lingering ghost of a patient’s perfume – clung to the air in the cramped nurses' station at St. Jude's Memorial Hospital.  Rain lashed against the grimy windows, a relentless rhythm mirroring the thudding in Eleanor's own chest.  It was 3:17 AM, the hour when the quiet before the dawn felt less peaceful and more like a precarious truce with the chaos that would inevitably erupt with the first rays of sunlight. St. Jude's, a sprawling, somewhat dilapidated structure clinging to a hillside overlooking the perpetually overcast city of Alderhaven, was a place where hope and despair intertwined like the tangled ivy that choked its aging brickwork.  Tonight, Eleanor felt the despair winning.
