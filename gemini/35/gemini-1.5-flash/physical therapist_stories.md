The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Mia” Hernandez’s hair across her face as she hurried down Bleecker Street, the scent of roasting chestnuts and damp asphalt clinging to the air.  Rain, a fine, persistent drizzle, plastered her already-soaked trench coat to her body.  Mia pulled her scarf tighter, the wool scratching against her chapped cheeks, a familiar discomfort she’d grown accustomed to during these relentless autumnal storms in Greenwich Village.  The flickering neon signs of the Italian restaurants and dimly lit bars cast a lurid glow on the slick cobblestones, reflecting in the puddles that mirrored the city's restless energy.  Inside her battered satchel, her well-worn physiotherapy notes felt heavy, a stark contrast to the lightness she hoped to bring to her patients, each one a unique puzzle demanding her unwavering attention and skillful touch.  Tonight, however, the weight felt particularly burdensome.  Her last appointment had been… unsettling, to say the least.
