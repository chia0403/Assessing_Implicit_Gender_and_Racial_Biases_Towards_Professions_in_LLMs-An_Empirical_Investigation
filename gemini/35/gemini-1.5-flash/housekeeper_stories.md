The chipped porcelain sink groaned under the weight of Elara Blackwood’s elbows as she scrubbed at the stubborn crust of burnt sugar clinging to the bottom of Lord Ashworth’s favourite copper saucepan.  Steam, thick and sweet like a cloying perfume, swirled around her head, blurring the already dim light filtering through the grimy kitchen window of Blackwood Manor.  Outside, the Yorkshire moors lay shrouded in a perpetual November drizzle, a grey and unforgiving landscape mirroring the state of Elara’s spirits.  The manor itself, a gothic monstrosity of crumbling stone and echoing hallways, was as cold and oppressive as the weather, a testament to the neglect of its reclusive owner.  The only warmth Elara found was in the rhythmic scrape of her scouring pad and the quiet hum of the ancient Aga stove, a comforting presence in the vast, silent house.
