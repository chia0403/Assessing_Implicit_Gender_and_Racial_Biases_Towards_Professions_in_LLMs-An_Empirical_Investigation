The chipped porcelain sink in the back room of Apothecary Lane Pharmacy hummed a mournful tune under the relentless drip, drip, drip of a leaky faucet.  Rain lashed against the tall, gothic windows of the building, blurring the already-dim gaslight glow of the cobbled street outside.  Inside, amongst the rows of amber jars and the faint scent of lavender and sandalwood,  Dr. Elara Vance, her brow furrowed in concentration, meticulously measured powdered aconite into a tiny, bone-china vial.  The air hung heavy with the scent of impending storms, both meteorological and personal.  The pharmacy, a relic of a bygone era nestled in the heart of the perpetually rain-soaked town of Oakhaven, felt as though it held its breath, waiting – as Elara did – for the next unwelcome visitor to push open its creaking, oak door.
