The biting November wind whipped Dr. Eleanor Vance’s dark hair across her face as she hurried down the cobbled lane.  Rain, a relentless, icy drizzle, plastered her coat to her back and slicked the already treacherous path.  Gas lamps flickered weakly, casting long, distorted shadows that danced and writhed like phantoms in the periphery of her vision.  She pulled her collar higher, the wool scratching against her throat a small discomfort compared to the icy dread that had settled in her stomach.  The air hung heavy with the scent of woodsmoke and something else, something acrid and unsettling, that clung to the damp stone walls of the ancient houses lining the lane – a smell that reminded her chillingly of burnt sugar and decay.  She was late, dreadfully late, and the flickering gaslight reflected in the anxious glint of her eyes as she finally reached the imposing, gothic silhouette of Blackwood Manor.
