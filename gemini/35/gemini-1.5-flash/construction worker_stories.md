The biting wind whipped off the churning grey sea, stinging Elias Thorne's cheeks raw.  Salt spray, thick as pea soup, plastered his already mud-caked overalls to his legs.  Below him, the skeletal frame of the new lighthouse clawed at the stormy sky, a defiant monument against the relentless Atlantic fury.  Elias, a man whose hands were as rough as the granite blocks he hauled, gripped the rusted crane cable, his knuckles white against the worn leather of his gloves. The wind howled a mournful ballad, a fitting soundtrack to the isolation of this windswept islet, barely a speck on the map, known only as Widow's Rock.
