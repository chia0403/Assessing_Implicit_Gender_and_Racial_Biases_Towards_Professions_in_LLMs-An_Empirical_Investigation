The scent of cedarwood and lavender hung heavy in the air, a fragrant counterpoint to the damp chill that seeped from the cobblestones outside.  Rain, a relentless drizzle that had been falling for three days, plastered the narrow alleyways of Prague’s Old Town to a glistening sheen.  Gas lamps flickered, casting long, wavering shadows that danced with the raindrops on the windowpane of  "U Ševce," a tiny tailor's shop tucked away between a butcher and a purveyor of fine porcelain.  Inside, Elias Thorne, a man whose nimble fingers were as familiar with the curves of a woman's bodice as they were with the straight lines of a military coat, meticulously stitched a button onto a velvet cuff.  His brow was furrowed in concentration, a single lamp illuminating the intricate work, while the rhythmic click-clack of his needle against the fabric filled the otherwise silent shop.
