The biting November wind whipped Dr. Eleanor Vance’s hair across her face as she wrestled the recalcitrant gate of the Blackwood Farm open.  Frozen mud clung to her wellington boots, a testament to the relentless downpour that had hammered the Cotswolds all day.  Blackwood Farm, nestled deep in a valley shrouded by mist, was a picture of gothic rural charm – a sprawling stone farmhouse, its windows like vacant eyes staring out from under a slate roof, surrounded by skeletal trees clawing at the bruised, grey sky.  The air hung heavy with the scent of damp earth and something else… something faintly metallic, almost coppery, that pricked at the back of Eleanor’s throat.  This wasn't just a routine farm call; she could feel it in her bones.
