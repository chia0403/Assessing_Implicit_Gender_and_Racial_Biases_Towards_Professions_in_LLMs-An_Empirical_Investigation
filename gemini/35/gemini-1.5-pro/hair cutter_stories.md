The rusty scissors, nestled in Elias Thorne's calloused hand, whispered against the worn leather of his strop.  The late afternoon sun, filtered through the grime-streaked window of Thorne's Tonsorial Parlour, painted stripes of dusty gold across the checkered linoleum floor.  The air hung thick with the mingled scents of bay rum, cheap tobacco, and the faint ghost of Mrs. Henderson's lilac perfume, lingering from her earlier perm.  Dust motes danced in the golden light, illuminating the faded grandeur of the small shop in Harmony Creek, a town clinging stubbornly to the banks of the Mississippi, somewhere between forgotten and gone.
