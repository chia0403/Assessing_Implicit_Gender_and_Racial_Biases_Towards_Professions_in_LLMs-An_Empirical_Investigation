The rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of O'Malley's, mimicking the relentless pounding in Elias Thorne's head.  Neon signs flickered through the grimy windows, casting a lurid, watery glow across the deserted bar. It was 2:17 am in the port town of Port Blossom, Maine, a place where the salt air hung thick with the scent of fish, regret, and the faint promise of escape.  Elias, wiping down the sticky mahogany counter with a damp rag, surveyed the empty stools and overflowing ashtrays.  Another slow Tuesday, bleeding into another lonely Wednesday.  Port Blossom was a town that chewed up dreams and spat out broken promises, and Elias, it seemed, was next on the menu.
