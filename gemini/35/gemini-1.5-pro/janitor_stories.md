The flickering fluorescent lights of the Zenith BioTech labs hummed a lonely tune, reflecting off the pristine, polished floors. Elias Thorne, his name tag slightly askew on his faded blue uniform, pushed his mop bucket down the deserted hallway.  It was 2:17 AM, the quietest hour in the building, a time when the relentless hum of scientific progress paused for breath.  Outside, a thick fog had rolled in from the San Francisco Bay, clinging to the glass walls of the Zenith tower like a spectral shroud.  Tonight, the fog felt heavier than usual, pressing against the building as if trying to seep in, to mingle with the sterile air and the secrets it held within.  Elias, a man as accustomed to the solitude of the night as he was to the gleam of freshly waxed linoleum, felt a prickle of unease, a sensation as unfamiliar to him as the faint, metallic scent that hung in the air.
