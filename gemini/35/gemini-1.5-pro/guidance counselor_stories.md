The fluorescent lights of Northwood High hummed a monotonous B flat, a soundtrack to Ms. Eleanor Vance's burgeoning headache. Rain lashed against the window of her small, cluttered office, blurring the already dreary November landscape into a watercolour wash of grey.  A half-eaten granola bar sat beside a precarious stack of college applications on her desk, a testament to the relentless pressure cooker that was high school guidance counseling during application season. Northwood, nestled in the heart of Vermont's Green Mountains, was a small town with big academic dreams, and Eleanor often felt the weight of those aspirations resting squarely on her shoulders.  This year, in particular, felt heavier.  There was a pervasive anxiety clinging to the hallways, a tension she couldn't quite put her finger on, and it was beginning to seep into her very bones.
