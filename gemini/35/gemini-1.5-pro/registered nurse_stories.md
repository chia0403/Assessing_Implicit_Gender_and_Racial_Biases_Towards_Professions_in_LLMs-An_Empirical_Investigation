The relentless Florida humidity clung to Amelia Grace Hawthorne like a damp shroud as she hurried across the employee parking lot of Cypress Key Memorial Hospital. The asphalt shimmered in the pre-dawn light, the air thick with the scent of blooming jasmine and exhaust fumes.  Palm trees swayed gently in the humid breeze, their fronds rustling like whispered secrets.  Amelia, her dark hair pulled back in a messy bun, clutched her thermos of lukewarm coffee, bracing herself for the controlled chaos that awaited her inside.  It was 5:45 am on a Tuesday, the beginning of what promised to be another grueling twelve-hour shift in the Emergency Department. The fluorescent glow spilling from the automatic doors painted the pavement a sickly yellow, a stark contrast to the vibrant hues of the approaching sunrise.  Beyond the doors lay a world of beeping monitors, hurried footsteps, and the constant, low thrum of human suffering and resilience.
