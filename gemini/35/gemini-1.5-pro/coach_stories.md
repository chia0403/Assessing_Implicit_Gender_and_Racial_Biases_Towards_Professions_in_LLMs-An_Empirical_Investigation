The biting wind whipped across the cracked asphalt of the municipal tennis courts, carrying the scent of decaying leaves and stale hot dogs from the abandoned concession stand.  Floodlights cast long, skeletal shadows of the chain-link fence across the cracked courts, illuminating patches of stubborn green weeds pushing through the worn surface.  This was the kingdom of Amelia “Amy” Rodriguez, head coach of the McKinley High Wildcats, a team whose reputation for tenacity was far more impressive than their win-loss record.  Tonight, however, even Amy's unwavering optimism was struggling under the weight of the impending regional semi-finals, and the distinct lack of enthusiasm radiating from the lone figure hunched on the bleachers.
