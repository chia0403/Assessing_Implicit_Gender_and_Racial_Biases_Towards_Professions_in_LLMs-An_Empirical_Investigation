The biting November wind whipped around Dr. Elias Thorne as he hurried across the cobblestone courtyard of the Château de Valois, his breath clouding the frigid air.  Gargoyles, perched high on the chateau's aged stone walls, seemed to leer down at him, their frozen grimaces mirroring the tightness in Elias's own jaw.  He wasn't used to the damp chill of the Loire Valley, having spent the last decade basking in the perpetual sunshine of Southern California.  But his grandmother, Geneviève Thorne, the formidable matriarch of the Thorne family, had summoned him to France with a cryptic message hinting at a health crisis, and Elias, bound by both familial duty and a genuine concern for the woman who had practically raised him, had dropped everything and flown across the Atlantic. The Château de Valois, a sprawling testament to centuries of Thorne family history – and eccentricity – loomed before him, its countless windows glowing warmly against the darkening sky.  Elias pushed open the heavy oak door, the scent of woodsmoke and aged tapestry enveloping him, a strange mixture of comfort and unease settling in his stomach. He had a feeling this wasn’t going to be a simple house call.
