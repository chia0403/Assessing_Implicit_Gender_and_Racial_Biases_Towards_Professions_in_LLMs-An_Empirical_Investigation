The salt spray stung Amelia Reyes's face, a welcome contrast to the burning frustration behind her eyes.  Her easel, precariously perched on the jagged rocks overlooking the churning Atlantic, vibrated with every crash of the waves.  The late afternoon sun, a bruised purple and angry orange, cast long shadows across the deserted beach of Monhegan Island, Maine.  September had arrived, bringing with it the biting wind and the exodus of summer tourists, leaving Amelia with the solitude she craved, yet somehow, it wasn't enough. For three weeks she had been trying to capture the untamed spirit of the ocean, the way the light danced on the waves, transforming them from sapphire to emerald to a frothy, opalescent white. But every canvas remained stubbornly blank, a testament to the growing chasm between the vision in her mind and the hesitant stroke of her brush.
