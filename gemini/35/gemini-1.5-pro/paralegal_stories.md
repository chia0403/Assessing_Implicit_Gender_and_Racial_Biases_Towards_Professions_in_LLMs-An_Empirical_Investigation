Rain lashed against the grimy windows of the Legal Aid office, mimicking the tempest brewing inside Amelia Reyes.  Fluorescent lights hummed overhead, casting a sterile glow on the overflowing filing cabinets and stacks of precariously balanced case files that threatened to topple at any moment.  The air hung thick with the scent of stale coffee and desperation, a familiar aroma in this corner of downtown Baltimore, where hope often felt as thin as the worn linoleum beneath Amelia's sensible pumps.  It was 5:57 pm on a Friday, and while most of the city was gearing up for a weekend reprieve, Amelia was staring at a subpoena that could unravel everything.
