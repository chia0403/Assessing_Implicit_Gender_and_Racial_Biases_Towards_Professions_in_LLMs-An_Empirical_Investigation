The flickering fluorescent lights of the Quick Stop buzzed like trapped wasps, a constant, irritating hum that mirrored the tension tightening in Amelia Reyes's shoulders. It was 11:47 pm, three minutes until closing, and the aisles of the convenience store, usually bustling with late-night snackers and gas-guzzlers, were eerily empty. Outside, the November wind howled against the glass doors, rattling the faded "Open 24 Hours" sign hanging crookedly above them.  Amelia, her name tag slightly askew on her faded blue polo shirt, idly tapped her chipped, cherry-red nails on the worn countertop, the faint scent of stale coffee and hot dogs clinging to the air.  She longed for the sweet release of midnight, the clang of the locked doors, and the silence that would follow. Tonight, however, a prickling unease settled in the pit of her stomach, a feeling she couldn't quite shake.  Something felt wrong.
