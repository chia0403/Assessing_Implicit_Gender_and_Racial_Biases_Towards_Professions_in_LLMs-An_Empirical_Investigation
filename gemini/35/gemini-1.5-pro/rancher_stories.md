The wind, sharp and smelling of impending snow, whipped across the Wyoming plains, biting at the exposed skin on Elias Vance's weathered face.  He pulled his Stetson lower, the brim brushing against the thick wool of his collar.  The sky, a bruised purple streaked with angry grey, mirrored the turbulent emotions churning within him.  His ranch, the Double Bar V, stretched out before him, a patchwork of browns and yellows against the encroaching winter white.  Generations of Vances had carved a life out of this unforgiving land, and Elias, the latest in that long line, felt the weight of that legacy pressing down on him like the heavy, snow-laden clouds overhead. This winter, harsher than any he could recall, threatened not just his livelihood, but the very survival of everything he held dear.
