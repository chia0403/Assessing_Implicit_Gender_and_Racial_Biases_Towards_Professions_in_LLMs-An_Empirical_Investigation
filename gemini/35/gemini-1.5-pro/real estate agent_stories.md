The crisp October air, scented with woodsmoke and decaying leaves, nipped at Amelia Grace Hawthorne's cheeks as she wrestled a stubborn "For Sale" sign into the frozen earth.  The wind, whistling down from the Shawangunk Ridge, tugged at the hem of her tailored camel coat and whipped strands of her auburn hair across her face.  Perched precariously on the edge of a crumbling asphalt driveway, Amelia surveyed the property: a dilapidated Victorian farmhouse, its peeling paint the color of a week-old bruise, slumped on a five-acre plot choked with overgrown weeds and gnarled apple trees.  Located miles outside the quaint, tourist-trap town of Sleepy Hollow, New York, the house exuded an unsettling aura of forgotten stories and whispered secrets.  Amelia shivered, not entirely from the cold. This listing, she had a feeling, was going to be more trouble than it was worth.
