The rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of Amelia Grace Holloway's tiny office, a rhythmic drumming that usually soothed her.  Today, however, it only amplified the throbbing ache behind her eyes.  Amelia, a chartered accountant with a penchant for cardigans and a deep-seated distrust of estimated figures, pushed a stray strand of mousy brown hair behind her ear and sighed. Her office, nestled above Mrs. Higgins' bakery in the quaint, perpetually damp seaside town of Port Blossom, Cornwall, usually smelled of vanilla and sugar.  This morning, though, the sweet aroma was battling with the metallic tang of fear.  Spread across her desk were not the usual invoices and tax returns, but a series of photographs, each one more unsettling than the last.  Photographs that suggested Port Blossom, her haven of predictable routine, harboured a secret far darker than the grey clouds gathering over the churning Atlantic.
