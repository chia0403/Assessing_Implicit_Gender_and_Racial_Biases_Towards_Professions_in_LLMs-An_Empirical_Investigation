The crisp October air, scented with woodsmoke and decaying leaves, nipped at Amelia Reyes' cheeks as she navigated the winding driveway of Blackwood Manor.  Built in the late 1800s, the once-grand Victorian house now slumped with an air of forgotten elegance.  Its paint peeled like sunburnt skin, and the intricate gingerbread trim was missing in patches, revealing the weathered wood beneath. Amelia, clutching her worn leather satchel, shivered not entirely from the cold. Blackwood Manor had a reputation, whispered amongst the locals in hushed tones – a place of shadows and sorrow.  This was Amelia’s newest assignment: to assess and assist the manor’s sole resident, the reclusive Mr. Elias Blackwood, recently returned after a debilitating stroke.
