The flickering gaslight cast elongated shadows across the rain-slicked cobblestones of Veridia Street, reflecting in the puddles like fractured stars.  A mournful saxophone solo bled from the dimly lit doorway of The Serpent's Kiss, a jazz club tucked away in the heart of New Orleans' French Quarter. Inside, the air hung thick with the scent of stale beer, cheap perfume, and the bittersweet tang of regret.  This was the world of Seraphina “Sera” Moreau, a singer whose voice, like aged bourbon, could both soothe and sting. Tonight, however, Sera’s usual fiery vibrato was laced with a tremor of unease.  A telegram, crumpled in the pocket of her emerald green gown, held news that threatened to unravel the delicate melody of her carefully constructed life.
