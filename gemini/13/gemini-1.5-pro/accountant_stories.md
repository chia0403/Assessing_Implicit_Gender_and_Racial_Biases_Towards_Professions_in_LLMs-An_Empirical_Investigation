Rain lashed against the ornate, leaded windows of Amelia Thorne's office, mimicking the rhythmic thump of her headache.  Each drop seemed to vibrate in time with the insistent ticking of the antique grandfather clock in the corner, a relic from a bygone era that felt entirely at odds with the sleek, modern architecture of the Thorne & Davies Accountancy firm. Amelia, her dark hair pulled back in a severe bun, rubbed her temples, the faint scent of lavender oil doing little to soothe the tension coiled tight in her shoulders.  Below, the city of New Birmingham sprawled, a glittering labyrinth of neon and steel reflecting in the rain-slicked streets, a far cry from the sleepy, industrial town it had been just a decade ago.  The sudden, explosive growth had brought with it a wave of new clients, each with increasingly complex and, at times, questionable finances.  It was this boom, and the whispers that accompanied it, that had kept Amelia chained to her desk for the past seventy-two hours.
