The flickering neon sign of the "Lucky Dragon" noodle shop cast a lurid glow on the rain-slicked streets of Kyoto.  Rain, a relentless drizzle that had been falling for what felt like a week, mirrored the persistent hum of anxiety in Elias Thorne's chest. Elias, a computer programmer with a reputation for solving the unsolvable and a penchant for ramen that bordered on obsession, hunched deeper into his worn trench coat, the collar pulled high against the damp chill.  He wasn't here for the noodles tonight, though.  He was here for the data.  The cryptic, encrypted data that had led him on a twisting, perilous chase through the labyrinthine backstreets of the ancient city, a chase that had already cost him more sleep than he cared to admit and a significant portion of his dwindling yen supply. The air, thick with the scent of soy sauce and desperation, held a promise – or perhaps a threat – of something far bigger than a simple programming puzzle.
