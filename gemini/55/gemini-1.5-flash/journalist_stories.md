The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Mia” Holloway’s auburn hair across her face as she hunched deeper into her threadbare coat.  Rain, a relentless, icy drizzle, plastered her already damp trench coat to her skin.  The air hung thick with the smell of coal smoke and brine, a familiar olfactory assault in the grimy port city of Grimsborough, nestled precariously between the churning grey sea and the brooding, rain-slicked cliffs.  Above her, the skeletal remains of a derelict crane clawed at the bruised twilight sky, a fitting backdrop to the grim scene unfolding below.  Mia, a journalist for the Grimsborough Gazette,  pressed her notepad closer, the damp paper sticking to her chilled fingers as she observed the flickering emergency lights reflected in the oily sheen of the harbour water.  Tonight’s story wouldn't be about the usual petty crime; this smelled of something far more sinister.
