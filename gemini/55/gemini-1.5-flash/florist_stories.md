The scent of rain-soaked petrichor mingled with the heady perfume of lilies and roses, clinging to the damp cobblestones of Charleston's French Quarter.  Gas lamps flickered, casting long, dancing shadows across the wrought-iron balconies and ivy-covered walls of Eliza “Ellie” Hawthorne’s shop, “Fleur de Sel.”  Inside, Ellie, her hands stained a delicate lavender from handling the day’s hydrangeas, carefully arranged a bouquet of deep crimson roses and fragrant jasmine, their velvety petals reflecting the warm glow of the single bare bulb hanging above her workbench.  The air hung thick with the unspoken promise of a storm, mirroring the turmoil brewing inside her own heart.
