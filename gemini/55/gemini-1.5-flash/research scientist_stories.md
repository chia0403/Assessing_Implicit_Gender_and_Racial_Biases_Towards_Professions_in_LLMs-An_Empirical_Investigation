Dr. Evelyn Reed adjusted her goggles, the faint scent of ozone and ozone-depleting chemicals a familiar perfume in her world.  The biting Antarctic wind howled around the geodesic dome of the Amundsen-Scott South Pole Station, a mournful counterpoint to the whirring of the sophisticated equipment humming within.  Inside, the air was surprisingly warm, a stark contrast to the -40°C blizzard raging outside.  Evelyn, however, barely registered the temperature.  Her gaze was fixed on the swirling, ethereal glow emanating from the experimental fusion reactor nestled in the heart of the laboratory – a swirling aurora borealis trapped in a glass cage, promising untold energy… or possibly, untold destruction.
