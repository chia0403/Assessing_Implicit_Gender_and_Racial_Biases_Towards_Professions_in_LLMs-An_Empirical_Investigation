The greasy scent of burnt oil and ozone hung heavy in the air, a familiar perfume to Elias Thorne, owner and sole proprietor of Thorne's Auto Repair.  Rain lashed against the corrugated iron roof of his workshop, a rhythmic drumming that competed with the whine of the ancient air compressor and the rhythmic clang of his own hammer as he wrestled with a particularly stubborn bolt on a vintage Mustang.  Thorne's, nestled in the shadow of the looming, rust-eaten industrial skeletons of the abandoned Zenith Steelworks, wasn't exactly a picture postcard.  It was, however, a sanctuary, a place where Elias felt more at home than anywhere else in the gritty, forgotten corner of Havenwood he called home.  The flickering fluorescent lights cast long, dancing shadows across the cluttered workspace, illuminating shelves overflowing with spare parts, tools hanging from every available surface, and a perpetually overflowing bin of discarded nuts, bolts, and dreams. Tonight, however, the usual comforting chaos felt charged, prickling Elias's skin with a sense of unease he couldn't quite place.
