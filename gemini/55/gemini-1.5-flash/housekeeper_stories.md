The chipped porcelain sink groaned under the weight of Eliza Blackwood’s scrubbing.  Rain lashed against the leaded-glass windows of Blackwood Manor, a sound that was as familiar to Eliza as the rhythmic squeak of her worn-out rubber gloves.  The manor, a gothic monstrosity perched precariously on a cliff overlooking the churning grey sea, seemed to exhale a damp chill that seeped into Eliza’s bones despite the thick wool sweater she wore.  Inside, the air hung heavy with the scent of old wood, dust, and a faint, unsettling sweetness – the lingering perfume of the former mistress, Lady Beatrice, whose sudden and mysterious death six months prior had left the house both eerily silent and brimming with unanswered questions. Eliza, however, was used to silence.  She'd spent the last decade of her life cleaning up the mess of other people's lives, both literal and figurative, within these imposing walls.
