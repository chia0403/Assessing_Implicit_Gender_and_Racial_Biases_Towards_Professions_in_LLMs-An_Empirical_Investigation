The salt spray stung Elias Thorne's face, a familiar kiss from the unforgiving Atlantic.  He braced himself against the railing of the *Seraphina*, a battered fishing trawler groaning under the weight of the relentless November wind.  The sky, a bruised purple merging with a churning grey sea, mirrored the turmoil in his gut.  Elias, a man whose life revolved around capturing the fleeting beauty of the world through his lens, found himself facing a subject far more challenging than any landscape: the impending storm, both literal and metaphorical, threatening to consume him whole.  The air hung thick with the smell of brine and diesel, a pungent perfume clinging to the small fishing port of Port Blossom, nestled precariously between the raging ocean and the brooding cliffs of Cornwall.  This was Elias's home, his sanctuary, and yet, he felt profoundly adrift.
