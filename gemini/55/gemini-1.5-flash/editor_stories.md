The chipped mug warmed Amelia "Mia" Hawthorne's hands, the lukewarm tea doing little to soothe the icy dread creeping up her spine.  Rain lashed against the tall, gothic windows of her office, each gust a mournful sigh echoing the turmoil within.  The gargoyle perched precariously above her window seemed to leer, its stone face slick with the relentless downpour.  Mia, editor-in-chief of *The Quill & Ink Review*, a venerable but increasingly precarious literary magazine housed in a crumbling building that smelled perpetually of old paper and damp earth, stared at the manuscript sprawled across her desk.  It wasn't the words themselves – though they were undeniably brilliant – that chilled her, but the cryptic postscript scrawled at the bottom, a single sentence penned in a spidery hand that sent a shiver of something akin to primal fear down her spine:  *They know what you did.*
