The flickering neon sign of "Kramer & DeVille, Attorneys at Law" cast a sickly yellow glow on the rain-slicked streets of Chicago.  A biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Holloway's hair across her face as she hurried down the alley, the scent of roasting chestnuts and something vaguely industrial battling for dominance in the damp air.  Amelia, a paralegal with a caffeine addiction and a talent for uncovering buried legal loopholes, shoved her hands deeper into her worn leather coat, the chill seeping into her bones despite the layers.  Inside the cramped, windowless office that served as her temporary refuge, the air hung thick with the scent of stale coffee and impending deadlines.  Tonight, however, the usual pressure was amplified tenfold.  The case file – thick, bulging, and radiating a palpable sense of danger – sat on her desk, a silent testament to the trouble that had begun brewing just hours ago.
