The fluorescent lights of the "Bargain Bonanza" hummed a monotonous tune, a counterpoint to the rhythmic squeak of Amelia Katherine "Amy" Finch's shoes as she paced the aisle.  Dust motes, illuminated like tiny, erratic stars, danced in the weak beams.  Amy, a woman whose meticulously applied lipstick belied the exhaustion etched around her eyes, adjusted the name tag pinned to her ill-fitting polyester uniform.  The air hung thick with the scent of stale popcorn and something vaguely chemical, a perfume only a discount store could cultivate.  Tonight, the usual Friday night chaos was amplified; the air crackled with a nervous energy that hinted at more than just the usual pre-weekend bargain hunters.  Amy, however, was oblivious, entirely consumed by the looming shadow of her sales quota and the ever-present pressure to move the remaining stock of novelty singing hamsters.
