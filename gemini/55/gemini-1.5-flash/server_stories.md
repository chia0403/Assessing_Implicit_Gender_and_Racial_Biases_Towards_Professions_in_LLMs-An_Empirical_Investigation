The chipped Formica tabletop vibrated faintly under Elara Jeannette Montgomery's hand as she balanced a precarious tower of three plates – a steaming plate of shrimp scampi, a side of garlic bread threatening to topple, and a wilting salad that looked suspiciously like it had seen better days.  Elara, or "Ellie" to the regulars of "The Salty Siren," a seafood shack clinging precariously to the edge of the churning Atlantic in the tiny, perpetually windswept town of Seabrook, sighed inwardly. This wasn't exactly the glamorous life she'd envisioned after graduating culinary school, but the rent was due, and the ocean breeze, however salty, did have a certain charm.  The flickering neon sign of the Siren cast a sickly yellow glow on the rain-lashed windows, highlighting the spray of salt that perpetually dusted everything inside.  Tonight, the storm was particularly vicious, promising a night of howling winds and potentially another power outage.  But tonight, Ellie also had a feeling something else – something far more unsettling – was brewing.
