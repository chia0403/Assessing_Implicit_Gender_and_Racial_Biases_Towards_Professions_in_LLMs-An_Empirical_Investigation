The biting November wind whipped Amelia Stone’s charcoal grey coat around her legs as she hurried across the deserted hospital parking lot.  Fluorescent lights hummed a discordant symphony above, casting a sickly yellow glow on the slick, rain-streaked asphalt.  The scent of antiseptic and something faintly metallic – the lingering ghost of a recent trauma, perhaps – hung heavy in the air.  St. Jude’s Regional Medical Center, a sprawling concrete behemoth perched precariously on the edge of the desolate plains of west Texas, was quiet tonight, a deceptive calm before the inevitable storm of the morning shift.  Amelia, a physician’s assistant with five years of experience etched onto her face in the form of fine lines around her tired eyes, wrestled with the heavy door, its automatic opener malfunctioning as usual.  Inside, the air was warmer, but the silence felt even more oppressive, a stark contrast to the chaotic energy she’d leave behind in just a few short hours.
