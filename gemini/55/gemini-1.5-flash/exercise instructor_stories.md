The stale scent of chlorine and sweat hung heavy in the air, a familiar perfume to Amelia “Mia” Hernandez.  It clung to the damp, grey concrete walls of the "Body Blitz" fitness centre, clinging even tighter to the worn, vinyl-covered weight benches.  Fluorescent lights hummed a monotonous tune above, casting a pallid glow on the rows of cardio machines, each one whispering its own digital tale of burnt calories and fleeting fitness resolutions.  Outside, the November wind howled a mournful counterpoint, rattling the single, grimy window overlooking the rain-slicked streets of Manchester. Mia, however, remained oblivious to the dreary November day.  Her focus was laser-sharp, trained on the five figures struggling through a particularly brutal set of burpees, their faces contorted in a mixture of pain and determination.  Tonight was "Inferno Night," and Mia, as always, was the fire.
