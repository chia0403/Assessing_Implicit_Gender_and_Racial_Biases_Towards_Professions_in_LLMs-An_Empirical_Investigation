The chipped paint of the courthouse’s Victorian facade flaked like dying skin under the relentless Montana sun.  Dust devils, miniature tornadoes of grit and despair, danced across the cracked asphalt of the parking lot.  Judge Amelia “Amy” Hawthorne, her sensible beige suit already clinging damply to her skin, surveyed the scene with a weary sigh.  The air hung thick with the scent of sagebrush and simmering resentment, a familiar perfume in the small, isolated town of Harmony Creek.  This wasn't the quaint, postcard-perfect Harmony Creek tourists envisioned; this was Harmony Creek wrestling with its demons, and Amy, despite her best intentions and a lifetime spent dispensing justice, felt increasingly powerless to soothe its savage heart.  Today’s case, a seemingly simple land dispute, promised to unearth far more than just property lines.
