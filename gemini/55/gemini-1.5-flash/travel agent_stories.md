The aroma of stale coffee and desperation hung heavy in the air, a familiar perfume to Amelia Hernandez, travel agent extraordinaire – or at least, that’s what her business card claimed.  Amelia, perched precariously on a wobbly stool behind her cluttered desk, surveyed her kingdom: a chaotic landscape of brochures promising sun-drenched beaches and snow-capped peaks, interspersed with overflowing filing cabinets threatening to topple like dominoes.  The fluorescent lights of "Wanderlust Travel," her one-person operation nestled above a laundromat in the bustling, slightly grimy, heart of San Francisco's Mission District, hummed a discordant soundtrack to her increasingly frantic tapping on the keyboard.  Rain lashed against the windowpanes, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her.  Today was the day – the day the biggest, most potentially lucrative (and nerve-wracking) client of her career was scheduled to arrive.
