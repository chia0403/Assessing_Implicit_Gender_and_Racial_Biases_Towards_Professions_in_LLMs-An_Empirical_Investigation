The chipped paint of the 'Welcome to Northwood High' sign flaked onto the already littered asphalt like a melancholic confetti.  Rain, the kind that clung to everything and smelled faintly of exhaust fumes and damp leaves, plastered Amelia Katherine Davies's auburn hair to her scalp.  Amelia, or Amy as most of her students – and a few of her colleagues – stubbornly insisted on calling her, sighed, the sound swallowed by the drumming of the downpour.  It was Monday, the first Monday back after a particularly chaotic fall break, and the air hung heavy not just with moisture but with the looming dread of impending deadlines, recalcitrant teenagers, and the gnawing suspicion that the lukewarm coffee she’d grabbed from the gas station wouldn't be enough to fuel her through the day. Northwood High, nestled in the perpetually overcast valley town of Havenwood, Oregon, felt particularly bleak under the grey sky.
