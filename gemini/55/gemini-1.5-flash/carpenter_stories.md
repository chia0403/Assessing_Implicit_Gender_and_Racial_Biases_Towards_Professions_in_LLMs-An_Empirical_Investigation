The salt spray stung Elias Thorne's face as he wrestled the stubborn oak plank onto the weathered scaffolding.  Below him, the churning grey expanse of the Atlantic roared its displeasure at the encroaching November storm.  The air, thick with the smell of brine and sawdust, carried the mournful cry of gulls overhead. Elias, a man carved by the sea wind and sun into a rugged silhouette, ignored the tempest brewing both above and within him. He needed to finish this, needed to get the last of the repairs done to the lighthouse before the full fury of the storm hit.  The beam groaned under his weight, a sound almost swallowed by the ocean's thunderous symphony.  His small, isolated workshop, perched precariously on the cliff edge overlooking the treacherous rocks of Widow's Cove, was a testament to his solitary life and his stubborn refusal to abandon this windswept corner of the world.
