The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Mia” Worthington’s tailored coat around her legs as she hurried across the plaza, the glass towers of Wall Street looming like jagged teeth against the bruised twilight sky.  The scent of rain, mingled with the exhaust fumes of idling limousines and the faint, metallic tang of fear, hung heavy in the air. Mia, a woman whose life was usually measured in milliseconds of market fluctuations and the precise tick of a Bloomberg terminal, felt a prickle of unease that had nothing to do with the Dow Jones.  It was a visceral, primal feeling, a tremor in the usually rock-solid foundation of her meticulously crafted world.  Tonight, the city felt different; colder, sharper, and laced with a foreboding that resonated deep within her bones.
