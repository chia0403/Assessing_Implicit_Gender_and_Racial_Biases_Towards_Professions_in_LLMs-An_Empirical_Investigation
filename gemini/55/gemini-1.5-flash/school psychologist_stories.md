The fluorescent lights of Northwood High hummed a monotonous tune, a counterpoint to the frantic tapping of Dr. Eleanor Vance’s pen against her overflowing notepad. Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of her office, blurring the already muted colours of the autumnal landscape beyond.  The air smelled faintly of stale coffee and the lingering scent of teenage angst – a familiar perfume in the hallowed halls of Northwood, a place where the weight of adolescent anxieties seemed to hang heavier than the damp October air.  Eleanor, a woman whose meticulously styled auburn hair barely concealed the exhaustion etched around her eyes, sighed.  Another crisis. Another student teetering on the precipice of something she wasn't quite sure she could help them navigate.  This time, it was fifteen-year-old Jason Miller, whose escalating self-harming behaviors had finally forced his parents to seek professional help.  The clock ticked relentlessly, each second a testament to the pressure building within the tightly sealed walls of her office.
