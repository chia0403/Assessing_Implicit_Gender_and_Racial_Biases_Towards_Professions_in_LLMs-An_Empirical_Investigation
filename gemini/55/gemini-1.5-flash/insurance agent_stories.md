The stale scent of cheap coffee and desperation hung heavy in the air of Amelia Hernandez's cramped office, clinging to the faded floral wallpaper like a persistent cough.  Rain lashed against the single, grime-streaked window of her insurance agency, “Hernandez & Associates,” a title that felt increasingly ironic considering the current state of her client roster – dwindling faster than her dwindling bank account.  Amelia Hernandez herself, a woman whose sharp wit was only matched by the sharper lines etched around her hazel eyes, stared out at the deluge, the rhythmic drumming a counterpoint to the gnawing anxiety in her gut.  This wasn't just a bad weather day; it was the culmination of months of dwindling business, a perfect storm of rising premiums and reluctant clients.  The only thing keeping her afloat was the stubborn refusal to admit defeat, a trait as ingrained as the smell of old paper in her office.
