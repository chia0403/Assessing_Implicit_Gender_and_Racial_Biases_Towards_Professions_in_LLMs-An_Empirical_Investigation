The salt spray kissed Dr. Eleanor Vance’s face as she leaned against the railing of the *Sea Serpent*, a rusty fishing trawler groaning under the weight of the churning Atlantic.  The air, thick with the smell of brine and diesel, was a stark contrast to the sterile, clinical scent of her usual workplace – the brightly lit, meticulously organized nutrition clinic in bustling Newport, Rhode Island.  Eleanor, however, found herself far from the comforting predictability of her meticulously planned meal plans.  The rhythmic thud of the engine, the cries of gulls overhead, and the endless expanse of grey-green ocean were a jarring, yet strangely exhilarating, change of pace.  This wasn't a relaxing vacation;  this was a rescue mission, of sorts, and its success hinged on her unique expertise – the ability to coax life back into bodies ravaged by malnutrition.  The small, cramped cabin behind her held not charts and medical journals, but crates overflowing with specially formulated nutrient pastes and protein bars, the tools of her unusual trade.
