The biting November wind whipped Amelia Katherine Finch’s hair across her face as she wrestled a recalcitrant bale of hay.  Pine needles, slick with frost, crunched under her worn work boots.  The air, thick with the scent of damp earth and woodsmoke, carried the faint, musky aroma of the animals huddled in their shelters – a comforting perfume to Amelia, the head caretaker at Whisperwind Wildlife Sanctuary.  Whisperwind wasn't just a sanctuary; nestled deep within the whispering pines of the Cascade Mountains, it was a sprawling, haphazard collection of enclosures, repurposed barns, and lovingly crafted habitats, clinging precariously to the steep hillside overlooking a churning, snow-flecked river.  The setting sun cast long, skeletal shadows from the towering pines, painting the already melancholic landscape in hues of bruised purple and fading orange. Tonight, however, the usual serene beauty of Whisperwind felt overshadowed by a palpable sense of unease.
