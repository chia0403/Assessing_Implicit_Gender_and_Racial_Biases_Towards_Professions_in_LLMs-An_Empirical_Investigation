The salt spray stung Amelia Hernandez’s face as she hauled the last crate of glistening, ice-packed scallops onto the creaking deck of the *Sea Serpent*.  The air, thick with the scent of brine and diesel, vibrated with the low thrum of the fishing trawler’s engine. Amelia, her flour-dusted apron clinging damply to her sturdy frame, wiped a stray strand of dark hair from her eyes.  The setting sun cast long, fiery fingers across the churning grey waters of the North Atlantic, painting the sky in hues of bruised purple and angry orange.  Tonight, the menu at the *Salty Siren*, the bustling seafood restaurant she owned and ran on the tiny island of Aisling, depended entirely on the bounty she'd just wrestled from the unforgiving sea.
