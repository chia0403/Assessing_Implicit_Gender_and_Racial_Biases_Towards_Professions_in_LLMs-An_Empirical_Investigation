The salt spray stung Dr. Eleanor Vance’s face as she wrestled the stubborn fishing net onto the deck of the "Sea Serpent."  The late afternoon sun, a bruised plum sinking below the choppy waters of the North Sea, cast long shadows across the small, weather-beaten trawler.  Gulls cried overhead, their calls a mournful counterpoint to the rhythmic chug of the engine.  Eleanor, a nutritionist renowned for her work on sustainable seafood diets, had traded her sterile laboratory for this chaotic, breathtaking expanse, hoping to uncover the secrets behind the dwindling herring population and the baffling rise in local cases of a strange, debilitating illness.  The air, thick with the smell of brine and diesel, held a subtle, almost metallic tang that she couldn't quite place – a smell that hinted at something far more sinister than a simple fishing crisis.
