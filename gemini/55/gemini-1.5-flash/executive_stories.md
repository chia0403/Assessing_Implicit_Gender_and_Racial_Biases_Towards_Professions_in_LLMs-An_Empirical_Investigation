The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s perfectly coiffed auburn hair across her face as she stepped out of the chauffeured limousine.  The air, thick with the scent of woodsmoke and impending snow, was a stark contrast to the sterile, climate-controlled environment she’d just left behind at the Pinnacle Group headquarters.  Before her stretched the imposing façade of Blackwood Manor, its gothic architecture a silhouette against the bruised purple twilight.  Amelia, Executive Vice President of Pinnacle's burgeoning biotech division, wasn’t known for her fondness of rustic retreats, particularly those shrouded in the kind of unsettling history Blackwood Manor whispered of.  But the board meeting – and the potential acquisition hanging in the balance – had demanded her presence, forcing her into this unwelcome, chilling embrace of the English countryside.
