The fluorescent lights of Northwood High hummed a monotonous tune, a soundtrack to the silent pre-dawn chaos. Papers littered the desks, half-eaten bagels sat in forgotten wrappers, and the air smelled faintly of stale coffee and desperation. Across the room, seated ramrod straight at her own desk, Ms. Eleanor Beatrice Ainsworth took a long, fortifying sip of black tea. She was the AP Literature teacher, a woman whose reputation preceded her like a thunderstorm, capable of instilling both awe and fear in equal measure. The clock on the wall ticked relentlessly, a grim reminder of the 7:00 AM bell that would unleash the tidal wave of caffeine-fueled teenagers into her carefully organized classroom. This morning, however, Eleanor felt a prickle of something other than dread. A feeling, unsettlingly close to excitement, buzzed beneath her skin. Today was the day they began dissecting *Hamlet*. And Eleanor Beatrice Ainsworth lived for the messy, beautiful, tragic madness of Shakespeare.
