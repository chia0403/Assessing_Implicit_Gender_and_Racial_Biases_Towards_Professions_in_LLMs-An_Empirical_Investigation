**Opening:**

The rhythmic clatter of Eleanor Beatrice Finch's typewriter was the only sound breaking the oppressive silence of the office. Sunlight, weak and watery, strained through the grimy windows, illuminating dust motes dancing in the air like tiny, mischievous spirits. Each ‘ding!’ of the carriage return echoed the dull thud of another wasted moment. She hated Mondays. Especially Mondays spent typing Mr. Abernathy’s increasingly bizarre collection of grocery lists. Today’s featured gooseberries, pickled onions, and a disturbingly specific brand of mustard. Honestly, did he think she cared? Did he even notice she existed beyond her ability to translate his garbled dictation into crisp, typed prose?

She paused, her fingers hovering over the keys. Had he just coughed up a lung again? The man was going to give himself consumption one of these days, and Eleanor would be left with a half-finished grocery list and a lifetime of resentment. She sighed, adjusting the collar of her starched blouse. At least the gooseberries were relatively straightforward.
