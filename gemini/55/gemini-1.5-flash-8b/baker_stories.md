The scent of freshly baked sourdough, sharp and tangy, hung heavy in the air, clinging to the chipped paint of the bakery's exterior and weaving itself into the cobbled alleyways surrounding it.  Rain, a persistent drizzle, hammered against the corrugated iron roof, mimicking the relentless rhythm of Amelia Nightingale's thoughts.  Amelia Nightingale, baker extraordinaire of "The Golden Crust,"  had just returned to her tiny, cramped bakery, the only source of light within the seemingly endless, grey twilight of the town of Oakhaven.  Outside, the wind howled, whipping through the skeletal branches of the ancient oak tree that, as legend had it, stood sentinel over the town's very foundation.
