Rain lashed against the corrugated iron roof of the Esperanza Medical Clinic, mimicking the frantic rhythm of Amelia Rodriguez-Diaz's heartbeat.  The clinic, a small, surprisingly cheerful building nestled amongst the sprawling, sun-baked mango groves of the rural Honduran town of San Isidro, was usually a haven of quiet efficiency.  Tonight, however, it buzzed with an anxious energy.  The air hung thick with the cloying scent of jasmine and fear.  Amelia, a seasoned Emergency Medical Care Technician (EMCT) with eyes the colour of a stormy sky and hands that moved with practiced grace, knelt beside a young boy, his small form trembling beneath the harsh fluorescent lights.  His name was Mateo, and his face was pale, etched with the imprint of a pain that seemed too young for him.  Outside, the storm raged, mirroring the tempest brewing within the small town.
