The flickering neon sign of the "Rusty Mug" cast a sickly yellow glow across Officer Amelia Hernandez's rain-slicked patrol car.  The air hung thick with the scent of stale beer and damp earth, a familiar perfume of the South Side of Havenwood.  Rain hammered against the corrugated metal roof of the parking garage, a relentless percussion that mirrored the frantic rhythm of Amelia's own thoughts.  A silent scream echoed in the emptiness of the deserted alley, a sound amplified by the oppressive silence.  This was the third missing person report in as many weeks, all of them young women, all of them vanishing without a trace.  Amelia, a seasoned detective with five years on the force, was determined to find them, but the trail was cold, leading only to more questions than answers.  The year was 2024.  The city of Havenwood, nestled between the towering skyscrapers of the new technological district and the sprawling, under-resourced old neighborhoods, was holding its breath.
