Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of the clinic, a relentless percussion that mirrored the insistent thrumming in Elara Nightingale's temples.  The air, thick with the smell of antiseptic and damp earth, hung heavy in the small, cramped physiotherapy clinic nestled in the heart of the Blackwood district.  Dust motes danced in the lone shaft of sunlight piercing the gloom.  Blackwood, a sprawling suburb perpetually teetering on the edge of neglect, wasn't exactly known for its vibrant medical facilities.  Elara, a physical therapist with a finely honed sense of empathy and a relentless drive, found herself battling not just the patients' physical limitations, but also the pervasive sense of hopelessness that clung to the neighbourhood like a shroud.  Today's patient, a young woman named Anya Petrova with a fractured tibia, was her latest challenge.  Elara knew the road to recovery wouldn't be paved with easy milestones in this neighborhood.
