The chipped Formica counter, stained a faded beige from years of spilled coffee and whispered secrets, reflected the harsh fluorescent lights of "The Snip & Sizzle."  Dust motes danced in the single beam of sunlight that pierced the grime-coated window, illuminating a single, vibrant strand of crimson hair lying abandoned on the floor.  Amelia Hawthorne, a woman whose sharp, intelligent eyes seemed perpetually searching for something beyond the predictable grey of her clientele, sighed, pushing a stray lock of her own dark hair behind her ear.  The rhythmic *click-clack* of her shears against the plastic comb was the only sound in the otherwise silent salon. Today, the only customer was a gaunt man in a threadbare suit, nursing a lukewarm cup of coffee and staring intently at the chipped paint of the wall opposite him.  The air hung heavy with the scent of stale cigarette smoke and cheap hairspray, a familiar aroma to Amelia, a scent that often held the faintest hint of unspoken anxieties and unfulfilled dreams.
