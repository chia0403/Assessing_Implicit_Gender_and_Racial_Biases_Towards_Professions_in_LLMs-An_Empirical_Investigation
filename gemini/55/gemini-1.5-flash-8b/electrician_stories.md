The flickering neon sign of "Sparky's Electric Repairs" cast a sickly yellow glow across the rain-slicked alleyway.  Rain hammered against the corrugated metal roof, a relentless percussion that drowned out the rhythmic clang of Amelia "Sparky"  Fitzwilliam's wrench against the exposed copper wiring.  The air, thick with the smell of ozone and damp earth, hung heavy in the claustrophobic space.  Amelia, a whirlwind of dark hair and even darker determination, wrestled with a stubborn junction box, sweat beading on her brow beneath the grime-coated safety glasses.  Tonight, the client wasn't a homeowner complaining about a faulty light fixture.  This was different. This was...urgent.
