The biting wind whipped across the desolate plains of the Nebraska panhandle, stinging Amelia "Amy" Cartwright's cheeks.  Dust devils danced across the cracked earth, swirling into miniature tornadoes that mirrored the chaos churning in her stomach.  The cracked asphalt of Highway 83 stretched before her, a ribbon of faded black disappearing into the horizon.  It was 2 AM, and Amy, a veteran trucker with the moniker "The Iron Horse," was miles from anywhere offering solace.  Her rig, a battered but trusty Freightliner Cascadia, groaned under the weight of a load of agricultural machinery, a silent testament to the endless cycle of the American heartland.  The town of Valentine, population barely 2,000, was a forgotten ghost town a hundred miles away; the closest spot of civilization in her peripheral vision.
