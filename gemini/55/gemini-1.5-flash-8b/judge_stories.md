The chipped porcelain of the teacup trembled in Judge Alistair Finch's hand, mirroring the tremor in his own heart.  Outside, the midday sun beat down on the cobbled streets of Alderwood, turning the already dusty market square into a shimmering mirage.  The scent of roasted nuts and stale bread mingled with the pungent aroma of the tanner's workshop, a constant backdrop to the quiet hum of the town.  Alderwood, nestled in the foothills of the Whispering Mountains, was a place of ancient traditions and hushed secrets, a place where even the most seasoned jurist like himself could find a disconcerting unease.

Today, that unease had thickened into a palpable dread.  The case before him, the case of the stolen moonstone, was far more complex than its simple surface suggested.  Not just any moonstone, but the legendary Starlit Moonstone, a gem said to hold the power of the whispering wind itself, and it was missing from the very heart of Alderwood.
