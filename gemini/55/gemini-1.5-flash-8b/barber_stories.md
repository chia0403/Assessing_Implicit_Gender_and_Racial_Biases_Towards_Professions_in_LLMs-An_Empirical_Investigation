The scent of lavender and something faintly metallic hung heavy in the air of "The Razor's Edge," a small barbershop tucked between a bustling bakery and a secondhand bookstore in the heart of Old Oakhaven.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of afternoon sunlight piercing the grimy window, illuminating the chipped porcelain sink and the array of gleaming razors lining the mahogany counter.  This was Bartholomew "Barty" Blackwood's domain, and for the past twenty years, it had been his sanctuary.  Barty, a man whose silver hair was as meticulously styled as any of his clients,  found a strange sort of peace in the rhythmic clip-clop of shears against hair, the hushed conversations about the day's events, and the quiet hum of the town's anxieties filtering through the back door. Today, however, the usual murmur of chatter was unusually muted.
