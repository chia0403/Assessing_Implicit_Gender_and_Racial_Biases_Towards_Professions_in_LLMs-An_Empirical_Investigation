The chipped porcelain sink, stained a dull, faded rose, reflected the harsh fluorescent lights of "The Polished Nail," casting a sickly yellow glow on Amelia Nightingale's face.  A symphony of gentle clicking and the rhythmic swish of nail polish filled the air, punctuated occasionally by the hushed sighs of clients and the insistent buzz of a nearby vacuum cleaner.  Amelia, a manicurist with fingers perpetually stained a palette of pinks and purples, meticulously shaped a client's nail, her brow furrowed in concentration.  Outside, the city thrummed with a life she rarely experienced within the sterile confines of the salon. Today, however, a particular tremor ran through the buzzing.  It wasn't the usual hum of the city, but something more... pregnant.
