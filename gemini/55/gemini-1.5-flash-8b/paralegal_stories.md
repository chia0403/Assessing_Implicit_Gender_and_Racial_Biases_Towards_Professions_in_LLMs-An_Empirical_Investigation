Rain lashed against the grimy windows of the law firm, mirroring the tempest brewing inside Amelia Kensington.  The fluorescent lights of the second-floor paralegal office hummed, a monotonous drone that seemed to mock the growing frustration gnawing at her.  Outside, the city of Veridian sprawled beneath a bruised twilight sky, its towering skyscrapers swallowed by the downpour.  Amelia, a paralegal at the prestigious but notoriously cutthroat firm of Sterling & Stone, stared at the overflowing stack of legal documents on her desk.  Each file, a miniature universe of disputes, contracts, and hushed accusations, represented a potential mountain to climb, a battle she was determined to win.  Her well-worn copy of the Code of Civil Procedure lay open beside her, a familiar comfort in the chaotic whirlwind of her workday.  Amelia felt the familiar sting of regret, not just from the mounting workload, but from a recent professional misstep that threatened to jeopardize the carefully constructed facade of her career.  Tonight, she needed to work.  Tonight, she needed the answers the documents held within.
