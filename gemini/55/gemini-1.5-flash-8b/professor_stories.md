The biting wind whipped Professor Eleanor Vance's cloak around her as she hurried across the cobbled plaza.  Dust motes danced in the pale winter light filtering through the skeletal branches of the ancient oak trees that bordered the square.  Dampness clung to the air, a chill that seeped into the very marrow of her bones, mirroring the unease that gnawed at the edges of her consciousness.  She was Professor Eleanor Vance, esteemed historian of forgotten civilizations, currently on sabbatical and, against her better judgment, drawn into the heart of the city of Veridia, a place whispered about in hushed tones and shrouded in mystery.  The city, nestled in a valley cradled by jagged peaks, seemed to hold its breath, waiting for something, or someone.  A palpable sense of foreboding settled over the place, thick and suffocating, like the miasma hanging over the swampy lands that lay just beyond the city walls.  Every shadowed alley, every crumbling stone building seemed to speak of secrets locked away for millennia.
