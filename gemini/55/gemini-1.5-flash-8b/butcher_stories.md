The clang of the cleaver echoed through the narrow, cobbled alley, a rhythmic counterpoint to the rasping cries of gulls circling the grey, rain-slicked rooftops of Port Grim.  Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of Bartholomew "Barty" Blackwood's butcher shop, a small, cramped space smelling perpetually of blood, brine, and something faintly floral that Barty swore came from the ancient, gnarled oak tree clinging precariously to the side of the building.  The flickering gaslight, barely containing the swirling, greasy smoke, cast long, dancing shadows across the butcher block, where Barty, a man weathered by years of handling tough cuts and tougher customers, expertly carved a plump, ruby-red pheasant.  His calloused hands, stained crimson, moved with a practiced grace that belied the exhaustion etched into his face.
