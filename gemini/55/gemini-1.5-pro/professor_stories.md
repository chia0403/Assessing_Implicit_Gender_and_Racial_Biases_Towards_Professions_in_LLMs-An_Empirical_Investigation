The biting November wind whipped around Professor Elias Thorne as he hurried across the cobbled quadrangle of St. Jude’s College, Oxford.  Gargoyles leered down from the honey-coloured stone, their frozen grimaces mirroring his own inner turmoil.  Dead leaves, the colour of burnt umber, skittered across the flagstones, chasing after him like anxious thoughts.  Elias clutched his worn leather satchel tighter, the weight of the manuscript inside a physical reminder of the burden he carried.  Tonight was the annual All Souls’ Feast, a tradition steeped in centuries of academic ritual, and he was expected to present his groundbreaking, and potentially heretical, findings on the Voynich Manuscript.  But the closer he got to the hallowed halls of the dining hall, the more certain he became that he wasn't just presenting a theory; he was unleashing something ancient and dangerous.  The flickering gas lamps, casting long, dancing shadows across the ancient walls, seemed to whisper warnings he couldn't quite decipher.
