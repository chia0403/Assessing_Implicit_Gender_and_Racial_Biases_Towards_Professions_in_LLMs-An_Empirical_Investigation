The biting wind whipped through the Cascade Mountains, carrying the scent of pine and the faint, metallic tang of approaching snow.  Dawn hadn't yet broken over the towering Douglas firs, their branches laden with a fresh dusting of frost, and the only light came from the flickering headlamp strapped to Elias Vance's forehead. Elias, his breath clouding in the frigid air, adjusted the thick wool cap pulled low over his ears and surveyed the behemoth before him: a Western red cedar, easily twice the width of his pickup truck, its bark a deep, furrowed tapestry of age.  Camp Hemlock, nestled deep in the Gifford Pinchot National Forest, was still asleep, oblivious to the silent battle Elias was about to wage against this giant of the woods.
