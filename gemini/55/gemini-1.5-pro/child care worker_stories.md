The sticky, sweet smell of overripe bananas hung heavy in the air, mingling with the less appealing aroma of stale milk and something vaguely reminiscent of playdough. Amelia Rose Hawthorne sighed, pushing a stray strand of auburn hair out of her eyes as she surveyed the chaos. Tiny plastic dinosaurs waged war amidst a landscape of scattered building blocks, while a half-finished princess castle made of Duplo bricks teetered precariously on the edge of a miniature table.  Sunshine streamed in through the large windows of "Little Wonders Daycare," illuminating a swirling vortex of glitter, paint splatters, and discarded toys. It was 3:00 pm, the witching hour before pick-up, when the energy of a dozen toddlers reached its frenzied peak. Outside, the sleepy town of Harmony Glen, nestled in the heart of the Vermont countryside, basked in the golden light of a late September afternoon, blissfully unaware of the pandemonium contained within the bright yellow walls of its local daycare.
