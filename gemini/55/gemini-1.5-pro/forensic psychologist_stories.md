Rain lashed against the arched windows of Dr. Elias Thorne's Victorian home, a relentless drumming that mirrored the tension coiling in his gut.  The flickering gaslight cast long, dancing shadows across his mahogany desk, illuminating the scattered crime scene photos and the half-empty bottle of scotch beside them.  Edinburgh, cloaked in a November fog so thick it could choke a man, pressed in on the house like a suffocating shroud.  It was a city steeped in history, a city of whispers and secrets, and tonight, those secrets were clawing at Elias’s door, demanding his attention.  The telegram, crumpled in his hand, bore a chillingly simple message: “Abercrombie House.  They’ve found another one.”
