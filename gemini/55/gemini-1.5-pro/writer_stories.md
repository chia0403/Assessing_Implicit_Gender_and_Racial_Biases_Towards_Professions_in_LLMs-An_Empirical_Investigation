Rain lashed against the attic window, a frantic drummer keeping time with the creaking timbers of the old house.  Dust motes, illuminated by the single bare bulb hanging precariously from the rafter, danced in the swirling air.  Eleanor Vance Holloway, her fingers stained with ink and her brow furrowed in concentration, hunched over a battered typewriter, the keys clacking a staccato rhythm against the storm's percussion.  The attic, her self-proclaimed sanctuary, was a cluttered haven crammed with overflowing bookcases, stacks of manuscripts, and forgotten treasures unearthed from the depths of the sprawling Victorian she'd inherited from her eccentric great-aunt.  Located on the windswept cliffs overlooking the churning grey expanse of the North Sea, Cliffside Manor was as isolated and tempestuous as Eleanor's current writing project, a gothic mystery novel that seemed to be mirroring the weather outside.
