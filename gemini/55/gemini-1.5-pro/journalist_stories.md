Rain lashed against the grimy windows of The Mariner's Rest, blurring the already fading neon sign advertising "Rooms To Let."  Inside, Amelia "Amy" Hawthorne, a journalist for the Boston Chronicle, huddled deeper into her worn trench coat, the damp chill seeping into her bones despite the mug of lukewarm coffee clutched in her hands. Salt spray hung heavy in the air, mingling with the stale scent of beer and fried fish that permeated the small, dimly lit tavern.  The isolated fishing village of Port Blossom, Maine, usually picturesque with its clapboard houses and lobster boats bobbing in the harbor, was tonight a desolate, windswept landscape shrouded in mist and shadowed by the looming threat of a storm brewing out at sea.  Amy had arrived only an hour ago, her small rental car barely making it through the flooded roads, drawn to this forgotten corner of the coast by a whispered rumour, a story too strange to ignore, a story about a missing fishing trawler and a catch that no one dared to speak of.
