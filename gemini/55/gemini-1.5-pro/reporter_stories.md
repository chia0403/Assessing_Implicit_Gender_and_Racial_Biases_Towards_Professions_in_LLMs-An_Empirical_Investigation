Rain lashed against the grimy windows of The Daily Clarion's office, mimicking the tempest brewing inside Amelia “Mia” Rodriguez.  Fluorescent lights hummed overhead, casting a sickly yellow glow on the cluttered desks and overflowing ashtrays.  Cigarette smoke, cheap coffee, and the faint scent of printer ink hung heavy in the air.  Ashford, Ohio, a town clinging to the rust belt like a barnacle to a sinking ship, was suffocating Mia.  Two years she'd been stuck here, chasing down stories about runaway shopping carts and the annual pie-eating contest, dreaming of the bylines that would carry her away to something bigger, something real.  Tonight, however, felt different.  The crumpled police blotter in her hand, damp from the relentless downpour, spoke of something darker than usual, something that whispered of a story worth fighting for.
