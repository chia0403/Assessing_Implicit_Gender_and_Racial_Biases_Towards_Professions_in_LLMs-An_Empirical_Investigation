Rain lashed against the arched windows of the Fitzgerald Building, blurring the already fading twilight into a smear of grey. Inside, on the twenty-second floor, Amelia Grace Holloway hunched deeper into her worn office chair, the flickering fluorescent lights reflecting off the thick legal documents spread across her desk. The air tasted of stale coffee and the faint, metallic tang of ozone from the constantly whirring printer in the corner. Amelia, a paralegal for the prestigious, if somewhat ruthless, law firm of Blackwood, Thorne & Davies, was facing a deadline tighter than a drum skin.  Outside, the Chicago wind howled like a banshee, a fitting soundtrack to the frantic energy thrumming through her veins.  The case, a hostile takeover involving two rival tech giants, was a monster, and Amelia was tasked with corralling the mountain of paperwork into some semblance of order before the partners arrived at 7 am for their pre-court strategy session.  She rubbed her tired eyes, pushing a stray strand of auburn hair behind her ear.  This was going to be a long night.
