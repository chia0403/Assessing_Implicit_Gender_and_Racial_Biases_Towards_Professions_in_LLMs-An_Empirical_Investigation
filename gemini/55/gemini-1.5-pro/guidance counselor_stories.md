The fluorescent lights of McKinley High School hummed a monotonous B-flat, a soundtrack Amelia Grace Hawthorne had grown accustomed to over her fifteen years as a guidance counselor.  Rain lashed against the large, grime-streaked windows of her office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside sixteen-year-old Jason Kepler, who sat across from her, hunched over like a wilting sunflower. Outside, the late October wind howled through the skeletal branches of the elm trees lining the school’s perimeter, scattering the already vibrant autumn leaves across the damp asphalt.  Inside, the air was thick with the unspoken anxieties of college applications, scholarship deadlines, and the looming uncertainty of the future.  Amelia, with her warm smile and a cup of chamomile tea steaming beside her, was the designated lighthouse in this storm.  This Tuesday, however, felt different. The usual teenage angst radiating from Jason was laced with something darker, something Amelia couldn’t quite decipher.
