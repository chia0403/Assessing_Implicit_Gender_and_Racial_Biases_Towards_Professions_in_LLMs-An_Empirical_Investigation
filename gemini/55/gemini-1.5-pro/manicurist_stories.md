The humid Miami air, thick with the scent of hibiscus and salt, clung to Emilia Reyes like a second skin. Inside "Nailed It," her tiny salon tucked between a laundromat and a bodega in Little Havana, the air conditioner battled valiantly, its whirring a constant soundtrack to Emilia's life.  Sunlight, fractured by the dusty blinds, painted stripes across the worn linoleum floor, illuminating flecks of glitter and stray rhinestones like fallen stars. Emilia, her name tag pinned crookedly to her flamingo-pink uniform, tapped a long, acrylic nail against the countertop, her gaze fixed on the empty pedicure chair. It was a Tuesday afternoon, usually her busiest time, but the streets outside were unusually quiet, a stillness that mirrored the growing unease in Emilia's stomach. Something was different. Something was off.
