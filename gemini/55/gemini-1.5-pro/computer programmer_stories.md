Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of Elias Thorne's apartment, blurring the already fading neon glow of downtown Seattle.  Thirty-two stories below, the emerald-green waters of Puget Sound churned restlessly, mirroring the turmoil in Elias's gut.  His apartment, usually a sanctuary of organized chaos – stacks of programming manuals interspersed with half-finished projects and empty ramen containers – felt oppressive tonight.  The air hung thick with the smell of burnt coffee and the low hum of his custom-built server rack, its blinking LEDs like the watchful eyes of some digital deity.  Tonight, however, even the familiar comfort of his code couldn't soothe him.  He stared at the cryptic error message flashing on his monitor, a single line of crimson text that threatened to unravel months of painstaking work.  He knew, with a chilling certainty, that this was more than just a bug.  This was a breach.
