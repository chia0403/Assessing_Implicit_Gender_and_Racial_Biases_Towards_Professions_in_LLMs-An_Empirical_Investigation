Rain lashed against the arched window of Dr. Eleanor Vance's office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her newest client.  The room, usually a haven of calm with its muted sage walls, soft lighting, and carefully chosen artwork depicting serene landscapes, felt charged with a nervous energy.  It was late October in Port Blossom, Maine, a town typically known for its picturesque harbor and vibrant fall foliage, but today, the charm was obscured by a thick, clinging fog that had rolled in off the Atlantic, bringing with it a damp chill that seeped into the bones. Eleanor, with her neatly coiled auburn hair and intelligent green eyes, sat across from a young man whose knuckles were white against the worn arms of his chair. He hadn't spoken a word since arriving, the silence punctuated only by the rhythmic drumming of the rain and the distant, mournful cry of a foghorn.
