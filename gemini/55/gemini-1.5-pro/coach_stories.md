The biting November wind whipped across the deserted bleachers of Harmon Field, stinging Amelia "Amy" Rodriguez's cheeks and tugging at the brim of her Chicago Cubs baseball cap.  Dead leaves, the color of burnt caramel, skittered across the cracked asphalt track circling the football field.  The floodlights, usually illuminating Friday night glories, were dark, casting long, skeletal shadows from the towering oaks that bordered the field.  Harmon Field, home of the struggling Mill Creek High Ravens, was a place of faded glory, a testament to a town that had seen better days, much like Amy herself.  Tonight, though, the silence held a different weight, a heavier anticipation.  Amy wasn't here for a game. She was here for a reckoning.
