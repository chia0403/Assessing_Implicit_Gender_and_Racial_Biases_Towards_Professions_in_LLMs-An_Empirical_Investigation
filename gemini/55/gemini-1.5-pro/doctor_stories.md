The biting wind whipped off the North Sea, stinging Dr. Elias Thorne's cheeks as he hurried along the cobbled lane.  The salt spray clung to his thick wool coat, leaving a faint, gritty residue.  Dusk was settling over the small fishing village of Port Blossom, Cornwall, painting the sky in bruised purples and angry greys.  Gas lamps flickered to life in the windows of the squat, stone cottages that huddled together against the elements, their warm glow a stark contrast to the encroaching darkness.  The air was thick with the smell of brine and woodsmoke, a familiar scent that Elias usually found comforting, but tonight, a knot of unease tightened in his stomach.  The telegram he clutched in his gloved hand, crumpled and damp, spoke of a sudden illness, a mysterious affliction that had gripped the village with alarming speed.
