The rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of The Rusty Nail, a rhythm that matched the thrumming anxiety in Elias Thorne's chest.  Elias, all sharp angles and quiet intensity, wiped down the sticky mahogany bar with a practiced hand, the damp rag leaving swirling trails in the dust.  The Nail, nestled in the forgotten corner of Port Blossom, Oregon, was usually a haven from the relentless coastal storms. Tonight, however, the wind howled like a banshee, rattling the mismatched windows and mirroring the turmoil brewing inside him. The only other occupant was old Man Hemlock, perpetually nursing a half-empty glass of whiskey in the darkest booth, his face obscured by shadows and the brim of his worn fedora. The clock above the chipped mirror behind the bar ticked towards midnight, each second echoing the unspoken question hanging heavy in the air:  what, or rather *who*, was Elias waiting for?
