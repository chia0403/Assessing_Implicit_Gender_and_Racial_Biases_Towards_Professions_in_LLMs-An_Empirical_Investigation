The biting November wind whipped around Agnes Blackwood's ankles as she trudged up the long, gravel driveway of Eventide Manor.  Dead leaves, the color of rust and bruised plums, swirled around her like disgruntled spirits.  Eventide, a hulking Victorian mansion perched on a windswept bluff overlooking the churning grey expanse of the North Sea, seemed to sigh and creak under the onslaught.  Agnes, her grey hair tucked firmly beneath a practical wool cap, clutched her worn carpet bag tighter, the chill seeping into the very marrow of her bones. She’d been housekeeper at Eventide for fifteen years, ever since the old master, Mr. Silas Thorne, had passed, leaving the estate to his eccentric nephew, whom Agnes had yet to meet.  Today was the day the new Mr. Thorne was due to arrive, and a strange, unsettled feeling, like the premonition of a storm, prickled at the edges of Agnes’s awareness. The grand oak doors of Eventide, adorned with grotesque gargoyles, loomed before her, promising both shelter and a mystery yet to unfold.
