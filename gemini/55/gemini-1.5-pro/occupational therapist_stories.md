The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne's auburn braid across her face as she hurried down Elm Street, the scent of woodsmoke and decaying leaves thick in the air.  Crisp, fallen maple leaves crunched under her sensible, waterproof boots.  The small town of Havenwood, nestled in the heart of the Vermont Green Mountains, was preparing for winter.  Shop windows displayed hand-knitted scarves and mittens, and the air buzzed with the quiet anticipation of the first snowfall.  Amelia, her breath puffing white clouds in the frigid air, clutched her oversized tote bag, which contained everything from therapeutic putty to a pediatric puzzle, ready for her next house call.  Her patient, a young boy recovering from a serious car accident, lived in a farmhouse just outside town, a place Amelia had come to think of as both a sanctuary and a battleground in the fight for his regained independence.
