The biting November wind whipped Amelia "Amy" Hawthorne's auburn braid across her face as she wrestled a giant ficus through the double doors of the Van Gelder mansion.  Gargoyles leered down from the eaves of the imposing brownstone, perched like grotesque, feathered pigeons against the bruised twilight sky of Manhattan's Upper East Side.  Inside, the cavernous foyer, dimly lit by a single, flickering chandelier, echoed with the rustle of dust sheets and the faint scent of mothballs.  This was Amy's latest project: to breathe life back into this Gilded Age relic, a task as daunting as scaling Everest in stilettos, particularly given the notoriously exacting nature of her client, Mrs. Van Gelder, the "Ice Queen" of Fifth Avenue.
