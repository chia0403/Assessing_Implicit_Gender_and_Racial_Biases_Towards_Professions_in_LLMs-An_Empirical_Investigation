The biting November wind whipped around Elias Thorne's ankles as he balanced precariously on the top rung of his ladder.  Tiny flakes of snow, the first of the season, danced around him like agitated moths against the backdrop of a bruised, twilight sky. Below him, the sprawling Victorian manor of Blackwood Hall hunched like a slumbering beast, its many darkened windows like vacant eyes staring out at the encroaching night.  Elias, his breath puffing out in ghostly clouds, tightened his grip on the corroded weather vane, its copper skin green with age and neglect.  He was perched atop the highest turret of the house, a dizzying spire that seemed to pierce the very heart of the gathering storm, tasked with replacing the antiquated lightning rod before the full fury of winter descended upon the isolated Yorkshire moors.
