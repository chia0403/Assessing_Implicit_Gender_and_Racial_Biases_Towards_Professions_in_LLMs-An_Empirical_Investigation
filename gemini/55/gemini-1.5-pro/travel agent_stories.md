Rain lashed against the window of "Wanderlust Voyages," blurring the neon pink flamingo that adorned the glass.  Inside, Amelia "Amy" Rose Hawthorne shivered, despite the humming space heater tucked beneath her desk. January in Cleveland was proving even drearier than usual, and the grey skies mirrored the current state of her business.  The travel posters plastered around the small office – vibrant beaches of Bali, the snow-capped peaks of the Himalayas, the bustling souks of Marrakech – felt mockingly cheerful in the face of the empty appointment book lying open before her.  Amy tapped a chipped, coral-pink nail against the worn cover, the only sound besides the relentless drumming of the rain and the occasional sigh escaping her lips. This was not how she’d envisioned her dream travel agency thriving, six months after its grand opening.
