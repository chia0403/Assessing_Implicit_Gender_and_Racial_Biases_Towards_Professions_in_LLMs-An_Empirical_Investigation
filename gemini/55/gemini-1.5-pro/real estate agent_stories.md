The salt-laced wind whipped Amelia "Amy" Hawthorne's auburn braid across her face as she wrestled a "For Sale" sign into the unforgiving Cornish soil.  Grey cliffs, streaked with white guano, loomed behind her, the crashing waves of the Celtic Sea a constant, restless soundtrack.  This desolate headland, overlooking the tiny fishing village of Portwenn, was the most challenging property Amy had ever tried to sell.  Windswept Cottage, as it was optimistically named, was a ramshackle two-bedroom affair clinging precariously to the cliff edge. Its peeling paint, boarded-up windows, and distinct air of impending collapse didn't exactly scream "dream home." But Amy, ever the optimist, saw potential beneath the decay, a diamond in the rough waiting to be unearthed.  Besides, the view, even on a blustery November day like this, was simply breathtaking.
