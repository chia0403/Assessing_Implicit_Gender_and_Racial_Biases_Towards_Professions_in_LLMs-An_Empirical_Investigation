The salt spray kissed Amelia Reyes's face as she gripped the railing of the *Sea Serpent*, a rickety but beloved ferry boat chugging its way towards Aisling Isle.  The emerald hump of the island, crowned with the crumbling, grey stone of Dunbroch Castle, grew larger with every churn of the propeller.  Amelia smoothed down her wind-whipped auburn braid and adjusted the microphone clipped to her collar.  Around her, the twenty-odd tourists huddled deeper into their brightly coloured rain jackets, oblivious to the raw, breathtaking beauty of the North Atlantic in early spring.  Aisling Isle, tucked away off the coast of County Donegal, Ireland, was renowned for its wild, untouched landscapes and the tragic legend of the Sorcha, the island’s mythical sea serpent, but today, it seemed, the only monster they’d encounter would be the capricious Irish weather.  Amelia, however, felt a prickle of anticipation she couldn't explain.  The air hummed with a strange energy, a palpable shift in the usual serenity of the island, and the closer they got, the stronger it became.
