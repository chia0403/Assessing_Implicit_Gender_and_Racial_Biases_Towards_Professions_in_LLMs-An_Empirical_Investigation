The biting November wind whipped Dr. Eleanor Vance’s auburn hair across her face as she hurried across the rain-slicked quad of Ashwood High.  Water pooled in the shadowed crevices of the ancient brick buildings, reflecting the bruised purple of the twilight sky.  Above, the skeletal branches of the oak trees clawed at the darkening heavens, their silhouettes stark against the fading light.  Ashwood, nestled in the heart of a sleepy, seemingly idyllic New England town, was deceptively peaceful. Beneath the veneer of picturesque colonial architecture and well-manicured lawns simmered a disquiet, a subtle unease that Dr. Vance, school psychologist for the past five years, had come to know intimately. Tonight, that unease felt particularly acute, a knot of apprehension tightening in her stomach with each crunching step on the gravel path.  The call had come just an hour ago – a student, a particularly troubled one, had vanished without a trace.
