The biting November wind whipped Amelia "Amy" Hernandez's dark hair across her face as she perched precariously on a steel beam, a hundred feet above the bustling city street below.  Construction dust, thick as pea soup, clung to her work boots and the faded denim of her overalls.  The skeletal frame of the new Zenith Tower, a monument to glass and ambition, clawed at the bruised twilight sky.  Amy, a veteran of countless scaffolding climbs and concrete pours, felt the familiar thrill of the height, a stark contrast to the gnawing anxiety churning in her stomach.  The city, a glittering tapestry of lights and shadows, stretched out beneath her like a rumpled carpet, but tonight, the usual beauty was lost in the weight of a secret she couldn't bear to keep any longer.
