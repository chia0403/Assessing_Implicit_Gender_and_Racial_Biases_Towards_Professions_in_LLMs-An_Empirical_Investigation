The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s auburn braid across her face as she hurried down Elm Street, the collar of her worn peacoat pulled high.  The scent of woodsmoke and damp leaves clung to the air, a familiar autumnal perfume that usually calmed her, but tonight, it did little to soothe the frantic flutter in her chest.  Ahead, nestled between a bakery that smelled perpetually of cinnamon and a dusty antique shop, stood Sunny Days Childcare, its cheerful yellow paint looking almost sickly under the grey, threatening sky.  Inside, three small, potentially very loud, very messy, and very hungry children awaited her – and Amelia, despite her twenty-two years, felt a familiar wave of pre-shift anxiety wash over her.
