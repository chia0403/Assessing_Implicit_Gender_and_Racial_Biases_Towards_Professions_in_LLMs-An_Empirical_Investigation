The fluorescent lights of the St. Jude's Medical Center phlebotomy lab hummed a monotonous tune, a soundtrack to Amelia Hernandez's carefully orchestrated morning.  Amelia, with her perpetually tired but kind eyes and a nametag that read simply "Amelia H.," already had three vials of blood meticulously labeled and spinning gently in the centrifuge.  The sterile scent of antiseptic mingled with the faint metallic tang of blood, a familiar perfume that clung to her like a second skin after twelve years in the profession.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows overlooking the city, a grey curtain obscuring the usually vibrant cityscape. The storm, Amelia mused, mirrored the tempest brewing in her own life; a tempest far less predictable and considerably more complicated than a simple blood draw.
