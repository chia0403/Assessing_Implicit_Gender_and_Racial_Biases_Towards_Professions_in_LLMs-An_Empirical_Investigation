The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s hair across her face as she wrestled the last stubborn bale of hay into the goat pen.  The air, thick with the smell of damp earth and woodsmoke, carried the faint, almost imperceptible tang of impending snow.  Around her, the sprawling grounds of Blackwood Manor – once a grand estate, now a somewhat dilapidated haven for rescued animals – groaned under the weight of encroaching twilight.  Ancient oaks, their branches gnarled like arthritic fingers, clawed at the bruised purple sky.  A lone raven perched on a crumbling chimney pot, its obsidian eyes fixed on Amelia with unnerving intensity.  She shivered, pulling her worn woolen scarf tighter, not entirely from the cold.  A feeling, unsettling and vaguely premonitory, prickled at the edge of her awareness. Tonight felt different.  Something was coming.
