The biting wind howled a mournful song across the desolate plains of Xylos, whipping snow into frenzied spirals that stung Elias Thorne’s face.  He hunched deeper into his thick furs, the rough wool scratching against his chapped skin a minor discomfort compared to the gnawing cold that seeped into his very bones.  Elias, a veteran guard of the Crimson Citadel, stood watch at the outermost tower, its crumbling stone a stark silhouette against the bruised purple of the twilight sky. The Citadel itself, once a proud bastion of power, now seemed to cower under the weight of the unending blizzard, its ramparts groaning under the relentless assault of wind and snow.  The only sound louder than the wind was the rhythmic clang of the Citadel’s ancient bell, tolling a somber rhythm that echoed the bleakness of the landscape.
