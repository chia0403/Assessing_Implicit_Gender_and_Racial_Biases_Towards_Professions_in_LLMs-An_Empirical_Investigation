The chipped porcelain mug warmed Judge Amelia “Amy” Hawthorne’s hands, the lukewarm chamomile tea doing little to soothe the icy dread that had settled in her stomach.  Rain lashed against the tall, gothic windows of her chambers, mirroring the tempest brewing within.  The air hung thick with the scent of old leather and damp stone, a familiar aroma in the ancient courthouse of Blackwood, a town steeped in history and shadowed by a long, brutal winter.  Outside, the wind howled a mournful dirge, its icy breath rattling the antiquated casements. Inside, the only light came from the flickering gas lamps, casting long, dancing shadows that seemed to writhe with the unspoken anxieties of the upcoming trial.  Amy stared out at the stormy landscape, the grey mirroring the grim reality of the case before her: the murder of young Elias Thorne, a crime that had shattered the fragile peace of Blackwood and threatened to unravel its very fabric.
