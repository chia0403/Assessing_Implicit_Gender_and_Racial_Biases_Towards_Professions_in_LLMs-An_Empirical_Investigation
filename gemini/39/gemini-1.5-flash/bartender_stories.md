The chipped Formica bar top felt cool beneath Elara Vance's fingertips as she polished a snifter, the rhythmic swish of the cloth a counterpoint to the low hum of conversation and clinking glasses.  Rain lashed against the grimy windows of "The Rusty Mug," blurring the neon glow of the liquor store across the street into a hazy, shimmering smear.  The air hung thick with the scent of stale beer, spilled whiskey, and the faint, almost imperceptible perfume of desperation – a familiar aroma to Elara, who'd been tending bar in this rain-soaked corner of New Orleans’s French Quarter for the better part of a decade.  Tonight, however, the usual Friday night buzz felt different, charged with an underlying tension that prickled at the edges of her senses, like the static before a storm.
