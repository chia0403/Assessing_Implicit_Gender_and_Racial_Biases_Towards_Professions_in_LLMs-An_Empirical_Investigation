The scent of lavender and burnt sugar hung heavy in the air, a peculiar perfume unique to "Shear Perfection," Amelia Katherine Finch's humble but beloved salon.  Rain lashed against the tall, gothic windows of the converted Victorian shopfront, blurring the already fading gaslight glow of the cobbled street outside.  Inside, Amelia, perched on a worn, leather-upholstered stool, hummed a jaunty, off-key tune as she meticulously snipped a stray strand of auburn hair from her latest client, a nervous-looking young woman whose eyes darted nervously towards the increasingly violent storm.  The only other sounds were the rhythmic snip-snip of Amelia's shears and the low rumble of the thunder echoing through the aged timber beams.  Amelia, with her fiery red hair escaping its usually precise bun, and nimble fingers that moved with the practiced grace of a concert pianist, was utterly oblivious to the tempest raging both inside and outside.  Tonight, however, a different kind of storm was brewing...
