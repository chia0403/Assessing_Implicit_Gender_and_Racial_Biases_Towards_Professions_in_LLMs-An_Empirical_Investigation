The fluorescent lights of "Sterling & Steele, Attorneys at Law" hummed a monotonous tune, a soundtrack to Amelia Grace Hawthorne's otherwise silent morning.  Amelia, or Amy as most people insisted on calling her – a familiarity she tolerated only because their alternative was a torrent of legal jargon –  sat perched at her immaculate reception desk, its polished surface reflecting the city skyline visible through the panoramic windows.  Rain lashed against the glass, a relentless drumming that mirrored the nervous thrum in her own chest.  It was the kind of grey, drizzly Monday morning that promised only tedious paperwork and the ever-present possibility of Mr. Sterling's unexpectedly volcanic temper.  Today, however, the usual Monday anxieties were amplified tenfold.  A particularly important – and notoriously demanding – client was expected at any moment, and Amy hadn't even managed to finish her lukewarm coffee.
