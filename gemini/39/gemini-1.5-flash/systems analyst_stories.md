The stale air of the server room hung heavy with the scent of ozone and desperation.  Fluorescent lights hummed a monotonous dirge above Amelia Katherine Reed, their flickering glow reflecting off the rows of blinking servers that lined the walls like metallic sentinels.  Amelia, a systems analyst for the notoriously chaotic tech startup "Innovate or Die,"  ran a hand through her already-messy auburn braid, the strands sticking stubbornly to her forehead.  Sweat beaded on her brow, not entirely from the oppressive heat of the room, but more from the pressure of the situation.  A critical system failure – a failure that threatened to bring the entire company, and potentially several others reliant on their platform, to its knees – was unfolding before her, and the clock was ticking down faster than she could type.
