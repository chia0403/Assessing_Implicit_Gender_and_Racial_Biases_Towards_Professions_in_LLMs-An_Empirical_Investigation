The biting November wind whipped Amelia Stone's hair across her face as she hurried down the cobbled lane, the scent of woodsmoke and brine clinging to the damp air.  She pulled her worn wool scarf tighter, the familiar sting in her eyes a testament to the relentless sea spray that perpetually kissed the cliffs of Port Blossom, Maine.  The small, picturesque fishing village, nestled between the unforgiving Atlantic and a range of brooding pines, was as much a character in her life as any human. Tonight, however, it felt particularly hostile, the shadows lengthening ominously as dusk bled into a bruised purple sky.  Amelia, a physician's assistant at the town's only clinic, tucked a stray strand of auburn hair behind her ear and quickened her pace, the rhythmic crunch of her boots on the gravel a counterpoint to the frantic hammering of her heart.  She was late, dreadfully late, and the call had been urgent.
