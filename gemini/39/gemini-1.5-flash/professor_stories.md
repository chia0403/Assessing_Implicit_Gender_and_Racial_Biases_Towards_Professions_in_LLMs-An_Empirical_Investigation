Professor Augustine Blackwood adjusted his spectacles, the thin wire frames pinching the bridge of his nose.  The biting November wind whipped through the skeletal branches of the ancient oaks lining the driveway of Blackwood Manor, their gnarled fingers scratching at the slate roof like restless ghosts.  Rain, a relentless, icy drizzle, plastered his tweed jacket to his back, mirroring the damp chill that had settled deep in his bones.  Blackwood Manor, a gothic behemoth perched precariously on the cliff overlooking the churning grey expanse of the North Sea, was less a home and more a brooding sentinel, its stone face etched with the secrets of centuries past.  Tonight, those secrets felt particularly close, whispering on the wind like the mournful cries of gulls lost in the storm.
