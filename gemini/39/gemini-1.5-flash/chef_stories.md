The humid air hung thick and heavy, a clinging shroud woven from the scent of frying garlic, simmering spices, and the faint, metallic tang of blood.  Chef Aurelia “Rella” Marigold, her brow slick with perspiration despite the whirring industrial fans overhead, surveyed her domain with a critical eye.  The kitchen of the *Serpent’s Kiss*, a notorious Bangkok street food stall known as much for its fiery curries as its questionable clientele, throbbed with a chaotic energy.  Woks sizzled like angry geckos, cleavers chopped with the rhythm of a relentless heartbeat, and the rhythmic clang of metal against metal provided the soundtrack to Rella’s nightly ballet of culinary chaos.  Tonight, however, the usual symphony of the kitchen was punctuated by a nervous tremor in her own hands.  A new ingredient, one whispered to possess almost magical properties, had found its way into her meticulously planned menu.  And Rella, despite her reputation for fearless experimentation, felt a prickle of unease crawling up her spine.
