The biting November wind whipped strands of Ms. Eleanor Vance’s auburn hair across her face as she hurried down Maple Street, the collar of her tweed coat pulled high.  Fallen leaves, the colour of burnt umber and rust, skittered across the pavement like frantic scurrying creatures, mirroring the nervous energy churning in her stomach.  Oakhaven Elementary School, a squat, brick building with windows like weary eyes staring out at the darkening sky, loomed ahead.  Inside, twenty-five third-graders – a kaleidoscope of chaotic energy, boundless curiosity, and occasional, unpredictable outbursts – awaited her.  Tonight, however, the usual pre-parent-teacher-night jitters were amplified tenfold.  This wasn't just any parent-teacher night; tonight, Eleanor was presenting her innovative, and possibly controversial, new classroom management technique to the entire school faculty.
