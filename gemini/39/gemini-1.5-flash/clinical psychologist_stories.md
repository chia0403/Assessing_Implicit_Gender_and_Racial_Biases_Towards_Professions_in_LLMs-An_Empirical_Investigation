The stale scent of antiseptic and despair clung to the air in Dr. Eleanor Vance’s office like a persistent cough.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of her high-rise practice, blurring the city lights into hazy streaks of gold and crimson.  Eleanor, her dark hair pulled back severely in a practical bun, steepled her fingers, the faint tremor betraying her own weariness.  The plush, velvet armchair opposite her desk remained empty, a silent testament to the evening's appointment – a young woman named Clara Bellweather, plagued by anxieties so profound they’d driven her to the edge of silence.  Outside, the city roared; inside, only the rhythmic tick-tock of an antique grandfather clock broke the oppressive quiet.  Tonight, however, even the familiar comfort of the clock felt unsettling, a counterpoint to the uneasy stillness that promised a particularly challenging session.
