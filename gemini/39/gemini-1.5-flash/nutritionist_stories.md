The chipped porcelain mug warmed Dr. Eleanor Vance’s hands, the lukewarm chamomile doing little to soothe the icy dread that had settled in her stomach.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of her office, blurring the already hazy cityscape of Vancouver.  Below, the twinkling lights of Gastown fought a losing battle against the encroaching darkness, mirroring the uncertainty that gnawed at Eleanor.  Her office, a stylish yet minimalist space perched on the 27th floor of the Burrard Place building, usually offered a sanctuary of calm – a haven of healthy eating philosophies and meticulously planned meal plans. Tonight, however, the sleek chrome and polished wood felt cold and unwelcoming. The scent of rain and impending storm replaced the usual aroma of freshly ground flaxseed and earthy spices.  The rhythmic drumming of the rain was a relentless counterpoint to the frantic thump-thump-thump of her own heart.
