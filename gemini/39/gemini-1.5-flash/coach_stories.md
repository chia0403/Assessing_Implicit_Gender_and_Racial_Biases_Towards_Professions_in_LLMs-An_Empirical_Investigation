The biting November wind whipped across the skeletal branches of the oaks lining the perimeter of the dilapidated baseball field, carrying with it the scent of damp earth and decaying leaves.  Dust motes, illuminated by the weak, dying sunlight, danced in the beams slicing through the gaps in the crumbling chain-link fence.  This wasn't exactly Wrigley Field, but for Elias "Eli" Thorne, head coach of the under-12 Westview Wildcats, it was home.  Home, or at least, the closest thing to it he had left since… well, since everything changed.  The Wildcats, a ragtag bunch of misfits and hopefuls, were huddled around him, their breath misting in the frigid air, their small faces etched with a mixture of apprehension and quiet determination.  Eli, his own breath clouding his worn, Red Sox-emblazoned cap, surveyed his team with a weary but unwavering gaze. Tonight’s game wasn’t just another game; it was a battle for something more.
