The flickering gaslight cast long, skeletal shadows across the plush velvet chairs in Dr. Eleanor Vance's waiting room. Rain lashed against the leaded glass windows of her Victorian-era practice, a relentless rhythm that mirrored the anxiety thrumming beneath the polished mahogany surface of her desk.  The scent of old books and damp earth mingled in the air, a peculiar perfume unique to her office nestled on the quiet, cobbled side street of Ashworth Lane in the heart of Blackwood, a town steeped in history and, some whispered, in secrets.  Tonight, however, the only secret Eleanor Vance was concerned with was the one hidden behind the apprehensive eyes of her next patient, a young woman named Clara Bellweather, whose appointment loomed like a question mark etched in the very fabric of the twilight.
