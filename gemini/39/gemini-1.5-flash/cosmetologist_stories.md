The scent of coconut and vanilla hung heavy in the air, a cloying sweetness that clung to the plush velvet chairs and the chipped porcelain sink of "Shear Perfection," a salon that hadn't seen a proper renovation since the Reagan administration.  Dust motes, illuminated by the single, flickering fluorescent tube above the styling station, danced in the afternoon sun that slanted through the grimy window.  Thirty-year-old Seraphina "Sera" Bellweather, her fingers stained a kaleidoscope of nail polish colors, sighed, the sound barely audible over the rhythmic whir of the hair dryer next door.  She meticulously cleaned a stray hair from the pristine surface of her manicure station, the chipped enamel a stark contrast to the vibrant turquoise of the polish she'd just applied.  This wasn't exactly the glamorous life she'd envisioned when she graduated top of her cosmetology class, but rent was due, and the promise of a "big break" felt further away than ever.
