The air hung thick and sweet in "Sugar Rush," a bakery so charming it bordered on cliché.  Sunlight, filtered through dusty rose curtains, dappled the countertops laden with pastries that looked too perfect to eat.  But Amelia Katherine Finch, owner and head cake decorator, knew better.  Beneath the flawless buttercream roses and sculpted chocolate swans, lurked a world of flour-dusted anxieties and the ever-present threat of a collapsed ganache. Today, however, the anxiety wasn't about a wonky wedding cake.  It was about the cryptic note tucked into the order for Mrs. Gable's seventy-fifth birthday cake – a simple, unassuming carrot cake, surprisingly requested with a single, unsettling addition:  "Three red roses, but only two stems."
