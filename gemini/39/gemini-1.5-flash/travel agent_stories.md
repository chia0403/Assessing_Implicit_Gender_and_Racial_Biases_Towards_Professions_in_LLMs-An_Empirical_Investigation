The chipped ceramic mug warmed Amelia Hernandez's hands, the lukewarm chamomile tea doing little to soothe the icy dread clenching her stomach.  Rain lashed against the windowpanes of her tiny, cluttered office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her.  Amelia, a travel agent whose specialty was crafting bespoke, adventurous itineraries for the world's most demanding clientele, had never encountered a request quite like this one.  The air in her office, usually alive with the scent of exotic spices and far-off lands gleaned from brochures scattered across her desk, was thick with the smell of impending disaster, and damp wool from her perpetually-worn cardigan. Outside, the neon glow of the "Wanderlust Adventures" sign flickered intermittently in the downpour, a fragile beacon in the grey, unforgiving London night.
