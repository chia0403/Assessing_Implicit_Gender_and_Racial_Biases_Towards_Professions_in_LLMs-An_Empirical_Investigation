The biting wind whipped across the frost-kissed fields of Abernathy Farm, swirling fallen leaves into miniature cyclones that danced across the barren furrows.  The sun, a pale disc in the iron-grey sky, offered little warmth to Elias Thorne, whose breath plumed out in ghostly puffs as he surveyed his land.  Elias, his weathered face etched with the lines of a life spent wrestling a living from the unforgiving soil, pulled his thick wool coat tighter around him.  Abernathy Farm, nestled in the shadow of the brooding Blackthorn Peaks, had been in his family for generations, a patchwork of fields and woodland passed down from father to son since time out of mind.  This year, however, the whispers of an early, brutal winter carried a chill deeper than any Elias had known before.
