The crisp October air, scented with woodsmoke and decaying leaves, whipped around Eleanor Vance’s ankles as she hurried across the cobblestone courtyard.  Greystone Manor, a sprawling gothic edifice perched on a bluff overlooking the churning Atlantic, seemed to absorb the wind’s howl rather than deflect it.  Gargoyles leered from the rain-slicked eaves, their granite faces mirroring Eleanor’s own anxieties.  She wasn't used to house calls, especially not to remote, possibly haunted, mansions owned by eccentric billionaires.  But Mr. Silas Blackwood, notorious recluse and heir to the Blackwood fortune, had specifically requested her, Eleanor Vance, RDN, for a consultation, and her student loans weren't going to pay themselves.
