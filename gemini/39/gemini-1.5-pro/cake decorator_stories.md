The scent of burnt sugar hung heavy in the air, a bitter counterpoint to the usual symphony of vanilla and almond that permeated Genevieve "Gen" Dubois's cake shop, "Batter Up."  Rain lashed against the large front window, blurring the neon "Open" sign and the view of the deserted main street of Havenwood, Oregon.  Havenwood, nestled between the imposing Cascade Mountains and the sprawling Willamette Valley vineyards, was usually a picture of charming, small-town life. But tonight, the storm mirrored the tempest brewing inside Gen.  Her award-winning buttercream roses, normally pristine and perfect, drooped sadly on the half-finished wedding cake for Mayor Thompson's daughter.  And the cake itself, a towering five-tiered monstrosity, leaned precariously, threatening to topple like a drunken giant.
