The wind, a constant companion in the vast emptiness of the Montana plains, whipped Amelia Rose Blackwood's hair across her face as she surveyed her land.  The Rocking B Ranch stretched out before her, a patchwork of emerald green pastures and golden, swaying fields of wheat under a sky so immense it felt like it could swallow her whole.  The early morning sun, just peeking over the sawtooth peaks of the Crazy Mountains to the east, painted the snow-capped summits a fiery orange, a stark contrast to the deep indigo shadows that still clung to the valleys.  The air, crisp and carrying the scent of pine and damp earth, held a promise of a scorching summer day to come.  Amelia, her calloused hands tucked deep into the pockets of her worn denim jacket, squinted at a distant cluster of her Hereford cattle, a frown creasing her brow. Something about their huddled posture didn't sit right.
