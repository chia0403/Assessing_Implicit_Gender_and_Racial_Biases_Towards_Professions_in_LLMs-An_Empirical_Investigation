The rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of Amelia Grace Holloway's office, a relentless rhythm that mirrored the throbbing in her temples.  Her office, tucked away in a forgotten corner of Grimshaw’s Department Store, was usually a haven of quiet order amidst the retail chaos.  Today, however, even the meticulously arranged stacks of invoices and precisely labeled file folders offered little solace. Grimshaw’s, a once-proud emporium in the heart of decaying Ashworth, was teetering on the brink of collapse, and Amelia, the store’s lead accountant, was staring into the abyss of its financial records.  Outside, the November wind howled, a mournful soundtrack to the slow death of a town built on textiles and dreams.  The air, thick with the scent of damp wool and impending doom, pressed in on Amelia, a tangible weight reflecting the burden she carried.  She knew, with a certainty that chilled her more than the draught snaking under the ill-fitting door, that the numbers wouldn't lie.  And the numbers were grim.
