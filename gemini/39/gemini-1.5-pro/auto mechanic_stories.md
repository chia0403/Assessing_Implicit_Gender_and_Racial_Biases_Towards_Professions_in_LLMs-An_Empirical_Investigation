The biting November wind whipped through the gap between Sal's Auto Repair and the abandoned feed store next door, whistling a mournful tune that echoed the state of Amelia "Mia" Rodriguez's bank account.  Grease smudged her cheek, a testament to the stubborn Ford pickup she'd been wrestling with all morning.  Sal's, nestled on the outskirts of Dustbowl, Arizona, a town clinging to Route 66 like a tumbleweed caught on barbed wire, wasn't exactly a goldmine. The rusted sign above the garage, featuring a cartoon wrench with a wink, promised "Miracles Happen Here," but lately, Mia suspected the only miracles involved keeping the place afloat. The desert sun, a pale ghost behind the overcast sky, cast long shadows across the gravel yard littered with engine parts and half-finished projects. Inside the garage, the air hung thick with the smell of oil, gasoline, and desperation.
