The salt spray kissed Amelia "Mia" Thorne's face as she leaned against the weathered railing of her balcony, the scent of brine mingling with the sweet perfume of honeysuckle climbing the latticework. Below, the sleepy Cornish village of Portwenn tumbled down the cliffs towards the churning Atlantic.  Pastel-coloured cottages, huddled together against the wind, looked like sugared almonds scattered across the rugged landscape.  A lone fishing boat, the "Sea Serpent," puttered back to harbour, its silhouette a stark black against the fiery sunset. Mia, wrapped in a thick wool shawl despite the July evening air, watched it disappear behind the headland, her mind already weaving a narrative around the weathered faces of the fishermen and the secrets hidden in their nets.  The deadline for her next novel loomed large, and the wild, untamed beauty of Cornwall, her adopted home, was proving less inspiring than usual. Writer's block, like the fog that often clung to the cliffs, had settled over her, thick and impenetrable.
