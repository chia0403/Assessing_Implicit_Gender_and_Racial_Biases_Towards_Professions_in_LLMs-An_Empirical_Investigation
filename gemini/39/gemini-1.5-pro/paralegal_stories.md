The fluorescent lights of Miller & Sterling hummed a monotonous tune, a soundtrack to Amelia Grace Holloway's Tuesday morning. Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of the 27th floor, obscuring the usually breathtaking view of the Chicago skyline.  The city, normally a vibrant tapestry of steel and glass, was reduced to a blurry grey canvas. Amelia, perched on the edge of her ergonomic chair, meticulously cross-referenced deposition transcripts with a stack of invoices, her brow furrowed in concentration.  A half-eaten container of Greek yogurt sat precariously on the corner of her desk, a testament to the urgency of her task. Mr. Miller, the firm's senior partner, needed this information before his 10 am court appearance, and Amelia, despite the gnawing feeling that something crucial was being overlooked, was determined to deliver. The air in her small, windowless office was thick with the scent of stale coffee and the faint metallic tang of impending stress.
