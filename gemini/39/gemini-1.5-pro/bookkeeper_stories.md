Rain lashed against the leaded windows of Bartholomew Quill's office, mimicking the frantic drumming of his fingers against the mahogany desk.  Bartholomew, a man whose life was normally as ordered as the columns in his meticulously kept ledgers, felt a knot of unease tightening in his stomach.  His office, usually a sanctuary of quiet efficiency within the bustling, gaslit streets of 1888 London, now felt oppressive.  The air, thick with the scent of damp wool and aging paper, seemed to vibrate with a tension Bartholomew couldn't quite place. Outside, the fog, a familiar blanket across the city, swirled with an unnatural, almost predatory energy.  It was a night for secrets, and Bartholomew Quill, bookkeeper for the esteemed firm of Messrs. Albright & Sons, was about to stumble upon one.
