The biting November wind whipped around Ms. Eleanor Vance as she hurried across the cracked asphalt of the St. Jude's School playground.  Her sensible brown coat flapped open, revealing a bright turquoise sweater beneath – a small rebellion against the grey sky and the even greyer mood that seemed to have settled over the fourth-grade class. St. Jude's, a small, brick-built Catholic school nestled in the heart of sleepy Ashwood, Vermont, usually buzzed with a gentle hum of activity. Today, however, a strange quiet had descended, punctuated only by the rustling of dried leaves skittering across the playground and the distant clang of the church bell.  Even the usually boisterous games of tag and hopscotch had been replaced by hushed whispers and anxious glances cast towards the woods bordering the playground’s edge. Something felt off, and Eleanor, with a knot tightening in her stomach, knew it wasn’t just the impending snowstorm.
