The biting wind whipped Amelia “Mia” Rodriguez’s dark hair across her face as she clung to the ladder, the rusty metal groaning beneath her weight. Below, a sickly orange glow pulsed from the broken windows of Mrs. Henderson’s antique shop, illuminating the swirling snowflakes that danced in the pre-dawn gloom.  The air was thick with the acrid smell of burning wood and something else, something chemical and sharp, that made Mia’s eyes sting.  Oakhaven, a normally peaceful town nestled in the foothills of the Appalachian Mountains, hadn't seen a fire this fierce in decades, and the frozen stillness of the January morning was shattered by the frantic shouts of her fellow firefighters and the mournful wail of the fire engine's siren.
