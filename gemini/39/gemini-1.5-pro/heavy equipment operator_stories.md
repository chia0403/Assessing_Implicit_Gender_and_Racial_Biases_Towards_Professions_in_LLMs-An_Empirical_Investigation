The biting wind whipped across the desolate expanse of the Athabasca oil sands, stinging Bernadette "Bernie" Kowalski's cheeks even through the layers of fleece and thermal wear.  Sunrise painted the eastern sky in bruised purples and hesitant oranges, a stark contrast to the inky blackness that had swallowed the world just an hour before. Bernie, perched high in the cab of her Caterpillar D11T dozer, watched the spectacle with a weary sigh, her breath fogging the window.  Around her, the Fort McMurray work site was slowly shuddering to life. The rhythmic rumble of idling engines, the clang of metal on metal, and the distant shouts of workers broke the pre-dawn silence. Floodlights illuminated the scarred landscape, revealing a chaotic tapestry of pipelines, conveyor belts, and towering extraction equipment, all dwarfed by the gargantuan tailings ponds shimmering ominously in the nascent light. Today was Bernie's 30th birthday, but the only present she expected was another grueling twelve-hour shift wrestling the earth.
