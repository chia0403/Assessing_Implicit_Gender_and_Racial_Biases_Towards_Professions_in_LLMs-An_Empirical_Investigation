Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of Elias Thorne's apartment, blurring the Seattle skyline into a watercolour mess of grey and neon. Inside, the only illumination came from the flickering glow of three monitors arranged in a semi-circle around Elias, their screens alive with lines of emerald green code. Empty takeout containers littered his desk, forming a greasy, cardboard mountain range around his keyboard.  Elias, his brown hair a tangled mess atop his head and his eyes bloodshot from a 48-hour coding binge, tapped a final key with a flourish. A low hum filled the room as the server in the corner whirred to life, the culmination of weeks of relentless work finally coming to fruition.  He'd done it. He'd finally cracked the encryption. Now, all he had to do was decide what to do with it.
