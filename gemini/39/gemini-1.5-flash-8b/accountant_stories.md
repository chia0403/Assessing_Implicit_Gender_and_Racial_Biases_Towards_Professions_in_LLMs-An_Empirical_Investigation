The flickering fluorescent lights of the accounting firm, "Numbers & Nuggets," cast long, skeletal shadows across Amelia Peabody's meticulously organized desk.  Papers, piled high like miniature mountains, whispered secrets of invoices, expense reports, and projected profit margins.  Amelia, a woman of precise movements and a calm demeanor, meticulously tallied a column of figures, her brow furrowed in concentration.  The air, thick with the scent of stale coffee and printer toner, hung heavy in the late-afternoon silence.  Outside, the city of Zenith hummed with a life Amelia often felt detached from, a life she navigated with her meticulously crafted spreadsheets and balance sheets.  This was her kingdom, a realm of numbers, where each decimal point held the key to success or failure. Amelia, with her sharp mind and even sharper pencil, was its undisputed queen.
