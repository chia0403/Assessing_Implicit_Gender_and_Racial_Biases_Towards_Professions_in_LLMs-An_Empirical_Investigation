The rain hammered against the arched windows of Amelia Nightingale’s office, a relentless percussion that mirrored the frantic rhythm of her thoughts.  Empty coffee cups, overflowing with the remnants of a stressful morning, sat clustered on her desk like casualties of a tiny war.  Outside, the city of Veridia, nestled amongst the mist-shrouded peaks of the Azure Mountains, seemed to hold its breath, a silent accomplice to Amelia's internal turmoil.  Veridia, a city renowned for its breathtaking beauty and even more breathtakingly exorbitant prices, was about to become the stage for a very complicated journey.  Amelia, a travel agent with a reputation for crafting bespoke experiences—from expeditions to the lost temples of the Amazon to meticulously planned Tuscan wine tours—was facing the most unusual and potentially lucrative, yet terrifyingly problematic, request of her career.  Her client, a reclusive billionaire with a penchant for secrecy and an unquenchable thirst for adventure, had just requested a meticulously designed "disappearance."
