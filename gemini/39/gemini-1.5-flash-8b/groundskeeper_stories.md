The chipped paint on the gazebo's railing, the same shade of faded ochre as Amelia Finch's gardening gloves, seemed to mock the meticulously trimmed boxwoods lining the perimeter of Blackwood Manor.  A chill wind, biting through Amelia's thin jacket, whipped around her as she checked the last of the meticulously arranged autumn blooms.  The air, thick with the scent of decaying leaves and damp earth, hung heavy in the pre-dawn gloom.  Blackwood Manor, shrouded in a tapestry of shadows, loomed like a gothic cathedral, its many windows reflecting the pale, ethereal light of the rising moon.  Amelia, her name whispered on the breeze, felt a prickle of unease.  This was her twenty-seventh consecutive autumn at Blackwood Manor, and yet, this autumn felt different.  Something, or someone, was watching.
