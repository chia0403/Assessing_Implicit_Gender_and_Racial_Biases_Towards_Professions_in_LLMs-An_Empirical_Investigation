The chipped porcelain sink, stained a sickly yellow from years of spilled tonic water and forgotten rinses, reflected the harsh fluorescent light above Amelia Blackwood's head.  Dust motes danced in the beams, mimicking the frantic flutter of her own heart.  The scent of lavender and stale cigarette smoke hung heavy in the air, a perpetual fog clinging to the small, cramped salon tucked away on a side street of the perpetually rainy city of Alderhaven.  Rain lashed against the grimy windowpane, a relentless counterpoint to the rhythmic snip-snip-snip of Amelia's shears.  Outside, the city throbbed with a life she rarely felt part of.  Inside, amongst the tangled knots of hair and whispered secrets, was where she found her own kind of peace.  Amelia, a skilled but often troubled hair cutter, was a woman who believed that the hidden stories were most often found in the strands of a client's hair.  Today's client, however, held a particularly thick, silent knot.
