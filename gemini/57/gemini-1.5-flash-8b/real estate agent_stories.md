The biting November wind whipped Amelia "Amy" Sterling's scarf around her neck, stinging her cheeks as she hurried down the cracked pavement of Elm Street.  The grey sky, a bruised plum colour, mirrored the dampness clinging to the peeling paint of the Victorian houses that lined the street.  Rain threatened, but Amy, a seasoned real estate agent with a reputation for closing deals, wasn't one to be deterred.  Today was the day she'd be showing the dilapidated but charming "Grey Gables" to a potential buyer who'd been circling the property for weeks. This wasn't just another house; this was her chance to prove herself, to climb higher on the ladder of success she'd been meticulously scaling.  The house, a dark, brooding silhouette against the gloomy backdrop, beckoned. Amy pulled her worn leather briefcase tighter, the weight of the sale resting heavily on her shoulders. The faint smell of damp earth and decaying leaves clung to the air, a symphony of autumn's final, melancholic notes.
