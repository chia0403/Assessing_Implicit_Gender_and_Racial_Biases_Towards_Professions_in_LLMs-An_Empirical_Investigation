Amelia Blackwood, a struggling novelist, stared at the blinking cursor on her screen, a silent accusation mocking the blank page that stretched before her like a vast, empty ocean.  Outside, the city of Veridia, a sprawling metropolis choked by smog and shadowed by towering skyscrapers, seemed to hold its breath in anticipation of the storm.  It was a city that felt as constricting and unforgiving as the blank page itself.  The Writers' Roost, a cramped, windowless room filled with the aroma of stale coffee and desperation, was her sanctuary, her prison, her only refuge from the relentless pressure to create.
