The biting November wind whipped Amelia Nightingale's scarf around her neck, stinging her cheeks with icy particles.  Below the overcast sky, the sprawling city of Veridia, nestled between the towering peaks of the Grey Mountains, seemed to shrink under the oppressive gloom.  Rain, a relentless curtain, plastered the cobblestones of the alleyway to a slick, unforgiving surface.  Amelia, Physician's Assistant at the Veridia General Hospital, hurried through the narrow passage, her footsteps echoing eerily in the empty space.  A single flickering streetlamp cast long, dancing shadows around her, highlighting the grime and decay clinging to the aging brick walls.  This particular alley, notorious for its late-night drug trade and whispered rumours of clandestine experiments, was hardly the most welcoming of shortcuts.  Tonight, however, it was the only way.
