The flickering fluorescent lights of the "Globetrotter's Groove" travel agency hummed a monotonous tune, a counterpoint to the frantic clatter of Amelia Blackwood's fingers across the keyboard.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of sunlight piercing the grime-coated window, illuminating a faded poster of the Taj Mahal, a stark contrast to the overflowing inbox of emails screaming for her attention. Amelia, a woman perpetually teetering on the precipice of caffeine-induced exhaustion, adjusted her oversized glasses and sighed.  Another whirlwind week in the life of a travel agent.  Her office, a small, cramped space crammed with overflowing filing cabinets and half-eaten pastries, was a microcosm of her chaotic life.  Maps of distant lands, tattered brochures, and a persistent scent of old coffee hung heavy in the air.  Amelia's meticulously organized, colour-coded planner, a testament to her organizational skills, was practically a battlefield of crossed-out dates, scribbled notes, and highlighted destinations.  Today, however, was different.  An email, with an almost unnervingly simple subject line, "A New World," had arrived.  It demanded her attention.
