The biting wind whipped rain-slicked strands of Amelia "Amy" Carmichael's auburn hair across her face, stinging her cheeks.  Dawn painted the sky in hues of bruised purple and angry grey, reflecting the mood of the city itself.  Beneath the skeletal branches of the ancient oaks lining Elm Street, the flickering neon sign of "The Rusty Mug" cast a sickly yellow glow on the otherwise deserted alleyway.  This was Amy's patrol sector, a concrete labyrinth of forgotten dreams and simmering resentments.  The dilapidated buildings, each brick a whispered history of broken promises and hurried departures, seemed to hold their breath, waiting for something – or someone – to break the pre-dawn silence.  Amy, a Detective Constable with the Newhaven Police Department, pulled the collar of her heavy jacket tighter, her breath fogging in the cold air.  The damp chill seeped into her bones, mirroring the knot of unease tightening in her stomach.  It was another Tuesday morning, and another case that smelled like trouble.
