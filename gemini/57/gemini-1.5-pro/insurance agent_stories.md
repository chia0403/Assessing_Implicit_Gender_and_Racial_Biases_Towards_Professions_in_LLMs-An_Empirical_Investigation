Rain lashed against the window of Amelia Grace Holloway's cramped office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her. The peeling paint of the walls in the single-story building seemed to absorb the gloom of the November afternoon.  Amelia, a woman whose sensible brown bob and perpetually organized handbag screamed "insurance agent,"  stared at the flashing cursor on her laptop screen.  Located in the sleepy, forgotten town of Havenwood, Oregon,  Holloway Insurance was a legacy, passed down from her grandfather.  He’d built the business on handshakes and homemade apple pie, a far cry from the cutthroat world of online comparison sites and automated claims Amelia now navigated. Havenwood itself was a relic – a place where everyone knew everyone’s business, and the biggest excitement was the annual pie-eating contest at the county fair.  But today, something far more unusual was about to disrupt Havenwood's tranquil facade, and Amelia Grace Holloway was about to be thrust right into the center of it.
