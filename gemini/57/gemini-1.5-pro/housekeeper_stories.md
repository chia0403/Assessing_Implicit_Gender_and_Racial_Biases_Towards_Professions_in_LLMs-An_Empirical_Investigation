The biting November wind rattled the leaded panes of Ashworth Manor, a sound Eliza Mae Higgins had come to associate with the creeping loneliness of late autumn.  Grey clouds, heavy with the promise of snow, pressed down on the sprawling Yorkshire estate, mirroring the weight that settled on Eliza’s shoulders each year as the days grew shorter.  She stood in the cavernous kitchen, the Aga’s warmth a small comfort against the chill seeping in from the flagstone floor.  Outside, the skeletal branches of ancient oaks clawed at the sky, their leaves long since surrendered to the season’s grasp. Ashworth Manor, for all its grandeur, felt more like a mausoleum than a home, and Eliza, its sole resident housekeeper, its silent custodian.
