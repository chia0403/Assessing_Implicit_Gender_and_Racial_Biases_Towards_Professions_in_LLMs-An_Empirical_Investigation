The rusty scissors whispered against Elias Thorne's calloused thumb, a familiar song of steel and anticipation. Elias, proprietor and sole stylist of Thorne's Tonsorial Parlour, stood bathed in the honeyed light filtering through the shop's large, dust-speckled window. The Parlour, nestled between Mrs. Higgins' perpetually overflowing bakery and a vacant storefront rumored to be haunted by a sock-puppet-loving poltergeist, was a haven of mahogany and worn leather in the heart of the otherwise chrome-and-glass cityscape of New Shanghai. Outside, levitating rickshaws zipped through neon-lit canyons, but within the Parlour, time seemed to slow, governed by the rhythmic snip-snip-snip of Elias's scissors and the low hum of the ancient, brass-plated hair dryer in the corner. The air hung thick with the scent of bay rum, talcum powder, and a faint, lingering aroma of Mrs. Higgins' cinnamon buns, a constant temptation for Elias. Today, however, a new scent, something sharp and metallic, subtly laced the air, making the hairs on the back of his neck prickle.
