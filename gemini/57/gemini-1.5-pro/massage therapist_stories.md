The late afternoon sun, filtered through the gauzy curtains of Healing Hands Massage Therapy, cast long shadows across the waiting room.  Dust motes danced in the golden light, illuminating the faint scent of lavender and chamomile that permeated the air.  Rain pattered gently against the large bay window, a soothing counterpoint to the soft, instrumental music drifting from a hidden speaker.  Eleanor Vance, LMT, adjusted the thermostat, a small frown creasing her brow.  The old building, a converted Victorian nestled in the heart of sleepy Port Blossom, Maine,  struggled to maintain an even temperature, particularly on days like this, caught between the lingering warmth of summer and the encroaching chill of autumn.  She glanced at the clock – 4:45 pm.  Mr. Henderson, her final appointment, was due any minute.  Eleanor smoothed her hands over her linen tunic, a nervous habit she’d never quite broken, and hoped he wouldn’t complain about the draft. He was a creature of habit, Mr. Henderson, and prone to complaining.
