The wind whispered through the tall stalks of Iowa corn, a dry rustle that spoke of both abundance and the coming chill of autumn. Dust motes danced in the slanted rays of the late afternoon sun, illuminating the weathered, red barn that dominated the landscape. This was Harmony Farms, and it belonged, every acre and ramshackle outbuilding, to Elias Samuel Blackwood.  Elias, his calloused hands shoved deep into the pockets of his worn overalls, surveyed his domain with a mixture of pride and weariness.  The harvest was almost in, but this year, the whispers in the corn seemed to carry something more than just the promise of winter.  A disquiet hung in the air, thick and heavy as the approaching twilight.
