The humid air hung heavy, thick with the scent of plumeria and exhaust fumes.  A kaleidoscope of tuk-tuks zipped through the chaotic Bangkok intersection, their horns blaring a discordant symphony.  Amelia “Amy” Zhao, adjusting the microphone clipped to her coral linen shirt, plastered a bright smile on her face.  The opulent facade of the Grand Palace shimmered in the morning sun, its gilded roofs a testament to centuries of Thai royalty.  Amy raised her hand, beckoning her small tour group closer. "Alright everyone," she announced, her voice projecting over the city's din, "Welcome to Wat Phra Kaew, the Temple of the Emerald Buddha.  Prepare to be amazed."
