Rain lashed against the mullioned windows of Elias Thorne's office, a rhythmic drumming that mirrored the anxious flutter in his chest. Elias, full name Elias Finch Thorne, meticulously adjusted the green banker's lamp on his mahogany desk, its pool of light illuminating the overflowing ledger before him.  His office, nestled in the attic of the imposing Thorne & Sons Shipping Company headquarters in Grimsby, Lincolnshire, usually offered a comforting view of the bustling docks. Tonight, however, all he could see was a swirling grey abyss punctuated by the fleeting, ghostly shapes of cranes.  The air, thick with the scent of salt and coal smoke, crackled with an unusual tension, a feeling Elias couldn't quite put his finger on, but one that made the hairs on the back of his neck prickle with unease. He dipped his pen in the inkwell, a shiver running down his spine as the grandfather clock in the hall below chimed eleven.  He was alone, save for the shadows that danced in the corners of the room, and the secrets hidden within the pages of the ledger.  Secrets that, he suspected, were about to surface.
