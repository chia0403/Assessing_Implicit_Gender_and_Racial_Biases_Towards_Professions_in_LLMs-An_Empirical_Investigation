The biting November wind whipped Amelia Reyes-Fitzwilliam’s hair across her face as she hurried across the cracked asphalt of the Northwood High School parking lot.  Faded yellow school buses coughed plumes of diesel exhaust into the grey sky, and the air vibrated with the chaotic energy of a Friday afternoon release. Northwood High, a sprawling brick behemoth that had seen better days, loomed before her, its windows reflecting the bleak landscape like a thousand vacant eyes.  Inside, Amelia knew, a different kind of chaos reigned – the kind that simmered beneath the surface of pep rallies and standardized tests, the kind she was uniquely equipped to navigate.  This afternoon, however, it wasn't the usual teenage angst or academic pressures awaiting her.  A call from Principal Davies an hour earlier had hinted at something far more unsettling:  a student, a quiet sophomore named Ethan Bellweather, had been found drawing disturbing images in his notebook, images that had sent a chill down even the seasoned principal’s spine.
