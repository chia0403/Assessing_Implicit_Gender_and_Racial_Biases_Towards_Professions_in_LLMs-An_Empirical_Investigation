The biting November wind whipped Amelia "Mia" Hernandez's chestnut hair across her face as she hurried down Bleecker Street, the scent of roasting chestnuts vying with the ever-present aroma of exhaust fumes.  Her breath puffed out in small, white clouds, momentarily obscuring the view of the vibrant, bustling Greenwich Village street ahead. Mia clutched her oversized tote bag, its contents – a kaleidoscope of meal prep containers and client files – jostling against her side. Above her, the naked branches of the sycamores lining the street clawed at the iron-grey sky. Mia was late, perpetually late, for her appointment with Mr. Henderson, a stubbornly carb-addicted playwright who swore his creative process depended on a daily intake of bagels and pasta.  Her office, nestled above a vintage bookstore on the corner of Bleecker and Christopher, promised warmth and the comforting aroma of her perpetually brewing herbal tea, but reaching it felt like a marathon through an arctic wind tunnel.
