The biting November wind whipped off Lake Michigan, rattling the frosted panes of Dr. Elias Thorne’s clinic and stinging Amelia Hayes’ exposed cheeks.  Amelia, whose full name was Amelia Grace Hayes, PA-C, tugged her scarf higher, the wool scratching against her chin.  The small town of Port Blossom, Michigan, normally picturesque with its Victorian architecture and quaint harbor, felt bleak and unforgiving under the steely grey sky. The holiday lights strung across Main Street, usually twinkling cheerfully by this time of year, remained dark and dormant, mirroring the unease that had settled over the town like a shroud.  A peculiar illness had begun to spread, baffling Dr. Thorne and leaving Amelia with a growing sense of dread. It wasn't the flu, nor any other common ailment she'd encountered in her five years of practice. This was something…different.
