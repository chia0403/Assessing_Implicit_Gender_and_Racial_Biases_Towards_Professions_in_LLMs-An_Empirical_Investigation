The flickering fluorescent lights of the D&M Architectural firm hummed a monotonous tune, a soundtrack to Amelia Grace Hawthorne's Tuesday morning. Outside, the Chicago wind howled, rattling the large windows overlooking the grey, churning waters of Lake Michigan. November had arrived with a vengeance, clawing at the city with icy fingers, and even the twenty-seventh floor of the Prudential Plaza couldn't escape its frigid breath. Amelia, hunched over her drafting table, rubbed her hands together for warmth, her breath fogging slightly in the chilled air.  The faint scent of stale coffee and blueprint ink hung heavy in the air, a familiar comfort in the otherwise sterile environment. Her desk, a small island in a sea of identical workstations, was meticulously organized: pencils sharpened to needle points in a ceramic mug, T-squares and triangles arranged just so, and a half-eaten bagel precariously balanced on a stack of blueprints for a new luxury condominium complex slated for the Gold Coast.  Today's task: detailing the intricate ductwork for the building's ventilation system, a task as tedious as it was essential.
