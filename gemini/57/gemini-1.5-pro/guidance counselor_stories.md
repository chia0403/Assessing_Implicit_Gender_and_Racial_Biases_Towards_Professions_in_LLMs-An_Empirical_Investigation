The fluorescent lights of McKinley High hummed a monotonous B-flat, a soundtrack perfectly suited to the beige walls and perpetually dusty air of Amelia “Amy” Rosario’s office.  Outside, the October wind whipped fallen leaves into frenzied spirals against the large, grimy window, mirroring the turmoil brewing inside Amy. It was a Monday, the start of a week already heavy with scheduled student meetings, overflowing with college applications, and peppered with the inevitable parent-teacher conferences that always seemed to land on her already crammed schedule. Amy sighed, the faint scent of stale coffee clinging to the air like a persistent ex-boyfriend.  McKinley High, nestled in the heart of the perpetually overcast town of Port Blossom, Oregon, wasn't exactly a hotbed of ambition.  Most students dreamed of escaping, not to glittering metropolises, but to the nearby logging towns, where the promise of a steady paycheck and a familiar routine held more appeal than any Ivy League acceptance letter.  And Amy, well, she was their reluctant shepherd, guiding them towards futures they often couldn't even envision for themselves.
