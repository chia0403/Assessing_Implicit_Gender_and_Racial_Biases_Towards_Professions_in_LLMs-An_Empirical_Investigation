Rain lashed against the grimy windows of The Daily Clarion's office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside Amelia “Amy” Reyes.  Fluorescent lights buzzed overhead, casting a sickly yellow glow over the cluttered desks and overflowing ashtrays. Cigarette smoke hung heavy in the air, a tangible representation of the stagnation that had settled over the once-thriving newspaper.  Amy, hunched over her typewriter, chewed on the end of a pen, her brow furrowed in concentration.  Ashbury, Ohio, population dwindling, was a town choked by its own dying industry, and Amy, its sole investigative journalist, felt the same slow suffocation creeping into her bones.  Tonight, however, something felt different.  A crumpled flyer advertising the annual Founder's Day Festival lay on her desk, a single, unsettling circle drawn around the date in thick red marker.  It had arrived anonymously, tucked inside a plain white envelope, and Amy, with a gut feeling as heavy as the storm outside, knew it was more than just a prank.
