Rain lashed against the grimy windows of The Daily Chronicle's office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside Amelia Grace Holloway.  Flickering fluorescent lights buzzed overhead, casting a sickly yellow glow on the cluttered desks and overflowing ashtrays.  Amelia, hunched over a chipped mug of lukewarm coffee, tapped a restless rhythm on her keyboard.  The clatter echoed through the near-empty newsroom, punctuated only by the rhythmic drumming of the rain and the occasional sigh of the lone copy editor battling a particularly stubborn crossword.  Trenton, New Jersey, a city usually steeped in a sleepy, post-industrial haze, felt tonight like the forgotten corner of a noir film, and Amelia, with her trench coat draped over the back of her chair and a knot of worry tightening in her stomach, was about to play the lead.  A crumpled fax lay beside her mug, its ink bleeding slightly from the damp.  It contained a single, chilling sentence: "They know."
