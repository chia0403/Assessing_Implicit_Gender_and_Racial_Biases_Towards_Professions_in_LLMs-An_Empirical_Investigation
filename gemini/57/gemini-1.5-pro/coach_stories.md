The biting November wind whipped across the cracked asphalt of the St. Jude's High School parking lot, sending fallen leaves swirling around Eleanor Vance’s ankles.  She pulled her threadbare Eagles coaching jacket tighter, the embroidered logo faded and frayed like the hopes of her girls' basketball team.  Fluorescent lights flickered erratically inside the gymnasium, casting long shadows that danced with the swirling dust motes visible in the pre-dawn gloom. St. Jude’s, nestled in the forgotten corner of Millhaven, Ohio, a town whose glory days had long since rusted away along with the nearby steel mill, was a place where dreams came to die, not flourish.  And for Coach Vance,  each lost game, each missed free throw, each forfeited practice due to players needing to work after-school shifts, felt like another nail in the coffin of her own fading aspirations.  This year, however, felt different.  A flicker of something… hope, maybe? … burned within her. This year, they had Maya.
