The tar hissed and bubbled under the July sun, the smell thick and pungent in the air. Elias "Eli" Thorne, his boots sticking slightly to the softened asphalt shingles, squinted at the shimmering heat haze rising from the sprawling terracotta roof of the Vandergelt mansion.  Below him, the manicured lawns of the estate stretched out like a verdant carpet, bordered by a pristine white fence that separated the opulence within from the rest of sleepy Oakhaven, Connecticut. Eli adjusted his sweat-soaked bandanna, a faded American flag flapping listlessly in the humid air, and wiped the back of his neck with a calloused hand.  He had a feeling this job, ostensibly a simple repair, was going to be more trouble than it was worth.  The air hung heavy, not just with the heat, but with a strange, almost electric tension that seemed to emanate from the house itself.
