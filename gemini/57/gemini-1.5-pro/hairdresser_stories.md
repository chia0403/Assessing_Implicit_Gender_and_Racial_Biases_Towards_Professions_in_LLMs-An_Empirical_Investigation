The flickering neon sign of "Shear Radiance" buzzed erratically, casting a purplish glow across the rain-slicked street. Inside, Amelia Rose Hawthorne, her name stitched in elegant cursive on her crisp white smock, sighed and wiped a stray strand of auburn hair from her forehead.  The clock above the mirrored wall read 9:57 pm, a full three minutes past her usual closing time in the sleepy, seaside town of Port Blossom, Maine.  The rhythmic swoosh of the waves against the nearby pier usually lulled her, but tonight, a different kind of restlessness hung in the air, thick and salty like the impending storm. The only other soul in the salon was Mrs. Henderson, her regular Tuesday night appointment for a blue rinse and set, now snoring softly under the hairdryer, oblivious to the ominous rattle of the shop door against the rising wind.
