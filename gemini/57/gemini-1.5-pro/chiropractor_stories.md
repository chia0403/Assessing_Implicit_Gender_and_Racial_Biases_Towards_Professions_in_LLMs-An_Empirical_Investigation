Rain lashed against the windows of Dr. Elias Thorne's chiropractic clinic, mirroring the tension knotting in his shoulders. The "Thorne Chiropractic & Wellness Center," a modest but meticulously kept space tucked between a laundromat and a perpetually empty storefront in the sleepy town of Havenwood, Oregon, usually hummed with the quiet murmur of soft music and the gentle clicks of adjusting vertebrae. But not tonight.  The clock on the wall ticked towards 9 PM, well past closing, and the only sound besides the downpour was the rhythmic squeak of Elias’s leather chair as he leaned back, staring at the swirling grey skies outside.  A half-eaten sandwich sat forlornly on a paper plate beside a thick textbook titled "Advanced Biomechanics of the Spine."  He rubbed his temples, the persistent headache a nagging reminder of the increasingly bizarre week he'd been having. It had started with the mysterious new patient…
