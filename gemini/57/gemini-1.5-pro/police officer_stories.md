The biting wind whipped off Lake Michigan, carrying the scent of stale beer and desperation down Clark Street. Detective Isabella “Izzy” Diaz shivered, pulling her threadbare Chicago Police Department jacket tighter around her.  Neon signs flickered erratically, casting an eerie glow on the rain-slicked pavement, illuminating the grimy storefronts and overflowing trash cans of Wrigleyville.  It was 3:17 am, hours past last call, and the usual post-game revelry had long since dissolved into a quiet unease.  Izzy, leaning against her unmarked Crown Vic, watched the few remaining stragglers stumble home, their laughter brittle and forced.  Something felt wrong.  The air, thick with impending rain, crackled with a tension she couldn't quite place.  Tonight, the usual Saturday night chaos had been replaced by a chilling stillness, a silence broken only by the distant wail of a siren and the rhythmic drip, drip, drip of water from a leaky fire escape overhead.
