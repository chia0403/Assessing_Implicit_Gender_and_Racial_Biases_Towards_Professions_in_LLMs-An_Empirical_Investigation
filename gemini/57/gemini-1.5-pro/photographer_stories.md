The biting wind whipped Amelia "Mia" Reyes's dark hair across her face as she adjusted the tripod on the precipice of Blackheart Cliff.  Below, the churning, iron-grey waters of the North Atlantic crashed against jagged, basalt columns, sending plumes of spray that kissed the rusted metal railing she gripped for balance.  The sky, a bruised purple canvas streaked with angry orange, promised a storm, but Mia, ever the dedicated photographer, was more concerned with capturing the last vestiges of daylight illuminating the abandoned lighthouse clinging precariously to the cliff edge.  Sligo, a small, windswept town on the western coast of Ireland, was her current obsession, its wild beauty and melancholic atmosphere the perfect backdrop for her next exhibition. This particular lighthouse, rumored to be haunted by the spirit of a heartbroken keeper, held a special allure.
