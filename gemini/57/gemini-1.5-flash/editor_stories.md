The chipped mug warmed Amelia Stone's hands, the lukewarm chamomile doing little to soothe the icy dread that had settled in her stomach.  Rain lashed against the tall, gothic windows of her office, each gust a mournful sigh echoing the turmoil inside her.  The room itself was a testament to controlled chaos: overflowing bookshelves groaned under the weight of manuscripts, rejected and accepted, their spines a kaleidoscope of vibrant colours and muted greys; stacks of galleys, their pages dog-eared and annotated, littered her desk; and a half-eaten croissant lay forgotten beside a precarious tower of dictionaries.  Outside, the city of London thrummed, a relentless pulse of life utterly disconnected from the quiet, almost suffocating tension within the walls of Stone & Quill Publishing House, where Amelia, Senior Editor, was facing a crisis that threatened to unravel everything she'd worked for.
