Professor Augustine Blackwood adjusted his spectacles, the faint scent of old paper and pipe tobacco clinging to the tweed of his elbow patches. Rain lashed against the tall, gothic windows of his study, the rhythmic drumming a counterpoint to the frantic scratching of his quill pen across the parchment.  The flickering gaslight cast long, dancing shadows across the walls lined with overflowing bookshelves, their contents a testament to a lifetime spent wrestling with the forgotten languages and esoteric lore of ancient civilizations.  Outside, the wind howled like a banshee, rattling the panes in their lead frames, a fitting prelude to the night’s unsettling discovery – a discovery that would drag Professor Blackwood from the comforting obscurity of his academic pursuits and thrust him into the heart of a centuries-old conspiracy.
