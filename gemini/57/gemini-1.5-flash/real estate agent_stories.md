The salt spray stung Amelia Katherine Hayes's face as she wrestled the stubborn For Sale sign from its rusted post.  The wind, a relentless Atlantic gale, howled a mournful tune around the dilapidated Victorian perched precariously on the cliff edge overlooking the churning grey sea.  Seagulls cried overhead, their calls echoing the desolate beauty of the scene – a scene Amelia, a real estate agent with a reputation for turning lemons into luxury, knew had potential, despite the current state of affairs.  The house, appropriately named "Cliffside Lament," was more than just weathered shingles and peeling paint; it was a canvas of neglect whispering stories of forgotten grandeur, a challenge she was eager to meet.  The only question was, could she find a buyer brave – or foolish – enough to take on the project?
