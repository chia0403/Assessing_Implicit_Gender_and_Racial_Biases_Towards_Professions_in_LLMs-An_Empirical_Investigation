The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s dark hair across her face as she hurried down the cobbled street, the collar of her thick wool coat pulled high.  Rain, a relentless drizzle that had been falling for days, slicked the already treacherous stones, threatening to send her sprawling.  The air hung heavy with the scent of woodsmoke and something else, something faintly metallic, that pricked at her nostrils and tightened the knot of unease already forming in her stomach.  This wasn't the picturesque coastal town of Port Blossom advertised in the tourist brochures; this was Port Blossom clinging to its secrets, a place where the salty tang of the sea fought a losing battle against the lingering chill of impending winter and the even colder breath of something sinister.  Amelia, a physician’s assistant at the town’s lone, understaffed clinic, was about to learn just how sinister.
