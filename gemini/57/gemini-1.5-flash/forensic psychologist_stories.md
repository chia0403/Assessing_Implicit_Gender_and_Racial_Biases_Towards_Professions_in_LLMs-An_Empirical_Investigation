The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Mia” Holloway’s trench coat around her legs as she navigated the uneven cobblestones of Edinburgh’s Old Town.  Gas lamps hissed and sputtered, casting long, dancing shadows that seemed to writhe and twist like the macabre figures haunting her latest case. The scent of peat smoke and damp earth hung heavy in the air, a smell she usually found comforting, but tonight it only served to amplify the unsettling chill that had settled deep in her bones. Rain, a relentless drizzle that had been falling for days, slicked the ancient stone buildings, making them gleam like the obsidian eyes of some watchful deity.  She pulled her collar higher, the worn leather offering little protection against the penetrating cold and the even colder reality awaiting her inside the imposing Georgian townhouse looming before her: the scene of a brutal murder, its secrets locked tight within its decaying walls.
