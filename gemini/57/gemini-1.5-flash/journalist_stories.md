The humid air hung heavy, thick with the scent of frying plantains and diesel fumes.  Cicadas buzzed a relentless symphony from the mango trees that lined Avenida Revolución, their shrill chorus a soundtrack to the simmering tension that clung to the city like a shroud.  Isabelle “Izzy” Moreau, a journalist with ink permanently stained under her fingernails and a weariness etched onto her face that belied her thirty-two years, sat perched on the cracked concrete steps of the Hotel Colonial, nursing a lukewarm café con leche.  The chipped paint of the building flaked onto her worn canvas shoes, a small, insignificant detail mirroring the crumbling façade of the political situation in San Juan de los Morros, Venezuela.  Izzy's notebook, filled with scribbled notes and crumpled receipts, lay open beside her, a testament to the weeks she'd spent chasing a story that threatened to swallow her whole.
