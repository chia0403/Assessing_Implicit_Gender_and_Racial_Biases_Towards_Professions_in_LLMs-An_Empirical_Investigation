The acrid bite of exhaust fumes and the greasy scent of frying onions hung heavy in the humid Miami air, a stark contrast to the sterile, antiseptic smell clinging to Dr. Evelyn Grace Rodriguez’s scrubs.  Sweat beaded on her forehead, not from the oppressive heat, but from the adrenaline still coursing through her veins.  The flashing red and blue lights of her ambulance, parked haphazardly across from a bustling pastel-colored bakery called “Dulces Sueños,” pulsed erratically, mirroring the frantic rhythm of her own heart.  The air vibrated with the chaotic symphony of a city on the edge; the insistent blare of car horns vying with the rhythmic chatter of Spanish from a nearby bodega and the mournful wail of a distant siren – a counterpoint to the urgent silence in the ambulance where her patient, a young woman with a pale face and a rapidly weakening pulse, lay struggling for breath. Evelyn, her usually crisp white coat rumpled and stained with what she hoped was only mango juice, leaned closer, the fluorescent glare of the overhead lights reflecting in her determined brown eyes.  This wasn't her first rodeo, but tonight felt different.  Tonight felt…wrong.
