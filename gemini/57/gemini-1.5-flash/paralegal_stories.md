The chipped Formica tabletop of Amelia Katherine "Amy" Holloway's desk felt clammy beneath her fingertips.  Rain lashed against the grimy windows of the Dolan & Wilkes law offices, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her.  The air hung thick with the scent of stale coffee and impending deadlines, a familiar aroma in the cramped, windowless back office she shared with three overflowing filing cabinets and a perpetually malfunctioning printer.  Outside, the neon lights of downtown Scranton cast a sickly yellow glow on the perpetually rain-slicked streets.  Inside, Amy stared at the mountain of paperwork – divorce petitions, restraining orders, and the ever-present threat of a lawsuit against a particularly belligerent hot dog vendor –  knowing that another all-nighter was inevitable.  The flickering fluorescent lights buzzed a discordant symphony to the rhythmic tap-tap-tap of her worn keyboard, a soundtrack to her relentless pursuit of legal order in a city that seemed perpetually teetering on the brink of chaos.
