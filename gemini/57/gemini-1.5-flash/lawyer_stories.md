The salt spray stung Amelia Hernandez’s face as she wrestled the battered fishing boat’s tiller.  The late afternoon sun, a bruised plum sinking towards the horizon, cast long, skeletal shadows across the churning grey water of the Puget Sound.  Amelia, a lawyer whose tailored suits were usually traded for the crisp starch of courtroom formality, felt the damp chill seep into her bones, a far cry from the heated air of Seattle’s Superior Court.  The scent of brine and diesel hung heavy, a stark contrast to the polished mahogany and leather she was accustomed to.  This wasn't some carefully constructed legal argument; this was a desperate race against the tide, a chase against the fading light, and a battle against a secret that threatened to capsize her life as surely as the unpredictable currents could swallow this small, creaking vessel whole.
