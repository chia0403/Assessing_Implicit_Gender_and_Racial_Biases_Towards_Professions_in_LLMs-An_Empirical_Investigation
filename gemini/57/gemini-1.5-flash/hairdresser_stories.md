The scent of rosemary and burnt sugar hung thick in the air, a perfume only Aurelia “Rella” Davenport could truly appreciate.  Rella, owner and sole stylist at "Shear Genius," a tiny salon tucked between a laundromat and a failing bakery on Bleecker Street, sighed, the breath misting in the chilly October air that seeped in through the slightly ajar back door.  Rain lashed against the grimy windowpanes, mirroring the storm brewing inside her.  The chipped porcelain sink was littered with half-empty bottles of vibrant hair dye, discarded foils shimmering like fallen stars, and a single, forlorn curling iron lay abandoned on a stained towel.  Outside, the city roared, a symphony of honking taxis and chattering pedestrians utterly oblivious to the quiet crisis unfolding within the four walls of her little sanctuary.  Tonight, Rella was facing a challenge far more intricate than a bad perm.  Tonight, she was wrestling with a broken heart, and the only tools she had were a pair of scissors and a bottle of surprisingly effective lavender shampoo.
