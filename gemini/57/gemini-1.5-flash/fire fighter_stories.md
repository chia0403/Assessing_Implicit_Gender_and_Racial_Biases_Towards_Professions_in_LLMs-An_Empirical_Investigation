The acrid bite of smoke stung Lieutenant Evelyn "Evie" Reed's nostrils, a familiar companion in the perpetual twilight of the burning warehouse.  Flames, a hungry orange maw, gnawed at the timber skeleton of the building, their roar a deafening counterpoint to the hiss of pressurized water and the crackle of collapsing wood.  The air hung thick and heavy with the scent of burning plastic, mingled with the metallic tang of blood – a sickening perfume that clung to everything in the desolate industrial wasteland of Port Blossom, Oregon.  Rain, a relentless drizzle, slicked the ground into a treacherous mire, mirroring the chaos within the inferno.  Around Evie, the cacophony of the firefight – the shouts of her crew, the desperate clang of metal on metal, the desperate rasp of air tanks – created a symphony of controlled panic, a soundtrack to their desperate ballet with death.
