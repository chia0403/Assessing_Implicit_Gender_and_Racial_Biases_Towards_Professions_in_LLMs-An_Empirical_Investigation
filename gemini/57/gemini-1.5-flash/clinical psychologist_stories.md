The stale scent of disinfectant and old coffee hung heavy in the air, a familiar perfume to Dr. Eleanor Vance, PhD.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of her office, blurring the already hazy cityscape of Seattle into a watercolour of grey and black.  The city, usually a vibrant tapestry of lights and sound at night, was tonight a muted, melancholic backdrop to the story unfolding before her. Eleanor, her auburn hair pulled back in a severe bun, leaned forward, the soft glow of her desk lamp illuminating the worry etched into the lines of her patient's face.  Across from her, hunched in a plush armchair, sat Mr. Arthur Finch, his hands clasped tightly in his lap, his eyes avoiding the direct gaze of the woman who held his fractured world in her professional hands.  The rain continued its relentless assault on the glass, mirroring the storm brewing within Mr. Finch, a storm Eleanor was tasked with navigating.
