The scent of sandalwood and chamomile hung heavy in the air, a comforting balm against the relentless Vancouver drizzle lashing against the windows of Serenity Spa.  Rain hammered a frantic rhythm on the corrugated iron roof, a counterpoint to the soft, rhythmic kneading of Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s hands.  Amelia, a massage therapist with fingers that seemed to possess an intuitive understanding of muscle knots and tense shoulders, worked with a practiced grace, her brow furrowed in concentration as she eased the tension from Mr. Henderson’s chronically stiff neck.  The spa, nestled in a quiet corner of Gastown, was a haven of calm, a world away from the city’s bustling energy, yet the storm outside seemed to mirror the tempest brewing within Amelia herself.  For tonight, a storm far bigger than any Vancouver downpour threatened to break.
