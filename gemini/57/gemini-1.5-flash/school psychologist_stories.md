The fluorescent lights of Northwood High hummed a monotonous tune, a soundtrack to Dr. Eleanor Vance’s increasingly frantic pacing.  It was 7:15 PM, the last sliver of sun bleeding out from behind the towering oaks that lined the school's perimeter, casting long, skeletal shadows across the nearly deserted parking lot.  Eleanor, her sensible cardigan pulled tight against the evening chill, stared out the window of her cluttered office, the scent of stale coffee and desperation clinging to the air like a second skin.  The overflowing in-tray on her desk, a testament to the emotional turmoil of hundreds of teenagers, seemed to mock her with its silent judgment.  Tonight, however, felt different. Tonight, the weight of her responsibilities felt heavier, more suffocating than usual, a premonition clinging to her like the damp autumn air. The cryptic note left anonymously on her desk – a single, blood-red maple leaf pressed between crisp white paper – was the final, unsettling piece of the puzzle, hinting at a storm brewing far beyond the usual teenage angst.
