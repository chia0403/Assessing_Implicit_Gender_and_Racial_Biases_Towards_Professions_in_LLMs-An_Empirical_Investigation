The scent of jasmine, heavy and sweet, hung in the humid air like a promise.  Cicadas buzzed a relentless rhythm against the backdrop of the turquoise Caribbean Sea, visible through the gaps in the bougainvillea that climbed the weathered walls of "Petals & Paradise," Amelia Hernandez's flower shop.  Amelia, a woman whose smile lines spoke of sun-drenched days and laughter as readily as the delicate calluses on her fingertips spoke of years spent coaxing life from soil and seed, hummed a lilting melody as she arranged a cascade of vibrant hibiscus blossoms.  The shop, a riot of colour and fragrance nestled in the heart of Port Royal, Jamaica, was her sanctuary, a tiny slice of paradise she'd painstakingly cultivated, blossom by blossom.
