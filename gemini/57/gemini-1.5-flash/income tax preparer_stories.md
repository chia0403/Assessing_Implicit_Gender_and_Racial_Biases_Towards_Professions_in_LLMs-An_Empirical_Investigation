The fluorescent lights of Mildred McMillan's cramped office hummed a discordant tune, a counterpoint to the frantic clatter of her keyboard.  February in Boise, Idaho, meant one thing: tax season.  Snow piled high against the single window, a silent, watchful observer of the chaos unfolding within.  The air, thick with the scent of stale coffee and desperation, hung heavy with the weight of unfiled returns.  Mildred, a woman whose grey bun was as tightly wound as her spreadsheets, squinted at the screen, a frown etching itself deeper into the lines around her already weary eyes.  This year, the mountain of paperwork felt particularly insurmountable, a jagged peak of W-2s, 1099s, and schedules C that threatened to bury her alive.  But Mildred McMillan, Income Tax Preparer Extraordinaire (at least, that's what her slightly faded business card claimed), wasn't one to back down from a challenge.  Not even this one.
