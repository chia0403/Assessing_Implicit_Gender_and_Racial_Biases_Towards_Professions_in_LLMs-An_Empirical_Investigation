The wind, a relentless sculptor, carved patterns into the snowdrifts that piled high against the weathered fence posts of the Bar None Ranch.  A biting chill, sharper than any knife, sliced through even the thickest wool of Caleb "Cal" Jericho's coat.  Cal, his breath clouding the frigid air, squinted at the relentless gray sky.  The Wyoming winter had a grip like a steel vise this year, and the promise of a blizzard, heavy and unforgiving, hung thick and suffocating as the snow itself.  The vast, lonely expanse of the ranch, normally a source of solace and pride, felt tonight like a frozen, indifferent tomb, swallowing the last vestiges of light.  The only sound, besides the mournful howl of the wind, was the rhythmic clip-clop of his worn leather boots against the frozen ground as he made his way towards the distant silhouette of the ranch house, a lone beacon against the encroaching darkness.
