The chipped porcelain teacup warmed Amelia Blackwood’s fingers, a meager comfort against the biting November wind that howled outside the crooked windowpanes of the “Singing Kettle” pub.  Rain lashed against the leaded glass, blurring the already indistinct gaslight glow of the cobbled street beyond.  Inside, the air hung thick with the smells of damp wool, stale beer, and the faint, lingering scent of woodsmoke.  Amelia, her voice as rich and smoky as that last scent, nervously tapped a rhythm on the worn tabletop with a long, elegant finger.  Tonight was the night.  Tonight, she would finally sing the ballad her grandmother had taught her, the one whispered to hold the power to mend broken hearts – a power Amelia desperately needed herself.
