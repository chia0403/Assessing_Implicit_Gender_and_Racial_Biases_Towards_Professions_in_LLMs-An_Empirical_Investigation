The biting November wind whipped Elias Thorne's already-chapped lips raw as he wrestled with a stubborn oak branch, its leaves clinging on with the desperate tenacity of a drowning man.  Elias, a man whose life was as predictable as the changing seasons of Blackwood Manor, hadn't expected this particular autumn to feel so…charged.  The air, thick with the smell of decaying leaves and damp earth, carried an undercurrent of something else entirely – a faint, almost imperceptible tremor of unease. Blackwood Manor, perched precariously on a cliff overlooking the churning grey expanse of the North Sea, was a breathtakingly beautiful place, but today, its beauty felt brittle, fragile, like a painted smile on a crumbling façade.
