The flickering neon sign of the "Lucky Dragon" noodle bar cast a lurid, greasy glow across the rain-slicked streets of Hong Kong.  A persistent drizzle, the kind that seeped into your bones and chilled you to the marrow, mirrored the unease churning in Amelia Chen's stomach.  Amelia, a systems analyst for the notoriously secretive Kwok Group, hunched deeper into her threadbare raincoat, the collar pulled high against the biting wind.  The city, usually a vibrant cacophony of sights and sounds, felt muted tonight, the usual boisterous energy replaced by a hushed, expectant stillness.  Even the ubiquitous aroma of street food seemed to hang heavy, pregnant with unspoken tension.  Amelia’s fingers, numb with cold, tightened around the worn leather strap of her messenger bag, its contents – a half-eaten bowl of char siu bao and a laptop brimming with encrypted data – her only companions in this increasingly unsettling night.
