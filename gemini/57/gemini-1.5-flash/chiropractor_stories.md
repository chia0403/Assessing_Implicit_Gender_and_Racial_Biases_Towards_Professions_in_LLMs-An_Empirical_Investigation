The scent of pine needles and damp earth clung to Dr. Eleanor Vance's tweed coat as she hurried across the windswept parking lot of her clinic, "The Spinal Alignment Center."  The late October sun cast long shadows across the weathered clapboard building, nestled between a bakery smelling perpetually of cinnamon and a hardware store that seemed to perpetually leak oil onto the pavement.  The air bit with a chill that promised a harsh winter in the small, isolated town of Havenwood, nestled deep within the whispering pines of the Cascade Mountains.  Eleanor, her breath misting in the frigid air, fumbled with her keys, the weight of a long day and the unspoken anxieties of her patients settling heavily on her shoulders.  She could already feel the familiar ache in her own lower back, a constant reminder of the physical toll her profession demanded.
