The salt-laced wind whipped Elias Thorne's weathered face as he wrestled the last stubborn plank into place.  Above him, the skeletal frame of the lighthouse, a lonely sentinel against the churning grey expanse of the Atlantic, clawed at the bruised sky.  Seabirds, their cries like mournful sirens, wheeled overhead, their shadows briefly eclipsing the already fading light.  Elias, a man carved by the harsh beauty of the Outer Hebrides as surely as the wood he shaped, paused, the hammer hanging limp in his calloused hand.  The scent of pine and brine filled his lungs, a familiar perfume that spoke of both his trade and his home, the tiny, windswept island of Eriskay.  He squinted, the horizon a blurry line where the sea met the bruised, purple sky, a storm brewing far out to sea, mirroring the turbulent emotions churning within him.
