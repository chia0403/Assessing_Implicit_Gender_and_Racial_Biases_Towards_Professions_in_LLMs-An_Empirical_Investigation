The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Amy”  Fitzwilliam’s hair across her face as she surveyed the sodden rugby pitch.  Floodlights cut through the swirling rain, illuminating the muddy expanse where her team, the battered but resilient Wolverines of St. Jude’s, were engaged in a training drill that looked less like organized sport and more like a desperate struggle against the elements.  The air hung thick with the scent of wet wool, damp earth, and the faintly metallic tang of blood – a small cut on one player's nose, nothing serious, but a testament to the ferocity of their practice.  St. Jude’s, nestled in the heart of the bleak, windswept Yorkshire moors, was as unforgiving as its weather, and Amy knew that to succeed here, her team needed grit, determination, and a healthy dose of stubborn refusal to give up, qualities she possessed in abundance.
