The chipped paint of the "Sunshine Smiles Daycare" sign flaked onto the overgrown lawn like a miniature, unsettling snowstorm.  A lone, rusty swing set creaked a mournful tune in the blustery November wind, a counterpoint to the cheerful, if slightly off-key, rendition of "The Wheels on the Bus" emanating from within.  Inside, amidst a whirlwind of tiny hands, sticky fingers, and the scent of crayon and slightly burnt toast, twenty-three-year-old  Elara Jeannette Davies navigated the chaotic landscape of her afternoon shift with the practiced calm of a seasoned general.  Her bright yellow cardigan, already stained with a mysterious brown substance (possibly chocolate, possibly something considerably less palatable), bore silent witness to countless battles fought and won against tantrums, spilled milk, and the relentless tyranny of naptime.
