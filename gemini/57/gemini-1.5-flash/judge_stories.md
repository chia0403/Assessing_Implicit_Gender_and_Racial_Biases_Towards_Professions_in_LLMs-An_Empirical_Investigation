The air hung thick and heavy with the scent of brine and decay, a smell Judge Abigail "Abby" Hawthorne had come to associate intimately with the coastal town of Seabrook.  Seagulls, their cries sharp and insistent, wheeled overhead, circling the dilapidated Victorian courthouse like vultures anticipating a feast. Rain, a relentless drizzle that had been falling for three days, plastered Abby's dark hair to her scalp and seeped into the worn leather of her briefcase.  The courthouse itself, a crumbling testament to a bygone era, groaned under the weight of the storm and the accumulated grievances of its inhabitants.  Inside, the dim, echoing hallways felt as claustrophobic as a submerged wreck, the only light filtering weakly through grimy, arched windows.  Tonight, however, the usual quiet melancholy of the Seabrook County Courthouse was replaced by a palpable tension, a silent hum that vibrated from the oak panels and seeped into Abby's bones. Tonight, she faced a case that would test not only her legal acumen, but also her resolve, and possibly, her very sanity.
