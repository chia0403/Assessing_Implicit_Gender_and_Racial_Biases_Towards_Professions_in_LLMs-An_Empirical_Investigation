The scent of aged paper and leather hung heavy in the air, a comforting perfume to Eleanor Vance, but a stifling one to most of the patrons shuffling through the hushed aisles of the Grandview Public Library. Dust motes, illuminated by the late afternoon sun slanting through the arched windows, danced in the stillness. Outside, the October wind howled a mournful ballad, rattling the panes and whipping fallen leaves into frenzied spirals.  Eleanor, perched behind the towering oak desk in the reference section, adjusted her spectacles, her gaze sweeping over the rows of towering shelves packed with the accumulated knowledge of centuries.  The library, a Gothic revival masterpiece built in 1898, felt less like a public building and more like a slumbering beast, its secrets whispered only to those who knew how to listen. Tonight, Eleanor suspected, it was about to speak.
