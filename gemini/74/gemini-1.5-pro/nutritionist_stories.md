The insistent drip from the leaky faucet echoed Amelia “Amy” Reyes’s growing frustration.  Her perfectly organized pantry, meticulously labelled with best-by dates and nutritional information, felt suffocating. Outside, the humid Charleston air hung heavy, thick with the scent of jasmine and impending rain.  Amy, a registered dietitian nutritionist known for her meticulous meal plans and unwavering dedication to healthy living, found herself craving a double-chocolate fudge brownie, a food group conspicuously absent from her meticulously crafted dietary guidelines.  It was a Wednesday afternoon in July, and for the first time in her career, Amy Reyes felt utterly, completely, and undeniably burnt out.  Her pristine, pastel-green office, usually a sanctuary of nutritional wisdom, felt more like a gilded cage.
