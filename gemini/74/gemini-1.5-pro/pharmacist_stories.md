The neon "Open 24 Hours" sign buzzed erratically, casting a sickly yellow glow across the rain-slicked asphalt of the deserted parking lot. Inside Bellweather Pharmacy, nestled between a perpetually closed donut shop and a laundromat that hummed a mournful tune, Elias Thorne, Pharm.D., meticulously counted out hydromorphone tablets.  The air hung thick with the cloying scent of antiseptic and something faintly metallic, a smell Elias had come to associate with the late-night anxieties of his clientele.  Shadowy figures darted past the windows, reflections distorted by the downpour, a constant reminder that in the heart of the city, even at 3:17 AM, life, in all its messy, desperate glory, pulsed on.
