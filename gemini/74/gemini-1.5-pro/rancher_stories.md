The wind whipped across the Montana plains, carrying the scent of sagebrush and the low rumble of distant thunder.  Dust devils danced across the parched earth, spiralling towards the vast expanse of the Rocking K Ranch, where forty-year-old Jedediah "Jed" Calhoun squinted against the approaching storm.  His weathered face, etched with lines of sun and worry, mirrored the cracked landscape.  The Rocking K, a patchwork of rolling hills, grazing land, and a meandering creek, had been in the Calhoun family for five generations, a legacy Jed carried with a mixture of pride and bone-deep exhaustion. This year, the relentless drought threatened to break both the land and the man.  The sky, bruised purple and pregnant with rain that never seemed to fall, reflected the heavy weight in Jed's heart. He knew, with a certainty that settled deep in his gut, that this storm, whatever it brought, would change everything.
