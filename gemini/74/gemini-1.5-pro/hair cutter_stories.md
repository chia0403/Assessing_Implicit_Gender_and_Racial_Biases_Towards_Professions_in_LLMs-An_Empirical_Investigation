The rusty bell above the door of "Shear Perfection" jingled merrily, a counterpoint to the low rumble of thunder echoing down Main Street.  Rain lashed against the large plate-glass window, blurring the already fading light of the late October afternoon. Inside, Amelia Rose Hawthorne, her hands calloused but deft from years of wielding scissors and combs, sighed and glanced at the empty barber chair.  The small town of Havenwood, nestled in the foothills of the Appalachians, was usually bustling this time of day, but the impending storm had seemingly driven everyone indoors. Amelia brushed a stray auburn curl from her forehead and idly rearranged the vintage shaving mugs lined up along the counter.  A faint scent of bay rum and sandalwood hung in the air, a comforting constant in the otherwise unpredictable weather.
