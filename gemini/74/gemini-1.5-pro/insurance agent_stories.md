The fluorescent lights of the Bartholomew & Crouch Insurance Agency hummed a monotonous tune, punctuated by the rhythmic tap-tap-tapping of Amelia Grace Holloway’s perfectly manicured fingernails on her antique oak desk. Outside, the late April air in the small town of Havenwood, Ohio, was thick with the scent of lilac and freshly mown grass, a stark contrast to the stale, paper-laden atmosphere of the office. Amelia, with her fiery red hair pulled back in a severe bun and her tailored navy suit, seemed just as out of place amidst the pastoral charm of Havenwood as a flamingo in a flock of pigeons. She’d arrived six months ago, fresh from the cutthroat world of corporate insurance in Chicago, a self-proclaimed exile seeking the supposed tranquility of small-town life.  Tranquility, however, had proven elusive. Havenwood, she was quickly discovering, held secrets darker than any insurance fraud she'd uncovered in the city.  And those secrets were about to land squarely on her meticulously organized desk.
