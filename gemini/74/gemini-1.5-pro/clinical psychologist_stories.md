The rain hammered against the arched windows of Dr. Elias Thorne's office, mimicking the relentless rhythm of anxiety that pulsed through his latest patient.  His office, nestled in a converted Victorian house on the edge of the historic district of Ashworth, Maine, usually offered a comforting, almost womb-like atmosphere.  Tonight, however, the storm had leached away the warmth, leaving the air thick with a damp chill that seeped into the very bones of the house. The flickering gaslight sconces cast elongated, dancing shadows across the deep crimson walls, lending an unsettling theatrical quality to the scene. Outside, the wind howled like a banshee, rattling the ancient oak branches against the glass, a soundtrack to the unraveling narrative Elias was patiently piecing together.  He leaned forward in his worn leather armchair, the firelight catching the glint of concern in his eyes as he listened intently.
