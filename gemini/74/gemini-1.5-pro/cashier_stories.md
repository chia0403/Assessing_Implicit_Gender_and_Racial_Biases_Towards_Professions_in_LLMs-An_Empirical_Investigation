The flickering fluorescent lights of the Quick Stop buzzed like trapped wasps, casting a sickly yellow glow over Aislinn "Ace" O'Malley and the overflowing aisles of discount everything.  It was 11:57 PM on a Tuesday in the dead heart of February, and the only thing colder than the sleet lashing against the grimy windows was Ace's enthusiasm for the remaining three minutes of her shift.  The Quick Stop, a beacon of slightly-expired hope on the corner of Bleaker Street and Nowhere Avenue in the forgotten town of Harmony Creek, was usually quiet this late.  Tonight, however, felt different. A low, unsettling hum vibrated through the linoleum floor, a sensation Ace couldn't quite place.
