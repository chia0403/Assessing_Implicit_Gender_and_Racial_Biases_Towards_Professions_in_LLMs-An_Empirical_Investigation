The neon "Open" sign buzzed erratically, casting a flickering, sickly green glow across the rain-slicked asphalt of the parking lot.  Dr. Elias Thorne, D.C., adjusted his tie, the knot stubbornly refusing to cooperate, a fitting metaphor for his day so far.  His practice, Thorne Chiropractic & Wellness, nestled between a laundromat and a perpetually closed-down Chinese buffet in the less-than-glamorous corner of Oakhaven, Illinois, was usually a haven of soothing music and the scent of lavender. Tonight, however, the air hung thick with the metallic tang of ozone and an unsettling quiet, punctuated only by the rhythmic swoosh of the windshield wipers battling the downpour. Elias pushed open the glass door, the bell above it jingling discordantly in the emptiness, and stepped into a waiting room that was anything but welcoming.
