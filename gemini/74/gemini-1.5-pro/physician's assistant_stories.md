The biting November wind whipped off Lake Erie, rattling the windowpanes of the small clinic and stinging Amelia Reyes's cheeks as she hurried across the parking lot.  Inside, the fluorescent lights hummed a monotonous tune against the backdrop of a gurgling coffee maker and the muffled sobs coming from exam room three. Amelia Reyes, PA-C, pulled off her wool cap, shaking loose strands of dark hair that had escaped her braid. The clinic, nestled between a laundromat and a perpetually closed Italian restaurant in the sleepy town of Port Blossom, Ohio, was her domain four days a week.  Today, judging by the overflowing waiting room and the palpable tension in the air, it promised to be one of those days that tested the limits of both her medical knowledge and her seemingly endless supply of patience.
