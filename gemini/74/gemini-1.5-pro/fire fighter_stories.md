The biting November wind whipped at Amelia “Mia” Rodriguez’s turnout coat as she stood on the precipice of what was once Mrs. Gable’s prize-winning rose garden. Now, only blackened stems and the acrid scent of smoke remained.  Embers, like malevolent fireflies, danced in the pre-dawn gloom, clinging stubbornly to the charred framework of the Victorian house that loomed over the garden.  Ash rained down like a perverse snow, coating everything in a fine grey film. The hoses snaked across the frost-covered lawn, pulsing with the lifeblood that had, just moments before, battled the inferno that had ripped through the heart of this historic Oakhaven neighborhood. The air, thick with the smells of burnt timber and wet earth, crackled with the aftermath of the blaze. A melancholic glow, the remnants of the fire’s fury, still pulsed deep within the skeletal remains of the house.
