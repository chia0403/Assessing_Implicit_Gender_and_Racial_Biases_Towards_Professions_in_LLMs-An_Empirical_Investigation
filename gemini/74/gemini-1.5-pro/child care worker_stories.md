The fluorescent lights of the "Little Wonders Daycare" hummed a monotonous tune, a counterpoint to the chaotic symphony of toddlers banging xylophones and shrieking with laughter.  Rain lashed against the large picture windows, turning the world outside into a blurry, grey canvas. Inside, amidst the vibrant chaos, Amelia Grace Holloway sighed, wiping a stray smear of grape jelly from her cheek with the back of her hand.  It was 3:15 pm, naptime had been a resounding failure thanks to a rogue thunderstorm, and Amelia, despite her genuine love for the tiny humans in her care, was beginning to crave the quiet solitude of her own apartment. Little Wonders, nestled in a converted Victorian house on a leafy street in the Boston suburb of Brookline, was usually a haven of playful learning and comforting routine. Today, however, felt more like navigating a particularly energetic hurricane in a rubber dinghy.
