The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne's dark hair across her face as she hurried across the deserted parking lot of St. Jude's Memorial Hospital.  Fluorescent lights bled from the windows of the ER, casting long, skeletal shadows of bare trees against the brick facade.  A thin layer of ice glazed the asphalt, reflecting the city lights back in a distorted shimmer.  Amelia, her breath puffing out in white clouds, clutched her thermos of lukewarm coffee tighter, a small comfort against the encroaching chill and the gnawing unease that had settled in her stomach since the shift change report. Tonight, a blizzard was brewing, both literally and figuratively, within the walls of St. Jude's.
