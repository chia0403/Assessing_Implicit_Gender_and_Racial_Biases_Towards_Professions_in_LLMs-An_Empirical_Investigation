The biting wind whipped across the frost-covered fields of Abernathy Farm, stinging Elias Thatcher's cheeks raw even through his thick woolen scarf. Dawn was still a hesitant promise in the east, painting the horizon a bruised purple, but Elias was already out, his breath clouding the frigid air.  Abernathy Farm, nestled in the rolling hills of the Derbyshire Dales, had been in his family for five generations. Its stone walls, weathered by centuries of sun and storm, stood as silent witnesses to the triumphs and heartbreaks of the Thatchers who had toiled this land before him.  This morning, however, the familiar comfort of the farm felt tinged with a gnawing unease.  The air hung heavy with a silence deeper than the usual stillness of a winter's morn. Even the sheep, usually bleating their morning greetings, huddled silently in the barn, their woolly bodies pressed close.
