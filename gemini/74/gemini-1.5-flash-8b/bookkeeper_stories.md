The chipped Formica countertop gleamed under the harsh fluorescent lights of the "Numbers N' Things" accounting firm.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of afternoon sunlight slicing through the grime-coated window, illuminating a swirling vortex of receipts, invoices, and meticulously organized spreadsheets.  Amelia Grace Thistlewick, a bookkeeper of practiced precision and surprisingly sharp wit, hunched over her aging desktop computer, a faint scent of stale coffee clinging to the air.  Her fingers, stained a pale ochre from years of handling ledgers, flew across the keyboard, a rhythmic tap-tap-tap that was her personal symphony.  Outside, the city roared – a cacophony of horns, sirens, and the distant rumble of subway trains – but inside the cramped office, Amelia, immersed in her meticulous work, was a world apart.
