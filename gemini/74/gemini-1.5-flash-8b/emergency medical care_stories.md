Rain lashed against the corrugated iron roof of the clinic, a relentless drumming that mirrored the frantic rhythm in Dr. Isabella "Izzy"  Rivera's chest.  The air, thick with the smell of antiseptic and fear, hung heavy in the small, cluttered examination room.  Fluorescent lights buzzed, casting harsh shadows on the chipped Formica table where a young man lay, his face pale and drawn, his chest rising and falling with ragged gasps.  Outside, the storm raged, mirroring the turmoil brewing within the small coastal town of Havenwood, a place where the sea held its breath and secrets, and where Izzy, Emergency Medical Technician and resident worrywart, was on her tenth call in as many hours.
