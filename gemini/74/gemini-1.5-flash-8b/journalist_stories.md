The biting November wind whipped Amelia Sparrow's crimson scarf around her neck, stinging her cheeks as she hurried down the cobbled alleyway.  Dust motes, illuminated by the flickering gas lamps, danced in the pale moonlight.  Rain, a relentless curtain for the past three days, had finally subsided, leaving behind a chilling dampness that clung to the ancient stones of the Old Quarter, clinging to the already weary smells of roasted chestnuts and brewing ale.  Amelia, a seasoned journalist for the *Chronicles of the City*, clutched a worn leather-bound notebook tighter in her hand, the paper within stained with the faintest smudges of ink and the faint scent of damp earth. Her destination: the crumbling tenement on Rue de la Mort, whispered about in hushed tones for weeks as the source of a peculiar, unnerving silence.
