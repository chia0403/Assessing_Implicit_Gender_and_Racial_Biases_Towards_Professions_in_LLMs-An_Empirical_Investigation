Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of the dilapidated firehouse, mirroring the relentless drumming in Sergeant Elias Thorne's head.  The air, thick with the smell of mildew and stale coffee, hung heavy in the cramped quarters.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of light piercing the gloom, illuminating a half-eaten plate of cold biscuits and a chipped mug, remnants of the previous shift's meager supper.  Outside, the city of Emberbrook, choked by a perpetual smog that seemed to cling to the skeletal skyscrapers like a shroud, was swallowed by the storm.  Elias, a seasoned firefighter with ten years on the force, knew this weather, this city, this firehouse, like the back of his hand. And tonight, it felt more ominous than usual.
