The biting wind whipped Dr. Eleanor Vance's lab coat around her, stinging her cheeks as she hurried across the desolate, snow-covered expanse of the Arctic research station.  Flickering fluorescent lights cast long, eerie shadows across the sterile white walls, highlighting the frost clinging to the metal railings.  The rhythmic, mechanical whirring of the life support systems sounded like a frantic heartbeat against the oppressive silence.  Outside, the aurora borealis painted the sky in vibrant hues of emerald and amethyst, a stark contrast to the stark reality of her surroundings.  The station, nestled deep within the frozen wilderness, felt more like a mausoleum than a haven for scientific discovery.  The air, though frigid, carried the metallic tang of something else entirely – something… wrong.  Her boots crunched on the ice, each step echoing eerily in the vast emptiness.  This was Projekt Aurora, and Eleanor knew, with a dread that settled deep in her stomach, that something had gone terribly awry.
