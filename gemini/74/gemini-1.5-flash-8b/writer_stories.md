The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Hawthorne’s hands, the faint scent of Earl Grey clinging to the steam.  Sunlight, fractured by the stained-glass window of the tiny café, painted the worn wooden table in shifting patterns of gold and emerald.  Outside, the cobbled streets of Prague hummed with the rhythmic clatter of trams and the murmur of a thousand languages.  Amelia, perched in her usual corner booth, stared out at the scene, a single, meticulously sharpened pencil balanced precariously on the page before her.  This was her sanctuary, her laboratory.  This was where, for the past three months, she had been attempting to coax a story from the labyrinthine corners of her mind.  The story, she knew, was there, buried beneath layers of frustration, doubt, and the persistent, gnawing feeling that she was simply not good enough.
