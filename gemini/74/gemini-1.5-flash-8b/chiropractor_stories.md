The flickering fluorescent lights of Dr. Eleanor Vance's chiropractic office cast long, skeletal shadows across the worn linoleum floor.  Dust motes danced in the lone shaft of sunlight piercing the grime-coated window, illuminating a single, almost-forgotten bouquet of wilting sunflowers on the reception desk.  The air, thick with the scent of antiseptic and lingering coffee, hummed with the quiet, rhythmic thrum of a nearby ventilation system.  This was a sanctuary from the chaos of the bustling city, tucked away on a side street in the heart of the relentless urban sprawl, yet it felt more like a forgotten relic.  Eleanor, Dr. Eleanor Vance, stared at the X-ray on the viewing screen, a frown creasing her brow.  The patient, a young woman named Chloe, had presented with a seemingly innocuous complaint—a persistent stiffness in her neck—but the X-ray revealed a subtle, concerning curve. This was no ordinary case.
