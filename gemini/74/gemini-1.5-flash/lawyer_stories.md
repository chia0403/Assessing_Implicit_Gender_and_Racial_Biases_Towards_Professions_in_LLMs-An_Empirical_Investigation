The salt spray stung Amelia "Mia" Hernandez's face as she wrestled the stubborn fishing boat's wheel.  The late afternoon sun, a bruised plum sinking below the horizon, cast long shadows across the churning grey water of the Monterey Bay.  Gulls cried overhead, their cries a mournful counterpoint to the rhythmic chug of the aging engine.  Mia, a lawyer specializing in maritime law with a reputation as sharp as the jagged rocks lining the coast, wasn't known for her seafaring skills, but today, she was far from the polished mahogany of her San Francisco office.  The scent of brine and diesel hung heavy in the air, a stark contrast to the usual perfume of expensive cologne and old books that clung to her tailored suits.  She was here, battling the unpredictable whims of the Pacific, because a desperate phone call had pulled her away from a closing argument, a call that promised a tangled web of secrets, lies, and a missing person far more valuable than any court case she'd ever handled.
