The biting wind whipped across the jagged peaks of the Dragon's Teeth mountains, carrying with it the scent of pine and the faint, metallic tang of blood.  A lone raven, black as the encroaching twilight, circled overhead, its harsh cry echoing the desolation of the landscape.  Below, nestled precariously in a narrow cleft of the rock face, stood the fortress of Blackrock Keep.  Its weathered stone walls, scarred by centuries of siege and storm, seemed to claw at the sky, a defiant monument to the unforgiving land.  Inside, pacing the ramparts, his breath misting in the frigid air, stood Guard Captain  Alistair Thorne.  His thick fur cloak did little to ward off the chill that penetrated to his very bones, a chill that mirrored the unease settling deep in his gut.  Something was wrong.  He could feel it in the silence, in the stillness of the usually bustling courtyard below, in the unsettling absence of the customary night watch calls.
