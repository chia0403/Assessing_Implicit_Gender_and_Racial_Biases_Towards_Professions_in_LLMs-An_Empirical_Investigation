The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Grace Hawthorne's fingers, a small comfort against the bone-chilling November wind howling outside the window of her cramped office. Rain lashed against the grimy panes, mirroring the turmoil brewing inside her.  Amelia, a secretary at the prestigious Blackwood & Thorne law firm, stared out at the relentless downpour, the city of London a swirling grey canvas beneath the bruised, pewter sky.  Her cramped workspace, barely larger than a walk-in closet, was a testament to her years of dedicated but largely unnoticed service.  The air hung thick with the scent of stale coffee and the faint, lingering perfume of expensive lilies, a stark contrast to the damp chill clinging to her shoulders.  A half-eaten packet of digestive biscuits sat beside a stack of files taller than her head – each one a testament to the relentless demands of her demanding superiors.  Tonight, however, the weight of the paperwork felt heavier than usual.  Something felt…off.  The silence of the otherwise bustling office after hours was unnerving, a silence broken only by the rhythmic drumming of the rain and the frantic thump-thump-thump of her own anxious heart.
