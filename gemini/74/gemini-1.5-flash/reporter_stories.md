The biting November wind whipped Amelia Hernandez’s auburn hair across her face as she hunched deeper into her worn trench coat.  Rain, a relentless, sideways assault, plastered her already damp bangs to her forehead.  The flickering neon sign of the Rusty Mug tavern cast a sickly yellow glow on the slick cobblestones of Grimsborough’s back alley, illuminating a scene far more grim than the promise of cheap beer and questionable company.  A crumpled newspaper lay half-submerged in a puddle, its headline – "Local Historian Vanishes" – a stark contrast to the overflowing dumpster beside it, groaning with the city's discarded secrets.  Amelia, a reporter for the Grimsborough Gazette known more for her tenacity than her charm, shivered, not entirely from the cold.  This alley, this city, held a story, and she intended to unearth it, even if it meant getting a little dirtier than she preferred.
