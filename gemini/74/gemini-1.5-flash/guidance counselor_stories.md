The stale scent of coffee and desperation clung to the air in Ms. Eleanor Vance's office, a scent as familiar to her as the worn, floral-patterned armchair opposite her desk.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of Northwood High, blurring the already indistinct view of the sprawling suburban landscape.  Inside, the air was thick with the muted hum of adolescent anxieties, a low thrum that vibrated even through the thick carpet.  Eleanor, a woman whose kind eyes held the weight of a thousand teenage confessions, sighed, the sound barely audible above the rhythmic drumming of the storm.  Tonight, the usual anxieties – college applications, failing grades, first heartbreaks – felt amplified, sharpened by the relentless downpour outside mirroring the tempest brewing within her latest student.
