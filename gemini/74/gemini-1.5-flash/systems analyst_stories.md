The flickering neon sign of the "Lucky Dragon" noodle shop cast a lurid green glow across Elias Thorne's rain-slicked trench coat.  Rain, a relentless drizzle that had been falling since before dawn, plastered his dark hair to his forehead.  He pulled his collar higher, the damp chill seeping into his bones despite the thick wool.  Elias, a systems analyst for the notoriously secretive firm, Obsidian Data Solutions, wasn't used to this kind of weather.  His usual commute involved the climate-controlled comfort of a company car, not navigating the treacherous, rain-lashed streets of Hong Kong's Wan Chai district on foot.  Tonight, however, the usual wasn't an option.  His meticulously planned schedule, usually a symphony of precision and efficiency, had been shattered. A frantic call from his boss, a clipped message delivered with an urgency that sent a shiver down his spine, had thrown him headlong into the humid, chaotic heart of the city's underbelly. The pungent aroma of fish sauce and exhaust fumes battled for dominance in the night air, a fitting backdrop to the unsettling task that lay ahead.
