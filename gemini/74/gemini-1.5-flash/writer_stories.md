The chipped paint of Amelia Katherine Blackwood's writing desk flaked onto the worn Persian rug, a miniature snowstorm mirroring the one raging outside.  Grey, bruised light filtered through the grime-coated windows of her attic room, illuminating dust motes dancing in the chilling air.  The only warmth came from the sputtering gas fire, its flickering flames casting long, distorted shadows across the overflowing bookshelves that lined three walls, their spines a testament to decades spent lost in worlds both real and imagined.  Amelia, bundled in a thick wool cardigan and her perpetually ink-stained fingers wrapped around a steaming mug of something vaguely resembling tea, stared at the blank page before her, the cursor blinking mockingly on her ancient laptop screen.  Outside, the wind howled like a banshee, a fitting soundtrack to the creative block that had clamped its icy jaws around her for the past three months.
