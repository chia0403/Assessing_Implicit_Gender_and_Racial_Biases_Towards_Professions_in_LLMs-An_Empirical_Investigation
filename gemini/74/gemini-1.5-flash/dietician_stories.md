The humid air hung heavy, thick with the scent of frying plantains and exhaust fumes.  Cicadas buzzed a relentless rhythm against the backdrop of salsa music spilling from a nearby bodega.  Dr. Eleanor Vance, RD, wiped a stray bead of sweat from her forehead, the crimson stain of a guava juice spill still clinging stubbornly to her crisp white lab coat.  The vibrant chaos of Santo Domingo’s Calle El Conde swirled around her, a sensory overload that normally invigorated her, but today, it only amplified the gnawing anxiety in her stomach.  She was late.  Terribly, unforgivably late.  And her client, Señora Emilia Rodriguez, wasn't known for her patience.  The meeting, scheduled for 8 AM sharp, was now pushing past 9, and Señora Rodriguez’s reputation for fiery temper was legendary even amongst the city's most seasoned healthcare professionals.
