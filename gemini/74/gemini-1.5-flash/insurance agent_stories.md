The chipped porcelain of Amelia Katherine Finch's desk felt cold against her cheek.  Rain lashed against the grimy windows of her office, a relentless drumming that mirrored the anxiety churning in her stomach.  Amelia, or Amy as most people called her (though she preferred the formality of her full name in business dealings, a subtle assertion of authority she desperately needed), stared at the overflowing inbox on her computer screen.  The flickering neon sign of "Finch & Associates – Insurance Solutions" outside cast a sickly yellow glow across the cramped, cluttered space, highlighting the dust motes dancing in the stagnant air.  The office, situated above a perpetually failing bakery with the alluring aroma of burnt sugar and despair, was a testament to her dwindling clientele and the overall bleakness of her recent professional trajectory.  Tonight, however, was different. Tonight, she was expecting a visitor – a visitor who could potentially revive her failing business, or utterly shatter it.
