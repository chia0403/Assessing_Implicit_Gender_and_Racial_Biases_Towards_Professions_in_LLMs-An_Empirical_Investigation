The chipped paint of the courthouse steps crunched under Amelia Hernandez's worn heels as she hurried towards the imposing granite edifice.  Rain, a relentless, grey drizzle that had been falling since dawn, plastered her dark hair to her forehead, clinging to the collar of her slightly-too-thin trench coat.  The air, thick with the scent of exhaust fumes and damp concrete, hummed with a low thrum of anxious energy – a symphony of whispered conversations, the click-clack of hurried footsteps, and the distant, mournful wail of a siren.  Inside, the fluorescent lights of the bustling lobby offered a stark, sterile contrast to the gloom outside. Amelia, a paralegal at the firm of Sterling & Croft, pushed through the revolving doors, the weight of three overflowing files – a testament to a week of relentless deadlines – digging into her already aching shoulders.  This case, the Peterson inheritance dispute, was threatening to unravel, and Amelia knew that today, more than ever, she was the glue holding it all together.
