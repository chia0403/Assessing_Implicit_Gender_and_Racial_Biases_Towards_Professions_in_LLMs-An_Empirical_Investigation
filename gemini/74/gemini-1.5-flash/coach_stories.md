The biting November wind whipped across the cracked asphalt of the abandoned baseball field, carrying with it the scent of damp earth and decaying leaves.  Coach Elias Thorne, a man whose weathered face mirrored the worn-out infield, pulled his worn, leather jacket tighter around him.  The setting sun cast long, skeletal shadows from the rusting outfield fence, emphasizing the desolate beauty of the place.  It had been years since a game had been played here, years since the laughter of children had echoed across the worn diamond. But tonight, under the bruised purple sky, a different kind of game was about to begin.  Elias wasn't preparing for a friendly match, or even a practice; tonight, the stakes were far higher.
