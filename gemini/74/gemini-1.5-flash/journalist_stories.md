The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Mia” Holloway’s trench coat around her legs as she hunched deeper into the shadows of the crumbling Victorian mansion.  Rain, a relentless drizzle that had been falling for three days, slicked the cobblestones of Blackwood Lane, turning the already ominous street into a treacherous maze of glistening darkness.  Above, the skeletal branches of ancient oaks clawed at the bruised twilight sky, their silhouettes jagged against the bruised purple and grey canvas.  Blackwood Manor, once the jewel of the town, now stood as a skeletal monument to forgotten grandeur, its stained-glass windows like vacant eyes staring out at a world that had long since moved on.  Mia, a journalist for the *Oakhaven Gazette*, clutched her worn notepad to her chest, the damp seeping through the paper, mirroring the creeping unease that settled in her stomach.  Tonight, she wasn't just chasing a story; she was chasing a ghost.
