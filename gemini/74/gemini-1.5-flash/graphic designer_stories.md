The chipped mug warmed Amelia Hernandez’s hands, the lukewarm chamomile doing little to soothe the icy dread churning in her stomach.  Rain lashed against the panoramic window of her Brooklyn loft, blurring the already hazy cityscape into a watercolor of greys and bruised purples.  Amelia, a graphic designer with a penchant for bold fonts and even bolder coffee, stared out at the deluge, the rhythmic drumming a counterpoint to the frantic thump-thump-thump of her own heart.  Her deadline for the Peterson & Sons rebranding campaign loomed, a monstrous, pixelated kraken threatening to drag her under.  The apartment, usually a haven of organized chaos – a carefully curated blend of vintage mid-century modern furniture and overflowing sketchbooks – felt suffocating, each meticulously placed object a silent accusation of her impending failure.  The air hung heavy with the scent of burnt coffee and the unspoken promise of a disastrous all-nighter.
