The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s dark hair across her face as she hurried down the cobbled street, the collar of her worn wool coat pulled high.  Rain, a fine, icy drizzle, plastered her auburn bangs to her forehead.  The gas lamps of  Whitstable, Kent, cast a sickly yellow glow on the slick, uneven pavement, reflecting in the puddles that gathered at the foot of the ancient, leaning buildings.  The salty tang of the North Sea hung heavy in the air, mingling with the scent of woodsmoke and something else, something faintly metallic… blood?  Amelia, a registered nurse at the small, but perpetually overwhelmed, Whitstable General Hospital, instinctively tightened her grip on her worn leather satchel.  Tonight felt different.  Heavier.  And the metallic scent seemed to be growing stronger with each hurried step she took towards the hospital's looming, gothic silhouette against the stormy sky.
