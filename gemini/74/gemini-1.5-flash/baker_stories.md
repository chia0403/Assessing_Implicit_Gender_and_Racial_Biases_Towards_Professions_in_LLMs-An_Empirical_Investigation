The aroma of cardamom and burnt sugar hung heavy in the pre-dawn air, a familiar perfume to Aurelia "Relia" Blackwood.  Relia, owner and sole operator of "The Crumb & The Kettle," shivered despite the warmth radiating from the brick oven, its belly glowing a fiery orange.  Outside, the cobblestone streets of Port Blossom were still cloaked in the inky blackness of a November morning, the only sounds the distant creak of a ship's mast and the rhythmic slap of waves against the harbor wall.  A thin layer of frost dusted the windowpanes of her tiny bakery, each delicate crystal a miniature masterpiece reflecting the faint, ethereal glow of the nascent sunrise. The bakery itself, nestled between a weathered bookshop and a bustling fishmonger, was a charmingly chaotic jumble of flour-dusted surfaces, copper pots gleaming under the weak light, and shelves overflowing with jars of exotic spices and sugared fruits.  Today, however, the usual comforting chaos felt charged with a nervous energy, a tremor of anticipation that even the comforting scent of baking bread couldn't entirely quell.
