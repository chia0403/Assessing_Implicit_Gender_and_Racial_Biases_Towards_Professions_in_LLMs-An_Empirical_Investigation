The biting November wind whipped Amelia Katherine Holloway’s auburn hair across her face as she wrestled the easel into position.  Its legs, stubbornly stuck in the mud of the overgrown coastal path, threatened to send the whole precarious structure tumbling into the churning grey sea below.  The sky, a bruised purple mirroring the stormy ocean, offered little in the way of inspiration, only the relentless, unforgiving power of nature.  But Amelia, bundled in a thick, wool coat that did little to ward off the chill, was undeterred.  Her weathered wooden palette, clutched in her gloved hand, held the promise of capturing the raw, untamed beauty of this desolate stretch of the Cornish coastline – a beauty that resonated deep within her soul, a beauty she felt compelled to translate onto canvas, no matter the tempest raging around her.
