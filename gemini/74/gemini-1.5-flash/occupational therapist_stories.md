The salt spray stung Eleanor Vance's face as she wrestled the stubborn kayak onto the rocky shoreline.  The wind, a relentless, howling banshee, whipped her already damp hair across her eyes.  She squinted, the grey expanse of the Atlantic stretching before her like a crumpled sheet of leaden cloth.  The air, thick with the smell of brine and seaweed, carried a hint of woodsmoke from the distant cottages clinging to the cliffs of the Isle of Skye.  Eleanor, an occupational therapist with hands as capable of kneading stubborn clay as they were of coaxing movement back into a paralyzed limb, shivered, pulling her waterproof jacket tighter. This wasn't exactly the typical setting for her work, but then again, neither was her latest patient.
