The humid air hung heavy, thick with the scent of jasmine and impending rain.  Cicadas buzzed a relentless chorus from the overgrown foliage surrounding the crumbling courthouse in the town of San Cristobal, a forgotten jewel nestled deep within the Colombian Andes.  Judge Isabella Sofia Hernandez, her crisp white judicial robe clinging uncomfortably to her skin, adjusted the silver chain of her crucifix, the cool metal a small comfort against the oppressive heat.  The chipped paint of her chambers peeled in delicate flakes, mirroring the slow, agonizing decay of the justice system she so desperately tried to uphold.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of sunlight piercing the gloom, illuminating the worn mahogany desk where a half-empty bottle of aguardiente sat beside a stack of files, each one a testament to the intricate web of violence and corruption that choked the very life out of this picturesque, but profoundly troubled, town.
