The biting November wind whipped Dr. Evelyn Reed’s auburn hair across her face as she wrestled the recalcitrant gate of Alderley Farm open.  Rust groaned a mournful protest under her efforts, each creak echoing across the desolate, windswept fields of the Yorkshire Dales.  The sky, a bruised purple bruised by the setting sun, mirrored the anxiety churning in her stomach.  Alderley Farm, perched precariously on a windswept hilltop, was notorious amongst the local farming community, not for its livestock, but for the peculiar misfortunes that seemed to plague it.  Evelyn, however, had been summoned by a desperate phone call – a late-night plea filled with hushed whispers and the distinct bleating of a sheep in distress.  The air smelled of damp earth and something else, something subtly metallic and unsettling, a scent that prickled the back of her neck even before she glimpsed the shadowy form of a lone figure waiting by the farmhouse, silhouetted against the dying light.
