The wind, a rasping whisper through the parched corn stalks, carried the scent of dust and desperation.  Elias Thorne, his face etched with the map of a life spent battling the unforgiving Arizona sun, squinted at the horizon.  The setting sun painted the jagged peaks of the Superstition Mountains a fiery orange, a cruel mockery of the barren landscape stretching before him.  His farm, Thorne's Bounty, once a vibrant green testament to his hard work, was now a cracked and brittle parody of its former self.  The well, usually brimming with life-giving water, had run dry weeks ago, leaving only a ghostly whisper of its former bounty.  This year, the drought was relentless, a merciless hand squeezing the life out of everything it touched.
