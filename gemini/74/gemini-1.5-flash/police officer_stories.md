The salt spray stung Detective Inspector Amelia Grace Blackwood’s face as the November wind howled a mournful dirge across the desolate stretch of Northumberland coastline.  Rain, thick and relentless, plastered her dark hair to her scalp, clinging to the collar of her sodden raincoat like a second skin.  Below the cliff edge, the North Sea churned, a frothing, grey beast clawing at the jagged rocks.  The air tasted of brine and something else… something faintly metallic, a scent that pricked at the back of her throat and tightened the knot of unease already forming in her stomach. This wasn't the picturesque postcard coastline tourists flocked to in the summer; this was a raw, unforgiving landscape, and tonight, it held a secret.
