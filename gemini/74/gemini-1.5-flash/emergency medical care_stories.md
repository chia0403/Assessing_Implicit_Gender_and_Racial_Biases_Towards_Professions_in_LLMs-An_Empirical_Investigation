The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Amy” Hernandez’s hair across her face as she wrestled the stretcher into the back of the ambulance.  Sirens wailed a mournful counterpoint to the rhythmic slap of the rain against the metal chassis.  The air hung thick with the scent of exhaust fumes, wet asphalt, and a faint, metallic tang that Amy recognized instantly – blood.  This wasn't the picturesque coastal town of Carmel-by-the-Sea advertised in tourist brochures; this was Carmel-by-the-Sea at 3:17 AM, raw and unforgiving, a place where the beauty was only skin deep, easily obscured by the brutal realities of life and death.  The flickering sodium-vapor streetlights cast long, distorted shadows, highlighting the slick, rain-streaked streets as Amy, a paramedic with five years’ experience under her belt, and her partner, Javier, prepared for another night on the front lines.
