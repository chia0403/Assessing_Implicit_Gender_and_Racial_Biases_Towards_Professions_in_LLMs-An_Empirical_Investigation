The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Katherine Holloway’s hands, a small comfort against the pre-dawn chill seeping through the drafty windows of her classroom.  Outside, the October wind howled a mournful tune through the skeletal branches of the ancient oak trees that guarded Northwood High School.  Inside, the air hung heavy with the scent of stale chalk dust and forgotten dreams – the familiar aroma of a Friday morning before the weekend rush.  Amelia, a veteran English teacher with ten years of witty retorts and well-worn copies of *Hamlet* under her belt, surveyed her domain: rows of neatly stacked desks, each bearing a faint patina of teenage angst, and a whiteboard still scarred with the remnants of yesterday's Shakespearean sonnets, now looking more like abstract art than iambic pentameter.  The only sound was the rhythmic ticking of the old grandfather clock in the corner, its pendulum swinging with the deliberate pace of a life lived slowly, a stark contrast to the frenetic energy that would soon flood the hallowed halls.
