The salt spray stung Elias Thorne's face as he wrestled the stubborn oak plank into place.  The air, thick with the smell of brine and sawdust, whipped around him, carrying the cries of gulls overhead.  Below, the churning grey expanse of the North Sea clawed at the weathered timbers of the small, ramshackle boathouse perched precariously on the cliffs of Cornwall.  Elias, his calloused hands stained a deep mahogany from years of working the wood, paused, the rhythmic clang of his hammer momentarily silenced. The relentless wind howled a mournful tune, a fitting soundtrack to the solitary task before him: repairing the ancient fishing boat, *The Wanderer*, a vessel as weathered and worn as its owner.
