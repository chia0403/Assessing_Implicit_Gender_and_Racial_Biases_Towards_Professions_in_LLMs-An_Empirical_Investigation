The salt spray stung Amelia Katherine Blackwood's face as she wrestled the stubborn fishing boat's tiller.  The churning grey of the North Sea mirrored the turmoil in her gut.  Amelia, more accustomed to the quiet clatter of her typewriter and the comforting scent of old paper, found herself utterly out of her element, the harsh wind a cruel counterpoint to the delicate prose she usually conjured.  She’d sought refuge on this remote island of Inishmore, a craggy emerald jewel thrust into the Atlantic’s maw, hoping the isolation would unlock the writer’s block that had gripped her for the past six months.  But the silence here, broken only by the cry of gulls and the relentless roar of the ocean, was proving a far more formidable antagonist than she’d anticipated.  The cottage she'd rented, perched precariously on the cliff edge, offered breathtaking views, but little in the way of comfort – a stark reminder of the desolate landscape mirroring the empty pages of her manuscript.
