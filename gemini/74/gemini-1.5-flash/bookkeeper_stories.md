The chipped porcelain teacup warmed Amelia Katherine Finch's fingers, a meager comfort against the November chill that seeped through the drafty windowpanes of her office.  Dust motes, disturbed by the faint breeze, danced in the single shaft of afternoon sunlight slicing through the gloom of Finch & Finch Accountants, a name that echoed with a faded grandeur it no longer possessed.  The building itself, a crumbling Victorian monstrosity on the edge of Grimsborough’s less desirable district, seemed to sigh under the weight of its own decaying elegance.  Amelia, a woman whose life was as meticulously organized as the ledgers she balanced, found a strange solace in this chaotic environment.  The clutter of files, the scent of aged paper and stale coffee, the rhythmic tap-tap-tap of her ancient typewriter – these were the comforting constants in a world increasingly prone to unexpected upheaval.  This particular Tuesday, however, promised to be anything but constant.  A new client, a Mr. Silas Blackwood, was due to arrive at any moment, and the rumors swirling around Blackwood Industries were as unsettling as the groaning floorboards beneath her feet.
