The fluorescent lights of Binder & Finch buzzed like angry bees, casting a sickly yellow glow on the spreadsheet swimming before Arthur Bertram Higgins. He blinked, his eyes gritty from staring at numbers for the better part of twelve hours. Outside, the late October wind howled, rattling the single-paned windows of the firm’s offices in the historic, and perpetually drafty, Flatiron Building. Rain lashed against the glass, blurring the already hazy Manhattan skyline into a watercolor wash of greys and browns. Arthur suppressed a shiver. Not from the cold – though the heat in the building was notoriously unreliable – but from the creeping feeling that something was deeply, irrevocably *wrong* with the financial statements of their client, the eccentric toy magnate, Bartholomew Button.
