The relentless Miami sun beat down on Ocean Drive, baking the Art Deco facades and turning the asphalt into shimmering mirage.  Inside "Pulse," a fitness studio so exclusive its entrance was concealed behind a bespoke juice bar,  Amelia "Amy" Rodriguez adjusted the microphone clipped to her neon pink sports bra.  Sweat beaded on her forehead, a testament to the killer warm-up she’d just put her 7:00 AM "Sunrise Sculpt" class through.  The air hung thick with the mingled scents of coconut water, expensive dry shampoo, and the faint, metallic tang of exertion.  Amy surveyed her domain – a room dominated by a wall of mirrors reflecting back the taut, toned bodies of Miami’s elite, all poised and ready for her next instruction. A nervous flutter danced in her stomach; this wasn't just any class.  This was the class. The one where Ricardo Vargas, the notoriously demanding owner of the "Body by Vargas" empire, would be observing her.  Her future at Pulse, maybe even her entire career, hinged on the next hour.
