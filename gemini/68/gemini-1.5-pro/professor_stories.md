The biting November wind whipped around Professor Alistair Finchley, tugging at the edges of his tweed overcoat as he hurried across the cobbled quadrangle of St. Jude’s College, Oxford.  Gargoyles leered down from the medieval stonework, their grotesque features softened by a dusting of snow.  The air, crisp and carrying the faint scent of woodsmoke, vibrated with the five o'clock chime from the chapel bell tower. Alistair clutched a leather-bound volume of Chaucer closer to his chest, the cold seeping through the worn fabric.  He was late, again, for his weekly meeting with the Dean, a meeting he’d been dreading all week.  The Dean, a man whose smile Alistair suspected was carved from granite, had summoned him to discuss certain… irregularities… in his recent research grant application. Irregularities that Alistair, a man of impeccable academic integrity (or so he told himself), couldn’t quite explain.  As he approached the imposing oak door of the Dean’s office, the first fat flakes of snow began to fall, swirling around him like a shroud of unease.
