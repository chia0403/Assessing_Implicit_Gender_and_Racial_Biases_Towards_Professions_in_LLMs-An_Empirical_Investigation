The biting November wind whipped through the gaps in the crumbling brickwork of the abandoned textile mill, swirling dust and dead leaves around Cassandra "Cass" Thorne's ankles.  Her breath puffed out in white clouds as she surveyed the cavernous space, its grimy windows offering a fragmented view of the bleak Manchester skyline.  The air hung heavy with the ghosts of industry past, the scent of oil and rust clinging to the decaying machinery. This wasn't the glamorous Hollywood set Cass was accustomed to.  This was raw, gritty, real.  And it was perfect.  This derelict mill, with its echoing emptiness and palpable history, was precisely the atmosphere she needed to bring her latest project, a gritty psychological thriller titled "The Weaver's Knot," to life.
