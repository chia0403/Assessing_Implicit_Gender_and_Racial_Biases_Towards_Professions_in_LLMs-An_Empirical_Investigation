The fluorescent lights of Mr. Elias Vance's classroom hummed a monotonous B-flat, a soundtrack to the pre-dawn quiet of Northwood High.  Elias, his tie loosened and sleeves rolled up, surveyed the battlefield: desks clustered in collaborative pods, each strewn with the detritus of yesterday's debate on Shakespeare's use of dramatic irony.  Outside, the October sky bled from bruised purple into a watery grey, mirroring the churning anxiety in Elias's stomach.  Northwood, nestled in the sleepy, apple-orchard-dotted hills of western Massachusetts, was usually a haven of tranquility.  But this year, a strange unease had settled over the town, a low-frequency hum beneath the surface of Friday night football games and parent-teacher conferences.  And Elias, with his uncanny knack for sensing the undercurrents of teenage angst, knew something was about to break.
