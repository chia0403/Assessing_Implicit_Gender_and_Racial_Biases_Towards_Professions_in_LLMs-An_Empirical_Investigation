Rain lashed against the attic window, a staccato rhythm accompanying the click-clack of Amelia Thorne's ancient typewriter.  The only light in the cramped space came from a bare bulb dangling precariously above her head, casting long, distorted shadows across the dusty, book-lined walls.  Stacks of manuscripts, teetering precariously like forgotten skyscrapers, threatened to topple onto the worn Persian rug beneath her feet.  Seaview Cottage, perched on the windswept cliffs overlooking the churning grey expanse of the North Sea, had always been Amelia's sanctuary, a place where words flowed as freely as the tides. But tonight, the usually inspiring isolation felt oppressive, the rhythmic crash of waves against the rocks below a mocking echo of the writer's block that had held her captive for weeks.
