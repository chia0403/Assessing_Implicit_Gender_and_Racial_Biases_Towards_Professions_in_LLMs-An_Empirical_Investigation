The mahogany desk, polished to a mirror sheen, reflected Judge Elias Thorne's grim countenance.  Rain lashed against the arched windows of his chambers, mimicking the tempest brewing within him.  Greyhaven, a city clinging to the craggy coastline of Maine, was perpetually shrouded in mist and melancholy, a fitting backdrop for the weight of decisions he carried.  This evening, however, the usual gloom felt amplified, a premonition clinging to the salty air. A single lamp illuminated the legal tomes lining the walls, casting long, distorted shadows that danced with the flickering gaslight. The case file lay open before him, the name "Evelyn Hayes" scrawled across the cover in stark black ink, a name that had already begun to haunt his dreams.
