The rain lashed against the grimy windows of the Busy Bee Diner, blurring the neon "Open 24 Hours" sign into a shimmering, indistinct blob.  Inside, the air hung thick with the scent of stale coffee, frying bacon, and a faint undercurrent of despair.  Amelia “Amy” Rodriguez, her name tag pinned slightly askew on her faded turquoise uniform, wiped down the sticky Formica countertop with a practiced hand, her thoughts far away from the clattering dishes and the desultory chatter of the few late-night patrons.  The Busy Bee, nestled between a pawn shop and a laundromat on a desolate stretch of Highway 17 just outside of Harmony Creek, Nevada, wasn’t exactly glamorous, but it was all Amy had.
