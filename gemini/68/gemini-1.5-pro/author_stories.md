The salt-laced wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne's hair across her face, stinging her eyes and blurring the already fading light of the Cornish coast.  Perched precariously on a lichen-covered rock overlooking the churning Atlantic, she clutched her notebook, the damp pages rippling in the gale. Tregenna Cottage, her rented sanctuary, a ramshackle stone dwelling clinging to the cliffside like a stubborn limpet, was barely visible through the swirling mist.  November had descended upon the tiny village of Portwenn with a vengeance, mirroring the tempest raging inside Amelia.  Her third novel, a gothic thriller set amidst the very cliffs she now surveyed, was stalled, the words trapped somewhere between her heart and her hand, like ships caught in the Doldrums. The deadline loomed, a menacing shadow stretching across the already bleak landscape of her creativity.
