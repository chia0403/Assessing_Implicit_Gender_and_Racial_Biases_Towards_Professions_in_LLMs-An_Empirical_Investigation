The biting November wind whipped across the desolate practice field, carrying the scent of damp earth and the faint, metallic tang of blood.  Coach Amelia "Amy" Reyes shivered, pulling her threadbare Eagles Academy jacket tighter around her. Floodlights cast long, skeletal shadows from the bleachers, the empty metal seats groaning in protest against the gusts.  Nestled in the heart of the forgotten Rust Belt town of Ashworth, Pennsylvania, Eagles Academy wasn’t exactly a breeding ground for champions.  The football team hadn't seen a winning season in over a decade, and the last time their trophy case had seen any action was when someone donated a half-eaten box of stale championship cookies from 1987.  But Amy, staring at the lone figure practicing under the stark white lights, felt a flicker of something akin to hope.  This kid, this scrawny, determined quarterback, might just be the spark Ashworth – and Amy – so desperately needed.
