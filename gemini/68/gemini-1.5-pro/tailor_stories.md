The clatter of horse-drawn carriages on cobblestones was a constant rhythm outside Elias Finch's small tailor shop, a counterpoint to the whisper of silk against his shears.  Elias, his full name Elias Peregrine Finch, a name as elaborate as the embroidery he sometimes crafted, stood hunched over his workbench, a beam of dusty afternoon sunlight illuminating the fine wrinkles around his kind eyes.  His shop, nestled between a bustling bakery and a somewhat dubious apothecary on Candlewick Lane in the perpetually foggy city of Aethelburg, was a haven of quiet industry.  Bolts of fabric, stacked from floor to ceiling in hues of twilight and jewel tones, filled the air with the faint, comforting scent of dyes and wool.  Pins and needles glittered like tiny constellations on a velvet cushion, and the rhythmic snip-snip of his scissors spoke of a life dedicated to the art of cloth and needle.  This afternoon, however, a different kind of rhythm disturbed the usual calm.  A frantic rapping at the door, sharp and insistent, echoed through the shop, followed by a voice, breathless and urgent.
