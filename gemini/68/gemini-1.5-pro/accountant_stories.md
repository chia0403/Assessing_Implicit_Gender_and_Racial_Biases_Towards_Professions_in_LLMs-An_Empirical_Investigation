Rain lashed against the window of Elias Thorne's office, blurring the already fading twilight into a hazy grey.  Elias, his tie loosened and his spectacles perched precariously on his nose, stared at the spreadsheet glowing on his monitor.  Numbers, usually his solace, swam before his eyes, a dizzying reflection of the turmoil churning within him.  His cramped office, perched high above the bustling streets of Victorian London on the fifth floor of Grimstone & Sons Accounting, usually felt like a haven, a sanctuary of order against the chaos of the city.  Tonight, however, it felt like a cage. The air was thick with the smell of damp wool and the faint, metallic tang of fear. Something wasn't adding up, and it wasn't just the columns on his ledger.
