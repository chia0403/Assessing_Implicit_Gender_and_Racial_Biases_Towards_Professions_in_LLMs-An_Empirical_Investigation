The wind, sharp and insistent as a chisel, whipped across the desolate plains of Aeridor, stinging Elias Thorne’s exposed cheeks.  He hunched deeper into his thick wool cloak, the rough fabric a poor substitute for the warmth of the forge he’d left behind hours ago. Above him, the sky bled from a bruised purple to the inky black that heralded the swift descent of night.  Elias’s breath plumed out in ghostly white puffs as he surveyed the monolithic silhouette looming against the darkening horizon: the Obsidian Quarry.  A jagged scar on the otherwise smooth landscape, the quarry was a place of both wonder and trepidation, a source of Aeridor’s famed blackstone, and the place where Elias, master stone cutter, earned his meager living.  Each swing of his hammer, each precise strike of his chisel, was a testament to his skill, a dialogue between him and the unyielding stone. Tonight, however, a different kind of conversation awaited him within the quarry's depths.
