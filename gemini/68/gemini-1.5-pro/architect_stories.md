The biting November wind whipped around Cassandra “Cassie” Moreau, plastering strands of her auburn hair across her face as she surveyed the skeletal frame of the "Skyreach" project.  Steel girders clawed at the overcast London sky, a stark contrast to the elegant Georgian townhouses huddled together for warmth across the street in Fitzrovia.  Cassie, bundled in a thick wool coat and sturdy boots, adjusted her hard hat, the rhythmic clang of metal echoing around the construction site.  Below, the chaotic ballet of cranes, diggers, and hurrying workers painted a vibrant picture against the muted backdrop of the city.  This was her design, her vision slowly solidifying into a reality of glass and steel, a testament to her ambition and an audacious attempt to reshape the London skyline.  But something was off. A nagging feeling, a subtle discord in the symphony of construction, tugged at the edge of her awareness.  It wasn't structural; the blueprints were flawless, she'd poured over them a thousand times. This was something else, something intangible and unsettling that clung to the air like the lingering scent of diesel.
