The biting November wind whipped around Amelia "Amy" Hawthorne's ankles as she stood on the porch of the dilapidated Victorian, the peeling paint fluttering like confetti.  Rain threatened, fat grey clouds gathering over the skeletal branches of the ancient oak that dominated the overgrown front yard.  The house, known locally as Blackwood Manor, perched precariously on a bluff overlooking the churning grey waters of the Strait of Juan de Fuca, just outside the quaint, tourist-trap town of Port Blossom, Washington.  Amy pulled her crimson scarf tighter around her neck, the vibrant color a stark contrast to the gloomy scene before her.  This listing was a gamble, a fixer-upper's dream… or nightmare, depending on your perspective. But the commission… the commission could change everything.  She just had to convince someone else to see the potential beneath the decay.
