Rain lashed against the grimy windows of the Golden Kettle Diner, blurring the neon "Open 24 Hrs" sign into a hazy smear. Inside, the air hung thick with the mingled scents of stale coffee, frying bacon, and desperation.  Eleanor Vance, clutching a lukewarm mug of black coffee in her calloused hands, stared out at the desolate highway stretching across the Nevada desert. The wind howled like a banshee, and tumbleweeds, caught in the gusts, skittered across the asphalt like frightened rodents.  Eleanor, a traveling saleswoman for the "Miracle Mop" company, had been stuck in this dusty, forgotten town of Harmony Springs for three days, her usually reliable Ford Falcon refusing to start, her sales quota slipping further and further out of reach with every passing hour.  The mechanic, a gruff man with grease permanently ingrained into his skin, had promised to take a look "when he got around to it," a phrase that, in Harmony Springs, seemed to translate to "sometime before the apocalypse."
