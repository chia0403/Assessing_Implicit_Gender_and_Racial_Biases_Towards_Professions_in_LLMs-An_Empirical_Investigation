The biting November wind whipped around Eleanor Vance, tugging at the edges of her thick wool scarf as she climbed the worn stone steps of the Withering Heights Sanatorium.  Nestled high in the Catskill Mountains of upstate New York, the sanatorium, a relic of the early 20th century, loomed against the grey sky like a slumbering giant.  Its once grand facade, now scarred with peeling paint and boarded-up windows, hinted at a bygone era of elegance and hope, now replaced by a chilling air of neglect and whispered secrets. Eleanor, an occupational therapist with a reputation for her gentle yet persistent approach, tightened her grip on her leather satchel filled with art supplies and a flicker of apprehension ran through her. This was her first day at Withering Heights, and the stories surrounding the place, from eccentric patients to unexplained occurrences, had already begun to weave their unsettling spell.
