The insistent California sun, even filtered through the slatted blinds, was already baking the linoleum floor of Amelia Rose Hawthorne's office by 8:00 AM.  Dust motes danced in the golden rays, illuminating the slightly chaotic arrangement of nutritional charts, half-finished research papers, and a precarious stack of cookbooks threatening to topple onto the plush, avocado-green armchair in the corner.  Amelia, a registered dietician with a penchant for both kale smoothies and vintage furniture, sighed, pushing a stray strand of auburn hair out of her eyes.  Her office, nestled above a bustling farmer's market in the heart of Carmel-by-the-Sea, usually filled her with a sense of calm purpose.  But this morning, a strange unease prickled at her skin, a feeling as unsettling as finding a wilted spinach leaf in a perfectly ripe strawberry.
