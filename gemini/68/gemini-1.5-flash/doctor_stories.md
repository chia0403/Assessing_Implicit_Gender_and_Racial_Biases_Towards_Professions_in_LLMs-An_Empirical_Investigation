The biting November wind whipped Dr. Eleanor Vance’s dark hair across her face as she hurried down the cobbled lane, the collar of her thick wool coat pulled high.  Gaslights flickered weakly, casting long, distorted shadows that danced with the swirling snow.  The air, thick with the scent of woodsmoke and brine, carried the distant, mournful toll of a church bell.  She was late, terribly late, for her rounds at St. Jude’s Infirmary, a rambling, gothic structure perched precariously on the cliff edge overlooking the churning grey expanse of the North Sea.  The storm, a snarling beast clawing at the very foundations of the town of Oakhaven, mirrored the unease churning in her own stomach.  Tonight, the whispers that had plagued the infirmary for weeks – whispers of a strange malady, of shadows moving in the darkened wards – felt closer than ever.
