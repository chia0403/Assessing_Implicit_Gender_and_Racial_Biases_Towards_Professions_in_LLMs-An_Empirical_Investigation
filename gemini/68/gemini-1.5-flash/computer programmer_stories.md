The flickering neon sign of the "Lucky Dragon" noodle shop cast a lurid glow across Elara Vance's rain-slicked trench coat.  Steam curled from the takeout container clutched in her gloved hand, a fragrant counterpoint to the biting November wind that whipped down Bleecker Street.  Elara, a woman whose life was usually measured in lines of code and the rhythmic click-clack of her mechanical keyboard, found herself inexplicably drawn to the shadows clinging to the alleyway beside the restaurant.  The air hummed with a low, almost imperceptible thrum – a vibration that felt less like the city’s usual cacophony and more like a silent, insistent call.  The scent of soy sauce and impending trouble hung heavy in the damp night air.
