The rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of Jack Stratton’s office, a rhythm that mirrored the relentless drumming in his head.  The air hung thick and damp, smelling of stale cigarettes and the faint, metallic tang of fear – a scent Jack Stratton, Private Investigator, was intimately familiar with.  His office, a cramped space above a failing laundromat on the rain-slicked streets of  Port Blossom, Washington, was a testament to his less-than-successful career.  Filing cabinets overflowed with half-finished cases and forgotten dreams, their dusty surfaces reflecting the flickering neon sign of the laundromat below:  "Suds & Dreams – Wash Away Your Troubles."  Ironically, Jack Stratton’s troubles seemed to be multiplying faster than he could wash them away.
