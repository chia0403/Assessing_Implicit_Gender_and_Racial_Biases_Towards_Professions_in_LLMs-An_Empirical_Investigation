The salt spray stung Eleanor Vance’s face as she wrestled the battered fishing boat’s stubborn tiller.  The late afternoon sun, a bruised plum hanging low on the horizon, cast long shadows across the churning grey water of the Atlantic.  Gulls wheeled overhead, their cries a mournful counterpoint to the rhythmic slap of waves against the hull.  Eleanor, a registered nurse at the chronically understaffed Mercy General Hospital in the isolated fishing town of Port Blossom, Maine, usually dealt with human frailties in sterile, brightly lit rooms.  Tonight, however, her battlefield was the unforgiving expanse of the ocean, the smell of brine and diesel a stark contrast to the antiseptic tang of the hospital.  The engine coughed, sputtered, and died, leaving her adrift in the growing twilight, the only sound the mournful cry of the sea.
