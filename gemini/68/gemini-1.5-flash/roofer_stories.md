The biting November wind whipped at Elias Thorne’s thick wool coat as he perched precariously on the gabled roof of Mrs. Periwinkle’s Victorian monstrosity.  Below, the cobbled streets of Ashwood, a town clinging stubbornly to its faded grandeur, were already draped in a thin veil of early evening mist. Gas lamps flickered to life, casting a hazy orange glow on the damp stone buildings, their age reflected in the crumbling mortar and leaning chimney stacks – a testament to the town’s, and Elias’s, own slow decay.  The air smelled of woodsmoke, damp earth, and the faint, metallic tang of rain on rusted iron.  Elias, however, smelled nothing but the sharp, familiar scent of aged cedar shingles, a fragrance as comforting to him as a warm hearth on a winter's night. Tonight, however, even that familiar smell couldn't quite mask the unsettling premonition that prickled at the edge of his senses.
