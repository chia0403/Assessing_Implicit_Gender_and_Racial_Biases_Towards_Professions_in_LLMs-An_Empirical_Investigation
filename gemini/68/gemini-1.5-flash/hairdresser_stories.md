The scent of lavender and burnt sugar hung heavy in the air, a peculiar perfume only Aurelia “Relly” Davenport could concoct.  Relly, owner and sole operator of "Shear Madness," a tiny salon wedged between a laundromat smelling faintly of chlorine and a bakery whose sugared delights constantly threatened to invade her workspace, sighed.  Rain lashed against the single, dusty window of her shop, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her. The chipped paint of the floral wallpaper peeled like sunburnt skin, a testament to the building's age and Relly's rather laissez-faire approach to maintenance.  Outside, the neon sign flickering erratically – a faded, pink "Shear Madness" – promised more glamour than the interior delivered. But for Relly, this small, chaotic space was sanctuary, a haven from the complexities of a life that, lately, felt as tangled as a badly-knotted wig.
