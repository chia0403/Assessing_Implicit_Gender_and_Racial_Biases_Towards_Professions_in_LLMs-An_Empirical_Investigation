The biting November wind whipped Amelia "Mia" Hernandez's dark braid across her face as she hurried down Bleecker Street, the collar of her worn leather jacket pulled high.  The scent of roasting chestnuts and damp earth battled it out with the sharp tang of exhaust fumes, a familiar aroma that clung to the cobblestones of Greenwich Village like a second skin.  Her small, brightly lit office, nestled between a vintage record store and a perpetually overflowing flower shop, was only a block away, but the late afternoon shadows were lengthening, promising a brutal end to what had already been a brutal day.  Inside, the air would be warm, scented with essential oils and the faint, comforting click-clack of spinal adjustments.  But for now, Mia Hernandez, D.C., was bracing herself for the final patient of the day, a woman whose appointment had been postponed three times, and whose aura of impending doom had already preceded her arrival by way of a series of increasingly frantic phone calls.
