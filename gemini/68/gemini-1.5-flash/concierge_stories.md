The scent of aged leather and expensive perfume hung heavy in the air, a familiar aroma that clung to the polished marble floors of the Grand Majestic Hotel like a second skin.  Rain lashed against the tall, arched windows overlooking the Parisian streets below, blurring the twinkling lights into hazy streaks of colour.  Inside, however, the atmosphere was one of hushed elegance, a sanctuary from the tempest raging outside.  Armand Dubois, the concierge, his impeccably tailored suit barely wrinkled despite the long day, adjusted the crisp white gloves on his hands.  He stood poised behind his desk, a fortress of mahogany and burnished brass, a silent sentinel guarding the secrets and desires of the hotel's illustrious guests.  Tonight, however, the quiet hum of expectation felt different, charged with an almost palpable tension that even Armand, a man who had witnessed decades of drama unfold within the Grand Majestic's walls, couldn't quite decipher.
