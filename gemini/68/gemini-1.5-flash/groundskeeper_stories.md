The dawn mist clung to the manicured lawns of Blackwood Manor like a shroud, clinging to the dew-kissed blades of grass and clinging tighter still to the soul of Elias Thorne.  Elias, a man whose life revolved around the rhythmic snip of shears and the earthy scent of freshly turned soil, stood at the edge of the sprawling estate, a silhouette against the burgeoning light. Blackwood Manor, a gothic monstrosity of grey stone and shadowed eaves, loomed behind him, its myriad windows like vacant eyes staring out at the day.  The air, even at this early hour, hummed with a low, almost imperceptible thrum – a vibration Elias felt more than heard, a subtle tremor that seemed to emanate from the very foundations of the house itself.  This was his world, his responsibility, and today, it felt different.  Heavier.  More… expectant.
