The biting November wind whipped Dr. Eleanor Vance’s auburn hair across her face as she hurried down Bleecker Street.  Rain, sleeting with the stubbornness of a New York winter refusing to yield, plastered her charcoal coat to her back.  The air, thick with the smells of roasting chestnuts and damp brick, did little to soothe the knot of unease tightening in her stomach.  She’d been late to her last two appointments, a fact she usually wouldn't tolerate, but tonight felt different.  A feeling, heavy and indefinable, clung to her like the city's pervasive chill, a premonition that tonight's patient wouldn't be a case study, but a turning point.  Her destination: a crumbling brownstone on the edge of Greenwich Village, its darkened windows staring out like vacant eyes, promising secrets within its shadowed walls.
