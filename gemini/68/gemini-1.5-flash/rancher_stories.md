The wind, a rasping baritone, whispered secrets across the parched earth of the Broken Spur Ranch. Dust devils, miniature tornadoes of ochre and brown, danced across the cracked plains, their swirling paths mirroring the turmoil in Elias “Eli” Blackwood’s gut.  The sun, a malevolent eye in the brassy sky, beat down relentlessly on the already wilting grasses, turning the landscape into a shimmering, almost hallucinatory expanse of heat haze.  Eli, his weathered face etched with the map of a lifetime spent battling the unforgiving elements, squinted against the glare.  The ranch, his family’s legacy for generations, was dying.  Not from drought alone, though that was a significant part of it, but from something far more insidious, a creeping shadow that threatened to consume everything he held dear.  The air hung heavy with the scent of dry earth and something else… something metallic, faintly sweet, and undeniably wrong.
