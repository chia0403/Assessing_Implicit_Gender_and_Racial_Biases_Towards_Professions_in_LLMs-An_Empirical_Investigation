The biting November wind whipped Amelia Hernandez’s trench coat around her legs as she hurried down Bleecker Street, the scent of roasting chestnuts and damp asphalt battling for dominance in the chilly air.  Rain, a fine, persistent drizzle, plastered her dark hair to her forehead, obscuring the already tired lines etched beneath her hazel eyes.  Amelia, a seasoned investigative reporter for the *New York Chronicle*, was late.  Again. Not that her editor, the notoriously grumpy Mr. Fitzwilliam, would care.  He'd probably already begun composing her obituary – professionally, of course – in his mind.  The flickering gaslights of Greenwich Village cast long, distorted shadows, making the familiar cobblestone streets seem both alluring and menacing, a fitting backdrop for the story that had pulled her from a much-needed, albeit short-lived, respite at home.
