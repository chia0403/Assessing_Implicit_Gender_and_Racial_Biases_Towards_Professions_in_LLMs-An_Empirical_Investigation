The chipped paint of Dr. Eleanor Vance's office door seemed to mirror the cracks in the facade of her latest patient.  Rain lashed against the tall, gothic windows of the Victorian building, a relentless drumming that matched the frantic rhythm of Eleanor’s own pulse.  She sat at her mahogany desk, the scent of old books and slightly stale coffee clinging to the air, a familiar comfort in the face of the emotional storms that regularly blew through her practice on Bleecker Street in Greenwich Village.  The flickering gaslight above cast long, dancing shadows, highlighting the dust motes swirling in the otherwise still room – a fitting backdrop for the unraveling of another human life.  Outside, the city roared, a symphony of sirens and distant shouts, a stark contrast to the hushed, expectant silence within.  Tonight, it was young Arthur Blackwood’s turn to face the music, or rather, to try to untangle it.
