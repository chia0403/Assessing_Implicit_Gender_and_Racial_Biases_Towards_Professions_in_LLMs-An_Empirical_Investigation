The flickering neon sign of Benny’s Burgers cast a lurid orange glow across the rain-slicked street, reflecting in the puddles that mirrored the city’s weary sigh.  A biting November wind whipped Amelia Katherine Hayes’s long, dark hair across her face as she hurried along Bleecker Street, the scent of exhaust fumes and damp concrete clinging to her coat.  Amelia, a paralegal with a caffeine addiction and a penchant for vintage detective novels, tucked a stray strand behind her ear, her gaze fixed on the imposing brick edifice of the Blackwood & Sterling law firm – a gothic monolith that seemed to suck the warmth from the very air around it.  Inside, a case awaited her, a case that smelled less of justice and more of something… rotten.
