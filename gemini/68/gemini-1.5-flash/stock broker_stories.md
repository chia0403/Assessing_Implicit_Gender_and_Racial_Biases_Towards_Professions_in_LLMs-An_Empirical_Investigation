The rain hammered against the panoramic windows of Avery Sterling’s 57th-floor office, blurring the already hazy cityscape of Chicago into a watercolor of greys and muted blues.  Avery, a name whispered with a mixture of awe and envy in the hushed hallways of Sterling & Pierce Securities, stared out at the storm, a half-empty glass of lukewarm scotch sweating in his hand.  The mahogany desk, usually a battlefield of meticulously organized spreadsheets and ringing phones, was deserted save for a single, crumpled napkin bearing the scrawled calculation of a devastating loss – a loss that felt far more personal than any portfolio downturn ever had.  The city lights flickered like dying embers below, mirroring the precarious flame of his own career.
