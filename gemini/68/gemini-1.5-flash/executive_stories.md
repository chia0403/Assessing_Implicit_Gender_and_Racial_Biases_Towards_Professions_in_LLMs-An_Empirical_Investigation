The mahogany desk, polished to a mirror sheen, reflected Amelia Katherine Hayes's sharp, tired gaze.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of her 72nd-floor office, blurring the cityscape of Hong Kong into a hazy watercolour.  The city, usually a thrumming symphony of neon and commerce, felt muted tonight, mirroring the dull ache behind Amelia’s eyes.  The scent of jasmine, usually a comforting presence from the nearby park, was overpowered by the sharp tang of ozone and the faint, metallic scent of blood – a scent Amelia knew intimately, a phantom reminder of a deal gone sour three years ago, a deal that still haunted her sleepless nights.  Tonight, however, the blood wasn't metaphorical.  A single, crimson droplet, impossibly perfect, sat glistening on her pristine, ivory business card, resting precisely in the centre of her meticulously organised desk.
