The stale air of the probation office hung heavy with the scent of cheap coffee and simmering resentment.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of afternoon sunlight slicing through the grimy window of Amelia Jean Holloway's cubicle.  Amelia, or "AJ" as her perpetually stressed colleagues called her, sighed, the sound swallowed by the low hum of the fluorescent lights.  Her desk, a battlefield of overflowing files, half-eaten granola bars, and the faint lingering aroma of yesterday's spilled coffee, reflected the chaos of her life.  Outside, the relentless Chicago wind howled a mournful tune, mirroring the bleakness that often settled over her heart.  This wasn't exactly the glamorous career she'd envisioned after graduating with a degree in sociology, but the city's ever-growing list of parolees needed someone, and she was it.  Today, however, felt particularly grim.  She had a meeting with Mr. Silas Blackwood, a man whose record was longer than her arm and whose charm was as thin as the paper she was currently reviewing.
