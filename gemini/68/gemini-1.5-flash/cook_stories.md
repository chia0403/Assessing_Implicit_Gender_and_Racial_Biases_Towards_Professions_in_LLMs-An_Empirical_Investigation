The salt spray stung Elara Thistlewick's face as she hauled the last crate of iridescent sea beans aboard the *Salty Siren*.  The late afternoon sun, a bruised plum sinking below the churning grey horizon of the Whispering Sea, cast long shadows across the deck, painting the already weathered wood in hues of amber and violet.  The air, thick with the smell of brine and something faintly floral – perhaps the lingering scent of the day's catch of moonpetal lilies – clung to her like a damp shawl. Elara, a cook renowned throughout the archipelago for her inventive seafood dishes and even more inventive curses, wiped her brow with the back of a flour-dusted hand, her gaze fixed on the distant, jagged peaks of the Isle of Aethelred, their silhouettes sharp against the fiery sky.  Tonight, she was cooking for royalty. And if the sea beans didn't arrive fresh, the king would have her head.  Or, at the very least, a thoroughly unpleasant tongue-lashing from his notoriously sharp-tongued queen.
