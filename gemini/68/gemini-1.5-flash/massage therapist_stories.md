The scent of sandalwood and chamomile hung heavy in the air, a comforting balm against the chill October wind rattling the windowpanes of Serenity Spa.  Rain lashed against the glass, a steady, rhythmic drumming that mirrored the soft kneading of Elias Thorne's fingers on his client's tense shoulders.  Elias, a man whose own hands were as capable of soothing the deepest anxieties as they were of expertly manipulating muscle knots, sighed inwardly.  He loved his job, the quiet sanctuary of his massage room a world away from the frenetic energy of the city that throbbed just beyond the frosted glass doors.  But tonight, the usual calming rhythm of his work felt strangely disrupted, a discordant note in the otherwise harmonious symphony of oils and whispers.  The client, a woman named Ms. Albright, was unusually still, her breaths shallow and uneven, a tension radiating from her that went far beyond the usual physical stiffness.  Tonight, Elias sensed something more was at play than just a bad day at the office.
