The November wind howled a mournful dirge around the craggy peaks of the Scottish Highlands, rattling the rain-lashed windows of Dr. Alistair Finch's dental practice.  Nestled in the tiny, perpetually damp village of Glencoe, the surgery was less a gleaming modern clinic and more a converted stone cottage, its walls thick with the centuries and its atmosphere thick with the scent of antiseptic and peat smoke.  Alistair, a man whose life seemed as stubbornly rooted to the glen as the ancient heather clinging to its slopes, sat hunched over his worn oak desk, the rhythmic tick-tock of a grandfather clock the only sound competing with the wind's lament.  He was reviewing patient files, the flickering gaslight casting long shadows that danced with the restless flames. The storm outside mirrored the turmoil brewing within him.
