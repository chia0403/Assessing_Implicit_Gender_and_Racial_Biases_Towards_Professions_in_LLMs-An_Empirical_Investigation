The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s hands, a meager comfort against the November chill that seeped through the drafty windows of Blackwood & Sons Accounting.  Rain lashed against the grimy panes, mirroring the tempest brewing in Amelia’s gut.  The air hung thick with the scent of stale coffee and desperation, a familiar perfume in this cavernous, dimly lit office building on the edge of the city's forgotten quarter.  Dust motes, illuminated by the sickly yellow glow of the overhead fluorescent lights, danced in the sluggish currents of air, each one a tiny, silent witness to the countless secrets whispered within these walls.  Amelia, a creature of meticulous routine, found even her usual sanctuary – the meticulously organized chaos of her desk – failing to offer solace this morning.  The looming shadow of Mr. Blackwood's infamous temper, combined with the cryptic, handwritten note she’d found pinned to her typewriter, threatened to unravel her carefully constructed composure.
