The fluorescent lights of Blackwood & Finch hummed a monotonous tune, a counterpoint to the rhythmic tap-tap-tapping of Amelia Katherine Hayes’s fingers on her keyboard.  Amelia, a woman whose life was meticulously organized down to the comma, found herself staring blankly at a spreadsheet titled “Q3 Projections – Branch 7.” The numbers, usually a source of comforting order, swam before her eyes, blurring into a chaotic jumble.  It wasn't the complexity of the figures that troubled her; it was the suffocating smell of stale coffee and desperation that permeated the air of Blackwood & Finch's cramped, windowless accounting office.  Outside, a relentless November rain lashed against the grimy panes, mirroring the storm brewing inside her.  This wasn't just another quarterly report; this report held the fate of Branch 7, and possibly, her own.
