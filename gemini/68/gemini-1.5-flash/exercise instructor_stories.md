The humid air hung heavy, thick with the scent of sweat and eucalyptus.  It clung to Amelia “Amy” Hernandez like a second skin as she surveyed her class, a kaleidoscope of glistening foreheads and strained smiles.  Fluorescent lights buzzed overhead in the mirrored walls of “Body & Soul Fitness,” casting a sterile glow on the rows of gleaming exercise bikes.  Amy adjusted the microphone clipped to her neon pink workout top, its bright color a stark contrast to the muted grey of the early morning. Outside, the Los Angeles dawn was just beginning to break, painting the sky in hues of bruised purple and hesitant gold, a stark contrast to the controlled chaos unfolding within the studio. Today’s class was “Inferno Burn,” and Amy, with her fiery red hair pulled back in a tight bun and a steely glint in her eye, was ready to ignite it.
