The humid air hung heavy, thick with the scent of frangipani and impending rain.  Cicadas buzzed a relentless, rhythmic chorus from the emerald canopy overhead, a soundtrack to the oppressive stillness that clung to the ancient Mayan ruins of Ek Balam.  Sweat beaded on the forehead of  Isadora "Izzy" Hernandez, her bright turquoise shirt clinging damply to her back.  She adjusted the brim of her wide-brimmed straw hat, a small smile playing on her lips despite the discomfort.  Another group of wide-eyed tourists, a motley crew of sunburnt retirees and wide-eyed teenagers, shuffled behind her, their cameras already clicking, eager to capture the mystique of this forgotten city.  Izzy cleared her throat, the sound swallowed by the vastness of the jungle surrounding them.  "Bienvenidos, everyone," she began, her voice a warm, confident contralto that cut through the humid hush, "to the lost city of Ek Balam..."
