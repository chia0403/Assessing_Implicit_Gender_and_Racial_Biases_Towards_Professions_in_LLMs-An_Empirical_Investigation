The fluorescent lights of the Zenith Corporation hummed, a relentless, almost imperceptible thrum that vibrated through Amelia Stone's weary bones.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of afternoon sunlight slicing through the grimy window of Reception, a space more akin to a waiting room in a dentist's office than the high-tech headquarters it purported to be.  Empty coffee cups, overflowing with the remnants of lukewarm instant, littered the chipped Formica surface.  Amelia, a young woman with perpetually tired eyes and a smile that struggled to reach her lips, sighed.  Another Tuesday.  Another barrage of cryptic requests and exasperated executives.  She tapped a pen against a stack of overdue invoices, the rhythmic click a counterpoint to the ceaseless clatter of the antiquated phone system.  Her nametag, crisp and stark against her faded uniform, read: Amelia Stone, Reception.
