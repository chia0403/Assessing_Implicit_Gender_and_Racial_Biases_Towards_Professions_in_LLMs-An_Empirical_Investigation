The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Blackwood's hands, the faint scent of Earl Grey clinging to the air like a forgotten memory.  Sunlight, fractured by the stained-glass window of the "Writer's Roost," painted the room in shifting hues of emerald and amethyst.  Dust motes danced in the beams, almost mocking the relentless quiet that settled over the small, cluttered room.  Amelia Blackwood, author of *Whispers in the Willow Creek Woods* and the soon-to-be-forgotten *The Serpent's Secret*, stared out at the grey, rain-slicked cobblestone street below.  The rhythmic drumming of the approaching afternoon rain against the roof tiles seemed to echo the relentless beat of deadlines in her mind.  Outside, the town of Oakhaven, nestled in the heart of the Whispering Woods, was already starting to slumber under the muted glow of a late autumn afternoon.
