The midday sun beat down on the cracked asphalt of Elm Street, shimmering off the peeling paint of the Victorian houses lining the narrow thoroughfare.  Dust motes danced in the air, thick and heavy with the scent of honeysuckle and something faintly metallic, like old pennies.  This was Amelia Blackwood’s territory, a patch of faded grandeur she’d been meticulously carving through for the past five years.  Amelia, a real estate agent with a sharp eye for potential and an uncanny ability to sniff out a hidden charm,  had spent countless hours studying the intricacies of this historic neighbourhood, deciphering the stories whispered by the weathered brick and the creaking floorboards.  Today, however, a different kind of story awaited her – one that smelled less of old pennies and more of unspoken secrets.
