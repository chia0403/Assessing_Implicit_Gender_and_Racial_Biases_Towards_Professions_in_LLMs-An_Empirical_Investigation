The chipped paint of the "Imagination Station" kindergarten classroom, a faded rainbow of blues and pinks, seemed to hum with a low, expectant energy.  Sunlight, fractured through the grimy windowpanes, illuminated dust motes dancing in the air.  A faint scent of cinnamon and crayon shavings hung heavy, a familiar aroma that always preceded the chaotic symphony of a new morning.  Miss Amelia Blackwood, a woman whose perpetual smile often masked a wellspring of quiet strength, surveyed her charges.  Today, a particularly boisterous group of four-year-olds clamored for attention, each vying for the opportunity to share their latest imaginative creations.
