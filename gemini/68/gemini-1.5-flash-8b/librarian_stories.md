The flickering fluorescent lights of the Dewey Decimal System hummed a monotonous tune, a soundtrack to Amelia Beatrice Nightingale's nightly ritual.  Dust motes danced in the pale glow, illuminating the towering shelves that housed the whispered secrets of countless forgotten ages.  Amelia, her worn cardigan pulled tight against the chill emanating from the ancient stone walls of the Blackwood Library, traced the spine of a leather-bound volume, its title a faded inscription:  "Chronicles of the Whispering Woods."  The library, a sprawling gothic edifice nestled deep within the Blackwood Forest, felt ancient and alive, a labyrinth of shadows and forgotten stories.  Rain lashed against the leaded windows, a relentless percussion that echoed the frantic beating of Amelia's heart.  Tonight, something was different.  A distinct tremor ran through the air, a subtle shift in the library's usual hushed solemnity. A single, brass bell, usually silent, chimed once, then twice, a preternatural sound that pierced the storm.
