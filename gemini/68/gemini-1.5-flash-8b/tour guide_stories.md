The biting wind whipped through the cobbled streets of Prague, stinging Amelia Petrova's cheeks as she adjusted her oversized scarf.  The scent of roasted chestnuts, a familiar comfort in the crisp autumn air, mingled with the pungent aroma of woodsmoke from the nearby chimney pots.  Rain, the relentless companion of Prague's November, slicked the cobblestones, reflecting the flickering gaslights in a shimmering, ethereal dance.  Amelia, a seasoned tour guide with a lifetime's worth of stories tucked inside her meticulously organised mind, surveyed the throngs of tourists – a kaleidoscope of nationalities and aspirations – gathered before the majestic St. Vitus Cathedral.  Today, she was leading her 'Hidden Histories of Prague' tour, a specialty that delved beyond the usual tourist traps into the less-trodden alleys and shadowy corners of the city.
