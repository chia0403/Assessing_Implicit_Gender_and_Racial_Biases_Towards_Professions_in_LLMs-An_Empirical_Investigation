Rain lashed against the Georgian windows of Amelia Blackwood's antique shop, a relentless drumming that echoed the frantic rhythm of her thoughts.  The shop, "Antiques & Accents," nestled on a quiet side street in the historic district of Oakhaven, was a labyrinth of forgotten treasures.  Dust motes danced in the shafts of light filtering through the stained-glass panels, illuminating rows of polished mahogany, chipped porcelain dolls, and tarnished silver tea sets.  Amelia, a whirlwind of dark curls and sharp, intelligent eyes, paced, a crumpled blueprint clutched in her hand.  Tonight was the deadline for the Vanderlyn commission, and the prospect of failing loomed as large as the looming storm.  This wasn't just a house; it was a family heirloom, a place steeped in generations of Vanderlyn history, and Amelia, despite her unwavering dedication to her craft, felt the weight of expectations crushing her.
