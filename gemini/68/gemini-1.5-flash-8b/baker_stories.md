The aroma of freshly baked sourdough, sharp and yeasty, clung to the air in the cobbled alleyway.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of sunlight piercing the gloom, illuminating the chipped paint of the bakery door and the worn, leather-bound ledger tucked under the counter.  This was "The Crusty Crumb," a small, unassuming bakery tucked away on the backstreets of Veridia, a city famed for its towering spires and opulent palaces, but also known for its hidden, shadowed corners.  Inside, amidst the comforting murmur of the oven and the gentle clinking of metal pans, stood Elara Seraphina, her brow furrowed in concentration.  A wisp of dark brown hair, escaping from her neatly bound bun, fell across her forehead as she meticulously weighed a handful of flour.  Elara, a baker with a heart as golden as the crusts of her bread, was determined to craft the perfect loaf, a task that often felt as weighty as the stone buildings that surrounded her.
