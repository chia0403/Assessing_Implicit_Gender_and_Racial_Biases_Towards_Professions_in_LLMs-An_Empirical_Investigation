Professor Amelia Petrova, renowned astrophysicist and head of the Theoretical Cosmology department at the University of Oxford, stared out the panoramic window of her office.  Crimson hues painted the sky above the rolling Oxfordshire hills, mirroring the turmoil churning within her.  Below, the ancient stone buildings of the university, steeped in centuries of scholarly debate, seemed to hold their breath, oblivious to the storm brewing inside her.  Her office, nestled high on the third floor of the Radcliffe Science Library, boasted a breathtaking view of the Cotswolds, but tonight, the landscape felt alien, menacing.  A single, flickering candle on her desk cast long shadows on the overflowing stacks of research papers, a testament to the sleepless nights spent deciphering cosmic anomalies.
