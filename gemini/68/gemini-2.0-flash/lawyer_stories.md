The fluorescent lights of the Carver County Courthouse hummed a monotonous drone, a soundtrack to the nervous tapping of feet and whispered anxieties that permeated the stale air. Outside, the Minnesota sky was a slate grey, threatening snow, but inside Courtroom 3, it was all controlled temperature and suppressed emotion. Abigail Eleanor Blackwood straightened the lapel of her navy blue suit, the crisp wool a shield against the gnawing pre-trial jitters. She could feel the weight of a thousand expectant eyes, even though most of them were imagined. The air smelled faintly of old paper and nervous sweat, a scent Abigail had come to associate with both victory and defeat. Today, she hoped, would be the former.
