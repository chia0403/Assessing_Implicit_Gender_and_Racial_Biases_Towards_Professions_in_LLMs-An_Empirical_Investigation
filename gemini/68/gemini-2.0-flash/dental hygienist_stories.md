The metallic tang of fluoride hung thick in the air, a smell Beatrice Ainsworth had long ago grown immune to, though she still caught the occasional grimace on new patients' faces. Sunlight streamed through the generous windows of Bright Smiles Dental, illuminating dust motes dancing in the air like tiny, frantic ballerinas. Outside, the vibrant hum of downtown Maplewood buzzed - a symphony of car horns, chattering pedestrians, and the rhythmic clanging of the trolley car that traced its route along Main Street. Beatrice, however, remained cocooned in the sterile calm of operatory number three, her focus narrowed to the intricate landscape of Mr. Henderson's molars.
