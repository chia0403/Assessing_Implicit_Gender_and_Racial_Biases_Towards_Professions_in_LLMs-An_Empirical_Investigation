The rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of The Rusty Mug, a rhythm that usually lulled Elara Reyes into a meditative state while she polished glasses.  Tonight, however, the incessant drumming only mirrored the anxiety churning in her stomach.  Nestled between a pawn shop and a perpetually closed fortune teller's parlor on the desolate edge of Port Blossom, Oregon, The Rusty Mug was rarely busy, especially not on a Tuesday night in November.  Yet, a palpable tension hung in the air, thicker than the cigarette smoke that usually clung to the worn velvet booths.  Elara wiped down the sticky mahogany bar top, her gaze fixed on the flickering neon sign in the window, its faded mermaid promising "Drinks as Deep as the Ocean."  The ocean, Elara thought grimly, probably held fewer secrets than this town tonight.
