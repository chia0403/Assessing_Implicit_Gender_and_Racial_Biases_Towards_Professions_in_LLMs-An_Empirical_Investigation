The biting November wind whipped down Cobblestone Alley, rattling the frosted panes of Elias Thorne’s shop window. Inside, nestled amongst bolts of velvet, tweed, and the faint scent of mothballs, Elias himself hunched over his workbench.  His spectacles perched precariously on his nose as he meticulously stitched the final golden braid onto a scarlet hussar’s jacket.  Elias Thorne, Tailor and Draper, was the most sought-after craftsman in the quaint, fog-wreathed port town of Hollow Creek, known for both his impeccable needlework and his uncanny ability to sense the stories woven into the very fabric of his customers' lives.  The shop, a two-story building squeezed between a bakery and a silversmith, was a haven of warmth and quiet industry, a stark contrast to the restless sea that crashed against the harbour walls just a stone's throw away.  Tonight, however, a different kind of restlessness permeated the air, a prickling anticipation that had nothing to do with the approaching winter storms.
