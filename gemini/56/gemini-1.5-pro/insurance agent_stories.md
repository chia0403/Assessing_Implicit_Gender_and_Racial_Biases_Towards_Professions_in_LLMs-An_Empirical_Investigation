Rain lashed against the stained-glass window depicting Saint Jude, patron saint of lost causes, and Amelia Finch, CLU, ChFC, found the irony almost comical.  Her office, nestled above Mrs. Higgins' perpetually fragrant bakery in the heart of quaint, crumbling Port Blossom, Maine, usually offered a charming view of the harbor.  Today, however, all Amelia could see was a grey, churning tempest mirroring the storm brewing within her own career.  The scent of cinnamon rolls, normally a comfort, now seemed to mock her dwindling client list and the stack of overdue bills threatening to bury her beneath a mountain of sugared despair. Amelia tapped a chipped, coral-colored nail against the worn oak of her desk.  This was not how she'd envisioned her life at thirty-two.
