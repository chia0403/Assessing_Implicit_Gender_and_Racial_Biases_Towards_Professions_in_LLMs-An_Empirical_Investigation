The biting November wind whipped around Dr. Eleanor Vance, stinging her cheeks as she hurried across the cobbled marketplace of Port Blossom, Maine.  Her breath plumed out in frosty puffs, momentarily obscuring the view of the colorful, albeit sparsely stocked, stalls.  The scent of brine and pine needles mingled in the air, a familiar fragrance that usually brought her comfort. Today, however, a knot of anxiety tightened in her stomach. Eleanor clutched her satchel closer, its leather worn smooth from years of use. Inside were the preliminary results of a nutritional study she'd been conducting within the small, isolated fishing community, and those results were far more alarming than she could have ever anticipated.  Port Blossom, a town seemingly untouched by the modern world's dietary woes, was harboring a secret, and Eleanor feared it was one that could unravel the very fabric of their existence.
