Rain lashed against the arched windows of Amelia Grace Holloway's office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her.  Grey London, usually a vibrant tapestry of bustling activity, was muted and monochrome through the streaming glass.  Her cramped office, perched high in a Victorian building overlooking Smithfield Market, felt less like a place of business and more like a damp, forgotten cupboard.  The scent of stale coffee and impending deadlines hung heavy in the air. Amelia, a chartered accountant known for her meticulous nature and unwavering adherence to the rules, tapped a perfectly manicured nail against the mahogany desk.  The figures on the spreadsheet before her refused to balance, and for the first time in her meticulously planned career, Amelia Grace Holloway felt a distinct sense of unease, a premonition that something was deeply, irrevocably, wrong.
