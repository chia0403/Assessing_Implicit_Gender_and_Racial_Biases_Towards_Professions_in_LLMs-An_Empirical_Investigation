The mahogany clock in Judge Amelia Iris Thorne's chambers chimed a sonorous five, the sound echoing in the cavernous room lined with legal tomes bound in cracked leather. Outside, the late November rain lashed against the tall windows of the San Francisco Hall of Justice, blurring the already fading daylight into a pearly grey.  A damp chill permeated the air despite the crackling fire in the hearth, a chill that mirrored the knot of unease tightening in Judge Thorne's stomach.  This wasn't the usual pre-holiday jitters; this was something colder, something that resonated with the unsettling case file sitting open on her desk, a case involving the disappearance of a renowned cryptographer and a cipher that seemed to whisper of something far more sinister than a simple missing person.
