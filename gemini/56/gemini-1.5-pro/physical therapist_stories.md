Rain lashed against the windows of the Tidewater Physical Therapy Clinic, blurring the already fading November light. Inside, the air hung thick with the scent of antiseptic and the faint, metallic tang of sweat. Eliza Jane Holloway, PT, DPT, adjusted the heat lamp over the treatment table, its orange glow painting the sterile white room in a more welcoming hue.  She glanced at the clock – 5:47 pm.  Almost time to go home to a steaming mug of tea and the comforting purr of her ginger cat, Marmalade.  Just one more patient.  And this one, Eliza knew, was going to be a challenge. Not because of the complexity of the injury, a relatively straightforward rotator cuff tear, but because of the patient himself – notoriously grumpy former baseball pitcher, Silas "The Hammer" Harding.
