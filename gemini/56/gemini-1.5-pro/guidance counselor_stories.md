The fluorescent lights of McKinley High hummed a monotonous B flat, a soundtrack to Amelia Hayes’s Tuesday morning.  Rain lashed against the window of her office, blurring the already dreary view of the student parking lot. Amelia, whose full name was Amelia Rose Hayes, tapped a chipped mug of lukewarm tea against her teeth, the ceramic a small comfort against the rising tide of paperwork threatening to engulf her desk.  McKinley High, nestled in the perpetually overcast town of Port Blossom, Oregon, wasn’t exactly a hotbed of Ivy League aspirations.  Most students dreamed of logging careers, fishing boats, or escaping the claustrophobic embrace of the coastal rainforest altogether.  Amelia, a transplant from sunny Southern California, understood the sentiment.  She just hoped she could help them find a way out, or perhaps, even more importantly, a reason to stay.
