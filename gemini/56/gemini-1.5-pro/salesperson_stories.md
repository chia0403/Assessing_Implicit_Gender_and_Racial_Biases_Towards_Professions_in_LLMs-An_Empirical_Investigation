The stale air hung heavy with the scent of cheap coffee and desperation in the breakroom of "Precipice Solutions," a company whose name ironically reflected its current financial state.  Rain lashed against the grimy windows, mirroring the bleakness in Amelia “Amy” Hawthorne’s hazel eyes.  Outside, the Chicago skyline, normally a beacon of ambition and success, was shrouded in a November fog. Amy, bundled in a slightly-too-large, charcoal grey blazer, picked at the remnants of a wilted salad, her mind a whirlwind of quota anxieties and unpaid bills.  This was her last chance. The Harrison account, notoriously difficult to land, was her final shot at redemption, at proving her worth in a cutthroat world of enterprise software sales.  If she failed, she wouldn't just lose her job; she’d lose everything.
