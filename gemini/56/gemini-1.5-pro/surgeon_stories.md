The biting wind whipped off the East River, stinging Dr. Elias Thorne’s cheeks as he hurried across the Roosevelt Island Tramway platform.  The city lights glittered below, a vast, indifferent constellation beneath the bruised purple of the pre-dawn sky. Elias tugged his threadbare scarf tighter, the damp wool scratching at his neck. He hadn’t had time to change out of his scrubs after a grueling twelve-hour shift at Manhattan General, and the faint metallic tang of antiseptic clung to him like a second skin.  The tram car lurched into motion, its steel cables groaning a mournful song against the wind, carrying him towards the stark, concrete silhouette of the Coler-Goldwater Specialty Hospital and Rehabilitation Center. This wasn’t where a top-tier cardiothoracic surgeon like him was supposed to end up, banished to a remote island hospital known more for its long-term care patients than its cutting-edge procedures. But Elias wasn't on Roosevelt Island to operate. He was there to visit his brother.
