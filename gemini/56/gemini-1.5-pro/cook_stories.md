The clatter of pots and pans was Evelyn Rose Mayweather's morning prayer.  The pre-dawn light, filtering through the grimy windows of The Salty Spoon Diner, painted stripes across the worn linoleum floor as she wrestled a stubborn lid off a stockpot. The aroma of simmering chicken broth mingled with the sharp tang of salt air wafting in from the nearby Monterey Bay.  The Salty Spoon, perched on a rocky outcrop overlooking the churning Pacific, was Evelyn's domain, her kingdom of chipped mugs, sticky menus, and the comforting sizzle of the grill.  It wasn't much, but it was hers, and within its grease-splattered walls, she reigned supreme. This morning, however, a different kind of tension hung in the air, thicker than the fog rolling in off the ocean.
