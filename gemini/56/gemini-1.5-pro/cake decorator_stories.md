The scent of burnt sugar hung heavy in the air, a stark contrast to the usual vanilla-infused sweetness that permeated Buttercup Bakery.  Eliza June Hemlock, her auburn braid unraveling with the frantic energy she poured into her work, stared with horrified fascination at the towering five-tier wedding cake slowly tilting towards disaster.  Outside, the late afternoon sun cast long shadows across the cobblestone streets of Willow Creek, a quaint town nestled in the Oregon mountains where life usually moved at the pace of melting honey. But inside the bakery, panic was rising like yeast in warm dough. The wedding was in three hours.  The bride, notorious for her exacting standards and penchant for fainting spells, was due to arrive any minute for a final cake inspection. And Eliza June, normally the picture of calm, creative competence, felt a tremor of genuine fear run down her spine as the leaning tower of buttercream and sugar flowers seemed to defy gravity itself.
