The biting November wind whipped around Amelia "Amy" Reyes, stinging her cheeks and tugging at the brim of her baseball cap.  Frozen breath plumed from her mouth as she surveyed the desolate baseball diamond of St. Jude's Orphanage in the small, forgotten town of Havenwood, Oregon.  Cracked wooden bleachers, scarred by decades of weather and neglect, groaned under the weight of the wind.  The infield, a patchwork of hardened dirt and stubborn weeds, was a far cry from the manicured greens she was used to.  But then again, everything in Havenwood seemed a far cry from Amy's previous life.  She gripped the worn leather of her coaching mitt tighter, a familiar comfort in this unfamiliar place.  The only sound besides the wind was the distant rhythmic clang of the orphanage's bell tower, a melancholic reminder of the lives contained within its aging brick walls, lives she was now tasked with, in some small way, changing.
