The biting wind whipped at Amelia "Amy" Reyes's thick ponytail as she wrestled with the rusted access panel on the side of the dilapidated lighthouse.  Salt spray stung her face, and the rhythmic crash of waves against the rocky shore of Deception Island echoed in her ears.  The sky, a bruise-coloured canvas streaked with angry grey, promised more of the same. Amy, a fourth-generation electrician known for her steady hand and even steadier nerves, had seen worse weather, but rarely in such an isolated location.  Deception Island, a horseshoe-shaped volcanic caldera in the South Shetland Islands, was home to a small research station and little else.  The lighthouse, abandoned for decades, was her current project:  bringing light back to this desolate, beautiful, and potentially dangerous corner of the world.
