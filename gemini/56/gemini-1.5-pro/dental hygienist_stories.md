The sterile scent of antiseptic hung heavy in the air, a familiar perfume to Amelia Hayes.  Fluorescent lights buzzed overhead, casting a stark white glow on the pristine turquoise walls of Gentle Dental, a squat, single-story building nestled between a bustling laundromat and a perpetually empty storefront in the sleepy town of Havenwood, Oregon.  Rain lashed against the large picture window overlooking Main Street, mirroring the constant drizzle that seemed to permeate Havenwood's very essence. Amelia, a woman whose bright smile contrasted sharply with the grey skies outside, adjusted her lilac scrubs and glanced at the clock.  2:57 pm.  Mr. Henderson, notorious for his elaborate tales of weekend fishing trips, was three minutes early.  Again.
