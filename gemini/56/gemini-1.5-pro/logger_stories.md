The biting wind whipped off the Skeena River, carrying the scent of pine resin and damp earth.  A thin layer of frost crunched under Elijah Jedediah Blackwood's heavy boots as he surveyed the stand of towering Douglas firs.  Dawn painted the eastern sky a bruised purple, barely illuminating the mist clinging to the valley floor.  Here, nestled deep in the coastal mountains of British Columbia, amidst the rugged wilderness that had been his family's domain for three generations, Elijah felt a sense of belonging that no city could ever offer.  This morning, however, the familiar comfort was laced with a disquiet he couldn't quite place.  The ancient forest seemed to hold its breath, waiting.
