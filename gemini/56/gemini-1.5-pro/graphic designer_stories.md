The biting November wind whipped around Eleanor Vance, plastering stray strands of auburn hair across her chapped lips.  She tugged her scarlet scarf tighter, the wool scratching against her chin as she hurried down Bleecker Street, the scent of roasted chestnuts and woodsmoke battling for dominance in the crisp air.  Eleanor’s gloved hands clutched a portfolio case, its worn leather a testament to countless pitches and late-night revisions.  Her destination: the monolithic glass and steel edifice of Vanderlyn Industries, a stark contrast to the charming brownstones that lined the rest of the block.  Eleanor, a freelance graphic designer whose reputation rested on her ability to blend the classic with the contemporary, had landed a meeting with Vanderlyn – a company known for its cutting-edge tech and equally cutting-edge coldness.  Today, her future, and possibly her rent, hinged on persuading them that their new line of smartwatches needed more than just sleek lines; they needed a soul.
