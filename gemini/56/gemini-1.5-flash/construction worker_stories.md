The biting November wind whipped around Elias Thorne’s ankles, stinging the exposed skin above his worn work boots.  He hunched deeper into his threadbare jacket, the collar scratching against his chapped cheeks.  The skeletal frame of the new skyscraper clawed at the bruised, grey sky, a monument to ambition rising above the gritty, industrial landscape of Grimsborough.  Below, the city sprawled – a tapestry woven from steel and concrete, punctuated by the occasional splash of bleak, forgotten green.  Elias, his breath misting in the frigid air, adjusted the hard hat perched precariously on his head.  Another day, another dizzying height, another chance to stare down at the relentless pulse of a city that both fascinated and repelled him. The clang of steel on steel echoed from the upper levels, a metallic symphony of progress that felt, to Elias, more like a relentless, unforgiving march.
