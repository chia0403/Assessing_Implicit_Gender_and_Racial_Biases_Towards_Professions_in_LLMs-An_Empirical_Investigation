The humid Louisiana air hung heavy, thick with the scent of honeysuckle and impending rain.  Cicadas buzzed a relentless, almost frantic soundtrack to the oppressive stillness that draped over the sleepy town of Bayou Lafourche.  Agnes Evangeline Dubois, RN, wiped a bead of sweat from her forehead, the dampness clinging to her crisp white uniform like a second skin.  The screen door of the small, shotgun-style house slammed shut behind her with a familiar metallic *thwack*, the sound swallowed instantly by the pervasive humidity.  Agnes sighed, the weight of the day already settling on her shoulders before the first patient even arrived.  Inside, the air conditioning offered a meager reprieve from the stifling heat, but the scent of brewing chicory coffee couldn't quite mask the faint, persistent aroma of antiseptic that clung to everything in her tiny, meticulously organized home.
