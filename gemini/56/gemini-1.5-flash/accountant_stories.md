The flickering gaslight cast long, skeletal shadows across the ledger pages, highlighting the meticulous script of Amelia Beatrice Hawthorne, Chartered Accountant.  Rain lashed against the tall, gothic windows of her office, a relentless drumming that mirrored the frantic beat of her own heart.  It was a November night in 1888, the kind that clung to the cobbled streets of Whitechapel like a shroud, thick with fog and the scent of coal smoke and something else… something acrid and unsettling that prickled at the back of Amelia’s neck.  Her normally pristine office, usually a sanctuary of order and precise calculations, felt tonight like a cage, the heavy oak desk a barrier against the unseen horrors lurking just beyond the frosted glass.  The quill slipped in her hand, a single ink blot marring the otherwise perfect column of figures, a tiny blemish mirroring the growing unease in her soul.
