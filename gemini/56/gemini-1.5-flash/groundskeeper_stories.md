The biting November wind whipped Elias Thorne’s already-chapped lips raw as he wrestled with a stubborn oak branch, its leaves a brittle, skeletal tapestry against the bruised purple sky.  Elias, groundskeeper for Blackwood Manor, had seen a thousand autumns paint the sprawling estate in shades of fiery red and gold, but this year felt different.  A chill deeper than the frost clinging to the wrought-iron gates seemed to permeate the very stone of the mansion itself, a silent, foreboding presence that even the crackling bonfire in the distance couldn't fully dispel. Blackwood Manor, with its gothic turrets piercing the twilight and its shadowed gardens whispering secrets to the wind, was a place of ancient beauty and unsettling stillness – and Elias, its solitary guardian, was growing increasingly uneasy.
