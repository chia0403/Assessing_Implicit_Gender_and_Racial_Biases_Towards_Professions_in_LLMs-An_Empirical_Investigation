The static crackled, a persistent, irritating hiss that mirrored the turmoil in Elias Thorne's gut.  Elias adjusted the dial on the ancient Zenith console, its bakelite casing warm beneath his fingertips, the smell of ozone faintly clinging to the air.  Rain lashed against the single grimy window of his workshop, a dilapidated shed perched precariously on the edge of the salt marshes bordering the desolate coast of North Norfolk.  Inside, the air hung thick with the scent of soldering flux, damp earth, and the lingering ghost of a thousand broken dreams –  dreams he’d tried, with varying degrees of success, to repair through the magic contained within the crackling tubes and whispering wires before him. Tonight, however, felt different.  The static wasn't just atmospheric interference; it felt… malevolent.
