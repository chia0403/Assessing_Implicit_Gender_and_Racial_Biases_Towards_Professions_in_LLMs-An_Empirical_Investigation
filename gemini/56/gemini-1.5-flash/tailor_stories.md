The scent of lavender and beeswax hung heavy in the air, a comforting balm against the biting November wind howling outside.  Inside, Elias Thorne, master tailor of Thorne & Son, meticulously pressed a sleeve, the iron a rhythmic counterpoint to the steady tick-tock of the grandfather clock in the corner.  His shop, a haven of aged mahogany and rich fabrics, was tucked away on a cobbled side street in the heart of Prague’s Old Town, its windows fogged with the breath of the city.  Outside, the Charles Bridge groaned under the weight of tourists and the relentless wind, a far cry from the quiet precision within Elias’s workshop, where the only sounds were the whisper of silk and the snip of his shears.
