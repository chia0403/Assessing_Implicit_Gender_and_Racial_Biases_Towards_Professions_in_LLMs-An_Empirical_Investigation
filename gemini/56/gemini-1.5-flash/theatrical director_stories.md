The biting November wind whipped Amelia Katherine Blackwood’s hair across her face as she wrestled with the recalcitrant stage door.  Rust groaned in protest with every heave, a sound strangely fitting for the dilapidated grandeur of the Blackwood Theatre.  Built by her great-grandfather, a flamboyant impresario with a penchant for melodrama and even more of a penchant for debt, the theatre was a crumbling monument to a bygone era, its once-glorious façade now scarred with peeling paint and boarded-up windows.  Inside, the scent of dust motes and forgotten dreams hung heavy in the air, a stark contrast to the vibrant chaos Amelia envisioned for her latest production:  a radical reimagining of Shakespeare’s *Macbeth*, set in a dystopian future ruled by corporate greed and algorithmic control.
