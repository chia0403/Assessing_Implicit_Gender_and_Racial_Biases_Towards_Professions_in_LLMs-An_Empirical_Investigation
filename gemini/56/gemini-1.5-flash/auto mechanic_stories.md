The wrench slipped in Amelia Hernandez's grease-stained hand, clattering onto the concrete floor of Hernandez Auto Repair with a metallic clang.  Dust motes, illuminated by the single bare bulb hanging precariously above her workbench, danced in the air, a swirling miniature galaxy reflecting the chaotic jumble of tools and parts surrounding her.  The air itself hung thick with the scent of burnt oil, gasoline, and something faintly floral – Amelia’s attempt at masking the pervasive aroma of engine grease with a cheap air freshener shaped like a cartoon cactus.  Outside, the relentless Arizona sun beat down on the corrugated iron roof of the small, ramshackle garage, baking the desert landscape to a shimmering haze.  Amelia sighed, the sound swallowed by the incessant drone of cicadas.  Another day, another engine refusing to cooperate. This time, it was a '67 Mustang, a beauty whose once-proud chrome was now dulled by years of neglect, mirroring the owner's apparent apathy.
