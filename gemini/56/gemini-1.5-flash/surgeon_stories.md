Inside, Dr. Elias Thorne, a man whose hands, though capable of the most delicate microsurgery, were currently clenched into fists of frustrated anxiety, stared out at the city lights twinkling like fallen stars below.  The operating theatre's antiseptic scent, usually a comfort, felt suffocating tonight, a stark contrast to the tempest raging outside, mirroring the turmoil within him. Elias Thorne, renowned for his steady nerve and unwavering precision, felt the familiar calm slipping, replaced by a gnawing dread that clawed at his composure.
