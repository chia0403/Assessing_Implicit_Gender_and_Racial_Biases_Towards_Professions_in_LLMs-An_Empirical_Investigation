The salt spray stung Elias Thorne's face as he wrestled the stubborn oak plank onto the makeshift scaffolding.  The wind, a relentless, howling banshee, threatened to rip the whole rickety structure – and him along with it – into the churning grey expanse of the North Sea.  Below, the fishing village of Port Blossom huddled against the cliffs like a cluster of weathered shells, its brightly painted houses barely visible through the driving rain.  Elias, his weathered hands raw and bleeding, ignored the biting cold and the gnawing fear in his stomach. He had a deadline, a promise to keep, and the storm, like the relentless grief that had shadowed him for the past year, would not deter him.
