The aroma of lavender and sandalwood hung heavy in the air, a deliberate counterpoint to the relentless drumming of rain against the leaded-glass windows of Serenity Spa.  Water cascaded down the slate roof in sheets, a soothing, almost hypnotic rhythm that Amelia “Mia” Hernandez found strangely comforting, a familiar counterpoint to the tense shoulders and knotted muscles she'd been untangling all day.  Mia, a petite woman with hands that possessed the surprising strength of a blacksmith and the delicate touch of a hummingbird, leaned back in her worn, yet comfortable, massage chair, the weight of the day pressing down on her as heavily as the storm outside.  Her small, cluttered office, tucked away in a quiet corner of the spa, was a sanctuary, a place where the worries of the outside world were supposed to melt away – but tonight, even her sanctuary felt besieged.
