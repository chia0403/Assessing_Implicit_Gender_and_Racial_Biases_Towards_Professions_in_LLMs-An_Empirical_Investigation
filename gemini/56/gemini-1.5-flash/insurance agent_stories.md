The humid Louisiana air hung heavy, thick with the scent of honeysuckle and impending rain.  Cicadas buzzed a relentless, hypnotic rhythm against the backdrop of a sky the color of bruised plums.  Agnes Periwinkle Button, a woman whose name suggested a delicate floral arrangement but whose spirit mirrored the resilient cypress trees lining the bayou, sat perched on the splintered porch of Madame Evangeline’s curio shop.  Her sensible beige shoes were dusted with the red clay of the unpaved road, and a stray strand of her perfectly coiffed auburn hair had escaped its bun, clinging damply to her cheek.  Agnes, a claims adjuster for Bayou Belle Insurance, wasn't here for curios; she was here for a claim – a rather unusual one, involving a swamp monster and a misplaced policy.
