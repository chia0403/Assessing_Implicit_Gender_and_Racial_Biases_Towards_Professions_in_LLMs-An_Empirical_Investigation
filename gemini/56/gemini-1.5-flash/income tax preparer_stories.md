The fluorescent lights of "Numbers & Needles" hummed a monotonous tune, a counterpoint to the frantic tapping of Amelia Hernandez's fingers on her keyboard.  The air, thick with the scent of stale coffee and desperation, hung heavy in the small, cluttered office.  Tax season was in full swing, a chaotic ballet of overflowing file cabinets, overflowing inboxes, and overflowing anxieties – all reflected in the harried expressions of the clients shuffling in and out.  Amelia, a woman whose sharp eyes belied her perpetually tired demeanor, sighed, pushing a stray strand of auburn hair behind her ear.  Outside, the late March wind howled a mournful symphony, a fitting soundtrack to the financial woes plaguing her clientele.  Tonight, however, a particularly thorny case awaited her – one that promised to test her skills and patience to their absolute limits.
