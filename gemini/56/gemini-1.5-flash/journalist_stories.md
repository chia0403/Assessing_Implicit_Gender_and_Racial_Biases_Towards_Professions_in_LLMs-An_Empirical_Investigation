The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Amy” Katherine Holloway’s hair across her face, stinging her cheeks a fiery red.  Rain, a relentless, icy drizzle, plastered her trench coat to her thin frame as she hunched deeper into the shadowed alleyway.  The air hung thick with the smell of rotting fish and something else… something acrid and metallic, clinging to the damp bricks like a second skin.  This wasn't the picturesque cobbled street showcased in the tourist brochures; this was Grimsborough’s underbelly, a labyrinth of forgotten warehouses and clandestine dealings tucked away behind the glittering façade of the city’s historic waterfront.  Amy, a journalist for the Grimsborough Gazette known for her tenacious reporting and even more tenacious coffee consumption, was about to uncover something far more sinister than a rat infestation.
