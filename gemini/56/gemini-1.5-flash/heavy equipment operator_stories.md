The diesel engine coughed, a throaty rumble that vibrated through Amelia “Amy” Stone’s worn work boots. Dawn bled a bruised purple across the eastern sky, painting the colossal shadow of the unfinished skyscraper in hues of violet and grey. Amy, perched high in the cab of her Caterpillar 336D, felt the familiar tremor of the machine settling into its work.  The air hung thick and heavy with the scent of exhaust fumes and damp concrete, a smell as ingrained in her as the calluses on her hands.  Below, the city of Denver still slumbered, oblivious to the monumental task unfolding above – a task that rested, quite literally, on Amy's shoulders.  This wasn't just another construction site; this was the Zenith Tower, the city's audacious bid for a new skyline, and Amy was its silent, powerful architect.
