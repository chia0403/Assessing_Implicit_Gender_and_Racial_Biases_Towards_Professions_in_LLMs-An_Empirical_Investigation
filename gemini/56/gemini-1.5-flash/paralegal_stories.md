The fluorescent lights of Miller & Zois hummed a monotonous tune, a counterpoint to the frantic clatter of keyboards echoing from the open-plan office.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of the 37th floor, blurring the already indistinct cityscape of Chicago into a grey, impressionistic smear.  Amelia “Mia” Hernandez, paralegal extraordinaire (at least, that's what she told herself during particularly grueling discovery phases), stared blankly at the mountain of paperwork threatening to engulf her desk.  Each file, a testament to someone else's misfortune, was a miniature Everest of legal jargon and meticulously documented heartache.  Tonight, however, the usual weary resignation was replaced by a knot of unease, a premonition that hung heavier than the humid Chicago air clinging to the glass.  The case she'd been working on for the past six months – the acrimonious divorce of a prominent tech entrepreneur – had taken a disturbing turn.  And Mia, with her keen eye for detail and uncanny ability to sniff out inconsistencies, had just unearthed something that smelled profoundly rotten.
