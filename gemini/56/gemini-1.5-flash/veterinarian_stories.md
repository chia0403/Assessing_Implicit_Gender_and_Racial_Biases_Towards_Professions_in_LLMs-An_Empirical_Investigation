The biting wind whipped Dr. Eleanor Vance’s auburn braid across her face as she wrestled the recalcitrant gate open.  Salt spray stung her eyes, a familiar discomfort on this windswept stretch of the Cornish coast.  Below her, the Atlantic churned a restless grey, crashing against the jagged rocks that guarded the tiny fishing village of Portwenn.  The air, thick with the smell of brine and seaweed, carried an undercurrent of something else – something faintly… feral.  Eleanor shivered, pulling her thick wool coat tighter. It wasn't just the cold; a prickling unease settled in her stomach, a feeling she couldn't quite place, but one that mirrored the unsettling silence emanating from the dilapidated cottage perched precariously on the cliff edge, its single, smoke-less chimney pointing accusingly at the stormy sky.  This was no ordinary house call.
