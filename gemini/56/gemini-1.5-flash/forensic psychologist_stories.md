The chipped porcelain doll lay nestled amongst the withered lilies, its painted eyes staring blankly up at the flickering candlelight.  Dr. Eleanor Vance, a forensic psychologist with eyes the colour of a stormy sea and a weariness etched deep into her fine lines, knelt beside the makeshift altar. The air in the abandoned Victorian mansion hung thick with the cloying sweetness of decaying flowers and something else… something acrid, metallic, that clung to the back of her throat like the taste of fear.  Dust motes danced in the single beam of her flashlight, illuminating the ornate, crumbling plasterwork and the shadows that seemed to writhe in the corners of the vast, echoing room. This wasn't her first crime scene, not by a long shot, but the unsettling stillness of Blackwood Manor, perched precariously on the windswept cliffs overlooking the turbulent Atlantic, felt different. This felt… personal.
