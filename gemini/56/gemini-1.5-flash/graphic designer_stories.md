The chipped mug warmed Amelia Katherine Holloway’s hands, the lukewarm chamomile doing little to soothe the gnawing anxiety in her stomach.  Rain lashed against the panoramic window of her studio apartment, blurring the already hazy cityscape of Birmingham, Alabama.  Each fat drop seemed to mirror the individual anxieties swirling within her – a looming deadline for the city's annual arts festival poster, a client threatening to pull the plug on a long-term contract, and the persistent, nagging feeling that she was creatively stagnating, her once vibrant palette dulled to a depressing monochrome.  The studio itself, a converted attic space with exposed brick and slanted ceilings, usually felt inspiring, a haven of creative chaos. Tonight, however, it felt like a cage. The scent of turpentine and old paint, normally comforting, now hung heavy, a cloying reminder of her unfinished work.
