The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Hernandez’s hands, the lukewarm tea doing little to soothe the icy dread that had settled in her stomach.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of the 47th floor, blurring the cityscape into a hazy watercolour of grey and black.  Amelia, executive secretary to the notoriously volatile CEO of Blackwood Industries, Silas Blackwood himself, stared out at the tempest, a mirror reflecting the storm brewing inside her.  The air in her impeccably organized office, usually fragrant with lemon verbena air freshener, now hung heavy with the scent of impending doom – a potent blend of fear and cheap instant coffee.  Outside, the city roared; inside, the silence was deafening, broken only by the rhythmic tap-tap-tap of her perfectly manicured fingernails against the polished mahogany desk.  Tonight, something felt different.  Tonight, the usual pre-deadline tension felt…lethal.
