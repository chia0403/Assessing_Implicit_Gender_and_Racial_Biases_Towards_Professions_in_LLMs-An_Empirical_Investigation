The stale air of the showroom hung heavy with the scent of new car leather and desperation.  Dust motes, illuminated by the harsh fluorescent lights, danced in the sickly yellow glow, mirroring the nervous flutter in Amelia Grace Hawthorne's stomach.  Amelia, a name whispered with a touch of faded glamour by her grandmother, felt anything but glamorous tonight.  Her perfectly coiffed auburn bun threatened to unravel, mirroring the unraveling of her sales quota for the month. Outside, the relentless Chicago wind howled a mournful dirge, a fitting soundtrack to the bleak reality of her impending performance review.  The gleaming chrome of the luxury sedans lining the showroom floor seemed to mock her with their unattainable shine.  Amelia, armed with a practiced smile that felt increasingly brittle, adjusted her crisp blazer and prepared to face another potential customer, another potential rejection.  Tonight, however, felt different.  The weight of expectation pressed down on her like a lead apron, and the usual thrill of the sale had been replaced by a chilling dread.
