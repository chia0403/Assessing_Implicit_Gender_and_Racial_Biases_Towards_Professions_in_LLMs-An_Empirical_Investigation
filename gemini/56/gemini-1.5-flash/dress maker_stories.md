The scent of lavender and old linen hung heavy in the air, a comforting blanket against the relentless July heat beating down on the cobbled streets of  Saint-Germain-des-Prés.  Sunlight, fractured by the stained-glass windows of the ancient church across the rue, dappled the workshop floor of  "Au Fil d'Or,"  a tiny, almost hidden boutique tucked away on a side street.  Dust motes, illuminated like tiny stars, danced in the shafts of light.  At her antique sewing machine, humming a quiet melody only she could hear, sat  Geneviève Antoinette Dubois, her nimble fingers coaxing life into a swathe of silk the colour of a summer sunset.  The hum of the machine and the rustle of silk were the only sounds that punctuated the lazy afternoon, a perfect counterpoint to the simmering chaos Geneviève was about to unleash upon her latest creation – a wedding dress for a notoriously demanding bride.
