The polished mahogany desk gleamed under the harsh fluorescent lights, reflecting Amelia Katherine Stone’s weary face back at her.  Amelia, CEO of Stone & Sons Holdings, rubbed her temples, the faint scent of sandalwood from her expensive hand cream doing little to soothe the throbbing ache. The only other sound, besides the drumming rain, was the low hum of the city itself, a constant, underlying thrum that mirrored the anxiety gnawing at her insides. Tonight, the weight of a billion-dollar empire felt less like a privilege and more like a suffocating weight pressing down on her chest.  The deal with PetroNova was on the precipice, and everything – her career, her family’s legacy, even her carefully curated sanity – hung precariously in the balance.
