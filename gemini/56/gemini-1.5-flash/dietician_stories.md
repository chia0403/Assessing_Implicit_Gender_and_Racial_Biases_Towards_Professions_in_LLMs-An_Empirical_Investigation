The chipped porcelain mug warmed Dr. Eleanor Vance's hands, the lukewarm chamomile tea doing little to soothe the icy knot of anxiety tightening in her stomach.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of her office, blurring the already hazy cityscape of Seattle sprawling below.  Grey, brooding skies mirrored the state of her current project:  the "Eat Your Feelings" weight-loss program for the notoriously difficult-to-please executives of Rainier Tech.  Eleanor, a registered dietician with a PhD in Nutritional Psychology, prided herself on her ability to tailor diets to individual needs, but this…this felt like trying to herd cats through a hurricane.  The faint scent of saltwater and pine – a constant reminder of the Olympic Mountains she so rarely found time to visit – did little to alleviate the pressure.  Her meticulously organized desk, usually a haven of calm, was currently buried under a mountain of spreadsheets, client profiles, and half-eaten energy bars, a testament to the relentless demands of her latest challenge.
