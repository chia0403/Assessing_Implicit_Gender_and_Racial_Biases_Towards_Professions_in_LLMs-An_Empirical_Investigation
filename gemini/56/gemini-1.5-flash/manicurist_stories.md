The scent of acetone and hibiscus hung heavy in the air, a cloying sweetness that clung to the plush velvet chairs and the chipped porcelain sink of Serenity Nails.  Rain lashed against the windowpanes of the small, brightly lit salon, blurring the neon glow of the "OPEN" sign into a hazy, ethereal rectangle.  Inside, amidst the cacophony of whirring electric files and the murmur of hushed conversations, sat Aurelia "Rella" Montenegro, her nimble fingers working a magic of their own on a client's already perfectly manicured nails.  The client, a woman whose diamonds glittered even under the subdued salon lighting, hummed a tuneless melody, oblivious to the storm raging outside, just as oblivious, perhaps, to the storm brewing within Rella herself.
