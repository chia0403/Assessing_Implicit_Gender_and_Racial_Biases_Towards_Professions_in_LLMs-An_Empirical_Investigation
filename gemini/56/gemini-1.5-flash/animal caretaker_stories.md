The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Amy” Hawthorne’s hair across her face as she wrestled a recalcitrant bale of hay.  The scent of damp earth and decaying leaves clung to the air, mingling with the sharper, more pungent aroma of  goat.  Amy, head zookeeper at the seemingly idyllic, but perpetually underfunded, Willow Creek Wildlife Sanctuary, fought back a shiver.  Willow Creek wasn't idyllic in the postcard sense; it was a sprawling, slightly ramshackle collection of enclosures nestled into a valley carved by a lazy river,  its charm built on years of accumulated neglect and patched-up repairs.  The ramshackle charm, however, was what drew the creatures and the dedicated few who cared for them.  Tonight, that dedicated few was just Amy, and the weight of the entire sanctuary seemed to rest squarely on her frostbitten shoulders.
