The biting November wind whipped across Elias Thorne's weathered face, stinging his eyes and tugging at the worn brim of his hat.  He hunched deeper into his thick wool coat, the chill seeping into his bones despite the layers.  Around him, the farm of Thorne's End – a patchwork of browning fields and skeletal trees – lay silent under a bruised, twilight sky.  The last of the harvest had been gathered weeks ago, leaving behind only the scent of damp earth and the rustling whisper of dying leaves. A lone crow perched on the weather-beaten barn, its harsh caw echoing the desolate mood that had settled over the land, a mood mirrored in the heavy heart of Elias Thorne himself.  The year had been unkind, a cruel mistress who had withheld her bounty, leaving Thorne's End facing a winter of hardship unlike any he had ever known.
