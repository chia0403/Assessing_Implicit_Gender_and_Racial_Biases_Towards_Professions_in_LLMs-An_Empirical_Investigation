The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s hair across her face, stinging her cheeks with icy rain as she hunched deeper into her threadbare coat.  The skeletal branches of the ancient oaks clawing at the bruised twilight sky mirrored the turmoil churning in her gut.  She stood on the windswept headland overlooking the churning grey expanse of the North Sea, the unforgiving waves a constant, rhythmic roar against the jagged cliffs of Cornwall.  The air smelled of salt and brine, a stark contrast to the cloying sweetness of the lilies she clutched – a morbid offering for the latest victim of the storm, whose body had been discovered washed ashore just hours ago, its features obscured by the relentless assault of the ocean. Amelia, a seasoned reporter for the *Cornish Gazette*, knew this grim scene all too well. This wasn't her first storm-related death; it was, however, the strangest.
