The chipped paint of the "Welcome to Northwood High" sign flaked onto the already overflowing ashtray of Ms. Eleanor Vance's battered Ford Focus.  Rain, a relentless, grey curtain, plastered itself against the windshield, blurring the already indistinct outline of the school buildings huddled against the wind-whipped pines.  Northwood, nestled deep in the Oregon Cascades, wasn't known for its sunny disposition, and tonight, its temperament matched Eleanor's own.  The late October air bit with a chill that seeped into her bones, a fitting mirror to the icy knot of dread tightening in her stomach.  Another late night grading papers – essays on Shakespeare’s Hamlet, predictably full of clichés and questionable grammar –  was bad enough, but the unsettling phone call from the principal earlier had added a layer of frost to the already freezing evening.  It had been brief, cryptic, and utterly terrifying.  "Ms. Vance," Principal Harding had said, his voice tight with a barely contained panic, "we need to talk.  About Mr. Finch."  And with that, Eleanor's carefully constructed evening, and possibly her entire life, had begun to unravel.
