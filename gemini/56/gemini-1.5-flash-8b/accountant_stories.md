The fluorescent lights of the accounting firm hummed, a monotonous soundtrack to Amelia Peabody's daily grind.  Sheets of paper, each one a testament to someone else's financial woes, piled precariously on her desk, a miniature Everest of receipts, invoices, and tax forms.  The air in the cramped office, located in a nondescript building on the outskirts of the bustling city of Oakhaven, was thick with the scent of stale coffee and desperation.  Amelia, a meticulous accountant with sharp, intelligent eyes, focused on the line item for "Miscellaneous Expenses" on the latest corporate audit.  Each number, each tiny detail, was a puzzle piece, a jigsaw that, if she put it together correctly, would expose the truth.  But beneath the surface of spreadsheets and balance sheets, Amelia sensed something amiss. Something deeply unsettling.
