The wind, a relentless, whispering coyote, whipped across the parched plains of the Painted Desert, carrying with it the scent of sagebrush and the faint, acrid tang of smoke.  Dust devils danced across the cracked earth, swirling the meager remnants of a recent, futile rain into the already-dry gullies.  Elias Thorne, a man weathered and worn like the ancient mesas that dotted the horizon, squinted against the harsh glare. His weathered hands, calloused from years of wrestling with stubborn cattle and unforgiving land, tightened around the reins of his lone, wiry mustang, a creature as resilient as the man himself.  The year was 1887, and the drought, a relentless beast, had claimed yet another spring.  Elias Thorne, rancher of the Sunstone Ranch, was staring down the barrel of disaster.
