The fluorescent hum of the server farm filled Elias Thorne's lungs, a constant, low thrum that had become his soundtrack.  Rows upon rows of gleaming, heat-spewing metal cabinets lined the cavernous room, stretching as far as the eye could see, swallowing the ambient light and breathing out a silent, digital symphony.  Dust motes danced in the beams of the overhead lights, illuminated by the ethereal glow of countless monitors displaying a kaleidoscope of data streams. Elias, clad in a faded black hoodie and jeans that had seen better days, hunched over a terminal, his eyes glued to the screen.  Outside, the city of Neo-Veridia pulsed with life, oblivious to the silent, intricate ballet unfolding within the subterranean heart of the tech conglomerate, "Chronos."  It was 3 AM, and Elias was wrestling with a rogue algorithm, a phantom error that had haunted him for the last three sleepless nights.
