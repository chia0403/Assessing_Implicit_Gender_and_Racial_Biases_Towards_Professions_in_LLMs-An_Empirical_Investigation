The chipped enamel of the chipped paint on the chipped wall reflected the fluorescent hum of the salon, a harsh counterpoint to the soft murmur of conversation and the rhythmic clip-clop of manicured nails.  Sunlight, fractured and weak, barely pierced the grime-coated windows, casting long, distorted shadows across the faded floral wallpaper. This was "The Polished Petal," a haven of pastel hues and synthetic smiles, and it was here, amidst the scent of artificial lavender and the whisper of hushed secrets, that  Amelia Juniper, manicurist extraordinaire, began her day.  Her meticulously painted nails, a perfect, coral symphony of crimson and gold, contrasted sharply with the weary, slightly bruised color of her own eyes.
