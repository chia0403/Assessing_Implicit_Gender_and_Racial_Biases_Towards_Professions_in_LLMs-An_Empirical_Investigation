The scent of ozone and cheap hairspray hung heavy in the air, clinging to the chipped Formica countertops and the dusty, velvet-lined chairs of "Scissors & Smiles," a salon tucked away on a side street of the perpetually rain-soaked city of Alderbrook.  Rain hammered against the single, grimy window, mimicking the frantic rhythm of Amelia Nightingale's own heart.  Today was a particularly chaotic Saturday, and the already-strained atmosphere thickened with the nervous energy of a dozen or so clients, each vying for Amelia's attention.  A cascade of unruly brunette hair, a frantic sigh, and the insistent ringing of the bell above the door signaled the arrival of another.  Amelia, already juggling a restless teenager and a meticulously groomed businesswoman, braced herself.  She knew this particular Saturday was going to be a doozy.
