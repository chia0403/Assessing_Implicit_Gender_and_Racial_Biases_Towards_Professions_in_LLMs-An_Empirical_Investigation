The chipped paint of the "Little Explorers" preschool scraped against Amelia Rose Hawthorne's tired fingers.  Dust motes danced in the afternoon sun slanting through the grimy windowpanes, illuminating a room crammed with colourful, yet faded, play-doh creations and half-finished drawings of fantastical beasts.  Amelia, a child care worker of twenty-two summers, sighed, the sound lost in the chaotic symphony of toddlers' laughter and the rhythmic clatter of tiny, determined feet.  The aroma of burnt toast, inexplicably present despite the absence of a functioning oven, hung heavy in the air.  Outside, the bustling city of Veridia thrummed with a life that seemed miles away from the suffocatingly quiet chaos of room three.  Today, however, the usual mix felt different.  A palpable tension hung in the air, thicker than the afternoon humidity, like a storm gathering on the horizon.
