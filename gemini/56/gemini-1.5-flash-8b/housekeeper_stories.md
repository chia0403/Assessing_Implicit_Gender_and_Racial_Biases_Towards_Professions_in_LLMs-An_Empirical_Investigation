The chipped porcelain sink, perpetually stained with the faintest whisper of turmeric, reflected Elara Blackwood's weary face.  Dust motes, like tiny, dancing specters, swirled in the single shaft of afternoon sun slicing through the grime-coated windowpanes of the sprawling, gothic manor.  Rain lashed against the leaded glass, a relentless drumming that echoed the frantic rhythm in her chest.  Elara, housekeeper for Lord Ashworth and his formidable family at Blackwood Manor, knew this was not just another Tuesday.  This was the Tuesday she'd been dreading since the cryptic note arrived earlier that morning.


**Setting:** Blackwood Manor, a sprawling, imposing gothic edifice perched atop a windswept hill overlooking a brooding valley.  The manor, shrouded in a perpetual twilight despite the midday sun, was a testament to its aristocratic past.  The grand halls were cavernous and echoing, filled with antique furniture and portraits of stern-faced ancestors.  Rain lashed against the ancient leaded glass windows, adding a sense of foreboding to the already heavy atmosphere.  A persistent, musty smell hung in the air, a blend of aged wood, decaying flowers, and something subtly sinister.
