The scent of lavender and eucalyptus hung heavy in the air, a fragrant curtain separating the world outside from the tranquil sanctuary of "The Kneading Nook." Rain hammered against the panoramic window overlooking Puget Sound, blurring the already hazy view of Bainbridge Island into a watercolour wash of greys and greens. Inside, the soft glow of salt lamps cast a warm, flickering light on the meticulously arranged crystals and plush, earth-toned furniture.

Here, nestled amongst fluffy blankets and the gentle hum of a white noise machine mimicking ocean waves, stood **Amelia Grace Carmichael**, adjusting the volume on a Spotify playlist filled with ethereal chimes. Amelia, with her perpetually damp, dark brown hair pulled back in a messy bun and the faint, lingering aroma of massage oil clinging to her organic cotton shirt, checked her watch. It was almost ten o'clock, which meant Mr. Henderson, a retired software engineer with a chronic case of tech neck, would be arriving any minute. She hoped, as she always did, that she could ease the knots in his shoulders, both physical and emotional. Today felt heavier than usual, even for Seattle, and Amelia knew that sometimes, the best medicine wasn't just working out the muscle, but providing a safe space for the tension to simply melt away.
