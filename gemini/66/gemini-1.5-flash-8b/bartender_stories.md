The flickering neon sign of "The Rusty Mug" cast a sickly yellow glow on the rain-slicked cobblestones outside.  Inside, the air hung thick with the scent of stale beer, cheap cigarettes, and something vaguely floral, likely clinging to the worn floral wallpaper.  Barnaby "Barty" Butterfield, a man whose face held the map of a thousand late nights and whispered secrets, wiped down the sticky mahogany bar with a practiced hand.  Rain hammered against the grimy windows, a relentless rhythm that mirrored the steady drumbeat of Barty's thoughts. He was thirty-seven, and, despite the practiced calm etched into his features, a simmering anxiety threatened to boil over.  Tonight, something felt different.
