The flickering neon sign of the "Golden Spoon Diner" cast a sickly yellow glow on Amelia Hayes's rain-slicked trench coat.  Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof, a relentless percussion that mirrored the frantic rhythm of her thoughts.  Amelia, a staff reporter for the *Crimson Chronicle*, hunched over a chipped Formica table, the aroma of stale coffee and frying grease clinging to the air.  Outside, the deserted street stretched into an endless gray panorama, the only sound the relentless pitter-patter of the downpour.  This was the heart of Havenwood, a forgotten town nestled deep within the Appalachian foothills, a place where time seemed to have lost its grip on the present. Amelia, however, was desperately trying to hold onto it, clinging to every detail she could gather. The story she was chasing – the disappearance of the town’s legendary clockmaker, Silas Blackwood – felt as slippery as the rain-slicked streets themselves.
