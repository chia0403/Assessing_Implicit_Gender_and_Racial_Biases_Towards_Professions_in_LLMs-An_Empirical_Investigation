The biting wind, a relentless grey claw, clawed at the skeletal branches of the ancient oaks that lined the winding approach to Blackwood Manor.  Rain, a relentless, icy curtain, blurred the already dim light filtering through the gloom.  Dr. Eleanor Vance, her breath puffing white in the frigid air, tightened her coat against the onslaught.  The manor itself, looming dark and imposing against the bruised sky, seemed to absorb the meager light, whispering secrets only the wind could carry.  Blackwood Manor, a place whispered about in hushed tones in the nearby village of Ashbrook, a place where shadows stretched longer than the whispers of the villagers, was the reason for her visit.
