The biting wind whipped at Elias Thorne's weathered face, stinging his eyes and tugging at the frayed edges of his wool cap.  He hauled himself up the dizzying slope of the slate roof, the rhythmic *clack* of his boots a lonely counterpoint to the mournful howl of the wind.  Below, the sprawling city of Aethelgard stretched out, a tapestry of cobbled streets and flickering gaslights, the entire scene framed by a bruised purple sky that seemed to bleed into the jagged peaks of the surrounding mountains.  Elias, a roofer for the esteemed House Blackwood, felt the familiar ache in his shoulder, a constant companion from years spent traversing the dangerous, treacherous roofs of this city built upon the slopes of the Grey Peaks.  Today's job, however, was particularly fraught.  Not just a simple repair, but the complex and delicate restoration of the ancestral crest atop the Blackwood mansion, a task fraught with both technical challenge and a hefty price for any failure.  His heart, a lead weight in his chest, hammered against the ribs he felt beginning to strain under the relentless pressure of the wind and the weight of expectation.
