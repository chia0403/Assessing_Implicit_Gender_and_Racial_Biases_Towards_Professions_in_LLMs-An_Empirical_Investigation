The fluorescent hum of the pharmacy, a sterile symphony of white and pastel, thrummed around Amelia "Amy"  Finch.  Dust motes danced in the afternoon sun slicing through the grimy, multi-paned windows of "Apothecary's Corner," a small, but surprisingly bustling, establishment nestled on the edge of the sprawling, industrial outskirts of Veridian.  The scent of antiseptic mingled with the faint, floral perfume of lilies, a testament to the shop's somewhat incongruous clientele: the working-class families of the district, the occasional elderly gentleman seeking remedies for his chronic cough, and the ever-present, mysterious whispers that circulated through the community like a particularly potent cough syrup.  Amy, a pharmacist with a perpetually tired smile and hands that moved with the practiced grace of a seasoned chef, meticulously ground the ingredients for a cough mixture, her mind already drifting to the insistent, throbbing pain in her own temples.
