The flickering fluorescent lights of "Serenity Spa" cast a sickly yellow glow over the polished granite countertops.  A faint scent of lavender and something subtly metallic hung in the air, a testament to the oils and lotions meticulously arranged in glass apothecary jars.  Rain hammered against the storefront, a relentless percussion that drowned out the low hum of the city outside.  Inside, nestled amongst the soft, muted colours and the comforting aroma, sat Amelia Petrova, a massage therapist with hands as warm and gentle as the summer sun.  Her brow was furrowed in concentration as she meticulously applied a warming balm to the aching shoulder of a client.
