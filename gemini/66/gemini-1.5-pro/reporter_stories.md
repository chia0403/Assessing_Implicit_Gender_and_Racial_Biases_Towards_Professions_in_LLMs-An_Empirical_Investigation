Rain lashed against the grimy windows of The Sentinel's office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside Beatrice "Bea" Flores.  Fluorescent lights hummed overhead, casting a sickly yellow glow on the cluttered desks and overflowing ashtrays that littered the newsroom.  Bea, her dark hair pulled back in a messy bun, chewed the end of her pen, staring at the blinking cursor on her computer screen.  Ashton, Pennsylvania, a former coal town clinging to the frayed edges of its past glory, was a place where secrets festered like black mold in abandoned mineshafts.  And Bea, a reporter for the town's struggling newspaper, was about to uncover one that would shake Ashton to its very foundations.
