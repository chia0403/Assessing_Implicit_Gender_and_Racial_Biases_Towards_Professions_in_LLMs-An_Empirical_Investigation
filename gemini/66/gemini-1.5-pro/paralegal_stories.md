The fluorescent lights of the Weathersby & Stone legal offices hummed a monotonous tune, a soundtrack to Amelia Reyes’ Tuesday morning. Rain lashed against the panoramic windows overlooking downtown Seattle, blurring the iconic Space Needle into a ghostly spire.  Amelia, her dark hair pulled back in a neat bun, meticulously organized a stack of depositions, her brow furrowed in concentration.  A half-empty mug of lukewarm coffee sat beside her, a testament to the early start she’d needed to make a dent in the mountain of paperwork for the upcoming Peterson vs. Evergreen Industries case. The air crackled with the unspoken tension of a looming deadline, a feeling as familiar to Amelia as the worn leather of her favorite briefcase.  This was her life: a whirlwind of legal jargon, frantic filing, and the constant pursuit of elusive documents.  She wouldn’t have it any other way.
