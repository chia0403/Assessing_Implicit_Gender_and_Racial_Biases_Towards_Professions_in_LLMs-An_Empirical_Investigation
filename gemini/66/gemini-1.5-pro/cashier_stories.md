The flickering fluorescent lights of the Quick Stop buzzed like angry wasps trapped in a glass jar. Rain hammered against the single pane window, blurring the neon glow of the "Open 24 Hours" sign.  Inside, the air hung thick with the combined scent of stale coffee, day-old hot dogs, and a faint, but persistent, odor of desperation.  Eleanor Vance, her name tag slightly askew on her faded blue uniform shirt, tapped a chipped, cherry-red nail against the worn countertop. It was 3:17 am, the deadest part of the night, a time when even the shadows seemed to slumber.  The Quick Stop, nestled between a pawn shop and a perpetually closed laundromat on the outskirts of Harmony Creek, Ohio, was her kingdom, and tonight, she was its sole ruler.
