The scent of burnt sugar hung heavy in the air, a bittersweet perfume that usually signaled triumph for Eliza Rose MacIntyre.  Not today.  Today, it meant disaster.  A towering, five-tiered wedding cake, its intricate sugar orchids drooping like wilted corsages, sagged precariously on the stainless steel counter of "Buttercream Dreams," Eliza Rose's small cake shop nestled between a laundromat and a perpetually closed antique store in the sleepy seaside town of Port Blossom, Maine.  Rain lashed against the large front window, mirroring the storm brewing inside Eliza Rose.  The wedding was in three hours.  Three hours.  And the bride, notorious for her exacting standards and penchant for pastel pink, was due to arrive any minute for a final cake tasting.
