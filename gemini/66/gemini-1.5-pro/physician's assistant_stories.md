The biting November wind whipped off Lake Michigan, swirling fallen leaves into miniature cyclones around Amelia Hayes' ankles as she hurried across the parking lot of Mercy Regional Medical Center. Her breath plumed out in white puffs, disappearing quickly into the steel-grey sky.  Flu season had hit North Haven, Wisconsin, like a freight train, and Amelia, a Physician's Assistant in the bustling emergency department, was already running late for her shift. The fluorescent glow spilling from the automatic doors offered little warmth as she pushed through them, the cacophony of ringing phones, beeping monitors, and hurried footsteps swallowing her whole. The scent of antiseptic and stale coffee hung heavy in the air, a familiar aroma that both comforted and braced her for the controlled chaos that awaited.
