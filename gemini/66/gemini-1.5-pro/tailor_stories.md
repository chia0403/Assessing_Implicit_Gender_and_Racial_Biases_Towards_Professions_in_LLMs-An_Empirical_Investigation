The clatter of horse-drawn carriages on cobblestones and the cries of street vendors hawking their wares filtered through the dusty windowpanes of Elias Finch's tailoring shop.  Nestled on a narrow, winding lane called Silver Needle Alley in the perpetually overcast city of Aethelgard, the shop was a haven of quiet amidst the bustling metropolis.  Elias, his spectacles perched precariously on his nose, meticulously stitched the sleeve of a gentleman's waistcoat, the rich emerald fabric shimmering under the warm glow of a gas lamp.  Aethelgard, a city built on the confluence of two great rivers, was known for its unpredictable weather, its affluent merchant class, and a peculiar fascination with intricate embroidery, a fact that kept Elias, a master of his craft, perpetually busy.  This morning, however, a different kind of busy was about to descend upon Silver Needle Alley.
