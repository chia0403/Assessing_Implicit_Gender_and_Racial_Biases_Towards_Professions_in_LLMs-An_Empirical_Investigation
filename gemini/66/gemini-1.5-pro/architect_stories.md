Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of Elias Thorne's office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside him.  Twenty-two stories above the relentless thrum of downtown Chicago, Elias, his full name Elias Vance Thorne, stared out at the blurred cityscape.  Gargoyles perched on the gothic revival skyscrapers across the street seemed to leer at him, their stone faces slick with rain, reflecting the sickly green glow of the 'L' train as it rattled past.  His office, a meticulously designed space of polished concrete, exposed brick, and strategically placed mid-century modern furniture, usually a sanctuary of calm, felt oppressive tonight. The blueprints for the Helios Tower, his most ambitious project to date – a spiraling glass and steel behemoth meant to pierce the very heart of the city – lay rolled up on his drafting table, untouched. They felt less like a testament to his talent and more like a physical manifestation of the crushing weight of expectation he carried.  The air hung heavy with the scent of petrichor and the faint, metallic tang of fear.  He knew, with a chilling certainty, that something was terribly wrong.
