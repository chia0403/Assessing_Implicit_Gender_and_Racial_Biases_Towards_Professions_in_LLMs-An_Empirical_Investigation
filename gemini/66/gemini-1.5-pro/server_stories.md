The rain lashed against the steamed-up windows of the Busy Bee Diner, blurring the neon sign's flickering promise of "Hot Coffee & Good Times." Inside, the air hung thick with the aroma of frying onions and stale coffee, a scent Amelia "Amy" Rodriguez had come to associate with both comfort and impending exhaustion.  It was a Tuesday night, the slowest night of the week, yet Amy felt a peculiar tension thrumming in the air.  The only other occupants of the diner were a truck driver hunched over a plate of greasy fries and a young couple whispering conspiratorially in a corner booth, their faces illuminated by the flickering candlelight. The Busy Bee, nestled on a lonely stretch of Highway 17 just outside of Harmony Creek, Nevada, usually offered a predictable, if monotonous, rhythm to Amy's life. Tonight, however, something felt different.  The wind howled outside, mimicking the unsettling churn in her stomach.
