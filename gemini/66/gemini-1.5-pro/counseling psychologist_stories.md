The rain hammered against the arched window of Dr. Eleanor Vance’s office, each drop a tiny, insistent drumbeat against the otherwise hushed backdrop of a Tuesday evening in November.  Her office, nestled on the second floor of a converted Victorian house in the sleepy, seaside town of Port Blossom, Maine, usually felt warm and inviting, a haven from the often-harsh realities that her patients brought through the door.  Tonight, however, the storm raging outside seemed to mirror the tempest brewing within Eleanor herself. The usually comforting scent of lavender and chamomile from the diffuser was overpowered by the metallic tang of ozone, and the flickering gaslight on her desk cast long, dancing shadows that exaggerated the lines of worry etched on her face.  She glanced at the antique clock on the mantelpiece – 7:52 pm.  Two minutes until her last appointment of the day, an appointment she was both dreading and strangely anticipating.
