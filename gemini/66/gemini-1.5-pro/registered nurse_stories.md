The biting wind whipped off Lake Superior, stinging Amelia Grace Hawthorne's cheeks as she hurried across the icy parking lot of St. Jude's Memorial Hospital.  The December sky, a bruised purple edged with gunmetal grey, promised snow.  Duluth, Minnesota, was beautiful, even breathtaking, in its winter finery, but tonight Amelia felt only a bone-deep chill that had nothing to do with the sub-zero temperatures.  Fluorescent lights spilled from the Emergency Room entrance, painting the swirling snow a sickly yellow, and Amelia braced herself.  Another double shift. Another night battling against the tide of illness and injury that never seemed to recede in this isolated, unforgiving corner of the world.  She pushed through the heavy doors, the blast of warm, antiseptic air doing little to thaw the knot of unease that had settled in her stomach.
