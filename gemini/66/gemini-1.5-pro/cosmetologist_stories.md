The flickering neon sign of "Shear Radiance" cast a lurid, magenta glow across the rain-slicked asphalt of Elm Street.  Inside, the air thrummed with the low hum of hair dryers and the rhythmic snip of scissors.  It was a Tuesday evening in October, the kind of night where the wind howled like a banshee and the rain lashed against the windows, deterring all but the most desperate from venturing out.  For Eliza Jane Harper, however, it was just another shift, another chance to transform the mundane into the magnificent, one head of hair at a time.  Eliza, with her vibrant turquoise streaks woven through her otherwise jet-black bob and a constellation of silver rings adorning her fingers, surveyed her small but impeccably clean salon.  The scent of coconut oil and hibiscus shampoo hung heavy in the air, a comforting aroma that belied the unsettling quiet settling in outside.  She was just polishing a row of emerald green nail varnish bottles when the bell above the door chimed, announcing a late and unexpected customer.
