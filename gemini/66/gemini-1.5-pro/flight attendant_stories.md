The recycled air hummed a monotonous tune, a familiar lullaby to Amelia "Mia" Rodriguez.  Fluorescent lights glinted off the polished galley cart as she arranged miniature bottles of gin and tonic, her movements practiced and precise. Outside, the sun was just beginning to paint the cotton-candy clouds with streaks of rose and gold, a breathtaking spectacle she'd witnessed a thousand times but never quite grew accustomed to.  Flight 427 from Seoul to Los Angeles was nearly at cruising altitude, the gentle rocking of the Boeing 787 suggesting a peaceful journey ahead.  But Mia, despite the serene tableau, felt a knot of unease tighten in her chest, a premonition as unsettling as turbulence on a clear day.
