The fluorescent lights of Northwood High hummed a monotonous B flat, a soundtrack to Ms. Eleanor Vance's Tuesday morning. Rain lashed against the window of her small, book-lined office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her.  Outside, the sprawling lawns of the prestigious Connecticut boarding school, usually a vibrant green, were dulled to an almost sickly olive.  October in Litchfield County had arrived with a vengeance, bringing with it not just the vibrant foliage the area was famous for, but also a chill that seeped into the very bones of the old stone buildings. Eleanor, wrapped in a thick cardigan despite the cranked-up radiator hissing beneath the windowsill, stared at the cryptic email on her computer screen.  The subject line read simply: "Emergency - Student X."
