Rain lashed against the Art Deco windows of the Grand Majestic Hotel, mimicking the frantic drumming in Beatrice "Bea" Aubergine's chest.  The lobby, usually a hushed sanctuary of polished marble and the scent of lilies, buzzed with a nervous energy.  A delayed flight from Milan had deposited a gaggle of disgruntled opera singers, their elaborate costumes crammed into overflowing Louis Vuitton suitcases, and a film crew was attempting to navigate the revolving doors with a temperamental Steadicam.  Bea, perched behind her mahogany concierge desk, a fortress against the rising tide of chaos, smoothed her emerald green uniform and plastered a serene smile on her face. Outside, the neon lights of Times Square reflected in the rain-slicked streets, painting the December night in an almost hallucinogenic glow. This, Bea thought, was going to be a long shift.
