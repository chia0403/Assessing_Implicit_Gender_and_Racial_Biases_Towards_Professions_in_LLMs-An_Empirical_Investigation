The biting November wind whipped Amelia Hernandez’s dark hair across her face as she hurried down the deserted, snow-dusted street.  The streetlights, struggling against the gathering gloom, cast long, skeletal shadows that danced with the swirling flakes.  The air, thin and sharp with the scent of woodsmoke and impending snow, stung her nostrils.  This wasn't the picturesque New England postcard she'd envisioned when she'd accepted the position at the small, isolated clinic in the town of Havenwood, nestled deep within the Green Mountains of Vermont.  Havenwood, it turned out, held a different kind of beauty – a stark, unforgiving beauty that reflected the lives of its inhabitants, a beauty Amelia was only beginning to understand.  The clinic, a weathered clapboard building with a porch sagging under the weight of years and snow, stood before her, a beacon of warmth in the encroaching night. Inside, the weight of her responsibilities – and perhaps a little bit of Havenwood’s own chilling magic – awaited.
