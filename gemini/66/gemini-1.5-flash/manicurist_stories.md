The air in "Nails & Tales" hung thick with the scent of acetone and lavender, a perfume as familiar to Seraphina "Sera" Bellweather as her own heartbeat.  Sera, a woman whose life seemed permanently painted in shades of blush pink and shimmering gold – mirroring the glittery polishes lining her meticulously organized shelves – sighed, the sound barely audible above the rhythmic whir of the electric file.  Rain lashed against the windowpanes of her tiny salon, nestled in the crooked, cobbled alleyway off Bleecker Street in Greenwich Village, transforming the already dimly lit space into a cozy, almost clandestine haven. Outside, the city roared; inside, only the gentle rasp of the file and the soft murmur of NPR broke the quiet.  Tonight, however, even the usual comforting routine felt…off. A premonition, sharp as the sting of a freshly-cut cuticle, pricked at the edges of her consciousness.
