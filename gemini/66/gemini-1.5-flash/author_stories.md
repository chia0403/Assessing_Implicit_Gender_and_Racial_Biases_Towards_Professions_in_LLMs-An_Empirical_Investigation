The salt spray stung Amelia Blackwood’s face as she wrestled the stubborn canvas awning of her seaside cottage.  October wind, sharp and insistent as a critic’s pen, howled around the ramshackle building, threatening to tear the already fraying fabric to shreds.  Seagulls, their cries echoing the mournful rhythm of the waves crashing against the jagged rocks below, wheeled overhead.  Amelia, her auburn hair escaping its haphazard bun to whip around her face, muttered a string of curses under her breath – a colorful collection she’d honed over years of battling both the elements and the blank page.  Her cottage, perched precariously on the cliff edge overlooking the churning grey expanse of the Atlantic, was as wild and untamed as the stories she wrote within its weathered walls.  The scent of brine and woodsmoke hung heavy in the air, a constant reminder of her isolation, a solitude she both craved and dreaded.
