The humid air hung heavy, thick with the scent of decaying leaves and something subtly…musky.  Cicadas buzzed a relentless, rhythmic chorus that vibrated in Elara Vance’s bones, a counterpoint to the rhythmic *thump-thump-thump* of her own heart.  Elara, a woman whose sun-kissed skin spoke of countless hours spent under the blazing Florida sun, squinted at the sprawling enclosure before her.  The tangled mass of vines and branches, a meticulously crafted replica of a South American rainforest, housed its most prized inhabitant:  a critically endangered golden lion tamarin named Sunny.  Tonight, however, Sunny was anything but sunny. His usually playful chirps were replaced by a distressed whimper, barely audible above the cacophony of the night.  The air itself seemed to hold its breath, anticipating whatever might unfold within the emerald depths of his jungle home.
