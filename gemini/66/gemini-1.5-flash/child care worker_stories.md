The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s hair across her face as she hurried down Elm Street, the collar of her worn peacoat pulled high.  The scent of woodsmoke and roasting chestnuts fought a losing battle against the damp chill clinging to everything.  Grey skies wept a fine drizzle, turning the cobblestones of the quaint village of Oakhaven into glistening, treacherous pathways.  Amelia, however, barely noticed.  Her mind was a whirlwind of to-do lists, forgotten grocery items, and the anxious flutter of a mother's last-minute phone call about a particularly troublesome nappy rash.  Oakhaven’s only childcare centre, "Little Acorns," awaited her, a chaotic haven of miniature humans and even tinier anxieties, and Amelia, with a sigh that held more resignation than dread, knew her day had begun.
