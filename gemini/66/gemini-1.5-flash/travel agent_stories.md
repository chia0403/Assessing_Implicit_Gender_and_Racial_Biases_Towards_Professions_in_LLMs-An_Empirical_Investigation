The chipped ceramic mug warmed Amelia Hernandez's hands, the lukewarm chamomile tea doing little to soothe the icy dread that had settled in her stomach.  Rain lashed against the panoramic window of her office, blurring the already hazy cityscape of San Francisco.  Below, cable cars clanged their melancholic song, a counterpoint to the frantic tapping of her fingers on the keyboard.  Amelia, owner and sole operator of "Wanderlust & Co.," a travel agency tucked away on a quiet side street in North Beach, stared at the blinking cursor on her screen.  The email sat unanswered, a stark, digital representation of the impending storm brewing in her professional – and possibly personal – life.  The client, a notoriously demanding tech mogul named Mr. Silas Blackwood, had requested a last-minute, highly customized itinerary for a trip to the remote Amazon rainforest, and Amelia was already questioning every life choice that had led her to this precise, rain-soaked moment.
