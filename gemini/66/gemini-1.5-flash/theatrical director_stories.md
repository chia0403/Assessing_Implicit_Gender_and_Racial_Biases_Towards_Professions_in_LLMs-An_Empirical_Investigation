The stale scent of sawdust and forgotten dreams hung heavy in the air, a familiar perfume to Isabella "Izzy" Moreau.  Dust motes, illuminated by the single, bare bulb dangling precariously above, danced in the cavernous space that was once the grand ballroom of the Blackwood Manor, now her makeshift theatre.  Cracked plaster revealed glimpses of faded frescoes depicting scenes of opulent balls and forgotten romances, a stark contrast to the chaotic jumble of props, costumes, and half-finished sets that now dominated the room.  Izzy, clad in paint-splattered overalls and a worn leather jacket, surveyed the scene with a critical eye, her brow furrowed in concentration.  The premiere of her ambitious new production, "The Obsidian Mirror," was less than a week away, and the Blackwood Manor, with its haunted history and crumbling grandeur, was proving to be as challenging a character as any in her play.
