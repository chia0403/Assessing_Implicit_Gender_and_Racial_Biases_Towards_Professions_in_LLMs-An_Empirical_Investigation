The rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of Jack Stratton’s office, a relentless percussion that mirrored the throbbing headache behind his eyes.  The air hung thick with the smell of stale coffee and something vaguely floral – probably the potpourri his landlady, Mrs. Periwinkle, insisted on.  It did little to mask the underlying aroma of damp wool and despair that seemed to cling permanently to the cramped, single-room office overlooking a grimy alley in the heart of San Francisco’s Tenderloin district.  Jack Stratton, Private Investigator,  sat slumped in his creaking chair, the flickering neon sign of the Lucky Dragon Noodle House casting an anemic glow across his weary face.  A half-empty bottle of bourbon, testament to a long night spent chasing a dead end, sat on his desk beside a stack of dog-eared case files and a flickering, perpetually malfunctioning desk lamp. The city outside roared, a chaotic symphony of sirens, car horns, and the muttered conversations of the nocturnal denizens who called these streets home.  Tonight, however, the usual urban cacophony was punctuated by a single, insistent knocking at his door.
