The humid Louisiana air hung heavy, thick as a swamp fog, clinging to Isabella “Izzy” Moreau like a second skin.  Cicadas shrieked a relentless symphony from the cypress trees bordering the bayou, their song a discordant soundtrack to the simmering frustration bubbling inside her.  Izzy, renowned for her gritty neo-noir thrillers, was not known for her patience, and patience was precisely what she lacked as she surveyed the ramshackle set of her latest project, “Midnight Bayou.”  The dilapidated plantation house, its once-grand facade crumbling under the relentless assault of time and weather, looked less like a location scout's dream and more like a condemned building awaiting demolition.  A half-dozen disgruntled crew members milled about, the air thick with the scent of sweat, swamp mud, and brewing discontent.  This wasn't the picturesque, atmospheric Louisiana she'd envisioned; this was a logistical nightmare wrapped in a shroud of mosquitos and simmering resentment.
