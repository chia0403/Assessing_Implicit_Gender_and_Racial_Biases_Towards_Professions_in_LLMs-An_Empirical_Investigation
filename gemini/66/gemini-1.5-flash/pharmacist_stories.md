The bell above the door of "Apothecary & Aisle" jingled a discordant C-sharp, a sound Amelia Hernandez, R.Ph., had grown accustomed to associating with the onset of another headache.  It was a Tuesday, already shaping up to be a particularly brutal one. Rain lashed against the tall, gothic windows of her small, independent pharmacy nestled on Bleecker Street in the West Village.  The air inside, thick with the scent of lavender and eucalyptus, offered little respite from the damp chill seeping in from the storm outside.  Water dripped relentlessly from a loose shingle somewhere above the ancient, oak shelving units groaning under the weight of countless jars and bottles, each containing a promise – or a threat – depending on its contents.  Amelia, her auburn braid escaping its haphazard bun, sighed, the sound swallowed by the rhythmic tick-tock of the grandfather clock in the corner, a constant reminder of time’s relentless march.
