The chipped paint of the "Guidance" sign above Ms. Eleanor Vance's door seemed to perfectly mirror the state of her inbox.  A cascade of emails, each one a miniature crisis – a failing grade, a fractured friendship, a looming expulsion – awaited her attention.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of Northwood High, blurring the already muted autumnal colours of the sprawling campus below. Inside, the air hung thick with the scent of stale coffee and teenage angst, a familiar perfume in the otherwise sterile environment of her small, cluttered office. Eleanor sighed, the sound swallowed by the rhythmic drumming of the storm.  The worn, floral armchair opposite her desk – usually occupied by anxious students – sat empty, a silent testament to the relative calm before the inevitable afternoon rush.  Today, however, felt different; a low hum of unease vibrated beneath the surface of the usual high school chaos.
