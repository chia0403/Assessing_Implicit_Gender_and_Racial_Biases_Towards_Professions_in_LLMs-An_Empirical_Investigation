The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s auburn hair across her face as she wrestled the heavy canvas sample case onto the porch.  Her breath plumed out in frosty white clouds, mirroring the apprehension swirling in her stomach.  The clapboard house, perched precariously on the edge of a windswept cliff overlooking the churning grey Atlantic, looked less like a potential client’s residence and more like a hermit’s last stand.  Salt spray stung her cheeks, and the mournful cry of gulls overhead only added to the desolate beauty – and the growing sense that this was going to be the most challenging sales call of Amelia's already demanding career.  The address, scrawled in elegant, spidery script on the crumpled order form, promised bespoke linens, but the reality hinted at something far more… enigmatic.
