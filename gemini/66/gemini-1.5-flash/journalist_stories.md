The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s hair across her face, stinging her cheeks a furious crimson.  Rain, a relentless, sideways drizzle, plastered her trench coat to her thin frame as she hunched deeper into the shadowed alleyway.  The air hung thick with the smells of rotting fish, damp earth, and something else… something metallic and faintly sweet, that prickled at the back of her throat.  She wasn't sure if it was the lingering scent of the city's grime or something far more sinister.  This wasn't the charming, postcard-perfect Dublin she'd read about in tourist brochures; this was a Dublin clawing at the edges of darkness, a city that whispered secrets in the shadows between cobblestone streets and gas-lamp glow.  Amelia, a seasoned investigative journalist for the *Irish Independent*, knew this darkness all too well. Tonight, however, it felt particularly… suffocating.
