The salt spray stung Professor Eleanor Vance’s face as she wrestled the stubborn research vessel, the *Argonauta*, closer to the jagged, obsidian cliffs of Isla Perdida.  The late afternoon sun, a bruised plum in the gathering twilight, cast long, skeletal shadows from the volcanic peaks, their jagged silhouettes clawing at the bruised purple sky.  The air hung heavy with the scent of brine and something else, something ancient and faintly unsettling – a metallic tang that clung to the back of her throat like the taste of fear.  Isla Perdida, the Lost Island, lived up to its name, shrouded in mist and mystery, a place whispered about in hushed tones by weathered sailors and dismissed as myth by most academics.  But Eleanor, a specialist in obscure pre-Columbian cultures, knew better.  She had dedicated the last ten years of her life to proving the existence of the legendary civilization of Xibalba, and she believed its heart beat somewhere deep within this unforgiving, breathtaking island.
