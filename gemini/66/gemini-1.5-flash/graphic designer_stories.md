The midday sun beat down on the cracked asphalt of Bleecker Street, baking the already stifling humidity of a July afternoon in New York City.  Amelia "Mia" Hernandez squinted, shielding her eyes with a hand that still bore the faint smudge of Pantone 185 C – a vibrant, almost aggressive, shade of orange that had stubbornly refused to be wiped away, mirroring the insistent thrum of anxiety in her chest.  The air hung thick with the scent of exhaust fumes, hot dogs, and the faint, underlying sweetness of melting ice cream, a symphony of urban smells that usually soothed her, but today only amplified her growing unease.  Mia tugged at the loose strap of her worn leather tote bag, the weight of her portfolio – her lifeline, her burden – pressing heavily against her shoulder.  She was late.  Terribly, disastrously late.  And not for a casual coffee date, but for the presentation that could make or break her fledgling graphic design career.
