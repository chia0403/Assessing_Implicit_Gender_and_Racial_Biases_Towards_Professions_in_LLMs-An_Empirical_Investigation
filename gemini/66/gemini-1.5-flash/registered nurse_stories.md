The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne's auburn braid across her face as she hurried down the cobbled street, the scent of woodsmoke and damp earth clinging to her wool coat.  Rain, a relentless drizzle that had been falling since dawn, plastered her dark eyelashes together, obscuring the already fading light.  Above, the skeletal branches of ancient oaks clawed at the bruised twilight sky, their silhouettes stark against the bruised purple and grey.  Amelia, a registered nurse at St. Jude's Hospital, tucked her chin deeper into her collar, the weight of her satchel – filled with lesson plans for her evening nursing student class – pulling at her shoulder.  She was late, already picturing the disapproving frown of Professor Davies, and the whispered criticisms of her perpetually punctual students.  But tonight, the lateness wasn't entirely her fault.  The storm, and a certain shadowed alleyway she’d just passed, had played a significant, and unsettling, part.
