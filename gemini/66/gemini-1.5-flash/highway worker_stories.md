The sun beat down on Darius "Dar" Blackwood's neck, a relentless hammer against the already simmering asphalt.  Sweat stung his eyes, blurring the already hazy horizon of the Nevada desert.  The air, thick with the smell of tar and exhaust fumes, vibrated with the low hum of Dar's rumbling grader, a behemoth of yellow steel that was his office for the better part of every day.  Dust devils, miniature tornadoes of grit and heat, danced across the newly laid highway like mischievous spirits, mocking his tireless efforts to tame this unforgiving landscape.  Ahead, the endless expanse of Interstate 15 stretched towards the shimmering mirage of Las Vegas, a promised land of cool casinos and flashing lights that felt a lifetime away from the sun-baked purgatory of his current reality.
