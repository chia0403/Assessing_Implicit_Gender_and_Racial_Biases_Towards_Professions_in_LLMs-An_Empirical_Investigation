The chipped ceramic mug warmed Amelia Katherine Finch’s hands, a small comfort against the biting November wind whistling through the gaps in the office window.  Rain lashed against the glass, blurring the already grimy cityscape of Manchester.  Amelia, a secretary at Blackwood & Sons, a venerable but distinctly dusty law firm, sighed, the sound swallowed by the rhythmic clatter of the ancient typewriter in the next cubicle.  Dust motes, illuminated by the single, flickering fluorescent light above her desk, danced in the air like tiny, malevolent spirits.  The air itself hung heavy with the scent of old paper, stale coffee, and a faint, unsettling sweetness – the lingering perfume of Mrs. Grimshaw, the senior partner's notoriously eccentric wife, who was rumored to communicate with the dead through her prize-winning orchids.  Amelia, however, was far more concerned with the stack of unsorted files threatening to topple from her already overflowing in-tray.  Today, amongst the usual contracts and court summons, something felt… different.  A subtle shift in the atmosphere, a tremor of unease that ran deeper than the usual pre-Christmas office stress.
