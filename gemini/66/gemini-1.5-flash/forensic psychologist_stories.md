The chipped porcelain doll stared blankly from the evidence bag, its painted smile a grotesque mockery of innocence.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of Dr. Eleanor Vance’s office, mirroring the storm raging inside her.  The city of Seattle sprawled beneath her, a glittering tapestry of lights woven through the relentless downpour.  Eleanor, a forensic psychologist renowned for her unnerving ability to dissect the darkest corners of the human psyche,  sat hunched over her desk, the flickering lamplight illuminating the fine lines etched around her tired hazel eyes.  The air hung heavy with the scent of old books and something else, something faintly metallic and undeniably unsettling – the lingering aroma of a crime scene she couldn’t quite shake.  Tonight, the case of the missing children – and this unsettling doll – was consuming her, threatening to pull her under the tide of a chilling investigation.
