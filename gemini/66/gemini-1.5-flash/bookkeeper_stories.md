The chipped porcelain teacup warmed Amelia Katherine Finch’s fingers, the lukewarm chamomile doing little to soothe the tremor that had settled in her hands.  Rain lashed against the leaded-glass windows of her small, cluttered office, mirroring the storm brewing inside her.  The air hung thick with the scent of aging paper, damp wool, and the faintly metallic tang of fear.  Amelia, a bookkeeper of impeccable precision and notoriously quiet demeanor, stared at the ledger spread across her desk, its neat columns of figures mocking her with their unwavering order.  Outside, the gas lamps of Grimsborough flickered in the downpour, casting long, distorted shadows that danced across the cobblestones of the narrow alleyway below her window.  This wasn't the usual drizzle that Grimsborough welcomed so often; this was a tempest, mirroring the chaos that had infiltrated the meticulous world of Finch & Finch, Accountants – a chaos that threatened to unravel everything Amelia held dear.
