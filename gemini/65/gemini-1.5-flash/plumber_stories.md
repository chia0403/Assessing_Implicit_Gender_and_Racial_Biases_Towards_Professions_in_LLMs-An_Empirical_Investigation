The greasy aroma of burnt coffee hung heavy in the air, clinging to the damp canvas of Amelia "Amy" Hernandez's overalls like a second skin.  Rain lashed against the grimy windows of her beat-up Ford Transit van, parked precariously on the verge of a cobbled lane that snaked its way down towards the brooding, grey North Sea.  The fishing village of Port Blossom, usually a postcard-pretty scene of pastel-coloured cottages and bobbing fishing boats, was today a sodden, windswept mess.  Amy shivered, not entirely from the cold.  The cryptic message on her answering machine – "The kraken's plumbing needs attending to, urgently" – still echoed unsettlingly in her ears.  This wasn't just another blocked drain in Mrs. Gable's kitchen; this felt… different.
