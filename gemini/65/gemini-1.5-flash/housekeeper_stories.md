The chipped porcelain sink wept a steady stream of lukewarm water, mirroring the relentless drizzle outside.  Rain lashed against the tall, gothic windows of Blackwood Manor, blurring the already indistinct shapes of the ancient oaks that clawed at the sky.  Inside, the air hung thick with the scent of beeswax polish and damp wool, a familiar perfume to  Elara Hawthorne, who stood scrubbing at a stubborn stain on a silver serving tray.  Elara, with her nimble fingers and a patience honed over years of service, barely registered the storm raging beyond the leaded panes.  Blackwood Manor held its own storms, far more treacherous than any weather, and Elara had weathered them all.
