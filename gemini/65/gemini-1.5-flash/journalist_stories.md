The chipped paint of the windowsill crumbled beneath Elara Vance’s elbow as she peered down at the teeming street below.  Rain, a relentless, grey curtain, sheeted down onto the grimy cobblestones of Prague’s Old Town Square, blurring the already indistinct shapes of the huddled figures scurrying for cover.  The air hung heavy with the scent of woodsmoke and damp earth, a familiar perfume that nonetheless failed to mask the underlying tang of something metallic, something…wrong.  Elara, a seasoned journalist for the Prague Post, adjusted her worn leather jacket, the collar pulled high against the biting wind.  Her breath plumed out in ghostly white clouds, mirroring the faint wisps of steam rising from the overflowing gutters. This wasn’t just another story; this felt like the opening act of something far darker, something that smelled not only of metal, but of secrets buried deep beneath the ancient city’s cobbled heart.
