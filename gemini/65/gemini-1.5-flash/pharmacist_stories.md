The chipped porcelain of the apothecary jar felt cool beneath Dr. Eleanor Vance’s fingertips.  Dust motes, illuminated by the single, sputtering gas lamp above, danced in the stagnant air of her shop, “Vance’s Remedies,” a name etched into the dusty windowpane in fading gold leaf.  Outside, the relentless November wind howled a mournful dirge through the narrow, cobbled streets of Oakhaven, a town clinging precariously to the edge of the brooding Blackwood Forest.  The scent of woodsmoke and damp earth battled with the more delicate fragrances of dried herbs and potent tinctures that hung heavy in the air, a familiar, comforting aroma that did little to soothe the knot of anxiety tightening in Eleanor’s stomach.  Tonight, the usual quiet of Oakhaven was punctuated not by the gentle creak of settling timbers, but by a sharp, insistent rapping at her door – a sound that promised anything but a peaceful evening.
