The chipped mug warmed Amelia Hernandez’s fingers, the lukewarm chamomile tea doing little to soothe the icy dread creeping up her spine.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of her Soho loft, blurring the already hazy cityscape into a watercolor wash of greys and bruised purples.  Amelia, a graphic designer whose apartment doubled as her studio, stared out at the storm, the rhythmic drumming a counterpoint to the frantic thump-thump-thump of her own heart.  The deadline for the Blackwood & Sons rebranding campaign loomed – a campaign that, if failed, could very well sink her fledgling business,  "Pixel Perfect Designs," and with it, her meticulously constructed life.  The air hung thick with the scent of drying ink and impending doom, a heady cocktail that Amelia had become all too familiar with lately.
