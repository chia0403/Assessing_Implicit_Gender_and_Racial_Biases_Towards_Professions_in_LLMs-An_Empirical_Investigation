The biting November wind whipped Dr. Eleanor Vance's auburn hair across her face as she navigated the overgrown path leading to Blackwood Manor.  Dead leaves crunched under her worn leather boots, a brittle counterpoint to the frantic beating of her own heart.  Blackwood, perched precariously on a cliff overlooking the churning grey sea, was a gothic monstrosity of crumbling stone and shadowed windows.  Its reputation preceded it – a history steeped in tragedy, whispered tales of madness and violence clinging to its decaying timbers like stubborn ivy.  Eleanor, a forensic psychologist renowned for her ability to unravel the complexities of the human psyche, especially in the most disturbed individuals, found herself strangely drawn, and unnerved, by the manor's oppressive atmosphere. Tonight, however, the unease was amplified tenfold; she wasn't here for a consultation, but for a very personal reason – the disappearance of her estranged brother, Thomas.
