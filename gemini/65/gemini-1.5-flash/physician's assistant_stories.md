The salt spray stung Dr. Evelyn Reed’s face as she wrestled the stubborn fishing net onto the deck of the *Serendipity*.  The late afternoon sun, a bruised plum in the churning grey sky, cast long shadows across the deck, highlighting the frantic flapping of a trapped seagull whose cries echoed the turmoil in Evelyn’s own stomach.  The small, battered fishing boat pitched violently, each roll threatening to send her tumbling into the icy Atlantic.  They were miles off the coast of Newfoundland, a desolate stretch of ocean known more for its unforgiving weather than its bounty, and Evelyn, a physician's assistant more accustomed to the sterile environment of a modern clinic, felt profoundly out of her element. The air smelled of brine, diesel, and something vaguely metallic that she couldn’t quite place.  This wasn't the kind of emergency room she was trained for.
