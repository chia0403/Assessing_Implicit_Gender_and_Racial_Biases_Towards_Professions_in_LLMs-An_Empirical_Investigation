The salt spray stung Elias Thorne's face as he wrestled the stubborn oak plank onto the scaffolding.  Below him, the churning grey expanse of the North Sea clawed at the pilings of the newly-constructed lighthouse, its relentless rhythm a counterpoint to the rhythmic thud of Elias's hammer.  The air, thick with the scent of brine and pine, carried the mournful cry of gulls overhead.  He perched precariously on the swaying platform, a hundred feet above the unforgiving rocks, his calloused hands gripping the rough wood with the practiced ease of a man who'd spent his life wrestling with the elements.  The unfinished lighthouse, a skeletal finger pointing accusingly at the stormy sky, was Elias’s current obsession, a testament to both his skill and his stubborn refusal to yield to the sea’s fury.
