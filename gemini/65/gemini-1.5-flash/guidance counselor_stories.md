The chipped paint of the "Guidance" sign on Mrs. Eleanor Vance's door was a perfect metaphor for the state of her inbox.  Overstuffed, neglected, and threatening to crumble entirely under the weight of its contents.  Eleanor Vance, forty-two years old and perpetually smelling faintly of stale coffee and regret, sighed, the sound swallowed by the hushed, almost reverent quiet of Northwood High's guidance office.  The air hung heavy with the scent of floor polish and teenage angst, a familiar and frankly depressing perfume.  Sunlight, weak and apologetic, filtered through the grimy windowpanes, illuminating dust motes dancing in the stagnant air.  Outside, the manicured lawns of Northwood High – a deceptive facade of perfect suburban tranquility – hid the simmering anxieties of hundreds of students, each with their own unique brand of chaos Eleanor was tasked with untangling.
