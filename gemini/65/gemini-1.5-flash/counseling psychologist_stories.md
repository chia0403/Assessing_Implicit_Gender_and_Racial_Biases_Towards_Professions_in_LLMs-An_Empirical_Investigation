The salt spray stung Dr. Eleanor Vance's face as she wrestled the stubborn sailboat's mainsail.  The late afternoon sun, a bruised plum in the rapidly darkening sky, cast long shadows across the choppy waters of the Puget Sound.  Seagulls cried overhead, their calls a discordant counterpoint to the rhythmic creak of the aging wooden hull.  Eleanor, her normally meticulous bun loosened and escaping its confines, fought to keep the small vessel on course, the wind threatening to push them onto the jagged rocks that fringed the island's shore.  It was a fitting metaphor, she thought grimly, for the turbulent inner lives of her patients; a constant battle against the elements, a precarious journey towards calmer waters. Tonight, however, the battle was hers alone, and the outcome, far from certain.
