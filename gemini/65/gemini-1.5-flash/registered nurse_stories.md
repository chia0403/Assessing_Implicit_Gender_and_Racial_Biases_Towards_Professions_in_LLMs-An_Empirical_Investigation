The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Holloway's dark hair across her face as she hurried down the rain-slicked streets of Galway.  The city, usually a vibrant tapestry of cobblestone and colorful buildings, was muted under a sky the color of bruised plums.  Torrential rain lashed against the ancient stone walls, each drop a tiny hammer blow against the medieval architecture.  Amelia, her sensible navy raincoat pulled tight, fought her way through the deluge, the scent of salty sea air and damp earth clinging to her like a second skin.  Tonight, the usually charming, bustling streets of Galway felt bleak and unforgiving, mirroring the turmoil churning within her.  She was late, desperately, terrifyingly late, and the weight of a life hung precariously in the balance.
