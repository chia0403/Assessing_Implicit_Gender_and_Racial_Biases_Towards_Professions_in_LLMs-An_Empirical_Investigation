The chipped paint of the oak door flaked onto Dr. Eleanor Vance’s sensible beige cardigan, a fitting metaphor for the crumbling state of Oakhaven High.  Rain lashed against the windows of her cramped office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside sixteen-year-old Jason Miller, currently sprawled on the threadbare couch, a whirlwind of adolescent angst and barely suppressed fury.  The air hung heavy with the scent of stale coffee and simmering teenage rebellion; a familiar aroma in Eleanor’s world, one she’d become intimately acquainted with over her decade spent navigating the emotional minefields of Oakhaven’s hallways.  The school, a hulking Gothic structure perched precariously on a windswept hill overlooking the churning grey sea, seemed to absorb the anxieties of its students, exuding a palpable sense of unease, especially on a day like this.
