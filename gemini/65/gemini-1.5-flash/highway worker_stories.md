The biting wind whipped across the desolate expanse of the Nevada desert, stinging Elias Thorne’s cheeks raw.  Dust devils, miniature tornadoes of grit and gravel, danced across the cracked asphalt of Highway 50, a mocking ballet against the backdrop of the pale, unforgiving sun.  Elias, his face grimy with a mixture of sweat and road grime, wrestled with a recalcitrant pothole patcher, its hydraulics groaning in protest against the frigid morning air.  The only sounds louder than the wind were the rhythmic thrum of the distant eighteen-wheelers and the occasional, mournful cry of a hawk circling far above.  This stretch of highway, miles from anywhere, felt more like the edge of the world than a vital artery connecting bustling cities.  The solitude was usually Elias’s solace, but today, a creeping unease, sharp as shattered glass, pricked at the edges of his weary mind.
