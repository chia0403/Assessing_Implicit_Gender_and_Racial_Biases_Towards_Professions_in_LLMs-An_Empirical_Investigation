The brass spittoon gleamed under the single, flickering gaslight, reflecting the anxious sweat beading on the forehead of Archibald "Archie" Finch.  Archie, a bellhop at the Grand Majestic Hotel – a decaying monument to a bygone era of opulence, now clinging precariously to its faded grandeur – adjusted the already impeccable knot of his burgundy cravat. The air hung thick with the cloying scent of stale perfume and desperation, a perfume that clung to the threadbare carpets and the chipped marble pillars as stubbornly as the dust motes dancing in the weak light.  Outside, the Chicago wind howled like a banshee, rattling the ancient windows of the hotel, a sound that mirrored the unease churning in Archie’s gut.  Tonight felt different. Tonight, the usual symphony of drunken laughter and hushed assignations was punctuated by a chilling silence, broken only by the rhythmic creak of the elevator and the relentless whisper of the wind.
