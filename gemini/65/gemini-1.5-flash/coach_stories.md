The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Amy” Hawthorne’s hair across her face as she surveyed the desolate, windswept soccer field.  Frozen clumps of grass crunched under her worn leather boots, the sound a stark counterpoint to the mournful wail of a distant foghorn.  The air, thick with the smell of salt and decaying seaweed, carried the faint, metallic tang of blood – a lingering reminder of the gulls’ recent feast.  This wasn't the manicured green of a championship stadium; this was the wind-battered pitch of the fictional island of Aisling, where the only thing more unpredictable than the weather was the ragtag team Amy coached, the Aisling Islanders.  Their upcoming match against the seemingly invincible North Coast Renegades felt less like a game and more like a kamikaze mission.  The setting sun painted the stormy sky in bruised purples and angry oranges, mirroring the turbulent emotions churning within Amy herself.
