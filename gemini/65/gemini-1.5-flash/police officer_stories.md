The salt spray stung Officer Amelia "Amy" Hernandez's face as the November wind whipped her hair across her eyes.  She wrestled the battered patrol car, a Ford Crown Victoria affectionately nicknamed "The Whale," onto the rutted, gravel track that snaked along the cliff edge overlooking the churning grey expanse of the Atlantic.  The air smelled sharp with brine and something else, something faintly metallic and unsettling, that clung to the back of her throat like the taste of old blood.  This was Montauk Point, Long Island, at the very easternmost tip of the country, a place where the land ended and the mysteries of the ocean began. Tonight, Amy suspected, those mysteries were about to get a whole lot closer.
