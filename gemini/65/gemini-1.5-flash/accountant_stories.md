The fluorescent lights of "DeWitt & Sons, Chartered Accountants" hummed a monotonous tune, a soundtrack to Amelia Hernandez's meticulously organized life.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of her 27th-floor office, blurring the cityscape into a hazy watercolour of grey and black.  Amelia, a woman whose perfectly-coiffed auburn bun never seemed to stray a millimetre from its position, tapped a perfectly manicured fingernail against the sleek surface of her mahogany desk.  The city below, a chaotic symphony of honking taxis and flashing sirens, felt a world away from the precise, ordered universe she inhabited within the four walls of her office.  Tonight, however, that meticulously crafted world was teetering on the brink of chaos.  A stack of financial statements, thicker than a small dictionary, sat before her, promising a night far removed from the quiet solitude she usually cherished.  The case of Barrington Industries, a seemingly innocuous food processing company, had become anything but.  And Amelia Hernandez, accountant extraordinaire, was about to find out why.
