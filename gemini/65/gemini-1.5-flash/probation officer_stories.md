The stale air hung heavy with the scent of cheap coffee and despair, clinging to the worn floral wallpaper of Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s cramped office like a persistent cough.  Rain lashed against the grimy windows of the probation department, a relentless rhythm mirroring the drumming anxiety in Amelia’s chest.  Outside, the city of Birmingham hummed a discordant symphony of sirens and car horns, a fitting soundtrack to the lives she oversaw – lives teetering precariously on the edge of chaos.  Amelia, a probation officer with five years’ experience and a weariness that belied her thirty-two years, stared at the overflowing in-tray, a mountain range of case files promising another day of navigating moral grey areas and broken promises. The chipped mug in her hand was lukewarm, a reflection of her own fading enthusiasm.
