The chipped paint of the windowsill dug into Dr. Eleanor Vance’s elbow as she stared out at the relentless grey of the November London sky.  Rain lashed against the panes, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her.  Her office, a cramped space above a bakery on a cobbled street in Notting Hill, usually smelled of warm sourdough and cinnamon. Today, however, the aroma was overpowered by the sharp, metallic tang of fear.  Eleanor Vance, PhD, Registered Dietitian, and champion of balanced nutrition, found herself facing a challenge far more complex than any dietary imbalance: a client who refused to eat.  Not out of anorexia, not out of a fad diet, but out of… something else entirely.  Something that smelled faintly of lavender and old secrets.
