The pre-dawn chill bit at Elara Vance’s cheeks as she wrestled the rusty gate open, the groan of metal a familiar counterpoint to the chirping of unseen birds nestled in the overgrown hedges of Whispering Pines Wildlife Sanctuary.  Elara, a woman whose hands were perpetually stained with mud and whose hair always seemed to contain a stray feather or two, pulled her worn woolen scarf tighter. The air hung heavy with the scent of damp earth and pine, a fragrance she’d come to associate not just with her job, but with the very rhythm of her life.  Whispering Pines, nestled deep in the rolling hills of Oregon, was more than just a sanctuary; it was a sprawling, chaotic testament to her unwavering dedication to the creatures great and small that called it home.  This morning, however, the familiar comfort of the sanctuary felt tinged with an unsettling unease.  A stillness, deeper than the usual predawn quiet, hung in the air.
