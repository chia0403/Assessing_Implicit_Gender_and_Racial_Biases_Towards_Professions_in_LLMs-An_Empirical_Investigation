The scent of aged paper and dust motes dancing in the lone shaft of afternoon sunlight was as familiar to Eleanor Vance as the rhythm of her own heartbeat.  Dust motes, she mused, were the librarians' tiny, silent companions, ever-present witnesses to the whispered secrets held within the towering shelves of the Ashworth Memorial Library.  Outside, the relentless November wind howled like a banshee, rattling the ancient stained-glass windows depicting long-dead saints and scholars, but inside, Eleanor, amidst the comforting quiet and the reassuring weight of centuries of accumulated knowledge, felt a sense of profound peace. The library, a gothic behemoth of grey stone nestled deep in the heart of the perpetually misty town of Oakhaven, was her sanctuary, her kingdom, and, tonight, the stage for a most peculiar discovery.
