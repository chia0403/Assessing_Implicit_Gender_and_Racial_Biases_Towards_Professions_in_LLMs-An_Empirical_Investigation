The biting November wind whipped Elias Thorne’s already-chapped lips raw as he wrestled the recalcitrant oak branch into submission.  Fallen leaves, the color of dried blood and rust, swirled around his worn boots, a swirling vortex mirroring the turmoil brewing inside him.  He worked in the manicured silence of Blackwood Manor’s sprawling grounds, the skeletal branches of ancient trees clawing at the bruised twilight sky.  Blackwood Manor itself, a gothic behemoth of grey stone that seemed to suck the very light from the day, loomed over him, a silent witness to centuries of secrets buried beneath its meticulously kept lawns.  Elias, Blackwood’s groundskeeper for the past twenty years, knew those secrets better than most.  He'd seen things grow and decay, not just in the earth beneath his calloused hands, but in the lives of the family who resided within the manor's cold embrace. Tonight, however, felt different. A stillness, heavier than usual, had fallen over the estate, a silence that spoke of something far more unsettling than the usual autumnal quiet.
