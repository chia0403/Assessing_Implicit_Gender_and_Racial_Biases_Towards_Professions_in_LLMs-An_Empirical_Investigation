The salt-laced wind whipped Amelia Katherine Hayes’s charcoal grey coat around her legs as she stood on the precipice of the crumbling cliff, the churning grey-green sea a relentless backdrop to her contemplation.  The air smelled of brine and impending storm, a fitting atmosphere for the legal battle brewing within her.  Below, the tiny village of Port Blossom clung precariously to the rocky coastline, its brightly painted houses a stark contrast to the looming, slate-grey sky.  This was her family’s home, a place of fierce beauty and equally fierce secrets, and Amelia, a sharp-tongued corporate lawyer from London, had returned not for a holiday, but to fight a case that threatened to shatter the fragile peace of this idyllic, yet undeniably treacherous, haven.
