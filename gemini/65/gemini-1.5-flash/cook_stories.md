The air hung thick and sweet, a cloying blend of roasting garlic, simmering tomatoes, and the faint, almost imperceptible musk of woodsmoke clinging to the aged timbers of the "Three Whispering Pines" restaurant.  Rain lashed against the leaded-glass windows, blurring the already indistinct view of the Cornish coastline.  Inside, however, the atmosphere was a comforting, crackling warmth.  This was where Amelia Katherine Finch, better known as Millie to her regulars and a whirlwind of controlled chaos in her own kitchen, held court.  Tonight, however, the usual comforting rhythm of her work was subtly off-kilter, a disquieting tremor underlying the familiar hum of the stovetop and the clatter of cutlery.  A storm was brewing, and it wasn't just the one raging outside.
