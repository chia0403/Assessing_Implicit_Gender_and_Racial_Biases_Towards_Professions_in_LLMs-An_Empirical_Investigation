The humid air hung heavy, thick with the scent of sweat and cheap disinfectant.  Fluorescent lights buzzed overhead, casting a sickly yellow glow on the chipped linoleum floor of the "Body Blitz" fitness center.  Rain lashed against the grimy windows, a relentless percussion to the thumping bass of the aerobics class currently underway.  Amelia "Mia" Hernandez, a woman whose sculpted biceps could curdle milk, surveyed her class with a steely gaze.  Her vibrant turquoise leggings and matching sports bra, however, belied the simmering frustration bubbling beneath her meticulously applied makeup.  This wasn't the glamorous, sun-drenched California studio she'd envisioned when she'd quit her corporate job to follow her fitness passion; this was a battle for survival, one sweat-soaked, poorly ventilated class at a time.
