The biting November wind whipped Dr. Eleanor Vance’s charcoal grey coat around her legs as she hurried across the deserted hospital courtyard.  Rain, a fine, persistent drizzle, plastered her dark hair to her scalp, obscuring the sharp angles of her face, usually so meticulously groomed.  The imposing granite facade of St. Jude’s General loomed before her, its gothic windows like vacant eyes staring out at the storm-wracked sky. Inside, however, a different kind of storm raged – a tempest of anxieties, emergencies, and the ever-present, silent pressure of life and death decisions that defined Eleanor’s existence.  The air hung thick with the scent of antiseptic and something indefinably metallic, a scent that clung to her like a second skin after sixteen years spent navigating the sterile, yet brutally visceral, world of the operating theatre.  Tonight, however, the usual pre-surgical tension was amplified tenfold.  A blizzard was brewing outside, and inside… well, inside, a battle was already underway.
