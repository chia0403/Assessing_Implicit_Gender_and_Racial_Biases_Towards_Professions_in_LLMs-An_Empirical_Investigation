The biting November wind whipped at Elias Thorne’s already chapped lips, stinging them like a swarm of angry bees.  He hunched deeper into his threadbare parka, the collar pulled high against the icy blast that swept across the skeletal remains of the St. Jude’s Redevelopment project.  Towering cranes, like metallic birds of prey, silhouetted against the bruised purple sky, were frozen mid-reach, their work suspended by the unexpected blizzard that had descended upon the city of Grimsborough.  Below, the half-finished concrete shell of what was supposed to be a luxury apartment complex stood stark and unfinished, a monument to stalled ambition and the brutal bite of winter.  The air, thick with the smell of damp concrete and the metallic tang of exposed steel rebar, carried the faint, almost imperceptible scent of woodsmoke from a distant house, a fragile reminder of warmth in this desolate landscape.
