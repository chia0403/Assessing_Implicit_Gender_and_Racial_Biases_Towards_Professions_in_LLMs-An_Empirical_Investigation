The fluorescent lights of "Sterling & Steele, Attorneys at Law" hummed a monotonous tune, a counterpoint to the insistent drip, drip, drip of a leaky faucet somewhere deep within the bowels of the building.  Amelia Grace Hawthorne, receptionist extraordinaire (or at least, that's what she told herself on particularly slow Tuesdays), meticulously arranged the already-perfect stack of brochures featuring the firm's smiling partners.  The air hung thick with the scent of stale coffee and expensive leather, a fragrance Amelia had come to associate with the relentless march of another workday.  Outside, the relentless Chicago wind howled a mournful dirge, rattling the windows in their frames, a stark contrast to the hushed, almost reverent atmosphere within the plush reception area.  This was Amelia's domain, a carefully constructed island of calm amidst the storm of legal battles raging just beyond the mahogany doors.
