The scent of lavender and sandalwood hung heavy in the air, a fragrant counterpoint to the damp chill clinging to the cobblestones outside. Rain lashed against the leaded glass windows of Serenity Now,  a tiny massage therapy studio tucked away on a narrow, almost forgotten alleyway in the heart of Edinburgh's Old Town.  Inside, the flickering candlelight cast long shadows across the polished wooden floors, illuminating dust motes dancing in the warm, amber glow.  Aisha Sharma, her nimble fingers already working their magic on a client’s tense shoulders, barely registered the storm raging outside.  The rhythmic kneading, the soft sighs escaping her client’s lips, were a familiar lullaby, a comforting routine in a life that often felt anything but serene.
