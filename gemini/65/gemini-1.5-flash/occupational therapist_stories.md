The salt spray stung Eleanor Vance’s face as she navigated the uneven cobblestones of Port Blossom’s harbor.  The late afternoon sun, a bruised plum in the thickening twilight, cast long shadows from the brightly painted fishing boats bobbing gently alongside the weathered docks.  The air, thick with the scent of brine and frying fish, carried the mournful cry of gulls overhead – a familiar soundtrack to Eleanor’s life in this charming, yet undeniably isolated, coastal town.  Eleanor, a registered occupational therapist with a penchant for sensible cardigans and an even more pronounced fondness for her patients, clutched a worn leather satchel containing her therapy materials.  Tonight, however, her visit wasn’t to the brightly lit community center; it was a house call, to a patient whose isolated circumstances presented a unique, and perhaps daunting, challenge.
