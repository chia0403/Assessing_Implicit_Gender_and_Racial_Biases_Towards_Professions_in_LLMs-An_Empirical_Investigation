The fluorescent lights of Amelia "Mia" Hernandez's office hummed a monotonous tune, a counterpoint to the frantic tapping of her keyboard.  Dust motes, illuminated like tiny planets, danced in the single shaft of afternoon sunlight slicing through the grimy window of her cramped storefront on the edge of downtown Oakland.  Tax season was in full swing, a brutal, beautiful beast that Mia wrestled yearly.  The air, thick with the scent of stale coffee and desperation, was punctuated by the occasional sob from a client in the waiting room – a plush, slightly-stained floral armchair that had witnessed more tears than a therapist’s couch.  The year was 2024, and Mia, a seasoned tax preparer with a sharp wit and even sharper pencil, was ready for battle.
