The humid air hung heavy, thick with the scent of frangipani and impending rain.  Cicadas buzzed a relentless symphony from the emerald canopy that draped over the ancient Mayan ruins of Tikal, their song a counterpoint to the rhythmic thud of Amelia Hernandez’s sandals on the crumbling limestone path.  Amelia, her dark hair pulled back in a practical braid that escaped the confines of her baseball cap, adjusted the strap of her worn canvas bag, its contents – a water bottle, a battered first-aid kit, and a well-thumbed guidebook – familiar companions on her daily treks through this breathtaking, yet unforgiving, jungle.  Today, she led a small group of tourists, their faces a mixture of awe and apprehension, deeper into the heart of the lost city, the setting sun painting the crumbling temples in hues of orange and blood red.
