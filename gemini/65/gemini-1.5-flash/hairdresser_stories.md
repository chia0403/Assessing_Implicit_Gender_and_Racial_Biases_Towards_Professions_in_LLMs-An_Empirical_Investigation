The scent of ozone hung heavy in the air, a metallic tang clinging to the damp strands of hair clinging to Amelia Hernandez's apron.  Rain lashed against the single, mismatched windowpane of "Shear Madness," her tiny salon nestled between a failing laundromat and a bodega perpetually smelling of stale tortillas.  The only illumination came from a flickering neon sign outside, its faded "OPEN" casting a sickly yellow glow across the chipped Formica countertop and the dusty bottles of hairspray lining the shelves.  Tonight, however, the usual symphony of city noises – the screech of tires, the rumble of the subway – was muted, swallowed by the relentless downpour that seemed intent on washing away the already precarious existence of both the salon and Amelia herself.
