The chipped porcelain teacup trembled in Judge Abigail "Abby" Thorne's hand, the floral pattern blurring slightly under the harsh glare of the morning sun slicing through the dusty attic window.  Dust motes, illuminated like tiny galaxies, danced in the shafts of light, a fitting accompaniment to the chaotic jumble of forgotten belongings surrounding her.  The air hung thick with the scent of aged paper, mothballs, and something else, something indefinably musty and melancholic, like a forgotten memory clinging to the rafters.  This wasn't the stately courtroom of the Superior Court of Alameda County, where Abby presided with steely-eyed impartiality; this was her grandmother's house, a rambling Victorian monstrosity perched precariously on a cliff overlooking the churning Pacific, a house overflowing with secrets as vast and unpredictable as the ocean itself.  The discovery of a hidden compartment in the antique writing desk, tucked away in the shadowed corner, had brought her here, to this suffocating haven of forgotten years, and to the beginning of a case far more personal, and far more perilous, than any she'd ever encountered in her distinguished career.
