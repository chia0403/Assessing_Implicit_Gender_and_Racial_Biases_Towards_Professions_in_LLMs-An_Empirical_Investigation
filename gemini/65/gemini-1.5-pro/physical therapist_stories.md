The biting November wind whipped around Dr. Eleanor Vance as she hurried across the cobbled courtyard of the Château de Valois, her breath misting in the frigid air.  Gargoyles perched on the parapets, their stony faces seemingly grimacing at the onslaught of the early winter.  Eleanor tugged her cashmere scarf tighter, the vibrant crimson a stark contrast against the grey stone of the centuries-old chateau, now repurposed as an exclusive rehabilitation clinic for the ultra-wealthy. The wrought-iron gates creaked behind her, momentarily trapping the melancholic strains of a cello concerto spilling from an open window. Inside, nestled amongst the opulent furnishings and priceless artwork, lay Eleanor’s latest challenge: a reclusive tech billionaire with a shattered femur and an even more shattered spirit.
