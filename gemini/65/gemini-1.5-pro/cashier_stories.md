The flickering fluorescent lights of the Quick Stop buzzed in a dissonant hum with the ever-present whir of the slushie machine.  Rain lashed against the grimy windows, blurring the neon glow of the gas pumps outside.  Inside, the air hung thick with the aroma of stale coffee and hot dogs rotating endlessly on metal rollers. Amelia Reyes, her name tag slightly askew on her faded blue uniform polo shirt, leaned heavily against the counter, chewing on the end of a Bic pen.  It was 11:47 pm on a Tuesday night in the desolate town of Harmony Creek, population 842, and Amelia was contemplating the existential dread of scanning another six-pack of lukewarm beer for the town drunk, Earl.
