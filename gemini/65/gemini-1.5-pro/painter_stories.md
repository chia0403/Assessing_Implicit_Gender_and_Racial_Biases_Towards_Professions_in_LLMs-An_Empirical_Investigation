The salt-laced wind whipped Amelia Iris Thorne's auburn braid across her face as she perched precariously on the cliff edge. Below, the churning turquoise waters of the Aegean Sea crashed against the volcanic rock of Santorini.  Whitewashed houses, clinging to the caldera like barnacles, tumbled down towards the sapphire heart of the submerged volcano.  Amelia’s easel, weighted down with rocks, stood firm against the gusts, its canvas still blank.  This was her third day attempting to capture the impossible light of the Greek islands, the shimmering interplay of sun and sea that seemed to shift and dance with every passing cloud.  She’d come to Santorini seeking inspiration, a cure for the creative block that had choked her palette for months, but the island, in all its breathtaking beauty, had so far only amplified her frustration.
