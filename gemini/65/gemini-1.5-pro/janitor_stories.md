The flickering fluorescent lights of the St. Jude's Hospital basement buzzed like trapped insects, their pale glow barely cutting through the perpetual gloom.  The air hung thick with the scent of disinfectant, mingling unpleasantly with the musty odor of old concrete and something faintly metallic.  Elias Finch, his name tag slightly askew on his faded blue uniform, pushed his mop bucket across the slick floor, the squeaky wheels echoing eerily in the deserted corridor. It was 3:17 am, the quietest hour in the hospital, a time when even the ghosts seemed to sleep.  Tonight, however, the silence was different, heavier, punctuated by a rhythmic dripping sound coming from somewhere deep within the labyrinthine bowels of the building.
