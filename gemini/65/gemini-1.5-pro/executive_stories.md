Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of Amelia Thorne's forty-second-floor office, blurring the already fading twilight view of Chicago's skyline. The city lights, usually a dazzling tapestry woven across the inky canvas of night, were smeared into hazy amber streaks.  Inside, the air crackled with a nervous energy. Amelia Thorne, Chief Operating Officer of Thorne & Blackwood Investments, tapped a manicured nail against the polished mahogany of her desk, her gaze fixed on the flashing red light of her voicemail indicator.  It was almost 7 pm on a Friday, and the storm mirrored the tempest brewing within the company.  The whispers had started weeks ago, growing louder with each passing day: a hostile takeover bid, orchestrated by a shadowy consortium no one seemed to be able to identify. And Amelia, despite her crisp white suit and carefully composed demeanor, felt the icy fingers of fear tightening around her heart.
