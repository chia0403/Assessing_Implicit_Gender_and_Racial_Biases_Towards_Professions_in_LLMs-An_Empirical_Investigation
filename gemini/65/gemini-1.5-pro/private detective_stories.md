Rain lashed against the grimy windows of Jasper "Jax" O'Malley's office, mimicking the tempest brewing inside his gut.  Neon signs from the dive bar across the street painted streaks of lurid pink and sickly green across the water-stained ceiling. Jax, his tie loosened and his trench coat still dripping onto the threadbare rug, stared down at the crumpled photograph clutched in his hand.  It depicted a young woman with eyes that held a haunting mix of vulnerability and defiance.  Amelia Reyes.  Missing for three days.  Vanished from the bustling heart of 1940s New Orleans, a city teeming with secrets as dark and murky as the Mississippi River that snaked through its core.
