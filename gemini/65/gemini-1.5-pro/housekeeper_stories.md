The biting November wind howled around the eaves of Blackwood Manor, rattling the ancient windowpanes like skeletal fingers tapping for entry.  Inside, the air was thick with the scent of beeswax and lemon oil, a fragrance meticulously maintained by Elina Theresa Petrova, the manor's live-in housekeeper.  The grand hall, with its soaring ceiling and imposing portraits of stern-faced Blackwood ancestors, was dim, illuminated only by the flickering gaslight sconces and the faint, ethereal glow filtering through the stained-glass window depicting St. George slaying a remarkably unconvincing dragon.  Elina, her grey hair pulled back in a tight bun, moved with practiced efficiency, polishing the already gleaming banister with a soft cloth.  Blackwood Manor, a sprawling gothic monstrosity perched on a windswept cliff overlooking the churning North Sea, had been her domain, her prison, and her sanctuary for the past twenty years.  And in its shadowed corners, secrets whispered like the ceaseless wind.
