The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne's auburn braid across her face as she adjusted the tripod on the precipice of Black Ridge.  Below, the sleepy town of Port Blossom, Maine, nestled against the churning Atlantic, its harbor lights twinkling like scattered diamonds on black velvet.  The air, thick with the scent of brine and pine, stung Amelia's cheeks, but she barely registered it.  Her focus, laser-sharp, was trained on the approaching storm front, a bruised purple mass rolling in from the east, promising a spectacular, if volatile, sunrise.  Amelia, a photographer known for capturing the raw, untamed beauty of the New England coast, had been chasing this particular storm for three days, driven by a premonition that tonight, tonight, she would capture something extraordinary.
