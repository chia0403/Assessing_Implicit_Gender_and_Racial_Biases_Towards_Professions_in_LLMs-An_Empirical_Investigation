The humid Miami air hung heavy, even at 6:00 AM. Dew clung to the palm trees lining Ocean Drive, shimmering like a million tiny diamonds as the sun peeked over the horizon, painting the sky in streaks of vibrant coral and gold.  A rhythmic thump-thump-thump pulsed from the Art Deco facade of the Solara Hotel, vibrating through the pavement and up into the still-sleepy bodies lounging on the balconies above. Inside, in the brightly lit, mirrored studio,  Isabelle "Izzy" Rodriguez, clad in electric blue leggings and a matching sports bra, bounced on the balls of her feet, her headset microphone secured and her energy already crackling like static electricity.  Twenty-five expectant faces, a mix of tanned tourists and dedicated locals, stared back at her, their yoga mats unrolled and water bottles at the ready. Izzy grinned, a flash of white against her sun-kissed skin.  "Alright, Solara Sunrise Squad!" she boomed, her voice amplified by the speakers. "Let's get this body moving!"
