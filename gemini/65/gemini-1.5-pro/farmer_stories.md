The biting wind whipped across the frost-covered fields of Abernathy Farm, stinging Elias Thorne's cheeks raw even through his thick beard.  The sun, a pale disc barely visible through the swirling grey clouds, offered little warmth on this December morning. Elias, his breath clouding the air in front of him, surveyed his land.  The skeletal remains of his corn crop, harvested weeks ago, jutted from the frozen earth like bony fingers reaching for a sky that offered no solace. Beyond the fields, the brooding silhouette of Blackwood Forest loomed, a dark sentinel against the oppressive winter sky. This was Elias's domain, his legacy, passed down through generations of Thornes.  And this winter, a harsher one than any he could recall, threatened to break it.
