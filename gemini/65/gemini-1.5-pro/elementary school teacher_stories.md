The fluorescent lights of Mrs. Eleanor Vance's classroom hummed a monotonous B flat, a soundtrack to the chaotic symphony of second graders preparing for dismissal. Rain lashed against the tall windows of Maplewood Elementary, blurring the vibrant autumn hues of the Vermont woods beyond.  Crayon shavings dusted the linoleum floor like confetti after a particularly rambunctious party.  A half-finished papier-mâché volcano, ominously titled "Mount Doom," sat precariously on a table near the reading nook, threatening to erupt not with lava, but with glitter and Elmer's glue. Eleanor, her auburn hair pulled back in a messy bun, surveyed the scene with a mixture of weariness and affection.  This controlled chaos was her life, and most days, she loved it. But today, a prickling sense of unease settled beneath her skin, a feeling she couldn't quite place.  It started with the whispered rhymes on the playground, the ones about the old maple tree at the edge of the woods, the one the children said was haunted...
