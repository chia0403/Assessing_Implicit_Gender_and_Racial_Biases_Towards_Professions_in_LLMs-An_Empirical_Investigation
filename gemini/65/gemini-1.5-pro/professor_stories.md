The biting November wind whipped around Professor Alistair Finchley, tugging at the edges of his tweed overcoat as he hurried across the cobbled quad of St. Jude's College, Oxford.  Gargoyles leered down from the medieval stonework, their grotesque features softened by a dusting of snow. The air, crisp and carrying the faint scent of woodsmoke, vibrated with the five o'clock chime from the chapel bell tower.  Alistair clutched a worn leather satchel overflowing with student essays, his breath puffing out in white clouds.  The light was already fading, casting long, skeletal shadows across the manicured lawns, and a premature darkness was settling over the ancient university.  He was late for his weekly meeting with the Bibliophiles, a clandestine society dedicated to the study of obscure and often forbidden texts, a meeting that promised to be particularly intriguing given the cryptic note he'd received that morning.
