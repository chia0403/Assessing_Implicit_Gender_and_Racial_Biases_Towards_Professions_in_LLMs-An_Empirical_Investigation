The flickering neon sign of "Shear Perfection" buzzed intermittently, casting a purplish glow across the rain-slicked street. Inside, Amelia "Amy" Dubois sighed, watching rivulets of water race each other down the salon window.  It was 7:52 pm on a Tuesday in late October, the kind of night that burrowed deep into your bones with a damp chill.  The usually bustling Clifton Heights neighborhood of Cincinnati was eerily quiet, the only sounds the hum of the faulty sign and the rhythmic swoosh of the windshield wipers on Amy’s vintage Vespa parked out front.  Amy, with her vibrant turquoise-streaked bob and an array of silver rings adorning her fingers, was starting to think she should just close up shop.  No walk-ins all evening, and her last appointment, Mrs. Henderson with her predictable perm, had left an hour ago, leaving behind the faint scent of ammonia and a twenty-dollar tip.  Just as Amy reached for the “Open” sign to flip it over, the bell above the door chimed, announcing a late arrival.
