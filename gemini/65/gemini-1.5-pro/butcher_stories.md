The cleaver, honed to a razor's edge, flashed in the dim, gas-lit confines of Muldoon's Meats.  Elias Finch, his apron stained crimson and ochre, hefted the heavy blade, its weight familiar and comforting in his calloused hand.  Outside, the cobbled streets of Gaslight Row, choked with a perpetual fog that clung to the damp brick buildings like a shroud, echoed with the cries of street vendors and the rumble of passing carriages. The year was 1888, and the shadows of Whitechapel, London, held a chill deeper than any November frost.
