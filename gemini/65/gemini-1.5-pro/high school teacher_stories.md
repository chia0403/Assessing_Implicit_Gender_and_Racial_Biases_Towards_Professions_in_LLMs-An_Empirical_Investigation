The biting November wind whipped around Eleanor Vance’s ankles as she hurried across the frost-covered parking lot of Northwood High.  Her breath plumed out in ghostly puffs, momentarily obscuring the looming brick façade of the school, a hulking behemoth that seemed to absorb all light and warmth.  Above the main entrance, the school motto, "Lux et Veritas" – Light and Truth – seemed ironically out of place in the pre-dawn gloom.  Eleanor clutched her thermos of lukewarm coffee tighter, a small, fragile source of comfort against the creeping cold. Inside, the fluorescent lights of the English department office buzzed, awaiting another day of Shakespearean analyses and grammar debates. But this Tuesday morning felt different.  A strange tension hung in the air, a palpable unease that Eleanor couldn't quite place, like the quiet before a storm.
