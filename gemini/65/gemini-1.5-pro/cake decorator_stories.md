The scent of burnt sugar hung heavy in the air, a stark contrast to the usual buttercream-and-rose perfume that permeated "Sugar & Bloom."  Eliza Jane Hollingsworth, her brow furrowed in concentration, stared intently at the scorched edges of the caramel she'd been attempting to spin into a delicate web for Judge Patterson's infamous Black Forest gateau. Outside, the late afternoon sun cast long shadows across the cobbled streets of Ashworth, a quaint New England town where gossip travelled faster than the horse-drawn carriages that clattered along Main Street.  Eliza Jane, known throughout Ashworth for her exquisite cake creations and equally sharp wit, had a reputation to uphold. This particular cake, however, seemed determined to test both her patience and her prowess.
