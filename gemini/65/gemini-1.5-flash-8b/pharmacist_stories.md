The flickering fluorescent lights of the "Apothecary's Corner" hummed a monotonous tune, a counterpoint to the rhythmic *click-clack* of Amelia Nightingale's heels against the worn linoleum floor.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of afternoon sun piercing the grime-coated window, illuminating the rows of meticulously organized bottles, jars, and tinctures.  Amelia, a pharmacist with a name whispered with reverence in the small, tight-knit community of Willow Creek, carefully labelled a batch of newly-mixed cough syrup.  Her brow, usually smooth and unblemished, was furrowed with a concentration that belied the calm expression she maintained.  Today, something felt…off.  Not just a single, isolated anomaly, but an unsettling sense of unease that clung to the air like the scent of lingering disinfectant.  Willow Creek, usually a picture of placid, quiet contentment, felt…charged.
