The scent of lilies, jasmine, and something faintly metallic hung heavy in the air, clinging to the chipped paint of Amelia Nightingale's tiny shop, "Blooms by the Bay."  Rain hammered against the corrugated iron awning, a relentless percussion that mirrored the frantic rhythm of Amelia's own heart.  Outside, the grey sky mirrored the gloom that had settled over the small coastal town of Seabreeze, a town that usually hummed with the cheerful clamor of tourists and the gentle rhythm of the waves.  Today, however, only the insistent drumming of rain against the weathered wood of the shop's front door was audible. Amelia, her dark hair pulled back in a tight bun, furiously arranged a bouquet of bruised purple irises, each stem like a weeping willow against the sudden chill in the air.
