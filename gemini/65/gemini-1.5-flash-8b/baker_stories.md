The scent of yeast and cinnamon, thick and sweet, hung heavy in the air of the tiny bakery, "The Golden Crust."  Rain lashed against the narrow, cobbled street outside, mirroring the frantic rhythm of Amelia Blackwood's hands as she kneaded the dough.  The tiny shop, tucked between a bustling apothecary and a weathered bookshop, was a haven of warmth and comforting aromas, a sanctuary from the relentless grey of the perpetually overcast city of Aethelgard.  Amelia, her auburn braid escaping its careful plait, pushed a stray strand of hair behind her ear, her brow furrowed in concentration.  Today's order was particularly complex, a dozen intricate pastries for the upcoming Lord Valerian's charity ball, and every ripple in the dough, every delicate swirl of icing, felt charged with the weight of expectation.
