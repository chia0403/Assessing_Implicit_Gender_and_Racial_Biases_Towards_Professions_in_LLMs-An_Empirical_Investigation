The flickering fluorescent lights of the abandoned factory hummed a mournful tune, a counterpoint to the rhythmic drip, drip, drip of water somewhere deep within the bowels of the building.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of pale sunlight slicing through a broken window, illuminating a scene of rusted machinery and forgotten dreams.  This was where Elias Thorne, electrician extraordinaire, found himself, a lone figure navigating the labyrinthine corridors of "The Ironclad," a once-mighty machine manufacturing hub now swallowed by the creeping encroachment of urban decay.  Elias, a man weathered by years spent wrestling with wires and volts, carried his worn toolbox like a shield against the encroaching gloom, his gaze sharp and focused on the task at hand: fixing the power supply to the old pneumatic hammer, a relic of a bygone era.
