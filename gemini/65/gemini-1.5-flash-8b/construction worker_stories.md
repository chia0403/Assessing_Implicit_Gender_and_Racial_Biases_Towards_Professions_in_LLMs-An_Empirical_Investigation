The biting wind whipped across the exposed steel skeleton of the skyscraper, stinging Elias Thorne's cheeks.  Dust, kicked up by the passing crane, swirled around him like a miniature, angry tornado.  Below, the sprawling city of Veridia shimmered in the late afternoon sun, a glittering tapestry of glass and concrete.  Elias, a seasoned construction worker with calloused hands and eyes that held the hard-won wisdom of a thousand building sites, gripped the rusted metal ladder, his breath misting in the frigid air. He was working on the 25th floor of the Zenith Tower, a project so ambitious it seemed to defy gravity.  For Elias, the relentless climb was a daily ritual, a rhythm that had become as familiar as the rhythmic clang of the cranes or the rumble of the distant subway.  Today, however, something felt different.  A tremor ran through him, an unease that went beyond the usual aches and pains of a hard day's work.  The world tilted slightly, and he almost lost his footing.
