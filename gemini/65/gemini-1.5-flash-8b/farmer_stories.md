The biting wind, a relentless harbinger of winter, whipped across the parched earth of the Whisperwind Valley.  Dust devils, the colour of burnt umber, danced across the cracked furrows of Elias Thornewood's farm, the once vibrant emerald now a faded, skeletal green.  Elias, a man weathered like the ancient stone walls surrounding his farmhouse, squinted into the grey sky.  He’d been a farmer all his life, a life woven into the very fabric of the valley, its rhythms and seasons marked by the turning of the soil, the rustling of leaves, and the rhythmic bleating of sheep. But this year… this year felt different.  The whispers, the strange, chilling whispers, had begun.  And they were getting louder.
