The biting wind whipped Amelia "Amy" Blackwood's trench coat around her legs, stinging her cheeks as she hunched deeper into the skeletal frame of the abandoned warehouse.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of pale sunlight piercing the grime-coated windows, illuminating a scene that seemed ripped from a forgotten history book: rusted machinery, splintered wood, and the ghostly echo of a forgotten era.  This was the heart of the forgotten industrial district, a place where the city's relentless march forward had left behind its broken promises and discarded dreams.  Amy, a seasoned reporter for the *Chronicle*, was here to investigate the disappearance of the local archivist, Elias Thorne, a man whose meticulously catalogued history of the district held a peculiar allure, a siren song of lost stories for the city's forgotten corners.  The cold, grey air, thick with the smell of damp earth and decay, seemed to press in on her, mirroring the growing unease in her stomach.  She pulled her worn leather notebook closer, the crisp feel of the paper a small comfort in the encroaching darkness.
