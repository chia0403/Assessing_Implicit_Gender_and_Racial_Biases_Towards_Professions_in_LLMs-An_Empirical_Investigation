The biting wind whipped Amelia Blackwood’s camera bag tighter against her chest, the icy rain stinging her cheeks like a thousand tiny needles.  The cobbled streets of Prague, normally a symphony of life and laughter, were eerily quiet, shrouded in a thick, grey fog that clung to the towering spires of the old town like a shroud.  Amelia, her breath puffing out in white clouds, hunched further into her worn leather coat, her fingers tightening around the cold metal of her Leica.  Today was supposed to be the day she finally captured the essence of the city's legendary "Midnight Hour."  A mythical time, they said, when the shadows danced and whispered secrets to the wind.  But the relentless fog was stealing the city’s soul, and with it, the atmosphere she so desperately sought.  Her assignment for the Prague Chronicle hung heavy in her mind: to document this elusive period.  The photo editor, a notoriously demanding man named Mr. Krupnik, wouldn't be pleased.
