The scent of beeswax and simmering chamomile hung heavy in the air of Amelia Blackwood's workshop, a cramped space nestled between a cobbler's stall and a spice merchant's cart in the bustling marketplace of Veridia.  Sunlight, fractured by the surrounding buildings, painted the dust motes dancing in the air with fleeting gold.  Amelia, her name whispered amongst the traders as a synonym for exquisite tailoring, hunched over her worktable.  Frayed thread snagged on a delicate ivory silk, a material shimmering with captured moonlight, mirrored the tension in her shoulders.  Around her, the clamor of Veridia – bartering voices, the rhythmic clang of blacksmith's hammers, the squeals of excited children – was a constant, insistent hum.  Today, however, it seemed to resonate with a particular, anxious pulse. The bustling market, usually a symphony of life, felt oppressive, heavy with unspoken anxieties.  Amelia, her fingers nimble as a wren's, struggled to ignore the growing unease.
