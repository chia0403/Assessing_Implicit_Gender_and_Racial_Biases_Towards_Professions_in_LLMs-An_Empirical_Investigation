The crimson sun, already sinking below the jagged peaks of the Copper Crag Mountains, painted the gravel pit a fiery orange.  Dust devils danced across the vast expanse, swirling like phantoms around Amelia "Mac" MacIntosh, the heavy equipment operator.  Mac, her face a roadmap of sun-baked lines, squinted at the increasingly shadowed horizon.  The air, thick with the metallic tang of crushed rock and the faint whisper of wind whistling through the canyons, vibrated with the low thrum of the Caterpillar D9R, her loyal companion, groaning under the weight of another mountainside.  This was her domain, the brutal, beautiful heartland of the Colorado outback, a landscape sculpted by time and tempered by the relentless, unforgiving sun.
