Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of the "Rusty Mug," a greasy spoon diner clinging precariously to the edge of the forgotten mining town of Ash Creek.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of light slicing through the gloom, illuminating the chipped Formica countertop and the weary faces of the regulars.  Elias Thorne, a lanky young man with eyes the colour of faded pennies, wiped down a sticky table with practiced efficiency.  He was a server, and, at 22, already felt the weight of the town's quiet desperation settling on his shoulders like a persistent fog.
