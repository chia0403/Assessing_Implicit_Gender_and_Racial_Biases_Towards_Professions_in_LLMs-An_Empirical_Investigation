The fluorescent lights of the law firm hummed, a monotonous counterpoint to the rhythmic tap-tap-tap of Amelia Hayes's fingers on the keyboard.  Dust motes danced in the lone shaft of afternoon sun slicing through the grimy window of her cramped office, illuminating a swirling vortex of legal documents – contracts, depositions, court filings – that seemed to grow with each passing hour.  Amelia, a paralegal at the prestigious (but notoriously cutthroat) firm of Sterling & Sterling, was buried under a mountain of paperwork, the relentless pressure a familiar weight on her shoulders.  Her meticulously organized files, meticulously color-coded, served as a fragile defense against the chaos threatening to engulf her.  The faint scent of stale coffee and legal pads hung heavy in the air, a constant reminder of the long hours and countless cases she'd navigated within these walls.  Today, however, the pressure felt different, laced with a thread of unease that she couldn't quite place.
