Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of Amelia Kensington's office, mirroring the tempest brewing within her.  The city of Aethelgard, nestled between the jagged peaks of the Whisper Mountains and the shimmering Azure Sea, was a symphony of muted grey this November.  Aethelgard, a metropolis renowned for its innovative architecture, felt strangely claustrophobic tonight.  Amelia, a rising star in the city's esteemed architectural firm, "Skyline Structures," stared out at the deluge, her brow furrowed in concentration.  Across her desk, blueprints of the "Aetherium Tower," a project Amelia had poured her heart and soul into, lay scattered, soaked through, their vibrant lines now smudged and barely discernible.  Her meticulously crafted design for the world's tallest sustainable structure, meant to house both residential and commercial spaces, seemed as fragile and fleeting as the downpour outside.
