The flickering gaslight cast long, dancing shadows across the chipped Formica counter, illuminating the swirling chaos of Amelia "Amy" Thistlewick's kitchen.  Flour dusted the air like a fine snow, clinging to her apron and the unruly curls escaping from her messy bun.  A symphony of sizzling bacon, clanging pans, and the rhythmic thump-thump-thump of her wooden spoon against the bottom of a copper pot filled the small, cramped space.  Outside, the wind howled a mournful dirge against the weathered clapboard walls of the boarding house, a stark contrast to the vibrant culinary storm brewing within. Amy, her face flushed with exertion and a hint of frantic energy, was preparing a feast for the month's end potluck.  It was a ritual, a small act of rebellion against the grey, predictable monotony of life in the forsaken mining town of Emberton, nestled deep in the treacherous foothills of the Whisper Mountains.
