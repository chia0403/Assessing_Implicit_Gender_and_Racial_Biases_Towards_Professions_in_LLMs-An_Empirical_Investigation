The biting wind whipped Dr. Eleanor Ainsworth's scarf across her face as she wrestled with the recalcitrant lock on the wrought iron gates. The setting was Bleakridge Manor, a gothic monstrosity perched precariously on the windswept cliffs of the Cornish coast. Tonight, the manor wasn't just a picturesque (if decaying) landmark; it was Eleanor's temporary office, her classroom, and her prison. The "Bleakridge Manor: History and Hauntings" seminar, a wild experiment dreamt up by the university to boost enrollment, had sounded infinitely more appealing in the fluorescent glow of the department meeting room. Now, surrounded by the churning grey of the Atlantic and the creaking groans of the ancient house, she felt a prickle of unease that had nothing to do with drafts.
