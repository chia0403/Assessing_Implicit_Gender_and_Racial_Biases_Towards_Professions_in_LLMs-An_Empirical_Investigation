The aroma of industrial-strength coffee and stale chalk dust clung to Miss Evelyn Reed like a second skin. It was a scent she'd come to both despise and find oddly comforting over her seven years teaching American Literature at Northwood High, a sprawling brick behemoth that looked more like a prison than a place of learning, especially on a Monday morning. Rain hammered against the grimy windows of her classroom, mimicking the frantic beat of Evelyn's own heart as she surveyed the battlefield before her. Twenty-eight surly teenagers slumped in mismatched desks, their faces illuminated by the pale glow of their phones, already tuned out before the bell even threatened to ring. Evelyn sighed, pushing a stray wisp of auburn hair behind her ear. This was going to be a long day.
