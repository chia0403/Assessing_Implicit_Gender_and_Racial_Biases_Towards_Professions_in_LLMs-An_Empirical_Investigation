The chipped paint of the "Welcome to Crestwood Middle School" sign flaked onto Dr. Eleanor Vance's sensible brown shoes as she navigated the chaotic pre-bell rush.  A cacophony of slamming lockers, shrill laughter, and the insistent blare of a misplaced phone ringtone assaulted her already weary senses. The air hung thick with the scent of overripe bananas and teenage angst, a familiar aroma that clung to the building like a second skin.  Crestwood Middle, nestled in the heart of the perpetually overcast town of Havenwood, Oregon, wasn't exactly a picture of idyllic suburban life.  It was, however, Eleanor’s professional battlefield, a place where she’d spent the last decade mediating playground squabbles, deciphering cryptic doodles on bathroom stalls, and trying, with varying degrees of success, to unravel the tangled webs of adolescent emotions. Today promised to be no different.  In fact, the ominous weight of Principal Henderson's urgent email still sat heavy in her stomach, a premonition of the turbulent day that lay ahead.
