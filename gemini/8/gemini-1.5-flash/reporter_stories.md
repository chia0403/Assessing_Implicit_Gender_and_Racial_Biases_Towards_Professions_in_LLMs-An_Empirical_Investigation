The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Mia” Hernandez’s trench coat around her legs as she hunched deeper into the shadowed alleyway.  Rain, a relentless drizzle that had been falling since dawn, slicked the cobblestones, reflecting the sickly yellow glow of a single, flickering streetlamp.  The air hung heavy with the scent of damp earth and something else, something acrid and faintly metallic that pricked at the back of her throat. This wasn't the charming, tourist-friendly side of Prague Mia usually covered for the *Prague Post*; this was the city's underbelly, a labyrinth of narrow passageways and forgotten corners where secrets festered and shadows held their breath.  Tonight, Mia was chasing a whisper, a rumour that had slithered out of the city’s murky depths: the reappearance of the  "Clockwork Killer," a phantom whose victims were as intricately assembled as the city’s famous astronomical clock, but far less beautiful.
