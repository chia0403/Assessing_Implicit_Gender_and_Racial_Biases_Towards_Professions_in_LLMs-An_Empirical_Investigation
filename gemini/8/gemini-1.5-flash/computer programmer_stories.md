The fluorescent lights of Anya Sharma’s cubicle hummed a monotonous tune, a counterpoint to the frantic clatter of her keyboard.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of the twenty-seventh floor, blurring the cityscape of Seattle into a hazy watercolour.  Anya, a lean woman with perpetually tired eyes and a caffeine-fueled intensity, squinted at the swirling lines of code on her monitor.  The air hung thick with the scent of stale coffee and the low thrum of a thousand servers working tirelessly in the bowels of the building.  Tonight, however, the usual comforting hum of technological efficiency felt more like a malevolent heartbeat.  Anya’s deadline loomed – a deadline that wasn’t just about meeting a project milestone, but about something far more sinister; something that had her fingers flying across the keys with a blend of fear and grim determination.
