The biting November wind whipped Amelia Katherine Hayes's auburn hair across her face as she hurried down Wall Street, the frantic rhythm of her heels a counterpoint to the city's relentless hum.  Rain, slick and unforgiving, plastered her tailored Burberry coat to her form, mirroring the tension tightening in her chest.  The skyscrapers, gothic giants clawing at the bruised twilight sky, seemed to loom even larger tonight, their glass facades reflecting the frantic, flickering lights of the trading floor she was about to enter.  Inside, the air hung thick with the scent of stale coffee, ambition, and the faint, metallic tang of fear – a familiar aroma to Amelia, a scent she’d learned to both tolerate and weaponize during her ten years at Sterling Hayes & Co., a firm ironically bearing a name that now felt less like a legacy and more like a looming, inescapable shadow.
