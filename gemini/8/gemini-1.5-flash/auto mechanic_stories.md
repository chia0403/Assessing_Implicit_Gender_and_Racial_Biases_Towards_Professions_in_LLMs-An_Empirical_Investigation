The greasy smell of burnt oil and ozone hung heavy in the air, a familiar perfume to Elias Thorne.  He wiped his brow with the back of a hand already stained with grime the color of motor oil mixed with brake dust.  Outside, the relentless Arizona sun beat down on the corrugated iron roof of Thorne's Auto Repair, baking the desert landscape into a shimmering haze.  Dust devils, miniature tornadoes of grit and sand, danced across the cracked asphalt of the yard, where discarded tires and rusting engine blocks lay scattered like fallen soldiers.  Inside, the air conditioning wheezed its last gasp, a counterpoint to the rhythmic clang of Elias's wrench against a stubborn bolt.  It was the kind of heat that seeped into your bones, a heat that Elias, despite having lived in the unforgiving desert his entire life, never quite seemed to adjust to.  But then again, neither did he seem to adjust to the relentless stream of broken-down vehicles that perpetually rolled into his yard, each a new challenge, a new puzzle to be solved with his worn-out tools and his equally worn-out patience.
