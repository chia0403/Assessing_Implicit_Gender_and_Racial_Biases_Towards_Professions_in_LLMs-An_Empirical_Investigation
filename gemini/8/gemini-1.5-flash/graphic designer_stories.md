The chipped mug warmed Amelia Stone's fingers, the lukewarm chamomile doing little to soothe the frantic fluttering in her stomach.  Rain lashed against the panoramic window of her Soho apartment, blurring the already hazy cityscape into a watercolour wash of greys and blacks.  Amelia, a graphic designer with a penchant for bold typography and an aversion to early mornings, stared out at the storm, her reflection – a pale, slightly stressed version of herself – superimposed over the chaotic scene.  The deadline for the Blackwood & Sons rebranding loomed, a monstrous, pixelated kraken threatening to drag her under.  Her meticulously organised desk, usually a testament to her controlled aesthetic, was currently a battlefield of scattered sketches, crumpled paper, and half-empty coffee cups, a chaotic counterpoint to the serene calm she desperately craved.
