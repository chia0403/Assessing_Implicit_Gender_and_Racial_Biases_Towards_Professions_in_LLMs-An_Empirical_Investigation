The salt spray stung Elias Thorne's face as he hauled the last of the day's catch – a glistening, stubborn halibut – onto the deck of the *Sea Serpent*.  The late afternoon sun, a bruised plum sinking into the churning grey waters of the North Atlantic, cast long shadows across the small fishing boat.  Elias, his hands raw and smelling powerfully of fish and brine, wiped his forehead with the back of a flour-dusted sleeve. The air hung thick with the tang of the ocean and the promise of a hearty stew, a stark contrast to the biting wind that whipped his dark, sea-tossed hair.  His tiny, weather-beaten galley, barely larger than a broom cupboard, awaited him, a haven of warmth and the comforting scent of simmering spices perched precariously on the edge of the churning sea.
