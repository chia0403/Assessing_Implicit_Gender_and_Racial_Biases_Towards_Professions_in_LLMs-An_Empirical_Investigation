The fluorescent hum of the office was a counterpoint to the frantic drumming of Amelia Grace Hawthorne's fingers on her mahogany desk.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of her 37th-floor office, blurring the cityscape of Chicago into a grey, impressionistic painting.  Amelia, a woman whose life was meticulously ordered – down to the precisely aligned pens in her holder – felt a tremor of unease far stronger than the shuddering of the skyscraper in the wind.  The city, usually a comforting backdrop to her world of spreadsheets and tax returns, felt ominous tonight, mirroring the unsettling contents of the encrypted email sitting unopened in her inbox.  It was from a client she’d never heard of, a cryptic message about a transaction so large it threatened to eclipse the entire Chicago Mercantile Exchange.
