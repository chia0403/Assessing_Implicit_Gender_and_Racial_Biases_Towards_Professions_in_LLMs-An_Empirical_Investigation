The salt spray stung Dr. Eleanor Vance's face as she wrestled the stubborn kayak onto the rocky shore.  The late afternoon sun cast long shadows across the jagged cliffs of the Isle of Skye, painting the turbulent Atlantic in fiery hues of orange and crimson.  The wind, a relentless, howling banshee, whipped her dark hair across her eyes, threatening to snatch away the battered clipboard clutched in her gloved hand.  Eleanor, a physical therapist renowned throughout the Highlands for her unconventional methods and even more unconventional patience, shivered, not entirely from the cold.  The air, thick with the scent of seaweed and peat smoke, held a tension that mirrored the turmoil brewing within her.  Her latest patient, a craggy-faced fisherman named Hamish MacIntyre, had been less than cooperative during their last session, a fact that weighed heavily on her as she began the trek back to her small, stone cottage perched precariously on the cliff edge.  This case, she knew, was going to be more challenging than any she'd faced before.
