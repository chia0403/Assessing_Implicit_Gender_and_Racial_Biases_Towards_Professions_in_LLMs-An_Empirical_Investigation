The chipped ceramic mug warmed Amelia Katherine Finch’s hands, a meager comfort against the pre-dawn chill clinging to the air-conditioned office.  Outside, the city of Birmingham still slumbered, a muffled hum of distant traffic the only indication of the waking world.  Amelia, however, was wide awake, the fluorescent lights of Blackwood & Sons’ reception area casting a harsh, pale glow on her neatly-styled auburn hair and the meticulously organized paperwork spread across her desk.  The aroma of stale coffee and printer toner hung heavy in the air, a familiar perfume to Amelia, who had inhaled its pungent blend for the past seven years, each inhalation another silent testament to her unwavering dedication to a job that often felt more like a gilded cage than a career.  Today, however, felt different.  A nervous energy, sharp and unsettling, prickled at the edges of her composure.  A feeling that something – or someone – was about to disrupt the carefully constructed routine of her life.
