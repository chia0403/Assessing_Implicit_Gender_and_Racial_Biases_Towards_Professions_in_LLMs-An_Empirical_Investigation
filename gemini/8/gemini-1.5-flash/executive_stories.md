The mahogany desk gleamed under the harsh fluorescent lights, a stark contrast to the storm raging outside.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of Amelia Katherine Hayes's 72nd-floor office, each gust a frantic percussion accompanying the rhythmic tap-tap-tap of her perfectly manicured nails against the polished surface.  Amelia, Executive Vice President of Hayes Industries – a company her grandfather had built from a single, dusty workshop – stared out at the city, a concrete jungle now shrouded in a violent, grey curtain. The weight of a multi-million dollar deal, one that could either catapult her family's legacy into a new stratosphere or shatter it irrevocably, pressed down on her like the unforgiving atmosphere. The scent of expensive, dark roast coffee, barely touched in its bone china cup, did little to soothe the churning anxiety in her stomach.
