The salt spray stung Dr. Elias Thorne's face as he wrestled the stubborn fishing boat's tiller.  The late afternoon sun, a bruised plum sinking towards the turbulent Atlantic, cast long, skeletal shadows across the churning grey water.  The air hung thick with the smell of brine and diesel, a familiar perfume to Elias, a scent that usually brought a sense of peace, but today only intensified the knot of anxiety tightening in his chest.  He was miles from the comforting lights of the mainland, miles from the sterile calm of his clinic in the small coastal town of Port Blossom, and a world away from the reason he was out here, battling a relentless sea in a borrowed fishing trawler: the desperate hope of saving a life.
