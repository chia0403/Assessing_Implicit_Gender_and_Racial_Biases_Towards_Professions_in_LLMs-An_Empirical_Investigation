The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Katherine Finch's hands, a meager comfort against the biting November wind whistling through the gaps in the boarded-up windows of the derelict showroom.  Dust motes, illuminated by the single flickering fluorescent light above, danced in the stagnant air, a silent ballet to the melancholic rhythm of rain drumming against the corrugated iron roof.  Amelia, or Amy as most people called her – though she preferred the formality lately – ran a hand through her already dishevelled auburn hair, the scent of stale coffee and despair clinging to it like a second skin.  This wasn't exactly the opulent, climate-controlled environment she’d envisioned when she’d first taken on the commission to sell “The Serenity Suite,” a ludicrously over-priced collection of bespoke, hand-carved furniture destined for… well, she wasn’t entirely sure anymore.  The potential client, a notoriously eccentric recluse named Mr. Silas Blackwood, had vanished without a trace three weeks ago, leaving Amy stranded in the crumbling husk of his former grandeur, a single, stubbornly blooming rose bush the only hint of life amidst the encroaching decay.
