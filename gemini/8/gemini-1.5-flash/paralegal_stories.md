The fluorescent lights of Miller & Zois hummed a monotonous tune, a soundtrack to Amelia Katherine Hayes's meticulously organized world.  Amelia, or Amy as most people – except her intimidatingly stern boss, Mr. Zois – called her, tapped a manicured fingernail against the cool, glass surface of her desk.  The air hung thick with the scent of old paper, stale coffee, and the faint, almost imperceptible, aroma of anxiety.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of the 27th-floor office, blurring the city skyline into a hazy watercolor of greys and blues, a fitting backdrop to the storm brewing in Amy's inbox.  A mountain of files, each promising a tangled web of legal complexities, threatened to engulf her.  Tonight, however, it wasn't the mountain that worried her; it was the single, unmarked manila envelope sitting conspicuously on top.  Inside, she suspected, lay the key to a case that could either make her career, or break it entirely.
