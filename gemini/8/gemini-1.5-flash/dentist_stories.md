The biting November wind howled a mournful tune around the gabled roof of Dr. Alistair Finch’s dental practice, rattling the frosted windowpanes like skeletal fingers.  Inside, however, the atmosphere was a carefully cultivated oasis of warmth. The scent of cinnamon and clove, a subtle counterpoint to the ever-present antiseptic tang, hung in the air.  A crackling fire in the hearth cast dancing shadows across the polished oak floors of the waiting room, illuminating a collection of antique medical instruments displayed in glass cases – a rather morbid, yet strangely comforting, testament to the history of Alistair Finch’s family practice, established in the quaint, coastal village of Port Blossom since 1888.  Outside, the grey North Sea churned restlessly, a stark contrast to the peaceful calm Alistair cultivated within his four walls.  Tonight, however, even his meticulously maintained sanctuary felt threatened by the storm brewing both outside and, he suspected, within.
