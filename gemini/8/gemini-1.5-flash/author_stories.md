The salt spray stung Amelia Katherine Blackwood's face, a familiar sting that mirrored the prickle of anxiety crawling beneath her skin.  The wind, a relentless, howling banshee, whipped her auburn hair across her face as she stood at the precipice of the craggy cliffs overlooking the churning grey expanse of the Atlantic. Below, the waves crashed against the jagged rocks with a violence that echoed the tempest brewing within her.  Amelia, a successful author of historical romances known for their sun-drenched Mediterranean settings, found herself utterly out of her element on this windswept, unforgiving stretch of the Cornish coast. The grey stone cottage, perched precariously on the cliff edge, was less a refuge and more a prison, its small, shuttered windows staring out at a sea that seemed to mock her with its endless, turbulent power. This was supposed to be her writer's retreat, her escape from the relentless demands of deadlines and publicity tours.  Instead, it felt like the perfect place to unravel.
