The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Amy” Hernandez’s dark hair across her face as she wrestled the heavy canvas door of the ambulance open.  The air, thick with the smell of woodsmoke and something acrid and unsettling, clawed at her lungs.  Around her, the sprawling, dilapidated houses of Oakhaven, Pennsylvania, huddled together like frightened animals under a bruised, twilight sky.  This wasn't the picturesque postcard town the brochures promised; Oakhaven, tonight, felt more like a forgotten corner of the world, clinging to the edge of a precipice.  Amy, a seasoned paramedic with five years under her belt at Allegheny County EMS, was used to the grit and grime of emergency calls, but even she felt a prickle of unease as she surveyed the scene before her: a flickering porch light, a cluster of worried faces, and the chilling silence broken only by the mournful sigh of the wind.
