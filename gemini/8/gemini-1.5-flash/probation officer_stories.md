The chipped paint of the probation office’s waiting room peeled like sunburnt skin, mirroring the slow, agonizing unraveling of Elias Thorne’s patience.  Elias, a man whose neatly pressed shirts and perpetually tired eyes spoke volumes about the impossible weight of his job, sighed, the sound swallowed by the muted hum of the fluorescent lights.  It was a Tuesday, the kind of Tuesday that began with a lukewarm coffee and the lingering scent of stale cigarettes, and promised to end with the gnawing feeling of inadequacy that clung to him like a second skin.  The rain outside lashed against the grimy windows of the city’s oldest municipal building, mirroring the storm brewing within the hearts of the individuals he was tasked with overseeing.  This wasn't the picturesque, reformed-criminal-finds-redemption storyline he'd imagined when he'd graduated top of his class; this was the grit, the grime, and the relentless, wearying battle against the inevitable.  Today's caseload included a repeat offender with a penchant for arson, a young mother struggling with addiction, and a man whose rage simmered just beneath the surface of his forced politeness.  Elias adjusted his tie, the knot feeling as constricting as the responsibilities he carried.
