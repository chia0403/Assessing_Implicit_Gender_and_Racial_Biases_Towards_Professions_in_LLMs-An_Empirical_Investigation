The humid air hung heavy, thick with the scent of frying plantains and diesel fumes.  Cicadas buzzed a relentless, rhythmic chorus, competing with the insistent honking of taxis weaving through the chaotic streets of Santo Domingo.  Dr. Elena Ramirez, a nutritionist with a perpetually windblown cascade of dark curls and eyes the color of warm honey, adjusted her linen blouse, the damp fabric clinging uncomfortably to her skin.  She’d arrived in the Dominican Republic just three days ago, a whirlwind of jet lag and nervous anticipation swirling around her like the dust devils that danced on the cracked pavements.  This wasn’t the sterile, climate-controlled environment of her Boston clinic.  This was…real.  This was where the fight for healthy eating truly began.
