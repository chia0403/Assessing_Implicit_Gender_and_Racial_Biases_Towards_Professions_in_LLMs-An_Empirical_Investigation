The salt spray kissed Amelia Katherine Reed’s face as she wrestled the stubborn latch on the weathered gate.  Seagulls wheeled overhead, their cries a discordant soundtrack to the rhythmic crash of waves against the craggy cliffs of Cornwall.  The late afternoon sun, a bruised plum in the rapidly darkening sky, cast long, skeletal shadows across the windswept headland.  Amelia, a real estate agent whose tailored blazer was doing its best to maintain its composure against the relentless gale, adjusted her sensible, but stylish, ankle boots and sighed.  This wasn’t exactly the brochure-perfect setting she was used to showcasing.  But then again,  “Cliffside Serenity,” the name of the property she was about to view, wasn’t exactly your average coastal cottage.  It was, to put it mildly, intriguing. And possibly, terrifying.
