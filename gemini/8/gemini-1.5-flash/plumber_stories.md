The wrench slipped, a metallic shriek slicing through the pre-dawn quiet of Oakhaven, a town clinging stubbornly to the edge of the whispering pines.  A plume of rusty water erupted, spraying Amelia "Amy" Hawthorne,  a woman whose calloused hands and grease-stained overalls belied the surprisingly delicate curve of her jawline, across the face and chest.  Amy swore, a low rumble that disappeared as quickly as it arrived, swallowed by the vast, echoing silence of the old Victorian mansion looming behind her.  The air hung thick with the smell of damp earth, decaying wood, and the faint, metallic tang of blood – a smell that, oddly enough, was far more familiar to Amy than the scent of roses that supposedly graced the manicured lawns of Oakhaven’s wealthier residents. This was no ordinary plumbing job.
