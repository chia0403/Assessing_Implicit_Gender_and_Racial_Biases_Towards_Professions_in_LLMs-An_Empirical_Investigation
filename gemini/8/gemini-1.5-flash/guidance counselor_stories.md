The chipped paint of the "Guidance Counselor" sign on Ms. Eleanor Vance's door seemed to mirror the cracks forming in the foundation of Northwood High.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows overlooking the perpetually muddy football field, a fitting backdrop for the tempest brewing inside.  The air hung heavy with the scent of stale coffee and teenage angst, a familiar perfume Eleanor had grown accustomed to over her fifteen years at the school.  The fluorescent lights hummed a monotonous tune, a counterpoint to the frantic scratching of a pen against paper – Eleanor’s own, as she meticulously filled out yet another referral form for a student spiraling into a crisis she felt woefully unequipped to handle.  The weight of a hundred unspoken anxieties pressed down on her, heavier than the November air.  Northwood High wasn't just a school; it was a pressure cooker, and Eleanor, its weary guardian angel, was beginning to feel the steam.
