The chipped paint of the “For Sale” sign flaked onto Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s sensible beige pumps as a gust of wind, smelling faintly of brine and decay, whipped around her.  Amelia, a woman whose life was meticulously organized in spreadsheets and categorized by risk assessments, felt a prickle of unease that had nothing to do with the crumbling clapboard of the beachfront property before her.  The ocean, usually a calming presence, churned restlessly against the shore, mirroring the turmoil churning within her.  This wasn't just another coastal property assessment for Hawthorne & Sons Insurance; this was personal.  The house, with its shadowed windows and overgrown garden, whispered secrets she wasn't sure she wanted to hear, secrets wrapped in the salty air and the mournful cry of gulls overhead.  The impending storm, both meteorological and metaphorical, felt poised to break.
