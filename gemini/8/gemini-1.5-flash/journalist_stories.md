The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Mia” Hernandez’s auburn hair across her face as she hunched deeper into her threadbare coat.  Rain, sleeting with icy fury, plastered her trench coat to her back, making the already arduous climb up the treacherous, moss-slicked steps of Blackwood Manor even more precarious.  Blackwood Manor, a gothic monstrosity clinging precariously to the cliff face overlooking the churning grey expanse of the North Sea, loomed before her like a skeletal finger pointing accusingly at the bruised, stormy sky.  Its darkened windows, like empty eye sockets, seemed to stare down at her, judging her intrusion. Mia, a journalist for the *Northern Chronicle*, felt the familiar prickle of apprehension, a feeling that had become her constant companion ever since she’d taken on the seemingly impossible task of unraveling the truth behind the Blackwood family’s disappearance – a disappearance shrouded in rumour, superstition, and a chilling silence that had settled over the isolated coastal village of Port Blossom like a shroud.
