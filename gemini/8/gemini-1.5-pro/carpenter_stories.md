The rasping screech of saw against seasoned oak echoed through the cavernous workshop, mingling with the scent of sawdust and beeswax.  Sunlight, fractured by the grimy panes of the tall, arched windows, illuminated swirling motes of dust dancing in the otherwise dim space.  Tools hung in meticulous rows on the far wall, gleaming like trophies in the partial light.  This was the domain of Elias Thorne, a carpenter renowned throughout the windswept coastal town of Port Blossom for his meticulous craftsmanship and the quiet strength that seemed to emanate from his very presence.  Outside, the relentless crash of the North Sea against the jagged cliffs provided a constant, rhythmic backdrop to Elias's work, a sound as familiar to him as his own heartbeat.  Today, however, a different kind of restlessness stirred within him, a premonition of change hanging heavy in the air like the impending storm brewing on the horizon.
