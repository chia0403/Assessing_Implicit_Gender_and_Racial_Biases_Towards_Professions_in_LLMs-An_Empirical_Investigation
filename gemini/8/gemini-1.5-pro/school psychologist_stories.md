The biting November wind whipped Amelia Reyes's dark hair across her face as she hurried across the cracked asphalt of the Northwood High School parking lot.  Flakes of early snow, fat and wet, clung to the shoulders of her teal wool coat.  The sky, a bruised purple-grey, promised more to come.  Northwood, a sprawling, single-story brick behemoth built in the 1960s, seemed to hunker down against the impending storm, its many windows glowing like wary eyes.  Inside, the fluorescent-lit hallways echoed with the cacophony of teenage chatter, a stark contrast to the quiet apprehension Amelia felt tightening in her chest. This wasn't just another Monday. Today, she was meeting with a student who had made a veiled threat online, and the air, both inside and out, felt thick with unspoken anxieties.
