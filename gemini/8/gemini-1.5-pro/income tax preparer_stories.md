Rain lashed against the window of Amelia Grace Holloway's cramped office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her. Tax season was officially over, yet the lingering scent of stale coffee and desperation clung to the air like a shroud. Amelia, whose normally meticulous desk was currently buried under a mountain of discarded receipts and half-eaten protein bars, rubbed her temples, trying to ward off the caffeine-withdrawal headache that threatened to split her skull in two.  Her office, tucked away above a laundromat in the perpetually damp basement of a crumbling Victorian in the small town of Port Blossom, Maine, was hardly glamorous, but it was hers.  And right now, the quiet hum of the dryers below offered a strangely comforting counterpoint to the anxieties swirling within her. This year, something felt… off.  The numbers weren't adding up, not just on her clients’ returns, but in her own life.  A creeping unease, like a misplaced decimal point, had been throwing everything into imbalance.
