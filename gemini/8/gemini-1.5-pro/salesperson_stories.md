The flickering neon sign of the "Last Chance Motel" buzzed intermittently, casting a sickly green glow across the rain-slicked asphalt of Highway 17.  A lone Ford Taurus, its "World's Best Grandma" bumper sticker peeling at the corner, idled by the gas pumps. Inside, Amelia Grace Holloway, regional sales representative for "Miracle Mop" and reigning champion of three consecutive company-wide sales contests, chewed on the end of a lukewarm french fry and considered her options.  The map spread across the passenger seat showed a desolate stretch of road ahead, punctuated only by the occasional blinking light of a lonely diner.  Her destination, the impossibly tiny town of Harmony Creek, population 482, was still another two hours away, and the November wind howled like a banshee just beyond the cracked window of her car. Amelia sighed, crumpling the greasy paper bag in her hand.  This was not how she envisioned spending her Friday night, but the quarterly quotas loomed large, and Harmony Creek represented the untapped potential she desperately needed.
