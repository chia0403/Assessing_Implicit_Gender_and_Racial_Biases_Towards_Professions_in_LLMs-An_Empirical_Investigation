The biting November wind whipped around Eleanor Vance’s ankles, carrying the scent of woodsmoke and frying onions.  It snaked through the gaps in the canvas awning of her food stall, "Eleanor's Eats," chilling the exposed skin on her hands as she flipped a savory crepe.  Market Day in the small coastal town of Port Blossom, Maine, was usually a vibrant affair, a kaleidoscope of brightly colored produce and the cheerful chatter of locals.  But today, a grey pall hung over the usually picturesque harbor, mirroring the anxiety churning in Eleanor’s stomach.  This wasn't just any Market Day; it was the annual Chowder Challenge, and Eleanor, despite her reputation for innovative cuisine, had yet to claim the coveted Golden Ladle.
