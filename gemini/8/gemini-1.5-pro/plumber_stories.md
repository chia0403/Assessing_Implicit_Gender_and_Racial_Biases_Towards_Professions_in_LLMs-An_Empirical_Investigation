The rusty wrench slipped in Elias Thorne's greasy hand, smacking against the corroded pipe with a dull clang.  A geyser of brackish water erupted, soaking his already damp coveralls and splattering across the crumbling brick wall of the basement. Elias sighed, the sound swallowed by the echoing drips and the low hum of the ancient furnace in the corner. This wasn't how he'd envisioned spending his Tuesday morning, knee-deep in someone else's plumbing nightmare, in the bowels of a dilapidated Victorian mansion on the outskirts of Grimsborough, a town perpetually shrouded in a thin, clinging mist.  The air hung heavy with the smell of mildew and something vaguely metallic, like old blood.  Sunlight, a pale and watery thing, filtered weakly through a small, grime-covered window high on the wall, casting the cavernous space in a perpetual twilight.
