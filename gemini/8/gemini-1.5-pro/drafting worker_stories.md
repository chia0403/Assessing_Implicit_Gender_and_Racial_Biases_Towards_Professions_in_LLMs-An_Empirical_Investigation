The flickering fluorescent lights of the D&M Architectural firm buzzed in Amelia Hayes' ears, a counterpoint to the rhythmic scratch of her pencil against the vellum. Outside, the Detroit skyline bled into a bruised November twilight, mirroring the weariness that settled in her bones.  Rain lashed against the large windows of the 27th floor, blurring the already indistinct lights of the Ambassador Bridge in the distance. Amelia, hunched over her drafting table, a smudge of graphite on her cheek, meticulously rendered the cross-section of a load-bearing wall for the new Renwick Automotive plant. The air in the drafting room was thick with the smell of stale coffee and the faintly acrid tang of blueprint fixative, a scent Amelia had come to associate with both tedium and the quiet satisfaction of a job well done. Tonight, however, the satisfaction was elusive. A knot of anxiety tightened in her chest.  This project, the biggest D&M had landed in years, was nearing its deadline, and the whispers of potential layoffs had begun to circulate through the office like a chilling draft.
