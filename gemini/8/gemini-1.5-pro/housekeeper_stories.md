The biting November wind howled a mournful tune around the eaves of Blackwood Manor, rattling the leaded panes of the conservatory windows where Elara Thistlewick, the housekeeper, was meticulously polishing the brass hinges.  Sunlight, weak and watery, filtered through the grimy glass, illuminating motes of dust dancing in the frigid air.  Blackwood Manor, perched precariously on a windswept cliff overlooking the churning grey expanse of the North Sea, had seen better days.  Salt spray had etched the stone facade, leaving streaks like tears down the weathered face of the building.  Inside, a labyrinth of echoing corridors and shadowy rooms held the secrets of generations of Blackwoods, secrets Elara, in her five years of service, had only begun to glimpse.
