Rain lashed against the window of Amelia Grace Hawthorne's cramped office, mimicking the drumming anxiety in her chest.  Fluorescent lights buzzed overhead, casting a sickly yellow glow over the stacks of claim forms threatening to avalanche off her desk. Outside, the neon glow of the "Lucky Clover Pawn" across the street bled into the grey afternoon of downtown Grimsborough, a city clinging to the frayed edges of its once-grand industrial past.  Amelia, a woman whose sensible brown bob and meticulously organized stationery belied a core of steel and a surprisingly sharp wit, tapped a chewed-down pen against a half-eaten tuna sandwich.  She was due at the Peterson house in fifteen minutes to assess the damage from last night's suspiciously convenient "kitchen fire," and something told her this wasn't going to be a standard insurance claim.
