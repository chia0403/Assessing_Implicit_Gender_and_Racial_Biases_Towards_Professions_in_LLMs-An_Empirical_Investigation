The flickering fluorescent lights of the Kellen & Dempsey law offices buzzed like trapped insects, a soundtrack Amelia Grace Holloway had grown accustomed to over the past five years. Rain lashed against the panoramic windows overlooking downtown Chicago, blurring the already fading twilight into a hazy grey.  November had descended upon the city like a damp, unwelcome guest, bringing with it a bone-chilling wind that gnawed at the exposed brick buildings and the spirits of those forced to brave the elements. Inside, however, the air was thick with the stale scent of burnt coffee and the low hum of overworked computers. Amelia, perched on the edge of her ergonomically disastrous chair, squinted at a mountain of discovery documents, each page a potential landmine in the ongoing Peterson vs. Zenith corporate espionage case. This was it, the final push. The trial was a mere two weeks away, and the senior partners, notoriously demanding even on good days, were operating at a level of stressed-out frenzy that bordered on the comical, if it weren't so terrifying.
