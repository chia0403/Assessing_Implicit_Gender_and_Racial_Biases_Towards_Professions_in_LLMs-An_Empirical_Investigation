The biting wind whipped off Lake Superior, stinging Elias Thorne's cheeks as he wrestled a length of heavy-gauge wire.  Ice clung to the metal, making his thick gloves slick and treacherous.  Above him, the skeletal frame of the Duluth Aerial Lift Bridge groaned against the gale, its warning lights blinking rhythmically against the bruised purple of the pre-dawn sky.  Elias, perched precariously on a narrow platform suspended hundreds of feet above the churning, steel-grey water, muttered a curse under his breath.  This wasn't how he'd envisioned spending his forty-second birthday.  The lift bridge, Duluth's crown jewel, had developed an electrical fault, and Elias, the city's most experienced and arguably most stubborn electrician, was the only one willing – or crazy enough – to tackle the repair in these conditions.
