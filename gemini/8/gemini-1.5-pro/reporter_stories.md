Rain lashed against the grimy windows of The Daily Clarion's office, mimicking the relentless drumming in Amelia "Mia" Rodriguez's head.  Fluorescent lights buzzed overhead, casting a sickly yellow glow across the cluttered room, a stark contrast to the inky blackness pressing in from outside.  Ash trays overflowed, crumpled newspapers littered the floor, and the air hung thick with the scent of stale coffee and deadline desperation.  Triton City, usually a glittering jewel nestled on the California coast, was tonight a shrouded, rain-soaked beast, and Mia, hunched over her desk, felt like she was trapped in its belly.  A half-eaten sandwich, curling at the edges, lay forgotten beside her notepad, its fate mirroring the story she was struggling to write.  The Mayor's corruption scandal, a story she'd been chasing for months, was slipping through her fingers like grains of sand, and tonight, the deadline felt less like a finish line and more like a firing squad.
