The biting November wind whipped around Cassandra “Cassie” Moreau, plastering strands of her auburn hair across her face as she surveyed the site.  Grey clouds, heavy with the promise of snow, scudded low over the desolate expanse of the abandoned shipyard in Reykjavik, Iceland. The air tasted of salt and diesel, a ghostly reminder of the industry that once thrived here.  Rusty cranes loomed like skeletal giants against the backdrop of snow-dusted Esja mountain, their silent forms mirroring the stillness that had settled over the area since the last fishing trawler departed a decade ago.  Cassie, clad in a thick wool coat and sturdy waterproof boots, pulled her scarf tighter, her breath puffing out in small white clouds.  This was it. The canvas for her most ambitious project yet – transforming this industrial graveyard into a vibrant, sustainable community space.
