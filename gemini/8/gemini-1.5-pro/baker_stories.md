The aroma of burnt sugar hung heavy in the air, a bittersweet perfume that clung to the brick walls of Buttercup Bakery.  Eloise Clementine Sparrow, her brow furrowed in concentration, peered into the oven. Flames danced around the edges of the pan, licking at the caramelizing crust of the tarte Tatin she'd been painstakingly crafting for the past hour.  Outside, the cobblestone streets of Port Blossom, Maine, shimmered under a layer of freshly fallen snow, the quaint seaside town transformed into a glittering winter wonderland.  It was Christmas Eve, and despite the festive cheer that permeated the salty air, a knot of anxiety tightened in Eloise’s chest.  This tarte Tatin, destined for the town's annual Christmas Eve feast, held more weight than its buttery layers suggested. It was her chance, her last chance, to prove to the town – and to herself – that she was more than just the baker’s daughter.
