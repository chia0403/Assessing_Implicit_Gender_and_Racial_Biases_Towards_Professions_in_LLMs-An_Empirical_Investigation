**Story Opening:**

The scent of lavender and regret hung thick in the air, a fragrant fog clinging to the faded floral wallpaper.  Beatrice Elara Finch, scissors poised mid-snip, stared at the reflection staring back at her.  It wasn't the silver-streaked hair of Mrs. Higgins, her regular Thursday 2 PM appointment, that held her attention.  It was the echo of a question she'd been dodging for years, a question etched into the worry lines blossoming around her own eyes:  Had she chosen the right life?  Mrs. Higgins, bless her heart, was oblivious, rambling on about the price of Brussels sprouts and the latest antics of her cat, Mr. Whiskers, as Beatrice's hand trembled ever so slightly. Today, the routine felt less like a comfort and more like a cage.
