The flickering neon sign of "Plumber Pete's Power Pipes" cast a sickly yellow glow over the rain-slicked alleyway.  Water, a relentless, grey sheet, poured from a sky that mirrored the mood of Bartholomew "Barty" Butterfield, a plumber in his late twenties.  Barty, a man whose small frame seemed perpetually at odds with the vast, complicated plumbing systems he navigated, was crouched beneath a rusted fire escape, his grease-stained hands wrestling with a stubborn drainpipe.  The alley reeked of mildew and despair, a symphony of dripping and gurgling that echoed the frustrations churning within Barty.  Tonight, the symphony was a particularly cacophonous sonata.
