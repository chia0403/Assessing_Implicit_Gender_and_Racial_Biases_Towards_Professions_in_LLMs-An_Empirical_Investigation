The chipped porcelain sink in the Grand Metropolis Hotel's staff restroom, perpetually stained a murky brown, reflected the flickering fluorescent light above.  Rain hammered against the antiquated windowpanes, a relentless percussion that echoed the frantic rhythm of Elias Thorne's heart.  He scrubbed at the grime, his calloused hands barely registering the soapy water.  The air, thick with the smell of stale cigarettes and disinfectant, hung heavy, mirroring the oppressive weight settling on his shoulders.  Elias, a bell hop at the Grand Metropolis for the past five years, felt a profound sense of foreboding.  Tonight, a notorious art collector, known only as "The Raven," was arriving, and rumors whispered through the hotel staff like a dangerous undercurrent—rumors about priceless stolen artifacts and a hidden, labyrinthine network of shadows lurking in the hotel's gilded façade.  The Grand Metropolis, a monument to faded grandeur, held secrets deeper than its mahogany-paneled hallways, and Elias, a quiet observer, was caught squarely in the crosshairs.
