The flickering neon sign of the Rusty Mug cast a sickly yellow glow across Amelia "Sparrow" Featherstone's face, highlighting the faint lines etched around her emerald eyes.  The air hung thick with the scent of stale beer, cheap cigarettes, and something indefinably…metallic.  Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof, a relentless counterpoint to the low, rhythmic thump of the jukebox playing a bluesy tune that felt both mournful and strangely comforting.  She wiped down a sticky mahogany bar top, the fine wood grain almost lost beneath the layers of grime and spilled drinks.  The Rusty Mug, tucked away on a side street in the perpetually overcast city of Ashhaven, was a haven of sorts – a haven for forgotten souls and whispered secrets.  Tonight, however, a new story was brewing, a story whispered not in hushed tones, but in the clinking of ice and the clinking of glasses.
