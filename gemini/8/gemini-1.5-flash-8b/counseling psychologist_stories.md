Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of Dr. Eleanor Vance's tiny, cluttered office, a rhythmic counterpoint to the frantic tapping of her fingers on the worn mahogany desk.  The air, thick with the scent of mildew and damp earth, clung to the walls, mirroring the oppressive weight settling in Eleanor's chest.  Outside, the sprawling metropolis of Veridian, a city choked by its own ambition and simmering anxieties, seemed to shrink into an indistinguishable grey blur.  Her office, nestled in a quiet alleyway behind a dilapidated bookstore, was a sanctuary, or perhaps, a prison, depending on the perspective of the individual currently seated across from her.  Today, it was Leo.  Leo, a sharp-edged young man with eyes that held the ghost of a thousand unspoken stories.
