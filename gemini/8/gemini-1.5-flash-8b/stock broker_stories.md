The flickering neon sign of "Bull's Eye Securities" cast a sickly yellow glow on the rain-slicked streets of downtown Chicago.  It was a Tuesday, the kind of day where the city felt both vibrant and weary, a symphony of honking taxis and the rhythmic pulse of the trading floor inside.  Elias Thorne, a stock broker with a perpetually tired gaze and a finely sculpted jawline that seemed to contradict his perpetually rumpled shirts, stared out the grimy window of his cramped office.  Outside, a deluge of rain hammered against the glass, mirroring the tempest raging within him.  He was down, deeply down, and the numbers on his computer screen mocked him with their cold, stark precision.  The market had turned, and Elias, caught in the maelstrom, had been swept away.
