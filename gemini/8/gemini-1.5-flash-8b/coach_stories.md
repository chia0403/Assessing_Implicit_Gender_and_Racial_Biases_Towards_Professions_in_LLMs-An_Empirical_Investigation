Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of the dilapidated gymnasium, mimicking the relentless drumming in Amelia Hawthorne's head.  The air, thick with the scent of mildew and stale gym socks, hung heavy in the cavernous space.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of sunlight piercing the gloom, illuminating a faded basketball hoop, its net frayed and almost invisible.  This was the hallowed (or, more accurately, hallowed-out) court of the Crestwood Cougars, a team perpetually teetering on the brink of obscurity.  Amelia, a woman whose sharp wit often cut deeper than any coach's whistle, knew that tonight, more than any other, she had to find a spark within her players, a spark that had somehow gone dormant this season.  The Cougars were facing their arch-rivals, the undefeated Ravens, and the pressure, like the rain, seemed to be unrelenting.
