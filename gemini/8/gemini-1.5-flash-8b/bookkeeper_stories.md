The chipped Formica countertop of the "Golden Quill" reflected the fluorescent hum of the office, a pale, sickly glow that somehow managed to highlight the dust motes dancing in the air.  Rain hammered against the single, grimy window, a relentless percussion that mirrored the rhythmic click-clack of Amelia Butterfield's keys on the worn keyboard.  Amelia, a meticulous bookkeeper with a shock of unruly auburn hair that seemed perpetually on the verge of escaping its carefully pinned confines, hunched over a mountain of invoices.  The air, thick with the scent of stale coffee and desperation, hung heavy over the small office, a testament to the late-night marathon of paperwork that was becoming the new normal. Tonight, it was more than just paperwork. Tonight, it was a matter of survival.
