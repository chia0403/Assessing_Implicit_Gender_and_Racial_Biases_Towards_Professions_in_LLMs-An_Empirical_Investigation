The biting wind whipped across the cobblestones of the Old Quarter, stinging Elara Everglen's cheeks.  Dust, kicked up by the ceaseless traffic of carts and carriages, coated her worn leather jerkin, a familiar grime that spoke of countless patrols and sleepless nights.  The air, thick with the smells of roasting meat, brewing ale, and something vaguely metallic that hinted at the city's shadowed underbelly, hung heavy over the narrow, twisting streets.  Elara, a Royal Guard stationed at the northern gate of Veridia, adjusted the strap of her worn leather satchel, her calloused fingers tightening around the hilt of her sword.  Dawn was painting the sky a bruised purple, hinting at the long, arduous day ahead, a day that would likely bring more of the same – the same whispers of unrest, the same restless eyes peering from shadowed doorways, the same nagging sense of something… amiss.  Veridia, once a beacon of prosperity, now felt like a city holding its breath, waiting for the storm to break.
