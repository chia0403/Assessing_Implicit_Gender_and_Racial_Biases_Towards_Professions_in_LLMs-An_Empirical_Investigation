The biting wind whipped across the cobbled streets of Port Alora, rattling the shutters of the little workshop nestled between a fishmonger's stall and a baker's overflowing oven.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of sunlight piercing the gloom, illuminating the intricate carving on a half-finished wooden chest.  Elias Thorne, a carpenter with hands as weathered and worn as the ancient oak timbers he worked with, hunched over his bench, the rhythmic clang of his hammer a lonely counterpoint to the city's boisterous murmurs.  He was a master craftsman, renowned for the beauty and durability of his work, yet today, the intricate floral designs on the chest seemed to mock him, their delicate curves reflecting the disquiet in his soul.  The chest, meant as a wedding gift for a young couple, now felt more like a heavy burden, a symbol of the unfulfilled promises that weighed upon him.
