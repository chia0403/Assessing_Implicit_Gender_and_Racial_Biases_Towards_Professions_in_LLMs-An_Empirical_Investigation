The rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of the precinct, a relentless percussion that mirrored the frantic rhythm of Detective Isabella "Izzy" Rossi's thoughts.  The flickering fluorescent lights of the small, cramped interrogation room cast long, dancing shadows, highlighting the grime that clung to the worn linoleum floor.  Outside, the city of Veridian sprawled beneath a bruised, pewter sky, a labyrinth of dimly lit alleys and towering, industrial warehouses that seemed to swallow the sound of the storm.  Izzy, her trench coat plastered to her frame, traced the condensation rings on the mug of lukewarm coffee before her.  The case, the one that had consumed her for the past forty-eight hours, felt as impenetrable as the storm clouds.
