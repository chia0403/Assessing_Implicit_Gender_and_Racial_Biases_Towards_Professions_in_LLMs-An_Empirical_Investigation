Rain lashed against the warped, tin roof of the dilapidated high school, a symphony of insistent drumming that mirrored the frantic rhythm of Amelia  Ashworth's thoughts.  The air, thick with the smell of mildew and stale coffee, hung heavy in the cavernous English classroom, a space that had seen better days, reflecting the weariness in Amelia's own soul.  It was the last day of school before the summer break, a period she normally anticipated with a mixture of trepidation and relief, but this year felt different.  Something about the relentless downpour, the hollow echo of the bell, and the unspoken anxieties hanging in the air, felt profoundly unsettling.  Outside, the overgrown field behind the school, usually a vibrant tapestry of autumnal hues, was now a muddy, indistinguishable expanse, swallowed by the encroaching storm.  Amelia, a Literature teacher at Blackwood High for five years, felt a knot of dread tighten in her stomach. This year, she feared the summer wouldn't bring the reprieve she desperately needed.
