The wind, a rasping whisper across the parched earth, carried the scent of dust and desperation.  Amelia Jean Hawthorne, her face etched with the lines of sun and worry, squinted at the horizon.  The sky, a brutal, unforgiving blue, offered no promise of rain.  Her farm, Hawthorne's Hope, nestled in the dusty heart of the Dust Bowl, was more accurately named Hawthorne's Despair these days.  Cracked earth, the colour of dried blood, stretched as far as the eye could see, a testament to the unrelenting drought that had choked the life from the land, and the hope from Amelia's heart.  The only sound louder than the wind was the incessant creak of her weathered farmhouse, a lonely sentinel against the vast, unforgiving landscape.
