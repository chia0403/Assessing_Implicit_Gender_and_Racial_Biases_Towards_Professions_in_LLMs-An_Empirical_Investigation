The recycled air hung thick with the scent of cheap perfume, stale coffee, and something vaguely floral that Amelia Katherine "Amy" Holloway suspected was emanating from the suspiciously shiny bald head of the man in 3B.  Amy, a veteran flight attendant for SkyHigh Airlines, surveyed the cabin of Flight 27B from the galley.  The pre-dawn light painted the clouds outside in bruised purples and angry oranges, a fitting backdrop for the irritable grumbling that punctuated the hushed murmurs of the passengers. They were seven hours into a transatlantic flight from JFK to Heathrow, and the charm of the initial complimentary beverage service had long since evaporated, leaving behind a residue of jet lag and simmering anxieties. The cabin lights were dimmed, a sickly yellow glow highlighting the sheen of spilled coffee on the carpet near row 12.  Amy sighed, the familiar weight of responsibility settling on her shoulders like a heavy blanket. This was going to be a long one.
