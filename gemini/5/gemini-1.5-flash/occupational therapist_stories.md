The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne's auburn hair across her face as she hurried down the cobbled street, the collar of her charcoal coat pulled high.  Rain, the kind that seemed to seep into your bones, plastered her dark trousers to her legs.  Ahead, the imposing stone façade of St. Jude's Rehabilitation Centre loomed, its gothic windows like vacant eyes staring out at the stormy grey sky.  Inside, a different kind of storm raged – a war fought not with wind and water, but with broken bones, shattered minds, and the relentless, often invisible, battles of recovery.  Amelia, an occupational therapist with a reputation for both her unwavering dedication and her unconventional methods, was about to enter the fray.
