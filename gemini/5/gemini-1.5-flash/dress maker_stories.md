The scent of lavender and old linen hung heavy in the air, a comforting shroud against the biting November wind rattling the windowpanes of "Stitches & Dreams."  Inside, amidst bolts of silk the colour of amethyst and sunset, and spools of thread like jewels scattered across mahogany tables, sat Aurelia Genevieve Beaumont.  Her fingers, nimble and quick, danced across a length of ivory lace, the needle flashing like a tiny silver comet as she painstakingly created the intricate floral pattern that would adorn the bodice of a wedding gown.  The shop itself, tucked away on a cobbled side street in the quaint, almost forgotten corner of  Little Venice, was a haven of quiet creativity, a stark contrast to the bustling, hurried life that thrummed just beyond its weathered oak door.
