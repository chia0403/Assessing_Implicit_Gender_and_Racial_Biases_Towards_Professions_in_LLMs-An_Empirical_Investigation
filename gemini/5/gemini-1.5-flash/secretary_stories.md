The chipped porcelain teacup warmed Amelia Featherstonehaugh’s fingers, a meager comfort against the biting November wind howling outside the office window.  Rain lashed against the grime-coated panes of Mr. Fitzwilliam’s imposing Victorian office building, mirroring the tempest brewing within Amelia herself.  She stared out at the grey, sodden cityscape of Manchester, the spires of the cathedral barely visible through the downpour, a stark contrast to the opulent, mahogany-lined sanctuary she occupied.  The air hung thick with the scent of old paper, expensive cigars, and a faint, unsettling undercurrent of impending doom – a scent Amelia had become intimately familiar with during her five years as Mr. Fitzwilliam’s personal secretary.
