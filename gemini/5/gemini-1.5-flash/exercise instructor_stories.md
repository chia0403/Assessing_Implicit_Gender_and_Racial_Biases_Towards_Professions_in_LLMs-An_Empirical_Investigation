The humid air hung heavy, thick with the scent of sweat and chlorine.  Fluorescent lights hummed a monotonous tune above the mirrored walls of "Body & Soul Fitness," reflecting the glistening bodies struggling through a particularly brutal Zumba class.  Sweat beaded on Amelia Grace Hawthorne's forehead, not from exertion – she’d finished her own workout an hour ago – but from the sheer pressure of managing a Friday night class bursting at the seams.  Amelia, or Amy as most of her clients affectionately (or sometimes exasperatedly) called her, adjusted her microphone, its cord snaking across the sticky floor like a restless serpent.  Her vibrant turquoise leggings and matching sports bra, usually a source of confident energy, felt constricting tonight. The weight of a looming deadline, a disastrous first date, and a mountain of laundry were all threatening to unravel her meticulously crafted, always-positive persona.  Tonight, even the upbeat Latin rhythms couldn't quite drown out the inner turmoil brewing beneath her expertly sculpted abs.
