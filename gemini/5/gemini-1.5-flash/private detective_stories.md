The rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of Jack Stratton's office, a relentless percussion that mirrored the throbbing headache behind his eyes.  Jack, a man whose face seemed perpetually etched with the weariness of a thousand unsolved cases, sat hunched over a chipped Formica desk, the harsh fluorescent light reflecting off his thinning hair.  The air hung thick with the scent of stale coffee and desperation, a familiar perfume in the cramped, cluttered space that served as both his office and living quarters.  This wasn't some glamorous downtown detective agency; this was the back room of a dilapidated pawn shop on the wrong side of the tracks in the perpetually rain-soaked city of Port Blossom, Oregon.  Outside, the wind howled a mournful tune, rattling the already loose windowpanes, and Jack knew, with a sinking feeling, that another night in the city of shadows was about to begin.
