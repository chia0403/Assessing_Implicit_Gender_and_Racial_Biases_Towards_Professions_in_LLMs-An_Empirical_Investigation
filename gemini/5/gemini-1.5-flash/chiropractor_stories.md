The scent of pine needles and damp earth hung heavy in the air, a stark contrast to the sterile, antiseptic smell usually clinging to Dr. Eleanor Vance’s chiropractic clinic.  Eleanor, however, wasn’t in her clinic, nestled comfortably amongst the rolling hills of Sonoma County, California.  Instead, she found herself perched precariously on the edge of a moss-covered boulder, the chilling October wind whipping her auburn hair across her face. Below her, the churning waters of the Russian River snaked their way through a dense redwood forest, the ancient trees standing sentinel like silent, watchful giants.  A battered, mud-caked Land Rover, its headlights still faintly glowing, sat abandoned a few yards away, a testament to the hasty escape that had brought her to this precarious spot.  The tranquil beauty of the setting offered little solace; a knot of icy dread tightened in her stomach, mirroring the sharp, persistent pain throbbing in her left shoulder – a pain that no amount of her own expert manipulations could seem to alleviate.
