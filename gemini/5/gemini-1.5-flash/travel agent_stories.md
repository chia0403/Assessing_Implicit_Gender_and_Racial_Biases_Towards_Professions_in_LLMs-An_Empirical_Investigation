The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Hernandez’s hands, the lukewarm chamomile doing little to soothe the frayed edges of her nerves.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of her office, blurring the already hazy cityscape of Dublin.  Amelia, a travel agent with a penchant for meticulously organised spreadsheets and a secret yearning for adventure she rarely indulged in, stared out at the grey expanse.  Her agency, "Wanderlust & Waypoints," nestled on the third floor of a building that smelled perpetually of old paper and slightly burnt coffee, was usually a whirlwind of excited chatter and the rustling of brochures.  Today, however, a heavy silence hung in the air, broken only by the rhythmic drumming of the rain and the occasional mournful sigh escaping Amelia's lips.  The impending cancellation of the "Grand Himalayan Trek" – her most ambitious, and personally satisfying, booking of the year – loomed like a particularly stubborn storm cloud.
