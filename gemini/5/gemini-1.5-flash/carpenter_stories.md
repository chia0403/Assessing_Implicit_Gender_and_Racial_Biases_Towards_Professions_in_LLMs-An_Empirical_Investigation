The salt-laced wind whipped Elias Thorne’s grey hair across his weathered face as he wrestled the stubborn oak plank.  His knuckles, thick and gnarled like the ancient branches of the cypress trees lining the cove, ached with the familiar, comforting pain of honest labor.  The air, thick with the smell of brine and sawdust, hung heavy above the small, ramshackle shipyard nestled on the windswept cliffs of Port Blossom, a village clinging precariously to the jagged coastline of Cornwall.  The sky, a bruised purple bleeding into a fiery orange sunset, mirrored the turbulent sea churning below, its waves crashing against the rocky shore with a relentless roar that echoed the turmoil brewing within Elias himself.  He hadn't built a boat in months, not since… well, since *it* happened.  The unfinished hull, a skeletal testament to his procrastination, sat half-submerged in the tide, a grim reminder of the task that lay unfinished, both in wood and in his heart.
