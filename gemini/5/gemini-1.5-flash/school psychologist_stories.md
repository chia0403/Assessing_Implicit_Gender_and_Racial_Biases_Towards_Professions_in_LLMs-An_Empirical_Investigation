The chipped paint of the oak door seemed to mirror the cracks in Dr. Eleanor Vance's composure.  Rain lashed against the leaded-glass windows of Northwood High, a rhythmic drumming that did little to soothe the frantic flutter in her chest.  The scent of stale coffee and something vaguely metallic – fear, perhaps? – hung heavy in the air of her small, cluttered office.  Outside, the wind howled a mournful tune, a counterpoint to the muffled sobs emanating from the room beyond her own, the room where seventeen-year-old Liam O'Connell sat, his future, it seemed, unraveling thread by thread.  This wasn't just another disciplinary referral; this was a crisis, one that felt heavier than any case file Eleanor had encountered in her ten years as a school psychologist.
