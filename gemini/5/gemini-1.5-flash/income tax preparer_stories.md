The fluorescent lights of Millie's "Taxes Done Right" hummed a monotonous tune, a counterpoint to the frantic clatter of her keyboard.  February was peak season, a blizzard of W-2s and 1099s threatening to bury Amelia Worthington, CPA, beneath a mountain of paperwork.  The air in her small, cluttered office – a converted spare bedroom in her Victorian house on the edge of Oakhaven, a town where time seemed to move at a slower, more deliberate pace than anywhere else – smelled faintly of stale coffee, printer ink, and the lingering scent of desperation.  Outside, a late winter sun cast long shadows across the snow-dusted lawns, a stark contrast to the chaos within.  Amelia, already fueled by three cups of lukewarm coffee and sheer willpower, adjusted her glasses and sighed, the weight of a thousand clients' financial futures pressing down on her weary shoulders.
