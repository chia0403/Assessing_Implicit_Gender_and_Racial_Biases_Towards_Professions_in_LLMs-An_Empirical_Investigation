The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s auburn hair across her face as she hurried down Bleecker Street.  Rain, the kind that chilled you to the bone despite layers of wool and cashmere, plastered her trench coat to her back.  Above, the gothic spires of St. Mark’s Church in-the-Bowery pierced the bruised twilight sky, their stone a stark contrast to the riot of neon signs flickering to life in the surrounding Greenwich Village shops.  Amelia, senior editor at the prestigious literary journal *The Quill*, tugged her scarf tighter, the damp chill seeping into her very marrow.  Tonight, however, the weather was the least of her worries.  A manuscript, a manuscript that promised to either catapult *The Quill* to unprecedented heights or sink it deeper into the already murky waters of dwindling subscriptions, lay nestled in her worn leather satchel.  Its fate, and perhaps her own, hung precariously in the balance.
