The chipped paint of the writing desk flaked onto Amelia Katherine Blackwood's manuscript, a tiny, ironic snowfall on the already bleak landscape of her latest novel.  Rain lashed against the leaded-glass windows of her secluded cottage, nestled deep within the brooding, windswept hills of the Cornish coast.  The only light came from a single, flickering oil lamp, casting long, dancing shadows that seemed to writhe in sympathy with the storm raging outside.  The air hung thick with the scent of woodsmoke and salt, a fragrance both comforting and isolating, mirroring Amelia's current state of mind.  Her deadline loomed, a monstrous, shadowy figure in the periphery, and the words, usually her loyal companions, remained stubbornly elusive.
