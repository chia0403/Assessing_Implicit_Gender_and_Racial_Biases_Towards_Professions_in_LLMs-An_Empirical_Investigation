The brass nameplate on the mahogany desk gleamed under the soft, filtered light of the Grand Majestic Hotel lobby: *Alistair Finch, Chief Concierge*.  Alistair, a man whose tailored suit seemed to anticipate his every move, adjusted his silver cufflinks, the faint chime a counterpoint to the hushed murmur of the lobby's opulent décor.  Outside, the Parisian rain hammered against the arched windows, blurring the twinkling lights of the Avenue des Champs-Élysées into shimmering streaks.  Inside, however, it was a haven of controlled calm; a sanctuary of plush carpets, towering potted palms, and the almost imperceptible scent of expensive leather and freshly brewed coffee.  Tonight, however, the usual placidity felt brittle, poised on the edge of something… unpredictable.  A nervous tremor in the usually seamless flow of the hotel's well-oiled machinery had Alistair’s sharp eyes scanning the elegant crowd, a silent question etched on his face.
