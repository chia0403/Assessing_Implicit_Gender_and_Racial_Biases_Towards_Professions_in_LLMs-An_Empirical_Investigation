The biting November wind whipped Amelia Katherine Davenport’s auburn hair across her face as she wrestled with the recalcitrant stage door.  Rust groaned in protest with every heave, a sound perfectly in keeping with the crumbling grandeur of the Blackwood Theatre.  Flaking paint, the colour of a bruise, peeled from the once-elegant façade, revealing layers of history beneath – a history Amelia was determined to resurrect, one creaky floorboard, one temperamental spotlight, at a time.  Inside, the auditorium remained shrouded in a pre-performance hush, a stark contrast to the chaotic storm raging outside.  The air hung heavy with the scent of dust, aged velvet, and the faint, lingering aroma of stale beer – the ghosts of countless productions past. This was her sanctuary, her battlefield, her Blackwood.  Tonight, the battle was for the soul of "The Raven's Shadow," and Amelia, armed with a worn copy of Poe and a caffeine-fueled desperation, was ready to fight.
