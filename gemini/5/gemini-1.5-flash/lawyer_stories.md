The biting November wind whipped Amelia Katherine Hayes’s charcoal grey coat around her legs as she hurried down Bleecker Street, the scent of roasting chestnuts and damp earth battling for dominance in the crisp air.  Rain, a fine, persistent drizzle, plastered her already unruly auburn curls to her scalp.  Ahead, the gargoyle perched atop the imposing brownstone of 147 Bleecker loomed like a gothic sentinel, its stony face grim against the bruised twilight sky.  Amelia, a junior associate at the prestigious law firm of Davenport & Thorne, usually found solace in the elegant chaos of Manhattan, but tonight, the city felt sharp and unforgiving, mirroring the knot of unease tightening in her stomach.  This wasn’t just another late-night filing; this was a meeting with Silas Blackwood, and Amelia had a feeling it wouldn't be pleasant.
