The salt spray stung Amelia Hernandez’s face as she wrestled the stubborn lock on the dilapidated beachfront property.  Seagulls shrieked overhead, their cries echoing the mournful groan of the aging timber frame.  Amelia, a real estate agent with sun-weathered skin and eyes the color of a stormy Atlantic, pulled the key free with a satisfying click.  The air inside the ramshackle cottage smelled of damp wood, seaweed, and something faintly… metallic. This wasn't just another fixer-upper;  this was the infamous Blackwood Manor, perched precariously on the windswept cliffs of Cape Cod, a property whispered about in hushed tones by locals and notorious for its string of unfortunate owners.  The real estate listing had simply called it "charmingly rustic," but Amelia knew better. This was a challenge, a puzzle wrapped in peeling paint and the scent of sea-soaked secrets.
