The stale air in the Palmer & Reed law offices hung thick with the scent of old paper and simmering resentment.  Fluorescent lights hummed a monotonous drone overhead, casting a sickly yellow glow on the towering stacks of files that threatened to topple like dominoes.  Amelia Grace Hawthorne, paralegal extraordinaire – or at least, that’s what her meticulously organized desk implied – meticulously sharpened her pencil, the rhythmic scratching a stark counterpoint to the muffled shouting echoing from Mr. Palmer’s office.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows overlooking the city, mirroring the tempest brewing within the firm.  Tonight, however, Amelia wasn’t concerned with the usual office drama. Tonight, she was on the verge of uncovering something far more significant, something that could unravel the carefully constructed facade of Palmer & Reed and expose a rot far deeper than overdue paperwork.
