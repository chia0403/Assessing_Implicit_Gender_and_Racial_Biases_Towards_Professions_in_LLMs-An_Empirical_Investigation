The rain hammered against the panoramic windows of Alistair Finch's office, a relentless percussion against the cityscape spread below.  Alistair, a man whose tailored suit always seemed to outshine the grey New York sky, stared out at the swirling lights of Midtown Manhattan.  His normally sharp, calculating gaze was softened by a weariness that went beyond the usual exhaustion of a long day on the floor of the New York Stock Exchange. Tonight, the flickering city lights reflected not ambition, but a gnawing unease, a premonition as sharp as the edge of a freshly sharpened scalpel.  He swirled the amber liquid in his crystal glass – a single malt, a rare indulgence – the ice clinking a mournful counterpoint to the storm raging outside.  The scent of expensive leather and old money hung heavy in the air, a stark contrast to the impending tempest brewing both inside and outside his meticulously organized world.
