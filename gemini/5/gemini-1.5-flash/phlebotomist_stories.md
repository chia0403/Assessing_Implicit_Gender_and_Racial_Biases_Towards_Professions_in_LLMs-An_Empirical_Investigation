The fluorescent lights of the Meadowbrook Medical Center hummed a monotonous tune, a counterpoint to the rhythmic *thump-thump-thump* of Amelia Grace Hawthorne's own heartbeat.  Amelia, a phlebotomist with hands as steady as a surgeon's and nerves forged in the crucible of a thousand screaming toddlers and fainting adults, found herself staring at the offending needle. It wasn't bent or dull, just...uncooperative.  The vial sat mockingly empty beside it, a tiny, clear testament to her current failure.  The waiting room, a sterile expanse of pale green chairs and hushed whispers, felt suddenly oppressive. The air hung thick with the scent of antiseptic and simmering anxieties, a familiar cocktail Amelia usually navigated with practiced ease.  But today, the usual calm efficiency had deserted her, leaving only a prickling sense of unease and the insistent, unwelcome sting of her own failing.
