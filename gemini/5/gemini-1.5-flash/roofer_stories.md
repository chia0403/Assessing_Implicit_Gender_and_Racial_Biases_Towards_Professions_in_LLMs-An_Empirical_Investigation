The wind, a snarling beast with teeth of ice, clawed at Elias Thorne’s worn work gloves as he perched precariously on the gabled roof of Mrs. Periwinkle’s Victorian monstrosity.  Below, the town of Oakhaven huddled beneath a bruised purple sky, the last vestiges of sunset bleeding into the encroaching twilight.  Each gust threatened to rip Elias from his precarious perch, sending him tumbling into the meticulously manicured rose bushes that choked the garden below – a fate far worse than a simple broken bone, considering Mrs. Periwinkle’s notoriously sharp tongue and even sharper shears.  The air, thick with the scent of damp earth and decaying leaves, carried the faint, metallic tang of blood – though whether that was from a grazed knee or something more sinister, Elias couldn't yet tell.
