The biting November wind whipped Amelia Katherine Finch’s hair across her face, stinging her cheeks with icy rain.  She pulled her worn woolen scarf tighter, the scent of damp earth and pine needles clinging to it like a second skin.  Amelia, or Amy as most people called her, hunched deeper into her oilskin jacket, the collar scratching at her neck, a familiar discomfort she barely registered.  She was used to discomfort, a necessary companion in her profession.  The sprawling grounds of Blackwood Manor, with its gothic architecture looming against the bruised twilight sky, were her domain.  It wasn't just the vast acreage of manicured lawns and ancient woodland she oversaw; it was the menagerie of creatures housed within the manor walls—from the pampered Persian cats dozing on velvet cushions, to the grumpy old tortoise sheltering in a sun-drenched conservatory, to the screeching flock of peacocks that held court in the overgrown aviary.  Tonight, however, a different kind of unease prickled at Amy’s skin, a feeling far colder than the November wind. Something was wrong, and her instincts, honed over years of caring for the creatures of Blackwood Manor, screamed at her to find out what.
