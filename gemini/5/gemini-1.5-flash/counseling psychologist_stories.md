The chipped porcelain mug warmed Dr. Eleanor Vance’s hands, the lukewarm chamomile doing little to soothe the icy knot in her stomach.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of her office, blurring the already hazy cityscape of Seattle.  Below, the Space Needle, usually a beacon of optimistic light, was swallowed by the swirling grey.  Inside, the air hung heavy with the scent of damp wool and the unspoken anxieties of the day’s appointments – a struggling teenager grappling with identity, a couple teetering on the brink of divorce, and a businessman whose carefully constructed façade was crumbling under the weight of an unacknowledged grief.  Eleanor sighed, the sound swallowed by the rhythmic drumming of the rain, and took another sip of the tepid tea.  The weight of her clients' lives, a familiar burden, pressed down on her, a constant reminder of the delicate balance between hope and despair that defined her profession. Tonight, however, felt different.  A particularly unsettling case, one that had burrowed its way into the crevices of her mind, refused to let go.
