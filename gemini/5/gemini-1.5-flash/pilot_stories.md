The biting Alaskan wind howled a mournful song around Captain Evelyn “Evie” Reed’s battered Cessna 185, its icy breath frosting the already-frosted wings.  Below, the Chugach Mountains clawed at the bruised purple sky, their snow-capped peaks disappearing into a swirling vortex of snow and cloud.  The only visible landmark for miles was the skeletal remains of a long-abandoned mining town, clinging precariously to a sliver of land between the churning river and the looming mountains – a stark, silent testament to the unforgiving nature of the Alaskan wilderness.  Evie checked her instruments, the flickering glow of the dials painting her face in an eerie, ethereal light.  Her breath plumed out in front of her, a fleeting white cloud against the vast, indifferent expanse of the Alaskan winter. Tonight, she wasn't just flying a plane; she was wrestling with the elements themselves.
