The fluorescent hum of the office was a dull counterpoint to the frantic drumming of Amelia Grace Hawthorne's fingers on her mahogany desk.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of her 37th-floor office, blurring the already hazy cityscape of London into an impressionistic smear of grey and black.  Amelia, a woman whose life was usually as meticulously ordered as her spreadsheets, felt a growing unease prickle at the edges of her composure.  The scent of old paper and impending deadlines hung heavy in the air, a familiar fragrance that tonight carried an unsettling undercurrent of something else entirely – something akin to fear.  Outside, the storm raged, mirroring the tempest brewing within her.  This wasn't just another late night crunching numbers for Worthington & Sons; this was different. This felt… wrong.
