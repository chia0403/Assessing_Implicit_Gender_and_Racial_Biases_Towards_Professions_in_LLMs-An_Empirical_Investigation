The stale scent of instant coffee and desperation clung to the air in Amelia “Mia” Hernandez’s cramped studio apartment.  Sunlight, fractured by the grimy windowpanes overlooking a perpetually grey alleyway in Brooklyn, barely illuminated the chaotic landscape of her workspace.  Half-finished design projects – vibrant sketches vying for space with crumpled paper balls and overflowing coffee cups – littered every surface.  A lone, flickering fluorescent tube buzzed overhead, its harsh light highlighting the dark circles under Mia’s tired eyes.  Today was the deadline for the "Save the Bees" campaign, a pro bono project she'd taken on partly out of genuine concern for the environment and partly out of a desperate need to bolster her dwindling portfolio.  The pressure was a palpable entity, a suffocating weight on her already overloaded chest.  Outside, the city roared – a symphony of car horns and distant sirens – but in Mia’s studio, only the relentless ticking of a vintage clock punctuated the silence between her frantic keyboard strokes.
