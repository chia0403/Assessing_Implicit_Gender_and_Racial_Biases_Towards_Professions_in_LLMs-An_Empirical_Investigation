The diesel engine coughed, a throaty rumble that vibrated through the steel bones of the Caterpillar D11T.  Dust, the fine ochre powder of the Arizona desert, swirled around Amelia “Amy” Hernandez as she eased the behemoth into gear.  The sun, a malevolent eye in the cloudless sky, beat down mercilessly on the expansive, sun-baked landscape.  Ahead, the raw earth yawned open, a gaping wound in the otherwise pristine expanse, a testament to the relentless march of the new highway construction.  Amy squinted, her weathered face partially shielded by the brim of her worn baseball cap, the sweat beading on her forehead despite the air conditioning struggling to keep up with the desert heat. The only sounds louder than the engine were the insistent chirping of unseen crickets and the distant, rhythmic thud of another machine, a lonely percussion in the vast, echoing silence of the Arizona landscape.  This was her world, a kingdom of steel and dirt, ruled by the precision of her hands and the power of the machine she commanded.
