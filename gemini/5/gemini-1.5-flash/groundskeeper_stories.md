The biting November wind whipped Amelia Blackwood’s hair across her face, stinging her cheeks a rosy red that contrasted sharply with the greying twilight.  She tugged her worn beanie lower, the wool scratching against her forehead, a familiar comfort in the chilling air.  The vast, manicured lawns of Blackwood Manor stretched before her, a sea of browning grass punctuated by the skeletal remains of summer flowers.  Fallen leaves, a kaleidoscope of russet, gold, and crimson, clung stubbornly to the ancient oak trees that guarded the estate, their gnarled branches clawing at the bruised purple sky.  Amelia, groundskeeper for the Blackwood family for the past fifteen years, surveyed her domain with a practiced eye, the scent of damp earth and decaying vegetation filling her lungs.  Tonight, however, the familiar tranquility of the grounds was disturbed by an unsettling stillness, a silence broken only by the mournful whisper of the wind.  Something felt…off.
