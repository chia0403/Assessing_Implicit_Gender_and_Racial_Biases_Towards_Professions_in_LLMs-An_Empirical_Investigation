The chipped porcelain sink groaned under the weight of Elara Vance's elbows as she scrubbed at the stubborn crust of burnt sugar clinging to the bottom of the copper pot.  Rain lashed against the tall, gothic windows of Blackwood Manor, each gust a mournful sigh echoing the emptiness that permeated the vast, echoing house.  The air, thick with the scent of damp stone and woodsmoke, held a chill that went deeper than the autumnal weather; it was the chill of neglect, of years spent gathering dust and shadows in the absence of laughter and life.  Elara, her knuckles white against the worn copper, sighed in return.  Blackwood Manor wasn't just a house; it was a mausoleum of forgotten memories, and she, its solitary caretaker, was its reluctant gravedigger.
