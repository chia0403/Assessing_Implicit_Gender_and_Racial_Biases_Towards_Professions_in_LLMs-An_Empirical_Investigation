The chipped porcelain sink groaned under the weight of Elias Thorne's elbows as he scrubbed at the stubborn coffee stain clinging to the otherwise immaculate stainless steel countertop.  Elias, a man whose life seemed to consist primarily of mops, buckets, and the lingering scent of industrial cleaner, sighed.  The sterile, antiseptic air of the Xylos Corporation headquarters hung heavy, a stark contrast to the gritty reality of his own life, one lived largely in the shadows of polished marble floors and hushed boardroom whispers.  He worked the graveyard shift, a lonely vigil amidst the sleeping giants of commerce, a custodian of silence in a building that hummed with ambition even in slumber. The fluorescent lights flickered overhead, casting long, skeletal shadows that danced with the dust motes swirling in the near-empty atrium.  Outside, the city of Veridia slept, oblivious to the quiet diligence of the man who kept its corporate heart clean.
