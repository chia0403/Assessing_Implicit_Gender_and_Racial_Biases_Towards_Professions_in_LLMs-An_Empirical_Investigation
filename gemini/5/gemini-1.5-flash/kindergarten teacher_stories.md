The scent of Play-Doh and slightly-too-sweet orange juice hung heavy in the air, a familiar perfume to Ms. Eleanor Vance.  Dust motes, illuminated by the late afternoon sun slanting through the oversized windows of Room 2B, danced in the golden light like tiny, mischievous fairies.  Outside, the manicured lawns of Oakhaven Elementary School were beginning to settle into the quietude of twilight, but inside, the chaos of kindergarten reigned supreme.  Crayon shavings littered the brightly colored carpet, a miniature Jackson Pollock masterpiece in the making.  A lone, abandoned stuffed unicorn lay forlornly on the reading rug, its plastic horn slightly askew.  Eleanor, perched on a miniature chair at her cluttered desk, sighed contentedly.  Another day survived.  Another wave of tiny humans successfully navigated the treacherous waters of learning their ABCs and sharing their crayons.
