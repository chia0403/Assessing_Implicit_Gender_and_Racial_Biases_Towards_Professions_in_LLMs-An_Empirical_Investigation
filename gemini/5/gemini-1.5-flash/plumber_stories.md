The biting November wind howled a mournful tune through the gaps in the boarded-up windows of Blackwood Manor, rattling the ancient panes like skeletal fingers.  Inside, a single bare bulb swung precariously above the head of Bartholomew "Barty" Butterfield III, the only plumber brave – or foolish – enough to answer the emergency call from the notoriously eccentric Lady Beatrice Ashworth.  Barty, a man whose calloused hands spoke volumes of years spent wrestling with stubborn pipes and temperamental boilers, shivered not just from the cold, but from a prickle of unease that had nothing to do with the drafty old house.  The air hung thick with the scent of damp earth and something else… something faintly metallic, like old blood.
