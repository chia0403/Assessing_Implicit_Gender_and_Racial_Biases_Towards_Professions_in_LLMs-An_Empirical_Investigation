The air hung thick and heavy, a humid blanket draped over the crumbling colonial architecture of Cartagena.  Cicadas buzzed a relentless, hypnotic rhythm against the backdrop of salsa music spilling from a nearby bar, a jarring counterpoint to the gnawing unease in Amelia Hernandez’s gut. Sweat beaded on her forehead, clinging to the unruly curls that escaped her perpetually damp ponytail.  She adjusted the strap of her worn leather shoulder bag, its weight familiar and comforting amidst the chaotic swirl of the city.  Amelia, a journalist with the grit of a seasoned investigator and the eyes of someone who'd seen too much, was perched on a cracked stone bench in the Plaza de San Domingo, the ancient cobblestones slick underfoot, her gaze fixed on the shadowed doorway of a dilapidated building across the square. This wasn't a tourist attraction; this was a lead, a whisper in the underbelly of the city she'd been chasing for months – a story that could unravel a web of corruption reaching the highest echelons of power.
