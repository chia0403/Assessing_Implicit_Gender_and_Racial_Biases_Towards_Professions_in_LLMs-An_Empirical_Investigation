The chipped Formica countertop reflected Amelia Blackwood's tired face, illuminated by the harsh fluorescent lights of the Northwood High guidance office.  Empty coffee cups, each with a faint ring of dried crema, sat like forgotten mourners around a half-eaten pastry.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of afternoon sun slicing through the grimy window, illuminating a faded poster advertising the upcoming "Future You" career fair.  Amelia, her name barely a whisper on her lips, sighed, pushing a stray strand of auburn hair behind her ear.  Another Tuesday, another avalanche of anxiety-ridden teenagers, each with their own unique brand of angst and unspoken desperation.  Outside, the clamor of teenage voices – a symphony of whispers, shouts, and muffled laughter – rose and fell like waves on the shore.  The air, thick with the scent of stale pizza and adolescent sweat, hung heavy, a tangible weight pressing down on her.  She had already heard the whispers, the hushed anxieties.  And tonight's meeting with 17-year-old Leo Rodriguez was shaping up to be the most challenging yet.
