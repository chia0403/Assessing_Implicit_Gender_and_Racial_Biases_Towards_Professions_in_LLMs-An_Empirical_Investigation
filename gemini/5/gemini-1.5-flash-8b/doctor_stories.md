Rain lashed against the corrugated iron roof of the clinic, a relentless drumming that mirrored the frantic rhythm of Dr.  Eleanor Vance's thoughts.  The flickering fluorescent lights cast long, distorted shadows across the cluttered examination room, highlighting the dust motes dancing in the humid air.  Outside, the emerald jungle of the Amazonian rainforest, usually a symphony of buzzing insects and chirping birds, was muted to a mournful hush.  This was no ordinary rainy season. This was the season of sickness, the season when the fever-ridden whispers echoed through the villages nestled amongst the towering trees.  Eleanor, a young doctor with eyes that held the weary wisdom of a seasoned veteran, traced a worn pen across a faded graph plotting the rising infection rate.  The chart, stark and horrifying, was a testament to the relentless advance of the mysterious illness sweeping through the remote communities.  She had been sent here, to the isolated village of San Vicente, a place shrouded in the deep green embrace of the rainforest, to combat the insidious spread.  Tonight, the fate of many hung in the balance.
