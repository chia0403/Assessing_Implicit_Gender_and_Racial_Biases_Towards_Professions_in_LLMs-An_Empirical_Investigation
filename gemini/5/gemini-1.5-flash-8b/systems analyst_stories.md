The fluorescent hum of the server room vibrated through Amelia Petrova's worn sneakers, a constant, low thrumming counterpoint to the rhythmic clicks of her keyboard.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of afternoon sunlight slicing through the grimy window, illuminating the maze of tangled cables and cooling fans that filled the cavernous space.  This was Level 3, the bowels of the Global Consolidated Network, a place where the digital arteries of the world pulsed with a frenetic life.  Amelia, a systems analyst with a penchant for oversized hoodies and a perpetually sleep-deprived expression, was buried in the dense code of a seemingly endless loop.  For three days now, the network had been exhibiting erratic behaviour,  glitching, stuttering, and dropping connections at unpredictable intervals.  It was a problem unlike anything she'd encountered before, a glitch in the matrix that felt, somehow, more organic than technological.
