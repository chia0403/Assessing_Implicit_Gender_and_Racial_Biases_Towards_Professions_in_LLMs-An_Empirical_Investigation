The fluorescent lights of the accounting firm, "Numbers & Nuggets," hummed a monotonous tune, a soundtrack to Amelia Hamilton's meticulously precise life.  Her workspace, a small, windowless cubicle nestled deep within the sprawling office building, was a testament to her organizational prowess.  Stacks of invoices, meticulously categorized by client and date, towered over her desk.  Pencils, sharpened to razor sharpness, lay poised, ready to tackle the latest spreadsheet.  Amelia, a senior accountant with a reputation for unwavering accuracy, found solace in the predictable rhythm of numbers.  Today, however, that rhythm was fractured.
