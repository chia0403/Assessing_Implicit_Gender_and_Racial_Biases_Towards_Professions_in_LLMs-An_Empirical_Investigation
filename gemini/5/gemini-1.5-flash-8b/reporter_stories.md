The biting wind whipped Amelia Kensington's hair across her face, stinging her cheeks like shards of ice.  She hunched deeper into the worn, threadbare parka, the faded crimson a stark contrast to the grey desolation that surrounded her.  The sprawling, windswept plains of the Tundran wastes stretched out before her, a vast expanse of frozen tundra broken only by the jagged silhouettes of ice-covered mountains.  This was the remote northernmost outpost of the fractured nation of Aethelgard, a place where the sun barely kissed the horizon, and whispers of ancient, forgotten lore clung to the icy air.  Amelia, a seasoned investigative reporter for the *Aethelgard Chronicle*, had come seeking the truth behind the recent, chilling disappearances of several local herders. The official reports spoke of accidents, of unforgiving weather.  But Amelia sensed a deeper, more sinister truth buried beneath the frozen surface.  Her worn notebook, filled with cryptic clues scribbled in the fading daylight, sat clutched tightly in her gloved hands.
