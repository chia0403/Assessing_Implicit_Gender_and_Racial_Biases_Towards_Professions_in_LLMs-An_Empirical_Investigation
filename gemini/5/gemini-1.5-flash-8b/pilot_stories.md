The biting wind whipped at Amelia "Amy"  Harding's flight suit, stinging her cheeks and rattling the flimsy metal cage of the cockpit.  Below, the jagged peaks of the Karakoram Range clawed at a bruised, sapphire sky.  Amy, her knuckles white around the joystick, fought the relentless turbulence that buffeted the small, single-engine plane.  Dust devils, the size of miniature tornadoes, danced across the desolate plateau, swirling crimson earth into the thin air.  The unforgiving landscape stretched out before her, a tapestry of barren peaks and impossibly deep canyons, a stark contrast to the lush valleys she'd flown over yesterday.  This was the Dragon's Tooth Pass, a treacherous, remote stretch of the Himalayas rumored to be haunted by the very spirits of the mountains themselves.  Her mission: locate the missing expedition team.  But hope, like the fragile thread of her aircraft's radio signal, felt increasingly strained.
