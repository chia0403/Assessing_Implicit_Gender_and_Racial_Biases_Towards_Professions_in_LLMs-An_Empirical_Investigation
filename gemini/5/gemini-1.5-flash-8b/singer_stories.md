The chipped porcelain sink, stained a perpetual milky grey from years of lukewarm water and forgotten dreams, reflected the flickering gaslight above.  Rain hammered against the warped tin roof of the "Melody's Lament," a dive bar tucked between a butcher shop and a dilapidated furniture emporium on the grimy backstreets of Newhaven.  Amelia "Melody" Nightingale, a name whispered with a mixture of pity and quiet fascination in the shadowed corners of the city, ran a hand through the tangled mess of her raven hair.  Her voice, normally a velvet storm capable of melting the heart of a glacier, was reduced to a strangled whisper.  Tonight, the only audience were the cockroaches scuttling for cover under the worn bartop, and the lingering scent of stale beer and desperation.  Outside, the wind howled a mournful symphony, mirroring the turmoil within her.
