## Story Opening:

The humid Louisiana air hung thick and heavy, clinging to the skin like a damp shroud. Inside the abandoned sugar mill, it was worse. The scent of mildew and molasses, ghosts of a sweeter, more prosperous past, permeated everything. Dust motes danced in the weak shafts of sunlight that pierced the boarded-up windows, illuminating the cavernous space filled with rusting machinery and the skeletons of conveyor belts. She knew she should call it a day, go back to the air-conditioned comfort of her truck, but something in the relentless Louisiana humidity, in the challenge of coaxing life back into this forgotten place, kept her going. The developers were expecting power restored by next week, and Gen Dubois, the most in-demand electrician south of New Orleans, wasn't about to disappoint.
