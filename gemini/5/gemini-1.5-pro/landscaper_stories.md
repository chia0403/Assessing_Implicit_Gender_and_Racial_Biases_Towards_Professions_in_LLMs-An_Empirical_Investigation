The biting wind whipped Amelia "Mia" Ramirez's braid across her face as she surveyed the frost-covered expanse of the Hemlock Estate.  Towering hemlocks, their namesakes, clawed at the bruised grey sky, casting long, skeletal shadows across the manicured lawns.  The estate, nestled deep within the Catskill Mountains of New York, was a monument to old money, a place where silence was broken only by the rustling of leaves and the distant, mournful cry of a hawk.  Mia, bundled in a thick canvas coat and sturdy work boots, inhaled deeply, the crisp air stinging her nostrils.  Winter was coming early this year, and with it, the annual frantic rush to prepare the grounds for its icy embrace.  This year, however, something felt different. A shiver ran down her spine that had nothing to do with the cold. There was a stillness in the air, a heavy expectancy, that made the hairs on the back of her neck prickle.
