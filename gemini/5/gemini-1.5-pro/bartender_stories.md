The rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of the Salty Siren, a rhythm Amelia “Amy” Reyes had come to find strangely comforting. Located on a windswept pier jutting out into the churning grey expanse of the North Atlantic, the bar was the last vestige of civilization before the endless ocean swallowed the horizon.  Inside, the air hung thick with the mingled scents of brine, stale beer, and desperation – a potent cocktail Amy had mastered the art of both serving and enduring.  Tonight, however, the usual Friday night fishermen and dockworkers were absent, replaced by a chilling emptiness that mirrored the storm raging outside. Only a flickering neon sign, depicting a mermaid with scales the color of seaweed and eyes like chips of sea glass, offered a splash of color against the gloom.  Amy, her dark hair pulled back in a tight bun, wiped down the already spotless mahogany bar with a practiced hand, her gaze fixed on the swirling mist that obscured the distant lighthouse beam.  Something felt wrong.  More than just the weather.
