The biting November wind whipped at Elias Thorne's thick tweed jacket as he surveyed the grounds of Eventide Manor.  Frost, delicate and lethal as shattered glass, crunched under his heavy boots.  The manor itself, a gothic monstrosity of grey stone and leaded glass windows, loomed over him, a silent, watchful presence against the bruised purple sky.  Ivy, the color of dried blood, clung to its aged walls, reaching skeletal fingers towards the manicured lawns Elias had spent the last fifteen years meticulously tending.  Eventide, perched on a windswept bluff overlooking the churning grey sea, was a place of stark beauty, a place of secrets, and Elias, more than anyone, knew the stories those whispering winds carried through the skeletal branches of the ancient oaks.
