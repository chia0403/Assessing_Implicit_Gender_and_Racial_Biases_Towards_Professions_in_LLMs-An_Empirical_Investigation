The biting November wind whipped around Dr. Eleanor Vance, plastering strands of her auburn hair against her chapped lips.  She tugged her wool coat tighter around herself and hurried across the cobblestone courtyard of St. Jude's Preparatory Academy, the imposing Gothic architecture looming above her like a disapproving headmaster. Gargoyles perched on the rain gutters, their stone faces seeming to leer at her as she climbed the worn stone steps leading to the heavy oak doors. Inside, the air was thick with the scent of beeswax and old books, a familiar aroma that usually brought her a sense of calm. But today, a nervous hum vibrated beneath the usual hushed atmosphere of the prestigious boarding school. A student, Thomas Prescott, had vanished two days earlier, and the tranquil facade of St. Jude's was cracking under the pressure of the unfolding mystery.
