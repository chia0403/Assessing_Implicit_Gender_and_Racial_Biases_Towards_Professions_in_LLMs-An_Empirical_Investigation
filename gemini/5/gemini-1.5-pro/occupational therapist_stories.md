The biting November wind whipped around Eleanor Vance's ankles as she hurried across the cobblestone courtyard of Elderwood Manor.  Flakes of snow, fat and lazy, began to drift down, catching in the gnarled branches of the ancient oak that dominated the center of the courtyard.  Elderwood, a sprawling Victorian mansion converted into a rehabilitation center nestled in the Catskill Mountains of upstate New York, usually exuded a comforting warmth.  But today, a peculiar chill permeated the air, a stillness that settled deep in Eleanor's bones, hinting at something more than just the approaching winter storm.  She clutched her satchel tighter, the leather cold against her gloved hand, and quickened her pace towards the heavy oak doors, eager to escape the unsettling quiet.
