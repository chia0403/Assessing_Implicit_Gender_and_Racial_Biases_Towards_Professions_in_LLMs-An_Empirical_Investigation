The bell above the door of Apothecary & Anodyne chimed a discordant melody as the last customer of the day, a Mrs. Higgins with a persistent cough and a penchant for gossip, finally departed.  Eleanor Vance Holloway, Pharm.D., let out a long, quiet breath she hadn’t realized she'd been holding.  The scent of eucalyptus and chamomile still hung heavy in the air, a comforting blanket against the encroaching chill of the late November evening.  Apothecary & Anodyne, nestled between a dusty antique shop and a perpetually bustling bakery on the cobblestone streets of Hollow Creek, Vermont, was Eleanor's haven.  Its shelves, lined with amber glass bottles and meticulously labelled tinctures, held not just remedies but also the weight of generations of Holloway family history.  The pharmacy had been in her family for over a century, a constant in the ever-changing currents of the small town.  Tonight, however, the familiar comfort felt different, tinged with a disquiet that settled deep in Eleanor’s bones.  The wind howled outside, rattling the ancient windowpanes, a sound that mirrored the unease stirring within her.
