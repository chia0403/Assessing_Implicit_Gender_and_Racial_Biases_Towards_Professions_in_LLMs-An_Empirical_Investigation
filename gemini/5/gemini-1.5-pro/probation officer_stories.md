The stale air in the probation office tasted of instant coffee and desperation. Fluorescent lights buzzed overhead, casting a sickly yellow glow on the chipped Formica desks. Rain lashed against the window, blurring the already grim view of downtown Bridgeport, Connecticut.  Eleanor Vance, a woman whose sensible brown bob and perpetually pursed lips belied a surprising well of empathy, tapped a pen against a thick file. It was labelled “Darius Jones – Case #23-47B – Grand Larceny.” Eleanor sighed. Darius was due in fifteen minutes, and if he didn’t show, it was back to the judge, and likely back to prison for him.  This was his last chance.
