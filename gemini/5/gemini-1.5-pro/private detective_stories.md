Rain lashed against the grimy windows of Elias Finch's office, a relentless drumming that mirrored the throbbing headache behind his eyes.  Neon signs from the strip club across the street painted lurid streaks across the water-stained walls, their garish glow a stark contrast to the dim, cigarette-smoke-haunted interior. Elias "Eli" Vance Finch, Private Investigator, sat slumped in his worn leather armchair, the springs protesting with every shift of his weight.  The office, tucked away on the third floor of a crumbling building in the heart of Los Feliz, Los Angeles, was a testament to both his chosen profession and his current financial straits.  Empty takeout containers littered the desk, vying for space amongst stacks of dusty files and a half-empty bottle of cheap bourbon.  The year was 1947, and the city of angels was wearing its darkest halo.
