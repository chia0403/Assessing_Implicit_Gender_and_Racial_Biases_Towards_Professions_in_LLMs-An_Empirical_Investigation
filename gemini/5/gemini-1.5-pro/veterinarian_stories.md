The biting wind whipped across the desolate expanse of the Mongolian steppe, rattling the windows of the Ger Veterinary Clinic and tugging at the brightly colored prayer flags strung above the door.  Inside, Dr. Naraa Altangerel, her breath misting in the frigid air, hunched over a struggling kid goat, its tiny legs twitching in her lap. The single bare bulb hanging from the ger's central pole cast long, dancing shadows across the worn woven walls, illuminating shelves crammed with dusty bottles and well-worn instruments.  Beyond the circle of light, the vast emptiness of the plains stretched towards a horizon blurred by swirling snow.  Winter had arrived early this year, and with it, a wave of illness that threatened the fragile livelihood of the nomadic herders scattered across the unforgiving landscape.
