The pungent aroma of perm solution hung heavy in the air, battling with the sickly sweet scent of hairspray.  Fluorescent lights buzzed overhead, casting a stark, unforgiving glow on the linoleum floor of "Curl Up & Dye," a small salon tucked between a laundromat and a pawn shop in the perpetually overcast town of Port Blossom, Oregon. Rain lashed against the windowpane, mimicking the rhythmic snipping of Amelia Grace Hawthorne's shears as she meticulously shaped Mrs. Periwinkle's poodle-like perm. Outside, the grey November sky wept, mirroring the general mood of the town, a place where dreams, much like freshly styled hair, tended to wilt under the relentless drizzle.
