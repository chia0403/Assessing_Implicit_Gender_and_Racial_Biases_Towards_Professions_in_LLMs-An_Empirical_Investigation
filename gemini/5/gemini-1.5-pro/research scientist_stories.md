The biting Antarctic wind whipped at Dr. Aris Thorne's parka, stinging his exposed cheeks despite the layers of thermal protection.  He squinted, his breath clouding the already limited visibility. The McMurdo Station, a cluster of prefabricated buildings resembling oversized shipping containers, was a distant orange glow against the endless expanse of white.  February, technically summer in this desolate land, offered little respite from the brutal cold. Aris, head of the Xenobiology research team, adjusted his goggles and checked the chronometer on his wrist. 03:17. Almost time.  Beneath the ice, in a cavern discovered only six months prior, something waited. Something that had defied every known law of terrestrial biology. And Aris, despite the gnawing anxiety in his gut, was about to meet it.
