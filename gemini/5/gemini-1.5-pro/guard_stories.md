The biting wind whipped across the obsidian plains of Xylos, stinging Elias Thorne’s exposed cheeks.  Crystalline shards of ice, no bigger than slivers of glass, danced in the gusts, pinging against his polished steel helmet.  He adjusted the grip on his spear, the cold metal biting into his leather gloves.  Above, two moons, one blood-red and the other a ghostly violet, hung low in the inky sky, casting long, distorted shadows across the jagged landscape.  Elias, his breath misting in the frigid air, stood vigilant at his post, Gate Seven of the Obsidian Citadel, the sole guardian against whatever lurked in the endless, desolate expanse beyond. For five years, he’d stood this watch, five years of uninterrupted solitude, broken only by the changing colours of the moons and the occasional howl of some unseen beast in the distance.  Tonight, however, felt different. The air crackled with an unnatural energy, and a low thrumming vibrated through the ground beneath his boots. Something was coming.
