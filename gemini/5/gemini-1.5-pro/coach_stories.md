The biting November wind whipped across the cracked asphalt of the St. Jude's High School parking lot, carrying with it the scent of decaying leaves and Mrs. Higgins' infamous burnt coffee from the teacher's lounge.  Floodlights cast harsh, skeletal shadows of the bleachers across the deserted football field.  Amelia "Amy" Catherine O'Connell, head coach of the St. Jude's Ravens girls' soccer team, hunched deeper into her worn varsity jacket, her breath fogging in the frigid air.  She watched a lone figure dribbling a soccer ball with a metronomic precision under the flickering lights.  The rhythmic thud of the ball against the worn turf was the only sound that broke the desolate quiet of the evening. Amy knew, without even having to see the girl's face, exactly who it was. This kind of dedication was a hallmark of only one player.  And with the regional championships only a week away, Amy was starting to feel a familiar knot of anxiety tighten in her stomach.  This year, everything was on the line.
