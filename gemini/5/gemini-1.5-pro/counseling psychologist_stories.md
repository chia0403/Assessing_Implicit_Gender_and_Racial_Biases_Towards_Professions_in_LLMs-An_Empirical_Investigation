The rain lashed against the gothic windows of Dr. Eleanor Vance's office, mimicking the tempest brewing inside her newest client.  Her office, nestled in a converted carriage house behind the sprawling, ivy-choked Hemlock Hall sanatorium in the sleepy, upstate New York town of Hollow Creek, usually felt like a haven.  Tonight, however, the usually comforting scent of aged leather and woodsmoke was overlaid with a palpable tension.  Outside, the wind howled through the ancient hemlocks that gave the town its name, their branches scraping against the glass like skeletal fingers.  Eleanor adjusted the teal shawl draped over her shoulders, the soft cashmere a small comfort against the creeping chill that seemed to emanate not from the storm, but from the man sitting across from her, his hands clenched tight in his lap.
