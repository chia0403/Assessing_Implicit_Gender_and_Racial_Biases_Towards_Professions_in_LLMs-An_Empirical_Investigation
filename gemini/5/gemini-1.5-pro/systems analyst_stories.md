Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of the Kestrel Corporation headquarters, blurring the already fading twilight view of the Seattle skyline.  Inside, the air crackled with a different kind of storm.  Amelia “Mia” Rodriguez, a systems analyst known for her calm demeanor and even calmer code, tapped a frantic rhythm on her desk with a pen, her brow furrowed in concentration.  The sprawling open-plan office, typically a hive of collaborative chatter, was eerily silent, save for the hum of overworked servers and the occasional hushed phone call.  Kestrel’s proprietary trading platform, the ironically named Icarus, had stumbled, and Mia, along with the rest of the IT department, was trapped in the freefall.  The clock on her monitor blinked 2:17 AM, a stark reminder of the hours bleeding into the next day, and the mounting pressure to resurrect Icarus before the Asian markets opened.
