Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of Amelia Vance's forty-second-floor office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her.  The Chicago skyline, usually a glittering tapestry of lights, was obscured by a thick, grey curtain of precipitation.  Amelia, CEO of Vance Consolidated Industries, tapped a manicured nail against the polished mahogany of her desk, her gaze fixed on the swirling vortex within her snifter of aged brandy. The city lights, like her carefully constructed plans for the company's future, were currently swallowed by the storm.  The mahogany desk, a Vance family heirloom passed down through generations of CEOs, felt less like a symbol of power and more like an anchor dragging her down into the depths of a problem she couldn't quite define, yet knew in her gut was about to explode. The air in the opulent office, usually scented with the subtle aroma of sandalwood, was thick with the electric tension of unspoken anxieties.  It was late, well past nine, and the only other light in the sprawling space emanated from the muted glow of her computer screen, displaying a starkly worded email that had arrived just minutes ago.  An email that threatened to unravel everything.
