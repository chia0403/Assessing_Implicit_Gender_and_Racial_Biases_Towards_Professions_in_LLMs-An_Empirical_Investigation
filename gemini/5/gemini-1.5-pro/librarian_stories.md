The frosted breath of November clawed at the leaded windows of the Alderanth Library, weaving intricate patterns that mirrored the frost ferns clinging to the panes. Inside, the air hummed with the quiet rustle of turning pages and the faint scent of aged paper and beeswax. Eleanor Vance, the head librarian, adjusted the emerald shawl draped over her shoulders, a pop of color against the muted browns and greys of the towering bookshelves. Alderanth, a town nestled deep within the whispering pines of the Blackwood Forest, was a place where stories were not just read, they were lived. And Eleanor, with her ink-stained fingers and a heart filled with literary lore, was its quiet custodian.  The library, a gothic structure of dark stone and stained glass, held more secrets than even its vast collection hinted at. And tonight, as the wind howled like a banshee in the eaves, one of those secrets was about to be unearthed.
