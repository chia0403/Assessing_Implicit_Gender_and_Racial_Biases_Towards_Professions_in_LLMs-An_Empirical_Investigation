The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne's auburn braid across her face as she fumbled with the lockbox on the wrought iron gate.  Frost crunched beneath her sensible heels, the sound echoing in the pre-dawn quiet of Elderwood Lane.  Towering oaks, skeletal against the bruised purple sky, lined the street, their branches interlaced like gnarled fingers.  Elderwood was the oldest, and arguably the most prestigious, neighborhood in Hollow Creek, Virginia.  A place where sprawling Victorian mansions whispered stories of old money and forgotten scandals, a place where Amelia hoped to finally solidify her reputation by selling the infamous Blackwood Manor.
