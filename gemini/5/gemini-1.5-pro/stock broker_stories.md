The biting November wind whipped around Amelia "Amy" Grace Hawthorne as she hurried across the cobblestones of Exchange Place, her breath misting in the frigid air.  The Trinity Church clock chimed six, a mournful clang that echoed through the canyons of Lower Manhattan.  Amy clutched her camel-hair coat tighter, the collar scratching against her cheek, and pushed through the revolving doors of the prestigious Sterling, Blackwood & Finch investment firm. Inside, the air thrummed with a nervous energy, a low-grade hum of ringing phones and hushed conversations that hinted at the volatile week the market had endured. Amy, with her crisp white blouse and tailored navy suit, looked the epitome of calm amidst the chaos, but beneath the composed exterior, a knot of anxiety tightened in her stomach.  This was her biggest deal yet, and the fate of not just her career, but potentially the entire firm, rested on her shoulders.
