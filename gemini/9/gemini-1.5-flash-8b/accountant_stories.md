The fluorescent lights of the accounting firm hummed, a monotonous drone that echoed Amelia Blackwood's growing anxiety.  It was a Tuesday, the day the quarterly reports were due, and the air in the cramped office on the fifth floor of the Zenith Tower hung thick with the scent of stale coffee and impending deadlines.  Amelia, a meticulous accountant with a perpetually furrowed brow and a sharp, almost predatory gaze, stared at the overflowing spreadsheet on her monitor.  Rows of numbers, each one a potential pitfall, snaked across the screen, mocking her with their impenetrable complexity.  Outside, the city thrummed with its chaotic symphony of honking cars and distant sirens, a stark contrast to the calculated precision she was desperately trying to maintain.  The Zenith Tower, a gleaming monument to corporate ambition, felt, to Amelia, like a gilded cage.
