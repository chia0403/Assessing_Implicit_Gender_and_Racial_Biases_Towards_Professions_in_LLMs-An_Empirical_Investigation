The biting wind whipped at Amelia Nightingale’s scarf, stinging her cheeks as she surveyed the sprawling ruins of Aerilon.  Dust, centuries old and imbued with the whispers of forgotten kings, swirled around her feet, clinging to the worn leather of her boots.  Sunlight, fractured by the jagged stonework of the collapsed temple, cast long, skeletal shadows across the deserted plaza.  Aerilon, once a vibrant city of emerald towers and sapphire rivers, now lay broken and silent, a testament to the passage of time and the capricious whims of history.  Amelia, a seasoned tour guide with eyes that held the wisdom of a thousand forgotten tales, traced the faint carvings on a crumbling obelisk, her voice, usually a calming balm to her visitors, now a hushed murmur to the empty air.  This was her most challenging tour yet.  Only a handful of intrepid adventurers had dared to follow the treacherous path to Aerilon’s heart since the tremors had swallowed the last of its civilization.
