The chipped porcelain sink, stained a perpetual, sickly yellow by years of spilled bleach and forgotten rinses, reflected the harsh fluorescent light of the salon.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of afternoon sun slicing through the grimy window, illuminating a strand of auburn hair, stubbornly clinging to a tangled mass on the floor.  This was Amelia "Sparkle"  Montgomery's domain, a haven of tangled tresses and whispered secrets, tucked away on a quiet side street in the heart of bustling, rain-slicked New York City.  Sparkle, her name a testament to the dreams she held within the confines of her tiny salon, "The Gilded Shear,"  was already wrestling with a particularly unruly client, their tension humming like a trapped hummingbird. The scent of hairspray, ozone, and something indefinably floral hung heavy in the air, a comforting, if slightly suffocating, aroma.
