Professor Eleanor Vance-Montgomery adjusted her spectacles, the faint scent of lilies and old leather clinging to the air around her.  The grand, if slightly creaking, oak desk before her hummed with the quiet thrum of a half-finished research project.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of afternoon sunlight piercing the gloom of her study, illuminating a collection of antique maps pinned to the wall, their faded colours whispering tales of forgotten empires.  Eleanor, a professor of Medieval History at the esteemed University of Ashwood, was known less for her charisma and more for her unwavering dedication to meticulously piecing together the fragments of history.  Today, however, a particularly stubborn puzzle refused to yield.
