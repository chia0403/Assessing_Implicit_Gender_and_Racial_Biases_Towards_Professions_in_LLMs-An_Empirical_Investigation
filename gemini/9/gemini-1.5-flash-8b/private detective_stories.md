Rain lashed against the grimy windows of the "Dusty Rose" detective agency, mirroring the tempest brewing inside Amelia “Ace” Cavendish.  The air hung thick with the smell of stale coffee and desperation.  Ace, a private investigator with a reputation for getting to the bottom of even the most tangled webs, hunched over a chipped mahogany desk, a half-eaten croissant clutched in her hand. The cramped office, located in a forgotten alleyway off the bustling cobblestone streets of  Newhaven, was her sanctuary – and her prison.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of afternoon light that pierced the gloom.  

Newhaven, a city steeped in history and shrouded in a perpetual fog that clung to the cobblestone streets like a shroud, was more than just a backdrop; it was a character in its own right, whispering secrets to the wind and holding grudges in the shadows.  Its ornate architecture, with its towering gothic spires and crumbling facades, seemed to hold the city's dark histories captive.  The scent of rain-soaked earth and damp stone was ubiquitous, punctuated by the rhythmic clang of carriage wheels and the occasional guttural cry of a street urchin.  Tonight, the city held its breath, anticipating a storm that threatened to engulf it.
