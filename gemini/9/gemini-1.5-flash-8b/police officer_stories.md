The biting wind whipped rain-slicked strands of Amelia "Amy" Harding's auburn hair across her face, stinging her cheeks.  The flickering neon sign of the "Rusty Mug" cast an eerie, sickly yellow glow on the already bleak alleyway.  This was the heart of the Underbelly, a district of fractured concrete and forgotten dreams, where the city's shadowed corners whispered tales of desperation and despair.  Amy, a seasoned Detective Harding, stood hunched beneath the warped awning, the scent of stale beer and something indefinably rotten clinging to the air.  Her trench coat, usually a reassuring weight of practicality, felt like a flimsy shroud against the encroaching chill.  A single streetlamp, its bulb sputtering weakly, cast long, dancing shadows that seemed to writhe with the restless energy of the city itself.  Around her, the city held its breath, a silent observer of the impending confrontation.
