The fluorescent lights of the Zenith Corporation hummed, a relentless, almost sinister, thrumming that echoed the anxiety settling in Amelia Blackwood's chest.  Rain hammered against the grimy, fire-escape-laden facade of the building, a relentless counterpoint to the incessant clicking of the antiquated, yet inexplicably reliable, reception desk computer.  Amelia, a petite woman with perpetually tired eyes and a scattering of freckles across her nose, adjusted the worn floral print scarf around her neck, the scent of damp earth clinging to it like a shroud.  She was, as always, the first face Zenith's clientele saw, the silent guardian of its labyrinthine corridors. Today, however, the air crackled with a different kind of tension.  A palpable expectancy, more ominous than usual, hung heavy in the air, thick as the smog that perpetually coated the city outside.
