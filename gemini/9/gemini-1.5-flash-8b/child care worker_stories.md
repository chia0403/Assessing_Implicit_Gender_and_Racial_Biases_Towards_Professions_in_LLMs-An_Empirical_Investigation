The chipped paint of the playroom walls, a faded shade of apricot, seemed to vibrate with the energy of ten small bodies.  Sunlight, filtered through the dusty windowpanes, cast long, dancing shadows across the colourful blocks and scattered teddy bears.  This was the Kingdom of the Kiddos, and Amelia Grace Hawthorne, a child care worker with eyes the colour of melted chocolate, was its benevolent, if slightly frazzled, queen.  Today, however, the kingdom felt less like a land of make-believe and more like a volcano about to erupt.  Four-year-old Leo, renowned for his dramatic meltdowns, was currently engaged in a full-scale battle with a brightly-coloured, yet stubbornly uncooperative, jigsaw puzzle.  His tiny fists pounded the unfinished masterpiece with the force of a miniature wrecking ball.
