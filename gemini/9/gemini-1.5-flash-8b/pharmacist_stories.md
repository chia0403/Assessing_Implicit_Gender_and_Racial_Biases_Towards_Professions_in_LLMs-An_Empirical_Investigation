The flickering fluorescent lights of the "Apothecary's Corner" hummed a monotonous tune, a stark contrast to the rhythmic thump-thump-thump of Amelia Hawthorne's heart.  Rain lashed against the storefront windows, blurring the already-murky cityscape into a grey, featureless canvas.  Amelia, her name embroidered subtly on the worn, white pharmacist's coat, meticulously checked the expiration dates on a stack of vials, her brow furrowed in concentration.  The air, thick with the scent of antiseptic and old paper, hung heavy in the small, cluttered shop.  Dusty shelves lined the walls, overflowing with jars of herbs, bottles of tinctures, and rows upon rows of meticulously arranged medications.  This wasn't just a pharmacy; it was a repository of whispered secrets and forgotten remedies, a haven nestled in the heart of a city that often forgot to listen.  Today, however, the whispers seemed louder than usual.
