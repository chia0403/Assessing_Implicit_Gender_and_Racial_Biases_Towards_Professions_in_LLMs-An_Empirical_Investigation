The humid air hung heavy, thick with the scent of jasmine and something faintly metallic, a whiff that clung to Amelia Josephine Blackwood like a second skin.  Sweat beaded on her brow despite the cool air conditioning that struggled to penetrate the bustling atmosphere of "Salon Serenity."  It was a Friday evening, the kind of night where the city outside pulsed with a million tiny lights, and the salon, nestled on a side street in the heart of the vibrant, but somewhat gritty, Brooklyn neighborhood of Bushwick, hummed with a contained energy.  Hair dryers whirred, scissors snipped with a rhythmic precision, and the hushed murmur of conversation, punctuated by the occasional happy sigh or frustrated mutter, filled the air.  Amelia, her fingers deftly maneuvering a pair of curling tongs, felt the familiar weight of responsibility settle on her shoulders. Tonight, she had more than just hair to style. Tonight, she had a life to alter.
