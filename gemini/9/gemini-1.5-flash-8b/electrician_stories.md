The flickering fluorescent lights of the abandoned warehouse hummed a mournful tune, a counterpoint to the rhythmic clang of Amelia Blackwood's screwdriver against the rusted metal conduit.  Dust motes danced in the lone shaft of sunlight piercing the grime-coated windows, illuminating a labyrinth of tangled wires and forgotten machinery.  Amelia, her face obscured by the grime of a thousand repairs, swore under her breath, a sound swallowed by the echoing silence.  The warehouse, a sprawling cavernous space, held the ghosts of forgotten industries, each corner whispering tales of forgotten dreams.  Amelia, a licensed electrician with a knack for the impossible, was its unlikely guardian. Tonight, however, even her expertise felt inadequate.
