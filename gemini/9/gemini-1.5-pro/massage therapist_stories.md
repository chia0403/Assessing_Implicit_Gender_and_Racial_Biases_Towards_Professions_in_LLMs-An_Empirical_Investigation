The rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of Serenity Now, sounding less like a soothing natural rhythm and more like a relentless army drumming on Amelia Grace Holloway’s last nerve.  The late November wind howled through the gaps in the eaves, whistling a mournful tune that perfectly matched the state of her business.  Serenity Now, Amelia's pride and joy, her sanctuary of scented oils and heated stones, nestled between a laundromat and a perpetually closed down fish and chip shop in the sleepy coastal town of Port Blossom, Oregon, was decidedly lacking in serenity.  The flickering neon sign outside, usually a beacon of calm in the otherwise drab strip mall, sputtered intermittently, casting a sickly green glow over the empty parking lot.  Inside, the only warmth came from the single Himalayan salt lamp illuminating the waiting area, casting long, dancing shadows that played tricks on Amelia’s tired eyes.  She perched on the edge of the worn velvet reception desk chair, chin resting on her palm, staring at the appointment book which, for the third day in a row, remained stubbornly blank.
