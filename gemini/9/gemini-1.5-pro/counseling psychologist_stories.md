The rain hammered against the arched window of Dr. Elias Thorne's office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside his newest patient.  His office, nestled in a converted Victorian house on the edge of the historic district of Ashwood, Pennsylvania, usually felt like a sanctuary.  Tonight, however, the flickering gaslight sconces and carefully chosen antique furniture seemed to amplify the pervasive tension.  Outside, the wind howled through the branches of the ancient oak tree, its skeletal fingers scraping against the glass, a counterpoint to the nervous tapping of a woman's foot against the Persian rug.  Elias, his hands steepled beneath his chin, watched her, his expression calm and reassuring, a stark contrast to the turmoil swirling around them.
