The biting November wind whipped around Eleanor Vance, making the already brittle leaves of the sycamore trees outside St. Jude's Rehabilitation Center skitter across the icy pavement.  Eleanor, her breath puffing out in white clouds, tugged her burgundy scarf tighter around her neck and hurried inside. The sterile, brightly-lit lobby of St. Jude's was a stark contrast to the grey, blustery world outside, but the underlying chill seemed to permeate even these thick, insulated walls.  This morning, it wasn't just the weather contributing to the chill.  A tension, thick and palpable, hung in the air, clinging to the nurses' hushed whispers and the strained smiles of the administrative staff.  Something was different. Something was wrong.
