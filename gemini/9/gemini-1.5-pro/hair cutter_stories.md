The flickering neon sign of "Clementine's Clips and Curls" cast a lurid, purple glow across the rain-slicked asphalt of Bleaker Street. Inside, amidst the scent of hairspray and anticipation, Clementine "Clem" Blackwood sharpened her shears, the metallic rasp a familiar counterpoint to the mournful saxophone solo drifting from the radio.  Bleaker Street wasn't exactly a hub of glamour, nestled as it was between a pawn shop and a twenty-four-hour laundromat, but it was Clem's kingdom.  Tonight, however, the air crackled with a different kind of energy, a nervous hum that had nothing to do with faulty wiring and everything to do with the stranger who had just walked in, his silhouette framed against the doorway like a character from a noir film.
