Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of Elias Thorne's office, blurring the already fading London skyline into a watercolour wash of grey. The shard-like fragments of the city lights, usually so sharp and defined, were diffused and softened, like stars seen through a telescope slightly out of focus. Elias, his full name Elias Vance Thorne, traced the outline of the Canary Wharf tower with his finger, a faint sigh escaping his lips. His charcoal suit, impeccably tailored, did little to conceal the tension knotted in his shoulders.  The office, perched atop the newly constructed Zephyr Tower, his latest architectural triumph, was a testament to his minimalist aesthetic: polished concrete floors, stark white walls, and furniture that seemed to float in the expansive space.  Tonight, however, the sleek, modern lines felt cold and sterile, mirroring the unease churning in his gut.  A single email, sitting unread in his inbox, pulsed with an ominous glow, its subject line a cryptic single word: "Lazarus."
