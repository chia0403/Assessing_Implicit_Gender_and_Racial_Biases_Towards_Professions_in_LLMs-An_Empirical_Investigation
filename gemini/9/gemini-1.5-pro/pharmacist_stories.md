The relentless Nevada sun beat down on the dusty windows of Yucca Springs Pharmacy, baking the "Now Hiring" sign taped to the glass a slightly darker shade of yellow. Inside, amidst the sterile scent of antiseptic and the faint aroma of cherry cough syrup,  Dr. Elias Thorne, PharmD, meticulously counted out hydromorphone tablets, his brow furrowed in concentration.  The air conditioning unit sputtered intermittently, struggling against the desert heat, and a single fly buzzed lazily against the fluorescent lights. Yucca Springs, a town clinging precariously to the edge of civilization and known more for its proximity to Area 51 than its pharmaceutical needs, wasn't exactly a bustling metropolis. But for Elias, it was home, or at least, the closest thing he'd had to one in a long time.
