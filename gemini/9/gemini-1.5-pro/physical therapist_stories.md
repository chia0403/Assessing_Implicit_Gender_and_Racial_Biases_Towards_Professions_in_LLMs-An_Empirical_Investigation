The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Mia” Rodriguez’s hair across her face as she hurried across the cobbled courtyard of the St. Jude’s Rehabilitation Center.  Gargoyles perched on the gothic building’s aged stone leered down at her, their frozen expressions mirroring the grim set of Mia’s jaw.  St. Jude’s, nestled in the heart of the perpetually overcast coastal town of Port Blossom, Maine, wasn’t exactly a beacon of cheer.  The salt-laced air, combined with the near-constant drizzle, seemed to seep into the very bones of the building, fostering an atmosphere of damp melancholy.  Yet, within these grey walls, Mia found her purpose, patching together broken bodies and spirits one painstaking session at a time.  This morning, however, the usual quiet hum of therapeutic activity was replaced by an unsettling stillness, a silence that prickled the back of her neck and set her on edge.
