The biting November wind whipped around Dr. Elias Thorne as he hurried across the cobbled courtyard of St. Jude's Hospital, the ancient stone buildings looming over him like disapproving gargoyles.  His breath plumed out in frosty puffs, illuminated by the sickly yellow glow of the gas lamps that flickered along the hospital walls.  Built in 1722 and clinging stubbornly to its outdated charm – and plumbing – St. Jude's was a labyrinth of echoing corridors and drafty operating theatres.  Tonight, however, the antiquated atmosphere was the least of Elias's worries.  A blizzard was brewing, threatening to cut off the hospital from the rest of Edinburgh, and inside its walls lay a patient whose life hung precariously in the balance.
