The rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of The Rusty Nail, a rhythm that matched the thump-thump-thumping of Elias Thorne's headache.  Neon beer signs flickered through the grime-streaked windows, casting a sickly yellow glow across the mostly empty bar.  It was a Tuesday night in Port Blossom, Maine, a town clinging to the rocky coastline like a barnacle, and the only patrons braving the November storm were two grizzled fishermen huddled in a corner booth, murmuring about the dwindling cod population.  Elias, his dark hair plastered to his forehead by the humidity, wiped down the sticky mahogany bar with a practiced hand, the scent of stale beer and lemon disinfectant battling for dominance in the air. The brass bell above the door chimed, a fragile sound swallowed by the wind’s howl, and Elias braced himself for another damp, disgruntled fisherman.  He wasn’t expecting what walked in.
