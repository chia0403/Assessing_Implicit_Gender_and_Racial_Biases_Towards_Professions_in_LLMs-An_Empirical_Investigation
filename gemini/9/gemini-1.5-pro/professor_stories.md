The biting November wind whipped around Professor Alistair Finchley, tugging at the edges of his tweed jacket as he hurried across the cobbled quadrangle of St. Jude's College, Oxford.  Gargoyles, slick with rain, leered down from the medieval stonework, and the gas lamps flickered, casting long, dancing shadows that seemed to mimic the anxieties swirling within him.  Alistair clutched a leather-bound journal to his chest, its pages filled with cryptic symbols and equations that had consumed his every waking thought for the past six months. Tonight, he believed, he finally held the key to unlocking their secrets, a key that could rewrite history itself, or perhaps, as a nagging voice in the back of his mind whispered, unravel it entirely.  The meeting was scheduled for midnight in the Radcliffe Camera, its iconic dome looming in the distance like a silent sentinel, guarding knowledge both sacred and profane.
