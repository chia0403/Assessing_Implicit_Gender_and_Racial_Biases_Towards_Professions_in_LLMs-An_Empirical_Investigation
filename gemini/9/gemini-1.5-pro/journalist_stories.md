Rain lashed against the grimy windows of The Anchor, blurring the neon glow of "Liquor & Lotto" across the street.  Amelia "Mia" Cortez shivered, pulling her threadbare coat tighter around her.  Inside, the air was thick with the smell of stale beer and desperation, a fitting aroma for a newspaper office on the verge of collapse. The clatter of a single, overworked printer echoed through the near-empty room, the rhythmic thumping a morbid heartbeat counting down The Port Blossom Gazette’s final days. Port Blossom, Oregon, a once-thriving fishing town, was now little more than a skeleton clinging to the rugged Oregon coast, and the Gazette, like the town itself, was slowly drowning. Mia stared out at the storm, the churning grey sea mirroring the turmoil in her gut.  Her editor’s words, delivered just an hour earlier, still rang in her ears: "One last story, Cortez.  Make it a big one, or we both go down with the ship."
