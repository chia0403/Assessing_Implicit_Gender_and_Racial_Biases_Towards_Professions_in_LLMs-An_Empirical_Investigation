The biting November wind whipped off Lake Michigan, tearing at the edges of Amelia “Mia” Rodriguez’s turnout coat as she stood on the roof of Engine 66. Below her, the city of Chicago shimmered, a mosaic of twinkling lights against the encroaching dusk.  The air vibrated with the low thrum of traffic and the distant wail of a siren, a sound so familiar it was almost comforting. Smoke, thick and acrid, billowed from a three-story brownstone a block over, staining the bruised twilight sky an angry orange.  The heat radiating from the inferno licked at Mia’s face, a tangible promise of the battle ahead.  Flames danced in the broken windows, hungry, relentless.  This wasn’t going to be an easy one.
