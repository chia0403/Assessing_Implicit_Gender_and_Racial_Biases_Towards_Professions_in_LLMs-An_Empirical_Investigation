The salt-laced wind whipped Aurelia "Aura" Moreau's hair across her face, stinging her cheeks as she struggled to hold her easel steady.  The slate-grey sky, bruised with the promise of a storm, mirrored the churning waters of the North Atlantic below.  Perched precariously on the windswept cliffs of Monhegan Island, ten miles off the coast of Maine, Aura was determined to capture the untamed beauty of the crashing waves against the jagged rocks. Her fingers, numb despite her woolen gloves, clutched her palette, a vibrant splash of color against the monochrome landscape. This isolated island, a haven for artists seeking inspiration in its raw, elemental power, had become Aura's refuge, her studio, and, for the past six months, her entire world.
