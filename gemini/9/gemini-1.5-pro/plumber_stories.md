The biting wind whipped across the desolate expanse of the Salt Flats, stinging Elias Thorne's cheeks as he wrestled a recalcitrant pipe wrench.  The setting sun painted the cracked earth in hues of bruised purple and fiery orange, casting long, distorted shadows that danced around his battered pickup truck. Elias, his full name Elias Vance Thorne, a third-generation plumber with hands as rough as pumice and a heart as sturdy as copper piping, squinted at the rusted wellhead jutting up from the parched earth like a skeletal finger.  The nearest town, Dust Devil Gulch, a speck of civilization clinging precariously to the edge of the flats, was a good twenty miles back, and the only sound besides the keening wind was the rhythmic clang of metal against metal. This was his last job of the day, a call-out to fix a busted pump for a reclusive old woman rumored to be more comfortable speaking with coyotes than people.  Elias hoped she had coffee. Strong coffee.
