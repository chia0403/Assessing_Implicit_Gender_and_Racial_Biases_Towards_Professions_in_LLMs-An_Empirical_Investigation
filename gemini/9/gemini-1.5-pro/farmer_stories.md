The biting wind whipped across the frost-covered fields of Abernathy Farm, swirling the dry, brittle leaves into miniature tornadoes that danced across the barren landscape. Elias Thorne, his weathered face etched with the lines of a life spent battling the elements, pulled his thick woolen scarf tighter around his neck and surveyed his domain.  The sun, a pale disc barely peeking over the serrated edge of the distant Blue Ridge Mountains, offered little warmth. November had arrived in the Shenandoah Valley of Virginia with a vengeance, painting the world in shades of grey and brown.  The harvest was in, the barns were full, and the earth lay dormant, awaiting the promise of spring. But in the stillness of that pre-dawn chill, Elias felt a growing unease, a prickling at the back of his neck that whispered of something amiss.
