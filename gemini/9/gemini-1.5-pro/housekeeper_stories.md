The grandfather clock in the hallway chimed six times, its hollow notes echoing through the cavernous silence of the Moreland estate.  Dawn seeped through the stained-glass windows of the grand staircase, painting the dust motes dancing in the air with fleeting rainbows. Amelia Rose Hawthorne, her hands already chapped from the early morning chill, adjusted her apron and surveyed her domain.  The Moreland Manor, a sprawling Victorian relic perched on a windswept bluff overlooking the churning grey waters of the North Atlantic, was her responsibility.  Every inch of its polished mahogany, every thread of its velvet drapes, every cold, echoing room rested on her capable, if weary, shoulders.  For twenty years, Amelia Rose had been the keeper of this house, and in a way, it had become the keeper of her, too.
