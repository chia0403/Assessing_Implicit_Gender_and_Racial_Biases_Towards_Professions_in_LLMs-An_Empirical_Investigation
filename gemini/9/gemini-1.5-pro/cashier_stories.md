The flickering fluorescent lights of the Quick Stop, a 24-hour convenience store nestled between a pawn shop and a perpetually closed dry cleaner, buzzed like trapped insects. Rain lashed against the grimy windows, blurring the neon glow of the "Open" sign into a hazy smear. Inside, the air hung thick with the cloying sweetness of stale coffee and the faint tang of desperation. Amelia Grace Hawthorne, her name tag slightly askew on her faded blue uniform, leaned against the counter, idly spinning a keychain featuring a miniature plastic hot dog. It was 3:17 am on a Tuesday, and for the third hour in a row, Amelia was the sole inhabitant of this brightly lit purgatory, save for the occasional scurrying cockroach.  The only sounds were the hum of the refrigerators, the drumming of the rain, and the insistent ticking of the clock above the lottery tickets, each tick a tiny hammer blow against the fortress of her boredom.
