The biting November wind whipped around Dr. Elias Thorne as he hurried across the cobbled courtyard of St. Jude's Hospital, the hem of his long coat flapping against his legs.  Gargoyles perched on the ancient stone walls, their grotesque faces seeming to leer down at him, frosted with a thin layer of ice. Above, a bruised sky threatened snow. Built in the 17th century, St. Jude's clung to the edge of the Yorkshire Moors like a stubborn weed, its imposing silhouette a stark contrast to the rolling, desolate landscape.  Inside, a labyrinth of dimly lit corridors and echoing wards housed secrets older than the stones themselves, and tonight, Dr. Thorne felt the weight of those secrets pressing down on him more than ever.
