The biting wind whipped off Lake Michigan, stinging Detective Isabella "Izzy" Diaz's cheeks and turning the falling snow into tiny, stinging needles.  The sodium vapor lamps lining the deserted South Chicago dock cast long, skeletal shadows that danced with the swirling flurries, giving the scene an unsettling, almost theatrical quality.  It was 3:17 AM, a time usually reserved for drunks, dreamers, and the desperately unlucky.  Tonight, Izzy belonged firmly in the last category.  A flickering red strobe light pulsed in the distance, painting the swirling snow crimson.  It was the only splash of color in a world of monochrome, beckoning Izzy toward the edge of the pier and the grim tableau that awaited her there.
