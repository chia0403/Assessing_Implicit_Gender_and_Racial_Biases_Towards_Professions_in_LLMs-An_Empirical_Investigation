The chipped ceramic mug warmed Amelia Katherine Hayes’s hands, the lukewarm chamomile tea doing little to soothe the icy dread that had settled in her stomach.  Rain lashed against the panoramic window of her Soho apartment, blurring the already hazy cityscape into a watercolor wash of greys and muted blues.  Amelia, a graphic designer whose portfolio boasted vibrant, almost aggressively cheerful designs, felt anything but cheerful tonight.  Her tiny, meticulously organized workspace – a chaotic constellation of Pantone swatches, half-empty coffee cups, and the ghostly glow of her iMac – was usually her sanctuary.  Tonight, it felt like a cage.  The deadline for the Blackwood & Sons campaign loomed, a monstrous, pixelated beast breathing down her neck, and the creative well, usually overflowing with ideas, had run frustratingly dry.  The only thing flowing freely was the rain, mirroring the torrent of self-doubt that threatened to overwhelm her.
