The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Mia” Holloway’s trench coat around her legs as she surveyed the scene.  Rain, a relentless, icy drizzle, plastered her dark hair to her scalp, mirroring the grim expression etched on her face.  The air hung thick with the scent of damp earth and something else… something metallic and acrid that clung to the back of her throat.  She stood on the edge of Blackwood Forest, a sprawling expanse of ancient oaks and twisted pines that clawed at the bruised twilight sky, just outside the sleepy village of Ashworth.  A single police car’s flashing blue light pulsed intermittently, a frantic heartbeat against the otherwise silent, brooding landscape.  This wasn’t Mia’s first foray into the darker corners of human nature, far from it, but something about this particular crime scene, the chilling stillness of the woods, the almost palpable sense of dread that permeated the air, sent a shiver down her spine that had nothing to do with the cold.
