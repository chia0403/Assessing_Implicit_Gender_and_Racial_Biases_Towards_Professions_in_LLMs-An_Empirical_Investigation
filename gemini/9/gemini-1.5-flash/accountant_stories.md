The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Hernandez’s hands, a meager comfort against the November chill seeping through the single-pane window of her cramped office. Rain lashed against the glass, a relentless drumming that mirrored the insistent thrum of anxiety in her chest.  Amelia, a meticulously organized accountant with a penchant for perfectly aligned paperclips and a crippling fear of deadlines, stared at the mountain of invoices sprawling across her desk.  The city outside, a blurry watercolor of grey and black, reflected the chaotic state of her own affairs.  Her tiny office, nestled above a bustling bakery on Bleecker Street in the West Village of New York City, usually smelled of warm cinnamon and sugar. Tonight, however, the sweet scent was overwhelmed by the bitter tang of impending doom – and possibly burnt coffee.
