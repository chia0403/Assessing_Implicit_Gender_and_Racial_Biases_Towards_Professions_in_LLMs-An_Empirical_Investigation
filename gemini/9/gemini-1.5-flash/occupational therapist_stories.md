The biting November wind whipped Amelia Hernandez's auburn hair across her face as she hurried down Bleecker Street.  Grey skies bled into the already dusky twilight, casting long shadows from the brownstones that lined the narrow Manhattan street.  The scent of roasting chestnuts and damp earth hung heavy in the air, a familiar, comforting aroma that did little to soothe the knot of anxiety tightening in her stomach.  Amelia, a registered occupational therapist specializing in pediatric rehabilitation, tucked her scarf tighter around her neck, the wool scratching against her already chapped skin.  Tonight, she wasn't facing a child struggling with fine motor skills or sensory processing disorder; tonight, she was facing Leo Maxwell, a notoriously difficult, seven-year-old boy and the newest – and potentially most challenging – patient at the Mulberry Street Pediatric Therapy Center.
