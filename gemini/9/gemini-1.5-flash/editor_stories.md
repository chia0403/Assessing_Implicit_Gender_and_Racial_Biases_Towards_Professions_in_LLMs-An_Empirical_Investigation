The chipped ceramic mug warmed Amelia Stone's fingers, the lukewarm chamomile tea doing little to soothe the icy dread that had settled in her gut.  Rain lashed against the tall, gothic windows of her office, blurring the already indistinct cityscape of London.  The gargoyle perched on the windowsill seemed to leer at her, its stone face slick with the downpour mirroring the grim expression on Amelia’s own.  Her cramped attic office, nestled high in the creaking timbers of a building that had witnessed centuries of London life, was usually a sanctuary – a haven of organized chaos filled with the scent of old paper and ambition.  Tonight, however, it felt like a cage.  The manuscript lay open on her desk, its pristine pages mocking her with their silent accusations.  Amelia, senior editor at Blackwood & Sons, knew this was the biggest decision of her career, and it was one she was desperately, terrifyingly unprepared to make.
