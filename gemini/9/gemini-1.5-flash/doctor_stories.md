The biting wind whipped Dr. Eleanor Vance’s dark coat around her legs as she hurried across the windswept moor.  Above, the bruised purple of twilight bled into the inky blackness of a November evening, the only illumination coming from the skeletal branches of ancient oaks clawing at the bruised sky.  Beneath her feet, the uneven, boggy ground threatened to swallow her whole with every step.  The isolated farmhouse, barely visible through the swirling snow, was a malevolent silhouette against the darkening landscape – a place where the whispers of the wind carried more than just the chill of the night.  Its single, flickering window offered a meager promise of warmth and, perhaps, a desperately needed explanation for the frantic summons she'd received just hours ago.
