The humid Louisiana air hung heavy, thick with the scent of jasmine and impending rain.  Cicadas buzzed a relentless, hypnotic chorus as Aurelia Evangeline Dupree, Realtor, wiped a bead of sweat from her brow with the back of her perfectly manicured hand.  Her crisp, white linen pantsuit, a testament to her unwavering professionalism, felt less crisp and more like a clinging second skin in the oppressive heat.  She surveyed the sprawling, moss-draped oaks surrounding the antebellum mansion before her, its once-grand facade now marred by peeling paint and the melancholic droop of neglected wisteria.  The property, known locally as Belle Reve, sat on a generous acreage overlooking the murky waters of Bayou Lafourche, a silent witness to centuries of secrets buried beneath its muddy banks.  Aurelia knew this sale, more than any other, would test her already considerable skills in navigating the treacherous waters of high-stakes real estate, and perhaps, more importantly, the tangled emotions of the seller.
