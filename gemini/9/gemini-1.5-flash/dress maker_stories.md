The scent of lavender and beeswax hung heavy in the air, a comforting blanket against the biting November wind howling outside.  Rain lashed against the leaded-glass windows of “Stitches & Dreams,” a tiny, almost impossibly cluttered dressmaking shop nestled on a cobbled lane in the heart of York’s historic Shambles.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of afternoon sunlight slicing through the grime, illuminating the kaleidoscope of fabrics spilling from overflowing baskets – silks the color of twilight, velvets as deep as midnight, and linens the pale hue of a winter moon.  Amidst this vibrant chaos, meticulously arranging the folds of a shimmering emerald gown, sat  Aurelia Blackwood, her needle flashing like a tiny silver hummingbird.  The rhythmic click-clack of her thimble against the fabric was the only sound besides the relentless drumming of the rain, a steady counterpoint to the tempest raging outside and the far more delicate storm brewing within her.
