The humid air hung heavy, thick with the scent of sweat and chlorine, a familiar perfume to Amelia “Amy” Hernandez.  Sweat plastered the thin cotton of her workout tank to her back as she surveyed the almost-empty spin studio at "Body & Soul Fitness."  The early morning sun, weak and watery through the large, arched windows, barely pierced the gloom.  Dust motes, illuminated by the faint glow of the emergency lights – a consequence of last night's unexpectedly severe thunderstorm – danced in the air, mirroring the nervous tremor in Amy's hands as she adjusted the microphone.  Outside, the remnants of the storm raged on, the wind howling a mournful counterpoint to the rhythmic whirring of the stationary bikes.  Today was the day. Today, Amy Hernandez, certified fitness instructor and accidental entrepreneur, was launching her new, high-intensity interval training (HIIT) class, “Phoenix Rising,” and the turnout, so far, was… underwhelming.
