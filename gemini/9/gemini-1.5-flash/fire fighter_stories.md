The acrid bite of smoke stung Lieutenant Evelyn "Evie" Reed's eyes, a familiar discomfort that did little to dull the adrenaline thrumming in her veins.  She coughed, the sound swallowed by the roar of the inferno consuming the old Victorian on Elm Street.  Flames, fat and orange as molten gold, licked at the upper floors, their hungry dance reflected in the shattered windowpanes.  The night was a chaotic symphony of sirens, crackling radio chatter, and the frantic shouts of her fellow firefighters.  Rain, a relentless, icy sheet, lashed against her soaked turnout gear, doing little to quell the heat radiating from the building, which threatened to collapse at any moment.  The air, thick with the smell of burning wood and something sickeningly sweet – probably melted plastics – clung to her like a second skin. This wasn't just another blaze; this was a fight for survival, both for the trapped occupants and for the brave souls battling the flames.
