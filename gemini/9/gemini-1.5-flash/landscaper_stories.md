The dawn painted the sky in bruised purples and angry oranges, a fitting backdrop for Elias Thorne's mood.  Dew clung to the emerald blades of ryegrass like tiny diamonds, but Elias barely noticed.  His breath plumed white in the pre-dawn chill as he surveyed the chaotic mess that was Mrs. Ainsworth’s once-pristine garden.  A riot of overgrown weeds, tangled vines resembling grasping claws, and a pathetic, skeletal rose bush – the whole thing looked like a scene from a particularly bleak horror film.  Elias Thorne, owner and sole operator of Thorne's Trim & Tune Landscaping, sighed, the sound lost in the rustling of the wind through the neglected branches of a weeping willow.  The air hung heavy with the scent of damp earth and impending doom – or at least, impending backache.  He adjusted the worn leather straps of his tool belt, the weight of his shears and trowel a familiar comfort against the churning uncertainty in his stomach. This wasn't just a garden; it was a battlefield, and Elias was about to wage war.
