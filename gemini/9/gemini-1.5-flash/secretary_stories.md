The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s hands, a meager comfort against the pre-dawn chill seeping through the single-pane window of her cramped office.  Rain lashed against the glass, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her.  Amelia, a secretary at Blackwood & Sons Law Firm for the past seven years, stared out at the sodden alleyway below, the flickering neon sign of Sal’s Deli casting a jaundiced glow on the overflowing bins.  The air hung heavy with the scent of damp concrete and something vaguely metallic – a smell she knew all too well, the pungent tang of impending trouble.  Inside, the cavernous office of Blackwood & Sons was still dark and silent, save for the rhythmic tick-tock of the ancient grandfather clock in the hallway, a metronome counting down to another day of carefully orchestrated chaos.
