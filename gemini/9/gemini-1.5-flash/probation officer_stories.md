The chipped paint of the probation office’s waiting room peeled like sunburnt skin, mirroring the slow, agonizing unraveling of Elias Thorne’s patience.  Rain lashed against the grimy windows of the building, a relentless drumming that matched the frantic beat of his own heart.  The air hung thick with the scent of stale coffee and simmering resentment, a familiar aroma Elias had come to associate with his job as a probation officer for the city of San Francisco.  He rubbed his tired eyes, the harsh fluorescent lights reflecting off the dark circles that had become permanent fixtures beneath them.  Today was particularly grueling; three mandatory check-ins, a court appearance, and the looming shadow of a high-profile case involving a young woman named Anya Petrova, whose potential for violent recidivism was, according to her psych eval, off the charts.  Elias sighed, the sound swallowed by the rhythmic percussion of the storm outside, knowing this day, like so many before it, would test the limits of his already frayed nerves.
