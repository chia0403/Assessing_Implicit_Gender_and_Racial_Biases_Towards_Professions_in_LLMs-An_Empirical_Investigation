The pre-dawn chill bit at Amelia “Mia” Hawthorne’s cheeks as she unlocked the bakery’s heavy oak door.  The air, still thick with the night’s dampness, hung heavy with the promise of rain, a scent she found strangely comforting, like the yeasty aroma already blooming from within the warm, inviting space of "The Flour Child."  Rows of gleaming copper pans reflected the dim light filtering through the dusty windowpanes, their surfaces still cool from the night, a stark contrast to the ovens, already humming with a low, comforting thrum. Outside, the cobbled streets of the quaint seaside town of Port Blossom were deserted, save for the occasional mewling cat and the distant cry of a gull. Inside, however, Mia's world was already waking, a symphony of flour, sugar, and the rich, earthy scent of freshly ground coffee beans about to begin its daily crescendo.
