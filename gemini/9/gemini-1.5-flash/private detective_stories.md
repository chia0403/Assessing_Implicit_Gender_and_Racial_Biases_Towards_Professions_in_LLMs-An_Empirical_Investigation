The rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of Jack Hartigan’s office, a relentless rhythm that mirrored the throbbing ache behind his left eye.  It had been a long night, punctuated by cheap bourbon and the sour taste of betrayal.  The flickering neon sign of the “Lucky Dragon” noodle bar across the alley cast a sickly yellow glow on the grime-coated window, illuminating the overflowing ashtray overflowing with crushed butts and the half-empty bottle of rye whiskey that sat stubbornly on his desk.  Jack Hartigan, Private Investigator,  wasn't known for his tidiness, and his office, a cramped space above a laundromat in the perpetually damp underbelly of San Francisco's Chinatown, reflected that perfectly. The air hung thick with the scent of damp wool, stale coffee, and something faintly floral – probably the lingering perfume of the woman who’d just walked out, leaving him with a cryptic note and a gut feeling that trouble was brewing, thick and potent as the fog rolling in off the bay.
