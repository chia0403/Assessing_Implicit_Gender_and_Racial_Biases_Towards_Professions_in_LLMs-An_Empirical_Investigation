The chipped paint of the "Sunshine Smiles" daycare center flaked like dying sunflowers under the relentless August sun.  Cicadas buzzed a relentless, hypnotic drone, a soundtrack to the chaos swirling inside.  Twenty-three miniature tornadoes of energy, ranging from toddlers clinging to their sippy cups to pre-schoolers engaged in a dramatic re-enactment of a dinosaur battle using blocks and shrieking, comprised Amelia Grace Hawthorne's current reality.  Amelia, with her perpetually smudged apron and hair pulled back in a messy bun that threatened to unravel at any second, navigated the room with the grace of a seasoned tightrope walker.  The air hung thick with the scent of crayons, spilled juice, and the faint, underlying aroma of something suspiciously like yesterday's tuna casserole.
