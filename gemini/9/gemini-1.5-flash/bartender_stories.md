The chipped Formica bar top gleamed under the sickly yellow light of the "Rusty Mug," reflecting the weary but practiced movements of Elias Thorne.  Rain lashed against the grimy windows, a relentless drumming that mirrored the steady thrum of discontent Elias felt deep in his bones.  The Rusty Mug, nestled on the wrong side of the tracks in the perpetually rain-soaked town of Havenwood, wasn't exactly the kind of place that attracted the city slickers or the high rollers.  It was a haven for the forgotten, the downtrodden, and the occasional lost soul seeking solace in the bottom of a glass.  Tonight, the clientele was particularly glum, a collection of weathered faces illuminated by the flickering neon sign outside, advertising lukewarm beer and even lukewarm hope. The air hung thick with the scent of stale beer, spilled whiskey, and a hint of desperation. Elias, with his calloused hands and eyes that had seen too much, was the silent observer, the keeper of secrets, the reluctant confidant to Havenwood's hidden sorrows.
