The rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of Anya Sharma’s tiny apartment, a relentless rhythm mimicking the frantic beat of her own heart.  Anya, a whirlwind of tangled brown hair and nervous energy usually contained by meticulously pressed blouses, was currently clad in a stained t-shirt and sweatpants, the remnants of a three-day coding frenzy.  Empty energy drink cans littered her desk, a testament to the sleepless nights spent wrestling with a particularly stubborn algorithm.  Outside, the neon glow of Mumbai’s relentless cityscape bled through the gaps in her curtains, painting shifting patterns on the walls of her cramped, but surprisingly cheerful, space.  The air hung thick with the scent of damp earth and frying pakoras from the street vendor below – a comforting aroma that did little to soothe her frayed nerves. Tonight, the success or failure of her project, a revolutionary new AI for medical diagnosis, hung precariously in the balance.
