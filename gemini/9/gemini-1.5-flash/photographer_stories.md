The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Mia” Hernandez’s hair across her face, stinging her cheeks a vibrant crimson that contrasted sharply with the muted greys of the Edinburgh cityscape.  Rain, a constant companion in this ancient city, plastered her dark, wool coat to her back.  From her vantage point atop Calton Hill, the wind howled a mournful symphony around the National Monument, its unfinished columns skeletal fingers against the bruised twilight sky.  Mia shivered, not entirely from the cold.  The weight of the assignment pressed down on her, a heavier burden than her battered Nikon D850 hanging from her neck.  She adjusted the camera strap, her gloved fingers finding purchase on the worn leather, a familiar comfort in this unsettling atmosphere.  She was here to capture the soul of Edinburgh, they'd said, but tonight, the city felt less soulful and more… menacing.  The impending storm, both literal and metaphorical, hung heavy in the air, mirroring the unease that had settled deep within her.
