The salt spray stung Professor Elara Vance's face as the wind howled a mournful dirge across the desolate, windswept cliffs of the Isle of Skye.  Grey, churning waves crashed against the basalt columns, their rhythmic roar a counterpoint to the frantic cawing of gulls overhead.  Elara, her tweed coat flapping wildly around her, clutched her worn leather satchel tighter, its contents – a collection of ancient Gaelic manuscripts – jostling against her ribs.  The late afternoon sun, a weak, watery orb sinking towards the turbulent Atlantic, cast long, skeletal shadows that danced across the crumbling remains of Dùn Bàn, a broch that had stood sentinel against the elements for centuries, a silent witness to untold stories.  Tonight, it seemed, it was about to witness another.
