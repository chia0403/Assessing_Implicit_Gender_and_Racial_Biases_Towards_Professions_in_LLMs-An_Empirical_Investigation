The scent of coconut and vanilla hung heavy in the air, a cloying sweetness that usually soothed Aurelia “Rella” Dubois, but tonight, it only served to amplify the gnawing anxiety in her stomach.  Rella, owner and sole operator of "Rella's Reflections," a small but perfectly formed salon nestled on the cobbled side street of Rue des Fleurs in the heart of New Orleans’ French Quarter, meticulously swept stray hairs from the polished mahogany floor. The wrought-iron lamps outside cast long, dancing shadows across the vibrant purple walls, adorned with vintage hairdressing posters and framed photographs of past satisfied clients, their smiles frozen in time like butterflies pinned to a board.  Tonight, however, the usual comforting charm of her salon felt oppressive, a gilded cage surrounding the unsettling truth that something was terribly wrong. The rhythmic clang of the streetcar on Royal Street, usually a familiar lullaby, felt like a relentless hammer against her already frayed nerves.
