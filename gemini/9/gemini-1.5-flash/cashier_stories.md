The fluorescent lights of "Quick Stop" hummed a monotonous tune, a soundtrack to Amelia Hernandez's Tuesday afternoon.  The air hung thick with the cloying sweetness of artificially flavored coffee and the faint, underlying aroma of stale chips.  Amelia, meticulously arranging a display of brightly colored candy bars – their wrappers gleaming under the harsh glare – sighed inwardly.  Another slow day in the heart of Harmony Creek, a town so quiet its only notable feature was the slightly off-kilter leaning of the town hall clock tower.  The only sounds besides the hum were the rhythmic tap-tap-tap of her pen against the countertop as she tallied sales and the occasional, distant rumble of a tractor somewhere out in the seemingly endless fields of corn.
