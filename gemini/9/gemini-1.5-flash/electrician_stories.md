The biting November wind whipped Amelia "Amy" Hernandez's hair across her face as she wrestled with a recalcitrant junction box.  Rain, sleeting now, plastered her already damp work overalls to her skin.  The old Victorian house hunched before her, a skeletal silhouette against the bruised purple twilight, its decaying gingerbread trim groaning under the weight of the storm.  Each gust rattled the loose shingles like skeletal fingers, and the rhythmic drip, drip, drip from the eaves was a relentless percussion against the symphony of the storm.  This was Blackwood Manor, notorious in the small town of Oakhaven for its eccentric former owner, its haunted reputation, and its perpetually faulty electrical system – a system Amy, the town's only qualified electrician, had sworn she'd never touch again. But the desperation in Mrs. Gable’s voice, the promise of a hefty sum, and the gnawing curiosity about the manor's dark history had proved too tempting to resist.
