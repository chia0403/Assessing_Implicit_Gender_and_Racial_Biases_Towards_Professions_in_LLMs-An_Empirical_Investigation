The chipped porcelain sink groaned under the weight of Elizabether "Beth" Cartwright's scrubbing brush.  Steam, thick as a shroud, rose from the boiling water, clinging to the ancient, stone walls of Blackwood Manor.  Outside, the wind howled a mournful tune, rattling the leaded panes in their deep-set frames, a sound that was as familiar to Beth as the rhythmic squeak of her worn-out rubber gloves.  Blackwood Manor, perched precariously on a windswept cliff overlooking the churning grey Atlantic, wasn't just a house; it was a mausoleum of secrets, each dusty corner whispering forgotten stories, each shadowed hallway echoing with the ghosts of its former inhabitants.  And Beth, with her calloused hands and weary heart, was their silent keeper.
