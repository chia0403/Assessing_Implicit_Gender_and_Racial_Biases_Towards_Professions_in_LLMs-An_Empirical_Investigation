The biting November wind whipped Amelia Hernandez’s dark hair across her face as she hurried down the snow-slicked steps of the St. Jude’s Community Clinic.  The scent of pine and woodsmoke, usually comforting, was overwhelmed tonight by the metallic tang of blood clinging to the frigid air.  St. Jude’s, nestled precariously on the edge of the sprawling, impoverished Pine Ridge Reservation in Montana, wasn't exactly known for its pristine surroundings, but tonight felt different.  Even the usually vibrant, if slightly chaotic, energy of the clinic's emergency room felt muted, replaced by a heavy, suffocating silence broken only by the rhythmic *beep* of heart monitors and the hushed whispers of the nursing staff. Amelia, her breath clouding in the frigid air, tugged her worn wool coat tighter around her, the familiar weight a small comfort against the foreboding chill that had settled over everything.
