The biting wind whipped Amelia “Mia” Hernandez’s coat tighter around her as she wrestled the recalcitrant gate open.  Rust groaned a mournful protest under the strain, mirroring the weariness settling deep in her bones.  The setting sun bled across the stark, snow-dusted landscape of the Colorado foothills, painting the jagged peaks in hues of bruised purple and fiery orange.  Below, nestled in a valley carved by centuries of glacial melt, lay the ramshackle collection of buildings that constituted the town of Aspen Glen – population: barely a thousand souls, and apparently, a significant number of injured elk.  The smell of pine and something vaguely…musky, hung heavy in the frigid air, a scent that promised a long night for Dr. Amelia Hernandez, veterinarian extraordinaire – or at least, the closest thing Aspen Glen had.
