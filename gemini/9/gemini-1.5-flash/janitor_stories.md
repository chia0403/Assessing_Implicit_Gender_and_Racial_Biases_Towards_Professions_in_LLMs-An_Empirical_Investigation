The fluorescent lights of the Blackwood Academy hummed a monotonous tune, a counterpoint to the rhythmic squeak of Elias Thorne's mop against the polished terrazzo floor.  Elias, a man whose life was measured in gallons of cleaning solution and the relentless march of dust bunnies, ran a damp hand across his brow, the sweat beading despite the chill of the October night seeping in through the high, arched windows.  Blackwood Academy, a gothic monstrosity perched precariously on a windswept cliff overlooking the churning grey Atlantic, was silent now, its once vibrant halls echoing only with the ghostly whispers of past generations and the relentless tick-tock of the grandfather clock in the entrance hall – a sound that Elias found far more unnerving than any ghost story whispered by the students.  The salt-laced wind howled outside, rattling the ancient panes, a mournful dirge that seemed to mirror the loneliness settling deep in Elias’s weary bones.
