The chipped paint of Amelia Hernandez’s desk matched the chipped optimism in her heart. Rain lashed against the window of "Wanderlust Adventures," a travel agency so quaint it bordered on pathetic.  Dust motes, illuminated by the single flickering fluorescent light above, danced in the stagnant air, a melancholic ballet mirroring the slow, agonizing decline of her business.  Amelia, a woman whose smile lines hinted at a life once filled with vibrant escapades, now sat hunched over a stack of brochures depicting sun-drenched beaches and snow-capped mountains – destinations she could only dream of visiting these days. The only sound louder than the drumming rain was the rhythmic tick-tock of an ancient grandfather clock in the corner, each second a hammer blow to her already dwindling hope.
