The aroma of lavender and sandalwood hung heavy in the air, a comforting blanket against the relentless Seattle drizzle lashing against the windowpanes of Serenity Touch Massage.  Rain hammered a steady tattoo on the slate roof, a rhythmic counterpoint to the soft, melodic chimes hanging above the reception desk.  Amelia Grace Hawthorne, her name as calming as the atmosphere she cultivated, sat hunched over her appointment book, the faint scent of peppermint clinging to her dark, curly hair.  The flickering candlelight on her desk reflected in her tired, hazel eyes, a stark contrast to the vibrant, joyful energy she usually projected.  Tonight, however, a bone-deep weariness settled in her bones, heavier even than the Seattle rain outside.  Another long day had ended, but the quiet of her usually peaceful sanctuary felt anything but.
