The flickering neon sign of "Benny's Bar & Grill" cast a lurid, greasy glow across the rain-slicked streets of Chicago's West Loop.  A biting November wind howled, whipping stray newspapers into frenzied dances around Amelia “Mia” Hernandez’s ankles as she hurried towards the entrance, her sensible heels clicking a staccato rhythm against the pavement.  Mia, a paralegal at the prestigious firm of Sterling, Vance & Davies, usually navigated the city’s labyrinthine streets with the cool efficiency of a seasoned detective. Tonight, however, the chill seeped deeper than just the weather; a bone-deep dread clung to her like the damp wool of her coat.  The urgent text message from her boss, Mr. Davies, pulsed insistently in her pocket:  "Be there. Now.  It's about the Hawthorne case."  The Hawthorne case – a multi-million dollar corporate espionage scandal –  was a viper’s nest of secrets, and Mia had a sickening feeling that tonight, she was about to be stung.
