The fluorescent lights of the OR hummed a monotonous song, a soundtrack Dr. Evelyn Anya Sharma had long ago learned to ignore. They reflected starkly off the polished stainless steel of the surgical instruments laid out on a meticulously arranged tray. Outside, the Seattle rain likely hammered against the windows of St. Jude's, blurring the city lights into a hazy kaleidoscope. Inside, however, was a dry, sterile world where precision reigned and seconds stretched into an eternity. Evelyn's breath fogged briefly against the inside of her mask as she leaned closer, her gloved hands poised over the open chest cavity before her. This was her domain, the battlefield where she waged war against disease, and tonight, the enemy was relentless.
