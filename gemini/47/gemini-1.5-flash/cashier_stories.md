The fluorescent lights of "Quick Stop & Shop" hummed a monotonous tune, a soundtrack to Amelia Hernandez's life.  The air, thick with the scent of stale coffee and slightly-spoiled milk, hung heavy and still in the early morning gloom.  Outside, the pre-dawn sky bled from bruised purple to a hesitant, watery grey, promising another day of relentless sun beating down on the dusty Arizona landscape.  Amelia, her dark hair pulled back in a practical ponytail, leaned against the counter, the rhythmic *beep* of the scanner a metronome marking the slow march of time until her shift officially began.  Her fingers, already aching from a previous night spent meticulously counting coins, idly traced the faded logo of the Quick Stop on the chipped Formica countertop.  Today, she hoped, would be different.  But then again, she always hoped that.
