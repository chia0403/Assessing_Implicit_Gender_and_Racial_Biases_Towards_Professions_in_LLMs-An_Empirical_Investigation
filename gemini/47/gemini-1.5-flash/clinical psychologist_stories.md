The stale scent of disinfectant clung to the air, a faint but persistent undercurrent to the aroma of brewing coffee – a scent Dr. Eleanor Vance, clinical psychologist, found oddly comforting.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of her office, blurring the already hazy cityscape of Seattle sprawled below.  Grey light, muted by the storm clouds, cast long shadows across her meticulously organized desk, the only bright spot a single, vibrant sunflower in a chipped ceramic vase – a gift from a particularly tenacious patient, a reminder that even amidst the bleakest storms, life stubbornly found a way to bloom.  Tonight, however, the sunflower felt more like a defiant challenge than a beacon of hope.  Eleanor stared out at the relentless rain, the rhythmic drumming a counterpoint to the frantic tapping of her pen against the notepad, a rhythm that mirrored the anxiety thrumming beneath her skin.  She had a new patient arriving shortly, a referral from the ER, and the details of the case were… unsettling.
