The rooster's crow, a raspy, disgruntled thing, ripped Elias Thorne awake before the first sliver of dawn touched the horizon.  Elias, a man whose hands were as rough as bark and whose face bore the map of a thousand sunrises, groaned and pushed himself up from his straw pallet. The air in his small, stone cottage hung heavy with the scent of woodsmoke and damp earth, a familiar perfume in the heart of the whispering valley.  The valley itself, nestled deep within the shadow of the brooding, jagged peaks of the Dragon's Teeth mountains, was a tapestry woven from emerald fields, silver ribbons of rushing streams, and the deep, somber purple of the ancient oak forest that clawed its way up the slopes.  This was home, a harsh but beautiful home, and Elias, despite the aches in his bones and the ever-present weight of worry, wouldn't have it any other way.
