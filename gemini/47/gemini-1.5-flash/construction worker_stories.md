The biting November wind whipped around Elias Thorne's ears, carrying the scent of damp concrete and exhaust fumes.  He tugged his worn, threadbare beanie lower, the rough wool scratching his scalp but offering little respite from the penetrating chill.  Below him, the skeletal framework of the new Zenith Tower clawed at the bruised twilight sky, a monument to ambition and steel that stood in stark contrast to the grimy, forgotten alleyway where Elias perched, a cigarette dangling precariously from his lips.  The city of Veridia sprawled before him, a glittering tapestry woven from millions of flickering windows, oblivious to the lonely figure perched on the edge of its relentless progress.  Elias, a third-generation construction worker whose calloused hands bore testament to years spent wrestling with rebar and concrete, was about to become intimately acquainted with a secret hidden beneath its gleaming surface.
