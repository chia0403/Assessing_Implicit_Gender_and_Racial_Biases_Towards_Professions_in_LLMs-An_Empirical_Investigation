The chipped paint of the dressing room smelled faintly of stale beer and desperation, a perfume as familiar to Seraphina “Sera” Nightingale as the scent of her own lavender perfume.  Rain lashed against the grimy window of the “Rusty Mug,” a dive bar tucked away on a cobbled alleyway in the shadow of Prague Castle.  The air hung thick with the promise of a stormy night, mirroring the turbulent state of Sera’s own heart.  Tonight, she wasn't singing for adoring fans or glittering spotlights; tonight, she sang for a handful of weary patrons and the hope of scraping together enough crowns to pay this month's rent.  The only illumination came from a single bare bulb swinging precariously above a cracked mirror, reflecting her pale face and the anxious glint in her emerald eyes.
