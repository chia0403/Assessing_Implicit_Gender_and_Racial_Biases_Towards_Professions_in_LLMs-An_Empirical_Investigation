The rasp of chisel on granite echoed through the narrow valley, a lonely sound swallowed almost instantly by the vast, indifferent silence of the Mourne Mountains.  Mist, thick as a shroud, clung to the slopes, obscuring the jagged peaks that pierced the bruised purple sky.  Below, nestled in a fold of the hills where the wind held its breath, lay the tiny village of Carrickfergus, its stone cottages huddled together like frightened sheep.  This was Elias Thorne's world, a world sculpted not just from stone, but from solitude and the rhythmic, relentless work of his hands.  Elias, his face weathered like the granite he shaped, was a stone cutter of renowned skill, but his heart, like the cold, grey stone of his quarry, seemed frozen in a perpetual winter.
