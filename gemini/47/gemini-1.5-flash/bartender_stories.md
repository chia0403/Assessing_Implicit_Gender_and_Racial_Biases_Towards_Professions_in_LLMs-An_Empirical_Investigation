The chipped porcelain mug warmed Elias Thorne's palm, the lukewarm tea doing little to soothe the icy prickle crawling up his spine.  Rain lashed against the warped windowpanes of the Rusty Mug, each gust a frantic drumbeat against the aged wood.  Inside, the dimly lit tavern was a haven of flickering gaslight and low murmurs, the air thick with the smells of stale beer, spilled whiskey, and the faint, lingering sweetness of pipe tobacco. Elias, his usual calm masked by a thin veneer of weary professionalism, wiped down the already spotless bar top with a practiced hand. Outside, the storm raged; inside, the Rusty Mug held its breath, waiting for whatever tempestuous drama the night might bring.
