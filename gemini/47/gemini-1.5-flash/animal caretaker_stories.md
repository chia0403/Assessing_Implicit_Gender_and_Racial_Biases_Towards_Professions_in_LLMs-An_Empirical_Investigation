The salt spray stung Amelia Hernandez's face as she wrestled a recalcitrant sea lion pup onto the scales.  The pungent aroma of fish guts and kelp hung heavy in the air, a familiar perfume in the cramped confines of the Seabrook Marine Rehabilitation Center.  Grey skies mirrored the turbulent ocean just beyond the weathered wooden fence, churning waves a constant backdrop to the cries of gulls and the mournful barks of the facility's residents: seals, sea lions, and the occasional, surprisingly grumpy, pelican.  Amelia, her bright yellow oilskins plastered to her lean frame, muttered under her breath, her dark braid whipping across her cheek as the pup, a sleek, indignant creature named Skipper, thrashed against her.  This was, as far as Amelia was concerned, a perfectly ordinary Tuesday.
