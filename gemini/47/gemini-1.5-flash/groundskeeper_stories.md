The biting November wind whipped Elias Thorne’s already-chapped lips as he wrestled with a stubborn oak branch, its leaves clinging to life like desperate memories.  Elias, a man whose life was measured in the slow, deliberate rhythm of the seasons, found himself unusually agitated.  The grounds of Blackwood Manor, normally a sanctuary of muted autumnal beauty, felt oppressive tonight, shrouded in a pre-winter gloom that mirrored the turmoil churning in his gut.  The ancient stone house, a gothic behemoth silhouetted against the bruised purple sky, seemed to watch him with cold, unblinking eyes from its myriad windows.  Fallen leaves, crisp and brittle underfoot, crunched like forgotten secrets with each heavy step he took across the expansive lawns, their ghostly whispers a stark counterpoint to the mournful sigh of the wind whistling through the skeletal branches of the ancient trees.  The air, thick with the scent of damp earth and decaying vegetation, held a chilling undercurrent – something metallic and… unnerving.
