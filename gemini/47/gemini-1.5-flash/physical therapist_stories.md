The biting November wind whipped Amelia Hernandez’s dark hair across her face as she hurried down Bleecker Street, the scent of roasting chestnuts and damp asphalt clinging to the air.  Rain threatened, a low, grey curtain hanging heavy over the already dusky cityscape.  Amelia, a physical therapist with hands that spoke a language of gentle pressure and firm resolve, pulled her worn leather satchel tighter against her side, the rhythmic thud against her hip a counterpoint to the city’s chaotic symphony.  Her destination: a cramped, sunless apartment above a bodega on the corner of Grove and Bleecker, home to her newest patient, a retired ballet dancer battling the unforgiving grip of arthritis.  The flickering neon sign of the bodega, casting a sickly yellow glow on the rain-slicked pavement, seemed to mock the vibrant life she knew Amelia would be attempting to coax back from the shadows.
