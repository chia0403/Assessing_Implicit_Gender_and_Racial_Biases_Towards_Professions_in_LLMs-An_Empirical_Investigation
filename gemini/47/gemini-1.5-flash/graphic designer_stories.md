The chipped mug warmed Amelia Hernandez’s hands, the lukewarm chamomile doing little to soothe the tremor in her fingers.  Rain lashed against the panoramic window of her Brooklyn apartment, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her.  From her perch on a battered, velvet-covered armchair, she stared out at the grey expanse of the East River, the churning water a chaotic counterpoint to the meticulously organized chaos of her workspace.  Empty coffee cups, sketches spilling from overflowing folders, and a half-finished design for a new brewery logo – a snarling, pixelated bulldog – littered the surface of her antique drafting table.  The air hung thick with the scent of burnt coffee and the faint, lingering sweetness of sandalwood incense, a testament to the long, caffeine-fueled nights she’d recently endured.  This wasn’t the calm, creative sanctuary she’d envisioned when she’d finally moved into this space; it felt more like a pressure cooker simmering on high.
