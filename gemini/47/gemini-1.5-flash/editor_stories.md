The biting November wind whipped Amelia Hernandez’s auburn hair across her face as she hurried down Bleecker Street, the scent of roasting chestnuts and damp pavement clinging to her wool coat.  Rain, a fine, persistent drizzle, plastered the posters advertising obscure avant-garde films to the brick walls of Greenwich Village.  Amelia, a senior editor at the prestigious literary journal *The Chiron Review*, pulled her scarf tighter, the collar chafing against her neck.  The manuscript clutched in her gloved hand, thick and weighty with the promise (or perhaps the threat) of genius, felt like a lead weight.  Tonight, she had a deadline. Not just any deadline – the final decision on whether or not to publish Elias Thorne’s long-awaited – and highly controversial – novel, *The Obsidian Mirror*. The fate of Thorne's career, and possibly the future of *The Chiron Review* itself, rested in her hands.
