The scent of rosemary and burnt sugar hung heavy in the air, a peculiar but familiar perfume for Aurelia "Relia"  Montclair.  Relia, with her nimble fingers stained a permanent henna-orange from years of handling dyes, perched on a worn, velvet-covered stool behind her antique barber's chair.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of sunlight slicing through the grimy window of her salon, "Scissors & Suds," nestled on a cobbled side street in the heart of Prague's Old Town.  The chipped paint of the building, a faded ochre, matched the autumn leaves swirling outside, mirroring the melancholic beauty that permeated both the city and Relia's small, but beloved, establishment.
