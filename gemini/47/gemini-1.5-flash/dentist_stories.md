The biting wind whipped Dr. Eleanor Vance’s tailored coat around her legs as she hurried across the deserted town square.  Snow, the kind that clung with icy tenacity, dusted the Victorian facades of Blackwood, transforming the usually quaint New England town into a frosted fairytale – a fairytale with a distinctly sinister undercurrent.  The single streetlamp casting a sickly yellow glow on the cobblestones barely pierced the gloom, its light swallowed by the towering shadow of Blackwood Manor, a gothic monstrosity perched on the hill overlooking the town like a brooding gargoyle.  Eleanor tugged her scarf tighter, the chill seeping into her bones despite the warmth of her wool lining.  Tonight, however, the biting wind wasn't what chilled her. It was the unspoken dread that hung heavy in the air, a tangible weight settling on Blackwood, a weight that even the crisp winter air couldn’t disperse.  The emergency call had been cryptic, the patient’s name withheld, the only clue a whispered address: Blackwood Manor.
