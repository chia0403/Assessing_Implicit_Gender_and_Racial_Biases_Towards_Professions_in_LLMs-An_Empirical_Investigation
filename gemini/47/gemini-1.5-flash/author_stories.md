The salt spray stung Amelia Grace Blackwood’s face as she wrestled with the recalcitrant fishing net.  The late afternoon sun, a bruised plum sinking into the churning Atlantic, cast long shadows across the jagged rocks of the Isle of Skye.  Amelia, a writer of gothic thrillers whose novels were as dark and brooding as the Scottish Highlands themselves, usually found inspiration nestled within the confines of her cozy cottage, surrounded by crackling fires and steaming mugs of tea. But today, the muse had eluded her, replaced by a stubborn knot of writer’s block that felt as tangled as the fishing net she now fought to unravel. The wind, a relentless, keening banshee, howled a mournful melody around her, mirroring the unsettled feeling that had taken root deep within her soul.  The scent of brine and seaweed filled the air, a stark contrast to the heady fragrance of ink and old paper she was accustomed to.
