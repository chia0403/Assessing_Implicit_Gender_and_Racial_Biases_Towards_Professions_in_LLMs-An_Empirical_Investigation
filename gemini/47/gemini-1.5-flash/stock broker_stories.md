The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne's tailored cashmere coat around her legs as she hurried across Wall Street, the frantic rhythm of her heels a counterpoint to the city's relentless hum.  Rain, sleeting in spite of the relatively mild temperature, plastered her auburn hair to her forehead, obscuring the sharp angles of her usually impeccably composed face.  The towering skyscrapers, normally symbols of ambition and power, felt tonight like oppressive monoliths, their glass facades reflecting a sky the colour of bruised plums.  Amelia, a junior partner at the prestigious firm of Blackwood & Thorne, hadn't felt this level of unease since the market crash of '08. Tonight, however, the tremor wasn't in the global economy, but in the very foundations of her own carefully constructed world.
