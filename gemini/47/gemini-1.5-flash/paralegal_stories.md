The stale air in the Legal Eagle’s office hung thick with the scent of cheap coffee and desperation.  Dust motes, illuminated by the single, flickering fluorescent tube above Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s desk, danced in the weak light like tiny, frantic souls.  Amelia, a paralegal with a perpetually tired expression and eyes that held the weary wisdom of someone twice her age, rubbed her temples.  Outside, the relentless Chicago wind howled a mournful ballad against the grimy windowpane, mirroring the turmoil brewing in her already overflowing inbox.  It was Tuesday, which meant another mountain of paperwork awaited, another day spent wrestling with incomprehensible legal jargon, and another evening spent wishing she’d chosen a career that didn’t involve the constant threat of existential dread.
