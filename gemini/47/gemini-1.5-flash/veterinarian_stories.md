The biting wind whipped Dr. Eleanor Vance's hair across her face as she wrestled the heavy oak door of the Alder Creek Animal Clinic open.  The scent of antiseptic and pine – a familiar comfort after a long day – battled with the raw, earthy smell of the approaching storm.  Alder Creek, nestled deep within the rugged, snow-dusted mountains of Montana, was a place where the wild things roamed freely, and often found their way, injured and frightened, onto her doorstep. Tonight, the air crackled with a different kind of tension, a premonition of something more than just a blizzard brewing.  The clinic, usually a beacon of warmth in the frosty landscape, felt strangely isolated, a lonely sentinel against the encroaching darkness.  The only sound louder than the wind was the rhythmic tick-tock of the grandfather clock in the waiting room, counting down the seconds until whatever was coming, arrived.
