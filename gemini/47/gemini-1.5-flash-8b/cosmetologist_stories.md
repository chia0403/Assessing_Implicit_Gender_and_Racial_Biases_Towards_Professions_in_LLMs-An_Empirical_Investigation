The humid air hung thick and heavy in the salon, a suffocating blanket woven from the scent of lavender, rosemary, and the faint, cloying sweetness of cheap hairspray.  Sunlight, filtered through the grime-coated windows, cast long, distorted shadows across the polished floors.  This was "The Gilded Scissors," a salon clinging precariously to its glory days, a place where faded posters of glamorous Hollywood divas competed with peeling paint and the lingering scent of yesterday’s regret.  

Anya Petrova, a cosmetologist with a heart as fiery as the crimson highlights she loved to weave into her clients’ hair, meticulously applied a final layer of color to a streak of auburn just below her client’s ear.  Anya, with her perpetually smudged eyeshadow and a tangle of auburn curls escaping her messy bun, traced the delicate line of a wave that mirrored the tempestuous sea she knew lurked within her.
