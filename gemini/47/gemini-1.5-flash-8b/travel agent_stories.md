The chipped ceramic mug warmed Amelia Nightingale's hands, the faint scent of Earl Grey clinging to the steam.  Sunlight, filtered through the dusty-rose curtains of her tiny office, painted streaks of gold across the overflowing paperwork on her desk.  Amelia, a travel agent with more "exotic" destinations tucked into her memory banks than she could comfortably catalogue, sighed, a sound like wind whistling through a mountain pass.  Outside, the London drizzle, a constant companion throughout her career, drummed against the windowpane, a persistent reminder of the mundane world waiting just beyond the threshold of her meticulously organized travel agency.  Amelia Nightingale, with her perpetually tired eyes and a laugh that held the echo of distant jungles and sun-drenched beaches, was bracing herself for another day of meticulously crafted itineraries, the kind that could whisk clients from the grey of their daily lives to the vibrant colours of far-flung lands.  But today, a particularly insistent whisper, more a tremor than a sound, whispered an unnerving message through the otherwise quiet office: something was amiss. The usual hum of the city had a slight, unsettling discordant note.  Her world, usually so meticulously organized, was about to unravel.
