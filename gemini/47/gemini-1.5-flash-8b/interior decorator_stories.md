The relentless midday sun beat down on the terracotta rooftops of the old quarter, baking the air thick and shimmering.  Dust motes danced in the hazy light, swirling around the intricate carvings of the ancient stone buildings.  Inside, nestled within a courtyard garden brimming with vibrant bougainvillea, lay Amelia Dubois, Interior Decorator Extraordinary.  Amelia, with her fiery red hair pulled back in a practical braid, and eyes the colour of a stormy sea, hunched over a worn leather-bound sketchpad.  Sunlight, filtered through the leaves, illuminated the meticulous pencil lines that mapped out a grand, yet subtly modern, design for the crumbling ancestral home of the wealthy and notoriously demanding, Lord Ashworth.  The scent of jasmine and damp earth clung to the air, a fragrant counterpoint to the cloying heat.  This was the heart of Marrakech, and Amelia was determined to breathe new life into its hallowed stones.
