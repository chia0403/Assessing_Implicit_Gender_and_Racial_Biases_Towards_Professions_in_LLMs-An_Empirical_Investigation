The scent of chamomile and something faintly metallic hung heavy in the air of "Shear Bliss," a small salon tucked away on a cobbled street in the heart of the bustling, yet somehow serene, city of Aethelgard.  Rain lashed against the narrow, arched windows, mirroring the tempest brewing inside Amelia Juniper, a young hair cutter with hands as steady as the ancient oak tables that lined the salon walls.  Amelia, with her perpetually tangled auburn braid escaping its velvet-lined braid keeper, was meticulously trimming the unruly curls of a client, a wiry woman named Elara, whose face, usually etched with a quiet strength, now bore an anxious frown.  The rhythmic snip, snip, snip of the shears echoed the frantic thumping of Amelia's own heart.  Today was the anniversary of her grandmother's disappearance.  Every year, this anniversary brought a surge of unsettling unease, a feeling as sharp and piercing as the edges of the razor blades she used to craft her art.
