Professor Eleanor Vance, a woman whose tweed jacket seemed perpetually rumpled and whose spectacles perched precariously on the bridge of her nose, stared out the rain-streaked window of her cluttered study.  The air, thick with the scent of old paper and damp earth, hung heavy within the small, ivy-covered cottage nestled on the edge of Blackwood Forest.  Autumn leaves, a riot of russet and crimson, swirled and danced in the relentless downpour, mirroring the turmoil churning within her.  Outside, the gnarled branches of ancient oaks clawed at the grey sky, their skeletal fingers reaching out as if grasping at something lost.  Blackwood Forest, for generations a place of whispered legends and forgotten paths, held a secret, a secret Professor Vance had dedicated her life to unearthing.  This rainy November evening, it felt as though the forest itself held its breath, waiting.
