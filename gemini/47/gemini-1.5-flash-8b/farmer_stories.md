The biting wind whipped across the skeletal branches of the gnarled apple trees, rattling the rusted corrugated iron roof of Elara's farmhouse.  Rain, in sheets now, hammered relentlessly against the metal, a relentless percussion that echoed the frantic rhythm in Elara Blackwood's heart.  Her small, isolated farm, nestled deep within the Valley of Whispering Pines, was more a testament to stubborn resilience than prosperity.  Stone walls, patched and crumbling, enclosed fields of frost-bitten turnips and struggling potatoes.  The valley itself, normally a verdant tapestry of emerald green, was now a muted palette of grey and brown, a landscape mirroring the despair creeping into Elara's soul.  Today was the day.  The bank representative was coming, and Elara knew, with a chilling certainty, that this might be the day she lost everything.
