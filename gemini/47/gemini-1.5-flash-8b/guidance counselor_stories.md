The flickering fluorescent lights of the high school hallway hummed a monotonous tune, a soundtrack to Amelia Grayson’s weary sigh.  October air, thick with the scent of damp earth and woodsmoke from the nearby forest, seeped in through the cracked windowpanes.  Dust motes danced in the pale light, mirroring the anxieties swirling in Amelia’s stomach.  She was a guidance counselor at Northwood High, a school tucked into the foothills of the Blue Ridge Mountains, a place where the weight of the future felt heavier than the crisp autumn leaves crunching beneath her boots.  Every day was a puzzle, a maze of complicated emotions and whispered anxieties, each student a unique piece vying for her attention.  Today, though, the puzzle felt impossibly fragmented, and the answers seemed further away than the distant peaks.  The chipped paint on the hallway walls, a faded shade of robin's egg blue, reflected her own bruised spirit.
