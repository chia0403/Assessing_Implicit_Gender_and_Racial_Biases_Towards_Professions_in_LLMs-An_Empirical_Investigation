Rain lashed against the arched windows of Eleanor Vance's attic apartment, mimicking the relentless drumming headache pounding behind her eyes.  The only light in the small, book-lined space came from the emerald green banker's lamp perched precariously on a teetering stack of manuscripts. Outside, the Parisian rooftops gleamed slickly under the November downpour, the gargoyles of Notre Dame, barely visible through the hazy grey, seeming to leer down at the city. Eleanor, senior editor at the prestigious publishing house, Éditions Lumière, pushed a stray lock of auburn hair out of her face and sighed. The manuscript in front of her, touted as the next literary sensation, was, in her professional opinion, utter drivel.  And she was on deadline.
