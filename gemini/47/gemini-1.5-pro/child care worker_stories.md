The air in the Bumblebrook Early Learning Center buzzed with the chaotic symphony of toddlerhood.  Tiny hands smeared finger paint across construction paper suns, miniature voices chanted a nonsensical rhyme about a purple pig, and the pervasive scent of apple juice and disinfectant hung heavy in the air. Amelia "Amy" Rose Hawthorne, her blonde braid slightly askew, knelt amidst the controlled chaos, mediating a fierce tug-of-war over a particularly coveted red block.  Outside, the late October wind rustled the leaves of the ancient oak that dominated the playground, its branches scraping against the large bay windows like skeletal fingers. Bumblebrook, nestled in the heart of the sleepy town of Harmony Creek, Vermont, was usually an oasis of playful calm, but today, a subtle undercurrent of unease thrummed beneath the surface, a feeling Amy couldn't quite put her finger on.
