The biting November wind whipped through the skeletal steel frame of the "Phoenix Tower," sending a shiver down Elias Vance's spine that had nothing to do with the cold.  Fifty stories below, the Chicago River churned a steely grey, mirroring the sky above.  Elias, perched precariously on a steel beam, tightened his grip on his wrench, the rough metal cold against his calloused fingers.  The wind howled, a lonely sound amplified by the echoing canyons of the half-finished skyscraper, and Elias felt a familiar knot of unease tighten in his gut.  This wasn’t just the usual height-induced jitters.  Something felt… off.  The air crackled with an unseen energy, and the shadows cast by the girders seemed deeper, more menacing than usual.  He’d felt it ever since they started digging the foundations, a subtle wrongness that clung to the site like the ever-present dust. And today, it was stronger than ever.
