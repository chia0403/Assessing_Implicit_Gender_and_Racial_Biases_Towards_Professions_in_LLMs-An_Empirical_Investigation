Rain lashed against the grimy windows of The Daily Clarion, mimicking the relentless drumming in Amelia Grace Holloway's head.  Neon signs from the strip clubs and pawn shops across Bleecker Street reflected in the slick pavement, painting the decaying brownstones in lurid hues of pink and green.  It was 2:17 AM in Greenwich Village, New York City, a time when the city exhaled its most potent mix of despair and desperation. Amelia, hunched over her battered typewriter, chewed on the end of a pen, the bitter ink staining her lower lip. The ashtray on her desk overflowed with crumpled cigarette butts, a testament to the dead-end she'd reached in her investigation into the string of disappearances plaguing the neighborhood.  Each victim, so far, had been a street artist, swallowed whole by the city's insatiable darkness.
