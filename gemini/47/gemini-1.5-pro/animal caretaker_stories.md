The biting wind whipped Amelia "Amy" Hawthorne's auburn braid across her face as she trudged through the ankle-deep snow towards the primate enclosure.  Dawn hadn't quite broken over the sprawling grounds of the Aisling Wildlife Sanctuary, nestled deep within the redwood forests of Northern California.  The air, crisp and pine-scented, was filled with the raucous calls of unseen birds and the low, rumbling grunts of Ziggy, the sanctuary’s resident grumpy baboon, who was clearly not a fan of the early hour.  Amy’s breath puffed out in white clouds, momentarily obscuring the frosted tips of the redwood giants that loomed around her like ancient sentinels. The sanctuary, a haven for rescued and rehabilitated animals, was her world, and each frosty morning brought a renewed sense of purpose.  Today, however, a strange tension hung in the air, a prickly feeling that had nothing to do with the cold.
