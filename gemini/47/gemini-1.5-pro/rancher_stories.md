The wind, a relentless sculptor, had carved deep lines into Elias Vance's face, mirroring the gullies it had etched into the parched earth of his ranch. The Double V, nestled in the heart of the Montana badlands, sprawled across thousands of acres of sun-baked prairie, dotted with stubborn clumps of sagebrush and the bleached bones of long-forgotten cattle.  The sky, a vast, unforgiving blue, offered no promise of rain, only the shimmering heat haze that danced above the cracked earth.  Elias, his weathered Stetson pulled low over his eyes, squinted at the horizon, a knot of worry tightening in his gut. The air, usually alive with the lowing of his Hereford herd, was eerily silent.
