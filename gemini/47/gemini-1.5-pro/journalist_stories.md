Rain lashed against the grimy windows of The Mariner's Rest, blurring the already fading neon sign that proclaimed it "Finest Grog in Grimsby."  Inside, the air hung thick with the mingled scents of stale beer, fried fish, and despair.  Eleanor Vance, a journalist with the Grimsby Gazette, hunched deeper into her threadbare coat, the damp seeping into her bones.  Across from her, a fisherman with eyes as grey and turbulent as the North Sea nursed a pint, his calloused hands trembling. Grimsby, a once-proud fishing port on the eastern coast of England, was now a skeleton of its former self, and tonight, Eleanor was chasing a story that smelled as fishy as the docks at low tide.
