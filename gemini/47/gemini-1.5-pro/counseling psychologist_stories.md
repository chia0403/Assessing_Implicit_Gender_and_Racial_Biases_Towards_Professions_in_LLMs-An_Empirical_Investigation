The rain hammered against the arched window of Dr. Elias Thorne's office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside his latest patient.  Elias, full name Elias Peregrine Thorne, adjusted his spectacles and made a note on his legal pad, the scratch of his pen a fragile counterpoint to the downpour. His office, nestled on the third floor of a converted Victorian townhouse in the historic Beacon Hill neighborhood of Boston, usually offered a calming view of the Charles River.  Today, however, the river was a churning, slate-grey mass, obscured by the sheets of rain.  The gas lamps, strategically placed around the room to create a warm, inviting ambiance, flickered nervously, casting long, dancing shadows that exaggerated the ornate carvings of the antique furniture. The air was thick with the scent of old books, leather, and the faint, lingering aroma of the Earl Grey tea Elias favored.  He glanced at the ornate clock ticking above the fireplace, its golden hands inching towards six o'clock.  The session was almost over, but the tension in the room had thickened, not dissipated.
