The flickering neon sign of "The Binary Bean" cast a lurid green glow across the rain-slicked streets of Neo-Kyoto. Inside, the air hung thick with the aroma of burnt silicon and cheap synthetic coffee.  Rain lashed against the panoramic window overlooking the city, the towering holographic advertisements momentarily distorted by the downpour.  Anika Sharma, hunched over a battered laptop at a corner booth, barely registered the tempest outside. Her fingers danced across the keyboard, lines of code cascading down the screen like a digital waterfall.  Anika, a freelance programmer known for her ability to crack even the most impenetrable firewalls, was deep in the throes of debugging a particularly nasty piece of malware, oblivious to the growing unease settling in the nearly empty cafe. The only other occupant, a man in a long, dark coat, sat nursing a steaming mug, his face obscured by the shadow of a wide-brimmed hat. He watched Anika with an unnerving intensity.
