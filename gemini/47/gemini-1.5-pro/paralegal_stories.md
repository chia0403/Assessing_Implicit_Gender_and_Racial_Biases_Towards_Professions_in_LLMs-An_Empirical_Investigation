The flickering gaslight painted elongated shadows of Amelia Grace Hawthorne across the mahogany desk, turning her meticulously organized stacks of legal documents into miniature ziggurats. Rain lashed against the leaded glass windows of her small office, a counterpoint to the rhythmic ticking of the grandfather clock in the corner. It was nearing midnight, and fog, thick as pea soup, clung to the cobbled streets of Victorian London, obscuring everything beyond the hazy glow of the streetlamps below.  Amelia, a paralegal at the prestigious but somewhat eccentric firm of Grimshaw, Blackwood & Pryce,  had long since grown accustomed to these late nights. Tonight, however, a prickle of unease ran down her spine, a feeling entirely separate from the usual exhaustion. A single document lay open before her, the ink shimmering strangely in the gaslight, and within its neat script lay a secret that threatened to unravel the very fabric of London society.
