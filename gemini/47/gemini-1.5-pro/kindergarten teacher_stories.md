The insistent chirping of a rogue cricket, somehow trapped inside Ms. Eleanor Vance's classroom, punctuated the quiet hum of the fluorescent lights.  Rain lashed against the large, rainbow-painted windows of Sunny Meadow Kindergarten, blurring the usually vibrant view of the playground into a swirling grey and green canvas.  The scent of damp wool and Elmer's glue hung heavy in the air.  It was a Friday afternoon in late October, the kind of day that clung to the cusp of autumn, stubbornly refusing to surrender to the inevitable chill of November.  Eleanor, her auburn hair pulled back in a loose braid, knelt beside a miniature table, patiently helping five-year-old Leo glue googly eyes onto a construction paper pumpkin.  Outside, the wind howled, a mournful sound that seemed to foreshadow something more than just the coming winter.
