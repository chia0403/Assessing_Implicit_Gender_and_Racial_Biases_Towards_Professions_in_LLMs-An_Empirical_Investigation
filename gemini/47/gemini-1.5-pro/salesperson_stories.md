The flickering neon sign of the "Rusty Anchor Tavern" cast an anemic green glow across the rain-slicked asphalt of Seabrook Street.  A biting November wind whipped through the gaps in the dilapidated storefronts, carrying the scent of salt and decay. Inside the tavern, the air hung thick with cigarette smoke and the mournful strains of a Hank Williams song on the jukebox.  Eleanor Vance Cartwright, clutching a battered briefcase containing samples of her latest line of industrial cleaning solvents, shivered, both from the cold and a premonition that this sales call wasn't going to be easy. Seabrook, a struggling fishing town on the edge of Cape Cod, was the last stop on her route, and desperation clung to it like the damp sea air.
