The biting wind whipped off the East River, stinging Dr. Elias Thorne's cheeks as he hurried across the Roosevelt Island Tramway platform.  The city lights, a shimmering tapestry woven across the inky blackness, normally captivated him, but tonight, they were a blurred backdrop to his racing thoughts.  It was just after 2:00 am, and the insistent buzzing of his pager had yanked him from a rare, deep sleep.  Roosevelt Island General, the monolithic, brick behemoth looming ahead, seemed to exude a palpable tension even from this distance.  Elias knew, with a sickening certainty, that whatever awaited him inside was going to be bad.  Very bad.
