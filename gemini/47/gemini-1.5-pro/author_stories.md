The salt spray stung Amelia Zephyr Thorne's face as she leaned against the railing of the ferry, the wind whipping her auburn braid against her wool coat. The Isle of Skye, a jagged silhouette against the bruised twilight sky, loomed larger with each churn of the propeller. Grey clouds, heavy with the promise of rain, clung to the peaks of the Cuillin mountains, shrouding them in an almost mythical mist.  Amelia, usually captivated by dramatic landscapes, felt a knot of unease tighten in her stomach. This wasn’t a research trip for her latest historical novel; this was a summons, a cryptic postcard from her estranged grandmother, Moira, beckoning her to Dunvegan, a village nestled on the island’s western shore, a place Amelia hadn’t seen since she was a child and a place shrouded in family secrets she’d long tried to ignore.
