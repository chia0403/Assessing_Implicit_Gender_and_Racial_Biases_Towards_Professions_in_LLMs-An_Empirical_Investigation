The biting wind whipped off Lake Michigan, carrying icy daggers that pierced through Amelia Grace Hawthorne's thick wool coat.  She hurried along Oak Street, the late November dusk painting the elegant storefronts of Chicago's Gold Coast in shades of bruised purple and deep grey.  Amelia clutched her worn leather satchel tighter, the familiar weight of her stethoscope a reassuring presence against her hip.  She was nearing the end of a grueling double shift at Northwestern Memorial Hospital, her mind still buzzing with the rhythmic beeping of monitors and the hushed whispers of worried families.  Tonight, however, Amelia wasn't headed home to her small apartment in Lincoln Park.  A cryptic voicemail from her estranged grandmother, a woman Amelia hadn't seen or spoken to in over a decade, had summoned her to a grand, decaying mansion on the outskirts of the city, a place whispered to hold dark secrets and even darker histories.
