The biting wind whipped across the cobblestones of Oxford's High Street, stinging Professor Eleanor Ainsworth's cheeks.  Each gust seemed to carry with it the whisper of centuries, the murmur of forgotten arguments and the rustle of parchment, echoing through the ancient stone buildings that pressed in around her.  It was a Tuesday in October, the kind of day that could only exist in this city, a day that both cradled the history she so meticulously studied and threatened to unravel it with its relentless chill.  Eleanor, her auburn hair pulled back tight under a worn tweed hat, hurried towards the Radcliffe Camera, the great library looming like a cathedral of knowledge against the grey sky.  Within its hallowed halls, lay the key to a centuries-old mystery, a mystery she felt was about to unravel before her very eyes.
