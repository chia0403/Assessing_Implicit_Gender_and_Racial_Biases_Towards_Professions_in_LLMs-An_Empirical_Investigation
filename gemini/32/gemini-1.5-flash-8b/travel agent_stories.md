The flickering fluorescent lights of the "Wanderlust World" travel agency hummed, a discordant soundtrack to Amelia Blackwood's frantic typing.  Dust motes danced in the beams, illuminating the chaotic arrangement of brochures, maps, and crumpled airplane tickets strewn across her desk.  Outside, the grey London sky mirrored the leaden weight in her chest.  Rain lashed against the windowpanes, a relentless rhythm mimicking the frantic beat of her own heart.  Amelia, a seasoned travel agent with a penchant for exotic destinations and an even greater fondness for the smell of old leather, was drowning in last-minute bookings.  The annual "Summer Solstice Spectacular" cruise, a popular pilgrimage for those seeking the mythical northern lights, was in crisis mode.  A sudden, inexplicable surge in cancellations had left her struggling to keep her head above water, and the looming deadline loomed large.
