The chipped Formica counter gleamed under the harsh fluorescent lights of "Hair Today, Gone Tomorrow," reflecting Amelia Penelope Featherstone's anxious face.  A single strand of auburn hair, stubbornly defying her meticulously crafted updo, tickled her cheek.  Today, a client's disastrous request threatened not just a perfectly sculpted bob, but Amelia's carefully constructed image of herself as a calm, collected, and supremely competent cosmetologist.  The tiny salon, a cramped space crammed with dryers, mismatched chairs, and the faint, sickly sweet scent of cheap hairspray, felt more like a pressure cooker than a haven.  Sunlight barely touched the alleyway outside, casting long shadows that stretched and warped the already narrow space.  The rhythmic *whirr* of the curling iron, a familiar comfort, seemed unusually loud.
