The chipped Formica counter, stained a sickly yellow from decades of spilled coffee and forgotten sugar packets, hummed with the low thrum of Amelia Butterfield's relentless typing.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of afternoon sun slicing through the grime-coated window of "Acorn Accounting," a small, unassuming office tucked away on a side street in the heart of the perpetually overcast town of Greyhaven.  Rain hammered against the antiquated metal roof, a constant, rhythmic percussion that seemed to underscore the meticulous, almost obsessive nature of Amelia's work.  Today, however, the drumming had a different, more urgent quality.  Bills piled high, invoices were late, and a looming deadline felt like a storm cloud about to burst.  Amelia, a bookkeeper for the past fifteen years, braced herself for the inevitable torrent.
