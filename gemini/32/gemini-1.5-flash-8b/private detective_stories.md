The flickering neon sign of the "Rusty Mug" cast a sickly yellow glow on the rain-slicked cobblestones of Alderwood, reflecting in the oily puddles that clung to the alley walls.  A chill wind, sharp and biting, whipped through the narrow passage, carrying the scent of damp earth and something acrid, almost metallic.  Detective Alistair Finch, all rumpled trench coat and perpetually furrowed brow, adjusted his fedora, the brim shadowing his eyes as he surveyed the scene.  Empty beer bottles, shattered glass, and a scattering of crimson-stained paper littered the ground.  This wasn't the glamorous, high-stakes world of the city's wealthy elite that Alistair preferred; this was the underbelly, the festering wound beneath the polished facade of Alderwood.


The year is 1938. Alderwood, a sprawling city nestled in the shadow of the looming, ever-present Appalachian mountains, was a place of contrasts.  Luxury mansions lined grand avenues, showcasing the opulence of the city's industrial titans. But behind those meticulously maintained facades lurked a labyrinth of back alleys, dimly lit pubs, and secret rendezvous points.  These were the battlegrounds of shady deals, whispered secrets, and the tangled web of criminal enterprises that Detective Alistair Finch navigated with his quiet, unwavering resolve.
