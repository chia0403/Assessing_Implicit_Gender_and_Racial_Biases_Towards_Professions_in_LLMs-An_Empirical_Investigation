The chipped paint of the playroom walls, a faded robin's egg blue, reflected the afternoon sun, casting long, dancing shadows across the scattered toys.  Amelia "Amy"  Fitzwilliam, her name a whispered murmur among the toddlers, sighed, smoothing a stray lock of auburn hair from her forehead.  The air, thick with the scent of crayons and unidentifiable baby food, buzzed with the low, rhythmic murmur of preschoolers.  Today, it was particularly chaotic.  Two-year-old Leo, renowned for his ability to dismantle Lego castles with alarming speed, was currently attempting to scale the jungle gym, while three-year-old Maya, whose vocabulary seemed to expand exponentially each week, was dramatically declaring, “I do not like this!” directly at Amy.
