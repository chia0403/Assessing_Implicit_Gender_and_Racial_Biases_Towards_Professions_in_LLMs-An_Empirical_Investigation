The salt-laced wind whipped Amelia "Amy" Reyes's auburn braid across her face as she surveyed the dilapidated Victorian perched precariously on the cliff overlooking the churning Pacific.  Seafoam sprayed the air, coating the "For Sale" sign staked into the overgrown lawn with a glistening layer of salty mist.  Greyhaven, Oregon, was a town clinging to the edge of the continent, its houses huddled together as if for warmth against the ceaseless onslaught of the ocean.  This particular house, however, seemed to relish the isolation, leaning into the wind like a stubborn old sea captain defying a storm. Amy tucked a stray strand of hair behind her ear and sighed. Selling this place was going to be a challenge, even for someone with her reputation as the most tenacious realtor on the Oregon coast.
