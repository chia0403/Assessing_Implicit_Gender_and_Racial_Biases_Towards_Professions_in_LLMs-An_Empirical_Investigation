The rain hammered against the arched window of Dr. Eleanor Vance's office, each drop a tiny explosion against the backdrop of the darkening Seattle sky.  Her office, nestled on the second floor of a converted Victorian house in the historic Capitol Hill neighborhood, usually felt warm and inviting, with its Persian rug, overstuffed armchair, and the faint scent of jasmine tea lingering in the air.  But tonight, the gloom had seeped in, mirroring the turbulent emotions of the young man sitting opposite her.  He nervously twisted a silver ring on his thumb, his gaze fixed on the intricate patterns of the rug, avoiding Eleanor's gentle, expectant eyes.  Outside, the wind howled, a mournful soundtrack to the story he was about to tell.
