The biting November wind whipped off Lake Michigan, rattling the large bay windows of Revive Physical Therapy. Inside, Eleanor Vance, PT, DPT, adjusted the thermostat a degree higher, the small click a stark contrast to the insistent howl outside.  Her clinic, nestled in a cozy brick building on a quiet street in Evanston, Illinois, usually exuded a warmth that matched Eleanor’s sunny disposition. Today, however, a palpable tension hung in the air, thicker than the swirling snow starting to accumulate on the window panes.  A canceled appointment slot loomed large on her otherwise full schedule, a blank space that echoed the unsettling quiet in the waiting room.  Eleanor tapped a pen against her pristine clipboard, a nervous habit she’d picked up in grad school.  Something felt off.  It wasn't just the weather.
