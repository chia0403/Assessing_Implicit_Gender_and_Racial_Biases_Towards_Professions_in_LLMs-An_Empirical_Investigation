The biting November wind whipped around Dr. Elias Thorne as he hurried across the cobblestone courtyard of St. Jude's Hospital, the gothic arches of the building looming above him like skeletal fingers against the bruised twilight sky.  Built in 1888, the hospital was a labyrinth of echoing corridors and dimly lit wards, a place steeped in history, both miraculous and tragic.  Tonight, the air crackled with a different kind of tension, a palpable anxiety that clung to the sterile white walls and seeped into Elias's very bones.  It had started with a whisper, a rumour of something…unusual…occurring in the operating theatres, something beyond the usual realm of medical anomalies. Now, as he pushed through the heavy oak doors of the surgical wing, Elias knew tonight would be a test of everything he believed in, everything he had sworn to uphold.
