The biting November wind whipped across the skeletal frame of the Skyreach Tower, rattling the loose safety netting and stinging the exposed skin of Elias Vance.  Fifty-three stories up, perched precariously on a steel beam that felt thinner than a pencil from that height, Elias adjusted his hard hat and spat a stream of brown tobacco juice into the swirling abyss. Below him, the city of Chicago spread out like a vast, grey circuit board, its streets clogged with tiny, crawling cars. The air was thick with the metallic tang of welding fumes and the low rumble of machinery echoing up from the concrete jungle below.  A fractured sunrise bled orange and purple across the eastern horizon, painting the unfinished skyscraper in a surreal, almost apocalyptic light.  Another day, another dizzying dance with gravity.
