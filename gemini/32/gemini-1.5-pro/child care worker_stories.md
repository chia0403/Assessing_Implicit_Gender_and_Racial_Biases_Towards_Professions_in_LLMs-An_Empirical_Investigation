The air in the Bumblebrook Daycare center hummed with the static electricity of twenty tiny humans in perpetual motion.  Crayola-bright drawings plastered every available surface, vying for attention with the alphabet letters cheerfully marching across the top of the wall. The scent of apple juice and disinfectant hung heavy in the air, punctuated by the occasional whiff of stale diaper.  Amelia Rose Hawthorne, her curly brown hair pulled back in a messy bun, knelt amongst a gaggle of toddlers, mediating a fierce tug-of-war over a plastic firetruck. Outside, a late October wind rattled the windows, hinting at the approaching chill of winter, but inside Bumblebrook, the temperature was a constant, humid summer, fueled by the boundless energy of its miniature inhabitants.
