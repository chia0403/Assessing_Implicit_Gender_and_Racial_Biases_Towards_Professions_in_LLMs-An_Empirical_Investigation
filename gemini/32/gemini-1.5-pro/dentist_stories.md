The biting wind whipped off the Puget Sound, rattling the "Open" sign hanging precariously from the chipped paint of Dr. Elias Thorne's dental practice.  Inside, the waiting room, usually bathed in the sterile glow of fluorescent lights, was shrouded in a dusky pre-dawn gloom. Rain lashed against the large picture window showcasing a rather unconvincing stock photo of a family with impossibly perfect teeth.  Elias, his reflection barely visible in the dark glass, adjusted his tie, a knot of anxiety tightening in his stomach. It wasn't the impending Monday morning rush that unnerved him, nor the persistent drip from the leaky ceiling he'd been meaning to fix for months.  It was the anonymous postcard sitting on his otherwise pristine desk, the single word scrawled across it in crimson ink sending a shiver down his spine: "Remember."
