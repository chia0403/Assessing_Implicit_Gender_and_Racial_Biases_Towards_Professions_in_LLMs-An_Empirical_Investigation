The biting wind whipped across the desolate expanse of the Athabasca oil sands, stinging Amelia “Milly” Petrova’s cheeks even through the thick balaclava she wore.  Dawn hadn’t yet broken, painting the sky a bruised purple-grey, but the floodlights illuminating Syncrude’s Mildred Lake mine site turned the swirling snow into a glittering, disorienting haze. Milly, perched high in the cab of her Komatsu 980 rigid-frame haul truck, felt a familiar thrill hum beneath the constant vibration of the massive diesel engine.  This, the largest mine in the world, was her kingdom.  Thirty-two tires taller than a man,  a payload capacity exceeding four hundred tons – this behemoth was her throne.  And today, like every day, she was ready to move mountains.
