Rain lashed against the arched windows of the Grimshaw Building, mirroring the tempest brewing inside Beatrice Amelia Higgins.  The mahogany-panelled office, usually a sanctuary of hushed efficiency, crackled with the unspoken tension radiating from Mr. Grimshaw’s inner sanctum. Beatrice, perched on the edge of her burgundy leather chair, nervously adjusted the pearl necklace her grandmother had bequeathed her.  It was a quarter past five on a Friday, the sky a bruised purple, and the rhythmic click of her boss’s antique clock felt less like the march of time and more like the ticking down of a bomb.  The Grimshaw Building, a gothic behemoth looming over Philadelphia’s financial district, usually exuded an air of quiet power. Today, however, it felt more like a mausoleum, holding its breath in anticipation of something unpleasant.
