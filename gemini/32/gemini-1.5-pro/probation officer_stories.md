Rain lashed against the grimy windows of Amelia "Amy" Reyes's Ford Focus, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her.  The flickering neon sign of the "Lucky Clover Motel" cast an eerie green glow across the rain-slicked asphalt of the parking lot.  Amy switched off the engine, the sudden silence amplifying the drumming of her fingers against the steering wheel.  Fallen Oak, a town clinging precariously to the edge of the Ozark Mountains in southern Missouri, was a place where secrets festered like mold in the damp undergrowth.  And tonight, Amy, a probation officer for the past five years,  was about to confront one of those secrets, tucked away in room 17.  The air hung heavy with the smell of pine needles and something else, something faintly metallic, that made the hairs on the back of her neck prickle.  This wasn't a routine check-in. This felt different. This felt dangerous.
