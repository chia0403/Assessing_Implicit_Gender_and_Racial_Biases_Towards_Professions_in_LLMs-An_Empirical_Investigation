The fluorescent lights of "Shear Radiance" hummed a relentless, high-pitched tune, reflecting off the chrome and glass surfaces like a trapped insect.  Outside, the humid Florida air hung heavy, promising another afternoon thunderstorm. Inside, the salon buzzed with the low murmur of hair dryers and the snip-snip-snip of scissors. Amelia “Amy” Rose Hawthorne, a woman whose own auburn hair was always styled in an effortlessly chic chignon, surveyed her domain with a practiced eye.  A wisp of hairspray hung in the air, mingling with the scent of coconut shampoo and the faint, metallic tang of perm solution.  It was a Thursday afternoon, typically slow, but today a strange tension filled the air, thick and cloying like the humidity outside. Amy couldn't quite place it, but it prickled at the back of her neck, a premonition of something about to change.
