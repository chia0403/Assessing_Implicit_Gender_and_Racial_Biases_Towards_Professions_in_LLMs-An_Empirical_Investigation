The biting November wind whipped around Dr. Elias Thorne's ankles as he hurried across the cobbled courtyard of St. Jude's Hospital.  Gas lamps flickered weakly against the encroaching gloom, casting long, skeletal shadows that danced with the swirling dust devils.  The year was 1888, and the fog-choked streets of Whitechapel, London, held a chilling secret, a predator stalking the shadows.  Elias, his dark hair plastered to his forehead with a mixture of rain and sweat, clutched his medical bag tighter, the cold leather a stark contrast to the frantic beating of his heart. He wasn't just battling the elements tonight; he was racing against a far more sinister enemy.
