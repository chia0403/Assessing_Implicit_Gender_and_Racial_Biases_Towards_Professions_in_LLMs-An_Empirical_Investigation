Rain lashed against the windows of Amelia “Amy” Rose Hawthorne’s office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her.  Her pristine white desk, usually a beacon of nutritional order, was currently awash in a chaotic jumble of research papers, blood work analyses, and half-eaten protein bars. Outside, the grey November sky pressed down on the quaint, seaside town of Port Blossom, Maine. The usually cheerful clapboard houses lining the harbor seemed to hunch against the wind, their brightly painted doors offering little solace against the encroaching gloom. Amy, normally energized by the brisk coastal air, felt the damp chill seep into her bones, a reflection of the unease that had settled over her ever since the first patient disappeared.
