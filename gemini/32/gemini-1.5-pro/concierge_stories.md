The opulent lobby of the Hotel Magnifique, all polished marble and glittering chandeliers, hummed with a low, constant thrum of activity.  Rain lashed against the arched windows overlooking the Place Vendôme in Paris, sending shivers through the impeccably dressed guests hurrying inside.  Geneviève Dubois, her name tag pinned precisely to her crisp navy uniform, stood at her mahogany concierge desk, a serene smile fixed on her face. It was the cusp of twilight, that magical hour when Paris transformed from the City of Lights into the City of Whispers.  Geneviève, a woman whose elegant posture belied a steel-trap mind and an uncanny ability to anticipate the desires of the hotel's elite clientele, considered this the most intriguing time of day. Tonight, however, the air crackled with an unusual tension, a premonition of something extraordinary about to unfold.  A single, blood-red rose lay on her desk, delivered just moments ago with no card, no note, just the faintest scent of something… sinister.
