The rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of the abandoned sugar mill, a relentless metallic drumming that mirrored the anxiety churning in Amelia Grace Holloway's stomach.  Rust-colored rivulets snaked down the grimy windows, obscuring the already fading light of the Louisiana dusk. The cloying sweetness of decaying sugarcane hung heavy in the air, a stark contrast to the acrid scent of gunpowder that clung to Amelia’s tailored wool coat.  She hadn’t expected her latest client consultation to take place in a derelict building on the outskirts of New Orleans, let alone involve a recently fired shotgun and a man claiming to be haunted by the ghost of a pirate.
