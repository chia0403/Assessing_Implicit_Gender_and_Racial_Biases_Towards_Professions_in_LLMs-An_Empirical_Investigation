The biting November wind whipped around Professor Alistair Finchley, tugging at the edges of his tweed overcoat as he hurried across the cobbled quad of St. Jude's College, Oxford.  Gargoyles leered down from the honey-colored stone buildings, their grimaces seeming to mirror his own anxieties.  A thin layer of frost crunched beneath his worn leather shoes, the sound swallowed by the clang of the chapel bell announcing the five o'clock evensong.  The light was fading fast, a bruised purple bleeding into the grey sky above the spires, and Alistair pulled his scarf tighter, the wool scratching against his beard.  He was late, unforgivably late, for a meeting with the Dean, a meeting that could determine the fate of his entire career.  And, perhaps even more pressing, the fate of the artifact.
