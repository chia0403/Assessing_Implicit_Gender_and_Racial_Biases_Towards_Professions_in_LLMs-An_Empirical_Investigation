Rain lashed against the grimy windows of The Daily Clarion's office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside Amelia “Mia” Rodriguez.  Fluorescent lights buzzed overhead, casting a sickly yellow glow on the cluttered desks and overflowing ashtrays. Cigarette smoke hung thick in the air, a stale perfume clinging to the threadbare carpet and chipped mugs.  Ash Wednesday, 1948, had dawned bleak and unforgiving in the city of New Olympus, a sprawling metropolis built on the backs of steel mills and coal mines nestled deep in the Appalachian Mountains. Mia, still in her damp trench coat, stared down at the crumpled telegram in her hand, the words blurring through a film of exhaustion.  It was an invitation, or perhaps more accurately, a summons, back to the forgotten Appalachian hollow where she'd been born – a place she'd sworn never to return to.
