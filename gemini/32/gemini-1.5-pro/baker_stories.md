The aroma of burnt sugar hung heavy in the air, a stark contrast to the usual comforting scent of yeast and cinnamon that permeated Eloise Beauregard's bakery.  Eloise, her auburn braid dusted with flour, stood frozen before the open oven, staring at the charred remains of what was supposed to be her pièce de résistance: a seven-tiered croquembouche, commissioned for the mayor's daughter's wedding.  Outside, the quaint cobblestone streets of Aubergine, nestled in the heart of the French Alps, were beginning to glimmer with the soft light of a late autumn dawn.  The usually picturesque snow-capped peaks that framed the village were shrouded in a thick, ominous fog this morning, mirroring the dread that was settling in Eloise's stomach. This was a disaster.  A sugary, caramelized disaster.
