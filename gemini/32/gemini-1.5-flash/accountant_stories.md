The chipped ceramic mug warmed Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s hands, the lukewarm tea doing little to soothe the icy dread that had settled in her stomach.  Rain lashed against the tall, gothic windows of her office, each gust a mournful sigh echoing the turmoil within her.  Amelia, a meticulous accountant known for her unwavering precision and even more unwavering adherence to deadlines, stared blankly at the spreadsheet glowing on her computer screen.  The numbers, usually her solace, swam before her eyes, a chaotic jumble of debits and credits that refused to reconcile.  This wasn't a simple discrepancy; this was a chasm, a gaping hole in the meticulously crafted financial tapestry of Thorne & Blackwood, a prestigious law firm that prided itself on its impeccable reputation – a reputation now hanging precariously by a thread, a thread Amelia was terrified she'd just unravelled. The air in her small, book-lined office, usually scented with the faint aroma of old paper and freshly brewed coffee, was thick with the metallic tang of fear and the damp chill of the November storm raging outside.
