The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Amy” Hernandez’s hair across her face as she surveyed the near-frozen expanse of the Northwood High School football field.  The stadium lights, skeletal fingers against the bruised purple twilight, cast long, distorted shadows that danced with the swirling leaves.  A thin layer of ice already coated the sidelines, a treacherous sheen underfoot.  The air hung heavy with the scent of damp earth and the faint, metallic tang of blood – a lingering reminder of the brutal scrimmage earlier that day.  Amy, bundled in a thick Northwood Wolverines parka that did little to combat the penetrating chill, rubbed her gloved hands together, the knuckles already stiff with cold.  Tonight, however, the physical discomfort was a minor distraction compared to the gnawing anxiety that settled in her stomach.  The regional playoffs were looming, and the Wolverines’ chances, once bright, were now flickering like a dying candle.
