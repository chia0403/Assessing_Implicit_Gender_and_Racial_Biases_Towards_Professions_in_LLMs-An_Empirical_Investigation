The humid Louisiana air hung heavy, thick and sweet with the scent of honeysuckle and impending rain.  Cicadas buzzed a relentless, rhythmic chorus that competed with the rhythmic *thump-thump-thump* of Amelia Jean Dupre’s worn leather pumps against the cracked pavement of Bayou Teche Drive.  Amelia Jean, or AJ as most people called her – a moniker she secretly despised, preferring the elegance of her full name – adjusted the oversized sunglasses perched atop her perfectly coiffed auburn hair.  The late afternoon sun, a fiery orb sinking below the cypress trees that lined the river, cast long shadows that stretched and distorted the already picturesque, yet undeniably swampy, landscape.  She was on her way to another showing, another potential sale, another chance to escape the stifling humidity of her personal life, all while navigating the treacherous waters of the Lafayette real estate market.
