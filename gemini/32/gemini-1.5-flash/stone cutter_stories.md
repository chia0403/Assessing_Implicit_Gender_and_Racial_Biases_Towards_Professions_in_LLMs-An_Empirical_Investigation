The wind, a rasping sculptor itself, whipped across the desolate moor, carrying with it the scent of brine and the mournful cry of gulls.  Arawak stone, the color of a bruised plum, lay scattered across the windswept plateau, its jagged edges catching the weak sunlight like shards of obsidian.  This was the quarry of Aethelred Stonehewer, a place as unforgiving as the man who worked it.  Aethelred, his face weathered like the very rock he shaped, stood hunched over a half-finished effigy, his hammer poised mid-air, a stark silhouette against the bleak, grey sky.  The only sound besides the wind was the rhythmic thump-thump-thump of his own heart, a counterpoint to the relentless battering of the waves far below.
