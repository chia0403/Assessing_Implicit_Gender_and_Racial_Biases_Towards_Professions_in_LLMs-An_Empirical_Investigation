The chipped paint of the fire escape flaked onto Amelia “Mia” Hernandez’s worn leather notebook, mirroring the disintegration of her carefully crafted composure.  Rain, a relentless, grey curtain, hammered against the corrugated iron roof of the dilapidated tenement building in the shadow of the Brooklyn Bridge.  Below, the East River churned, a restless, mirroring image of the anxiety churning in Mia’s gut.  The air, thick with the smell of salt and decay, clung to her like a second skin.  This wasn't the glamorous, bright-lights journalism she'd dreamed of at Columbia; this was the gritty, unforgiving reality of chasing a story in Red Hook, a story that might just cost her more than a few sleepless nights.
