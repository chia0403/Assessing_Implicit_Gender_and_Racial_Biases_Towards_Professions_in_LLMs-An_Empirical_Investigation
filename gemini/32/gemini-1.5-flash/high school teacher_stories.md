The chipped paint of the classroom clock, a faded floral pattern barely visible beneath decades of grime, seemed to mock Ms. Eleanor Vance’s meticulous lesson plan.  It was already ten minutes past the bell, and the lingering scent of stale coffee and teenage angst hung heavy in the air of Northwood High’s Room 207.  Outside, the October wind howled a mournful tune, rattling the ancient windowpanes and whipping fallen leaves into frantic spirals across the cracked asphalt of the parking lot.  Inside, the usual cacophony of shifting desks and hushed whispers was replaced by an unnerving silence.  Eleanor, a woman whose auburn hair was perpetually escaping its sensible bun and whose cardigan always bore the faint aroma of cinnamon, adjusted her glasses and surveyed her empty classroom with a growing unease.  This wasn't like her students.  Not even close.
