The rain hammered against the towering glass facade of the Zenith Hotel, each drop a tiny percussionist in the symphony of the city's nocturnal hum.  Inside, however, the atmosphere was one of hushed opulence, a stark contrast to the tempest raging outside.  Aromas of freshly brewed coffee and expensive leather mingled in the air, a comforting perfume for the weary traveler.  Behind the polished mahogany desk in the hotel's exclusive concierge lounge sat Aurelia Genevieve Dubois, her silver-grey hair pulled back in a neat chignon, her expression a calm mask that belied the whirlwind of requests already swirling in her mind.  Tonight, however, felt different.  A certain electric anticipation thrummed in the quiet elegance, a feeling that even the rhythmic tick-tock of the grandfather clock couldn't quite mask.
