The chipped mug warmed Amelia Stone's hands, the lukewarm tea doing little to soothe the icy dread clenching her stomach.  Rain lashed against the tall, gothic windows of her office, each gust a mournful sigh echoing the turmoil within.  Amelia, a woman whose life was usually meticulously organized – down to the precisely aligned spines of her overflowing bookshelves – felt utterly adrift.  Her office, a sanctuary usually humming with the quiet industry of editing, was a chaotic mess.  Stacks of manuscripts, each promising a gripping tale of adventure or romance, now seemed like mocking reminders of her own stalled narrative.  Outside, the London sky wept a grey, unforgiving torrent, mirroring the storm brewing inside the esteemed, if slightly crumbling, walls of Blackwood & Sons publishing house.
