The biting November wind whipped Amelia Katherine Hayes's auburn hair across her face as she wrestled with her tripod on the windswept cliffs of Dunmore Head.  Spray from the churning Atlantic Ocean, the colour of bruised plums, stung her cheeks.  Below, the jagged rocks, dark and ominous against the churning grey sea, clawed at the frothing waves.  Amelia, bundled in layers of wool and waterproofs, shivered less from the cold and more from a nervous anticipation that had been simmering beneath her skin for the past three days. This wasn't just another assignment for *The Irish Times*; this was a chase.  A chase for the elusive, the almost mythical, the Green Flash.
