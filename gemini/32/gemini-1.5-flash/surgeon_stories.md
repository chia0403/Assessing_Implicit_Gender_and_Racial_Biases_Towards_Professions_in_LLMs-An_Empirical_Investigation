The sterile scent of antiseptic hung heavy in the air, a familiar perfume to Dr. Elias Thorne, though tonight it did little to mask the coppery tang of blood that clung to the polished chrome of the operating table.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of the penthouse surgical suite, blurring the already indistinct cityscape of Neo-Kyoto into a hazy watercolor of neon and lightning.  Below, the city thrummed with a restless energy, a stark contrast to the tense silence that held Elias and his surgical team captive within the gleaming, technologically advanced operating theatre.  The patient, a young woman barely out of her teens, lay still beneath the surgical lamps, her breathing shallow and erratic, a life hanging precariously in the balance.  Elias felt the familiar tremor of adrenaline, not fear, but the sharp, focused energy that accompanied the high-stakes dance of life and death he performed nightly.
