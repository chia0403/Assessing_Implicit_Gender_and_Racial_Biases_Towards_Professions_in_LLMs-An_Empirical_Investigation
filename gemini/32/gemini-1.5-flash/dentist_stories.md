The chipped porcelain of Dr. Alistair Finch's waiting room held a faint, unsettling sweetness, a cloying blend of antiseptic and stale floral perfume.  Dust motes, illuminated by the weak afternoon sun struggling through the rain-streaked windowpanes, danced in the silent air.  Alistair, his name a whisper against the ticking grandfather clock in the corner, leaned back in his worn leather chair, the scent of cinnamon and cloves clinging faintly to his starched white coat.  Outside, the November wind howled a mournful dirge through the skeletal branches of the ancient oak that dominated the small town of Oakhaven's single, unassuming square.  The storm, mirroring the turmoil brewing inside him, showed no sign of abating.
