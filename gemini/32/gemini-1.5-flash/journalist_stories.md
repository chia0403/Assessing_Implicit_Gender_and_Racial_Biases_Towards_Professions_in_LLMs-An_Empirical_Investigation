The biting November wind whipped Amelia Hernandez’s auburn hair across her face as she huddled deeper into her threadbare coat, the collar scratching against her chapped cheeks.  Rain, a relentless drizzle that had been falling for three days, plastered her already damp trench coat to her body.  The air hung thick with the smell of coal smoke and rotting seaweed, a familiar perfume to the residents of Aisling, a crumbling coastal town clinging precariously to the jagged cliffs of County Clare, Ireland. Amelia, a journalist for the Dublin Gazette, shivered, pulling her worn leather gloves tighter.  The rhythmic crash of waves against the craggy rocks was a constant, almost deafening, soundtrack to the town’s quiet despair.  She’d come to Aisling seeking the truth behind the disappearances, a string of unsettling incidents that had the locals whispering of ancient curses and restless spirits.  But the truth, she suspected, might be far more sinister, and far closer to home than anyone imagined.
