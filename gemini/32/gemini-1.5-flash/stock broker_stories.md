The rain hammered against the panoramic windows of Avery Sterling’s office, a relentless drumming that mirrored the frantic rhythm of his own heart.  Below, the city of Chicago sprawled, a concrete jungle shimmering under the bruised purple of a twilight sky.  Avery, a man whose tailored suit usually suggested effortless control, felt anything but.  His normally pristine mahogany desk was a battlefield of crumpled spreadsheets and half-empty coffee cups, the air thick with the scent of stale espresso and simmering anxiety.  The city lights reflected in his tired eyes, highlighting the network of fine lines etched around them – the map of a life lived at breakneck speed, a life increasingly defined by the volatile fluctuations of the market.
