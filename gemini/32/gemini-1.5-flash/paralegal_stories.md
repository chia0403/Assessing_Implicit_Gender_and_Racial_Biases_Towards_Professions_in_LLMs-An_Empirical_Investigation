The flickering neon sign of the Golden Gavel bar cast a lurid, emerald glow across Amelia Hernandez’s face as she hurried down Bleecker Street.  Rain, slick and relentless, plastered her dark hair to her forehead, mirroring the grim set of her jaw.  The air, thick with the scent of exhaust fumes and damp concrete, did little to soothe the churning anxiety in her stomach.  Amelia, a paralegal at the prestigious (and notoriously cutthroat) law firm of Sterling & Croft, wasn't usually out this late, especially not on a Wednesday.  But tonight, the usual humdrum of legal briefs and overflowing inboxes had been replaced by a far more urgent matter – a cryptic phone call, a whispered threat, and the unsettling feeling that someone was watching her.  The city, usually a vibrant tapestry of life, felt suddenly menacing, its shadows teeming with unspoken dangers.
