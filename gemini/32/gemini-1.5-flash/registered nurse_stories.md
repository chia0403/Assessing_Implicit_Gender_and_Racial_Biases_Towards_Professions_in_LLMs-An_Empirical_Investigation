The salt-laced wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s hair across her face as she hurried along the deserted beach, the grey sky mirroring the turbulent churning in her gut.  Sand, fine as powdered sugar, crunched under her worn leather boots, each step a counterpoint to the rhythmic crash of waves against the jagged cliffs of Cornwall.  The air, thick with the scent of brine and seaweed, offered little comfort.  Amelia, a registered nurse at Truro County Hospital, usually found solace in the raw beauty of the Cornish coast.  Tonight, however, the wildness of the landscape only amplified the storm brewing inside her.  The emergency call, received just an hour ago, had been brief, chillingly so:  "Urgent.  Need a nurse.  Island of St. Michael's.  Bring morphine."  The island, shrouded now in a pre-dawn gloom, lay a precarious half-mile offshore, reachable only by a rickety old fishing boat – and Amelia's heart was already racing faster than any tide.
