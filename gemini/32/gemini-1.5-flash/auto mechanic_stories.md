The greasy overalls clung to Elias Thorne’s broad shoulders, smelling faintly of burnt motor oil and stale coffee.  He wasn't a pretty picture, not with his hands perpetually stained black, a smudge of grease permanently imprinted on his cheek, and his hair perpetually escaping the confines of his worn baseball cap.  But Elias, a man whose life revolved around the rhythmic clang of wrenches and the satisfying purr of a finely tuned engine, wouldn’t have it any other way. His kingdom wasn't a castle, but Thorne's Auto Repair, a ramshackle garage nestled precariously between a laundromat perpetually smelling of bleach and a perpetually empty storefront that promised "Fortune Telling - Psychic Readings" in faded, peeling letters.  The late afternoon sun cast long shadows across the dusty floor, illuminating a chaotic landscape of discarded parts, half-finished projects, and the comforting scent of gasoline. Tonight, however, the usual symphony of clanking metal was absent, replaced by a chilling silence only broken by the distant wail of a police siren.
