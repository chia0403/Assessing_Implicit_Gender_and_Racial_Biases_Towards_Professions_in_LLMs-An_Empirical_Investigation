The stale scent of bleach and despair clung to the air, a familiar perfume in Dr. Eleanor Vance's world.  Rain lashed against the grimy windows of the interrogation room, mirroring the tempest brewing within the suspect.  Eleanor, her auburn hair pulled back in a severe bun that betrayed neither her exhaustion nor the grim determination etched on her face, adjusted the microphone on the table. Across from her, slumped in a chair far too large for his slight frame, sat Arthur Jenkins, his eyes hollow pools reflecting the flickering fluorescent lights above. The city of Manchester, a brooding giant shrouded in a November drizzle, pressed against the walls of the precinct, its damp breath seeping into every corner, a silent witness to the unraveling of another broken life.  The case file, thick with disturbing photographs and chilling witness statements, lay open on the table between them, a stark reminder of the darkness that Eleanor Vance, forensic psychologist extraordinaire, was tasked with navigating.
