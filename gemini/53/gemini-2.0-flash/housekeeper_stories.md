The chipped porcelain teacup rattled in Maria Isabella Rodriguez’s hand, mirroring the tremor in her own. Sunlight, thick and golden as honey, streamed through the massive, arched windows of the Palmerton estate’s east-facing dining room, illuminating dust motes dancing in the air. Usually, Maria found the sun welcoming, a cheerful companion in her solitary work. But this morning, the bright light felt like an accusation, highlighting the untouched, gleaming expanse of the mahogany table, the perfectly aligned silverware reflecting her own anxious face back at her. This was her fourth week working at the Palmerton estate, and today was Mrs. Palmerton's birthday. Maria needed to make sure that everything was perfect.
