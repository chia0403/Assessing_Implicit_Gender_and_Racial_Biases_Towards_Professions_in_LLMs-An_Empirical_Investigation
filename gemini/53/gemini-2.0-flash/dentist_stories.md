The biting wind whipped Dr. Eleanor "Ellie" Ainsworth's auburn hair across her face as she wrestled with the heavy oak door of her practice, "Ainsworth Dental Wellness," against a particularly vicious gust. The sky over Port Blossom, Maine, was a bruised purple-grey, mirroring the color of the sea churning restlessly against the jagged coastline.

Port Blossom, population 2,347 (give or take a seasonal lobster fisherman), was a place where time moved at a glacial pace, and gossip traveled faster than the morning fog. The town was a patchwork of weathered clapboard houses, brightly painted fishing boats bobbing in the harbor, and the ever-present scent of brine and fried clams. It was, for all intents and purposes, a picturesque postcard, except when the late autumn storms rolled in, turning the charming seaside haven into a blustery, unforgiving beast.

Ellie, huddled in her thick, hand-knitted fisherman's sweater, glanced at the clock tower looming over the town square. 8:15 AM. Late. Mrs. Hathaway, her first patient of the day, was notoriously punctual and possessed a tongue sharper than any dental pick. Ellie shuddered, not from the cold, but from the impending verbal lashing she was about to endure.
