Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of the probation office, mimicking the relentless drumbeat of Amelia Hayes’ thoughts.  The fluorescent lights flickered, casting long, unsettling shadows that danced with the droplets sliding down the grimy windowpanes.  Outside, the grey Manchester skyline, usually a panorama of industrial grit and towering architecture, was obscured by a thick, suffocating fog.  This wasn't just any Tuesday; this was the Tuesday following a particularly volatile week, a week that had almost seen her crack under the strain of the city's underbelly.  Amelia, a probation officer with the Manchester Metropolitan Probation Service, stared at the overflowing inbox on her computer screen.  Each unread email represented a life, a history, a potential future – some bright, some bleak, and most somewhere in between.  Her name, etched into the letterhead in pale blue ink, seemed almost ironic, a stark contrast to the turbulent emotions brewing inside.
