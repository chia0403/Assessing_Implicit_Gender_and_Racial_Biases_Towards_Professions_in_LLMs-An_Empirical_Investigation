The scent of aged paper and dust, a familiar comfort, clung to the air in the hushed reading room of the Grand Oak Library.  Sunlight, filtered through the tall, arched windows, painted intricate patterns on the polished oak floor.  Amelia Seraphina Blackwood, a librarian with eyes the colour of faded amethyst and a patience that rivalled the library's oldest tomes, adjusted her spectacles and sighed. Another Tuesday.  Another avalanche of overdue novels, overdue lives, and overdue anxieties swirling around the towering shelves.  The library, a sanctuary of silence, was, today, anything but.  A frantic tapping from the far end of the room, a rhythmic drumming against the ancient wooden desk, jolted her from her contemplation.
