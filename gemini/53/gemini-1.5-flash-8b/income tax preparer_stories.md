The fluorescent lights of the "Tax Relief Express" hummed a monotonous tune, a soundtrack to Amelia Butterfield's meticulously organized life.  Paperclips clicked, pens scratched across forms, and the occasional frustrated sigh punctuated the otherwise quiet rhythm of the small office tucked away on a bustling side street in downtown Peoria.  Amelia, a tax preparer with a penchant for precision and a surprisingly soft laugh, stared at the meticulously crafted spreadsheets on her desk.  The year 2024 was shaping up to be another marathon, a race against time and the complexities of the IRS code.  Outside, the early morning sun painted the brick buildings a muted orange, casting long shadows that seemed to stretch and twist like the deductions and credits she was diligently calculating.  The aroma of stale coffee and slightly burnt popcorn clung to the air, a familiar companion in her world of numbers and deadlines.
