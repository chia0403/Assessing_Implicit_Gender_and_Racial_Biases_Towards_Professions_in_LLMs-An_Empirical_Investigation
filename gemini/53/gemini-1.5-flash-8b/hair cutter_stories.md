The chipped porcelain sink, stained a perpetual beige from years of lukewarm water and the relentless spray of countless haircuts, reflected the flickering fluorescent lights of "Snip-Snap Scissors."  Dust motes danced in the meagre rays, illuminating the faded posters advertising last year's spring sale and the faint scent of lavender-scented hairspray clinging to the air.  This was Amelia Penelope Featherstone's domain, her sanctuary, a small, cramped salon nestled on the backstreet of a city that seemed to have forgotten its own quiet corners.  Amelia, her name a delicate whisper amidst the constant snip-snip-snip of shears, meticulously trimmed the ends of a woman's auburn tresses.  The woman, Mrs. Gable, a renowned author of children's books, sat slumped in the plastic chair, her eyes closed, a faint sigh escaping her lips.  Outside, the city roared.
