The biting wind whipped across the desolate expanse of the Arizona desert, stinging Elias Thorne's cheeks and clawing at the tattered collar of his worn work jacket.  Dust devils, miniature tornadoes of ochre and gray, danced across the cracked earth, swirling around the colossal, rust-eaten frame of the excavator.  Elias, a seasoned heavy equipment operator with calloused hands and eyes that held the weary wisdom of a thousand sunrises, meticulously checked the hydraulics, the hissing of escaping air a constant, low hum against the silence of the vast, empty landscape.  He was alone, miles from the nearest town, the only company the skeletal remains of a forgotten mine.  His job: to unearth the forgotten remnants of a project long abandoned.
