The biting wind whipped across the desolate plains of the Dakotas, stinging Amelia "Millie"  McCarthy's cheeks and rattling the half-empty coffee mug in her gloved hand.  Dust devils danced across the cracked earth, swirling like miniature tornadoes, mirroring the churning anxieties in her stomach.  The faded, turquoise paint of her rig, the "The Prairie Cyclone," gleamed faintly in the fading light, a lonely beacon against the endless horizon.  She was hours from the nearest town, hours from any semblance of civilization, and the only company she had were the whispering prairie grasses and the rhythmic thump-thump-thump of the truck's diesel engine.  This stretch of Route 87, a forgotten highway snaking through the heart of nowhere, held a secret, one that whispered promises of both danger and redemption, a secret that Millie, with her weathered face and calloused hands, was about to confront.
