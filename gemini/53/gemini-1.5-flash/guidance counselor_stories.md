The chipped ceramic mug warmed Eleanor Vance's hands, the lukewarm chamomile tea doing little to soothe the icy knot in her stomach.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of her office, blurring the already hazy cityscape of Portland, Oregon.  Grey skies mirrored the grey, overwhelmed expression on the face of her next appointment, a young woman hunched in the waiting area, her backpack practically swallowing her whole.  Eleanor sighed, the scent of old paper and desperation clinging to the air, a familiar perfume in her small, cluttered office at Lincoln High.  The faint, floral scent of her own hand lotion – a desperate attempt at injecting some cheer – did little to combat it.  This wasn't just another Tuesday; this felt like the culmination of a particularly brutal semester.
