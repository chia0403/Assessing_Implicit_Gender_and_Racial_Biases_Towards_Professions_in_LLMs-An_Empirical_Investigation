The chipped porcelain sink groaned under the weight of a mountain of greasy dishes, the remnants of Lord Ashworth's extravagant dinner party still clinging stubbornly to their surfaces.  Steam, thick and fragrant with the lingering scent of rosemary and roast lamb, hung heavy in the air of the cavernous kitchen.  This wasn't just any kitchen; it was the heart of Blackwood Manor, a sprawling gothic monstrosity perched precariously on the windswept cliffs overlooking the churning grey sea.  For Elara Genevieve Ainsworth, however, it was simply another battlefield in her daily war against grime and disorder.  The rhythmic clatter of cutlery against ceramic was the soundtrack to her life, a constant, predictable rhythm in a house where everything else seemed poised on the brink of chaos.
