The biting November wind whipped Amelia "Amy" Hernandez’s hair across her face as she surveyed the desolate, windswept soccer field.  Fallen leaves, the color of rust and decay, clung stubbornly to the damp grass, mirroring the grim mood settling over the beleaguered players of the Westview High Wildcats.  The skeletal branches of the ancient oak trees lining the perimeter creaked like mournful ghosts, their silhouettes sharp against the bruised purple twilight. The air hung heavy with the scent of damp earth and the faint, metallic tang of blood – a stray cut on one of the players, probably, Amy thought grimly.  This wasn't just another loss; this was a season unraveling, thread by agonizing thread.  And Amy, the newly appointed coach of the Wildcats, felt the weight of its failure pressing down on her like a physical burden.
