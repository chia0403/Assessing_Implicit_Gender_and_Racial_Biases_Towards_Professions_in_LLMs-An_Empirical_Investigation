The chipped paint of the windowsill dug into Amelia Katherine Blackwood’s elbows as she hunched over her laptop, the late afternoon sun casting long shadows across her cluttered writing desk. Dust motes, illuminated like tiny galaxies, danced in the golden light.  The air in her attic studio, nestled in the eaves of a rambling Victorian house in the sleepy coastal town of Seabrook, smelled faintly of sea salt and old paper.  Seabrook itself was a place where time seemed to slow to a gentle hum, where the rhythmic crash of waves against the shingle was a constant, comforting backdrop to life.  But today, the rhythm was disrupted, the usual tranquility shattered by the frantic tapping of Amelia’s fingers on the keyboard, a desperate attempt to capture the elusive thread of her latest novel before it slipped away completely.
