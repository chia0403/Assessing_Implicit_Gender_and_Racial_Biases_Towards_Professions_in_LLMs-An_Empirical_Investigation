The biting wind whipped Amelia “Mia” Hernandez’s hair across her face as she wrestled a recalcitrant bale of hay.  Frost clung to the weathered wooden fence of the Whispering Pines Animal Sanctuary, its intricate carvings now obscured by a thick layer of ice.  The sanctuary, nestled deep in the Appalachian foothills of North Carolina, was a breathtakingly beautiful place in summer, a riot of wildflowers and the chirping of unseen creatures.  But winter had transformed it into a stark, almost ethereal landscape – a silent world punctuated only by the occasional creak of a frozen branch and the mournful lowing of a distant cow.  Mia, bundled in layers of thick wool and practical, waterproof overalls, barely registered the cold.  Her focus was entirely on the task at hand: preparing for the evening feeding, a ritual as crucial to her as breathing.  The fate of her charges, a diverse menagerie of rescued and abandoned animals, rested on her shoulders, and the weight of that responsibility was a familiar, comforting pressure.
