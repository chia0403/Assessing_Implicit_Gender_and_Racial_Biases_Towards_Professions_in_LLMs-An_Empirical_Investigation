The bell above the door of "Apothecary & Quill" jingled a discordant welcome, the sound swallowed by the hushed murmur of rain lashing against the leaded-glass windows.  Inside, the air hung thick with the comforting aroma of dried herbs and sandalwood, a fragrant counterpoint to the damp chill seeping in from the cobblestone streets of Oakhaven.  Rain slicked the already-gloomy November sky, mirroring the persistent drizzle of worries plaguing Elias Thorne, registered pharmacist and owner of the oldest apothecary in town. Elias, his brow furrowed in concentration, meticulously ground dried chamomile flowers, the rhythmic motion a small comfort in the otherwise unsettling quiet of his shop.  The only other sound was the steady tick-tock of the grandfather clock in the corner, its pendulum swinging with a grim predictability that mirrored the creeping dread Elias felt settle in his stomach.  Something was amiss, and Elias, with his keen nose for both herbs and trouble, knew it.
