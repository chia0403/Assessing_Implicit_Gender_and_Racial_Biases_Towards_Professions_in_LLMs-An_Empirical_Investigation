The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Katherine Stone’s hands, a meager comfort against the November chill that seeped through the drafty windows of Blackwood & Sons.  Rain lashed against the leaded glass panes, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her.  The air hung thick with the scent of old paper, stale coffee, and something vaguely floral – the desperate attempt of a wilting potpourri to mask the underlying aroma of desperation clinging to the office like a shroud.  Amelia, her auburn hair pulled back in a severe bun, stared out at the grey expanse of the London street, the rhythmic drumming of the rain a counterpoint to the relentless tapping of her own fingers on the mahogany desk. Blackwood & Sons, solicitors to the city's elite, felt more like a mausoleum than a place of business; a fitting backdrop for the secrets it held, and the secret Amelia herself was desperately trying to keep.
