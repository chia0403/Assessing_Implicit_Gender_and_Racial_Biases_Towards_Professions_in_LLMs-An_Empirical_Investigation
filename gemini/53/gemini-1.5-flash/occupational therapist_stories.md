The salt spray stung Eleanor Vance’s face as the ferry churned through the choppy waters of Puget Sound.  Rain, a constant companion in this corner of Washington State, lashed down, blurring the already indistinct outlines of the San Juan Islands.  Eleanor, a seasoned occupational therapist with a reputation for her unconventional methods, hunched deeper into her worn raincoat, the damp chill seeping into her bones despite the layers.  Her destination: Orcas Island, a place of rugged beauty and, she suspected, simmering secrets.  The small, battered suitcase at her feet contained not just her professional tools – adaptive utensils, sensory toys, and meticulously crafted assessment forms – but also a growing unease that had settled in her stomach like a stubborn knot. This wasn't a typical assignment; this felt… different.
