The salt spray stung Dr. Eleanor Vance's face as she wrestled the sputtering outboard motor into submission.  Seagulls wheeled overhead, their cries a harsh counterpoint to the rhythmic slap of waves against the hull of her small fishing boat, *The Scalpel*.  The air hung thick and heavy with the scent of brine, seaweed, and something else…something faintly sweet and unsettling.  Eleanor squinted at the grey, churning expanse of the Atlantic, the rugged, wind-whipped coastline of the Outer Hebrides a jagged silhouette against the bruised purple of the approaching dusk.  This wasn't a typical house call, not by a long shot.  Tonight, her patient wasn't a pampered Persian or a prize-winning poodle, but something far more…mysterious.
