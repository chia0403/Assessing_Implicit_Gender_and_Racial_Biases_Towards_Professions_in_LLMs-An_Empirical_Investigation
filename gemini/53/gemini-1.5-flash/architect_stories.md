The biting November wind whipped Amelia Hawthorne’s auburn hair across her face as she stood on the precipice of the cliff, the churning grey sea a churning beast below.  The air smelled sharply of brine and something else…something faintly metallic, like old blood.  Amelia, a woman whose life was usually defined by precise angles and meticulously crafted blueprints, felt a disquieting sense of imbalance, a dissonance between the stark, geometric lines of the abandoned lighthouse looming behind her and the chaotic fury of the ocean before her.  This wasn't the serene, inspirational landscape she'd envisioned when she’d accepted the commission to restore the crumbling structure; this was something… more.  The lighthouse, perched precariously on the jagged, wind-sculpted cliffs of Cornwall’s dramatic coastline, felt less like a building and more like a sentinel, guarding a secret it was unwilling to relinquish.
