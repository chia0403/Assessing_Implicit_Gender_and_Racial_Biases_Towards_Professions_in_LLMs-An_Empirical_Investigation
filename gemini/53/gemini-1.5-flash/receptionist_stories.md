The fluorescent lights of "Sterling & Steele, Attorneys at Law" hummed a monotonous tune, a counterpoint to the relentless tap-tap-tap of Penelope Anne Featherstonehaugh's perfectly manicured fingernails against the Formica reception desk.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows overlooking the grey, brooding cityscape of Manchester, mirroring the storm brewing inside Penelope.  It wasn't just the weather; it was the impending arrival of Mr. Silas Blackwood, a notoriously difficult client known for his sharp tongue and even sharper suits.  Penelope adjusted her perfectly-placed pearl earrings, a tiny act of rebellion against the suffocating predictability of her day, and the looming shadow of Blackwood's appointment at 2:15 pm.  The air hung thick with the scent of old leather and expensive coffee, a perfume as potent and unsettling as the impending storm itself.
