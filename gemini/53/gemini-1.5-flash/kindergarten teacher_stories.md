The chipped paint of the "Welcome to Ms. Eleanor Vance's Classroom!" sign threatened to peel off in larger flakes with every gust of wind whistling through the gaps in the aging school's window frames.  Inside, however, a vibrant chaos reigned.  Crayon dust motes danced in the sunbeams that slanted across the worn wooden floor, illuminating a miniature landscape of overturned building blocks and discarded construction paper.  The air hung thick with the sweet, slightly sour scent of finger paints and impending nap time.  Thirty-five five-year-olds, a kaleidoscope of bright sweaters and mismatched shoes, whirled around Eleanor Vance, their teacher, a whirlwind of calming reassurance and playful redirection.  Eleanor, with her perpetually smudged lipstick and a smile that could melt glaciers, was, for all intents and purposes, the eye of that particular storm.
