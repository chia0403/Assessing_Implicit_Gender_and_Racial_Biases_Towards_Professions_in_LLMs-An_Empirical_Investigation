The chipped porcelain teacup warmed Amelia Finch’s fingers, a small comfort against the November chill that seeped through the single-paned window of her office.  Rain lashed against the glass, mirroring the tempest brewing in her gut.  Amelia, a meticulous bookkeeper with a penchant for perfectly balanced ledgers and even more perfectly pressed blouses, stared at the overflowing in-tray on her mahogany desk.  The air, thick with the scent of old paper and damp wool, hung heavy with the unspoken anxieties of the failing textile mill, Finch & Sons, a business that had been her family's lifeblood for generations.  Its crumbling brick façade, visible from her window, stood as a stark testament to its dwindling fortunes – a reflection of the precarious balance sheet she wrestled with every day.
