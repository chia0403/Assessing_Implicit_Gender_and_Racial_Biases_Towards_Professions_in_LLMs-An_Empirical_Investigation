The fluorescent lights of "Quick Stop Mart" hummed a monotonous tune, a soundtrack to Amelia Hernandez's existence.  Amelia, meticulously straightening a stack of lottery tickets already perfectly aligned, sighed inwardly.  The air hung heavy with the scent of stale coffee and desperation, a familiar perfume of this late-night shift.  Outside, the relentless Texas summer heat shimmered on the cracked asphalt of the parking lot, promising another sweltering day.  Rain hadn't graced these parched lands in weeks, mirroring the drought in Amelia's own spirits.  The digital clock above the register blinked 3:17 AM, each second ticking away with the slow, deliberate pace of molasses in January.
