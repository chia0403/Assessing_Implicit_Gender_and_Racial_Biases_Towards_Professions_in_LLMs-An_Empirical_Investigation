The sterile scent of ozone hung heavy in the air, a familiar perfume to Seraphina “Sera” Bellweather.  Fluorescent lights hummed a monotonous tune above her, reflecting off the polished stainless steel of the dental equipment.  Sera, her nimble fingers already deftly working a miniature scaler against a plastic training model, barely registered the sound.  Outside, the rhythmic crash of the Pacific Ocean against the cliffs of Carmel-by-the-Sea was a far cry from the muted whir of the suction hose.  But today, the ocean's roar was a distant dream, swallowed by the urgent need to perfect her technique before Dr. Albright’s notoriously picky patient, the reclusive billionaire, Mr. Silas Blackwood, arrived for his appointment. The fate of her bonus, and perhaps her sanity, depended on it.
