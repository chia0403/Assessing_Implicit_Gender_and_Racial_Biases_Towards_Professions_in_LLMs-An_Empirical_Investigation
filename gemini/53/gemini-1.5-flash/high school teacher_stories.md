The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Hernandez’s hands, the lukewarm tea doing little to chase away the November chill that seeped through the drafty window of her classroom.  Rain lashed against the ancient panes of Westview High, a relentless rhythm that mirrored the drumming anxiety in her chest.  Outside, the sprawling oaks of the school grounds were skeletal against the bruised purple sky, their branches clawing at the downpour. Inside, the air hung thick with the scent of old textbooks, chalk dust, and the faint, lingering sweetness of yesterday’s spilled orange juice – a testament to the chaotic energy of her sophomore English class.  Amelia sighed, the sound barely audible above the drumming rain.  The upcoming parent-teacher conferences loomed, a stark reminder of the weight of responsibility she carried, not just for the curriculum, but for the fragile young lives entrusted to her care.
