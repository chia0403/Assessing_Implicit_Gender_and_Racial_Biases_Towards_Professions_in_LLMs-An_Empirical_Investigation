The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Holloway’s hair across her face as she hunched deeper into her threadbare coat.  Rain, sleeting with the ferocity of a vengeful god, plastered her already damp trench coat to her skin.  She pulled her scarf tighter, the wool scratching against her chapped cheeks, and squinted at the imposing Victorian mansion looming before her.  Gothic arches, shadowed by overgrown ivy that seemed to writhe in the wind, framed windows like vacant eyes staring out into the storm.  This was Blackwood Manor, perched precariously on the cliff edge overlooking the churning grey expanse of the North Sea, a place whispered about in hushed tones by the locals of the isolated coastal village of Seabrook.  Amelia, a seasoned investigative journalist for the *Daily Chronicle*, had arrived to unravel the truth behind the unsettling rumours – rumours of disappearances, strange lights, and a legacy of unsettling family secrets.
