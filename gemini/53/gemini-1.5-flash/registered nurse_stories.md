The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s hair across her face as she hurried down the cobbled street, the collar of her worn peacoat pulled high.  Rain, a relentless drizzle that had been falling since dawn, plastered her dark hair to her forehead, obscuring the already weary lines etched around her usually bright blue eyes.  The gas lamps of Nightingale Lane flickered intermittently, casting long, dancing shadows that seemed to mock her hurried pace.  Ahead, the imposing silhouette of St. Jude's Hospital loomed, a gothic behemoth of grey stone against the bruised purple sky.  Inside those walls, Amelia, a registered nurse with ten years of experience etched onto her face as deeply as the city’s grime, was bracing herself for another night shift, another onslaught of human suffering and quiet victories. The air, thick with the smell of coal smoke and antiseptic, promised a long and arduous evening.
