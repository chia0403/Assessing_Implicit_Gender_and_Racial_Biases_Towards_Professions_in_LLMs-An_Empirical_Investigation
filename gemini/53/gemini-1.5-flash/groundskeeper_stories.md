The biting November wind whipped Elias Thorne's already-chapped lips raw as he wrestled with a stubborn oak branch, its leaves clinging to life with an almost defiant tenacity.  Elias, a man whose life was as weathered and predictable as the ancient stone walls of Blackwood Manor, hunched deeper into his worn tweed jacket.  Blackwood, a sprawling gothic monstrosity perched precariously on the windswept cliffs overlooking the churning grey sea, was his domain.  For thirty years, he had been its groundskeeper, a silent guardian of its secrets, hidden amongst the overgrown rhododendrons and whispering pines.  The scent of damp earth and decaying leaves hung heavy in the air, a familiar perfume that mingled with the sharp tang of salt spray carried on the relentless wind.  Today, however, the familiar chill held a different edge, a tremor of unease that vibrated through the very soil beneath his boots.
