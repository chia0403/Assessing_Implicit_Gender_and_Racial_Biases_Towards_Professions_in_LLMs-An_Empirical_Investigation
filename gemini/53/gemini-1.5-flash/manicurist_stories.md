The scent of acetone and lavender hung thick in the air, a cloying sweetness that clung to the plush velvet chairs and the chipped porcelain sink of Serenity Nails.  Rain lashed against the windowpanes of the tiny salon, blurring the already fading neon sign proclaiming its name.  Inside, amidst the muted chaos of whirring electric files and the rhythmic tap-tap-tapping of nail brushes, sat Seraphina “Sera” Bellweather, her nimble fingers working a masterpiece on a client's meticulously shaped almond nails.  The woman, a harried-looking lawyer in a power suit, barely registered Sera's presence, her gaze fixed on the shimmering cityscape outside, a storm mirroring the one brewing inside her.  Sera, however, was oblivious, lost in her own world of carefully chosen colours and precise strokes, each layer of polish a tiny act of creation against the relentless grey of the Seattle evening.
