The wind, a relentless sculptor, carved patterns into the snowdrifts that piled high against the weathered fence posts.  Amelia “Amy”  Jeanette Blackwood squinted against the biting January air, the breath misting before her face like a phantom’s sigh.  The vast expanse of the Blackwood Ranch, stretching out before her like a rumpled, snow-covered blanket, seemed endless under the pale, indifferent sky.  The only sound louder than the wind’s howl was the rhythmic crunch of her worn leather boots against the frozen ground as she made her way towards the distant silhouette of the bunkhouse, a lone sentinel against the icy wilderness of the Wyoming plains.  The setting sun bled crimson and orange across the horizon, painting the snow a breathtaking but deceptive beauty.  Underneath that serene surface, Amy knew, lay the harsh reality of a winter threatening to bury her dreams – and maybe her livelihood – beneath its icy grip.
