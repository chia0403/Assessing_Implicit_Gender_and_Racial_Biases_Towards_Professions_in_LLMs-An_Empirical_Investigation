The biting wind whipped at Sergeant Elias Thorne’s worn greatcoat, stinging his cheeks and tugging at the brim of his fur-lined cap.  He shifted his weight from one frost-numbed foot to the other, his breath puffing out in ragged white clouds against the pre-dawn gloom.  The obsidian walls of Blackrock Fortress loomed around him, their jagged peaks scraping at the bruised, violet sky.  The air hung heavy with the scent of brine and something else, something acrid and metallic that clung to the back of his throat – the ever-present reminder of the ceaseless, grinding war that raged beyond the fortress walls.  This wasn't the quiet, predictable patrol he'd been promised; this was the treacherous, windswept precipice known as the Widow's Walk, and Elias knew, with a certainty that chilled him more than the wind, that something was wrong.
