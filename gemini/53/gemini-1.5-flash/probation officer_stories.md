The chipped paint of the probation office's waiting room peeled like sunburnt skin, mirroring the frayed edges of Elias Thorne's patience.  Elias, a man whose weary eyes held the weight of countless broken promises and half-hearted attempts at redemption, adjusted his tie, the knot feeling as constricting as the bureaucratic red tape he navigated daily.  Rain lashed against the grimy windows of the city's oldest municipal building, a rhythmic drumming that seemed to echo the relentless beat of his own weary heart.  The air hung thick with the smell of stale coffee and simmering anxieties, a familiar perfume in the cramped space where hope and despair wrestled for dominance.  Today, the scent was particularly pungent, a premonition perhaps, as Elias prepared for his meeting with  Marcus “Razor” Riley, a man whose reputation for violence preceded him like a storm cloud.
