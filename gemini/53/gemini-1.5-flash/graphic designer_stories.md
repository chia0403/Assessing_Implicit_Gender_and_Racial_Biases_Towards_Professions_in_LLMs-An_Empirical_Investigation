The chipped mug warmed Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s hands, the lukewarm chamomile tea doing little to soothe the icy dread coiling in her stomach.  Rain lashed against the oversized window of her studio apartment, a relentless drumming that mirrored the frantic rhythm of her own pulse.  Grey light, filtered through the storm clouds, barely illuminated the cluttered space: a chaotic ballet of overflowing sketchbooks, half-finished canvases leaning precariously against the walls, and a mountain of design textbooks threatening to topple from a wobbly stack.  The air hung thick with the scent of linseed oil and desperation.  Amelia, a graphic designer with a penchant for vintage typefaces and an unfortunate allergy to deadlines, stared at the blinking cursor on her laptop screen, the looming shadow of the Blackwood & Sons campaign deadline – and the potential loss of her biggest client – pressing down on her like a physical weight.  The city outside, usually a vibrant tapestry of lights and sounds, was tonight a muted, melancholic canvas, reflecting the storm raging both within and without.
