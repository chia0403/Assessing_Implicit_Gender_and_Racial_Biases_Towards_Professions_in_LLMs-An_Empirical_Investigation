The chipped paint of Dr. Eleanor Vance's office door seemed to mirror the cracks appearing in her own carefully constructed composure.  Rain lashed against the tall, gothic windows of Blackwood Manor, a converted Victorian mansion that housed her practice on the edge of the brooding Dartmoor moors.  Inside, the air hung thick with the scent of damp stone and woodsmoke, a stark contrast to the sterile clinical environment she usually preferred.  Tonight, however, the comforting familiarity of her meticulously organized space offered little solace.  Eleanor Vance, Ph.D., found herself staring at the swirling storm outside, a reflection of the turmoil brewing within her latest, and perhaps most perplexing, patient.
