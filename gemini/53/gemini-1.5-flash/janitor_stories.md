The chipped porcelain sink wept a steady drip, a counterpoint to the rhythmic swish of Amelia Hernandez's mop.  Fluorescent lights hummed a tired tune above, casting a sickly yellow glow across the polished marble floors of the Grand Majestic Hotel.  It was 3:17 AM, the hour when the city outside, a roaring beast only moments before, finally succumbed to a weary slumber.  But within the Grand Majestic, Amelia was still awake, a solitary sentinel in a cavernous space that held the hushed secrets of countless opulent lives.  The lingering scent of expensive perfume and stale champagne clung to the air, a ghostly reminder of the revelry that had ended hours ago, leaving her to clean up the remnants of champagne wishes and caviar dreams.
