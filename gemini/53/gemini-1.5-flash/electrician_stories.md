The salt spray stung Elias Thorne's face as he wrestled the heavy coil of armored cable onto the swaying platform. Below him, the churning grey expanse of the North Sea swallowed the fading light, the only sound louder than the wind the rhythmic clang of the waves against the legs of the oil rig, *Triton's Fury*. Elias, a man whose calloused hands spoke more eloquently of his trade than any resume ever could, adjusted his safety harness, the familiar weight a small comfort in the vast, unforgiving landscape.  The biting wind whipped his thick, dark hair across his eyes, momentarily obscuring the flickering emergency lights of the rig, a stark contrast to the inky blackness of the approaching storm.  He was three hundred meters above the ocean, perched precariously on a metal birdcage, with a thousand volts of raw power humming in his very hands, and tonight, the power was failing.
