The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s auburn hair across her face as she hurried down Bleecker Street, the scent of roasting chestnuts and damp leaves battling for dominance in the crisp air.  Rain threatened, a brooding grey sky mirroring the turmoil brewing within her.  Amelia, acquisitions editor at the prestigious Blackwood Press, clutched a steaming takeaway coffee – its warmth a small comfort against the chill that seeped into her bones, a chill that had nothing to do with the temperature.  The city, usually a vibrant symphony of noise and energy, felt muffled, muted, a reflection of the leaden weight settling on her chest.  Blackwood’s annual holiday party loomed, a glittering, stressful affair that promised more champagne-fueled gossip than genuine camaraderie, and Amelia, fresh off a disastrous meeting with her notoriously demanding boss, felt utterly unprepared to face it.  The manuscript she’d been championing for months, a breathtaking debut novel, was teetering on the brink of rejection, and the pressure was crushing her.
