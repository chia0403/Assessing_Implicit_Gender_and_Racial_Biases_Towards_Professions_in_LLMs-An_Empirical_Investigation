The scent of lilies, sharp and sweet, hung heavy in the air, a stark contrast to the biting November wind rattling the windowpanes of "Petals & Promises."  Inside, Amelia Hawthorne, her fingers stained a delicate shade of lilac from handling hydrangeas, meticulously arranged a cascade of deep crimson roses.  Rain lashed against the glass, blurring the already fading light of the late afternoon, transforming the cobblestone street outside into a shimmering, watery canvas.  Amelia's small shop, tucked away on a quiet lane in the heart of historic Charleston, South Carolina, was a haven of warmth and fragrant beauty, a vibrant splash of color against the grey backdrop of the approaching storm. The air, thick with the perfume of a thousand blooms, offered a temporary respite from the chill that seeped into the very bones of the old city.
