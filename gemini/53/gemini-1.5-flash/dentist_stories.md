The stale scent of antiseptic and impending doom hung heavy in the air, a familiar perfume to Dr. Alistair Finch.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of his practice, blurring the already hazy cityscape of Manchester into a watercolour wash of greys and blacks.  Alistair, his reflection a tired, slightly bloodshot version of the man he saw in the mirror each morning, meticulously polished his dental instruments, the rhythmic gleam a counterpoint to the furious drumming of the storm.  Outside, the wind howled like a banshee, a fitting soundtrack to the grim premonition that had settled in his gut since the cryptic phone call an hour ago.  It had been a woman’s voice, low and husky, promising him a case that would “make his teeth ache in a whole new way.”
