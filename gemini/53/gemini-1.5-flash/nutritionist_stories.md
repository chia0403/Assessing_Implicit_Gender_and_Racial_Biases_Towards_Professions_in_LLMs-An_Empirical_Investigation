The salt spray stung Dr. Eleanor Vance's face as she wrestled the stubborn canvas awning of her seaside clinic into submission.  The wind, a relentless, howling banshee straight off the Atlantic, threatened to rip the whole thing from its moorings.  Eleanor, however, was made of sterner stuff than flimsy fabric.  Her clinic, “Sea & Sage Nutrition,” perched precariously on the windswept cliffs of Port Blossom, Maine, was as much a testament to her resilience as it was to her passion for healthy living.  The scent of brine and pine mingled with the faint, earthy aroma of seaweed, a constant reminder of both the harsh beauty and the abundant bounty of her coastal home.  Today, however, the bounty felt less abundant, overshadowed by the approaching storm brewing both in the sky and, she suspected, in the heart of her next client.
