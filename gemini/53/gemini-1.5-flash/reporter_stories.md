The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s hair across her face, stinging her cheeks a fiery red.  She tugged her worn wool coat tighter, the collar scratching against her already chapped skin.  Rain, sleeting in furious bursts, hammered against the corrugated iron roof of the dilapidated newsstand she’d sought refuge in. The flickering neon sign of "Joe's Last Stand" above cast a sickly yellow glow on the overflowing bins and sodden newspapers strewn across the alleyway behind.  This wasn’t exactly the glamorous backdrop Amelia had envisioned for her investigative piece on the city’s burgeoning black market organ trade, but then again, rarely were things as they appeared in the glossy brochures of ambitious twenty-something reporters.  The scent of stale coffee and something vaguely acrid – possibly rotting fish – hung heavy in the air, a grimly fitting perfume for the city of Veridia, a metropolis where shadows stretched long and secrets festered deep.
