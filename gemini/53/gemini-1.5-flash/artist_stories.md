The biting November wind whipped Amelia Blackwood’s unruly auburn curls across her face as she wrestled the heavy canvas onto the easel.  Salt spray, thick as sea-foam, clung to the coarse linen, mirroring the texture of the churning grey sea that stretched before her, a relentless expanse swallowing the bruised purple of the twilight sky.  Amelia shivered, not entirely from the cold.  The isolated clifftop studio, perched precariously on the jagged Cornish coast, felt as wild and untamed as the landscape it commanded.  The only sound besides the wind’s mournful howl was the rhythmic crash of waves against the rocks far below, a constant, almost hypnotic percussion accompanying the frantic strokes of her brush.  Tonight, the sea wasn't just her subject; it was a reflection of the tempest brewing within her.
