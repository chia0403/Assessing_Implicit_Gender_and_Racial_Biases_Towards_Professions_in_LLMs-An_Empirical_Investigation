The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Amy” Hernandez’s tailored coat around her legs as she hurried down Wall Street, the relentless rhythm of her heels a counterpoint to the city’s cacophony.  Grey skies mirrored the grim expression etched onto her face; the kind of expression that comes from staring at plummeting stock values for twelve hours straight.  The scent of roasting chestnuts from a nearby vendor barely cut through the metallic tang of rain and exhaust fumes, a stark contrast to the opulent aroma of mahogany and expensive leather that permeated the plush, oak-panelled offices of Hernandez & Associates, her own brokerage firm, just a block away. Inside, amidst the hushed whispers and the frantic tapping of keyboards, a battle raged – a battle Amy was determined to win, even if it meant sacrificing a sliver of her already depleted sanity.
