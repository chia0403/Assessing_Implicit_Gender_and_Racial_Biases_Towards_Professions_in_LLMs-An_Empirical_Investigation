The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s dark hair across her face as she hurried down the snow-dusted street of Port Blossom, the salty tang of the ocean a stark contrast to the icy air.  Port Blossom, nestled on a craggy peninsula jutting into the churning grey expanse of the Atlantic, was a town clinging to the edge of the world, both beautiful and unforgiving. Its charm lay in the quaint, brightly painted fishing cottages huddled together against the relentless wind, a charm Amelia, a physician’s assistant at the town’s lone, understaffed clinic, often found herself appreciating more than the harsh realities of her job. Tonight, however, appreciation was a luxury she couldn't afford. The rhythmic clang of the old town bell, usually a comforting sound, now felt like a frantic heartbeat echoing her own growing anxiety.  A blizzard was brewing, and she was already late.
