The salt spray stung Detective Inspector Amelia Grace Hawthorne's face as the November wind whipped her dark coat around her legs.  The air hung thick with the smell of brine and decaying seaweed, a familiar, pungent perfume of the Cornish coast.  Below her, the churning grey Atlantic slammed against the jagged rocks of Porthcurno, the waves spitting white froth in a chaotic ballet of destruction.  The dramatic cliffs, usually a breathtaking sight, tonight served as a stark backdrop to the grim scene unfolding at their base: a small, overturned fishing boat, its net snagged on a submerged boulder, and the inert form of a man half-submerged in the icy water.
