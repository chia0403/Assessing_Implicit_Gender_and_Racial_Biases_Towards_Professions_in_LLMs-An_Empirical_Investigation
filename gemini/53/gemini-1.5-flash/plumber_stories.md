The wrench slipped, a metallic shriek echoing through the oppressive silence of the abandoned Blackwood Manor.  Dust motes, disturbed by the sudden movement, danced in the single shaft of moonlight slicing through a grime-coated window.  A chill, deeper than the October night outside, snaked down Bartholomew "Barty" Butterfield's spine.  Barty, a plumber whose hands were more accustomed to the greasy embrace of drainpipes than the icy grip of ancient stone, wasn't supposed to be here.  He'd been promised a straightforward job – unclogging a drain in the manor's west wing – not a foray into a crumbling mausoleum that seemed to whisper secrets from beyond the grave.  The air hung heavy with the scent of damp earth and something else… something acrid and unsettling, like burnt sugar and decay.
