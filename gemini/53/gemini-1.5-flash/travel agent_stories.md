The scent of old paper and exotic spices hung heavy in the air, a perfume uniquely Amelia “Mia” Hernandez’s own.  Mia, a travel agent whose office resided in a charming, if slightly dilapidated, Victorian building overlooking the perpetually grey San Francisco Bay, sighed, the sound swallowed by the rhythmic clack of her vintage typewriter. Rain lashed against the tall, arched windows, mirroring the storm brewing inside her.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of afternoon sunlight slicing through the gloom, illuminating a chaotic desk piled high with brochures advertising sun-drenched beaches, snow-capped mountains, and ancient ruins – a stark contrast to the dreary scene outside.  The brass nameplate on her desk, slightly tarnished with age, proclaimed simply: *Amelia Hernandez, Purveyor of Escapes*.  Today, however, escape seemed a distant, improbable dream.
