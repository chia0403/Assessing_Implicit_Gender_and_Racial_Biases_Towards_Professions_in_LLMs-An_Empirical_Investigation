The flickering neon sign of the "Lucky Dragon" noodle shop cast a lurid, orange glow across Elara Vance's rain-streaked window.  Rain lashed against the glass, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her.  Elara, a woman whose fingers danced across keyboards with the grace of a concert pianist but whose personal life resembled a tangled mess of spaghetti code, stared at the blinking cursor on her laptop screen.  The deadline for Project Chimera – a top-secret AI project for OmniCorp – loomed, a monstrous, digital hydra with a thousand impossible demands.  Outside, the humid Hong Kong night pulsed with a chaotic energy that mirrored the frantic rhythm of the code she was wrestling with.  The scent of soy sauce and frying garlic drifted in on the wind, a tantalising distraction from the relentless demands of the machine, and the even more relentless demands of her own overactive mind.
