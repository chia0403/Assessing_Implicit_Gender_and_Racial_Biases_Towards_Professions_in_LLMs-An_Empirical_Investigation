The fluorescent lights of Eldridge & Sons hummed a monotonous tune, a soundtrack to Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s meticulously organized world.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of her 27th-floor office, blurring the city lights of Chicago into a hazy, impressionistic painting.  Amelia, a woman whose life revolved around balanced ledgers and perfectly aligned spreadsheets, found a strange comfort in the storm's chaotic energy – a stark contrast to the rigid precision she demanded of her own existence.  Tonight, however, even the rhythmic drumming of the rain couldn't drown out the unsettling tremor in her gut.  A tremor that had nothing to do with the city's notoriously temperamental weather, and everything to do with the encrypted email sitting, unopened, in her inbox.
