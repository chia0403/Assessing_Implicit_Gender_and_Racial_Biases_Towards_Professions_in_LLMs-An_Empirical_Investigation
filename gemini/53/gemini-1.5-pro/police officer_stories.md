The biting November wind whipped off Lake Erie, stinging Detective Isabella “Izzy” Diaz’s cheeks as she surveyed the scene.  Cleveland’s industrial Flats district, usually a symphony of rumbling trucks and clanking machinery, was eerily silent.  Sodium vapor lamps cast a sickly yellow glow over the abandoned warehouse, their light reflecting off the oily puddles that pocked the asphalt.  A single, flickering bulb illuminated a crudely spray-painted tag on the brick wall: a crimson skull with an X scrawled across its forehead.  Izzy pulled her collar higher, the damp chill seeping into her bones. This was the third such tagging in as many weeks, each more elaborate, each more menacing. And this one…this one had a body.
