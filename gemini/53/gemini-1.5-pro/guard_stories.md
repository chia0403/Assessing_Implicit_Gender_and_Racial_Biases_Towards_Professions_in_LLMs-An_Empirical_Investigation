The biting wind whipped across the obsidian plains of Xylos, stinging Elias Thorne's exposed cheeks.  Crystalline dust, sharp as ground glass, swirled around his thick, fur-lined boots.  He adjusted the grip on his frost-etched spear, its tip gleaming faintly in the perpetual twilight that cloaked the desolate landscape.  Elias, a seasoned Guard of the Obsidian Citadel, stood at his post on the western rampart, his breath clouding the frigid air.  For five years he'd endured this bleak vigil, his gaze fixed on the horizon, watching for any flicker of movement amidst the jagged, black rock formations that resembled the teeth of some monstrous, buried beast. Tonight, however, felt different.  A low, guttural hum resonated from deep within the earth, a sound Elias had never heard before, a sound that sent shivers down his spine despite the layers of protective furs.
