Rain lashed against the stained-glass window depicting Saint Jude, patron saint of lost causes, a fitting emblem for The Rusty Nail.  Smoke hung thick in the air, a mix of cheap tobacco and desperation.  The only light came from the flickering neon sign outside and the dim, amber glow behind the bar where Elias Thorne, his face etched with the weariness of a thousand confessions, polished a glass with a practiced hand. The Nail, nestled in the forgotten corner of Port Blossom, Oregon, was the kind of place where secrets came to die, and Elias, with his quiet demeanor and ever-present flask, was their undertaker.  Tonight, however, the secrets felt heavier, the rain colder, and Elias had a feeling another lost cause was about to walk through the door.
