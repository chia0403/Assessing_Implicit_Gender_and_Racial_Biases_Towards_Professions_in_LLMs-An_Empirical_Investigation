The biting November wind whipped around Cassandra "Cassie" Moreau, plastering strands of her auburn hair across her face as she surveyed the site.  Grey clouds, heavy with the promise of snow, scudded low over the derelict Victorian mansion that loomed before her like a skeletal finger pointing accusingly at the sky.  Ivy, thick as pythons, choked the ornate stonework, and the once-grand windows were now vacant sockets staring out onto the overgrown gardens of Eventide Manor, nestled deep in the fog-shrouded moors of Yorkshire, England. Cassie, bundled in a thick wool coat and sturdy boots, pulled her scarf tighter around her neck, the chill seeping into her bones despite the layers. This was her latest project, a daunting restoration commissioned by an eccentric recluse she'd yet to meet.  The manor, rumored to be haunted, had stood empty for decades, a monument to forgotten grandeur and whispered secrets.  And Cassie, armed with her blueprints and an unwavering determination, was about to breathe life back into its crumbling walls.
