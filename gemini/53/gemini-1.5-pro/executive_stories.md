Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of Amelia Vivienne Holloway's forty-second-floor office, blurring the already fading twilight cityscape of Chicago into a hazy impressionist painting.  The wind howled a mournful tune around the sleek, steel-and-glass skyscraper, mirroring the turmoil churning within Amelia.  Her tailored Alexander McQueen suit, usually a source of power and confidence, felt constricting, the silk scarf at her throat suddenly too tight.  Scattered across her ebony desk, amidst the polished chrome and crystal accessories, lay the remnants of her meticulously crafted five-year plan – now shredded, both literally and figuratively, by the terse email she’d received just an hour ago from the CEO. The subject line: "Restructuring."  Amelia, Chief Operating Officer of Winslow Technologies and a woman who had built her career on meticulous planning and flawless execution, was about to face the greatest upheaval of her professional life. The city lights, shimmering through the rain-streaked glass, seemed to mock her with their indifferent brilliance.  She picked up a heavy crystal paperweight, cool and smooth against her palm, and stared into its depths, wondering if it would be enough to hold down the rising tide of anxiety threatening to consume her.
