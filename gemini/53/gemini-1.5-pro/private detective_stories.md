Rain lashed against the grimy windows of Silas Finch's office, mimicking the drumming anxiety in his chest.  Neon light from the liquor store across the street painted the rain-slicked asphalt a lurid, flickering green.  Silas, full name Silas Blackwood Finch, sat hunched over his desk, the single lamp illuminating a constellation of dust motes dancing in the stale air. His office, nestled above a bowling alley in the less-salubrious end of San Francisco's Mission District, wasn't much, but it was his.  The peeling wallpaper, the perpetually overflowing ashtray, the faint aroma of stale coffee and desperation - these were the familiar comforts of his solitary existence.  Tonight, however, even the familiar felt alien.  A crumpled cigarette pack lay empty beside a half-eaten pastrami on rye, both testament to the restless hours Silas had spent waiting for a client who, as the relentless ticking of the Bakelite clock on the wall confirmed, was now unforgivably late.
