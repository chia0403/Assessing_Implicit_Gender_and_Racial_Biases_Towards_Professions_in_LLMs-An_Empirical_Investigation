The clatter of horse-drawn carriages on cobblestones and the cries of street vendors hawking their wares filtered through the grimy windows of Elias Finch's tailoring shop.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of afternoon sun that pierced the gloom, illuminating the chaos within.  Elias, his spectacles perched precariously on his nose, knelt amidst a riot of fabrics: bolts of emerald velvet, rolls of sapphire silk, and lengths of crimson brocade tumbled from overflowing shelves.  His shop, nestled between a bustling fishmonger and a suspiciously quiet apothecary on the crooked lane known as Crow's Foot Alley in the lower reaches of Old Quarterbrook, wasn't grand, but it was his.  And within its cramped confines, amidst the pungent scent of dyes and the rhythmic snip-snip of his shears, Elias Finch crafted magic with needle and thread.
