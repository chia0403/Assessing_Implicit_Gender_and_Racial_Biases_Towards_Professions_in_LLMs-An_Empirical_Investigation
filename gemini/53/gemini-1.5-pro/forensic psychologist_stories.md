Rain lashed against the gothic windows of Dr. Elias Thorne's Edinburgh flat, mirroring the tempest brewing within him.  The gnawing unease, a familiar companion these last few months, tightened its grip.  His usually meticulously organized study, a sanctuary of leather-bound books and meticulously filed case notes, felt stifling tonight.  Outside, the wind howled through the narrow wynds and cobbled streets of the Old Town, a mournful soundtrack to his restless pacing.  He’d received the call just an hour ago, Detective Inspector Davies' voice tight with urgency, summoning him to the scene of a crime unlike any Edinburgh had seen.  Elias, a forensic psychologist renowned for his ability to dissect the darkest corners of the criminal mind, knew, with a chilling certainty, that this case would burrow deep under his skin, a splinter in his psyche he wouldn’t be able to remove.
