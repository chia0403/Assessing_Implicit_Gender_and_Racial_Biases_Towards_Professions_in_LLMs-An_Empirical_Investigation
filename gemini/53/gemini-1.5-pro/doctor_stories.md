The biting wind whipped off the North Sea, rattling the windows of Dr. Elias Thorne's surgery.  Salt spray clung to the glass, blurring the already fading light of the late November afternoon.  His surgery, perched precariously on the edge of the cliff in the small, isolated fishing village of Port Blossom, Yorkshire, usually offered a breathtaking view of the churning grey expanse. Today, however, the beauty was lost on him. The only thing Elias saw was the relentless, unforgiving ocean that had claimed so many lives, and threatened to claim another.
