The rain lashed against the arched windows of Amelia Grace Holloway's office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her.  Her name, usually spoken with a degree of reverence in the hallowed halls of the Charleston County Courthouse, felt like a lead weight in her chest. The antique brass clock on her mahogany desk chimed seven times, each clang echoing the emptiness of the room.  Outside, the historic gas lamps of Charleston, South Carolina flickered in the wind, casting long, dancing shadows that stretched across the cobblestone streets and painted grotesque shapes on the walls of her office.  This room, usually a sanctuary of legal strategy and sharp wit, now felt like a prison. Amelia pushed a stray strand of her auburn hair behind her ear, her emerald green eyes fixed on the single document lying on her otherwise pristine desk. It was an eviction notice, not for a client, but for her.  Her ancestral home, the very foundation of her lineage in this city, was slipping through her fingers like grains of sand.
