The biting November wind whipped across the cracked asphalt of the St. Jude's High School parking lot, sending brittle brown leaves skittering across Amelia “Amy” Reyes’s path.  She clutched her worn clipboard tighter, the frayed edges digging into her palm. Above, the floodlights cast a sickly yellow glow on the empty football field, starkly contrasting with the inky black of the encroaching woods beyond.  Amy exhaled, a puff of white momentarily obscuring the rusted bleachers. Another late night, another empty practice field. This was her life now: Head Coach Amelia Reyes, ruler of a kingdom of ghosts. St. Jude's Raiders hadn't won a game in three years, and the ghosts of seasons past seemed determined to haunt her every waking moment – and quite a few of the sleeping ones, too.
