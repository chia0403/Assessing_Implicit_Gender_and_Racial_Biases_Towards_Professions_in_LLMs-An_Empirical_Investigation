The chipped porcelain flamingo perched precariously on the edge of Amelia "Amy" Rose Hawthorne's desk vibrated with the insistent buzzing of her phone.  Rain lashed against the window of her tiny travel agency, "Wanderlust Whispers," blurring the neon glow of the "Open" sign reflected in the puddles forming on the cobblestone street outside. Located in a tucked-away corner of the French Quarter in New Orleans, Wanderlust Whispers exuded a chaotic charm. Brochures advertising exotic locales overflowed from wicker baskets, vintage travel posters plastered the walls, and a faint scent of sandalwood incense mingled with the ever-present aroma of chicory coffee brewing in the back room. Amy, her auburn hair escaping its messy bun, sighed and pushed a stray strand behind her ear. October in New Orleans was usually balmy, but this year, a stubborn chill lingered in the air, mirroring the unease that had settled in her gut since the mysterious postcard arrived three days ago.
