The salt-laced wind whipped Amelia Reyes's dark braid across her face, stinging her eyes as she surveyed the scene before her.  Grey cliffs, jagged and formidable, plunged into the churning turquoise of the Celtic Sea.  Below, nestled precariously on a narrow shelf of rock, the village of Port Blossom clung to existence, its whitewashed cottages huddled together like sheep against a storm.  Amelia, perched on a windswept bluff overlooking the scene, clutched her worn leather satchel tighter, the canvas boards within rattling softly.  This was it.  This was the view that had haunted her dreams for months, the landscape that demanded to be captured, to be wrestled onto canvas in a riot of blues and greys and the startling white of crashing waves.  This remote corner of Ireland, shrouded in myth and whispers of ancient magic, was where Amelia hoped to finally find the inspiration she'd been desperately seeking, the spark to ignite her stagnant creativity.
