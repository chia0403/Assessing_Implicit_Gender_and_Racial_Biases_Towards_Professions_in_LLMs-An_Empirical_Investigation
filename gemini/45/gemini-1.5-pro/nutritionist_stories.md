The Himalayan salt lamp cast a warm, apricot glow across Dr. Eleanor Vance's cluttered desk, illuminating stacks of research papers on the benefits of fermented foods and the dangers of artificial sweeteners. Rain lashed against the window of her small, second-story office overlooking the bustling, spice-scented streets of Marrakech's medina. The scent of mint tea, brewing in a silver pot on a low table nearby, mingled with the petrichor drifting in through the slightly ajar window. Eleanor, a woman whose vibrant red hair was a stark contrast to her crisp white lab coat, tapped a pen against her teeth, a furrow in her brow as she reviewed the latest bloodwork results of a prominent Moroccan businessman who insisted his diet of tagine and couscous was the pinnacle of healthy eating.  It was going to be a long night.
