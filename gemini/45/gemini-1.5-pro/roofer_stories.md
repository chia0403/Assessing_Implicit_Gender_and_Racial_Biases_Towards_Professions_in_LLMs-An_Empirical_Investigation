The tar hissed and bubbled, a miniature black volcano on the shimmering shingles.  Elijah "Eli" Blackwood adjusted his thick gloves, the August sun beating down on the back of his neck with the force of a blacksmith's hammer.  Sweat stung his eyes, blurring the already dizzying view from atop Mrs. Hawthorne's Victorian mansion.  Below him, the manicured lawns of Ashworth, Connecticut, stretched out like a patchwork quilt, each meticulously tended square a testament to the town's quiet, suffocating prosperity. Eli, however, felt no kinship with the manicured perfection. He preferred the precarious heights, the rough texture of shingles beneath his calloused fingers, the solitude broken only by the caw of a crow circling lazily overhead. This was his world, a world apart from the manicured lawns and hushed secrets of Ashworth.  And today, his world held a secret of its own.
