The siren's wail sliced through the thick, humid air of New Orleans, bouncing off the wrought-iron balconies and brick facades of the French Quarter. Rain, the kind that came in sheets and smelled of river mud and stale beignets, lashed against the windshield of Medic One.  Inside, Amelia “Mia” Catherine Moreau, her dark hair plastered to her forehead, gripped the steering wheel, her knuckles white.  The dispatcher’s frantic voice crackled over the radio: "Multiple victims, possible explosion, Bourbon Street. Medic One, expedite." Mia glanced at her partner, Marcus, who was already prepping their trauma kit, his face grim.  Bourbon Street on a Friday night. It was never good, but tonight felt… different.  The air itself seemed to hum with a nervous energy, as if the city held its breath, waiting for the next blow.
