The biting November wind whipped around Agnes Periwinkle, tugging at the edges of her sensible brown coat as she wrestled the heavy oak door of Eventide Manor closed behind her.  Dead leaves, the color of bruised plums, swirled across the expansive, gravelled drive.  Eventide, a gothic monstrosity of turrets and gargoyles perched precariously on the windswept cliffs overlooking the churning grey sea, was a grand house, undeniably.  But grand in a decaying, slightly sinister way, like a forgotten queen still clinging to the tattered remnants of her finery. Agnes, however, was unfazed. She’d been housekeeper at Eventide for fifteen years and had grown accustomed to its drafty hallways, its creaking floorboards, and the unsettling portraits of stern-faced ancestors that seemed to follow her with their eyes.  Today, however, something felt different.  A prickling unease settled in the pit of her stomach, a feeling she couldn't quite place, as she made her way through the cavernous entrance hall towards the kitchen.  The air was thick with the scent of beeswax and something else… something faintly metallic… that made her nostrils twitch.
