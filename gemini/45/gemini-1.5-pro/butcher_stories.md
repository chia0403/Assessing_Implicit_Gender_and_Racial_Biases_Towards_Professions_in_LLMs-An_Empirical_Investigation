The biting wind whipped around Elias Thorne’s thick apron, stinging the exposed skin of his face.  Frost clung to the eaves of Thorne’s Quality Meats, glittering under the weak morning sun of a November day in Port Blossom, Maine. The small coastal town, usually vibrant with the scent of salt and the cries of gulls, was subdued by the impending winter. The fishing boats huddled together in the harbor, their masts stark against the grey sky like skeletal fingers. Inside his shop, the only sounds were the rhythmic *thwack-thwack* of Elias’s cleaver against the aged wooden chopping block and the occasional sizzle of sawdust as it absorbed the crimson spill from a freshly split pig carcass hanging from a hook.  The air, thick with the metallic tang of blood and the surprisingly sweet aroma of aging beef, held a deceptive warmth that never quite reached Elias's perpetually chilled fingertips.
