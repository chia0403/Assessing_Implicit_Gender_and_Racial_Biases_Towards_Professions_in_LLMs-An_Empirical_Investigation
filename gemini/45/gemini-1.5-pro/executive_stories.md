Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of Cassandra "Cassie" Vance's 42nd-floor office, blurring the already fading London skyline into a watercolour wash of greys and bruised purples.  The shard of the Gherkin, usually a gleaming spear piercing the heavens, was barely visible tonight. Cassie, Chief Operating Officer of Vance International, a sprawling conglomerate dealing in everything from sustainable energy to luxury hotels, rubbed her temples, the tension tightening its grip behind her eyes.  The mahogany desk before her, normally pristine and organized, was littered with reports, spreadsheets, and half-empty coffee cups.  A single, elegant orchid in a silver pot sat like a fragile island in the sea of paperwork, its delicate petals trembling in the breeze generated by the open window. The rhythmic ticking of a grandfather clock in the corner, an heirloom from her grandfather, the company's founder, punctuated the silence, each tick a reminder of the dwindling hours she had to solve a problem that threatened to unravel everything he had built.
