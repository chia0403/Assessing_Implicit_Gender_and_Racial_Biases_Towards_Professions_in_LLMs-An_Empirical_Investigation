The biting wind whipped across the exposed cliff face, stinging Amelia “Millie” Reyes’s cheeks and tearing at the edges of her thick wool cap. Below, the churning grey expanse of the North Atlantic crashed against the jagged rocks of Skellig Michael, a jagged tooth of an island jutting out from the Irish coast.  Seven hundred precarious steps spiraled down from the ancient monastic settlement where Millie lived and worked, connecting her to the small, storm-battered harbor far below.  Up here, amidst the crumbling beehive huts and the cries of circling gannets, Millie was the sole caretaker, not just of the historic site itself, but of its most unique inhabitants – a small, fragile colony of puffins, clinging to existence on the very edge of the world.
