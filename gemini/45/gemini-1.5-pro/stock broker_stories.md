Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of Elias Thorne's forty-second-floor office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside him.  The city lights of Chicago, usually a glittering spectacle, were smeared into hazy orange streaks.  Elias, his tie loosened and his expensive suit rumpled from a day wrestling with a volatile market, stared out at the storm. Below, the Chicago River, usually a placid ribbon reflecting the sky, churned like a muddy serpent.  His office, a testament to his success as a senior broker at Blackwood & Sterling, was a stark contrast to the chaos outside.  Dark wood paneling, leather furniture, and a collection of framed financial awards lined the walls, trophies of a career built on calculated risk and unwavering ambition. But tonight, even the comforting hum of the state-of-the-art computer system couldn't drown out the nagging unease that gnawed at him.  Something was off.  The numbers weren't adding up, and the whispers he'd been hearing in the back rooms and hushed phone calls were starting to coalesce into a chilling premonition.
