The biting wind whipped across the desolate plains of Nevada, rattling the corrugated iron shack that served as both Silas Finch's home and workshop.  Inside, amidst a chaotic symphony of wires, vacuum tubes, and half-dismantled radios, Silas hunched over a workbench, the faint orange glow of a soldering iron illuminating his focused face.  Dust motes danced in the flickering light, clinging to the tangled beard that hid the lower half of his face.  Static crackled from a nearby speaker, the erratic bursts punctuating the silence of the vast, empty landscape. The year was 1957, and for Silas, the only connection to the world beyond the horizon was the fragile web of radio waves he painstakingly coaxed into existence.
