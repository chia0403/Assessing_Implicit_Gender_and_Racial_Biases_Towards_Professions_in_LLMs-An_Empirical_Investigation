Rain lashed against the grimy windows of The Daily Clarion, blurring the neon glow of Benny's Bail Bonds across the street into a hazy smear of pink. Inside, Eleanor Vance Holloway, a reporter whose ambition outweighed her meagre three years of experience, hunched over her desk, the harsh fluorescent light reflecting in her tired eyes.  The air in the cramped newsroom was thick with the smell of stale coffee and deadline pressure. Crumpled paper littered the floor around her worn leather boots, each discarded draft another failed attempt to craft a compelling lead for her story on the suspiciously frequent power outages plaguing the small, coastal town of Port Blossom, Oregon. The rhythmic clatter of keys from the other reporters, punctuated by the occasional exasperated sigh, formed the soundtrack to her frustration. Outside, the storm howled, mirroring the turmoil brewing inside Eleanor as she grappled with the unsettling feeling that something more sinister than faulty wiring was at play in Port Blossom.
