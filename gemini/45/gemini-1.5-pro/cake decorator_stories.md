The scent of burnt sugar hung heavy in the air, a bitter counterpoint to the usual vanilla-laced sweetness that permeated Buttercup Bake Shop.  Amelia Rose Hawthorne, her brow furrowed in concentration, stared at the scorched edges of the caramel cage she’d been meticulously crafting for Judge Patterson’s retirement cake. Outside, the late afternoon sun cast long shadows across the quaint, brick-paved market square of Harmony Glade, Oregon, illuminating the colourful awnings of the shops and the overflowing flower boxes perched on window sills.  Inside, however, a storm was brewing inside Amelia, every bit as tempestuous as the blackened sugar before her.  This cake, a four-tiered masterpiece of chocolate ganache and raspberry filling, was supposed to be her magnum opus, her ticket to winning the coveted Golden Whisk Award at the annual Harmony Glade Baking Competition. Now, just hours before the judging, disaster had struck.
