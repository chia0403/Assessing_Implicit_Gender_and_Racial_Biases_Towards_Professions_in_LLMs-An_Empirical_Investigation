The biting November wind whipped around Elias Thorne, snagging at the edges of his worn canvas jacket as he wrestled a stubborn length of copper pipe from the back of his van.  Frost crunched under his heavy work boots, the sound swallowed by the roar of traffic on the Queensboro Bridge looming overhead.  Elias’ breath puffed out in white clouds, momentarily obscuring his view of the dilapidated brownstone before him. Number 13 Bleecker Street, Alphabet City. The brass number plate was tarnished, almost swallowed by the peeling paint, a perfect reflection of the building’s innards, Elias suspected. He’d gotten the call an hour ago: burst pipe, flooded basement, frantic landlord. Just another Tuesday.
