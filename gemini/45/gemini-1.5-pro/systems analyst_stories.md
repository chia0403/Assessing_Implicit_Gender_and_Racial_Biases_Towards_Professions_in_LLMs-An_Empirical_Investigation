The flickering fluorescent lights of the server room hummed a monotonous B-flat, a counterpoint to the insistent whir of cooling fans.  Dust motes danced in the pale beams, illuminating the endless rows of blinking servers that formed the digital heart of Wainwright Industries.  It was 3:17 am on a Tuesday, and Elias Thorne, Senior Systems Analyst, was hunched over a console, his brow furrowed in concentration.  The air, usually chilled to a brisk 68 degrees, felt thick and suffocating with the weight of the problem at hand.  A critical system, responsible for routing all of Wainwright's North American logistics, had inexplicably gone offline an hour ago, and Elias, summoned from the merciful embrace of sleep, was now locked in a silent battle with the digital behemoth. Outside, the sprawling industrial complex on the outskirts of Chicago lay quiet under a blanket of early morning fog, oblivious to the frantic activity unfolding within its technological core.
