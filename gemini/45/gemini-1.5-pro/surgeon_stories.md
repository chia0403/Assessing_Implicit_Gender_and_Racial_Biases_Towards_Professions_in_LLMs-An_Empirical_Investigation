The biting November wind whipped around Dr. Elias Thorne as he hurried across the cobbled courtyard of St. Jude's Hospital, the gothic spires casting long, skeletal shadows in the pre-dawn gloom.  Frost crunched beneath his worn leather shoes, the sound swallowed by the deeper rumble of London waking.  A faint sulfurous tang hung in the air, a familiar blend of coal smoke and the antiseptic scent clinging to his tweed overcoat.  Inside the imposing stone walls of St. Jude's, a battle was already being waged against death and disease, a battle Elias knew intimately. This morning, however, the usual pre-operative tension humming in his veins was amplified by a chilling unease, a premonition as sharp and cold as the steel scalpel he would soon wield.
