The biting November wind whipped around Professor Alistair Finch, tugging at the edges of his tweed jacket as he hurried across the cobbled quadrangle of St. Jude’s College, Oxford.  Gargoyles leered down from the honey-colored stone walls, their expressions mirroring the grim set of Alistair's own face.  Rain, fine and persistent, slicked the ancient stones, reflecting the flickering gas lamps that dotted the walkways.  Alistair clutched a leather-bound volume of Chaucer to his chest as if it were a shield against the encroaching darkness and the unsettling feeling that something was profoundly wrong.  It wasn't just the weather, though the ceaseless drizzle mirrored his own internal gloom.  It was the letter he’d received that afternoon, a cryptic message postmarked from Venice, a city he hadn’t visited in over a decade. A city he associated with the happiest, and most tragic, period of his life.
