The biting November wind whipped around Eleanor Vance's ankles as she hurried across the cracked asphalt of the Northwood High parking lot.  Fluorescent lights buzzed from within the squat, brick building, casting a sickly yellow glow onto the already dreary landscape.  Dead leaves, the colour of rust and decay, skittered across the pavement, chasing after her fraying tweed coat. Northwood, nestled deep in the forgotten corner of rural Pennsylvania, wasn't exactly known for its charm.  Eleanor had been teaching English Literature there for fifteen years, and while the breathtaking foliage of autumn had once captivated her, now she mostly saw it as a harbinger of the long, bleak winter ahead, much like the relentless cycle of grading papers and battling student apathy that defined her existence.  This particular Monday morning, however, felt different. A strange tension hung in the air, thicker than the usual pre-assembly malaise, and Eleanor couldn't shake the feeling that something unsettling was about to happen.
