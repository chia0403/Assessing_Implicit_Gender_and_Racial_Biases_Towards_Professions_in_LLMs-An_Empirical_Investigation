The biting November wind whipped around Eleanor Vance as she wrestled a stubbornly oversized sample book of Italian silks out of her vintage Porsche 912.  Fallen leaves, the color of burnt umber and ochre, swirled around her ankles.  Greystone Manor loomed before her, a gothic behemoth of weathered stone and leaded glass, perched precariously on a windswept bluff overlooking the churning grey expanse of the Atlantic.  The salty air carried the faint cry of gulls and the deeper, more mournful moan of the foghorn from the lighthouse a mile down the coast.  Eleanor, her auburn hair escaping its chignon, shivered, not entirely from the cold.  This commission, redecorating the entirety of Greystone Manor for its reclusive new owner, was both her biggest opportunity and, she instinctively felt, her greatest challenge yet.  The heavy oak door, studded with iron, creaked open just a crack, revealing a sliver of shadowy hall and the pale, watchful face of a housekeeper.
