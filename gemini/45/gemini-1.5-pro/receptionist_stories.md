The insistent chirping of the ancient, wall-mounted intercom sliced through the quiet hum of the fluorescent lights.  Amelia Grace Hawthorne sighed, pushing a stray strand of auburn hair behind her ear.  Another Tuesday morning at the Grand Majestic Hotel, a faded relic of Art Deco glamour clinging precariously to its five-star rating on the edge of Brighton's crumbling seafront.  The lobby, once a glittering expanse of polished marble and velvet drapes, now boasted a slightly threadbare carpet and an assortment of potted ferns struggling under the weight of decades of neglect.  Outside, the grey November sky mirrored the churning, steel-coloured sea.  Amelia pressed the intercom button, her voice professionally bright.  "Reception, how may I assist you?"
