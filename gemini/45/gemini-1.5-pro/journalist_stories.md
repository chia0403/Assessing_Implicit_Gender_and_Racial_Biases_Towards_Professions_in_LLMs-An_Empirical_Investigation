Rain lashed against the grimy windows of The Daily Clarion's office, mirroring the tempest brewing in Amelia "Amy" Reyes' stomach.  It was almost midnight, the air thick with the smell of stale coffee and deadline panic.  Flickering fluorescent lights cast long shadows across the cluttered desks, illuminating crumpled drafts and half-eaten takeout containers.  Ashbury, Ohio, a town clinging to the rust belt like a stubborn weed, usually settled into a quiet hum by this hour. Tonight, however, a different kind of tension hung in the air, a static buzz that Amy could practically taste.  She tapped a chipped mug against her teeth, the only sound competing with the rhythmic clatter of her keyboard and the relentless drumming of the rain.  Her latest piece, an exposé on the suspiciously lucrative dealings of Ashbury's newly elected mayor, was due in less than an hour, and something felt… off.
