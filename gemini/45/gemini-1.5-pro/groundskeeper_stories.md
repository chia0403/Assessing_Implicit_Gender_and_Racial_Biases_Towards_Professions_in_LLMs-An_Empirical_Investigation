Athelney House, a sprawling Victorian mansion of dark brick and leaded glass, stood silhouetted against the lightening sky, its many windows like vacant eyes staring out at the meticulously ordered world Elias maintained.  The air, crisp and carrying the scent of freshly cut grass and the distant, brackish tang of the sea, held a peculiar stillness this morning, a stillness that prickled Elias's skin and made the hairs on the back of his neck stand on end.
