The biting wind whipped across the desolate expanse of the Klondike, stinging Rosalind "Roz" Murphy's cheeks even through her thick wool scarf.  Dawn hadn't quite cracked the horizon, painting the snow-covered peaks a bruised purple.  The air, crisp and clean, smelled of pine and diesel.  Roz, bundled in layers of Carhartt and fleece, exhaled a plume of white that vanished quickly into the frigid air. Her breath puffed out like a miniature steam engine, mirroring the behemoth she was about to wake: a Komatsu D575A-3 Super Dozer, affectionately nicknamed "The Beast."  It sat patiently in the frozen earth, a hulking metal giant against the vast, unforgiving landscape of the Yukon goldfields.  The job today was to clear a path through a particularly treacherous section of frozen creek bed, preparing for the next stage of the mining operation. It was dangerous work, demanding respect for both the machine and the elements. But for Roz, it was just another Tuesday.
