The fluorescent lights of the Quick Stop hummed a monotonous B flat, punctuated by the rhythmic beeping of the ancient cash register.  Rain lashed against the grimy windows, mirroring the streaks of dirt left by countless hurried hands.  Inside, the air hung thick with the cloying sweetness of stale coffee and the faint tang of desperation.  This was Elara Josephine Reyes' kingdom, a seven-by-seven foot plexiglass castle where she reigned supreme over expired lottery tickets, lukewarm hot dogs, and the occasional belligerent drunk.  Tonight, however, felt different. A chill, deeper than the November rain, snaked through the aisles, settling at the base of Elara's spine.  The bell above the door chimed, announcing a customer, but the silhouette that filled the doorway wasn't one she recognized.
