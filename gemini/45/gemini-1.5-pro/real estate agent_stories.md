The crisp October air, scented with woodsmoke and decaying leaves, nipped at Amelia Hayes' exposed cheeks.  She tugged her burgundy scarf tighter, the cashmere a comforting warmth against the chill.  Her heels clicked on the uneven flagstone path leading to the sprawling Victorian mansion, its gingerbread trim a stark contrast against the slate-grey sky.  This was Oakhaven Manor, perched on a hill overlooking the sleepy town of Hollow Creek, Connecticut. Amelia, a real estate agent renowned for selling the "unsellable,"  had been tasked with finding a buyer for this magnificent, yet undeniably eerie, property.  It had sat vacant for a decade, whispered rumours swirling around it like the autumn wind.  Today, she hoped to change that.
