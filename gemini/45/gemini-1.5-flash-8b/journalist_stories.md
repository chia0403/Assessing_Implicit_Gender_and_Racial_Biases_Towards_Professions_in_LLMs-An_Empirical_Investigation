Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of the "Golden Spoon," a greasy spoon diner perpetually shrouded in a haze of cigarette smoke and the lingering aroma of burnt coffee.  Amelia "Amy" Hawthorne, a freelance journalist with eyes the colour of stormy seas and a perpetually rumpled trench coat, hunched over a chipped Formica table, meticulously jotting notes in a worn leather-bound notebook.  Outside, the skeletal branches of the ancient oak trees that lined the dusty Main Street of Oakhaven clawed at the inky sky, mirroring the storm brewing inside her.  Oakhaven, nestled deep in the Appalachian foothills, was a town steeped in secrets, whispers carried on the wind and etched into the very stone of its forgotten past.  Its citizens, hardened by time and hardship, guarded their stories with the same ferocity as the mountain peaks that surrounded them.  Amy, however, was determined to unearth the truth, a truth she suspected was buried beneath the seemingly placid surface of this quaint, yet unsettling town.  Her assignment: to uncover the mystery behind the disappearance of the town's beloved, and eccentric, librarian, Mr. Silas Blackwood.
