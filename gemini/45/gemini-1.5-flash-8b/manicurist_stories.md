The scent of jasmine and something vaguely chemical, probably disinfectant, clung to the air in the tiny salon, a fragrant prison of chipped nail polish and whispered anxieties.  Sunlight, fractured by the frosted windowpane, painted irregular stripes across the polished surfaces of the manicure tables.  This was Amelia Nightingale's domain, a haven of sculpted tips and hushed conversations, a microcosm of beauty and quiet desperation.  Amelia, a manicurist with fingers like finely honed tools and eyes that held the secrets of countless hand-painted designs, meticulously filed a client's nails, the rhythmic click-click-click of the emery board a counterpoint to the faint hum of the city outside.
