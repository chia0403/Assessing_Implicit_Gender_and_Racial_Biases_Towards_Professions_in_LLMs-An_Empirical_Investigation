The chipped Formica countertop gleamed under the fluorescent hum of the office, reflecting Amelia Hawthorne's tired eyes.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of afternoon sun slicing through the grimy window of Room 307, illuminating a swirling vortex of papers, legal pads, and half-empty coffee cups.  Amelia, a secretary at the prestigious (and notoriously demanding) Hawthorne & Hartlaw Law Firm, sighed, the sound swallowed by the hushed clatter of keyboards and the rhythmic *tap-tap-tap* of her own fingers on the worn-out typewriter.  It was a Tuesday, a day like any other in the endless cycle of deadlines and whispered anxieties.  Outside, the city thrummed with a life she rarely experienced, confined as she was to the sterile, slightly musty atmosphere of the high-rise building.
