The humid air hung heavy, thick with the scent of jasmine and something faintly metallic, like old pennies left out in the rain.  Sunlight, fractured by the dense canopy of the mango trees, dappled the courtyard of Dr. Anya Sharma's clinic, "Nourish & Thrive," in a kaleidoscope of gold and emerald.  Anya Sharma, a nutritionist with a reputation for meticulously tailored meal plans and a bedside manner as sharp as her analysis, sat hunched over a stack of medical reports, the fluorescent hum of the clinic a constant low thrumming against the rhythmic chirping of unseen cicadas.  Her brow was furrowed, a fine network of lines etching themselves into her already thoughtful expression.  Today's case was particularly perplexing.
