The fluorescent lights of the "Little Explorers" daycare hummed, a monotonous soundtrack to the chaotic symphony of three-year-olds.  Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof, mimicking the rhythmic thump-thump-thump of Amelia "Amy"  Featherstone's heart.  Amy, a child care worker with a perpetually tired smile and hands perpetually stained with glitter glue, surveyed the room.  Toddlers were scattered like colourful pebbles across the playroom floor, some engrossed in a surprisingly detailed fort constructed from blankets and chairs, others engaged in a ferocious battle of plastic swords.  The air hung thick with the scent of crayons, spilled milk, and the faintest whiff of desperation. It was a Tuesday, and Amy was already on her third cup of lukewarm coffee.
