Professor Alistair Finch-Hastings, a man whose tweed jacket seemed perpetually slightly askew and whose spectacles perched precariously on the bridge of his nose, paced the dimly lit library.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of afternoon sun piercing the tall, arched windows, illuminating the spines of ancient tomes crammed onto every available shelf.  The air hung thick with the scent of aged paper and forgotten lore.  This was the hushed sanctuary of the Blackwood Institute of Arcane Studies, a place where whispers of forgotten magic still lingered, a place where Professor Finch-Hastings spent his days, chasing the shadows of the past.  Outside, the bustling city of Aethelgard throbbed with life, a stark contrast to the quiet, almost spectral, atmosphere within the library.  Aethelgard, a city built on the ruins of an ancient civilization, whispered of forgotten gods and powerful artifacts.
