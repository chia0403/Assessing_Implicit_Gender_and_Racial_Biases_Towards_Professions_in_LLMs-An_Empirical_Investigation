The fluorescent lights of the Hawthorne High guidance office hummed, a monotonous soundtrack to Amelia Nightingale's weary existence.  Empty coffee cups, piled precariously on the overflowing desk, mirrored the overflowing anxieties swirling in her head.  Outside, the crisp autumn air whispered through the bare branches of the ancient oaks that lined the school's perimeter, a stark contrast to the suffocating atmosphere within.  Hawthorne High, a school renowned for its academic rigor and the quiet desperation it fostered in its students, was a place Amelia had dedicated the last five years of her life to navigating. Today, another storm was brewing, and it smelled faintly of desperation.
