The biting wind whipped Amelia Blackwood's crimson scarf around her neck, stinging her cheeks as she surveyed the desolate expanse of the abandoned quarry.  Dust devils danced across the cracked earth, mimicking the frantic, restless energy that churned within her.  Autumn had arrived in the Finger Lakes region with a vengeance, painting the surrounding hillsides in hues of burnt orange and bruised purple, but the quarry, a gaping maw in the landscape, remained stubbornly grey, a monument to forgotten dreams and broken promises. Amelia, owner and operator of "GreenThumb Gardens,"  a small, but fiercely independent landscaping business, knelt, tracing the jagged edges of a shattered limestone slab with a calloused finger. This was the latest project, a restoration of sorts, a whisper of verdant life breathed into a place that had swallowed beauty whole.
