The fluorescent lights of the "Apex Solutions" reception hummed, a monotonous drone that echoed the quiet anticipation in Amelia Rosewater's chest.  The polished mahogany desk, a stark contrast to the chipped paint on the walls, reflected the muted October sunlight slanting through the dusty windowpanes.  Amelia, a wisp of a woman with hair the colour of a faded autumn leaf, meticulously rearranged the stack of incoming faxes.  Each crisp, white sheet felt like a tiny, silent judgment, waiting to be deciphered.  Apex Solutions, a company specialising in obscure biotech patents and equally obscure clientele, was a world built on hushed conversations and confidential papers.  Today, a single, thick envelope, addressed simply in elegant script to "Dr. Alistair Finch," lay on the pile, radiating a palpable aura of mystery.  Amelia, the silent guardian of this hushed world, was about to unlock its secrets.
