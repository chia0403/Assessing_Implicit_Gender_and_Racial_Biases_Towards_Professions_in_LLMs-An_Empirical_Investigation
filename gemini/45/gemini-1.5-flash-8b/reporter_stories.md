The biting wind of the Alaskan tundra whipped Amelia Blackwood's scarf around her face, stinging her cheeks.  She squinted through the swirling snow, the frosted landscape blurring into a monochrome canvas.  The small, weather-beaten cabin, nestled precariously on a windswept ridge, was the only point of reference in the swirling white.  Inside, the faint glow of a kerosene lamp promised a fragile warmth.  Amelia, a seasoned reporter for the Anchorage Daily News, felt a knot of unease tighten in her stomach.  This was it. The remote outpost of Glacier Gulch, where whispers of the "Frostbite Five" had been circulating for weeks, now a chilling reality.
