The biting wind howled a mournful dirge through the skeletal branches of the ancient oaks that lined the cobbled streets of Oakhaven, a town shrouded in perpetual twilight.  Rain, thick and grey, plastered the cobblestones with a slick sheen that mirrored the perpetually damp despair clinging to the town.  This was the jurisdiction of Judge Alistair Finchwick, a man whose steely gaze, usually reflecting the unwavering impartiality of the law, often seemed clouded by the same grey mist that perpetually settled over Oakhaven.  He shivered, not from the cold, but from the profound emptiness that settled within him whenever he entered the Court of Oakhaven.  Today's case promised to be no different.
