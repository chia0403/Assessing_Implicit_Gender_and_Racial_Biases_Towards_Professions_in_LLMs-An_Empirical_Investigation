Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of the precinct, a relentless drumming that mirrored the frantic rhythm of Detective Isabella "Izzy" Diaz's thoughts.  The flickering fluorescent lights cast long, skeletal shadows across the worn linoleum floor, highlighting the dust motes dancing in the air.  The air itself, thick with the smell of stale coffee and desperation, hung heavy in the small, cramped interrogation room.  It was a Tuesday night in the grimy, industrial heart of Alderwood, a town where the shadows seemed to swallow the city lights whole.  The once vibrant murals adorning the walls of the station, depicting local history and sporting heroes, were now faded and peeling, reflecting the city's own muted vibrancy.  Izzy, a seasoned detective with eyes the color of stormy seas and a spirit hardened by too many sleepless nights, stared at the quivering figure hunched in the chair opposite her.  This case, unlike the others, felt different.  This felt personal.
