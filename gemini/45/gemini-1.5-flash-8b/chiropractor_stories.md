The flickering fluorescent lights of Dr. Amelia Hayes’s office cast long, distorted shadows across the worn linoleum floor.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of sunlight piercing the grime-coated window, illuminating a chipped porcelain doll, its painted eyes staring blankly ahead.  Amelia, Dr. Amelia Hayes, her name etched in faded gold on the dusty door frame, sighed. Another Tuesday. Another patient complaining of a crick in their neck, a twinge in their back, a general feeling of being “out of sorts.”  The small, cluttered office, smelling faintly of antiseptic and old paper, felt more like a purgatory than a haven for healing.  Bookshelves, overflowing with spine-creaking tomes on anatomy and biomechanics, lined one wall, their spines a chaotic blend of medical jargon and esoteric drawings.  A half-eaten bowl of trail mix sat on her desk, a testament to the long hours she’d already put in.  Outside, the city hummed, a constant, insistent drone that echoed the rhythmic thrum of tension Amelia carried within her own body.
