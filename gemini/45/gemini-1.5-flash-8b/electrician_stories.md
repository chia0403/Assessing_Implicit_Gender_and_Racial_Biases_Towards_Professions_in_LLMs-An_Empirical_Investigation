The flickering fluorescent lights of the abandoned warehouse hummed a mournful tune, mirroring the anxious thrum in Elias Thorne's chest.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of afternoon sun piercing the grime-coated windows, illuminating the skeletal remains of machinery and shattered crates.  Elias, a seasoned electrician with calloused hands and eyes that held the ghost of countless late nights, adjusted his headlamp, the beam cutting through the oppressive stillness.  This wasn't your typical job site.  This was a forgotten corner of the city, a place whispers claimed held secrets older than the building itself.  He was here to fix the power, but the true source of the trouble might lie beyond the frayed wires and sparking transformers.
