The biting wind whipped through the crenellated walls of the Citadel, stinging Elara Blackwood's cheeks and rattling the iron bars of her watchtower.  Below, the sprawling city of Aethelgard, bathed in the sickly yellow glow of the dying sun, stretched out like a tapestry of shadowed roofs and flickering candlelight.  Towering spires, skeletal fingers reaching towards the bruised sky, punctuated the landscape, their intricate carvings barely visible in the gathering gloom.  Elara, clad in the worn, grey tunic of a Royal Guard, shivered, not just from the cold, but from a sense of foreboding that clung to her like the clinging mist.  Tonight, the usual murmur of the city was strangely muted, replaced by an unnerving silence, a silence that whispered of hidden things and lurking shadows.  The air itself seemed to press down, heavy with an unspoken dread, a suffocating weight that she felt in her bones.  She adjusted the heavy crossbow slung across her back, the familiar weight a comfort in the face of the unknown. Tonight, her vigilance was paramount.
