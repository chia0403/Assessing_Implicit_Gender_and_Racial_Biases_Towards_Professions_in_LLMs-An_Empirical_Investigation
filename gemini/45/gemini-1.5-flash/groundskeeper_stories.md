The first tendrils of dawn, pale and hesitant, barely touched the dew-kissed emerald of the Blackwood Manor lawns.  A chill wind, carrying the scent of damp earth and woodsmoke, whipped around Elias Thorne's worn tweed jacket as he surveyed his domain.  Elias, a man whose life was as meticulously ordered as the manicured hedges lining the long gravel drive, adjusted the worn leather gloves on his calloused hands.  Blackwood Manor, a gothic behemoth of grey stone perched precariously on the edge of a whispering wood, was his responsibility, his kingdom, and, lately, his prison.  The vast, unsettling grounds stretched before him, a tapestry of carefully cultivated beauty concealing, Elias suspected, secrets far darker than the deepest shadows cast by the ancient oaks.
