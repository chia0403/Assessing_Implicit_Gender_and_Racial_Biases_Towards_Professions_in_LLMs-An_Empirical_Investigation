The scent of lavender and ozone hung heavy in the air, a peculiar cocktail only found in the cramped, cluttered confines of "Shear Perfection," a hair salon nestled between a laundromat and a failing bakery on Bleecker Street.  Dust motes, illuminated by the harsh fluorescent lights, danced in the sluggish afternoon air.  Thirty-year-old Aurelia "Reli" Montenegro, her fingers stained a kaleidoscope of colours from years of handling dyes, expertly snipped a strand of unruly blonde hair, her brow furrowed in concentration.  The rhythmic *snip, snip, snip* of her shears was the only sound louder than the rhythmic thump-thump-thump of the washing machine next door.  Reli, with her own unruly cascade of raven hair usually tucked into a messy bun, was known as much for her precise cuts as for her uncanny ability to listen, to absorb the unspoken anxieties and triumphs that clung to each client like stray hairs. Today, however, felt different.  A strange, almost electric tension crackled in the air, heavy as the humidity clinging to the New York City summer.
