Professor Augustine Blackwood adjusted his spectacles, the faint scent of old paper and pipe tobacco clinging to his tweed jacket.  Rain lashed against the gothic windows of his study, each drop a tiny percussionist in the symphony of the storm raging outside.  The wind howled like a banshee, rattling the ancient oak frame of the window and momentarily extinguishing the flickering candlelight illuminating his cluttered desk.  Papers, overflowing with indecipherable equations and scrawled diagrams, were piled haphazardly amongst stacks of leather-bound books, their spines cracked and faded with age.  Outside, the grounds of Blackwood Manor, a sprawling estate shrouded in mist and looming shadows, were lost in the tempest's fury.  Tonight, however, the storm raging within Augustine Blackwood's soul threatened to eclipse even the fury of nature.  He had received a letter, a cryptic missive delivered by a breathless messenger only hours ago, a letter that spoke of forgotten prophecies and a shadowed power stirring in the very heart of the ancient Blackwood family.
