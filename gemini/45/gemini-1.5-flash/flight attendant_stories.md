The recycled air hung thick with the scent of stale coffee and anxiety, a familiar perfume to Seraphina “Sera” Anya Petrova.  Outside, the bruised purple of twilight bled into the inky black of a Pacific Ocean that stretched out below, a vast, uncaring mirror reflecting the storm brewing not just outside, but within Flight 747 from Honolulu to San Francisco. Sera, perched precariously on a jump seat tucked away in the galley, sipped lukewarm chamomile tea, its floral aroma doing little to soothe the knot of dread tightening in her stomach.  The hum of the engines vibrated through the metal floor, a constant, almost hypnotic thrum that did nothing to mask the low, almost imperceptible tremor running through the fuselage.  This wasn’t turbulence; this was something else entirely.  Something far more sinister, something that whispered in the shadows between the rows of sleeping passengers, a promise of chaos hidden beneath the veneer of a seemingly ordinary transpacific flight.
