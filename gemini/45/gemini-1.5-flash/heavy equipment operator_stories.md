The diesel engine coughed, a low rumble that vibrated through Elias Thorne's worn work boots and up into his chest.  Dust, the fine, ochre powder of the Arizona desert, swirled around the massive Caterpillar D11T as Elias wrestled the controls, coaxing the behemoth into a slow, deliberate turn. The sun, a malevolent eye in the brassy sky, beat down relentlessly on the already baking earth, turning the air shimmering and thick.  The only other sounds were the drone of the machine's hydraulics and the distant, lonely cry of a hawk circling high above the desolate expanse of the abandoned copper mine.  Elias, sweat plastering his grizzled brown hair to his forehead, adjusted his cracked leather baseball cap, the brim offering little respite from the relentless glare. He was halfway through clearing a particularly stubborn section of collapsed tailings, a task that would take him the rest of the day, and likely, the better part of tomorrow. The air hung heavy with the scent of hot metal and the lingering metallic tang of copper, a smell Elias knew as well as he knew his own name.
