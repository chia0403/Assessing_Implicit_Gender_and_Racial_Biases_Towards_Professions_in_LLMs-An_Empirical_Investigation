The air hung thick and sweet in the "Flour Power" bakery, a cloying blend of vanilla, buttercream, and the faint, almost imperceptible tang of almond extract that clung to everything.  Dust motes, illuminated by the warm glow of the hanging Edison bulbs, danced in the shafts of sunlight filtering through the lace-curtained windows.  Twenty-three-year-old  Eleanor Vance, her fingers stained a delicate shade of pastel pink from the rose buttercream she’d been meticulously piping onto a three-tiered wedding cake, sighed contentedly.  The quiet hum of the refrigerator, the rhythmic whir of the mixer in the back, and the gentle clinking of teaspoons – it was the soundtrack to her life, a life lived in a sugary symphony of flour and frosting.  But tonight, the usual peaceful rhythm was about to be shattered.  A frantic phone call, crackling with static and tinged with desperate urgency, had just ripped through the tranquility, announcing the arrival of a cake catastrophe of epic proportions.
