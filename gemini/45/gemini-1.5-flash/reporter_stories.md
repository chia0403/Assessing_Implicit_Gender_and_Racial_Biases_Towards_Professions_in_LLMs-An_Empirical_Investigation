The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Mia” Hernandez’s auburn hair across her face as she hunched deeper into her worn trench coat.  Rain, a relentless drizzle that had been falling since dawn, plastered her already damp bangs to her forehead.  The flickering neon sign of the "Last Call Lounge" cast a sickly yellow glow on the rain-slicked alley, highlighting the overflowing dumpster and the scattered debris – a testament to the late-night revelry that had spilled out onto the grime-stained bricks.  The air hung heavy with the scent of stale beer, damp concrete, and something else… something faintly metallic that sent a shiver down Mia's spine, even colder than the wind.  This wasn't the glamorous side of Chicago she usually reported on; this was the underbelly, the city's whispered secrets seeping from the cracks in the pavement. And tonight, she felt a growing certainty that one of those secrets was about to be revealed.
