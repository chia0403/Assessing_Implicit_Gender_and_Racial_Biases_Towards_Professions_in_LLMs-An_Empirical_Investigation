The polished mahogany desk gleamed under the harsh fluorescent lights, reflecting the stark lines of Amelia Katherine Holloway's face.  Amelia, Senior Vice President of Acquisitions at Kestrel Industries, stared out the panoramic window of her 37th-floor office, the cityscape of Chicago a blurry tapestry of steel and glass in the pre-dawn gloom.  Rain lashed against the windowpanes, mimicking the tempest brewing inside her.  The aroma of expensive, darkly roasted coffee, barely touched in the elegant bone china cup, did little to soothe the knot of anxiety tightening in her stomach.  This wasn't the usual pre-meeting jitters; this felt like the tremor before a seismic shift.  The deal, the biggest of her career, hung precariously in the balance, and the weight of its potential success – or catastrophic failure – pressed down on her with the force of the city's relentless rain.
