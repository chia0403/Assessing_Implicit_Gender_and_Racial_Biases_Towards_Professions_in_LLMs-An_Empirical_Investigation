The chipped paint on the windowsill felt rough beneath Ms. Eleanor Vance’s fingertips as she stared out at the relentless downpour.  October in Portland, Oregon, had decided to unleash its full, grey fury, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her office.  Rain lashed against the glass, mimicking the frantic rhythm of her tapping pen against the stack of student files before her.  The air in the Northwood High School guidance office hung thick with the scent of stale coffee and simmering anxieties – a familiar aroma that had become the soundtrack to Eleanor's life these past ten years.  The muted hum of the fluorescent lights and the occasional muffled shout from the hallway provided a stark counterpoint to the storm raging outside, a storm she felt increasingly mirroring the turmoil she saw reflected in the worried faces of her students every day.
