The chipped paint of the "Sunshine Smiles" daycare center peeled like sunburnt skin under the relentless August sun.  Cicadas buzzed a relentless chorus, a soundtrack to the sticky humidity that clung to everything, even twenty-seven-year-old Elara Vance's perpetually damp forehead.  Elara, whose name was often whispered with a mix of awe and exasperation by the other staff, knelt beside a miniature sandbox battlefield, mediating a dispute over a plastic dump truck between four-year-old twins, Leo and Liam O'Malley, whose identical scowls were only slightly less ferocious than their miniature construction equipment. The air hung thick with the smell of sunscreen, slightly burnt graham crackers, and the faint, underlying scent of something vaguely resembling despair.
