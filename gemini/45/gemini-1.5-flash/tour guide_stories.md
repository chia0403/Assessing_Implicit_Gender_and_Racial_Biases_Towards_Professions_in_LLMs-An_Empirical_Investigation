The humid air hung heavy, a thick, sweet blanket woven from the scent of frangipani and exhaust fumes.  Sweat beaded on Amelia Hernandez’s brow, despite the relative coolness of the early morning in Cartagena.  The cobblestones beneath her worn leather sandals were slick with dew, reflecting the nascent sunlight in a thousand tiny, shimmering diamonds.  Amelia adjusted the microphone clipped to her collar, its plastic feeling clammy against her skin.  She glanced at her watch, the luminous hands indicating 8:57 am – precisely two minutes until the "Enchanted City: A Walking Tour of Cartagena's Hidden Gems" was scheduled to begin.  The small group of expectant tourists, a motley crew of sun-kissed faces and wide-eyed wonder, were already gathered, their cameras poised, ready to capture the magic Amelia promised.
