The salt spray stung Dr. Eleanor Vance's face as she wrestled the stubborn canvas awning of her clinic into submission.  The wind, a relentless, howling banshee straight off the churning grey Atlantic, threatened to rip the whole thing from its moorings.  Seagulls, squawking like disgruntled gossips, wheeled overhead, their cries swallowed by the roar of the ocean.  Her clinic, "Coastal Chiropractic," perched precariously on the edge of the windswept cliff overlooking the churning waters of the Irish Sea in the tiny, isolated village of Portwenn, Cornwall.  The scent of brine and seaweed hung heavy in the air, a constant, bracing reminder of the wild beauty – and the unpredictable temper – of her surroundings.  Inside, the warmth of the small waiting room was a welcome contrast to the icy blast outside, the only sounds the gentle crackle of the fire in the hearth and the rhythmic tick-tock of a grandfather clock, its pendulum swinging with unwavering patience in the face of the storm’s fury.
