The fluorescent lights of Mildred McMillan’s cramped office hummed a monotonous tune, a counterpoint to the frantic tap-tap-tapping of her fingers on the keyboard.  Dust motes, illuminated like tiny suns, danced in the pale beams, swirling lazily around the overflowing filing cabinets that lined the walls.  The air hung thick with the scent of stale coffee, cheap printer ink, and the faint, underlying aroma of desperation – the collective scent of tax season.  Mildred McMillan, a woman whose neatly coiffed auburn bun seemed to defy the chaos surrounding her, squinted at the screen.  Another W-2, another 1099, another potential audit waiting to happen.  This year, however, felt different.  A creeping unease, sharper than the usual pre-April 15th anxieties, settled in her stomach.  It had started with the mysterious package, delivered anonymously that morning…
