The biting wind whipped Dr. Eleanor Vance's dark hair across her face as she wrestled the heavy oak door of Blackwood Manor open.  The hinges groaned a mournful protest, a sound swallowed almost immediately by the desolate howl that swept across the windswept moors surrounding the crumbling gothic structure.  Rain lashed against the stained-glass windows, casting fractured rainbows across the damp stone floor of the entrance hall.  The air hung heavy with the scent of damp earth and something else, something indefinably ancient and unsettling, like the lingering breath of forgotten sorrows.  Blackwood Manor, perched precariously on the edge of the cliff, seemed to lean into the storm, a brooding silhouette against the bruised purple sky.  Inside, Eleanor knew, lay a mystery far more chilling than the tempest raging outside.
