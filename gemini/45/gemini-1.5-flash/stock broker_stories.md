The rain hammered against the panoramic windows of Avery Finch's office, each drop a tiny, insistent drumbeat against the city's glittering, indifferent skyline.  Below, the streets of Manhattan throbbed with a chaotic energy that mirrored the frantic pulse in Avery's own chest.  He stared at the flickering numbers on his multiple monitors, the green and red a dizzying kaleidoscope reflecting the fluctuating fortunes of his clients and, more importantly, himself.  The air hung thick with the scent of expensive leather and stale coffee, a familiar aroma that clung to the plush, mahogany-lined office like a second skin.  Avery Finch, Senior Vice President at Stratton Oakmont Securities,  was about to make a decision that could either solidify his reputation as a shrewd operator or shatter it beyond repair.  The weight of millions – and perhaps his entire career – rested on his shoulders, as heavy as the relentless New York rain.
