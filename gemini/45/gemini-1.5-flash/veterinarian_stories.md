The salt spray stung Dr. Eleanor Vance's face as she wrestled the stubborn fishing net free from the barnacle-encrusted hull of the *Sea Serpent*.  The late afternoon sun, a bruised plum sinking below the choppy waters of the Irish Sea, cast long shadows across the weathered planks of the small harbour in Port Isaac.  The air, thick with the scent of brine, seaweed, and something faintly fishy that Eleanor couldn't quite place, carried the mournful cry of gulls overhead.  This wasn't exactly the typical day for a veterinary surgeon, but then again, nothing was typical in Port Isaac, a picturesque village clinging precariously to the Cornish cliffs, where the only things more unpredictable than the weather were its inhabitants and their assorted animals.
