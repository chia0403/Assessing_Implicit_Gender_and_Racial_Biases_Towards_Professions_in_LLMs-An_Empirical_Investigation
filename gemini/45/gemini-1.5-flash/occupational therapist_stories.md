The salt spray stung Dr. Eleanor Vance's face as she wrestled the stubborn fishing net from its tangled heap on the deck of the *Sea Serpent*.  The late afternoon sun, a bruised plum sinking towards the churning Atlantic, cast long shadows across the weathered wood.  The air, thick with the smell of brine and diesel, vibrated with the rhythmic chug of the small boat’s engine.  Eleanor, her usually pristine white coat now speckled with seawater and fish scales, squinted at the horizon.  The tiny island of Inishmaan, a smudge of emerald green against the darkening grey, was their destination – and the scene of her latest, most challenging assignment.  This wasn't a sterile hospital ward; this was a fight for rehabilitation fought against the raw, untamed beauty of the Irish coast.
