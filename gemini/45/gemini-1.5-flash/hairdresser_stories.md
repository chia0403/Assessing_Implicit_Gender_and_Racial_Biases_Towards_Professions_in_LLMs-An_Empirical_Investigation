The scent of lavender and burnt sugar hung heavy in the air, a perfume as familiar to Seraphina “Sera” Nightingale as her own reflection.  Rain lashed against the mismatched panes of glass that made up the front window of "Shear Perfection," her tiny salon nestled on the cobbled side street of Lavender Lane.  The streetlamps, struggling against the downpour, cast a flickering, orange glow on the damp bricks, illuminating a scene of picturesque decay – a charmingly crooked building next to a crumbling Victorian, all under the watchful gaze of a gnarled oak tree that seemed to lean conspiratorially over the rooftops. Inside, however, Sera's salon was a haven of warmth and controlled chaos.  The air crackled with the low hum of the hairdryer and the rhythmic snip-snip of scissors, a comforting counterpoint to the storm raging outside.  Tonight, though, the usual comforting rhythm was punctuated by a nervous flutter in Sera's chest.  A new client was arriving, and not just any client…
