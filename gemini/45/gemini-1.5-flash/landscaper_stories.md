The dawn mist clung to the manicured lawns of Willow Creek Estates like a reluctant ghost, clinging to the dew-kissed blades of emerald grass and whispering secrets only the rising sun could understand.  A faint, almost imperceptible chill still lingered in the air, a stark contrast to the burgeoning heat promised by the fiery orb peeking over the horizon.  Amelia “Mia” Hawthorne, her breath puffing out in small white clouds, adjusted the worn leather strap of her gardening gloves.  The dew-soaked grass tickled her ankles as she surveyed her domain – a sprawling, meticulously planned community of identical, yet somehow uniquely charming, homes, each a canvas waiting for her artistic touch.  Today's canvas was the sprawling estate at the end of Hemlock Lane, belonging to the notoriously reclusive Mr. Silas Blackwood, a man whose reputation was as thorny and impenetrable as the overgrown wisteria threatening to consume his neglected rose garden.
