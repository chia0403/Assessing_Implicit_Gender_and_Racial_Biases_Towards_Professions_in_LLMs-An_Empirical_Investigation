The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s hair across her face as she hurried down the cobbled street, the collar of her worn wool coat pulled high.  Rain, sleeting with a ferocity that belied the early hour, plastered her dark auburn curls to her scalp.  Ahead, the gas lamps of Nightingale Lane flickered weakly against the encroaching gloom, casting long, skeletal shadows that danced and writhed like phantoms.  Amelia, a registered nurse at St. Jude’s Hospital, usually found the quaint, if slightly dilapidated, charm of the lane comforting.  Tonight, however, the picturesque setting felt ominous, mirroring the churning anxiety in her stomach.  The emergency call had been cryptic, the voice on the other end breathless and strained, speaking of a "terrible accident" at Blackwood Manor, a place steeped in local legend and shrouded in an unsettling, almost palpable silence.
