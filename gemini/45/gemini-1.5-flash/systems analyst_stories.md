The air hung thick and humid, a clinging blanket woven from the scent of exhaust fumes and frying plantains.  Rain, the kind that hammered down in sheets rather than drizzled, lashed against the corrugated iron roof of the "Cyberia Cafe," its rhythmic drumming a counterpoint to the frantic clatter of keyboards inside.  Amelia Hernandez, a systems analyst whose life was usually measured in gigabytes and milliseconds, found herself staring out at the downpour, a half-finished cup of lukewarm café con leche forgotten on the sticky table before her.  The flickering neon sign outside, promising "High-Speed Internet – Guaranteed!" felt cruelly ironic, given the current state of her mission.  She was stuck in San Juan, Puerto Rico, far from the climate-controlled server rooms she usually called her office, wrestling with a problem that was far more tangled than any network she'd ever debugged.
