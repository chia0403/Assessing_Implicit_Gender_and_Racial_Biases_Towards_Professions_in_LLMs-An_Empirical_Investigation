The salt spray stung Dr. Eleanor Vance's face as she wrestled the stubborn sailboat’s mainsail.  The late afternoon sun, a bruised plum sinking towards the churning grey Atlantic, cast long shadows across the deck of the *Sea Serpent*, a vessel more charming than seaworthy, if Eleanor were honest.  The air, thick with the smell of brine and something faintly metallic, carried the mournful cry of gulls overhead.  She was anchored a mile off the craggy coast of Cornwall, the jagged silhouette of Land’s End a jagged tooth against the darkening sky.  Eleanor, a clinical psychologist specializing in trauma and grief, had sought this isolation, this raw, untamed beauty, not for respite, but for a confrontation.  The case files, meticulously organized in a waterproof satchel beside her, detailed the unraveling of a man named Arthur Blackwood, a renowned maritime artist who had lost his wife at sea six months prior and now seemed intent on joining her.
