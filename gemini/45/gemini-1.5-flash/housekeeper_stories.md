The chipped porcelain sink groaned under the weight of Elara Vance's scrubbing.  Water, the color of weak tea stained with the grit of a century's worth of grime, swirled around a tarnished silver candlestick, its once-elegant curves now dulled by neglect.  Elara, her knuckles bone-white against the rough bristles of the brush, sighed.  The air in the sprawling Victorian mansion hung heavy with the scent of dust motes and forgotten lilies, a perfume as familiar to her as her own breath.  Rain lashed against the tall, gothic windows of Blackwood Manor, a relentless drumming that mirrored the persistent ache in her shoulder, a legacy of years spent battling the relentless tide of dust and decay within its imposing walls.  Outside, the wind howled like a banshee, a fitting soundtrack to the secrets she suspected, and the ones she already knew, lay hidden within the shadowed corners of this magnificent, crumbling prison.
