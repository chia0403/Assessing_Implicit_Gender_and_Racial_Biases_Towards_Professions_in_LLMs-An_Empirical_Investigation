The scent of woodsmoke and damp wool hung heavy in the air, clinging to the cobblestones of Oakhaven like a persistent fog.  Rain, a fine, insistent drizzle, slicked the narrow alleyway behind the tailor's shop, mirroring the sheen of Elias Thorne's meticulously polished shears. Elias, a man whose life was measured in the precise stitch and the perfectly pressed seam, stood hunched over a half-finished coat, the flickering gaslight casting long, dancing shadows that seemed to mimic the restless energy humming beneath his usually calm exterior.  His shop, "Thorne's Threads," was a haven of quiet industry, a sanctuary of silks and satins, tucked away from the bustling marketplace that throbbed just beyond the worn oak door.  Tonight, however, the usual peaceful rhythm of his work was shattered by the frantic tapping of a gloved hand against the grimy pane.
