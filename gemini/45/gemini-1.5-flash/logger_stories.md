The biting wind howled a mournful tune through the skeletal branches of the ancient Douglas firs, whistling a song only Silas "Spike" Blackwood truly understood.  The air, thick with the scent of pine and damp earth, hung heavy and cold over the remote logging camp nestled deep within the Siskiyou Mountains of Oregon.  Snow, a fine, almost ethereal powder, dusted the ragged peaks in the distance, a stark contrast to the dark, brooding forest that swallowed the small cluster of cabins whole.  Spike, his beard frosted with ice crystals, gripped his steaming mug of coffee, the warmth a paltry defense against the relentless chill that seeped into his bones.  The fire in the cookhouse crackled merrily, but even its vibrant flames seemed dwarfed by the immensity of the surrounding wilderness, a wilderness Spike had known and loved, and feared, since he was a boy.
