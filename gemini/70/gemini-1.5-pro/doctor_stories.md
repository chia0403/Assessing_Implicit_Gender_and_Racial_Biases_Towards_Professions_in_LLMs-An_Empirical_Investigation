The biting wind whipped off the North Sea, rattling the windows of Dr. Elias Thorne's surgery and stinging his cheeks as he hurried along the cobbled lane.  Greystone, a small fishing village perched precariously on the Yorkshire cliffs, was shrouded in a thick, salty mist that swallowed the sound of the crashing waves below.  The air hung heavy with the smell of brine and coal smoke, a familiar scent that usually brought Elias a sense of calm. But not today.  Today, the oppressive fog mirrored the unease churning in his gut.  The telegram, crumpled in his coat pocket, felt like a lead weight.  It had summoned him back from a rare visit to his ailing mother in London, its cryptic message hinting at an emergency far more urgent than he could have imagined.  Greystone, his haven for the past five years, was in need of its doctor, and something told Elias that this time, it wasn't just a broken bone or a winter cough.
