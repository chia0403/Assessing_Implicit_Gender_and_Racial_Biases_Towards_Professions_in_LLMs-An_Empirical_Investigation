The biting wind whipped off Lake Michigan, rattling the "Now Open" sign hanging precariously from the freshly painted awning of Aligned Spine & Wellness.  Dr. Elias Thorne, his breath clouding in the frigid Chicago air, watched the sign sway with a mixture of hope and apprehension. It was a bleak January afternoon, the kind that gnawed at exposed skin and made the greystone buildings lining Clark Street appear even more imposing. Inside, the small waiting room, decorated in calming shades of blue and green, was empty save for a wilting peace lily and the rhythmic tick-tock of a vintage grandfather clock Elias had inherited from his grandmother.  This was it. His first day in his own practice, a dream years in the making, and the city, it seemed, was conspiring to freeze his ambition solid before it even had a chance to blossom.
