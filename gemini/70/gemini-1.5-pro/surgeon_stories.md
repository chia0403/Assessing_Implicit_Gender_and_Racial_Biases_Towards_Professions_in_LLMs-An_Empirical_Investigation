The biting wind whipped off the North Sea, rattling the windows of Dr. Elias Thorne's coastal cottage. Rain lashed against the panes, mimicking the frantic rhythm of his own heartbeat. Inside, the fire crackled merrily in the hearth, casting flickering shadows that danced across the worn Persian rug and up the bookshelves crammed with medical texts and nautical charts. Elias, still in his scrubs, though now covered by a thick, cable-knit sweater, stared out at the turbulent grey expanse.  The storm had cut off the small island of Ailsa Craig from the mainland for two days now, the ferry service suspended indefinitely. He was stranded, along with the island's two hundred and fifty inhabitants, and a particularly tricky surgical case.
