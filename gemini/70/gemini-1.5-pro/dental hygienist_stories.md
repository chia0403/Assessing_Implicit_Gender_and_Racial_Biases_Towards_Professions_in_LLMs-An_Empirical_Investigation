The biting November wind whipped around Amelia Grace Hawthorne's ankles as she hurried across the cobblestone street, the scent of woodsmoke and roasted chestnuts thick in the air. Her breath puffed out in white clouds, mirroring the plumes escaping the chimneys of the quaint, snow-dusted shops lining High Street in the village of Hollybrook. Amelia clutched her worn leather satchel tighter, its contents – a half-finished knitting project and a thermos of chamomile tea – clinking softly.  Hollybrook, nestled deep in the Vermont countryside, was picture-postcard perfect, all snow-covered pines and twinkling fairy lights strung across the storefronts. But beneath the idyllic façade, a chilling unease had settled over the village, an unease that even the festive cheer couldn't quite mask. And Amelia, despite her gentle nature and dedication to scraping plaque, found herself increasingly caught in its icy grip.
