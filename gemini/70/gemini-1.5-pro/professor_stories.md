The biting November wind whipped around Professor Alistair Finch, tugging at the edges of his tweed overcoat as he hurried across the cobbled quadrangle of St. Jude's College, Oxford. Gargoyles leered down from the medieval stonework, their frozen expressions mirroring the grim set of Alistair's jaw.  Frost crunched beneath his worn leather shoes, the sound sharp against the hushed reverence of the ancient university. The buttery lights of the library spilled onto the manicured lawn, offering a small island of warmth in the encroaching darkness.  Alistair was late.  Uncharacteristically late.  The weekly meeting of the Bibliophiles' Society, a gathering he cherished, was already underway, and tonight, of all nights, he was meant to be presenting his latest research on the cryptic symbolism within a newly discovered 15th-century alchemical manuscript.  A manuscript that, until a few days ago, he had believed to be nothing more than a clever forgery.
