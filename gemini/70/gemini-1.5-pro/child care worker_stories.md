The air in the Bumblebrook Daycare center vibrated with the chaotic energy of twenty toddlers released from their post-nap slumber.  Sunlight streamed through the large windows, illuminating a swirling kaleidoscope of finger paints, scattered blocks, and tiny, sticky handprints. Amelia "Amy" Rose Hawthorne sighed softly, tucking a stray strand of auburn hair behind her ear.  Her lavender cardigan, once pristine, now boasted a Jackson Pollock-esque splatter of apple juice and what she sincerely hoped was yogurt.  Bumblebrook, nestled in the heart of the sleepy, seaside town of Port Blossom, Maine, was usually a haven of giggles and gurgles. Today, however, felt different.  A subtle tension thrummed beneath the surface, a collective restlessness that prickled Amy's skin and made the hairs on her arms stand on end.
