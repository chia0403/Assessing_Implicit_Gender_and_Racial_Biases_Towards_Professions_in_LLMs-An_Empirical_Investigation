Rain lashed against the stained-glass peacock that perched above the entrance to The Gilded Lily, turning the vibrant blues and greens into shimmering, oily streaks. Inside, the air hung thick with the smell of stale beer, cheap whiskey, and the faint, lingering scent of Mrs. O’Malley’s lilac perfume – a permanent fixture since her wake three months prior.  Eliza Jane Holloway, her fiery red hair pulled back in a tight bun, polished glasses with a practiced hand, the rhythmic clinking a counterpoint to the mournful sax solo crackling from the ancient jukebox in the corner. The Lily, nestled in the heart of forgotten factory town, Ashworth, Pennsylvania, was rarely busy on a Tuesday night, especially one as wet and dreary as this. But Eliza knew, from years of experience, that the quietest nights often held the most interesting stories.
