The biting November wind whipped through the gaping maw of Benny's Auto Repair, swirling dust and dead leaves across the cracked concrete floor.  Grease stained every surface, from the calendar featuring a bikini-clad woman inexplicably holding a wrench to the chipped enamel sink in the corner.  Fluorescent lights hummed overhead, casting a sickly yellow glow that did little to dispel the pervasive chill.  Eleanor "Ellie" Vance, her hands calloused and blackened with grime, hunched over the open hood of a cherry-red '67 Mustang, the sweet scent of motor oil battling the acrid tang of burnt rubber.  Outside, the desolate landscape of the Nevada desert stretched as far as the eye could see, the jagged peaks of the Black Mountains silhouetted against a bruised twilight sky. The only sound louder than the wind was the mournful howl of a coyote somewhere in the distance. This was Ellie's kingdom, her sanctuary, her prison.
