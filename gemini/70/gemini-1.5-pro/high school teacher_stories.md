The biting November wind whipped around Amelia Hayes’ ankles as she hurried across the parking lot of Northwood High, the tattered hem of her wool coat flapping like a raven’s wing.  Fluorescent lights buzzed from the windows of the almost deserted school, casting long, geometric shadows across the asphalt.  Amelia clutched a stack of graded essays to her chest, their weight a familiar comfort against the gnawing anxiety that always seemed to accompany late nights at school.  Northwood, nestled in the heart of the sleepy, forested town of Hemlock Creek, Oregon, was more than just a place of employment for Amelia; it was her refuge, her battleground, and sometimes, her prison.  The towering pines that ringed the school grounds whispered secrets in the wind, secrets she sometimes felt she shared, secrets of unfulfilled dreams and a persistent longing for something more.  Tonight, however, the only secret the wind seemed to carry was the promise of snow, and with it, the inevitable chaos of a school day disrupted.
