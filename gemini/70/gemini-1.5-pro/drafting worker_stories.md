The flickering fluorescent lights of Darrow & Sons Architectural Design hummed a monotonous tune, a counterpoint to the frantic scratching of pencils on vellum.  Dust motes danced in the artificial glow, illuminating the cramped, windowless room known as "the bullpen."  Here, amidst a labyrinth of drafting tables laden with T-squares, triangles, and French curves, Elias Thorne meticulously rendered the cross-section of a multi-story parking garage. The air hung thick with the scent of graphite, stale coffee, and the faint, metallic tang of eraser shavings.  Outside, the Chicago wind howled a wintery lament, rattling the single, grimy pane of glass high above Elias's head, but down here in the bullpen, it was just another Monday.
