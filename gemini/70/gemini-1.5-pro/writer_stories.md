Rain lashed against the attic window, a relentless drumming that mirrored the frantic rhythm in Amelia Thorne's chest.  Loose plaster dust, disturbed by the vibrations, rained down onto the worn wooden desk where her typewriter sat, a slumbering beast. The only light in the cramped, eaves-dropped space came from a single bare bulb hanging precariously from a rafter, casting long, dancing shadows that flickered across stacks of manuscripts and overflowing bookshelves.  Outside, the wind howled through the skeletal branches of the ancient oak that guarded her grandmother’s crumbling Victorian manor on the windswept cliffs of Cornwall, England. Amelia, usually soothed by the wildness of the coast, found herself trapped in a cage of her own making, the words she desperately needed eluding her like smoke.  Her latest novel, a gothic mystery set within the very walls that surrounded her, was stalled, a victim of writer’s block as impenetrable as the fog rolling in off the sea.
