The scent of sandalwood and something vaguely metallic hung heavy in the air of "The Razor's Edge," a tiny barbershop tucked away on a cobbled street in the shadowed heart of Old Vizima.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of sunlight piercing the gloom, illuminating the intricate carvings on the antique wooden chair in which **Bartholomew "Barty" Blackwood**, a man whose hands seemed to possess a life of their own, meticulously trimmed a particularly unruly fringe.  Barty, with his sharp, almost hawk-like features, and a pair of eyes that had seen a thousand tales whispered in the hushed tones of the street outside, was a master of his craft, renowned throughout Vizima not just for his precision, but for his uncanny ability to read the silent narratives etched upon his clients' faces.  Today, however, a particular tension clung to the air, a palpable hum of unspoken anxieties that vibrated even through the rhythmic click-clack of his clippers.  Outside, the city stirred, a restless symphony of clattering carts, shouted barters, and the distant, mournful wail of a horn.
