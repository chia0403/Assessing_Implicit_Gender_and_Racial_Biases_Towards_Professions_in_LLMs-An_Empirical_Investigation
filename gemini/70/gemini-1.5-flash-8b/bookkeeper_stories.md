The chipped Formica countertop reflected Amelia Blackwood's weary face.  Dust motes, illuminated by the single fluorescent light above, danced in the stale air of "Accountants Anonymous," a cramped office tucked away on a side street in the perpetually overcast city of Greyhaven.  Rain hammered against the grimy windowpanes, a relentless percussion echoing the rhythmic click-clack of Amelia's worn-out keyboard.  Outside, the world seemed to be holding its breath, waiting for the storm to pass, just as Amelia was waiting for the day to end.

The year was 2042.  Greyhaven, once a vibrant metropolis, now felt like a mausoleum, its vibrant past buried beneath a blanket of economic hardship and technological stagnation.  The city's once-booming industries had crumbled, leaving behind a landscape of decaying factories and abandoned skyscrapers.  Only the relentless churn of the city's bureaucratic cogs, powered by countless bookkeepers like Amelia, kept the faintest flicker of civilization alive.

Amelia, a meticulous bookkeeper for the rapidly failing Greyhaven Transit Authority, was tasked with balancing the books for a system that seemed determined to go bankrupt.  Each ledger entry felt like a grim, symbolic dance with disaster. Every calculation, every entry, a tiny, desperate attempt to maintain order in a collapsing world.
