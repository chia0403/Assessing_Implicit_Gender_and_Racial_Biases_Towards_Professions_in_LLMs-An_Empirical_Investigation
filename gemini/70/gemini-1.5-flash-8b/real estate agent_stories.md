The midday sun beat down on the cracked asphalt of Elm Street, shimmering like a mirage across the peeling paint of the houses lining the narrow, tree-lined road.  Dust motes danced in the hazy air, carrying the faint scent of honeysuckle and something else, something acrid and metallic, like old blood.  Amelia "Amy" Cartwright, her tailored linen suit straining against the oppressive heat, adjusted her sunglasses and smoothed the already-wrinkled fabric of her floral scarf.  The humid air hung heavy, a tangible weight pressing down on her as she surveyed the dilapidated Victorian house, Number 27 Elm, with a mixture of apprehension and morbid curiosity.  The property was a fixer-upper of legendary proportions; a whispered reputation among local realtors preceded it – a reputation she was all too familiar with now.  It hadn’t sold in over a decade.
