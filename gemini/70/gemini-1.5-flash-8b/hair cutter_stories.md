The chipped paint on the door of "Scissors & Souls" whispered tales of forgotten afternoons and whispered secrets.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of sunlight piercing the gloom of the tiny salon, illuminating a swirling vortex of crimson highlights and the almost-too-perfect symmetry of a freshly cut pixie.  This was Amelia "Ash" Ashworth's domain, a haven she'd carved from a forgotten corner of the city, a sanctuary where the rhythmic snip-snip of shears was the soundtrack to lives transformed, albeit sometimes temporarily.  Ash, a woman whose fiery auburn hair always seemed to catch the light, leaned over the plush velvet armchair, her brow furrowed in concentration as she delicately trimmed a strand of raven hair that fell across the client's face.
