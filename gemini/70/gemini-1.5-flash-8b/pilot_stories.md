The biting wind whipped at Amelia "Amy" Blackwood's flight jacket, stinging her cheeks like a thousand tiny needles.  Below, the jagged peaks of the Himalayas clawed at the bruised twilight sky.  She gripped the controls of the single-engine Cessna, the rhythmic thump of the engine a lonely counterpoint to the howling blizzard.  The world outside was a swirling grey chaos, a landscape sculpted by time and fury.  Amy, her face etched with the familiar lines of sleepless nights and near-misses, felt a familiar knot of apprehension tighten in her stomach.  She was alone, two thousand feet above the world,  marooned in a blizzard that seemed determined to swallow her whole.  Her mission:  to deliver a vital package to a remote research outpost, a task that had already taken a toll on her already fragile resolve.   The outpost, nestled high in the valley, was only a pinprick of light against the overwhelming darkness.
