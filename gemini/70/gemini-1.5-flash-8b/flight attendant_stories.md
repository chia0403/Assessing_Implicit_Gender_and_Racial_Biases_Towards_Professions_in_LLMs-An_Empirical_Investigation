The humid air hung thick and heavy, a suffocating blanket draped over the sprawling tarmac at Kuala Lumpur International Airport.  Rain lashed against the corrugated metal roofing of the terminal, mimicking the relentless pounding in Elara Nightingale's chest.  Her name tag, clipped to her crisp uniform, proclaimed "Flight Attendant,  Malaysia Airlines."  But right now, the polished professionalism felt like a mask, as fragile and easily shattered as the fragile glass of the window overlooking the chaos.  Outside, a Boeing 787-8 Dreamliner,  registered 9M-XXX, sat gleaming, awaiting its departure, an emblem of a journey that felt far removed from Elara's current turmoil.  A single, insistent drop of rain echoed the pounding headache that throbbed behind her eyes.  The smell of exotic spices, usually a comforting presence in the airport, felt almost acrid now.
