The aroma of freshly baked sourdough, sharp and yeasty, clung to the air of the cobbled alleyway, mingling with the damp chill of a London winter morning.  Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of Amelia Thistlewick's tiny bakery, "The Doughnut Delight," a name that, frankly, did little justice to the exquisite loaves and pastries that emerged from its cramped confines.  Amelia, a woman whose fingers, stained a perpetual honey-brown from flour and sugar, moved with the precision of a seasoned conductor, was already in her element.  She expertly kneaded a ball of dough, her brow furrowed in concentration, her mind already conjuring the perfect, golden crust for the day's batch of pain au chocolat.
