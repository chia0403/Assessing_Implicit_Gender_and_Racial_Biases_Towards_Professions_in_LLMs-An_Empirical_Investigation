The chipped paint of Dr. Eleanor Vance's waiting room seemed to sigh with the weight of unspoken anxieties.  Dust motes, illuminated by the weak afternoon sun slanting through the grimy windowpanes, danced in the quiet air, a miniature ballet of unresolved issues.  Eleanor, her name etched in elegant script on the frosted glass door, sat at her mahogany desk, the polished surface reflecting the worried frown etched onto her own face.  The scent of old books and slightly stale coffee hung heavy in the air, a fitting aroma for the place where lives were laid bare, examined, and hopefully, pieced back together.  Rain lashed against the windows of her small, but comfortable, office nestled above a bakery in the historic, but slightly run-down, district of Willow Creek.  The rhythmic drumming was a counterpoint to the frantic tapping of her pen against the notepad, a rhythm reflecting the turmoil brewing inside her newest client, whose arrival was imminent.
