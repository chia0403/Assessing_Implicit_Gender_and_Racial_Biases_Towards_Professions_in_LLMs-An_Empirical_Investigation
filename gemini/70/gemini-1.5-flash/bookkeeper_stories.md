The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Beatrice Hawthorne’s fingers, the lukewarm tea doing little to soothe the icy dread that had settled in her stomach.  Rain lashed against the single, grime-streaked window of her office, blurring the already indistinct gaslight glow of the cobbled street below.  Amelia, a bookkeeper of meticulous habit and even more meticulous accounting, usually found solace in the precise order of her ledger books – the neat columns of debits and credits a comforting counterpoint to the chaotic world outside.  But tonight, the numbers swam before her eyes, a dizzying vortex reflecting the turmoil in her soul.  Her tiny office, perched precariously above a bustling fishmonger's on the rain-slicked streets of Grimsborough, felt less like a sanctuary and more like a cage.  The scent of brine and decay, usually a familiar background hum, now seemed to press in on her, a suffocating reminder of the unpleasant truth she'd discovered nestled amongst the meticulously recorded transactions of Mr. Silas Blackwood's shipping company.
