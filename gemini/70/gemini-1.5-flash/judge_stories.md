The air hung thick and heavy with the scent of salt and decay, a miasma clinging to the weathered clapboards of the courthouse like a shroud.  Seagulls cried incessantly overhead, their mournful cries a counterpoint to the rhythmic crash of waves against the jagged rocks that ringed the tiny island of Aisling.  Inside, the air was only marginally better; a blend of aged parchment, damp stone, and the faintly metallic tang of fear.  Judge  Eleanor Vance, her dark hair pulled back in a severe bun that betrayed neither her age nor her weariness, sat behind the imposing oak bench, her gaze fixed on the defendant.  The single flickering gas lamp above cast long, dancing shadows that seemed to writhe with the unspoken anxieties filling the cramped courtroom.  This wasn't the grand, marble hall of a metropolitan court; this was the Isle of Aisling’s only courtroom, a place where secrets, like the island’s treacherous currents, could easily pull you under.
