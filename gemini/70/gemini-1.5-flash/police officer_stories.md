The salt spray stung Detective Inspector Ava Sharma’s face as the November wind howled a mournful dirge across the jagged cliffs of Cornwall.  Rain, the colour of bruised plums, lashed against the windscreen of her battered Land Rover Defender, blurring the already treacherous coastal road into a swirling grey canvas.  Below, the Atlantic churned, a restless beast clawing at the granite rocks with a fury that mirrored the turmoil in Ava’s own gut.  The body, discovered half-submerged amongst the seaweed and barnacles clinging to the Devil's Tooth rock, hadn't been claimed by the sea entirely; it was only a matter of time.  And time, Ava knew, was running out.
