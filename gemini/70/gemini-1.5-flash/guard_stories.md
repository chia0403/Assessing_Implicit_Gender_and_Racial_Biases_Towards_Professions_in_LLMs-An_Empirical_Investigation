The biting wind howled a mournful dirge across the skeletal remains of the Obsidian Cliffs, whipping snow into frenzied dervishes that stung the exposed skin of Sergeant Valerius Thorne.  He pulled his thick fur-lined cloak tighter, the rough wool scratching against his chainmail beneath.  The air, thin and icy at this altitude, tasted of frost and despair.  Far below, the sprawling city of Porthaven glittered like a fallen star, its myriad lights a deceptive promise of warmth and safety, a stark contrast to the desolate, windswept plateau where Valerius stood guard.  His post, a crumbling stone turret overlooking the treacherous Devil's Pass, was as lonely as it was perilous; a stark reminder that the peace within Porthaven's walls was a fragile thing, constantly threatened by the shadowed wilderness beyond.
