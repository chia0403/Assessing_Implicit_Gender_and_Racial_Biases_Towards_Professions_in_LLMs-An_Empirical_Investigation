The chipped porcelain sink groaned under the weight of Elara Vance’s elbows as she scrubbed at the stubborn crust of burnt sugar clinging to the bottom of Lord Ashworth’s favorite casserole dish.  Outside, the November wind howled a mournful tune through the ancient oaks that guarded Blackwood Manor, their skeletal branches clawing at the gothic stone façade.  Inside, the air hung thick with the scent of beeswax polish and something else, something subtly metallic and faintly…off. Elara, though accustomed to the oddities of Blackwood, felt a prickle of unease crawl up her spine.  It wasn't just the lingering chill that seeped from the shadowed corners of the house, a chill that no amount of crackling fires in the vast drawing-room could dispel.  It was something more insidious, a dissonance in the usual quiet rhythm of the manor, a silence punctuated only by the frantic scratching of something unseen behind the tapestry in the west wing.
