The biting November wind whipped Dr. Eleanor Vance’s auburn hair across her face as she hurried down Bleecker Street, the scent of roasting chestnuts and damp brick battling for dominance in the crisp air.  Greenwich Village, usually a charming tapestry of bohemian grit and upscale boutiques, was cloaked in a moody, pre-storm gloom that mirrored the turmoil churning in Eleanor’s stomach.  Rain threatened, promising to transform the already slick cobblestones into treacherous skating rinks, a fitting analogy for the precarious balance of her latest client's health.  Ahead, the flickering neon sign of "The Gilded Carrot," a health-food restaurant that ironically specialized in decadent vegan fare, beckoned her. It was here, amidst the kale smoothies and cashew cheese, that Eleanor would attempt to navigate the complex dietary needs – and equally complex personality – of her newest challenge:  a celebrated, yet notoriously difficult, Broadway actress named Seraphina Dubois.
