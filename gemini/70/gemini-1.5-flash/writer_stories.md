The chipped mug warmed Amelia Blackwood’s hands, the lukewarm chamomile doing little to soothe the icy dread that had settled in her stomach.  Rain lashed against the single-pane window of her writing shed, a relentless drumming that mirrored the frantic beat of her heart.  The shed itself, perched precariously on the edge of a windswept Cornish cliff, offered a breathtaking view of the churning Atlantic – a view Amelia rarely appreciated these days.  The wild beauty, once her muse, now felt like a menacing presence, mirroring the turbulent landscape of her own life, a life she was struggling to capture in the pages of her unfinished manuscript.  The air hung thick with the scent of brine and damp earth, a pungent aroma that did nothing to mask the metallic tang of fear clinging to the air.
