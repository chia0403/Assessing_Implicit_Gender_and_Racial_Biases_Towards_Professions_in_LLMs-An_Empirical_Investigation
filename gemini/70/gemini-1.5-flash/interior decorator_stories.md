The chipped paint of the front door, a faded cerulean barely clinging to the warped wood, was the first clue.  Then came the overgrown wisteria, its purple tendrils snaking across the porch like grasping fingers, threatening to swallow the entire Victorian structure whole.  Isabelle "Izzy" Moreau, renowned interior decorator and champion of forgotten beauty, sighed, a delicate plume of breath misting in the crisp autumn air.  This wasn't just a renovation; this was an archeological dig disguised as a design project.  The sprawling, moss-covered manor house, perched precariously on the cliff overlooking the churning grey sea of the Cornish coast, seemed to whisper tales of faded grandeur and long-forgotten secrets. The salt-laced wind whipped around her, carrying the scent of brine and decay, a perfume as intriguing as the challenge that lay before her.
