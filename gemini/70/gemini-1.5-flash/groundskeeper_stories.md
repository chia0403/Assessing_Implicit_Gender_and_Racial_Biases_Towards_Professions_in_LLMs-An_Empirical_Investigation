The biting November wind whipped across the manicured lawns of Blackwood Manor, sending a flurry of fallen leaves skittering across the meticulously raked gravel paths.  Elias Thorne, his breath clouding in the frigid air, adjusted the worn leather straps of his gardening gloves.  The skeletal branches of ancient oaks clawed at the bruised twilight sky, their silhouettes casting long, skeletal fingers across the grounds. Blackwood Manor itself, a gothic behemoth of grey stone, loomed in the distance, its countless windows like vacant eyes staring out into the encroaching darkness. Elias, groundskeeper to the eccentric and reclusive Lord Ashworth for the past fifteen years, shivered, but not entirely from the cold.  A feeling deeper than the chill of the autumn evening settled in his bones, a premonition as unsettling as the rustling whispers in the overgrown hedges.
