The fluorescent lights of the Blackwood Academy gymnasium hummed a monotonous tune, a counterpoint to the squeak of Elias Thorne's worn sneakers on the polished floor.  Dust motes, illuminated like tiny stars in the cavernous space, danced in the weak currents of air stirred by his slow, methodical movements.  Elias, a man whose life was measured not in triumphs but in the meticulously swept corners and sparkling chrome of the hallowed halls of Blackwood, ran a damp cloth over the bleachers, the scent of lemon cleaner a faint, almost imperceptible fragrance against the lingering aroma of sweat, stale popcorn, and the faint, almost imperceptible scent of fear that clung to the very wood itself.  Blackwood Academy wasn't just a school; it was a gothic monument to privilege, perched precariously on a windswept cliff overlooking the churning grey sea, a place where secrets, like the dust Elias collected, settled deep into the cracks and crevices of its ancient stone.
