The fluorescent lights of the server room hummed a monotonous tune, a counterpoint to the frantic clicking of Amelia Katherine Stone's keyboard.  Amelia, a systems analyst for the notoriously secretive biotech firm, BioGenesis, felt the familiar prickle of adrenaline crawling up her spine.  The air, thick with the scent of ozone and stale coffee, vibrated with the low thrum of thousands of hard drives spinning in unison, a mechanical heartbeat echoing the frantic rhythm of her own pulse. Outside, the Seattle rain lashed against the panoramic windows, blurring the already indistinct city lights into shimmering streaks of grey and black.  Tonight, however, the storm raging outside paled in comparison to the digital tempest brewing within the bowels of BioGenesis.  Amelia’s eyes, magnified by the glare of the monitors, were fixed on a cascade of error messages, each one a tiny, malevolent flag in a sea of code gone rogue.  This wasn’t a simple glitch; this was a deliberate attack, and Amelia was the only one who could stop it.
