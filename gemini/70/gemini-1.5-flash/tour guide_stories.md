The humid air hung heavy, thick with the scent of frangipani and something vaguely unsettling –  a metallic tang that clung to the back of Amelia Hernandez’s throat.  Amelia, a woman whose smile lines spoke of countless sunrises witnessed from breathtaking vantage points, adjusted the brim of her wide-brimmed hat, shielding her eyes from the relentless glare of the Guatemalan sun.  She surveyed the assembled group – a disparate collection of tourists, their faces a mixture of anticipation and apprehension –  standing before the crumbling facade of the ancient Mayan city of Tikal.  Giant kapok trees, their trunks thicker than any car, clawed at the sky, casting long, dancing shadows that seemed to whisper secrets only the jungle understood.  The air itself hummed with a low, almost imperceptible thrum, a sound Amelia had come to recognize as the heartbeat of the rainforest. Today, however, it felt different, faster, more insistent. This wasn't just the usual thrill of exploring a lost civilization; this felt… charged.
