The biting November wind whipped Amelia Katherine Holloway's auburn hair across her face as she surveyed the skeletal frame of the Zenith Tower.  Rain, a relentless drizzle that had been falling since dawn, plastered her charcoal-grey coat to her skin.  Below, the city of Prague hummed with a damp, muted energy; the Charles Bridge, usually a vibrant tapestry of tourists and locals, was sparsely populated, each figure swallowed by the swirling grey mist that clung to the Vltava River.  The Zenith, a futuristic marvel of glass and steel rising defiantly against the gothic spires of the Old Town, was Amelia's masterpiece, her most ambitious project yet. But tonight, under this unforgiving sky, it felt less like a triumph and more like a monument to her mounting anxieties.
