The chipped ceramic mug warmed Amelia Josephine Finch's hands, the lukewarm tea doing little to dispel the chill that clung to the cavernous office like morning mist.  Rain lashed against the tall, gothic windows of Blackwood & Sons, a relentless drumming that echoed the frantic thrum of Amelia's own anxieties.  She sat hunched over her ancient Underwood typewriter, the keys clicking a counterpoint to the storm outside, its rhythmic tap-tap-tap a stark contrast to the turbulent storm brewing within the imposing, oak-paneled walls of the firm.  The air hung heavy with the scent of old paper, leather-bound books, and the faint, metallic tang of fear.  This wasn't just any fear; it was the specific, suffocating dread that accompanied a looming deadline, a ruthless CEO, and a secret she couldn't possibly reveal.
