The salt spray stung Eleanor Vance’s face as she wrestled the stubborn fishing net free from a jagged rock.  Seagulls cried overhead, their cries a mournful counterpoint to the rhythmic crash of waves against the rugged Cornish coastline.  The wind, a relentless, icy hand, whipped her already wind-chapped cheeks a deeper crimson.  Eleanor, a registered occupational therapist with a penchant for adventure (and perhaps a slightly reckless disregard for personal safety), wasn’t here for a holiday.  Her battered Land Rover, affectionately nicknamed “The Kraken,” sat perched precariously on the clifftop overlooking the churning grey sea, a testament to her determination to reach this remote, windswept cove.  This wasn't a typical Tuesday for an OT; today, Eleanor was on the hunt for a particularly elusive patient.
