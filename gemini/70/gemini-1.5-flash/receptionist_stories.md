The fluorescent lights of "Sterling & Steele, Attorneys at Law" hummed a monotonous tune, a soundtrack to Amelia Katherine Hayes's otherwise silent existence.  Amelia, or Amy as she preferred, sat perched on her ergonomically designed chair – a cruel joke, given the sheer discomfort of eight hours spent fielding calls from irate clients and aggressively cheerful insurance adjusters – behind a desk cluttered with half-empty coffee cups and legal pads overflowing with scribbled notes.  The air hung thick with the scent of stale coffee and expensive leather, a potent aroma that spoke volumes about the firm's image of success and Amy's own quiet desperation to escape it.  Outside, the relentless Chicago wind howled a counterpoint to the sterile calm of the reception area, rattling the tall windows overlooking the bustling city streets.  Today, however, the usual urban symphony felt amplified, a premonition, perhaps, of the day's chaos yet to unfold.
