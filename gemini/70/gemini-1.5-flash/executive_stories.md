The mahogany desk, polished to a mirror sheen, reflected Amelia “Mia” Davenport’s grim expression.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of her 57th-floor office, blurring the already hazy cityscape of Hong Kong.  Below, the neon lights of Kowloon flickered like dying fireflies, a stark contrast to the sterile, almost clinical perfection of her workspace.  Mia, Executive Vice President of Global Operations for Kestrel Industries, hadn't slept properly in three days.  The looming deadline for the Jakarta project, coupled with the sudden and inexplicable disappearance of her lead engineer, had settled upon her like a shroud of suffocating anxiety. The scent of expensive jasmine tea, meant to soothe, did little to quell the storm brewing inside her.  Tonight, however, felt different.  Tonight, the storm threatened to break.
