The salt spray stung Dr. Eleanor Vance’s face as she wrestled the stubborn fishing boat's helm.  The grey, churning Atlantic gnawed at the hull, a relentless beast mirroring the turmoil in her own heart.  It was a November evening, the air thick with the smell of brine and the mournful cry of gulls, and the tiny island of Inishmore loomed ahead, a jagged silhouette against the bruised purple sky.  The only light visible on the island emanated from a single, flickering window high on the cliffs, a beacon of both hope and apprehension for Eleanor.  She'd been summoned, urgently, by a cryptic telegram – a patient, the message had simply stated,  a matter of life and death.  The sea, usually a source of solace, felt tonight like a cruel mistress, testing her resolve as much as her seamanship.
