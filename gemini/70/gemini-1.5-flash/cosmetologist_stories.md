The scent of burning sugar and burnt caramel hung heavy in the air, a stark contrast to the usually delicate aroma of lavender and rose that permeated Seraphina "Sera" Bellweather's salon.  Smoke, thick and acrid, snaked from beneath the door of "Shear Perfection," her little haven nestled on the cobbled side street of Oakhaven, a town where time seemed to move at the pace of a snail leisurely munching on a dandelion.  Oakhaven, with its ivy-covered buildings and gas lamps that flickered romantically in the twilight, was usually a picture of quaint charm. Tonight, however, it felt more like the opening scene of a gothic novel, and Sera, usually a picture of calm amidst the chaos of hairspray and hot rollers, found herself gripping a fire extinguisher, her heart hammering a frantic tattoo against her ribs.
