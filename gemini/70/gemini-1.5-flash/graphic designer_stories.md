The chipped mug warmed Amelia Hernandez’s fingers, the lukewarm chamomile doing little to soothe the knot of anxiety twisting in her stomach.  Rain lashed against the panoramic window of her Brooklyn loft, mirroring the storm brewing inside her.  Grey light, fractured by the downpour, painted the room in muted tones – a stark contrast to the vibrant explosion of colour usually found on her design boards.  Empty coffee cups and discarded sketches littered the vast, open-plan space, testament to the all-nighter she'd just pulled.  Amelia, a graphic designer whose portfolio boasted sleek corporate branding and whimsical children's book illustrations, currently stared blankly at her computer screen, a pristine white canvas mocking her creative paralysis. The deadline for the Peterson & Sons rebranding loomed, a behemoth of a project that threatened to swallow her whole.  The air hung heavy with the scent of stale coffee and the faint, lingering aroma of lavender from the aromatherapy diffuser she'd forgotten to switch off.
