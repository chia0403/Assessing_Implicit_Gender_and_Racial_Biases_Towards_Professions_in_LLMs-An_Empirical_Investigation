The scent of lavender and burnt sugar hung heavy in the air, a peculiar perfume only Amelia “Mia”  Fitzwilliam’s salon, “The Snip & Sip,” could conjure.  Rain lashed against the tall, narrow windows of the Victorian shopfront, blurring the gas lamps into hazy halos on the cobbled street outside.  Inside, however, the atmosphere was a comforting cocoon of warm light and gentle chatter.  The low hum of the hair dryers was a familiar lullaby, punctuated by the rhythmic snip-snip-snip of Mia’s shears, a sound she’d known since she was a child helping her grandmother in their tiny village salon.  Tonight, though, the usual comforting rhythm felt discordant, a counterpoint to the frantic drumming of rain and the nervous energy that crackled around her latest client.
