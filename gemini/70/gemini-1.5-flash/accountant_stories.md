The fluorescent hum of the office was a dull thrumming counterpoint to the frantic tap-tap-tap of Elara Vance's fingers on her keyboard.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of the 47th floor, blurring the already hazy cityscape of Chicago into a charcoal smudge.  Elara, a meticulous accountant with a penchant for perfectly aligned spreadsheets and even more perfectly pressed blouses, squinted at the screen.  The numbers, usually her solace, swam before her eyes, a chaotic jumble defying her attempts at order.  The deadline for the Peterson-Montgomery merger loomed, a monstrous, looming shadow threatening to swallow her whole.  The air in the office, normally sterile and efficient, now felt thick with the tension of impending disaster.  Outside, the city raged; inside, Elara fought a battle of her own against a sea of illogical entries and a looming sense of dread.
