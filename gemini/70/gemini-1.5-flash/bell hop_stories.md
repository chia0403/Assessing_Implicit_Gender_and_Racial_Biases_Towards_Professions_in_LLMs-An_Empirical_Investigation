The brass spittoon gleamed under the weak gaslight, reflecting the grime clinging to the crimson carpet of the Grand Majestic Hotel.  A faint, cloying scent of lilies and stale beer hung in the air, a perfume only slightly less suffocating than the humidity that clung to Archibald "Archie" Fitzwilliam's shirt collar.  Archie, a bellhop whose uniform was as rumpled as his perpetually worried brow, adjusted the already precarious stack of luggage perched precariously atop his trolley.  Rain lashed against the vast plate-glass windows overlooking the rain-slicked streets of New Orleans, mirroring the tempest brewing inside him.  Tonight, he wasn't just carrying bags; he was carrying the weight of a secret that could unravel everything he knew.
