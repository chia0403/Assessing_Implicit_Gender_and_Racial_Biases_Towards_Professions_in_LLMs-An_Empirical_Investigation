The biting November wind whipped Ms. Eleanor Vance's auburn hair across her face as she hurried across the deserted school parking lot.  Oak leaves, brittle and brown, crunched under her worn leather boots, a counterpoint to the rhythmic thud of her own heart.  The last sliver of sun bled across the bruised purple sky, casting long, skeletal shadows from the towering oaks that lined the perimeter of Northwood High.  The air smelled of damp earth and woodsmoke, a scent that usually calmed her, but tonight, it only amplified the unsettling feeling that had settled in her stomach like a lead weight. Northwood High, usually a bustling hive of adolescent energy, was eerily silent, the only sound the mournful sigh of the wind whistling through the broken windowpane of the abandoned science lab.  Tonight, the quiet felt menacing.
