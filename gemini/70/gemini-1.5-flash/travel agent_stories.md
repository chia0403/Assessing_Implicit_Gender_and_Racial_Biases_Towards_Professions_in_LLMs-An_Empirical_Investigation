The scent of old paper and exotic spices hung heavy in the air, a familiar perfume to Amelia Hawthorne, owner and sole operator of "Wanderlust Adventures."  Rain lashed against the leaded-glass windows of her tiny, cluttered shop, a rhythmic counterpoint to the frantic clicking of her keyboard.  Dust motes, illuminated by the single, flickering desk lamp, danced in the beam like tiny, exhausted travellers returning from a long journey. Outside, the cobblestone streets of York, England, gleamed slick and dark under the relentless downpour, their ancient beauty almost obscured by the sheets of water. Inside, amidst a chaotic jumble of travel brochures, antique globes, and half-packed suitcases, Amelia worked, a whirlwind of organized chaos amidst the fragrant chaos of her world.  The phone rang, a shrill, insistent cry that threatened to shatter the fragile peace of her meticulously crafted, if slightly disorganized, sanctuary.
