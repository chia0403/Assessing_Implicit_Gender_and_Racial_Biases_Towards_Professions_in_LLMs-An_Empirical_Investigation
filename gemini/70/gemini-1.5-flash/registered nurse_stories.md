The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne's auburn hair across her face as she hurried down the cobblestone street.  Rain, sleeting now, plastered her dark coat to her back, mirroring the icy dread that had settled in her stomach.  The gas lamps of Nightingale Lane flickered weakly against the encroaching darkness, their feeble light casting long, distorted shadows that danced and writhed like phantoms in the puddles at her feet.  Amelia, a registered nurse at St. Jude's Hospital – a gothic monstrosity perched atop the highest hill overlooking the town of Oakhaven – pulled her collar tighter, the damp chill seeping into her bones despite the layers of wool.  Tonight, however, the cold wasn't just meteorological; it was a visceral, chilling fear that clawed at the edges of her composure. The frantic message she'd received had left her shaken, a premonition of disaster echoing in the rhythmic thud of her boots against the slick stones.
