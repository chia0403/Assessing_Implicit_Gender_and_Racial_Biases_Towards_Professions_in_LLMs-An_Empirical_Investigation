The chipped porcelain doll stared blankly from its perch on the dusty bookshelf, a silent witness to the chaos that Amelia Grace Hathaway knew as Tuesday.  Rain lashed against the single-paned windows of Sunnyside Daycare, a rambling Victorian house clinging precariously to the edge of a windswept cliff overlooking the churning grey sea.  The air inside, despite the storm raging outside, was thick with the cloying sweetness of spilled juice and the faint, underlying tang of something vaguely resembling old gym socks.  Amelia, twenty-two and already sporting the weary grace of a seasoned warrior, sighed, the sound barely audible above the cacophony of tiny voices squabbling over a particularly well-worn plastic firetruck.  This, she reflected, was precisely why she’d chosen child care as a career – the sheer, unadulterated joy of managing a room full of pint-sized tyrants.
