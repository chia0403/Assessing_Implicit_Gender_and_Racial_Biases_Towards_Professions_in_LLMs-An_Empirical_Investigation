The fluorescent lights of "Amelia Hernandez, Certified Public Accountant" hummed a monotonous tune, a soundtrack to the annual tax season frenzy.  Amelia Hernandez herself, however, found the rhythmic thrum strangely soothing, a counterpoint to the chaotic symphony of ringing phones and frantic clients that filled her small, cluttered office.  The air smelled faintly of stale coffee, printer ink, and the lingering scent of desperation – a potent blend uniquely associated with April 15th.  Stack upon stack of tax returns, some neatly organized, others threatening to topple in a paper avalanche, surrounded her desk, a testament to the mountain of paperwork she was still wrestling with, just three days before the deadline.  Outside, the relentless Chicago wind howled a mournful counterpoint to the frantic activity within, rattling the windowpanes of her second-floor office above a struggling bakery specializing in elaborately decorated cupcakes – a delicious distraction she rarely allowed herself.
