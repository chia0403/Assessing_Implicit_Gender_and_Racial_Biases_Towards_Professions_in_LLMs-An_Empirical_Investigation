The rooster crowed, a raucous announcement that the pre-dawn chill clinging to the dew-kissed fields of the Shenandoah Valley hadn't yet loosened its grip.  Elias Blackwood, his breath misting in the frigid air, rubbed sleep from his eyes and squinted towards the east.  The sky, a bruised purple and bleeding orange, promised another day of back-breaking labor on his small farm, nestled amongst rolling hills that had been in his family for generations.  The air, sharp with the scent of pine and damp earth, was a familiar comfort, a stark contrast to the anxieties churning in his gut.  This year's harvest was crucial, a matter of survival for Elias and his aging mother, and the whispers of blight spreading through the valley were a constant, gnawing fear.
