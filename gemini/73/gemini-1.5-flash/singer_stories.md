The stale air hung thick with the scent of cheap beer and desperation, clinging to the peeling paint of the Blue Moon Saloon like a stubborn shroud.  Dust motes, illuminated by the single, flickering bulb above the stage, danced in the sluggish currents stirred by the sporadic coughs and shuffling feet of the handful of patrons.  Tonight, however, the usual Friday night doldrums were punctuated by a nervous energy, a ripple of anticipation that vibrated through the room like a low hum.  For tonight, Seraphina “Sera” Nightingale, with her voice like spun moonlight and a heart full of bruised hope, was singing.  The battered upright piano, its ivory keys yellowed with age and wear, stood silent, awaiting the touch that would unleash its captive melodies.  Outside, the relentless Texas wind howled a mournful counterpoint to the quiet tension within.
