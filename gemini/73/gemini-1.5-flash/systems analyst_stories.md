The flickering neon sign of the "Lucky Dragon" noodle bar cast a lurid glow on the rain-slicked streets of Hong Kong.  A persistent drizzle, the kind that seeped into your bones as much as your clothes, plastered Amelia Zhang's dark hair to her forehead.  Amelia, a systems analyst for the notoriously secretive tech firm, OmniCorp Asia, pulled her collar higher, the damp chill doing little to distract her from the throbbing headache that had plagued her since sunrise.  The city’s usual cacophony – the relentless honking of taxis, the chattering crowds, the distant rumble of the Star Ferry – felt muted, replaced by a low, almost subsonic hum that vibrated in her chest.  It was a hum she hadn't noticed before, but now, it felt ominous, a discordant note in the symphony of urban life.  Tonight, however, the usual challenges of navigating Hong Kong's labyrinthine streets were the least of her worries.  Tonight, the system was down. And Amelia knew, with a chilling certainty, that it wasn't a simple software glitch.
