The biting November wind whipped Amelia “Amy” Hawthorne’s perfectly sculpted bob across her face as she navigated the slick granite steps of the Zenith Tower.  Rain, a relentless drizzle that seemed determined to penetrate even her bespoke cashmere coat, plastered her impeccably tailored trousers to her legs.  Amy, Senior Vice President of Global Acquisitions for Hawthorne Industries – a fact subtly, yet undeniably, conveyed by the diamond-encrusted 'H' subtly glittering on her lapel – barely registered the chill.  The panoramic view from the fifty-second floor, normally a source of quiet satisfaction, was today obscured by a curtain of grey.  Inside, the air crackled with a tension thicker than the fog clinging to the city below.  It was the kind of tension that promised a boardroom showdown, the kind that Amy Hawthorne, seasoned veteran of countless corporate battles, relished.  But this time felt…different.  The air hummed not just with ambition, but with something darker, something that smelled faintly of betrayal.
