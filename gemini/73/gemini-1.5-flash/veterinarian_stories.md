The biting wind whipped Dr. Eleanor Vance's auburn braid across her face as she wrestled the heavy oak door of the veterinary clinic open.  Salt spray stung her eyes, a familiar counterpoint to the scent of fish and brine that clung perpetually to the air in Port Blossom, Alaska.  The small, timber-framed building groaned under the weight of the relentless November gale, its windows rattling like skeletal fingers against the glass. Inside, the comforting warmth of the waiting room, usually alive with the chirps of birds and the soft whines of anxious dogs, was muted, almost eerily silent.  Only the rhythmic tick-tock of the grandfather clock in the corner offered a steady beat against the tempest raging outside.  Tonight, however, even that seemed to pulse with a foreboding rhythm, matching the unease that had settled deep in Eleanor's gut.
