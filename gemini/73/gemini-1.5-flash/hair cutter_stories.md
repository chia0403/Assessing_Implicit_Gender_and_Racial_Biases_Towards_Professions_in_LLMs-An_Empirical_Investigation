The scent of ozone hung heavy in the air, a metallic tang clinging to the damp strands of hair strewn across the worn wooden floor of "Shear Madness," Amelia Rose Hawthorne's tiny salon. Rain lashed against the single, grime-streaked window, blurring the already indistinct view of the cobbled alleyway behind her shop.  Amelia, with her fingers stained a kaleidoscope of dye colours and a perpetual smudge of charcoal dust on her cheek,  leaned back in her worn barber’s chair, the rhythmic whir of her clippers momentarily silenced. The storm outside mirrored the tempest brewing inside her – a tempest of unpaid bills, a demanding landlord, and a persistent ache in her left wrist that threatened to end her career before it truly began.  But tonight, even the relentless rain couldn’t quite drown out the insistent jingle of the shop bell.
