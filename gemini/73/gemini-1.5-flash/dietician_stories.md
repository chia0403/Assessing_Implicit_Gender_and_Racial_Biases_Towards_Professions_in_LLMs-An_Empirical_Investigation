The salt spray stung Eleanor Vance's face as she wrestled the stubborn fishing net onto the deck of the *Sea Serpent*.  The late afternoon sun, a bruised plum in the churning grey sky, cast long shadows across the cluttered deck, highlighting the iridescent sheen of the freshly-caught mackerel.  The air, thick with the smell of brine and diesel, was far removed from the sterile, clinical scent of the hospital kitchen where Eleanor usually spent her days.  This, however, was her sanctuary, the rugged beauty of the Outer Hebrides a stark contrast to the polished chrome and sterile white of the city hospital where she worked as a dietician.  The tiny island of Berneray, with its windswept hills and tenacious heather, held a peace she couldn't find anywhere else, a peace desperately needed after the week she'd just had.
