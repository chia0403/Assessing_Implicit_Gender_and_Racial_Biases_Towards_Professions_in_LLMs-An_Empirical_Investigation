The salt spray stung Dr. Eleanor Vance's face as she wrestled the stubborn fishing net from its tangle in the barnacle-encrusted pilings.  The air, thick with the smell of brine and decaying seaweed, hung heavy over the tiny, windswept island of Aisling.  Gulls cried overhead, their mournful calls echoing the loneliness that had begun to seep into Eleanor's bones since she’d arrived three weeks ago. Aisling, with its population barely exceeding two hundred souls and its sole clinic consisting of one dusty examination room and a waiting area furnished with mismatched chairs, was a far cry from the bustling city physiotherapy practice she’d left behind in Dublin. But the allure of escaping the relentless demands of her career, and perhaps, more importantly, her own turbulent past, had been too strong to resist.  The rhythmic crash of waves against the rocks provided a constant, hypnotic backdrop to the quiet turmoil brewing within her.
