The salt spray stung Amelia Stone's face as she wrestled the stubborn fishing boat's tiller.  The late afternoon sun, a bruised plum sinking below the choppy waters of the Irish Sea, cast long shadows across the grey, windswept cliffs of County Kerry.  The air, thick with the scent of brine and peat smoke, carried the mournful cry of gulls overhead.  Amelia, a solicitor with a reputation for sharp wit and even sharper legal skills in Dublin’s bustling courts, felt a world away from the polished mahogany desks and hushed whispers of her usual environment.  This rugged, unforgiving landscape, however, felt strangely familiar, a homecoming of sorts to the wild spirit she’d tried so hard to tame beneath the veneer of professional success.  The small, battered fishing boat, christened *The Banshee's Kiss*, rocked violently beneath her, mirroring the turmoil that had brought her to this remote corner of Ireland.
