The recycled air hung thick and sweet with the scent of jasmine and desperation.  Amelia Grace Hawthorne, a woman whose smile could charm a badger out of its sett and whose weary eyes held the secrets of a thousand turbulent flights, adjusted her meticulously knotted silk scarf.  The fluorescent lights of the nearly-deserted Kuala Lumpur International Airport hummed a discordant symphony against the frantic thump of her own heart. Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of the departure lounge, mirroring the storm brewing inside her.  Her latest flight, a seemingly innocuous hop from Singapore, had ended not with the usual polite disembarkation and the promise of a quiet layover, but with a frantic phone call, a whispered threat, and a hastily scribbled note tucked inside a discarded duty-free magazine.  The note, scrawled in a hurried hand, contained only three words: "Find the Sapphire Serpent."
