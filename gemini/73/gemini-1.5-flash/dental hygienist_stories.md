The sterile scent of ozone hung heavy in the air, a familiar perfume to Amelia Grace Hawthorne.  It clung to her scrubs, to the polished chrome of the instruments arrayed on her tray, and even, she suspected, to the very pores of her skin after a long day. Outside, the late afternoon sun cast long shadows across the bustling streets of downtown Seattle, painting the rain-slicked pavement in hues of amber and grey. But Amelia, tucked away in the hushed quiet of Dr. Chen’s pristine dental practice, was oblivious to the city’s vibrant pulse.  Her world, for the next hour, consisted solely of Mrs. Henderson’s surprisingly robust set of molars and the rhythmic whir of the ultrasonic scaler in her hand.
