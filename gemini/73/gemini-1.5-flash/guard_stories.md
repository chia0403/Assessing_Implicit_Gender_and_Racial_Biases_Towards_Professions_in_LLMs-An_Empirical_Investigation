The biting wind, a relentless sculptor of ice, whipped across the obsidian peaks of the Dragon's Tooth mountains.  A sliver of moon, pale and wan, offered meager illumination to the jagged, snow-covered ramparts of Fort Ironhold.  Sergeant Valerius Thorne, his breath misting in the frigid air, adjusted the thick fur collar of his greatcoat, the wool scratching against his chapped cheeks.  The fort, a brutal testament to human defiance against the encroaching wilderness and the rumored horrors that dwelled within it, groaned under the weight of the relentless storm.  Its stone walls, scarred by centuries of siege and weathering, seemed to huddle together against the unforgiving night, a silent sentinel guarding the precarious peace of the valley below.
