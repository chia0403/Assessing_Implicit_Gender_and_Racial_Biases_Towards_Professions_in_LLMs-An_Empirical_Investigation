The fluorescent lights of Blackwood & Finch hummed a monotonous tune, a soundtrack to Amelia Katherine Hawthorne's meticulously organized world.  Amelia, a woman whose life revolved around balanced ledgers and perfectly aligned spreadsheets, sat perched on her ergonomically designed chair, the faint scent of lavender hand lotion clinging to the air around her.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of her 27th-floor office, blurring the cityscape into an impressionistic painting of grey and black.  Inside, however, everything was sharply defined, every file precisely labeled, every pen perfectly aligned in its holder.  Tonight, though, the usual calm was shattered.  A crumpled, anonymous note lay on her pristine desk, its single, stark sentence a jarring discord in the symphony of her perfectly ordered existence:  "Your past is catching up."
