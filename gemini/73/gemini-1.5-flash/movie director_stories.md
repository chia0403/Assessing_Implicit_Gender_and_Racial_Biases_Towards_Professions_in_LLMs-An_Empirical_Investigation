The humid Louisiana air hung heavy, thick with the scent of cypress and impending rain.  Cicadas buzzed a relentless, almost frantic rhythm against the backdrop of the creaking, moss-draped oaks that lined the driveway of Belle Reve Plantation.  The grand, decaying mansion, a relic of a bygone era, loomed before Isabella “Izzy” Moreau, its white paint peeling like sunburnt skin, mirroring the frayed edges of her own patience. Izzy, a director whose name was whispered with a mixture of awe and apprehension in Hollywood circles, adjusted her sunglasses, the lenses reflecting the stormy sky above.  Her latest project, a historical epic about the scandalous Belle Reve family, was already proving to be more challenging than any she'd faced before.  The whispers she’d heard – about the plantation's haunted history, about strange occurrences that drove previous film crews away – had seemed like superstitious nonsense until she arrived. Now, standing on this threshold of crumbling grandeur, she felt a prickle of unease she couldn't quite dismiss.
