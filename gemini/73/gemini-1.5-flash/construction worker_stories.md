The biting November wind whipped at Elias Thorne’s exposed neck, stinging the already raw skin beneath his stubble.  He tugged his worn, navy blue hard hat lower, the familiar weight a small comfort against the relentless assault of the elements.  Above, the skeletal frame of the new skyscraper clawed at the bruised, grey sky, a monument to ambition and, Elias suspected, a monument to his impending exhaustion.  He perched precariously on a steel beam, a hundred feet above the bustling, oblivious city of Chicago, the cacophony of traffic far below a muted drone compared to the clang and shriek of the construction site.  The air hung thick with the scent of wet concrete, diesel fumes, and the metallic tang of freshly welded steel – the familiar, gritty perfume of Elias’s life.
