The static crackled, a relentless, hissing whisper that gnawed at the edges of Elias Thorne’s concentration.  Dust motes, illuminated by the single bare bulb hanging precariously above his workbench, danced in the stagnant air of the shack.  Elias, a man whose knuckles were perpetually stained with the grime of decades spent wrestling with recalcitrant vacuum tubes and temperamental transmitters, hunched over a tangled mass of wires, his brow furrowed in a frown that mirrored the complexity of the circuit before him.  The shack itself, perched precariously on a windswept bluff overlooking the churning grey expanse of the North Sea, smelled faintly of salt, ozone, and the bitter tang of old solder.  Outside, the relentless wind howled a mournful dirge, a constant companion to the lonely rhythm of the radio’s hiss.  Tonight, however, the hiss was different, punctuated by a strange, rhythmic pulse… a pulse that held Elias captive, a siren song of mystery pulling him further into the heart of the storm.
