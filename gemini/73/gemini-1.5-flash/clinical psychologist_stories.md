The biting November wind whipped Dr. Eleanor Vance’s auburn hair across her face as she hurried down the rain-slicked cobblestones of Edinburgh’s Old Town.  Gas lamps flickered, casting long, skeletal shadows that danced with the restless movement of the city.  Above, the imposing silhouette of Edinburgh Castle loomed, a granite sentinel watching over the swirling chaos below.  The scent of woodsmoke and damp earth hung heavy in the air, a perfume both comforting and unsettling, mirroring the turmoil Eleanor felt inside.  Her worn leather satchel, heavy with case files, swung against her hip, each jostle a jarring reminder of the weight of her profession. Tonight, however, the weight felt heavier than usual.  Tonight, she was facing a case that threatened to unravel even her carefully constructed composure.
