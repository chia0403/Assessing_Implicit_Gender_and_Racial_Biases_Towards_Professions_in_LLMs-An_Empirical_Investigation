The salt spray stung Detective Inspector Ava Thorne's face as the wind howled a mournful dirge across the windswept cliffs of Cornwall.  The late afternoon sun, a bruised plum hanging low on the horizon, cast long, skeletal shadows from the jagged rocks onto the churning grey sea below.  The air, thick with the scent of brine and something else – something acrid and unsettling – clawed at the back of her throat.  Ava pulled her worn wool coat tighter, the familiar weight a small comfort against the chilling desolation of Porthcurno’s deserted cove. This wasn't the postcard-perfect Cornwall; this was a landscape etched with secrets, and tonight, it seemed determined to keep them.
