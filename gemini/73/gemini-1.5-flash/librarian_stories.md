The scent of aged paper and leather, a comforting aroma usually associated with quiet contemplation, was today battling a sharp, metallic tang.  Dust motes, illuminated by the single shaft of afternoon sun slicing through the gothic window of the Grandchester Public Library, danced in the disturbed air.  Eleanor Vance, Head Librarian and keeper of countless forgotten stories, frowned. The metallic smell – it was blood. Not the faint, dry scent of a historical text spilled upon, but something…fresh.  The usually serene atmosphere of the Grandchester Public Library, a three-story behemoth of Victorian architecture looming over the sleepy town square, felt violently unsettled.
