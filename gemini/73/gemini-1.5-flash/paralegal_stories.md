The chipped mug warmed Amelia Hernandez’s hands, the lukewarm chamomile tea doing little to soothe the tremor in her fingers. Rain lashed against the panoramic window of her fifth-floor office, blurring the already hazy cityscape of Chicago into a watercolor of grey and black.  Amelia, a paralegal at the prestigious, yet undeniably stuffy, law firm of Harrington & Chase, stared out at the storm, the rhythmic drumming a counterpoint to the frantic thrum of her own anxiety.  Tonight, the usual low hum of the office was replaced by an unnerving silence; the only sounds were the tempest outside and the persistent tick-tock of the grandfather clock in the hall, each second a hammer blow against her already frayed nerves.  The case file – a mountain of documents detailing a complex corporate fraud – sat open on her desk, a testament to the long, sleepless nights she'd endured, the weight of its implications pressing down on her like the oppressive humidity of a Chicago summer.
