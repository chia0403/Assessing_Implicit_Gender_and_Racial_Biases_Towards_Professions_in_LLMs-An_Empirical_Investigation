The relentless drone of the fluorescent lights in Northwood High hummed a discordant symphony to the rhythmic tap-tap-tap of Ms. Eleanor Vance's pen against her grading rubric.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows overlooking the perpetually soggy football field, blurring the already indistinct lines of the October twilight.  Inside, the usually vibrant teacher's lounge was deserted, the lingering scent of lukewarm coffee and stale pastries a testament to the day's frantic energy now spent.  Eleanor, however, remained, a solitary island of red-penned fury amidst the abandoned mugs and crumpled papers.  She sighed, the sound barely audible above the drumming rain, and ran a hand through her already chaotic auburn curls, the weight of a particularly challenging AP English literature essay pressing heavily on her mind.  The essay, a mangled interpretation of *Hamlet*, was only the latest in a series of frustrations that had been building since the first bell this morning.
