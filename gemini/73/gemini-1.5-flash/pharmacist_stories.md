The chipped porcelain sink groaned under the weight of Amelia Hernandez's elbows as she stared out the grimy window of her pharmacy.  Rain, a relentless grey curtain, hammered against the glass, mirroring the storm brewing inside her.  Above the incessant drumming, the rhythmic squeak of the old refrigerator, a faithful companion for twenty years, was almost a comfort.  Amelia, a woman whose age was etched not just in the fine lines around her eyes but in the calloused hands that meticulously counted pills, wiped a stray tear with the back of her hand.  The air hung heavy with the scent of lavender and antiseptic, a familiar fragrance that tonight did little to soothe her frayed nerves.  Her small, family-run pharmacy, Hernandez Apothecary, nestled on the edge of the perpetually rain-soaked town of Port Blossom, felt less like a haven and more like a cage.
