The biting November wind whipped around Amelia "Amy" Rodriguez as she surveyed the muddy, churned-up field.  Floodlights cast long, skeletal shadows from the spindly oaks surrounding the perimeter of Kepler High School's football field.  Steam rose from the worn, plastic cups of hot chocolate clutched by the huddled parents on the metal bleachers.  The score, a dismal 0-21, glared from the weathered scoreboard like a neon accusation. Amy, head coach of the Kepler Kestrels, pulled her thick, navy blue Kepler Kestrels jacket tighter around her.  Another loss. Another crushing defeat in a season already riddled with them.  This wasn't how she'd envisioned her first year as head coach.  She'd arrived in Kepler, Kansas, a tiny town clinging to the edge of the vast prairie, brimming with optimism and innovative plays. Now, with the season nearing its end, all she felt was the bone-deep chill of disappointment and the gnawing suspicion that she was in way over her head.
