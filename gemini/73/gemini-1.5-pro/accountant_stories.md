Rain lashed against the mullioned windows of Amelia Grace Hawthorne's office, mimicking the frantic drumming of her fingers against the mahogany desk. Outside, the gothic spires of St. Jude's Church were swallowed by the November gloom that had settled over the quaint, coastal town of Port Blossom, Maine.  Amelia, a meticulous accountant known for her unwavering calm and precise nature, found herself staring at a ledger that just didn't balance.  A discrepancy of $17,492.31 glared back at her, a crimson stain on the otherwise pristine financial tapestry of the esteemed Bellingham Shipping Company. This wasn't just a misplaced decimal point; this was a gaping hole, a void that threatened to unravel the very fabric of the town's oldest and most prosperous business.  And in a town as small and interconnected as Port Blossom, Amelia knew the ripple effects would be devastating.
