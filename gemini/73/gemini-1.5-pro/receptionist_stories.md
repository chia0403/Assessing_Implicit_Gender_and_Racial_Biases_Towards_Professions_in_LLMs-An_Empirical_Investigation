The flickering fluorescent lights of the Grand Majestic Hotel lobby cast a sickly yellow glow on Esmeralda "Esme" Rodriguez's perfectly applied red lipstick.  It was 3:17 am, the witching hour in a place where the witches wore sequins and the spells involved complimentary champagne and upgraded suites. The Grand Majestic, perched precariously on the edge of the Santa Monica cliffs, was a monument to faded Hollywood glamour, a place where the ghosts of starlets and studio executives still roamed the dimly lit hallways.  Tonight, however, the only ghost Esme was concerned with was the one haunting the antiquated reservation system, which was currently blinking a cryptic error message at her. Outside, the Pacific Ocean roared its restless lullaby, a stark contrast to the unnerving silence within the opulent, yet eerily empty, lobby.
