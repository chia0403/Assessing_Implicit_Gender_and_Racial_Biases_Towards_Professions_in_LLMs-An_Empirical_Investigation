Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of Amelia "Amy" Sharma's 48th-floor office, blurring the already fading twilight view of the Chicago skyline.  The wind howled a mournful song, mimicking the churning anxiety in Amy's stomach. Scattered across her mahogany desk were the remnants of a hastily eaten takeout dinner: a crumpled plastic container stained with tikka masala and a half-empty carton of lukewarm miso soup.  The air hung heavy with the scent of stale curry and impending doom.  Tomorrow was the shareholder's meeting, and the fate of Sharma Solutions, the tech company her father had built from the ground up, rested squarely on her shoulders.  For the last six months, Amy had worked tirelessly, fueled by copious amounts of coffee and an unwavering determination to prove she was more than just the founder's daughter.  But the numbers didn't lie. Profits were plummeting, competitors were circling like sharks, and the whispers in the corridors had grown into a deafening roar.
