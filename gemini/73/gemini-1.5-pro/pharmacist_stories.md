The relentless Nevada sun beat down on the dusty asphalt of Harmony Creek, a town so small it boasted only one blinking traffic light and a single, perpetually overcrowded diner. Inside Harmony Creek Pharmacy, the air conditioning hummed a monotonous tune, a stark contrast to the dry, crackling heat outside.  Eleanor Vance, Pharm.D., meticulously counted out metformin tablets, her brow furrowed in concentration.  The pharmacy, with its worn linoleum floor and shelves packed with remedies for everything from athlete's foot to heartache, was her sanctuary, a place of order and precision in a world that often felt chaotic and unpredictable.  This Tuesday, however, felt different.  A low hum of unease vibrated beneath the usual quiet murmur of the pharmacy, a premonition that hung in the air like the scent of the approaching desert storm.
