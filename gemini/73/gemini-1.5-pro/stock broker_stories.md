Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of Alistair Finch’s forty-second-floor office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside him.  The Chicago skyline, usually a glittering tapestry of steel and glass, was obscured by a thick, grey curtain of precipitation.  From his perch in the heart of the financial district, Alistair, his full name Alistair Leopold Finch III, could barely make out the churning waters of Lake Michigan.  The digital clock on his Bloomberg terminal blinked 3:57 AM, the pre-market numbers already painting a grim picture.  His tie, a Hermès silk power tie in a shade somewhere between cerulean and despair, felt like a noose tightening around his neck. Today was the day the Vandergelt acquisition was set to finalize, a deal Alistair had staked his reputation, and possibly his career, on.  And everything, absolutely everything, was going sideways.
