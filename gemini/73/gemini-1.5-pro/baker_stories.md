The aroma of cardamom and burnt sugar hung heavy in the air, a comforting blanket against the pre-dawn chill.  Amelia “Millie” Blackwood, her apron dusted with flour and a smudge of raspberry jam on her cheek, hummed a jaunty tune as she wrestled a particularly stubborn batch of croissant dough.  Her bakery, "The Whispering Oven," nestled between a dusty antique shop and a perpetually bustling florist in the heart of Oakhaven, a small town where gossip travelled faster than the morning delivery van.  The shop's front window, still dark save for the warm glow emanating from within, displayed a tempting array of pastries, their sugared surfaces catching the faint light of the streetlamp outside. Oakhaven might still be asleep, but Millie's day, and the sweet symphony of her baking, had already begun.
