Rain lashed against the panoramic window of Elias Thorne's 27th-floor apartment, mirroring the storm brewing inside him.  The city lights of Chicago, usually a dazzling spectacle, were blurred into hazy streaks of amber and crimson. Elias, a systems analyst for the prestigious firm of  Goldman & Sterling Technologies, pushed a hand through his already disheveled dark hair. His tie hung loose around his neck, and the remains of a microwaved dinner sat congealing on the coffee table. It was 2:17 AM on a Tuesday, and Elias, for the third night in a row, was staring at lines of code scrolling across his laptop screen, a cryptic message flashing ominously at the bottom: "ACCESS DENIED."  This wasn't just any system error; this was Goldman & Sterling's proprietary trading platform, and Elias had a sinking feeling that something far more sinister than a faulty firewall was at play.
