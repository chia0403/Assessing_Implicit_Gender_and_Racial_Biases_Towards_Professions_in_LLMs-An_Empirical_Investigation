The biting November wind whipped around Elias Thorne, tugging at the brim of his tweed cap and sending a flurry of crimson and gold leaves swirling around his worn leather boots.  He hunched deeper into his thick wool coat, the scent of damp earth and decaying foliage clinging to the air. Elias was the groundskeeper of Eventide Manor, a sprawling gothic mansion perched precariously on a cliff overlooking the churning grey waters of the North Sea. The manor, with its crumbling stone gargoyles and ivy-choked towers, had stood for centuries, a silent witness to the changing tides and the secrets whispered on the wind.  This morning, however, a new secret had taken root in the manicured lawns of Eventide, a secret Elias had unearthed amongst the frost-kissed dahlias, and one he knew would change everything.
