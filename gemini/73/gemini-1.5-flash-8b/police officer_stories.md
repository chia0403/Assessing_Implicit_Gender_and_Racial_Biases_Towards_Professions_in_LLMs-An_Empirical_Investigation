Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of the precinct, a relentless drumming that mirrored the frantic rhythm of Detective Isabella "Izzy" Diaz's thoughts.  The flickering fluorescent lights cast long, unsettling shadows across the cramped interrogation room, highlighting the dust motes dancing in the stagnant air.  The scent of stale coffee and fear hung heavy, a palpable shroud over the entire station.  It was a Tuesday night in the gritty, perpetually overcast town of Havenwood, a place where the rhythm of life was as muted and grey as the weather outside.  The town, nestled deep in the Appalachian foothills, was a tapestry woven from generations of hard work and secrets.  Each weathered house, each shadowed alley, held whispers of the past, just waiting to be unearthed.
