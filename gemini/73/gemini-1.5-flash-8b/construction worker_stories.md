The biting wind whipped Amelia Petrova's hard hat around, stinging her cheeks with icy rain.  Grey clouds, thick and ominous, hung low over the skeletal frame of the new skyscraper, its uncompleted facade a stark, unfinished canvas against the bruised sky.  The construction site, nestled precariously between a bustling highway and a sprawling, overgrown park, hummed with the ceaseless clang of metal on metal, the rhythmic thrum of heavy machinery, and the shouts of foremen issuing barked instructions in a language Amelia, fluent in five, still found distinctly grating.  This was her domain, this concrete jungle of early morning frost and late afternoon shadows, a place where steel and grit reigned supreme.  Today, however, something felt different.  A subtle shift in the usual cacophony, a whisper of unease among the workers, hung heavy in the air.  The towering crane, a skeletal giant reaching for the sky, groaned in protest.
