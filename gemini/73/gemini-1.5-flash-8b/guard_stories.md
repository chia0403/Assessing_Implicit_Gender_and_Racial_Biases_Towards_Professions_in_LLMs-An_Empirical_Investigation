The biting wind whipped across the cobblestones of the Grey Quarter, stinging Elara Stoneheart’s cheeks.  Dust, stirred from the ceaselessly grinding gears of the Clockwork Heart – the city's massive central engine – swirled around her, obscuring the already dim alleyways.  A sickly yellow light, filtered through the grime-coated windows of the nearby workshop, cast long, skeletal shadows that danced with the gusts.  The air itself seemed to hum with a low, metallic thrum, a constant reminder of the city's beating heart, a heart she was sworn to protect.  Elara, a guard of the Imperial Citadel, adjusted the worn leather straps of her breastplate, the familiar weight a comfort in this relentless storm of the urban night.  Her breath frosted in the air, a fragile whisper in the city's metallic roar.
