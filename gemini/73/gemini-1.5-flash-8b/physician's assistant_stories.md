The humid air hung heavy, thick with the scent of jasmine and something indefinably metallic, clinging to the cobblestones of the alley.  Rain, a relentless, almost desperate downpour, hammered against the corrugated iron roof of the clinic.  Inside, Dr. Anya Petrova, Physician's Assistant, adjusted the sterile gloves on her hands, the cool latex a stark contrast to the oppressive heat.  The fluorescent lights buzzed overhead, casting a harsh glare on the cluttered examination room.  Empty IV bags lay draped over the metal stands, like ghostly shrouds.  A single, flickering candle on a nearby table cast dancing shadows on the walls, barely enough light to illuminate the worn, leather-bound medical journals scattered on the small mahogany desk.  Today, like every other day in the crumbling, impoverished section of the city known as the Whispering Quarter, was another day filled with stories whispered on the wind, and urgent needs.
