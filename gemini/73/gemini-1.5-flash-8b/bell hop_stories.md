The brass bell, a chipped and tarnished monument to forgotten grandeur, jangled a lonely, mournful note as Bartholomew "Barty" Butterfield, bell hop extraordinaire at the Grand Victoria Hotel, wrestled a particularly stubborn suitcase onto the luggage cart.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of sunlight piercing the gloom of the lobby.  The air, thick with the scent of old leather and faintly antiseptic disinfectant, hung heavy, mirroring the oppressive silence that had settled over the sprawling hotel, a mausoleum of marble and mirrors.  

Autumn leaves, a kaleidoscope of crimson and gold, were plastered to the grand windows overlooking the sprawling, fog-shrouded city of Oakhaven. The city, usually vibrant with the bustle of commerce and revelry, felt shrouded in a perpetual twilight, a chilling contrast to the extravagant pomp and circumstance that once defined the Grand Victoria.
