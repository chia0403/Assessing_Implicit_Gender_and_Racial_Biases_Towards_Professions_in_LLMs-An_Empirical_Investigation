The chipped paint of the playroom walls, a faded avocado green, seemed to absorb the afternoon sun, turning it into a sickly, muted yellow.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of light piercing the gloom, illuminating tiny, discarded plastic dinosaurs scattered amongst half-eaten snacks and discarded crayons.  This was the sanctuary, or at least, the designated 'safe space', of the Little Explorers Childcare Centre.  Eleven-year-old  Amelia "Amy"  Hart, a child care worker, sighed, her fingers tracing the faded outline of a sun painted on the wall. Today, chaos was just a different kind of quiet.  The usual boisterous energy was absent, replaced by an unnerving stillness that felt heavier than the damp air clinging to the room.  Amy, whose brown hair was pulled back in a messy bun, surveyed the room, her gaze falling on the group of children huddled around a particular corner.  A knot of anxiety tightened in her stomach.  Something was wrong, and she felt a tremor of responsibility that went deeper than the usual concerns of scraped knees and spilled milk.  She needed to find out what was brewing in this tiny, sun-dappled universe of hers.
