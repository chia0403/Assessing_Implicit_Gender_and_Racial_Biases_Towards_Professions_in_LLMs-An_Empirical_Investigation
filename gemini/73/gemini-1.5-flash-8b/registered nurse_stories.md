The fluorescent lights of the emergency room hummed, a relentless soundtrack to the chaotic symphony of sirens wailing outside and the rhythmic thud-thud-thud of a heart monitor beeping erratically.  Rain lashed against the windows of St. Jude's, blurring the already hazy cityscape into a muted grey.  Inside, Amelia Hayes, Registered Nurse, scrubbed her hands with an almost obsessive intensity, the antiseptic smell stinging her nostrils.  Another night, another onslaught of the city's ills.  The sterile white walls, usually a comforting backdrop to her routine, felt suffocating tonight.  The air itself seemed thick with unspoken anxieties, with the weight of lives hanging precariously in the balance.  Amelia took a deep breath, the scent of bleach and fear mingling in her lungs, and adjusted her crisp, navy blue scrubs.  It was going to be a long shift.
