The flickering neon sign of the "Whispering Siren" cast an eerie, pulsating orange glow across the rain-slicked cobblestones.  A chill wind, sharp and biting, whipped through the narrow alleyway, carrying with it the scent of damp earth and the faint, intoxicating perfume of jasmine from a nearby florist's shop.  Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roofs, a relentless percussion that drowned out all but the low thrum of city life seeping through the stone walls.  This was the heart of the Lower Quarter, a labyrinth of shadowed streets and hidden courtyards where Amelia Nightingale, a young singer with eyes the colour of stormy seas, sought refuge from the storm raging within her.  Amelia, haunted by a past she desperately tried to bury, found solace only in the hushed melodies that poured from her throat.  Tonight, she planned to sing.
