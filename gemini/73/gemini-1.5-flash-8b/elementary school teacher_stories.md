The chipped paint of the Mississippian Elementary School gymnasium echoed Amelia "Amy" Butterfield's frantic footsteps.  Dust motes danced in the afternoon sun slanting through the grimy windows, illuminating a scene that was anything but serene.  Empty juice boxes lay scattered on the basketball court, testament to the chaotic lunchtime melee that had just concluded.  Amy, a third-grade teacher with a perpetually tired smile and eyes that held the weight of a thousand tiny, worried faces, clutched a crumpled piece of paper in her hand.  It was a drawing, a vibrant explosion of colour that contrasted sharply with the muted tones of the gym.  A picture of a dragon, its scales shimmering with hues of sapphire and gold, lay beneath a single, perfectly formed tear-shaped drop of crimson paint.  The drawing was undeniably the work of Leo, the shy boy whose art was his language, the boy who was suddenly, inexplicably, missing.
