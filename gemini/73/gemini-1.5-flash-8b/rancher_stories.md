The wind, a relentless, whispering beast, whipped across the vast, ochre plains of the Badlands, stinging Amelia Blackwood's cheeks and tugging at the frayed edges of her worn denim shirt.  Dust devils, miniature tornadoes of swirling earth and dried grass, danced across the cracked parched land, mocking the dwindling water supply in the rusted water troughs.  It was a brutal summer in the heart of Montana, and Amelia Blackwood, a rancher of the Blackwood family's sprawling cattle ranch, knew the desperation in the air as keenly as the searing sun beat down.  Her family had been tending this land for generations, but this year felt different.  The drought was a monster, relentless and unforgiving.  The once lush green valleys were now skeletal landscapes.  And with them, the livelihood of her family, and the future of her beloved Blackwood Ranch.
