The chipped porcelain sink, stained a perpetual, dull rose by years of lukewarm water and spilled dye, reflected Amelia Blackwood's worried frown.  Sunlight, weak and filtered through the grime-coated windows of "The Gilded Shear," cast long, dancing shadows across the small, cluttered salon.  Dust motes, like tiny, glittering specks of defiance, danced in the air, mocking the relentless hum of the old hair dryer.  Amelia, a woman of thirty-five with eyes the colour of faded plums and hair the shade of midnight, traced the chipped edge of a chipped mug with her finger.  The salon, once a vibrant haven for the fashionable elite of Willow Creek, now felt more like a forgotten relic. The bell above the door jingled softly, a meager sound that seemed to echo the hollow feeling in Amelia's chest.
