The flickering neon sign of the "Golden Gecko" cast a sickly yellow hue across Amelia Petrova's face, highlighting the dark circles beneath her eyes.  Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of the ramshackle diner, a rhythmic counterpoint to the frantic tapping of her keyboard.  Outside, the dusty, windswept plains of the Arizona Territory stretched endlessly, a vast ochre canvas speckled with the skeletal remains of long-gone settlements.  Amelia, a seasoned investigative journalist for the *Tucson Star*, hunched over her worn laptop, the flickering screen casting a pale glow on her meticulously organised notes.  The air, thick with the scent of burnt coffee and desperation, hung heavy in the diner, mirroring the suffocating weight of the case.  This remote outpost, known as Dust Devil Gulch, was the epicentre of a burgeoning mystery, and Amelia was determined to unearth the truth, no matter the cost.
