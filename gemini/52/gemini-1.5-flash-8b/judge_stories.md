The biting November wind whipped through the narrow alleyways of Alderwood, rattling the ancient, iron-barred windows of the Magistrate's Court.  Dust motes danced in the single shaft of sunlight piercing the gloom, illuminating a swirling vortex of legal documents and the anxious faces of those awaiting Justice.  Within the stone chamber, lit by a sputtering gas lamp, sat Judge Elara Vance, her sharp grey eyes scanning the rows of hushed petitioners.  The air hung thick with the scent of damp earth and simmering discontent.  Today, the case of the stolen silk banners, a matter of grave import for the esteemed House of Ashworth, was to be heard.  Alderwood, a city steeped in ancient traditions and whispered secrets, held its breath.
