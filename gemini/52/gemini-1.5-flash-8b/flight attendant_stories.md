The humid air hung thick and sweet, a suffocating blanket draped over the tarmac.  Sweat beaded on Amelia Rodriguez's forehead, clinging to the crisp white uniform that felt heavy against her skin.  Below, the sprawling concrete maze of LAX swallowed the sun, casting long, skeletal shadows across the jet bridge.  The rhythmic thump of the plane's engines was a relentless, insistent pulse against the oppressive silence of the pre-dawn hours.  Amelia, a flight attendant for Aerolineas del Sol, felt the familiar knot of anxiety tighten in her stomach.  This wasn't just another flight. This was Flight 273, the first leg of a transcontinental route, carrying a high-profile client, Mr. Alistair Finch, and his entourage, to a crucial business summit in New York.  And rumours had been swirling through the crew, rumours of secrets and deals worth more than mountains of gold.
