The flickering neon sign of "Wall Street Wealth" cast a sickly yellow glow on the rain-slicked cobblestones of New York's Financial District.  A biting wind whipped through the alley, carrying the scent of stale coffee and impending doom.  Inside, huddled over a mountain of spreadsheets and overflowing takeout containers, sat Amelia "Amy" Sterling, a stock broker with a reputation as sharp as her tailored suits and a smile as practiced as her market analysis.  The year was 2024, and the whispers of a looming economic downturn echoed in the hushed, high-pressure environment of the brokerage firm.  Amy, with her perfectly sculpted platinum blonde hair and steely gaze, felt the tremors beneath the surface.  This wasn't just another market fluctuation; something felt fundamentally wrong.
