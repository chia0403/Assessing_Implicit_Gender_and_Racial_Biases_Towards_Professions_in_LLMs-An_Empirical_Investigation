The biting wind whipped Amelia Blackwood's scarf around her neck, stinging her cheeks as she hurried down the cobbled alley.  Dust motes danced in the fading light, illuminating the chipped, faded murals depicting mythical beasts that lined the narrow passageway.  Rain, a relentless curtain in the hours before nightfall, had finally abated, leaving behind a damp chill that seeped into the very bones. This was the heart of the Quarter, a labyrinthine district of the city, known for its hidden courtyards, shadowy taverns, and whispered rumours.  Amelia, a staff writer for the *Chronicles of the City*, a respected, if somewhat stuffy, newspaper, was on a deadline.  Tonight, the story was more than a tale; it was a life, a life she was determined to unearth.  Her quarry?  The enigmatic Monsieur Dubois, a reclusive watchmaker rumoured to hold the key to the city's most guarded secret.
