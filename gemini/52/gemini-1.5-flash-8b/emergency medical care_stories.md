The biting wind whipped Amelia "Em" Carmichael's crimson jacket around her, stinging her cheeks as she surveyed the chaotic scene from the rooftop of the abandoned hospital.  Below, the flickering neon sign of the "Serenity Sanitarium" cast a sickly yellow glow on the overflowing parking lot.  Sirens wailed a mournful symphony, their insistent cries echoing off the skeletal frame of the building.  This wasn't your typical Friday night call.  Em, a Paramedic with the Metro Emergency Response Team, knew, instinctively, that tonight was different, far more sinister than a drunk driver or a heart attack.  The palpable tension, thick as the frigid air, hung heavy in the pre-dawn chill.

The abandoned hospital, a silent testament to forgotten lives and medical failures, had stood on the outskirts of the city for decades.  Its decaying brick facade, pocked with bullet holes and scarred with graffiti, seemed to mirror the wounds of the city itself.  Rust-eaten metal cages and broken window frames offered glimpses into empty rooms, echoing with the ghosts of patients and the whispers of unanswered questions.  The air hung thick with the metallic tang of blood and the faint, cloying sweetness of something else... something unnatural.   The once pristine, now crumbling medical equipment lay scattered like fallen soldiers.  Inside, amidst the growing chaos, Em knew she was facing more than a medical emergency; she was facing the unknown.
