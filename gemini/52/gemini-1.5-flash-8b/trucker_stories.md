The biting wind whipped across the desolate plains of Wyoming, stinging Amelia "Ace" Cartwright's cheeks.  Dust devils, miniature tornadoes of grit and grime, danced across the cracked earth, swirling around the rusted cab of her rig, the "Ironheart."  A single, skeletal juniper tree stood sentinel against the endless, ochre expanse, its branches clawing at the bruised sky.  Ace, a woman carved from granite and hardened by the relentless highway, adjusted the worn leather strap of her worn-out driving gloves, her breath clouding in the frigid air.  The flickering neon sign of the "Last Chance Diner" barely held onto its precarious position on the dusty roadside, a faded beacon promising greasy spoon comfort and lukewarm coffee in the face of this unforgiving landscape.  It was 2 AM, and Ace was a thousand miles from civilization, a thousand miles from the whispers of her past.
