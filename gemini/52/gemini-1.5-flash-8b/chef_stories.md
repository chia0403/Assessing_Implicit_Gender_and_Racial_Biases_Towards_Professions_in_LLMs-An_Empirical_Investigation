The humid air hung heavy in the kitchen, thick with the scent of roasting garlic and simmering tomatoes.  Rain hammered against the corrugated iron roof of the "Salty Siren," a beachfront shack more ramshackle than restaurant, but beloved by the locals for its fresh seafood and even fresher gossip.  Chef Amelia Rodriguez, her name a whispered legend amongst the fishermen and beachcombers who frequented the place, wiped a bead of sweat from her brow.  The rhythmic clang of pots and pans, the occasional exasperated sigh from her sous-chef, Leo, and the rhythmic pulse of the waves crashing on the shore formed the soundtrack to her culinary symphony. Today, however, the music felt off-key.  A persistent ache in her wrist, a phantom pain from an old kitchen accident, throbbed in time with the storm outside.
