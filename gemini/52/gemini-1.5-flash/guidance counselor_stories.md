The stale scent of coffee and despair hung heavy in the air, a familiar perfume in the office of Ms. Eleanor Vance, guidance counselor extraordinaire – or at least, that’s what the overly-enthusiastic school newsletter called her.  Eleanor sighed, the sound swallowed by the thick, beige carpet of her small, windowless office in Northwood High.  Rain lashed against the unseen panes, mirroring the tempest brewing inside her.  The overflowing inbox on her ancient computer mocked her with a blinking cursor, a tiny digital metronome counting down to the inevitable avalanche of teenage anxieties, parental pressures, and college application crises.  Outside, the late October wind howled a mournful dirge, a fitting soundtrack for the impending doom of another Monday.
