The biting wind whipped across the desolate plains, carrying with it the scent of salt and something else… something acrid, metallic, that clung to the back of Sergeant Valerius Thorne’s throat.  He pulled his thick wool cloak tighter, the rough fabric scratching against the worn leather of his breastplate.  The skeletal remains of wind-twisted trees clawed at the bruised twilight sky, their branches like skeletal fingers reaching for the distant, flickering lights of the Citadel of Veridia – his post for the last five years, a lonely vigil against the encroaching darkness of the Whisperwind Waste.  Valerius shifted his weight, the cold seeping into the very marrow of his bones, a familiar discomfort compared to the gnawing unease that had settled in his gut since the sun dipped below the horizon.  Tonight, something felt… different.
