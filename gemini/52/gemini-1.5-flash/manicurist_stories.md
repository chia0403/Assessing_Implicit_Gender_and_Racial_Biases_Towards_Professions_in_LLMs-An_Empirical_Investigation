The scent of acetone and lavender hung heavy in the air, a cloying sweetness that clung to the plush velvet chairs and the chipped porcelain sink of "Nails by Nella."  Nella Esposito, a woman whose energy outstripped the flickering neon sign outside her tiny salon, hummed along to a scratchy Dean Martin record, her nimble fingers working magic on a client's already impeccably manicured nails.  Outside, the relentless New York City rain hammered against the grimy windowpanes, mirroring the tempest brewing in the heart of her latest customer, a high-powered lawyer with a face as tight as a drum.  The air crackled not just with the electric nail dryer, but with unspoken tensions, the kind Nella had become adept at sniffing out in the quiet intimacy of her nail salon.
