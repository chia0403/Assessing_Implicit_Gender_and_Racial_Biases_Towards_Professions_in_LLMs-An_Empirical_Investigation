The chipped paint of the alphabet border peeled away like sunburnt skin, mirroring the slow, agonizing unraveling of Ms. Eleanor Vance’s carefully constructed morning routine.  Rain lashed against the oversized windows of Room 2B at Sunny Meadows Elementary, blurring the already indistinct shapes of the playground equipment into grey, sodden blobs.  Inside, a cacophony of pre-dawn sniffles and the insistent squeak of tiny sneakers on polished linoleum battled for dominance.  The air hung thick with the cloying sweetness of spilled juice and the faintly metallic tang of a scraped knee – the familiar, slightly nauseating aroma of a kindergarten classroom before the sun had properly risen.  Eleanor, clutching a lukewarm mug of coffee that tasted suspiciously like dishwater, braced herself.  Another Monday.  Another adventure in the land of glitter glue and questionable hygiene.
