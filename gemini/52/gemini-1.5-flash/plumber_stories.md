The biting November wind howled a mournful tune around the crumbling Victorian eaves of 13 Grimalkin Lane, rattling the loose windowpanes like skeletal fingers.  Inside, however, a comforting warmth radiated from the ancient Aga stove, its cheerful glow reflecting off the chipped porcelain sink and the overflowing toolbox of Bartholomew "Barty" Butterfield, master plumber. Barty, a man whose hands were more familiar with corroded pipes than polished silverware, sat nursing a mug of lukewarm tea, his brow furrowed in concentration as he studied a particularly stubborn blueprint spread across his workbench, a tangle of faded ink and cryptic notations. The smell of damp plaster and old copper hung heavy in the air, a familiar perfume in the cluttered workshop that had been his sanctuary for over thirty years.
