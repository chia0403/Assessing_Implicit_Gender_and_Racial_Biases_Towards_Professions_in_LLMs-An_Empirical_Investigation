The salt spray stung Amelia Stone’s face as the ferry churned through the choppy waters of Puget Sound.  Rain, a constant companion in this corner of Washington State, lashed against the windows of the small, overcrowded vessel, blurring the already indistinct outlines of Bainbridge Island.  Amelia, a lawyer with a reputation as sharp as the basalt cliffs that guarded the island's shores, pulled her worn leather trench coat tighter. The damp chill seeped into her bones, mirroring the icy dread that had settled in her gut since the cryptic phone call earlier that morning.  The case, involving a missing antique map and a family feud as tangled as the island's ancient forests, felt different; darker, more sinister than the usual property disputes and corporate mergers that filled her Seattle office.  This felt… personal.
