The chipped porcelain sink groaned under the weight of Elias Thorne's elbow as he scrubbed at a stubborn smear of what he suspected, but couldn't quite be sure, was congealed gravy.  The air in the men's restroom of the Grand Majestic Theatre hung thick with the cloying sweetness of stale perfume and the sharp, metallic tang of desperation – a familiar bouquet to Elias, who had spent the last fifteen years navigating the labyrinthine corridors and shadowy corners of the building, armed with nothing but a mop and a bucket.  The Grand Majestic, a crumbling relic of a bygone era, stood proud yet weary on the edge of the city’s revitalized downtown, its ornate façade a stark contrast to the grimy reality hidden within its aged walls.  Tonight, however, even the usual oppressive silence felt…different.  A prickling unease, sharper than the smell of bleach, settled in Elias's gut.  He straightened, the fluorescent lights flickering overhead, casting long, distorted shadows that danced and writhed like restless ghosts.
