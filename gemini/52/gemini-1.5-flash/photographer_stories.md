The biting wind whipped Amelia Jean Holloway’s hair across her face, stinging her cheeks a vibrant crimson that mirrored the setting sun bleeding across the desolate Icelandic landscape.  Jagged, black basalt columns clawed at the bruised sky, their sharp edges silhouetted against the fading light.  Amelia shivered, pulling her thick wool scarf tighter around her neck, the chill seeping into her very bones despite the layers of thermal underwear beneath her rugged parka.  Her breath plumed out in ghostly white clouds, momentarily obscuring the viewfinder of her trusty Leica M6, a loyal companion on countless expeditions.  This, however, was different. This felt… ominous. The air hung heavy with the scent of salt and something else, something ancient and unsettling, clinging to the lonely stretch of Reynisfjara black sand beach like a shroud.
