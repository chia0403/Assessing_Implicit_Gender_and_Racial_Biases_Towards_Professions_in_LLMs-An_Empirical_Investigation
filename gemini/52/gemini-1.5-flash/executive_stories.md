The mahogany desk, polished to a mirror sheen, reflected Amelia Katherine Hayes's severe expression back at her.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of her 57th-floor office, blurring the already hazy cityscape of Hong Kong below.  The city, usually a vibrant tapestry of neon and towering skyscrapers, was muted tonight, shrouded in a damp, almost melancholic grey.  Inside, Amelia, CEO of Hayes Global Holdings, felt a mirroring dampness settle in her bones, a chill that had nothing to do with the weather.  The air hung thick with the scent of expensive leather and brewing tension; the kind that clung to the air like the clinging humidity of a Hong Kong monsoon.  Tonight, the weight of her empire felt less like a crown and more like a suffocating tiara.
