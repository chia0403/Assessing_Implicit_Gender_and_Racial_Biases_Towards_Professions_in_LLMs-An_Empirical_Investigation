The biting November wind whipped across the cracked asphalt of the deserted high school track, carrying with it the scent of damp leaves and the faint, lingering aroma of chlorine from the nearby pool.  The skeletal branches of the ancient oak trees clawed at the bruised twilight sky, their silhouettes stark against the dying light.  Amelia “Amy” Katherine Davies, head coach of the Northwood High Wolverines track and field team, pulled her worn, navy blue windbreaker tighter around her.  Her breath plumed out in ragged white clouds, mirroring the exhaustion that settled deep in her bones after another grueling day of practice.  The stadium lights hummed a lonely tune, casting long, distorted shadows that danced with the swirling leaves.  This wasn't the glamorous world of televised championships; this was the quiet, often thankless reality of shaping young athletes, one blister and one aching muscle at a time.
