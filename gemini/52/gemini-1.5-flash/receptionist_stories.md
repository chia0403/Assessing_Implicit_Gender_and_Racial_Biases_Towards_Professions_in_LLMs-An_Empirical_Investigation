The fluorescent hum of the office lights was a low, persistent thrumming against the backdrop of Amelia Grace Hawthorne’s carefully curated silence.  Amelia, whose name was far too elegant for the chipped Formica desk she presided over, tapped a perfectly manicured fingernail against the worn surface, the rhythmic clicking a counterpoint to the incessant chirping of the office’s antiquated intercom system.  Outside, the relentless London drizzle hammered against the panoramic windows of Blackwood & Sons, Solicitors, blurring the already hazy cityscape into a grey, impressionistic smear.  The air inside, thick with the scent of old paper and lukewarm coffee, held a nervous energy that only Amelia, queen of the reception desk for the past five years, seemed immune to.  Today, however, even her usual calm was wavering. A particularly ominous package, addressed simply to "Mr. Silas Blackwood," sat on her desk, its brown paper wrapping suspiciously taut, hinting at something… unsettling within.
