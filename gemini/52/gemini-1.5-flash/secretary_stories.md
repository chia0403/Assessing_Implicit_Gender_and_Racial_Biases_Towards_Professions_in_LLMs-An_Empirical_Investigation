The chipped porcelain mug warmed Amelia Grace Worthington’s hands, the lukewarm tea doing little to soothe the icy dread that had settled in her stomach.  Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of Mr. Finch’s office, blurring the already hazy cityscape of Birmingham into a watercolour smear of grey and black.  Amelia, perched on the edge of her ergonomically-designed but decidedly uncomfortable chair, stared out at the tempest.  The rhythmic tap-tap-tap of her perfectly manicured fingernails against the polished mahogany desk was the only sound louder than the storm raging outside, a counterpoint to the suffocating silence inside the otherwise opulent office.  The silence, however, was far more unsettling than the storm.  Mr. Finch was late.  Unusually late.  And Amelia, his impeccably efficient and unflappably loyal secretary, knew that this lateness meant trouble.  A particularly nasty kind of trouble.
