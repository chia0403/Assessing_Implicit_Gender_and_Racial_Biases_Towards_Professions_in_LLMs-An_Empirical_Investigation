The wind, a rasping whisper against the parched earth, carried the scent of sagebrush and something else, something acrid and unfamiliar.  Dust devils, miniature tornadoes of red dirt, danced across the cracked expanse of the Bar None Ranch, their frantic twirls mirroring the turmoil in Jedediah "Jed" Stone's gut.  The sun, a malevolent eye in the brassy sky, beat down mercilessly on the endless miles of withered grass, baking the already brittle land.  Jed, his face etched with the deep lines of sun and hardship, squinted against its glare, his hand instinctively going to the worn brim of his Stetson.  The Bar None, his family's legacy for generations, was drying up, both literally and metaphorically, and the drought, relentless and unforgiving, threatened to swallow it whole.
