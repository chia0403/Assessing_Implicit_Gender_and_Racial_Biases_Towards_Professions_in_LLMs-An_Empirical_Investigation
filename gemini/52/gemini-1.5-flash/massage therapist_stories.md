The aroma of sandalwood and bergamot hung heavy in the air, a comforting blanket against the relentless November drizzle lashing against the leaded-glass windows of Serenity Now Massage Therapy.  Rain hammered a frantic rhythm on the slate roof, a counterpoint to the soft, rhythmic squeak of Elara Vance’s knuckles as she kneaded tension from a client’s tight shoulders.  Elara, her name a whispered promise of calm, had the kind of hands that seemed to understand the language of muscle and bone, coaxing knots loose with a gentle persistence that bordered on magic.  The cozy, candlelit room, decorated with calming earth tones and the soft glow of Himalayan salt lamps, was a sanctuary, a refuge from the grey, blustery world outside. Tonight, however, even the sanctuary felt threatened.  A sudden, sharp crack of thunder echoed through the old Victorian building, followed by a power cut that plunged the room into darkness, leaving only the faint glimmer of the dying candles.
