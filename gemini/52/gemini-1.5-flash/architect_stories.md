The biting November wind whipped Amelia Katherine Hayes's charcoal-grey coat around her legs as she stood perched precariously on the scaffolding, the city of Prague sprawling beneath her like a meticulously crafted toy.  Gargoyles, leering from the rooftops of centuries-old buildings, seemed to watch her with ancient, knowing eyes.  Below, the Vltava River snaked its way through the heart of the old town, its surface reflecting the bruised purple of the twilight sky.  The air, thick with the scent of woodsmoke and roasted chestnuts, carried the faint, almost imperceptible hum of a city that never truly slept.  Amelia, her breath misting in the cold, tightened her grip on her blueprints, the crisp paper crinkling in her gloved hand.  This wasn't just another renovation; this was the restoration of a lifetime, a chance to mend the broken heart of a city, one meticulously placed stone at a time.  The weight of history, and the daunting task ahead, pressed down on her shoulders heavier than any load-bearing beam.
