The relentless, humid air of Bangkok hung heavy, thick with the scent of jasmine and exhaust fumes.  Sweat beaded on the forehead of Dr. Anya Sharma, clinging to the crisp white cotton of her blouse as she navigated the chaotic tangle of motorcycles and tuk-tuks on Sukhumvit Road.  The midday sun beat down mercilessly, turning the city's concrete jungle into a shimmering oven. Anya, a registered dietitian with a specialization in Southeast Asian cuisine, adjusted the strap of her worn leather satchel, its contents – a battered copy of "Thai Food for Healthy Living" and a notebook overflowing with scribbled notes – jostling against her ribs.  She wasn't here for a relaxing vacation, though;  this sweltering heat was the backdrop to a mission far more challenging than any nutritional seminar she'd ever led.  She was in Bangkok to unravel a mystery that could have far-reaching consequences for the health of millions.
