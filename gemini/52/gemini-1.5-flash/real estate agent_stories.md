The humid Charleston air hung heavy, thick with the scent of jasmine and impending rain.  Cicadas buzzed a relentless symphony from the ancient oaks that draped their moss-laden branches over the wrought-iron gates of Magnolia Row.  Amelia “Mia” Davenport, a real estate agent whose smile could charm the birds from the trees (and often did, judging by the quantity of chirping around her), adjusted the crisp white linen of her summer dress.  The impending storm mirrored the turmoil brewing inside her.  This wasn't just another million-dollar listing; this was *the* listing – the sprawling antebellum mansion, "Grey Gables," perched precariously on the bluff overlooking the Ashley River, a house steeped in history and, according to local legend, a generous helping of ghosts.
