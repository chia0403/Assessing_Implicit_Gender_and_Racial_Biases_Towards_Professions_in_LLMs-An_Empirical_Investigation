The biting wind howled a mournful song through the skeletal branches of the ancient Douglas firs, their needles frosted with a layer of rime that crunched like shattered glass under Jedediah "Jed" Stone's heavy boots.  The air hung thick with the scent of pine and damp earth, a familiar perfume that clung to Jed like a second skin after fifty years spent amongst these giants.  He stood on the precipice of Widow's Peak, a treacherous slope overlooking the churning grey waters of Lost Lake, his axe leaning against a snow-laden cedar, its polished head gleaming faintly in the weak winter sunlight.  The logging camp, a collection of ramshackle cabins huddled together for warmth against the unforgiving wilderness, lay nestled a mile below, a cluster of faint, smoky breaths against the vast, snow-covered landscape of the Cascade Mountains.
