The biting November wind whipped Dr. Eleanor Vance’s auburn hair across her face as she hurried across the rain-slicked quad of Ashwood High.  Leaden skies mirrored the grim mood that seemed to have settled over the sprawling, gothic-style building; a mood she, as the school psychologist, was acutely aware of.  Gargoyles perched menacingly from the eaves, their stone faces seeming to leer down at the hurried students below, their breaths puffing white clouds in the frigid air.  The scent of damp earth and decaying leaves mingled with the faint, metallic tang of fear – a scent Eleanor had become disturbingly familiar with over her five years at Ashwood.  Tonight, however, the fear felt different, thicker, heavier, clinging to the very air itself.  A fresh wave of anxiety washed over her as she pulled her coat tighter, the impending meeting with the principal already a knot of dread in her stomach.  This wasn't just another disciplinary issue; this was…something else entirely.
