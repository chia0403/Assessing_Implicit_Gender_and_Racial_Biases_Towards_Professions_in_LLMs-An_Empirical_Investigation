The biting wind whipped Dr. Eleanor Vance’s long, dark coat around her legs as she hurried across the deserted cobblestone square.  The skeletal branches of the ancient oaks clawed at the bruised twilight sky above the village of Oakhaven, their silhouettes stark against the bruised purple and angry orange of the setting sun.  A thick, clinging mist, smelling faintly of brine and decay, clung to the weathered stone buildings, their gabled roofs hunched like weary shoulders beneath the weight of the approaching storm.  The only sound louder than the wind was the rhythmic clang of the iron bell in the distant church tower, a mournful toll that seemed to echo the desolate mood of the village itself. Oakhaven, nestled deep within a valley shrouded by the brooding peaks of the Mourne Mountains, had always been a place steeped in mystery, but tonight, it felt especially… haunted.
