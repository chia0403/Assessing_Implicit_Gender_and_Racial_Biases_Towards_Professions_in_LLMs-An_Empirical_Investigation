The biting November wind whipped Amelia Hernandez’s auburn hair across her face as she hurried down Bleecker Street, the scent of roasting chestnuts and damp leaves clinging to the air.  Rain, a fine, persistent drizzle, plastered her charcoal-grey coat to her back.  Amelia, a graphic designer with a penchant for vintage finds and an even stronger penchant for strong coffee, usually found solace in the chaotic beauty of Greenwich Village.  Tonight, however, the usual charm felt muted, overshadowed by the gnawing anxiety churning in her stomach.  Her deadline for the Reimagining Roma campaign – a project she’d poured her heart and soul into for the past three months – loomed like a storm cloud, threatening to unleash a deluge of criticism from her notoriously demanding client, Signor Bellini. The flickering neon signs of the surrounding shops cast long, distorted shadows, mirroring the unsettling uncertainty swirling within her.
