The wrench slipped in Amelia Hernandez's grease-stained grip, the metallic clang echoing unnaturally loud in the cavernous silence of Hernandez Auto Repair.  Dust motes, illuminated by a single bare bulb hanging precariously above her workbench, danced in the shafts of moonlight filtering through the grimy windows.  Outside, the desert wind howled a mournful song, rattling the corrugated iron siding of the ramshackle building that served as both her workplace and her home, nestled on the outskirts of the forgotten town of Dustbowl, Arizona. The air, thick with the scent of burnt oil and the metallic tang of fear, held a stillness that was far more unsettling than the usual cacophony of engine groans and hammering that normally filled her nights.  Tonight, only the wind and the ghosts of past repairs kept her company.
