The chipped mug warmed Amelia “Mia” Holloway’s hands, the lukewarm tea doing little to chase away the November chill that seeped through the drafty window of her attic office.  Rain lashed against the ancient, leaded panes, mirroring the storm brewing inside her.  Dust motes, illuminated by the single, bare bulb hanging precariously above her cluttered desk, swirled in the weak light like restless spirits.  Mia, editor-in-chief of the obscure but fiercely independent literary journal *The Quill and Inkpot*, surveyed her domain with a sigh.  Books, manuscripts, and rejection slips overflowed every available surface, creating a precarious ecosystem of paper and ambition in the cramped space.  The air hung heavy with the scent of old paper, damp wool, and the faint, lingering aroma of Earl Grey – a testament to countless late nights spent wrestling with prose and the vagaries of aspiring authors. Tonight, however, the aroma was overshadowed by the sharp, metallic tang of impending disaster.  A manuscript, the most promising she’d seen in years, lay open on her desk, its pages stained with a dark, spreading blot – a blot that wasn't ink.
