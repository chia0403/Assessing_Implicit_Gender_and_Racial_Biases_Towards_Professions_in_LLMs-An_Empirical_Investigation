The biting November wind whipped at Elias Thorne’s exposed neck, stinging the raw skin under his stubble.  He pulled his worn denim jacket tighter, the scent of sawdust and diesel clinging to the thick fabric like a second skin.  Above him, the skeletal frame of the new city hall clawed at the bruised, twilight sky, a monument to ambition and poured concrete – and Elias’s increasingly aching back.  The construction site sprawled across three city blocks, a chaotic symphony of clanking metal, roaring engines, and the shouts of men battling the encroaching darkness and the ever-present threat of deadlines.  Dust, thick and gritty, coated everything, a fine film over the already grimy city that lay slumbering beneath the half-finished behemoth.
