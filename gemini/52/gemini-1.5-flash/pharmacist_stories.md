The chipped porcelain of the apothecary’s mortar felt cool beneath Elara Vance’s fingertips, a familiar comfort in the chaotic symphony of the Nightingale Pharmacy.  Rain lashed against the tall, gothic windows, blurring the gaslights of Oakhaven into hazy halos.  The air, thick with the scent of chamomile and eucalyptus, was punctuated by the sharp tang of iodine and the insistent cough of a man wrestling with a particularly virulent case of bronchitis in the waiting area.  Elara, her dark hair pulled back in a severe bun, meticulously measured powdered aconite, the amber light catching the fine dust motes swirling in the air like tiny, malevolent stars.  Outside, the wind howled a mournful dirge, a fitting soundtrack to the anxieties that seemed to cling to the damp stone walls of the ancient pharmacy like clinging ivy.
