The chipped paint of the fire escape flaked onto Dr. Eleanor Vance’s sensible beige shoes as she leaned precariously over the railing, the scent of frying onions and desperation clinging to the humid Brooklyn air. Below, the cacophony of sirens warred with the rhythmic thwack of a bodega owner’s cleaver, a soundtrack to the simmering anxieties of her patients, anxieties that often manifested not in their hearts but on their plates.  Eleanor, a registered dietitian with a specialty in disordered eating, lived and breathed the frantic pulse of her vibrant, yet often unhealthy, neighborhood.  This wasn't some sterile clinic; her office was a cramped, sun-drenched apartment overlooking the relentless energy of Bedford Avenue, a constant reminder of the battles waged daily against the tyranny of sugar and the seductive whispers of processed foods.
