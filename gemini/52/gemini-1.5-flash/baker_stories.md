The pre-dawn chill bit at Elara Genevieve Hawthorne's cheeks as she unlocked the bakery doors.  A thin veil of mist clung to the cobblestones of Lavender Lane, blurring the gaslights into hazy halos.  The air, still heavy with the scent of woodsmoke and damp earth, was slowly yielding to the sweeter, more intoxicating aroma that spilled from Elara's shop, "The Flour Child."  Inside, the warmth radiated from the ancient brick oven, a comforting counterpoint to the biting November wind that howled a mournful tune around the gables of the quaint, half-timbered houses lining the lane.  This was Oakhaven, a town steeped in history and secrets, a place where the past lingered like the scent of gingerbread lingering on a baker's apron, and where Elara, with flour dusting her eyelashes and a lifetime of recipes in her heart, was about to begin another day.
