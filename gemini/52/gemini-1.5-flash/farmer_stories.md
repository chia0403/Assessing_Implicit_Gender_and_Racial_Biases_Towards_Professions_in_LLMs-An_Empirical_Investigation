The wind, a rasping, icy hand, clawed at Elias Thorne's weathered face as he surveyed his frost-bitten fields.  Dawn painted the sky in bruised purples and angry oranges, a stark contrast to the lifeless grey of the wheat stalks bowed low under the weight of the unrelenting winter.  Elias, his breath misting in the frigid air, pulled his worn wool cap lower over his ears, the thick fabric doing little to ward off the penetrating chill.  His farm, Thorne’s End, nestled in the valley below the jagged peaks of the Whisperwind Mountains, was a haven of stubborn resilience, clinging to life despite the harshness of the unforgiving landscape.  This year, however, even Elias’s renowned tenacity was being tested to its limits.  The winter had been brutal, the harshest in living memory, and the meager harvest had left him with little more than worry lines etched deep into his brow and a gnawing fear in his gut.
