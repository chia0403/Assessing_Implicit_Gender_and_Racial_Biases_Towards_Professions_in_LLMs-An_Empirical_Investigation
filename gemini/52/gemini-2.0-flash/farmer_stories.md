The wind, a relentless, biting hand, whipped across the endless expanse of his fields, turning the golden stubble left over from the harvest into a swirling, dusty haze. Ezekiel "Zeke" Bartholomew traced the furrows with his worn boot, his breath misting in the frigid air. The setting sun bled across the horizon, painting the sky in hues of bruised purple and fiery orange, a stark contrast to the monotone browns and greys that dominated the land.

This was Havenwood Valley, nestled between the jagged peaks of the Whisperwind Mountains and the murky waters of the Slow Creek. A place carved out by generations of farmers, men and women who had stubbornly wrung a living from the unforgiving soil. The air hung heavy with the scent of damp earth and the promise of winter, a promise Zeke knew Havenwood Valley always kept. It was a beautiful, brutal place, and Ezekiel Bartholomew, farmer, born and bred, knew every inch of it. Today, however, the beauty felt more like a taunt, and the brutality a looming threat. He had a feeling in his gut, a deep, unsettling churn, that told him this winter would be unlike any he had seen before.
