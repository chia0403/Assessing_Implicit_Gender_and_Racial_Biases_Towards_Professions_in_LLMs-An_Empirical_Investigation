The late afternoon sun, filtered through the dusty blinds of his office, cast stripes across the tired face of Theodore Maxwell Cartwright. He leaned back in his squeaky chair, the springs groaning a mournful tune, and rubbed the bridge of his nose. The air hung thick with the ghost of teenage angst, a potent blend of Axe body spray, desperation, and the lingering scent of microwaved pizza from the student lounge. Another day at Northwood High, another tidal wave of college applications, parental anxieties, and the ever-present existential dread of impending adulthood, had washed over him. Mr. Cartwright, Guidance Counselor, sighed. He was bone-weary. He just wanted to go home, put his feet up, and maybe, just maybe, lose himself in a good documentary about competitive cheese sculpting.
