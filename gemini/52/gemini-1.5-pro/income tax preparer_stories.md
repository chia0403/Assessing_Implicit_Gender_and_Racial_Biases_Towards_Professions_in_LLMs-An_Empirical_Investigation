Rain lashed against the window of Amelia Persephone Higgins's tiny office, mimicking the frantic drumming of her fingertips on the worn desktop.  Outside, the neon glow of "Larry's Liquors" reflected in the puddles gathering on the cracked asphalt of the Northwood strip mall parking lot.  Inside, a single flickering fluorescent bulb cast a sickly yellow light over stacks of tax forms, receipts, and half-eaten bags of cheese puffs.  Tax season was nearing its end, but for Amelia, the real chaos was just beginning.  A new client, a Mr. Bartholomew Quibble, had walked in an hour ago with a shoebox overflowing with what he claimed were deductible expenses, including a receipt for a llama farm visit and another for a lifetime supply of glitter.  Amelia sighed, pushing a stray curl out of her eye. This was going to be a long night.
