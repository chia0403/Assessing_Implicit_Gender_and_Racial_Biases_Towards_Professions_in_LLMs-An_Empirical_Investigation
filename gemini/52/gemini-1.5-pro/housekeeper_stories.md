The biting November wind whipped around Agnes Periwinkle, stinging her cheeks as she wrestled the heavy oak door of Eventide Manor closed.  Dead leaves, the colour of burnt toffee, swirled around her ankles. Eventide perched precariously on a cliff overlooking the churning grey expanse of the North Sea, a gothic monstrosity of turrets and gargoyles. It was a magnificent house, Agnes had to admit, but magnificent in the way a snarling wolf might be magnificent - beautiful, yes, but undeniably dangerous.  The salty air permeated everything, leaving a fine film of grit on the towering windows and a perpetual tang on Agnes' tongue.  She pulled her threadbare shawl tighter around her shoulders, the damp chill seeping into her bones despite her efforts.  Inside, a single light flickered in the vastness of the entrance hall, beckoning her into the echoing silence that held more secrets than dust motes.
