The biting wind whipped off the jagged peaks of the Dragon's Tooth mountains, carrying with it the scent of pine and the faint, metallic tang of snow.  The sky, a bruised purple, promised another blizzard before nightfall. Elias Thorne, his calloused hands already numb despite the thick wool gloves, squinted at the granite face before him.  He stood on a narrow ledge, carved into the mountainside itself, high above the valley floor where the village of Oakhaven huddled like a clutch of brown mushrooms against the encroaching winter.  His breath plumed white in the frigid air as he traced the lines he'd painstakingly etched into the stone, the blueprint for his next masterpiece: a griffon, wings outstretched, poised to take flight from the mountain's heart.  The work was dangerous, solitary, and often thankless. But for Elias, there was no other life. The mountain was his canvas, the granite his clay, and the biting wind his constant companion.
