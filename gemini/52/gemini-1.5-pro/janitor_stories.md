The flickering fluorescent lights of the Zenith Laboratories sub-basement buzzed like trapped insects, their pale glow barely penetrating the corners where dust bunnies gathered like clandestine meetings.  The air hung thick with the metallic tang of disinfectant and something else, something vaguely chemical and unsettling that clung to the back of Elias Thorne's throat.  Elias, his name tag slightly askew on his faded blue uniform, pushed his mop bucket across the cracked concrete floor, the squeaking wheels a mournful soundtrack to his solitary task. It was 3:17 AM, the witching hour in the otherwise bustling research facility, a time when the scientists retreated to their brightly lit labs upstairs and left Elias to commune with the shadows and the secrets they held.  Tonight, however, the silence was different.  It was heavier, pregnant with an unnerving stillness that prickled Elias's skin. He stopped mopping, the dripping strands of his mop suspended mid-air, and listened.
