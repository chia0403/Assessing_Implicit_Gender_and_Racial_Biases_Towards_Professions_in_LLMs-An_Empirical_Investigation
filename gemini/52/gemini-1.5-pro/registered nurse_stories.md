The fluorescent lights of St. Jude's Hospital Emergency Room hummed a monotonous tune, punctuated by the rhythmic beeping of heart monitors and the hurried shuffle of rubber-soled shoes.  The air, thick with the metallic tang of blood and the antiseptic sting of disinfectant, clung to Eleanor Vance's scrubs like a second skin. Rain lashed against the windows, mirroring the tempest brewing inside Eleanor as she surveyed the controlled chaos around her. A gurney bearing a young boy with a fractured femur screeched to a halt a few feet away, while across the room, a man clutching his chest doubled over in pain.  It was a Tuesday night, but in the ER, every night felt like a Friday night gone wrong.  Eleanor, a Registered Nurse with ten years under her belt, braced herself.  This was just the beginning of her shift.
