Rain lashed against the panoramic windows of Amelia Vance’s forty-second-floor office, the city lights blurring into a shimmering, impressionistic canvas.  Amelia, Chief Operating Officer of Vance International, tapped a manicured nail against the cool glass, a frown etching itself between her perfectly arched brows.  The Chicago skyline, usually a source of invigorating energy, tonight reflected her own internal turmoil.  The mahogany desk behind her, usually pristine, was a chaotic landscape of spreadsheets, market reports, and half-empty coffee cups.  The air hung heavy with the scent of burnt coffee and impending crisis.  Her father, the formidable CEO and founder of the company, had summoned her for a meeting first thing the next morning, and the cryptic nature of his message – “Amelia, we need to talk.  It’s about the future.” – had left her with a knot of unease that tightened with each passing gust of wind.
