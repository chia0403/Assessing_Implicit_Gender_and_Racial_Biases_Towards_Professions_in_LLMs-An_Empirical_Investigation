The bell above the door of "Nailed It," a small but impeccably clean salon tucked between a laundromat and a struggling Vietnamese restaurant, jingled merrily.  Rain lashed against the large front window, blurring the neon "OPEN" sign into a hazy glow.  Inside, Eliza Grace Hawthorne, her fiery red hair pulled back in a neat bun, sighed, glancing at the empty appointment book.  It was a Tuesday afternoon in November, the kind of dreary day that even the most dedicated beauty enthusiasts opted to stay home and binge-watch reality TV. The air smelled of acetone and something vaguely floral, a scent Eliza had long stopped noticing.  Outside, the grey Portland sky pressed down on the rain-slicked streets, reflecting the glum mood settling over Eliza as she idly buffed a chipped, flamingo-pink nail on her own hand.
