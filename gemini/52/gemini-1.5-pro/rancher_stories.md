The wind, sharp and smelling of impending snow, whipped Amelia “Amy” Grace Holloway’s dark braid against her cheek as she surveyed the eastern fence line of the Broken Spur Ranch.  Twenty thousand acres of rolling Wyoming grassland stretched before her, dusted with early November frost, the tips of the distant Tetons just visible above the swirling grey clouds. The ranch, nestled in the valley carved by the Little Wind River, had been in Amy’s family for five generations, each one weathering harsher winters and leaner years than the last. This winter, however, promised to be different. The unease that had settled in her gut with the first migrating geese hadn’t lifted, a feeling as heavy and cold as the iron brand she carried in her saddlebag.
