The biting November wind whipped Amelia "Amy" Hawthorne's auburn braid across her face as she trudged across the frozen mud of the Yorkshire Moors.  The sky, a bruised purple-grey, threatened snow.  Ravens croaked overhead, their calls echoing across the bleak landscape. Amy pulled her thick wool coat tighter around her, the scent of damp earth and pine needles clinging to the air.  She wasn't headed home to the warm hearth of her cottage; her destination was the sprawling, stone-walled sanctuary known as Wildfell Manor for Injured Wildlife, a place both haven and hospital for creatures great and small.  Amy, the head caretaker, knew each and every one of its residents, from the orphaned badger cubs nestled in their burrow to the majestic golden eagle recovering from a broken wing in the aviary.  Tonight, however, a new arrival awaited her, and a shiver of anticipation, mingled with apprehension, ran down her spine.
