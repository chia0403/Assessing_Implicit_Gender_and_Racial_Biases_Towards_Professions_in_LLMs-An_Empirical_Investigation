The biting November wind whipped Amelia Grace Hawthorne's auburn braid across her face as she hurried up the cracked concrete steps of the Withering Pines Elder Care Facility.  A rusted wrought-iron sign, shaped like a forlorn, drooping pine tree, creaked ominously above the entrance.  The sky, a bruised purple-grey, mirrored the grim facade of the building. Withering Pines, nestled deep in the Catskill Mountains, had seen better days.  Decades of harsh winters and dwindling funds had left it looking more like a gothic asylum than a place of healing and respite.  Amelia, however, saw beyond the peeling paint and overgrown ivy.  She saw the potential for connection, the opportunity to bring light and purpose into the lives of the residents within.  Today, though, a knot of unease tightened in her stomach.  Mrs. Petrov, one of her most cherished patients, had taken a turn for the worse overnight, and Amelia felt a premonition that this crisp autumn morning held more than just the usual routines.
