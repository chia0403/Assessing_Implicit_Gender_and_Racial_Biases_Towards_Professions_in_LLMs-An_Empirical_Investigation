The biting wind whipped across the frost-covered fields of Abernathy Farm, stinging Elias Thorne's cheeks as he surveyed his land.  The first rays of a pale winter sun, struggling through the iron-grey sky, illuminated the skeletal remains of his corn stalks, now brittle and brown.  Abernathy Farm, nestled in the rolling hills of the Derbyshire Dales, had been in Elias's family for five generations, each one wrestling a living from the unforgiving, yet breathtakingly beautiful, landscape.  This year, however, felt different. The frost had come earlier than usual, clinging to the earth with a tenacity that hinted at a long, harsh winter.  Elias pulled his thick wool coat tighter around him, a knot of unease tightening in his stomach.  He had a feeling this winter wasn't just going to be cold; it was going to be hungry.
