The fluorescent lights of the Weathersby, Finch, and Higgins law office hummed a monotonous B flat, a soundtrack Amelia Grace Holloway had grown accustomed to over the past five years. Rain lashed against the panoramic windows overlooking downtown Charleston, blurring the steeple of St. Michael's Church into a watery smudge.  The scent of stale coffee and impending deadlines hung heavy in the air, a familiar aroma in the perpetually overworked office.  Amelia, perched on the edge of her worn leather chair, sifted through a mountain of depositions related to the ongoing Pettigrew Plantation land dispute, a case that had become her personal Everest. Outside, the cobblestone streets gleamed under the downpour, and the Spanish moss hanging from the live oaks dripped like mournful widows. Inside, the clock ticked relentlessly towards five, a beacon of hope in a sea of legal jargon.
