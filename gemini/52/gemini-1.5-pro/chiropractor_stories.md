The neon "Open" sign buzzed erratically, casting a sickly green glow across the rain-slicked asphalt of Elm Street.  Inside, Dr. Elias Thorne, D.C.,  sighed, the sound swallowed by the rhythmic drumming of the downpour against the large plate-glass window of Thorne Chiropractic.  The clock on the wall ticked towards 8:00 pm, the usual closing time, but Elias wasn’t packing up. He rarely did anymore.  The small town of Havenwood, nestled deep in the Oregon wilderness, had a surprising lack of back pain sufferers, or perhaps they just preferred the dubious ministrations of old Mrs. Hemlock, the town’s resident “bone-setter” who operated out of a ramshackle cabin on the outskirts.  Elias ran a hand through his thick, dark hair, the fluorescent lights glinting off the faint silver streaks at his temples. The silence in his meticulously clean office, broken only by the occasional groan of the building settling, was starting to feel less like peace and more like a tomb. Tonight, however, something felt different.  A low growl of thunder rumbled in the distance, and a chill, sharper than the autumn air, snaked through the room. Elias glanced towards the door, a strange premonition tightening in his chest.
