The crimson velvet curtains of the Moulin Rouge quivered, a silent prelude to the eruption of music and light that was about to engulf the Parisian night.  The air, thick with the scent of absinthe and anticipation, crackled with an electric energy.  Outside, the cobbled streets of Montmartre, slick with a recent rain, reflected the gaslights in a shimmering mosaic.  But inside, the world existed only within the confines of the famed cabaret.  It was here, in this crucible of dreams and desires, that Seraphina Moreau, her heart hammering a frantic rhythm against her ribs, prepared to take the stage.
