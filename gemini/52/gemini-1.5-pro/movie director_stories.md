The biting November wind whipped around Anastasia “Stasia” Volkov, plastering strands of her fiery red hair across her face. She tugged her thick wool coat tighter, the expensive fabric offering little solace against the Chicago chill.  The skeletal branches of the oak trees lining Michigan Avenue clawed at the steel-grey sky, mirroring the anxiety clawing at her insides. Stasia squinted at the imposing façade of the Chicago Theatre, its ornate terracotta detailing a stark contrast to the gritty reality of the pre-dawn hour.  This wasn't a premiere, not a celebratory screening. This was a meeting, a last-ditch effort to salvage her dream, her vision, her magnum opus: "The Clockwork Heart."
