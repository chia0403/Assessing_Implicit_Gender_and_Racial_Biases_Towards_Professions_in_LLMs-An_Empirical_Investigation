The biting November wind whipped off the Puget Sound, carrying the scent of brine and cedar, and rattling the mismatched panes of The Salty Spoon's front window.  Inside, the air was thick with the aroma of simmering cioppino and baking sourdough.  Amelia "Amy" Reyes, her dark hair pulled back in a messy bun, surveyed her domain with a critical eye.  The Salty Spoon, perched precariously on a bluff overlooking the churning grey water, was her life's work, a testament to years of scrimping, saving, and sheer stubborn determination.  It wasn't much – just a handful of tables crammed into a space that had once been a bait shop – but it was hers.  And tonight, the night of the annual Fishermen's Feast, it was going to be packed.
