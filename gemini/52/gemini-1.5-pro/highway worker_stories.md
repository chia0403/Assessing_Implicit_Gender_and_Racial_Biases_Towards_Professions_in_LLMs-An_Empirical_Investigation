The biting wind whipped across the desolate stretch of I-95, stinging Elias "Eli" Vance's cheeks even through the thick knit of his orange beanie.  Pre-dawn darkness still clung to the Virginia countryside, the only illumination coming from the pulsating amber lights of his work truck and the distant, ghostly glow of Richmond, thirty miles south. Eli, his breath fogging in the frigid air, stamped his feet against the asphalt, trying to ward off the creeping numbness.  He was perched precariously on the edge of the highway shoulder, a sheer drop to a ravine on one side, the rush of early morning traffic a constant, vibrating threat on the other.  His job today: replace a series of damaged reflector markers along this particularly treacherous curve, a job made infinitely harder by the black ice shimmering invisibly beneath a thin dusting of snow.
