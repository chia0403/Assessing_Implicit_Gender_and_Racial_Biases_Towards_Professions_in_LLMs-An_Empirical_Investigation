Rain lashed against the grimy windows of The Daily Clarion's office, mirroring the tempest brewing inside Amelia "Mia" Rodriguez.  Fluorescent lights hummed overhead, casting a sickly yellow glow on the cluttered desks and overflowing ashtrays. Cigarette smoke, cheap coffee, and the faint scent of mildew hung heavy in the air. Plymouth, Pennsylvania, a town whose heyday as a coal mining hub had long since passed, seemed to be perpetually shrouded in a film of grit and despair.  It was the kind of place where ambitions went to die, and yet, here Mia was, hunched over her typewriter, two years into a career that felt less like journalism and more like chronicling the slow, agonizing decline of a forgotten town. Tonight, however, was different.  A crumpled note sat on her desk, the ink bleeding slightly from the rain that had seeped through the cracked windowpane.  It contained a single, chilling sentence: "They aren't accidents."
