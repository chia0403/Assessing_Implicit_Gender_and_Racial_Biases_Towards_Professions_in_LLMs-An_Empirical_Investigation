Rain lashed against the window of "Wanderscape Travel," mimicking the tempest brewing inside Penelope "Penny" Ramirez.  The fluorescent lights hummed a monotonous tune, at odds with the frantic tapping of Penny's fingers on her keyboard. Outside, the Chicago wind howled through the canyons of steel and glass, a familiar soundtrack to her life.  The agency, tucked between a bustling pizzeria and a dry cleaner with perpetually steamed windows, was usually a haven of brightly coloured brochures and the tantalizing aroma of far-off destinations. Today, however, the air hung heavy with the scent of stale coffee and impending disaster.  A cancelled Antarctic expedition, a misplaced honeymoon couple in Bora Bora, and now, the insistent buzzing of her phone promised another travel catastrophe about to unfold.
