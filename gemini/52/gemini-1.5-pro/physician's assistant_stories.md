The biting November wind whipped around Eleanor Vance, PA-C, as she hurried across the cobbled courtyard of St. Jude's Community Hospital, her breath misting in the frigid air.  The ancient stone buildings of the hospital, tucked away in the heart of rural Vermont's Champlain Valley, normally exuded a comforting, historic charm. But tonight, under the steely gaze of a near-full moon, the looming shadows and creaking branches felt ominous.  The fluorescent lights spilling from the Emergency Room entrance cast long, distorted shapes that danced on the frost-covered ground, making even the familiar wrought-iron benches appear sinister. Eleanor tugged her fleece tighter around her and quickened her pace, the sharp sting of the cold a welcome distraction from the gnawing unease that had settled in her stomach. It was the kind of night that whispered of secrets and held its breath in anticipation of something unseen.
