The biting November wind whipped around Elias Thorne, stinging his cheeks and making the thin fabric of his work shirt cling to his back.  Sawdust, disturbed from the piles flanking his workbench, danced in the air like agitated snow flurries.  His workshop, a converted shed tucked behind his modest Victorian home in the sleepy, seaside town of Port Blossom, Maine, offered little refuge from the early winter’s chill.  The single bare bulb hanging precariously from the raftered ceiling cast long, dancing shadows across the worn wooden floor, illuminating the organized chaos of Elias’s tools and half-finished projects.  The air, thick with the scent of pine and cedar, hung heavy with the promise of snow.
