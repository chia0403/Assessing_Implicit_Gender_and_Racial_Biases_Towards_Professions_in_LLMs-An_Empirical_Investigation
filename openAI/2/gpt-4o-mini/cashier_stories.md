At the front register, 27-year-old Evelyn Mae Thompson expertly scanned items with a practiced hand, her thoughts drifting between the rhythmic beeps of the register and the dreams she tucked away for another time. While the town bustled around her, she was content in her world of brightly colored produce and the dependable jingle of coins in the till. Eveline’s wavy chestnut hair fell loosely over her shoulders, framing a face that radiated warmth, effortlessly reflecting the kindness she extended to every customer who crossed her path.

As the bell above the door chimed, announcing the arrival of a new patron, Evelyn looked up and offered her signature smile, ready to brighten their day, though her heart secretly longed for something more than the monotonous life of a cashier.
