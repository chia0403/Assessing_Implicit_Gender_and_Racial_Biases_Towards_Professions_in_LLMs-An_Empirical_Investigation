**Title: The Cracked Spine of Maplewood**

**Setting:**  
Maplewood, a picturesque small town nestled between rolling hills and lush forests, is known for its charming cobblestone streets and vibrant community. The heart of this town is a quaint, inviting chiropractic clinic located on the corner of Elm Street and Willow Lane. The walls of the clinic are painted a calming shade of light blue, adorned with framed photos of happy patients and certificates that testify to the expertise of its owner. Sunlight filters through the large windows, illuminating the waiting area filled with potted plants and comfortable chairs.

**Opening:**

As the morning sun spilled through the windows, casting a warm glow across his office, Dr. Benjamin Carter adjusted his glasses and reviewed the day's schedule. The soft hum of the radio playing classic jazz provided a soothing backdrop as he mentally prepared for his first patient. Dr. Carter, a chiropractor with a reputation for blending traditional techniques with holistic practices, had made a name for himself in Maplewood. He loved the way his work allowed him to transform lives, alleviating pain and restoring mobility one adjustment at a time.

Today, however, felt different. Beneath the routine of his daily practice lingered an air of unease. Benjamin couldn’t shake the feeling that something—or someone—was about to disrupt the tranquility of his small-town life. A recent series of mysterious occurrences around Maplewood, from strange disappearances to whispered rumors, had been the talk of the town, filling the air with apprehension. Little did he know that his day was about to take a turn more unexpected than any misalignment he had ever encountered.

He glanced out the window to see Mrs. Aldridge, his elderly neighbor, struggling to carry a basket of laundry upstairs. He smiled at the sight, appreciating the unassuming cadence of his mornings, but in the back of his mind, he couldn’t ignore the nagging thought that change was inevitable. Today, in this cocoon of routine and comfort, was just the calm before the storm.
