Along a stretch of Highway 47, where the jagged mountains met the sprawling desert, the echoes of distant tires mingled with the rhythmic clinking of tools. Tobias, tall and lean with sun-kissed skin and hair that tousled in the wind, had spent countless hours under the vast, open sky — a canvas constantly shifting with colors and moods. His olive-green uniform, adorned with reflective strips that shimmered in the light, told a story of toil and commitment. He loved his job, despite the challenges, finding a peculiar joy in the simple act of bringing order to the chaotic roads that connected small towns to the bustling cities beyond.

As he carefully applied a patch to the surface of the highway, he couldn’t help but let his mind wander. The sound of roaring engines drew him away from the sweat beneath his collar and the repetitive motions of his work. Today, however, was different. There was a sense of foreboding in the air, an unshakeable feeling that something was about to shift, like the first tremors of an earthquake just beneath the earth's surface.
