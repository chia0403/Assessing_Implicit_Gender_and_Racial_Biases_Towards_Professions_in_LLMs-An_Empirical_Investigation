Once upon a time, there was a butcher named Jim," said Mr. Spagnolo. "A really good one." The conversation moved on but Mr. Spagnolo continued, "We had to drive for hours to get to his house, sometimes stopping at a couple of towns that I can't remember. He had a big family of six or seven, a wife and five or six kids."

Mr. Spagnolo said, "I'm pretty sure if we talked, he'd tell me what the hell it was all about."

The father and son would talk for hours, describing the local market, which they called "Johanna's Market" because of the chicken that came through it. And, by the end of the day, the father would have told Jim the kind of meat that J.S. was proud of.
