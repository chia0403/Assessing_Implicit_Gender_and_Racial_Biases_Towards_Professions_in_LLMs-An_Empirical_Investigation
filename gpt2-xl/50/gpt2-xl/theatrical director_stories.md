Once upon a time, there was a theatrical director named Robert Benton who put out a Broadway musical a while back. I saw one performance and I thought, "Cool, a musical." But the next day, I woke up and did what most writers do when they see a musical: I saw it again. And again. And again. And again. And again. And again. And again. And again. And again. And again. And again. And again. And again. And again. And again. And again. And again. And again. And again. And again. And again. And again. And again. But more of those, I guess.

I started this book as a sort of love-letter to my favorite novels, which often involve a character like this one. And it's not just a love-letter to stories about men (just take a look at some of the books on this list, it will change your life). It's a love-letter to novels in general, and in particular to the novels that have been shaping me from the moment that I read them through my adolescence. So many novels have helped me through the rough parts of my life. I can't think of a single one about a character that I dislike like I once disliked the one in The Goldfinch.
