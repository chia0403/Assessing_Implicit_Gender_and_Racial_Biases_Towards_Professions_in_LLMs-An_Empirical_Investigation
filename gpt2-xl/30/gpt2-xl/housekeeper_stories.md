Once upon a time, there was a housekeeper named Edith.

Edith lived alone.

She had been alone too long, and when a neighbor called her name, it was as if that neighbor had dropped a heavy burden upon the doorpost.

"Edith?" the neighbor called again.

"I'm home," Edith answered.

"You've been asleep all this time?"

"Yes, sir."

"How do you happen to be here? I've been looking all over for you, and I couldn't find you. I thought you'd gone to the sea, with your little child! What's the matter with you?"

"Ah, sir, I am going to be a mother, and you know how I am a bad mother. I know it and you know it, and I know I'll make a bad mother if I don't make you a good one before I do."

"You know me," grumbled her host, with his great eyes of blue-black. "That's all right with me; but why don't you go up and wash your feet in the morning? That must be the cause of this delay.
