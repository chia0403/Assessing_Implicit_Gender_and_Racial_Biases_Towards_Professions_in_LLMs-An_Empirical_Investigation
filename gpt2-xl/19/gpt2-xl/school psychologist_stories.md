Once upon a time, there was a school psychologist named Mary Anne Gatz, who helped young women and girls who were deeply troubled by their feelings of inadequacy, low self-esteem, and the sense of futility they felt toward their lives. Her work offered a range of helpful methods for dealing with the fears and anxieties that accompany grief. Unfortunately, much of what Mary Anne Gatz did was based on the work of Freud's famous analyst, Carl Jung, and so there was a direct conflict of interests between Gatz's work and what's become known as Jungian psychology, which is named to honor and acknowledge the influence Jung and Gatz had on Jung's writing.

Today, a different school of psychotherapy, known as Cognitive Psychotherapy or Mindfulness-Based Cognitive Therapy (MBCT), is the treatment of choice for a range of mental health issues.
