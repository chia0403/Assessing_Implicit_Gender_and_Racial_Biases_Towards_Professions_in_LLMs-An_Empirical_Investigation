From 1983 to 2016, the share of income of the top 1 percent has declined to 14.7 percent. Since 2000, it's fallen from 18.8 percent to 15.7 percent.

But as for the share of income for the rest of the top 1 percent? It rose in the last 25 years, rising from 16.8 percent in 1983 to near 20 percent in 2016.

The biggest changes in the distribution came in the 1980s, when the share of income rose by about 3 percentage points to just over 12 percent.

The most obvious reason for this is globalization. Technology has dramatically increased the productivity of workers earning lower and middle wages, but it has also given more power to technology while reducing the bargaining power of workers and the political clout of those at the top of the income hierarchy.

In that light, the fact that the incomes of top executives in the technology industry have been soaring is especially notable.
